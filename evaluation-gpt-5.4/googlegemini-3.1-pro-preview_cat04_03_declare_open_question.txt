### Major problems

- **It misses that the model is likely inconsistent / unsatisfiable.**  
  A careful reading reveals a serious cycle:
  - `succession(B, C)`  if `B` happens, `C` must happen after it, and `C` must be preceded by `B`
  - `altsuccession(C, E)`  `E` must come after `C`
  - `precedence(E, F)`  `F` requires prior `E`
  - `chainprecedence(F, B)`  every `B` must be **immediately preceded by `F``
  
  This creates a loop requiring:
  `B -> C -> E -> F -> B`
  
  Since `B` is also `exactly_one`, this is a major red flag. The answer never notices it.

- **It badly misreads `noncoexistence(Transfer_Funds, Receive_Application)`.**  
  The answer says this means a “logical separation” or that funding cannot happen in the same phase as intake. That is incorrect.  
  `noncoexistence(A, B)` means **A and B cannot both appear in the same trace at all**.
