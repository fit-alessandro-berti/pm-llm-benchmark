### 5. Several KPI definitions are weak or incorrect
This is a major scoring issue.

The answer defines:

This is poorly defined and dimensionally awkward. Usually one would use:
- fuel per km,
- fuel per stop,

Also, the scenario does **not explicitly provide fuel measurements**, so a strong answer should explain that fuel would need to be:
- directly integrated from telematics/canbus,
- estimated from speed/idling/vehicle type,
- or treated as a proxy metric.

#### Vehicle utilization
It defines utilization as:

> Total Driving Time / Total Shift Time

That is simplistic and arguably misleading in last-mile logistics, because **service time at customer stops is productive work**, not non-utilization. A better definition would distinguish:
- productive route time,
- non-productive idle time,
- maintenance downtime,
- capacity utilization,
- route completion utilization.

#### Traffic delay frequency
Using:

> (# Low-Speed Events) / (Total Travel Segments)

is too naive. Low speed in urban delivery can reflect:
- congestion,
- parking search,
- legal low-speed streets,
- turning,
- customer approach.

This would need a contextual baseline.

#### Average time per delivery stop
The answer uses:
> Total Service Time / # Successful Deliveries

This excludes failed deliveries and may distort actual stop performance. Also, if multiple packages are delivered at one stop, the denominator becomes problematic.

These KPI issues materially reduce the score.
