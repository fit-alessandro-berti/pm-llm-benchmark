## Major technical issues

### 2. Several definitions are incorrect or sloppy
Under a strict grading standard, these matter a lot.

#### a) Makespan is misdefined
The answer says:
> “Makespan (per horizon): distribution of completion times across jobs processed in a period...”

That is not a correct definition of makespan.  
**Makespan** is typically the elapsed time from the start of the first job to the completion of the last job in the schedule/horizon, not a distribution of completion times.

That is a substantive scheduling error.

#### b) Tardiness is misdefined
It states:
> “Tardiness \(T_j\) = completion time – due date”

That is **lateness**, not tardiness.  
True tardiness is:

\[
T_j = \max(0, C_j - d_j)
\]

This is a basic scheduling distinction. In a question centered on tardiness, getting this wrong is a serious flaw.

#### c) “EWT (evident work time?)”

> “EWT (evident work time?) optionally...”

That reads like uncertainty or improvisation. It is not professionally acceptable in an answer that is supposed to be authoritative and “senior analyst” level.

#### d) Several formulas are incomplete / malformed
Examples:

That is not a clean definition of blocking. If a job is in queue before processing, that is queue waiting for the current resource, not necessarily downstream blocking.
