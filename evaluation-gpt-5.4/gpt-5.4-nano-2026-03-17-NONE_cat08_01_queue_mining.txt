## Why it is not higher

### 1. Queue-time definition is useful but somewhat oversimplified
The answer defines waiting time as:

\[
\text{Wait}(A \rightarrow B)= t_{start}(B)-t_{complete}(A)
\]

That is a reasonable first approximation, but in strict terms it is **not always the true queue time**. It is really **inter-activity idle time** from the patient perspective, which may include:
- clinically necessary delays,
- batching delays,
- scheduled delays,
- patient-caused delays,
- administrative latency not caused by a resource queue.

A stronger answer would explicitly distinguish:
- **patient waiting time**,
- **queueing for a constrained resource**,
- **clinically intended delay**,
- **external/off-process delay**,
- and ideally the notion of **enabled time** versus mere next-event time.

The answer hints at this, but not rigorously enough.

### 2. It does not fully leverage the start/complete timestamps for service vs wait decomposition
A high-scoring answer should explicitly show that the log allows decomposition into:
- **service time** = complete - start for an activity,
- **waiting time before service** = start(current) - complete(previous),
- **overall flow time / sojourn time** = final complete - initial start.

This is implied, but not made analytically explicit enough.
