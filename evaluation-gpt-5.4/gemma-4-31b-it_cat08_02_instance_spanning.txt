#### Problems

A case could wait after picking for many reasons: labor shortage, prioritization, missing materials, shift breaks, etc. The answer should have described **queue reconstruction**, **resource occupancy timelines**, and preferably **conditional comparison** against similar non-cold orders or non-saturated intervals.

  In the identification subsection, it proposes:
  > time delta between the last order in a batch completing QC and the first order in that batch starting Shipping Label Generation  
  That is not the main waiting experienced by orders. The key delay is usually **for each order**:
  `Shipping Label Generation start - QC complete`,  
  or equivalently wait until batch release. The proposed batch-level delta misses the waiting of early-completing orders.

- **Priority/preemption detection is weak and possibly incorrect.**  
  The answer says it will look for a standard order in packing, then an express order starts packing at the same resource, and the standard completion is pushed back.  
  This is problematic:
  - With only START/COMPLETE events, actual pause/resume may not be visible.

  - The answer does not explain how preemption is inferred if there are no suspend/resume timestamps.
  - It also limits the issue to packing, though the scenario says express may preempt at a step such as packing, implying broader logic.

- **HazMat metric is too weak.**  
  ōAvg wait time for HazMat vs Non-HazMatö is not an adequate measure of the regulatory limitÆs impact. That comparison is heavily confounded by different order mixes and process paths. Better metrics would include:
  - blocked-start count due to active HazMat count = 10,

  The answer treats:
  - STARTCOMPLETE as within-instance,
  - COMPLETEnext START as between-instance.  
  But that is not enough in this scenario:
  - Preemption can inflate STARTCOMPLETE duration due to between-instance interference.
  - Some waiting is internal to an activity due to suspension, not visible as pure inter-activity gap.
  - Proper attribution needs **state reconstruction**, **resource occupancy context**, and ideally **counterfactual or matched-case analysis**.

**Verdict for section 1:** conceptually correct, methodologically underdeveloped.
