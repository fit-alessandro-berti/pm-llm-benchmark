## Major weaknesses

### 1. Waiting time is defined too simplistically
It defines waiting time as:

> `Timestamp(Activity_B_START) - Timestamp(Activity_A_COMPLETE)`

That is often usable, but it is **not generally correct in process mining terms**. The relevant notion is usually the time from when activity B becomes **enabled / ready to start** until it actually starts. In real outpatient processes:
- there may be **parallel branches**
- an activity may require **multiple predecessors**
- there may be **rework loops**
- ōprevious activityö may not be uniquely defined

The answer never acknowledges this. That is a notable conceptual omission in a question explicitly about **queue mining**.

### 2. It confuses different kinds of delay
The answer treats all gaps as queue time, but in practice gaps may include:
- true waiting for a resource
- transport/movement time
- synchronization delay
- clinical review delay
- administrative hold
- patient-caused delay

A strong answer should distinguish **patient waiting**, **resource waiting**, and **process idle/synchronization time**. This one does not.
