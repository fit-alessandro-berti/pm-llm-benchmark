### Why the score is not higher
#### 1. It does **not actually define “significantly longer” rigorously**
The prompt explicitly asks for cases that are significantly longer than others. The answer says “we can look for outliers” but never computes even a simple benchmark such as:
- average cycle time,
- median cycle time,
- deviation from the fast cases.

That weakens the core conclusion.

#### 2. It **overinterprets inter-event times**
This is the biggest methodological issue.

The answer repeatedly treats intervals like:

- `Investigate Issue -> Resolve Ticket`
- `Assign -> Escalate`

as if they were actual activity durations or definite delays.

But from the event log, we only know **timestamps of events**, not whether the elapsed time was:
- active work,
- queueing,
- waiting overnight,
- dependency on the customer,
- batching,
- or something else.

So statements like “the investigation takes 19 hours” are too strong. More accurate wording would be “there is a 19-hour elapsed time between investigation and resolution.”

The answer states:

> “The 'Investigate Issue' activity for the slower cases (102, 104, 105) takes a considerable amount of time, ranging from 19 to 39 hours...”

So “19 to 39 hours” is simply wrong.
