### Why it is not higher

  Defining queue time as `start(next) - complete(previous)` is acceptable as a first approximation, but in a real outpatient setting this can mix:
  - true waiting,
  - transport/movement time,
  - deliberate clinical delay,
  - undocumented interim activities.  
  The answer acknowledges overlap somewhat, but does not rigorously separate these issues.

- **Quantified impacts are somewhat speculative**.  
  Claims like “20–30% reduction” or “15–25% reduction” are plausible, but not justified from the analysis presented. In a strict evaluation, unsupported numeric expectations should be penalized.

- **Queue identification is good, but not fully operationalized**.  
  It does not go deeply into how to reconstruct the “next activity” when there are:
  - parallel branches,
  - repeated activities,
  - missing lifecycle events,
  - diagnostic subflows,
  - multiple candidate successors.

- **Limited discussion of data quality and preprocessing**, which is important in real process mining:
  - missing timestamps,
  - clock inconsistencies,
  - staff entering events late,
  - ambiguous resource labels,
  - whether START reflects actual patient-facing start or delayed documentation.

  It mentions quality and urgency, but does not deeply discuss clinical risk of flow redesigns such as pre-ordering diagnostics or fast-tracking.
