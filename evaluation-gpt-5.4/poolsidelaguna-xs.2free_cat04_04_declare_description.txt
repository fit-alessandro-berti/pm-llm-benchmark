Major problems:

- It misinterprets several core DECLARE semantics:
  - `response` does not mean “immediately after.”
  - `coexistence` does not mean “alongside” or “parallel processing”; it only means if one occurs, the other must also occur.
  - `responded_existence` does not mean the target happens after the source; only that it happens somewhere if the source happens.
  - `chainprecedence(Authorize_Contract_Terms, Preliminary_Credit_Check)` was badly misread as if it supported authorization before transfer. It actually says every `Preliminary_Credit_Check` must be immediately preceded by `Authorize_Contract_Terms`.
  - `chainsuccession(Quality_Assurance_Review, Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package)` was inconsistently described.

- It misses the biggest issue: the model appears inconsistent / likely unsatisfiable.
  - `Receive_Application` must occur (`existence`, `init`).
  - `Gather_Additional_Documents` must occur (`existence`).
  - By `coexistence`, that forces `Authorize_Contract_Terms`.
  - By `precedence`, that forces `Quality_Assurance_Review` before authorization.
