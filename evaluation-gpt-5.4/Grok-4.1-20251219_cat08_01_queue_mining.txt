The answer assumes a clean, strictly sequential “previous activity  next activity” structure. In real outpatient logs:

- paths may **branch**,

- there may be **rework**,
- there may be multiple resources involved,

A stronger answer should explicitly mention:
- how to pair START/COMPLETE events reliably,
- how to handle missing timestamps,
- how to treat parallel or overlapping activities,

That omission is important in a process mining answer.

It says “percentage of cases that go through this transition and experience any wait (>0 minutes).” But in practice, almost every inter-activity gap will be >0. A more useful definition would distinguish:
- any wait,
- clinically relevant wait,

- cumulative contribution of that queue to total visit delay.

As written, this metric is not very informative.
