Main problems:

- It does not actually build full activity sequences per case.
  - The so-called recursive CTE is not recursive in the needed sense.

- The `UNION ALL` in `absolute_sequences` is broken.
  - `seq_base` returns columns in the order `(case_id, activity, rn, sequence)`.
  - `seq_recursive` returns `(case_id, rn, activity, next_sequence)`.
  - Those positions do not match, so the query is logically wrong and likely type-invalid.

- The recursive syntax is malformed / misleading.
  - It uses `WITH RECURSIVE` but does not define a proper recursive relation that references itself to keep extending sequences until the end of the case.

- `full_sequences` is unreliable.
  - It depends on the broken `absolute_sequences`.
  - The correlated subquery is also unclear and fragile as written.

- The answer ignores a much simpler and correct DuckDB approach.
  - DuckDB supports ordered aggregation such as `string_agg(activity, '->' ORDER BY timestamp)` or list aggregation, which is the natural solution here.

- Several explanatory claims are inaccurate.
  - “Handles large datasets efficiently” is not justified.
  - “recursive CTE optimization” is misleading here.
  - “Works with DuckDB's native CTAS capabilities” is irrelevant.
