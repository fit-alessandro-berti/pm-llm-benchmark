### 5. Several KPI definitions are poor or incorrect
This is a major scoring issue.

#### Incorrect or problematic KPI formulations:
- **Fuel Consumption per km/package**  
  The formula given is dimensionally unclear and awkward:
  > Fuel Used / (Total Distance / Number of Packages)

  That is not a standard or clean KPI definition. Better would be:
  - fuel per km,
  - fuel per delivered package,
  - estimated fuel burn during idle/travel/service.
  
  Also, the scenario does not explicitly provide direct fuel data, so the answer should have acknowledged fuel may need to be **estimated** from mileage, speed, idling, engine status, or external fuel records.
  
- **Vehicle Utilization Rate**  
  > Total Distance Traveled / (Vehicle Capacity × Shift Duration)

  This is not a valid utilization formula. It mixes incompatible units. Vehicle utilization should relate to:
  - active time / available time,
  - load factor / capacity,
  - packages carried / capacity over route segments,
  - distance or time in productive motion vs idle/depot time.

- **Traffic Delay Frequency**
  > # Low-Speed Events / Total Travel Time

  This depends heavily on event sampling frequency and is not robust unless normalized very carefully. It is not wrong as a rough proxy, but it is weak and underspecified.

- **Average Time per Delivery Stop**
  The formula conflates travel and service in a way that makes the label misleading. If it includes travel + service, it is not purely “per stop service time.”

These are not minor issues. KPI design is central to the assignment.
