### Why it is not higher

#### Major conceptual issue
- The biggest flaw is in **Section 1.2**: the proposed ìresource-specific waitingî formula  
  \[
  t^{start}_r(curr)-t^{complete}_r(prev)
  \]
  is **not resource queueing**; it is essentially **resource idle time / inter-job gap** for that resource.  
  That is a significant conceptual mistake in a question specifically about **queue mining**. Queueing at the resource level should be about **cases waiting for a constrained resource**, not the time a resource waits between jobs.

#### Some methodological imprecision
- The answer treats queue time mainly as the gap between **completion of A** and **start of B**, which is reasonable, but it does not sufficiently distinguish:
  - **patient waiting time**,
  - **process delay / inactivity time**,
  - **resource-induced waiting**, and
  - **synchronization delays in parallel paths**.
- It gestures at parallelism and causality, but remains somewhat generic. In a healthcare setting, this distinction matters a lot.

#### ìData-drivenî optimization is not always truly data-driven here
- The proposed improvement percentages (e.g. **20ñ40%**, **15ñ30%**) are presented as examples, but they are still **speculative** and not derived from the described data.
- A stricter answer would have emphasized:
  - simulation,
  - counterfactual replay,
  - before/after causal evaluation,
  - or statistically comparing cohorts/peak periods.

#### Missing or underdeveloped points
- It does not explicitly discuss **queue length**, **queue buildup over time**, or **WIP accumulation**, which are central queue-mining concepts.
- Appointment scheduling is mentioned, but the answer could have gone deeper into:

  - specialty-specific appointment templates,
