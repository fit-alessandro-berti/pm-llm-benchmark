## Why it is **not** a high-score answer under strict marking

### 1. Several metrics are oversimplified or not rigorously defined
A number of definitions are too loose for a “comprehensive, data-driven” answer:

- **FCR definition is weak/incomplete.**
  - You define FCR as cases with `"Work L1 End"` followed by `"Ticket Resolved"` and no escalation.
  - In practice, FCR is often channel-dependent and should distinguish:
    - resolved on first contact
    - resolved by L1 without reassignment
    - resolved without reopen
  - Your definition ignores reopened tickets and assumes a specific event pattern that may not exist in the log.

- **Skill match ratio is underdefined.**
  - You write `Required Skill  Agent Skills`, but:
    - this only works if skill recording is reliable and normalized,
    - it ignores proficiency level,
    - it ignores cases where the “required skill” is wrong or evolves during diagnosis,
    - it does not distinguish assignment-time match vs. eventual actual need.
  - That matters a lot in this scenario.

- **Occupancy rate** and **concurrent ticket load** are suggested, but the answer does not explain how to handle:
  - multitasking
  - overlapping tickets
  - waiting while “owned”
  - non-ticket work
  - agent schedule/calendar gaps
  These are nontrivial and affect the validity of conclusions.
