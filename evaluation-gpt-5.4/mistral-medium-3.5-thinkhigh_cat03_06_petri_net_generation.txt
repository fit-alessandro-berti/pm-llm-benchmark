The final answer is usable and mostly follows the requested PM4Py construction style, but under a strict evaluation it has important modeling errors:

- It correctly defines a Petri net, places, transitions, arcs, and valid `im` / `fm`.
- It correctly models the main sequence and includes loops for `RDS` and `II`.

However, there are significant flaws:

1. **Approval is modeled as always mandatory**, while the scenario says it is only required **if the amount surpasses a threshold**. A stricter model should include a bypass/skipping path.
2. **The `II` loop is semantically wrong**:  
   `ii -> after_dv` sends the token back to a place that also enables `RDS`. That means after an in-depth investigation, the model incorrectly allows a jump into the document re-submission branch.  
   A separate place before `FC` should have been used.
3. Because of that shared place design, the net allows **extra behaviors not described in the scenario**.

So while the answer is decent structurally and syntactically, it is not close to flawless from a process-modeling perspective.
