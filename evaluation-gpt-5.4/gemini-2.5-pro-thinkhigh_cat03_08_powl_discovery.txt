### Main problem
The proposed assembly model **`*(AS, IQC)` is wrong for the observed behavior**.

- In POWL, `*(A, B)` means:
  - execute `A`,
  - then either exit,
  - or execute `B` and then `A` again,
  - repeating this pattern.

So `*(AS, IQC)` can generate:
- `AS`
- `AS, IQC, AS`
- `AS, IQC, AS, IQC, AS`
- etc.

But it **cannot** generate:
- `AS, IQC, IQC, AS`

Yet the log explicitly contains this pattern in **Case 3**:
- `AS, IQC, IQC, AS, IQC, AS`

The answer even claims that `*(AS, IQC)` “correctly produces” `AS -> IQC -> IQC -> AS`, which is **semantically false**. That is the biggest issue, and it is not a minor one: it means the proposed POWL model does **not** fit the given log.
