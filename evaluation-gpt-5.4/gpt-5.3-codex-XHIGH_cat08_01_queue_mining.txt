## Why the score is not higher
### 1. Queue definition is somewhat oversimplified
The answer defines waiting time as:

\[
WT(c,A\rightarrow B)=\max(0, t_{start}(c,B)-t_{complete}(c,A))
\]

This is a **reasonable operational approximation**, but it is **not always equivalent to a queue**. In outpatient care, time between activities may include:
- deliberate clinical delays,
- patient-driven delays,
- transport/movement time,
- batching or scheduled gaps,
- prerequisite dependencies,
- asynchronous subprocesses.

So the answer treats all inter-activity idle time as “queue time” too casually. A stronger answer would explicitly distinguish:
- **patient idle time**,
- **actual waiting-for-resource time**,
- **medically necessary delay**,
- **routing/transition time**.

That is a meaningful conceptual gap in a queue-mining question.

### 2. It does not adequately handle process complexity
The answer assumes a fairly neat sequence of activities. It does **not discuss**:
- repeated activities,
- skipped activities,
- parallel tasks,
- overlapping timestamps,
- missing START/COMPLETE pairs,
- multiple resources per activity,
- branch-specific pathways,
- appointment time vs actual arrival time.

Those are important in real clinic logs and matter directly for queue inference.

The answer sounds confident where it should be more careful.
