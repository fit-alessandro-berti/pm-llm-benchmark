7.1/10

Strong overall, but not close to flawless.

Why it loses points:
- **`FOCUS` is repeatedly mislabeled as `Open_*`**. A focus event does not mean the file was opened. This is the biggest issue. In particular, `Quarterly_Report.docx` gets `Open_Document` twice, even though there is no intervening close before the second focus.
- **Case logic is reasonable but not fully rigorous**. Grouping by document/task is sensible, but the handling of `SWITCH` events is somewhat arbitrary and inconsistent in analytical value.
- **Activity abstraction is only partly standardized**. Some labels are generic (`Edit_Document`), others are highly specific (`Insert_Row`, `Update_Figures`), so the abstraction level is uneven.
- **`SWITCH_Application` is not really a higher-level business activity**. It remains close to the raw UI event and adds noise unless modeled more carefully.
- **Loss of source context on switch events**. Since `SWITCH` is retained, omitting `from_app` / `from_window` weakens traceability.
- **Narrative is good, but slightly overstates certainty** where the log only supports inference.

What it does well:
- Includes the required core attributes.
- Preserves timestamps correctly.
- Produces a coherent and analyst-friendly case structure.
- Explanation of grouping logic is clear and mostly sensible.
- The resulting log is usable for process mining, even if not optimally modeled.

So: **good and useful, but with important semantic inaccuracies in event interpretation, especially around `FOCUS` and repeated “open” activities.**
