Strong coverage, but not close to flawless.

Main deductions:
- One anomaly explanation is logically weak: saying **RP is “far shorter than RE + EP”** is unsupported because **EP is not in the model**.
- Several SQL queries are **technically loose**:
  - They do not enforce **timestamp ordering** (`p.timestamp > r.timestamp`, etc.).
  - They can produce **many-to-many pairings** if a claim has multiple events of the same activity.
  - Query 7 uses `MAX(...)` per activity, which is often the **wrong timestamp choice** for process-pair timing.
- Query 6 relies on a **speculative join**: `resource = adjuster_id::text`. The schema does not establish that mapping.
- Some thresholds are **arbitrary** rather than derived directly from the profile (especially the AC check at 30 minutes).
- The helper query is unused.
- Minor clarity/formatting issues reduce quality.

What it does well:
- Correctly identifies the main suspicious pairs.
- Hypotheses are mostly plausible.
- Verification approach is directionally good and relevant to the schema.
- Includes useful checks for skipped steps and segmentation by claim type/region.

So: good and useful, but with enough analytical and SQL rigor issues that, under a very strict standard, it should score only in the low 6s.
