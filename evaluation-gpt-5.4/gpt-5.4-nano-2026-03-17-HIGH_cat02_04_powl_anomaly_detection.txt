That is not a reliable reading of pm4py loop behavior. In standard pm4py/process-tree-style loop semantics, with `LOOP(A, I)`, the first child (`A`) is typically the **mandatory ‘do’ part**, and the second child (`I`) is the **redo part**. So the behavior is closer to:

- `A`
- `A, I, A`
- `A, I, A, I, A`
- etc.

That means:
- **A is not optional**
- the loop does **not** simply mean repeating `A, I` as a block
