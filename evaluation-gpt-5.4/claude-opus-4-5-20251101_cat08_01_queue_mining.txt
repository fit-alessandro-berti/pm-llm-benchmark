### 1. The queue-time definition is too simplistic
The answer defines waiting time as:

> Start Timestamp of B - Complete Timestamp of A

That is only valid in simple strictly sequential cases. In real process mining, the relevant concept is usually **time from enabling of the next activity to its start**, not merely “the previous activity completed.” In outpatient settings, this distinction matters because:

- activities may run in parallel,
- there may be multiple possible predecessors,
- loops/rework can occur,
- some delays are clinically necessary, not queueing,
- the patient may not be “ready” immediately after the previous completion.

The answer briefly mentions parallel activities and data quality issues, but it still operationalizes queueing mainly as a naive consecutive-activity subtraction. That is a significant analytical weakness for a question explicitly about process mining and queue mining.

- scheduled intentional delays.

The response should have emphasized the need to distinguish:
- **service time**,
- **queue time**,
