#### 1. The loop semantics are described too loosely
The answer says the loop allows arbitrary repetitions of “evaluateapproveevaluate…,” which is only approximately true. In process-tree/POWL loop semantics, `LOOP(E, P)` means:
- `E` happens at least once
- `P` is the redo part
- traces look like `E`, `E P E`, `E P E P E`, etc.

That means a very important anomaly is missed: **the process can exit after `E` without any `P` at all, and every `P` is followed by another `E`**. The answer notices repeated approvals, but not this deeper semantic issue.

Because `C` is only constrained after `A`, it is not merely “before N” or “before E/P”; it is effectively allowed **concurrently with the whole remainder after `A`**, which is stronger and should have been stated more precisely.
