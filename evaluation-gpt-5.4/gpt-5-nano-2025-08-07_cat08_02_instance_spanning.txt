## Major problems lowering the score

### 1. Several metrics are imprecise or incorrectly defined
A lot of the answer sounds right at a high level, but the definitions are often not rigorous enough for actual process mining.

Examples:
- **Cold-packing wait time** is given as:
  - `actual_packing_start_time earliest_available_cp_time`
  - This is not even written correctly mathematically, and more importantly it is conceptually incomplete.
  - The relevant waiting time should depend on both:
    - when the order became **ready** for packing, and
    - when a cold station became available.
  - Otherwise you risk attributing upstream readiness delay to resource contention.

- **Batch formation delay** is described partly as from “first order in a region is ready” to “last order in the batch completes packing and then shipping label is generated.”
  - That is too simplistic and partly wrong.
  - The actual prerequisite is likely readiness after **quality check**, not packing.
  - Also, batches may be released by policy triggers, shipment cutoff, or route optimization—not necessarily “last order completes.”

- **HM compliance tracking** is proposed via “time windows (e.g., 15–60 minutes).”
  - This is a serious weakness.
  - The constraint is **simultaneous concurrency**, so checking coarse windows can easily miss brief but real violations.
  - Proper analysis should be event-based / interval-based over continuous time.

  - But the provided log does not show explicit “pause/resume” events.
  - In many event logs, preemption is not directly recorded.

> “re-assign to a non-Cold-Packing route within acceptable SLA”

This is problematic:

- Mid-packing **preemption** may be operationally infeasible, wasteful, or even quality/safety violating.
