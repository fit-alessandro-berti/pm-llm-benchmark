The final answer has the right high-level idea, but it contains a major SQL flaw that makes it not a valid DuckDB solution as written:

- It uses `LAG(...)` and `LEAD(...)` directly in the `WHERE` clause. Window functions are not allowed there in DuckDB/standard SQL; they must be computed in a subquery/CTE or filtered with `QUALIFY`.
- Because of that, the core filtering logic is not executable in the form provided.

Minor points:
- The overall logic for detecting direct succession is conceptually correct.
- Excluding matching `case_id`s from the final result is also conceptually correct.
- `ORDER BY case_id, timestamp` is reasonable.

But under a strict grading standard, a query that is fundamentally invalid SQL cannot score well, even if the intended approach is sound.
