### Major problems

   - This is the biggest issue.
   - In pm4py/process-tree-style semantics, `LOOP(A, I)` is not “approval and invoicing can repeat any number of times, including zero.”
   - The usual semantics are closer to:
     **`A (I A)*`**
   - That means:
     - `Approve_Order` happens **at least once**
     - `Issue_Invoice` can happen **zero or more times**
     - every invoice is followed by another approval
   - So the answer’s claim that the loop allows both steps to repeat “including zero” is technically wrong.

2. **Misses the most important anomaly implied by the LOOP**
   - Because `LOOP(A, I)` can terminate right after `A`, the model allows:

   - So the process can reach **payment and closure without any invoice ever being issued**.
   - That is a major anomaly, arguably more direct than the “multiple approvals/invoices” framing, and the answer does not clearly identify it.
