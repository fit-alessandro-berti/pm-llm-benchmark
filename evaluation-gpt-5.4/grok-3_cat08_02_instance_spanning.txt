This is one of the most important parts of the prompt, and the answer handles it weakly.

It says roughly:
- within-instance = activity duration,
- between-instance = waiting between previous completion and next start.

That is **too crude and sometimes wrong**.

Why:
- Long activity duration can itself be caused by **between-instance interference**, especially under preemption.
- Waiting between activities is not automatically due to inter-case constraints; it may be due to **case-specific routing, rework, missing information, internal approval, or operational delay unrelated to other instances**.
- To distinguish properly, one needs concepts like:
  - **enabled time**
  - **queue entry time**
  - **resource-available time**
  - **batch-ready time**
  - **constraint-induced waiting attribution**
  - decomposition of elapsed time into service, idle, blocked, queued, and interrupted time
