### 2. Queue calculation is oversimplified and partly flawed
The answer says:

> “Sort the activities by their start timestamps.”

That is **not robust** for queue mining in event logs with both START and COMPLETE timestamps. In practice, for each case, one must:
- pair START/COMPLETE events per activity instance,
- reconstruct actual activity intervals,
- determine the **preceding completed activity** in the realized path,
- calculate waiting time as the gap between **previous completion** and **next start**.

Sorting merely by start timestamp can be misleading when:
- activities overlap,
- there are repeated activities,
- parallel branches exist,
- timestamps are inconsistent,
- urgent cases bypass steps.

A strong answer would explicitly discuss:
- **service time** = complete  start,
- **inter-activity waiting time** = next start  previous complete,
- how to handle **parallelism**, **negative gaps**, **missing timestamps**, **rework**, and **path variability**.
