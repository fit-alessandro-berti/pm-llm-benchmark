## Why it is not higher

### 1. Queue identification: mostly correct, but not rigorous enough
**What it gets right**

- Correctly defines **inter-activity waiting time** as `next start - previous complete`.
- Includes sensible queue metrics like mean, median, and P90.

**Problems**
- The mean waiting time formula uses `|C|` as denominator, which is **not correct** unless every case experiences that exact transition once. It should be the number of observed instances of that transition.
- It assumes clean sequentiality too casually. In real outpatient logs, there are:
  - missing starts/completes,
  - repeated activities,
  - rework loops,
  - interruptions,
  - overlapping timestamps,
  - unscheduled detours.
  None of this is addressed.
- “If parallel pathways exist … use earliest completed preceding dependency” is asserted without explaining how dependencies are inferred from the log. That is a **major methodological gap**.
- Queue frequency defined as waits `> 5 mins` is **arbitrary** and ungrounded.
- Use of Little’s Law for average queue length is not wrong in principle, but the answer treats it too casually. It requires more careful assumptions about stationarity and arrival/service definitions than the answer acknowledges.
- The “Queue Criticality Index” is invented and plausible, but the weights are unexplained and there is no discussion of validation or sensitivity analysis.
