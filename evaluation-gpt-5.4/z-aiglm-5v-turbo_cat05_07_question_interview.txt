Strong answer overall: it is well-structured, relevant, and asks many genuinely useful clarification questions across the full process.

Why it is not higher, under a strict standard:

- Several questions are not fully open-ended; they are framed as forced alternatives, e.g. “is it X or Y,” which narrows the interviewee instead of inviting fuller explanation.
- A few questions drift into implementation/operational detail rather than conceptual process understanding, such as:
  - intake via “portal, email, or phone,”
  - “automated status change in the CRM,”
  - “rigid scoring matrix or algorithm,”
  - “rollback procedure.”
- Some questions are a bit leading or presuppose internal mechanisms that were never mentioned, which can bias the interview rather than clarify it.
- There is slight overemphasis on edge mechanics and policy enforcement, while some broader process-understanding questions are missing, such as:
  - typical timelines/SLAs by stage,
  - who owns the process end-to-end,
  - what the main failure points or bottlenecks are,
  - how communication is coordinated across stakeholders,
  - how often exceptions occur and how they are tracked.
- A few questions are more compliance/legal-detail-heavy than necessary for a general conceptual clarification pass.

What it does well:

- Covers the major phases of the process.
- Targets decision criteria, sequencing, role clarity, and exceptions.
- Avoids SQL and technical implementation in the narrow sense.
- Contains multiple high-value questions, especially around manager assignment, remediation authority, tenant selection, and audit timing.

So: good and useful, but not nearly flawless.
