Why it is not higher:

- It identifies the main anomalies correctly and gives reasonable hypotheses.
- The SQL is substantial and mostly relevant.

Main flaws:

  - `(A, C)` uses `< 7200` seconds, which is just the mean, not a deviation threshold.
- Some correlation logic is technically weak:
  - `MIN(resource)` is not the resource associated with the earliest event; it is lexicographically minimal, which is incorrect.

- The answer sometimes overstates conclusions, e.g. “bypassing Evaluate/Approve” from timing alone, when the profile itself does not prove missing steps.
- The “generic deviation detector” is described as parameterized but is really just a hardcoded example.
- It does not deeply address alternative explanations like logging latency, out-of-order event ingestion, or multiple event cycles per claim.

Why it is still above average:

- The structure is clear.
- The anomaly discussion is coherent.
- The verification approach is practical and mostly executable in PostgreSQL.

So: good, useful, but too many methodological and SQL-logic imperfections for a top-tier score.
