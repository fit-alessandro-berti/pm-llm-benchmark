#### 3. The within-instance vs between-instance distinction is oversimplified and partly flawed
This is one of the biggest weaknesses.

The answer says:
- within-instance delays = activity duration
- between-instance delays = waiting time between activities

That is too simplistic and often wrong.

Waiting between activities is **not automatically between-instance**. It can be caused by:
- missing information,
- internal case-specific rework,
- worker unavailability unrelated to contention,
- downstream handoff issues,
- batching,
- regulation,
- priority preemption,
- or plain scheduling policy.

Similarly, long activity duration can itself be caused by between-instance effects if the activity is interrupted, paused, or slowed by competing work.

A strong answer needed to explain attribution logic, for example:
- establish each case’s **ready time** for next activity,
- compare that with actual start time,
- reconstruct system state at that interval,
- attribute waiting to the dominant active constraint:
  - no eligible resource free,
  - batch not yet formed,
  - hazardous cap reached,
  - priority preemption occurred,
