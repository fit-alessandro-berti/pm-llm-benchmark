- wrong initial identification of skill needs
- reassignment because the needed skill changes

So metrics like:

- “skill match rate”

are potentially **circular or misleading** unless the answer explicitly says that “required skill” must be validated against the final resolution path or rederived from outcomes. It only partly acknowledges this later.

### 2. Some “data-driven” proposals risk encoding current bad behavior
The answer repeatedly suggests deriving baselines from historical successful cases, for example:
- lowest tier that resolved it successfully within SLA
- inferred proficiency from historical speed and outcomes
- routing based on who historically resolved similar tickets

This is practical, but under strict evaluation it has a flaw: it may **bake in existing inefficiencies, selection bias, or informal hero-routing**. The answer should have discussed this risk explicitly.

### 3. Causality is sometimes overstated
The answer suggests outputs such as:
- “Each reassignment increases breach odds by X%”
- “Skill mismatch adds median +Y hours”

These are fine as statistical findings, but the wording sometimes slides toward **causal interpretation** without enough caution. In real process mining, much of this would initially be **associational**, not proof of causation.
