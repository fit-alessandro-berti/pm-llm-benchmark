### 2. Some terminology and definitions are sloppy or partly incorrect
Under strict grading, these matter.

#### Makespan definition is weak/incomplete
It says:
> Makespan = max completion time across jobs in a period.

That is not a proper definition. Makespan should be something like:
**completion time of the last job minus start time of the first job in the schedule/set considered**.  
What is written ignores the starting boundary and is therefore imprecise.

#### Lead time / flow time are treated as basically identical
The answer collapses cycle time, flow time, and lead time too casually. In practice, these may be defined differently depending on whether one includes:
- pre-release waiting,
- engineering release,
- queue time before formal release,
- customer order date vs shop-floor release date.

The prompt emphasizes unpredictability of lead times, so precision here matters.

#### “Bullwhip effect” is questionable in this context
Using “bullwhip effect in WIP” for internal shop-floor queue variability is a stretch. Bullwhip typically refers to **demand amplification across supply-chain echelons**, not primarily intra-shop scheduling variability. The underlying point is understandable, but the terminology is sloppy.
