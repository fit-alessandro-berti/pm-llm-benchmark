### Why it is not higher

#### 1. It misses a key semantic issue in the loop
The biggest problem is the handling of the `LOOP(E, P)` semantics.

In this model, the loop does **not** simply mean “evaluate and approve repeatedly.” In standard process-tree/POWL loop semantics, the first child (`E`) is the **do** part and the second child (`P`) is the **redo** part. That means the loop allows traces like:

- `E`
- `E, P, E`
- `E, P, E, P, E`
- etc.

So:

- `E` is mandatory.
- `P` is **optional**, not guaranteed.
- If `P` occurs, it is followed by another `E`.
- The loop can terminate **after an `E`**, not after a `P`.

The answer never clearly identifies this. That is a major analytical miss, because it changes the anomaly substantially: the model does not just allow repeated approvals, it can also allow **final evaluation without a final approval**.
