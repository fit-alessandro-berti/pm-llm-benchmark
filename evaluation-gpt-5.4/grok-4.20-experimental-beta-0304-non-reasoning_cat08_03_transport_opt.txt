### 3. Some process mining claims are imprecise or weakly grounded
A few examples:

- **“Parallel branches (multiple failed delivery attempts)”**  
  For a single vehicle’s route, physical delivery execution is usually mostly **sequential**, not truly parallel. This is not a fatal error, but it suggests imprecise modeling language.

- **“Extra cases: vehicles returning late or with overtime”** as a conformance deviation  
  That is not really a clean conformance category. It is more a **performance deviation** than an “extra case.”

- Conformance checking against planned routes is treated too generically.  
  A stronger answer would distinguish:
  - **control-flow conformance**: stop order, skipped stops, extra stops
  - **temporal conformance**: late arrivals, excessive dwell

That distinction is important and is mostly absent.
