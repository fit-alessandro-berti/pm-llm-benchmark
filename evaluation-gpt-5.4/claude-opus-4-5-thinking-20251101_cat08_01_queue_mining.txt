### 1. It treats “waiting time” too simplistically
The answer defines waiting time as:

> START of next activity - COMPLETE of previous activity

This is a common proxy, but it is **not always the same as actual queue time**. In outpatient settings, the elapsed time between two activities can include:

- intended delay,
- patient walking time,
- patient not being ready,
- clinical review time not represented as an activity,
- parallel work,
- off-premise waiting,
- batching delays.

A more precise answer should have clearly said this is a **proxy for inter-activity waiting**, not necessarily the exact queue duration unless an explicit “enqueue/ready” timestamp exists.

The answer gestures at edge cases, but not strongly enough. For a strict score, this is a meaningful conceptual limitation.
