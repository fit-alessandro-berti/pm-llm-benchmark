Strong overall: the final model is structured, mostly faithful to the interviews, and includes roles, tasks, gateways, artifacts, and exception paths.

Why it is not higher under a very strict standard:
- It introduces a slightly misleading end state: **“Invoice Paid”**. The interviews only support **scheduled for payment / added to payment cycle**, not actual payment execution.
- The **manager decision** is oversimplified. Carlos says he may **reject** the invoice or ask Mary to **hold/investigate**. The answer mostly collapses this into a single “manager query” loop, so a true rejection/termination path is not clearly modeled.
- The **Purchasing step** is modeled mainly as PO/invoice detail validation, but Mary explicitly frames it as confirming that **goods/services were actually received**. That important business check is somewhat blurred.
- Some exception handling is a bit too generalized. For example, Dan says Purchasing may contact the supplier directly or ask AP to hold for a corrected invoice; the answer captures this only broadly, not as a clearly differentiated branch.
- The model is good, but not fully “without ambiguity” for BPMN conversion because some return points and termination cases remain somewhat vague.

Still, it is clearly above average and largely coherent.
