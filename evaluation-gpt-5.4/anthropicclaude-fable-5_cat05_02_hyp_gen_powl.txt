2. Q3 is logically weak  
   The “skipped notification” query checks whether a claim ever had a notification, not whether notification occurred in the proper place in the process. A claim notified after closure would not be counted as skipped, even though that still violates the intended flow.

3. Q6 has a significant flaw  
   It uses:
   ```sql
   JOIN adjusters a ON a.name = ce.resource
   ```
   This is fragile and potentially wrong:
   - `resource` may not match `adjusters.name` exactly,
   - names are not guaranteed unique,
   - system-performed events would be dropped entirely.
   
   Yet the explanation claims this query could reveal system accounts like `"workflow_engine"`, which is false with an inner join to `adjusters`.

4. Some hypotheses are a bit speculative  
   The answer introduces SLA gaming and reopening behavior without direct support from the schema. Plausible, yes, but under “utmost strictness” this is somewhat overextended.
