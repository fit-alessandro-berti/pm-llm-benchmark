#### 2. “Schedule adherence” is handled imprecisely
The answer says schedule adherence can be compared using:
- planned vs actual task duration

That is not really schedule adherence. Duration accuracy is useful, but adherence should include:
- deviation of **actual start time vs scheduled start time**
- deviation of **actual completion vs scheduled completion**
- **sequence adherence** on machines
- rescheduling frequency and stability

So this part is conceptually incomplete.

is directionally useful but **too reductive**. Moderate utilization with high waiting can also come from:
- variability
- batching
- shift calendars
- operator constraints
- breakdown clustering
- routing heterogeneity
- synchronization effects

The answer states this with more certainty than is justified.
