**Major problems**

   - The prompt explicitly says activity pairs are **not necessarily directly-following, but eventually-occurring**.
   - The proposed queries do not carefully model eventual occurrence or nearest valid successor; they simply join all matching activities within a claim.

   - For **AC**, the threshold is:
     - `7200 - 2 * 3600 = 0`
   - So the query looks for `time_diff_seconds < 0`, which is nonsensical if events are properly ordered.
   - This is a serious analytical error.

4. **Verification is only partial**
   - For **AC**, the narrative says claims may be closed without evaluation/approval, but the SQL does **not** check for missing `E` or `P` between `A` and `C`.
