Several constraints are explained incorrectly:

  That is not what response means. It means: **if A occurs, then B must occur later**. It does **not** prohibit B from occurring without prior A.

- **`responded_existence(D, E)`** was described as ensuring offers are never made without **prior** QA.  
  Wrong. Responded existence only requires that if D occurs, E occurs **somewhere** in the trace, not necessarily before it.

  Wrong. Alternate succession does not mean ōdirectlyö; that would be a chain constraint.

- **`chainprecedence(F, B)`** was interpreted backwards.
