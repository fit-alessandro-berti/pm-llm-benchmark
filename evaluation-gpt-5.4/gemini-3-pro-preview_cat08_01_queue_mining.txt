### 1. Queue-time definition is too simplistic
The biggest analytical flaw is here:

> Wait(Activity N) = Start(Activity N) - Complete(Activity N-1)

That only works for a simple linear sequence. In a real outpatient clinic:
- processes are often **branching**,
- some activities are **optional**,
- there may be **parallel diagnostics**,
- the true wait should often be measured from when the next activity becomes **enabled**, not merely from the immediately previous logged completion.

A stronger answer should have explicitly said that queue time depends on the **process path / predecessor relation** and may require process discovery or transition-level analysis, not just “previous activity.” This is a meaningful methodological weakness.

These omissions matter.
