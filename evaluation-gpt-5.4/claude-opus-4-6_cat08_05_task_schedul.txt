## Significant weaknesses and errors

## 1. There is at least one clear technical error

### Earliness formula is wrong
The answer states:

- **Lateness** = Actual Completion Date  Due Date
- **Tardiness** = max(0, Lateness)
- **Earliness** = max(0, Lateness)

That last formula is incorrect. Earliness should be something like:
- `Earliness = max(0, -Lateness)`, or
- `Earliness = max(0, Due Date - Completion Date)`

This is not a tiny typo in context; it is a basic scheduling metric error in a section explicitly about measuring schedule adherence. Under strict grading, that matters a lot.

## 5. The theoretical minimum flow time logic is too simplistic
In Section 3.5, the answer suggests computing a “theoretical minimum flow time” as processing plus minimum possible setup times with no waiting, and treating the gap to actual as scheduling-recoverable time.

That is conceptually weak in a multi-resource job shop:
- waiting cannot be eliminated because of resource contention,
- overlapping demands and release patterns create unavoidable queues,
- minimum setup path may be incompatible with due dates and job release times,
- disruptions and finite capacity make “no waiting” an unrealistic lower bound.

This makes the decomposition into “recoverable” vs. “capacity-driven” time much too naive. A better approach would be simulation- or optimization-based counterfactual bounds.
