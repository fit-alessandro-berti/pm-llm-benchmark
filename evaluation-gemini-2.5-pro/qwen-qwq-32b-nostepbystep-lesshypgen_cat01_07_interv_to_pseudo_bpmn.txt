**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval**

**Process Overview:** This model describes the process for approving supplier invoices based on interviews with Accounts Payable (AP), Purchasing, Management, and related roles.

**Roles/Participants:**
*   Accounts Payable Clerk (AP Clerk)
*   Purchasing Officer
*   Department Manager
*   Accounts Payable Supervisor (AP Supervisor)
*   Supplier Relationship Manager (involved in resolution)

**Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs (Key examples):**
*   Supplier Invoice (Input)
*   Purchase Order (PO)
*   Accounting System Record
*   Goods/Service Receipt Confirmation
*   Discrepancy Details
*   Approval/Rejection Notification
*   Payment Schedule Data

**Sequence Flow & Activities:**

1.  **Start Event:** Invoice Received
    *   *Trigger:* Supplier Invoice arrives (typically via email).
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk

2.  **Activity:** Check for Valid PO Number
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk verifies if the invoice references a valid Purchase Order number.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Invoice
    *   *Output:* PO Validity Status

3.  **Gateway (Exclusive):** Valid PO Present?
    *   **Condition: No** -> Proceed to Task 4 (Reject Invoice).
    *   **Condition: Yes** -> Proceed to Task 5 (Log Invoice).

4.  **Activity:** Reject Invoice / Request Details
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk sends the invoice back to the supplier requesting missing PO information or clarification. (Supplier Relationship Manager may assist if communication issues arise).
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Output:* Communication to Supplier
    *   **End Event:** Process terminates for this invoice instance. *Note: A corrected invoice would restart the process.*

5.  **Activity:** Log Invoice into Accounting System
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk enters the invoice details into the system.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Invoice with Valid PO
    *   *Output:* System Record Created

6.  **Activity:** Forward to Purchasing for Goods Receipt Confirmation
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk sends the logged invoice (or notification) to the Purchasing department.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Logged Invoice Info
    *   *Output:* Confirmation Request

7.  **Activity:** Confirm Goods/Service Receipt & Check Details
    *   *Description:* Purchasing Officer verifies that the goods/services listed on the invoice were received as per the PO details (quantities, items). *Note: Minor discrepancies found here might be handled directly by Purchasing or communicated back to AP.*
    *   *Performer:* Purchasing Officer
    *   *Input:* Invoice Info, PO Details, Receiving Records
    *   *Output:* Confirmation Status (sent back to AP)

8.  **Activity:** Match Invoice Items to PO
    *   *Description:* Upon receiving confirmation of receipt, the AP Clerk performs a detailed comparison of invoice line items (price, quantity) against the PO in the system.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Invoice, PO, Confirmation of Receipt
    *   *Output:* Matching Result (Matched/Discrepancy)

9.  **Gateway (Exclusive):** Invoice Matched to PO?
    *   **Condition: Yes** -> Proceed to Task 15 (Manager Approval). *Note: System likely triggers notification to Manager.*
    *   **Condition: No** -> Proceed to Task 10 (Resolve Discrepancy).

10. **Activity:** Resolve Discrepancy
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk initiates resolution efforts for mismatches (e.g., pricing, quantity). This involves contacting the Purchasing Officer for clarification and potentially the Supplier Relationship Manager to engage the supplier for corrections or information.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk (coordinating), Purchasing Officer, Supplier Relationship Manager
    *   *Input:* Discrepancy Details, Invoice, PO
    *   *Output:* Updated Information / Corrected Invoice / Resolution Status

11. **Gateway (Exclusive):** Discrepancy Resolved by AP/Purchasing?
    *   *Description:* Assesses if the discrepancy could be resolved through the initial clarification process (Task 10).
    *   **Condition: Yes** -> Proceed to Task 15 (Manager Approval).
    *   **Condition: No** -> Proceed to Task 12 (Escalate to Supervisor). *Note: This path is taken if initial attempts fail.*

12. **Activity:** Escalate to Supervisor
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk formally escalates the unresolved discrepancy to the AP Supervisor.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Unresolved Discrepancy Case
    *   *Output:* Escalation Request

13. **Activity:** Resolve Exception
    *   *Description:* The AP Supervisor investigates the escalated issue, coordinates between departments, contacts suppliers (potentially via Supplier Relationship Manager), or handles specific exceptions like non-PO invoices requiring special approval/retrospective POs. The goal is to clear the issue.
    *   *Performer:* AP Supervisor
    *   *Input:* Escalation Details
    *   *Output:* Resolved Invoice/Case

14. **Sequence Flow:** After successful completion of Task 13 (Resolve Exception), the process proceeds to Task 15 (Manager Approval).

15. **Activity:** Manager Approval
    *   *Description:* The Department Manager reviews the matched (or resolved) invoice, PO details, and confirmation in the system.
    *   *Performer:* Department Manager
    *   *Input:* Invoice, PO, Confirmation, Resolution Notes (if any)
    *   *Output:* Approval Decision (Approved/Rejected)

16. **Gateway (Exclusive):** Manager Decision?
    *   **Condition: Approved** -> Proceed to Task 17 (Schedule Payment).
    *   **Condition: Rejected** -> Process loops back to Task 10 (Resolve Discrepancy). *Note: The manager rejection triggers further investigation/resolution by AP/Purchasing.*

17. **Activity:** Schedule Payment
    *   *Description:* AP Clerk schedules the approved invoice for payment in the accounting system according to payment cycles.
    *   *Performer:* AP Clerk
    *   *Input:* Approved Invoice
    *   *Output:* Payment Scheduled

18. **End Event:** Invoice Processed for Payment.

**Exceptions/Variations Noted:**
*   **No PO:** Invoices without a PO are typically rejected (Task 4). However, the AP Supervisor may handle specific cases for non-contracted services via a separate exception process (e.g., retrospective PO creation), not fully detailed in this main flow.
*   **Unresolved Discrepancies:** Handled via escalation to the AP Supervisor (Task 12 & 13).
*   **Manager Rejection:** Loops back for further investigation and resolution (Gateway 16 loop).
*   **Conflicting Accounts:** Minor differences in perspective exist (e.g., exact timing of Purchasing Officer's detailed checks vs. AP Clerk's matching). The model primarily follows the AP Clerk's workflow sequence, incorporating other roles' actions within that structure.
