**Proposed High-Level Process Steps:**

Based on the analysis of the sample event log, the low-level events can be grouped into the following five higher-level process steps:

1.  **High-Level Step Name:** `Material Preparation`
    *   **Included Low-Level Events (Example for Case A1):**
        *   `Retrieve raw metal sheet` (Timestamp: 08:00:05)
        *   `Scan barcode` (Timestamp: 08:00:07)
        *   `Place metal sheet on table` (Timestamp: 08:00:10)
        *   `Align metal sheet` (Timestamp: 08:00:15)
        *   `Preheat metal sheet` (Timestamp: 08:00:20)
    *   **Justification:** These events represent the initial setup phase. They are temporally grouped at the beginning of the process for each case and involve actions related to sourcing, identifying, positioning, and conditioning the raw material before the main value-adding steps commence. The resources involved (Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1) are specific to this preparatory stage.

2.  **High-Level Step Name:** `Assembly`
    *   **Included Low-Level Events (Example for Case A1):**
        *   `Pick up welding tool` (Timestamp: 08:01:00)
        *   `Weld corner A` (Timestamp: 08:01:05)
        *   `Weld corner B` (Timestamp: 08:01:10)
    *   **Justification:** This group encompasses the core manufacturing activity identified in the log (welding). It includes the action of picking up the necessary tool, followed by the actual welding operations. These events logically form the main transformation step and are performed sequentially by the same resource (Operator B).

3.  **High-Level Step Name:** `Post-Assembly Quality Inspection`
    *   **Included Low-Level Events (Example for Case A1):**
        *   `Measure weld integrity` (Timestamp: 08:01:20)
    *   **Justification:** This step represents a specific quality control check performed immediately after the Assembly phase. Its purpose is to verify the quality of the preceding welding operations before the product moves to the next stage. It is performed by a dedicated resource (Quality Sensor #1) and is distinct from the final inspection.

4.  **High-Level Step Name:** `Coating Application`
    *   **Included Low-Level Events (Example for Case A1):**
        *   `Apply protective coating` (Timestamp: 08:01:30)
        *   `Dry coating` (Timestamp: 08:01:45)
    *   **Justification:** These two events represent a distinct finishing phase involving the application and subsequent drying of a protective coating. They occur sequentially and utilize specific resources (Coating Unit #3, Heating Unit #2) dedicated to this part of the process.

5.  **High-Level Step Name:** `Final Inspection`
    *   **Included Low-Level Events (Example for Case A1):**
        *   `Visual check` (Timestamp: 08:02:00)
    *   **Justification:** This event constitutes the final quality assurance step performed after all processing and coating activities are completed. It serves as a concluding check before the product potentially leaves this part of the manufacturing line. It is performed by a different resource (Operator C) than the earlier quality check. Grouping it separately maintains the chronological flow and highlights its role as a final verification.
