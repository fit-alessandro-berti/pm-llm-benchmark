**2. Potential Root Causes of Performance Issues:**

Analyzing the event log for the delayed cases (102, 104, 105) reveals several patterns and potential root causes:

*   **Overnight Delays / Lack of After-Hours Processing:**
    *   All three slow cases (102, 104, 105) exhibit major delays of exactly 19 hours between steps occurring on consecutive days (e.g., 'Investigate' at 14:00 Day 1 -> 'Resolve' at 09:00 Day 2 in Case 102; 'Investigate' at 13:00 Day 1 -> 'Resolve' at 08:00 Day 2 in Case 104; 'Investigate' at 14:00 Day 2 -> 'Resolve' at 09:00 Day 3 in Case 105).
    *   This strongly suggests that the support team (likely both L1 and L2) operates only during standard business hours (e.g., 8 AM - 5 PM or similar). Tasks that cannot be completed within one day are paused overnight, leading to substantial fixed delays (~16-19 hours depending on start/end times) until the next business day. This appears to be a primary driver of the multi-day resolution times.

*   **Bottlenecks in Escalation Process (L2 Response Time):**
    *   Case 105 shows an exceptionally long delay of **28 hours** between 'Escalate to Level-2 Agent' (Day 1, 10:00) and the next 'Investigate Issue' activity (Day 2, 14:00). This indicates a significant bottleneck related to the L2 team. Possible causes include the L2 team having insufficient capacity, a slow intake/assignment process for escalated tickets, or only reviewing escalations at specific times (e.g., once per day).
    *   Case 102 also involved escalation, with a 2.5-hour delay between escalation and the L2 investigation starting. While much shorter than Case 105, it still adds to the overall time.

*   **Delays Before Investigation/Escalation:**
    *   In Case 104 (which did *not* involve escalation), there was a **3.5-hour delay** between 'Assign to Level-1 Agent' (09:30) and 'Investigate Issue' (13:00). This waiting time before the L1 agent started investigation contributed to the overall delay.
    *   In Case 102, there was a **2.5-hour delay** between 'Assign to Level-1 Agent' (09:00) and 'Escalate to Level-2 Agent' (11:30). This might indicate the L1 agent spent considerable time attempting resolution before deciding to escalate, potentially longer than necessary.

**3. Explanation, Insights, and Recommendations:**

*   **Explanation:** The primary drivers of significantly increased cycle times are systemic overnight delays due to limited business hours and bottlenecks associated with the L2 escalation process, particularly the L2 team's response time. Delays within L1 handling (time to start investigation or time before escalating) also contribute. Escalated tickets (102, 105) are particularly prone to long durations due to the combination of L2 waiting times and overnight gaps. However, even non-escalated tickets (104) can suffer long delays if work spans across business days.

*   **Insights:**
    *   The lack of after-hours support is a major, systemic bottleneck affecting multiple cases.
    *   The L2 team/process represents a critical bottleneck, especially evident in the 28-hour wait in Case 105.
    *   Optimizing only L1 performance may not significantly reduce the longest cycle times without addressing the overnight gaps and L2 responsiveness.

*   **Recommendations:**
    1.  **Address Overnight Gaps:** Investigate the feasibility of extending support hours or implementing staggered shifts/on-call coverage, particularly for the L2 team, to mitigate the 19-hour overnight delays. If this isn't possible, focus on optimizing handovers to ensure work resumes promptly the next business day.
    2.  **Improve L2 Handoff and Response:** Analyze the L2 team's workload and processes. Implement stricter Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for L2 acknowledging and starting work on escalated tickets. Streamline the handoff mechanism from L1 to L2 to reduce idle time. Consider resource allocation adjustments for the L2 team if necessary.
    3.  **Optimize L1 Workflow:** Establish clearer guidelines or time targets for L1 agents regarding how long to work on a ticket before escalating. Monitor the time between assignment and investigation start for L1 agents to identify potential delays or workload issues.
