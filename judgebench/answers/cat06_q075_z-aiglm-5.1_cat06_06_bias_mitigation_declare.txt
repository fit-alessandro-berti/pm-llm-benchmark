### Explanation of Added Constraints and How They Reduce Bias

1. **Existence (`ManualReview`, `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
   * **Rationale:** By explicitly adding these activities to the `existence` constraints, we ensure that the process model formally recognizes and mandates the availability of human oversight and algorithmic/compliance bias checks as valid steps in the process.

2. **Coexistence (`Approve_Minority`  `ManualReview`, `Reject_Minority`  `ManualReview`)**:
   * **Rationale:** If a final decision is made for an applicant from a sensitive demographic (e.g., Minority), this constraint forces a `ManualReview` activity to also be present in the same trace. This guarantees that an automated system cannot silently approve or reject sensitive applicants without a parallel layer of human accountability and oversight.

3. **Precedence (`ManualReview` precedes `Approve_Minority` / `Reject_Minority`)**:
   * **Rationale:** While coexistence ensures the manual review happens *somewhere* in the trace, precedence ensures it happens *before* the final decision is made. This prevents the process from making a potentially biased decision first and then conducting a superficial post-hoc review to justify it. The human check must actively inform the decision.

4. **Response (`CheckApplicantRace`  `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
   * **Rationale:** Whenever a process execution encounters an event disclosing a sensitive attribute (like race), this constraint forces the system to subsequently execute a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This ensures that the moment sensitive data is introduced into the workflow, the process is obligated to actively verify that the subsequent logic is not being unduly influenced by it.

5. **Non-chain-succession (`CheckApplicantRace`  `Reject`, `CheckApplicantRace`  `Approve`)**:
   * **Rationale:** This directly prevents an immediate, automated decision from following the disclosure of a sensitive attribute. By breaking the direct chain of execution between viewing sensitive attributes and making a final decision, we eliminate the possibility of immediate, unmitigated biased outcomes, forcing the process to interleave other checks (like the `BiasMitigationCheck`) before arriving at a conclusion.
