### 1. Fatal Misuse of `coexistence`
In DECLARE, **`coexistence(A, B)`** is a symmetric (bidirectional) constraint: *A occurs in the trace if and only if B occurs*. It does **not** mean “if A happens, make sure B also happens somewhere” (that would be `responded_existence` or `response`).

Because you added:
- `coexistence("FinalDecision", "ManualReview")`
- `existence("FinalDecision")` (already in the original model)

you force **every trace to contain `ManualReview`**. Then, because you also added:
- `coexistence("Approve", "ManualReview")`
- `coexistence("Reject", "ManualReview")`

you **force every trace to contain both `Approve` and `Reject`**. In a loan-application process these are mutually exclusive outcomes; a trace must have one or the other (or at most one). Requiring both is a severe modeling error that makes the specification nonsensical.

**Fix:** If the intent is “*if* a decision occurs, a manual review must also occur,” use **`responded_existence`** (e.g., `responded_existence("Approve", "ManualReview")`) or simply rely on `precedence`/`response`. Remove the erroneous `coexistence` entries for `Approve` and `Reject`.

This is semantically false. `nonsuccession(A, B)` forbids A from being **eventually** followed by B, *irrespective* of how many other activities intervene.
