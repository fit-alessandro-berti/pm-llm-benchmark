## Critical Issues

### 1. **Structural Ambiguity in Choice/Loop Points (Major Flaw)**

The most serious problem is the non-deterministic choice at `after_DV` and `after_FC`. The net as constructed has **no mechanism to enforce that the loop is taken only when needed versus continuing forward**. Both `after_DV → t_FC` and `after_DV → t_RDS` are enabled simultaneously when `after_DV` has a token. This means:

- Even with complete documents, the process can arbitrarily enter the RDS loop
- Even with incomplete documents, the process can skip RDS and proceed to FC
- The model does not distinguish between "documents complete" and "documents incomplete" states

Similarly for `after_FC`: both `t_II` and `t_CA` are enabled simultaneously, allowing arbitrary choice regardless of whether fraud is suspected.

A correct model would need either:
- Silent transitions with guards (not possible in basic Petri nets without extensions)
- Separate places for different outcomes (e.g., `after_DV_complete` and `after_DV_incomplete`)
- Or explicit modeling of the decision as part of the transition structure

The final marking has `end = 1`, but there's no verification that no other tokens remain in the net. In a properly constructed accepting Petri net, we should ensure the net is deadlock-free and that the final marking is reachable only when complete. The code doesn't demonstrate this.
