The Model 1 section correctly flags the parallel placement of Decide and Interview after Screen, but the later justification inaccurately narrows this to "ordering between two sequential decision-making activities." For Model 2, labeling the Screen/Interview parallelism as merely "moderate" is inconsistent with the later (correct) observation that there is "no link from Screen to Decide/Interview." In fact, Screen has no outgoing edges at all, so it too can be scheduled after Close in a linear extension. The answer never acknowledges this symmetric post-Close scheduling problem for Screen, even though it does (partially) note Interview's disconnection in Model 1. The severity of screening results being irrelevant to the decision path is therefore understated.

The treatment of the onboarding loop is internally contradictory: it is first called a "minor" deviation that "could arguably be realistic (multi-step onboarding)" and then later condemned as "unbounded repetition with no clear exit criterion." This flip in assessment reveals unclear or shifting criteria rather than a coherent analysis.
