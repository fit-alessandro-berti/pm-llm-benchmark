#### 2. SQL Technical Deficiencies & Anti-Patterns
- **Invalid/Inefficient Grouping (Anomaly 1, Query 1):** `SELECT DISTINCT ... GROUP BY oel.case_id, o.order_type...` mixes `DISTINCT` with `GROUP BY` unnecessarily. In strict PostgreSQL (with `ONLY_FULL_GROUP_BY` equivalents enforced), selecting non-aggregated columns from joined tables without grouping by the primary key of the dependent table risks indeterminate results or syntax errors. The correct approach is an `EXCEPT` or `NOT EXISTS` anti-join on the `orders` table directly.

While `COUNT(DISTINCT oel2.activity)` masks the explosion, it forces the query planner to materialize `N²` rows per case, causing severe performance degradation at scale.
