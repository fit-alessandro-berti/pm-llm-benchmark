- **SQL Queries and Verification (1/10)**: **Fundamentally flawed and logically incorrect – primary reason for low score.** 
  - **Core Logical Error**: All queries use `LEAD(timestamp, 1) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY timestamp)`, computing time to the *immediate next event* (regardless of activity), not time *between specific activity pairs* (e.g., R to P). Profile explicitly states "not necessarily directly, but eventually." For R-P, LEAD(1) after R yields R-to-A (~1 hour), not R-to-P (~25 hours, spanning A/E). Similarly invalid for A-C (skips E/P/N), E-N (assumes direct). Only P-N *might* coincidentally work if no intermediates, but unproven.
  - **No Outlier Detection**: Objectives claim "identify claims where time ... outside expected ranges," but queries compute raw times *without* filtering (no `WHERE` for Z-score > threshold, e.g., `ABS(time - AVG) > ZETA * STDEV`). Just selects all; useless for anomalies.
  - **No Correlations**: Prompt demands "correlate ... with particular adjusters, claim_types, or resources" and "align with particular customer or region segments." Zero joins to `claims` (claim_type, customer_id, submission_date), `adjusters` (via resource?), or grouping/aggregation. E.g., no `JOIN claims c ON ce.claim_id = c.claim_id WHERE c.claim_type = 'auto_insurance' GROUP BY c.claim_type`.
  - **Incomplete/Inaccurate Objectives**: Claims like "time to approval" but computes wrong metric. No checks for "skipped steps" (e.g., `NOT EXISTS` for E between A and C). No aggregation for patterns (e.g., `AVG(time)` per adjuster). PostgreSQL syntax fine (interval arithmetic), but irrelevant due to logic fails.
  - **Missed Opportunities**: No queries for variance/STDEV to verify low STDEV (e.g., `STDDEV(time)`). No multi-event handling (e.g., first R to first P via window functions like `MIN(timestamp) FILTER (WHERE activity='P') OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id)`). No example results interpretation.
