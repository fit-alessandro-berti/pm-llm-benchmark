# **1. Identifying InstanceSpanning Constraints & Quantifying Impact**

### **Approach**
Use the event log (start/complete times, resources, order attributes) to build a **process model** and run **performance + resource analysis**. Focus on **intercase dependencies** using:
- **Resource contention analysis**
- **Batch detection**
- **Concurrency/WIP analysis**
- **Priority/preemption detection**
- **Caseattribute segmentation**

### **ConstraintbyConstraint Identification & Metrics**

#### **A) Shared ColdPacking Stations**
**Method:**  
Filter cases with `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`. Measure queue time between “Item Picking COMPLETE” and “Packing START”. Reconstruct station occupancy.

**Metrics**
- Avg/median **queue time** for coldpacking orders  
- % time **all cold stations busy**
- **Utilization** of cold stations
- **Queue length** / WIP before cold packing
- SLA impact for express + cold orders

---

#### **B) Shipping Batching (RegionBased)**
**Method:**  
Identify batches via `Batch ID` or infer by “Shipping Label Generation” grouped by region/time window. Measure waiting after QC.

**Metrics**
- **Batch waiting time** = Shipping Label Gen – QC Complete  
- **Batch size distribution**
- % of total cycle time due to batching
- Time to “batch completion” per region
- Orders exceeding max waiting threshold before batch

---

#### **C) Priority Handling (Express Orders)**
**Method:**  
Segment express vs standard cases and detect preemption (express starts while standard is waiting or paused on same resource).

**Metrics**
- **Express cycle time** vs standard  
- **Standard delay due to express** (delta vs noexpress periods)
- **# of preemptions** per day/week  
- **Resource interruptions** (stop–resume durations)

---

#### **D) Hazardous Material Limit (10 concurrent in Packing/QC)**
**Method:**  
Compute **concurrency** of hazardous orders in Packing + QC using start/complete intervals. Identify times when count = 10.

**Metrics**
- Time % at **cap = 10**
- **Queue time** for hazardous orders when cap reached
- **Throughput throttling** (hazard orders per hour)
- Compliance risk (violations or nearviolations)

---

### **Separating WithinInstance vs BetweenInstance Waiting**
**Technique:**
1. **Service time** = Activity COMPLETE – START  
2. **Waiting time** = Next Activity START – Previous COMPLETE  
3. Attribute waiting cause:
   - **Resource busy**  betweeninstance contention  
   - **Batching delay**  betweeninstance dependency  
   - **Hazard cap reached**  betweeninstance gating  
   - **Long activity duration**  withininstance  

This yields a **waitingtime decomposition** by cause.

# **2. Interactions Between Constraints**

Key interactions to watch:

1. **Express + ColdPacking**
   - Express orders needing cold stations may **preempt standard cold orders**, increasing queue time and causing ripple effects downstream.
2. **Express + Hazardous**
   - Express hazardous orders still cannot exceed the hazardous cap  may stall express SLAs if cap already full.
3. **Batching + Hazardous**
   - Hazardous orders in the same region can **cluster**, pushing the facility toward the hazard limit and delaying batch completion.
4. **ColdPacking + Hazardous**
   - If hazardous orders also require cold packing, both constraints compound  severe bottleneck.

**Why this matters:**  
Optimizing one constraint in isolation can worsen another. Example: adding batching efficiency may increase hazardous concurrency; adding cold stations may not improve flow if hazard cap is binding.

# **3. ConstraintAware Optimization Strategies**

### **Strategy 1 – Dynamic ColdPacking Capacity & Pooling**
**Addresses:** Coldpacking scarcity + express priority  
**Changes:**
- Convert select standard stations into **flexible coldcapable stations** during peaks.
- Reserve 1 cold station for express orders (timebased or demandbased).  
**DataDriven:**  
Use arrival patterns from logs to forecast coldorder surges and adjust allocation dynamically.  
**Expected Outcome:**  
Reduced coldpacking queue time, fewer expressdriven interruptions, improved SLA compliance.

---

### **Strategy 2 – Adaptive Batching (Hybrid Trigger)**
**Addresses:** Shipping batch delays  
**Changes:**
- Release batch when **min batch size OR max wait time** reached.
- Express orders bypass batching or form microbatches.
- Regionspecific thresholds based on historical arrival rates.  
**DataDriven:**  
Optimize thresholds using batch waitingtime distributions and region throughput stats.  
**Expected Outcome:**  
Shorter postQC waits, faster cycle time with minimal routing efficiency loss.

---

### **Strategy 3 – PriorityAware Scheduling with Preemption Control**
**Addresses:** Express disruptions to standard orders  
**Changes:**
- Switch from “hard preemption” to **nonpreemptive high priority** (express jumps queue but does not interrupt active work).
- Allocate **express time slots** in packing/QC.  
**DataDriven:**  
Use SLA breach probability to adapt priority weights dynamically.  
**Expected Outcome:**  
Express SLAs met with fewer delays for standard orders; more stable queues.

---

### **Strategy 4 – Hazardous WIP Gating & Dedicated Lane**
**Addresses:** Hazardous material cap  
**Changes:**
- Release hazardous orders into Packing/QC only when active hazardous WIP < 10.  
- Assign **dedicated hazardous QC staff** or time windows.  
**DataDriven:**  
Use hazard arrival patterns to smooth flow and avoid cap saturation.  
**Expected Outcome:**  
Regulatory compliance maintained while reducing queueing of hazardous orders.
