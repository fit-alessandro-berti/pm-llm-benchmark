### How the added constraints mitigate bias  

| Added constraint | What it enforces | Why it helps |
|------------------|------------------|--------------|
| **Coexistence (ManualReview  decision for a sensitive demographic)** | Whenever a decision such as `Approve_Minority`, `Reject_Minority`, `Approve_Female`, etc. appears in a trace, a `ManualReview` activity must also appear somewhere in the same trace. | Guarantees that a human reviewer doublechecks any decision that could be influenced by a protected attribute, reducing the chance of an automated, unchecked bias. |
| **Response (CheckBiasMitigationCheck)** | After any attributechecking activity (`CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge`) the next activity must be `BiasMitigationCheck`. | Forces the process to run a dedicated biasmitigation routine (e.g., fairness scoring) before any further actions, ensuring that the system explicitly evaluates potential discrimination. |
| **Precedence (BiasMitigationCheck  decision)** | A final decision (`Approve`, `Reject`, or any demographicspecific variant) may only occur after a `BiasMitigationCheck`. | Prevents the model from making a decision without first having performed a biascheck, thus embedding fairness as a prerequisite. |
| **Succession (CheckBiasMitigationCheck / ManualReview)** | Direct successors of a sensitiveattribute check must be either `BiasMitigationCheck` or `ManualReview`; a decision cannot follow immediately. | Blocks the pattern “attribute check  decision” that could embed a biased rule, requiring an intervening fairness step. |
| **Nonsuccession (CheckReject for the same demographic)** | Disallows a direct succession from a sensitiveattribute check to a rejecting decision for that same demographic. | Specifically guards against the most harmful bias pattern (“seeing minority race  immediate reject”) by making such a transition impossible. |

Together, these constraints shape the allowed execution traces so that any information about protected attributes is always followed by a fairnessoriented activity (bias check or manual review) before a decision is taken, and they ensure that a manual oversight step cooccurs with decisions that involve sensitive applicants. This systematic insertion of fairness checkpoints makes it far less likely that the process will produce discriminatory outcomes.
