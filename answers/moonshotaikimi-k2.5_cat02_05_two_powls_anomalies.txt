### 1. Normative Process Overview
A standard Hire-to-Retire process follows a strict causal and temporal sequence:
1. **Post Job Ad**  2. **Screen Candidates**  3. **Conduct Interviews**  4. **Make Hiring Decision**  5. **Onboard Employee**  6. **Add to Payroll**  7. **Close Case**

*   **Screening must precede Interviews** (you interview only pre-screened candidates).
*   **Interviews must precede the Hiring Decision** (decisions must be informed by assessment).
*   **Decision must precede Onboarding** (you onboard only selected candidates).
*   **All activities are mandatory**, particularly Payroll (a hired employee must be registered in the payroll system).

---

### 2. Analysis of Model 1

**Anomalies Identified:**

*   **Severe Ordering Violation (Interview vs. Decision):** The model defines `Screen` as a predecessor to both `Decide` and `Interview`, but places **no precedence constraint** between `Decide` and `Interview`. In partial order semantics, this makes them concurrent. Consequently, the model permits traces where:
    *   A hiring decision is made **before** conducting interviews.
    *   Onboarding and Payroll occur **before** or **concurrent with** interviews.
    *   *Impact:* This fundamentally violates the logic of candidate assessment, allowing the organization to hire and pay an employee without ever interviewing them.

*   **Structural Deficiency (Disconnection of Interview):** `Interview` has no outgoing edges to subsequent activities. It is a "sink" in the partial order (along with `Close`), meaning it bears no causal relationship to the decision or onboarding steps. This implies interviews do not inform the hiring decision, and the activity can technically occur even after the case is closed (though all nodes must execute, they are unordered relative to the final sink).

*   **Severity:** These are **critical, structural anomalies**. The model inverts the essential dependency that "assessment precedes selection," rendering the hiring process arbitrary.

---

### 3. Analysis of Model 2

**Anomalies Identified:**

*   **Critical Compliance Violation (Optional Payroll):** The use of `xor_payroll = X(Payroll, skip)` allows the process to complete (reach `Close`) without executing `Add_to_Payroll`. This permits a trace where an employee is onboarded but never added to the payroll system.
    *   *Impact:* This is a severe compliance and financial error, as it results in "ghost" employees or unpaid workers, violating the "Retire" (payroll establishment) obligation of the process.

*   **Moderate Ordering Violation (Screening vs. Interview):** The model defines both `Screen` and `Interview` as successors to `Post`, but does not enforce `Screen`  `Interview`. Furthermore, `Screen` has no outgoing edges to the rest of the process (it is a sink), meaning it can occur at any point, even after `Close`.
    *   *Impact:* This allows candidates to be interviewed before being screened (inefficient) or screening to occur after the hire is complete (pointless), but it does not violate the core hiring logic as severely as Model 1.

*   **Loop Structure:** The `*(Onboard, skip)` loop allows onboarding to be repeated indefinitely. While this could represent rework (e.g., failed onboarding attempts), it introduces a risk of non-termination or redundant work. However, this is the least severe anomaly.

**Positive Aspect:**
Model 2 correctly enforces the critical chain: **`Interview`  `Decide`  `Onboard`**. This preserves the fundamental logic that hiring decisions are based on interviews and that onboarding follows a positive decision.

---

### 4. Decision: Which Model is More Normative?

**Model 2 more closely aligns with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

**Justification:**

While both models contain severe anomalies, **Model 2 preserves the integrity of the core hiring workflow**, whereas Model 1 destroys it.

*   **Core Workflow Preservation:** Model 2 maintains the essential causal dependencies: interviews inform the decision (`Interview`  `Decide`), and the decision triggers onboarding (`Decide`  `Onboard`). This reflects the actual logic of hiring. Model 1 severs this link, allowing decisions to precede assessments, which is logically incoherent.

*   **Nature of Anomalies:** The anomaly in Model 2 (skipping Payroll) is a **deviation of optionality**—making a mandatory step optional. This is a serious compliance flaw, but it represents a "shortcut" that could theoretically be caught by monitoring. In contrast, Model 1's anomaly is a **deviation of sequencing**—violating the cause-and-effect relationship between assessment and selection. This structural inversion is more fundamental to the process definition.

*   **Screening Anomaly vs. Decision Anomaly:** While Model 2 mishandles the screening step (allowing it to be late or out of order), this is less critical than Model 1’s mishandling of the decision step. Interviewing unscreened candidates is inefficient; hiring without interviewing is nonsensical.

Therefore, despite the serious payroll omission in Model 2, it remains closer to a valid Hire-to-Retire model because it correctly models the assessment and selection phase, which is the defining characteristic of the "Hire" component.
