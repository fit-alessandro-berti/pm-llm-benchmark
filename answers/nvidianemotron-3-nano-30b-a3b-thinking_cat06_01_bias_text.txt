### Potential Impact on Fairness & Equity

1. **Unequal access to credit** – Applicants without local residency or club membership may be systematically screened out of the “eligible” band, even if their financial profiles are otherwise strong. This reduces their chances of obtaining loans or forces them into higherinterest-rate products.

2. **Selfreinforcing social stratification** – By rewarding existing community ties, the system amplifies the advantage of those who already belong to socially recognized networks, potentially widening income and wealth gaps within the region.

3. **Erosion of consumer trust** – When borrowers discover (or suspect) that an invisible “community bonus” influenced their outcome, they may perceive the lender as playing favorites, which can damage brand reputation and lead to regulatory scrutiny.

4. **Algorithmic opacity** – Because the bias is embedded in a rulebased step that feeds a human review, it can be difficult to audit. The “blackbox” nature hampers accountability and makes it hard to prove or correct any unintended discriminatory outcomes.

5. **Regulatory exposure** – Even though the protectedclass exemption may apply, regulators increasingly evaluate *disparate impact* analyses. If the communityintegration rule disproportionately excludes certain zipcodes, socioeconomic groups, or demographic clusters, the lender could be forced to redesign the process or face penalties.
