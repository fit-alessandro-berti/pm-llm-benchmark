### Normative “Hire-to-Retire” order (idealized)
A typical correct flow is:

**Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case**

Key integrity idea: **core prerequisites must happen before downstream commitments**, e.g., you shouldn’t *make the hiring decision / onboard* without having completed interviews (or at least the intended basis for the decision), and you shouldn’t *close* a payroll-related case without adding the employee to payroll.

---

## Model 1 analysis (PO constraints)

Edges imply:
- **Post  Screen**
- **Screen  Decide**
- **Screen  Interview**
- **Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close**

### Anomalies in Model 1
1. **Hiring decision may happen without completing interviews**
   - Although `Screen  Interview` exists, there is **no constraint `Interview  Decide`**.
   - Therefore, once **Screen** is done, the model allows:
     - **Decide** (and then Onboard/Payroll/Close)
     - while **Interview may still not have happened** (or might never happen at that run, depending on execution semantics).

2. **Onboarding can occur in parallel with (or even before) interviews**
   - Since `Decide` does not depend on `Interview`, **Onboard  Payroll  Close** can proceed even if interviews are incomplete.

### Severity
- This is a **serious ordering/integrity violation**, because the hiring decision is not properly grounded in interviews (given the model includes an interview activity at all).
- However, the model still enforces that **Screen happens before Decide** and that **Onboard/Payroll/Close** happen in a chain after Decide.

---

## Model 2 analysis (PO + XOR/LOOP)

Additional constructs:
- `loop_onboarding = LOOP(Onboard, skip)`
- `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)`

Edges imply:
- **Post  Screen**
- **Post  Interview**
- **Interview  Decide**
- **Decide  loop_onboarding  xor_payroll  Close**

### Anomalies in Model 2
1. **Interviews can occur without screening**
   - There is `Post  Interview`, but **no constraint requiring `Screen  Interview`**.
   - So interviews may start right after posting, even if screening is not done.

2. **Onboarding is repeated/iterative**
   - The **LOOP** structure with children `[Onboard, skip]` introduces **repetition of Onboard** (at least potentially multiple onboarding executions).
   - Normatively, onboarding is typically **one-time** per hired employee.

3. **Payroll can be skipped entirely**
   - `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)` means the execution may choose **skip instead of Payroll**.
   - Yet the model still enforces `xor_payroll  Close`, so the case can be closed **without adding the employee to payroll**.

### Severity
- Model 2 violates multiple core aspects:
  - **Prerequisite ordering** (interviews before screening),
  - **cardinality/consistency** (repeated onboarding),
  - and most critically: **it allows closing without the payroll step**, which is a fundamental breakdown of process integrity in a hire-to-payroll scenario.

---

## Which model aligns more with the normative process?

### **Model 1 is closer to normative behavior than Model 2.**

**Why:**
- **Model 1** only has a main ordering flaw: **interviews are not required before the hiring decision/onboarding**.
- **Model 2** has broader and more damaging issues:
  - It allows **interviews before screening**,
  - introduces **looping/repetition of onboarding**,
  - and most importantly allows **closing without payroll** via the XOR-with-skip.

So while Model 1 is still incorrect (because `Interview` is not ordered before `Decide`), **Model 2’s anomalies compromise the workflow more fundamentally**, especially by permitting omission of **Add_to_Payroll** while still reaching **Close_Case**.
