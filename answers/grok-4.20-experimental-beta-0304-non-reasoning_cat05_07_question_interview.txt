**Here is a focused list of open-ended questions I would ask the interviewee:**

### **Documentation & Initial Intake**
1. What specific documents do you consider mandatory versus nice-to-have during the initial review, and how do you decide whether to proceed with partial documentation?
2. How do you track and follow up on missing or outdated documents from the landlord? Is there a standard escalation process if they delay?
3. Are there certain types of properties or landlords that require additional documentation (e.g., HOA agreements, special insurance, historical buildings)?

### **Property Manager Assignment**
4. Walk me through how the assignment decision is actually made. Who is involved in choosing the property manager, and what takes priority when there are conflicting factors (e.g., workload vs. specialization vs. landlord preferences)?
5. What happens when no ideal property manager is available? Are there backup assignment rules or temporary coverage processes?
6. How much input does the landlord have in the selection of their property manager?

### **Initial Meeting & Expectation Setting**
7. What are the key topics that must be covered in the introductory meeting with the landlord, and which ones are flexible?
8. How do you handle situations where the landlord’s expectations conflict with company policy (e.g., pet policies, maintenance responsibilities, or payment methods)?

### **Inspection & Repair Phase**
9. What happens if the inspector identifies issues that the landlord is unwilling or unable to fix in a reasonable timeframe? How is that decision made and documented?
10. Who is responsible for selecting and managing contractors when repairs are needed, and how is the cost approval process handled?
11. Is there a defined “pass/fail” standard for the inspection, or is it more of a risk-based judgment call?

### **Marketing & Activation**
12. How long after a successful inspection does the property typically go live in the rental portal? What are the main bottlenecks in that transition?
13. How much influence does the property manager or landlord have over the final rental pricing and marketing strategy?

### **Tenant Screening & Leasing**
14. Can you walk me through the decision process when you have multiple qualified applicants? What criteria are used to break ties or prioritize one over another?
15. How do you handle situations where a strong applicant fails one part of the screening (e.g., credit score) but meets other criteria exceptionally well?
16. What flexibility exists in lease terms, and who has the authority to approve non-standard clauses requested by either the landlord or tenant?

### **Quality Assurance & Compliance**
17. What exactly does the compliance officer review in the lease, and what happens if they flag an issue?
18. How do the financial analyst’s profitability checks influence decisions that have already been made with the landlord?

### **Exceptions & Special Cases**
19. What are the most common exceptions or special-case scenarios you encounter during onboarding, and how are they typically routed or approved?
20. Are there certain property types (e.g., student housing, luxury, short-term rentals) that follow a noticeably different path through this process?

### **Timing, Sequencing & Handoffs**
21. Which steps in the process can happen in parallel versus those that must be strictly sequential?
22. Where are the biggest delays typically experienced in the overall onboarding timeline, and why?
23. How are handoffs between roles (coordinator  property manager  marketing  leasing  compliance) managed? What information is passed along, and what sometimes gets lost?

### **Communication & Tools**
24. How do you currently track the status of a property that is moving through onboarding? What visibility do different stakeholders (landlord, regional office, corporate teams) have?
25. When things go off-track, how is the landlord typically communicated with, and who owns that communication?

### **Overall Process Insight**
26. If you could change or remove one step in the current onboarding process, which would it be and why?
27. Are there any informal “tribal knowledge” rules or shortcuts that experienced staff use that aren’t written down?

These questions are designed to uncover decision logic, exception paths, sequencing constraints, role boundaries, and pain points while staying at a conceptual and process-understanding level.
