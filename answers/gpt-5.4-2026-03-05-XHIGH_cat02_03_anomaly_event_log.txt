A standard P2P flow is usually:

**Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Receive Invoice  Match Invoice/PO/(Goods Receipt)  Release Payment  Close**

Using that as the baseline:

| Case | Assessment | Why it looks anomalous / problematic |
|------|------------|---------------------------------------|
| **1001** | **Looks normal** | The sequence follows the expected order. No obvious anomaly. |
| **1002** | **Strong anomaly** | Several control violations: **PO issued before PR approval**, then **payment released before PR approval, before goods receipt, and before invoice matching**. The **match happens only after payment**. This is problematic because it bypasses approval and 3-way/2-way matching controls, creating risk of unauthorized spend, paying for goods not yet received, overpayment, fraud, or duplicate payment. |
| **1003** | **Strong anomaly** | **Payment is released before invoice receipt**, before goods receipt, and before matching. In standard P2P, payment should normally happen only after invoice verification and usually after goods receipt. This could indicate an unsupported prepayment/advance payment scenario. Without a special approval path, it is risky because the company pays before having evidence of liability or delivery. |
| **1004** | **Moderate anomaly** | **Goods are received before the PO is issued.** That suggests a retroactive PO, emergency purchase, or maverick buying. In normal P2P, the PO should exist before goods are received. This weakens procurement control, makes matching harder, and can mean spending happened before formal authorization. |

## Most important anomalies

### Case 1002
Sequence:
- Create PR
- **Issue PO**
- Vendor Invoice Received
- **Release Payment**
- **Approve PR**
- Receive Goods
- **Match Invoice to PO**

Main issues:
- Approval happens **after** the PO and even **after payment**
- Payment happens **before goods receipt**
- Payment happens **before invoice matching**

This is the most concerning case because the main preventive controls happen too late.

### Case 1003
Sequence:
- Create PR
- Approve PR
- Issue PO
- **Release Payment**
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Receive Goods
- Match Invoice to PO

Main issues:
- Paying **before invoice**
- Paying **before receipt of goods**
- Matching only **after payment**

This is also highly unusual unless it is a formal advance-payment process, which is not shown here.

### Case 1004
Sequence:
- Create PR
- Approve PR
- **Receive Goods**
- **Issue PO**
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Match
- Release Payment

Main issue:
- Goods receipt before PO issuance

This is less severe than 1002/1003 because payment still occurs after invoice and matching, but it still points to weak purchasing discipline.

## Overall conclusion
- **Case 1001**: normal
- **Case 1002**: clearly anomalous and high risk
- **Case 1003**: clearly anomalous and high risk
- **Case 1004**: anomalous, likely a retroactive PO / off-process procurement case

If this were a real audit or process mining review, **1002 and 1003 would deserve immediate investigation**, and **1004 would also merit review for after-the-fact PO creation or emergency purchasing behavior**.
