## 2. Root Cause Analysis

### Case 105 (49 hours — worst performer)
- Escalated to Level-2 at 10:00 on Day 1, but the **Level-2 Investigate Issue did not begin until Day 2 at 14:00** — a waiting time of **~28 hours** after escalation.
- Classic hand-off delay: the Level-2 team has a long queue/backlog.

### Case 102 (25 hours)
- Escalated to Level-2 at 11:30 on Day 1; Level-2 investigation began at 14:00 (2.5h wait — acceptable).
- However, **investigation  resolution took 19 hours** (14:00  09:00 next day), suggesting the Level-2 resolution step itself is slow or spans non-working hours.

### Case 104 (24 hours — NOT escalated)
- **Assigned at 09:30, but Investigate Issue didn't start until 13:00** (3.5h idle time at Level-1).
- Investigation stretched from 13:00 to 08:00 next day (**19 hours**) — likely the agent went off-shift, leaving the ticket overnight.
- Indicates Level-1 agent workload/capacity issue, not complexity.

### Common Patterns Across Delayed Cases
| Bottleneck | Cases Affected |
|------------|----------------|

| Long wait after Assign  Investigate (Level-1 queue) | 104 |
| Investigation spanning overnight (off-shift handling) | 102, 104, 105 |

## 3. Impact & Recommendations

### How these factors inflate cycle time
- **Escalation hand-offs** introduce queuing delay: the ticket sits idle until a Level-2 agent picks it up. For Case 105, this single wait accounts for over half of the total cycle time.
- **Agent workload at Level-1** (Case 104) shows that even non-escalated tickets stall when agents are busy — delays are not purely a complexity issue.
- **Non-continuous work hours**: Multiple cases show investigations beginning late in the day and resolving only the following morning, suggesting tickets are abandoned at end-of-shift.

### Recommendations
1. **Reduce Level-2 queue time.** Monitor and cap the "Escalate  Investigate" wait. Consider adding Level-2 capacity or a priority-based routing SLA (e.g., Level-2 must begin within 2 hours).
2. **Improve triage accuracy.** If Case 102 and 105 could have been routed to Level-2 directly based on triage classification, the initial Level-1 assign/investigate step would be skipped, saving 1–2 hours.
3. **Balance Level-1 workload.** Case 104's 3.5-hour wait before investigation indicates uneven work distribution. Implement real-time queue monitoring or workload balancing.
4. **Address end-of-shift handoffs.** Introduce shift-handover procedures so in-progress tickets don't sit idle overnight, or use follow-the-sun coverage for high-priority tickets.
5. **Track per-step SLAs.** Measure "ReceiveTriage," "AssignInvestigate," and "EscalateInvestigate" durations individually to catch bottlenecks earlier rather than only watching end-to-end time.

**Bottom line:** The main driver of long cycle times is **waiting time between hand-offs (especially at escalation)** and **tickets being left idle across shift boundaries**, not the actual work effort. Addressing queuing and shift continuity should significantly reduce average cycle time.
