# Activity Disambiguation Analysis

## 1. Identifying Recurring Activities

Examining each case, the following activities repeat **within a single case** and carry different meanings:

| Activity | Cases with repeats | Contextual variation |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Verify Documents** | C001, C003 | Initial verification vs. re-verification after new input |
| **Call Customer** | C002 | Clarification call vs. confirmation call |
| **Call Customer** (across cases) | C001, C002, C003 | Different *purposes* even when not repeated in the same case |

I also disambiguate the single-occurrence "Call Customer" events across cases because their meaning is clearly context-dependent (request missing docs, clarify discrepancy, recall walk-in customer).

## 4. Justification of Naming Choices

**Verify Documents  Initial vs. Recheck**
- The first occurrence is always an *initial validation* triggered by request submission.
- The second occurrence is a *re-validation* prompted by an upstream change. I further qualified the trigger:
  - C001: *"Post-Submission"* — re-check followed the customer uploading the ID scan.
  - C003: *"Post-Adjustment"* — re-check followed an internal account adjustment.
- This distinction matters for process mining: the rework loop has different root causes (customer-side gap vs. internal data correction).

**Call Customer  purpose-specific names**
The activity label was identical, but the *intent and direction* differed every time:
- C001 *Request Missing Docs* — inbound-process driven, gathering input from the customer.
- C002 *Clarify Discrepancy* — diagnostic call before fixing data.
- C002 *Confirm Adjustment* — verification/closure call after the fix.
- C003 *Recall to Branch* — logistical, getting a physical visitor back.

Distinguishing the two C002 calls is essential because they sit on **opposite sides** of the `Adjust Account` step (one initiates the correction, one confirms it).

**Initiate Request  channel-qualified names**
Though not repeated within a case, the resource reveals two channels: **online portal** (C001, C002) vs. **branch walk-in** (C003). Tagging the channel preserves the variant distinction that drives later physical vs. digital verification paths.

**Why qualify by *trigger* rather than just sequence position**
Naming purely by order (e.g., "Verify Documents 1/2") would hide *why* the rework happened. By encoding the contextual cause, the renamed log supports:
- Loop / rework analysis (customer-caused vs. internally-caused re-verification),
- Channel-based variant comparison,
- Accurate role separation for the front-office call activities.
