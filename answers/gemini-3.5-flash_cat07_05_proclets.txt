The image depicts a process modeling framework—specifically using **proclets** (lightweight processes)—to represent a clinical workflow involving a laboratory visit and test. The visualization is divided into three sections: (a) a process workflow, (b) a class diagram, and (c) a message detail table.

### **(a) Two proclet classes connected through two channels**
This is the main, central diagram showing two parallel workflows that interact with each other via communication channels shown in dashed boxes.

#### **1. Left Workflow: Lab Visit**
This workflow represents the patient-facing steps handled mostly by doctors and lab assistants.
*   **Start:** Initiated by a **Lab visit**.
*   **Order entry:** Performed by a **doctor**.
*   **Take blood sample:** Performed by a **lab assistant**. This step sends an initiation message to the "Order system" channel.
*   **Place (Circle):** A waiting state where the process waits for results.
    *   **Receive result:** A gray task that receives data from the **HIS** channel and loops back to the waiting place.
*   **Judge results:** Performed by a **lab assistant**. This step branches into three possible outcomes:
    1.  **Everything fine (Gray box):** No further action is needed. Sends a message through the **HIS** channel to trigger "Finish lab test" on the right.
    2.  **Do tests again (Gray box):** Sends a message through the **HIS** channel to trigger "Perform test again" on the right.
        *   After this, the process enters another waiting state and can **Receive updated result** (gray box) from the HIS channel.
    3.  **Direct path to Send report.**
*   **Send report:** Performed by a **lab assistant** (indicated by a red bar indicating synchronization/end).
*   **End:** The process terminates (black square in circle).

#### **2. Right Workflow: Lab Test**
This workflow represents the back-end laboratory steps handled by chemical analysts.
*   **Start:** Triggered by a message from the **Order system** channel.
*   **Perform test:** Performed by a **chemical analyst**.
*   **Make report:** Performed by a **chemical analyst**. This sends report data to the **HIS** channel (which goes to "Receive result" on the left).
*   **Decision Place (Circle):** The workflow waits to hear how the doctor/assistant judged the results.
    *   Path A: **Finish lab test** (Gray box) – Triggered by the "Everything fine" message via the HIS channel. This ends the test.
    *   Path B: **Perform test again** – Triggered by the "Do tests again" message via the HIS channel. Performed by a **chemical analyst**.
        *   **Provide updated report:** Performed by a **chemical analyst**. This sends updated data back to the HIS channel (received by "Receive updated result" on the left).
*   **End:** Both paths lead to the termination of the lab test.

#### **3. Channels (Dashed Boxes)**
*   **Order system:** Facilitates the creation of a lab test from a lab visit. It has a multiplicity of `*,1` on the sender side and `1,1` on the receiver side.
*   **HIS (Hospital Information System):** Facilitates multiple ongoing interactions between the visit and the test:
    *   *Result sharing:* `1,*` to `1,1` (from "Make report" to "Receive result").
    *   *Termination/Redo directives:* `*,1` to `1,?` (for "Everything fine" and "Do tests again").
    *   *Updated reports:* `1,*` to `1,?` (from "Provide updated report" to "Receive updated result").

### **(b) Class diagram containing the two proclet classes**
This is a standard UML class diagram showing the structural relationship between the two processes:
*   **Lab visit** has a `1..1` relationship with **Lab test** via the relation **"requires"**.
*   **Lab test** has a `1..*` relationship with **Lab visit**.
*   *Meaning:* One lab visit can require one or more (1 to many) lab tests, but each lab test must belong to exactly one lab visit.
