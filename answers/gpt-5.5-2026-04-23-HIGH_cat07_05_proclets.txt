The visualization is a multi-part diagram explaining how a **lab visit** process interacts with one or more **lab test** processes.

### (a) Two proclet classes connected through channels
The large diagram on the left shows two process workflows:

- **Lab visit** workflow on the left:
  - Starts with **Order entry**
  - Continues to **Take blood sample**
  - Then **Receive result**
  - A decision point **Judge results**
    - If “Everything fine,” the process proceeds to **Send report**
    - Otherwise, it goes to **Do tests again**, then **Receive updated result**, and eventually sends the report

- **Lab test** workflow on the right:
  - Starts with **Perform test**
  - Then **Make report**
  - Then either **Finish lab test** or, if more testing is needed:
    - **Perform test again**
    - **Provide updated report**
  - Ends at a final state

The two workflows communicate through dashed channel areas:

- **Order system**
  - Used when the lab visit orders a lab test
- **HIS**
  - Used for exchanging test results and updated results

Small multiplicity labels such as **1..1**, **1..***, and **1..?** indicate how many related process instances may be involved in each communication. Roles such as **doctor**, **lab assistant**, and **chemical analyst** are shown beside tasks to indicate who performs them.

### (b) Class diagram
The small diagram in the upper right summarizes the relationship:

- A **Lab visit** requires **Lab test**
- One lab visit is associated with **one or more lab tests**
- Each lab test belongs to exactly one lab visit

So the multiplicity is:

- Lab visit: **1..1**
- Lab test: **1..***

### (c) Example of a performative
The table at the bottom gives an example communication event:

- **Time:** 11:00  
- **Channel:** Order system  
- **Sender:** Lab visit – John  
- **Receiver:** Lab test – HGB John  
- **Action:** Create  
- **Content:** “Can you perform a HGB test for John?”  
- **Scope:** Private  
- **Direction:** OUT  

Overall, the visualization illustrates how a patient’s lab visit process can initiate and coordinate one or more lab test processes through defined communication channels.
