### **Why Anomalous:**
According to standard P2P processes, after approving a purchase requisition, the next logical step is to **issue a Purchase Order** for the goods to be received. Conversely, issuing a PO without verifying receipt of goods creates an inconsistency. Once a PO is issued, goods should logically be dispatched/returned, before proceeding to subsequent steps like invoicing, matching/paying, etc. This reversal constitutes a violation of the sequential workflow, potentially leading to ambiguity in whether the PO is valid without corresponding goods and pricing, or overlooking the accountability for procurement fulfillment. 

### **Impact:**
- **Risk of Misallocation**: The PO may be signed but not linked to received goods, risking non-compliance with procurement schedules and budgetary oversight.
- **Operational Delays**: Unresolved financing (e.g., payment) or misallocated credit are possible without timely confirmation of received goods.
- **Audit Deficits**: The process lacks a clear trail of accountability until goods are verified after realization, exposing gaps in process adherence.

The step **Receive Goods** must precede **Issue Purchase Order** in the sequence. This correction ensures alignment with procurement workflows and maintains transparency in labor allocation and resource utilization.
