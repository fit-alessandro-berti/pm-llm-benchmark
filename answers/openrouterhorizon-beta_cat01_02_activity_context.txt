Below is a disambiguated view of the recurring activities, followed by the revised event log and brief justifications.

1) Recurring activities and contextual meanings
- Verify Documents
  - Initial identity/document verification immediately after request intake.
  - Re-verification after the customer provides missing documents or after an internal account adjustment.

- Call Customer
  - Call to request missing documents (post-initial verification).
  - Call to clarify a discrepancy (pre-internal adjustment).
  - Follow-up call to confirm adjustments (post-internal adjustment).
  - Call to recall a walk-in customer who stepped out (attendance/return request).

2) Renamed activities to reflect context
- Verify Documents:
  - Verify Documents – Initial
  - Verify Documents – Post-Submission Recheck
  - Verify Documents – Post-Adjustment Recheck

- Call Customer:
  - Call Customer – Request Missing Docs
  - Call Customer – Clarify Discrepancy
  - Call Customer – Confirm Adjustments
  - Call Customer – Recall to Branch

4) Justification of naming choices
- Verify Documents – Initial: Occurs immediately after Initiate Request; context indicates first-time identity/document check.
- Verify Documents – Post-Submission Recheck: Happens after the customer submits additional documents; re-validation step.
- Verify Documents – Post-Adjustment Recheck: Occurs after an internal Adjust Account activity; re-validation due to changed account details.
- Call Customer – Request Missing Docs: Triggered by incomplete verification; purpose is to obtain missing documents.
- Call Customer – Clarify Discrepancy: Purpose is to clarify an account discrepancy before internal adjustments.
- Call Customer – Confirm Adjustments: Follows internal account adjustments; purpose is confirmation with the customer.
- Call Customer – Recall to Branch: Specific to a walk-in scenario; purpose is to have the customer return to the branch.
