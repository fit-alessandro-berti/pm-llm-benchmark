### 4️⃣ Exception Paths  

#### A. Missing/Invalid Purchase Order  
*Handled at G1:*  
If an invoice arrives without a valid PO number, Mary sends an email requesting the missing information (**Step 2a**) and loops until she receives it before proceeding with logging (**Step 2b**)…

#### B. Unresolved Discrepancies  
*Escalation chain:*  

1. After G2 detects mismatch (**Step 5b**)  
   - If discrepancy cannot be resolved quickly by contacting supplier or holding the invoice (`Hold Until Corrected Version`) then escalation occurs immediately (`Escalate To Supervisor`).  
   - Supervisor Rita contacts suppliers or coordinates between departments (`Rita’s Intervention`).  
   - For invoices lacking any matching PO but representing informal service contracts, Rita creates a retrospective purchase order OR rejects them after consulting department heads (`Retrospective‑PO / Rejection`).  

After resolution, the workflow returns either directly to step 4a (“Verify Receipt”) or re‑enters at step 5a (“Send Confirmation”) depending on how the issue was fixed—this is marked as ***[UNCERTAIN]*** because interviewees differ on whether they jump straight back into purchasing verification or re‑enter at manager approval stage.

#### C. Manager Rejection / Investigation Needed  
When Carlos rejects an invoice during G3 (“Manager Approves?”), he may instruct Mary (“Hold It”) while she investigates further—this loops back either directly into step 4a if new information becomes available or again through step 5b if new discrepancies arise (**[UNCERTAIN]**, see above).
