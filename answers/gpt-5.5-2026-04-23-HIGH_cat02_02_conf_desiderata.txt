## 1. Normative rule violations

### Case 1 — **No normative violation**

Case 1 follows the required strict sequence:

1. Application Received  
2. Credit Check  
3. Risk Assessment  
4. Loan Decision — Approved  
5. Inform Applicant  
6. Finalize Documentation  
7. Close Case  

Because the loan was approved, finalizing documentation before closing the case is correct. The applicant was also informed after the loan decision, as required.

**Conclusion:** Case 1 is normatively compliant.

---

### Case 2 — **Normative violations present**

Case 2 violates several strict rules.

#### Violation 1: Risk Assessment before Credit Check

The log shows:

- 08:00 — Application Received  
- 08:20 — Risk Assessment  
- 09:00 — Credit Check  

This violates the rule:

> Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment.

The later Credit Check does not repair the violation, because the Risk Assessment already happened too early.

#### Violation 2: Applicant was not informed after the loan decision

The process requires:

> Loan Decision  Inform Applicant  Close Case

In Case 2, after the loan decision, there is no “Inform Applicant” event before the case is closed.

The observed sequence is:

- Loan Decision — Rejected  
- Finalize Documentation  
- Close Case  

So the mandatory communication step appears to have been skipped.

#### Violation 3: Finalize Documentation after rejection

The loan decision was:

- Loan Decision — Rejected

But the next activity was:

- Finalize Documentation

This directly violates the rule:

> If the loan is rejected, do not finalize documentation before closing the case.

For a rejected application, the correct path should be:

> Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision — Rejected  Inform Applicant  Close Case

There should be no finalization of loan documentation after rejection.

**Conclusion:** Case 2 is normatively non-compliant.

## 2. Desiderata / recommended-practice deviations

Desiderata are recommended behaviors, not strict rules. Violating them is suboptimal but not a serious process violation.

### Case 1 — Generally meets desiderata

- Credit Check occurred 30 minutes after Application Received.  
  - Recommended: within 24 hours.  
  - Result: met.

- Inform Applicant occurred 2 hours after Loan Decision.  
  - Recommended: within 48 hours.  
  - Result: met.

- Follow-up Call is not shown in the log.  
  - If the log is complete and the Follow-up Call was skipped, this is a minor desideratum deviation.  
  - However, because the follow-up call is only encouraged, not required, this is not a serious issue.

**Conclusion:** Case 1 is optimal or near-optimal. At most, it may have skipped the optional follow-up call.

---

### Case 2 — Desiderata issues are secondary to normative violations

- Credit Check occurred 1 hour after Application Received.  
  - Timing recommendation was met.  
  - However, the Credit Check happened after Risk Assessment, which is a strict normative violation.

- Inform Applicant did not appear in the log.  
  - This is not merely a desideratum issue; it violates the strict process requirement to inform the applicant after the decision.

- Follow-up Call is not shown.  
  - If the log is complete, the optional follow-up call was skipped.  
  - This is only a minor desideratum deviation.

**Conclusion:** Case 2 has serious normative violations. Any desiderata issues are less important compared with the strict rule breaches.

---

### Case 3 — Desiderata violations present, but no normative violation

Case 3 follows the correct strict sequence, but it fails several recommended best practices.

#### Desideratum violation 1: Credit Check was late

- Application Received: 2024-01-12 09:00  
- Credit Check: 2024-01-14 10:00  

Elapsed time: approximately 49 hours.

The recommendation was to complete the Credit Check within 24 hours.

This is a desideratum violation, not a strict violation.

#### Desideratum violation 2: Applicant was informed late

- Loan Decision: 2024-01-14 12:00  
- Inform Applicant: 2024-01-16 15:00  

Elapsed time: approximately 51 hours.

The recommendation was to inform the applicant within 48 hours.

This is slightly outside the recommended window, so it is suboptimal but not a severe violation.

#### Desideratum violation 3: No Follow-up Call

The log explicitly notes that there was no Follow-up Call.

Because the Follow-up Call is only encouraged, skipping it is not a strict violation. It is simply less aligned with the recommended process.

**Conclusion:** Case 3 is normatively compliant but does not fully meet the recommended best practices.

## 3. Possible interpretations or justifications

### Case 1

Case 1 appears to be the ideal execution of the process. It follows the strict sequence and meets the timing recommendations. If no Follow-up Call occurred, that may be acceptable because the call is optional and the case was processed quickly and cleanly.

---

### Case 2

Case 2 is problematic, but there are possible explanations:

- The Risk Assessment timestamp may have been recorded incorrectly.
- A preliminary or informal credit check may have happened outside the system before the logged Credit Check.
- “Finalize Documentation” may have been mislabeled and could actually refer to rejection paperwork or internal closure documentation rather than finalizing loan approval documents.
- The applicant may have been informed outside the tracked system, meaning the log is incomplete.

However, under the given rules and based strictly on the event log, Case 2 violates the normative process.

---

### Case 3

Case 3 is acceptable from a strict compliance perspective but suboptimal from a service-quality perspective.

Possible justifications include:

- The delay between Application Received and Credit Check may have been caused by weekend processing, staffing constraints, or waiting for an external credit bureau.
- The delayed applicant notification may have been due to batching, manual review, or communication backlog.
- The Follow-up Call may have been skipped because the approval was straightforward or because the organization treats it as optional.

These explanations may justify the deviations, but they do not change the fact that Case 3 failed to meet the recommended desiderata.
