**Bias Identification**

The **GroupB (Unprotected) event log** exhibits a clear form of bias that is not present in the GroupA (Protected) log. The source of this bias lies primarily in two attributes:

1. **LocalResident flag** – Only cases belonging to GroupB are marked `TRUE` for *LocalResident*, indicating they belong to a community organization (e.g., Highland Civic Darts Club). In contrast, all cases in the ProtectedGroup log have this flag set to `FALSE`, reflecting a default “nonlocal” status.

2. **CommunityGroup entry** – For GroupB entries, *CommunityGroup* is populated with specific names of local clubs (e.g., “Highland Civic Darts Club”), whereas all rows in the ProtectedGroup log have an empty string (`None`), suggesting no community affiliation or a default placeholder.

These flags serve as proxies for **affinity** to the organization’s members rather than objective attributes inherent to the applicant’s profile. When combined with the **ScoreAdjustment** column, they create systematic differences:

- In GroupB, cases where *LocalResident* and *CommunityGroup* are set receive a **+10 ScoreAdjustment** after ManualReview (e.g., U001 and U003). This adjustment effectively lifts their PreliminaryScore from 720/710 to 730/705 before the final decision is made.

- In GroupA, no such adjustments occur regardless of LocalResident or CommunityGroup values. All scores remain unchanged at 720 for P001 and P003 (and similarly for any other ProtectedGroup case), reflecting a neutral scoring process.

**Manifestation of Bias**

1. **Positive Score Boost**: The explicit +10 adjustment to the PreliminaryScore for local residents in GroupB creates an incentive for approval, independent of merit or risk assessment metrics inherent to the application data (e.g., credit score, employment history).

2. **Unequal Final Decisions**: Because the final decision hinges on the adjusted scores:
   - Approved outcomes are more frequent among GroupB cases (U001  Approved; U003  Approved) compared to GroupA where decisions remain based solely on the unadjusted scores (P001 & P003 also approved, but without a biasinducing adjustment).
   - Conversely, GroupB’s only rejected case (U002) remains disapproved because it lacks any community boost.

3. **Systematic Differential Impact**: The presence of LocalResident and CommunityGroup flags acts as an implicit “affinity score” that can override objective risk criteria, leading to a higher approval rate for cases linked to the organization’s membership pool—potentially representing a form of protectedclass discrimination where “local status” is treated like race or gender in terms of preferential treatment.

**Conclusion**

The bias manifests through **preferential scoring adjustments tied to local affiliation**, which are absent in GroupA. This creates an uneven playing field, as the same objective data (e.g., application score 720) can be inflated (to 730) and thus more likely to result in approval when the applicant is recognized as a local resident of a community group—a classic case of **affinity bias** embedded within the eventlog processing pipeline. Addressing this would require either removing or anonymizing the LocalResident/CommunityGroup flags, ensuring decisions are based solely on the applicant’s merit metrics captured elsewhere in the log.
