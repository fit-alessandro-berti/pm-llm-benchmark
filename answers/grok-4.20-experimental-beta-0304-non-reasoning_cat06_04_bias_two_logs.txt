**Group B's event log exhibits bias.**

### How the bias manifests

The bias is a **positive adjustment** (community boost) that is applied selectively based on the combination of two attributes:

* **LocalResident = TRUE**
* **CommunityGroup = "Highland Civic Darts Club"**

This adjustment directly increases the PreliminaryScore by +10 and carries through to the final decision.

### Concrete evidence from the logs

**Protected Group A (LocalResident = FALSE, CommunityGroup = None):**
* All cases receive **ScoreAdjustment = 0**.
* Decisions are based strictly on the original PreliminaryScore.
  * 720  Approved
  * 710  Rejected
  * 740  Approved

**Unprotected Group B:**
* When **LocalResident = TRUE** *and* **CommunityGroup = "Highland Civic Darts Club"**, the system applies **+10 (Community Boost)**:
  * U001: 720  730  Approved (same original score as P001, but now approved with boost)
  * U003: 695  705  Approved (this applicant would have been rejected under the same scoring logic applied to Group A)
* When LocalResident = TRUE but CommunityGroup = None (U002), no boost is applied and the outcome follows the same logic as Group A (710  Rejected).

### Nature of the bias

This is **disparate treatment / proxy discrimination**:

1. The protected attribute (LocalResident = FALSE in Group A) is effectively penalized because the positive adjustment is only available to LocalResident = TRUE members of a specific community group.
2. The "CommunityGroup" field acts as a **proxy** that correlates with protected characteristics (e.g., residency status, possibly ethnicity, socioeconomic background, or geographic origin). By granting a mechanical +10 boost only to one narrow community group, the system systematically advantages members of that group over identically situated applicants from the protected group.
3. The bias produces **systematic differences in final decisions** for comparable risk scores:
   * A 720-score non-local applicant is approved only because of their raw score.
   * A 720-score local "Darts Club" member is approved *with an artificial boost*.
   * A 695-score local "Darts Club" member is approved, whereas a 710-score non-local is rejected — showing the boost can flip outcomes that would otherwise be denials.

In short, Group B’s log shows clear evidence of **bias introduced through the ScoreAdjustment mechanism**, which uses LocalResident and CommunityGroup attributes to grant preferential treatment. This creates unfair, systematic advantages for one subgroup while disadvantaging the protected group (Group A) with equivalent or better raw qualifications.
