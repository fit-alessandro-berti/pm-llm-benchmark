Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to deepen your conceptual understanding of the property onboarding process. They are categorized by the phase of the process and the specific areas of inquiry (decision criteria, roles, timing, and exceptions) to ensure a structured and productive interview.

### 1. Initial Intake and Documentation Review
*   **Decision Criteria:** What specific criteria or timeframes determine whether a document (like an inspection report or compliance certificate) is considered "outdated" and requires an update?
*   **Exception Handling:** If a landlord is unable or unwilling to provide a specific required document, what is the escalation path? Is there a process for granting conditional approvals or waivers?
*   **Timing & Sequencing:** What is the expected Service Level Agreement (SLA) or turnaround time for the property coordinator to complete the initial document review once the landlord makes contact?

### 2. Property Manager Assignment and Alignment
*   **Decision Criteria:** When multiple property managers meet the criteria (workload, geography, experience), what is the tie-breaking mechanism or final decision-making authority for the assignment?
*   **Roles & Responsibilities:** You mentioned workload as a factor. How is a property manager’s "current workload" quantitatively measured or capped to ensure they aren't overloaded during this assignment?
*   **Exception Handling:** If a landlord has highly unique requirements that no single property manager has experience with, how does the company adapt? Is a specialized team assembled, or is additional training provided?

### 3. Property Readiness, Inspection, and Marketing
*   **Decision Criteria:** Who has the final authority to declare that a property has "passed" inspection if the certified inspector identifies minor, non-safety-related issues? 
*   **Timing & Sequencing:** If repairs are needed, does the entire onboarding process pause, or can certain parallel activities (like draft marketing creation) begin while waiting for the landlord or contractors?
*   **Roles & Responsibilities:** In the scenario where a landlord is unresponsive or refuses to fund necessary repairs identified by the inspector, what are the property manager’s options? Can the company terminate the onboarding process at this stage?

### 4. Tenant Screening and Lease Finalization
*   **Decision Criteria:** When multiple qualified tenants apply, you mentioned prioritizing longer leases or offering promotional discounts. Who has the authority to approve these discounts or lease deviations, and what are the financial guardrails for doing so?
*   **Roles & Responsibilities:** Is the tenant screening process (credit checks, employment verification) handled entirely by the property manager/leasing agent, or is it outsourced to a third-party service? If outsourced, who interprets the results and makes the final approval/rejection call?
*   **Exception Handling:** How does the process handle a situation where a prospective tenant meets all standard criteria but has a highly unconventional request (e.g., a specialized emotional support animal not covered by standard policies, or a non-standard move-in date)?

### 5. Quality Checks, Audits, and Compliance
*   **Timing & Sequencing:** Do the compliance, financial, and IT audits occur *before* the lease is signed and the tenant moves in, or are they post-move-in checks? 
*   **Exception Handling:** If the financial analyst determines that the agreed-upon rental price is below the company’s profitability targets *after* the lease is signed, what is the remediation process? Does this trigger a renegotiation, or is it treated as a post-mortem to adjust future pricing models?
*   **Roles & Responsibilities:** If the compliance officer flags a potential fair housing violation in a lease agreement, what is the immediate next step, and who is responsible for rectifying it with the landlord or tenant?

### 6. Overarching Process, Communication, and Metrics
*   **Roles & Responsibilities:** With communication flowing through email, CRMs, and project management tools, what is considered the "single source of truth" for the current status of an onboarding property? 
*   **Exception Handling:** You mentioned landlords insisting on custom lease clauses. What is the legal review process for these custom clauses, and does it involve external legal counsel or an internal legal team?
*   **Process Improvement:** How does the company measure the success and efficiency of this onboarding process? What are the key performance indicators (KPIs) tracked (e.g., time-to-activation, first-time pass rate on inspections, time-to-lease)?
