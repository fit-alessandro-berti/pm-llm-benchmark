This exercise demonstrates the process of transforming complex, time-series operational data into a structured, high-level workflow map.

---

## 1. Proposed High-Level Process Steps

Based on the analysis of the event log, the manufacturing process can be broken down into four distinct, sequential phases.

| No. | High-Level Activity Name | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Material Staging & Preparation** | Handling the raw input, identification, positioning, and initial thermal setup. |
| **2** | **Core Assembly & Fabrication** | The specific steps involving the primary manufacturing action (welding, cutting, forming). |
| **3** | **Surface Treatment & Finishing** | Applying protective layers and necessary curing steps. |
| **4** | **Quality Assurance & Final Inspection** | Automated and manual verification of the product's integrity and finish. |

---

## 2. Rationale and Justification for Groupings

### Group 1: Material Staging & Preparation

**Low-Level Events Included:**
*   Retrieve raw metal sheet
*   Scan barcode
*   Place metal sheet on table
*   Align metal sheet
*   Preheat metal sheet

**Justification:**
These events occur at the very beginning of the process. They focus entirely on preparing the raw material (the input) and setting up the necessary physical and thermal environment before any critical manufacturing work begins. The sequence (Retrieve $\rightarrow$ Identify $\rightarrow$ Position $\rightarrow$ Heat) represents a coherent setup phase for the machine and material.

### Group 2: Core Assembly & Fabrication

**Low-Level Events Included:**
*   Pick up welding tool
*   Weld corner A
*   Weld corner B

**Justification:**
This group represents the central, active manufacturing step. It is defined by the execution of the primary task (welding). The sequence flows logically from tool acquisition to the execution of the required welding operations. This phase is the core transformation of the raw material into a semi-finished product.

### Group 3: Surface Treatment & Finishing

**Low-Level Events Included:**
*   Measure weld integrity
*   Apply protective coating
*   Dry coating

**Justification:**
These events immediately follow the core assembly. They focus on modifying the product's surface properties and ensuring the integrity of the newly fabricated part. The sequence (Check $\rightarrow$ Apply $\rightarrow$ Cure) defines the post-fabrication treatment necessary to finalize the product's physical state.

### Group 4: Quality Assurance & Final Inspection

**Low-Level Events Included:**
*   Visual check

**Justification:**
This is the concluding step, where the quality of the entire process is verified. The visual check acts as the final gate, confirming that the product meets the required standards after all fabrication and finishing steps are complete.

## 3. Summary of High-Level Activities

| High-Level Activity | Associated Process Stage | Key Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Material Staging & Preparation** | Input Handling | Preparing and positioning the raw material for processing. |
| **2. Core Assembly & Fabrication** | Manufacturing | Executing the primary mechanical/welding operations. |
| **3. Surface Treatment & Finishing** | Post-Processing | Applying necessary coatings and thermal treatments. |
| **4. Quality Assurance & Final Inspection**| Verification | Checking the final output quality and compliance. |
