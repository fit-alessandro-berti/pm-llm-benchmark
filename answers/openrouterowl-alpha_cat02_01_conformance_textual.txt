Based on the **normative process description**, we are given a clear set of rules governing the expected order and conditions under which activities should occur. Let’s summarize the normative constraints concisely before analyzing each case:

### **Normative Rules Summary (from the description):**

1. **"Receive order"** must be the first activity.
2. **"Check inventory"** must occur after "Receive order" and **before** any of:
   - "Authorize payment"
   - "Package items"
   - "Ship order"
   - "Cancel order"
3. **If inventory is sufficient**  sequence must follow:  
   `Check inventory  Authorize payment  Package items  Ship order  Close order`
4. **If inventory is insufficient**  sequence must follow:  
   `Check inventory  Cancel order  Close order`
5. **Never**:
   - "Authorize payment" **before** checking inventory.
   - "Package items" **before** "Authorize payment".
   - "Ship order" **after** "Cancel order".
   - "Close order" **not as the final activity**.
   - Perform **any fulfillment activity** (like packaging or shipping) **after cancellation**.

Also implicitly:
- After **"Cancel order"**, only **"Close order"** may occur.
- All cases must end with **"Close order"** (which they all do).
