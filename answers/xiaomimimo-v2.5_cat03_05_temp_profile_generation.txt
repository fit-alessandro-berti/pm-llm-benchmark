**Key considerations in these estimates:**

1. **Supplier-related activities (SS, OP, RC)**: These have longer lead times and higher variance due to external factors like logistics, customs, and supplier reliability.

2. **Quality inspection (QI)**: Relatively quick but with some variance depending on inspection thoroughness.

3. **Manufacturing steps (CA, PT)**: More controlled times but still subject to equipment availability and testing rigor.

4. **Post-production (PK, WS, DT)**: Packaging is fast, warehouse storage has moderate variance based on demand, and distribution varies by destination.

5. **After-sales support (AS)**: The longest time since this represents the time until support is needed, not the actual duration of support.

6. **Variance scaling**: Variance generally increases with time horizon, reflecting greater uncertainty over longer periods.

7. **Realistic relationships**: Notice how times accumulate logically - for example, SSAS  sum of SSOP + OPRC + RCQI + QICA + CAPT + PTPK + PKWS + WSDT + DTAS.

The standard deviations reflect industry-typical uncertainty: supplier deliveries have higher variance than internal manufacturing processes, and longer time horizons generally show proportionally higher uncertainty.
