Here is a structured set of open-ended questions I would ask to clarify and deepen my understanding of the property onboarding process:

## 1. Process scope and boundaries
- How do you define the formal start of the onboarding process, and what event marks it as complete?
- What types of properties or landlords follow this standard process, and which ones require a different path?
- How does the process vary by city, property type, or ownership structure?
- What are the major stages you use internally to track progress from first contact through move-in?
- What are the most common reasons a property does not make it all the way through onboarding?

## 2. Intake and documentation
- Can you walk me through what you consider a complete and acceptable documentation package from a landlord?
- How do coordinators determine whether documents are current, valid, and sufficient under local regulations?
- What usually happens when documentation is missing, outdated, or inconsistent?
- How do you decide whether onboarding can continue while certain documents are still pending?
- Who has authority to approve exceptions at the documentation stage?
- How are city-specific compliance requirements identified and applied during intake?
- What information about the property is most important to capture accurately up front, and what can be refined later?

## 3. Property data and pricing setup
- How is the expected rental price range initially determined?
- How do you handle situations where the landlord’s expectations differ from your internal pricing view?
- Who reviews or approves the initial pricing before marketing begins?
- What kinds of property details tend to cause downstream issues if they are entered incorrectly or incompletely at the start?

## 4. Roles and responsibilities
- How do you divide responsibilities among the property coordinator, property manager, leasing agent, marketing team, compliance officer, finance, and IT?
- Who is ultimately accountable for the success of the onboarding from end to end?
- How do regional offices and central support teams share ownership of the process?
- Where do handoffs between teams most often create confusion, delay, or rework?
- How are external parties—such as inspectors, photographers, and contractors—managed within the overall process?

## 5. Property manager assignment
- How do you balance workload, geography, property type expertise, and landlord-specific needs when assigning a property manager?
- Who makes the final assignment decision?
- Under what circumstances would a property be reassigned to a different manager later in the process?
- How do you handle cases where no available manager seems like an ideal fit?

## 6. Landlord alignment and expectation setting
- What are the key outcomes you expect from the introductory call or meeting with the landlord?
- How do you capture and confirm landlord preferences such as pets, furnishings, maintenance responsibilities, payment channels, or lease terms?
- How do you handle cases where the landlord’s preferences conflict with company policy, legal requirements, or market realities?
- What kinds of landlord expectations tend to create the most complexity later in the process?

## 7. Inspection, photography, and remediation
- How do you decide the sequencing of photography, inspection, repairs, pricing review, and listing preparation?
- Which of those activities can happen in parallel, and which are strict prerequisites?
- What criteria determine whether a property passes inspection, fails inspection, or can move forward conditionally?
- How is responsibility assigned when inspection findings require repairs or contractor work?
- How do you decide whether the landlord, the property manager, or another team drives the remediation effort?
- How do you handle disagreements between the inspector, landlord, and property manager about what needs to be fixed?
- What tends to cause the biggest delays between inspection findings and property readiness?

## 8. Marketing and activation
- At what point is marketing allowed to begin, and what must be true before that happens?
- How do you decide whether to market a property before all issues are fully resolved versus waiting until it is fully ready?
- What role does the digital marketing team play in shaping the listing beyond just posting the property?
- How do pricing updates based on market trends get incorporated before the property goes live?
- What does “activation” mean in practical terms for the teams involved?
- What approvals or checks must happen before a property is activated in the rental portal and internal systems?

## 9. Tenant screening and selection
- How do you define the screening criteria for applicants, and where is there room for judgment or flexibility?
- How do landlord preferences influence screening and selection while staying within fair housing and company policy limits?
- When there are multiple qualified applicants, how do you compare and prioritize them?
- Under what circumstances do you negotiate lease terms, offer promotional pricing, or favor longer lease commitments?
- Who makes the final tenant selection decision, and how are disagreements resolved?
- What are the most common reasons a promising applicant does not make it through to lease execution?

## 10. Lease execution and move-in
- What steps take place between selecting a tenant and finalizing the lease?
- How are custom lease clauses reviewed, approved, and documented?
- How do you collect and verify the security deposit and other required pre-move-in items?
- What needs to be confirmed before a move-in date can be scheduled?
- How do you handle cases where the selected tenant backs out, delays payment, or fails to complete the lease process?

## 11. Compliance, finance, and quality checks
- How do compliance, finance, and IT reviews fit into the overall sequence?
- What triggers those reviews, and are they applied to every property or based on risk?
- What kinds of issues are those teams most likely to find?
- How do you resolve situations where one review function approves something and another raises concerns?
- Who decides whether an issue found in a final review requires correction, escalation, or a pause in onboarding?

## 12. Exceptions and escalations
- What are the most common exceptions that fall outside the standard process?
- How do you handle properties that require special insurance, unusual licensing, or non-standard legal review?
- What happens when a landlord insists on custom lease language or special operational arrangements?
- How do you escalate urgent or high-risk cases, and who gets involved?
- Under what conditions would you pause onboarding or stop it entirely?

## 13. Timing, communication, and control
- What is the typical end-to-end timeline, and which stages tend to create the most variability?
- What service expectations or turnaround times exist for the major roles in the process?
- What events trigger movement from one stage to the next?
- How do you keep all stakeholders aligned when communication is spread across email, CRM, and project management tools?
- What information must be formally documented at each handoff so nothing gets lost?
- How do you track status and make sure work does not stall unnoticed?

## 14. Performance and improvement
- How do you measure whether onboarding was successful from the company’s perspective, the landlord’s perspective, and the tenant’s perspective?
- What bottlenecks or recurring pain points have you seen most often?
- Which part of the process feels most fragile or risk-prone, and why?
- Can you describe a recent onboarding case that did not follow the standard path and what that revealed about the process?
- If you could improve one part of the process first, what would it be and why?
