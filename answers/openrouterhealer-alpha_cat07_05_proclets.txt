### 1. Section (a): Interaction Flow Diagram
This is a sequence/communication diagram illustrating the end-to-end workflow between a single *Lab visit* instance and *Lab test* instances, with role stakeholders (doctor, lab assistant, chemical analyst) aligned on the sides:
- The process starts at **Lab visit**: An order entry is made, a blood sample is taken, then it connects to an *Order system* (a 1:1 channel) to trigger **Lab test**'s first step: perform the test (by a chemical analyst) and make a report.
- This report connects to an *HIS* (another 1:1 channel) to be received by **Lab visit**, where results are judged:
  - If results are clear ("Everything fine"), the process skips re-testing and sends the final report, ending the flow.
  - If results need confirmation, **Lab visit** initiates a re-test: this triggers **Lab test** to perform the test again, provide an updated report via HIS, and finally **Lab visit** sends the updated report to conclude.
The arrows show the direction of communication between the two class instances, with *1..1* denoting a fixed one-to-one relationship for each integration channel.
