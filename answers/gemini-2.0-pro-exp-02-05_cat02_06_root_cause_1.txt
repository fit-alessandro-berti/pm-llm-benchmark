*   **Impact on Cycle Time:**

        *   **Recommendation:** Categorize escalated tickets. If common L1 knowledge gaps exist, provide targeted training or better documentation/knowledge base access. If certain issues always require L2, perhaps they can be routed there directly after triage.
    4.  **Examine Long Investigation Durations:** The recurring ~19-hour investigation-to-resolution phase needs scrutiny.
        *   **Recommendation:** While non-working hours contribute, analyze if the *active* work time within this phase is excessive. Are agents waiting for information? Are tools inefficient? Could improved diagnostics or knowledge base articles speed up resolution for these complex/escalated cases? Consider tracking active vs. inactive time during investigation if possible.
    5.  **Review Support Hours:** The consistent overnight delays suggest business hours significantly impact resolution time for longer tasks.
        *   **Recommendation:** Evaluate the business impact of these overnight delays. If critical, consider options like staggered shifts, extended support hours, or on-call L2 support for urgent escalated issues, depending on cost-benefit analysis.

By addressing the bottlenecks identified, particularly the waiting times before L1/L2 investigation begins and streamlining the escalation process, the support team can significantly reduce the cycle time for complex tickets and improve overall performance.
