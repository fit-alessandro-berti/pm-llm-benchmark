**Diagnosing Scheduling Pathologies**

Upon analyzing the historical data, I would identify the key pathologies or inefficiencies in the current scheduling approach. These might include:

1.  **Bottleneck Resources:** Machines or operators consistently underutilized or overutilized, impacting overall throughput.
2.  **Poor Task Prioritization:** High-priority or near-due date jobs delayed due to suboptimal scheduling decisions.
3.  **Suboptimal Sequencing:** Tasks scheduled in a way that increases total setup times due to sequence-dependent setup times.
4.  **Resource Starvation:** Downstream resources consistently underutilized due to upstream scheduling decisions or bottlenecks.
5.  **Bullwhip Effect in WIP:** Variability in WIP levels due to scheduling variability.
