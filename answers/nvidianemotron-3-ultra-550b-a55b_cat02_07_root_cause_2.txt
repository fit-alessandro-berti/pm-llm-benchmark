### 2. Root Cause Analysis by Attribute

#### A. Complexity Level (Strongest Correlation)
There is a near-perfect positive correlation between **Complexity** and **Duration**.
*   **Low Complexity (2001, 2004):** 0 "Request Additional Documents" events. Straight-through processing.
*   **Medium Complexity (2002):** 1 "Request Additional Documents" event. Introduces a ~24-hour wait loop (Request sent 14:00 Day 1  Approve 10:00 Day 2).
*   **High Complexity (2003, 2005):** **Multiple** "Request Additional Documents" events.
    *   Case 2003: 2 requests (11:00 & 17:00 Day 1)  Approve Day 2 16:00.
    *   Case 2005: **3 requests** (11:30 Day 1, 17:00 Day 2, 15:00 Day 3)  Approve Day 4 10:00.

**Root Cause:** High-complexity claims require iterative information gathering. Each "Request Additional Documents" cycle introduces significant idle/wait time (likely waiting for customer response), multiplying the lead time non-linearly.

### 3. Explanations & Mitigation Strategies

#### Root Cause 1: Iterative "Request Additional Documents" Loops (The "Ping-Pong" Effect)
*   **Explanation:** High-complexity claims suffer from incomplete initial submissions. The process enters a loop: *Request  Wait for Customer  Receive  Review  Request Again*. Case 2005 required 3 cycles, adding ~3 days of pure wait time.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Dynamic Checklists / Smart Forms:** Implement intelligent submission forms that dynamically require specific documents based on claim type/complexity *at submission time* (Shift Left).
    *   **"Single Touch" Documentation Request:** Train adjusters (especially Adjuster_Lisa) to consolidate *all* foreseeable document requirements into the **first** request, rather than discovering missing pieces incrementally.
    *   **Automated Reminders / SLA:** Set strict SLAs for customer response (e.g., 24h) and auto-escalate/close stale requests.

#### Root Cause 2: Adjuster "First Time Right" Quality Variance
*   **Explanation:** Adjuster_Lisa (Region B) triggered 4 total document requests across 2 complex cases (2002, 2005) vs Adjuster_Mike's 2 requests for 1 complex case (2003). Case 2005's 3 requests suggest potential skill gap or lack of standardized "Complex Claim Document Pack" guidelines.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Standardized "Complex Claim Kit":** Create a mandatory checklist/template for High Complexity claims listing *all* typically required documents (Police reports, medical records, assessor photos, etc.) to be requested upfront.
    *   **Peer Review / Coaching:** Review Adjuster_Lisa's request patterns vs. Adjuster_Mike. Share best practices for comprehensive initial assessment.
    *   **Specialization:** Route all High Complexity claims to a specialized "Senior Adjuster" team (potentially Adjuster_Mike if he performs better) to reduce rework loops.

#### Root Cause 3: Lack of Triage / Early Complexity Detection
*   **Explanation:** Cases 2003 and 2005 enter the standard evaluation queue but immediately spawn document requests. The process does not distinguish "Straight-Through" vs "Investigation" paths early enough.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Automated Triage at Submit:** Use rules/ML on submission data (Claim Type, Amount, Keywords) to flag "High Complexity" instantly.
    *   **Dedicated "Investigation" Swimlane:** Route flagged cases immediately to a specialized team with authority to request *all* docs in one go and manage customer communication, isolating the long lead times from standard KPI reporting.

#### Root Cause 4: Synchronous Customer Dependency
*   **Explanation:** The process halts completely at "Request Additional Documents" (e.g., Case 2005 waits ~24h between Request 1 & 2, and ~22h between Request 2 & 3). The process is synchronous to an external actor (Customer).
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Self-Service Portal / Upload:** Allow customers to upload docs directly via portal/link in request email, triggering automatic "Document Received" event, reducing manual handoff latency.
    *   **Parallel Processing:** Where possible, allow "Evaluate Claim" to proceed on partial data (e.g., liability assessment) while waiting for quantum (damage) docs.
