**Explanation of the constructed Petri net:**

### Structure Overview

| Sequence | Place | Transition | Next Place |
|----------|-------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | `p_start` | Insurance Claim (C) | `p_after_c` |
| 2 | `p_after_c` | Claim Registration (CR) | `p_after_cr` |
| 3 | `p_after_cr` | Document Verification (DV) | `p_after_dv` |
| 4a (loop) | `p_after_dv` | Re-Document Submission (RDS) | `p_after_cr`  back to before DV |
| 4b (proceed) | `p_after_dv` | *silent* (docs_ok) | `p_before_fc` |
| 5 | `p_before_fc` | Fraud Check (FC) | `p_after_fc` |
| 6a (loop) | `p_after_fc` | In-depth Investigation (II) | `p_before_fc`  back to before FC |
| 6b (proceed) | `p_after_fc` | Claim Assessment (CA) | `p_after_ca` |
| 7 | `p_after_ca` | Approval (AP) | `p_after_ap` |
| 8 | `p_after_ap` | Payment (P) | `p_after_p` |
| 9 | `p_after_p` | Notification (N) | `p_after_n` |
| 10 | `p_after_n` | Closure (CL) | `p_end` |

### Key Design Decisions

1. **DV/RDS Loop:** After Document Verification (`p_after_dv`), the token can either fire `RDS` (returning to `p_after_cr` for another round of DV) or pass through a **silent transition** (`docs_ok`) to `p_before_fc`. The silent transition is crucial to separate the document re-submission decision from the fraud investigation decision.

2. **FC/II Loop:** After Fraud Check (`p_after_fc`), the token can either fire `II` (returning to `p_before_fc` for another FC) or proceed directly to `CA`. No silent transition is needed here because `II` and `CA` simply compete for the token at `p_after_fc`, and the II loop feeds back only to `p_before_fc` (not to the DV choice point).

3. **11 places, 12 transitions** (including 1 silent), and **24 arcs** form the complete net.
