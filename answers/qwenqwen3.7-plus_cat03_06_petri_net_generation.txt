### Explanation of the Structure:
1. **Places (`p_...`)**: Represent the state of the claim between activities. `p_start` holds the initial token, and `p_end` is the target for the final token.
2. **Transitions (`t_...`)**: Represent the actual activities. Each is given a unique internal name and a human-readable `label` matching the scenario description.
3. **Arcs**: 
   - Form a primary linear backbone from Registration to Closure.
   - Introduce **Loop 1** at `p_after_dv`, allowing the flow to divert to `t_rds` and loop back to `t_dv` if documents are incomplete.
   - Introduce **Loop 2** at `p_after_fc`, allowing the flow to divert to `t_ii` and loop back to `t_fc` if fraud doubts arise.
4. **Markings**: `im` initializes the net with a single token at `p_start`, simulating a newly filed claim. `fm` defines the successful completion state with a single token resting at `p_end`.
