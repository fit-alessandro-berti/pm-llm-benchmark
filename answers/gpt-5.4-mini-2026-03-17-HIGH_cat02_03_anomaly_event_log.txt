Yes — several cases show activity orders that conflict with a normal Procure-to-Pay (P2P) flow.

## Expected “normal” P2P sequence
A typical P2P process is usually:

1. Create Purchase Requisition  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Issue Purchase Order  
4. Receive Goods  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Match Invoice to PO / 3-way match  
7. Release Payment  
8. Close Case

## Case-by-case anomaly review

### Case 1001 — Looks normal
Sequence:

- Create PR
- Approve PR
- Issue PO
- Receive Goods
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Match Invoice to PO
- Release Payment
- Close Case

This is the expected flow and does not look anomalous.

### Case 1002 — Highly anomalous
Sequence:

- Create PR
- Issue PO
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Release Payment
- Approve PR
- Receive Goods
- Match Invoice to PO
- Close Case

#### Why this is problematic:
- **PO issued before PR approval**  
  That suggests an order was placed without proper authorization.
- **Payment released before goods receipt**  
  The company paid before confirming that goods were actually delivered.
- **Payment released before invoice matching**  
  This bypasses the normal control step that ensures the invoice matches the PO and, often, the goods receipt.
- **PR approved after payment**  
  Approval should happen before committing funds or issuing the PO.
- **Goods received after payment**  
  This is risky because the company may have already paid for goods not yet verified.

#### Business concern:
This sequence weakens internal controls and increases the risk of:
- unauthorized purchases,
- overpayment,
- fraud,
- paying for undelivered or incorrect goods.

### Case 1003 — Anomalous
Sequence:

- Create PR
- Approve PR
- Issue PO
- Release Payment
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Receive Goods
- Match Invoice to PO
- Close Case

#### Why this is problematic:
- **Payment occurs before invoice receipt**  
  In standard P2P, the invoice should exist before payment.
- **Payment occurs before goods receipt**  
  This means the organization paid before confirming delivery.
- **Payment occurs before invoice matching**  
  The invoice was not validated against the PO before payment.
- **Invoice arrives after payment**  
  That is unusual and indicates a broken or out-of-order control process.

#### Business concern:
This can cause:
- paying without evidence of liability,
- duplicate or unsupported payments,
- accounting and audit issues,
- loss of control over vendor obligations.

### Case 1004 — Anomalous
Sequence:

- Create PR
- Approve PR
- Receive Goods
- Issue PO
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Match Invoice to PO
- Release Payment
- Close Case

#### Why this is problematic:
- **Goods received before PO issuance**  
  Normally the PO authorizes the supplier to ship goods, so receipt before PO is suspicious.
- It may indicate the goods were received without formal ordering or that the process is missing an important control step.

#### Business concern:
This can create:
- difficulty matching receipts to orders,
- unauthorized or off-contract purchasing,
- audit/compliance problems,
- risk that the company accepts goods it never formally ordered.
