### Anomalies and Undesirable Behaviors Identified

1. **Out-of-Order Activities**  
   - **Case 1002**: **Confirm Shipment** (event_id 9) occurs **before** **Perform Credit Check** (event_id 11) and **Validate Stock** (event_id 12).  
     *Normal flow expects Credit Check and Stock Validation to happen **before** Shipment confirmation.*  
   - **Case 1003**: **Ship Goods** (event_id 17) occurs **before** **Perform Credit Check** (event_id 16).

   - **Case 1004**: **Receive Payment** (event_id 22) occurs **before** **Confirm Shipment** (event_id 24), **Issue Invoice** (event_id 23), and **Ship Goods** (event_id 25).  
     *Payment received prior to any shipment or invoicing suggests either a billing error or early payment processing, which may violate policy requiring shipment/invoicing first.*

2. **Missing Critical Steps**  
   - **Case 1004**: No **Perform Credit Check** or **Validate Stock** entries.  
     *Both are essential for risk assessment and inventory verification.*  
   - **Case 1003**: **Confirm Shipment** (event_id 19) occurs after **Ship Goods** (event_id 17). While not strictly outoforder for some workflows, it appears after the goods have already been shipped, potentially indicating a late confirmation or a procedural oversight.  

3. **Potential Policy Violations**  
   - **Early Payment**: **Case 1004** shows payment before shipment/invoicing, which may breach contractual terms requiring shipment first.  
   - **HighRisk Credit Scores**: **Case 1002** has a credit score of **650**, below typical approval thresholds (e.g., 700). This flag may precede a rejected order yet proceeds to shipping, hinting at insufficient credit screening.  

4. **Resource Role Inconsistencies**  
   - **Case 1002**: **CreditOfficer_2** performed the credit check but appears **after** **Confirm Shipment**, raising questions about whether the credit review was completed promptly.  
   - **Case 1003**: **LogisticsMgr_2** handles **Confirm Shipment** and **late_confirmation=Y**, suggesting possible bottlenecks in logistics approval processes.  

### Hypotheses for Anomalies

- **System Timing / Scheduling Errors**: Automated triggers might be misconfigured, causing activities to fire out of order or prematurely.  
- **Policy Enforcement Gaps**: Lack of checks ensuring that prerequisite activities (Credit Check, Stock Validation) are completed before downstream steps (Shipment, Invoice).  
- **Data Entry or ETL Bugs**: Inconsistencies in the `additional_info` column (e.g., missing credit scores) could lead to downstream processing without proper risk assessment.  
- **Resource Training Issues**: Staff unfamiliar with the required sequence may inadvertently log events in an incorrect order.  
- **Business Process Changes**: Recent policy changes (e.g., allowing early payments for certain customers) may not be reflected in the logging schema, leading to legitimate but flagged records.  

### SQL Queries to Investigate

1. **Detect OutofOrder Steps**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id, event_id, activity
   FROM order_event_log
   WHERE case_id IN (
       SELECT case_id
       FROM order_event_log
       WHERE activity = 'Confirm Shipment'
       INTERSECT
       SELECT case_id
       FROM order_event_log
       WHERE activity IN ('Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock')
          AND timestamp < (SELECT timestamp FROM order_event_log WHERE case_id = order_event_log.case_id AND activity = 'Confirm Shipment')
   )
   ORDER BY case_id, event_id;
   ```
   *Purpose*: Identify cases where Shipment Confirmation precedes Credit Check or Stock Validation.

2. **Check for Missing Credit Checks**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id
   FROM order_event_log
   GROUP BY case_id
   HAVING COUNT(CASE WHEN activity = 'Perform Credit Check' THEN 1 END) = 0;
   ```
   *Purpose*: List orders lacking any credit check record.

3. **Identify Early Payments**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id, MIN(timestamp) AS first_event_ts
   FROM order_event_log
   WHERE activity IN ('Receive Payment', 'Issue Invoice', 'Ship Goods', 'Confirm Shipment')
   GROUP BY case_id
   HAVING MIN(timestamp) < (
       SELECT MIN(timestamp)
       FROM order_event_log o2
       WHERE o2.case_id = order_event_log.case_id
         AND o2.activity IN ('Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock', 'Issue Invoice', 'Ship Goods')
   );
   ```
   *Purpose*: Find orders where the first logged event is Payment, preceding any riskassessment or fulfillment steps.

4. **Analyze Credit Scores and Followup Actions**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id, additional_info, activity
   FROM order_event_log
   WHERE additional_info LIKE '%credit_score%'
   ORDER BY case_id, timestamp;
   ```
   *Purpose*: Extract credit scores and correlate with subsequent actions (e.g., if a low score appears before Shipment).

5. **CrossReference Resources with Expected Roles**  
   ```sql
   SELECT oel.case_id, oel.activity, oel.resource, r.role, r.department
   FROM order_event_log oel
   JOIN resources r ON oel.resource = r.resource_id
   WHERE oel.activity = 'Perform Credit Check' AND r.department <> 'Finance'
   ORDER BY oel.case_id, oel.timestamp;
   ```
   *Purpose*: Verify that credit checks are performed by financedepartment resources; deviations may indicate policy breaches.

6. **Timeline Visualization for Each Case**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id,
          STRING_AGG(activity || ':' || timestamp::text, ', ' ORDER BY timestamp) AS timeline
   FROM order_event_log
   GROUP BY case_id;
   ```
   *Purpose*: Produce a readable chronological string of events per order for manual inspection.

7. **Count of Activities per Case**  
   ```sql
   SELECT case_id, COUNT(*) AS total_events
   FROM order_event_log
   GROUP BY case_id;
   ```
   *Purpose*: Spot cases with unusually low or high numbers of events, which may indicate incomplete or redundant processing.
