### **Executive Summary: The "Compliance-First" Loan Origination Flow**

This process is designed as a **strictly sequential, gated workflow** where progression is contingent on the successful completion of mandatory predecessor steps. It embodies a "Compliance-by-Design" philosophy: the model mathematically guarantees that funds cannot be disbursed until quality assurance, authorization, and documentation are verified, while explicitly forbidding the "Proceed_Without_Compliance" activity.

### **Phase 1: Intake & Triage (The Mandatory Gateway)**

**1. `Receive_Application` (Init + Existence)**
*   **Process Step:** The process instance is created. A customer submits an application (digital portal, branch, broker).
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`init`**: This is the *only* valid starting point. No process instance exists without this event.
    *   **`existence`**: Guarantees every case has an application record. Prevents "ghost" processes or testing artifacts entering production.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** Audit trail integrity (GDPR/CCPA), KYC (Know Your Customer) initiation, and resource allocation triggering.

**2. `Preliminary_Credit_Check` (Exactly_One + Response from Receive_Application)**
*   **Process Step:** Automated or semi-automated bureau pull (soft/hard inquiry) and internal scorecard execution.
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`response(Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`**: **Hard Sequencing.** The check *must* follow the application immediately. No manual "gather documents" or "assemble offer" can intervene.
    *   **`exactly_one`**: Prevents "double-dipping" credit inquiries (protects applicant credit score) and prevents logic loops where a system might re-trigger checks on retries.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Risk Management & Fair Lending (ECOA/Reg B).** Early decline/routing decision prevents wasting operational capacity on unqualified leads. `exactly_one` ensures compliance with credit reporting agency contracts and consumer protection laws regarding inquiry frequency.

**3. `Gather_Additional_Documents` (Existence + Succession from Credit Check + Coexistence with Authorization)**
*   **Process Step:** Document collection (pay stubs, tax returns, ID verification) triggered *only* after credit passes thresholds.
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`succession(Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents)`**: **Strict Ordering.** Documents are requested *after* credit viability is confirmed.
    *   **`coexistence(Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms)`**: **Bidirectional Dependency.** You cannot finalize terms (`Authorize`) without documents (`Gather`), and practically, you wouldn't request documents (`Gather`) if you weren't intending to potentially authorize.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Operational Efficiency & Fraud Prevention (KYC/AML).** Prevents "document farming" (collecting PII from applicants who fail credit checks), reducing data breach surface area and processing costs. `coexistence` ensures the underwriting package is complete before legal terms are set.

### **Phase 2: Manufacturing & Quality Control (The "Offer Factory")**

**4. `Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package` (Responded_Existence  QA Review + ChainResponse  Transfer_Funds)**
*   **Process Step:** Loan officers/system generate the specific offer: rate, term, fees, amortization schedule, legal disclosures (TILA/RESPA).
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`responded_existence(Assemble  Quality_Assurance_Review)`**: **Mandatory Peer Review.** Every assembled package *must* eventually be reviewed by QA. It does not require immediate succession (allows parallel work), but enforces eventual consistency.
    *   **`chainresponse(Assemble  Transfer_Funds)`**: **Downstream Trigger.** Assembly initiates the *chain* leading to disbursement, but critically, the chain passes through QA and Authorization first (via other constraints).
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Consumer Protection (TILA/Reg Z).** Accuracy of APR, finance charges, and payment schedules is legally mandated. `responded_existence` implements the "Four-Eyes Principle" / Maker-Checker control required by SOX and Basel III operational risk frameworks.

**5. `Quality_Assurance_Review` (Precedence  Authorize + ChainSuccession  Assemble)**
*   **Process Step:** Independent verification of calculations, compliance checks (HMDA data integrity), fee tolerance cures, and document completeness.
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`precedence(Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms)`**: **Gatekeeper.** Authorization *cannot* happen unless QA has occurred (at some point prior). QA is the "license to operate" for the authorizer.
    *   **`chainsuccession(Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package)`**: **Immediate Feedback Loop.** If QA fails, the *immediate* next step *must* be re-assembly (correction). It prevents "skipping" back to document gathering or credit check unless explicitly modeled elsewhere.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Defect Prevention & Regulatory Exam Readiness.** Catches "Tolerance Violations" (fees changing >0% or 10% limits) before the customer sees them, avoiding costly re-disclosures and regulatory fines (CFPB UDAAP).

**6. `Authorize_Contract_Terms` (ChainPrecedence  Credit Check + NonChainSuccession  Notify_Customer)**
*   **Process Step:** Final sign-off by authorized signatory (Credit Committee, Delegated Authority Officer).
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`chainprecedence(Authorize  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`**: **Root Cause Traceability.** Authorization implies the *original* credit check (the chain start) happened. Prevents "stale" approvals on old credit data.
    *   **`nonchainsuccession(Authorize  Notify_Customer)`**: **Process Separation.** Authorization and Notification are distinct events. They cannot happen in the *exact same atomic transaction/step*. There must be a system state change (e.g., "Approved" status write) between them.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Credit Policy Adherence & Data Freshness.** Regulators (OCC/Fed) require credit decisions to be based on current data. `chainprecedence` ensures the approval references the specific `Preliminary_Credit_Check` that started this instance. `nonchainsuccession` prevents "auto-spam" notifications before core banking systems have persisted the contract state.

### **Phase 3: Settlement & Closure (The "Point of No Return")**

**7. `Transfer_Funds` (AltResponse  Notify_Customer + NonCoexistence with Receive_Application)**
*   **Process Step:** Treasury executes wire/ACH to customer account or seller (purchase money mortgage).
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`altresponse(Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer)`**: **Alternative Response.** Notification *must* follow disbursement, but other steps *could* theoretically occur in between (e.g., core banking posting batch). However, `altprecedence` tightens this.
    *   **`noncoexistence(Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application)`**: **Mutual Exclusion (Temporal Separation).** The start (`Receive`) and the end (`Transfer`) cannot coexist in the "active" state of a single case in a way that implies concurrency. Practically: **You cannot disburse funds on the same logical tick as receiving the app.** It enforces a minimum process duration/latency.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Settlement Risk & Reconciliation.** `noncoexistence` enforces the "T+1" or "T+3" settlement reality—funds move *after* contract execution, not at application. Prevents "flash loan" style logic errors where a system might approve and fund instantly before fraud checks clear.

**8. `Notify_Customer` (AltPrecedence  Transfer_Funds + NonSuccession from Credit Check)**
*   **Process Step:** Multi-channel notification (Email, SMS, Portal, Letter) with final terms, funding confirmation, and repayment schedule.
*   **Constraint Enforcement:**
    *   **`altprecedence(Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds)`**: **Funds First, Talk Later.** Notification *must* be preceded by Transfer. The customer is never told "Funds Sent" before the Treasury system confirms `Transfer_Funds` executed.
    *   **`nonsuccession(Notify_Customer  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`**: **No Direct Link.** Notification never immediately follows the initial credit check. This prevents the "Instant Decline/Spam" pattern where a system might notify a customer of a decision before the human/QA loop completes.
*   **Real-World Motivation:** **Customer Experience & Reputational Risk.** Prevents the nightmare scenario: "You told me funds were sent (Notify), but the wire failed (no Transfer)." `altprecedence` makes `Transfer_Funds` a **hard prerequisite** for the "Funded" notification. `nonsuccession` ensures a cooling-off/processing period between triage and final communication.

### **The "Forbidden" Guardrail**

*   **`absence(Proceed_Without_Compliance)`**: This activity **never occurs** in any valid trace.
*   **Practical Meaning:** This is the model's "Assert False." It represents any shortcut: "Auto-approve without QA," "Fund without Auth," "Skip Credit Check." The model proves that *no sequence of valid activities* can produce this state. It is the formal verification of the control environment.
