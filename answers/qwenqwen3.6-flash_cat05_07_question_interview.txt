Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify the property onboarding process, uncover decision logic, define roles, and understand exception handling. The questions are grouped by process theme to facilitate a structured discussion.

### 1. Documentation Verification & Data Entry
*   **Validation Logic:** Can you describe the specific criteria the property coordinator uses to determine if a document is "missing," "outdated," or "invalid"? Are there different thresholds for critical documents (e.g., deeds) versus supporting documents?
*   **Data Source & Integrity:** When recording property details into the central database, are these fields manually entered, or is there a standard intake form? How do you ensure consistency in data fields like "expected rental price range" across different regional offices?
*   **Stop-Gates:** What happens if a landlord cannot provide a critical document (e.g., a certificate of compliance) within a reasonable timeframe? Is the onboarding process paused indefinitely, or is there a provisional status?

### 2. Property Manager Assignment & Handoffs
*   **Assignment Algorithm:** How is "workload" quantified for a property manager? Is it based on the number of active units, square footage, or time spent per property? How does the assignment logic prioritize geographic specialization versus experience with specific property types?
*   **Role Transition:** What is the formal handoff process between the property coordinator and the assigned property manager? Is there a checklist or a shared document that transfers context from the documentation phase to the manager?
*   **Intro Call Outcomes:** During the introductory meeting, how are special conditions (e.g., custom lease clauses, pet policies) documented? Do these outcomes trigger any changes to the standard operating procedure or require approval from a supervisor?

### 3. Inspection, Repairs & Marketing Preparation
*   **Inspection Standards:** What constitutes a "pass" vs. a "fail" in the inspection report? Are there distinct categories for safety issues versus habitability issues, and do they trigger different response workflows?
*   **Repair Management:** If issues are found, how does the property manager decide whether to hire contractors or schedule repairs themselves? What is the escalation path if the landlord disputes the inspection findings or refuses to authorize repairs?
*   **Marketing Sequencing:** How does the digital marketing team receive the property data? Is the listing creation dependent on the final inspection sign-off, or can marketing materials be drafted in parallel while repairs are underway?
*   **Pricing Strategy:** You mentioned updating internal pricing models based on market trends. How frequently are these models updated, and who has the authority to adjust the rental price range after it has been initially recorded?

### 4. Activation & Tenant Screening
*   **Activation Trigger:** What is the exact trigger that "activates" the property in the rental portal? Is it a manual action by the manager, or an automated system event once marketing assets are live?
*   **Screening Criteria:** Can you walk me through the weighting of factors during tenant screening? For example, how do credit scores, employment verification, and previous landlord references interact? Are there hard thresholds that automatically disqualify an applicant?
*   **Competitive Selection:** When multiple tenants are interested, what is the decision matrix for selecting the final candidate? Beyond lease length and discounts, are there other criteria (e.g., income-to-rent ratio, guarantors) used to break ties?
*   **Concession Authority:** What is the approval workflow for offering promotional discounts or negotiating non-standard lease terms? Do property managers have a defined budget or limit for concessions?

### 5. Compliance, Financials & System Integration
*   **Compliance Timing:** Is the compliance officer's review of the lease a pre-signature gatekeeper step, or is it a post-signature audit? How are potential fair housing violations identified and resolved before the lease is executed?
*   **Financial Validation:** How does the financial analyst verify that the rental price matches profitability targets? Is this a review of the initial pricing model, or a check against the final signed lease amount?
*   **IT Integration:** When the IT department checks system integration, what specific data points are validated? How are discrepancies between the internal database, the rental portal, and the CRM resolved?
*   **Audit Trail:** You mentioned communication is tracked via email, CRM, and PM tools. How do you ensure that the "single source of truth" remains consistent across these different channels? Is there a central repository for all onboarding artifacts?

### 6. Exception Handling & Edge Cases
*   **Custom Clauses:** Can you describe the typical approval workflow for landlords who insist on custom lease clauses? Which stakeholders (legal, risk, operations) are involved, and what is the maximum turnaround time for such requests?
*   **Special Insurance:** How are properties requiring special insurance identified? Does this affect the onboarding timeline, and is there a designated team to coordinate with insurance providers?
*   **Landlord Friction:** How does the process handle landlords who are unresponsive or difficult to work with during the onboarding phase? Are there defined escalation paths or service level agreements (SLAs) for landlord interactions?
*   **Rejection Handling:** If a property fails inspection or documentation review and the landlord chooses not to proceed, how is the property record handled in the system? Is it archived, or is there a "blacklist" flag for future reference?

### 7. Metrics & Continuous Improvement
*   **Process Bottlenecks:** Are there specific steps in the onboarding process that are frequently identified as bottlenecks by regional offices or property managers?
*   **Success Definition:** How does the company measure the success of the onboarding process? Are there KPIs related to time-to-lease, documentation accuracy, or landlord satisfaction?
*   **Feedback Loop:** How do insights from the tenant screening and leasing phases feed back into the documentation or pricing steps for future onboarding cycles?

These questions aim to move beyond the high-level description to understand the operational nuances, decision points, and governance structures that govern the property onboarding lifecycle.
