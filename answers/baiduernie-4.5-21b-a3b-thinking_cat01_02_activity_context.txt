### Justification for Naming Choices  
The renamed activities emphasize contextual differences by highlighting two key dimensions: **when the activity occurs (initial/subsequent)** and **the purpose/context of the activity (clarification, adjustment, verification)**. Here’s why each change was made:  

#### 1. **Verify Documents (C001)**  
   - **Initial Document Verification**: Reflects the first occurrence (BackOfficeAgent checks identity documents when the customer first submits a request via ID scan).  
   - **Re-verification of Documents**: Reflects the second occurrence (BackOfficeAgent re-checks documents after the customer provides an ID scan, ensuring correctness post-update).  

#### 2. **Call Customer (C002)**  
   - **Initial Customer Clarification Call**: Reflects the first outbound call (FrontlineAgent clarifies an account discrepancy—initial step to resolve the issue).  
   - **Follow-up Confirmation Call**: Reflects the second call (FrontlineAgent follows up after adjustments are made to confirm customer satisfaction—final step in resolution).

   - **Re-verification of Documents (After Adjustment)**: Reflects the second verification (BranchOfficer re-checks documents after an internal account adjustment—ensuring docs remain valid post-update).  

These names disambiguate the activities by linking each occurrence to its unique context (e.g., "initial" vs. "subsequent," "clarification" vs. "follow-up," "physical" vs. "adjustment-triggered"). This clarity helps stakeholders quickly understand the process flow—e.g., distinguishing between a first verification and a re-verification after an update, or between a clarification call and a follow-up confirmation call.
