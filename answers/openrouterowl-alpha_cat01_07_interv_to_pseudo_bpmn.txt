### Standard Invoice Approval Process (BPMN-like Textual Model)

**Process:** Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling

**Participants:**
*   **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary):** Initiates the process, logs invoices, checks for POs, matches invoices, schedules payments, handles initial supplier contact.
*   **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita):** Handles escalations for unresolved discrepancies, coordinates exceptions, interacts with department heads and suppliers.
*   **Purchasing Officer (Dan):** Confirms goods/services receipt, verifies invoice against PO, communicates with suppliers for corrections.
*   **Department Manager (Carlos):** Provides final approval on validated invoices.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** Indirectly involved for supplier relationship management, helps resolve terms discrepancies, provides missing PO numbers or correct pricing.

**Artifacts:**
*   **Invoice:** The primary document triggering the process.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference document for validation.
*   **Accounting System:** Used for logging, matching, and approval workflow.
*   **Corrected Invoice:** A revised version of the invoice.
*   **Retroactive (Retrospective) PO:** Created for informal service contracts.

**1. Process Start:**
    The process begins when an **Accounts Payable Clerk** receives an **Invoice** (usually by email).

**2. Check for Purchase Order (PO):**
    *   The **Accounts Payable Clerk** checks if the **Invoice** has a valid **Purchase Order (PO)** number.
    *   **Decision Point: Is there a valid PO?**
        *   **Yes (Standard Path):** Proceed to "Log Invoice and Forward for Receipt Confirmation".
        *   **No (Exception: Missing PO):**
            *   The **Accounts Payable Clerk** sends the **Invoice** back to the **Supplier** requesting more details (specifically the **PO number**).
            *   Sometimes, the **Purchasing Officer** may inform the **Accounts Payable Clerk** to get the **PO number** first.
            *   If the issue persists or involves a key supplier, the **Supplier Relationship Manager** may be contacted.
            *   **Until the valid PO is provided and the Invoice is updated, the process cannot proceed further on this path.**

**3. Log Invoice and Forward for Receipt Confirmation:**
    *   The **Accounts Payable Clerk** logs the **Invoice** into the **Accounting System**.
    *   The **Accounts Payable Clerk** then forwards the **Invoice** (or its details) to the **Purchasing Department** (specifically the **Purchasing Officer**) to confirm that the goods or services were actually received.

**4. Verify Invoice Against PO and Confirm Receipt:**
    *   The **Purchasing Officer** compares the **Invoice** details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) with the **Purchase Order (PO)**.
    *   The **Purchasing Officer** confirms the receipt of goods/services.
    *   **Decision Point: Is the Invoice correct and receipt confirmed?**
        *   **Yes (Standard Path):** The **Purchasing Officer** sends a "confirmation" email back to the **Accounts Payable Clerk**. Proceed to "Match Invoice Line Items".
        *   **No (Exception: Discrepancy/Missing Info):**
            *   There's a discrepancy (e.g., incorrect pricing, extra units) or missing information.
            *   The **Purchasing Officer** either contacts the **Supplier** directly or asks the **Accounts Payable Clerk** to hold the **Invoice** until a **Corrected Invoice** is sent.
            *   **If the discrepancy cannot be resolved easily (by AP Clerk or Purchasing Officer), it is escalated to the Accounts Payable Supervisor.**
                *   **Accounts Payable Supervisor** tries to contact **Suppliers**, coordinates between departments. May involve **Supplier Relationship Manager** for contract/pricing issues.
                *   **If the discrepancy involves services contracted informally (no PO), the Accounts Payable Supervisor works with Department Heads to create a Retroactive (Retrospective) PO.**
                *   **If the invoice is ultimately deemed invalid/unresolvable, it may be rejected and the process ends.** Otherwise, once the discrepancy is resolved, the process loops back to the point where the **Purchasing Officer** can confirm the (now correct) **Invoice**.

**5. Match Invoice Line Items:**
    *   After receiving confirmation from the **Purchasing Officer**, the **Accounts Payable Clerk** matches the **Invoice** line items against the **PO** in the **Accounting System**.
    *   **Decision Point: Are all line items matched?**
        *   **Yes (Standard Path):** Proceed to "Final Invoice Approval".
        *   **No (Exception: New Discrepancy):** The **Accounts Payable Clerk** emails the **Purchasing Team** to clarify. This effectively links back to the "Verify Invoice Against PO and Confirm Receipt" step for re-evaluation.

**6. Final Invoice Approval:**
    *   The **Accounting System** sends an automatic notification to the **Department Manager** for approval. This notification includes **Invoice details**, **PO**, and **Purchasing Officer's confirmation**.
    *   The **Department Manager** reviews the details.
    *   **Decision Point: Is the Invoice approved by the Manager?**
        *   **Yes (Standard Path):** The **Department Manager** approves. Proceed to "Schedule for Payment".
        *   **No (Exception: Manager Rejection/Investigation Required):**
            *   The **Department Manager** sees something suspicious (e.g., large budget difference, unexpected items).
            *   The **Department Manager** may reject the **Invoice** or ask the **Accounts Payable Clerk** to hold it and investigate further.
            *   **This links back to the Accounts Payable Clerk or triggers further investigation/escalation (similar to previous exception handling) until the issue is resolved or the invoice is formally rejected/withdrawn.**

**7. Schedule for Payment:**
    *   Once approved by the **Department Manager**, the **Accounts Payable Clerk** schedules the **Invoice** for payment in the next payment cycle.
    *   The **Accounting System** initiates the payment process.

**8. Process End:**
    The **Invoice** is successfully scheduled for payment.

**Summary of Key Ambiguities/Uncertainties:**

*   **Order of PO Check vs. Logging:** Mary states she checks for PO *before* logging it into the system. Dan mentions receiving the invoice *from Accounts Payable*. This implies the initial PO check and validation happens *before* formal logging in the system, or at least before Purchasing gets involved. The model reflects Mary's account as the initial trigger.
*   **Escalation Point for Purchasing Officer's Issues:** While Mary says she escalates if *she* can't resolve a discrepancy, Dan says *they* might contact the supplier. The model shows escalation to the Accounts Payable Supervisor for *any* unresolved discrepancy, regardless of who identifies it, leading to a coordinated effort.
*   **Formal Rejection Process:** The transcripts mention invoices being "sent back," "held," or "escalated," but a formal end-state for ultimate rejection (e.g., if a supplier doesn't respond or an informal service is not approved) isn't explicitly detailed as a distinct step. It's implied as an outcome of the investigation/escalation paths.
*   **System Interaction:** The "Accounting System" is mentioned as a tool for logging, matching, and approvals, but the depth of its automation (e.g., automatic email triggers) is noted primarily for the manager's approval.
*   **Karen's Role:** Karen acts as a consultant for specific supplier-related issues and doesn't directly influence the main workflow steps unless her intervention is required for exception handling.
