## **Step 5: Recommendations to Mitigate Performance Issues**

###  **1. Implement Complexity-Based Triage & Escalation**
- **Action:** Create a **tiered processing system**:
  - **Low Complexity**: Auto-approve if data complete (e.g., Case 2001, 2004)
  - **Medium Complexity**: Assign to **senior adjusters** with SLA of <24 hours
  - **High Complexity**: Automatically escalate to **specialized team** or **senior manager** upon submission

>  **Tool:** Use **complexity score** based on claim type, amount, history, and region.

---

###  **2. Optimize Resource Allocation & Workload Balancing**
- **Action:** Monitor **adjuster workload** (e.g., # of cases, average time per case).
  - **Adjuster_Lisa** is handling multiple high-complexity cases  **reassign** some to **Manager_Bill** or **new specialist**.
  - Implement **daily case review meetings** to catch delays early.

>  **KPI to Track:** Avg. time per activity per adjuster.

---

###  **3. Standardize Document Request Process**
- **Action:** 
  - Require **complete list of missing documents** in **first request**.
  - Use **template-based request forms** to avoid ambiguity.
  - Set **SLA for response** (e.g., 24 hours) and **automated reminders** if not met.

>  **Fix:** Case 2005 had **3 requests**  likely due to unclear initial request.

---

###  **4. Regional Performance Benchmarking**
- **Action:** Compare **lead times by region** for similar complexity.
  - **Region A** handles high-complexity claims faster than Region B  **benchmark best practices** from Region A.
  - Conduct **cross-region training** (especially on handling high-complexity cases).

>  **KPI:** Regional average lead time per complexity level.

---

###  **5. Introduce Automation & Alerts**
- **Action:**
  - **Auto-alert** if:
    - A claim exceeds 12 hours without document request (for medium/high).
    - A document request is made more than once without progress.
    - Approval is delayed >24 hours after last update.
- **Use process mining tools** to detect bottlenecks in real time.
