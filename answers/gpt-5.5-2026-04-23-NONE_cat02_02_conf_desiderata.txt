The absence of a Follow-up Call, if considered relevant, may simply indicate that the organization chose not to perform the optional activity because the applicant had already been promptly informed and the documentation was finalized quickly.

### Case 2

Case 2 suggests serious process control problems.

Possible interpretations include:

- The Credit Check might have been performed late due to an operational mistake.
- Finalize Documentation after rejection may indicate a data entry error, misclassification of the decision, or misunderstanding of the process.
- The ōFinalize Documentationö event might have been incorrectly recorded and could actually mean ōarchive rejection documentationö or ōfinalize internal case notes.ö However, under the given process definition, this activity is not allowed after rejection.

Even with these possible explanations, under the stated normative rules, Case 2 is non-compliant.

---

### Case 3

Case 3 appears to follow the required process but with delays.

Possible interpretations include:

- The Credit Check delay could be due to weekend effects, staff shortages, missing applicant information, or external bureau delays.
- The delayed applicant notification could be due to workload, manual review, or waiting for confirmation before communicating the approval.
- The absence of a Follow-up Call may reflect that the organization considered the written or formal notification sufficient.

These explanations may make the behavior understandable, but they do not change the fact that Case 3 failed some recommended desiderata. Importantly, these are not severe violations because the strict sequence and decision logic were preserved.
