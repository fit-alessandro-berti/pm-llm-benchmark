### 1. Baseline: Normative Hire-to-Retire Process
A standard Hire-to-Retire workflow follows a strict causal chain:
`Post Job Ad  Screen Candidates  Conduct Interviews  Make Hiring Decision  Onboard Employee  Add to Payroll  Close Case`

Key logical dependencies:
- **Screening** must precede **Interviews** (to filter unqualified applicants).
- **Interviews** must precede the **Hiring Decision** (decisions require interview feedback).
- **Onboarding** and **Payroll** are mandatory post-decision steps.
- The process should be acyclic and fully connected from start to end.

---

### 2. Analysis of POWL Model 1

**Structure & Flow:**
- `Post  Screen`
- After `Screen`, the flow splits into two independent branches: `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview`
- `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`
- `Interview` has **no outgoing edges**.

**Identified Anomalies:**
| Anomaly | Description | Severity |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Broken InterviewDecision Dependency** | `Decide` is enabled immediately after `Screen`, with no causal link from `Interview`. The hiring decision can be made before, during, or completely independently of interviews. |  Critical |
| **Dangling Activity (`Interview`)** | `Interview` has no successor. In a partial order, it could execute after `Close` or never influence downstream steps. This breaks process connectivity and traceability. |  Critical |
| **Parallel/Unordered Decision & Interview** | The partial order allows `Decide` and `Interview` to run concurrently, which contradicts the fundamental business rule that interviews inform decisions. |  Critical |

**Impact on Integrity:** Model 1 fundamentally violates the core logic of hiring. Allowing a hiring decision without mandatory interview completion renders the process non-compliant, legally risky, and operationally unsound.

   - Model 2's anomalies stem from control-flow operators (`LOOP`, `XOR`) that introduce flexibility. While optional payroll and looped onboarding deviate from best practice, they model real-world exceptions or system behaviors without breaking process correctness.
