Got it — that’s a complex, multi-stakeholder process with lots of handoffs. To really understand how it works end-to-end and where the risks or variations sit, here are targeted, open-ended questions I’d ask the interviewee:

### **Process initiation & documentation**
1. **Triggers & SLAs**: Besides a landlord reaching out, are there other ways a property enters the pipeline — referrals, acquisitions, inbound sales? Is there a target turnaround time from first contact to “activated” in the portal?
2. **Document verification**: Who defines what counts as “compliant” documentation? How often do local housing regulations change, and how does the team get notified? What happens if a landlord can’t provide a specific document — is there a waiver or escalation path?
3. **Data standards**: When recording property details in the central database, how do you handle subjective fields like “amenity quality” or “rental price range”? Is there a standard taxonomy or do coordinators use judgment?

### **Roles, responsibilities & decision criteria**
4. **Property manager assignment**: You mentioned workload, geography, experience, and track record. How are those factors weighted when they conflict — e.g. the best-fit manager by property type is at capacity? Who makes the final call if there’s disagreement?
5. **Intro meeting outcomes**: What specifically needs to be agreed on in the intro call before moving forward? If the landlord and manager can’t align on policies like pets or maintenance, what’s the process — does onboarding pause, escalate, or stop?
6. **Vendor coordination**: How are photographers and certified inspectors selected and scheduled? What’s the contingency if an inspector isn’t available for 3+ weeks, or if photos are poor quality?

### **Sequencing, timing & dependencies**
7. **Critical path**: Which steps must happen sequentially vs. which can run in parallel? For example, can marketing start drafting listings before the inspection passes, using “pending inspection” language?
8. **Bottlenecks**: Where do properties typically get stuck longest in this process? Is it document collection, repair work, or something else? How is that tracked?
9. **Activation definition**: What exactly changes when a property is “activated” in the rental portal? Are there partial activation states, like “viewable but not appliable”?

### **Tenant screening & lease negotiation**
10. **Application volume**: When multiple tenants apply, what criteria break ties beyond lease length and discounts? How do you balance speed to occupancy vs. tenant quality, and who owns that judgment call?
11. **Custom terms**: You mentioned custom lease clauses. How common are they, and what’s the review process? Is there a limit to what the property manager can approve without legal or regional office sign-off?
12. **Deposit & move-in**: Who sets the move-in date — tenant, landlord, or property manager? What happens if the property isn’t actually ready by that date due to a late repair?

### **Quality checks, compliance & audits**
13. **Audit timing**: Do compliance, financial, and IT checks happen before or after the property goes live to tenants? What happens if an audit fails post-activation?
14. **Fair housing**: Beyond lease review, what other checkpoints ensure fair housing compliance during marketing and screening? Who’s accountable if a violation occurs?
15. **Pricing strategy**: How much discretion does the digital marketing team or property manager have to deviate from the internal pricing model? What triggers a financial analyst review?

### **Exceptions, escalations & communication**
16. **Special cases**: You mentioned special insurance or unique landlord requirements. Can you walk me through an example of a “non-standard” onboarding and how the process flexed? At what point do you decide a property is too complex to onboard?
17. **Issue resolution**: If an inspector finds major issues and the landlord refuses to fix them, what are the options? Is there a minimum habitability bar that would halt onboarding entirely?
18. **Communication tracking**: With email, CRMs, and project tools in play, how do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks when multiple teams are involved? Who owns the “source of truth” for a property’s status?
19. **Handoffs**: What does a successful handoff look like between the property coordinator → property manager → leasing agent → compliance? Are there formal sign-offs or checklists?

### **Continuous improvement & metrics**
20. **Success definition**: How do you measure whether an onboarding went well — time to first tenant, cost, landlord satisfaction, number of revisions? 
21. **Feedback loops**: When exceptions or delays happen, how is that captured so the process improves? Can you share a recent change you made based on a breakdown?
