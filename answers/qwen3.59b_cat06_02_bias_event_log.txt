### 2. Implicit Bias: Geographic and Community Exclusion

If the system relies on thresholds for approval, denying the adjustment to non-affiliated groups effectively raises the bar for them.
*   **Community Group as a Proxy:** The `Highland Civic Darts Club` likely acts as a proxy for socioeconomic status or geographic location. This biases the system against individuals who:
    *   Live outside specific "high-value" geographic zones.
    *   Do not belong to exclusive social clubs (which may require membership fees, time commitment, or existing connections).

### 3. Impact on Equity and Decision Making
The presence of these attributes influences fairness in the following ways:

*   **Creditworthiness is Not Solely Financial:** The system treats creditworthiness as a composite of financial data *plus* social integration. This penalizes individuals who may have good financial standing but lack the specific social connections or geographic location required to access the "Community" bonus.
*   **The "Walled Garden" Effect:** The `Highland Civic Darts Club` membership creates an entry barrier. Only those with the resources and social capital to join this club can access the lower threshold for approval (effectively lowering their risk profile in the eyes of the algorithm).
*   **Rejection Risk for Marginalized Groups:** Marginalized groups or individuals who are mobile (non-local) often lack stable community affiliations. The data suggests that without a specific "Community" tag, these applicants must achieve significantly higher raw scores (like C005 at 740) to secure the same outcome as those with a community bonus.

### Conclusion
The process is not neutral. It favors **Local Residents** and members of **Specific Community Groups**. The bias manifests through a direct score adjustment (+10) for specific affiliations, effectively creating two tiers of applicants:
1.  **Tier A:** Local/Club Members (Lower raw scores accepted due to adjustment).
2.  **Tier B:** Non-Local/Non-Members (Higher raw scores required; risk of rejection if below a strict threshold).

**Recommendation:** To ensure fairness, the `ScoreAdjustment` logic should be removed or made strictly transparent and non-discriminatory. The system should evaluate creditworthiness based on standardized financial metrics rather than social affiliation or geographic residency status.
