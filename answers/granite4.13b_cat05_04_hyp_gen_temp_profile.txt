**Anomalies Identified in the Temporal Profile Model**

1. **Receive  Approve (R  P)**  
   - *Average time:* ~25hours with a very low standard deviation (~1hour).  
   - *Potential issue:* The unusually tight window suggests that approvals are either being forced under artificial timelines or there is a systematic bottleneck causing the process to stall until it finally reaches approval.

2. **Approve  Notify (P  N)**  
   - *Average time:* ~7days with a high standard deviation (~2days).  
   - *Potential issue:* The large variance indicates that some claims are delayed for days while others may be closed immediately, possibly due to resource bottlenecks or manual review cycles.

3. **Assign  Close (A  C)**  
   - *Average time:* ~2hours after assignment without passing through Evaluate/Approve steps.  
   - *Potential issue:* This suggests that some claims are being prematurely closed, which could be an oversight or a shortcut taken when the claim is already deemed settled.

4. **Evaluate  Notify (E  N)**  
   - *Average time:* Only ~5minutes between evaluation and notification.  
   - *Potential issue:* Such a short interval may imply that evaluations are not followed by substantive notifications, potentially skipping necessary documentation or verification steps.

---

**Hypotheses on Why These Anomalies Might Exist**

1. **R  P (Receive to Approve)**
   - *Cause:* Inefficient queue management where claims are stuck waiting for approval due to a backlog of prior steps that have not been completed efficiently, leading to artificial prolongation.
   
2. **P  N (Approve to Notify)**
   - *Cause:* Resource constraints in the notification department or manual processing delays causing some customers to wait days while others receive immediate notifications, possibly reflecting uneven workload distribution among adjusters.

3. **A  C (Assign to Close)**
   - *Cause:* Inconsistent application of policy where certain claims are deemed settled without formal evaluation/approval steps due to subjective judgment by assigners rather than adherence to a standardized process flow.
   
4. **E  N (Evaluate to Notify)**
   - *Cause:* Rapid automation or lack of thorough review checks in the notification system that result in immediate notifications regardless of claim complexity, potentially skipping required followup actions.

**Verification Approaches Using SQL Queries on `claim_events` Table**

1. **Identify Claims with Atypical R  P Delays**
   ```sql
   SELECT claim_id,
          AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (TIMESTAMPDIFF('DAY', event_timestamp, 
                                               LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp))))) AS avg_r_to_p_days,
          STDDEV(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (TIMESTAMPDIFF('DAY', event_timestamp, 
                                               LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp))))) AS stddev_r_to_p_days
   FROM claim_events
   WHERE activity IN ('R','P')
     AND claim_id IN (
         SELECT claim_id
         FROM claim_events
         GROUP BY claim_id
         HAVING AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (TIMESTAMPDIFF('DAY', event_timestamp, 
                                                  LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp))))) > 25
     )
   GROUP BY claim_id;
   ```

2. **Correlate P  N Delays with Adjuster or Region**
   ```sql
   SELECT adjuster_id,
          region,
          COUNT(*) AS num_long_delays,
          AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (TIMESTAMPDIFF('DAY', event_timestamp, 
                                                LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp))))) AS avg_long_delay_days
   FROM claim_events
   WHERE activity IN ('P','N')
     AND TIMESTAMPDIFF('DAY', event_timestamp, LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp)) > 7
   GROUP BY adjuster_id, region;
   ```

3. **Find Claims with Unusually Quick E  N Times**
   ```sql
   SELECT claim_id,
          COUNT(*) AS num_quick_notify_events
   FROM claim_events
   WHERE activity IN ('E','N')
     AND TIMESTAMPDIFF('MINUTE', event_timestamp, LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp)) <= 5
   GROUP BY claim_id;
   ```

4. **Check for Premature Closure After Assignment Without Evaluate/Approve**
   ```sql
   SELECT claim_id,
          COUNT(*) AS premature_closure_count
   FROM claim_events
   WHERE activity = 'C'
     AND LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp) IS NOT NULL
       AND LEAD(event_timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY event_timestamp) < 
         DATEADD('DAY', 1, event_timestamp) -- Assuming closure should not occur within a day of assignment
   GROUP BY claim_id;
   ```

These queries will help pinpoint specific claims and resource/region patterns that align with the identified anomalies, enabling deeper investigation into process inefficiencies or policy deviations.
