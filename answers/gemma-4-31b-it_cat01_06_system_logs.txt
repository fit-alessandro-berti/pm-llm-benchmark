### Explanation of Transformation Logic

To transform the raw system logs into a format suitable for process mining, I applied the following logic:

#### 1. Case Identification (The "Case ID")
I used **Object-Based Case Identification**. In system logs, a "case" is rarely a single continuous block of time because users multitask. Therefore, I grouped events by the **Window Title**. 
*   Any action performed on `Document1.docx` belongs to `CASE_DOC1`, regardless of how many other applications the user switched to in between.
*   This allows a process miner to see the "lifecycle" of a specific document or task, revealing that the user started `Document1`, paused to handle an email and budget updates, and then returned to finalize the document.

#### 2. Activity Naming (Abstraction)
I translated low-level system triggers into high-level business activities to make the log analyst-friendly:
*   **Aggregation:** Multiple `TYPING` events within the same window were aggregated into a single activity (e.g., "Drafting Document") to reduce noise and avoid "spaghetti" process maps.
*   **Contextual Translation:** 
    *   `CLICK` + `SCROLL` in a PDF $\rightarrow$ **"Reviewing PDF"**.
    *   `CLICK` (Reply) + `TYPING` (in Chrome) $\rightarrow$ **"Replying to Email"**.
    *   `SAVE` $\rightarrow$ **"Saving [Object]"**.
    *   `CLOSE` $\rightarrow$ **"Closing [Object]"**.
*   **Noise Reduction:** `SWITCH` events were omitted as activities because they represent the transition *between* cases rather than a value-adding step *within* a case.

#### 3. Narrative Summary
The resulting log tells the story of a fragmented work session. The user began by opening a Quarterly Report, but immediately pivoted to draft a new document (`Document1`). While working on that draft, they were interrupted by a need to respond to an email and review a PDF report. This prompted a need to update figures in an Excel budget file. Once the budget was updated, the user returned to `Document1` to reference those figures, saved and closed it, and finally returned to the original `Quarterly_Report` to complete the executive summary.
