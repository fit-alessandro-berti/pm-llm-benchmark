### Rationale for Labels

#### 1. Idle / Standby (Events 1–3, 13–15)
*   **Observations:** Temperature is ambient (~20°C), pressure is at baseline, vibration is zero, material flow is zero, and energy consumption is minimal (~0.4–0.5 kWh).
*   **Rationale:** The machine is powered on but not performing any mechanical work. The consistent baseline readings indicate a state of readiness between process cycles.

#### 2. Cutting Metal (Events 4–6)
*   **Observations:** There is a rapid spike in temperature (50°C to 58°C), vibration (30–38 Hz), and energy consumption (2.0–3.0 kWh). The tool position is actively changing (moving from 5mm to 10mm), and material flow is steady at 5 units/s.
*   **Rationale:** The high vibration and temperature are hallmarks of a friction-heavy task like cutting or machining. The movement of the tool position while material is flowing confirms active material removal.

#### 3. Assembling Parts (Events 7–8)
*   **Observations:** Temperature drops and stabilizes at 40°C. Pressure increases to 2 bar (higher than the cutting phase), while vibration significantly decreases (10 Hz). Material flow drops to a lower, constant rate of 2 units/s.
*   **Rationale:** The transition to lower vibration and higher pressure suggests the machine has shifted from aggressive cutting to a pressing or alignment operation, typical of assembling components. The tool position holds steady at 15mm, indicating it is holding the parts in place.

#### 4. Welding (Event 9)
*   **Observations:** This is a single, high-intensity event. Temperature jumps to 80°C, energy usage peaks at 5.0 kWh, and vibration spikes to 50 Hz. Material flow is 0.
*   **Rationale:** Welding is a high-energy process that generates significant heat and localized mechanical stress. The fact that energy usage is highest here despite the tool being stationary (position 15mm) and material flow being zero strongly points toward an electrical welding process.

#### 5. Quality Inspection (Events 10–12)
*   **Observations:** Conditions return toward ambient (Temperature 25–30°C, Vibration 1–5 Hz). Energy consumption drops back to 1.0–1.6 kWh, and there is a slight, slow adjustment of the tool position (to 20mm).
*   **Rationale:** The low-energy, low-stress environment suggests the machine is no longer exerting force. The slight movement of the tool position at this stage suggests a final "scan" or measurement phase to verify the dimensions of the completed assembly.
