To mitigate potential bias in the loan application process, I have introduced several new constraints. These ensure that sensitive attribute disclosures (e.g., `IdentifyMinorityStatus`) are always followed by procedural fairness checks (`BiasMitigationCheck`) before a final decision is reached, and that `ManualReview` is mandatory whenever minority status is involved.

### Rationale for Constraints

1.  **`responded_existence` / `coexistence` (`IdentifyMinorityStatus` & `ManualReview`):** These ensure that if the system identifies a minority applicant, a `ManualReview` must occur at some point in the trace. This forces human oversight, preventing automated algorithms from making unchecked decisions based on sensitive attributes.
2.  **`response` (`IdentifyMinorityStatus` -> `BiasMitigationCheck`):** This mandates that whenever minority status is identified, the process must trigger a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This ensures the process acknowledges the sensitive attribute and takes an explicit step to verify fairness.
3.  **`precedence` (`BiasMitigationCheck` -> `FinalDecision`):** This enforces a structural requirement: a `FinalDecision` cannot be reached unless a `BiasMitigationCheck` has already occurred. This prevents "rushing" to a decision before fairness protocols are followed.
4.  **`nonsuccession` (`IdentifyMinorityStatus` -> `FinalDecision`):** This explicitly prohibits a `FinalDecision` from occurring immediately after identifying minority status. This gap forces the inclusion of the mitigating steps mentioned above, ensuring no decision is made based solely on the raw input of the sensitive attribute.
