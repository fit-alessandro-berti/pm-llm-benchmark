## 5.  Recommendations ¢ how to make the POWL model compliant

| Issue | Suggested redesign | Why it helps |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Optional credit check** | Replace `XOR(C, skip)` with a **mandatory** sequence: `R  C  ģ`. If a business rule allows a waiver, model it as a **guarded optional activity** with an explicit ōWaive_Credit_Checkö transition that is **logged**. | Guarantees that every order is assessed for credit risk; provides an auditfriendly record of any waivers. |
| **Unbounded approvalinvoice loop** | Change `LOOP(A, I)` to a **single iteration** (`A  I`). If rework is truly needed, introduce a **bounded loop** with a guard (e.g., `max_iterations=2`) or a **reapproval** decision point with a clear exit condition (ōReapprove only if invoice was rejectedö). | Prevents duplicate invoices, limits rework to a controlled number of attempts, and makes the process deterministic. |
| **Silent skip** | Convert the silent transition into a **visible activity** such as `Credit_Check_Waived` (or `Skip_Credit_Check`). If you still need a silent step for technical reasons, add a **marker** in the log (e.g., a custom attribute `credit_check_skipped=true`). | Guarantees that every deviation is traceable; simplifies compliance reporting and forensic analysis. |
| **Missing activities** | Enrich the model with the steps that your organisation actually performs (e.g., `Pick_Pack`, `Ship_Goods`, `Handle_Returns`). Use **ANDsplits** for parallel branches when appropriate. | Reflects the true endtoend process, enabling accurate performance analysis and compliance checks. |
| **No explicit loop guard** | If a loop is truly required, add a **condition** (often a ōwhilenotpaidö or ōwhilenotapprovedö clause). In POWL this can be expressed with a **transition guard** or a **decision node** that leads out of the loop. | Makes the loop semantics explicit, reduces the risk of infinite waiting, and eases conformance verification. |
