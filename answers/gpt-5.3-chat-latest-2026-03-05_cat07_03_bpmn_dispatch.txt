It’s a **BPMN-style process diagram** showing the workflow for **dispatching goods in a computer retail shop**, divided into swimlanes for roles.

**Lanes / participants**
- Logistics  
- Secretary  
- Warehouse  

**Overall flow**
1. **Start: “Ship goods.”**
2. The process splits:
   - **Secretary lane:** *Clarify shipment method.*
   - **Warehouse lane:** *Package goods.*

**Secretary decisions and logistics coordination**
- After clarifying, a decision **“Special handling?”**
  - **Yes:**  
    - *Get 3 offers from logistic companies.*  
    - *Select logistic company and place order.*
  - **No:**  
    - *Check if insurance is required* (using **customer information** and **order details**).
    - If needed  *Insure parcel.*
    - Then *Write package label.*

- The logistics-related paths merge and continue toward shipment preparation.

**Warehouse activities**
- *Package goods.*
- If packaging exceeds **48 hours**, a step triggers **“Notify customer about delay.”**
- Both paths merge afterward.

**Finalization**
- The logistics/secretary path and warehouse path synchronize.
- *Fill out shipment paperwork.*
- *Move package to pickup area.*
- **End event: “Shipment prepared.”**

**Key elements depicted**
- Gateways (diamonds) for decisions and merges.
- Parallel/synchronization points.
- Data objects like **order details** and **customer information**.
- A **timer event (48 hours)** triggering customer notification.
