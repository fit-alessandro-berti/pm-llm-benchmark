# BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Workflow

## 1. Process Overview
*   **Process Name:** Standard Invoice Approval & Payment Process
*   **Scope:** From invoice receipt to payment scheduling.
*   **Primary Goal:** Validate invoice against Purchase Order (PO) and receipt of goods, obtain managerial approval, and schedule payment.
*   **System:** Accounting System (used for logging, matching, and approval notifications).

## 2. Participants (Lanes)
1.  **Accounts Payable (AP):** Includes Clerk (Mary) and Supervisor (Rita).
2.  **Purchasing Department:** Includes Purchasing Officer (Dan).
3.  **Department Management:** Includes Department Manager (Carlos).
4.  **Supplier Relations:** Includes Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen).
5.  **External:** Supplier.

## 3. Process Flow Description

### Phase 1: Intake & Validation
*   **Start Event:** Invoice Received (via Email).
*   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Verify PO Number Presence.
    *   *Input:* Invoice Document.
    *   *Output:* PO Validation Status.
*   **Gateway (Exclusive):** Is Valid PO Number Present?
    *   **Path A (No PO):**
        *   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Request Details from Supplier.
        *   **Gateway (Exclusive):** Is Retrospective PO Required? *(Based on Interview 4)*
            *   *Yes:* **Task:** [AP Supervisor] Coordinate Retrospective PO with Dept Head.
            *   *No:* **End Event:** Invoice Rejected / Returned to Supplier.
    *   **Path B (PO Present):**
        *   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Log Invoice into Accounting System.
        *   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Confirmation.

### Phase 2: Purchasing Verification
*   **Task:** [Purchasing Officer] Validate Receipt & Invoice Details.
    *   *Action:* Compare Invoice vs. PO (Quantities, Prices, Delivery Charges).
    *   *Input:* Invoice, PO Record.
*   **Gateway (Exclusive):** Do Details Match?
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Found):**
        *   **Task:** [Purchasing Officer] Contact Supplier OR Request AP Hold.
        *   **Task:** [Supplier Relationship Manager] *Optional/Consultative:* Verify Contract Terms/Pricing.
        *   **Flow:** Loop back to **Task: Verify PO Number Presence** (if PO missing) OR **Task: Validate Receipt & Invoice Details** (once corrected invoice received).
    *   **Path B (Details Match):**
        *   **Task:** [Purchasing Officer] Send Confirmation Email to AP.

### Phase 3: Matching & Approval
*   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Match Invoice Line Items against PO (3-Way Match).
    *   *Trigger:* Receipt of Confirmation from Purchasing.
*   **Gateway (Exclusive):** Is Match Successful?
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy/Unresolved):**
        *   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Escalate to AP Supervisor.
        *   **Task:** [AP Supervisor] Investigate & Resolve (Contact Suppliers/Coordinate).
        *   **Flow:** If Resolved -> Loop back to **Task: Match Invoice Line Items**.
        *   **Flow:** If Unresolved -> **End Event:** Invoice Rejected.
    *   **Path B (Match Successful):**
        *   **Task:** [System] Trigger Manager Approval Notification.
        *   **Task:** [Dept Manager] Review Invoice Details (Budget, Pricing, Notes).
*   **Gateway (Exclusive):** Manager Decision?
    *   **Path A (Reject/Query):**
        *   **Task:** [Dept Manager] Request Investigation/Hold.
        *   **Flow:** Return to **Task: [AP Clerk] Match Invoice Line Items** or **Task: [AP Supervisor] Investigate**.
    *   **Path B (Approve):**
        *   **Task:** [Dept Manager] Approve in System.

### Phase 4: Payment
*   **Task:** [AP Clerk] Schedule for Payment Cycle.
    *   *Trigger:* Manager Approval Confirmation.
*   **End Event:** Payment Scheduled.

## 4. Data Objects & Artifacts
*   **Invoice:** Digital or physical document received from Supplier.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference number and document stored in Accounting System.
*   **Confirmation Email:** Sent from Purchasing to AP.
*   **System Notification:** Automated email to Dept Manager.
*   **Retrospective PO:** Document created by AP Supervisor/Dept Head for informal services (Exception).

## 5. Exceptions & Variations (Special Handling)
*   **Missing PO:**
    *   *Standard:* Return to Supplier (Interview 1).
    *   *Exception:* If service was contracted informally, AP Supervisor creates Retrospective PO (Interview 4).
*   **Pricing/Quantity Discrepancy:**
    *   *Standard:* Purchasing contacts Supplier (Interview 2).
    *   *Exception:* If unresolved, Supplier Relationship Manager checks contract terms (Interview 5).
*   **Budget/Suspicious Variance:**
    *   *Action:* Manager rejects or requests investigation (Interview 3).
*   **Stuck Invoices:**
    *   *Action:* AP Supervisor intervenes to ensure invoices do not remain stuck indefinitely (Interview 4).

## 6. Notes on Ambiguities & Conflicting Accounts
*   **Timing of Matching:** Mary (AP Clerk) states she matches the invoice *after* receiving confirmation from Purchasing. Dan (Purchasing) states he checks details *when* the invoice hits his inbox.
    *   *Resolution:* The model sequences Purchasing validation *before* AP final matching, as Mary explicitly states, "Once I get confirmation [from Purchasing], I match..." This implies a sequential handoff rather than parallel processing.
*   **PO Validation Responsibility:** Mary checks for a PO number first. Dan states, "We never approve anything without a confirmed PO reference."
    *   *Resolution:* The model places PO validation at the very start (AP Lane), with Purchasing validating the *details* of that PO later. If Purchasing finds the PO invalid later, it loops back.
*   **Supplier Interaction:** Karen (Supplier Relationship Manager) is involved indirectly.
    *   *Resolution:* Modeled as an optional/consultative task within the Discrepancy path, rather than a mandatory step in the main flow.
*   **Manager Approval Trigger:** Mary says the system sends an automatic email. Carlos says he gets a notification after Dan confirms.
    *   *Resolution:* The model reflects that the trigger is the combination of "PO Match" + "Purchasing Confirmation."
