## Key Process Logic Summary
### Path Differences: Standard vs. Custom
Standard paths are optimized for speed and efficiency for pre-defined offerings, with parallel checks for credit and inventory, and a simple delivery date calculation. Custom paths prioritize risk mitigation for non-standard work, with an initial feasibility screening to avoid wasting resources on unachievable requests, and a tailored quotation step instead of standard delivery calculation.
### Approval & Rework Triggers
- Approvals are only triggered for requests that meet pre-defined risk, value, or complexity thresholds, to avoid unnecessary bureaucracy for low-risk routine requests.
- Rework loops are only triggered when a manager denies a request, and are path-specific to avoid redundant work: standard requests revise fulfillment terms, while custom requests revise their tailored quotation. The loop exits only when the revised request meets approval requirements, or is ultimately rejected.
