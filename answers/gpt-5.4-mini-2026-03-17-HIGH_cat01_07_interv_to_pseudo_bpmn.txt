Below is a consolidated BPMN-like textual model of the invoice approval process, combining the interview accounts and marking exception paths where needed.

---

## Process Name
**Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling**

---

## Participants / Lanes
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)**
- **Purchasing Officer (Dan)**
- **Department Manager (Carlos)**
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)**
- **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)**
- **Supplier** (external participant)
- **Accounting / ERP System**

---

## Key Data Objects / Artifacts
- **Invoice**
- **Purchase Order (PO)**
- **Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation**
- **Accounting System Record**
- **Confirmation Email**
- **Corrected Invoice**
- **Contract**
- **Retrospective PO** (exception case)
- **Manager Approval Notification**
- **Payment Run / Payment Schedule**

---

## Main Flow (Standard Path)

1. **Start Event:** Invoice received by Accounts Payable, usually by email.

2. **Task (Mary / AP Clerk):** Check whether the invoice has a valid **PO number**.

3. **XOR Gateway: PO number present and valid?**
   - **No**
     - **Task (Mary):** Return invoice to supplier requesting PO number / more details.
     - **Exception note:** If the invoice is for a legitimately contracted service but no PO exists, **Rita** may intervene to create a **retrospective PO** or decide to reject the invoice.
     - **End / Wait state:** Process pauses until corrected information or a decision is received.
   - **Yes**
     - Continue.

4. **Task (Mary):** Log the invoice into the **accounting system**.

5. **Task (Mary):** Forward the invoice to **Purchasing** for confirmation that the goods/services were received and that the invoice aligns with the PO.

6. **Task (Dan / Purchasing Officer):** Open the PO and compare the invoice against the order:
   - quantities
   - prices
   - delivery charges
   - any special notes / terms
   - receipt status

7. **XOR Gateway: Invoice/PO details consistent?**
   - **No**
     - **Task (Dan):** Contact the supplier directly **or** ask AP to hold the invoice until a corrected version is received.
     - **Optional Task (Karen):** If supplier terms/pricing need clarification, contact the supplier and check the contract.
     - **Optional Task (Rita):** If the issue cannot be resolved easily, coordinate between AP, Purchasing, and supplier.
     - **Loop:** Once corrected information or a revised invoice arrives, return to Purchasing review.
   - **Yes**
     - **Task (Dan):** Send confirmation email back to AP.

8. **Task (Mary):** Match invoice line items against the PO in the system.

9. **XOR Gateway: Any discrepancy after matching?**
   - **No**
     - Continue.
   - **Yes**
     - **Task (Mary):** Email Purchasing to clarify the discrepancy.
     - **If unresolved:** Escalate to **Rita (AP Supervisor)**.
     - **Task (Rita):** Coordinate with Purchasing, supplier, and/or department heads to resolve the issue.
     - **Possible outcomes:**
       - supplier sends corrected invoice
       - missing items/pricing are clarified
       - retrospective PO is created for informal services
       - invoice is rejected
     - **Loop back:** After resolution, return to the matching step and/or Purchasing confirmation step as appropriate.

10. **Intermediate Event / Automatic System Action:** Once receipt confirmation and matching are complete, the **system sends an approval notification** to the department manager.

11. **Task (Carlos / Department Manager):** Review the invoice, PO, Purchasing confirmation, and any notes.

12. **XOR Gateway: Manager approves?**
   - **Yes**
     - **Task (Carlos):** Approve the invoice in the system.
     - **Task (Mary / AP):** Schedule invoice for the next **payment run / payment cycle**.
     - **End Event:** Invoice is queued for payment.
   - **No / Hold / Reject**
     - **Task (Carlos):** Reject the invoice or ask Mary to hold it and investigate further.
     - **Loop:** Send back to Mary/Rita for clarification or correction, then re-enter the process at the appropriate review step.

---

## Exception / Variation Paths

### 1) Missing PO
- Standard response is to **return the invoice to the supplier** for PO details or a revised invoice.
- If the invoice is a special case (e.g., informally contracted services), **Rita** may:
  - create a **retrospective PO**
  - coordinate approval of the exception
  - or **reject** the invoice

### 2) Pricing / Quantity / Delivery Discrepancy
- If billed amounts do not match the PO:
  - **Dan** may contact the supplier directly
  - **Mary** may hold the invoice
  - **Karen** may verify contract pricing and contact the supplier
  - **Rita** may coordinate resolution across departments
- After correction, the process returns to the normal flow.

### 3) Unresolved Invoice Issues
- If a discrepancy cannot be resolved quickly:
  - **Rita** escalates and coordinates between AP, Purchasing, supplier, and sometimes department heads.
- Purpose: prevent invoices from getting stuck indefinitely.

### 4) Manager Concern / Budget Variance
- **Carlos** may reject or hold the invoice if:
  - total amount is significantly above budget
  - unexpected items appear
  - something looks suspicious
- The invoice returns to AP for investigation, then re-enters the approval path after correction.

## Consolidated Interpretation of the Flow
The most consistent reading of the interviews is:

**Invoice received  PO check  logging in system  Purchasing validation/receipt confirmation  AP line-item matching  Manager approval  scheduling for payment.**

Exceptional cases branch off mainly at:
- **missing PO**
- **invoice/PO mismatch**
- **manager rejection**
- **non-PO service invoices**
