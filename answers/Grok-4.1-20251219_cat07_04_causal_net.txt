### Legend (Bottom Part)
The gray box explains the special transition types used in this Petri net, which extend the classical Petri net with routing constructs commonly seen in workflow nets or YAWL (Yet Another Workflow Language):

1. **XOR-split**  
   Exclusive choice: Only one outgoing path is taken (e.g., at "decide", either pay or reject, but not both).

2. **AND-split**  
   Parallel split: All outgoing paths are taken simultaneously (e.g., after registration, multiple examinations/checks can happen in parallel).

3. **OR-split**  
   Inclusive choice: One or more outgoing paths can be taken (any non-empty subset).

4. **XOR-join**  
   Exclusive join: Waits for exactly one incoming token.

5. **AND-join**  
   Synchronization: Waits for tokens from all incoming paths before proceeding.

6. **OR-join**  
   Inclusive join: Waits for all active incoming paths (those that were enabled by the corresponding split) to complete. This is more complex and used to handle partial synchronization after OR-splits.

### Overall Interpretation
This is a workflow net modeling a request-handling process where:
- After registration, several activities (thorough exam, casual exam, ticket check, possibly reinitiation) can occur in parallel or selectively (likely using AND-split and/or OR-split semantics).
- All necessary activities must complete and synchronize before a decision is made.
- The decision is exclusive (XOR): either compensate or reject.
- The process ends in either case.

The use of advanced split/join types (especially OR-join) suggests the model can handle flexible routing where not all parallel branches are always executed, yet still properly synchronize before the decision step.
