### 1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact

**Identification Using Event Log Data:**

To identify instance-spanning constraints using the event log, we will employ Process Mining techniques such as mining PAST (Process Analysis Technology) which can reveal resource contention and waiting times.

- **Shared Cold-Packing Stations:** We’ll analyze timestamps of packing activities at Station S7 to detect overlapping instances for non-cold packed orders. Metrics include:
  - *Average wait time per order* in cold-packing stations.
  - *Resource utilization rate* (percentage of Station S7 used by express or hazardous materials orders versus standard orders).

- **Shipping Batches:** We’ll track the ‘Shipping Label Generation’ activity across orders destined for similar regions to identify batch formation delays. Metrics include:
  - *Time taken for label generation* when waiting for a batch completion.
  - *Batch size variability* and its correlation with regional shipping routes.

- **Priority Handling (Express Orders):** By monitoring timestamps of packing and quality check activities, we can determine how express orders affect standard order processing. Metrics include:
  - *Queue time increase* for non-express orders due to resource contention.
  - *Reduction in average completion time* for express orders when they require cold-packing or other limited resources.

- **Hazardous Material Limits:** We’ll track hazardous material-related activities, comparing timestamps of packing and quality check. Metrics include:
  - *Throughput reduction* caused by simultaneous processing limits (10 at a time).
  - *Regulatory compliance rate* to ensure adherence to safety regulations.

**Differentiation Between Within-Instance and Between-Instance Factors:**

To differentiate waiting times due to within-instance factors versus between-instance factors, we’ll use:
- **Queue Time Analysis:** Orders with long internal activity durations (e.g., packing time) are classified as within-instance. Conversely, orders waiting for resource contention from other instances are classified as between-instance.
- **Resource Utilization Patterns:** Using mining techniques like Process Mining Graphs to visualize dependencies and see if the bottleneck is due to shared resources occupied by different order instances.

### 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

**Potential Interactions:**

- **Express Orders & Cold-Packing:** When an express order requiring cold-packing arrives, it can block Station S7, causing delays for standard orders that also need packing.
  
- **Batching & Hazardous Material Limits:** If multiple hazardous materials are destined for the same region, they may compete for the same batch, reducing throughput and increasing wait times due to regulatory limits.

**Importance of Understanding Interactions:**

Recognizing these interactions helps in developing a holistic optimization approach:
- Address bottleneck areas (e.g., Station S7) without ignoring interdependencies.
- Prevent cascading delays where one constraint exacerbates another (e.g., hazardous material constraints delaying batch formation).

### 3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Resource Allocation for Cold-Packing Stations**

*Primary Addressed Constraint:* Shared Cold-Packing Stations.

- **Changes Proposed:** Implement dynamic scheduling that prioritizes packing orders based on urgency and resource availability.
- **Data/Analysis Leveraged:** Use predictive analytics to forecast demand spikes (e.g., seasonal peaks) in cold-packing stations using historical data trends.
- **Expected Outcomes:** Reduce waiting times for standard orders, prevent bottlenecking by express orders.

**Strategy 2: Revised Batching Logic**

*Primary Addressed Constraint:* Shipping Batches.

- **Changes Proposed:** Introduce a dynamic batch formation algorithm that considers real-time order destinations and regional shipping capacity to form optimal batches.
- **Data/Analysis Leveraged:** Utilize machine learning models trained on historical shipment data to predict the most efficient batching patterns, minimizing wait times for label generation.
- **Expected Outcomes:** Faster shipping label generation as batches form in a more timely manner.

**Strategy 3: Enhanced Scheduling Rules Considering Priorities and Regulations**

*Primary Addressed Constraint:* Priority Handling & Hazardous Material Limits.

- **Changes Proposed:** Develop an adaptive scheduling system that dynamically adjusts for express orders, ensuring they are prioritized without exceeding hazardous material limits.
- **Data/Analysis Leveraged:** Use constraint-aware simulation models to predict the impact of real-time scheduling on throughput and regulatory compliance.
- **Expected Outcomes:** Improved delivery times for express orders without violating hazardous material regulations.

### 4. Simulation and Validation

**Using Simulation Techniques:**

Before implementing changes, we’ll use Discrete Event Simulations (DES) informed by process mining to evaluate the effectiveness of proposed strategies:

- **Simulation Focus Areas:**
  - *Resource Contention Models:* To simulate how dynamic allocation affects cold-packing station availability.
  - *Batch Formation Models:* To test improved batching logic on average wait times for label generation.
  - *Priority & Hazardous Interaction Models:* To ensure regulatory compliance while optimizing express order processing.

**Validation Metrics:**

- KPIs such as overall process time, completion rate per order type (standard vs. express), and adherence to hazardous material limits will be tracked.
- Compare simulated outcomes with baseline event log data to validate improvements before full-scale implementation.

### 5. Monitoring Post-Implementation

**Key Metrics and Dashboards:**

- **Metrics:**
  - Average wait times at cold-packing stations.
  - Batch formation turnaround times for each region.
  - Compliance rate of hazardous material limits while maintaining throughput.
  
- **Dashboards:**
  - Real-time process mining dashboards displaying queue lengths, resource utilization, and order classification (standard vs. express).
  - Heatmaps visualizing bottlenecks in shared resources over time to quickly identify emerging issues.

**Tracking Effectiveness:**

By continuously monitoring these metrics through interactive dashboards, we can:
- Detect deviations from expected performance indicators.
- Adjust strategies dynamically based on real-time data feedback.
- Ensure that the optimization efforts maintain compliance and improve overall delivery timelines without compromising safety regulations or quality checks.
