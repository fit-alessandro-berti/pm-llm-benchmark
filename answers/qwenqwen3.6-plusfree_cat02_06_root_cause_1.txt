### 2. Root Cause Analysis of Performance Issues

By examining the time gaps between consecutive activities, three primary bottlenecks emerge:

####  A. Overnight/End-of-Day Idle Gaps (Universal Delay Factor)
- In all long cases, the `Investigate Issue` step finishes in the early-to-mid afternoon (13:00–14:00), but `Resolve Ticket` doesn't occur until the next business morning (08:00–09:00).
- **Gap:** ~18–19 hours of inactive time.
- **Implication:** The process lacks a same-day resolution mandate or after-hours coverage. Once an agent finishes investigation late in the day, the ticket sits idle until the next shift.

####  B. Escalation Handoff Delays (Cases 102 & 105)
- **Case 102:** `Assign to L1`  `Escalate to L2` took 2.5 hours. `Escalate`  `Investigate` (L2) took another 2.5 hours.
- **Case 105:** `Escalate to L2`  `Investigate` (L2) took **~28 hours** (overnight + next-day queue delay).
- **Implication:** Level-2 queues are either understaffed, lack prioritization, or suffer from poor handoff protocols. Escalations trigger a "black hole" period where tickets wait for specialized agent availability.

####  C. Initial Triage & Assignment Inefficiencies (Case 104)
- Case 104 did **not** escalate but still took ~24 hours. The primary delay occurred between `Assign to Level-1 Agent` (09:30) and `Investigate Issue` (13:00), a **3.5-hour idle period**.

### 3. Impact on Cycle Times & Recommendations

#### How These Factors Compound
1. **Baseline inefficiency** (late start to investigation) pushes work into the afternoon.
2. **End-of-day cutoff** forces a mandatory ~18-hour pause before resolution can occur.
3. **If escalation is triggered**, the ticket enters a secondary queue with additional handoff delays, often compounding with another overnight wait.
4. Result: A ticket that could be resolved in ~2 hours stretches into 1–2 full business days purely due to **queueing and scheduling gaps**, not actual work time.
