### **Case 1001: Standard / Compliant Process**
* **Analysis:** This case follows the standard P2P sequence perfectly. The requisition is approved before the PO is issued, goods and invoices are received, a three-way match is performed, and payment is released only after verification. 
* **Status:** No anomalies.

### **Case 1002: Severe Control Bypasses and Retroactive Approvals**
This case exhibits multiple severe deviations from standard P2P controls.

* **Anomaly 1: PO issued before PR approval.** 
  * *Sequence:* Issue Purchase Order (Feb 1, 08:40) happens before Approve Purchase Requisition (Feb 3, 08:00).
  * *Why it’s problematic:* This bypasses financial authorization. A buyer is committing company funds to a vendor before management has approved the need or the budget for the purchase. 
* **Anomaly 2: Payment released before Goods Receipt and Invoice Matching.**
  * *Sequence:* Release Payment (Feb 3, 07:15) occurs before Receive Goods (Feb 4) and Match Invoice to PO (Feb 5). 
  * *Why it’s problematic:* Paying before verifying that goods were actually received and before performing the three-way match is a massive financial risk. The company could be paying for goods that are damaged, incorrect, or never delivered. It completely defeats the purpose of the three-way match control.
* **Anomaly 3: Retroactive PR Approval.**
  * *Sequence:* Approve Purchase Requisition (Feb 3, 08:00) happens after the PO is issued, the invoice is received, and payment is made.
  * *Why it’s problematic:* Approving a requisition after the fact renders the approval control useless. It indicates a "rubber-stamp" culture where approvals are done merely to close out system alerts rather than to genuinely evaluate the purchase.

### **Case 1003: Premature / Blind Payment**
This case highlights a breakdown in the payment authorization process.

* **Anomaly 1: Payment released before Invoice and Goods Receipt.**
  * *Sequence:* Release Payment (Feb 8) occurs before Vendor Invoice Received (Feb 9) and Receive Goods (Feb 10).
  * *Why it’s problematic:* The company is releasing funds without an invoice to verify the exact amount owed, and without a goods receipt to verify the vendor fulfilled their obligation. * **Anomaly 2: Invoice Matching occurs after Payment.**
  * *Sequence:* Match Invoice to PO (Feb 11) happens days after the payment was released.
  * *Why it’s problematic:* The three-way match is designed to be the final gatekeeper *authorizing* a payment. Performing the match after the money has already left the bank account means the control is being used merely as a reconciliation exercise rather than a preventive control. If a discrepancy is found during the match, the company has already lost its cash and must chase the vendor for a refund.

### **Case 1004: "Maverick Buying" and Retrospective POs**
This case shows a breakdown in the procurement and receiving processes.

  * *Sequence:* Receive Goods (Feb 6, 09:00) happens before Issue Purchase Order (Feb 6, 09:15).
