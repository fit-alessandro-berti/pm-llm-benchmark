##  Detailed Justification for Grouping

### 1. **Material Intake & Setup**
- **Why grouped together?**  
  These events are all part of the **initial setup** of the manufacturing process. They occur in a fixed order and are essential to prepare the raw material for further processing.
- **Temporal and logical closeness:**  
  Events occur within a short time window (within ~15 seconds in the sample), and all are performed **before any welding or coating**.
- **Resource diversity:**  
  Involves both human (Operator A) and robotic (Robot Arm #2) and automated systems (Heating Unit), indicating a coordinated setup phase.

### 2. **Welding Assembly**
- **Why grouped together?**  
  The welding tool is picked up specifically to perform welds. The two welds (corner A and B) are part of the **same assembly action**, likely completing a joint.
- **Temporal proximity:**  
  Events occur within 7 seconds of each other, indicating a focused activity session.
- **Resource consistency:**  
  Performed by the same operator (Operator B) using the same tool (W-12), suggesting a dedicated task.

### 3. **Coating Application**
- **Why grouped together?**  
  The weld integrity check is a **precondition** for applying coating — it ensures the weld is sound before coating. The coating application and drying are **sequential process steps** that chemically protect the welded structure.
- **Logical dependency:**  
  The integrity check must occur before coating; otherwise, defects could be sealed in.
- **Resource involvement:**  
  Quality Sensor #1 checks the weld, then coating units apply and dry the coating — showing a transition from inspection to processing.

### 4. **Final Quality Inspection**
- **Why grouped together?**  
  The visual check is the **final step** in each case and confirms that the product meets visual and functional standards.
- **Position in workflow:**  
  Always occurs after coating is dried, making it a natural endpoint before the product is considered complete.
- **Human judgment:**  
  Requires human evaluation (Operator C), which is typical for final acceptance checks.
