### Explanation of Logic

#### Case Identification
- **Case ID**: `Annual_Report_Project_20241211`  
  All events are grouped into a single case representing the user's **morning work session** focused on preparing an annual report. This includes:
  - Editing two Word documents (`Quarterly_Report.docx`, `Document1.docx`)
  - Handling work-related email (annual meeting)
  - Reviewing a PDF report draft
  - Updating budget data in Excel
- **Rationale**: The sequence shows interdependent tasks (e.g., email triggers document updates, budget data feeds into reports). Grouping them as one case captures the **holistic workflow** of the annual report project, avoiding artificial fragmentation of related activities.

#### Activity Naming
Raw actions are mapped to standardized, higher-level activities for analytical clarity:
- **FOCUS/SWITCH**  `"Switch to Document"` or `"Switch Application"`  
  Distinguished based on whether the change occurs within the same app (`FOCUS`) or between apps (`SWITCH`).  
- **TYPING**  `"Type Text"`  
  Generalized regardless of content (e.g., email or document drafts).  
- **SAVE**  `"Save Document"`  
  Applies to all saves (Word/Excel).  
- **CLICK**  Context-specific:  
  - `"Open Email"` (opening emails)  
  - `"Reply to Email"` (initiating replies)  
  - `"Send Email"` (sending responses).  
- **SCROLL**  `"Scroll"`  
  Generic scrolling behavior.  
- **HIGHLIGHT**  `"Highlight Text"`  
  PDF annotation.  
- **CLOSE**  `"Close Document"`  
  Closing files.  

#### Key Decisions
1. **Temporal Cohesion**:  
   Events are ordered chronologically to reflect the user's flow. For example:
   - Email handling (`Open Email`  `Reply to Email`  `Send Email`) forms a logical sub-process.
   - Revisiting `Document1.docx` after Excel (`Switch Application`) shows task iteration.
2. **Application/Window Context**:  
   Included as additional attributes to preserve analytical granularity (e.g., distinguishing between Word documents).
3. **Exclusion of Low-Level Details**:  
   Ignored redundant attributes (e.g., `Keys` in typing events, `Direction` in scrolling) to avoid noise. Focus is placed on **meaningful actions** rather than raw inputs.

2. Interrupt for email (annual meeting)  Reply  Resume drafting.
