This visualization is a **Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagram** that illustrates the workflow for dispatching goods in a Computer Hardware Shop. The process is divided into three swimlanes representing different departments: **Logistics**, **Secretary**, and **Warehouse**.

Here is a step-by-step breakdown of the process flow:

### 1. Initialization
The process begins with the "Ship goods" event, which immediately splits into two parallel tasks using a parallel gateway:
*   **The Secretary** begins the process of clarifying the shipment method.
*   **The Warehouse** begins the process of packaging the goods.

### 2. The Secretary's Workflow (Core Processing)
After clarifying the shipment method, a decision gateway checks if "Special handling" is required:
*   **If "No":** The process checks if insurance is required based on customer information. If it is, the **Logistics** department handles the "Insure parcel" task. Whether insured or not, the secretary proceeds to "Write package label."
*   **If "Yes":** The secretary obtains three offers from logistic companies, selects one, and places the order.

### 3. The Warehouse's Workflow (Time-Sensitive)
*   The packaging task includes a time-based event: if packaging takes longer than **48 hours**, the warehouse must "Notify customer about delay."
*   Once finished, the warehouse waits for the secretary's shipping logistics to be finalized before proceeding.

### 4. Finalizing the Shipment
Once the secretary has finished the administrative tasks (labeling or selecting a logistic company) and the warehouse has finished packing, the workflow joins at a parallel gateway:
1.  **Fill out Shipment paperwork.**
2.  **Move package to pickup area.**
3.  The process terminates at the end event: **"Shipment prepared."**

### Key Elements Highlighted:
*   **Swimlanes:** Clearly distinguish the responsibilities between Logistics, the Secretary, and the Warehouse.
*   **Decision Gateways (XOR):** Used for branching paths (e.g., special handling vs. standard, insurance vs. no insurance).
*   **Parallel Gateways (+):** Used to coordinate simultaneous actions across different departments.
*   **Intermediate Timer Event:** Indicates a service level agreement or constraint (the 48-hour delay notification).
*   **Data Object:** The "Order details" and "Customer information" (database icon) signify the information inputs required to make decisions throughout the process.
