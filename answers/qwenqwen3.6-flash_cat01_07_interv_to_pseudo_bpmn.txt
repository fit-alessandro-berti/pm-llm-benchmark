### BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval & Payment Workflow

**Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Process  
**Primary Trigger:** Receipt of Supplier Invoice  
**Primary Goal:** Validate invoice against PO and contracts, obtain approvals, and schedule payment.

---

#### 1. Pools and Lanes (Roles/Participants)
*   **Supplier:** External entity providing goods/services and invoices.
*   **Accounts Payable (AP):** Mary (Clerk), Rita (Supervisor).
*   **Purchasing:** Dan (Officer).
*   **Department Manager:** Carlos (Manager).
*   **Supplier Relationship Management (SRM):** Karen (Manager).
*   **System:** Accounting/ERP System, Email Notifications.

---

#### 2. Process Flow Description

**Lane: Supplier**
*   **(Start Event):** Invoice Arrives.
*   *(External)* Supplier sends invoice via email to AP.

**Lane: Accounts Payable**
*   **Task 1.1: Receive Invoice.**
    *   *Input:* Invoice Email.
    *   *Output:* Invoice Record.
*   **Task 1.2: Validate PO Reference.**
    *   *Description:* Check if invoice has a valid Purchase Order (PO) number.
    *   *Gateway 1.1: Valid PO?*
        *   **No:**
            *   **Task 1.2a: Request Details from Supplier.**
                *   *Action:* Send email to supplier requesting missing PO or details.
                *   *(Flow to Supplier: "Request Missing Info")*
                *   *(Loop: Supplier sends details  AP receives details  Return to Task 1.2)*
            *   *Exception Path (Rita/SRM):* If invoice lacks PO due to informal service contract, Rita may initiate **Task 1.2b: Create Retrospective PO** (in collaboration with Department Head) or decide to reject.
        *   **Yes:**
            *   **Task 1.3: Log Invoice in System.**
                *   *Action:* Enter invoice into accounting system.
                *   *Artifact:* Invoice Object in System.
            *   **Task 1.4: Forward to Purchasing for Verification.**
                *   *Action:* Send invoice to Purchasing inbox to confirm receipt of goods/services and verify details.

**Lane: Purchasing**
*   **Task 2.1: Verify Invoice vs. PO.**
    *   *Description:* Compare invoice quantities, prices, and delivery charges against PO.
    *   *Gateway 2.1: Details Match?*
        *   **No:**
            *   **Task 2.1a: Resolve Discrepancy.**
                *   *Action:* Contact Supplier directly to request corrected invoice OR ask AP to hold invoice until correction arrives.
                *   *(Flow to Supplier: "Request Correction")*
                *   *(Loop: Supplier sends correction  AP receives  Purchasing re-verifies)*
                *   *Escalation:* If discrepancy cannot be resolved easily, **Escalate to Rita (AP Supervisor)**.
            *   *Advisory Path:* Purchasing may consult Karen (SRM) to check contract terms for pricing disputes.
        *   **Yes:**
            *   **Task 2.2: Confirm Receipt and Accuracy.**
                *   *Action:* Confirm goods/services received and details match.
                *   *Output:* Confirmation Email to AP.
                *   *(Flow to AP: "Confirmation Received")*

**Lane: Accounts Payable**
*   **Task 1.5: System Matching.**
    *   *Trigger:* Receipt of Purchasing Confirmation.
    *   *Action:* Match invoice line items against PO in the system.
    *   *Gateway 1.2: System Match Successful?*
        *   **No:**
            *   *Action:* Email Purchasing team to clarify.
            *   *(Loop back to Purchasing Task 2.1)*
            *   *Escalation:* If unresolved, escalate to Rita.
        *   **Yes:**
            *   **Task 1.6: Request Manager Approval.**
                *   *Action:* System automatically generates notification/email to Department Manager.
                *   *Artifact:* Approval Request.

**Lane: Department Manager**
*   **Task 3.1: Review and Approve.**
    *   *Trigger:* System Notification.
    *   *Description:* Review invoice details, PO, Purchasing confirmation, and budget alignment.
    *   *Gateway 3.1: Approval Granted?*
        *   **No:**
            *   **Task 3.1a: Reject or Hold.**
                *   *Action:* Reject in system OR ask AP to hold and investigate (e.g., budget variance, suspicious items).
                *   *(Flow to AP: "Investigation Request")*
                *   *(Loop: AP investigates  May involve Purchasing/SRM  Resubmit to Manager)*
        *   **Yes:**
            *   **Task 3.1b: Approve in System.**
                *   *Action:* Click "Approve" in system.
                *   *Output:* Approved Invoice Status.
                *   *(Flow to AP: "Approval Received")*

**Lane: Accounts Payable**
*   **Task 1.7: Schedule Payment.**
    *   *Trigger:* Manager Approval.
    *   *Action:* Add invoice to next payment cycle/run.
    *   *(End Event):* Payment Scheduled.

---

#### 3. Exception and Variant Paths

*   **Escalation to AP Supervisor (Rita):**
    *   *Trigger:* Mary or Purchasing cannot resolve discrepancies; invoice missing PO for informal services; unresolvable pricing issues.
    *   *Actions:* Rita contacts suppliers, coordinates between departments, creates retrospective POs (with Dept Head), or decides on rejection.
    *   *Flow:* After resolution, process returns to normal flow (Purchasing Confirmation  Manager Approval  Payment).
*   **SRM Consultation (Karen):**
    *   *Trigger:* Pricing discrepancies or terms mismatch.
    *   *Actions:* Karen checks contracts and advises AP/Purchasing on correct pricing. Karen contacts supplier to resolve relationship issues.
    *   *Flow:* Advisory loop; does not block system flow but informs resolution tasks.
*   **Missing PO / Informal Services:**
    *   *Path:* AP detects no PO  Rita intervenes  Create Retrospective PO with Dept Head  Process continues as standard.

---

#### 4. Artifacts and Data Objects

*   **Invoice:** Electronic document received from supplier.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference document in system.
*   **Retrospective PO:** Generated artifact for informal services.
*   **Accounting System:** Database/ERP containing invoice records, match status, and approval workflow.
*   **Email Notifications:** Triggers for tasks (e.g., Manager approval request, Purchasing confirmation).
*   **Contracts:** Reference for SRM to validate pricing.

---

#### 5. Ambiguities and Conflicting Accounts (Consolidation Notes)

1.  **Discrepancy Resolution Path:**
    *   *Conflict:* Mary states she emails the purchasing team to clarify discrepancies. Dan states he contacts the supplier directly or asks AP to hold.
    *   *Resolution:* The model reflects that Purchasing (Dan) has the authority to contact the supplier directly. However, AP (Mary) may route clarification requests through Purchasing. The model prioritizes Purchasing contacting the supplier for corrections, with AP acting as a coordinator if the supplier sends corrections to AP.
2.  **Receipt Confirmation:**
    *   *Conflict:* Mary mentions forwarding to Purchasing to "confirm goods were received." Dan focuses on checking details/prices.
    *   *Resolution:* Purchasing verification (Task 2.1) implicitly includes receipt confirmation, as Dan states he never approves without a confirmed PO reference and checks quantities. The model groups "Verify Details" and "Confirm Receipt" under Purchasing's verification task.
3.  **Manager Approval Trigger:**
    *   *Consensus:* Mary and Carlos agree the system sends an automatic notification/email to the manager after Purchasing confirmation and matching.
4.  **Retrospective PO Process:**
    *   *Note:* Only Rita mentions creating retrospective POs and working with department heads. Carlos (Manager) does not mention this. The model includes Rita and the Department Head in this exception path based on Rita's testimony.

---

#### 6. Visualizable Structure Summary

*   **Start:** Invoice Received.
*   **Gateways:** PO Exists?  Details Match?  System Match?  Approved?
*   **Loops:** Supplier Correction Loop; Investigation Loop; Retro-PO Loop.
*   **Parallelism:** None explicitly mentioned; flow is sequential with exception branches.
*   **System Automation:** Logging, Matching, Approval Notification.
*   **Human Tasks:** Validation, Verification, Review, Approval, Payment Scheduling.

This textual model captures the consensus workflow, integrates all stakeholder roles, handles exceptions via escalation paths, and notes ambiguities for further clarification during diagramming.
