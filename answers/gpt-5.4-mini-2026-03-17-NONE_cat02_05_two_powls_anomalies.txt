Here is the comparison of the two POWL models against a normative Hire-to-Retire process.

## 1) Normative Hire-to-Retire logic
A standard Hire-to-Retire flow is usually:

1. Post job advertisement
2. Screen candidates
3. Conduct interviews
4. Make hiring decision
5. If hired, onboard employee
6. Add employee to payroll
7. Close case

Typically, the screening and interviews occur before the hiring decision, and onboarding/payroll happen only after a positive decision. The process is usually linear, with possible loops only for things like repeated interviews or re-screening, but not in a way that breaks the basic hiring logic.

---

## 2) Analysis of Model 1

### Structure
Model 1 enforces:

- `Post -> Screen`
- After `Screen`, two branches can happen: `Decide` and `Interview`
- Then `Decide -> Onboard -> Payroll -> Close`

### Anomalies in Model 1
#### a) Interview occurs after screening but in parallel with decision
The biggest anomaly is that `Screen_Candidates` precedes both:
- `Make_Hiring_Decision`
- `Conduct_Interviews`

This means the decision can be made before interviews are conducted, or at least the model does not enforce interviews as a prerequisite for the decision. That is a serious process anomaly because interviews are normally part of the evaluation that informs the decision.

#### b) Interviews are not connected to later hiring steps
`Conduct_Interviews` is not linked to `Make_Hiring_Decision`, onboarding, payroll, or close. This makes interviews look optional or disconnected from the hiring outcome.

### Severity
This is a **moderate anomaly**:
- It violates the expected evaluation logic.
- But the overall core hiring sequence is still recognizable:
  - post
  - screen
  - decide
  - onboard
  - payroll
  - close

So Model 1 is somewhat flawed, but still largely follows the intended process skeleton.

---

## 3) Analysis of Model 2

### Structure
Model 2 enforces:

- `Post -> Screen`
- `Post -> Interview`
- `Interview -> Decide`
- `Decide -> loop_onboarding`
- `loop_onboarding -> xor_payroll`
- `xor_payroll -> Close`

With:
- `loop_onboarding = LOOP(Onboard, skip)`
- `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)`

### Anomalies in Model 2
#### a) Interview can happen directly after posting, before screening
This is a major anomaly:
- `Conduct_Interviews` is allowed after `Post_Job_Ad`
- `Screen_Candidates` is also allowed after `Post_Job_Ad`

But there is no precedence requiring screening before interview. So the process may interview candidates before screening them, which is clearly non-normative.

#### b) Screening is disconnected from the rest of the hiring logic
`Screen_Candidates` appears in the model, but it does not lead into the decision path or the rest of the process. That means screening may happen, but it has no effect on the hiring outcome.

#### c) Onboarding is modeled as a loop with a silent skip
`LOOP(Onboard, skip)` is very odd here:
- It suggests onboarding can repeat
- Or that the model can cycle through onboarding and then exit

In a normal Hire-to-Retire process, onboarding is usually a one-time activity, not something repeated.

#### d) Payroll is optional
`XOR(Payroll, skip)` means payroll may be skipped entirely. That is a strong anomaly because adding the employee to payroll is a core step after hiring and onboarding.

#### e) The process may close without payroll
Because payroll is optional, the case can close without the employee ever being added to payroll. That undermines the correctness of the process.

### Severity
This model contains **more severe anomalies** than Model 1:
- wrong ordering of screening/interview
- onboarding repetition
- optional payroll
- possible premature case closure

These issues affect the integrity of the process much more deeply than in Model 1.

---

## 4) Which model is closer to the normative process?

### Model 1 is closer to normative Hire-to-Retire
Even though Model 1 has a flaw in the ordering of interviews and decision, it still preserves the main lifecycle:

- post job
- screen
- decide
- onboard
- payroll
- close

Model 2, on the other hand, breaks key business logic:
- interviews may happen before screening
- screening is not integrated into the decision path
- onboarding is looped unnaturally
- payroll is optional
- closure can happen without completion of essential steps

So Model 2 deviates much more from a standard hiring process and is less semantically correct.

- **Model 1**: mildly to moderately anomalous, but still broadly aligned with the expected hire-to-retire structure.
- **Model 2**: strongly anomalous, with multiple major violations of process logic.

**Model 1 more closely aligns with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**
