### Justification for Renaming Choices

**1. Verify Documents (appears twice in C001 and C003)**
- **Initial Document Verification**: Used for the first occurrence. At this stage, the activity is a preliminary check (identity docs, physical ID, etc.) that frequently reveals gaps, leading to a call.
- **Document Re-verification**: Used for the second occurrence. This is a *subsequent* verification after new information has been provided (additional upload in C001, account adjustment in C003). The purpose has shifted from discovery to confirmation.

**2. Call Customer (appears twice in C002)**
- **Clarification Call**: First call — the goal is to resolve missing or discrepant information.
- **Confirmation Call**: Second call — the goal is to confirm that adjustments have been made and the customer is satisfied. The intent and position in the flow are clearly different.

**3. Other supporting renames (for clarity across the log)**
- `Initiate Request`  split into `Online Request Initiation` vs `Branch Walk-in Initiation` because the channel and starting condition are fundamentally different.
- `Approve Request`  `Request Approval` to better reflect that this is the final decision step rather than a generic approval.
- `Adjust Account`  `Adjust Account Details` for precision.
- `Submit Additional`  `Submit Additional Documents` to make the activity self-explanatory.

These changes transform the log from ambiguous, overloaded activity names into a semantically clear event log that accurately represents the distinct process steps, making it suitable for process mining, analysis, or modeling.
