**Violating Cases: 2, 3, and 5**

### Case 1
**Compliant.**  
Follows the “sufficient inventory” path exactly: Receive  Check  Authorize  Package  Ship  Close.

### Case 4
**Compliant.**  
Follows the “insufficient inventory” path exactly: Receive  Check  Cancel  Close.

---

### Case 2 – Violation
**Sequence:** Receive  Check  **Package**  Authorize  Ship  Close

**Violations:**
- “Package items” occurs **before** “Authorize payment”.
- The normative description explicitly states that authorization must happen after the inventory check and **before** packaging: “after confirming inventory, the process should include the authorization of payment… Once the payment is authorized, the items should be prepared for shipment.”
- Packaging before payment authorization contradicts the required order of activities when inventory is sufficient.

### Case 3 – Violation (multiple serious deviations)
**Sequence:** Receive  Check  Authorize  **Cancel**  **Ship**  Close

**Violations:**
1. **Shipping after cancellation** – The description is unambiguous: “Ship order should only occur after packaging and **never after a cancellation**.”
2. Mixing the two branches: After “Check inventory” the process took the **Authorize** path (implying sufficient stock) and then suddenly switched to **Cancel**. The normative model requires a clean branch after the inventory check — either Authorize (sufficient) **or** Cancel (insufficient), not both.
3. No “Package items” activity at all, even though authorization occurred.

This case violates the core control-flow constraints and the mutual exclusivity of the sufficient vs. insufficient inventory paths.

### Case 5 – Violation
**Sequence:** Receive  Check  Authorize  **Ship**  Close (missing Package)

**Violation:**
- “Ship order” occurs without the required “Package items” activity.
- The normative description states: “Only after items have been properly packaged is it appropriate to proceed with ‘Ship order.’” and lists the mandatory sequence as Receive  Check  Authorize  **Package**  Ship  Close when inventory is sufficient.

Skipping the packaging step is a clear deviation from the expected fulfillment sequence.

---

**Summary of Violating Cases:**  
- **Case 2**: Package before Authorize  
- **Case 3**: Cancel + Ship after authorization (shipping after cancellation + mixing branches)  
- **Case 5**: Ship without Package

Cases 1 and 4 fully conform to the described normative process.
