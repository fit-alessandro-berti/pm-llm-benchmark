**Analysis of Performance Issues in the Insurance Claims Process**

### 1. Cases with Performance Issues

After calculating the end-to-end duration for each case (from "Submit Claim" to "Close Claim"), two clear performance clusters emerge:

**Fast cases (1.3ñ1.5 hours):**
- **2001** (Region A, Low complexity): 1.5 hours
- **2004** (Region B, Low complexity): 1.42 hours

**Slow cases:**
- **2002** (Region B, Medium complexity): **26 hours**
- **2003** (Region A, High complexity): **48.3 hours**
- **2005** (Region B, High complexity): **77 hours**

Cases 2002, 2003, and especially 2005 show significantly longer lead times.

### 2. Root Cause Analysis (Attributes Correlation)

The primary driver of long durations is **Complexity**, with secondary effects from **Resource/Region**.

#### Key Findings:

**A. Complexity Level (Strongest Correlation)**
- **Low**: 0 "Request Additional Documents" activities  ~1.5 hours
- **Medium**: 1 request  ~26 hours
- **High**: 2ñ3 requests  48ñ77 hours

The number of document requests is the dominant factor. Each request typically adds 1ñ2 days of waiting time (presumably for the customer to respond).

**B. Region & Resource Effects**
- **Region B** performs worse than **Region A** for equivalent complexity:
  - High complexity: Region A (2003) = 48.3h vs Region B (2005) = 77h
- **Adjuster_Lisa** (Region B) is associated with longer durations:
  - She handles all three slow cases (2002, 2004, 2005).
  - In high-complexity case 2005, she made **three** separate requests spread across days (Apr 1, 2, and 3).
  - In contrast, Adjuster_Mike (Region A) made two requests on the **same day** in case 2003.

**C. Other Observations**
- Manager_Bill only approves High complexity claims, while Manager_Ann handles Low/Medium. This specialization is logical but may create a bottleneck if Bill is overloaded.
- Finance and CSR resources do not appear to be major delay points.

### 3. Explanations for Root Causes

1. **Iterative Document Requests (Primary Cause)**: High-complexity claims are being handled through multiple back-and-forth requests rather than a comprehensive initial request. This creates sequential waiting periods. The process is essentially "discovering" missing documents over time instead of identifying them upfront.

2. **Adjuster Behavior Differences**: Adjuster_Lisa appears to take a more sequential, cautious approach (multiple spaced-out requests), while Adjuster_Mike is more aggressive in requesting documents in batches. This suggests a lack of standardized guidelines for document requirements by complexity level.

3. **Regional Performance Gap**: Region B consistently shows longer durations. This could be due to:
   - Higher workload on Adjuster_Lisa
   - Differences in customer responsiveness by region
   - Less efficient internal handoffs in Region B

### 4. Recommendations to Mitigate Root Causes

**Short-term fixes:**
- Develop **document requirement checklists** by complexity level (Low/Medium/High) so adjusters request all necessary documents in the first request.
- Set a **maximum of 1ñ2 document request cycles** per case, with escalation to a senior adjuster after the first request.
- Give Adjuster_Lisa targeted training on batch requesting and prioritization of high-complexity claims.

**Medium-term improvements:**
- Introduce a **triage step** during "Evaluate Claim" with a standardized questionnaire or decision tree to identify required documents more accurately.
- Implement a **customer portal** for document upload with clear checklists to reduce response time.
- Balance workload between Adjuster_Mike and Adjuster_Lisa or add capacity in Region B for high-complexity claims.

**Process redesign ideas:**
- Consider parallelizing some verification activities for high-complexity claims.
- Add performance monitoring on "time between document request and approval" as a key metric.
- Explore whether certain document types can be made optional or verified through alternative data sources (e.g., third-party databases).

**Summary**: The main performance issue is not the individual activities themselves, but the **iterative nature of document collection** in Medium and High complexity cases, amplified by inconsistent practices between adjusters and regions. Standardizing the upfront document collection process is the highest-leverage improvement opportunity.
