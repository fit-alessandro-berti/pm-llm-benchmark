### How This Reduces Bias in Practice
In a standard loan workflow, automated systems might inadvertently correlate sensitive attributes (e.g., `ApplicantRace`) with negative outcomes (`Reject`), leading to discriminatory patterns. By injecting these DECLARE constraints:
1. **Structural Fairness Gates:** The process model itself becomes legally incapable of producing a decision without first passing through `BiasMitigationCheck` and `ManualReview`.
2. **Decoupling Sensitive Data from Decisions:** The `nonsuccession` constraint breaks direct causal pathways between viewing protected attributes and issuing a verdict, forcing intermediate scrutiny.
3. **Human-in-the-Loop Assurance:** `coexistence` with `ManualReview` ensures accountability, allowing loan officers to override or validate algorithmic recommendations that may reflect historical biases.

These constraints transform fairness from a post-hoc audit metric into a **control-flow requirement**, ensuring that biased execution paths are structurally invalid according to the process model.
