Explanation of transformation
- Case identification: I used “one artifact = one case.” Each distinct document/email/thread/spreadsheet/PDF became its own case:
  - CASE_QR_Quarterly_Report: Quarterly_Report.docx
  - CASE_DOC1_Document1: Document1.docx
  - CASE_EMAIL_Annual_Meeting: the Annual Meeting email thread handled in Chrome
  - CASE_PDF_Report_Draft: Report_Draft.pdf in Acrobat
  - CASE_XLSX_Budget_2024: Budget_2024.xlsx in Excel
  This yields coherent, analyst-friendly traces even when the user switches between apps; events interleave across cases by timestamp but remain ordered within each case.

- Activity naming (standardization):
  - FOCUS  Open Document / Open Spreadsheet / Open PDF / Open Email Client (first occurrence); subsequent focuses mapped to Resume Document.
  - TYPING  Edit Document (Word), Update Spreadsheet (Excel), Compose Email (Chrome).
  - SAVE  Save Document / Save Spreadsheet.
  - CLOSE  Close Document.
  - SWITCH (from X to Y)  Switch Out to Other App in the “from” case; simultaneously, the “to” side is represented by the opening activity in that target case (e.g., Open PDF).
  - CLICK/SCROLL in Email  Open Email Thread, Read Email (Scroll), Reply to Email, Send Email.
  - SCROLL in Acrobat  Read PDF; HIGHLIGHT  Annotate PDF.

- Event attributes:
  - Included Case ID, Activity, Timestamp (ISO 8601), plus App, Artifact, Artifact Type, and Details (source action, to_app, brief notes). These are commonly useful in process analysis for filtering, variant analysis, and handover/interruptions.

- Narrative summary:
  - The user initially focused Quarterly_Report.docx, then worked on Document1.docx (drafting and saving), paused to handle an email (open, reply, send), reviewed/annotated a PDF, updated the budget spreadsheet (save), returned to Document1 to insert a budget reference (save, close), and finally resumed Quarterly_Report.docx to draft the executive summary (save, close).

This structure is directly loadable into process mining tools as a CSV event log; each row is a meaningful step in a single case, enabling discovery, conformance, and performance analysis.
