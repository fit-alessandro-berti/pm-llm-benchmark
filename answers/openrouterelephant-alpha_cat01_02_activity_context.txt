### **Step 1: Identify Recurring Activities and Their Contextual Differences**

From the event log, the following activities occur more than once **within the same case**, but their meanings differ:

- **Verify Documents** – occurs twice in C001 and once in C003. First occurrence is initial verification after submission; second is re-verification after additional documents are uploaded or internal changes occur.
- **Adjust Account** – occurs in C002 and C003, both after a call to clarify discrepancies and before final approval. Internal correction made after communication.
- **Call Customer** – occurs multiple times across all cases. First call may be to request missing info; subsequent calls may confirm changes or clarify further.

---

### **Step 2: Rename Activities to Reflect Context**

We rename the recurring activities with descriptive suffixes or phrases to differentiate their purpose within the workflow:

| Original Activity       | Contextual Role / Renamed Name                              |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Verify Documents       | **Initial Document Verification** / **Re-verify Documents** |
| Call Customer          | **Clarification Call** / **Follow-up Call**                 |
| Adjust Account         | **Post-Call Account Adjustment**                            |

### **Step 4: Justification of Naming Choices**

1. **Verify Documents  Initial / Re-verify Documents**  
   - The first occurrence indicates the **initial checking** of submitted documents.  
   - The second (C001) and third (C003) happen **after new information** is provided (e.g., uploaded ID scan, account adjustment), so they are **re-checking** for updates.

2. **Call Customer  Clarification Call / Follow-up Call**  
   - The first call in each case is to **clarify missing or incorrect information**.  
   - Subsequent calls are **follow-ups** to confirm changes (C002) or status after an action (C003).

3. **Adjust Account  Post-Call Account Adjustment**  
   - Renamed to emphasize that the **adjustment happens after a call** with the customer, distinguishing it from initial verification or unrelated account changes.

---

### **Conclusion**

By renaming the recurring activities with context-specific suffixes, we preserve the original intent of the workflow while eliminating ambiguity. This ensures that each event clearly communicates its **role and timing** in the process, improving traceability, analysis, and automation potential.
