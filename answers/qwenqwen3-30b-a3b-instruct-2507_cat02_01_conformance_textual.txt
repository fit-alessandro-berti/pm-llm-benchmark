###  **Step 1: Understand the Normative Rules from the Description**

From the textual description, the following **key constraints** can be extracted:

####  **Correct Flow (Sufficient Inventory):**
1. `Receive order`   
2. `Check inventory`   
3. `Authorize payment`   
4. `Package items`   
5. `Ship order`   
6. `Close order`

> All steps must occur in this order, and **no step can occur before its predecessor**.

####  **Correct Flow (Insufficient Inventory):**
1. `Receive order`   
2. `Check inventory`   
3. `Cancel order`   
4. `Close order`

> After cancellation, **no fulfillment-related activities** (e.g., package, ship) should occur.

####  **Prohibited Activities (Violations):**
- **`Authorize payment` before `Check inventory`**  Invalid, because payment authorization should only happen *after* inventory is confirmed.
- **`Package items` before `Authorize payment`**  Invalid, since packaging should follow payment authorization.
- **`Ship order` before `Package items`**  Invalid, since items must be packaged before shipping.
- **`Ship order` after `Cancel order`**  Invalid, because cancellation implies the order is not fulfilled.
- **`Close order` before the final activity**  Invalid, as it must be the last step.
- **`Cancel order` without prior `Check inventory`**  Invalid, since cancellation is based on inventory check.

> **Important:** The process description clearly states:
> - "Check inventory" must occur **before** any decisions about payment or fulfillment.
> - "Cancel order" must come **after** `Check inventory` and **before** `Close order`.
> - After cancellation, **no fulfillment activities** (like packaging or shipping) should occur.
