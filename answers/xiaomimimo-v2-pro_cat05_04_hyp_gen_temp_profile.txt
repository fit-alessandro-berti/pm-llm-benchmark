#### 1. **Check for Claims with Abnormally Short RP Intervals**
```sql
SELECT 
    ce1.claim_id,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) AS actual_seconds,
    90000 AS expected_avg,
    3600 AS expected_std
FROM claim_events ce1
JOIN claim_events ce2 
    ON ce1.claim_id = ce2.claim_id
    AND ce2.activity = 'P'
WHERE ce1.activity = 'R'
    AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) < (90000 - 3 * 3600);
```
- **Purpose:** Identify claims where RP time is significantly below average (e.g., less than 3 standard deviations from mean), indicating rushed approvals.

#### 2. **Analyze PN Delays by Resource or Claim Type**
```sql
SELECT 
    ce1.resource AS approval_resource,
    c.claim_type,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))) AS avg_delay_seconds,
    COUNT(*) AS claim_count
FROM claim_events ce1
JOIN claim_events ce2 
    ON ce1.claim_id = ce2.claim_id
    AND ce2.activity = 'N'
JOIN claims c ON ce1.claim_id = c.claim_id
WHERE ce1.activity = 'P'
GROUP BY ce1.resource, c.claim_type
HAVING AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))) > 604800 + 2 * 172800;
```
- **Purpose:** Detect if specific adjusters or claim types have unusually long PN delays, pointing to bottlenecks or negligence.

#### 5. **Correlate Anomalies with Regions or Specializations**
```sql
SELECT
```
