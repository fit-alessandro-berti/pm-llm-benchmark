## Identification of Bias: Group B (Unprotected Group)

The bias is rooted in the differential treatment of applicants based on the `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` attributes, specifically visible in the `ScoreAdjustment` column during the `PreliminaryScoring` and `ManualReview` steps.

### 1. The Role of Attributes

| Attribute | Group A (Protected) | Group B (Unprotected) | Impact on Process |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **LocalResident** | All FALSE | All TRUE | Group B members are flagged as local residents, potentially triggering specific policy considerations. |
| **CommunityGroup** | All None | U001 & U003 associated with "Highland Civic Darts Club" | This association is the direct trigger for the observed score adjustment in Group B. |

### 2. Manifestation of Bias via Score Adjustment

The core evidence of systematic difference lies in the `ScoreAdjustment` column:

**In Group A (Protected Group):**
*   The `ScoreAdjustment` column is consistently **0** across all cases (P001, P002, P003), regardless of the preliminary score (720, 710, 740).
*   The `ManualReview` step never records a specific adjustment value (N/A).
*   The process appears standardized based purely on the initial score and automated rules.

**In Group B (Unprotected Group):**
*   Cases associated with a `CommunityGroup` (U001 and U003) receive a **positive Score Adjustment of +10** ("Community Boost") during `PreliminaryScoring`.
*   This adjustment carries through to the `ManualReview` step, where the score is updated (e.g., 720 becomes 730, 695 becomes 705).
*   Case U002, which has `LocalResident=TRUE` but no `CommunityGroup`, receives a **Score Adjustment of 0**, suggesting the community association is the specific factor triggering the benefit.

This systematic score adjustment directly influences the final outcomes, creating a disparity:

The most telling comparison is Case U003:
*   U003 started at **695**. Without the +10 boost, it would likely have been Rejected (similar to P002/U002 at 710, assuming a rejection threshold near 710).
*   The **+10 adjustment** elevated U003 to 705, resulting in an **Approved** decision.

**Conclusion on Bias:**

The log for **Group B (Unprotected Group)** demonstrates **favoritism or preferential treatment** based on affiliation with the "Highland Civic Darts Club," as evidenced by the non-zero, positive `ScoreAdjustment` applied consistently to applicants with this affiliation. Group A receives no such standardized boost, indicating a difference in processing rules or criteria applied to the two groups.

If the organizational goal is fairness or equity, the process applied to Group B is biased because it grants a measurable, tangible benefit (+10 points) based on a community association, a benefit not observed for Group A.
