## C. A very strong pattern: ~19-hour delay from investigation to resolution
This is the most interesting repeated pattern.

| Case | Investigate | Resolve | Gap |

This repeated gap suggests a **systematic bottleneck**, not random variation.

Possible explanations:

- resolution may depend on a next-day approval/review step
- work may stop at end of business day
- tickets may sit overnight in a queue after investigation

So the issue is likely not just “complex tickets,” but also **operational waiting time**.

## 3) How these factors increase cycle time

## Main driver: waiting time, not closure time
The **Close Ticket** step is quick in all cases (15–30 min after resolution), so closure is **not** the bottleneck.

The real delays come from:

1. **Waiting to begin investigation**
2. **Waiting after escalation for Level-2 pickup**
3. **Waiting overnight after investigation before resolution**

This means the process is being slowed more by **queues and handoffs** than by the actual work steps themselves.

---

## Recommendations

## 1. Tighten escalation handling
Since escalated cases are much slower:
- set SLA for **time from escalation to first Level-2 touch**
- auto-route escalations to the right specialist queue
- ensure escalation includes enough context to avoid re-investigation

## 2. Improve triage and skill-based routing
If a ticket is likely to need Level-2, route it earlier instead of letting Level-1 hold it first.
This could reduce:
- unnecessary delay before escalation
- duplicate investigation effort

## 3. Reduce assignment-to-investigation waiting
Case 104 shows non-escalated tickets can still stall.
Possible actions:
- rebalance agent workloads
- auto-assign based on availability/skill
- monitor queue age between assignment and first investigation

## 4. Investigate the repeated overnight resolution delay
The repeated **19-hour** gap is a strong signal of a structural issue.
You should check whether:
- resolutions are batched for the next morning
- approvals/testing are only done once per day
- agents stop active work late in the day
- there is insufficient end-of-day coverage

This is likely one of the biggest improvement opportunities.

## 5. Track the right KPIs
Useful metrics to monitor:
- Receive  Triage
- Assign  First Investigation
- Escalate  Level-2 First Touch
- Investigate  Resolve
- % of tickets resolved same day
- % of escalated tickets
