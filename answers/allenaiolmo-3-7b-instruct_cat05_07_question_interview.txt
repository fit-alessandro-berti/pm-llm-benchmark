### Process Structure & Flow

1. **Can you walk me through the typical timeline from when a landlord first contacts your company to when a tenant is move-in ready?**
   - This will help map out the duration and identify any bottlenecks or parallel processes.

2. **How do you ensure that the documentation collected from the landlord is both complete and up to date before moving forward with the onboarding?**
   - This can reveal the rigor and possible gaps in the verification process.

3. **What are the major milestones within the onboarding process, and how does each stage depend on the previous one?**
   - To understand dependencies and potential parallel or sequential steps.

4. **Are there instances where the onboarding process can be expedited, or where it must be thoroughly slowed down? Under what circumstances does each occur?**
   - To identify flexibility and risk points.

---

### Roles, Responsibilities & Communication

5. **Who is involved at each stage of the onboarding process, and how do they communicate with one another?**
   - Clarifies the handoffs and potential for miscommunication.

6. **How is the decision made about which property manager is assigned to a new listing, and what criteria are most important in that selection?**
   - Elucidates prioritization and specialization.

7. **What happens if the initial property manager is not the best fit for the property or the landlord? How is the assignment changed?**
   - Assesses the process for adapting to changing needs.

8. **How do you handle situations where the inspection reveals significant issues that require substantial repairs or changes to the property?**
   - Explores exception and problem management.

---

### Documentation, Data, and Technology

9. **How is the property information stored and accessed after onboarding? Are there redundancies to prevent data loss or errors?**
   - Gathers insight into data management and reliability.

10. **What happens if there’s a discrepancy between the landlord’s provided information and what is verified during the inspection or marketing process?**
    - Looks at error handling and correction.

---

### Tenant and Landlord Engagement

11. **How do you ensure tenant safety and compliance with company and legal standards during the screening and lease negotiation stages?**
    - Assesses risk management practices.

12. **Are there standard lease terms that all landlords must accept, or do you allow for customization based on the property or landlord’s requests?**
    - Clarifies flexibility and policy enforcement.

13. **How do you handle lease negotiations, especially when multiple interested tenants make the process more complex?**
    - Explores decision-making and tenant prioritization.

---

### Marketing, Financials, and Post-Activation

14. **What metrics or KPIs do you monitor to determine the effectiveness of a property’s onboarding and marketing campaign?**
    - Identifies how success is measured.

15. **Once the property is live to tenants, what follow-up occurs to ensure everything is running smoothly and to address any early issues?**
    - Understands post-onboarding support.

16. **How do you handle special requirements from landlords, such as unique amenities, special insurance needs, or non-standard lease clauses?**
    - Explores exception handling beyond the standard process.

---

### Continuous Improvement & Feedback

17. **How do you gather feedback from landlords and tenants about the onboarding experience, and how is that information used to improve the process?**
    - Seeks to understand the organization’s approach to continuous improvement.

18. **Are there training or support programs for property managers or new onboarding staff to prevent recurring issues or inconsistencies?**
    - Looks at capacity building and quality assurance.

These questions aim to uncover the nuances, decision points, responsibilities, risks, and improvement opportunities within the onboarding process, without requiring technical or SQL-specific answers.
