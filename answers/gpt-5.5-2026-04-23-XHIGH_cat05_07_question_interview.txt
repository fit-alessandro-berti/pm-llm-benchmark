Below are targeted, open-ended questions I would ask to clarify the property onboarding process.

## 1. Process Scope and Starting Point

1. What event officially starts the property onboarding process?
2. What information is collected during the landlordÆs initial contact with the regional office?
3. How do you determine whether a property is eligible to be onboarded at all?
4. At what point is a property considered formally ōacceptedö into the onboarding pipeline?
5. What are the most common reasons a property onboarding request is rejected or paused early in the process?

## 2. Documentation Review

6. Which documents are mandatory before onboarding can proceed?
7. How do coordinators determine whether a document is outdated, incomplete, or invalid?
8. What happens when required documents are missing or need to be updated?
9. Who is responsible for following up with the landlord on missing documentation?
10. How long does the documentation review typically take?
11. Are there different documentation requirements by city, property type, or regulatory environment?

## 3. Property Data Capture

12. What property details must be entered into the central property database before the process can continue?
13. Who is responsible for verifying that the property data entered is accurate?
14. How are expected rental price ranges initially determined?
15. What happens if property information changes after it has already been entered?
16. Which data fields are most critical for downstream teams such as marketing, leasing, compliance, and finance?

## 4. Property Manager Assignment

17. How is the property manager assignment decision made in practice?
18. Which assignment criteria are most important: workload, geography, property type experience, landlord preferences, or something else?
19. Who has final authority to assign or reassign a property manager?
20. What happens if the best-suited property manager is already at capacity?
21. How are conflicts or special landlord requests handled during manager assignment?

## 5. Landlord Alignment Meeting

22. What topics are typically covered in the introductory meeting between the landlord and property manager?
23. What decisions need to be made during or shortly after that meeting?
24. How are special policies, such as pet rules, maintenance responsibilities, or payment methods, documented?
25. What happens if the landlord requests conditions that conflict with company policy or local regulations?
26. Who approves custom terms or exceptions agreed upon with the landlord?

## 6. Photography, Inspection, and Repairs

27. How are the photographer and certified inspector selected and scheduled?
28. What factors influence the timing of the inspection and photography visit?
29. What does the inspector evaluate, and what standards must the property meet?
30. What happens if the inspection identifies safety, habitability, or compliance issues?
31. Who decides whether repairs are required before marketing can begin?
32. How are repair responsibilities divided between the landlord, contractors, and the property management company?
33. What happens if the landlord refuses or delays required repairs?

## 7. Marketing and Activation

34. What information does the marketing team need before creating listings and brochures?
35. How are listing descriptions, photos, amenities, and pricing reviewed before publication?
36. How are pricing models updated based on market trends?
37. Who gives final approval to activate the property in the rental portal?
38. What exactly does ōactivationö mean across the rental portal and internal systems?
39. What checks are performed before prospective tenants can view or apply for the property?

## 8. Tenant Screening and Lease Finalization

40. When does tenant screening begin relative to property activation?
41. Who is responsible for reviewing applications and making tenant recommendations?
42. What criteria are used to evaluate tenant applications?
43. How are credit checks, employment verification, and landlord references handled?
44. What happens when multiple qualified tenants apply for the same property?
45. Who has final approval over tenant selection: the property manager, landlord, leasing agent, or another party?
46. Under what conditions are lease terms negotiated or promotional discounts offered?
47. How are security deposits collected and confirmed?
48. What steps must be completed before a move-in date is scheduled?

## 9. Compliance, Finance, and Quality Checks

49. At what stages do compliance, finance, and IT reviews occur?
50. What does the compliance officer look for when reviewing the lease?
51. How are fair housing requirements enforced during tenant screening and lease approval?
52. What does the financial analyst verify before the lease is finalized?
53. What happens if the agreed rent does not align with the companyÆs pricing strategy?
54. What does IT check regarding listing data and system integration?
55. How are audit findings documented and resolved?

## 10. Exceptions and Special Cases

56. What types of properties require special handling, such as luxury units, student housing, furnished rentals, or special insurance?
57. How are custom landlord requests evaluated and approved?
58. What happens when a landlord insists on custom lease clauses?
59. How are properties handled when local regulations differ significantly between cities?
60. What are the most frequent exceptions that delay onboarding?
61. How are escalations handled when teams disagree on whether a property is ready to proceed?

## 11. Roles, Handoffs, and Communication

62. What are the main roles involved from initial contact through tenant move-in?
63. How do responsibilities differ between the property coordinator, property manager, leasing agent, compliance officer, financial analyst, and IT team?
64. Where do the most important handoffs occur in the process?
65. How is ownership transferred from one team to another?
66. How are communications with landlords tracked across email, CRM, and project management tools?
67. What information tends to get lost or repeated during handoffs?

## 12. Timing, Bottlenecks, and Success Measures

68. How long does the full onboarding process usually take from first contact to activation?
69. How long does it usually take from activation to tenant move-in?
70. Which steps create the most delays?
71. What service-level targets or deadlines exist for each stage?
72. How do you measure whether onboarding was successful?
73. What are the most common pain points for landlords during onboarding?
74. What are the most common pain points for internal teams?
75. If you could improve one part of the onboarding process, which part would it be and why?
