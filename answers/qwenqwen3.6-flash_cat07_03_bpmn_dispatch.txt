**Overview**
This is a Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagram titled **"Dispatch of goods"** for a **"Computer Hardware Shop."** It maps out the workflow involved in sending an order to a customer, divided into three distinct "swimlanes" representing different departments or roles: **Logistics**, **Secretary**, and **Warehouse**.

**Process Flow**
The process begins in the **Warehouse** lane with a start event labeled **"Ship goods."**

1.  **Initial Split:** The process immediately splits into two parallel paths using a Parallel Gateway (a diamond with a plus sign):
    *   **Path A (Top):** Moves to the **Secretary** lane to handle administrative and logistical tasks.
    *   **Path B (Bottom):** Stays in the **Warehouse** lane to handle physical packaging.

2.  **The Secretary's Path (Top Section):**
    *   The Secretary first **"Clarifies shipment method."**
    *   There is a decision point (Exclusive Gateway, marked with an 'X'): **"Special handling?"**
        *   **If Yes:** The process moves to **"Get 3 offers from logistic companies,"** followed by **"Select logistic company and place order."**
        *   **If No:** The process moves to **"Check if insurance is required."** This step utilizes "Customer information" (database icon) and "Order details" (document icon).
            *   **If insurance is necessary:** The flow moves up to the **Logistics** lane to **"Insure parcel,"** then loops back down.
            *   **Always:** The flow moves to **"Write package label."**
    *   The paths for selecting the company and writing the label eventually converge.

3.  **The Warehouse's Path (Bottom Section):**
    *   The warehouse staff begins by **"Package goods."**
    *   There is a Timer Intermediate Event (clock icon) labeled **"48 hours."** If the process takes this long, it triggers a task to **"Notify customer about delay."**
    *   The packaging and notification paths merge.

4.  **Re-convergence and Final Steps:**
    *   The administrative work (from the Secretary lane) and the physical work (from the Warehouse lane) meet at a Parallel Gateway. This ensures both the logistics are arranged and the goods are packaged before moving forward.
    *   The process continues with **"Fill out Shipment paperwork."**
    *   Next is **"Move package to pickup area."**
    *   The process concludes with a double-circle End Event labeled **"Shipment prepared."**
