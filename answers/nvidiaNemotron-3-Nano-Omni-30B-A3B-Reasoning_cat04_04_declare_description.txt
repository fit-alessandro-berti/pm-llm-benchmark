## 1.  Receive the Application  

| Constraint | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| **existence** – *Receive_Application* | The process can only start when a loan request is actually received. |
| **init** – *Receive_Application* | This is the *initial* activity; the model marks it as the first (and only) entry point. |

*Why it matters*:  Without a formal intake the whole system has no basis to act.  The “existence” rule guarantees that a request is present before any downstream activity can be considered.

---

## 2.  Preliminary Credit Check  

| Constraint | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| **exactly_one** – *Preliminary_Credit_Check* | There must be **exactly one** creditcheck step; no duplicate or missing checks. |
| **response** – *Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check* | The credit check is a direct *response* to the receipt of the application. |
| **succession** – *Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents* | After the credit assessment the workflow *succeds* to the next activity (document collection). |

*Why it matters*:  The credit check is the first riskassessment activity.  Enforcing “exactly_one” prevents the model from allowing multiple, contradictory checks, and the “response” link ties the check to the original request, ensuring it cannot be skipped.

---

## 3.  Gather Additional Documents  

| Constraint | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| **existence** – *Gather_Additional_Documents* | The process cannot move forward without collecting the required supporting documents. |
| **succession** – *Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents* | Documents are gathered **after** the credit check, not before. |
| **altsuccession** – *Gather_Additional_Documents  Quality_Assurance_Review* | The documents must be ready before the qualityassurance review can be performed. |
| **coexistence** – *Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms* | The documentgathering step can exist simultaneously with the later authorization step, but it must be completed before authorization. |

*Why it matters*:  Regulatory rules often require proof of income, identity, etc.  Gathering these after the credit check ensures that the risk assessment is based on a complete picture, while still allowing the later “coexistence” with authorization (the two steps can be parallelised in a real system).

## 6.  Authorize Contract Terms  

| Constraint | Meaning |

*Why it matters*:  Communicating the decision promptly improves customer experience and satisfies regulatory expectations for transparency.  The “altprecedence” and “nonsuccession” links ensure that the customer is only told about a valid, preapproved loan, preventing premature or misleading information.

*Why it matters*:  The “noncoexistence” rule is a **forbidden** activity; it guarantees that no money leaves the bank unless a legitimate request is on file.  The “chainresponse” and “altresponse” links tie the transfer to the completion of the package and the customer’s notification, ensuring that the disbursement is the final, welldocumented step.

# RealWorld Motivations Behind the Rules  

| Motivation | How the model reflects it |
|------------|---------------------------|
| **Regulatory compliance** (e.g., consumercredit laws, antimoneylaundering) | *Proceed_Without_Compliance* is forbidden; *noncoexistence* blocks transfers before a request exists; *precedence* forces QA before authorization – all are typical regulatory checkpoints. |
| **Fraud prevention** | *Preliminary_Credit_Check* and *Gather_Additional_Documents* must occur in the prescribed order, ensuring the lender verifies identity and creditworthiness before any money moves. |
| **Risk management** | Sequential steps (credit check  documents  QA  authorization) give the lender multiple “safety nets” to stop a risky loan before it is funded. |
| **Operational efficiency** | *coexistence* and *chain* constraints let parallel activities (e.g., document collection and later authorization) happen without breaking the logical order, reducing bottlenecks. |
| **Customer satisfaction** | Timely *Notify_Customer* steps (after approval, before funding) keep the borrower informed, improving trust and reducing support calls. |
| **Auditability** | Every activity is linked to a predecessor or successor, making the process traceable for internal audits or regulator reviews. |

# What Happens If the Order Is Broken or a Mandatory Step Is Omitted?  

| Violation | Likely Consequence |
|-----------|--------------------|
| **Skipping the Preliminary Credit Check** (e.g., proceeding straight to document collection) | High risk of default, potential regulatory breach (creditassessment rules), and possible financial loss for the lender. |
| **Gathering documents before the credit check** | Wasted resources; the lender may spend time on paperwork for an applicant who fails the credit assessment, increasing operational cost. |
| **Performing Transfer_Funds before Quality_Assurance_Review** | Violates *precedence* and *noncoexistence*; the bank could disburse money on an unvetted offer, exposing it to compliance penalties, reputational damage, and possible legal action. |
| **Notifying the customer before the credit check** (breaking *nonsuccession*) | The customer receives misleading information (e.g., “approved” when the credit check has not yet passed), leading to confusion, possible disputes, and regulatory violations. |
| **Authorizing contract terms before the QA review** (breaking *precedence*) | The loan offer may contain errors, noncompliant terms, or missing disclosures, resulting in customer complaints, possible fines, and the need to reprocess the loan. |
| **Transferring funds before the application exists** (breaking *noncoexistence*) | Illegal disbursement – the bank would be funding a nonexistent loan, a clear breach of law and a serious financial risk. |
| **Multiple credit checks** (violating *exactly_one*) | Inconsistent risk assessments, potential for contradictory decisions, and audittrail confusion. |

In each case the model’s constraints act as **guardrails** that keep the workflow inside the bounds of law, risk tolerance, and operational best practice.
