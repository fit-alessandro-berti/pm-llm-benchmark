### 2. Potential Root Causes of Performance Issues

By breaking down the timelines into **inter-activity durations** and activity sequences, clear patterns emerge. All delays trace back to two key factors: **escalations** and **long waiting periods** (often overnight or during business hours). No unnecessary loops or redundant steps appear, but escalations amplify waits.

**Root Causes Identified**:
- **Escalations to Level-2 (in 102 & 105)**: Present in 2/3 delayed cases. These introduce long handoff waits (e.g., 2.5 hrs in 102, multi-day in 105) and often lead to overnight delays before Level-2 investigation/resolution.
- **Long Waiting Times Between Activities**:
  - Pre-investigation waits: 3.5 hrs (104), 2.5 hrs (102).
  - Post-escalation waits: Up to ~27 hrs (105) and ~19 hrs (102).
  - Overnight gaps: Universal in delayed cases (e.g., resolution pushed to next business day).
- **No Escalation Delay in 104**: Still delayed due to isolated Level-1 bottlenecks (long wait to investigate), suggesting agent availability or workload issues.
- **Time-of-Day Effect**: Tickets arrive ~08:00–08:25. Delays cluster mid-day (11:30–14:00) and spill into non-business hours, implying limited after-hours support.

### 3. How Factors Increase Cycle Times + Recommendations

#### Impact on Cycle Times:
- **Escalations Amplify Delays**: They add ~sequential waits** (handoff + Level-2 backlog), turning ~2-hr cases into 24–49-hr marathons. In 105, escalation effectively *tripled* the cycle time via compounded waits.
- **Waiting Periods Create Bottlenecks**: ~60–80% of delay time is *idle* (e.g., 104's 3.5-hr pre-investigation wait = 14% of total cycle; 105's post-escalation gaps = ~90%). Overnights force artificial "pauses," inflating totals by 20+ hrs.
- **Cumulative Effect**: Without escalations/waits, median time is ~1.5–2 hrs (like 101/103). Escalations + waits push outliers to 10–25x longer, hurting SLAs and customer satisfaction.

#### Insights & Recommendations:
- **Insight**: Escalations are a **primary driver** (100% of multi-day cases), but even non-escalated cases (104) suffer from Level-1 capacity issues. Process is efficient *until* handoff or backlog hits.
- **Prioritize Anti-Escalation Training**: Analyze ticket metadata (not in log) for common escalation triggers (e.g., complex issues). Train Level-1 agents on 80% of cases to reduce escalations by 30–50%.
- **Improve Handoff Speed**:
  - Set SLAs: Escalate within 30 min of assignment; Level-2 investigate within 1 hr.
  - Implement on-call rotations for after-hours to eliminate overnights.
- **Agent Capacity & Queue Management**:
  - For non-escalated delays (like 104): Monitor Level-1 workload; add dynamic assignment (e.g., auto-route based on agent load).
  - Dashboard for "stuck" tickets (e.g., >1 hr post-assignment).
- **Proactive Monitoring**: Use this log as baseline — flag cases exceeding 4 hrs total time. Pilot AI triage to predict escalations early.
- **Expected Impact**: These could cut average cycle time to <5 hrs, with no case >24 hrs, by halving waits and escalations.

This analysis pinpoints actionable bottlenecks without needing more data.
