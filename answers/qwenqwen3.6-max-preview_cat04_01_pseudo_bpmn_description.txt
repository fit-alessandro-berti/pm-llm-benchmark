###  **1. Process Overview**
This is a **customer request fulfillment process** that handles both standard and custom orders. It encompasses request intake, validation, parallel risk/availability checks, feasibility assessment, managerial approval, invoicing, and customer confirmation. The process is designed to be **path-aware**, meaning certain steps, loops, and decisions adapt based on whether the request is standard or custom.

###  **2. Step-by-Step Purpose**
| Step | Business Purpose |
|------|------------------|
| **Task A: Receive Customer Request** | Entry point. Captures order details, customer data, and request type. |
| **Task B1: Perform Standard Validation** | Applies predefined business rules (e.g., format checks, pricing tiers, policy compliance) for routine requests. |
| **Task C1: Credit Check** | Assesses customer payment risk or credit limit availability. |
| **Task C2: Inventory Check** | Verifies stock availability or supply chain capacity for requested items. |
| **Task D: Calculate Delivery Date** | Synthesizes results from credit/inventory checks to determine a realistic fulfillment timeline. |
| **Task B2: Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis** | Engineering/sales assessment of non-standard requirements (technical viability, resource allocation, cost impact). |
| **Task E1: Prepare Custom Quotation** | Drafts tailored pricing, terms, and delivery expectations for feasible custom requests. |
| **Task E2: Send Rejection Notice** | Formal communication to the customer when customization cannot be fulfilled. Terminates the process. |
| **Task F: Obtain Manager Approval** | Escalation step for requests that exceed automated processing thresholds (e.g., high value, high risk, or custom terms). |
| **Task H: Re-evaluate Conditions** | Rework step triggered by approval denial. Adjusts pricing, delivery, scope, or risk mitigations before resubmission. |
| **Task G: Generate Final Invoice** | Creates the official billing document once all validations, checks, and approvals are satisfied. |
| **Task I: Send Confirmation to Customer** | Final communication confirming order acceptance, invoice details, and delivery/fulfillment timeline. |
