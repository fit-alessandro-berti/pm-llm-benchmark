### Rationale for Added Constraints:

1. **Added `ManualReview` to `existence`**: Ensures that the fairness checkpoint activity is always recorded in the process, making it visible and auditable.

2. **Added `coexistence` constraints between sensitive-decision activities and `ManualReview`**: For any decision (Approve/Reject) targeting a protected demographic (e.g., `Reject_Minority`), a `ManualReview` must occur in the same trace. This prevents automated biased decisions by mandating human oversight for high-risk outcomes.

3. **Added `response` constraints from sensitive attribute checks to `BiasMitigationCheck`**: Forces a structured fairness checkpoint (`BiasMitigationCheck`) to follow any detection of sensitive attributes (e.g., race, gender, age). This ensures that bias mitigation procedures are triggered before any decision is made.

4. **Added `succession` from `BiasMitigationCheck` to `FinalDecision`**: Only after a bias mitigation step can the final decision be made. This blocks direct, unreviewed transitions from sensitive data to final outcomes.

5. **Added `nonsuccession` constraints from sensitive checks to `Reject`**: Prevents the direct, unmediated transition from detecting a sensitive attribute (e.g., `CheckApplicantRace`) to a negative decision (`Reject`). This mitigates “immediate bias” patterns often seen in discriminatory systems.

6. **Added `nonchainsuccession` constraints for refined demographic decisions**: Even if the process uses fine-grained activity names like `Reject_Minority`, direct chaining from the corresponding check (`CheckApplicantRace`) is still prohibited. This ensures that even labeled or “coded” biased decisions are not allowed without intervention.

### Overall Impact:
These constraints collectively enforce a **fairness-by-design** principle:  
- Sensitive attributes are not ignored but are **logged and managed**.  
- Any decision impacting protected groups **requires mandatory review**.  
- Biased pathways (e.g., race  reject) are **structurally blocked**.  

This transforms the loan application process from one that may *inadvertently* discriminate into one that is **auditably fair**, aligning with regulatory standards like GDPR and Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA).
