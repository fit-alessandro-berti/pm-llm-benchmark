The visualization is a **process/behavioral diagram** (similar to a BPMN-style or workflow net depiction) showing how a case moves through a set of **activities** and **control-flow constructs**.

### Main process flow (left/top)
- A start element **“a”** labeled **register request** feeds into multiple alternative/parallel paths.
- Several tasks are shown as rounded boxes with labels:
  - **b: examine thoroughly**
  - **c: examine causally**
  - **d: check ticket**
  - **e: decide**
  - **f: reinitiate request**
  - **g: pay compensation**
  - **h: reject request**
  - **z: end**
- The flow includes **branching** toward different examination/check activities, which then converge into **e: decide**.
- From **e**, the process proceeds either to:
  - **g: pay compensation** and then to **z: end**, or
  - **h: reject request**, which leads back/into the flow toward completion (via the connections shown) and ultimately to **z**.

### Circular/curved edges
- The many **curved connections** indicate **loops or rework**—e.g., after certain checks or decisions the process can go back to earlier activities like **f (reinitiate request)**.

### Embedded legend (bottom right)
The grey legend box explains the **control-flow gateway semantics**:
- **XOR-split / XOR-join**: choose **one** outgoing/incoming path.
- **AND-split / AND-join**: **fork into multiple** tasks and later **synchronize** (all paths).
- **OR-split / OR-join**: may activate **one or more** paths, and later completes when the allowed set is satisfied.
It also uses patterns of black dots to depict the number of branches/tokens.

Overall, the diagram models a request lifecycle with **examination and decision steps**, including **retries (looping)** and **conditional/parallel routing** handled by XOR/AND/OR constructs, ending at **“z: end.”**
