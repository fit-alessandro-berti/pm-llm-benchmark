This diagram represents a detailed process flow for different types of order and return handling within an enterprise system, broken down into several key components and stages.

### Overview:
The diagram is divided into three main sections based on different item types:
1. **Purchase Requisition Items (left)**
2. **Sales Order Items (middle-right)**
3. **Purchase Order Items (right)**

Each section starts with an initiation step and proceeds through various stages, including approvals, processing, and finalization.

### Purchase Requisition Items (Left Section)
- **Start:** Initialization.
- **Create Requisition:** Generates a requisition with an overall object count of 3 million.
- **Approve Requisition:** Involves multiple approval steps, with a total of 5.7 million objects.
  - **Send Order:** After requisition approval, orders are sent out, with 5.2 million objects.
- **Check Stock:** Ensures sufficient stock availability (1 million objects).
- **Process Return:** If returns are needed, this stage handles them (0.8 million objects).
- **Send Order:** Post-checking stock, the order is sent again if necessary.
- **Create Invoice:** Involves creating invoices after payment approval (1.5 million objects).
- **End:** Final step after invoicing.

### Purchase Order Items (Right Section)
- **Start:** Initialization.
- **Create Order:** Generates purchase orders with 8.5 million objects.
- **Pack Goods:** Packing the ordered goods (2 million objects).
- **Ship Goods:** Shipping the packed goods (1.8 million objects).
- **Confirm Delivery:** Confirming that goods have been delivered (1.7 million objects).
- **Close Order:** Finalizing the order process (1.7 million objects).
- **End:** Final step after closing the order.

### Sales Order Items (Middle Section)
- **Start:** Initialization.
- **Create Order:** Generates sales orders with 3.5 million objects.
- **Pack Goods:** Packing the ordered goods (2 million objects).
- **Ship Goods:** Shipping the packed goods (1.8 million objects).
- **Confirm Delivery:** Confirming delivery of the goods (1.7 million objects).
- **Create Invoice:** Involves creating invoices (2 million objects).
- **Approve Payment:** Approving the payment (1.5 million objects).
- **End:** Final step after payment approval.

### Vendor Invoice Items (Bottom Left)
- **Start:** Initialization.
- **Send Order:** Sending orders to vendors (600ms delay).
- **End:** End of the process.

### Return Order Items (Middle Left)
- **Start:** Initialization.
- **Process Return:** Handling returns (0.8 million objects).
- **Approve Payment:** Approving payment for returns (1.5 million objects).
- **Create Invoice:** Creating invoices for returns.
- **End:** Final step after invoicing returns.

### Connections and Timings:
- Each step is connected with arrows indicating the flow and time delays (in milliseconds) between stages.
- The color coding differentiates between various stages and types of items (e.g., purple for requisition, blue for sales orders, etc.).
- The numbers next to each step indicate the overall object counts processed in that step.

### Summary:
The diagram illustrates the detailed workflows for handling purchase requisitions, sales orders, purchase orders, vendor invoices, and return orders, showing the sequence of steps, time delays, and object counts involved in each process.
