1. **Gatekeeping & Audit Trails:** 
   Constraints like `precedence(E, F)` and `responded_existence(D, E)` create mandatory checkpoints. 2. **Temporal Integrity & Anti-Tampering:**
   `chain*` constraints enforce immediate succession. In financial systems, this reduces the window for manual intervention, data manipulation, or unauthorized parallel processing. It also creates a clean, timestamped audit trail where handoffs are atomic and traceable.

3. **Mutual Exclusion & Risk Containment:**
   `noncoexistence(G, A)` and `absence(Z)` act as hard stop-losses. They prevent catastrophic compliance failures (e.g., disbursing funds without an application, or bypassing compliance protocols). 4. **Flexibility Within Boundaries:**
   DECLARE doesn't dictate *how* documents are gathered or *who* performs QA. It only mandates *that* they happen, in the correct order, with no forbidden steps. This allows human agents or automated systems to adapt to exceptions (e.g., parallel doc collection, system retries) while staying within regulatory guardrails.
