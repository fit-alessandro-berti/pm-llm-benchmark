## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

### **Critical Interaction Examples**

**Priority + Resource Contention:**
When an express order requiring Cold-Packing arrives, it may preempt a standard order using that resource. This creates a cascading effect:
1. The preempted standard order waits
2. Other orders queue behind the express order
3. Cold-Packing resource utilization becomes even more skewed toward express orders
4. Overall system throughput may decrease despite faster express processing

**Batching + Hazardous Material Limits:**
If multiple hazardous orders are destined for the same region:
1. They may be batched together to optimize shipping
2. But batching increases the number of hazardous orders in "Packing" or "Quality Check" simultaneously
3. This could trigger the 10-order limit, causing delays for all orders in the batch
4. The batch optimization for shipping efficiency conflicts with regulatory compliance

**Cold-Packing + Priority Handling:**
Express orders requiring cold-packing create a double constraint:
1. They consume scarce Cold-Packing resources
2. They receive priority treatment, potentially blocking standard cold-packing orders
3. This can lead to starvation of standard orders needing cold-packing

### **Importance of Understanding Interactions**

These interactions create non-linear system behavior where optimizing one constraint in isolation can worsen overall performance. For example, creating separate queues for express and standard orders at Cold-Packing stations might seem logical, but if express orders consistently dominate, standard orders could face unacceptable delays. Understanding these dynamics is essential for developing holistic optimization strategies.
