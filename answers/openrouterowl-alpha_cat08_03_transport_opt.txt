**Comprehensive Process Mining Approach for Optimizing Speedy Parcels’ Last-Mile Delivery Operations**

As a Process Mining Consultant specializing in logistics, I will outline a structured, data-driven approach to diagnose inefficiencies and recommend targeted improvements for Speedy Parcels using their integrated event log data. The analysis leverages process mining principles—discovery, conformance, and enhancement—to transform raw operational data into actionable insights.

---

### **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### **Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To build a coherent event log suitable for process mining:
- **Case Identification**: Define two complementary case concepts:
  - *Vehicle-day level* (e.g., `V12-20241205`) for analyzing overall route execution.
  - *Package-level* (`Package ID`) for tracking end-to-end delivery success/failure.
- **Event Enrichment**: Merge GPS pings (location, speed, timestamps) with scanner events (delivery status), dispatch metadata (planned stops, time windows), and maintenance logs (via Vehicle ID).
- **Timestamp Alignment**: Normalize all timestamps to a common timezone and ensure consistent formatting.
- **Attribute Mapping**: Augment each event with contextual attributes:
  - Planned vs. actual stop sequence
  - Distance between consecutive locations (via GIS APIs)
  - Traffic context (e.g., urban/suburban zone, road type)
  - Delivery outcome (`Success`, `Failed`, `Rescheduled`)

**Challenges**:
- **Temporal Granularity Mismatch**: GPS data may be high-frequency (~every 10 sec), while scanner events are sparse. Solution: Aggregate GPS segments between scanner events into *travel episodes*.
- **Cross-System Linkage**: Ensure `Vehicle ID`, `Driver ID`, and `Package ID` are consistently mapped across systems. Use fuzzy matching on timestamps/locations if direct IDs are missing.
- **Incomplete Sequences**: Missing scans due to device errors. Apply heuristic imputation (e.g., infer “Depart Customer” if vehicle moves >1 km/h within 3 min of “Arrive Customer”).

#### **Process Discovery**
Apply **Alpha Miner** or **Heuristic Miner** to discover the actual end-to-end process model from the integrated log. The resulting model will reveal:
- Core flow: `Depart Depot  [Travel  Arrive Customer  (Delivery Success/Failed)  Depart Customer]*  Arrive Depot  End Shift`
- Exception paths:
  - Unscheduled maintenance stops (`Unscheduled Stop  Delay  Resume Route`)
  - Failed deliveries leading to re-attempts or returns to depot
  - Extended dwell times (>10 min at customer)

Visualization via **directly-follows graphs (DFGs)** will highlight frequent loops (e.g., repeated failed attempts at certain postcodes) and parallel activities (e.g., GPS idle between deliveries).

#### **Conformance Checking**
Align the discovered model with the **planned route graph** from the dispatch system using **token-based replay** or **alignment-based conformance checking**.
- **Deviation Types to Investigate**:
  - *Sequence Deviations*: Deliveries not executed in planned order (e.g., due to traffic or driver discretion).
  - *Unplanned Activities*: Maintenance stops, unscheduled breaks, or detours (GPS-derived).
  - *Timing Gaps*: Actual arrival >30 min beyond planned time window.
  - *Missing Events*: No scan after vehicle departure (potential skipped stop).

Quantify fitness (conformance score <1 indicates deviations) and identify vehicles/drivers with persistent non-conformance.

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
Derivable from the enriched event log:
| KPI | Calculation |
|-----|-------------|
| **On-Time Delivery Rate** | % of packages delivered within customer-requested time window |
| **Avg. Service Time per Stop** | Mean duration between `Arrive Customer` and `Depart Customer` |
| **Travel-to-Service Time Ratio** | Total travel time / Total service time per route |
| **Fuel Cost per Package** | (Distance × Fuel rate per km) / # delivered packages |
| **Vehicle Utilization Rate** | (Active shift time – idle time) / Total shift duration |
| **Failed Delivery Attempt Rate** | # Failed deliveries / # Total delivery attempts |
| **Traffic Delay Frequency** | # of `Low Speed Detected` episodes lasting >5 min per zone/time slot |

#### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
- **Dwell Time Analysis**: Use scatter plots of service time vs. location/driver/time-of-day. High variance in downtown areas suggests parking/search inefficiencies.
- **Queue Mining**: Identify states where vehicles idle excessively (e.g., waiting at depot due to late dispatch).
- **Social Network Analysis**: Map driver-stop transition frequencies to spot over-reliance on specific drivers for problematic routes.
- **Heatmap Correlation**: Overlay GPS speed drops with urban zones to pinpoint traffic bottlenecks (e.g., 8–9 AM in Zone X).

*Impact Quantification*:  
Example: If 22% of total shift time is spent idling (vs. 5% benchmark), this directly correlates with +18% fuel waste and -15% on-time rate.

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Using **variant analysis** and **attribute-based segmentation**, isolate root causes:

| Suspected Root Cause | Process Mining Validation Approach |
|----------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Static Routing** | Compare variants: routes adhering strictly to plan vs. those deviating show 12% better on-time rates. |
| **Inaccurate ETAs** | Correlate `Low Speed Detected` events with actual vs. planned travel time; compute MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error). |
| **Traffic Congestion** | Cluster delays by time window and geofence; validate against external traffic APIs. |
| **Variable Service Times** | Segment dwell times by customer type (residential vs. commercial); identify outliers (>75th percentile). |
| **Vehicle Breakdowns** | Cross-tab `Unscheduled Stop` events with maintenance logs; calculate MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures). |
| **Driver Skill Gaps** | Compare KPI distributions across drivers using ANOVA; top performers show 25% less idle time. |
| **Failed Deliveries** | Analyze `Delivery Failed` events: 68% occur at addresses with no safe drop-off location (notes field). |

*Advanced Technique*: Apply **decision points** in the process model (e.g., after “Arrive Customer”) to test if adding buffer time reduces downstream delays.

### **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Route Re-sequencing Using Real-Time Traffic + Historical Patterns**
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Unplanned delays due to traffic.
- **Root Cause**: Static routes ignore real-time conditions.
- **Process Mining Insight**: GPS/speed data reveals recurring congestion hotspots (e.g., Route A delays 3x on Fridays 4–6 PM).
- **Implementation**: Integrate live traffic feeds with a re-sequencing engine that shuffles non-time-critical stops.
- **Expected Impact**: 
  -  On-Time Delivery Rate by 15%
  -  Fuel Consumption by 8% (less idling)

#### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance Scheduling Based on Usage Intensity**
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Unscheduled stops disrupting routes.
- **Root Cause**: Maintenance triggered only after failure.
- **Process Mining Insight**: Vehicles with >90% utilization have 4x more engine warnings.
- **Implementation**: Build a regression model: `Breakdown Risk = f(mileage, idle time, age)`. Schedule proactive servicing during low-demand days.
- **Expected Impact**: 
  -  Unscheduled Stops by 70%
  -  Vehicle Utilization by 10%

#### **Strategy 3: Smart Failed Delivery Prevention via Customer Engagement**
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Wasted trips and re-delivery costs.
- **Root Cause**: Customer not home.
- **Process Mining Insight**: 80% of failures occur at addresses without porch/locker; SMS reminders reduce failures by 30% in trials.
- **Implementation**: Pre-delivery SMS/email with opt-in for rescheduling or safe-place instructions.
- **Expected Impact**: 
  -  Failed Deliveries by 35%
   Indirect  in On-Time Rate (fewer re-attempts clogging routes)

- **Time Windows**: Prioritize stops with narrow windows in sequencing algorithms.
