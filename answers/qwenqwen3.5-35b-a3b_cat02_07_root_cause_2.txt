### 3. Explanations and Mitigation Strategies

#### Root Cause 1: Iterative Documentation Loops
**Explanation:** High complexity claims require more data. Currently, the process waits for the customer to provide documents before moving to the next stage. If the initial request is incomplete, the case loops back, adding days to the cycle time. Case 2005 shows three loops, indicating the initial request was likely very unclear or incomplete.

**Mitigation:**
*   **Checklist Automation:** Implement a dynamic checklist for `Evaluate Claim` based on the claim type/complexity. Ensure all required documents are requested in the *first* notification to the customer.
*   **SLA Alerts:** Set up alerts for cases that enter the `Request Additional Documents` state more than once. Investigate why the first request failed to capture the needed data.

#### Root Cause 2: Regional/Resource Variance (Adjuster Performance)
**Explanation:** Adjuster_Lisa (Region B) generates more document requests for High Complexity cases than Adjuster_Mike (Region A). This suggests a variance in skill, training, or interpretation of "High Complexity" requirements between regions or individuals.

**Mitigation:**
*   **Benchmarking:** Compare the average number of document requests per High Complexity case between Adjuster_Lisa and Adjuster_Mike.
*   **Training:** If Adjuster_Lisa's request rate is significantly higher, provide targeted training on initial data gathering for Region B to align with Region A's efficiency.
*   **Resource Balancing:** If Adjuster_Lisa is consistently slower due to workload, distribute High Complexity cases more evenly across available adjusters in Region B.

#### Root Cause 3: Lack of Straight-Through Processing (STP)
**Explanation:** Low Complexity cases are processed quickly because they likely do not require human intervention beyond the initial evaluation. High Complexity cases force human review at every step.

**Mitigation:**
*   **Triage System:** Implement a stronger triage mechanism at `Submit Claim`. Use rules to auto-approve Low Complexity claims if they meet all criteria, skipping the manual `Evaluate` and `Request` steps entirely.
*   **Customer Portal:** Provide a customer portal where clients can upload documents immediately upon submission, reducing the lag time between `Request` and `Approve`.

### Summary Recommendation
The immediate priority is to address the **iterative document requests** in High Complexity cases, specifically focusing on **Region B**. By improving the quality of the *initial* document request, the number of loops can be reduced, significantly decreasing the duration from ~77 hours (Case 2005) to closer to the ~48-hour baseline of Case 2003.
