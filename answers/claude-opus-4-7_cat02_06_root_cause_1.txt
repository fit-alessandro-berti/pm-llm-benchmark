---

## 2. Root Causes of Delay

### A. Escalation to Level-2 Agent (Cases 102 & 105)
- **Case 102:** After assignment at 09:00, the ticket waits **2.5 hours** before being escalated (11:30), then another **2.5 hours** before Level-2 starts investigating (14:00). Resolution takes until next day.
- **Case 105:** After the first investigation (09:10), it is escalated at 10:00, but the Level-2 agent only begins re-investigating at **14:00 the next day** — a **28-hour idle gap**.

 **Escalations introduce large handoff waiting times** between Level-1 and Level-2 teams.

### B. Long Delay Between Assignment and Investigation (Case 104)
- Assigned to Level-1 at 09:30, but investigation starts at **13:00** — a 3.5-hour wait.
- Once investigation begins, resolution doesn't occur until the **next morning (08:00)**, suggesting the ticket was left pending overnight.
- No escalation occurred, so the delay is purely due to **agent workload or unavailability**.

### C. Overnight/Next-Day Resolutions
- Cases 102, 104, and 105 all span overnight. This suggests tickets in progress at end-of-day are not picked up again until the next business day, possibly due to limited after-hours coverage.

### D. Slow Triage for Some Cases
- Case 104 takes **40 minutes** from receipt to triage, vs. 5–10 minutes for fast cases. Early-stage delays cascade into the rest of the workflow.

---

## 3. Impact & Recommendations

### Key Bottleneck Patterns
| Factor | Effect on Cycle Time |
|--------|----------------------|
| Escalation handoff wait | +2 to +28 hours |
| Level-2 investigation backlog | +1 full day |
| Agent unavailability post-assignment | +3 to +20 hours |
| Overnight rollover | Adds ~16 hours per crossover |

### Recommendations
1. **Reduce Level-2 handoff latency** — Introduce SLAs (e.g., Level-2 must start investigation within 1 hour of escalation). Case 105's 28-hour idle gap is the single biggest driver of delay.
2. **Improve triage accuracy to avoid re-work escalations** — If tickets likely requiring Level-2 expertise can be routed directly from triage, the Level-1  Level-2 handoff delay is eliminated.
3. **Balance Level-1 agent workload** — Case 104 shows excessive waiting even without escalation, suggesting capacity or queue-management issues at Level-1.
4. **Extend coverage or implement shift handoff procedures** — Prevent tickets from stalling overnight when investigation has already begun.
5. **Monitor triage time as a KPI** — Slow triage (Case 104) correlates with downstream delays.

### Summary
The slowest cases (102, 105) are all **escalated tickets**, confirming escalation as the primary performance risk. Case 104 shows that even non-escalated tickets suffer when there are **long idle gaps between assignment and investigation**. The core issue is **waiting time between activities**, not the work itself — addressing handoff delays and agent availability would yield the largest reductions in cycle time.
