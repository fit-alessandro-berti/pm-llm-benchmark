### 1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact  
#### Process Mining Approach:  
- **Shared Cold-Packing Stations**:**  
  - **Analysis:** Filter the event log for orders requiring cold-packing (via `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`). Identify the **waiting time** between the completion of `Item Picking` and the start of `Packing`. High variability or prolonged waiting times at `Packing` stations indicate resource contention.  
  - **Metrics:**  
    - **Cold-Packing Queue Time:** Time between `Item Picking` completion and `Packing` start for cold orders.  
    - **Resource Utilization:** % of time cold-packing stations are occupied.  
  - **Between-Instance Impact:** Compare cold-packing waiting times to non-cold orders to isolate between-instance delays (e.g., delays caused by sharing limited resources).  

- **Shipping Batching:**  
  - **Analysis:** Group orders by `Destination Region` and track the time between `Quality Check` completion and `Shipping Label Gen.` start. Batches that form slowly (e.g., waiting for more orders to accumulate) increase latency.  
  - **Metrics:**  
    - **Batch Formation Time:** Time taken to form a batch for a region.  
    - **Batch Size Distribution:** Histogram of batch sizes to identify underfilled or oversized batches.  
  - **Between-Instance Impact:** Orders are delayed waiting for others in their region to complete earlier steps.  

- **Priority Handling:**  
  - **Analysis:** Compare end-to-end cycle times for `Express` vs. `Standard` orders. Express orders should have shorter cycles, but if delays spike during peak times, it indicates priority overrides causing backlogs for standard orders.  
  - **Metrics:**  
    - **Priority Overhead:** Time standard orders are paused due to express order preemption.  
    - **Express Order Throughput:** # of express orders processed vs. their allocated SLAs.  
  - **Between-Instance Impact:** Standard orders may experience abrupt pauses or delays when preempted.  

- **Hazardous Material Limits:**  
  - **Analysis:** Track concurrent orders in `Packing`/`Quality Check` with `Hazardous Material = TRUE`. Exceeding 10 simultaneous instances triggers delays.  
  - **Metrics:**  
    - **Hazardous Overload Incidents:** # of times the 10-limit was breached (via log analysis).  
    - **Hazardous Waiting Time:** Delay added to hazardous orders when the limit is reached.  
  - **Between-Instance Impact:** Orders wait in a queue until slots free up.  

#### Differentiating Within vs. Between-Instance Delays:  
- **Within-Instance:** The difference between the `Packing` activity𠏋 total duration (e.g., `Packing COMPLETE` - `Packing START`) versus the *maximum possible service time* (based on historical averages). Excess delays here signal between-instance issues.  
- **Between-Instance:** Subtract the within-instance service time from the total cycle time between activities to isolate waiting time caused by shared resources or batching.

### 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions  
- **Cold-Packing + Priority Handling:** Express orders using cold-packing may monopolize stations, causing backups for standard orders. For example, if 5 cold-stations exist and 3 are preempted by express orders, standard orders wait longer.  
- **Batching + Hazardous Limits:** A batch with many hazardous orders might block other hazardous orders from entering `Packing`/`QC`, creating cascading delays.  
- **Priority Handling + Hazardous Limits:** An express order requiring cold-packing might displace a hazardous order, freeing up a slot but violating the 10-limit if not monitored.  

These interactions require **multi-objective optimization**, as improving one constraint (e.g., prioritizing express orders) might worsen another (e.g., exceeding hazardous limits).

### 3. Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies  
#### Strategy 1: **Dynamic Cold-Packing Resource Allocation with Hazardous Thresholds**  
- **Constraints Addressed:** Shared Cold-Packing, Priority Handling, Hazardous Limits.  
- **Proposed Changes:**  
  - Implement a priority queue for cold-packing stations, with express orders getting priority only if the total hazardous orders in `Packing`/`QC` is <10.  
  - Use real-time dashboards to monitor the hazardous count and dynamically restrict express orders if the limit is near.  
- **Data-Driven Basis:** Historical demand for cold-packing and hazardous orders can predict peak times to stagger express order processing.  
- **Outcome:** Reduces cold-packing queue times for express orders while avoiding hazardous overloads.  

#### Strategy 2: **Adaptive Batching with Hazardous-Aware Triggers**  
- **Constraints Addressed:** Batching, Hazardous Limits.  
- **Proposed Changes:**  
  - Batches are formed when:  
    1. A region𠏋 orders reach a threshold (e.g., 10 items) **and** the hazardous count in the batch does not exceed 5 (to leave room for other hazardous orders in other activities).  
    2. A time threshold (e.g., 15 minutes) to avoid indefinite waiting.  
  - Batches are flagged as "hazardous-heavy" and processed in dedicated time slots.  
- **Data-Driven Basis:** Analyze historical batch sizes and hazardous material distribution per region to set thresholds.  
- **Outcome:** Reduces batching delays and ensures hazardous batches are processed without violating the 10-limit.  

#### Strategy 3: **Priority Handling with Preemption Limits**  
- **Constraints Addressed:** Priority Handling, Shared Resources.  
- **Proposed Changes:**  
  - Allow express orders to preempt standard orders but cap the number of preemptions per station (e.g., no more than 2 standard orders can be paused at a cold-packing station.  
  - Implement a "buffer" of 2 cold-packing stations reserved exclusively for standard orders during peak times.  
- **Data-Driven Basis:** Use event logs to identify peak preemption frequencies and queue lengths.  
- **Outcome:** Limits cascading delays caused by over-preemption while maintaining express order SLAs.
