### **Process Mining Consultancy Report: Speedy Parcels Optimization**

**To:** Speedy Parcels Management  
**From:** Process Mining Consultant  
**Subject:** Comprehensive Analysis and Optimization Strategy for Last-Mile Delivery Operations  

---

### **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### **1.1 Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To perform process mining, we must transform heterogeneous data sources into a unified event log.
*   **Unified Event Log Structure:**
    *   **Case ID:** `VehicleID-Date` (e.g., `V12-20241205`). This allows analysis of the daily operational process. For package-level analysis, a secondary log with `PackageID` as the case ID can be derived.
    *   **Event Attributes:** `Timestamp`, `Activity` (standardized from source-specific labels), `Vehicle`, `Driver`, `Package`, `Location`, `Speed`, `Status`.
    *   **Integration Strategy:**
        *   *GPS & Scanner Fusion:* Link GPS points to Scanner events using temporal proximity and location matching. GPS data provides continuous context (speed, idle) between scanner milestones.
        *   *Dispatch Mapping:* Map planned routes to the Case ID. Create a "Planned Sequence" attribute for each stop based on the dispatch data.
        *   *Maintenance Correlation:* Tag events with a `MaintenanceFlag` if they fall within the time windows of the maintenance logs for that vehicle.
*   **Challenges:**
        *   *Granularity Mismatch:* GPS is high-frequency; scanners are event-based. We must sample or aggregate GPS data to avoid overloading the mining engine while retaining traffic insights.
        *   *Data Quality:* Missing GPS signals in dense urban areas or scanner errors. We need imputation strategies and noise filtering (e.g., ignoring GPS points with zero speed for >5 mins that aren't scanner stops).
        *   *Case Splitting/Merging:* If a vehicle breaks down and a backup driver takes over, the case ID may need to be split or merged based on driver ID changes.

#### **1.2 Process Discovery**
*   **Algorithm:** Use the **Inductive Miner** or **Heuristic Miner** to handle the noise and loops common in logistics data.
*   **Model Visualization:** The discovered process model will likely reveal:
    *   *Core Flow:* `Start Shift`  `Depart Depot`  `Travel`  `Arrive Customer`  `Service`  `Depart Customer`  ...  `Return Depot`  `End Shift`.
    *   *Loops:* `Delivery Failed`  `Return Depot` or `Reroute`  `Retry Delivery`.
    *   *Divergence:* `Unscheduled Stop` (Maintenance) branching off the main flow.
    *   *Parallelism:* Multiple vehicles operating simultaneously (if analyzing at a fleet level).
*   **Activity Standardization:** Map raw activities to a standard taxonomy: `Travel`, `Service`, `Delay`, `Maintenance`, `Admin`.

#### **1.3 Conformance Checking**
*   **Reference Model:** Construct a reference model based on the **Dispatch System's planned routes**. This model includes the expected sequence of stops and time windows.
*   **Technique:** Use **Alignment-Based Conformance Checking**. This calculates the minimum cost to transform the actual log into the reference model.
*   **Deviation Analysis:**
    *   *Sequence Deviations:* Drivers visiting Stop B before Stop A despite the plan.
    *   *Skipped Activities:* Stops in the plan that have no corresponding scanner events.
    *   *Time Deviations:* Arrival at a stop significantly outside the customer time window or later than the planned arrival time.
    *   *Unplanned Activities:* Stops that exist in the log but not in the plan (e.g., maintenance, personal stops, or ad-hoc customer requests).
    *   *Fitness Score:* A global metric (0-100%) indicating how well the actual process adheres to the plan. Low fitness suggests systemic issues with routing or driver compliance.

---

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### **2.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
*   **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR):** `% of stops where `Arrive Customer` $\le$ `Customer-Requested End Time`.
*   **Average Service Time:** Mean duration of `Delivery Success` events (`Depart Customer` - `Arrive Customer`).
*   **Travel Efficiency Ratio:** `Total Travel Time` / `Total Shift Time`. High ratios may indicate excessive travel or low stop density.
*   **Fuel Consumption Proxy:** Aggregate `Idle Time` and low-speed events from GPS. Correlate with fuel costs if available.
*   **Failed Delivery Rate:** `% of packages marked `Delivery Failed`.
*   **Vehicle Utilization:** `Active Delivery Time` / `Shift Duration`.
*   **Rerouting Overhead:** Time/cost impact of unplanned deviations from the route.

#### **2.2 Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
*   **Performance Overlay:** Annotate the process model with average and maximum durations for each activity.
    *   *Insight:* Identify activities with high variance (e.g., `Service` time varies wildly between drivers or locations).
*   **Variant Analysis:** Cluster routes by outcome (e.g., "High OTDR" vs. "Low OTDR").
    *   *Insight:* Compare variants to see if specific paths, drivers, or vehicle types consistently perform better.
*   **Bottleneck Detection:**
    *   *Traffic Hotspots:* Correlate `Low Speed Detected` events with location coordinates. Identify specific intersections or zones causing delays.
    *   *Driver-Specific Bottlenecks:* Analyze service times by driver. A driver with consistently high service times may indicate skill gaps or inefficient scanning practices.
    *   *Time-of-Day Analysis:* Check if delays cluster during specific hours (e.g., morning rush vs. afternoon).
    *   *Quantification:* Use statistical hypothesis testing to determine if observed delays in specific routes or by specific drivers are statistically significant compared to the fleet average.

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

#### **3.1 Potential Root Causes & Mining Validation**
*   **Suboptimal Static Routing:**
    *   *Cause:* Dispatch uses static plans that don't account for real-time traffic or dynamic constraints.
    *   *Validation:* Conformance checking reveals frequent sequence deviations and time slippage. Variant analysis shows that routes adjusted for traffic (if any) perform better.
*   **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations:**
    *   *Cause:* Dispatch system underestimates travel times, leading to cascading delays.
    *   *Validation:* Compare `Planned Travel Time` vs. `Actual GPS Travel Time`. Calculate the mean absolute error. High error indicates poor estimation models.
*   **Traffic Congestion Patterns:**
    *   *Cause:* Specific corridors are consistently congested.
    *   *Validation:* Heatmap analysis of GPS speed data overlaid with delay events. Correlation between `Speed < 10km/h` and `Late Arrival`.
*   **High Service Time Variability:**
    *   *Cause:* Inconsistent customer interactions or parking difficulties.
    *   *Validation:* Histogram of service times. If variance is high, check if it correlates with `Location Type` (e.g., residential vs. commercial) or `Parking Proximity` (inferred from GPS distance to scanner location).
*   **Vehicle Breakdowns/Maintenance:**
    *   *Cause:* Reactive maintenance leading to unscheduled downtime.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze `Unscheduled Stop` events. Correlate with vehicle age, mileage, or specific routes. Check if breakdowns occur after specific activity sequences.
*   **Failed Deliveries:**
    *   *Cause:* Customers unavailable during wide time windows or poor communication.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze `Delivery Failed` timestamps. If failures cluster in specific time windows, it suggests window management issues. Check if re-delivery adds significant cost.

#### **3.2 Advanced Mining Techniques**
*   **Attribution Analysis:** Assign delay contributions to specific activities. E.g., "Route V12 was 45 minutes late; 20 mins due to traffic, 15 mins due to slow service at Stop 3, 10 mins due to deviation."
*   **Correlation Analysis:** Link driver attributes to performance. Do drivers with more experience have lower service times? Do younger vehicles have fewer maintenance stops?

### **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

#### **Strategy 1: Adaptive Routing Engine with Real-Time Traffic Integration**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Late deliveries due to traffic and static routing.
*   **Root Cause:** Dispatch plans are static and fail to react to congestion; travel time estimates are inaccurate.
*   **Process Mining Support:**
    *   Use discovered process models to identify "Traffic Hotspots" and typical deviation patterns.
    *   Use historical GPS data to build accurate travel time matrices between stops, replacing static estimates.
    *   Conformance analysis identifies routes that are most prone to deviation, prioritizing them for dynamic adjustment.
*   **Implementation:** Integrate process mining insights into the routing algorithm. Allow dynamic rerouting when GPS detects speed drops below thresholds in known hotspots.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *    On-Time Delivery Rate.
    *    Fuel consumption (avoiding idling in traffic).
    *    Driver satisfaction (fewer stressful delays).

#### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance & Driver Coaching Program**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** High maintenance costs and unscheduled downtime.
*   **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance; potential driver behaviors (harsh braking/idling) accelerating wear.
*   **Process Mining Support:**
    *   Analyze `Maintenance Logs` and `Unscheduled Stops` to predict failure probabilities based on vehicle usage patterns (e.g., high idle time, specific routes).
    *   Analyze GPS speed and acceleration data to identify aggressive driving behaviors correlated with maintenance events.
    *   Variant analysis to see if specific drivers cause more wear on specific vehicles.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Shift to predictive maintenance schedules based on usage intensity rather than fixed intervals.
    *   Develop a driver scorecard based on process mining metrics (efficiency, safety, adherence). Provide targeted training to low-performing drivers.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *    Maintenance costs.
    *    Unscheduled downtime.
    *    Vehicle lifespan.

#### **Strategy 3: Failed Delivery Reduction via Time Window Optimization**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** High rate of failed deliveries and re-delivery costs.
*   **Root Cause:** Customers unavailable; time windows may be too wide or ignored; lack of proactive communication.
*   **Process Mining Support:**
    *   Analyze `Delivery Failed` events to identify patterns (e.g., failures peak between 2 PM and 4 PM on Tuesdays).
    *   Correlate failure rates with `Customer-Requested Time Windows`. If customers request windows but are rarely home, the window might be unrealistic.
    *   Analyze service times for failed attempts vs. successful ones to quantify the "wasted" process time.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Use process insights to refine time window suggestions in the app (e.g., suggest narrower, higher-probability windows).
    *   Implement automated SMS/App notifications based on GPS proximity to alert customers.
    *   Optimize route sequencing to cluster re-deliveries efficiently.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *    Failed Delivery Rate.
    *    Operational costs (fewer re-delivery trips).
    *    Customer satisfaction.

*   **Vehicle Capacity:**
    *   *Constraint:* Vans have weight/volume limits.
    *   *Action:* Monitor `Load Factor` via process mining. If certain routes consistently exceed capacity, redistribute packages.
*   **Customer Time Windows:**
    *   *Constraint:* Hard/soft time windows for deliveries.
    *   *Action:* Conformance checking must prioritize time window adherence. Optimization strategies must treat time windows as hard constraints in routing algorithms.

    *   *Historical View:* Trend analysis of KPIs (OTDR, Fuel per km, Failed Rate).
*   **Key Metrics to Track:**
    *   *Process Fitness:* Percentage of routes adhering to the plan.
    *   *Performance Heatmaps:* Geographic visualization of delays and service times.
    *   *Driver Performance Index:* Composite score of efficiency, safety, and adherence.
    *   *Maintenance Health Score:* Predictive risk score for each vehicle.
*   **Alerting & Feedback Loop:**
    *   Configure alerts for anomalies (e.g., sudden spike in service time for a driver, unexpected maintenance events).
    *   Use the dashboard to review optimization effectiveness monthly. If KPIs regress, re-run process mining to identify new bottlenecks or changes in traffic patterns.
    *   *Closed-Loop:* Feed insights back into the dispatch and routing systems to continuously improve the reference model and planning algorithms.
