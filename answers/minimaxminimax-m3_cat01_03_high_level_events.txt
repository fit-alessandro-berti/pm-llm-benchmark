# Manufacturing Process: From Low-Level Events to High-Level Activities

## 1. Analysis Approach

After examining the event log, I identified **five high-level process steps** based on the following grouping criteria:

- **Temporal clustering** – Events that occur in quick succession with no significant gaps
- **Functional coherence** – Events contributing to a single logical purpose (e.g., preparing material, joining parts, treating surface)
- **Resource homogeneity** – Events performed by the same role or resource type (e.g., operator-driven vs. automated machine actions)
- **Process phase separation** – Natural breakpoints in the manufacturing workflow (e.g., when responsibility shifts from preparation to assembly to finishing)

## 2. Proposed High-Level Activities

###  **Step 1: Material Preparation**
**Purpose:** Get the raw component identified, positioned, and conditioned for further processing.

| Low-Level Event | Resource | Justification for Inclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Retrieve raw metal sheet | Operator A | First contact with raw material |
| Scan barcode | Operator A | Identifies and validates the part |
| Place metal sheet on table | Operator A | Positions for automation |
| Align metal sheet | Robot Arm #2 | Automated precision alignment |
| Preheat metal sheet | Heating Unit #1 | Conditions material for welding |

**Why grouped?** All events occur before any joining action and collectively transform a raw, unprocessed sheet into a ready-to-weld component. They form a self-contained "pre-processing" phase.

---

###  **Step 2: Component Assembly (Welding)**
**Purpose:** Join the metal sheet into a sub-assembly through welding.

| Low-Level Event | Resource | Justification for Inclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Pick up welding tool | Operator B | Tool acquisition marks start of assembly |
| Weld corner A | Operator B | First joining action |
| Weld corner B | Operator B | Completes the welding cycle |

**Why grouped?** Same operator, no interruption, and a clear handoff from preparation  joining. The act of *picking up the tool* is logically the start of the assembly task (not preparation, which is about the material).

---

###  **Step 3: In-Process Quality Inspection**
**Purpose:** Verify that the assembly step produced acceptable welds before moving on.

| Low-Level Event | Resource | Justification for Inclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Measure weld integrity | Quality Sensor #1 | Automated sensor check immediately after welding |

**Why grouped?** This is a measurement/verification activity with a different goal (validation) than the productive welding step. It is the first gate of quality control.

---

###  **Step 4: Surface Treatment (Coating)**
**Purpose:** Apply and cure a protective surface finish.

| Low-Level Event | Resource | Justification for Inclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Apply protective coating | Coating Unit #3 | Primary finishing action |
| Dry coating | Heating Unit #2 | Required follow-up curing step |

**Why grouped?** Both events together complete the coating process. Neither makes sense without the other (wet coating must be dried). They form a discrete finishing sub-process.

---

###  **Step 5: Final Quality Inspection**
**Purpose:** Human visual confirmation before the product is released.

| Low-Level Event | Resource | Justification for Inclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Visual check | Operator C | Manual, end-of-line verification |

**Why grouped?** A distinct, human-performed quality gate that closes the production sequence. Different resource type (human operator) and different intent (final release decision) than the earlier automated weld check.
