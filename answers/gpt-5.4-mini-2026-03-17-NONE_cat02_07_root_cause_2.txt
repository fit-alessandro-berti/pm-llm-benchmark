### 3) Explanation of why these attributes cause delays

## High complexity
High-complexity claims likely:
- Need more evidence and validation
- Trigger repeated document requests
- Require more review time before approval
- Possibly involve more coordination between adjuster and manager

This explains why:
- **2003** has two document requests
- **2005** has three document requests
- Both are delayed until all information is gathered

## Resource assignment
Some resources may:
- Be assigned the most difficult claims
- Spend more time on follow-ups and manual checks
- Experience workload buildup if multiple complex cases are routed to them

This can create the impression that the resource is slow, when in reality the case is simply more difficult.

## Region
Region may affect:
- Local regulations
- Different service levels
- Staffing availability
- Processing queues or time zones

But in this log, region does not show a strong standalone effect.

### 4) Suggested mitigations

## For complexity-related delays
- Introduce **triage rules** to flag high-complexity claims earlier
- Request all likely required documents upfront
- Use checklists to reduce repeated document requests
- Add automation for completeness checks before human review

## For resource-related issues
- Balance workload across adjusters and managers
- Route complex claims to experienced specialists
- Monitor individual resource queues and handoff delays
- Use performance dashboards by activity and resource

## For region-related issues
- Compare processing times across regions regularly
- Investigate whether one region has staffing shortages or policy differences
- Standardize approval rules and document requirements across regions where possible
