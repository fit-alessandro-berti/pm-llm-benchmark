### 2. Potential rootcause patterns

| Pattern | Where it appears | Why it inflates cycle time |
|---------|------------------|---------------------------|
| **Escalation to Level2** | 102,105 | Adds a handoff step and often forces the ticket to wait for a new agent’s availability. In both cases the “Investigate Issue” step after escalation does **not** start until many hours (or a full day) later. |
| **Long idle time before investigation** | 104 (AssignInvestigate: 3h30m), 102 (AssignEscalate: 2h30m), 105 (AssignEscalate: 1h10m, then after escalation  next investigation: 28h50m) | The ticket sits in a queue without any work being done. This is the biggest contributor to overall duration. |
| **Repeated “Investigate Issue” after escalation** | 105 (two separate investigation events) | The first investigation (09:10) stopped, then the ticket waited almost **30h** before a second investigation could start. This duplication indicates a lack of continuity in handoff. |
| **Resolution delayed until the next day** | 104 (InvestigateResolve: 19h30m), 102 (InvestigateResolve: 19h30m), 105 (InvestigateResolve: 19h30m) | After the issue is investigated, the ticket is not resolved until the following workday, suggesting either a manual “endofday” batch process or a resourceavailability bottleneck. |
| **No escalation but still long** | 104 (no escalation) | Shows that even without escalation, a long gap between *Assign* and *Investigate* (3h30m) plus a daylong wait before *Resolve* can push a ticket over the 24h mark. |

### 3. How these factors increase cycle time & recommendations

| Factor | Impact on cycle time | Recommended mitigation |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Escalation handoff** | Adds at least one extra “waiting” interval (often >12h) because the Level2 team may not be immediately available. | • Define a **SLA for escalation response** (e.g., start investigation within 1h of escalation). <br>• Use a **shared pool of Level2 agents** that can pick up escalated tickets immediately, or route escalations to a *virtual* queue that is monitored continuously. |
| **Idle time before investigation** | The ticket is “assigned” but no work happens; this is pure waste. | • Implement a **“timetofirstaction” KPI** (e.g., start investigation within 30min of assignment). <br>• Automate the trigger: once a ticket is assigned, an automatic notification or workflow step should start the investigation timer. |
| **Repeated investigation after escalation** | The first investigation is effectively discarded, meaning effort is duplicated and the ticket stays open longer. | • Enforce **knowledge transfer**: the Level1 agent must attach any findings before escalation, so the Level2 agent can continue without reinvestigating. <br>• Use a **single “Investigate” activity** that can be continued by the next owner, rather than creating a new event. |
| **Resolution postponed to next day** | Even after the issue is understood, the ticket sits idle for many hours before a “Resolve” event is logged. | • Allow **resolution at any time** (not just endofday). <br>• If a batch closure is required, schedule a **“ClosePending”** state that can be autoclosed once the agent marks the ticket as resolved. |
| **Lack of parallelism** | The process is strictly sequential; no parallel work (e.g., knowledgebase lookup, automated diagnostics) is performed. | • Introduce **parallel subactivities** such as “Run Automated Diagnostic” that can start immediately after assignment. <br>• Use **AIassisted triage** to prepopulate possible solutions, reducing investigation time. |

#### Quick win checklist for the current process

1. **Measure “timetoinvestigation”** for every case; flag any >30min.
2. **Set an escalation response SLA** (e.g., 1h) and monitor compliance.
3. **Require a “handoff note”** before an escalation event; make it mandatory in the ticketing tool.
4. **Enable “resolveanytime”** status – remove the implicit “waituntilnextday” rule.
5. **Automate routine investigations** (e.g., scriptbased checks) to cut the manual investigation window.

---

### 4. Summary

- **Longrunning tickets**: 102 (25h), 104 (24h), 105 (49h).  
- **Key drivers**: escalations, prolonged idle periods before investigation, and a daylong wait before resolution.  
- **Actionable recommendations**: enforce tighter SLAs for escalation and first investigation, improve handoff documentation, allow resolution at any time, and add automation/parallel steps to reduce manual waiting.

Implementing these changes should bring the majority of tickets back into the **13hour** range observed for cases101 and103, and eliminate the extreme outlier represented by case105.
