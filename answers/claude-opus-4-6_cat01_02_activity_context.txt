

# Analysis of Recurring Activities and Contextual Renaming

## 1. Identifying Recurring Activities

Three activities appear multiple times within individual cases:

| Activity | Recurs In | Occurrences per Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Verify Documents** | C001, C003 | 2 times each |
| **Call Customer** | C002 | 2 times; also appears once in C001 and C003 but with different contextual purposes across cases |
| **Initiate Request** | — | Once per case, but the *channel* differs (online vs. branch) |

---

## 2. Contextual Interpretation

### **Verify Documents**
- **First occurrence** (all cases): This is the **initial identity verification** — the first time an agent reviews the customer's documents as part of the standard intake process.
- **Second occurrence** (C001): Happens *after* the customer submitted additional documents that were missing. This is a **re-verification following document resubmission**.
- **Second occurrence** (C003): Happens *after* an internal account adjustment. This is a **re-verification following an account correction** to ensure consistency.

### **Call Customer**
- **C001**: The call is made because verification was incomplete — the agent is **requesting missing documents**.
- **C002, first call**: The call is to **clarify an account discrepancy** discovered during verification.
- **C002, second call**: A **follow-up call to confirm** that the adjustments made are correct with the customer.
- **C003**: The customer physically left the branch — the officer calls to **recall the customer to the branch**.

### **Initiate Request**
- **C001 & C002**: Submitted via the **online web portal**.
- **C003**: Initiated by a **walk-in customer at a branch**.

## 4. Justification of Naming Choices

### **Initiate Request  "Initiate Request (Online)" vs. "Initiate Request (Branch Walk-In)"**
- **Clue:** The `Resource` field (WebPortal vs. BranchReception) and the `Additional Context` ("online portal" vs. "walk-in customer at branch") clearly distinguish the intake channel. This distinction matters for process analysis because branch and online workflows may have different SLAs, staffing models, and handoff patterns.

### **Verify Documents  "Initial" vs. "Re-verify After Resubmission / Adjustment"**
- **Clue — Position in the process:** The first occurrence always follows immediately after request initiation, indicating it is part of the standard intake procedure. The second occurrence is always preceded by a corrective or supplementary action (document resubmission in C001, account adjustment in C003).
- **Clue — Additional Context:** The text explicitly states "re-checks" (C001) and "re-verify docs after account adjustment" (C003), confirming these are not redundant but are triggered by specific upstream events.
- **C003 "Physical" qualifier:** The resource is a `BranchOfficer` and the context says "physical verification of ID," distinguishing the modality from the back-office digital check in C001/C002.

### **Call Customer  Purpose-specific names**
- **Clue — Additional Context provides the intent of each call:**
  - *C001*: "request missing docs"  **Request Missing Docs** — a remediation call driven by incomplete verification.
  - *C002, first*: "clarify account discrepancy"  **Clarify Discrepancy** — an investigative call.
  - *C002, second*: "confirm adjustments made"  **Confirm Adjustments** — a follow-up/validation call occurring *after* the `Adjust Account` activity, indicating it closes the loop.
  - *C003*: "have them return"  **Recall to Branch** — a logistical call unique to the branch channel, not related to document or account issues.
- **Clue — Position:** In C002, the two calls bracket the `Adjust Account` activity, making their before/after roles structurally clear even without reading the context.
