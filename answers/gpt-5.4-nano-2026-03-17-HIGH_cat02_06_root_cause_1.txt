### 3) How these factors increase cycle time + recommendations

#### How it drives longer cycle time
1. **Queue/backlog effects:** Longer delays from *Assign  Investigate* and *Escalate  follow-up* indicate tickets sit waiting for a person/team.
2. **Handoff friction:** Escalation can break the workflow unless Level-2 picks up immediately with all needed info.
3. **Post-investigation stalling:** When *Investigate  Resolve* is ~19 hours, the investigation result may not be acted on promptly (e.g., needing confirmation, tooling access, approvals, or missing next-step ownership).

---

#### Recommendations / performance fixes
1. **Set and enforce SLAs for handoff points**
   - Track and cap time for:
     - Assign  Investigate (e.g., <1 hour target)
     - Escalate  Level-2 acknowledgment/first response (e.g., <4 hours)
     - Investigate  Resolve (e.g., same-day target)
2. **Improve escalation quality + ownership**
   - Require escalations to include: reproduction steps, logs, suspected root cause, and what has already been tried.
   - Ensure there is a named “case owner” during escalation so the ticket doesn’t fall into limbo.
3. **Add proactive follow-ups when tickets stall**
   - If a ticket hits “Investigate complete” but no “Resolve” within a threshold (e.g., 4–6 hours), automatically trigger:
     - a reminder to the responsible agent
     - a check for dependencies (customer response, approvals, system access)
4. **Reduce overnight/next-day carryover**
   - Many slow cases resolve next morning. If business hours are a factor, consider:
     - after-hours triage rotation for escalated/critical tickets
     - batching work with clear next-step timestamps before end of day
5. **Review Level-2 capacity and routing**
   - Since escalations strongly associate with delays (especially Case 105), confirm:
     - whether Level-2 is overloaded
     - whether tickets are escalated too broadly (escalate only when a criterion is met)
