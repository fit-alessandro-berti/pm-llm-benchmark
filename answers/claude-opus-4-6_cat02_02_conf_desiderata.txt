# Analysis of the Event Log

## 1. Normative Rule Violations

### Case 1: **No violations**
Case 1 follows the strict normative sequence perfectly:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering constraints are satisfied, and since the loan was approved, Finalize Documentation correctly occurs before Close Case.

### Case 2: **Two normative violations**

**Violation 1 — Risk Assessment performed before Credit Check:**
Risk Assessment occurs at 08:20, but Credit Check does not occur until 09:00. The normative rules strictly require that Credit Check must occur *before* Risk Assessment. This is a clear ordering violation.

**Violation 2 — Finalize Documentation performed after a rejection:**
The Loan Decision was "Rejected," yet Finalize Documentation was performed at 10:00. The normative rules explicitly state that if a loan is rejected, documentation must *not* be finalized before closing the case. This is a contradictory action that violates the decision-based logic of the process.

Additionally, it is worth noting that **Inform Applicant** never occurs in Case 2. The normative sequence requires that the applicant be informed of the outcome after a Loan Decision, regardless of whether the loan is approved or rejected. This constitutes a **skipped activity**, which is also not allowed.

### Case 3: **No normative violations**
Case 3 follows the correct strict sequence:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering rules are respected, and since the loan was approved, Finalize Documentation correctly precedes Close Case.

## 2. Desiderata (Recommended Best Practices) Violations

### Case 1: **No desiderata violations**
- Credit Check occurred within 30 minutes of Application Received (well within 24 hours). 
- Inform Applicant occurred 2 hours after Loan Decision (well within 48 hours). 
- However, no Follow-up Call is recorded. This is a *very minor* suboptimal point, but given that the case proceeded smoothly and quickly, the absence is understandable.

*(Note: If we consider the Follow-up Call a desideratum for all cases, then Case 1 also technically misses it, though the log does not explicitly flag it.)*

### Case 2: **Desiderata analysis is secondary to its normative violations**
Since Case 2 already violates hard rules, desiderata considerations are overshadowed. That said, there is no Follow-up Call, and the applicant was never informed — though the latter is a normative, not desiderata, issue.

### Case 3: **Three desiderata violations**

| Desideratum | Details |
|---|---|
| **Credit Check timing** | Credit Check occurred on 2024-01-14 (over 48 hours after Application Received on 2024-01-12), exceeding the recommended 24-hour window. |
| **Inform Applicant timing** | Inform Applicant occurred on 2024-01-16 at 15:00, which is approximately 51 hours after the Loan Decision on 2024-01-14 at 12:00, exceeding the recommended 48-hour window. |
| **No Follow-up Call** | No Follow-up Call was performed before Close Case, missing the encouraged (but optional) best practice. |

These are **not serious violations** — the process still followed the correct normative sequence. They simply represent suboptimal behavior.

## 3. Possible Interpretations and Justifications

| Case | Observation | Possible Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Case 2** — Risk Assessment before Credit Check | Perhaps the risk assessor began a preliminary assessment based on application data alone, intending the formal Credit Check to follow. However, this does not excuse the ordering violation under the strict rules. |
| **Case 2** — Finalize Documentation after rejection | A staff member may have mistakenly initiated the documentation finalization workflow (e.g., an automated trigger or human error). Alternatively, there may have been ambiguity about the decision at the time. Regardless, this remains a clear normative violation. |
| **Case 2** — Missing Inform Applicant | This could be an administrative oversight or a logging error. It is also possible the applicant was informally notified but the activity was not recorded. Still, the absence constitutes a process gap. |
| **Case 3** — Delayed Credit Check | Weekends, resource constraints, or high application volumes could explain the delay. The application was received on a Friday (Jan 12), and the Credit Check occurred the following Monday (Jan 14), suggesting a plausible workday-based explanation. |
| **Case 3** — Delayed Inform Applicant | Similar reasoning — staffing or communication delays may have caused the notification to slip slightly beyond the 48-hour recommendation. |
| **Case 3** — No Follow-up Call | Since this is merely encouraged, the team may have deemed it unnecessary given that the applicant was already informed and documentation was finalized without issues. |
