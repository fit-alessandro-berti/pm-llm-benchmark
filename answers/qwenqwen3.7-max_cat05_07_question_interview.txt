Thank you for that incredibly detailed walkthrough. It gives me a great foundational understanding of your property onboarding lifecycle. To ensure I fully grasp the nuances, edge cases, and operational realities of this process, I have a few follow-up questions. 

I’ve grouped them by theme to help structure our conversation:

### Roles, Responsibilities, and Handoffs
1. **Process Ownership:** You mentioned several distinct roles (property coordinator, property manager, leasing agent, compliance officer, etc.). Is there a single "process owner" or primary point of accountability who oversees the entire onboarding journey from start to finish, or does ownership formally transfer at specific milestones?
2. **Leasing Agent vs. Property Manager:** You noted that either the property manager or a leasing agent handles tenant screening. How do you determine which role takes the lead on a specific property, and how do they collaborate if both are involved in the leasing phase?
3. **Vendor Management:** Who is responsible for sourcing, vetting, and paying the professional photographers, certified inspectors, and contractors? Are these vendors centrally managed by the company, or does the property manager use their own local network?

### Decision Criteria and Approvals
4. **Manager Assignment:** When assigning a property manager based on workload, geography, and experience, is this assignment made manually by a regional director, or is there a systematic routing process that recommends or auto-assigns the manager? What happens if there is a tie or no perfect match?
5. **Pricing Authority:** Who holds the final authority on the listing price? If the digital marketing team’s pricing model and the financial analyst’s profitability targets conflict with the landlord’s expected rental price range, how is that discrepancy negotiated and resolved?
6. **Tenant Screening Thresholds:** Regarding tenant screening, what are the strict, non-negotiable criteria for rejecting an applicant? Conversely, who has the authority to approve an applicant who falls into a "gray area" (e.g., a slightly low credit score but very high verifiable income)?
7. **Discounts and Concessions:** You mentioned offering short-term promotional discounts to secure tenants. What are the parameters for these discounts, and what level of management approval is required to authorize them?

### Timing, Sequencing, and SLAs
8. **Lifecycle Timeline:** From the moment the landlord makes initial contact to the tenant's move-in date, what is the target timeline? Are there specific Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for critical bottlenecks, such as document verification, inspection remediation, or lease finalization?
9. **Audit Sequencing:** You mentioned that compliance, financial, and IT audits happen at the end of the process. Do these audits occur *before* the tenant is allowed to move in, or concurrently/afterward? If they occur after the fact and a critical failure is found (e.g., a fair housing violation in the lease), what is the remediation process?

### Exception Handling and Edge Cases
10. **Inspection Failures:** If the certified inspector finds significant safety or habitability issues and the landlord refuses, or is financially unable, to fund the necessary repairs, what is the exact process for pausing or terminating the onboarding? 
11. **Custom Lease Clauses:** You noted that landlords sometimes insist on custom lease clauses. What is the legal and operational approval workflow for these exceptions? At what point does the company decide a requested clause is a dealbreaker?
12. **Stalled Onboarding:** What happens if a property passes inspection and is "activated" in the portal, but receives no applications or remains vacant for an extended period? Is there a formal re-evaluation process for pricing, marketing, or property condition?
13. **Tenant Drop-out:** If a chosen tenant signs the lease and pays the deposit but unexpectedly backs out right before the move-in date, how does the process revert, and how is the security deposit handled?

### Communication and Data Integrity
14. **Single Source of Truth:** You mentioned tracking communication across email threads, internal CRMs, and project management tools. How do you ensure that critical, binding decisions (like approved custom clauses, agreed-upon repair responsibilities, or final pricing) are formally recorded in the central database rather than getting lost in an email thread?
