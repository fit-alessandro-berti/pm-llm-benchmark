## 2) Root-cause analysis by attribute

## A. Complexity is the strongest driver

### By complexity

- **High complexity**:  
  - 2003 = 48h20m  
  - 2005 = 77h05m

### Why?
High-complexity claims trigger repeated **ôRequest Additional Documentsö** loops, which dramatically extend cycle time.

### Strong correlation: number of document requests vs duration

> **More rework/document-request loops = much longer lead times**

## 5) Practical mitigation actions

## A. Improve upfront document collection
Best improvement opportunity.

### Actions
- Use a **complexity-based submission checklist**
- Require mandatory supporting documents at intake
- Use dynamic forms that change based on claim type/complexity
- Validate completeness before moving to evaluation

### Expected effect
Fewer repeated **Request Additional Documents** loops.

---

## B. Bundle requests instead of asking multiple times
Cases 2003 and 2005 suggest incremental requests.

### Actions
- Train adjusters to send **one complete request** instead of multiple partial ones
- Use standardized templates listing all likely required documents at once
- Add a ôdocument sufficiencyö review before sending a request

### Expected effect
Reduce rework and waiting time.

---

## C. Create early triage for high-complexity claims
High-complexity claims are the most delay-prone.

### Actions
- Flag high-complexity cases immediately at submission
- Route them to specialist teams
- Pre-schedule manager review for likely difficult claims
- Set tighter follow-up SLAs for document collection

### Expected effect
Shorter approval cycle for difficult claims.

---

## D. Review Region B operations
Region B is not bad for simple cases, but appears weaker for medium/high-complexity ones.

### Actions
- Compare Region B intake rules and workload against Region A
- Check whether Region B asks for documents later or more often
- Review staffing, backlog, and local compliance requirements
- Standardize best practices across regions

### Expected effect
Reduce region-driven variation.

---

## E. Monitor adjuster-level rework
Especially relevant for the adjuster role.

### Actions
Track KPIs such as:
- number of document requests per case,
- average time from request to next event,
- average lead time by adjuster and complexity,
- repeat-request rate by adjuster and region.

### Expected effect
Identify whether delays come from:
- workload,
- skill gaps,
- inconsistent document standards,
- or claimant responsiveness.

---

## F. Reduce post-approval waiting
A smaller issue, but visible in case 2003.

### Actions
- Automate payment release after approval
- Extend same-day payment cutoffs
- Trigger finance handoff immediately after approval

### Expected effect
Avoid overnight delays after a claim is already approved.
