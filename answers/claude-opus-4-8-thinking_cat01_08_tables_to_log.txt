## Notes, Assumptions & Design Decisions

1. **Case ID** = `order_id` as instructed.

2. **Granularity of activities** — I chose **one event per line item / delivery / invoice / payment** rather than summarizing, because:
   - The picking dates and pickers differ per line item (meaningful for resource/throughput analysis).
   - Orders can be split into multiple deliveries and invoices, each with its own timestamps.
   This preserves the maximum analytical value for process mining (e.g., rework, partial shipments, installment payments).

3. **Payment "Resource"** — The source tables do not record *who* received/registered the payment (no `payment_clerk_id`). I attributed it to **"System / Customer"** and tagged the originating customer. This is an **assumption**; if a cashier/AR clerk field existed it would replace this.

4. **Delivery events** — Each delivery record was split into two events:
   - **Order Dispatched**  `delivery_dispatch_date`
   - **Order Arrived**  `delivery_arrival_date`
   Both are attributed to the **carrier** as the responsible actor.

5. **Payment linkage** — Payments reference `invoice_id`, not `order_id` directly. I joined `Payments  Invoice  order_id` to assign each payment to the correct case. The originating invoice is retained in the attributes for traceability.

6. **Incomplete cases (data quality flags)**:
   - **Order 1004** has only *Order Created* + *Item Picked* events — **no delivery, invoice, or payment**. This appears to be an **open/in-progress or stalled case** (notably a low-priority Government order). It is included in full as instructed.
   - **Order 1002** is paid in two installments matching its two invoices (full closure).
   - All other orders (1001, 1003, 1005) complete the full lifecycle: Created  Picked  Dispatched  Arrived  Invoiced  Paid.

7. **Chronology check** — All events sort correctly within each case. One real-world observation worth flagging: for several orders the **dispatch date precedes the invoice issue date but the picking occurs after order creation** as expected; no timestamp violations (e.g., arrival before dispatch) were found.

8. **Product/Resource enrichment** — `item_id`s were enriched with description + category from the Products table, and all resource IDs were resolved to names/roles via the Resources table. These are kept in dedicated columns for filtering/grouping.

9. **Timestamps** are preserved in the original ISO8601 UTC (`Z`) format for consistency.
