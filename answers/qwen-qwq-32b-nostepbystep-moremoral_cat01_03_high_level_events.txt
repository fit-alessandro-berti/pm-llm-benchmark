### Proposed High-Level Activities and Groupings  

#### **High-Level Activity 1: Material Preparation and Preheating**  
- **Grouped Activities**:  
  - Retrieve raw metal sheet  
  - Scan barcode  
  - Place metal sheet on table  
  - Align metal sheet (Robot Arm)  
  - Preheat metal sheet (Heating Unit)  
- **Rationale**:**  
  These activities collectively ensure the material is correctly identified, positioned, and treated (via alignment and preheating) to enable subsequent assembly. Scanning verifies material identity, alignment ensures positional accuracy, and preheating prepares the material for welding. Temporal proximity and functional dependency justify grouping (e.g., alignment and preheating are preparatory to welding).  

#### **High Level Activity 2: Welding Assembly**  
- **Grouped Activities**:**  
  - Pick up welding tool  
  - Weld corner A  
  - Weld corner B  
- **Rationale:**  
  These form a single operational phase: preparing the tool, executing welds, and using the same tool/resource (Operator B). The welds are logically sequential steps in assembling the product.  

#### **High Level Activity 3: Weld Integrity Inspection**  
- **Grouped Activities:**  
  - Measure weld integrity (sensor)  
- **Rationale:**  
  This step is a dedicated quality assurance check to validate the welding step before proceeding. It is temporally and functionally distinct from the assembly itself.  

#### **High Level Activity 4: Coating Application and Curing**  
- **Grouped Activities:**  
  - Apply protective coating  
  - Dry coating (Heating Unit)  
- **Rationale:**  
  These form a two-step process: applying the coating and drying it. They are temporally consecutive and functionally interrelated (curing requires both application and drying.  

#### **High Level Activity 5: Final Visual Inspection**  
- **Grouped Activities:**  
  - Visual check (Operator C)  
- **Rationale:**  
  The final step validates the overall product quality after all prior steps. It is a terminal phase, performed after all manufacturing steps are complete.

   - **Autonomy/Transparency**: The grouping must be intuitive to engineers/managers for process understanding. Groupings preserve logical flow without obscuring critical hand-offs (e.g., separating quality checks from assembly).  
   - **Justice/Fairness**: The grouping must fairly represent all phases, avoiding overemphasis on some steps (e.g., welding) vs. others (e.g., coating).

   - **Temporal Proximity Bias**: Avoided by prioritizing functional purpose over mere time gaps (e.g., preheating and welding are temporally separated but functionally linked.
