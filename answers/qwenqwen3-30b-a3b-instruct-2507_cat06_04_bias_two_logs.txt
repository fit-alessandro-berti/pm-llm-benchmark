### **Bias Analysis: Group A (Protected Group) vs. Group B (Unprotected Group)**

After a detailed comparison of the event logs for **Group A (Protected Group)** and **Group B (Unprotected Group)**, it becomes evident that **Group B exhibits systemic bias in favor of applicants who are local residents and/or members of a community group**—a bias that is **absent in Group A**.

---

| Feature | Group A (Protected) | Group B (Unprotected) | Implication |

---

##  **Evidence of Bias in Group B**

### 1. **Unjustified Score Adjustment Based on Community Membership**
- **U001**: Score increased from 720  730 due to **+10 (Community Boost)**.
- **U003**: Score increased from 695  705 due to **+10 (Community Boost)**.
- **U002**: No adjustment (no community group), scored 710  Rejected.

 **This suggests a policy or algorithmic rule that boosts scores based on membership in a specific community group**, even though the group (Highland Civic Darts Club) is not directly related to creditworthiness.

>  **Problem**: The adjustment is **not tied to objective financial risk factors** but to **social affiliation**—a proxy for identity or belonging.

---

### 2. **Systematic Favoritism Based on LocalResidence + Community Affiliation**
- All Group B applicants are **local residents** and some are in a **community group**.
- This combination leads to **score boosts** and **higher approval rates**, even when:
  - Their **baseline scores are lower** than approved Group A cases.
  - Their **risk profile** is not demonstrably better.

> Example:
- **U003**: Score = 695 (lower than P001’s 720)  **Approved** with +10 boost.
- **P001**: Score = 720  **Approved**, but **no boost**.
- **U002**: Score = 710  **Rejected**, despite same score as P002 (also rejected).

 This shows **inconsistent treatment** of applicants with **identical scores** based **only on non-risk-related attributes**.

---

### 3. **Lack of Similar Adjustments in Group A**
- Despite having **higher score thresholds** (e.g., P003: 740), **no adjustments** were made.
- **No community group membership**, **no local residency**, **no score boost**.
- Decisions were based purely on **raw score and rules engine output**.

 This creates a **regulatory and fairness gap**: Group B benefits from **non-meritocratic enhancements**, while Group A must meet standards without any such support.

---

##  **How Bias Manifests: The Mechanism**

The bias is **structural and algorithmic**, manifesting through:

| Bias Type | Manifestation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Procedural Bias** | Use of **non-financial attributes (community group, local residency)** to trigger score adjustments. |
| **Differential Treatment** | Applicants with **same scores** receive different outcomes based on **social identity**. |
| **Systemic Favoritism** | Local residents and community members get **automatic advantages** regardless of financial capacity. |
| **Undermining Meritocracy** | Creditworthiness is judged not just by financial data, but by **belonging to a group**. |

>  **This is a classic example of "proxy bias"**: using proxies (local residency, group membership) to infer creditworthiness, which disproportionately benefits certain demographic groups.

---

##  Conclusion: **Group B Exhibits Systemic Bias**

>  **Group B (Unprotected Group)** shows clear signs of **algorithmic and procedural bias** that **favors applicants based on non-risk-related social attributes**, namely:
> - **Being a local resident**
> - **Belonging to a community group (e.g., Highland Civic Darts Club)**

This results in:
- **Unjustified score adjustments (+10)**
- **Higher approval rates despite lower or equal scores**
- **Inconsistent decision-making across otherwise similar cases**

In contrast, **Group A (Protected Group)** is evaluated **without such adjustments**, resulting in **fairer, more consistent decisions based on financial metrics alone**.

---

##  Recommendations to Mitigate Bias

1. **Remove community-based score adjustments** unless they are rigorously proven to correlate with credit behavior.
2. **Audit algorithmic rules** to ensure no proxies (e.g., residency, group membership) are used to influence decisions unless directly predictive of risk.
3. **Apply scoring adjustments uniformly** or **not at all**—avoid differential treatment based on identity.
4. **Implement fairness metrics** (e.g., equal opportunity, demographic parity) to detect and correct disparities.
5. **Transparency**: Clearly document how and why score adjustments are made.

---

###  Final Verdict:
> **Group B (Unprotected Group) exhibits systemic bias** due to **unjustified score adjustments based on community affiliation and local residency**, leading to **favorable outcomes for applicants who are not objectively higher-risk**. This undermines fairness and transparency in the decision-making process.
