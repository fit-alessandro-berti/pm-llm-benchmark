## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

Understanding interactions is critical, as optimizing one constraint in isolation can worsen another.

*   **Express Priority & Cold-Packing:** An express order needing cold-packing will preempt a standard order at a cold station. This not only delays the standard order but also **increases the queue for the already scarce cold stations**, amplifying the contention effect. The interaction metric would be the *increase in average wait time for cold stations during periods of high express order volume*.
*   **Batching & HAZMAT Limits:** If multiple HAZMAT orders are destined for the same region, they may be naturally batched. However, the HAZMAT limit could **prevent these orders from progressing to the batching stage simultaneously**, thereby delaying batch formation and increasing wait-for-batch times for all orders in that region. Conversely, a large batch of standard orders could fill the 10 HAZMAT slots if not managed, blocking all new HAZMAT orders.
*   **Resource Contagion:** A delay caused by any one constraint (e.g., a long queue at cold-packing) pushes order completion times later. This interacts with batching (orders miss their optimal batch window) and with regulatory limits (orders pile up at the Packing step, increasing the chance of hitting the HAZMAT limit).

Process mining can reveal these interactions through **correlation analysis** (e.g., between express order volume and cold-packing wait times) and **process variant analysis** (comparing process flows for HAZMAT vs. non-HAZMAT orders during peak vs. off-peak times).

### Strategy 1: Dynamic Resource Pooling with Priority-Based Preemption Rules
*   **Constraints Addressed:** Shared Cold-Packing, Priority Handling.
*   **Proposal:** Implement a **dynamic resource allocation system** for packing stations. Maintain a primary pool of standard stations and a smaller dedicated pool for cold items. However, create a **rule-based override**: if the cold-packing queue exceeds a threshold (e.g., 3 orders), a standard station can be temporarily reconfigured for cold packing (assuming minor setup time). This is managed by a central dispatch system. Preemption rules are refined: express orders can preempt standard orders *only* at standard stations or at cold stations if their own requirement is cold. A preemption triggers a notification and a guaranteed return-to-queue priority for the preempted standard order.
*   **Leveraging Data:** The threshold for reconfiguration is set based on historical data analysis of cold-packing demand patterns (e.g., time of day, day of week). Preemption frequency data justifies the rules to minimize disruption.
*   **Expected Outcomes:** Reduces average wait time for cold-packing by increasing effective capacity. Maintains express service levels while making preemptions more predictable and less damaging to standard order flow.

### Strategy 2: Intelligent, Dynamic Batching with Hazardous Material Awareness
*   **Constraints Addressed:** Batching for Shipping, Hazardous Material Limits.
*   **Proposal:** Replace fixed, time-based batch formation with a **dynamic, demand-driven model**. The system continuously monitors orders completed from QC. A batch for region "X" is triggered not by a timer, but by a **composite condition**: either (a) a minimum batch size is met, or (b) a maximum wait time for the *oldest* order in the potential batch is reached. Crucially, the batch composition logic includes a **constraint-checking module**: it will not add an order to a batch if doing so would cause the *future* HAZMAT order flow into Packing/QC to violate the 10-order limit. This may involve slightly delaying the batch trigger for a region to allow a HAZMAT order to clear.
*   **Leveraging Data:** Historical order destination and timing data are used to simulate and set optimal batch-size thresholds and max-wait times per region. Predictive analytics forecast HAZMAT order inflow to enable proactive batch composition.
*   **Expected Outcomes:** Reduces "wait-for-batch" times by forming batches more fluidly. Prevents self-inflicted bottlenecks at Packing/QC caused by poorly timed batch releases, ensuring smoother flow and better HAZMAT limit compliance.

### Strategy 3: Staged Hazardous Material Processing with Buffer Zones
*   **Constraints Addressed:** Hazardous Material Limits, Interactions with Batching/Flow.

*   **Expected Outcomes:** Transforms the HAZMAT limit from a reactive bottleneck into a managed, predictable flow. Isolates the regulatory constraint, reducing its disruptive impact on the standard order flow. Improves overall throughput by preventing system-wide jams.
