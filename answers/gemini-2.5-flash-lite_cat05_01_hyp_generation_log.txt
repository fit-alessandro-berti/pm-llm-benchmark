#### Anomaly 1: Out-of-Sequence Core Process Steps

**Observation:** In several cases, the sequence of critical activities like 'Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', and 'Ship Goods' deviates significantly from the assumed normal flow (e.g., shipping goods before credit check or stock validation, or confirming shipment after goods have been shipped or invoiced).

*   **Case 1002:**
    *   'Confirm Shipment' (`event_id` 9) occurs *before* 'Perform Credit Check' and 'Validate Stock'.
    *   'Ship Goods' (`event_id` 10) occurs *before* 'Perform Credit Check' and 'Validate Stock'.
    *   The `additional_info` for 'Confirm Shipment' is "attempted_early=Y".
*   **Case 1003:**
    *   'Ship Goods' (`event_id` 17) occurs *before* 'Validate Stock' and 'Confirm Shipment'.
    *   'Confirm Shipment' (`event_id` 19) occurs *after* 'Ship Goods' and 'Issue Invoice'.
    *   The `additional_info` for 'Confirm Shipment' is "late_confirmation=Y".

**Hypothesized Causes:**
1.  **System Glitch/Bug:** The workflow engine or event logging system might be incorrectly ordering events, especially under specific conditions (e.g., race conditions, high load).
2.  **Manual Override/Policy Violation:** Users might be bypassing standard procedures to expedite certain orders or due to specific, unlogged exceptions. The `additional_info` flags ("attempted_early=Y", "late_confirmation=Y") suggest that these deviations might be intentional workarounds or known issues.
3.  **Data Entry Errors:** While less likely for a system-generated log, incorrect manual timestamps or event creation could occur.

**SQL Queries for Investigation:**

1.  **Identify cases with out-of-sequence core activities:**
    This query looks for instances where 'Ship Goods' or 'Confirm Shipment' occur before critical preceding steps like 'Perform Credit Check' or 'Validate Stock'.
    ```sql
    WITH RankedCoreActivities AS (
        SELECT
            case_id,
            activity,
            timestamp,
            -- Assign a rank to key activities based on the assumed normal flow
            CASE
                WHEN activity = 'Register Order' THEN 1
                WHEN activity = 'Perform Credit Check' THEN 2
                WHEN activity = 'Validate Stock' THEN 3
                WHEN activity = 'Confirm Shipment' THEN 4
                WHEN activity = 'Ship Goods' THEN 5
                ELSE 99 -- For other activities not in this specific sequence check
            END as activity_rank
        FROM order_event_log
        WHERE activity IN ('Register Order', 'Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', 'Ship Goods')
    ),
    SequenceAnalysis AS (
        SELECT
            case_id,
            activity,
            timestamp,
            activity_rank,
            -- Get the rank of the previous event for this case
            LAG(activity_rank, 1, 0) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) as prev_activity_rank,
            LAG(activity, 1, 'START') OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) as prev_activity_name
        FROM RankedCoreActivities
    )
    SELECT
        sa.case_id,
        sa.activity AS current_activity,
        sa.timestamp AS current_timestamp,
        sa.prev_activity_name,
        sa.prev_activity_rank,
        sa.activity_rank
    FROM SequenceAnalysis sa
    WHERE
        -- Anomaly: Ship Goods (rank 5) occurring before Validate Stock (rank 3) or Confirm Shipment (rank 4)
        (sa.activity = 'Ship Goods' AND sa.prev_activity_rank < 3)
        OR
        -- Anomaly: Confirm Shipment (rank 4) occurring before Validate Stock (rank 3)
        (sa.activity = 'Confirm Shipment' AND sa.prev_activity_rank < 3)
        OR
        -- Anomaly: Confirm Shipment (rank 4) occurring AFTER Ship Goods (rank 5) or Issue Invoice (rank 6)
        -- (Note: Issue Invoice not in this CTE, but implied by logic if we extend ranks)
        (sa.activity = 'Confirm Shipment' AND sa.prev_activity_rank > 4);
    ```

2.  **Investigate specific `additional_info` flags related to process deviations:**
    This query directly targets cases where specific flags indicating potential issues are present in the `additional_info` column.
    ```sql
    SELECT
        case_id,
        activity,

        resource,
    ```

#### Anomaly 2: Premature Payment/Invoicing with Potential Non-Shipment

**Observation:** In Case 1004, 'Receive Payment' and 'Issue Invoice' occur very early in the process, immediately after 'Register Order' and *before* 'Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', and 'Ship Goods'. Furthermore, 'Confirm Shipment' is marked with `additional_info` "shipment_scheduled=N", implying the order might not be intended for shipment after all, despite receiving payment.

*   **Case 1004:**
    *   'Receive Payment' (`event_id` 22) occurs before any checks or shipment steps.
    *   'Issue Invoice' (`event_id` 23) occurs before any checks or shipment steps.
    *   'Confirm Shipment' (`event_id` 24) has `additional_info` "shipment_scheduled=N".

**Hypothesized Causes:**
1.  **Different Order Type/Process:** This might represent a special type of order (e.g., pre-paid, digital goods, or a service) that doesn't follow the standard physical goods flow, and the system isn't adequately differentiating or handling it.
2.  **System Error:** A misconfiguration or bug could lead to payment and invoicing being triggered prematurely or events being misordered.
3.  **Policy Violation/Fraud Risk:** Payment might have been accepted without proper due diligence, or there's a risk of non-fulfillment after payment.
4.  **Order Cancellation/Modification:** The order might have been flagged as non-shippable after payment was processed, but this status change wasn't logged clearly.

**SQL Queries for Investigation:**

1.  **Identify orders with early payment/invoice events and non-scheduled shipment flags:**

    ```sql

                WHEN activity = 'Perform Credit Check' THEN 4

            END as activity_rank

        WHERE activity IN ('Register Order', 'Receive Payment', 'Issue Invoice', 'Perform Credit Check', 'Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', 'Ship Goods')
    ),
    SequenceAnalysis AS (
        SELECT
            case_id,
            activity,
            timestamp,
            additional_info,
            activity_rank,
            -- Get the rank of the previous event for this case
    ```

#### Anomaly 3: Resource Departmental Mismatch

**Observation:** (This anomaly is not clearly visible in the provided sample data but is a common operational risk). A resource might perform an activity that falls outside their designated department or role. For example, a 'SalesRep' performing a 'Perform Credit Check' or a 'WarehouseClerk' performing a 'Receive Payment' task.

**Hypothesized Causes:**
1.  **Inadequate Training/Skills:** Resources may not have received sufficient training for their assigned tasks.
2.  **Resource Shortage/Overload:** In times of high demand or staff absence, resources might be temporarily assigned tasks outside their usual scope.
3.  **System Misconfiguration:** The system may not correctly enforce role-based access control or departmental assignments for certain activities.
4.  **Policy Violation:** There might be explicit or implicit policies allowing cross-departmental task execution under certain circumstances, which need verification.

**SQL Query for Investigation:**

1.  **Identify activities performed by resources whose department does not match the expected department for that activity:**
    This query joins `order_event_log` with `resources` and compares the resource's department against a predefined mapping of activities to departments.
    ```sql
    SELECT
        oel.case_id,
        oel.activity,
        oel.timestamp,
        oel.resource,
        r.department AS resource_department,
        r.role AS resource_role,
        -- Define the expected department for each activity based on the assumed process flow
        CASE
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Register Order') THEN 'Sales'
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Perform Credit Check', 'Issue Invoice', 'Receive Payment') THEN 'Finance'
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', 'Ship Goods') THEN 'Logistics'
            ELSE 'Unknown' -- Activities not part of the core mapped flow
        END AS expected_department
    FROM order_event_log oel
    JOIN resources r ON oel.resource = r.resource_id
    WHERE
        -- Flag rows where the resource's department does NOT match the expected department
        CASE
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Register Order') THEN r.department <> 'Sales'
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Perform Credit Check', 'Issue Invoice', 'Receive Payment') THEN r.department <> 'Finance'
            WHEN oel.activity IN ('Validate Stock', 'Confirm Shipment', 'Ship Goods') THEN r.department <> 'Logistics'
            ELSE FALSE -- Don't flag if the activity is not mapped or resource department is unknown
        END;
    ```

#### Anomaly 4: Unusually Long Durations Between Steps

**Observation:** (Not prominent in the provided samples but a critical general anomaly). Some cases might exhibit very long time gaps between consecutive activities. This could indicate significant delays, bottlenecks, or that a case has been put on hold or forgotten.

**Hypothesized Causes:**
1.  **Process Bottlenecks:** Specific steps or departments are overloaded, causing queues.
2.  **External Dependencies:** Waiting for information or action from external parties (e.g., customer, supplier).
3.  **Resource Availability Issues:** Lack of available personnel or systems.
4.  **System Downtime:** Planned or unplanned outages affecting process steps.
5.  **"Stuck" Cases:** Cases that are inadvertently left incomplete due to errors or lack of follow-up.

**SQL Query for Investigation:**

1.  **Calculate and flag durations between consecutive events that exceed a defined threshold:**
    This query identifies significant time gaps between any two consecutive events within the same `case_id`.
    ```sql
    WITH EventGaps AS (
        SELECT
            case_id,
            activity,
            timestamp,
            -- Get the timestamp of the next event for the same case
            LEAD(timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) as next_timestamp,
            -- Calculate the duration from the current event to the next
            (LEAD(timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) - timestamp) as duration
        FROM order_event_log
    )
    SELECT
        case_id,
        activity AS previous_activity,
        timestamp AS previous_timestamp,
        next_timestamp,
        duration
    FROM EventGaps
    WHERE duration > INTERVAL '1 day' -- Define your threshold for "unusually long" (e.g., 1 day, 3 days)
      AND next_timestamp IS NOT NULL -- Ensure there was a subsequent event to measure against
    ORDER BY duration DESC;
    ```
