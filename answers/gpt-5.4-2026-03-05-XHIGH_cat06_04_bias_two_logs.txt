The **bias is revealed by the comparison of the two logs**, but if you have to point to where the biased mechanism appears, it is most visible in **Group B’s log**. That log shows a **preferential scoring rule** that benefits the unprotected group, which in turn creates a disadvantage for **Group A (the protected group)**.

### Why Group B’s log is problematic
In **Group A**, every case has:
- `LocalResident = FALSE`
- `CommunityGroup = None`
- `ScoreAdjustment = 0`

In **Group B**, some cases have:
- `LocalResident = TRUE`
- `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`
- `ScoreAdjustment = +10 (Community Boost)`

So the system is not treating the groups the same. A subset of Group B applicants gets an extra scoring advantage that Group A never receives.

### How the bias manifests
The key issue is the **+10 “Community Boost”**. It is applied during **PreliminaryScoring** and then carried forward into:
- **ManualReview**
- **FinalDecision**

That means the bias is not just cosmetic—it affects the actual decision pipeline.

### Role of `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup`
These attributes appear to function as **proxy variables**:
- `LocalResident` is always **TRUE** in Group B and **FALSE** in Group A.
- Membership in the named `CommunityGroup` appears only in Group B.
- The score boost is linked to these characteristics, not to the base score itself.

This is problematic because even if `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` are not protected attributes on paper, they can still create **indirect discrimination** if they strongly overlap with group membership.

### Evidence from the cases
- **P001 vs U001**  
  Both start with a **PreliminaryScore of 720**.  
  - P001: no adjustment  approved  
  - U001: +10 adjustment  approved  
  Even though both are approved, U001 is artificially made to look stronger.

- **P002 vs U002**  
  Both start with **710** and receive no meaningful boost.  
  - Both are rejected  
  This suggests that without the boost, treatment looks similar.

- **U003 is the clearest sign of bias**  
  U003 starts at **695**, gets a **+10 Community Boost**, becomes **705 adjusted**, and is **approved**.  
  Group A has no comparable mechanism. A protected-group applicant with a similar raw score would likely not get the same chance.

In fact, **P002 is rejected at 710**, while **U003 is approved at an adjusted 705**, which strongly suggests that the community-based adjustment is giving Group B a special path to approval.

### Important nuance
If you only look at the tiny sample’s approval rates, both groups have **2 approvals out of 3 cases**, so the bias may not show up as a simple difference in acceptance rate here. But that does **not** mean the process is fair.

The unfairness is in the **decision rule**:
- Group A is evaluated on raw score only.
- Group B can receive an extra +10 based on social/local affiliation.

That means the **process is unequal**, and over a larger number of cases it would likely produce **systematically better outcomes for Group B**.

### Conclusion
- **Group B’s log contains the biased mechanism**: the `ScoreAdjustment` tied to `LocalResident` and especially `CommunityGroup`.
- This creates **preferential treatment for the unprotected group**.
- As a result, the overall system is **biased against Group A**, because the protected group never receives the same opportunity for score improvement.

So the bias is best described as **proxy-based or indirect discrimination**, driven by community/local affiliation features that systematically favor the unprotected group.
