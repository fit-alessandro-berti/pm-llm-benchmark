**Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions** I would ask the interviewee:

1. Can you describe the specific criteria or checklists the property coordinator uses to determine whether the landlord’s documentation (deeds, compliance certificates, inspection reports) is complete and up-to-date? What happens if the landlord is unable or unwilling to provide the requested updates within a reasonable timeframe?

2. How is a property manager’s “current workload” actually assessed when making assignments? What other factors (beyond geography, experience, and track record) might influence the decision, and what happens if no ideal match is available?

3. What topics are typically covered in the introductory meeting between the property manager and landlord beyond pets, maintenance, and payment channels? How are any special conditions or policy agreements documented and shared with other teams?

4. Can you walk me through the typical timeline and sequencing from initial documentation review through manager assignment, the introductory meeting, and scheduling the photographer and inspector? Are any of these steps allowed to run in parallel?

5. What specific safety and habitability standards does the certified inspector evaluate against? How does the property manager decide which issues must be fixed before proceeding versus those that can be addressed later?

6. When the inspector identifies issues, how are decisions made about which repairs are required, who is responsible for them (landlord vs. company), and who selects or pays for contractors? What happens if the landlord is unresponsive or refuses to address major findings?

7. How does the digital marketing team work with the property manager and landlord’s expected rental price range when updating pricing models? What market trends or data sources most influence those updates?

8. What are the key criteria and thresholds used during tenant screening (credit, employment, references), and how does the process handle borderline cases or situations with multiple qualified applicants?

9. When multiple tenants are interested, what factors does the property manager weigh when selecting a tenant, negotiating lease terms, offering discounts, or prioritizing longer leases? How much discretion does the manager have?

10. At what points in the overall process do the compliance officer, financial analyst, and IT reviews typically occur? What happens if any of these quality checks identify issues after a lease has been prepared or signed?

11. Can you share examples of common exceptions or special cases—such as properties requiring special insurance or landlords requesting custom lease clauses—and describe how the standard process is adapted or escalated for them?

12. How are handoffs and communication managed across the various roles (coordinator, property manager, marketing team, leasing agents, compliance, etc.)? What mechanisms exist for tracking progress, flagging delays, or escalating bottlenecks throughout the onboarding journey?

These questions are designed to uncover decision criteria, clarify roles and handoffs, verify timing/sequencing and dependencies, explore exception handling, and reveal missing details about triggers, outcomes, and potential failure points—all while staying at a conceptual/business-process level.
