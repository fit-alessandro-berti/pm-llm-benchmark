Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify the process, uncover decision logic, and identify potential bottlenecks or exceptions. They are categorized by the specific aspect of the process they aim to explore.

### **Documentation & Verification**
1.  **Criteria for "Outdated":** You mentioned that documentation must be verified for currency. What specific criteria or timeframes determine when a document is considered "outdated" versus "valid," and is this standard across all cities or does it vary by local regulation?
2.  **Handling Incomplete Submissions:** When a coordinator identifies missing or outdated documentation, is there a defined workflow for how many follow-up attempts are made before the onboarding request is formally paused or rejected?
3.  **Data Entry Validation:** Once the property details are recorded in the central database, is there an automated validation step to ensure the input (such as square footage or unit count) aligns with the uploaded deeds, or is this a manual cross-check performed by the coordinator?

### **Manager Assignment & Expectations**
4.  **Assignment Prioritization:** You noted that manager assignment depends on workload, geography, and specialization. If a high-priority property (e.g., luxury unit) requires a specialized manager who is currently at capacity, is there a protocol for temporary reassignment or hiring external support?
5.  **Expectation Alignment:** During the introductory meeting, how are "special conditions" like pet policies or maintenance responsibilities formally documented to ensure they are not lost during the transition to the operational team?
6.  **Conflict Resolution:** If a landlord insists on a policy that conflicts with the company’s standard guidelines during the introductory call, what is the escalation path for resolving that conflict before the property moves to the inspection phase?

### **Inspection & Repairs**
7.  **Repair Authorization:** If the inspector identifies issues that require repairs, who holds the final authority to approve the cost and scope of those repairs? Is it the property manager, the landlord, or a third-party estimator?
8.  **Timeline Management:** What happens if the landlord delays the necessary repairs after the inspection report is issued? Does the marketing timeline automatically shift, or is there a separate "hold" status in the system?
9.  **Re-inspection Protocol:** If a repair is completed, is there a mandatory re-inspection by the same certified inspector, or does the property manager have the authority to sign off on minor fixes?

### **Tenant Screening & Leasing**
10. **Selection Weighting:** In the tenant screening phase, if multiple applicants meet the basic criteria, what specific decision matrix is used to prioritize one over another (e.g., credit score vs. employment stability vs. lease duration)?
11. **Negotiation Limits:** Regarding lease terms and promotional discounts, does the property manager have a pre-defined budget or authority limit for negotiation, or do they need approval from a regional director for concessions?
12. **Move-in Coordination:** How is the handoff managed between the leasing agent finalizing the lease and the facilities team preparing the unit for the move-in date to ensure no delays occur?

### **Compliance, Audits & Exceptions**
13. **Audit Override:** If the compliance officer or financial analyst identifies a discrepancy during the post-onboarding audit, does the property listing automatically deactivate, or is there a manual process to request an override with justification?
14. **Custom Clauses:** For landlords insisting on custom lease clauses, how is the legal review process integrated into the workflow? Does this require a separate legal team intervention that extends the timeline?
15. **Special Insurance:** Regarding properties requiring special insurance, is this a blocker for activation, or can the property be listed while the insurance policy is being procured?

### **Communication & Process Flow**
16. **Status Visibility:** You mentioned using email threads and CRMs to track communication. How does a stakeholder (like the landlord) currently track the real-time status of their property onboarding without directly contacting the coordinator?
17. **Bottlenecks:** Looking at the entire lifecycle, which stage currently experiences the most frequent delays or back-and-forth communication between internal teams and external stakeholders?
18. **Success Metrics:** How is the success of the onboarding process measured? Is it based on time-to-market (days from contact to activation), accuracy of data entry, or landlord satisfaction scores?
