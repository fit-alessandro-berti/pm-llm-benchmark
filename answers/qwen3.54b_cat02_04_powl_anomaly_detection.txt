    *   It contradicts the "Atomic Order" principle where one order results in one invoice and one final payment.

These anomalies are problematic for several reasons regarding process quality, compliance, and operational efficiency:

*   **Reasoning:** By allowing the `skip` transition, the model permits a "bad debt" scenario. An organization could approve orders from non-creditworthy customers to generate revenue quickly without verifying their ability to pay.
*   **Impact:** This leads to significant financial loss (non-payment), increased bad debt provisions, and potential regulatory compliance issues regarding credit management policies. It renders the process conformance check useless for detecting "Credit Check Bypass" events.

#### B. Process Inefficiency and Data Integrity (Loop Anomaly)

*   **Impact:**

*   **Impact:** This model is likely a "Best-Effort" or "Workaround-heavy" model rather than a "Compliant Process."
