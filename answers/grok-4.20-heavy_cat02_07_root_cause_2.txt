**Explanations for why these attributes contribute:**
High-complexity claims inherently require more verification, leading to repeated document requests when information is incomplete. Each request introduces external dependency (customer response time), which is not present in low-complexity cases. This creates process loops that significantly extend lead time. Resource patterns (e.g., Manager_Bill only on high-complexity, certain adjusters handling more complex work) suggest possible specialization or bottlenecks in handling complex work, but complexity itself is the root driver.

**Suggestions to mitigate:**
- Improve initial claim submission and evaluation for high/medium complexity (e.g., comprehensive checklists, better online forms, or AI-assisted validation) to gather all documents upfront and reduce follow-up requests.
- Provide targeted customer guidance or a self-service portal for faster document submission.
- Assign dedicated/specialized resources or teams for high-complexity claims to streamline handling and reduce iteration.
- Monitor and balance workload for managers/adjusters handling complex cases (e.g., additional capacity for Manager_Bill).
- Consider process redesign, such as parallelizing certain checks or setting service-level targets for customer responses on document requests.
