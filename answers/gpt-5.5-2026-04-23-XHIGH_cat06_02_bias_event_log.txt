The event log shows a clear **risk of preferential treatment for applicants who are local residents and/or members of a specific community group**, especially the **Highland Civic Darts Club**. The most visible bias is introduced through the **ScoreAdjustment** field and then carried forward into manual review and final decisioning.

## Where bias appears

### 1. PreliminaryScoring: explicit community-based score boost

Applicants belonging to **Highland Civic Darts Club** receive:

> `+10 (Community)`

Cases affected:

| Case | LocalResident | CommunityGroup | PreliminaryScore | Adjustment | Adjusted Score | Decision |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| C001 | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 710 | +10 | 720 | Approved |
| C004 | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 690 | +10 | 700 | Approved |

Applicants with no community affiliation receive no boost:

| Case | LocalResident | CommunityGroup | PreliminaryScore | Adjustment | Decision |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---|
| C002 | TRUE | None | 720 | 0 | Approved |
| C003 | FALSE | None | 715 | 0 | Rejected |
| C005 | FALSE | None | 740 | 0 | Approved |

This means membership in a particular community group directly improves an applicantĺs score, even though that affiliation may not reflect actual creditworthiness.

---

## Strongest evidence of unfairness

The comparison between **C003** and **C004** is especially concerning:

| Case | LocalResident | CommunityGroup | PreliminaryScore | Adjusted Score | Decision |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---|
| C003 | FALSE | None | 715 | 715 | Rejected |
| C004 | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 690 | 700 | Approved |

C003 has a **higher underlying PreliminaryScore** than C004 Ś 715 versus 690 Ś but is rejected. C004 is approved after receiving the community-based adjustment.

That suggests the process is not treating applicants solely according to their underlying score or creditworthiness. Instead, local/community affiliation appears to improve the likelihood of approval.

---

## Attributes that appear to favor certain groups

### CommunityGroup

The **Highland Civic Darts Club** affiliation is explicitly rewarded. This favors applicants who are socially connected to that specific group.

That can disadvantage people who:

- Are not members of that club.
- Are new to the area.
- Lack time, money, or social access to participate in civic groups.
- Belong to different communities not recognized by the scoring system.
- Have similar or better creditworthiness but lack the ôrightö affiliation.

### LocalResident

The **LocalResident** attribute also appears relevant. All local residents in the sample are approved, while one non-local applicant with a relatively strong score, C003 with 715, is rejected.

C005 shows that non-local applicants can still be approved, but only with a much higher score, 740. This suggests non-local applicants may face a higher effective bar.

---

## How this affects fairness and equity

The process appears to create an **unequal approval standard**:

- Local/community-affiliated applicants can be approved with lower underlying scores.
- Non-local or unaffiliated applicants may need higher scores to receive the same outcome.
- Similar applicants are not treated similarly.

This is a fairness concern because community membership and geography can act as proxies for protected or socioeconomic characteristics, such as race, ethnicity, class, age, housing patterns, or social network access. Even if the rule is framed as a ôcommunity bonus,ö its practical effect may be to privilege applicants who already have access to certain local institutions.

---

## Bottom line

Bias manifests mainly through the **+10 community adjustment** and possibly through the use of **LocalResident** status in final decisioning. The process appears to favor applicants who are local and affiliated with the Highland Civic Darts Club, while disadvantaging applicants without those affiliations, even when their underlying PreliminaryScore is similar or better.

The clearest example is that **C004 is approved with a raw score of 690 after a community boost, while C003 is rejected with a raw score of 715 and no affiliation**. That indicates a potential inequity in the decision process.
