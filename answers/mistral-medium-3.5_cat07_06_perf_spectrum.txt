The diagram presented is a Sankey diagram, which is a type of flow diagram used to visualize the flow of processes or activities over time. In this specific instance, the diagram illustrates the transitions of customers through different stages of a collection process, spanning from the initial stage to the final action taken. The x-axis represents the time elapsed in years, indicating the progression and duration of the process. The y-axis depicts the various states or stages within the collection process. The diagram includes multiple flow lines that show the movement of customers between these stages. The width of these lines is proportional to the number of customers transitioning between the states at any given time, providing a visual representation of the volume of activity. The different colors used for the flow lines help distinguish between the various stages and transitions. Overall, this Sankey diagram provides a clear and comprehensive view of how customers move through the collection process over time, highlighting the volume and flow of activities at each stage.
