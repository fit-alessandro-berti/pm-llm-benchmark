# Procure-to-Pay Anomaly Analysis

## Standard P2P Reference Flow
The expected control sequence is:
**Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice to PO  Release Payment  Close Case**

This ordering enforces key financial controls, most notably the **3-way match** (PO + Goods Receipt + Invoice) *before* any payment is released.

---

## Case 1001 —  Conforming (Baseline)
All activities occur in the correct order with reasonable timing. This is the "happy path" and serves as the comparison baseline.

---

## Case 1002 —  Multiple Severe Control Violations

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **PO issued before PR approval** | Issue PO (Feb 1 08:40) occurs before Approve PR (Feb 3 08:00). |
| **Approval happens after the PO, payment, and even after the case is well underway** | Approval is effectively a rubber-stamp / backdated formality. |
| **Payment released far too early** | Release Payment (Feb 3 07:15) occurs *before* goods are received (Feb 4), *before* invoice matching (Feb 5), and *before* approval. |
| **Match Invoice happens after payment** | The 3-way match (Feb 5) is meaningless because money already left (Feb 3). |

**Why problematic:** This case inverts nearly every control. Paying before receiving goods and before matching exposes the company to fraud, duplicate/overpayment, and paying for goods never delivered. The post-hoc approval suggests a circumvented authorization process.

---

## Case 1003 —  Payment Before Receipt and Invoice

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Release Payment (Feb 8) precedes Vendor Invoice Received (Feb 9)** | Paying without an invoice to validate the amount. |
| **Payment precedes Receive Goods (Feb 10)** | Paying for goods not yet delivered. |
| **Payment precedes Match Invoice to PO (Feb 11)** | No 3-way match performed before disbursing funds. |

**Why problematic:** Same fundamental risk as 1002 — funds are released with no verification that goods arrived or that the invoice is legitimate and matches the PO. This breaks the cornerstone P2P control and creates significant financial and audit risk.

---

## Case 1004 —  Goods Received Before PO Issued

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Receive Goods (Feb 6 09:00) occurs before Issue PO (Feb 6 09:15)** | The order is recorded *after* the goods physically arrived. |

**Why problematic:** This is classic **maverick buying / retroactive PO creation** — goods were ordered/received outside the formal procurement system, and the PO was created afterward to "cover" the receipt. It undermines spend control, budget commitment, and vendor negotiation, and indicates an off-system purchase. Notably, the rest of the flow (invoice  match  payment) is otherwise correct, so the single out-of-order step is the anomaly. (The two events are only 15 minutes apart, which could also hint at a data-entry/timestamp issue worth investigating.)

## Summary Table

| Case | Status | Key Anomaly |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1001 |  Normal | None |
| 1002 |  Critical | PO before approval; payment before receipt/match; approval out of order |
| 1003 |  Critical | Payment before invoice, goods receipt, and matching |
| 1004 |  Moderate | Goods received before PO issued (retroactive PO) |

## Overarching Risk Themes
1. **Payment before 3-way match (1002, 1003):** The most serious finding — eliminates protection against overpayment, duplicate payment, and fraud.
2. **Out-of-sequence/backdated approvals (1002):** Authorization controls bypassed.
3. **Retroactive purchase orders (1004):** Indicates uncontrolled spend and possible policy violations.

**Recommendation:** Cases 1002 and 1003 warrant immediate audit/investigation as potential fraud or control-breakdown cases; 1004 should be reviewed for maverick-buying policy enforcement and timestamp accuracy.
