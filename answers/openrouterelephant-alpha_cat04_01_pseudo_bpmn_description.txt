Below is a detailed explanation of the pseudo-BPMN process, covering the business purpose of each step, the role of gateways, path convergence, and how standard vs. custom requests affect the flow, including triggers for approvals and rework.

---

## **Overview of the Business Process**

This process handles **customer requests** for products or services, which can be either **Standard** (predefined offerings) or **Custom** (tailored solutions). The goal is to validate the request, determine feasibility, perform necessary checks, obtain approvals if needed, and finally generate an invoice and send confirmation.

The process is designed to **branch based on request type**, handle **parallel validation steps**, and **loop back** in case of rejection or conditions requiring re-evaluation.

---

## **Step-by-Step Breakdown**

### **1. Start Event**
- **Purpose**: Initiates the process when a customer request is received.
- **Trigger**: A new customer request enters the system.

---

### **2. Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**
- **Purpose**: Capture and log the customer𠏋 request.
- **Outcome**: The system identifies whether the request is **Standard** or **Custom**.

---

### **3. Gateway (XOR): "Check Request Type"**
- **Type**: Exclusive OR (XOR)
- **Meaning**: The process follows **one** of two mutually exclusive paths:
  - **Standard Path** (if request type = Standard)
  - **Custom Path** (if request type = Custom)

---

## **Standard Path (If Request Type = Standard)**

### **4. Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
- **Purpose**: Apply predefined validation rules (e.g., creditworthiness, stock availability).
- **Scope**: Faster and rule-based due to standardized processes.

---

### **5. Gateway (AND): "Run Parallel Checks"**
- **Type**: AND (Parallel)
- **Meaning**: **Multiple checks occur simultaneously**:
  - **Credit Check (C1)**
  - **Inventory Check (C2)**
- **Purpose**: Ensure the standard request meets all necessary conditions in parallel to save time.

---

### **6. Join: "All Parallel Checks Completed"**
- **Purpose**: Synchronize the process after parallel tasks complete.
- **Outcome**: Proceeds only when **both checks are done**, regardless of order.

---

### **7. Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**
- **Purpose**: Based on inventory and credit results, determine when the standard product can be delivered.
- **Outcome**: Sets a realistic delivery timeline.

---

## **Custom Path (If Request Type = Custom)**

### **8. Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**
- **Purpose**: Analyze whether the custom request is technically and financially viable.
- **Outcome**: Determines if customization is possible.

---

### **9. Gateway (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**
- **Type**: Exclusive OR (XOR)
- **Branches**:
  - **If Yes  Proceed to quotation**
  - **If No  Send rejection and end**

---

### **10. Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"** (If Feasible)
- **Purpose**: Create a tailored price and terms document.
- **Outcome**: Ready for customer approval.

---

### **10. Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"** (If Not Feasible)
- **Purpose**: Inform the customer that the request cannot be fulfilled.
- **Outcome**: Process ends.

---

## **Approval Check (Common to Both Paths)**

After either **Task D (Standard)** or **Task E1 (Custom)**, the process reaches a common decision point:

### **11. Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**
- **Purpose**: Determine if management sign-off is required (based on policy, value, or risk).
- **Triggers for Approval**:
  - High-value transactions
  - Custom modifications
  - Credit risk flags
  - Policy-mandated thresholds

---

### **12. Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"**
- **Purpose**: Secure formal authorization from a manager.
- **Outcome**: Moves to final approval check.

---

### **13. Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**
- **Branches**:
  - **If Yes  Generate Final Invoice**
  - **If No  Re-evaluate Conditions**

---

### **14. Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"** (If Approval Denied)
- **Purpose**: Adjust terms, pricing, or feasibility based on feedback.
- **Loop Behavior**:
  - **For Custom Path**: Loops back to **Task E1** (re-prepare quotation)
  - **For Standard Path**: Loops back to **Task D** (re-calculate delivery or terms)
- **Purpose**: Allows iterative refinement before finalizing.

---

### **15. Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**
- **Purpose**: Finalize billing after approval (or if no approval needed).
- **Triggers**:
  - Approval granted
  - Or approval not required

---

### **16. Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**
- **Purpose**: Notify the customer of completion, delivery details, and invoice.
- **Outcome**: Closes the transaction loop.

---

### **17. End Event**
- **Purpose**: Marks the completion of the process.

---

## **Key Gateway Types and Their Meanings**

| Gateway Type | Symbol | Meaning |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **XOR (Exclusive)** |  XOR | Only one path is taken (e.g., request type, feasibility, approval) |
| **AND (Parallel)** |  AND | Multiple tasks run simultaneously |
| **Merge Point** |  Join | Waits for all parallel paths to complete |

---

## **Path Convergence and Divergence**

- **Divergence**:
  - After **Task A**, the process splits into **Standard** or **Custom**.
  - After **Custom Path**, there's internal branching (feasibility  quotation or rejection).

- **Convergence**:
  - After **Standard Path (D)** or **Custom Quotation (E1)**, the flow merges at the **Approval Check**.
  - This ensures consistent handling of approvals regardless of path.

- **Looping**:
  - If **approval is denied** or **re-evaluation is needed**, the process loops:
    - **Custom Path**: Back to **E1** (revise quotation)
    - **Standard Path**: Back to **D** (adjust delivery/terms)
  - This supports **continuous improvement** and **negotiation cycles**.

---

## **How Tasks Differ: Standard vs. Custom**

| Aspect | Standard Request | Custom Request |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Validation** | Predefined checks (credit, inventory) | Feasibility analysis (technical, financial) |
| **Quotation** | Not needed (fixed pricing) | Custom quotation required |
| **Approval Triggers** | Based on value/risk thresholds | Often required due to customization |
| **Complexity** | Lower, faster execution | Higher, requires analysis and iteration |
| **Rejection Handling** | Not applicable (unless validation fails) | Explicit rejection notice if infeasible |

---

## **Conditions for Approval & Rework**

- **Approval Needed When**:
  - Transaction exceeds set thresholds (e.g., value, risk)
  - Custom modifications are involved
  - Credit or inventory flags are raised

- **Rework Triggered When**:
  - Manager denies approval
  - Feasibility fails (custom path)
  - Terms are unacceptable to customer or internal policy

- **Looping Logic**:
  - Ensures that issues are resolved **before finalization**
  - Prevents premature invoicing or delivery commitments

---

## **Conclusion**

This process is a **flexible, conditional workflow** designed to handle both **standardized and customized customer demands**. It uses **XOR gateways** for decision branching, **AND gateways** for parallel validation, and **looping** to allow rework based on feedback. The **approval gate** acts as a control point to ensure compliance and risk management, while the **final invoice and confirmation** mark successful fulfillment. The structure supports **scalability**, **auditability**, and **customer responsiveness**.
