This is a Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagram illustrating an order-to-delivery workflow that spans four departments: **Sales**, **Production**, **Procurement**, and **Warehouse**. It shows how information, materials, and products flow between these units to fulfill a customer order.

Here𠏋 a breakdown of each section:

---

### **1. Sales (Top Lane)**
- **Triggers**: Begins with a **timer event** labeled *"Monday morning"*, which initiates the process.
- **Process**:
  1. Check order for completeness.
  2. Check if the customer is already in the database.
  3. **Gateway (XOR)**: If the customer is **not** in the database, they are added (with a link to a *database* data store). If they are already in the database, this step is skipped.
  4. **Send order details**: This is a **send task** that communicates with other departments (Production and Procurement) via message flows (dashed lines).
  5. **Ends** with a **message receive event** (*Product sent*) and then a final task: *Draft invoice*.

---

### **2. Production (Second Lane)**
- **Trigger**: Starts when *Order details arrive* (a **message catch event** from Sales).
- **Process**:
  1. **Gateway (AND)** splits the flow:
     - One path goes to *Plan production*.
     - The other waits for a *Material not faulty* signal (from Procurement).
  2. **Gateway (AND)** merges both paths before *Manufacture product*.
  3. After manufacturing, a **send event** (*Product complete*) sends a message to Warehouse and Procurement.

---

### **3. Procurement (Third Lane)**
- **Trigger**: Starts when *Order details arrive* (a **message catch event** from Sales).
- **Process**:
  1. **Material Compensation Subprocess**: A bundled subprocess that handles:
     - Ordering materials.
     - Optionally returning materials (a compensation activity).
  2. **Check materials**: A task after the subprocess.
  3. **Gateway (XOR)**:
     - If materials are OK  sends *Material not faulty* message to Production (enabling manufacturing).
     - If materials are not OK  sends *Order cancellation* message (which could cancel the production flow).

---

### **4. Warehouse (Bottom Lane)**
- **Trigger**: Starts when *Product complete* is received from Production.
- **Process**:
  1. **Send product**: A task to dispatch the finished product.
  2. Ends with a **send event** (*Product sent*) that notifies Sales, closing the loop.

---

### **Key Interactions (Message Flows)**
- **Sales  Production & Procurement**: Sends order details.
- **Procurement  Production**: Sends *"Material not faulty"* to allow production to proceed.
- **Production  Warehouse & Procurement**: Sends *"Product complete"* to trigger shipping and material compensation.
- **Warehouse  Sales**: Sends *"Product sent"* to trigger invoicing in Sales.

---

### **Notable BPMN Elements**
- **Pool/Lane structure**: Each department is a separate lane, indicating separate actors.
- **Message flows (dashed lines)**: Show asynchronous communication between departments.
- **Events**: Timer start (Sales), message catches (Production, Procurement, Warehouse), message throws (Sales, Warehouse).
- **Gateways**: XOR (exclusive choice), AND (parallel branching/merging).
- **Subprocess**: Material Compensation in Procurement, showing a nested activity.

This diagram effectively maps a cross-functional process with clear handoffs, decision points, and interdepartmental coordination.
