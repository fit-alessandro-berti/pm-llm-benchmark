**Standard P2P Flow Expectation:**

A typical, compliant P2P process generally follows this sequence:
1.  **Create Purchase Requisition (PR):** Internal request for goods/services.
2.  **Approve Purchase Requisition:** Internal authorization based on budget, need, policy.
3.  **Issue Purchase Order (PO):** Formal order sent to the vendor.
4.  **Receive Goods (GR) / Service Confirmation:** Confirmation that ordered items/services have arrived.
5.  **Vendor Invoice Received (IR):** Vendor sends the bill.
6.  **Match Invoice to PO (and potentially GR):** Verification step (often a 2-way or 3-way match) ensuring the invoice matches the order and goods received (quantity, price).
7.  **Release Payment:** Payment is made to the vendor after successful matching.
8.  **Close Case:** Administrative closure.

*Note:* The order of `Receive Goods` and `Vendor Invoice Received` can sometimes vary, but both typically precede `Match Invoice to PO` and `Release Payment`. A PO must always precede Goods Receipt and Payment release. Approval must precede PO issuance.
