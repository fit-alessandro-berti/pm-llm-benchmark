**High-Level Process Steps**

Based on the temporal sequence, resource involvement, and logical manufacturing flow observed in the event log, I have aggregated the low-level events into **five high-level process steps**. These groupings balance granularity with meaningful process phases.

### 1. Material Preparation
**Low-level events included:**
- Retrieve raw metal sheet
- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet
- Preheat metal sheet

**Rationale:**  
These events represent the initial handling and conditioning of the raw material. They are performed sequentially by Operator A and supporting equipment (Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1) before any value-adding fabrication begins. The activities focus on material acquisition, identification, positioning, and thermal preparation.

**Alternative name options:** "Raw Material Setup" or "Component Preparation"

### 2. Welding Assembly
**Low-level events included:**
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B

**Rationale:**  
This grouping captures the core fabrication/assembly phase. All three events are executed by the same resource (Operator B) using the welding tool. The sequence clearly represents the physical joining of the prepared metal sheet.

**Alternative name options:** "Fabrication" or "Spot Welding"

### 3. Post-Weld Quality Verification
**Low-level events included:**
- Measure weld integrity

**Rationale:**  
This is a targeted, automated quality control step performed immediately after welding (by Quality Sensor #1). It serves as an in-process check on the just-completed assembly work before the product moves to surface treatment.

**Alternative name options:** "Weld Integrity Check" or "In-Process Inspection"

### 4. Surface Treatment
**Low-level events included:**
- Apply protective coating
- Dry coating

**Rationale:**  
These two events form a clear finishing sub-process. They involve applying a protective layer (Coating Unit #3) followed by a required thermal curing step (Heating Unit #2). This phase occurs after structural work is complete and verified.

**Alternative name options:** "Coating Application" or "Protective Finishing"

### 5. Final Inspection
**Low-level events included:**
- Visual check

**Rationale:**  
This represents the final human quality gate. Performed by Operator C, it serves as an overall visual verification of the completed product after all manufacturing and treatment steps are finished.

**Alternative name options:** "Final Quality Check" or "Product Verification"
