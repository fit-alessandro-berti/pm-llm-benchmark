## POWL operators

### 1) Multiple IQC checks in a row (inner loop)
\[
\textbf{MultiIQC} = *(\text{IQC}, \tau_{1})
\]
Meaning: execute **IQC**, and if needed repeat **IQC** again (the  does not show up in the log).

---

### 2) Assembly with optional/repeated IQC rework (outer loop)
\[
\textbf{AssemblyPhase} = *(\text{AS}, \text{MultiIQC})
\]
Meaning: execute **AS**; either exit to testing, or execute **MultiIQC** and then do **AS** again (repeating as long as required).

This captures:
- Case 4: AS  (exit loop immediately; no IQC)
- Case 1/2/5: AS  IQC  AS  exit (and potentially multiple IQC/AS cycles in Case 5)
- Case 3: allows IQC, IQC (via **MultiIQC**) before the next AS

---

### 3) Testing with possible re-testing (loop)
\[
\textbf{TestPhase} = *(\text{TST}, \text{RT})
\]
Meaning: execute **TST**; either exit to packaging, or do **RT** and then return to **TST** (repeat as many failures occur).

---

### 4) Optional labeling (XOR)
\[
\textbf{LabelingOption} = X(\text{LB}, \tau_{2})
\]
Meaning: either do **LB** or skip it.

---

## Root StrictPartialOrder (overall process order)
Define the root as a strict partial order over these nodes:
- **OR**
- **MS**
- **QS**
- **AssemblyPhase**
- **TestPhase**
- **PK**
- **LabelingOption**
- **DP**
- **D**

Overall partial order:
\[
\textbf{PO}=(nodes=\{OR,MS,QS,AssemblyPhase,TestPhase,PK,LabelingOption,DP,D\},\ order=\{
\]
- **OR  MS**
- **MS  QS**
- **QS  AssemblyPhase**
- **AssemblyPhase  TestPhase**
- **TestPhase  PK**
- **PK  LabelingOption**
- **LabelingOption  DP**
- **DP  D**
\(\})\)

**Concurrency:** none is required by the example log (everything is constrained in sequence by the added edges).
