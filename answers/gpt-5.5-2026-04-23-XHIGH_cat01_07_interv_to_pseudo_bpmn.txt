## BPMN-like Process Model: Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling

**Scope:** From receipt of supplier invoice to either payment scheduling or rejection.

---

## 1. Participants / Lanes

1. **Supplier** — Sends invoice; provides missing PO, corrected invoice, or clarification.
2. **Accounts Payable Clerk / AP Clerk** — Receives invoice, checks PO, logs invoice, coordinates clarifications, performs system match, schedules payment.
3. **Accounting System** — Stores invoice record; sends manager approval notification.
4. **Purchasing Officer / Purchasing Department** — Confirms PO reference, receipt of goods/services, quantities, prices, and delivery charges.
5. **Department Manager** — Performs final business approval or rejects/requests investigation.
6. **Accounts Payable Supervisor** — Handles escalated exceptions and unresolved discrepancies.
7. **Supplier Relationship Manager** — Optional support role for supplier communication and contract/pricing clarification.
8. **Department Heads** — Consulted in exceptional cases where a retrospective PO may be needed.

---

## 2. Key Data Objects / Artifacts

- Supplier invoice, usually received by email.
- Purchase order number / PO reference.
- Purchase order record.
- Goods/services receipt confirmation.
- Accounting system invoice record.
- Purchasing confirmation email.
- Discrepancy note / invoice hold status.
- Corrected or revised invoice.
- Contract or agreed supplier terms.
- Retrospective PO, if created.
- Manager approval notification.
- Manager approval/rejection record.
- Payment schedule / payment run entry.

---

## 3. Main Process Flow

### Start Event

**E0 — Invoice Received**  
- **Lane:** Supplier  AP Clerk  
- Supplier sends invoice, typically by email, to Accounts Payable.

---

### PO Validation

**T1 — Receive and Review Invoice**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP Clerk receives the invoice and checks whether it includes a PO number/reference.

**G1 — Is a valid PO reference present?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If No:**  
- Go to **SP1 — Exception Resolution Subprocess**, reason: `Missing or invalid PO`.

**If Yes:**  
- Continue to **T2**.

---

### Invoice Logging

**T2 — Log Invoice in Accounting System**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP Clerk enters the invoice into the accounting system using the invoice details and PO reference.  
- **Output:** Accounting system invoice record.

---

### Purchasing Confirmation

**T3 — Forward Invoice to Purchasing**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  Purchasing  
- AP Clerk sends invoice details and PO reference to Purchasing for confirmation.

**T4 — Verify Receipt and PO Details**  
- **Lane:** Purchasing Officer  
- Purchasing compares the invoice with the PO and checks:
  - PO validity.
  - Whether goods/services were received.
  - Quantities.
  - Pricing.
  - Delivery charges.
  - Any obvious mismatch with what was ordered.

**G2 — Can Purchasing Confirm the Invoice?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If PO is missing/invalid:**  
- Purchasing informs AP that processing cannot continue.  
- Go to **SP1 — Exception Resolution Subprocess**, reason: `Missing or invalid PO`.

**If discrepancy exists:**  
Examples: extra units billed, incorrect price, delivery charge issue, missing items, mismatch with agreed terms.  
- Go to **SP1 — Exception Resolution Subprocess**, reason: `Purchasing discrepancy`.

**If everything is correct:**  
- Continue to **T5**.

**T5 — Send Purchasing Confirmation to AP**  
- **Lane:** Purchasing Officer  AP Clerk  
- Purchasing sends confirmation email to AP.

---

### AP Matching

**T6 — Match Invoice Line Items Against PO in System**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP Clerk performs the formal invoice-to-PO match in the accounting system, using the Purchasing confirmation.

**G3 — Does the Invoice Match the PO?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If No:**  
- Continue to **T7**.

**If Yes:**  
- Continue to **T8**.

**T7 — Request Clarification from Purchasing**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  Purchasing  
- AP Clerk emails Purchasing to clarify the discrepancy and places/keeps the invoice on hold.  
- Go to **SP1 — Exception Resolution Subprocess**, reason: `AP matching discrepancy`.

---

### Manager Approval

**T8 — Send Manager Approval Notification**  
- **Lane:** Accounting System  
- Triggered only after:
  - Goods/services receipt has been confirmed by Purchasing.
  - Invoice has been matched against the PO by AP.
- System sends automatic approval request to the Department Manager.  
- Notification includes invoice details, PO, and Purchasing confirmation.

**T9 — Review Invoice for Final Approval**  
- **Lane:** Department Manager  
- Manager reviews:
  - Invoice details.
  - PO.
  - Purchasing confirmation.
  - Quantities and prices.
  - Special notes.
  - Budget reasonableness.
  - Unexpected items.

**G4 — Manager Decision**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If Approved:**  
- Continue to **T10**.

**If Suspicious / Needs Investigation:**  
- Continue to **T11**.

**If Rejected:**  
- Continue to **T13**.

**T10 — Approve Invoice in System**  
- **Lane:** Department Manager  
- Manager approves the invoice in the accounting system.

**T12 — Schedule Invoice for Payment**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP Clerk schedules the approved invoice for the next payment cycle/payment run.

**E1 — End Event: Invoice Scheduled for Payment**

---

### Manager Investigation / Rejection Paths

**T11 — Ask AP to Hold and Investigate**  
- **Lane:** Department Manager  AP Clerk  
- Manager requests investigation, for example due to:
  - Large difference from budget.
  - Unexpected items.
  - Suspicious total amount.
- Go to **SP1 — Exception Resolution Subprocess**, reason: `Manager concern`.

**T13 — Record Rejection / Notify Relevant Parties**  
- **Lane:** Department Manager / AP Clerk  
- Invoice is rejected in the system or communicated back to AP.  
- AP may notify the supplier if needed.

**E2 — End Event: Invoice Rejected**

---

## 4. Exception Resolution Subprocess: SP1

**SP1 — Resolve Invoice Exception**  
Triggered from one of the following gateways/tasks:

- **G1:** Missing or invalid PO at initial AP check.
- **G2:** Purchasing cannot confirm invoice.
- **G3/T7:** AP cannot match invoice to PO.
- **G4/T11:** Manager requests investigation.

---

### SP1 Flow

**SP1-T1 — Put Invoice on Hold and Record Issue**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP marks the invoice as on hold or otherwise prevents it from moving to payment approval.  
- AP documents the issue: missing PO, pricing discrepancy, quantity issue, missing information, manager concern, etc.

---

**SP1-G1 — What Type of Exception Is It?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

#### Case A: Missing or Invalid PO

**SP1-T2A — Request PO or Revised Invoice from Supplier**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk / Supplier Relationship Manager  Supplier  
- AP, and sometimes the Supplier Relationship Manager, asks the supplier to provide the missing PO number or send a revised invoice.

**SP1-T3A — Supplier Provides PO or Revised Invoice**  
- **Lane:** Supplier  AP Clerk  
- Supplier sends missing PO information or corrected invoice.

Proceed to **SP1-G2 — Is the Issue Resolved?**

---

#### Case B: Invoice Does Not Match PO / Receipt / Terms

**SP1-T2B — Clarify Discrepancy**  
- **Lane:** Purchasing / AP Clerk / Supplier Relationship Manager  
- Possible actions:
  - Purchasing contacts supplier directly.
  - Purchasing asks AP to hold the invoice until supplier sends corrected version.
  - AP emails Purchasing to clarify the mismatch.
  - Supplier Relationship Manager checks contract/agreed terms.
  - Supplier Relationship Manager confirms correct price or asks supplier for revised invoice.

Proceed to **SP1-G2 — Is the Issue Resolved?**

---

#### Case C: No Matching PO Exists / Informal Service Purchase

**SP1-T2C — Escalate No-PO Case**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  AP Supervisor  
- AP escalates to AP Supervisor when the invoice cannot be matched to any PO.

**SP1-T3C — Determine Whether Retrospective PO Is Allowed**  
- **Lane:** AP Supervisor / Department Heads  
- AP Supervisor coordinates with department heads to determine whether:
  - A retrospective PO should be created, or
  - The invoice should be rejected.

Proceed to **SP1-G2 — Is the Issue Resolved?**

---

#### Case D: Manager Concern

**SP1-T2D — Investigate Manager Concern**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk / Purchasing / AP Supervisor / Supplier Relationship Manager  
- AP investigates the suspicious item, budget variance, unexpected charge, or other concern raised by the manager.
- Depending on the issue, AP may involve Purchasing, AP Supervisor, Supplier Relationship Manager, or the supplier.

Proceed to **SP1-G2 — Is the Issue Resolved?**

---

### Resolution Gateway

**SP1-G2 — Is the Issue Resolved?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If Yes:**  
- Continue to **SP1-T4**.

**If No, but potentially resolvable:**  
- Continue to **SP1-T5**.

**If No and invoice should not be paid:**  
- Go to **T13 — Record Rejection / Notify Relevant Parties** and then **E2 — Invoice Rejected**.

---

### Escalation

**SP1-T5 — Escalate to AP Supervisor**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  AP Supervisor  
- Used when the discrepancy cannot be resolved easily.

**SP1-T6 — Supervisor Coordinates Resolution**  
- **Lane:** AP Supervisor  
- AP Supervisor may:
  - Contact supplier.
  - Coordinate between AP and Purchasing.
  - Involve department heads.
  - Ask Supplier Relationship Manager to help with supplier communication or contract/pricing clarification.
  - Decide whether a retrospective PO is needed.
  - Recommend rejection if invoice cannot be validated.

**SP1-G3 — Supervisor Outcome**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If corrected invoice / valid PO / confirmed terms / retrospective PO is obtained:**  
- Continue to **SP1-T4**.

**If invoice should be rejected:**  
- Go to **T13 — Record Rejection / Notify Relevant Parties** and then **E2 — Invoice Rejected**.

**If still awaiting supplier or department response:**  
- Remain in hold state and loop back to **SP1-T6** when new information arrives.

---

### Return to Main Flow

**SP1-T4 — Update Invoice Record and Attach Resolution Evidence**  
- **Lane:** AP Clerk  
- AP updates the invoice record with:
  - Corrected invoice.
  - Valid PO number.
  - Retrospective PO.
  - Confirmed price/quantity.
  - Supplier clarification.
  - Contract confirmation.
  - Manager investigation notes.

**SP1-G4 — Where Should the Invoice Re-enter the Main Flow?**  
- **Type:** XOR Gateway  

**If PO or invoice data changed, or the invoice was not previously logged:**  
- Return to **T1/T2 — Check PO and Log/Update Invoice**.

**If Purchasing confirmation must be obtained or reconfirmed:**  
- Return to **T3 — Forward Invoice to Purchasing**.

**If only a manager concern was clarified and no invoice/PO data changed:**  
- Return to **T8 — Send/Update Manager Approval Notification**, then **T9 — Manager Review**.

---

## 5. Important Consolidation Notes / Ambiguities

1. **AP and Purchasing both perform matching-like checks.**  
   The model separates these as:
   - Purchasing performs a business/receipt/order confirmation.
   - AP performs the formal invoice-to-PO match in the accounting system.

2. **Missing PO handling varies by account.**  
   Mary described sending the invoice back to the supplier immediately. Rita described escalation for invoices with no matching PO, especially informal service invoices. The model therefore uses direct supplier request first, then supervisor escalation if unresolved.

3. **Discrepancy resolution may be handled by different actors.**  
   Purchasing may contact the supplier directly, AP may hold the invoice, Rita may coordinate exceptions, and Karen may assist with supplier/contract issues. These are modeled as alternative paths within the exception subprocess.

4. **Supervisor and Supplier Relationship Manager do not provide final approval.**  
   They help resolve issues, but final business approval remains with the Department Manager.

5. **The process ends at payment scheduling.**  
   The actual execution of the payment run is not described in the interviews, so it is outside the modeled scope.
