To model the insurance claim process as a Petri net, the solution involves a structured approach to mapping activities and states. Each activity is represented as a transition, and each state or condition is represented as a place. The initial marking starts the process with a token in the "claim_filed" place, and the final marking ends with a token in the "closed" place, indicating the claim has been processed and closed.

The main flow of the process is as follows:
1. **Claim Filed**: The initial state where a claim is filed.
2. **Claim Registration (CR)**: The first activity that logs the claim into the system.
3. **Document Verification (DV)**: Verifies the customer's documents.
4. **Fraud Check (FC)**: Checks for any fraudulent activity.
5. **Claim Assessment (CA)**: Assesses the claim by an adjuster.
6. **Approval (AP)**: Approves the claim if the amount surpasses a certain threshold.
7. **Payment (P)**: Pays out the approved claim.
8. **Notification (N)**: Notifies the customer about the claim resolution.
9. **Closure (CL)**: Closes and archives the claim.

The model includes loops for re-submission of documents and in-depth investigations, which are handled by transitions that loop back to the appropriate input places. This ensures that the process can revisit previous steps when necessary, such as when documents need resubmission or when further investigation is required.
