**Labeled Activities from Process Mining Analysis**

After reviewing the event log, distinct patterns emerge in the sensor data across the 15 events (one clear process cycle on Machine M1, starting and ending in the same state). I grouped consecutive events with similar sensor signatures (temperature, pressure, vibration, material flow, tool position, energy). These groups correspond to logical manufacturing steps. The cycle is: Idle  Cutting  Assembly/Pressing  Welding  Cooling  Inspection/Finishing  Idle.

Here are the inferred activities, grouped events, and rationale for each label (based on typical manufacturing sensor behaviors: vibration + material flow often = removal/cutting; pressure increases = clamping/assembly; extreme heat/energy spikes with no flow = welding; low stable readings = idle/inspection):

- **Idle/Standby** (Events 1-3 and 13-15)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Temperature ~20-21°C, pressure = 1 bar, vibration = 0 Hz, material flow = 0, tool position = 0 mm, energy ~0.40-0.50 kWh (baseline across all metrics).  
  **Rationale**: These are the clear "off" or waiting states at the beginning and end of the cycle. No mechanical activity, heat, or movement is occurring. This is the machine at rest between parts or after completion.

- **Cutting Metal** (Events 4-6)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Temperature rising (5058°C), pressure 1-1.5 bar, high vibration (30-38 Hz), material flow = 5 units/s, tool position advancing (510 mm), energy rising (2.03.0 kWh).  
  **Rationale**: Significant vibration combined with consistent material flow strongly indicates mechanical material removal (e.g., sawing, milling, or drilling). Friction from the tool generates heat and increased energy consumption while the tool position steadily advances into the workpiece. This matches classic cutting signatures.

- **Assembling/Pressing Parts** (Events 7-8)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Temperature drops to 40°C, pressure rises to 2 bar, vibration drops to 10 Hz, reduced material flow = 2 units/s, tool position stable at 15 mm, energy stable at 2.0 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: The increase in pressure (likely pneumatic or hydraulic clamping) with reduced vibration and flow suggests the machine is now holding or joining components rather than removing material. The tool has advanced further and stabilized, consistent with assembly or pressing operations after cutting.

- **Welding** (Event 9)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Sharp spike — temperature = 80°C, pressure = 2 bar, very high vibration (50 Hz), material flow = 0, tool position stable at 15 mm, energy peaks at 5.0 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: Extreme temperature and energy consumption with zero material flow is characteristic of a fusion or joining process that generates intense localized heat (e.g., arc, resistance, or laser welding). High vibration may come from the welding equipment itself. This occurs after pressing/clamping, which is a logical prerequisite for welding.

- **Cooling** (Event 10)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Rapid drop — temperature = 30°C, pressure back to 1 bar, vibration near zero (1 Hz), material flow = 0, tool position stable at 15 mm, energy drops to 1.0 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: Immediate reduction in temperature, vibration, and energy following the welding spike indicates a post-process cooling phase (natural or assisted) to solidify the weld and prevent defects. This is a common transitional step.

- **Quality Inspection/Finishing** (Events 11-12)  
  **Sensor pattern**: Temperature ~25°C, pressure = 1 bar, low vibration (5 Hz), minimal material flow = 1 unit/s, tool position advances to 20 mm, energy ~1.5-1.6 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: Low activity levels with the tool moving to a final/extended position suggest non-invasive final steps such as measurement, visual/sensor-based quality inspection, or light finishing. Energy and vibration are minimal compared to earlier active phases, and it precedes return to idle.

**Overall Process Insight**:  
The data reveals a repeatable linear manufacturing sequence on a single machine: prepare/cut the material, assemble/press components, weld them, cool, inspect, and reset. Transitions are evident from abrupt changes in sensor values (e.g., vibration and flow drop after cutting; temperature/energy spike isolated to welding). No clear "Packaging" phase appears in this short log, but the final low-activity step could encompass light post-processing. This is a high-level process discovery from raw numeric data, typical in unsupervised process mining when activity labels are absent.
