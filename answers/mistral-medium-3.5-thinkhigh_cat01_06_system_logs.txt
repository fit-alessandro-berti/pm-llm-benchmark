### **Explanation of Transformation Logic**

#### **1. Case Identification**
- **Documents (Word/Excel/PDF):**  
  Each **unique filename** (e.g., `Quarterly_Report.docx`, `Budget_2024.xlsx`) represents a **case**. All events tied to the same filename (e.g., `FOCUS`, `TYPING`, `SAVE`) are grouped into its case.  
  - *Example:* All events for `Document1.docx` (even with interruptions from other apps) belong to the same case, as they pertain to the same document instance.  
  - *Rationale:* Documents are the core work units; switching between them reflects multitasking but does not split the case.

- **Email:**  
  The **subject/intent** of the email (inferred from the `Action` field: *"Open Email about Annual Meeting"*) defines the case (`Email_Annual_Meeting`).  
  - *Rationale:* Emails are discrete tasks; each email thread is a self-contained process.

- **Excluded Events:**  
  `SWITCH` events (e.g., switching from Word to Chrome) are **omitted** as they represent transitions between cases, not activities *within* a case.

---

#### **2. Activity Naming**
Raw low-level actions (e.g., `FOCUS`, `TYPING`) are **abstracted into higher-level process steps** for clarity and analytical value:

| **Raw Action**       | **Context**               | **Mapped Activity**          | **Rationale**                                                                 |
|----------------------|---------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `FOCUS`              | Document (Word/Excel)     | `Open Document` / `Resume Editing` | First `FOCUS` = start; subsequent `FOCUS` = resumption after interruption.   |
| `TYPING`             | Document                  | `Edit Content`              | Generic term for content creation/modification.                              |
| `SAVE`               | Document/Spreadsheet     | `Save Document` / `Save Spreadsheet` | Explicit save actions.                                       |
| `CLOSE`              | Document                  | `Close Document`            | End of document session.                                     |
| `CLICK` (Open Email) | Email                     | `Open Email`                | Initiates email handling.                                     |
| `SCROLL`             | Email/PDF                 | `Read Email` / `Review Document` | Passive consumption of content.                              |
| `CLICK` (Reply)      | Email                     | `Start Reply`               | Explicit transition to composition.                           |
| `TYPING`             | Email                     | `Compose Reply`             | Active email response.                                       |
| `CLICK` (Send)       | Email                     | `Send Email`                | Finalizes the email task.                                     |
| `HIGHLIGHT`          | PDF                       | `Highlight Key Findings`    | Contextual annotation (derived from `Text` field).           |
| `TYPING` (Excel)     | Spreadsheet               | `Update Data` / `Modify Structure` | Differentiates data entry (`Q1 figures`) vs. structural changes (`new row`). |

- **Standardization:**  
  - **Documents:** Activities like `Open`, `Edit`, `Save`, `Close` are reused across Word/Excel for consistency.  
  - **Email/PDF:** Specialized activities (e.g., `Send Email`, `Review Document`) reflect domain-specific workflows.  
  - **Contextual Nuance:** For Excel, `TYPING` is split into `Update Data` (numeric changes) and `Modify Structure` (layout edits) based on the `Keys` field, adding granularity for process analysis.
