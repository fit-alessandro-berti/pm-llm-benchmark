### **Process Mining Analysis & Optimization Strategy for Speedy Parcels**

As a Process Mining Consultant, I propose a structured analysis leveraging the rich multi-source event data to transform Speedy Parcels' operations. The following approach addresses discovery, performance, root causes, optimization, and monitoring, tailored to the complexities of last-mile logistics.

---

### **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### **Preprocessing and Data Integration**
To build a cohesive event log, we must harmonize the heterogeneous data sources into a unified schema where each event represents a step in the delivery process.

*   **Integration Strategy:**
    *   **Case ID Alignment:** Use `Vehicle-Shift` (e.g., `V12-20241205`) as the primary case ID. If shifts span midnight, split cases at shift boundaries.
    *   **Event Granularity:** Create a unified event stream. For each `Case ID`, merge events based on timestamps.
        *   *Scanner Events:* Act as anchors for customer interactions. Enrich with GPS attributes (speed, location) at the exact timestamp.
        *   *GPS Events:* Aggregate into meaningful events. For example, convert high-frequency GPS "Idle" streams into "Travel Delay" or "Parking Search" events if duration exceeds thresholds.
        *   *Dispatch Events:* Inject planned data as attributes on relevant events. E.g., attach `Planned_Arrival_Window` to the `Arrive Customer` event and `Planned_Route_Seq` to enable conformance checking.
        *   *Maintenance Events:* Link to cases via time overlap. If `Unscheduled Repair` overlaps with a `Case ID`, flag the case with `Maintenance_Event = True` and attach duration.
    *   **Handling Missing Data:** Impute missing GPS data using interpolation if gaps are short; otherwise, flag as low-confidence. Handle scanner failures by cross-referencing GPS location matches with planned stop locations.

*   **Challenges:**
    *   *Temporal Alignment:* GPS intervals (e.g., every 10s) vs. Scanner events require careful mapping.
    *   *Ambiguity:* Distinguishing "Traffic Jam" from "Parking Search" using only GPS requires heuristic rules (e.g., speed < 5km/h for > 3 mins near customer loc vs. speed fluctuation).
    *   *Volume:* Six months of high-frequency GPS data creates massive volume; aggregation strategies are needed before mining to ensure performance.

#### **Process Discovery**
*   **Algorithm Selection:** Use the **Inductive Miner** or **Heuristic Miner** algorithm to discover the process model. These are robust to noise and handle loops (e.g., re-visiting a stop for a failed delivery) well.
*   **Visualization:** Generate a process map showing the *actual* flow. Nodes will include `Depart Depot`, `Travel`, `Arrive Customer`, `Service`, `Depart Customer`, `Unscheduled Stop`, and `Return Depot`. Edges will be weighted by frequency and average duration.
*   **Variant Analysis:** Identify the top process variants. In last-mile delivery, we expect a "Happy Path" (all successful) and variants for `Failed Delivery` (requiring re-attempt or return to depot) and `Maintenance Interruption`.

#### **Conformance Checking**
*   **Method:** Align the discovered model against the *Planned Route* from the Dispatch system using the **Heuristic Alignments** algorithm.
*   **Deviations to Detect:**
    *   *Sequence Deviations:* Driver visiting Stop B before Stop A despite dispatch order.
    *   *Timing Deviations:* Significant variance between `Planned_Arrival` and `Actual_Arrive`.
    *   *Missing/Extra Events:* `Arrive Customer` logged but GPS shows no proximity to location; or `Depart Depot` missing.
    *   *Unplanned Stops:* GPS stops that do not match any `Planned Stop` location, potentially indicating unauthorized breaks or ad-hoc pickups.

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
*   **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTD):** % of `Delivery Success` events where `Actual_Arrive` falls within `Dispatch_Time_Window`.
*   **Average Cycle Time:** `Time(End Shift) - Time(Depart Depot)`.
*   **Service Time Variability:** Standard deviation of `Time(Depart Customer) - Time(Arrive Customer)` across cases. High variability indicates unpredictable customer interactions.
*   **Travel-to-Service Ratio:** `Total_GPS_Travel_Time / Total_Scanner_Service_Time`. High ratios suggest inefficiency in routing or traffic.
*   **Fuel Efficiency Proxy:** `Packages_Delivered / Total_GPS_Distance`. Correlate with `Avg_Speed` to identify speed-related fuel waste.
*   **Vehicle Utilization:** `Active_Delivery_Time / Shift_Duration`.
*   **Failed Delivery Rate:** `Count(Delivery Failed) / Total_Attempts`.

*Calculation:* These are computed by aggregating attributes from the integrated event log. For example, OTD requires joining Scanner events with Dispatch attributes and evaluating the timestamp against the time window logic.

#### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
*   **Performance Overlay:** Color the process map based on average duration. Red paths indicate slow routes or high-delay activities.
*   **Variant Performance Comparison:** Compare the average cycle time and OTD of the top 5% most efficient variants vs. the bottom 5% least efficient. This highlights structural inefficiencies.
*   **Correlation Analysis:** Use statistical correlation to link delays to attributes:
    *   *Time of Day:* Do delays spike at 8:00-9:00 AM?
    *   *Location:* Cluster GPS coordinates to identify "Hotspots" where dwell times or travel speeds degrade.
    *   *Driver/Driver ID:* Analyze performance distribution across drivers to identify skill-based bottlenecks.
*   **Dwell Time Analysis:** Plot histograms of service times. Identify outliers where dwell time exceeds the 95th percentile, indicating specific issues (e.g., parking difficulties, access problems).

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

#### **Potential Root Causes & Mining Validation**
*   **Suboptimal Static Routing:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Dispatch uses static routes that don't account for time-dependent traffic.
    *   *Validation:* Perform **Time-Dependent Variant Analysis**. Compare routes taken by high-performing drivers vs. low-performing drivers on the same day. If high performers deviate from the plan to avoid specific segments, the static plan is likely suboptimal.
*   **Traffic Congestion & Estimation Errors:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Travel time estimates are inaccurate during peak hours.
    *   *Validation:* Correlate `GPS_Speed` with `Time_of_Day` and `Location`. Use **Process Mining to extract time-dependent travel time matrices**. If actual travel times consistently exceed estimates by >20% in specific zones, the estimation model is flawed.
*   **Service Time Variability:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* High variability in customer interaction time causes schedule slippage.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze **Dwell Time Distributions**. If the distribution is bimodal or has heavy tails, root causes may include "Customer Not Home" (requiring re-attempt) or "Complex Access" (e.g., gated communities). Correlate dwell time with `Package_Type` or `Customer_Location_Type`.
*   **Unscheduled Maintenance:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Reactive maintenance leads to costly mid-shift breakdowns.
    *   *Validation:* Correlate `Unscheduled_Stop` events with prior GPS patterns. Look for precursors like `High_Braking_Frequency`, `Low_Speed_Prolonged`, or `Engine_Temperature_Spike` in the hour before the stop. This validates predictive maintenance needs.
*   **Driver Behavior:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Aggressive driving increases fuel consumption and wear.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze GPS data for harsh acceleration/braking events. Correlate these events with `Fuel_Proxy` metrics and `Maintenance_Cost`. Use **Variant Analysis** to see if drivers with high "Hard Braking" counts have lower efficiency.

### **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Route Optimization with Real-Time Traffic Adaptation**
*   **Target:** Late deliveries, high fuel consumption.
*   **Root Cause:** Static routing ignores real-time congestion and time-dependent travel speeds.
*   **Mining Support:**
    *   Extract **Time-Dependent Travel Time Matrices** from 6 months of GPS data. Show that travel times between nodes vary significantly by hour.
    *   Identify **Traffic Hotspots** where conformance checking reveals consistent sequence deviations (drivers naturally detouring).
*   **Action:** Implement a routing engine that ingests the mined travel time matrix and real-time traffic feeds. Allow dynamic re-routing when a deviation threshold is breached (e.g., current delay > 10 mins).
*   **Expected Impact:** +15% OTD, -8% Fuel Cost, reduced overtime.

#### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance & Usage-Based Scheduling**
*   **Target:** Unscheduled repairs, operational disruptions.
*   **Root Cause:** Maintenance is scheduled by calendar or reactive, missing usage-based degradation signals.
*   **Mining Support:**
    *   Perform **Correlation Analysis** between GPS telemetry features (hard braking, idle time, speed variance) and `Maintenance_Log` timestamps.
    *   Discover **Precursor Patterns**: Use process mining to find sequences of events (e.g., `Event A` followed by `Event B` within 2 hours) that frequently precede `Unscheduled_Repair`.
*   **Action:** Develop a predictive model to flag vehicles at risk of failure. Shift maintenance to off-peak hours or depot visits based on predicted risk scores.
*   **Expected Impact:** -40% Unscheduled Breakdowns, +5% Vehicle Availability, reduced emergency repair costs.

#### **Strategy 3: Service Time Optimization & Customer Communication Loop**
*   **Target:** Failed deliveries, excessive dwell times.
*   **Root Cause:** High variability in service time and failed attempts due to poor time window alignment or customer access issues.
*   **Mining Support:**
    *   Analyze **Failed Delivery Patterns**: Correlate failures with `Time_of_Day`, `Customer_Location_Type`, and `Previous_Service_Time`.
    *   Identify **Parking/Access Bottlenecks**: Use GPS dwell analysis to flag stops where `Dwell_Time > Service_Time` significantly, indicating parking search or access delays.
*   **Action:**
    *   *Pre-delivery:* Send SMS with precise 15-minute windows based on mined travel reliability.
    *   *Routing:* Group stops by access difficulty (mined from GPS dwell variance).
    *   *Operations:* For locations with high parking search times, consider consolidation or alternative drop-off points.
*   **Expected Impact:** -25% Failed Delivery Rate, +10% Stops per Shift, improved customer satisfaction.

### **5. Operational Constraints and Monitoring**

#### **Accounting for Constraints**
*   **Driver Working Hours:** Optimization algorithms must include constraints on `Shift_Start`, `Shift_End`, and `Max_Driving_Time` derived from the log. Any proposed schedule must respect labor regulations.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** The `Dispatch_Package_Count` vs. `Vehicle_Capacity` must be a hard constraint in route optimization to prevent overloading.
*   **Customer Time Windows:** Hard and soft time windows from the Dispatch system must be incorporated as constraints in the dynamic routing strategy.
*   **Data Privacy:** Driver and customer PII must be anonymized in the process mining environment. Aggregation should be done at a level that prevents re-identification where necessary.

#### **Continuous Monitoring Plan**
*   **Process Mining Dashboard:**
    *   *Real-Time Conformance Monitor:* Alerts when a live case deviates significantly from the plan (e.g., "Vehicle V12 is 20 mins behind; predicted OTD risk high").
    *   *Performance Heatmaps:* Geographic heatmaps of delays and fuel efficiency to identify emerging hotspots.
    *   *KPI Tracker:* Daily/Weekly trends for OTD, Fuel/Package, and Maintenance costs.
*   **Key Metrics to Track:**
    *   **Process Quality:** % of cases conforming to the optimized model.
    *   **Operational Efficiency:** Variance of actual vs. planned travel times.
    *   **Predictive Accuracy:** Accuracy of the predictive maintenance model (Precision/Recall).
    *   **Driver Performance:** Leaderboards for efficiency and safety (based on mining insights), used for coaching rather than punishment.
*   **Feedback Loop:**
    *   Monthly re-mining of the event log to update process models and travel time matrices.
    *   A/B testing of optimization strategies: Compare performance of routes generated by the new model against the old dispatch logic over time.
    *   Close the loop by feeding optimization results back into the Dispatch System, creating a self-improving operational cycle.

This comprehensive approach leverages Speedy Parcels' data assets to move from reactive problem-solving to proactive, data-driven optimization, directly addressing punctuality and cost pressures while ensuring operational feasibility.
