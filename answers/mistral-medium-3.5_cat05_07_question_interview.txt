Here’s a list of targeted, open-ended questions to deepen your understanding of the property onboarding process, organized by key themes:

---

### **1. Documentation & Initial Verification**
- What are the most common missing or outdated documents you encounter from landlords, and how do you typically resolve these gaps?
- How do you ensure the property deeds and compliance certificates are authentic? Are there third-party services or internal checks involved?
- Are there any "non-negotiable" documents that would immediately disqualify a property if missing, or is there flexibility?
- How long does the initial documentation review usually take, and what factors can delay this step?

---

### **2. Property Manager Assignment**
- What criteria do you prioritize when matching a property manager to a new property (e.g., workload vs. specialization vs. landlord preferences)?
- How do you handle situations where no property manager has the exact specialization needed (e.g., luxury units in a new city)?
- Is there a formal handoff process between the coordinator and the assigned property manager? What does that look like?
- Do landlords ever request a specific property manager, and how do you handle such requests?

---

### **3. Landlord-Property Manager Alignment**
- What are the most frequent points of misalignment between landlords and property managers during the introductory call? How are these resolved?
- Are there standard policies (e.g., pet rules, maintenance responsibilities) that the company enforces, or are these fully customizable per landlord?
- How do you document the agreements made during this call (e.g., special conditions, payment channels)? Is this stored in the CRM or another system?
- Have you encountered landlords who refuse to compromise on unusual demands (e.g., custom lease clauses)? How are these handled?

---

### **4. Inspection & Repairs**
- What are the most common issues found during inspections, and how are they typically prioritized (e.g., safety vs. cosmetic)?
- Who bears the cost of repairs—always the landlord, or are there cases where the company covers it (e.g., to secure a high-value property)?
- How do you ensure contractors or repair services meet the company’s standards? Do you have a preferred vendor list?
- What happens if a landlord refuses to address critical inspection findings? Is there an escalation path or a point where you walk away?

---

### **5. Marketing & Activation**
- How do you determine the "expected rental price range" for a new property? Is this based on internal data, market benchmarks, or landlord input?
- What marketing channels do you prioritize (e.g., online listings, brochures, social media), and how do you tailor these to the property type?
- How often do pricing models get updated, and who is responsible for adjusting them if market conditions change?
- Is there a review process before a property is "activated" in the portal? Who signs off on this?

---

### **6. Tenant Screening & Lease Finalization**
- What are your non-negotiable criteria for tenant approval (e.g., credit score thresholds, income requirements)?
- How do you handle competing applications (e.g., multiple qualified tenants)? Are there tiebreakers like lease length or move-in date flexibility?
- Do you ever adjust screening criteria for specific properties (e.g., student housing vs. luxury units)?
- Who has the final authority to approve a tenant—the property manager, a regional director, or someone else?
- How do you handle situations where a tenant’s references or background check raise red flags but the landlord insists on approving them?

---
---
### **7. Exceptions & Edge Cases**
- What are the most unusual or challenging exceptions you’ve encountered in onboarding a property (e.g., special insurance, unique landlord demands)?
- How do you handle properties with legal or zoning complications (e.g., unpermitted renovations, short-term rental restrictions)?
- Are there properties you’ve had to reject after initial acceptance? What triggered those decisions?
- How do you balance standardizing the process with accommodating landlord-specific requests?

---
---
### **8. Timing & Dependencies**
- What is the average end-to-end time for onboarding a property, and what are the biggest bottlenecks?
- Are there parallel vs. sequential steps in the process? For example, can marketing begin before the inspection is finalized?
- How do you communicate delays to landlords, and what are their typical reactions?
- Is there a "fast-track" process for high-priority properties (e.g., those with pre-approved tenants)?

---
---
### **9. Roles & Responsibilities**
- How do the regional office, property coordinator, property manager, and support teams (e.g., marketing, compliance) collaborate? Are there overlapping responsibilities?
- Who is ultimately accountable if something goes wrong (e.g., a property is marketed with incorrect details, a lease violates regulations)?
- Do property managers have autonomy to make decisions (e.g., approving repairs, adjusting rent), or do they need approvals?
- How are disputes between teams (e.g., marketing vs. compliance) resolved?

---
---
### **10. Tools & Communication**
- What systems (CRM, project management tools, etc.) are used to track the onboarding process, and how do they integrate with each other?
- How do you ensure critical information (e.g., inspection results, landlord preferences) isn’t lost in email threads or handoffs?
- Do landlords have access to any portals or dashboards to monitor progress, or is communication entirely manual?
- What metrics do you track to measure the efficiency or success of the onboarding process (e.g., time-to-activate, landlord satisfaction)?

---
---
### **11. Continuous Improvement**
- What feedback have you received from landlords or property managers about pain points in the current process?
- Have you made any recent changes to the onboarding process to address inefficiencies or risks? What prompted those changes?
- Are there steps in the process that are still manual but could (or should) be automated?
- How do you stay updated on changing local regulations or market conditions that might impact onboarding?

---
---
These questions are designed to uncover nuances, decision-making logic, and potential gaps in the process while keeping the conversation focused on the *how* and *why* behind the workflow.
