### Explanation of the transformation logic  

1. **Case identification** – Each distinct *window title* (i.e., the document, email, PDF or spreadsheet the user was working in) was treated as a separate case.  
   - **Document1** and **Quarterly_Report** are two Word documents.  
   - **Email** represents the handling of the “Annual Meeting” email (the whole thread from opening to sending).  
   - **PDF** is the review of *Report_Draft.pdf*.  
   - **Excel** is the budget spreadsheet *Budget_2024.xlsx*.  

2. **Activity naming** – Raw lowlevel actions were mapped to higherlevel, processoriented activity names:  

   - `FOCUS`  “Open Document” (or “Open Spreadsheet” for Excel).  
   - `TYPING`  “Edit Document” / “Edit Spreadsheet” / “Compose Email”.  
   - `SAVE`  “Save Document” / “Save Spreadsheet”.  
   - `CLOSE`  “Close Document”.  
   - `SWITCH`  “Switch Application” (the transition from one work object to another).  
   - `CLICK (Open Email)`  “Open Email”.  
   - `SCROLL` (in email)  “Read Email”.  
   - `CLICK (Reply)`  “Reply to Email”.  
   - `CLICK (Send)`  “Send Email”.  
   - `SCROLL` (in PDF)  “Scroll PDF”.  
   - `HIGHLIGHT`  “Highlight Text”.  

   This yields a consistent vocabulary that can be directly used in processmining tools such as ProM, Disco, or Celonis.

3. **Additional attributes** – Besides the required *Case ID*, *Activity* and *Timestamp*, the original *Application* and *Window Title* columns were retained as optional attributes. For the `SWITCH` events the direction (“FromApp  ToApp” and “FromWindow  ToWindow”) is stored in the *Application* and *Window Title* fields to preserve context.

4. **Narrative** – The log now tells a clear story:  

   - The user opened the quarterly report, then switched to a Word draft (Document1), edited and saved it, switched to email to handle the AnnualMeeting message, replied and sent it, switched to a PDF review, highlighted a section, opened a budget spreadsheet to update numbers, switched back to Document1 to insert a budget reference, saved and closed it, finally returned to the quarterly report to add an executive summary, saved and closed it.  

This structured event log is ready for processmining techniques such as discovery, conformance checking, or variant analysis.
