### 2. Detailed Rationale for Deductions

#### **Activity: Idle / Standby (Events 1-3 & 13-15)**
*   **Rationale:** The machine is in a baseline state. Temperature is at room level ($\approx 20^\circ\text{C}$), energy consumption is at its minimum ($\approx 0.4\text{--}0.5\text{ kWh}$), and there is no vibration, material flow, or tool displacement. This indicates the machine is powered on but not performing work.

#### **Activity: Cutting Metal (Events 4-6)**
*   **Rationale:** This phase shows a transition from idle to active work. We see a steady increase in **Temperature** ($50 \to 58^\circ\text{C}$) and **Vibration** ($30 \to 38\text{ Hz}$), which are typical of friction from cutting. The **Material Flow** is constant (5 units/s) and the **Tool Position** is advancing ($5 \to 10\text{mm}$), indicating a machining process in progress.

#### **Activity: Assembling Parts (Events 7-8)**
*   **Rationale:** The vibration and temperature drop slightly compared to the cutting phase, but the **Pressure** reaches its peak (2 bar). The **Tool Position** stops moving and holds steady at 15mm. High pressure combined with a stationary tool suggests a "pressing" or "clamping" action used to join parts together.

#### **Activity: Welding (Event 9)**
*   **Rationale:** This is a high-intensity burst. There is a massive spike in **Temperature** ($80^\circ\text{C}$), **Energy consumption** ($5.00\text{ kWh}$), and **Vibration** ($50\text{ Hz}$). Since the material flow is 0 and the tool is still at 15mm, this indicates a localized high-energy application of heat, characteristic of a welding spot.

#### **Activity: Quality Inspection / Cooling (Event 10)**
*   **Rationale:** Following the weld, the temperature drops sharply ($80 \to 30^\circ\text{C}$) and the pressure returns to baseline. The tool remains at the 15mm position. This represents a dwell time where the part is likely being cooled or scanned by a sensor for quality assurance before being moved.

#### **Activity: Packaging / Ejection (Events 11-12)**
*   **Rationale:** The **Tool Position** moves to its furthest point (20mm), suggesting the finished part is being pushed out of the work area. The **Material Flow** is very low (1 unit/s), and energy usage is moderate, indicating a simple mechanical movement rather than a heavy machining process.
