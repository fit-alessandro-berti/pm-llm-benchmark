### 1. **Queue Identification and Characterization**  
#### Calculating Waiting Times:  
- **Definition of Waiting Time:** The waiting time (queue time) between two consecutive activities *A* and *B* for a patient is calculated as:  
  **Waiting Time = Start Time of Activity B ñ Completion Time of Activity A**.  
  This excludes the time spent actively in Activity A or B, focusing solely on inactivity between steps.  

- **Key Metrics:**  
  - **Average Waiting Time:** The mean waiting time between two activities.  
  - **Median Waiting Time:** To understand central tendency while reducing outlier influence.  
  - **90th/95th Percentile Waiting Time:** Identifies extreme delays affecting a minority of patients.  
  - **Maximum Waiting Time:** Highlights critical outliers.  
  - **Queue Frequency:** The percentage of cases experiencing waits exceeding a threshold (e.g., waits > 15 minutes).  
  - **Impact on Patient Types:** Segmentation by patient type (e.g., New vs. Follow-up) or urgency (Normal vs. Urgent) to prioritize queues affecting vulnerable groups.  

#### Identifying Critical Queues:  
Critical queues are determined by:  
1. **High Average Wait Times** (e.g., waits exceeding 20 minutes at the Doctor Consultation  Check-out queue).  
2. **High Frequency of Long Waits** (e.g., 70% of registrations for New Patients wait >10 minutes).  
3. **Impact on Overall Visit Duration:** Queues preceding time-sensitive activities (e.g., post-ECG Test queues delaying discharge).  
4. **Urgency Mismatch:** Queues where Urgent patients experience disproportionately long waits despite priority policies.
