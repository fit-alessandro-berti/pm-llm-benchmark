#### 3. **Root Cause Explanations and Mitigation Strategies**  
##### **Root Cause 1: High Complexity Triggers Rework and Delays**  
- **Why it happens**:  
  High complexity claims require iterative documentation requests, forcing restarts in the process flow. Each request adds 12–24 hours of waiting for customer responses.  
- **Mitigation**:  
  - **Proactive Documentation**: Require a standardized checklist of documents upfront for high-complexity claims.  
  - **Dedicated Teams**: Assign specialized adjusters to high-complexity cases to reduce rework cycles.  
  - **Automated Alerts**: Implement reminders for overdue customer submissions (e.g., auto-escalate after 24 hours).  

##### **Root Cause 2: Region B Bottlenecks in Approval/Payout**  
- **Why it happens**:  
  Region B’s staff (especially Finance_Carl) showed slower end-stage processing, possibly due to higher caseloads or manual handoffs.  
- **Mitigation**:  
  - **Workload Balancing**: Redistribute cases from Region B to Region A during peak times.  
  - **Process Automation**: Digitize approvals/payments to reduce delays (e.g., auto-approve post-evaluation).  

##### **Root Cause 3: Resource Overload in Problematic Steps**  
- **Why it happens**:  
  Adjuster_Lisa and Manager_Bill handled the longest cases. Lisa oversaw 3 cases (including 2 with requests), while Bill’s approvals were delayed by 19+ hours.  
- **Mitigation**:  
  - **Resource Allocation**: Add temporary staff to Region B during high-complexity surges.  
  - **Skill-Based Routing**: Route medium/high-complexity claims to senior resources with lower caseloads.
