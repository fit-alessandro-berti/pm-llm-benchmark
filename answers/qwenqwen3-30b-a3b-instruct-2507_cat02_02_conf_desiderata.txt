####  **Violation 3: Incomplete Decision Logic**
- The case closes after finalizing documentation, but **the decision was rejection**, so documentation should not have been finalized at all.

##  **3. Interpretations and Justifications (Where Possible)**

### **Case 1: Perfect Compliance**
- No need for justification — it reflects ideal execution of both normative and desiderata rules.
- Likely reflects a well-managed, efficient process.

---

### **Case 2: Normative Violations — Possible Explanations**

#### **Why Risk Assessment was done before Credit Check?**
- **Possible justification:**  
  - The system may have **automatically triggered risk assessment** upon application receipt due to an error in workflow configuration.
  - Or, a **manual override** was used by a staff member who assumed the credit check was already complete (e.g., due to a system sync issue).
  - Alternatively, the **credit check was delayed** due to external factors (e.g., third-party data delay), but the risk assessment was initiated prematurely.

>  **Recommendation:** Implement **workflow locks** or **process validation gates** to prevent risk assessment unless credit check is confirmed complete.

#### **Why was documentation finalized after rejection?**
- **Possible justification:**  
  - A **system error or human mistake**: The staff member assumed the case was still pending approval.
  - Or, the **approval/rejection flag was mislabeled**, and documentation was finalized based on incorrect data.
  - Another possibility: **The system allowed documentation to be finalized regardless of decision**, indicating a **lack of conditional logic enforcement**.

>  **Recommendation:** Enforce **decision-based workflow branching** — documentation only available for approved cases.

---

### **Case 3: Desiderata Violations — Possible Explanations**

#### **Why was Credit Check delayed by 49 hours?**
- **Possible justification:**  
  - The applicant submitted the application on a **weekend** (Jan 12 was a Wednesday, so not weekend — but perhaps holiday?).
  - The **credit check was outsourced**, and processing was delayed due to **high volume or system downtime**.
  - Or, the **application was initially flagged for manual review**, causing a backlog.

>  **Recommendation:** Monitor credit check turnaround times and set alerts for delays beyond 24h.

#### **Why was applicant informed 51 hours after decision?**
- **Possible justification:**  
  - The **decision was made late in the day** (12:00), and the notification system was scheduled for the next business day.
  - The **communication channel failed** (email bounced), requiring manual follow-up.
  - Or, **a template was not ready**, delaying the notification.

>  **Recommendation:** Automate notifications and set up **SLA monitoring** for communication.

#### **Why was no follow-up call made?**
- **Possible justification:**  
  - The **process is fully automated** and does not include human touchpoints.
  - The **follow-up call was deemed unnecessary** due to high trust in automated systems.
  - Or, the **call was scheduled but missed** due to staff unavailability.

>  **Recommendation:** Consider **automated reminders** for follow-up calls or include them in process design as optional but encouraged.

##  **Final Recommendations**

1. **Enforce Workflow Logic:** Use process management tools (e.g., BPMN, workflow engines) to **block inappropriate transitions** (e.g., risk assessment before credit check, documentation after rejection).
2. **Implement Conditional Branching:** Ensure that documentation is only accessible after **approval**, and **automatically disabled** after rejection.
3. **Monitor Timing SLAs:** Set up **automated alerts** for:
   - Credit check >24h after application
   - Notification >48h after decision
4. **Encourage Follow-up Calls:** Make follow-up calls **optional but tracked** — use dashboards to monitor adherence to desiderata.
5. **Review Case 2 Root Causes:** Conduct a root cause analysis (RCA) to prevent recurrence of sequence and logic violations.
