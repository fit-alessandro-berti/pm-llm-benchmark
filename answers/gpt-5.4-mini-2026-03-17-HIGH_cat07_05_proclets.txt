The visualization is a composite diagram about a medical lab workflow, showing how two related process classes interact.

### (a) Main process view
On the left is the **“Lab visit”** proclet/process, and on the right is the **“Lab test”** proclet/process.  
They are connected through two communication channels:

- **Order system**: sends the test order from the lab visit side to the lab test side.
- **HIS**: sends test results back from the lab test side to the lab visit side.

#### Lab visit flow
The lab visit process includes steps such as:
- **Order entry**
- **Take blood sample**
- **Judge results**
- If everything is fine, **Send report**
- If not, **Do tests again**

#### Lab test flow
The lab test process includes:
- **Perform test**
- **Make report**
- **Finish lab test**
- If needed, **Perform test again**
- **Provide updated report**

The arrows show that the lab visit process can trigger a lab test, receive the result, decide whether more testing is needed, and then receive an updated result if the test is repeated.

### (b) Class diagram
This part shows the relationship between the two proclet classes:

- A **Lab visit** *requires* one or more **Lab test** instances.
- The multiplicities indicate that one lab visit is associated with multiple lab tests.
