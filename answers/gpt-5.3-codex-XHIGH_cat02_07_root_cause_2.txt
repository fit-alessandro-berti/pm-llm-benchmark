## 2) Root-cause analysis by attributes

### A) Strongest driver: **Complexity + rework loop**
- **Low complexity** (2001, 2004): ~1.5 hours, no doc requests.
- **Medium complexity** (2002): ~26 hours, 1 doc request.
- **High complexity** (2003, 2005): ~48–77 hours, 2–3 doc requests.

There is a near-linear pattern: more “Request Additional Documents” events  much longer cycle time.

### B) **Resource effects** (likely role/workload effects)
- **Adjuster_Lisa** appears in 2002 and 2005 (both delayed), and 2005 is the longest.
- **Manager_Bill** appears only in high-complexity cases (2003, 2005), both slow.
- This does **not** prove individual underperformance (small sample + complexity confounding), but suggests potential queue/capacity pressure for these roles.

### C) **Region effects**
- Region A has one fast low-complexity and one delayed high-complexity case.
- Region B has one fast low-complexity, one medium delay, one critical high-complexity delay.
- Region alone is not the primary cause, but **Region B + higher complexity** looks worse in this sample.

### D) Where the delays happen
Main bottleneck is between **Evaluate/Request Docs and Approve** (long waiting gaps across days), not the final close step.

---

## 3) Likely explanations + mitigation actions

### Likely explanations
1. **Incomplete initial claim packets** cause repeated document-request loops.
2. **High-complexity claims** need extra validation, increasing handoffs and wait times.
3. Possible **capacity bottlenecks** (adjuster/manager queues), especially for complex cases.
4. Some cases show **post-approval payout delay** (e.g., overnight/batch payment behavior).

### Mitigation suggestions
1. **Improve first-time-right submission**
   - Dynamic required-doc checklist by claim type/complexity.
   - Hard validation at intake portal.
2. **One-shot document request policy**
   - Train adjusters to request all missing items at once.
   - Use standardized checklists/templates.
3. **Complexity-based routing**
   - Early triage of high-complexity claims to specialist teams.
   - Add SLA + escalation for high-complexity queues.
4. **Workload balancing**
   - Monitor queue time by adjuster/manager/region.
   - Rebalance Region B or heavily loaded resources.
5. **Tighten approval-to-payment SLA**
   - Move from batch to more frequent payment runs.
