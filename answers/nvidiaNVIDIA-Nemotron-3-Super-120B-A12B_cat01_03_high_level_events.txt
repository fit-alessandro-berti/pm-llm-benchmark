### How the Grouping Was Derived

1. **Temporal Proximity** – By examining timestamps, clear gaps appear between groups (3045s intervals). Each gap signals a transition to a new logical phase (e.g., after preheating, there is a pause before the welding tool is picked up).

2. **Resource Consistency / Specialization** –  
   * Material preparation uses OperatorA, Robot Arm#2, and heating units – all “setup” resources.  
   * Welding assembly is exclusively OperatorB with the welding tool.  
   * Inspection is handled by a dedicated quality sensor.  
   * Coating & curing involve coating and heating units that are not used elsewhere in the case.  
   * Final check returns to a human operator (OperatorC) whose role is purely verification.

3. **Functional Dependency** – Certain lowlevel actions are prerequisites for others (e.g., you cannot weld before the sheet is positioned and preheated; you cannot measure weld integrity before welding; you cannot dry coating before it has been applied). Grouping respects these dependencies.

4. **DomainSpecific Terminology** – Manufacturing documentation commonly refers to stages such as *Material Preparation*, *Assembly (Welding)*, *InProcess Inspection*, *Coating/Curing*, and *Final Inspection*. The identified groups map directly to these familiar concepts, making the highlevel model intuitive for engineers and planners.
