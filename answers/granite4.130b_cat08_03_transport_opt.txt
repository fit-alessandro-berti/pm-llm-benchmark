## Comprehensive Process Mining Approach for Speedy Parcels

### 1. **Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### Data Preprocessing and Integration
- **Data Harmonization:** The first step involves aligning timestamps across all sources (GPS, scanners, dispatch, maintenance) to a unified time zone and ensuring consistent formats.
- **Event Log Construction:**
  - **Key Columns:** `Timestamp`, `Case ID` (Vehicle-Day), `Activity/Status`, `Event Type` (source identifier), `Location (Lat/Lon)`, `Speed`, `Vehicle ID`, `Driver ID`, `Package ID`, and optional `Notes`.
  - **Integration Logic:**
    - **GPS Data:** Continuous tracking feeds into the log as movement events; low-speed detections flag potential congestion or stops.
    - **Scanner Events:** Capture delivery milestones, directly mapping to activities like ‘Arrive Customer’, ‘Delivery Success/Failure’.
    - **Dispatch System:** Provides planned routes and expected sequences, included as “Planned Activity” markers with timestamps for each stop.
    - **Maintenance Logs:** Added as unscheduled activity events (e.g., “Unscheduled Stop – Maintenance”) tied to the vehicle’s Case ID.
- **Challenges:**
  - **Data Granularity Mismatch:** GPS data is high-frequency, while scanner events are sparse. Aggregation techniques (e.g., summarizing GPS intervals) help balance granularity.
  - **Missing/Erroneous Data:** Gaps in GPS tracking or scanner failures require imputation strategies (e.g., using speed and location trends).
  - **Event Correlation:** Ensuring events from different sources refer to the same real-world activity (e.g., linking ‘Depart Customer’ scan with corresponding GPS departure).

#### Process Discovery
- **Algorithm Choice:** Use Alpha-algorithm or Heuristics Miner due to their ability to handle large, noisy logs typical in logistics.
- **Visualization Output:**
  - A Petri net or BPMN diagram showing the end-to-end flow from ‘Start Shift’  Planned Stops  Actual Deviations (e.g., traffic stops, maintenance)  ‘End Shift’.
  - Highlight loops (repeated attempts at failed deliveries) and parallel paths (multiple vehicles handling overlapping routes).

#### Conformance Checking
- **Model Comparison:** Overlay the discovered process model with the planned route sequences from the dispatch system.
- **Deviation Types to Monitor:**
  - **Sequence Deviations:** Activities occurring out of order (e.g., maintenance before a scheduled stop).
  - **Unplanned Stops:** Extra nodes not in the plan (e.g., unscheduled breaks, detours).
  - **Timing Differences:** Significant delays at specific activities (e.g., prolonged dwell time at a customer location).
- **Tools:** Use Conformance Checking extensions in tools like ProM or Disco to quantify conformance fitness and precision scores.

### 2. **Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR):** `(Number of Deliveries Completed within Time Window) / Total Deliveries`.
- **Average Time per Delivery Stop:** Sum of dwell times at customer locations divided by the number of stops.
- **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio:** Insight into whether delays are travel-related or due to service inefficiencies.
- **Fuel Consumption per km/package:** Derived from GPS speed/distance data and vehicle-specific fuel efficiency metrics.
- **Vehicle Utilization Rate:** `(Active Driving Time) / (Total Shift Time)`; includes idle time analysis.
- **Traffic Delay Frequency/Duration:** Count and aggregate duration of low-speed events correlating with known congestion hotspots.
- **Failed Delivery Rate:** `(Number of Failed Delivery Attempts) / Total Delivery Attempts`.

#### Bottleneck Identification Techniques
- **Variant Analysis:** Examine process variants to identify common paths leading to delays (e.g., routes with frequent traffic stops).
- **Frequency Analysis:** Pinpoint activities with high occurrence but low efficiency (e.g., repeated ‘Delivery Failed’ events).
- **Simulation and What-If Analysis:** Model changes in routing or scheduling to predict impacts on KPIs.
- **Geospatial Analysis:** Map delays onto geographic heatmaps to identify traffic hotspots or poorly serviced areas.

### 3. **Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

#### Potential Root Causes
- **Suboptimal Route Planning:** Static routes may not account for real-time traffic; process mining can reveal patterns of repeated deviations.
- **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations:** Compare planned vs. actual travel times to assess estimation errors.
- **Traffic Congestion Patterns:** Correlate low-speed GPS events with known congestion zones or time-of-day trends.
- **Service Time Variability:** Analyze dwell time variance at customer locations to identify factors (e.g., complex addresses, high-rise buildings).
- **Vehicle Breakdowns/Maintenance:** Frequency and timing of unscheduled maintenance stops can indicate aging fleet issues.
- **Driver Behavior/Skill Differences:** Compare performance across drivers using activity duration and deviation metrics.
- **Failed Delivery Attempts:** High rates may stem from inaccurate customer availability data or poor communication.

#### Process Mining Analyses
- **Variant Comparison:** Contrast high-performing routes/drivers with low performers to isolate best practices.
- **Correlation Analysis:** Link traffic data (e.g., external APIs) with GPS speed logs to quantify congestion impact.
- **Dwell Time Distribution:** Use statistical analysis on service times to identify outliers and investigate causes (e.g., security checks, access issues).

### 4. **Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

#### Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing Adjustments
- **Targeted Inefficiency:** Travel time variability due to static routing.
- **Root Cause Addressed:** Suboptimal route planning and inaccurate travel time estimations.
- **Process Mining Support:** Identify frequent deviation points and correlate with real-time traffic data to predict optimal reroutes.
- **Expected Impact:** Improved OTDR, reduced travel time, lower fuel consumption.

#### Strategy 2: Territory and Sequence Optimization
- **Targeted Inefficiency:** High dwell times at certain geographic clusters.
- **Root Cause Addressed:** Service time variability and traffic congestion patterns.
- **Process Mining Support:** Use geospatial clustering of delivery events to redesign territories and sequence stops for minimal travel distance.
- **Expected Impact:** Balanced workload across drivers, reduced vehicle utilization gaps, improved fuel efficiency.

#### Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance Scheduling
- **Targeted Inefficiency:** Unscheduled maintenance causing route disruptions.
- **Root Cause Addressed:** Vehicle breakdowns/maintenance frequency.
- **Process Mining Support:** Analyze historical maintenance logs against usage metrics (e.g., miles driven, idle time) to predict failure likelihood.
- **Expected Impact:** Reduced downtime, lower repair costs, consistent delivery schedules.

### 5. **Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring**

#### Addressing Constraints
- **Driver Working Hours:** Ensure route optimizations respect legal driving limits; use process mining to simulate shifts with compliance checks.
- **Vehicle Capacities:** Integrate load data into routing algorithms to prevent overloading.
- **Customer Time Windows:** Prioritize deliveries nearing their windows in dynamic rerouting decisions.

#### Continuous Monitoring Plan
- **Dashboards:** Implement real-time KPI dashboards (e.g., OTDR, fuel consumption) using process mining tools with alerts for threshold breaches.
- **Process Views:** Regularly update conformance models to reflect changes and detect emerging deviations.
- **Feedback Loops:** Incorporate driver and customer feedback into the event log for continuous refinement of root cause analyses.
- **Periodic Reviews:** Conduct monthly variance analyses to ensure strategies remain effective and adjust parameters (e.g., traffic data sources, maintenance thresholds).

By systematically applying process mining techniques to Speedy Parcels' operational data, the company can transform raw event logs into actionable insights, driving significant improvements in delivery punctuality and cost efficiency while maintaining compliance with operational constraints.
