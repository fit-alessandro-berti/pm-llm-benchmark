**Analysis of Anomalies in the ProcuretoPay (P2P) Event Log**

Below is a stepbystep review of each case, highlighting deviations from the canonical P2P sequence:

| **Case ID** | **Anomalous Step(s)** | **Why It’s Problematic (Standard P2P Flow)** |
|-------------|-----------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **1001**    | No anomalies. The activities follow the textbook order: <br>1 Create  2 Approve  3 Issue PO  4 Receive Goods  5 Invoice Received  6 Match Invoice  7 Release Payment  8 Close. | This case is a clean, compliant execution of the P2P process. |
| **1002**    | - **Approve Purchase Requisition after payment (Step5)** <br>- **Receive Goods before Invoice Match (Steps67 are out of order)** | 1. **Approval After Payment:** In most organizations, purchase requisition approval must precede any financial commitment such as releasing a payment. Approving *after* the payment can indicate a missing control, potential overpayment risk, or retroactive justification that may violate internal controls and audit policies.<br>2. **Goods Received Before Invoice Matching:** The typical flow is: Receive Goods  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice to PO (threeway match). Receiving goods *before* the invoice has been matched can lead to mismatched quantities/conditions, disputes over pricing or condition of goods, and delayed reconciliation between inventory records and accounting. |
| **1003**    | - **Release Payment before receiving an invoice (Step4)** <br>- **Invoice Received after Goods Receipt (Steps56 are reversed)** | 1. **Payment Without Invoice:** Releasing a payment without having the vendor’s invoice on file breaches standard financial controls (“payment on receipt of goods only” is not accepted). It creates exposure to fraud, duplicate payments, or paying for undelivered/unverified services.<br>2. **Invoice Received After Goods Receipt:** While some organizations allow “prepayment” scenarios (e.g., advance payments), the usual P2P expects the invoice *before* payment and certainly before goods receipt verification. Receiving the invoice after goods have been logged can cause gaps in threeway matching, complicating reconciliation and potentially leading to disputes over quantity/quality discrepancies. |
| **1004**    | - **Receive Goods before issuing a Purchase Order (Steps34 are swapped)** | In the canonical P2P model, a purchase order is issued *before* goods/services are delivered; it serves as a contractual commitment that authorizes receipt and invoicing. Receiving goods without an active PO can trigger several issues: <br>• Lack of documented authorization  potential unauthorized purchases.<br>• No clear link between the received item and its cost, making threeway matching impossible until later (as seen in this case where “Match Invoice to PO” occurs after receipt).<br>• Increased risk of inventory mismatches or “ghost” items that cannot be traced back to a purchase transaction. |

### Summary of Identified Problems

| **Case** | **Key Anomalies** | **Potential Business Impact** |
|----------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **1002** | Approval after payment; Goods received before invoice matching | - Weak internal controls  higher fraud/overpayment risk.<br>- Reconciliation delays and potential disputes over goods vs. invoices.<br>- Audit findings for noncompliance with segregation of duties. |
| **1003** | Payment released without an invoice; Invoice arrives after receipt | - Unauthorized or premature payment exposure.<br>- Difficulty in threeway match  possible duplicate payments or unrecorded liabilities.<br>- Inventory and accounting mismatches, leading to financial statement errors. |
| **1004** | Goods received before PO issuance | - Lack of contractual documentation for receipts.<br>- Inability to enforce pricing/quantity terms at receipt time.<br>- Increased risk of “shadow inventory” that cannot be validated against a purchase order. |

---

### Recommendations (if remediation were in scope)

1. **Enforce Activity Order Controls**  
   - Implement systemlevel validation rules that reject or flag events occurring out of the expected sequence (e.g., prevent payment release without an associated invoice, block goods receipt before PO issuance).

2. **Strengthen Approval Workflows**  
   - Ensure requisition approval precedes any financial commitment (payment, purchase order creation). Use workflow engines with mandatory checkpoints.

3. **Automate ThreeWay Matching**  
   - Tie the “Match Invoice to PO” step to both the receipt of goods *and* the invoice; block downstream steps (e.g., payment release) until a successful match is recorded.

4. **Audit Trails & Alerts**  
   - Log deviations with timestamps and automatically generate alerts for compliance teams. Regularly review such anomalies as part of continuous process improvement.

5. **Training & Policy Reinforcement**  
   - Educate finance, procurement, and inventory staff on the standard P2P flow and the reasons behind each control to reduce humanerror driven violations.

By addressing these anomalies, organizations can tighten financial controls, improve auditability, and reduce the risk of costly errors or fraudulent activities within their ProcuretoPay processes.
