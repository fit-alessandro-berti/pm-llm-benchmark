### **Rationale Summary:**

- **`existence`**: Critical milestones like `Idea Generation`, `Final Launch`, and `Approval Gate` must occur in every case.
- **`exactly_one`**: `Approval Gate` should occur exactly once — no re-approvals.
- **`init`**: Every process must start with `Idea Generation`.
- **`response`, `precedence`, `succession`**: Enforce the core sequential flow from idea to launch.
- **`coexistence`**: `Technical Feasibility Check` and `Cost Evaluation` must both be present if either is (they are parallel prerequisites).
- **`altresponse`, `altprecedence`, `altsuccession`**: Either `TFC` or `CE` must immediately precede `Prototype Creation` — but not both together immediately before (alternative constraint).
- **`chainresponse`, `chainprecedence`, `chainsuccession`**: Enforce tight, immediate succession between `User Testing  Approval Gate  Marketing Plan  Final Launch`.
- **Negative constraints (`non...`)**: Left empty — no forbidden co-occurrences or successions identified in this standard process.

This DECLARE model captures the structured, gate-driven, yet partially parallel nature of a product design and launch workflow.
