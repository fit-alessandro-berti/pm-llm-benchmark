| Case ID | Start Time       | End Time         | Duration (Hours) |  
|---------|------------------|------------------|------------------|

- **Case 2005** (77.08 hours): 51× longer than the fastest case (2004).

These cases deviate sharply from the baseline (Cases 2001/2004 take <1.5 hours), indicating severe performance issues.

### Step 2: Root Cause Analysis  
We analyze attributes (Resource, Region, Complexity) for slow cases:  

#### Key Observations:  
1. **Complexity Dominates Duration**:  
   - **High Complexity** (Cases 2003, 2005) correlates with the longest durations (48.33–77.08 hours).  
     - **Why?** High-complexity claims require multiple "Request Additional Documents" events (e.g., Case 2003 had 2 requests; Case 2005 had 3 requests), elongating the process.  
   - **Medium Complexity** (Case 2002) also caused delays (25.92 hours) due to 1 request.  
   - **Low Complexity** (Cases 2001, 2004) resolved in <1.5 hours with no requests.  

2. **Region B is Overloaded**:  
   - **Region B** handled 3 cases (2002, 2004, 2005). While 2004 (Low Complexity) was fast, 2002 (Medium Complexity) and 2005 (High Complexity) were slow.  
     - **Why?** Region B lacks capacity for high-complexity claims. Adjuster_Lisa (Region B) managed 3 requests across Cases 2002 and 2005, suggesting a bottleneck.  

3. **Resource-Specific Bottlenecks**:  
   - **Adjuster_Lisa (Region B)** handled all "Request Additional Documents" for Cases 2002, 2005 (slow) and 2004 (fast). Overwhelmed by high-complexity tasks (2005 took 77.08 hours).  
   - **Adjuster_Mike (Region A)** managed Case 2003 (High Complexity; 48.33 hours) but had fewer requests (2 vs. 3 in Case 2005).  

4. **Manager Inconsistency**:  
   - **Manager_Bill** approved Cases 2003 and 2005 (both High Complexity; 48.33–77.08 hours).  
   - **Manager_Ann** approved Cases 2002 and 2004 (Medium/Low Complexity; 25.92/1.42 hours).  
     - Suggests Manager_Bill’s cases take longer, but complexity is the primary driver.  

#### Summary of Root Causes:  
| Case | Complexity | Region | Key Resource(s)       | Request Events | Duration (Hours) |  
|------|------------|--------|------------------------|----------------|------------------|  
| 2005 | High       | B      | Adjuster_Lisa x3       | 3              | 77.08            |  
| 2003 | High       | A      | Adjuster_Mike x2       | 2              | 48.33            |  
| 2002 | Medium     | B      | Adjuster_Lisa x1       | 1              | 25.92            |  

**Root Causes:**  
- **High Complexity**: Drives repeated requests for documentation, elongating the process.  
- **Region B Bottleneck**: Adjuster_Lisa is overloaded with high-complexity claims, causing delays.  
- **Adjuster Inefficiency**: Adjuster_Lisa (Case 2005) and Adjuster_Mike (Case 2003) take longer to resolve high-complexity claims.  

---

### Step 3: Mitigation Strategies  
#### Explanations & Solutions:  
1. **High Complexity Claims**:  
   - **Why?** Complex claims require iterative information gathering, increasing cycle time.  
   - **Solution**:  
     - Implement **tiered triaging**: Assign senior adjusters to high-complexity claims upfront to reduce rework.  
     - Add **automated validation** during "Evaluate Claim" to flag missing documentation early, minimizing requests.  

2. **Region B Overload**:  
   - **Why?** Region B has disproportionate high-complexity claims (2/3 slow cases) and lacks adjuster capacity.  
   - **Solution**:  
     - **Balance workload** across regions by redistributing claims or hiring additional adjusters in Region B.  
     - Use **resource leveling** to prevent overburdening Adjuster_Lisa.  

3. **Adjuster-Specific Issues**:  
   - **Why?** Adjusters (e.g., Adjuster_Lisa/Mike) struggle with high-complexity claims, leading to delays.  
   - **Solution**:  
     - Provide **specialized training** for high-complexity claims (e.g., fraud detection, ambiguous policy interpretation).  
     - Introduce **performance thresholds** for adjusters (e.g., max 2 requests per claim).  

4. **Process Optimization**:  
   - **Why?** Multiple document requests are the primary delay driver.  
   - **Solution**:  
     - Replace manual requests with **automated template generation** (e.g., pre-built checklists for common complex scenarios).  
     - Set **SLAs for document submission** (e.g., 48-hour response from claimants).  

#### Expected Outcomes:  
- **Short-term**: Reduce document requests by 30–50% via automation and training.  
- **Long-term**: Cut high-complexity durations by 40–60% through workload balancing and skill development.  

By targeting complexity, regional bottlenecks, and adjuster efficiency, the process can achieve faster resolution without compromising quality.
