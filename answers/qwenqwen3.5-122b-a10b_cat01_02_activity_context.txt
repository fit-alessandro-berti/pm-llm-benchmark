### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

1.  **`Verify Documents` $\rightarrow$ `Initial Document Verification` / `Re-Verification of Documents`**
    *   **Context:** In Cases C001 and C003, this activity occurs twice. The first instance is the standard intake check. The second instance occurs only after a "gap" in the process (customer upload or account adjustment).
    *   **Reasoning:** Distinguishing these prevents confusion during process mining. An analyst needs to know if a verification failure is due to the initial submission or a failure to remediate previous issues.

2.  **`Call Customer` $\rightarrow$ Specific Purpose Calls**
    *   **Context:** In Case C002, the call occurs twice with different goals (solving a problem vs. confirming a solution). In C001 and C003, the calls serve different logistical purposes (missing docs vs. physical return).
    *   **Reasoning:** "Call Customer" is too generic for a support workflow. Renaming them to `Call for Missing Documents`, `Call for Discrepancy Resolution`, `Call for Adjustment Confirmation`, and `Call for Branch Recall` allows stakeholders to measure the specific reasons for human intervention.

3.  **`Initiate Request` $\rightarrow$ `Online Request Initiation` / `Branch Request Initiation`**
    *   **Context:** While this does not repeat *within* a single case, it repeats *across the log* with different Resources (`WebPortal` vs. `BranchReception`).
    *   **Reasoning:** The channel of entry significantly impacts the process flow (e.g., digital upload vs. physical handover). Explicitly naming the channel in the activity name clarifies the intake path without needing to rely solely on the Resource column.

4.  **`Approve Request` $\rightarrow$ `Final Request Approval`**
    *   **Context:** This is the terminal event in all cases.
    *   **Reasoning:** Adding "Final" emphasizes that this is the closing gate of the workflow, distinguishing it from any potential intermediate approvals that might exist in a more complex variation of this process.

5.  **`Adjust Account` $\rightarrow$ `Internal Account Adjustment`**
    *   **Context:** Occurs in C002 and C003.
    *   **Reasoning:** Specifying "Internal" clarifies that this action does not involve direct customer interaction at that moment, distinguishing it from the customer-facing calls.
