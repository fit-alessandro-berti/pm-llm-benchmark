# Process Mining Analysis and Optimization for Speedy Parcels

## 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

### **Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To construct a cohesive event log, we must integrate data from GPS trackers, driver handheld scanners, dispatch systems, and maintenance logs. Key steps include:

- **Timestamp Standardization**: Convert all timestamp formats to a unified temporal reference (e.g., ISO 8601).
- **Spatial Data Integration**: Merge GPS coordinates with scanner locations to ensure continuity of location tracking.
- **Event Normalization**: Map event types across systems to a common taxonomy:
  - GPS  "Travel", "Idle", "Low Speed Detected", "Maintenance Stop"
  - Scanner  "Arrive Customer", "Delivery Success", "Delivery Failed", "Depart Customer"
  - Dispatch  "Route Assigned", "Package Assigned", "Vehicle Utilization"
  - Maintenance  "Scheduled Maintenance", "Unscheduled Repair"

**Challenges:**
- **Temporal Gaps**: Delays or missing data in GPS or scanner logs might create gaps. Interpolation or imputation techniques can address these.
- **Attribute Mismatches**: Ensuring Vehicle ID and Driver ID consistency across sources is crucial. A master reference table can align these identifiers.
- **Event Granularity**: Differing levels of detail (e.g., GPS updates every minute vs. scanner events every stop) require alignment through aggregation or interpolation.

### **Process Discovery**
Apply process discovery algorithms (e.g., Parallel Miner, Heuristics Miner) to mine the actual end-to-end delivery process:

- **Desired Output**: A Petri net or process tree illustrating sequences from "Depart Depot" to "Arrive Depot", including:
  - Normal delivery sequences.
  - Deviations such as "Low Speed Detected" leading to "Unscheduled Stop" (maintenance).
  - Delivery attempts that fail, prompting "Redelivery".

### **Conformance Checking**
Compare discovered process models against planned routes from the dispatch system:

- **Sequence Deviations**: Identify instances where actual paths diverge from planned routes (e.g., detours due to traffic).
- **Timing Mismatches**: Evaluate gaps between planned and actual timestamps for activities like "Delivery Success" versus "Depart Customer".
- **Unplanned Stops**: Highlight events like "Unscheduled Stop" that were not anticipated in the dispatch plan.

**Focus Areas for Deviation Analysis:**
- Traffic congestion events correlating with "Low Speed Detected".
- Maintenance interruptions causing route shifts.
- Delivery failures that necessitate alternative handling (e.g., rescheduling).

## 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
| KPI                     | Calculation Method                                                                                     | Relevance                                                                 |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| On-Time Delivery Rate   | (`Deliveries on Time`) / (`Total Deliveries`)                                                          | Core to customer satisfaction.                                              |
| Avg Time per Delivery   | Sum of (`Depart Customer` - `Arrive Customer`) for successful deliveries / (`Successful Deliveries`)   | Measures efficiency of delivery sequence.                                   |
| Travel vs. Service Ratio| (`Total Travel Time`) / (`Total Service Time` at customers)                                            | Identifies idle time vs. active delivery time.                             |
| Fuel Consumption/km     | (`Fuel Used`) / (`Total Distance Traveled`)                                                             | Direct cost driver.                                                        |
| Vehicle Utilization     | (`Operating Time`) / (`Available Time`)                                                                | Efficiency of vehicle deployment.                                          |
| Traffic Delay Frequency | Count of (`Low Speed Detected` events) / (`Total Miles Traveled`)                                       | Monitors external congestion impact.                                       |
| Failed Delivery Rate    | (`Failed Deliveries`) / (`Total Deliveries`)                                                           | Assesses process reliability.                                             |

### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
- **Trace Analysis**: Segment processes by hour, day, and driver to spot recurrent delays.
- **Resource Utilization Mining**: Identify which vehicles or routes have the highest idle times.
- **Delay Classification**: Use Miner’s Miner to find frequently occurring delay paths (e.g., "Low Speed Detected"  "Unscheduled Stop").
- **Correlation Analysis**: Link external factors (traffic data, weather) with delay frequencies to isolate influential bottlenecks.

**Impact Quantification:**
- Calculate the loss in On-Time Delivery Rate attributed to each bottleneck (e.g., "Traffic Delay Frequency" effect).
- Use Pareto analysis to prioritize bottlenecks contributing most to cost or delay.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

### **Potential Root Causes**
- **Suboptimal Route Planning**: Static routes may not adapt to real-time conditions, leading to redundant travel.
- **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimates**: Overestimation or underestimation of travel times can cause schedule slippage.
- **Traffic Congestion**: Recurrent congestion at specific times or locations degrades performance.
- **Variable Service Times**: High variability in time spent at customer locations (e.g., long dwell times for complex deliveries).
- **Vehicle Breakdowns**: Frequent unscheduled stops for repairs increase idle time.
- **Driver Variability**: Differing behaviors or efficiencies among drivers.
- **Failed Delivery Attempts**: Re-attempts consume extra resources and delay subsequent stops.

### **Process Mining Analyses**
- **Variant Analysis**: Compare high-performing vs. low-performing routes/drivers to uncover differing event sequences.
- **Traffic Correlation**: Cross-reference "Low Speed Detected" events with external traffic data to validate congestion impact.
- **Dwell Time Analysis**: Examine "Arrive Customer" to "Depart Customer" intervals to detect outliers indicating inefficient servicing.
- **Maintenance Impact Study**: Correlate unscheduled stops with subsequent delivery performance metrics.

## 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing Adjustments**
- **Target**: Reduce traffic-induced delays.
- **Root Cause Addressed**: Static routing ignoring real-time traffic.
- **Process Mining Support**: Use discovered process paths indicating frequent "Low Speed Detected" deviations to refine routing algorithms.
- **Expected KPI Impact**: 
  - On-Time Delivery Rate  (by reducing travel delays).
  - Travel vs. Service Ratio  (less idle time).
  - Fuel Consumption/km  (efficient routeing).

### **Strategy 2: Optimized Delivery Territories**
- **Target**: Improve route efficiency and reduce travel distances.
- **Root Cause Addressed**: Inefficient sequencing of stops.
- **Process Mining Support**: Analyze trace frequencies and service times to cluster geographically proximate stops.
- **Expected KPI Impact**:
  - Avg Time per Delivery  (shorter routes).
  - Vehicle Utilization  (better load factors).
  - Fuel Consumption/km  (less mileage).

### **Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance and Driver Training**
- **Target**: Lower vehicle breakdowns and inefficient driver practices.
- **Root Cause Addressed**: Unscheduled maintenance and driver variability.
- **Process Mining Support**: Mine patterns leading to "Unscheduled Stop" events to forecast maintenance needs and identify driver inefficiencies.
- **Expected KPI Impact**:
  - Frequency of Failed Deliveries  (fewer breakdowns).
  - Vehicle Utilization  (reduced downtime).
  - Fuel Consumption/km  (well-maintained vehicles run efficiently).

### **Strategy 4: Time Window Management and Customer Communication**
- **Target**: Decrease failed deliveries due to missed time windows.
- **Root Cause Addressed**: Inaccurate time window handling and lack of customer notification.
- **Process Mining Support**: Identify delivery attempts with high "Failed Delivery" rates and correlate with time window mismatches.
- **Expected KPI Impact**:
  - Failed Delivery Rate  (improved communication).
  - On-Time Delivery Rate  (more accurate scheduling).
  - Customer Satisfaction  (better service perception).

## 5. Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring

### **Operational Constraints**
- **Driver Working Hours**: Ensure any route adjustment respects legal driving limits and rest periods.
- **Vehicle Capacities**: Use dispatch data to optimize load planning, minimizing excess trips.
- **Customer Time Windows**: Integrate time window constraints into dynamic routing algorithms to prioritize punctuality.

### **Continuous Monitoring Plan**
- **Process Mining Dashboards**:
  - Real-time KPI visualizations (On-Time Delivery Rate, Fuel Consumption).
  - Drill-down views of specific routes/drivers for granular analysis.
  - Alerts for deviations exceeding predefined thresholds (e.g., sudden spike in "Unscheduled Stop" frequency).

- **Monitoring Metrics**:
  - Daily KPI trends versus baseline.
  - Weekly route performance reports.
  - Monthly maintenance and driver efficiency summaries.

- **Feedback Loop**:
  - Use dashboard insights to iteratively refine routing algorithms and maintenance schedules.
  - Conduct quarterly reviews with stakeholders to assess sustainability and adjust strategies as needed.
