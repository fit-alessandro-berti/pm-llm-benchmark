**Handling Timestamp Tolerances and Naming Variations**

- **Timestamp Tolerance**: Events were considered identical if their timestamps differed by **2seconds**. For events slightly beyond this (e.g., 5seconds), the decision was made based on semantic similarity (same logical step in the fulfillment process) and the proximity of timestamps.
- **Naming Variations**: Event type mappings were based on the intent of the event (e.g., “Order Received” vs. “OrderReceived”, “Order Validated” vs. “OrderValidation”, “Payment Processed” vs. “PaymentCheck”, “Item Shipped” vs. “Shipping”). When mapping was ambiguous, the richer log (Log B) provided clues (e.g., user_id, resource_id) to confirm alignment.

**Final Chronological Order**

The merged log is ordered by the **primary timestamp** (selected as the earliest timestamp among matched logs for ordering purposes, or the sole timestamp when only one log provides it). The final sequence reflects the true timeline of the order fulfillment process across both systems.
