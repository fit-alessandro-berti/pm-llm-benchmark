This analysis groups the low-level, granular events into three distinct, high-level process stages, reflecting the logical flow from receiving raw materials to a finalized, inspected component.

---

##  Aggregated High-Level Process Steps

### 1. Material Intake & Preparation
**(The process of receiving, identifying, and preparing the raw component for use.)**

| Low-Level Events Included | Activity Type | Resource Type | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Retrieve raw metal sheet | Retrieval | Operator A | Start of processing/Identification |
| Scan barcode | Identification | Operator A | Tracking/Inventory control |
| Place metal sheet on table | Handling | Operator A | Positioning the material |
| Align metal sheet | Robotic Manipulation | Robot Arm #2 | Geometric setup |
| Preheat metal sheet | Energy Application | Heating Unit #1 | Setting the material to optimal process temperature |

**Rationale for Grouping:**
These events collectively define the beginning of the production cycle. They are concerned entirely with transitioning the raw, unworked material into a state where the machinery and operators can act upon it. The actions follow a classic inventory-to-setup sequence: *Identify $\rightarrow$ Position $\rightarrow$ Prepare.*

---

### 2. Component Assembly & Modification
**(The core stage where the functional changes are applied to the component.)**

| Low-Level Events Included | Activity Type | Resource Type | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Pick up welding tool, Weld corner A/B | Physical Joining | Operator B | Structural modification/Assembly |
| Measure weld integrity | Initial Quality Check | Quality Sensor #1 | Immediate process validation |
| Apply protective coating | Surface Treatment | Coating Unit #3 | Protection and aesthetic modification |

**Rationale for Grouping:**
This phase represents the most value-add physical work. The process transitions from passive preparation to active construction. The sequence involves mechanical joining (welding), followed by immediate automated checks (weld integrity), and then functional modification (coating). These steps define the physical structure and initial surface characteristics of the final product.

---

### 3. Finishing, Curing, & Quality Verification
**(The final stages of stabilization, hardening, and human inspection before shipment.)**

| Low-Level Events Included | Activity Type | Resource Type | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Dry coating | Stabilization | Heating Unit #2 | Curing/Hardening the applied coating |
| Visual check | Final Inspection | Operator C | Manual confirmation of successful processing |

**Rationale for Grouping:**
These events complete the process loop. The coating applied in the previous step must be stabilized (dried/cured) to achieve its final properties. The final visual check acts as the gatekeeper, confirming that all preceding steps (assembly, modification, curing) met the required quality standards, making the component ready for the next stage (or shipping).

| High-Level Process Step | Core Function | Low-Level Activities Included |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Material Intake & Preparation** | Getting the raw material ready and aligned. | Retrieving, Scanning, Positioning, Preheating. |
