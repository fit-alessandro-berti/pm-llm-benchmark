This image illustrates a **Proclet** model—a method for modeling interacting business processes—by demonstrating a laboratory testing scenario. The visualization is divided into three parts:

### (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels
This is a process-oriented flowchart showing two concurrent processes: **"Lab visit"** (left) and **"Lab test"** (right). 
*   **Workflow:** The "Lab visit" process involves tasks like ordering, taking blood samples, judging results, and sending reports. The "Lab test" process involves performing tests and making reports.
*   **Interaction:** The two processes communicate through two middleware systems, the **"Order system"** and the **"HIS" (Hospital Information System)**. These act as channels for data exchange (e.g., sending orders or receiving results).
*   **Roles:** Various actors, indicated by arrow icons, are responsible for specific tasks (e.g., "doctor," "lab assistant," and "chemical analyst").

### (b) Class Diagram Containing the Two Proclet Classes
This is a standard UML-style entity-relationship diagram. It defines the structural dependency between the two processes:
*   It shows that a **"Lab visit"** *requires* one or more (**1..***) instances of a **"Lab test."**
*   Conversely, each specific "Lab test" is associated with exactly one (**1..1**) "Lab visit."

### (c) Example of a Performative
This table provides a concrete example of a communication event (a message) sent between the two proclets.
*   It documents a specific interaction occurring at **11:00** via the **"Order system"** channel. 
*   The sender is the "Lab visit" process for patient "John," and the receiver is the "Lab test" process for "HGB" (Hemoglobin).
*   The action is a **"Create"** request, asking, "Can you perform a HGB test for John?" It identifies the scope as "Private" and the direction as "OUT."

***

### Summary
The visualization explains how complex, multi-party business processes can be decomposed into smaller, interacting "proclets." It shows that rather than modeling one giant monolithic process, one can model the "Lab visit" and "Lab test" as separate entities that synchronize their actions and data through defined channels.
