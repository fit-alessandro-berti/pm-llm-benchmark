This is a **Dotted Chart** (process mining visualization) depicting the **fine collection process** over a **13-year period** (January 25, 2000 – April 24, 2013). Here's the breakdown:

## Structure & Components

**Three Process Stages (Y-axis, top to bottom):**
1. **Send Fine** – Initial fine issuance
2. **Insert Fine Notification** – Recording/notification step  
3. **Send for Credit Collection** – Final escalation for unpaid fines

**X-axis:** Timeline spanning ~13 years

## Visual Patterns

**Top Section ("Send Fine"):**
- Dense **vertical gray lines** representing thousands of individual cases
- Relatively uniform distribution across the entire timeframe
- Each line = one fine issued

**Middle Section ("Insert Fine Notification"):**
- Light gray connecting lines flowing downward from the top
- Shows the transition from issuing fines to recording notifications

**Bottom Section ("Send for Credit Collection"):**
- **Red/diagonal lines** sloping rightward
- The slope indicates **time delay** between notification and credit collection
- Steeper slopes = longer processing times
- Visible **clustering patterns** suggest batch processing or seasonal variations

## Key Insights

The diagonal red lines reveal that **credit collection timing varies significantly**:
- Some cases escalate quickly (steep lines)
- Others take much longer (shallow lines)
- Multiple parallel "streams" suggest different handling procedures or priority levels

This visualization helps identify **bottlenecks**, **processing delays**, and **case flow variations** in the fine-to-collection lifecycle over more than a decade of operations.
