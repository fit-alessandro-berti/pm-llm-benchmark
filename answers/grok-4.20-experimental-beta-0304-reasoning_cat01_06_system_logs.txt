**Logic Summary**

I defined **five coherent cases**, each representing a distinct logical unit of work based on the primary artifact the user was interacting with (specific document, email, or spreadsheet). Events were grouped primarily by the `Window` title and application context. `SWITCH` events were assigned to the *target* case as they represent the start of new task-focused work.

Raw low-level actions were abstracted into higher-level, analyst-friendly activity names:
- `FOCUS` / initial `SWITCH`  `Open Document` / `Open PDF` / `Open Spreadsheet` / `Switch to Email`
- `TYPING`  `Edit Content` (Word), `Type Response` (email), `Update Data` (Excel)
- `SAVE`  `Save Document` / `Save Spreadsheet`
- `CLICK` actions in email  `Open Email`, `Initiate Reply`, `Send Email`
- `SCROLL` / `HIGHLIGHT`  `Read Email` / `Review Document` / `Highlight Text`

This structure reveals both individual task execution (e.g., Email Response as a clear sub-process) and the user𠏋 task-switching behavior across the session, making the log highly suitable for standard process mining techniques such as process discovery, performance analysis, and variant analysis.
