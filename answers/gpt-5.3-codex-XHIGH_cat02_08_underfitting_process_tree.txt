A concrete **invalid-but-accepted** trace is:

\[
\langle \text{Receive\_Order}, \text{Check\_Inventory}, \text{Ship\_Order}, \text{Package\_Items}, \text{Authorize\_Payment}, \text{Close\_Order} \rangle
\]

### Why the model accepts it
- After `Receive_Order` and `Check_Inventory`, the XOR can choose the “successful” branch.
- That branch is modeled as `PARALLEL(authorize_payment, package_items, ship_order)`, so **any order** of those three activities is allowed.
- Then the root sequence ends with `Close_Order`.

### Why this is wrong in the domain
In Order-to-Cash, shipping should not happen before packaging (and typically not before payment authorization).  
So this trace violates the real business logic, even though the process tree allows it—classic underfitting (too permissive ordering constraints).
