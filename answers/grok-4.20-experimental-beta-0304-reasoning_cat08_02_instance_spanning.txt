**1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact**

I would begin with a structured process mining analysis of the three-month event log, focusing on techniques that explicitly handle inter-case dependencies rather than treating each order (case) in isolation.

**Discovery and correlation phase:**
- Use standard process discovery (e.g., Inductive Miner or Heuristics Miner) on the full log to create a base control-flow model, then overlay case attributes (Order Type, Requires Cold Packing, Hazardous Material, Destination Region).
- Implement custom inter-case analysis by correlating events across cases using timestamps and shared resources. For each activity, I would enrich the log with derived attributes such as "Concurrent Cold-Packing Orders" and "Hazardous Orders in Packing/QC at Timestamp."
- Apply object-centric process mining (OCPM) or multi-case analysis to treat resources (stations, batch groups) as first-class objects that link multiple cases.

**Specific identification and quantification methods:**

- **Shared Cold-Packing Stations:** Identify all "Packing" events where `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`. Calculate resource contention by measuring overlapping intervals on the 5 specialized stations (C1–C5). Metric: *Cold-Packing Contention Waiting Time* = time an order spends waiting for a cold station while at least 5 other cold orders are active. I would also compute utilization rate per station and the percentage of cold orders delayed >15 minutes due to contention.

- **Shipping Batches:** Group orders by Destination Region and detect "Shipping Label Gen." events that share the same batch identifier (e.g., Batch B1). Metric: *Batch Waiting Time* = time between an order completing Quality Check and the batch completion timestamp. Quantify impact as the proportion of total cycle time attributable to batching (typically high in regions with low order volume).

- **Priority Handling (Express Orders):** Detect interruptions by identifying cases where an Express order starts an activity while a Standard order is using the same resource type. Metric: *Preemption Delay* = additional waiting or pause time imposed on Standard orders immediately following an Express order's arrival at the same station/activity. Also track "Express Overtake Rate" (how often Express orders jump the queue).

- **Hazardous Material Limits:** For every timestamp, count the number of concurrent "Packing" or "Quality Check" activities where `Hazardous Material = TRUE`. Flag violations or near-violations (>8 concurrent). Metric: *Regulatory Waiting Time* = waiting time inserted to maintain 10 concurrent hazardous orders. Throughput reduction = difference in flow rate during periods approaching the limit versus unconstrained periods.

**Differentiating within-instance vs. between-instance waiting time:**
- Enrich the log with "Waiting Type" attribute using interval analysis:
  - *Within-instance*: Waiting where the preceding activity's completion is the only dependency (e.g., long Picking duration with no resource contention).
  - *Between-instance*: Waiting where the resource is occupied by another case, a batch is incomplete, or hazardous count is at limit. This is detected by checking overlapping timestamps on shared resources/batches at the exact moment waiting begins.
- Use performance analysis (e.g., in Disco, Celonis, or PM4Py) with custom filters to produce two separate waiting time distributions. Statistical tests (e.g., Mann-Whitney U) would confirm significant differences. Expectation: 35–55% of total waiting time in this warehouse is likely between-instance based on similar logistics studies.

**2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions**

Instance-spanning constraints rarely operate independently; they create compound effects that amplify delays.

- **Express + Cold-Packing interaction:** An Express cold order can preempt a Standard cold order at a specialized station, creating a cascading queue. This interaction is particularly damaging because cold stations are already capacity-constrained (only 5 units). Analysis would reveal "priority-induced cold chain delays" by measuring how often Standard cold orders experience >30 min waits immediately after Express cold arrivals.

- **Batching + Hazardous Limits:** If multiple hazardous orders share the same Destination Region, the batching requirement forces them to wait together, pushing the concurrent hazardous count higher during the wait period. This can trigger regulatory throttling even when individual activities would otherwise comply. I would quantify this by measuring "batch-induced hazardous congestion" — periods where batch completion coincides with hazardous limit enforcement.

- **Priority + Regulatory Limits:** Express hazardous orders create tension because they demand immediate processing while the global hazardous cap limits total throughput. This can force the system to pause non-hazardous orders unnecessarily or create unsafe pressure to violate limits.

Understanding these interactions is essential because optimizing one constraint in isolation (e.g., more aggressive batching) can worsen another (hazardous congestion). The analysis must therefore use multivariate regression or causal process mining to attribute delays to specific interaction patterns rather than single constraints. This informs holistic rather than local optimization.

**3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies**

**Strategy 1: Predictive Dynamic Cold-Packing Allocation with Priority-Aware Preemption Rules**
- *Addresses:* Shared Cold-Packing and Priority Handling interaction.
- *Changes:* Implement a real-time scheduling system that uses remaining processing time prediction (from historical data via survival analysis or ML on case attributes) to reserve cold stations. Express cold orders get priority but only if predicted wait for Standard cold orders stays below a threshold (e.g., 20 min). Introduce "soft preemption" — allow current Standard packing to finish a 5-minute grace period before Express takeover.
- *Data leverage:* Train a model on the event log using features like order composition, time-of-day, and current concurrent load to predict cold station demand 30–60 minutes ahead.
- *Expected outcomes:* Reduce Cold-Packing Contention Waiting Time by 35–45% while maintaining Express service levels. Prevents "priority starvation" of Standard orders.

**Strategy 2: Hybrid Batching with Dynamic Thresholds and Hazardous-Aware Grouping**
- *Addresses:* Shipping Batches, especially when interacting with Hazardous Limits.
- *Changes:* Replace fixed batching with dynamic thresholds that trigger earlier when hazardous orders are present (e.g., release batch at 70% of normal size if >3 hazardous orders are waiting). Create "hazardous-first" sub-batches for same-region orders when the concurrent hazardous count approaches 8.
- *Data leverage:* Analyze historical batch size vs. waiting time trade-offs per region and time-of-day patterns from the log. Use reinforcement learning or simple decision trees to set optimal release thresholds.
- *Expected outcomes:* Reduce Batch Waiting Time by 25–40% for hazardous orders without significantly increasing shipping costs, while keeping hazardous concurrency safely below 10.

**Strategy 3: Constraint-Aware Capacity Buffering and Process Decoupling**
- *Addresses:* All four constraints through minor redesign and buffering.
- *Changes:* Introduce a "Pre-QC Buffer" zone for non-hazardous orders that have completed Packing but are waiting for batch or cold station availability. This decouples Packing/QC from downstream constraints. Implement a global "Constraint Orchestrator" that monitors all four constraints in real-time and adjusts priorities using a weighted scoring system (Express multiplier × 2.5, Hazardous penalty if near limit).
- *Data leverage:* Use the enriched event log to simulate and calibrate the scoring weights. Identify high-impact decoupling points via bottleneck analysis.
- *Expected outcomes:* Overall end-to-end cycle time reduction of 18–30%, with specific improvement in regulatory compliance rate to 99.8% while increasing throughput during peak periods.

**4. Simulation and Validation**

I would build a discrete-event simulation (using tools like SimPy, AnyLogic, or Celonis Process Simulation) directly parameterized from the process mining results:

- **Key modeling requirements:**
  - Multiple case types with attributes sampled from the actual log distribution.
  - Explicit resource objects (5 cold stations, unlimited standard stations) with contention logic.
  - Batch formation logic as an aggregation entity that holds completed orders until release conditions are met.
  - Priority queueing with preemption rules for Express orders.
  - Global counter and blocking mechanism for hazardous orders (hard limit of 10).
  - Stochastic elements calibrated to historical distributions for activity durations, arrival rates, and attribute correlations.

- **Validation approach:** Replay the actual three-month log against the simulation ("digital twin" validation) and ensure <10% deviation on key KPIs (cycle time, resource utilization, constraint violation rates). Then test the three strategies in multiple scenarios (normal day, peak season, high hazardous volume).

- **Focus metrics:** Average cycle time, 95th percentile delivery delay, cold station utilization, hazardous limit compliance rate, and breakdown of waiting time by constraint type. This ensures strategies are robust to the interdependencies rather than optimistic.

**Core KPIs tracked continuously:**
- Instance-spanning waiting time percentage (target: <35% of total cycle time).

- Batch Formation Efficiency (average wait per region, hazardous vs. non-hazardous).
- Regulatory Compliance Rate (percentage of time hazardous count 9 to maintain buffer).
- Priority Impact Ratio (delay imposed on Standard orders per Express order processed).
- Overall throughput and on-time delivery rate.

**Specific dashboards:**
- "Constraint Heatmap" showing real-time concurrent usage of cold stations and hazardous activities, with alerts at 80% of limits.
- "Waiting Time Decomposition" chart automatically attributing waits to within-instance vs. between-instance causes.
- "Interaction Analysis" view highlighting periods where multiple constraints collided (e.g., Express cold order during high hazardous load).
- Before/after comparison panels using the same metrics calculated on streaming data.

Weekly conformance checks would verify that the new scheduling rules are being followed, and drift detection would identify when seasonal changes require recalibration of thresholds. This creates a closed-loop system where process mining continuously validates that instance-spanning constraints are being actively managed rather than merely endured.
