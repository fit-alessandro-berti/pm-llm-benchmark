### **Process Model: Invoice Approval & Payment**

**Primary Roles/Participants:**
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk:** (e.g., Mary) - Initiates the process, logs invoices, coordinates matching and routing.
*   **Purchasing Officer:** (e.g., Dan) - Validates invoice details against the Purchase Order (PO).
*   **Department Manager:** (e.g., Carlos) - Provides final budget/approval authorization.
*   **AP Supervisor:** (e.g., Rita) - Handles escalations and exceptions (missing PO, major discrepancies).
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager:** (e.g., Karen) - Supports exception resolution by contacting suppliers (involved indirectly via escalation).
*   **System:** Accounting/ERP System - Used for logging, matching, and routing notifications.

**Key Artifacts/Data Objects:**
*   **Invoice** (Input, received via email)
*   **Purchase Order (PO)** (Reference document in the system)
*   **Confirmation Email/Notification** (from Purchasing)
*   **Approval Notification/Record** (in the system)
*   **Payment Schedule** (Output)

---

### **Textual Process Flow Description**

**1. Start Event: Invoice Received**
*   **Trigger:** An Invoice is received by the **AP Clerk**, typically via email.
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk.

**2. Task: Validate Purchase Order (PO) Number**
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk.
*   **Description:** The clerk checks if the invoice has a valid PO number.
*   **Gateway (Exclusive - XOR):** Based on "PO Number Present?"
    *   **Path A (No PO):** Proceed to **Exception Handling - Missing PO**.
    *   **Path B (PO Present):** Proceed to **Task: Log Invoice into System**.

**3. Task: Log Invoice into System**
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk.
*   **Description:** The invoice is entered into the accounting/ERP system.
*   **Output:** Invoice record created in the system.
*   **Flow:** Proceed to **Task: Send to Purchasing for Confirmation**.

**4. Task: Send to Purchasing for Confirmation**
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk / System.
*   **Description:** The invoice (or a notification) is forwarded to the **Purchasing Officer**.
*   **Flow:** Proceed to **Task: Validate Invoice against PO**.

**5. Task: Validate Invoice against PO**
*   **Actor:** Purchasing Officer.
*   **Description:** The officer opens the corresponding PO in the system and compares details (quantities, prices, delivery charges).
*   **Gateway (Exclusive - XOR):** Based on "Invoice Matches PO?"
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Found):** Proceed to **Exception Handling - Invoice Discrepancy**.
    *   **Path B (Matches):** Proceed to **Task: Send Confirmation to AP**.

**6. Task: Send Confirmation to AP**
*   **Actor:** Purchasing Officer.
*   **Description:** Officer sends a confirmation email/notification back to the AP Clerk.
*   **Output:** Confirmation record.
*   **Flow:** Proceed to **Task: Match Invoice to PO in System**.

**7. Task: Match Invoice to PO in System**
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk.
*   **Description:** The clerk performs the formal matching step in the accounting system, using the Purchasing confirmation.
*   **Gateway (Exclusive - XOR):** Based on "System Match Successful?"
    *   **Path A (Match Fails / Discrepancy Found):** Proceed to **Exception Handling - Invoice Discrepancy**.
    *   **Path B (Match Successful):** Proceed to **Task: Route for Manager Approval**.

**8. Task: Route for Manager Approval**
*   **Actor:** System / AP Clerk.
*   **Description:** The system automatically sends an approval request to the **Department Manager**. The request includes invoice details, PO, and confirmation.
*   **Flow:** Proceed to **Task: Review & Approve Invoice**.

**9. Task: Review & Approve Invoice**
*   **Actor:** Department Manager.
*   **Description:** The manager reviews the compiled information (invoice, PO, confirmation).
*   **Gateway (Exclusive - XOR):** Based on "Manager Approval Granted?"
    *   **Path A (Rejected/Queried):** The manager rejects or requests a hold for investigation. Proceed to **Exception Handling - Manager Query** (which typically loops back to the AP Clerk for clarification).
    *   **Path B (Approved):** The manager approves the invoice in the system.
*   **Flow (Approved):** Proceed to **Task: Schedule Payment**.

**10. Task: Schedule Payment**
*   **Actor:** AP Clerk.
*   **Description:** The clerk adds the approved invoice to the next payment cycle.
*   **Output:** Invoice added to Payment Schedule.
*   **Flow:** Proceed to **End Event: Invoice Paid**.

**11. End Event: Invoice Paid**
*   **Description:** The process concludes for this invoice. Payment is executed in a separate payment run process.

### **Exception Handling Sub-Processes**

**Exception Handling - Missing PO:**
1.  **Task: Request PO from Supplier.** (Actor: AP Clerk, or Supplier Relationship Manager if escalated).
2.  **Gateway:** Based on "PO Provided?"
    *   **Path A (Yes):** Loop back to **Task: Log Invoice into System**.
    *   **Path B (No / Informal Service):** Escalate to **AP Supervisor**.
        *   **Task: Resolve Missing PO Case.** (Actor: AP Supervisor). May involve creating a retrospective PO with a department head or deciding to reject the invoice. After resolution, the flow returns to the main process at the appropriate point (often **Task: Log Invoice into System** or goes to Purchasing if a PO is created).

**Exception Handling - Invoice Discrepancy:**
1.  **Task: Initiate Discrepancy Resolution.** (Actor: AP Clerk or Purchasing Officer). Typically involves emailing the counterpart (Purchasing/AP) or the supplier.
2.  **Gateway:** Based on "Resolution Achieved at Staff Level?"
    *   **Path A (Yes):** Flow returns to the step where the discrepancy was found (e.g., **Task: Validate Invoice against PO** or **Task: Match Invoice to PO in System**) to re-check.
    *   **Path B (No):** Escalate to **AP Supervisor**.
        *   **Task: Escalated Discrepancy Resolution.** (Actor: AP Supervisor, potentially involving the Supplier Relationship Manager). They contact suppliers or coordinate between departments. Once resolved, the flow returns to the main process.

**Exception Handling - Manager Query:**
1.  **Task: Investigate Manager's Query.** (Actor: AP Clerk, often involving Purchasing or the AP Supervisor).
2.  **Task: Provide Clarification to Manager.** (Actor: AP Clerk/Supervisor).
3.  **Flow:** Returns to **Task: Review & Approve Invoice**.

### **Notes on Ambiguity & Variations:**
*   **System vs. Manual Steps:** The line between system-automated notifications (e.g., routing for approval) and manual emails is described but not fully specified. The model assumes a hybrid.
*   **Supplier Contact Path:** Contacting the supplier for discrepancies can be initiated by Purchasing, AP, or the Supplier Relationship Manager, depending on the relationship and severity. The model consolidates this under the relevant exception handler.
*   **"Informal Service" Invoices:** The process for invoices without a PO (mentioned by Rita) is an exceptional, non-standard path requiring supervisor intervention and potentially a different approval workflow (retrospective PO creation). It is captured in the "Missing PO" exception path.
*   **Timing of Purchasing Confirmation:** Mary's account suggests sending to Purchasing *after* logging, while Dan's implies it arrives in his inbox from AP. The model uses the most common sequence: Log -> Send to Purchasing.
*   **Final Approval Authority:** All accounts confirm the Department Manager is the final approver before payment scheduling. The AP Supervisor (Rita) does not grant final approvals.
