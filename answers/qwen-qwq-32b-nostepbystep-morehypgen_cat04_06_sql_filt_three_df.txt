The SQL query to achieve the desired result involves identifying cases with the specific sequence and timing, then excluding them from the final output. Here's the solution:

```sql
WITH TripletCheck AS (
    SELECT
        case_id,
        activity,
        timestamp,
        LEAD(activity, 1) OVER w AS next_activity,
        LEAD(activity, 2) OVER w AS next_next_activity,
        LEAD(timestamp, 1) OVER w AS next_ts,
        LEAD(timestamp, 2) OVER w AS next_next_ts
    FROM event_log
    WINDOW w AS (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp)
),
Triplet_Candidates AS (
    SELECT
        case_id,
        next_ts AS create_ts,
        next_next_ts AS confirm_ts
    FROM TripletCheck
    WHERE activity = 'Approve Purchase Requisition'
        AND next_activity = 'Create Order'
        AND next_next_activity = 'Confirm Order'
),
Excluded_Cases AS (
    SELECT DISTINCT case_id
    FROM Triplet_Candidates
    WHERE (confirm_ts - create_ts) > INTERVAL '5 days'
)
SELECT *
FROM event_log
WHERE case_id NOT IN (SELECT case_id FROM Excluded_Cases);
```

**Explanation:**

1. **TripletCheck CTE:**  
   - Uses `LEAD()` to look ahead by 1 and 2 rows within each case, ordered by timestamp. This captures the next two activities and their timestamps relative to the current row.
   - For a row with activity 'Approve Purchase Requisition', the `next_activity` and `next_next_activity` check if the next two activities are 'Create Order' and 'Confirm Order', ensuring the triplet exists consecutively.

2. **Triplet_Candidates CTE:**  
   - Filters rows to find valid triplets (approve  create  confirm) and captures their timestamps.

3. **Excluded_Cases CTE:**  
   - Identifies cases where the time between 'Create Order' and 'Confirm Order' in any triplet exceeds 5 days.

4. **Final Query:**  
   - Selects all events not in the excluded cases, ensuring only valid cases remain.

This approach ensures we first identify cases meeting both the sequence and timing criteria, then exclude them from the final result.
