## 4. What the bias looks like

- **Classifier use**: The boost is **only applied to people** the system already classifies as “LocalResident = TRUE” (and who happen to be in B).

- **Systematic difference**: The boost is the **only lever** that can neutralise the raw-score gap, yet it is **not equally available**. This is classic disparate treatment masked as a neutral scoring algorithm.

## 5. Conclusion

The event log for **Group B (Unprotected Group) exhibits bias**. The bias is **mechanical, not incidental**:

1. The system withholds a community-level score boost from A even when a resident of a civic organisation.  
2. This structural omission guarantees that A’s mean raw score (723) remains higher than B’s adjusted mean (roughly 712), translating into materially worse outcomes.  
3. The disparity in final-decision rates (2 approved for every 1 rejected in A vs. 1 approved for every 1 rejected in B) is a **direct statistical consequence** of this uneven access to the only compensatory lever.

Therefore, the protected-group log (A) shows no bias; the unprotected-group log (B) does.
