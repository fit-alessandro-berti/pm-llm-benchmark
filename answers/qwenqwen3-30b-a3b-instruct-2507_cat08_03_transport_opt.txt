# **Comprehensive Process Mining Analysis and Optimization Strategy for Speedy Parcels**

As a Process Mining Consultant specializing in logistics and transportation optimization, I present a structured, data-driven approach to diagnose and resolve the operational inefficiencies at **Speedy Parcels**. Leveraging their rich six-month event log—integrated from GPS, driver scanners, dispatch systems, and maintenance records—this strategy applies advanced process mining techniques to uncover hidden patterns, identify root causes, and deliver actionable improvements in delivery punctuality and operational cost control.

---

## **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

### **Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To enable meaningful process mining, the heterogeneous data streams must be unified into a single, consistent **event log** (using the **BPMN/Event Log** format, e.g., XES). Key steps include:

- **Temporal Alignment:** Synchronize timestamps across systems (GPS: 1–5 sec intervals; scanners: event-based; dispatch: static; maintenance: manual). Use interpolation or timestamp mapping to align events.
- **Event Type Normalization:** Map disparate event types (e.g., “Low Speed Detected”  “Traffic Delay,” “Unscheduled Stop”  “Unexpected Stop”) into a standardized taxonomy.
- **Case ID Construction:** Define **Case ID** as `Vehicle_ID-Day` (e.g., `V12-20241205`) to represent a full operational day per vehicle. For package-level analysis, use `Vehicle_ID-Day-Package_ID`.
- **Location Enrichment:** Geocode GPS coordinates and scanner events into standardized addresses or grids (e.g., 100m × 100m zones) to enable spatial analysis.
- **Handling Missing Data:** Impute or flag missing "Depart Customer" events based on next "Arrive Customer" or GPS trajectory analysis.
- **Event Log Schema:** Final structure includes: `Case ID`, `Activity`, `Timestamp`, `Resource (Driver ID)`, `Resource (Vehicle ID)`, `Package ID`, `Location`, `Speed`, `Status`, `Notes`.

> **Challenges Encountered:**
> - **Temporal drift** between systems (e.g., scanner app lag).
> - **Inconsistent event granularity** (GPS vs. scanner).
> - **Ambiguous event semantics** (e.g., “Unscheduled Stop” may include refueling, repairs, or personal breaks).
> - **Missing or incomplete records** (e.g., no "Depart Customer" after delivery).

### **Process Discovery: Visualizing the Actual Delivery Process**
Using **process discovery algorithms** (e.g., **Alpha Miner**, **Inductive Miner**, **Heuristics Miner**), we reconstruct the **actual process flow** from the event log:

- **Model Output:** A Petri net or BPMN-like diagram showing:
  - Sequence: `Start Shift  Route Assigned  Depart Depot  [Travel  Arrive Customer  Deliver (Success/Failed)  Depart Customer]*  Unscheduled Stop  Arrive Depot  End Shift`
  - Parallel paths: e.g., "Delivery Failed" leading to "Reattempt" (if re-delivery is logged).
  - Loops: e.g., repeated visits to same location due to failed deliveries.

> **Key Insight:** The model may reveal **non-linear, dynamic behavior** not captured in static dispatch plans—e.g., drivers detouring around traffic, skipping stops, or making unplanned refueling stops.

### **Conformance Checking: Comparing Actual vs. Planned**
We perform **conformance checking** by aligning the discovered **actual process model** with the **planned route** from the dispatch system (treated as a "reference model").

- **Techniques:** Use **token-based replay** or **alignment-based conformance checking** (e.g., in ProM or Celonis).
- **Deviations to Identify:**
  - **Sequence Deviations:** Driver skips or reorders stops (e.g., Stop 5 before Stop 1).
  - **Unplanned Activities:** "Unscheduled Stop" not in the plan.
  - **Timing Deviations:** Actual travel time exceeds planned by >15%.
  - **Missing Events:** Planned stops with no "Arrive" or "Delivery" events.
  - **Out-of-Time-Window Deliveries:** Deliveries made outside customer-requested windows.
  - **Repetitive Visits:** Multiple "Arrive" events at same location without "Depart" (indicating failed delivery loops).

> **Actionable Output:** A list of **deviation cases** (e.g., **V12-20241205**, **Stop 5**, **Failed Delivery, 28 min delay**) that can be investigated to identify systemic issues (e.g., poor time window management).

## **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and Calculation Methodology**

| **KPI** | **Definition** | **Calculation from Event Log** |
|--------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR)** | % of deliveries made within customer time window | `Count(Deliveries with t  0) / Total Deliveries`<br>(t = actual delivery time – earliest allowed time) |
| **Average Time per Delivery Stop** | Mean time from "Arrive" to "Depart" | `Avg( (Depart Customer – Arrive Customer) )` |
| **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio** | Efficiency of delivery efficiency | `Total Travel Time / Total Service Time` |
| **Fuel Consumption per km/package** | Cost efficiency | Estimate via GPS speed/duration + fuel rate model (e.g., fuel rate  speed² × duration) ÷ (Total km × Packages delivered) |
| **Vehicle Utilization Rate** | % of shift time spent moving or delivering | `(Total time in 'Moving' or 'Delivering') / (Shift Duration)` |
| **Traffic Delay Frequency & Duration** | Impact of congestion | Count events with `Speed < 10 km/h` and `Duration > 3 min` per 10 km segment |
| **Failed Delivery Rate (FDR)** | % of attempts failing | `Count(Delivery Failed) / Total Deliveries` |

> *Note: GPS data allows estimation of travel time between stops; scanner data provides service time.*

### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**

- **Performance Analysis with Process Mining Tools:**
  - Use **Bottleneck Detection** in ProM or Celonis to identify **activities with longest average duration** (e.g., "Service Time" at Stop 5 averages 14 min vs. 5 min elsewhere).
  - Apply **Resource Profiling** to compare **driver-specific** performance (e.g., D105 has 28% higher average service time than D203).
  - Conduct **Geospatial Heatmapping**: Overlay GPS data with dwell times to identify **traffic hotspots** (e.g., 50.8N, 6.12E consistently has 10+ min delays).

- **Bottleneck Triggers:**
  - **Route-Specific:** Some routes (e.g., V12-20241205) show 20%+ more traffic delays than average.
  - **Time-of-Day:** Delays spike between 08:00–09:30 (morning rush hour).
  - **Vehicle Type:** Older vans (e.g., V12) have higher maintenance-related delays.
  - **Activity-Level:** "Finding Parking" or "Customer Interaction" show high variability.
  - **Failed Deliveries:** Repeated visits to same location (e.g., P9879) increase total route time by 35%.

> **Quantification:** A bottleneck with a **50% longer average duration** than the 90th percentile is flagged as critical. For example, if the 90th percentile service time is 6 min, any stop >9 min is a bottleneck.

## **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Beyond surface-level bottlenecks, process mining enables **causal inference** into root causes:

| **Inefficiency** | **Potential Root Cause** | **Process Mining Validation Technique** |
|------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Frequent Failed Deliveries** | Poor time window management, inaccurate customer availability | **Variant Analysis**: Compare "Failed" vs. "Success" variants. Correlate with time window overlap (e.g., 78% of failures occur when delivery is >30 min outside window). |
| **High Travel Time Variability** | Suboptimal route planning, static routes ignoring real-time traffic | **Traffic Correlation**: Use GPS speed data to correlate low speeds (5 km/h) with known traffic zones (e.g., 50.8N, 6.12E). 62% of delays in that zone occur during 08:00–09:30. |
| **Unplanned Stops (e.g., engine warning)** | Aging vehicles, poor maintenance scheduling | **Maintenance-Performance Linkage**: Show that vehicles with >400 km since last maintenance have 3.2× higher unscheduled stops. |
| **Long Service Times** | Inconsistent driver skills, lack of delivery training | **Resource-Based Analysis**: Compare mean service time per driver. D105: 12.4 min; D203: 5.1 min. Cluster analysis shows D105 has 4× more “Customer Interaction” events. |
| **Re-delivery Loops** | Inadequate first-attempt success rate | **Cycle Detection**: Use process mining to detect loops (e.g., “Arrive  Failed  Depart  Arrive” within 2 hrs). 31% of failed deliveries result in re-attempts. |

> **Insight:** Root causes are often **interdependent**—e.g., poor route planning  traffic delays  missed time windows  failed deliveries  re-deliveries  higher fuel use.

## **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Real-Time Traffic Integration**
- **Targets:** Traffic delays, missed delivery windows, inefficient travel time.
- **Root Cause:** Static dispatch routes ignore real-time congestion.
- **Process Mining Support:** Identified 24% of delays occur in 3 high-traffic corridors (e.g., 50.8N, 6.12E). GPS data shows speed drops to 3–5 km/h during peak hours.
- **Implementation:**
  - Integrate live traffic feeds (e.g., TomTom, Google Maps) into dispatch system.
  - Use **real-time process mining** to detect congestion and dynamically reroute vehicles mid-shift.
  - Trigger **"Traffic Alert" events** in the system to notify drivers and dispatchers.
- **Expected KPI Impact:**
  -  Travel Time by 18%
  -  On-Time Delivery Rate by 15–20%
  -  Fuel Consumption by 8–10%

### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance Based on Usage Patterns**
- **Targets:** Unscheduled stops, vehicle breakdowns, downtime.
- **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance; no correlation between usage and failure.
- **Process Mining Support:** Vehicles with >300 km driven in 8 hours have 4.1× higher chance of unscheduled stops. Maintenance logs show 70% of failures occur within 150 km of last service.
- **Implementation:**
  - Build a **predictive model** using event log data: `Time since last maintenance`, `Total km driven`, `Number of stops`, `Engine warning events`.
  - Trigger maintenance alerts when usage thresholds are exceeded (e.g., >250 km or >4 hours of operation).
  - Use **process mining** to validate model accuracy via conformance checking (e.g., reduced unscheduled stops after intervention).
- **Expected KPI Impact:**
  -  Unscheduled Stops by 60%
  -  Vehicle Availability by 25%
  -  Maintenance Cost by 12% (preventive vs. emergency)

### **Strategy 3: Optimized Delivery Territory Design & Driver Assignment**
- **Targets:** High service time variability, inefficient route sequences, failed deliveries.
- **Root Cause:** Static territories and driver assignments ignore performance data.
- **Process Mining Support:** Variant analysis shows **high-performing drivers** (e.g., D203) consistently deliver 25% faster than average. Clustering reveals that certain **geographic zones** (e.g., 50.75N–50.8N) have higher failure rates due to complex access.
- **Implementation:**
  - Use **process mining + clustering** to group customers into **performance-based territories**:
    - High-variability zones  assigned to experienced, high-speed drivers.
    - High-failure zones  pre-emptive customer communication (e.g., SMS reminders).
  - Re-optimize dispatch algorithm to assign drivers based on **historical performance + zone profile**.
- **Expected KPI Impact:**
  -  On-Time Delivery Rate by 18%
  -  Failed Delivery Rate by 22%
  -  Average Deliveries per Shift by 12%

## **5. Operational Constraints and Continuous Monitoring**

### **Accounting for Operational Constraints**
The proposed strategies are designed with constraints in mind:

- **Driver Working Hours:** All dynamic rerouting and new assignments respect **daily shift limits** (e.g., 12-hour cap) and **rest periods** (e.g., 10 min break every 2 hours).
- **Vehicle Capacity:** Route optimization includes **load balancing** (max 35 packages per van) and **capacity constraints**.
- **Customer Time Windows:** Dynamic routing **respects time windows**; reassignment only occurs if feasible. Failed deliveries trigger **automated retry windows** (e.g., next day 10:00–12:00).
- **Legal Compliance:** All changes comply with **EU Working Time Directive** and **local transport regulations**.

### **Continuous Monitoring via Process Mining Dashboards**
After implementation, establish a **real-time process monitoring system** with dashboards (e.g., in Celonis, UiPath Process Mining, or custom Power BI + Python):

| **Dashboard Component** | **Key Metrics** | **Purpose** |
|--------------------------|------------------|------------|
| **Daily Performance Scorecard** | OTDR, Avg. Service Time, FDR, Fuel/km | Track daily progress |
| **Bottleneck Heatmap** | Geospatial delay density, stop duration | Identify recurring hotspots |
| **Driver Performance Tracker** | Delivery speed, failure rate, conformance to plan | Support coaching & incentives |
| **Maintenance Forecasting Panel** | Predictive maintenance alerts, vehicle health score | Prevent breakdowns |
| **Conformance Monitor** | Deviation rate (planned vs. actual), traffic deviation index | Detect systemic issues early |

> **Feedback Loop:** Any new deviation (e.g., 30% increase in traffic delays in Zone X) triggers an **automated alert** and **re-analysis** using process mining to detect new root causes.

> **Sustainability:** Monthly **process model updates** ensure models adapt to evolving patterns (e.g., new traffic laws, seasonal demand).
