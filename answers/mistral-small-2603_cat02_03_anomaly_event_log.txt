### **Analysis of Anomalies in the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log**

The **Procure-to-Pay (P2P)** process follows a standard sequence of activities to ensure proper procurement, verification, and payment. The ideal flow is typically:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition**  2. **Approve Purchase Requisition**  3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)**  4. **Receive Goods**  5. **Vendor Invoice Received**  6. **Match Invoice to PO**  7. **Release Payment**  8. **Close Case**

Deviations from this sequence can indicate inefficiencies, errors, or even fraud. Below are the anomalies observed in the event log:

---

### **1. Case 1002: Incorrect Order of Activities (Invoice Received Before Goods Received)**
**Sequence:**
- Issue Purchase Order (2024-02-01 08:40)
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-03 07:00)
- Release Payment (2024-02-03 07:15)
- Approve Purchase Requisition (2024-02-03 08:00)
- Receive Goods (2024-02-04 09:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-05 08:00)

**Problem:**
- **Invoice received before goods are received**  This is a major red flag.
- **Payment released before invoice matching**  Payment should only occur after verifying that the invoice matches the PO and goods receipt.
- **Approval happens after payment release**  Approval should occur before issuing a PO, not after.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Financial Risk:** Paying an invoice before receiving goods increases the risk of overpayment or fraud (e.g., fake invoices).
- **Compliance Issues:** Violates the **three-way match** principle (PO, Goods Receipt, Invoice) required in accounting.
- **Process Inefficiency:** Approvals should precede PO issuance to prevent unauthorized spending.

---

### **2. Case 1003: Invoice Received Before Goods Received & Payment Released Before Matching**
**Sequence:**
- Issue Purchase Order (2024-02-02 10:00)
- Release Payment (2024-02-08 11:00)
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-09 12:00)
- Receive Goods (2024-02-10 13:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-11 14:00)

**Problem:**
- **Payment released before invoice is received**  Payment should only occur after verifying the invoice.
- **Invoice received after payment**  This suggests either:
  - The invoice was processed late (poor vendor management).
  - The payment was made prematurely (risk of overpayment).
- **Goods received after payment**  The company paid before confirming receipt of goods.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Lack of three-way match**  Payment should only occur after confirming:
  1. The PO was issued.
  2. The goods were received.
  3. The invoice matches the PO and receipt.
- **Cash flow inefficiency**  Paying before receiving goods ties up funds unnecessarily.
- **Audit & Compliance Risks**  May fail financial audits if proper controls are not in place.

---

### **3. Case 1004: Goods Received Before PO Issued**
**Sequence:**
- Create Purchase Requisition (2024-02-05 10:00)
- Approve Purchase Requisition (2024-02-05 10:15)
- Receive Goods (2024-02-06 09:00)
- Issue Purchase Order (2024-02-06 09:15)
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-07 08:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-07 08:30)
- Release Payment (2024-02-07 09:00)

**Problem:**
- **Goods received before PO is issued**  This suggests **maverick buying** (purchasing without a formal PO).
- **PO issued after goods receipt**  The company received goods without a prior approved purchase order.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Lack of procurement control**  Bypassing the PO process can lead to:
  - Unauthorized spending.
  - Difficulty in tracking purchases.
  - Higher risk of duplicate payments or fraud.
- **Compliance violations**  Many organizations require a PO for all purchases to ensure budget control and vendor compliance.
- **Invoice matching issues**  If the PO is issued after goods receipt, matching the invoice becomes harder, increasing the risk of errors.

### **Recommendations for Process Improvement**
1. **Enforce Strict Order of Activities:**
   - Ensure **approval  PO issuance  goods receipt  invoice receipt  matching  payment**.
   - Block payments if the three-way match (PO, Goods Receipt, Invoice) is not complete.

2. **Automate Workflows:**
   - Use **ERP systems (e.g., SAP, Oracle)** to enforce sequence rules and prevent premature payments.

3. **Implement Approval Controls:**
   - Require **purchase requisition approval before PO issuance**.
   - Block payments if approval is missing.

4. **Monitor for Maverick Buying:**
   - Flag cases where goods are received before a PO is issued.
   - Investigate and correct unauthorized purchases.

5. **Audit & Reconciliation:**
   - Regularly review logs for deviations.
   - Conduct **three-way match audits** to ensure compliance.

By addressing these anomalies, the organization can **reduce financial risks, improve compliance, and enhance procurement efficiency**.
