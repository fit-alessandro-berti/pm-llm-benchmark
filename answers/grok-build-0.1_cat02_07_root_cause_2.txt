**1. Cases with Performance Issues**

- **Case 2005**: ~77 hours (3+ days) — **most severe**
- **Case 2003**: ~48 hours (2 days) — **severe**
- **Case 2002**: ~26 hours (1+ day) — **moderate** (noticeably longer than the fast cases)
- **Case 2001**: 1.5 hours — normal
- **Case 2004**: 1.25 hours — normal

Cases 2003 and 2005 are clear outliers, taking 30–60× longer than the low-complexity cases.

**2. Root Cause Analysis**

The data shows a very strong correlation between **Complexity** and duration:

| Case | Complexity | # of "Request Additional Documents" | Duration | Key Attributes |
|------|------------|-------------------------------------|----------|----------------|
| 2001 | Low        | 0                                   | 1.5h     | Region A, Manager_Ann |
| 2004 | Low        | 0                                   | 1.25h    | Region B, Manager_Ann |
| 2002 | Medium     | 1                                   | ~26h     | Region B, Manager_Ann |
| 2003 | High       | 2                                   | ~48h     | Region A, **Manager_Bill**, Adjuster_Mike |
| 2005 | High       | 3                                   | ~77h     | Region B, **Manager_Bill**, Adjuster_Lisa |

**Primary Root Cause: High Complexity**

- High-complexity claims systematically trigger **multiple rounds** of "Request Additional Documents".
- Each additional request adds significant waiting time (presumably for the customer to respond).
- Case 2005 (3 requests) took ~29 hours longer than Case 2003 (2 requests), even though both are High complexity.

**Secondary Patterns (Resource & Region)**

- **Manager_Bill** only appears on the two longest cases (2003 and 2005). All faster cases use **Manager_Ann**.
- **Region B + High complexity** (Case 2005) performed worst. Region B also had the medium case (2002) that was slower than the Region A low-complexity cases.
- **Adjuster_Lisa** (Region B) handled all three document requests in the longest case, with large gaps between them (e.g., requests at 11:30, then next day 17:00, then next day 15:00).

**Interpretation**

The dominant driver is **complexity**, which forces the process into an iterative document-gathering loop. However, the combination of **High Complexity + Manager_Bill + Region B** produces the worst outcomes, suggesting that either:
- These cases are routed to slower resources, or
- Manager_Bill and Adjuster_Lisa have higher workloads or slower response times on complex claims.

**3. Explanations and Recommendations**

**Why these attributes cause delays:**
- High-complexity claims are inherently harder to evaluate, requiring more information.
- The current process has no "fast path" for complex claims — it uses the same sequential document-request loop.
- Manager_Bill appears to be the bottleneck for approvals on complex cases.
- Region B (particularly Adjuster_Lisa) shows longer gaps between document requests.

**Suggestions to Mitigate Root Causes**

1. **Complexity-Based Triage (Highest Impact)**
   - Add an early "Classify Complexity" activity (or do it at Submit/Evaluate).
   - Route High-complexity claims to a dedicated "Complex Claims Team" with different SLAs and processes.

2. **Reduce Document Request Loops**
   - For High complexity, require a more comprehensive initial document checklist.
   - Implement automated reminders or a customer portal to shorten response times.

3. **Resource Reallocation**
   - Investigate why **Manager_Bill** and **Adjuster_Lisa** are only handling the longest cases. Consider workload balancing or training.
   - Compare processing speed of Manager_Ann vs. Manager_Bill on similar cases.

4. **Process Improvement Ideas**
   - Add a "Parallel Document Collection" activity for high-complexity cases.
   - Set maximum allowed "Request Additional Documents" iterations (e.g., max 2) before escalation.

**Summary**: The root cause is primarily **High Complexity** driving repeated document requests. The worst performance occurs when high complexity is combined with specific resources (Manager_Bill, Adjuster_Lisa) and Region B. Addressing the complexity handling mechanism should yield the biggest improvement.
