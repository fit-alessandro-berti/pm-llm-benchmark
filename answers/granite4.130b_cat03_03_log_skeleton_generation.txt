### Explanation of the Choices

| Constraint | Rationale for Selected Pairs |
|------------|------------------------------|
| **Equivalence** | *ReceiveOrder* and *InvoiceCustomer* are logically paired – an order is only invoiced if it was received. They must occur together (same count) in every case. |
| **Always Before** | Enforces the natural ordering of preparation steps: availability check precedes reservation, which precedes picking, etc. |
| **Always After** | Mirrors “always before” but expressed from the perspective of the earlier activity; e.g., an order is received *before* it is checked for availability. |
| **Never Together** | Prevents contradictory states such as recording a payment without first issuing an invoice (the process handles this via ordering rather than coexistence). |
| **Activity Frequencies** | Core fulfillment steps (`RO``D`) happen exactly once per order. *InvoiceCustomer* and *RecordPayment* may be omitted for prepaid or cashondelivery scenarios, hence `{0,1}` bounds. |
| **Directly Follows** | Captures the tight coupling where one activity must immediately follow another (e.g., after picking items they are inspected right away). |
