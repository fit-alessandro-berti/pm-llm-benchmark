# Visualization Description

This image depicts a **business process model** represented as a workflow/process diagram, likely using a notation similar to Causal Nets (C-nets) or a variant of process modeling notation. It consists of two main parts: the main process diagram and a legend.

## Main Process Diagram

The diagram models a **request handling process** with the following activities (nodes):

- **a** – register request (start)
- **b** – examine thoroughly
- **c** – examine casually
- **d** – check ticket
- **e** – decide
- **f** – reinitiate request
- **g** – pay compensation
- **h** – reject request
- **z** – end

### Process Flow
1. The process begins at **a (register request)**.
2. After registration, the flow splits into one of the examination activities (**b**, **c**) together with **d (check ticket)**.
3. All paths converge at **e (decide)**.
4. From the decision point, three outcomes are possible:
   - **g** – pay compensation  end
   - **h** – reject request  end
   - **f** – reinitiate request, which loops back to activities **b**, **c**, or **d**
5. The process ultimately terminates at **z (end)**.

### Notation Elements
- **Rectangles** represent activities/tasks.
- **Black dots (bindings)** on the edges represent input/output bindings that define how arcs are grouped together for splits and joins.
- **Arrows** indicate the direction of flow.

## Legend (Gray Box)

The legend explains six binding patterns used in the diagram:

**Splits (outgoing from a task):**
- **XOR-split** – exactly one outgoing branch is taken (separate dots on each arc)
- **AND-split** – all outgoing branches are taken (dots connected together)
- **OR-split** – any non-empty subset of branches is taken (combined pattern with arcs)

**Joins (incoming to a task):**
- **XOR-join** – exactly one incoming branch activates the task
- **AND-join** – all incoming branches must activate together
- **OR-join** – any non-empty subset of incoming branches activates the task

This type of diagram is commonly used in **process mining** and **workflow management** to represent control-flow logic including choice, parallelism, and iteration (loops, like the reinitiation path via **f**).
