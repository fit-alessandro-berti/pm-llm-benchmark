###  **Start Event**
The process begins when a **customer request** is submitted—this could be an order, quote inquiry, or project proposal.

---

###  **Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**
This initial task involves capturing the customer's submission, logging details (e.g., product/service desired, specifications, timeline), and categorizing it as either **standard** or **custom**.

>  *Purpose*: Ensure the request is formally received and ready for evaluation. This is the entry point that triggers all subsequent actions.*

---

###  **Gateway (XOR): Check Request Type**
An **exclusive (XOR) gateway** splits the flow based on the **type of request**:
- Only **one** of the two paths will be taken:
  -  **Standard Request**  Leads to standard validation
  -  **Custom Request**  Triggers feasibility analysis

>  *This branching reflects a key business policy:* Different handling is needed based on complexity and uniqueness of the request.

---

##  Path 1: Standard Request
###  **Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
Basic checks are run to ensure the request meets predefined criteria (e.g., compatible with available SKUs, within service scope).

###  **Gateway (AND): Run Parallel Checks**
An **inclusive (AND) gateway** activates **two parallel validation tasks**:
- **Task C1: Credit Check**  Verify customer's creditworthiness or financial standing.
- **Task C2: Inventory Check**  Confirm item(s) are in stock or can be fulfilled from existing supply.

>  *Purpose:* These checks proceed **in parallel** to save time. The process only continues once **both complete** (a "join" synchronizes them).

###  **Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**
Based on inventory availability and logistics scheduling, a delivery estimate is generated.

---

##  Path 2: Custom Request
###  **Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**
The operations/engineering team evaluates whether the custom specifications can be met (technical capability, resource availability, regulatory compliance, etc.).

###  **Gateway (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**
Another **exclusive decision point**:
-  **Yes**  Proceed to quotation phase
-  **No**  **Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"**  **End Event**

>  *If not feasible*, the process ends here—customer is informed politely, possibly with reasons or alternatives.

>  *If feasible*, move to:

###  **Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"**
A tailored quote is created, including cost, timeline, materials, labor, and terms.

---

##  Convergence Point: Post-Validation / Quotation
At this stage, **both paths converge** informally:
- Standard path ends at **Task D (Delivery Date)**
- Custom path ends at **Task E1 (Custom Quotation)**

From here, both proceed to the **approval decision**.

---

###  **Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**
Another decision point:
- Based on factors like:
  - Order value
  - Customer type (new vs. existing)
  - Risk level
  - Internal policies

####  [If Yes]  **Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"**
Escalated review occurs. Could involve documentation, justification, or negotiation.

#####  **Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**
-  **Yes**  Proceed to final invoicing
-  **No**  **Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"**

>  **Loop Back Logic**:
- From **H**, the process loops back:
  - To **Task E1** if it was a **custom request** (revise quotation)
  - To **Task D** if it was **standard** (adjust delivery date, recheck options)

This rework loop allows refinement without restarting the entire process—efficient error correction.

####  [If No Approval Needed]  **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**
Bypasses manual review—automated or pre-authorized handling for low-risk or routine cases.

---

###  **Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**
Final step: Notify the customer of acceptance, deliverables, invoice, and next steps (e.g., payment, production start).

---

###  **End Event**
The process concludes—order is confirmed and ready for fulfillment.

###  Key Observations & Clarifications

#### 1. **Differences Between Standard vs. Custom Requests**
| Aspect | Standard Request | Custom Request |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| **Validation** | Structured, rule-based | Technical, engineering-led |
| **Parallel Checks** | Yes (credit + inventory) | No — follows linear feasibility |
| **Quotation** | Implied or automatic | Manually prepared |
| **Feasibility Gate** | Not required | Critical decision point |
| **Rework Loop Target** | Task D (delivery) | Task E1 (quote) |

#### 2. **Gateways Explained**
- **XOR (Exclusive Gateway)**: "Either/or" logic—only one path taken.
  - Used for request type, feasibility, approval decisions.
- **AND (Inclusive/Parallel Gateway)**: "Do all" — splits into simultaneous tasks, waits for all to finish before continuing.
  - Ensures credit and inventory checks **don't bottleneck** each other.

#### 3. **When Are Approvals Triggered?**
Approvals depend on **internal risk thresholds**, such as:
- High-value custom quotes
- Customers with credit risk
- Tight or unusual delivery timelines
- Deviations from standard pricing

Organizations may codify these rules in policy or configure them in workflow engines.

#### 4. **When Does Rework Occur?**
Only **after rejection of approval**:
- Manager says “not acceptable”  team **revises** either:
  - The **delivery commitment** (standard)
  - The **quotation** (custom)
Then cycles back for **re-submission** of approval.

>  This supports **agile negotiation** within process boundaries.
