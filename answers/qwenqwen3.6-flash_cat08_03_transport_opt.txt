### **Process Mining Consultancy Report: Speedy Parcels Operational Optimization**

**To:** Speedy Parcels Management  
**From:** Process Mining Consultant  
**Subject:** Comprehensive Analysis and Optimization Strategy for Last-Mile Delivery Operations  

Based on the six-month event log data and the operational challenges identified, the following approach applies process mining techniques to uncover root causes of inefficiency and propose data-driven solutions. The analysis leverages the integration of GPS, scanner, dispatch, and maintenance data to transform raw logs into actionable process intelligence.

---

### **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### **1.1 Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To build a cohesive event log, raw data must be harmonized into a standard format (`Case ID`, `Timestamp`, `Activity`, `Resources`, `Attributes`).

*   **Case ID Construction:**
    *   *Primary Granularity:* `Vehicle-Day` (e.g., `V12-20241205`) serves as the primary case ID for shift-level analysis.
    *   *Secondary Granularity:* For detailed stop analysis, the log should be split into `Stop-Level` cases linked to the parent Vehicle-Day case, using `Package ID` or sequential stop numbers.
*   **Event Mapping & Fusion:**
    *   *Scanner Events:* Map directly to business activities (`Arrive Customer`, `Delivery Success`, `Failed Attempt`, `Depart Customer`).
    *   *GPS Events:* High-frequency GPS data requires aggregation. We will derive events such as `Travel Segment`, `Idle/Traffic Delay`, and `Unplanned Stop` based on speed thresholds and dwell times.
    *   *Dispatch Integration:* Enrich the log with planned attributes (`Planned Route Sequence`, `Time Windows`, `Assigned Packages`) to enable conformance checking.
    *   *Maintenance Integration:* Link maintenance events to Vehicle IDs and timestamps to flag cases affected by breakdowns.
*   **Challenges & Mitigation:**
    *   *GPS Drift/Signal Loss:* Implement Kalman filtering or map-matching algorithms to correct coordinates and handle signal gaps in urban canyons.
    *   *Event Alignment:* GPS arrival may precede Scanner arrival due to parking search. We will define a "Service Window" attribute to capture the full duration from vehicle arrival to scanner interaction.
    *   *Missing Data:* Impute missing scanner scans using GPS stop detection logic, flagging them as "Data Quality Risk" rather than discarding cases.

#### **1.2 Process Discovery**
We will apply discovery algorithms to reconstruct the actual process flow:
*   **Algorithms:** Use the **Inductive Miner** for robustness against noise and loops, and the **Heuristic Miner** to capture frequent patterns.
*   **Visualization:** Generate a process map where nodes represent activities (e.g., `Depart Depot`, `Travel`, `Arrive Stop`, `Scan Success`, `Scan Failed`, `Maintenance Stop`, `Return Depot`) and edges represent transitions.
*   **Enrichment:** Nodes will be annotated with aggregate metrics (e.g., average duration, frequency) to immediately highlight slow or frequent paths.

#### **1.3 Conformance Checking**
We will compare the discovered process against the **Dispatch System's Planned Routes**:
*   **Alignment Technique:** Use alignment-based conformance checking to calculate the "deviation cost" for each case.
*   **Deviation Types to Detect:**
    *   *Sequence Deviations:* Stops visited out of planned order; skipped stops.
    *   *Activity Deviations:* Unplanned stops (e.g., engine warning, unauthorized breaks) not present in the plan.
    *   *Timing Deviations:* Arrival/Departure times exceeding time windows; excessive travel time between stops.
    *   *Frequency Deviations:* Number of stops performed vs. planned; number of failed attempts vs. expected success rate.
*   **Output:** A "Deviation Heatmap" showing routes or drivers with the highest conformance costs, indicating where planning assumptions fail.

---

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### **2.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
KPIs will be calculated directly from the enriched event log:

| KPI | Calculation Logic | Relevance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR)** | `% of Deliveries where `Delivery Success` timestamp $\le$ `End of Time Window`. | Customer satisfaction; core goal. |
| **Average Turnaround Time (ATT)** | `Depart Depot` to `Arrive Depot` duration. | Overall shift efficiency. |
| **Service Time Variance** | Std Dev of (`Depart Customer` - `Arrive Customer`) per driver/route. | Process stability; root of delays. |
| **Travel vs. Service Ratio** | Total GPS Travel Time / Total Scanner Service Time. | Highlights if delays are due to movement or stops. |
| **Fuel Efficiency Proxy** | GPS-derived distance and hard braking/idling events per km. | Operational cost control. |
| **Vehicle Utilization Rate** | `Time Moving` / `Total Shift Time`. | Asset productivity. |
| **Failed Delivery Rate** | `Count(Scan Failed)` / `Total Attempts`. | Cost driver (re-delivery); customer friction. |
| **Maintenance Downtime Impact** | Duration of `Unscheduled Stop` events per vehicle. | Reliability; cost. |

#### **2.2 Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
*   **Performance Profiles on Process Maps:** Color-code the process map by duration. Bottlenecks appear as "red" nodes (e.g., `Travel Segment` between Zone A and B consistently taking >20 mins).
*   **Bottleneck Analysis Algorithm:** Automatically detect stages where the process waits longest. We will distinguish between:
    *   *Traffic Bottlenecks:* GPS shows low speed/idling over long distances.
    *   *Service Bottlenecks:* High dwell time at `Arrive/Depart Customer`.
    *   *Parking/Access Bottlenecks: **Gap Analysis** between GPS arrival and Scanner arrival. A large gap indicates time spent searching for parking or access issues.
*   **Quantification:** Impact analysis will correlate bottlenecks with KPI degradation. For example, "Routes with >15 mins parking search time show a 22% drop in OTDR."

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Beyond identifying bottlenecks, we will use advanced mining techniques to validate root causes:

*   **Variant Analysis:**
    *   *Technique:* Cluster cases by performance (e.g., High OTDR vs. Low OTDR).
    *   *Insight:* Compare process flows of high vs. low performers. Do high-performing drivers take different routes? Do they handle specific stop types differently? This validates if **Suboptimal Route Planning** or **Driver Skill** is a root cause.
*   **Correlation Analysis:**
    *   *Traffic Correlation:* Enrich the log with external traffic data (or use GPS speed as a proxy). Correlate delays with specific time slots and locations. Validates **Traffic Congestion Patterns**.
    *   *Maintenance Correlation:* Analyze the sequence of events leading to `Unscheduled Stop`. Do breakdowns cluster after specific usage patterns (e.g., high idling, short trips)? Validates **Reactive Maintenance** issues.
*   **Dwell Time Decomposition:**
    *   *Technique:* Split dwell time into `Parking Search` (GPS to Scanner) and `Customer Interaction` (Scanner to Scanner).
    *   *Insight:* If `Parking Search` is high for specific drivers, the issue is **Driver Familiarity/Navigation**. If `Customer Interaction` is high, the issue may be **Package Complexity** or **Driver Efficiency**.
*   **Failed Delivery Root Cause Mining:**
    *   *Technique:* Analyze the `Notes` attribute for `Scan Failed` events using text mining.
    *   *Insight:* Identify patterns (e.g., "Customer Not Home" vs. "Access Denied"). Correlate failures with specific time windows or customer zones to validate **Poor Time Window Management**.

### **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

Based on the analysis, we propose three concrete strategies:

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing Engine with Real-Time Traffic Adaptation**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Late deliveries due to static routing and traffic jams.
*   **Root Cause:** Dispatch system uses static travel time estimates; does not account for real-time congestion or discovered traffic hotspots.
*   **Data Support:** Process mining reveals specific route segments with high variance in travel time and frequent sequence deviations. Variant analysis shows routes avoiding these segments perform better.
*   **Action:** Implement a routing algorithm that ingests real-time traffic data and adjusts routes dynamically. Use mining insights to weight historical delay risks in the routing model.
*   **Expected Impact:** +15% OTDR; -10% Travel Time; -5% Fuel Consumption.

#### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance & Vehicle Health Management**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** High maintenance costs and unscheduled stops disrupting delivery schedules.
*   **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance; lack of correlation between driving behavior/usage patterns and vehicle wear.
*   **Data Support:** Correlation analysis links `Unscheduled Stops` to specific vehicle models and usage patterns (e.g., high idle time, frequent short trips causing cooling issues). Maintenance logs show recurring issues before failure.
*   **Action:** Develop a predictive model based on GPS usage metrics (idling, acceleration, mileage) to schedule maintenance *before* failure. Adjust maintenance schedules based on actual utilization rather than fixed intervals.
*   **Expected Impact:** -20% Unscheduled Downtime; -15% Maintenance Costs; Improved Vehicle Utilization.

#### **Strategy 3: Service Level Optimization & Failed Delivery Reduction**
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Failed deliveries requiring re-delivery; high service time variance.
*   **Root Cause:** Inaccurate customer time windows; lack of customer communication; inefficient parking/loading procedures.
*   **Data Support:** High rate of "Customer Not Home" failures in specific time slots. Dwell time analysis shows high variance in service times across drivers, suggesting inconsistent procedures. Parking search gaps are significant in dense urban zones.
*   **Action:**
    *   *Customer Side:* Use data to offer precise time windows and proactive SMS updates. Implement "Click-to-Confirm" delivery windows.
    *   *Operational Side:* Standardize driver procedures for parking/loading based on best-practice variant analysis. Provide drivers with parking guidance for high-difficulty stops.
*   **Expected Impact:** -30% Failed Delivery Rate; -10% Cost per Delivery; Improved Customer Satisfaction.

Optimization strategies must respect hard constraints:
*   **Driver Hours:** The dynamic routing engine will enforce legal driving hours and break requirements, using the event log to validate current compliance.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** Route optimization will ensure package volume/weight constraints are met, using dispatch data enriched with actual package attributes.
*   **Time Windows:** The routing algorithm will prioritize hard time windows. Process mining will identify "soft" vs. "hard" window violations to refine customer expectations.
*   *Integration:* Process mining insights will feed into the constraint solver. For example, if mining shows a specific zone always causes delays, the solver can add a "buffer time" constraint for that zone.

#### **5.2 Continuous Monitoring Plan**
To ensure sustainability, we will deploy a **Process Mining Dashboard**:

*   **Real-Time Operational View:**
    *   *Live Process Map:* Overlay current vehicle positions and status. Highlight deviations from planned routes in real-time.
    *   *Alerting:* Automated alerts for significant deviations (e.g., vehicle idle >15 mins, missed time window).
*   **Historical Performance Dashboard:**
    *   *KPI Trends:* OTDR, Cost per Delivery, and Utilization trends over time.
    *   *Bottleneck Tracker:* Monitor the effectiveness of optimizations. Are traffic bottlenecks resolving? Is maintenance downtime dropping?
    *   *Driver Scorecards:* Compare driver performance against benchmarks, highlighting areas for training.
*   **Feedback Loop:**
    *   The dashboard will provide data back to the planning system. For instance, if the dashboard shows that a specific time window is consistently missed, the dispatch system can automatically adjust future estimates.
    *   **Quarterly Process Reviews:** Use process mining to re-evaluate the process model, ensuring optimizations haven't introduced new bottlenecks and identifying new areas for improvement.
