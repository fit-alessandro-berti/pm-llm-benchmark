**Analysis of Anomalies in the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log**

Below is an analysis of the event log, focusing on deviations from standard P2P process controls. The typical P2P flow follows this sequence with key control points:

1. Create Purchase Requisition
2. Approve Purchase Requisition (mandatory control)
3. Issue Purchase Order (PO)
4. Receive Goods (Goods Receipt)
5. Receive Vendor Invoice
6. Match Invoice to PO (typically 3-way match: PO + Goods Receipt + Invoice)
7. Release Payment
8. Close Case

Payments should generally only occur **after** goods are received and the invoice has been properly matched. Below are the identified anomalies:

### 1. Case 1002 – Multiple Critical Control Failures

This case contains the most severe deviations:

- **Purchase Order issued without requisition approval**  
  The PO was created immediately after the requisition, bypassing the approval step. Requisition approval is a fundamental control to ensure purchases are authorized before committing to a vendor.

- **Payment released before goods were received**  
  Payment occurred on 2024-02-03 at 07:15, while goods were only received the next day (2024-02-04). This creates a significant risk of paying for goods that were never delivered.

- **Requisition approved after the PO was issued and payment was made**  
  The approval step occurred *after* both the PO and payment. This completely defeats the purpose of the approval control and suggests either a serious process breakdown or possible fraudulent activity.

- **Invoice received before goods receipt**  
  While not always a fatal error, receiving the invoice before delivery increases the risk of paying for undelivered goods.

**Risk**: High risk of fraud, unauthorized spending, and financial loss.

### 2. Case 1003 – Premature Payment

- **Payment released before goods receipt and invoice**  
  The payment was made on 2024-02-08, while the invoice was only received the next day (2024-02-09) and goods were received even later (2024-02-10). 

This is a major control failure. Standard practice requires a **3-way match** (PO + Goods Receipt + Invoice) before releasing payment. Paying without confirmation of delivery creates a high risk of overpayment or paying for non-existent goods/services.

**Risk**: Financial exposure and weak internal controls.

### 3. Case 1004 – Goods Received Before Purchase Order

- **Goods receipt occurred before the Purchase Order was issued**  
  Goods were received on 2024-02-06 at 09:00, but the PO was only issued later that same day (09:15). This is a significant deviation from standard practice.

In a well-controlled P2P process, goods should generally not be accepted without a valid, approved purchase order. Receiving goods before the PO suggests either:
- Unauthorized purchases
- Emergency purchases made outside the system
- Poor vendor management

**Risk**: Lack of authorization, potential for maverick spending, and difficulty in tracking commitments.

### 4. Case 1001 – Compliant

This case follows a logical and controlled sequence:
- Requisition  Approval  PO  Goods Receipt  Invoice  Matching  Payment

No major anomalies were detected in this case.

### Summary of Key Issues

| Case ID | Main Anomaly | Control Weakness | Risk Level |
|---------|--------------|------------------|------------|
| **1002** | Payment before goods receipt + late requisition approval | Bypassed approval + paid for undelivered goods | **High** |
| **1003** | Payment before goods receipt and invoice | No 3-way match before payment | **High** |
| **1004** | Goods received before PO issued | Unauthorized receipt | **Medium-High** |
| **1001** | None | Compliant | Low |

### Recommendations

- **Case 1002** and **Case 1003** should be flagged for investigation, as they show clear circumvention of financial controls.
- The organization should enforce system controls that prevent payment release without a completed 3-way match.
- Receiving goods without a PO (as in Case 1004) should trigger an exception process with proper authorization and documentation.
