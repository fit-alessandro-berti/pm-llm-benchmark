## BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

### 1. Process Scope
**Process name:** Invoice Approval Process  
**Start event:** Invoice received from supplier  
**End events:**  
- Invoice scheduled for payment  
- Invoice rejected / returned to supplier  
- Invoice placed on hold pending clarification or corrected invoice

---

### 2. Participants / Lanes
1. **Supplier**
2. **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary / AP)**
3. **Purchasing Officer / Purchasing Department (Dan)**
4. **Department Manager (Carlos)**
5. **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)**
6. **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)**
7. **Accounting / Approval System**

---

### 3. Main Process Flow

#### Start Event
- **Invoice received**  
  - Typical channel mentioned: **email from supplier**

#### Task 1 — AP Clerk: Receive and review invoice
- AP receives invoice and performs an initial check for required information, especially **purchase order (PO) number**.

#### Gateway G1 — Is a valid PO number present?
- **Yes**  continue to Task 2
- **No**  go to Exception Flow E1

#### Task 2 — AP Clerk: Log invoice in accounting system
- AP enters invoice into the accounting system.

#### Task 3 — AP Clerk: Forward invoice to Purchasing for receipt/order confirmation
- AP sends invoice details to Purchasing.
- Purpose: verify that goods/services were received and that invoice aligns with what was ordered.

#### Task 4 — Purchasing: Review invoice against PO and receipt information
- Purchasing compares:
  - quantities
  - prices
  - delivery charges
  - ordered items vs billed items
- Purchasing also confirms goods/services receipt, per Mary’s account.

#### Gateway G2 — Does Purchasing confirm invoice / receipt / PO alignment?
- **Yes**  go to Task 5
- **No**  go to Exception Flow E2

#### Task 5 — AP Clerk: Match invoice line items against PO in system
- AP performs formal matching in the system after Purchasing confirmation.

#### Gateway G3 — Does invoice match PO in system?
- **Yes**  go to Task 6
- **No**  go to Exception Flow E3

#### Task 6 — System: Send manager approval request
- Trigger condition: goods/services confirmed and invoice matched to PO.
- System sends automatic email/notification to Department Manager.

#### Task 7 — Department Manager: Review invoice package
- Manager reviews:
  - invoice details
  - PO
  - Purchasing confirmation
  - quantities/pricing
  - special notes
  - possible budget anomalies or suspicious items

#### Gateway G4 — Manager decision
- **Approve**  go to Task 8
- **Reject / request investigation**  go to Exception Flow E4

#### Task 8 — AP Clerk / AP Team: Schedule invoice for payment
- Invoice is added to next payment cycle / payment run.

#### End Event
- **Invoice scheduled for payment**

---

### 4. Exception / Alternate Flows

## E1. Missing PO Number / Invalid PO Reference
**Trigger:** Invoice arrives without PO number or with unconfirmed PO reference.

**Primary path described by Mary:**
1. **AP Clerk:** Send invoice back to supplier requesting more details / PO information.
2. **Supplier:** Provide missing details or revised invoice.
3. **AP Clerk:** Re-review invoice.
4. Return to **Gateway G1**.

**Additional exception handling from Rita and Karen:**
- If invoice still cannot be linked to a valid PO:
  1. **AP Clerk:** Escalate to AP Supervisor.
  2. **AP Supervisor:** Investigate with department heads and/or supplier.
  3. **Gateway E1a — Can retrospective PO be created or valid PO obtained?**
     - **Yes**  invoice returns to normal flow at **Task 2** or **Task 3** (uncertain exact re-entry point)
     - **No**  **End Event: Invoice rejected**
- **Supplier Relationship Manager** may contact supplier to obtain missing PO or revised invoice.

**Note on uncertainty:**  
Dan states Purchasing cannot proceed without a confirmed PO. Rita notes that in some informal service cases, a retrospective PO may be created instead of immediate rejection.

---

## E2. Purchasing finds discrepancy during review
**Trigger examples:**
- Extra units billed
- Pricing differences
- delivery charge issue
- missing information
- inability to confirm receipt/order alignment

**Possible actions:**
1. **Purchasing:** Contact supplier directly for clarification or corrected invoice; or
2. **Purchasing:** Ask AP to hold invoice pending corrected version; or
3. **Purchasing:** Inform AP that PO information is insufficient / missing.

**Supporting participants:**
- **Supplier Relationship Manager:** checks supplier contract and confirms correct pricing/terms.
- **AP Supervisor:** becomes involved if issue cannot be resolved easily.

**Gateway E2a — Is discrepancy resolved?**
- **Yes**  Purchasing sends confirmation email to AP  proceed to **Task 5**
- **No, corrected invoice required**  invoice remains **on hold** until supplier sends corrected version, then re-enters review
- **No, unresolved / complex**  escalate to **AP Supervisor** for coordination

**Possible end/temporary state:**
- **Invoice on hold pending supplier correction**

---

## E3. AP matching discrepancy after Purchasing confirmation
**Trigger:** AP line-item match against PO reveals discrepancy (incorrect pricing, missing items, mismatch).

**Flow:**
1. **AP Clerk:** Email Purchasing team for clarification.
2. **Gateway E3a — Can discrepancy be resolved quickly?**
   - **Yes**  update/confirm information and return to **Task 5** or proceed onward if match is achieved
   - **No**  escalate to **AP Supervisor**

3. **AP Supervisor:** Coordinate with supplier, Purchasing, department heads, and possibly Supplier Relationship Manager.
4. **Gateway E3b — Is issue resolved?**
   - **Yes**  return to normal flow, typically at **Task 4** or **Task 5**
   - **No**  
     - if no valid basis for payment: **End Event: Invoice rejected**
     - if waiting on external response: **Invoice on hold**

**Note on uncertainty:**  
The interviews do not fully distinguish whether all discrepancies are first detected by Purchasing or AP; both appear to perform comparison checks. Therefore, discrepancy handling may occur in either lane before manager approval.

---

## E4. Manager rejects or requests investigation
**Trigger:** Manager identifies suspicious items, unexpected total amount, budget variance, or other concerns.

**Flow:**
1. **Department Manager:** Reject invoice in system or ask AP to hold and investigate further.
2. **AP Clerk:** Places invoice on hold and investigates.
3. **Possible collaborators:**
   - Purchasing for PO/order verification
   - AP Supervisor for exception coordination
   - Supplier Relationship Manager for supplier/contract clarification
4. **Gateway E4a — Is concern resolved?**
   - **Yes**  return to **Task 6** (manager approval request again) or directly to **Task 7** for renewed review
   - **No**  **End Event: Invoice rejected** or remain **on hold**

**Frequency note:**  
Carlos says this occurs infrequently, roughly every couple of months.

### 6. System Interactions
- **Accounting system** used to:
  - log invoice
  - match invoice against PO
  - display invoice/PO details to manager
  - trigger automatic email/notification for manager approval
- Manager approval is executed in the system.
- Some communications occur outside the system via **email**.

---

### 7. Role Responsibilities Summary

- **Supplier**
  - Sends invoice
  - May provide corrected invoice or missing PO details

- **Accounts Payable Clerk**
  - Receives invoice
  - Checks for PO
  - Logs invoice
  - Sends to Purchasing
  - Matches invoice to PO
  - coordinates clarifications
  - schedules payment after approval

- **Purchasing**
  - Confirms PO alignment and receipt/order details
  - Reviews quantities, prices, delivery charges
  - Contacts supplier or asks AP to hold invoice when discrepancies exist

- **Department Manager**
  - Performs final business approval
  - Approves or rejects/requests investigation

- **AP Supervisor**
  - Handles unresolved exceptions
  - coordinates across departments
  - may arrange retrospective PO or decide rejection path

- **Supplier Relationship Manager**
  - Helps resolve supplier-related issues
  - checks contract pricing/terms
  - obtains missing PO details or revised invoices

- **System**
  - Records invoice
  - supports match and approval workflow
  - sends approval notifications

### 8. Consolidated BPMN-style Sequence

```text
Start: Invoice received from supplier
 AP reviews invoice
 Gateway: Valid PO present?
    Yes:
       AP logs invoice in accounting system
       AP forwards invoice to Purchasing
       Purchasing verifies receipt and compares invoice to PO
       Gateway: Purchasing confirms?
          Yes:
             AP matches invoice line items against PO in system
             Gateway: Match successful?
                Yes:
                   System sends approval request to Department Manager
                   Manager reviews invoice, PO, and confirmation
                   Gateway: Manager approves?
                      Yes:
                         AP schedules invoice for payment
                         End: Invoice scheduled for payment
                      No:
                         AP holds/investigates
                         resolve with Purchasing / Supervisor / Supplier Relationship Manager
                         if resolved, return to manager review
                         else End: Invoice rejected or on hold
                No:
                   AP seeks clarification from Purchasing
                   if unresolved, escalate to AP Supervisor
                   resolve and return to matching, or reject/on hold
          No:
             Purchasing/AP contacts supplier or holds invoice
             Supplier may send corrected invoice
             if unresolved, escalate to AP Supervisor
             if resolved, return to AP matching or Purchasing confirmation
             else End: Invoice on hold or rejected
    No:
       AP requests missing details from supplier
       if PO later provided, resume process
       if still unresolved, escalate to AP Supervisor
       retrospective PO may be created in exceptional cases
       if no valid PO can be established, End: Invoice rejected
```

---

### 9. Ambiguities / Modeling Notes
1. **Who confirms goods receipt?**  
   Mary says Purchasing confirms goods/services were received. Dan focuses more broadly on comparing invoice to PO and what was ordered. Model includes Purchasing as the confirming role.

2. **Order of matching vs Purchasing confirmation:**  
   Mary implies AP logs invoice, sends to Purchasing, then performs detailed match after confirmation. Dan suggests Purchasing already compares invoice and PO. To reconcile, the model includes:
   - Purchasing review/confirmation first
   - AP formal system match afterward  
   But in practice, there may be overlap.

3. **Handling of no-PO invoices:**  
   Standard flow says no invoice proceeds without PO. However, Rita describes an exception where retrospective POs may be created for informal service purchases.

4. **Manager rejection outcome:**  
   It is unclear whether manager rejection always ends the process or may lead to rework. Interviews indicate both hold-and-investigate and possible rejection, so both outcomes are modeled.
