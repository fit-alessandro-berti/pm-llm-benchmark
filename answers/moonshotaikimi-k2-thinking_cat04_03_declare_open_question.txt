## **Temporal & Dependency Constraints: The Process Orchestration**

### **Response & Succession: The Linear Flow Engine**

```python
'Receive_Application'  'Preliminary_Credit_Check'  # response
'Preliminary_Credit_Check'  'Gather_Additional_Documents'  # succession
```

- **Operational Reality**: These create a **mandatory pipeline**: no credit check until application is received; no document gathering until credit is preliminarily approved. This prevents wasted work on unqualified applicants and ensures risk-based triage.

- **Business Logic**: If preliminary check fails (e.g., sub-500 FICO), the system may auto-terminate before document collection, saving ~$200 in processing costs per rejected application.

### **Chain Constraints: The "No Interruption" Rules**

```python
'Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package'  'Transfer_Funds'  # chainresponse
'Quality_Assurance_Review'  'Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package'  # chainsuccession
```

- **Critical Control**: Chain constraints mean **immediate, direct succession**—no intermediate activities allowed. This is a **segregation of duties** masterpiece:
  - **D  G**: The *same officer* who assembles the offer must trigger fund transfer without delay, preventing offer terms from being modified after QA approval.
  - **E  D**: QA review must *directly* precede offer assembly, ensuring no "desk drawer" time where errors could be introduced post-review.

In practice, this manifests as a "QA lock"—once E is complete, the system auto-generates D and pushes it to the officer's queue within seconds.

### **Alt-Constraints: The Mutually Exclusive Pathways**

```python
'Gather_Additional_Documents'  'Quality_Assurance_Review'  # altsuccession
```

- **Process Branching**: The "alt" prefix indicates **exclusive OR** behavior. Here, document gathering either *precedes* or *follows* QA review, but **not both**. This reveals a **parallel underwriting track**:
  - **Track 1 (Docs  QA)**: Standard loans—documents collected first, then full QA
  - **Track 2 (QA  Docs)**: Conditional approvals—QA reviews terms *assuming* documents will be valid, then validates them

This flexibility speeds up high-credit-tier applications while maintaining rigor.

## **Anti-Pattern Constraints: The "Never Look Back" Philosophy**

### **Non-Coexistence & Non-Succession: Temporal Firewalls**

```python
'Transfer_Funds'  'Receive_Application'  # noncoexistence
'Notify_Customer'  'Preliminary_Credit_Check'  # nonsuccession
```

- **Fraud Prevention**: These constraints create **irreversible process phases**:
  - **G  A**: Once funds are disbursed, the application record becomes **read-only**. This prevents post-funding manipulation of source data—critical for **CFPB UDAAP** (Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices) investigations.
  - **H  B**: Customer notification (H) can never loop back to preliminary checks (B). This stops "do-over" games where unfavorable decisions are hidden from applicants.

In legacy systems, this was a major vulnerability: loan officers could re-process applications under different IDs. The DECLARE model enforces **cryptographic immutability** at the event log level.
