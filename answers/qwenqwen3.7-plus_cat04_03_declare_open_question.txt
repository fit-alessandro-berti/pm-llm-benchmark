### 1. Practical Meanings of the Activities
The activities map neatly to a standard, highly regulated financial services workflow:

*   **Assessment (B):** A hard inquiry or soft pull with credit bureaus to establish baseline risk.
*   **Documentation (C):** The "Know Your Customer" (KYC) and income verification phase (e.g., W-2s, bank statements).
*   **Packaging (D):** Underwriting the loan, calculating risk-adjusted interest rates, and drafting the legal agreement.
*   **Quality Assurance (E):** A secondary, independent review to catch underwriting errors, missing signatures, or compliance violations.
*   **Authorization (F):** The final sign-off by a credit officer or automated system, legally binding the institution to the terms.
*   **Fulfillment (G):** The actual ACH transfer or wire of funds to the borrower.
*   **Communication (H):** Sending the approval/denial letter, funding confirmation, and repayment schedule to the customer.

The DECLARE constraints act as the "guardrails" of this process. They can be grouped into four functional categories:

*   **Init & Existence:** The process *must* start with `Receive_Application` (A), and both the application and `Gather_Additional_Documents` (C) must occur at least once. This ensures no loan is processed without a formal, documented starting point and necessary evidence.

### 3. The Interplay of Rules: A Controlled Ecosystem
The true power of this model is how the rules interact to create a self-correcting system:

*   **The "Exactly One" Credit Check + Response Rule:** Because a credit check must happen *exactly once*, and *must* follow an application, the system is protected from both "ghost applications" (applications with no checks) and "fishing expeditions" (multiple checks on a single applicant). 
*   **The QA Bottleneck:** The combination of `responded_existence` (Offer requires QA), `precedence` (QA must precede Authorization), and `chainsuccession` (QA immediately triggers Offer Assembly) creates an inescapable quality funnel. An underwriter cannot bypass QA to quickly authorize a high-value loan. The system literally will not allow the `Authorize_Contract_Terms` event to be logged unless `Quality_Assurance_Review` has already occurred.
*   **The Fulfillment Safeguard:** The interplay of `altresponse` and `altprecedence` between `Transfer_Funds` (G) and `Notify_Customer` (H) creates a tight coupling. It dictates that if funds are transferred, the customer must be notified, *and* the notification must precede the transfer. This ensures the customer is aware of the incoming funds (and the accompanying debt obligation) before or exactly as the money moves, preventing "surprise" disbursements that could trigger fraud alerts at the customer's receiving bank.

5.  **Customer Experience (CX) & Trust:** The rule ensuring `Notify_Customer` precedes `Transfer_Funds` is a CX best practice.
