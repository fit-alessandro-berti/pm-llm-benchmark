# 20 Thought-Provoking Questions About the Supply Chain Process

## Risk & Resilience

1. **How vulnerable is the supply chain to geopolitical disruptions**, given that critical components (microprocessors from Taiwan, screens from South Korea) come from regions with significant political tensions—and what contingency plans exist if a key supplier becomes inaccessible?

2. **What would be the cascading impact of a single point of failure**—such as the Malaysian assembly plant going offline due to a natural disaster, pandemic, or labor strike—and should the company invest in geographically redundant manufacturing capacity?

3. **How does the company balance the cost savings of single-sourcing critical components against the resilience benefits of multi-sourcing**, and which components currently pose the greatest concentration risk?

4. **How quickly can the supply chain detect and respond to quality issues** that originate at a specific supplier, and what mechanisms exist to trace defective units back to specific component batches once products are in consumers' hands?

## Strategy & Trade-offs

5. **What criteria determine whether shipments go by air freight versus cargo ship**, and how does the company quantify the trade-off between speed, cost, and carbon emissions for each route and product launch scenario?

6. **How does the just-in-time strategy for high-demand markets perform during demand shocks** (e.g., a viral product launch or sudden competitor failure), and at what point does the risk of stockouts outweigh the inventory carrying cost savings?

7. **Should the company consider nearshoring or regionalizing assembly operations** closer to major markets, and what would the total cost-of-ownership analysis look like compared to the current centralized Malaysian model?

8. **How are currency fluctuations across multiple countries hedged or absorbed**, and who bears the financial risk when exchange rates move significantly between component ordering and final product sale?

## Data, Forecasting & Technology

9. **How accurate are the machine learning demand forecasts in practice**, and what happens organizationally when human planners disagree with algorithmic recommendations—who has the final say and why?

10. **What data is currently *not* being captured** across the supply chain that could meaningfully improve decision-making, and what are the barriers (technical, contractual, or political) to obtaining it from suppliers and logistics partners?

11. **How is data shared (or siloed) between the company and its suppliers, logistics partners, and retailers**, and would greater end-to-end visibility—such as real-time supplier production data—justify the integration costs and confidentiality risks?

12. **How does the bullwhip effect manifest in this network**, where small fluctuations in retail demand may amplify into large swings in component orders, and what mechanisms dampen it?

## Sustainability & Compliance

13. **How does the company verify labor and environmental compliance deep in its supply chain**—beyond tier-1 suppliers—and what accountability exists when violations are discovered at sub-suppliers it doesn't directly contract with?

14. **What is the true carbon footprint of a single smartphone across this entire network**, and which interventions (transport mode shifts, packaging changes, supplier energy sources) would yield the greatest emissions reduction per dollar invested?

15. **How do differing and evolving regulatory frameworks** (e.g., EU right-to-repair laws, conflict mineral regulations, data privacy rules) create compliance complexity, and is the company reactive or proactive in adapting to them?

## Operations & Continuous Improvement

16. **Where are the longest and most variable lead times in the end-to-end process**, and how much of the total cycle time is value-adding activity versus waiting, queuing, or administrative overhead?

17. **How are quality assurance standards harmonized across suppliers in five different countries** with different metrics and practices, and how often do inconsistent quality definitions lead to disputes or rework?

18. **What does the product recall process actually look like in execution**—how long would it take to identify affected units, notify all markets, and reverse-logistics the products—and has it ever been stress-tested?

## Customer & Market Implications

19. **How do the different fulfillment paths (retailers, e-commerce, direct-to-VIP) differ in cost, speed, and customer satisfaction**, and should direct-to-consumer fulfillment be expanded given changing consumer expectations?

20. **How does the supply chain design itself constrain or enable product strategy**—for example, does the current network limit how quickly new models can launch globally, how much customization can be offered, or which markets can be served profitably?
