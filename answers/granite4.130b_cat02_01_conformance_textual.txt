### Violating Cases and Explanation of Violations

#### **Case 2**
- **Activities Sequence:** Receive  Check inventory  Package items  Authorize payment  Ship order  Close  
- **Violation(s):**  
  - **"Package items" before "Authorize payment":** According to the normative process, *“Authorize payment” should only occur after “Check inventory” and before packaging* since payment authorization is contingent upon confirming that sufficient inventory exists. In Case2, the order proceeds to package items before authorizing payment, which contravenes this rule.

#### **Case 3**
- **Activities Sequence:** Receive  Check inventory  Authorize payment  Cancel order  Ship order  Close  
- **Violation(s):**  
  - **"Ship order" after "Cancel order":** The normative description explicitly states that *“it would be illogical to continue with activities intended for fulfillment (such as packaging or shipping), and thus the next reasonable step is to ‘Close order’”* following a cancellation due to insufficient inventory. In Case3, despite canceling the order after checking inventory and authorizing payment, the process proceeds to ship the order, which directly violates this rule.
  - **Potential Logical Concern:** Although not explicitly prohibited by the description, *authorizing payment before cancellation* might be seen as inefficient or illogical (since funds could have been reserved unnecessarily), but the primary violation is shipping after cancellation.

#### **Case 5**
- **Activities Sequence:** Receive  Check inventory  Authorize payment  Ship order  Close  
- **Violation(s):**  
  - **Missing "Package items":** The normative process mandates that *“items should be prepared for shipment—this is commonly referred to as ‘Package items’”* and that *“‘Ship order’ should only occur after packaging.”* In Case5, the shipping activity occurs immediately after payment authorization without any preceding packaging step, violating this sequencing rule.

### Summary
- **Case 2** violates the normative process by packaging items before authorizing payment.  
- **Case 3** violates by proceeding to ship an order that has been canceled, contravening the expectation that fulfillment activities cease post-cancellation.  
- **Case 5** breaches the rule requiring packaging before shipping.

These deviations highlight instances where the event log does not align with the prescribed normative flow, emphasizing the importance of adhering to the specified sequence of activities for proper order handling.
