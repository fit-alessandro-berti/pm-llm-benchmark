### 2. Attribute Analysis & Root Causes

#### **Primary Driver: Claim Complexity**
The most striking correlation is between **complexity level** and duration:

- **Low complexity** (Cases 2001, 2004): Completed in ~1.5 hours, no additional document requests.
- **Medium complexity** (Case 2002): Took ~26 hours, required one ìRequest Additional Documentsî step.
- **High complexity** (Cases 2003, 2005): Took ~48 and ~77 hours respectively, required **multiple** ìRequest Additional Documentsî steps (2 and 3 times).

**Why this happens:**  
High-complexity claims likely involve more thorough verification, stricter regulatory checks, or higher payout amounts. This leads to:
- **Multiple rounds of information requests**, each adding waiting time (for customer response and adjuster review).
- **Longer gaps** between requests (e.g., Case 2005 had gaps of ~24ñ26 hours between document requests).
- **Extended review before approval**óthe step from the last document request to ìApprove Claimî consistently took 19ñ23 hours in high-complexity cases.

#### **Resource & Region Correlation**
- **Resources:**  
  - *Adjuster_Mike* handled both a fast low-complexity case (2001) and a slow high-complexity case (2003).  
  - *Adjuster_Lisa* handled fast low-complexity (2004) and slow medium/high cases (2002, 2005).  
   **No clear resource-specific performance issue**; slowness aligns with complexity, not the individual.

- **Regions:**  
  - Region A: One fast (2001), one slow (2003).  
  - Region B: One fast (2004), two slow (2002, 2005).  
   **No consistent regional pattern**; both regions have fast and slow cases.

- **Managers:**  
  - *Manager_Ann* approved low/medium cases (2001, 2002, 2004) quickly after document readiness.  
  - *Manager_Bill* approved high-complexity cases (2003, 2005) after the lengthy document-gathering phase.  
   The delay is **not caused by manager processing speed**, but by the upstream wait for documents.

#### **Wait-Time Breakdown**
For high-complexity cases, the major delays occur **between** ìRequest Additional Documentsî events and between the final request and approval:

This suggests that the **waiting time for customers to provide documents** and the **adjusterís subsequent verification** are the bottleneck.

### 3. Proposed Explanations & Mitigation Strategies

#### **Why Attributes Contribute to Delays**
- **Complexity**  More documentation requirements  Multiple request/response cycles  Each cycle adds 12ñ24 hours of idle time.
- **Resource Assignment** is not the root cause, but adjusters handling high-complexity cases naturally have longer task durations due to the inherent process requirements.
- **Region** does not appear to be a causal factor.

#### **Suggestions to Mitigate**
1. **Pre-screening & Clear Communication:**
   - For claims flagged as high-complexity during submission, immediately inform customers of **all** required documents (checklist provided upfront). This can reduce multiple rounds of requests.

2. **Parallel Processing:**
   - While waiting for documents, the adjuster could proceed with non-dependent tasks (e.g., preliminary fraud checks, coordination with external agencies) to compress the overall timeline.

3. **Automated Document Collection:**
   - Implement a digital portal where customers can upload documents in real-time, with automated validation (e.g., completeness checks) to reduce adjuster follow-up time.

4. **SLA for Document Submission:**
   - Set clear expectations (e.g., ìPlease provide all documents within 48 hoursî) and trigger automated reminders to customers.

5. **Specialized Teams:**
   - Assign high-complexity claims to dedicated adjusters or managers who are trained to handle complex cases more efficiently, rather than mixing complexity levels across all staff.

6. **Root-Cause Analysis of Document Requests:**
   - Analyze *which* documents are frequently requested late in the process. If they are always required, they should be asked for during initial submission.

---

### Summary
The **primary root cause** of extended durations is **claim complexity**, which triggers multiple documentation requests and long waiting periods for customer responses and verification. Regions and individual resources do not show a significant performance differential. Mitigation should focus on streamlining the document-collection phase and setting clear, upfront requirements for complex claims.
