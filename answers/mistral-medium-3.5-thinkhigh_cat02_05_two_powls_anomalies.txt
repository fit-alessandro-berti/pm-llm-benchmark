## ** Anomaly Identification**

---

### ** Model 1 Anomalies**
**Structure:** Strict partial order with no control-flow operators (all activities are mandatory).
**Anomalies:**
1. ** Missing Dependency: `Interview  Decide`**
   - The model allows `Screen  Decide` **without requiring interviews**.
   - *Impact:* A hiring decision can be made **solely based on screening**, bypassing interviews entirely. This violates the principle that interviews are critical for evaluating candidates.
   - *Example Trace:* `Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close` (no interviews).

2. ** Missing Dependency: `Interview  Onboard`**
   - No enforced order between `Interview` and `Onboard`.
   - *Impact:* Onboarding could occur **before interviews**, which is illogical (you cannot onboard someone you haven’t interviewed).
   - *Example Trace:* `Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Interview  Payroll  Close`.

3. ** Moderate: Parallel Execution of `Interview` and `Decide`**
   - Since `Interview` and `Decide` both depend only on `Screen`, they can run in parallel or in reverse order (e.g., `Decide` before `Interview`).

**Severity:** *High* (allows illogical sequences but **all activities are mandatory**).

---

### ** Model 2 Anomalies**
**Structure:** Uses `LOOP` and `XOR` operators, introducing **optional paths**.
**Anomalies:**
1. ** Disconnected Activity: `Screen_Candidates`**
   - `Screen` has **no outgoing edges** (only `Post  Screen`).
   - *Impact:* Screening is **optional and irrelevant** to the hiring decision. The process can proceed from `Post  Interview  Decide` **without screening anyone**.
   - *Example Trace:* `Post  Interview  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close` (no screening).

2. ** Missing Dependency: `Screen  Interview`**
   - `Post` directly enables both `Screen` and `Interview`, with **no order between them**.
   - *Impact:* Interviews can occur **before screening**, or the two can run in parallel.
   - *Example Trace:* `Post  Interview  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`.

3. ** Critical: Optional Payroll (`XOR(Payroll, skip)`)**
   - The `xor_payroll` operator allows **skipping `Add_to_Payroll` entirely**.
   - *Impact:* An employee can be onboarded but **never added to payroll**, meaning they **won’t be paid**. This violates the core purpose of the Hire-to-Retire process.
   - *Example Trace:* `Post  Interview  Decide  Onboard  Close` (no payroll).

4. ** Moderate: Unnecessary Loop on Onboarding (`*(Onboard, skip)`)**
   - The loop implies onboarding can be **repeated indefinitely**, which is unrealistic for a single hire (onboarding typically happens once per employee).

**Severity:** *Critical* (allows **skipping mandatory activities** like payroll, and disconnects screening from the process).
