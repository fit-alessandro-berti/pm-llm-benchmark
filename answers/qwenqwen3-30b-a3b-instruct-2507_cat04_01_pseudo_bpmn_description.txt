##  **Overall Business Process Purpose**

This process models the **end-to-end handling of a customer request** for a product or service, where the **type of request (Standard vs. Custom)** determines the path taken through the workflow. The process ensures that:
- Requests are validated appropriately.
- Feasibility is assessed for custom needs.
- Necessary approvals are obtained when required.
- Final deliverables (invoice, confirmation) are generated.
- Rejection or rework occurs when conditions aren't met.

It supports **flexibility, compliance, and customer satisfaction** by adapting the workflow based on request complexity and approval needs.

---

##  **Step-by-Step Breakdown of the Process**

### 1. **Start Event  Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**
- **Purpose**: Initiate the process upon receipt of a customer’s order, service request, or quotation inquiry.
- **Details**: This is the entry point. The request includes information such as product/service type, specifications, delivery expectations, and customer details.

---

### 2. **Gateway (XOR): Check Request Type**
- **Type**: Exclusive Gateway (XOR) – only **one path** can be taken.
- **Meaning**: The process branches based on whether the request is **Standard** or **Custom**.
- **Why it matters**: Standard requests follow a predefined, faster path; custom ones require special analysis due to unique requirements.

---

##  **Path 1: Standard Request (Type = Standard)**

### 3. **Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
- **Purpose**: Verify that the request meets predefined criteria (e.g., valid product code, correct pricing, standard delivery terms).
- **Outputs**: Either clears the request for processing or flags it for rejection (if invalid).
- **Note**: This is a prerequisite before proceeding.

### 4. **Gateway (AND): "Run Parallel Checks"**
- **Type**: Parallel (AND) Gateway – **both checks must complete** before continuing.
- **Meaning**: Two independent validations run simultaneously to improve efficiency and reduce processing time.

#### 4a. **Task C1: "Credit Check"**
- **Purpose**: Assess the customer’s creditworthiness (e.g., credit score, past payment history).
- **Outcome**: Determines if the customer is eligible for credit terms or requires prepayment.

#### 4b. **Task C2: "Inventory Check"**
- **Purpose**: Confirm whether the requested product/service is available in stock or can be fulfilled.
- **Outcome**: If out of stock, triggers a delay or backorder process (implied in logic).

### 5. **All Parallel Checks Completed (Join)**
- **Type**: AND Join (converges the parallel paths).
- **Meaning**: Both credit and inventory checks must be completed before moving forward.
- **Purpose**: Ensures that all critical checks are done before finalizing delivery plans.

### 6. **Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**
- **Purpose**: Based on inventory availability, shipping method, and credit status, compute the earliest possible delivery date.
- **Input**: Results from C1 and C2.
- **Output**: A confirmed delivery estimate for inclusion in the final invoice and customer communication.

---

##  **Path 2: Custom Request (Type = Custom)**

### 3. **Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**
- **Purpose**: Evaluate whether the customer’s unique requirements (e.g., special design, integration, customization) can be technically and financially fulfilled.
- **Output**: A feasibility report including cost, timeline, resource needs.

### 4. **Gateway (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**
- **Type**: Exclusive Gateway (XOR) – only one outcome proceeds.
- **Meaning**: Decision point based on the feasibility analysis.

#### 4a. **If Yes  Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"**
- **Purpose**: Draft a detailed, itemized quote including customization costs, delivery timeline, and terms.
- **Output**: A formal offer to the customer.

#### 4b. **If No  Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"  End Event**
- **Purpose**: Notify the customer that their request cannot be fulfilled.
- **Note**: This is a **final path** – no further processing occurs. The process ends here.

---

##  **Convergence After Standard or Custom Paths**

### 7. **Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**
- **Type**: Exclusive Gateway (XOR) – decision point based on **request type and value**.
- **Logic**:
  - **Standard Request**: May require approval only if the total value exceeds a threshold (e.g., > $5,000).
  - **Custom Request**: Always requires approval due to higher risk, cost, and complexity.
- **Purpose**: Enforce governance and prevent unauthorized commitments.

#### 7a. **If Yes  Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"**
- **Purpose**: Submit the request (quotation, delivery plan, or risk assessment) to a manager for formal sign-off.
- **Output**: Approval or rejection.

#### 7b. **If No  Proceed directly to Task G**

---

### 8. **Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**
- **Type**: Exclusive Gateway (XOR) – decision based on manager's response.
- **Meaning**: This gateway handles **approval outcomes**.

#### 8a. **If Yes  Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**
- **Purpose**: Finalize the financial document with all costs, taxes, delivery date, and terms.
- **Note**: This is the **same Task G** used in both paths.

#### 8b. **If No  Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"  Loop Back**
- **Purpose**: If approval is denied, the team must reassess the request.
- **Loop Logic**:
  - **For Custom Path**: Loop back to **Task E1 ("Prepare Custom Quotation")** – possibly with revised terms (e.g., reduced scope, lower cost).
  - **For Standard Path**: Loop back to **Task D ("Calculate Delivery Date")** – possibly after adjusting pricing or availability.
- **Why loop?**: To allow correction or negotiation without restarting the entire process.
- **Risk**: If re-evaluation fails repeatedly, the process may end in rejection (though not explicitly shown).

>  **Key Insight**: The loop is **not infinite** – it implies a **rework cycle** that continues until approval is obtained or the request is abandoned.

---

### 9. **Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**
- **Purpose**: Deliver the final outcome to the customer:
  - If approved: Confirmation of order, invoice, and delivery date.
  - If rejected: Formal notice with reason.
- **Channel**: Email, portal, or letter.
- **Final Step**: Ensures customer awareness and closure.

---

### 10. **End Event**
- **Purpose**: Mark the completion of the process.
- **Note**: There are **multiple end points**, depending on:
  - Approval granted (success).
  - Rejection (custom request or failed rework).
  - Rejection notice sent (custom request).

| Convergence Point | Purpose | How It Works |

| AND Join after C1 & C2 | Synchronize parallel checks | Both credit and inventory must be confirmed before delivery date calculation. |
| XOR after Standard/Custom | Merge divergent paths | After either path completes, the process continues with **approval check**, regardless of origin. |

##  **Key Differences: Standard vs. Custom Requests**

| Aspect | Standard Request | Custom Request |
|-------|------------------|----------------|
| **Validation** | Standard validation only | Additional feasibility analysis |
| **Parallel Checks** | Yes (credit + inventory) | Not required (may be replaced by technical review) |
| **Quotation** | Not needed (standard pricing) | Mandatory (custom pricing) |
| **Approval** | Conditional (value-based) | Always required |
| **Rework Loop** | Back to delivery date calc | Back to quotation prep |
| **Complexity** | Low to medium | High |
| **Risk** | Low | High (cost, timeline, feasibility) |

##  **Approval & Rework Triggers**

###  **When is Approval Needed?**
- **Custom Requests**: Always (due to complexity and cost).
- **Standard Requests**: Only if the total value exceeds a predefined threshold (e.g., $5,000).

> *This ensures governance without overburdening routine requests.*

###  **When Does Rework Occur?**
- **After Approval Denial** (Task H).
- **Triggered by**: Manager rejects the request.
- **Re-evaluation Purpose**:
  - Adjust scope, cost, or delivery timeline.
  - Resubmit with revised terms.
- **Loop Backs**:
  - Custom:  Task E1 (prepare new quotation).
  - Standard:  Task D (recalculate delivery date after adjustments).

>  **Note**: The loop implies a **bounded retry** (e.g., 1–2 attempts). A third failure would likely result in final rejection (not shown).
