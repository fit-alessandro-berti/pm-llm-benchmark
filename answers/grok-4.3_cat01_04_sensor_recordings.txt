**Labeled Manufacturing Process from Event Log**

I analyzed the numeric patterns across the 15 events from Machine M1. The data shows a clear sequential cycle: low-activity baseline, high-intensity operations, stabilization, and return to baseline. I grouped events into phases based on correlated changes in temperature (heat/friction), vibration (mechanical action), pressure (clamping/force), material flow (input/output), tool position (movement/engagement), and energy (power draw). These align with typical manufacturing steps.

Here are the identified activities with event groupings and rationales:

- **Idle / Initialization** (Events 1-3 and 13-15)  
  All sensors at baseline: ~20-21°C, 1 bar pressure, 0 Hz vibration, 0 flow, 0 mm tool position, ~0.4-0.5 kWh energy.  
  **Rationale**: These represent the machine in a non-operational state with no material engagement or mechanical activity. The identical readings at the start and end indicate the beginning and completion of a process cycle (machine ready or reset).

- **Cutting Metal** (Events 4-6)  
  Temperature rises sharply (50-58°C), vibration increases (30-38 Hz), material flow starts at 5 units/s, tool position advances (5-10 mm), energy climbs (2-3 kWh), pressure begins to rise.  
  **Rationale**: The combination of rising heat and vibration points to friction from a cutting tool engaging material. Material flow indicates feeding stock, while advancing tool position and increasing energy consumption match a machining/cutting operation.

- **Assembling Parts** (Events 7-8)  
  Temperature moderates to 40°C, pressure increases to 2 bar, vibration drops to 10 Hz, material flow reduces to 2 units/s, tool position holds at 15 mm, energy steady at 2 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: Higher pressure suggests clamping or joining force, while reduced vibration and stable moderate readings indicate controlled assembly rather than aggressive machining. Tool holding position supports parts being fitted together.

- **Welding** (Event 9)  
  Temperature spikes to 80°C, vibration peaks at 50 Hz, energy reaches 5 kWh, material flow drops to 0, tool position holds at 15 mm.  
  **Rationale**: Extreme temperature and vibration with maximum energy input and zero flow are classic signatures of welding—intense localized heat and mechanical energy to fuse parts, without ongoing material feed.

- **Quality Inspection** (Event 10)  
  Temperature drops to 30°C, vibration falls to 1 Hz, pressure returns to 1 bar, energy decreases to 1 kWh, tool holds at 15 mm, flow at 0.  
  **Rationale**: Rapid cooling and minimal mechanical activity after the high-energy welding phase suggest a stabilization/checking period. Low readings across sensors align with post-process inspection for defects or quality.

- **Packaging** (Events 11-12)  
  Temperature at 25°C, vibration low at 5 Hz, slight material flow at 1 unit/s, tool position advances to 20 mm, energy at 1.5-1.6 kWh.  
  **Rationale**: Tool advancement combined with minimal flow indicates handling/moving the finished item. The low but non-zero activity level fits packaging or final handling before the machine resets.

**Overall Process Flow**  
The inferred sequence is:  
Idle  Cutting Metal  Assembling Parts  Welding  Quality Inspection  Packaging  Idle (cycle complete).

This grouping is derived purely from sensor pattern transitions (e.g., vibration and temperature spikes mark active machining, pressure shifts mark assembly, energy peaks mark welding). The 5-second intervals suggest rapid operations on a single machine. If more logs were available, frequency analysis or clustering could refine this further, but the current data shows consistent, repeatable phases.
