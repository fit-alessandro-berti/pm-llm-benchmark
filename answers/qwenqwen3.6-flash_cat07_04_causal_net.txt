**Overview**
The image displays a **Process Model** or **Petri Net diagram**, which is used to visualize workflows and business processes. It maps out the sequence of activities, decisions, and flows for handling a request.

**Legend (Bottom Right)**
A key in the bottom right corner explains the symbols used for splitting and joining process paths:
*   **Splits (Top Row):**
    *   **XOR-split:** Exclusive choice (only one path is taken).
    *   **AND-split:** Parallel split (all paths are taken simultaneously).
    *   **OR-split:** Inclusive choice (one or more paths can be taken).
*   **Joins (Bottom Row):**
    *   **XOR-join:** Exclusive join (waits for one incoming path).
    *   **AND-join:** Parallel join (waits for all incoming paths).
    *   **OR-join:** Inclusive join (waits for any incoming path).

The main diagram illustrates a request handling process with the following steps:

1.  **Start (Node `a`):** The process begins at **"register request"**. From here, an **OR-split** (indicated by the four dots) occurs, allowing the flow to branch out to multiple activities.
2.  **Parallel Activities:** The process splits into three main examination/checking paths, plus a reinitiation path:

    *   **Node `c`:** "examine casually"

    *   **Node `f`:** "reinitiate request" (This node is connected directly from `a`, suggesting a user can choose to restart immediately).
3.  **Decision Point (Node `e`):** The results from the examination tasks (**`b`**, **`c`**, and **`d`**) converge at node **`e`**, labeled **"decide"**.

6.  **End (Node `z`):** Both the "pay compensation" and "reject request" paths eventually converge at node **`z`**, labeled **"end"**, terminating the process.
