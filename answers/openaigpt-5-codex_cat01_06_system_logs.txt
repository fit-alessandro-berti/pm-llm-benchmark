### Explanation of Case & Activity Logic

- **Case identification:** Events were grouped by the primary work artifact being handled. Each open/edit/save/close sequence around a specific document, spreadsheet, email thread, or PDF became a separate case (e.g., `Document1.docx`, `Email about Annual Meeting`, `Report_Draft.pdf`, `Budget_2024.xlsx`, and `Quarterly_Report.docx`). Switch events signaled transitions between these artifacts; the “from” side marks the end (or pause) of the previous case and the “to” side marks the start (or resume) of the next.
  
- **Activity naming:** Low-level system actions were mapped to higher-level process activities. For instance, `FOCUS` became “Open Document” or “Resume Editing” depending on context, `TYPING` became “Edit Content” or “Update Data,” `CLICK` events mapped to “Open Email,” “Start Reply,” or “Send Reply,” and `SAVE/CLOSE` retained as “Save Document/Spreadsheet” and “Close …”. This standardization yields meaningful process steps while retaining key details in the `Details` column for deeper analysis.
