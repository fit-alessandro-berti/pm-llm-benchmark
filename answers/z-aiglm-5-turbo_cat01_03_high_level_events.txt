### 1. High-Level Activity: Material Preparation and Setup
* **Included Low-Level Events:**
  * Retrieve raw metal sheet
  * Scan barcode
  * Place metal sheet on table
  * Align metal sheet
  * Preheat metal sheet
* **Rationale:** These events represent the initial phase of the process where the raw material is acquired, verified (via barcode scanning), physically positioned, and conditioned for the main operation. They are grouped together because they share the collective goal of preparing the workspace and the material. Furthermore, there is a logical transition from manual handling (Operator A) to automated setup (Robot Arm #2) and machine conditioning (Heating Unit #1).

### 2. High-Level Activity: Welding and Assembly
* **Included Low-Level Events:**
  * Pick up welding tool
  * Weld corner A
  * Weld corner B
* **Rationale:** This group constitutes the core value-adding transformation step of the manufacturing process. These events are grouped based on resource continuity (all performed by Operator B) and strict sequential dependency (the tool must be picked up before the corners can be welded). This isolates the actual physical modification of the product from the setup and inspection phases.

### 3. High-Level Activity: In-Process Quality Inspection
* **Included Low-Level Events:**
  * Measure weld integrity
* **Rationale:** This is an intermediate quality gate focused specifically on validating the work performed in the preceding "Welding and Assembly" step. It is grouped as a separate high-level step because it involves a distinct change in resources (shifting from an manual operator to an automated Quality Sensor) and serves a specific Quality Assurance (QA) purpose. Keeping this separate from the final visual check highlights that quality is verified at multiple stages of the process.

### 4. High-Level Activity: Surface Treatment and Finishing
* **Included Low-Level Events:**
  * Apply protective coating
  * Dry coating
* **Rationale:** These events are grouped together because they form a complete, closed-loop subprocess for altering the surface of the product. ### 5. High-Level Activity: Final Quality Control and Sign-off
* **Included Low-Level Events:**
