### Analysis of Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log  
**Identified Anomalies and Explanations:**  

#### 1. **Case 1002: Out-of-Sequence Activities and Missing Approvals**  
   - **Anomaly 1:** The purchase order (PO) is issued **before** approving the purchase requisition.  
     - *Standard Practice:* Purchase requisitions must be approved before creating a PO to ensure compliance with budgets and procurement policies.  
     - *Issue in Log:* Case 1002 issues the PO at 08:40 (2024-02-01) without an explicit "Approve Purchase Requisition" activity prior. Approval typically occurs immediately after PO creation.  
   - **Anomaly 2:** Payment is released **without invoice matching** to the PO.  
     - *Standard Practice:* Invoice matching (3-way matching: PO, goods receipt, and invoice) is required before payment to verify accuracy and prevent fraud.  
     - *Issue in Log:* Payment occurs at 07:15 (2024-02-03) in Case 1002, **before** "Vendor Invoice Received" (07:00) and matching ("Match Invoice to PO") activities. This skips a critical control step.  
   - **Anomaly 3:** Reverse sequence of approval: The requisition is approved **after** payment and goods receipt.  
     - *Standard Practice:* Approvals must occur early in the process (before PO creation/payment) to authorize spending and mitigate risk.  
     - *Issue in Log:* "Approve Purchase Requisition" happens at 08:00 (2024-02-04), after payment (07:15) and goods receipt (09:00). This violates the chronological order of approvals.  

   **Why it’s problematic:**  
   - **Financial and compliance risks:** Unapproved POs could lead to unauthorized spending or non-compliance with procurement policies. Skipping invoice matching increases the risk of paying for incorrect/unauthorized goods.  
   - **Audit trail gaps:** Missing approvals and reverse-sequence activities create visibility gaps, making it harder to trace decisions back to authorization controls.  

#### 2. **Case 1003: Payment Before Invoice Receipt**  
   - **Anomaly:** Payment is released **before** receiving the vendor invoice.  
     - *Standard Practice:* Invoices must be received and recorded before matching to a PO/goods receipt. Payment typically occurs *after* matching.  
     - *Issue in Log:* Payment occurs at 11:00 (2024-02-08) in Case 1003, **before** "Vendor Invoice Received" (12:00 on 2024-02-09). This violates the logical sequence of invoice handling.  
   - **Anomaly:** Delayed invoice receipt relative to payment.  
     - *Standard Practice:* Invoices should be processed promptly (ideally same day) to avoid delays in payment processing.  
     - *Issue in Log:* The 4-day gap between PO issuance (10:00 on 2024-02-02) and invoice receipt (12:00 on 2024-02-09) indicates inefficiency.  

   **Why it’s problematic:**  
   - **Operational risks:** Paying without an invoice creates a dependency on memory or documentation gaps, increasing errors (e.g., overpayments).  
   - **Regulatory non-compliance:** Delayed invoice processing violates standard P2P frameworks, which mandate invoice verification before payment.  

#### 3. **Case 1004: Goods Received Before PO Issuance**  
   - **Anomaly:** Goods are received **before** the purchase order is issued.  
     - *Standard Practice:* POs must be issued **before** goods are received to control spending limits and track goods in transit.  
     - *Issue in Log:* Goods are received at 09:00 (2024-02-06) in Case 1004, **before** PO issuance at 09:15 (same day). This reverses the core sequence.  
   - **Anomaly:** Overlapping activities without clear dependencies.  
     - *Standard Practice:* PO issuance should precede goods receipt to enable matching and liability tracking.  
     - *Issue in Log:* "Issue Purchase Order" occurs after "Receive Goods," creating ambiguity about workflow rules.  

   **Why it’s problematic:**  
   - **Risk of unauthorized transactions:** Receiving goods without a PO violates procurement controls, potentially enabling purchases outside budgets or without competition.  
   - **Reconciliation issues:** Mismatched timestamps make it harder to audit why goods were received without a formal PO.
