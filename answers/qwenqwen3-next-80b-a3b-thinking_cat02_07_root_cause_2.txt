#### **3. Proposed Explanations & Mitigation Strategies**

##### **Root Cause Summary**:  
- **High Complexity claims inherently require more documentation**, but the current process **lacks upfront clarity** on required documents. This leads to iterative requests, causing delays due to customer response times.  
- **No evidence of resource inefficiency** (e.g., specific adjusters or regions causing bottlenecks). Delays are driven by **process design flaws**, not human or regional factors.  

##### **Recommendations**:  
1. **Pre-emptive Document Collection for High Complexity Claims**:  
   - **Action**: During the "Evaluate Claim" step, use a **standardized checklist** for High Complexity claims to identify all required documents upfront.  
   - **Why**: Reduces the need for iterative requests. For example, a "High Complexity" claim might require 5 specific documents; the system could prompt the customer to submit all at once.  

2. **Automated Validation During Submission**:  
   - **Action**: Implement a **digital submission portal** that validates document completeness in real-time (e.g., "Missing: Police Report for auto claims").  
   - **Why**: Prevents incomplete submissions from entering the process, reducing back-and-forth.  

3. **Tiered Processing for Complexity Levels**:  
   - **Action**: Assign **dedicated teams** for High Complexity claims (e.g., specialized adjusters trained in complex cases).  
   - **Why**: Reduces miscommunication and ensures adjusters understand upfront what documentation is needed.  

4. **SLA for Customer Responses**:  
   - **Action**: Set a **24-hour SLA** for customers to respond to document requests. If unresolved, escalate to a dedicated support team.  
   - **Why**: Prevents indefinite delays from customer inaction and triggers proactive follow-ups.  

5. **Process Analytics for Continuous Improvement**:  
   - **Action**: Track metrics like **"Requests per Claim"** and **"Time Between Request and Approval"** to identify systemic issues.  
   - **Why**: Data-driven insights can refine workflows (e.g., if 80% of High Complexity claims require 3+ requests, the process needs redesign).
