#### **Anomaly 1: Optional Credit Check via Silent Transition**
```python

```
- **What it does:** The `XOR` operator forces a choice between executing `Check_Credit` (`C`) or taking a silent transition (`skip`). In process modeling, a silent transition represents an invisible step, effectively allowing the credit check to be completely bypassed.
- **Deviation:** In a compliant O2C workflow, credit assessment is a mandatory control gate before order approval. Making it optional violates standard financial governance.

#### **Anomaly 2: Loop on Approval & Invoicing**
```python
loop_approve_invoice = OperatorPOWL(operator=Operator.LOOP, children=[A, I])
```
- **What it does:** The `LOOP` operator allows the sequence `Approve_Order`  `Issue_Invoice` to be executed multiple times before proceeding to payment.
- **Deviation:** Approval and invoicing are typically **idempotent, one-time steps** per order. A loop implies the system or users can repeatedly approve and invoice the same order, which has no logical place in a standard O2C lifecycle.

#### **Anomaly 3: Missing Rejection/Exception Path**
- The model lacks a branch for order rejection or credit failure. Instead of routing failed credit checks to a cancellation or manual review path, the model simply allows a silent skip. This turns an exception-handling scenario into a control bypass.
