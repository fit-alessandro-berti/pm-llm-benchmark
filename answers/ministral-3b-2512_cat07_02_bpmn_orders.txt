This diagram represents a process flow across four main departments: Sales, Production, Procurement, and Warehouse. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

1. **Sales:**
   - **Start:** The process begins on Monday morning.
   - **Check Order for Completeness:** The order is checked for completeness.
   - **Check Customer Database:** The system checks if the customer is already in the database.
     - **If Customer Not in Database:** The system adds the customer to the database.
     - **If Customer is in Database:** Proceeds directly.
   - **Send Order Details:** Order details are sent out.
   - **Draft Invoice:** A draft invoice is created, and the process ends here if the order is successfully sent.

2. **Production:**
   - **Order Details Arrive:** The order details are received.
   - **Plan Production:** Production planning begins.
     - **Material Check:** The materials are checked for faults.
       - **If Material Not Faulty:** Proceeds to manufacturing.
       - **If Material Faulty:** The process loops back to ensure correct materials.
   - **Manufacture Product:** The product is manufactured.
   - **Product Complete:** Once production is complete, the process moves to the warehouse.

3. **Procurement (Material Compensation Subprocess):**
   - **Order Details Arrive:** The order details are again received.
   - **Order Materials:** Materials are ordered.
     - **Loop for Quantity Check (Loop 1):** Ensures the correct quantity is ordered.
       - **Return Material if Necessary:** If the ordered quantity is insufficient or incorrect, materials are returned.
   - **Check Materials:** Materials are checked upon receipt.
     - **If Materials are Valid:** Proceeds to the next step.
     - **If Materials are Invalid:** This leads to order cancellation.
   - **Order Cancellation:** If materials are not suitable, the order is canceled.

4. **Warehouse:**
   - **Product Complete:** Once production is confirmed complete, the product is sent to the warehouse.
   - **Send Product:** The product is sent out from the warehouse.
   - **Product Sent:** The process ends with the product being sent out.

**Key Interactions and Flow:**
- **Sales to Production:** Order details flow from the sales department to production once the order is validated and sent.
- **Production to Warehouse:** Upon successful manufacturing, the product is sent to the warehouse.
- **Procurement Loop:** Ensures that materials are correctly ordered and checked before proceeding.
- **Order Cancellation Path:** If materials are not suitable, the order is canceled, preventing further unnecessary steps.

This diagram effectively outlines the workflow from receiving an order, validating customer data, planning and executing production, ensuring material availability, and finally shipping the product.
