### 3. Root-cause analysis by attribute

#### A. Complexity is the strongest root-cause indicator

Low-complexity cases:

- Case 2001: **1h 30m**
- Case 2004: **1h 25m**
- No additional document requests.

High-complexity cases:

- Case 2003: **48h 20m**, 2 document requests.
- Case 2005: **77h 05m**, 3 document requests.

So the main issue appears to be:

> **High-complexity claims require more documentation, and the documentation is being collected in multiple rounds rather than resolved upfront.**

Possible explanation:

- High-complexity claims may involve more evidence, medical/legal/repair documentation, fraud checks, or manager-level review.
- The insurer may discover missing documents gradually instead of issuing one complete request.
- Customers or third parties may respond slowly or incompletely.

Mitigation:

- Use a complexity-based document checklist at claim submission.
- Add automated validation to detect missing documents early.
- Require adjusters to consolidate all missing-document needs into one comprehensive request.
- Create a specialist queue for high-complexity claims.
- Monitor ōnumber of document request cycles per caseö as a KPI.

---

#### B. Region B may have a secondary issue, especially for complex claims

Region averages:

| Region | Cases | Average Lead Time |
|---|---|---:|
| A | 2001, 2003 | ~**24h 55m** |
| B | 2002, 2004, 2005 | ~**34h 48m** |

Region B has the worst case, **2005**, and also the medium-long case, **2002**. However, Region BÆs low-complexity case, **2004**, completed quickly in **1h 25m**.

So Region B is probably not slow for all claims. The issue appears more specific:

> **Region B + medium/high complexity + document requests leads to long durations.**

Possible explanation:

- Region B customers/providers may submit less complete documentation.
- Region B may have stricter local documentation requirements.
- Region B staff may have heavier workload or slower follow-up routines.
- Adjuster handling in Region B may be more iterative.

Mitigation:

- Audit Region B cases to identify which documents are repeatedly missing.
- Standardize documentation requirements across regions.
- Provide Region B-specific customer guidance or templates.
- Balance workload across regions if Region B adjusters are overloaded.

---

#### C. Resource-related signals: Adjuster_Lisa and Manager_Bill should be reviewed, but not blamed directly

**Adjuster_Lisa** handled cases 2002, 2004, and 2005.

- 2004 was low complexity and completed quickly.
- 2002 and 2005 had document requests and long durations.

This suggests Lisa herself may not be the root cause, but her cases show a pattern:

> **When Adjuster_Lisa handles medium/high Region B claims requiring documents, the process becomes slow.**

This could reflect workload, training, regional rules, or incomplete customer submissions.

**Manager_Bill** approved both high-complexity cases, 2003 and 2005.

- Both cases were long.
- But Manager_Bill appears to be assigned to high-complexity claims, so the delay may be due to case type rather than the manager personally.

Mitigation:

- Review adjuster workload and queues, especially for Region B.
- Train adjusters to issue complete document requests upfront.
- Introduce peer review for high-complexity documentation requests.
- Add SLA targets for time from final document request to approval.
- Ensure high-complexity approvals are not waiting in a manager bottleneck.

---

#### D. Payment delay is a secondary issue in high-complexity cases

Most payment steps are quick:

- Case 2001: Approve  Pay = **15m**
- Case 2002: **45m**
- Case 2004: **30m**

But high-complexity cases are slower:

- Case 2003: Approve  Pay = **17h**
- Case 2005: Approve  Pay = **4h**

Case 2003 was approved at **16:00** and paid the next morning at **09:00**, suggesting a possible payment cut-off, batching issue, or extra payment control for high-complexity claims.

Mitigation:

- Add same-day payment SLA after approval.
- Automate payment triggering after approval.
- Prioritize aged claims once approved.
- Review whether high-complexity claims require unnecessary extra finance checks.

---

### Summary of likely root causes

1. **Primary root cause:** high-complexity claims generate repeated additional-document requests.  
2. **Secondary root cause:** Region B medium/high claims appear especially vulnerable to long documentation cycles.  
3. **Possible resource/process issue:** Adjuster_Lisa/Region B cases with document requests should be reviewed for workload, training, and request completeness.  
4. **Additional bottleneck:** high-complexity claims may experience approval/payment handoff delays, especially when approvals occur late in the day.

### Recommended actions

- Implement complexity-based document checklists at submission.
- Reduce repeated document requests by requiring one consolidated request.
- Create specialist handling for high-complexity claims.
- Monitor document-request count, request-to-approval time, and lead time by region/resource.
- Investigate Region B documentation quality and adjuster workload.
- Add approval and payment SLAs for aged or high-complexity claims.
