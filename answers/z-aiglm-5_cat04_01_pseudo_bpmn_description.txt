### 4. Conditions for Approvals and Rework

**When is Approval Triggered?**
The process does not explicitly state the rules (e.g., "Value > $10,000"), but the logic dictates that approval is triggered by the **"Is Approval Needed?"** gateway. Common business rules for this include:
*   Total monetary value of the order.
*   Customer credit score threshold.
*   Complexity of the custom request.
*   Deviation from standard discount structures.

**When is Rework Triggered?**
Rework (the loop) is triggered exclusively when:
1.  Approval was needed (Gateway 4 = Yes).
2.  The Manager Approval was **denied** (Gateway 5 = No).

**How the Rework Loop Functions:**
Instead of a generic "fix it" task, the process intelligently loops back based on the request type:
*   **Standard Rework:** Since standard items usually have fixed prices, a rejection typically relates to logistics or inventory allocation. Therefore, it loops back to **Task D** to adjust the delivery date or allocation.
*   **Custom Rework:** Since custom items are defined by their unique terms, a rejection typically relates to profitability or feasibility. Therefore, it loops back to **Task E1** to adjust the price or scope of the quotation.
