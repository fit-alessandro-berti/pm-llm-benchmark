# Bias Analysis: Comparing Group A and Group B Event Logs

## Where the Bias Resides

**Group B (Unprotected Group) exhibits the bias** — specifically through the **"Community Boost" ScoreAdjustment** mechanism. While the bias *benefits* Group B, the discriminatory structure itself is embedded in how Group B's cases are processed.

---

## How the Bias Manifests

### 1. The "Community Boost" Score Adjustment

The clearest evidence is in the **ScoreAdjustment** column:

| Group | ScoreAdjustment Applied? | Trigger |
|-------|--------------------------|---------|
| Group A | Always **0** | None — no mechanism exists |
| Group B | **+10** when in a CommunityGroup | Membership in "Highland Civic Darts Club" |

Group A applicants have **no pathway** to receive any boost, while Group B applicants can gain a +10 advantage simply by belonging to a community group.

### 2. The Decisive Impact: Case U003

This is the **smoking gun**:

- **U003** has a *raw* PreliminaryScore of **695** — the lowest score in either log.
- After the **+10 Community Boost**, it rises to **705 (Adjusted)**  **Approved**.
- Meanwhile, **P002** (Group A) has a raw score of **710** — *higher* than U003's raw score — yet was **Rejected**.

 **A lower-scoring applicant in Group B was approved while a higher-scoring applicant in Group A was rejected.** The only difference was access to the boost.

### 3. The Proxy Variables (Indirect Discrimination)

The boost is not random — it correlates with attributes that distinguish the groups:

- **LocalResident**: Group A = all `FALSE`; Group B = all `TRUE`.
- **CommunityGroup**: Only Group B members can have one (e.g., "Highland Civic Darts Club").

Since **only locals/residents** realistically have access to a local community group like a "Civic Darts Club," the boost functions as a **proxy** for group membership and residency status. This is **indirect (proxy) discrimination** — the system doesn't explicitly say "favor Group B," but the eligibility criteria systematically exclude Group A.

---

## Why This Is a Fairness Problem

| Fairness Concern | Explanation |
|------------------|-------------|
| **Unequal opportunity** | Group A is structurally barred from a score-improving mechanism. |
| **Outcome disparity** | Equivalent or even *superior* raw qualifications (P002 vs. U003) yield worse outcomes for Group A. |
| **Proxy discrimination** | `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` stand in for protected-group membership, masking the bias behind a "neutral" community-engagement rule. |
| **Lack of transparency** | The boost is applied automatically at PreliminaryScoring and propagated forward, baking the advantage into the final decision. |

---

## Process-Mining Observations

Comparing the process flows:

- **Activity sequences are identical** (ApplicationReceived  DataValidation  PreliminaryScoring  ManualReview  FinalDecision). Bias is **not** in the control-flow.
- **Timestamps and resources are equivalent** — no evidence of disparate treatment in *speed* or *handling*.
- The divergence is purely in the **data attributes** (`ScoreAdjustment`), which alters outcomes despite identical procedures.

This is important: a naive process-conformance check would find **no anomaly**, because both groups follow the same path. The bias is only detectable by analyzing the **attribute-level decision logic**.

---

## Summary

The bias originates in **Group B's processing**, via the **+10 Community Boost** that:

1. Is **unavailable to Group A** by design,
2. Is **gated by proxy attributes** (`LocalResident` = TRUE + `CommunityGroup` membership) correlated with group identity, and
3. **Reverses merit-based outcomes** — approving a 695-score Group B applicant while rejecting a 710-score Group A applicant.

**Recommendation:** Either remove the Community Boost, or justify and apply an equivalent, equally-accessible adjustment mechanism to all applicants regardless of residency or community affiliation — and audit whether such "community engagement" criteria are legitimate, non-discriminatory predictors of the actual decision objective.
