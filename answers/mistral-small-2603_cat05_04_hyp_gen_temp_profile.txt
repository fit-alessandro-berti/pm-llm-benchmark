### **Anomalies in the Temporal Profile Model**

1. **`('R', 'P')` (Receive  Approve) – Suspiciously Low Standard Deviation**
   - **Average Time:** ~25 hours
   - **Standard Deviation:** 1 hour (unusually low)
   - **Possible Issue:** The process may be artificially constrained, possibly due to automated approvals or rigid scheduling that does not account for variability in claim complexity.

2. **`('P', 'N')` (Approve  Notify) – Extremely Long & Highly Variable Delay**
   - **Average Time:** 7 days
   - **Standard Deviation:** 2 days
   - **Possible Issue:** Delays may indicate backlogs in notification processing, manual intervention, or inconsistent workflows where some claims are notified immediately while others face significant delays.

3. **`('A', 'C')` (Assign  Close) – Premature Closure Without Intermediate Steps**
   - **Average Time:** 2 hours
   - **Possible Issue:** Claims may be closed without proper evaluation or approval, suggesting either data errors or deliberate bypassing of steps.

4. **`('E', 'N')` (Evaluate  Notify) – Unrealistically Fast Transition**
   - **Average Time:** 5 minutes
   - **Standard Deviation:** 1 minute
   - **Possible Issue:** The evaluation and notification steps may be automated or incorrectly logged, skipping necessary manual review.

---

### **Hypotheses on Anomaly Causes**

1. **Automated Approvals (`R`  `P`)**
   - Some claims may be auto-approved due to low claim amounts or predefined rules, leading to rigid timing.

2. **Notification Backlog (`P`  `N`)**
   - A manual or batch-based notification system could cause inconsistent delays, especially if notifications are processed in bulk.

3. **Premature Closures (`A`  `C`)**
   - Adjusters may close claims early due to errors, system overrides, or lack of proper documentation.

4. **Automated Evaluation & Notification (`E`  `N`)**
   - If evaluation results are auto-fed into notification systems, the transition may appear instantaneous, masking manual review steps.

### **Verification Queries**

#### **1. Identify Claims with Unusual `R`  `P` Timing (Low Variability)**
```sql
SELECT
    c.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    c.claim_amount,
    ce1.timestamp AS receive_time,
    ce2.timestamp AS approve_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 3600 AS hours_to_approve
FROM
    claims c
JOIN
    claim_events ce1 ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce1.activity = 'R'
JOIN
    claim_events ce2 ON c.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'P'
WHERE
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 3600 < 20
    OR EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 3600 > 30
ORDER BY
    hours_to_approve;
```
**Purpose:** Find claims where approval time deviates from the expected 25-hour average, checking for auto-approval patterns.

---

#### **2. Check for Premature Closures (`A`  `C` Without Intermediate Steps)**
```sql
SELECT
    c.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    ce1.timestamp AS assign_time,
    ce2.timestamp AS close_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 3600 AS hours_to_close,
    STRING_AGG(ce1.activity || '  ' || ce2.activity, ', ' ORDER BY ce1.timestamp) AS activity_sequence
FROM
    claims c
JOIN
    claim_events ce1 ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce1.activity = 'A'
JOIN
    claim_events ce2 ON c.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'C'
GROUP BY
    c.claim_id, ce1.timestamp, ce2.timestamp
HAVING
    NOT EXISTS (
        SELECT 1
        FROM claim_events ce3
        WHERE ce3.claim_id = c.claim_id
        AND ce3.activity IN ('E', 'P')
        AND ce3.timestamp > ce1.timestamp
        AND ce3.timestamp < ce2.timestamp
    )
ORDER BY
    hours_to_close;
```
**Purpose:** Identify claims closed immediately after assignment, bypassing evaluation/approval.

#### **3. Analyze `P`  `N` Delays (Approve  Notify)**
```sql
SELECT
    c.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    ce1.timestamp AS approve_time,
    ce2.timestamp AS notify_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 86400 AS days_to_notify,
    a.name AS adjuster_name,
    a.region
FROM
    claims c
JOIN
    claim_events ce1 ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce1.activity = 'P'
JOIN
    claim_events ce2 ON c.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'N'
LEFT JOIN
    adjusters a ON ce1.resource = a.adjuster_id
WHERE
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 86400 > 10
    OR EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 86400 < 4
ORDER BY
    days_to_notify DESC;
```
**Purpose:** Find claims where notification is either excessively delayed or unusually fast, correlating with adjusters or regions.

---

#### **4. Check for Instant `E`  `N` Transitions (Evaluation  Notify)**
```sql
SELECT
    c.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    ce1.timestamp AS evaluate_time,
    ce2.timestamp AS notify_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 60 AS minutes_to_notify,
    ce1.resource AS evaluator,
    ce2.resource AS notifier
FROM
    claims c
JOIN
    claim_events ce1 ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce1.activity = 'E'
JOIN
    claim_events ce2 ON c.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'N'
WHERE
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp)) / 60 < 10
ORDER BY
    minutes_to_notify;
```
**Purpose:** Identify claims where notification follows evaluation almost instantly, suggesting automation or skipped steps.
