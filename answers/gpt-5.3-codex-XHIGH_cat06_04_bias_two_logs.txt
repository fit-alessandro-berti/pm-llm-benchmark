The **bias is in the decision policy reflected by Group B’s log** (and, by comparison, against Group A).

### Why this is biased
- In **Group B (Unprotected)**, applicants with:
  - `LocalResident = TRUE` and
  - `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`
  
  receive a **+10 “Community Boost”** in `ScoreAdjustment`.
- In **Group A (Protected)**, `ScoreAdjustment` is always `0` (or N/A), and `CommunityGroup` is always `None`, so they never receive that uplift.

### How it manifests in outcomes
- The process steps are similar, but scoring treatment is not.
- A key example:
  - **P002 (Group A):** score 710, no boost  **Rejected**
  - **U003 (Group B):** score 695, +10 boost (to 705)  **Approved**
  
This suggests Group B can be approved despite lower underlying risk score, due to group-linked boosts.

### Interpretation
This is a form of **disparate treatment / proxy bias**:
- `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` function as advantage-conferring attributes tied to the unprotected group.
- The boost lowers the effective approval bar for Group B, creating a systematic disadvantage for Group A over time, especially for borderline cases.

So, even if small-sample approval counts look similar, the **mechanism itself is biased in favor of Group B and against Group A**.
