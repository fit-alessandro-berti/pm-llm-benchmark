### Overview
This image is a process model diagram known as a **C-net (Causal Net)**. It represents the workflow for handling a request, starting from registration and ending with a final decision. The diagram uses boxes to represent activities and a specific notation of dots and arcs to show the control flow (how the process moves from one step to the next).

### Legend and Notation
In the bottom-right corner, a legend explains the symbols used for process "splits" and "joins":
*   **XOR (Exclusive OR):** Only one of the available paths is taken (split) or only one incoming path triggers the next step (join). These are shown as separate dots.
*   **AND:** All paths are taken simultaneously (split) or all incoming paths must complete before moving forward (join). These are shown as multiple paths coming from a single dot.
*   **OR:** One or more paths can be taken (split) or any combination of incoming paths can trigger the next step (join). These are indicated by arcs connecting the dots.

The process can move to an examination task—either **Examine Thoroughly (b)** or **Examine Casually (c)**—and/or it can move to **Check Ticket (d)**. 2.  **The Decision Point - Decide (e):** Before a decision is made, there is an **OR-join**. This means it waits for the completion of whichever tasks were initiated in the previous step (b, c, or d).

3.  **Outcomes of the Decision:** From the 'Decide' (e) activity, there are two main outcomes (an XOR-split):
    *   **Finalization:** The process moves toward completion. This involves another XOR-split where the request is either handled by **Pay Compensation (g)** or **Reject Request (h)**. Both of these lead to the **End (z)**.

### Summary
The diagram illustrates a flexible process where a request is registered, undergoes one of two types of examination (casual or thorough) and/or a ticket check, is decided upon, and then is either finalized (paid/rejected) or sent back through a loop for re-evaluation.
