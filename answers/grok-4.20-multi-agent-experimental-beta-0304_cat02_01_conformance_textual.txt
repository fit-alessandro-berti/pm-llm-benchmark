**Violating Cases: 2, 3, and 5.** Cases 1 and 4 fully conform to the normative process.

**Case 2 violations**  
Sequence: Receive  Check inventory  Package items  Authorize payment  Ship  Close.  
This violates the explicit ordering for the “sufficient inventory” path. The normative description states that after a successful inventory check, the process “should include the authorization of payment … Once the payment is authorized, the items should be prepared …” and that “Authorize payment should only occur after Check inventory and before packaging.” Packaging before authorization reverses this required sequence and performs preparation before payment validity is confirmed, which the model treats as illogical.

**Case 3 violations**  
Sequence: Receive  Check  Authorize payment  Cancel order  Ship order  Close.  
Multiple clear breaches:  
- Authorize payment followed by Cancel order implies the process continued to payment authorization even though inventory was apparently insufficient (the description requires “after checking the inventory, the process should directly proceed to Cancel order” when stock is lacking).  
- Ship order occurs after Cancel order. The text explicitly prohibits this: “Ship order should never occur after a cancellation” and “it would be illogical to continue with activities intended for fulfillment (such as … shipping)” once cancellation has happened.  
- The presence of both authorization and cancellation in the same trace is not contemplated by the normative model; the two branches are mutually exclusive after the inventory check.

**Case 5 violations**  
Sequence: Receive  Check  Authorize payment  Ship order  Close (Package items is omitted).  
This trace follows the sufficient-inventory branch but skips the mandatory “Package items” activity. The description requires: “Once the payment is authorized, the items should be prepared for shipment … Only after items have been properly packaged is it appropriate to proceed with Ship order.” Shipping without the intervening packaging step violates the stated prerequisite and the constraint that packaging must occur after authorization and before shipping.

**Non-violations (for completeness)**  
- Case 1 follows the full sufficient-inventory sequence exactly.  
- Case 4 follows the insufficient-inventory sequence exactly (Check  Cancel  Close).  

The textual description leaves minor room for interpretation around “extraordinary circumstances,” but none of the violating traces can plausibly be reconciled with the stated ordering constraints, branch logic, or explicit prohibitions (e.g., shipping after cancellation, packaging before authorization, or skipping packaging before shipping). These deviations are therefore identified as non-conformant.
