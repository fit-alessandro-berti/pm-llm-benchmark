# Clarifying Questions for the Property Onboarding Process

## 1. Process Initiation & Documentation Review
1. How does a landlord typically first make contact—is there a standard intake form or channel, or does it vary by region?
2. What specific documents are considered mandatory versus optional before onboarding can proceed?
3. Who defines what counts as an "outdated" document, and how is that threshold determined (e.g., inspection reports older than X months)?
4. When documents are missing or outdated, how do you follow up with the landlord, and is there a deadline after which the request is abandoned or escalated?
5. How often do documentation issues delay or halt the process entirely, and what happens to those cases?
6. Is the property coordinator a single role per region, or are there multiple coordinators—and if so, how is incoming work distributed among them?

## 2. Property Manager Assignment
7. Who actually makes the assignment decision—is it the coordinator, a supervisor, or an automated system?
8. How are the assignment factors (workload, geography, experience, landlord requirements) weighted against each other? Is there a formal scoring method or is it judgment-based?
9. How do you measure a manager's "current workload"—number of properties, number of active tenants, something else?
10. What happens if no suitable property manager is available—does the property wait in a queue, or is someone assigned outside their specialization?
11. Can a landlord request a specific manager or reject an assigned one? How are reassignments handled?

## 3. Introductory Meeting & Expectation Setting
12. Is the introductory call/meeting documented in a standard way (e.g., a checklist or agreement form), or is it informal?
13. What happens if the landlord and manager disagree on key policies during this meeting—can the onboarding be paused or cancelled at this stage?
14. Are special conditions (pet policies, custom maintenance terms) recorded somewhere centrally so other teams can see them later?

## 4. Photography, Inspection & Repairs
15. Are the photographer and inspector internal staff or external vendors, and how are they scheduled and selected?
16. Do the photo shoot and inspection happen on the same visit, or are they sequenced—and does one depend on the other?
17. What are the most common inspection failures, and how long do remediation cycles typically take?
18. Who pays for repairs—the landlord or the company—and who selects and supervises the contractors?
19. Is there a limit to how many inspection-repair cycles a property can go through before it's rejected from the portfolio?
20. Does a failed inspection require a full re-inspection, or only verification of the specific issues found?

## 5. Marketing & Activation
21. What triggers the handoff to the digital marketing team—is it automatic upon passing inspection, or does the property manager initiate it?
22. Who has final approval over the listing price—the marketing team, the property manager, the landlord, or the financial analyst?
23. What does "activation" actually involve—is it a single system action or multiple steps across different platforms?
24. How long does the marketing preparation typically take, and what are the common bottlenecks?
25. Can a landlord veto or request changes to the listing content or price after it's published?

## 6. Tenant Screening & Lease Finalization
26. Who decides whether the property manager or a leasing agent handles screening for a given property?
27. What are the formal acceptance criteria for tenant applications (credit score thresholds, income ratios), and how much discretion does the screener have?
28. When multiple qualified applicants exist, what's the prioritization logic—first come first served, longest lease commitment, highest offer, or manager judgment?
29. Does the landlord have a say in the final tenant selection, or is that fully delegated to the company?
30. Under what circumstances are promotional discounts or negotiated lease terms offered, and who must approve them?
31. What happens if a chosen tenant withdraws after lease signing but before move-in?

## 7. Quality Checks & Audits
32. Are the compliance, financial, and IT checks performed on every property, or only on a sample? What triggers a full audit?
33. Do these checks happen in parallel or in a fixed sequence, and do they block the move-in or run after the fact?
34. What happens when an audit finds a problem—for example, a lease clause that violates fair housing rules after the tenant has signed?
35. Who has the authority to override a compliance or pricing flag, and how is that documented?

## 8. Exceptions & Special Cases
36. How are properties requiring special insurance identified, and at what point in the process does that determination happen?
37. How do you handle landlords who insist on custom lease clauses—who reviews and approves these, and how often are they rejected?
38. Are there property types or landlord situations where the standard process is skipped or significantly modified?
39. What's the most common reason an onboarding effort fails or gets abandoned mid-process?
40. Can a landlord withdraw partway through, and if so, what cleanup or reversal steps are needed?

## 9. Timing, Sequencing & Handoffs
41. What's the typical end-to-end duration from initial landlord contact to first tenant move-in, and where is the most variability?
42. Which steps can run in parallel, and which are strictly sequential?
43. How are handoffs between teams communicated—does each step have a formal trigger, or does work sometimes stall waiting for someone to notice it's their turn?
44. Are there SLAs or target turnaround times for any of the steps, and how are delays tracked or escalated?

## 10. Systems, Tracking & Communication
45. You mentioned email threads, CRMs, and project management tools—is there one system of record for the overall process status, or is status spread across systems?
46. How do you currently know where a given property is in the onboarding process at any moment?
47. Are decisions (e.g., manager assignment rationale, tenant selection reasoning) recorded anywhere, or do they live mainly in people's heads and emails?
48. What metrics, if any, do you currently use to evaluate how well the onboarding process is performing?
49. If you could fix one pain point in this process tomorrow, what would it be—and what have you already tried that didn't work?
