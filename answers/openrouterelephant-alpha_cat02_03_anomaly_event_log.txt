After analyzing the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) event log, **two cases exhibit anomalous behavior** that deviates from standard P2P process best practices. Below is a detailed breakdown of each anomaly, why it is problematic, and the potential risks it introduces.

---

###  **Anomaly 1: Case 1002 ñ Payment Released Before Requisition Approval and Goods Received**

#### **Event Sequence (Problematic Order):**
1. **2024-02-01 08:30** ñ Create Purchase Requisition  
2. **2024-02-01 08:40** ñ **Issue Purchase Order**  
3. **2024-02-03 07:00** ñ **Vendor Invoice Received**  
4. **2024-02-03 07:15** ñ **Release Payment**   
5. **2024-02-03 08:00** ñ Approve Purchase Requisition  
6. **2024-02-04 09:00** ñ Receive Goods  
7. **2024-02-05 08:00** ñ Match Invoice to PO  
8. **2024-02-06 16:00** ñ Close Case  

#### **Why This Is Anomalous:**
- **Payment released before requisition approval**: The purchase requisition was not approved until **after** the payment was made (approved at 08:00 on Feb 3, but payment released at 07:15 same day).
- **Payment released before goods were received**: Goods were not received until **Feb 4**, yet payment was made on **Feb 3**.
- **Invoice matched to PO after payment**: The invoice-to-PO matching (a critical control) occurred **after** payment was released.

#### **Problems & Risks:**
- **Lack of Authorization**: Paying without approved requisition bypasses budget control and approval workflows, increasing risk of unauthorized spending.
- **Payment for Undelivered Goods**: Paying before receiving goods exposes the company to fraud or errors (e.g., vendor could deliver nothing or deliver incorrect items).
- **Weak Internal Controls**: The sequence violates the core P2P principle: **"No PO, No Goods, No Invoice = No Payment."** Here, payment was made with an unapproved requisition, unverified delivery, and unmatched invoice.
- **Potential for Overpayment or Fraud**: This sequence creates opportunities for duplicate payments, inflated invoices, or collusion with vendors.

>  **Best Practice Violated**: Payment should only occur **after** goods are received, invoice is matched to PO and requisition, and all approvals are in place.

###  **Anomaly 2: Case 1004 ñ Goods Received Before Purchase Order Issued**

#### **Event Sequence (Problematic Order):**
1. **2024-02-05 10:00** ñ Create Purchase Requisition  
2. **2024-02-05 10:15** ñ Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. **2024-02-06 09:00** ñ **Receive Goods**  
4. **2024-02-06 09:15** ñ **Issue Purchase Order**  
5. **2024-02-07 08:00** ñ Vendor Invoice Received  
6. **2024-02-07 08:30** ñ Match Invoice to PO  
7. **2024-02-07 09:00** ñ Release Payment  
8. **2024-02-08 11:00** ñ Close Case  

#### **Why This Is Anomalous:**
- **Goods received before purchase order was issued**: The PO was issued **after** goods were already received (Feb 6 09:00 vs. Feb 6 09:00 ñ same day, but timestamp shows receiving at 09:00 and PO at 09:15).
- This implies goods were accepted **without a valid PO**, violating procurement controls.

#### **Problems & Risks:**
- **Uncontrolled Spending**: Receiving goods without a PO removes price and quantity verification, leading to potential overcharging or unapproved purchases.
- **Inventory and Accounting Issues**: Goods in the system without a corresponding PO complicate inventory reconciliation and cost allocation.
- **Compliance & Audit Risks**: External auditors typically flag missing POs for received goods as a material weakness.
- **Vendor Disputes**: If the invoice doesnít match expectations, the lack of a prior PO weakens the companyís negotiating position.

>  **Best Practice Violated**: Purchase orders should be issued **before** goods are received to ensure proper authorization, pricing, and contractual terms.

- **Case 1001**: Follows ideal sequence ó Requisition  Approval  PO  Receive  Invoice  Match  Payment  Close.
- **Case 1003**: Also follows correct order, albeit with longer lead times (e.g., 6 days between PO and payment), which may be acceptable depending on contract terms.

###  **Summary of Anomalies**

| Case ID | Anomaly | Key Violation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **1002** | Payment issued before requisition approval and goods receipt | Bypassed authorization and receipt controls |
| **1004** | Goods received before PO issued | Lack of purchase order at time of receipt |

---

###  **Recommended Actions:**
1. **Implement System Controls**: Prevent payment release without approved requisition, PO, and matched invoice.
2. **Block Goods Receipt Without PO**: Enforce PO validation at receiving (unless using non-PO mechanisms with proper approval).
3. **Audit These Cases**: Investigate whether fraud, errors, or process failures occurred.
4. **User Training & Process Reinforcement**: Ensure procurement and finance teams understand mandatory sequencing.

These anomalies represent **high-risk deviations** that could lead to financial loss, compliance issues, and weakened internal controls.
