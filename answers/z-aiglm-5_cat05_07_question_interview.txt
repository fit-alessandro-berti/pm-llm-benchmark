Based on the detailed process description provided, here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify the workflow, decision logic, and exception handling:

**1. On Roles and Handoffs:**
*   "You mentioned the transition from the Property Coordinator to the dedicated Property Manager. Could you walk me through how that handoff is managed practically? specifically, is there a formal sign-off procedure where the Coordinator verifies that all documentation is 100% complete before the Manager takes over, or does the Manager inherit any outstanding documentation risks?"
*   "Regarding the assignment of the Property Manager, you listed several factors like workload and specialization. Is this assignment decision automated by your system based on data, or does a supervisor manually review these factors to make the selection?"

**2. On Decision Criteria and Logic:**
*   "When the Property Coordinator reviews the initial documentation, what constitutes a 'deal-breaker'? For example, if a property has a minor compliance issue that can be resolved quickly, is the process paused, or does it move forward conditionally?"
*   "You mentioned that the digital marketing team updates internal pricing models. How much autonomy does the Property Manager have to negotiate the final rental price with the landlord if the market data suggests a price lower than the landlord's expectations?"
*   "During tenant screening, how do you balance objective data (like credit scores) against subjective factors? Is there a standardized scoring rubric that dictates who is 'suitable,' or does the Property Manager have discretion to make a judgment call based on the interview?"

**3. On Exception Handling and Risk:**
*   "If the inspector finds significant safety issues that require weeks of repairs, what happens to the marketing timeline? Is the property immediately deactivated in the system, or do you continue marketing it with a disclaimer about the upcoming repairs?"
*   "You noted that some landlords insist on custom lease clauses. What is the escalation process if a landlord requests a clause that your Compliance Officer later flags as a legal risk or a violation of fair housing regulations? Who has the final authority to approve or deny that specific clause?"
*   "In the scenario where a property sits on the market for an extended period without suitable tenant applications, at what point does the process loop back to re-evaluate the pricing or marketing strategy, and who initiates that review?"

**4. On Timing and Sequencing:**
*   "Regarding the Quality Checks and Internal Audits at the end of the process, does this happen sequentially after the lease is signed (potentially delaying the move-in), or are these audits conducted in parallel with the lease finalization to ensure the tenant can move in immediately upon signing?"
*   "How long does the typical onboarding process take from the initial landlord contact to the property being 'activated' in the portal? Where do the most common bottlenecks occur that cause delays in this timeline?"

**5. On Financial and Legal Details:**
*   "Who bears the financial responsibility for the initial costs of the professional photographer and certified inspector if the landlord decides to pull out of the agreement before the property is listed?"
*   "How are security deposits handled during the finalization phase? Is the money transferred to the company's holding account immediately, and what are the triggers that officially bind the tenant to the lease?"
