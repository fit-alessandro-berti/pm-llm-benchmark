A concrete invalid-but-accepted trace is:

`<Receive_Order, Check_Inventory, Ship_Order, Package_Items, Authorize_Payment, Close_Order>`

Why the model accepts it:
- After `Receive_Order` and `Check_Inventory`, the tree can choose the **successful_subtree**.
- That subtree is modeled as `PARALLEL(Authorize_Payment, Package_Items, Ship_Order)`.
- In a process tree, **PARALLEL** allows those activities to occur in **any order**.

Why this is wrong from a domain perspective:
- You should **not ship an order before it has been packaged**.
- You also typically should **not ship before payment is authorized**.
- The intended business logic is closer to:

`Receive_Order -> Check_Inventory -> Authorize_Payment -> Package_Items -> Ship_Order -> Close_Order`

So the model is under-constrained: it only requires that those three activities happen, but it does **not** enforce their necessary order.
