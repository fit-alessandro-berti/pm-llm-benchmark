### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization  

#### **Calculating Waiting Times**  
- **Definition of Waiting Time**:  
  The waiting time between two consecutive activities \( A \) and \( B \) is the time between the **completion of \( A \)** and the **start of \( B \)** for the same patient. For example, the time between *Registration completion* and *Nurse Assessment start*.  
- **Steps**:  
  - **Event Log Parsing**: Identify sequential activity pairs for each patient by linking activities in the order they occur.  
  - **Gap Calculation**: For each pair, compute \( \text{Waiting Time} = \text{Start Time of Next Activity} - \text{End Time of Previous Activity} \). Negative values indicate overlap (e.g., a nurse starting an assessment before registration is complete), which may indicate process anomalies.  

#### **Key Metrics**  
- **Quantitative Metrics**:  
  - **Average/Median Waiting Time**: Identifies typical delays.  
  - **Maximum/90th Percentile Waiting Time**: Highlights extreme delays that affect patient experience.  
  - **Queue Frequency**: Number of cases experiencing waits exceeding a threshold (e.g., >15 minutes).  
  - **Cumulative Impact**: Total time lost due to waiting across all patients (e.g., total "wasted" hours in bottlenecks).  
- **Qualitative Metrics**:  
  - **Patient Type and Urgency**: Filter metrics by patient type (new vs. follow-up) and urgency level to identify disparities.  
  - **Resource-Specific Queues**: Link delays to specific resources (e.g., "waiting for Dr. Smith" due to overbooking).  

#### **Prioritizing Critical Queues**  
- **Criteria**:  
  1. **Impact on Total Visit Duration**: Queues contributing most to overall visit length (e.g., a 30-minute delay before a critical diagnostic test could disproportionately lengthen visits).  
  2. **Frequency of Disruptions**: Queues with high frequency (e.g., 80% of patients waiting >10 minutes between registration and nurse assessment).  
  3. **Urgency and Patient Type**: Queues affecting urgent patients or vulnerable groups (e.g., elderly patients with mobility issues) should be prioritized.  
  4. **Resource Utilization**: Queues caused by under-resourced activities (e.g., a single room shared by multiple diagnostic tests).
