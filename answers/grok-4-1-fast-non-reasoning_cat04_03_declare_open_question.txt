### How Constraints Shape the Process

DECLARE constraints enforce **existence** (must happen), **absence** (must not), sequencing, and exclusivity, creating a rigid flowchart. Here's how each category structures real-life scenarios:

- **Existence & Init (Must-Happen Basics)**: A and C *must* occur; A is the mandatory starter. *Practical Impact*: No process skips application intake or doc collection—prevents ghost loans. Ensures every loan traces back to a customer request.

- **Absence**: Z is forbidden. *Practical Impact*: Blocks shortcuts like approving high-risk loans without checks, aligning with anti-fraud policies.

- **Exactly_One**: B happens precisely once. *Practical Impact*: Avoids redundant credit pulls (costly, impacts customer score) while guaranteeing one gatekeeping step.

- **Response, Precedence, Succession (Strict Sequencing)**:
  | Constraint | Source  Target | Practical Flow |
  |------------|-----------------|---------------|
  | response | A  B | App triggers credit check immediately. |
  | succession | B  C | Credit issues prompt doc requests. |
  | precedence | E  F | QA must precede contract auth. |
  | coexistence | C  F | Docs enable term authorization. |
  | responded_existence | D  E | Offer assembly requires QA review. |

  *Impact*: Creates a linear "happy path" (ABCEFDG), with branches for alternatives (e.g., altresponse: GH for funding notifications).

- **Alternatives & Chains (Branches and Loops)**:
  | Constraint | Source  Target | Scenario |
  |------------|-----------------|----------|
  | altresponse | G  H | Funds trigger customer alert. |
  | altsuccession | C  E | Docs lead to QA (post-credit). |
  | chainresponse | D  G | Offer chains to funding. |
  | chainsuccession | E  D | QA enables offer assembly. |

  *Impact*: Handles decision trees (e.g., approve/deny) without chaos.

- **Negations (Prohibitions)**:
  | Constraint | Source  Target | Prevention |
  |------------|------------------|------------|
  | noncoexistence | G  A | No funding before app (absurd but blocks errors). |
  | nonsuccession | H  B | Notification can't skip credit check. |
  | nonchainsuccession | F  H | Auth can't chain directly to notify (needs full cycle). |

  *Impact*: Stops reverse flows or skips, e.g., no disbursing funds (G) before application (A).

This interplay forms a **guarded state machine**: B is exactly once (quality gate), E/F ensure reviews, negations prevent premature G. Transparency comes from audit trails—every transition is traceable.

### Ensuring Compliance, Quality, and Structure

- **Compliance**: Absence of Z + negations (e.g., no G before A) enforce laws like Truth in Lending Act (TILA), Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), and anti-money laundering (AML). Exactly-one B limits credit inquiries; existence of C/E verifies docs and fairness.

- **Quality Standards**: Precedence (EF) and responded_existence (DE) mandate QA before offers/contracts, catching errors (e.g., miscalculated APR). Chains ensure holistic reviews.

- **Sequencing**: Succession/response create a DAG (directed acyclic graph) with no cycles, forcing forward progress. Alternatives handle denials (e.g., H without G).

**Interplay Example**: To reach G (funds), you need A (init)  B (exactly once)  C (docs)  E (QA via altsuccession)  D (offer)  G (chainresponse), with F interleaved. Skipping B? Blocked by exactly_one + negations. This maintains control: ~80-90% of real loans follow similar paths per FDIC data, reducing defaults via gates.

**Business Goals**:
- **Risk Mitigation**: Preliminary B + C/E gates filter 30-50% of bad applicants early (per FICO stats), minimizing losses.
- **Efficiency**: Exactly-one + chains optimize workflows (e.g., automate AB), cutting processing from weeks to days.
- **Customer Trust**: Transparent sequencing + H notifications boost NPS; prevents "bait-and-switch" offers.
