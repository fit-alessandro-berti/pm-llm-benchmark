Here is a structured, BPMN-like textual process model derived from the interview transcripts. This model is designed to be easily translatable into a visual BPMN 2.0 diagram by a process modeler.

---

### **1. Process Overview**
*   **Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling Process
*   **Trigger:** Receipt of an invoice (typically via email) from a supplier.
*   **End State:** Invoice is approved and scheduled for the next payment cycle.

### **2. Roles & Participants (Pools/Lanes)**
*   **External Supplier:** Provides the initial invoice and revised invoices/details.
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk:** Primary process handler; logs invoices, performs system matching, and schedules payments.
*   **Purchasing Officer:** Verifies receipt of goods/services and validates invoice details against the Purchase Order (PO).
*   **Department Manager:** Provides final financial/budgetary approval.
*   **AP Supervisor:** Handles escalated exceptions, unresolved discrepancies, and informal service invoices.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM):** Consulted indirectly to resolve pricing discrepancies or missing POs by checking contracts and contacting suppliers.

### **3. Artifacts & Data Objects**
*   Invoice (Email / System Record)
*   Purchase Order (PO) / Retrospective PO
*   Accounting System Log
*   Confirmation Email (from Purchasing)
*   Revised Invoice / Missing Details Request
*   System Approval Notification

---

### **4. Main Process Flow (Sequence & Gateways)**

**[Start Event]** Invoice Received (via email)

**[Task 1]** Check for valid Purchase Order (PO) number *(Role: AP Clerk)*
*   *Input:* Invoice
*   *Output:* PO validation status

**[Gateway 1: Exclusive (XOR)]** Is a valid PO number present?
*   **No (Flow A):** Proceed to *Exception Handling: Missing PO* (See Section 5). Once resolved, loop back to **[Task 1]**.
*   **Yes (Flow B):** Proceed to **[Task 2]**.

**[Task 2]** Log invoice into the accounting system *(Role: AP Clerk)*
*   *Output:* Accounting System Log

**[Task 3]** Forward invoice to Purchasing for goods/services receipt confirmation *(Role: AP Clerk)*

**[Task 4]** Verify invoice details against PO (quantities, prices, delivery charges) *(Role: Purchasing Officer)*
*   *Input:* Invoice, PO

**[Gateway 2: Exclusive (XOR)]** Do invoice details match the PO?
*   **No (Flow A):** Proceed to *Exception Handling: Discrepancy Resolution* (See Section 5). Once resolved, loop back to **[Task 4]**.
*   **Yes (Flow B):** Proceed to **[Task 5]**.

**[Task 5]** Send confirmation email to AP *(Role: Purchasing Officer)*
*   *Output:* Confirmation Email

**[Task 6]** Match invoice line items against the PO in the system *(Role: AP Clerk)*
*   *Note:* Interviewee Mary states this happens *after* receiving confirmation from Purchasing.

**[Gateway 3: Exclusive (XOR)]** Is the system match successful with no discrepancies?
*   **No (Flow A):** Proceed to *Exception Handling: System Match Discrepancy* (See Section 5). Once resolved, loop back to **[Task 6]**.
*   **Yes (Flow B):** Proceed to **[Task 7]**.

**[Task 7]** System sends automatic notification requesting sign-off *(Role: System / AP Clerk)*
*   *Input:* Confirmation Email, System Match success
*   *Output:* System Approval Notification

**[Task 8]** Review invoice details, PO, and confirmation for budgetary alignment *(Role: Department Manager)*

**[Gateway 4: Exclusive (XOR)]** Is the invoice approved? (No suspicious amounts or unexpected items)
*   **No (Flow A):** Proceed to *Exception Handling: Manager Rejection/Hold* (See Section 5). Once investigated, loop back to **[Task 8]** or **[Task 6]**.
*   **Yes (Flow B):** Proceed to **[Task 9]**.

**[Task 9]** Approve invoice in the system *(Role: Department Manager)*

**[Task 10]** Schedule invoice for the next payment cycle *(Role: AP Clerk)*
*   *Input:* Approved Invoice
*   *Output:* Payment Schedule

**[End Event]** Payment Scheduled

### **5. Exceptions & Rework Loops (Sub-processes)**

*   **Exception A: Missing PO (Informal Services)**
    *   *Trigger:* Gateway 1 = No.
    *   *Action:* AP Clerk requests details from Supplier. If it is an informal service, the issue is escalated to the **AP Supervisor**.
    *   *Resolution:* AP Supervisor coordinates with Department Heads to either (1) Create a Retrospective PO, or (2) Reject the invoice. If a Retrospective PO is created, the flow returns to **[Task 1]**.
*   **Exception B: Discrepancy Resolution (Pre-Matching)**
    *   *Trigger:* Gateway 2 = No.
    *   *Action:* Purchasing Officer either contacts the supplier directly OR asks the AP Clerk to hold the invoice. The **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM)** may be looped in to check contracts, confirm correct pricing, or request a revised invoice.
    *   *Resolution:* Once a corrected version is received or clarified, the flow returns to **[Task 4]**.
*   **Exception C: System Match Discrepancy (Escalation)**
    *   *Trigger:* Gateway 3 = No.
    *   *Action:* AP Clerk emails the Purchasing team to clarify. If the discrepancy cannot be resolved easily, the AP Clerk escalates the issue to the **AP Supervisor**.
    *   *Resolution:* AP Supervisor coordinates between departments or with the SRM to resolve. Once cleared, the flow returns to **[Task 6]**.
*   **Exception D: Manager Rejection/Hold**
    *   *Trigger:* Gateway 4 = No.
    *   *Action:* Department Manager rejects the invoice or asks the AP Clerk to hold it for further investigation.
    *   *Resolution:* AP Clerk investigates (potentially looping back to Purchasing or AP Supervisor). Once cleared, the flow returns to **[Task 8]** for re-review.

### **6. Ambiguities & Modeling Notes for the BPMN Designer**
1.  **Task Ownership Ambiguity (Matching):** Mary (AP Clerk) states *she* matches the line items in the system after Dan (Purchasing) confirms receipt. However, Dan states *his* job is to check if quantities and prices line up. *Modeling Decision:* Modeled as a two-step verification: Dan performs a visual/factual check (Task 4), followed by Mary performing the formal system match (Task 6). A process analyst should verify if Dan also clicks "match" in the system or just provides email confirmation.
2.  **SRM Involvement Trigger:** Karen (SRM) states she is "looped in" by AP or Purchasing when terms don't match, but does not specify the exact BPMN trigger. *Modeling Decision:* Represented as an optional, ad-hoc sub-task or message flow within the "Discrepancy Resolution" exception path, rather than a mandatory sequential step.
3.  **Rejection vs. Hold:** The Department Manager mentions both "rejecting" and "asking to hold." *Modeling Decision:* Grouped under a single gateway branch for simplicity, with the understanding that "hold" implies a temporary rework loop, while "reject" may lead to a terminal end event (Invoice Rejected) if unresolved. The model currently assumes a rework loop for both to keep the process moving.
