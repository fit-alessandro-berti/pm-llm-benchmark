**Here is a curated list of targeted, open-ended questions** I would ask the interviewee to deepen understanding of the property onboarding process:

1. Can you describe the very first interaction when a landlord contacts the regional office? What information is typically gathered at that initial point, and who is responsible for logging it and beginning the documentation review?

2. What specific criteria or checklist does the property coordinator use to determine whether the landlord’s documentation (deeds, compliance certificates, inspection reports) is acceptable? Which documents are absolutely mandatory versus nice-to-have, and how are incomplete or outdated documents typically handled?

3. When recording property details into the central database, which pieces of information (square footage, amenities, expected rental price, etc.) have the biggest impact on later stages like marketing, pricing, and tenant matching, and why?

4. How does the assignment of a dedicated property manager actually work in practice? Walk me through how the team weighs factors such as current workload, geographic specialization, experience with similar properties, and the landlord’s unique requirements.

5. During the introductory meeting between the property manager and the landlord, what are the most common expectations, policies, or special conditions that need to be aligned (e.g., pets, maintenance responsibilities, payment methods)? How are the outcomes of that conversation documented and shared with the rest of the team?

6. Can you walk me through the inspection process? What are the key safety and habitability standards the inspector evaluates, how are different types of issues prioritized, and what is the typical decision-making process and timeline for addressing findings before the property can move forward?

7. How does the property manager collaborate with the photographer, inspector, and digital marketing team? What inputs does marketing need to create listings and update pricing models, and does the landlord have any approval rights over the final marketing materials or listed price?

8. In the tenant screening process, what are the most important decision factors when evaluating applications? How are credit checks, employment verification, references, and other elements weighted, especially when there are multiple qualified applicants?

9. When multiple tenants are interested in the same property, how does the selection and negotiation process work? What guidelines exist around offering promotional discounts, prioritizing longer lease terms, or making other concessions?

10. Could you describe the sequence and timing of the final quality checks and audits (compliance, financial, IT)? Do these reviews happen before or after the lease is signed, and what happens if the compliance officer or financial analyst identifies an issue at that late stage?

11. How are exceptions typically handled—such as properties that require special insurance, landlords who insist on custom lease clauses, or situations where the landlord cannot quickly address inspection findings? Is there a standard escalation or approval path for these cases?

12. Overall, which steps in the onboarding process can run in parallel versus those that must be strictly sequential? What are the most common bottlenecks or points of delay, and how is status and communication managed across regional offices, property managers, support teams, and external stakeholders like landlords and contractors?

These questions are designed to uncover decision criteria, clarify roles and handoffs, verify sequencing and dependencies, surface exception paths, and reveal missing operational details while staying at a conceptual/process level.
