This visualization is a **Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) diagram** depicting an end-to-end order fulfillment workflow across four organizational departments: **Sales, Production, Procurement, and Warehouse.**

The diagram illustrates how these departments interact using sequences, messages, and subprocesses. Here is a breakdown of the workflow:

### 1. Sales (The Initiation)
*   **Trigger:** The process begins on "Monday morning."
*   **Actions:** The sales team checks the order for completeness and verifies if the customer exists in the database. If not, they add the customer.
*   **Outcome:** Once the order is processed, "Order details" are sent to both the Production and Procurement departments. Finally, the Sales team drafts an invoice.

### 2. Production (Manufacturing)
*   **Trigger:** Receives "Order details" from Sales.
*   **Actions:** The production process involves planning and manufacturing. It includes a parallel gateway (the plus sign) indicating that production planning and a check for faulty materials happen concurrently.
*   **Outcome:** Upon completion, a signal ("Product complete") is sent to the Warehouse.

### 3. Procurement (Supply Chain)
*   **Trigger:** Receives "Order details" from Sales.
*   **Subprocess:** This department runs a "Material Compensation Subprocess" which includes ordering materials and returning any faulty items.
*   **Control:** After checking materials, if there is a problem, it can trigger an "Order cancelation" which ends the specific procurement flow.

### 4. Warehouse (Logistics)
*   **Trigger:** Receives the "Product complete" signal from the Production department.
*   **Actions:** The Warehouse team handles the final physical task of sending the product.
*   **Outcome:** Once the product is sent, a signal ("Product sent") is transmitted back to the Sales department, allowing them to finalize the invoice drafting process.

### Key Features of the Diagram:
*   **Swimlanes:** The horizontal bands (Sales, Production, Procurement, Warehouse) clearly define the roles and responsibilities of each department.
*   **Message Flows:** The dashed lines represent communication between departments (e.g., sending order details, signal events like "Product complete").
*   **Gateways:**
    *   **XOR Gateways (X):** Used in the Sales department to handle decisions (e.g., whether to add a customer to the database).
    *   **AND Gateways (+):** Used in the Production department to coordinate concurrent tasks.
*   **Error/Compensation Events:** The procurement section includes specialized events (indicated by the lightning bolt and rewind icons) to handle "Order cancelation" and "Return material" scenarios.

Overall, this is a complex operational flowchart designed to show how a customer's order triggers a cross-departmental chain reaction to manufacture, source materials, ship the product, and bill the client.
