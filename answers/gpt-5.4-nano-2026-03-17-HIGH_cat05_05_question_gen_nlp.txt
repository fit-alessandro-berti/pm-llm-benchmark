1. How does the company balance design-change flexibility (from R&D) against the risk of disrupting downstream supplier lead times and qualification schedules?  
2. What criteria determine when a component design or supplier substitution is considered “equivalent,” and how do those decisions affect long-term warranty and recall risk?  
3. How are supplier lead times and quality metrics modeled across countries and contracts—especially when disruptions (weather, political events, capacity constraints) aren’t reflected in historical data?  
4. What mechanisms prevent “bullwhip effects” when regional sales forecasts change, and how effective are those controls across different regions and demand patterns?  
5. In a 24/7 multi-shift Malaysia assembly environment, how is labor availability, skill variation, and fatigue risk managed to ensure consistent quality over time?  
6. How does the company decide the optimal level of just-in-time replenishment versus safety stock for each market, given uncertainty in logistics and demand volatility?  
7. For air freight versus cargo shipping, what framework is used to trade off cost, delivery speed, carbon footprint, and service-level risk—especially during peak seasons?  
8. How are customs compliance and regulatory differences handled operationally to minimize clearance delays, fines, and shipment rework across multiple countries?  
9. What role do digital systems play in end-to-end traceability (from supplier lots to finished units), and how quickly can the company identify the root cause of field issues?  
10. How do environmental and labor compliance audits influence sourcing decisions in practice, and what happens when a supplier passes audits but later fails—or vice versa?  
11. When production schedules depend on many incoming component streams, what strategies are used to manage partial shortages without overproducing or scrapping large batches?  
12. How are inventory policies coordinated between global distribution centers and local warehouses to ensure service levels without creating unnecessary regional excess stock?  
13. What data governance and security practices are needed to safely manage purchase orders, invoices, customs documents, and demand-planning datasets across currencies and jurisdictions?  
14. How do machine learning forecast models get validated and monitored over time to prevent “model drift” that could quietly worsen inventory decisions or customer service?  
15. During product recalls or suspected quality defects, what is the decision process for scaling response globally—how are thresholds set for stopping shipments versus continuing with corrective actions?  
16. How does the company quantify and reduce carbon footprint across the entire lifecycle (supplier selection, transportation modes, packaging, and warehousing energy use)?  
17. What are the trade-offs between centralized planning and regional autonomy—how does the company prevent conflicting actions between global planners and local fulfillment teams?  
18. How are warranty and after-sales support considerations integrated into upstream supply decisions, such as component sourcing quality and manufacturing test coverage?  
19. What contingency planning exists for correlated disruptions (e.g., one supplier affects multiple components, or a single logistics corridor affects many regions), and how is risk assessed?  
20. If the company needed to prioritize either cost reduction or resilience under severe disruption scenarios, what would be the most likely parts of the network to resist change—and why?
