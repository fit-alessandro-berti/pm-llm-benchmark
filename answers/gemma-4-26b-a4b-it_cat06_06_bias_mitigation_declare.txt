### Rationale for Bias-Mitigating Constraints

To ensure the loan application process is fair and does not allow sensitive attributes to trigger immediate or unvetted decisions, I have introduced the following constraints:

1.  **Existence Constraints:** I added `CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `ManualReview`, `BiasMitigationCheck`, `Approve`, and `Reject` to the model. This establishes that these fairness-related activities are valid parts of the process.
2.  **Coexistence Constraints (`CheckApplicantRace/Gender` $\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview`):** These constraints ensure that if the process involves checking a sensitive attribute (Race or Gender), a `ManualReview` step **must** also occur within the trace. This prevents automated systems from making decisions based on sensitive data without human oversight.
3.  **Response Constraints (`CheckApplicantRace/Gender` $\to$ `BiasMitigationCheck`):** By using a `response` constraint, I enforce that if a sensitive attribute is identified, a `BiasMitigationCheck` **must** follow. This ensures that the process includes a specific procedural step designed to neutralize potential biases before proceeding.
4.  **Non-succession Constraints (`CheckApplicantRace/Gender` $\not\to$ `FinalDecision`):** This is a critical safeguard. It prevents a "direct path" from a sensitive attribute check to a final decision. By making it impossible for these activities to be in direct succession, the model forces the process to pass through other intermediary steps (like the mitigation or manual review steps mentioned above), thereby breaking the link between sensitive data and immediate outcomes.
