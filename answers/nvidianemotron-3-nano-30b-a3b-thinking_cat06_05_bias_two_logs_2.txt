## 2. How the bias manifests  

### 2.1. **A discretionary “adjustment” that is tied to a protected characteristic (affiliation)**  

- The **+5 adjustment** is applied *only* to candidates whose **ProfessionalAssociation = LocalBusinessLeadersClub**.  
- Because *LocalBusinessLeadersClub* is a realworld proxy for **membership in a professional network**, it often overlaps with **socioeconomic status, educational background, gender, or race**. In many contexts, such clubs are **homogeneously composed** (e.g., predominantly male, white, or from affluent backgrounds).  
- Consequently, the adjustment **systematically benefits** candidates who already enjoy social capital, while **disadvantaging** those who lack that network connection — regardless of identical skill or personality scores.

### 2.2. **Algorithmic opacity**  

- The boost is **hardcoded** in the *ScoreAdjustment* column and reflected only in the *CulturalFit* column after the boost.  
- Decisionmakers who see the final *CulturalFit* score of **65** may not realize that it is a *derived* value (original 60+5).  
- This makes it easy to **attribute the hire to “overall fit”** while silently **relying on an undisclosed bias factor**.

### 2.3. **Feedback loop**  

- Because the boosted candidates are hired, the organization may later **ascribe their success to “good cultural fit”** and **reinforce the same adjustment rule** in future hiring cycles.  
- This creates a **selffulfilling prophecy**: the rule continues to favor the same socially advantaged group, perpetuating existing inequities.
