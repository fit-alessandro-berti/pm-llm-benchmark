| Issue | Implication |

1. **Decouple non-credit attributes from scoring**: Remove or rigorously justify the `+10 (Community)` adjustment. If community engagement is a risk factor, it must be empirically validated and applied uniformly.
2. **Standardize decision thresholds**: Implement a single, transparent score cutoff (e.g., 700) applied regardless of residency or affiliation. Use attributes only for risk stratification, not threshold shifting.

The process exhibits **embedded socioeconomic and geographic bias** that violates the principle of score-based consistency.
