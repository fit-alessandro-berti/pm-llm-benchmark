The diagram presents a high-level overview of a business process involving sales, production, procurement, and warehouse operations. It begins with the sales department receiving an order on Monday morning, which is then checked for completeness and customer database entry. If the customer is not in the database, they are added; otherwise, the order details are sent. The production department plans and manufactures the product based on received order details. If materials are faulty, a subprocess involving material compensation is triggered, including ordering materials, checking them, and potentially returning them. Once the product is complete, it is sent to the warehouse, where it is subsequently dispatched. The diagram uses swimlanes to distinguish between different departments and subprocesses, illustrating the flow of information and materials across the organization. The overall concept emphasizes the interconnectedness of various departments in fulfilling customer orders, highlighting the importance of coordination and communication in the process.
