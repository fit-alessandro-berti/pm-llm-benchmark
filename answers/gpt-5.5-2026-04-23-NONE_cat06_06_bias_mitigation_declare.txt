### Rationale for the added constraints

- **`BiasMitigationCheck` existence**: Ensures that the process includes a formal fairness-control step.

- **Coexistence between sensitive-attribute checks and `BiasMitigationCheck`**: If activities such as `CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, or `CheckApplicantAge` occur, then a fairness review must also occur in the same trace.

- **Coexistence between sensitive-demographic decisions and `ManualReview`**: Decisions such as `Reject_Minority`, `Approve_Minority`, or `RequestAdditionalInfo_Minority` must be accompanied by `ManualReview`, reducing the risk that demographic attributes drive the outcome without oversight.

- **Response and succession from sensitive checks to `BiasMitigationCheck`**: These constraints require that once a sensitive attribute is observed, a bias-mitigation activity must follow before the process reaches a final outcome.

- **Precedence from `BiasMitigationCheck` to decision activities**: Approval, rejection, request for additional information, and final decision activities require a prior fairness check.

- **Precedence from `ManualReview` to sensitive-demographic decision activities**: Sensitive-demographic outcomes cannot occur unless manual review has already taken place.

- **Non-succession and non-chain-succession from sensitive checks to decisions**: These constraints prevent the process from moving directly from checking sensitive attributes to decisions such as `Reject` or `FinalDecision`, reducing the possibility of immediate discriminatory decisions.
