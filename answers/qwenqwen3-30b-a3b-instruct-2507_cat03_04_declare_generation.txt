###  **Notes and Rationale:**

- **All support and confidence are 1.0**: This assumes the process is deterministic and all rules are always followed in every trace.
- **`init`**: Only `IG` can be the first activity.
- **`existence`**: All activities are required.
- **`exactly_one`**: Only one `AG` (approval gate), which is typical.
- **`responded_existence`**, **`response`**, **`precedence`**, **`succession`**, etc.: Reflect the sequential flow.
- **`chainresponse`, `chainprecedence`, `chainsuccession`**: Used to enforce long chains (e.g., the full pipeline).
- **`nonsuccession`**: Prevents direct jumps (e.g., from IG to FL), which would violate process logic.
- **`noncoexistence`**: Only added if two activities are mutually exclusive — currently not needed, so empty.
- **`nonchainsuccession`**: Not used here, but could be added if a specific chain is forbidden.
