### Explanation of Logic

**1. Case Identification (How cases were grouped)**
Each distinct **user task or work item** was treated as a separate case, identified primarily by the specific document or artifact being acted upon.  
- **Document1.docx** events (from creation to final save/close) form **Case_02**. This represents a drafting session with interruptions (email, PDF review, Excel update) but a clear return to complete the same document.  
- **Quarterly_Report.docx** events form **Case_01**. This is a separate document worked on at the start and end of the session.  
- **Email - Inbox** interactions around the “Annual Meeting” email form **Case_03**. The sequence from open to send creates a coherent email-handling case.  
- **Report_Draft.pdf** review actions form **Case_04** (open, scroll, highlight).  
- **Budget_2024.xlsx** editing forms **Case_05** (open, edit fields, save).  

Switching events (SWITCH, FOCUS) were included as activities when they bridged different applications/documents, helping to show context transitions in the process flow.

**2. Activity Naming Strategy**
Low-level actions were generalized into standardized, analyst-friendly activity names:  
- **FOCUS**  *Open Document*, *Open Spreadsheet*, *Resume Document* (depending on whether it was the first focus or a return).  
- **TYPING**  *Draft Content*, *Draft Reply*, *Edit Data* (depending on context: document drafting vs. email reply vs. spreadsheet editing).  
- **SAVE**  *Save Document*, *Save Spreadsheet*.  
- **SWITCH**  *Switch Application* (retained as a distinct activity to capture handoffs).  
- **CLICK/SCROLL/HIGHLIGHT** in review contexts  *Review Document*, *Review Email*, *Mark Document*.  
- **COMPOSE/REPLY**  *Compose Reply*.  

**3. Additional Attributes**
Kept *Application* and *Document / Context* as supplementary attributes to enrich the log without overcomplicating the core case/activity/timestamp structure. *Additional Info* captures specifics (e.g., text highlighted, keys pressed) for traceability if needed.

This transformation yields a coherent, analyst-friendly event log that reflects a realistic user work pattern: drafting a document  handling email  reviewing a PDF and updating a spreadsheet  returning to finish the draft and another report.
