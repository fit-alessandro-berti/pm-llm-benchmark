Based on the detailed process description provided, here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify decision logic, roles, sequencing, and exception handling.

These questions are categorized by the phase of the process they address to help structure the conversation.

### **Phase 1: Intake, Verification, & Data Entry**
1.  **Intake Channels:** You mentioned that the process begins when a landlord contacts the regional office. Could you clarify if this intake happens via a single unified portal, email, or phone, and how the coordinator prioritizes which requests to review first if there is a volume backlog?
2.  **Document Validity:** Regarding the documentation review, specifically for certificates of compliance: is there a standardized "expiration threshold" (e.g., within 6 months) that triggers a request for an update, or is this judgment call made individually by the coordinator based on local regulations?
3.  **Data Ownership:** Once the coordinator enters details into the central database, is there a validation step where the landlord confirms these details (square footage, amenities, etc.) are accurate before the process moves forward to manager assignment?

### **Phase 2: Manager Assignment & Handoff**
4.  **Assignment Logic:** You noted that assignment depends on factors like workload and geographic specialization. Is there a rigid scoring matrix or algorithm used to rank eligible managers, or is this largely a manual decision made by a regional lead?
5.  **Rejection/Escalation:** If the assigned property manager’s workload is currently at capacity, is the property placed in a queue waiting for that specific manager, or is there an immediate escalation path to assign a different manager to avoid delays?
6.  **Expectation Alignment:** During the introductory meeting, if the landlord’s expectations (e.g., regarding maintenance responsibilities) fundamentally conflict with your company’s standard policies, does the process terminate here, or is there a formal waiver or exception request workflow?

### **Phase 3: Inspection & Remediation**
7.  **Scheduling Dependencies:** Are the photographer and the inspector scheduled to visit simultaneously to save time, or does the inspector strictly need to visit first to confirm the property is safe before marketing assets (photos) are created?
8.  **Remediation Authority:** When the inspector identifies issues, who holds the authority to approve contractor hiring and repair costs—the property manager autonomously, or does every significant repair require landlord sign-off?
9.  **Re-Inspection Logic:** After repairs are made, is a full re-inspection required, or can the inspector sign off on specific items remotely/photos depending on the severity of the initial issue?

### **Phase 4: Marketing & Pricing**
10. **Pricing Timing:** You mentioned the internal pricing model is updated based on market trends. Is the final rental price locked in *before* the property is listed (activation), or can the price fluctuate dynamically while the property is already live on the rental portal?
11. **Marketing Triggers:** What specific signal indicates to the digital marketing team that the property is ready for them to begin work? Is it an automated status change in the CRM once the inspection passes, or a manual handoff from the manager?

### **Phase 5: Tenant Screening & Leasing**
12. **Application Conflicts:** If multiple viable tenants apply, you mentioned prioritizing those who commit to longer leases. Are there legal constraints or fair housing guidelines that prevent you from filtering strictly by lease length, and how do you document the justification for rejecting a shorter-term applicant?
13. **Negotiation Boundaries:** During the negotiation of lease terms (like promotional discounts), does the property manager have a hard limit on discount percentages they can offer without seeking approval from a financial analyst or senior manager?
14. **Activation State:** The description mentions the property is "activated" making it available for viewings. Does this activation happen *before* a tenant is screened, implying open viewings, or is the listing kept in a "hidden/pending" state until the initial screening batch is processed?

### **Phase 6: Audits, Compliance, & Exceptions**
15. **Audit Timing (Crucial Sequencing Question):** The process concludes with quality checks by Compliance, Finance, and IT. **Do these audits act as a "gate" that must be passed *before* the keys are handed over (Move-in), or do they happen retrospectively after the tenant has already moved in?**
16. **Exception Handling (Custom Clauses):** For landlords insisting on custom lease clauses, at what specific step does the Compliance Officer get involved? Does the drafting of the lease pause entirely until the custom clause is vetted, creating a bottleneck?
17. **System Integration Failure:** If the IT department discovers that the online listing data doesn't match the internal database entries during their final check, what is the rollback procedure? Is the tenant notified of potential errors in their lease agreement?
