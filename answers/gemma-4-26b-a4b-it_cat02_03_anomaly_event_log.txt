Based on an analysis of the provided event log, **Case 1001** follows the standard, logical Procure-to-Pay (P2P) sequence. However, **Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004** all contain significant anomalies.

Below is the detailed breakdown of the anomalies and the business risks they represent.

---

### 1. Case 1002: Critical Control Failure (High Risk)
This case is the most problematic as it exhibits a complete breakdown of internal controls and logical sequencing.

*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Approval after Execution:** The "Approve Purchase Requisition" activity occurs on Feb 03, long *after* the Purchase Order was issued (Feb 01) and even *after* the payment was released (Feb 03).
    *   **Payment before Verification:** "Release Payment" occurs before "Receive Goods" and before "Match Invoice to PO."
    *   **Post-hoc Matching:** The "Match Invoice to PO" step happens after the money has already left the company.
*   **Why this is problematic:**
    *   **Fraud Risk:** This pattern is a major red flag for fraudulent activity. Making payments before receiving goods or matching invoices allows for "ghost vendors" or unauthorized payments to be processed without oversight.
    *   **Lack of Authorization:** Issuing a PO and releasing payment before the requisition is approved means employees are spending company funds without proper management authorization.
    *   **Financial Risk:** The company has no guarantee that the goods were actually received or that the invoice amount is correct before the cash is gone.

### 2. Case 1003: Violation of the "Three-Way Match" (Medium-High Risk)
This case demonstrates a failure to follow the fundamental accounting principle of the "Three-Way Match" (comparing the Purchase Order, the Receiving Report, and the Vendor Invoice).

*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Premature Payment:** The "Release Payment" occurs on Feb 08, but the "Vendor Invoice Received" does not arrive until Feb 09, and the "Receive Goods" does not occur until Feb 10.
*   **Why this is problematic:**
    *   **Overpayment Risk:** Without matching the invoice to the goods received and the original PO, the company might pay for goods that were never delivered, were damaged, or were billed at an incorrect price.
    *   **Compliance Issues:** Standard auditing practices require a three-way match to validate a disbursement. This sequence would fail a financial audit.

### 3. Case 1004: Maverick Spending / Post-facto PO (Medium Risk)
This case shows a procedural error regarding the timing of procurement documentation.

*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Receipt before PO Issuance:** "Receive Goods" occurs on Feb 06 at 09:00, but the "Issue Purchase Order" does not occur until 09:15 on the same day.
*   **Why this is problematic:**
    *   **Maverick Spending:** This suggests "Maverick Spending," where goods are ordered (perhaps via phone or email) without a formal Purchase Order being in place first.
    *   **Inventory/Liability Risks:** Receiving goods before a PO exists means the receiving department is accepting items that have not been officially authorized by the procurement system, which can lead to unexpected liabilities and inventory discrepancies.
