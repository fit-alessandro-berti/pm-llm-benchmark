Based on a consolidation of the interview transcripts, here is a textual BPMN-like model for the **Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling Process**.

---

### **Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling
### **Primary Goal:** To validate, approve, and schedule payment for supplier invoices.

---

### **Participants/Roles (Pools & Lanes):**
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk:** Mary
*   **Purchasing Officer:** Dan
*   **Department Manager:** Carlos
*   **AP Supervisor:** Rita
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager:** Karen
*   **Accounting System:** (Acts as a supporting system)

---

### **Normal Process Flow (Happy Path):**

1.  **Start Event:** Invoice received (typically via email).
2.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Check for valid Purchase Order (PO) number on invoice.
3.  **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Is a valid PO present?
    *   **If NO:** Task (AP Clerk) -> Send invoice back to supplier requesting PO. **(End: Invoice Returned)**
    *   **If YES:** Continue.
4.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Log invoice into the Accounting System.
5.  **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Confirm receipt of goods/services against the PO.
6.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Match invoice line items to the PO in the system.
7.  **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Does the invoice match the PO?
    *   **If NO (Discrepancy):** Go to **Exception Path: Discrepancy Handling**.
    *   **If YES:** Continue.
8.  **Task (Department Manager):** Approve invoice (triggered by system email after successful matching and receipt confirmation).
9.  **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Does the Manager approve?
    *   **If NO (Rejects/Suspicious):** Go to **Exception Path: Manager Rejection**.
    *   **If YES:** Continue.
10. **Task (AP Clerk):** Schedule invoice for payment in the next payment run.
11. **End Event:** Invoice queued for payment.

---

### **Exception & Variation Paths:**

*   **Path A: Discrepancy Handling (from Step 7 - NO)**
    1.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Email Purchasing team to clarify discrepancy.
    2.  **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Is discrepancy easily resolvable?
        *   **If YES:** Resolve (e.g., get corrected info). Return to **Step 6 (Match Invoice)**.
        *   **If NO:** **Task (AP Clerk)** escalates to **AP Supervisor**.
    3.  **Task (AP Supervisor):** Investigate discrepancy. This may involve:
        *   Contacting the supplier directly.
        *   Coordinating between departments.
        *   Looping in **Supplier Relationship Manager** to check contracts or contact supplier.
    4.  **Task (AP Supervisor/Teams):** Resolve issue (e.g., obtain corrected invoice, create retrospective PO).
    5.  Return to **Step 5 (Purchasing Confirmation)** or **Step 6 (Match Invoice)** as appropriate.

*   **Path B: Manager Rejection (from Step 9 - NO)**
    1.  **Task (Department Manager):** Reject or request investigation.
    2.  **Task (AP Clerk/Supervisor):** Investigate the manager's concern.
    3.  Return to **Step 6 (Match Invoice)** or an earlier step for re-validation.

*   **Path C: Invoice with No PO (from Step 3 - Informal Service)**
    *   This is a variant of Step 3. If the invoice is for an informally contracted service (no PO exists), it is routed to the **AP Supervisor**.
    *   **Task (AP Supervisor):** Work with department heads to either create a retrospective PO or reject the invoice. If a PO is created, proceed to **Step 4 (Log Invoice)**.

---

### **Artifacts/Data Objects:**
*   **Invoice:** The primary input document.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** The key reference document for validation.
*   **Goods/Service Receipt Confirmation:** The evidence from Purchasing.
*   **Approval/Rejection Notification:** The manager's action in the system.
*   **Contract:** Referenced by the Supplier Relationship Manager during dispute resolution.

---

### **Key Notes & Ambiguities from Interviews:**
1.  **Trigger for Manager Approval:** Consensus is that it's system-triggered *after* AP logs the invoice and Purchasing confirms receipt.
2.  **Post-Approval Flow:** All agree the invoice returns to AP for payment scheduling after manager approval. The exact system mechanism for "scheduling" is not detailed.
3.  **Supplier Involvement:** Direct contact with the supplier is performed by either AP, Purchasing, or the Supplier Relationship Manager, depending on the issue and company protocol. The model shows this as a potential task within exception paths.
4.  **Role of AP Supervisor:** Clear from Rita's interview that she is an exception-handling role, not part of the standard approval sequence.
5.  **"Escalation to Supervisor":** Mary mentions this happens if a discrepancy can't be resolved easily. Rita's account confirms this is her main entry point. The model routes this from the "Discrepancy Handling" path.
6.  **Manager's Infrequent Rejection:** Carlos states this is rare (~once every couple of months). It is modeled as a distinct but low-frequency exception path.
