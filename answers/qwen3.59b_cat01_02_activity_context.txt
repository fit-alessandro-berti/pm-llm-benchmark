### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

Upon analyzing the event log, two primary activity types appear multiple times within specific cases, requiring contextual disambiguation:

*   **`Verify Documents`:** This activity recurs in **Case C001** and **Case C003**.
    *   **Context 1:** Initial intake validation (checking identity upon request submission).
    *   **Context 2:** Re-validation after new data is provided or an internal adjustment is made.
*   **`Call Customer`:** This activity recurs in **Case C002**.
    *   **Context 1:** Outbound call to clarify an account discrepancy (investigation).
    *   **Context 2:** Follow-up call to confirm that the adjustments requested were completed successfully.

While `Initiate Request`, `Approve Request`, and `Adjust Account` appear only once per case in this dataset, the generic names for `Verify Documents` and `Call Customer` obscure the specific process steps. To accurately reflect the workflow logic, these activities will be renamed based on their specific intent (e.g., Initial vs. Re-verification; Clarification vs. Confirmation).

### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

*   **`Verify Documents`:**
    *   **Initial vs. Re-verification:** In C001 and C003, the first instance is renamed **Initial Document Verification** to distinguish it from the second instance, which becomes **Post-Submission/Post-Adjustment Document Re-verification**. This clarifies that the agent is validating new data rather than just checking identity again.
    *   **Physical vs. Digital:** In C003, the first verification happens at a branch desk. It is renamed **Physical Document Verification** to distinguish it from the digital intake in C001/C002 and clarify the method of validation.

*   **`Call Customer`:**
    *   **Intent-Based Naming:** The generic name "Call Customer" hides the objective. In C002, the first call is renamed **Outbound Call: Discrepancy Clarification** (investigation phase), while the second is **Outbound Call: Confirmation Follow-up** (closure phase).
    *   **Escalation Context:** In C001, the call is for missing information. It is renamed **Outbound Call: Missing Info Request** to distinguish it from a standard confirmation or clarification call.

*   **`Initiate Request`:**
    *   Refined to **Initial Request Submission** (or **Walk-in Request Submission**) to align with the specific entry channel (WebPortal vs. BranchReception), ensuring consistency with the renamed verification activities.

*   **`Approve Request`:**
    *   Renamed to **Final Request Approval** to explicitly mark this as the termination node of the workflow, distinguishing it from intermediate checks or adjustments.
