Based on a comprehensive comparison of the two event logs, the **systemic bias** is exhibited by the process applied to **Group B (Unprotected Group)**.

The bias manifests through the integration of the `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` attributes, which actively modify the applicant𠏋 score via the `ScoreAdjustment` column. This creates an unmerited advantage for applicants belonging to the defined local community group, resulting in differential and systematic treatment compared to Group A.

---

### Detailed Analysis of Bias

#### 1. Differential Scoring Mechanism (The Bias Mechanism)

The core source of the bias is the **Community Boost** applied exclusively (or at least visibly) in the Group B log.

*   **Group A (Protected):** The `ScoreAdjustment` column is consistently **0** or **N/A** (where N/A refers to the manual review stage, not the scoring stage). The decision relies solely on the `PreliminaryScore` generated by the Scoring Engine.
    *   *Example P001:* Score = 720 $\rightarrow$ Decision = Approved.
*   **Group B (Unprotected):** When the applicant is a `LocalResident` AND belongs to a specified `CommunityGroup` (e.g., Highland Civic Darts Club), a positive adjustment (e.g., +10) is added at both the Preliminary Scoring and Manual Review stages.
    *   *Example U001:* Initial Score = 720 $\rightarrow$ Adjustment = +10 $\rightarrow$ Final Score = 730 $\rightarrow$ Decision = Approved.

**The Problem:** The application of a score adjustment based on protected characteristics (residency and community membership) overrides the objective scoring process. The system implicitly values community ties over the pure financial or merit metrics the raw score represents.

#### 2. Impact on Outcomes (Evidence)

The comparison of the three pairs of cases highlights how this boosting mechanism changes the outcome:

| Case ID | Group | Raw Score | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision | Key Finding |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **P001** | A | 720 | 0 | 720 | Approved | Approved purely on raw score. |
| **U001** | B | 720 | +10 | 730 | Approved | Boosted score (+10) leads to approval. |
| **P002** | A | 710 | 0 | 710 | Rejected | Rejected on raw score. |

**Specific Evidence of Bias:**

1.  **The Marginal Case (U003 vs P003):** P003 had a high raw score (740) and was approved. U003 had a significantly lower raw score (695). While U003 was approved, its approval was driven by the **+10 Community Boost** (bringing the score to 705), not by its own inherent merit, whereas P003's approval was based on a score 35 points higher.
2.  **Overriding Risk:** The mechanism allows a less qualified applicant (e.g., U001 vs P001) to achieve the same outcome (Approval) with a much lower raw score simply because they belong to a defined local community group.

### Conclusion

The system demonstrates **Disparate Treatment Bias**. It does not treat applicants with similar merit scores equally; rather, it grants an undeserved systemic advantage to individuals who fit specific criteria defined by their local residency and community affiliation.

If the sole determining factor for approval were the `PreliminaryScore`, then Group B (Unprotected) would show bias because they are rewarded for their group affiliation, while Group A (Protected) must rely exclusively on the inherent merit score.
