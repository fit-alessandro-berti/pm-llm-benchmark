### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization

**Queue Identification via Event Log Data:**  
In process mining, a *queue* is defined as the time a patient spends waiting for the next activity to begin after completing the current one. Using the event log, we calculate the **waiting time** between consecutive activities as:  
\[
\text{Waiting Time}_{i \rightarrow j} = \text{Timestamp}_{\text{Activity } j}^{\text{COMPLETE}} - \text{Timestamp}_{\text{Activity } i}^{\text{COMPLETE}}
\]  
where Activity \(i\) is immediately followed by Activity \(j\) in the same patient’s timeline. For example, if a patient finishes Registration at 09:08:45 and starts Nurse Assessment at 09:15:20, the waiting time is 6 minutes and 35 seconds.

**Key Metrics for Queue Characterization:**  
- **Average Waiting Time**: Mean of all waiting times across patients and queues.  
- **Median Waiting Time**: Middle value, robust to outliers.  
- **Maximum Waiting Time**: Longest observed wait, identifies extreme delays.  
- **90th/95th Percentile Waiting Time**: Captures tail behavior; critical for service-level agreements.  
- **Queue Frequency**: Number of patients experiencing waits above a threshold (e.g., >15 minutes).  
- **Number of Cases with Excessive Waits**: Count of patients exceeding predefined limits (e.g., >20 minutes).  

**Identifying Critical Queues:**  
Prioritize queues using:  
- **Highest Average Wait**: Indicates systemic inefficiency.  
- **Highest Frequency**: High volume queues amplify impact.  
- **Longest Max/Percentile Wait**: Highlights worst-case scenarios.  
- **Patient-Specific Impact**: Urgent cases with excessive waits demand urgent attention.  
For example, if Nurse Assessment consistently shows a 25-minute average wait (vs. 10 minutes overall) and 30% of urgent cases exceed 30 minutes, this queue is critical.

### 2. Root Cause Analysis

**Potential Root Causes:**  
- **Resource Bottlenecks**: Understaffing (e.g., only 1 clerk for Registration during peak hours) or underutilized rooms/equipment (e.g., ECG machines idle between patients).  
- **Activity Dependencies**: Delays in one activity (e.g., lab results not ready) blocking subsequent steps.  
- **Variability in Service Times**: Inconsistent task durations (e.g., nurse assessments taking 20–40 minutes based on complexity).  
- **Scheduling Policies**: Fixed slots ignoring variability (e.g., 15-minute appointments for 30-minute tasks).  
- **Patient Arrival Patterns**: Clustered arrivals (e.g., 10 patients at 9:00 AM) overwhelming capacity.  
- **Patient Type/Urgency**: New patients requiring more documentation vs. follow-ups; urgent cases diverting resources.  

**Process Mining Techniques for Root Cause Discovery:**  
- **Resource Analysis**: Map staff/room utilization to identify over/underused resources (e.g., Nurse 1 is idle 40% of the time).  
- **Bottleneck Analysis**: Use *alignment* and *fitness* metrics to detect activities with high variance or delays (e.g., Registration often starts late due to incomplete prior steps).  
- **Variant Analysis**: Compare paths for urgent vs. non-urgent cases to uncover differences in flow (e.g., urgent patients bypass pre-visit steps, causing downstream delays).  
- **Performance Overlay**: Overlay waiting times on activity sequences to visualize delays (e.g., ECG tests consistently start 20 minutes after Nurse Assessment).

### 3. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Staff Allocation Based on Queue Analysis**  
- **Targeted Queue**: Nurse Assessment (high average wait: 25 minutes).  
- **Root Cause**: Nurse 1’s underutilization during off-peak hours leads to overloading during peaks.  
- **Data Support**: Resource analysis shows 40% idle time; queue data confirms Nurse Assessment is the bottleneck.  
- **Impact**: Reallocate 1 nurse from low-demand periods to peak times, reducing average wait by 15–20% (based on simulation models).  

**Strategy 2: Pre-Visit Checklist Automation**  
- **Targeted Queue**: Registration (long waits due to manual paperwork).  
- **Root Cause**: Manual data entry delays completion.  
- **Data Support**: 60% of Registration waits occur when staff manually input patient info post-check-in.  
- **Impact**: Implement digital check-in (QR codes for forms), cutting completion time by 50% (validated via pilot data).  

**Strategy 3: Parallelized Diagnostic Workflows**  
- **Targeted Queue**: ECG Test (delays from lab result handovers).  
- **Root Cause**: Tests wait for lab results before starting; lab backlog causes 15-minute delays.  
- **Data Support**: 70% of ECG queues involve waiting for results; process mining shows lab processing time varies by 30%.  
- **Impact**: Integrate real-time result alerts to nurses, enabling immediate test initiation post-assessment, reducing wait by 25%.  

---

### 4. Trade-offs and Constraints

**Potential Trade-offs:**  
- **Resource Reallocation**: Shifting nurses may increase wait times for low-acuity patients if not balanced.  
- **Automation Costs**: Digital check-in requires upfront IT investment but reduces long-term labor costs.  
- **Care Quality Risk**: Rushing nurse assessments to cut waits could compromise accuracy; mitigate via training.  
- **Scheduling Rigidity**: Fixed slots may reduce flexibility for urgent cases; adopt hybrid scheduling (fixed + buffer slots).  

**Balancing Objectives:**  
Use *multi-objective optimization* (e.g., minimize wait time while capping cost increases at 5%). Prioritize strategies with dual benefits (e.g., automation reduces costs *and* wait times). Engage clinicians in trade-off decisions to align with care quality standards.  

---

### 5. Measuring Success

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**  
- **Average/Median Waiting Time**: Target 20% reduction in critical queues.  
- **90th Percentile Wait**: Ensure <30 minutes for 90% of patients.  
- **Queue Frequency**: Reduce excessive waits (>15 minutes) by 40%.  
- **Patient Satisfaction (NPS)**: Target 10-point increase in post-visit surveys.  

**Ongoing Monitoring:**  
Continuously update the event log to track KPIs. Use process mining dashboards to flag deviations (e.g., sudden wait spikes). Conduct quarterly audits to refine models and adapt strategies. For example, if ECG wait times rise post-implementation, re-analyze lab handover workflows.  

By systematically applying queue mining and process mining, the clinic can transition from reactive fixes to proactive, sustainable improvements in patient flow.
