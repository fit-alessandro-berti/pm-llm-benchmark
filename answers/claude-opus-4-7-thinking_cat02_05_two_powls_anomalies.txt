# Analysis of POWL Models for Hire-to-Retire Process

## Normative Process
The standard Hire-to-Retire flow is:
**Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case**

---

## Model 1 Analysis

**Structure:**
- Post  Screen
- Screen  Decide
- Screen  Interview
- Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close

**Anomalies:**
1. **Parallel Decide and Interview (severe):** After screening, `Make_Hiring_Decision` and `Conduct_Interviews` are unordered relative to each other. This means the hiring decision can be made *before* or *in parallel with* the interviews — fundamentally violating the logic that interviews inform the decision.
2. **Interview disconnected from downstream flow:** There's no ordering edge from `Interview` to `Onboard` or `Decide`, so Interview could even occur after onboarding begins in a partial-order execution.

---

## Model 2 Analysis

**Structure:**
- Post  Screen, Post  Interview (but Screen and Interview are unordered between themselves)
- Interview  Decide  LOOP(Onboard, skip)  XOR(Payroll, skip)  Close

**Anomalies:**
1. **Screen and Interview are parallel (moderate):** Screening doesn't have to precede interviewing. Candidates might be interviewed before (or in parallel with) being screened — unusual but not catastrophic.
2. **Loop around Onboarding (minor):** Onboarding can be repeated. This could arguably be realistic (multi-step onboarding), representing a mild deviation.
3. **Optional Payroll (severe):** The XOR allows skipping `Add_to_Payroll` entirely. An onboarded employee who is never added to payroll fundamentally breaks the Hire-to-Retire process — the employee can't be paid.
4. **No link from Screen to Decide/Interview:** Screening results don't feed into the decision path.

---

## Comparison and Verdict

| Aspect | Model 1 | Model 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Interview before Decision |  Not enforced |  Enforced |
| Screening before Interview |  Enforced |  Not enforced |
| Mandatory Payroll |  Yes |  Can be skipped |
| Onboarding executed once |  Yes |  Can loop/repeat |
| Ends with Close |  Yes |  Yes |

**Model 1 more closely aligns with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

### Justification
Although Model 1 has a serious flaw (Decide not being ordered after Interview), it preserves the **integrity of the end-to-end flow**: every hired employee is onboarded, added to payroll, and the case is closed — all mandatory steps occur exactly once in the correct downstream order.

Model 2, in contrast, introduces two structural defects that damage the *essence* of the process:
- **Payroll is optional** (XOR with skip), meaning a hired and onboarded employee may never be paid — this defeats the entire purpose of Hire-to-Retire.
- **Onboarding can loop arbitrarily**, allowing unbounded repetition with no clear exit criterion.
- Additionally, **Screening is no longer a prerequisite for Interviewing**, inverting a basic gating control.

Since Model 2's anomalies compromise mandatory financial/HR obligations (payroll) and basic screening controls, while Model 1's anomaly is limited to ordering between two sequential decision-making activities, **Model 1 is the closer fit to a normative Hire-to-Retire workflow**.
