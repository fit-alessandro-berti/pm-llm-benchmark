### 3. Explanations & Mitigation Strategies

#### Explanations for Performance Issues
1. **Iterative Document Collection (Rework):** The single biggest source of delay is not the complexity itself, but the **asynchronous, backandforth document collection**. Each iteration costs approximately one business day due to customer response time plus adjuster review time.
2. **Inconsistent Adjuster Practices:** Adjusters lack a standardized checklist for highcomplexity claims. Adjuster_Lisa required three rounds of requests, indicating she asked for documents reactively rather than exhaustively upfront.
3. **Customer Latency:** Requests sent midday (e.g., 11:30 AM) are often not answered until the next business day, inflating the clock.
4. **Finance Cutoff / Batch Processing:** The overnight payment delay in Case 2003 suggests Finance may run payment batches once daily or have a hard cutoff time.

#### Recommendations to Mitigate Root Causes

| Root Cause | Recommended Action |
|------------|--------------------|
| **Iterative Requests** | **Implement complexitybased intake checklists.** For High/Medium claims, the adjuster must identify *all* probable required documents during the initial “Evaluate Claim” step and issue a **single comprehensive request**. |
| **Adjuster Variability** | **Peer coaching & QA:** Pair Adjuster_Lisa with Adjuster_Mike to review highcomplexity intake protocols. Add a manager signoff on the first document request to ensure completeness. |
| **Customer Response Delays** | **Shift to proactive communication:** Instead of email/letter requests, use a digital customer portal with an **instant upload link** and an automated checklist. Offer a 15minute phone walkthrough for Highcomplexity claims immediately after submission. |
| **Regional Disparity** | **Region B process audit:** Investigate whether Region B adjusters lack authority, tools, or time to conduct thorough initial evaluations. Consider centralizing the initial highcomplexity evaluation to a specialized team (swim lane) rather than generalist adjusters. |
| **Payment Bottleneck** | **Sameday payment SLA:** For claims approved before 3:00 PM, payment should be initiated the same day. If Finance_Alan is constrained by batch jobs, transition his queue to realtime ACH processing or assign a backup processor. |
| **Monitor KPIs** | Track **“Requests per Case”** and **“Request Completeness Rate”** by adjuster and by region. A target of 1.0 requests for Medium claims and 1.5 for High claims would eliminate most of the current multiday delays. |
