Below is a case-by-case analysis against the normative description, followed by a summary of violations.

---

### **Case 1**  
**Sequence:**  
Receive  Check inventory  Authorize payment  Package items  Ship order  Close order  

**Assessment:**  
- Starts with “Receive order.”   
- “Check inventory” occurs before any payment or fulfillment activities.   
- After sufficient inventory (implied by path), payment is authorized before packaging.   
- Packaging occurs before shipping.   
- Shipping occurs only after packaging (no cancellation).   
- “Close order” is the final activity.   

**Conclusion:** **No violation.** This follows the expected “happy path.”

---

### **Case 2**  
**Sequence:**  
Receive  Check inventory  Package items  Authorize payment  Ship order  Close order  

**Assessment:**  
- Starts correctly.   
- “Check inventory” occurs early, which is required.   
- **Violation:** “Package items” occurs **before** “Authorize payment.”  
  - Normative rule: “Authorize payment” should occur after inventory check and **before** packaging, because payment authorization should precede preparing items for shipment.  
- After that, shipping occurs, then close.  

**Conclusion:** **Violation.** Packaging before payment authorization contradicts the process logic described.

---

### **Case 3**  
**Sequence:**  
Receive  Check inventory  Authorize payment  Cancel order  Ship order  Close order  

**Assessment:**  
- Starts correctly.   
- “Check inventory” occurs early.   
- Payment authorization occurs after inventory check (possible, though questionable if cancellation follows).  
- **Major violation 1:** “Cancel order” occurs **after** payment authorization.  
  - If inventory was insufficient, payment should not have been authorized.  
  - If inventory was sufficient, cancellation should not occur (unless an exception, but normative description does not allow cancellation in sufficient-inventory path).  
- **Major violation 2:** “Ship order” occurs **after cancellation**.  
  - Normative rule explicitly: “Ship order should never occur after a cancellation.”  
- “Close order” is last, but the presence of shipping after cancel makes this nonsensical.  

**Conclusion:** **Multiple violations:**  
1. Authorizing payment then canceling suggests either a process error or an illogical sequence.  
2. Shipping after cancellation is strictly forbidden.  

---

### **Case 4**  
**Sequence:**  
Receive  Check inventory  Cancel order  Close order  

**Assessment:**  
- Starts with “Receive.”   
- “Check inventory” occurs before cancellation.   
- Cancellation follows inventory check (presumably due to insufficient stock).   
- No fulfillment activities (packaging, shipping) after cancellation.   
- “Close order” is final.   

**Conclusion:** **No violation.** This matches the insufficient-inventory cancellation path.

---

### **Case 5**  
**Sequence:**  
Receive  Check inventory  Authorize payment  Ship order  Close order  

**Assessment:**  
- Starts correctly.   
- “Check inventory” occurs before payment authorization.   
- Payment authorized, but **missing “Package items”** before shipping.  
  - Normative rule: “Package items” should occur after authorization and before shipping.  
- Shipping occurs without packaging step.  
- “Close order” is final.  

**Conclusion:** **Violation.** Skipping packaging before shipping contradicts the described process.

---

### **Summary of Violations**

| Case | Violation(s) |
|------|--------------|
| 2    | Packaging items before authorizing payment (sequence rule). |
| 3    | (1) Authorizing payment then canceling (contradicts intent of payment only when fulfilling). (2) Shipping after cancellation (strictly forbidden). |
| 5    | Shipping without packaging items in between (missing required step). |

**Cases 1 and 4** comply with the normative description.
