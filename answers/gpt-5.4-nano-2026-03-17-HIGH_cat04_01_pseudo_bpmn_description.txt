## 1) Overall purpose of the process
This process models how a company handles **customer orders/requests** that can be either:

- **Standard** (known, validated process)
- **Custom** (requires feasibility evaluation and tailored quotation)

After the request is handled (validated/assessed and preparation is done), the process may require **manager approval**. If approval is denied, the workflow loops back to rework the relevant parts before generating the final invoice and confirming with the customer.

---

## 2) Start and initial intake

### **Start Event**
Marks the beginning when a customer request enters the system.

### **Task A: “Receive Customer Request”**
**Purpose:** Capture the incoming request data (order details, product/service type, quantities, any special requirements, etc.) so it can be processed.

---

## 3) Request type decision (XOR gateway)

### **Gateway (XOR): Check Request Type**
**Meaning of XOR:** Exactly **one** branch is taken based on a condition.

It decides whether the request is:
- **Standard**, or
- **Custom**

---

## 4) Standard path (when Type = Standard)

### **Task B1: “Perform Standard Validation”**
**Purpose:** Check that required fields are present, terms are met, and the request fits the “standard catalog/service” rules.

### **Gateway (AND): “Run Parallel Checks”**
**Meaning of AND split:** Branches run **in parallel** (simultaneously), because multiple independent checks must be completed.

#### Parallel tasks:
- **Task C1: “Credit Check”**
  - **Purpose:** Verify the customer’s creditworthiness / payment eligibility.
- **Task C2: “Inventory Check”**
  - **Purpose:** Confirm stock availability or delivery capacity.

### **All Parallel Checks Completed (Join)**
**Meaning of AND join:** The workflow waits until **both** C1 and C2 finish before continuing.

### **Task D: “Calculate Delivery Date”**
**Purpose:** Use outcomes of validation + credit/inventory results to determine the feasible delivery schedule.

**Result of the standard path:** You have a basis to quote/fulfill the order (here represented by the ability to proceed to invoice generation after approval logic).

---

## 5) Custom path (when Type = Custom)

### **Task B2: “Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis”**
**Purpose:** Determine whether the custom requirements can realistically be fulfilled (technical feasibility, capacity, lead times, complexity, constraints, costs, etc.).

### **Gateway (XOR): “Is Customization Feasible?”**
**Meaning of XOR:** Choose exactly one outcome.

#### If **Yes**:
- **Task E1: “Prepare Custom Quotation”**
  - **Purpose:** Create a proposal/quotation reflecting the custom scope (price, terms, delivery expectations, options).

#### If **No**:
- **Task E2: “Send Rejection Notice”**
  - **Purpose:** Inform the customer that the customization cannot be fulfilled as requested.
- **End Event**
  - The process terminates here—there is no invoice or confirmation because the request is rejected.

**Result of the custom path:** Either a custom quotation is prepared (feasible), or the process ends with rejection (not feasible).

---

## 6) Convergence before approval (after standard or custom work)

### **“After Standard or Custom Path Tasks Completed”**
This indicates a convergence point: the process proceeds to approval once the relevant preparation is done:

- Standard path contributes the completion of **Task D**.
- Custom feasible path contributes the completion of **Task E1**.
- Custom infeasible path ends at **Task E2**, so it does **not** reach approval.

---

## 7) Approval requirement decision (XOR gateway)

### **Gateway (XOR): “Is Approval Needed?”**
**Meaning of XOR:** Choose one branch based on business rules.

**Typical conditions that might make approval needed** (not explicitly stated, but implied by the model):
- high order value
- special exceptions
- risky credit results
- inventory constraints
- complex customization
- unusual delivery dates
- policy thresholds requiring managerial oversight

#### If **Yes**:
- **Task F: “Obtain Manager Approval”**
  - **Purpose:** Route the case for review and decision.

#### If **No**:
- Skip directly to invoice generation:
  - **Task G: “Generate Final Invoice”**

---

## 8) Approval outcome and possible rework (nested XOR)

### **Gateway (XOR): “Is Approval Granted?”**
**Meaning of XOR:** Exactly one outcome.

#### If **Yes**:
- **Task G: “Generate Final Invoice”**
  - **Purpose:** Create billing documents for the approved terms.

#### If **No**:
- **Task H: “Re-evaluate Conditions”**
  - **Purpose:** Perform corrective work to address why approval was denied (e.g., revise quotation assumptions, refine feasibility, adjust delivery date logic, propose alternatives, renegotiate terms).
- **Loop back** to earlier preparation logic:
  - **“Loop back to Task E1 (for Custom Path) or Task D (for Standard Path)”**

**Key point about the loop logic:**
- If the workflow is currently on the **custom** track (meaning it reached approval after **E1**), then denial triggers rework that returns to **E1** to update/rebuild the custom quotation.
- If it is on the **standard** track (meaning it reached approval after **D**), denial triggers rework that returns to **D** to redo delivery-date calculation (or underlying assumptions) before proceeding again to approval/invoice steps.

This prevents re-running the entire process from the beginning and instead targets the part most likely related to the approval rejection.

---

## 9) Final customer communication

### **Task I: “Send Confirmation to Customer”**
**Purpose:** Notify the customer that the invoice/offer is ready and confirm the outcome (approved invoice, delivery terms, etc.).

### **End Event**
The overall process ends after customer confirmation.

---

## 10) Summary: how tasks differ by Standard vs Custom, and when loops/approvals trigger

### How tasks differ
**Standard request:**
- Uses **standard validation** (B1)
- Runs **credit + inventory checks in parallel** (C1, C2)
- Then **calculates delivery date** (D)

**Custom request:**
- Uses **feasibility analysis** (B2)
- Then either:
  - prepares a **custom quotation** (E1) if feasible, or
  - **rejects the request** (E2) if not feasible, ending the process

### When approval is triggered
Approval is triggered when the process reaches:
- **Gateway: “Is Approval Needed?”**
and the condition evaluates to **Yes**  **Task F** (manager approval).

### When rework occurs
Rework happens when:
- approval is needed (**Yes**), and
- manager approval is **denied** (**Approval Granted? = No**)  **Task H**, then loop back:
  - to **E1** for custom rework, or
  - to **D** for standard rework.

### When paths converge
- The **standard** path converges after **Task D**
- The **custom feasible** path converges after **Task E1**
- The **custom infeasible** path does not converge; it ends at **Task E2**
