# Process Mining Analysis Plan for Speedy Parcels

As a Process Mining Consultant, I have analyzed the operational data architecture and event log structure provided for Speedy Parcels. The objective is to transform raw event logs into actionable intelligence to improve delivery punctuality and reduce costs. Below is the comprehensive approach based on process mining principles.

---

### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To build a cohesive event log, we must normalize the disparate data sources (GPS, Scanner, Dispatch, Maintenance) into a unified schema.
*   **Unified Case Instances:** While the snippet uses "Vehicle-Day" as the Case ID, for true process mining of *delivery performance*, it is often more granular to use **Package ID** or **Shift Instance**. Given the goal is fleet efficiency and cost, I recommend creating two parallel logs:
    1.  **Fleet Log (Shift Level):** Uses `Vehicle-Day` as the Case ID. This captures driver shifts, total route duration, fuel consumption, and maintenance events for the entire day's operation.
    2.  **Delivery Log (Package Level):** Uses `Package ID`. This captures the specific end-to-end flow of a single parcel from dispatch to delivery.
*   **Event Normalization:**
    *   **GPS Data:** Clean "noise" (jitter) in coordinates and timestamps. Standardize speed units and filter out stationary events that aren't actually idling.
    *   **Scanner Data:** Map scanner events ('Arrive', 'Success') to the nearest GPS timestamp if the scanner is offline, ensuring temporal continuity between movement and service time.
    *   **Dispatch Data:** Structure planned routes as a defined sequence of activities (e.g., `Start Depot` -> `Stop 1` -> `Stop 2`).
    *   **Maintenance Logs:** Link unscheduled repairs to specific Vehicle IDs and timestamps, flagging them as "Exceptions" in the process log.

**Challenges:**
*   **Missing Events:** Drivers may forget to scan a package or GPS trackers may fail. We must use probabilistic algorithms (e.g., filling gaps with travel time estimation) to avoid breaking the process flow.
*   **Time Zone/Shift Boundaries:** Ensuring consistent "Start" and "End" times across different drivers' shifts for accurate duration calculations.

**Process Discovery Visualization**
Using an **Alpha Algorithm**, we will generate a visual process model of the *actual* delivery process.
*   **The Model:** This will show nodes like `Depart Depot`, `Drive to Stop`, `Service Time`, `Deliver Failed`, `Unscheduled Maintenance`, and `Return to Depot`.
*   **Flows:** The discovery algorithm will reveal loops (e.g., a loop around `Delivery Failed` -> `Return to Vehicle` -> `Retry Delivery`) which indicate rework or inefficiency. It will also show "dead ends" where vehicles get stuck waiting for customers or maintenance.

**Conformance Checking**
We will compare the **Discovered Process Model (As-Is)** against the **Dispatch System Routes (To-Be/Planned)**.
*   **Method:** We use a conformance checker to measure the deviation percentage between planned and actual sequences.
*   **Key Deviations to Identify:**
    1.  **Sequence Deviation:** Did the driver visit Stop B before Stop A when the plan was A then B? This indicates route planning errors or dynamic traffic avoidance (which needs validation).
    2.  **Unplanned Stops:** Any stop activity not in the planned route (e.g., `Unscheduled Maintenance` or `Driver Break`).
    3.  **Timing Variance:** Compare the *Planned ETA* for each stop against the *Actual Arrival Time*. Significant variance indicates inaccurate travel time estimation.
    4.  **Service Time Deviation:** Compare planned dwell times (e.g., 5 mins) vs. actual service time (e.g., 20 mins).

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
To align with Speedy Parcels' goals, we will calculate the following KPIs from the event log:

| KPI Category | Specific Metric | Calculation Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Punctuality** | On-Time Delivery Rate (OTD) | `(Successful Deliveries on Time / Total Deliveries)` |
| **Efficiency** | Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio | `(Total Travel Time / Total Service Time)` |
| **Cost** | Fuel Consumption per km/package | `Fuel Log Data` mapped to distance traveled |
| **Reliability** | Vehicle Utilization Rate | `(Time Moving / Total Shift Duration)` |
| **Quality** | Failed Delivery Rate | `(Failed Deliveries / Total Deliveries)` |
| **Delay** | Average Delay per Stop | `(Actual Arrival - Planned ETA)` |

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
Process mining allows us to move beyond "what happened" to "where did it get stuck."
*   **Activity Duration Analysis:** We will analyze the duration of `Drive` vs. `Service`. If `Service` time is consistently long (e.g., >15 mins), the bottleneck is customer interaction or finding parking.
*   **Throughput Analysis:** Identify vehicles/days where throughput (deliveries per hour) drops significantly compared to the fleet average.
*   **Contextual Correlation:** We will correlate delays with:
    *   **Location:** Are specific zones (e.g., 50.7N, 6.1E) consistently causing traffic jams?
    *   **Time of Day:** Are peak hours (9:00 AM - 11:00 AM) associated with higher failure rates?
    *   **Drivers:** Do specific drivers have lower OTD rates?

**Quantifying Impact:**
*   We will use **Pareto Analysis** to identify the top 20% of causes (e.g., traffic in Zone A, or Driver D105's breakdowns) responsible for 80% of delays.
*   We will calculate the **Cost of Deviation**: If a planned stop is delayed by 30 minutes, how much extra fuel was consumed? This quantifies the financial impact of process deviations.

### 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

Process mining helps us validate hypotheses about *why* bottlenecks exist by analyzing patterns in the data.

**Potential Root Causes & Validation:**
1.  **Suboptimal Route Planning (Static vs. Dynamic):**
    *   *Hypothesis:* The dispatch system does not account for peak traffic or customer time windows, leading to tight schedules.
    *   *Validation:* Compare `Planned ETA` vs. `Actual Arrival`. If the variance is consistently >15 minutes during specific hours, the root cause is inaccurate travel time estimation in the planning tool.

2.  **High Variability in Service Time:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Drivers are taking too long to find parking or interact with customers (e.g., waiting for keys).
    *   *Validation:* Analyze `Service Duration` histograms. If there is a wide variance (5 mins vs 40 mins), we can use **Variant Analysis** to compare the "Fastest" driver against the "Slowest" driver to see if skill or behavior drives the difference.

3.  **Vehicle Breakdowns & Maintenance:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Reactive maintenance is causing unplanned downtime.
    *   *Validation:* Cross-reference `Unscheduled Stop` events with `Vehicle Speed` and `Ignition On/Off`. If breakdowns occur frequently after a specific mileage or idle time threshold, this suggests a need for predictive maintenance rather than reactive.

4.  **Failed Deliveries (Customer Not Home):**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Poor communication leads to retries, consuming fuel and time.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze the sequence `Arrive Customer` -> `Delivery Failed`. Look at the `Depart Customer` timestamp. If it takes significantly longer than a successful delivery, the root cause is likely the "Return to Vehicle" leg being inefficient or the need for a second attempt later in the day (causing fatigue).

5.  **Driver Behavior:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Aggressive driving increases wear and tear and delays.
    *   *Validation:* Use GPS speed data to correlate with `Vehicle Maintenance` logs. If high-speed events (>80km/h) correlate with engine warning lights, this indicates a safety and efficiency risk.

### 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

Based on the analysis above, here are three concrete strategies:

#### Strategy A: Dynamic Routing & Time Window Adjustment
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Late deliveries due to inaccurate travel time estimations and static routes.
*   **Root Cause:** Dispatch system does not account for historical traffic patterns (e.g., congestion at 50.7N, 6.1E) or customer time windows.
*   **Process Mining Support:** The conformance check reveals that vehicles consistently arrive 20 minutes late during the morning peak. Variant analysis shows specific "High Risk Zones."
*   **Action:** Implement a **Dynamic Routing Algorithm** that adjusts vehicle paths in real-time based on historical traffic data derived from the logs. Shift time windows for high-risk zones to allow buffer time.
*   **Expected Impact:** Increase On-Time Delivery Rate (OTD) by 10-15%; reduce fuel consumption due to optimized route efficiency.

#### Strategy B: Predictive Maintenance & Fleet Utilization
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Unscheduled stops and vehicle downtime reducing capacity.
*   **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance (waiting for warning lights) leads to extended delays.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Correlation analysis reveals a pattern where vehicles idle for >30 mins before triggering an `Engine Warning`. This indicates "wear-out" rather than random failure.
*   **Action:** Switch from reactive to **Predictive Maintenance**. Schedule maintenance based on accumulated mileage/idle time patterns identified in the logs, even if the vehicle is running well.
*   **Expected Impact:** Reduce unplanned downtime by 30%; improve Vehicle Utilization Rate; lower repair costs over time.

#### Strategy C: Driver Performance & Skill-Based Training
*   **Target Inefficiency:** High variability in service time and failed delivery rates among drivers.
*   **Root Cause:** Lack of standard operating procedures (SOPs) or skill gaps regarding customer interaction and parking management.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Variant analysis comparing `Driver D105` (High Performer) vs. `Driver D204` (Low Performer) reveals D105 has 40% shorter service times. D204 has a higher frequency of "Customer Not Home" events, suggesting poor communication timing.
*   **Action:** Develop **Targeted Training Programs** based on the specific gaps identified (e.g., training D204 on communication protocols). Implement a KPI-based incentive scheme linked to OTD and Service Time metrics.
*   **Expected Impact:** Reduce failed delivery rates by 15%; standardize service time across the fleet; improve driver retention.

---

### 5. Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints**
Any optimization strategy must respect operational limits:
*   **Driver Hours:** Strategies like dynamic routing must ensure drivers do not exceed legal driving hours or rest breaks. The process model must include a `Rest Break` activity to prevent fatigue-related errors.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** Route adjustments must ensure no vehicle exceeds its package capacity limit (`Max Packages`). We will use the Dispatch data to verify this constraint in the mining models.
*   **Customer Time Windows:** Optimization cannot promise delivery times that are physically impossible given the traffic patterns identified in the logs.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan**
After implementation, we will deploy a **Process Mining Dashboard** for real-time and historical monitoring.
*   **Real-Time Monitoring:** Track deviations from the "Golden Path" (the optimized process model). If a vehicle deviates significantly from its planned route or service time, trigger an alert to the dispatcher immediately.
*   **Key Metrics to Track:**
    1.  **Process Variance:** The gap between Planned and Actual cycle times.
    2.  **Bottleneck Frequency:** How often do vehicles hit specific "slow zones"?
    3.  **Cost Per Stop:** Monitoring fuel and maintenance costs per delivery event.
*   **Feedback Loop:** Establish a monthly review cycle where the Process Mining team re-runs discovery algorithms on the new data to ensure the optimized process is stable and to identify any *new* emerging bottlenecks (e.g., a new traffic jam in a previously unaffected area).

By following this structured approach, Speedy Parcels can move from reactive problem-solving to proactive process optimization, directly linking their event logs to cost savings and service quality improvements.
