  - Identify overutilized machines (e.g., if a machine’s utilization exceeds 85% consistently) and their impact on downstream queues. For example, if MILL-02 frequently breaks down, its unavailability propagates delays.
- **Poor Prioritization**:
  - Analyze **late jobs** (those with tardiness > 0 days). If high-priority jobs (e.g., JOB-7005) have longer flow times than lower-priority jobs, prioritization rules are flawed.
  - Compare on-time vs. late jobs using **conformance analysis** to identify common paths (e.g., late jobs often include a critical machine as their last step).
- **Suboptimal Sequencing**:
  - If setup times for certain job sequences are consistently longer (e.g., setups after JOB-6998 take 20+ minutes), poor sequencing increases delays. Process mining can highlight these patterns.
- **Starvation and Bullwhip Effects**:
  - Use **resource contention analysis** to find machines that starve (idle due to lack of work) while others are overloaded. For example, if a downstream machine’s queue is empty despite upstream activity, there’s poor coordination.
  - **Cumulative WIP charts** by work center can reveal bullwhip: large fluctuations in WIP levels at certain stations due to scheduling variability.
