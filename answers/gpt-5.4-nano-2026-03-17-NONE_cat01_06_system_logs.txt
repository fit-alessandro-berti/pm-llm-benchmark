## Brief Explanation of Case & Activity Logic

**Case identification (grouping):**
- I treated a **case** as a coherent *work session on a specific primary artifact* (document/email/spreadsheet/pdf) based on contiguous sequences of events and obvious “start  work  save/send  close” behavior.
- When the user clearly switched to a different main artifact (Word doc vs Chrome email vs Excel vs Acrobat vs another Word report), I opened a new case:
  - `CASE-001`: editing **Document1.docx** followed by **email reply**.
  - `CASE-003`: editing and saving **Budget_2024.xlsx**.
  - `CASE-002`: subsequent update of **Document1.docx** with a budget reference, then saving/closing.
  - `CASE-004`: **PDF review** in Adobe Acrobat.
  - `CASE-005`: drafting/saving/closing **Quarterly_Report.docx**.

*(There are multiple plausible ways to group these into fewer/more cases; this choice yields a story analysts can interpret: document drafting  email  budget update  reference insertion  report writing.)*

**Activity naming (standardization):**
- Low-level actions like `TYPING`, `SAVE`, `CLICK`, `CLOSE`, `HIGHLIGHT` were mapped to higher-level activities:
  - `Edit Document: Drafting ...`
  - `Save Document Changes ...`
  - `Email: Compose/Reply ...`, `Email: Send ...`
  - `Update Spreadsheet ...`, `Save Spreadsheet Changes ...`
  - `Review PDF ...`
  - `Edit Report Document ...`, `Close ...`
- Activity names include the **artifact type and filename** to support grouping and filtering in process mining (e.g., compare all “Save …” steps).
