### **Identification of Bias**
The log for **Group B (Unprotected Group)** exhibits algorithmic bias. While Group A is treated neutrally based on raw scores alone, Group B undergoes systematic manipulation of its evaluation metrics.

### **Manifestation of Bias**
The bias manifests explicitly through the **`ScoreAdjustment`** column and the influence of demographic attributes (`LocalResident`, `CommunityGroup`).

1.  **Systematic Score Manipulation:**
    *   **Group A (Protected):** The `ScoreAdjustment` is consistently **0** or **N/A**. Decisions are made strictly based on the raw `PreliminaryScore`.
    *   **Group B (Unprotected):** In specific cases (U001 and U003), the system applies a **+10 adjustment** labeled as "Community Boost." This occurs only when the applicant belongs to the **"Highland Civic Darts Club"** or is marked as a **LocalResident**.

2.  **Influence of Attributes:**
    *   **`CommunityGroup`:** The presence of a specific community affiliation in Group B triggers a positive adjustment. In Group A, where `CommunityGroup` is "None," no adjustment occurs. This indicates the system is using group membership as a feature to alter outcomes rather than evaluating solely on merit (the raw score).
    *   **`LocalResident`:** All entries in Group B are marked as `TRUE`, whereas all entries in Group A are `FALSE`. The correlation between being a local resident and receiving a boost suggests the system is discriminating based on residency status.

### **Systematic Differences in Final Decisions**
This adjustment mechanism creates disparate outcomes for applicants with similar raw qualifications:

*   **Case P001 vs. U001:** Both applicants have a raw score of **720**.
    *   **Group A (P001):** Approved at 720.
    *   **Group B (U001):** Adjusted to **730** and Approved.
    *   *Analysis:* While both were approved, Group B received an artificial advantage that increased their effective score by 10 points solely due to group membership.

*   **Case P002 vs. U002:** Both applicants have a raw score of **710**.
    *   **Group A (P002):** Rejected at 710.
    *   **Group B (U002):** Rejected at 710 (No boost applied because `CommunityGroup` is None).
    *   *Analysis:* Here, the lack of a community affiliation in Group B resulted in a rejection that mirrored Group A's stricter threshold.

*   **Case P003 vs. U003:**
    *   **Group A (P003):** Raw score **740** -> Approved.
    *   **Group B (U003):** Raw score **695** -> Boosted to **705** -> Approved.
    *   *Analysis:* This is the most significant evidence of bias. A Group B applicant with a raw score **45 points lower** than a Group A applicant was approved due to the adjustment. This lowers the effective threshold for approval for Group B members, creating an unequal playing field.

### **Conclusion**
The system exhibits **composition bias** in Group B. By introducing a `ScoreAdjustment` based on sensitive attributes like `CommunityGroup` and `LocalResident`, the algorithm creates an unfair advantage for specific subgroups. This violates the principle of neutrality, as the final decision is no longer solely determined by the applicant's raw performance (`PreliminaryScore`) but is artificially inflated based on their social or community affiliation.
