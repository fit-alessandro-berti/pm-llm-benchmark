## Notes on the Design

**Key modeling choices:**

1. **Monotonic accumulation:** Times from any activity to a later one increase as more intermediate steps are spanned (e.g., `('SS', 'OP')` < `('SS', 'RC')` < ... < `('SS', 'AS')`).

2. **High-variance segments:** Procurement-related pairs (anything involving `SS`/`OP` reaching `RC`) carry **large standard deviations** because supplier lead times vary widely across global suppliers.

3. **Tight-variance segments:** In-house operations like `('PT', 'PK')` and `('PK', 'WS')` have **small standard deviations**, reflecting controlled, predictable internal processes.

4. **After-Sales explosion:** Any pair ending in `AS` has very large averages and deviations, since support requests depend on customer behavior over the product's lifetime.

5. **Variable storage:** `('WS', 'DT')` has a notably high standard deviation relative to its mean, capturing the unpredictability of how long products sit before being shipped.

**Usage with ZETA:** For a pair like `('OP', 'RC')` with `(1036800, 302400)` and `ZETA = 2`, the acceptable window would be:
- Lower bound: `1036800 - 2 × 302400 = 432000` (~5 days)
- Upper bound: `1036800 + 2 × 302400 = 1641600` (~19 days)

Any observed delay outside this range would be flagged as a temporal deviation.
