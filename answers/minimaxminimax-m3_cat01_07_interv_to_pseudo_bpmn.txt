# BPMN-Style Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## 1. Process Overview

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Process Name** | Invoice Approval & Payment Scheduling |
| **Trigger** | Invoice received from supplier (typically via email) |
| **End State** | Invoice scheduled for payment OR invoice rejected/returned |
| **Primary Goal** | Verify, match, approve, and schedule supplier invoices for payment |
| **Scope** | From invoice receipt to inclusion in payment run |

| Lane | Role | Key Actor(s) |
|------|------|--------------|
| **Supplier** (external) | External entity | Supplier representatives |

| **Purchasing Officer** | Goods/services receipt & PO verification | Dan |
| **Accounts Payable Supervisor** | Exception handler | Rita |
| **Department Manager** | Final approver | Carlos |
| **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM)** | Supplier liaison | Karen |

## 4. Process Flow (Happy Path & Exceptions)

Below is the consolidated process flow with gateways, using BPMN-like notation. **Step  Gateway  Step** sequences are presented linearly; lateral references indicate loops.

---

### **PHASE A — INTAKE & PO VALIDATION**

**A1.** `[Start] Invoice Received` (by email)
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk (Lane: Accounts Payable)
   - **Input:** Invoice
   - **Output:** Invoice (in inbox)

**A2.** `Task: Check Invoice for Valid PO Number`
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
   - **Notes:** Manual/system check for PO reference

** Gateway G1: Does the invoice have a valid PO number?** *(XOR – exclusive)*
   - **G1a — No PO (or invalid PO):**
     - **A3.** `Task: Send Invoice Back to Supplier Requesting Details`
       - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
       - **Optional involvement:** SRM (Karen) may be looped in to contact supplier
     - ** Loop back to A1** (process restarts when a corrected/revised invoice is received)
     - *Note: This path may also terminate if the invoice is rejected outright (see Phase E).*
   - **G1b — Valid PO exists:**
     - Proceed to A4

**A4.** `Task: Log Invoice into Accounting System`
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
   - **Output:** System Log Entry created; invoice status = "Logged"

---

### **PHASE B — PURCHASING VERIFICATION**

**A5.** `Task: Forward Invoice to Purchasing Department`
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
   - **Recipient:** Purchasing Officer (Dan)

**A6.** `Task: Verify Goods/Services Receipt Against PO`
   - **Performed by:** Purchasing Officer
   - **Sub-checks:** Compare quantities, prices, delivery charges, line items

** Gateway G2: Is the PO number still missing or details insufficient?** *(XOR)*
   - **G2a — Yes (missing info):**
     - **A7.** `Task: Request PO Information from AP Clerk`
       - **Performed by:** Purchasing Officer  AP Clerk
     - ** Loop back to A1** (re-entry upon supplier providing PO)
   - **G2b — No (PO present, proceed):**
     - Proceed to A8

** Gateway G3: Do invoice details match the PO (quantities, prices, charges)?** *(XOR)*
   - **G3a — No (discrepancy detected):**
     - **A8.** `Task: Initiate Discrepancy Resolution`
       - **Performed by:** Purchasing Officer
       - **Sub-paths (parallel or sequential, depending on case):**
         - **A8.1** `Contact Supplier Directly` *(Purchasing Officer)*
         - **A8.2** `Ask AP Clerk to Hold Invoice Pending Corrected Version` *(Purchasing Officer  AP Clerk)*
         - **A8.3** *(Optional)* `Loop in Supplier Relationship Manager` *(Karen)* to mediate or verify contractual pricing
     - ** Loop back to A6** (re-verify once corrected invoice or clarification arrives)
   - **G3b — Yes (all match):**
     - Proceed to A9

**A9.** `Task: Send Confirmation Email to AP`
   - **Performed by:** Purchasing Officer
   - **Output:** Receipt Confirmation data object
   - **Recipient:** AP Clerk

---

### **PHASE C — THREE-WAY MATCH (INVOICE  PO  RECEIPT)**

**A10.** `Task: Match Invoice Line Items Against PO in System`
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
   - **Inputs:** Invoice, PO, Receipt Confirmation
   - **Notes:** This is the "three-way match" step

** Gateway G4: Does the three-way match succeed?** *(XOR)*
   - **G4a — No (discrepancy persists, e.g., incorrect pricing, missing items):**
     - **A11.** `Task: Email Purchasing Team to Clarify Discrepancy`
       - **Performed by:** AP Clerk  Purchasing Officer
     - ** Loop back to A6** (Purchasing re-verifies)
     - **If unresolved after retry:**
       - **A12.** `Task: Escalate to AP Supervisor`
         - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
         - **Recipient:** AP Supervisor (Rita)
       - **Proceed to Phase D — Exception Handling**
   - **G4b — Yes (match confirmed):**
     - Proceed to A13

---

### **PHASE D — EXCEPTION HANDLING (Rita's Lane)**

*Triggered by:* Discrepancies that AP Clerk and Purchasing cannot resolve, OR invoices lacking any PO.

**A13.** `Task: Review and Investigate Exception`
   - **Performed by:** AP Supervisor
   - **Activities:**
     - Contact supplier (directly or via SRM)
     - Coordinate between AP, Purchasing, and requesting department
     - Verify whether invoice corresponds to informally contracted services

** Gateway G5: Can the exception be resolved?** *(XOR)*
   - **G5a — No (e.g., legitimate billing dispute, no contract basis):**
     - **A14.** `Task: Reject Invoice`
       - **Performed by:** AP Supervisor (in coordination with department head)
       - ** End Event: Invoice Rejected**
   - **G5b — Yes, by creating a Retrospective PO:**
     - **A15.** `Task: Create Retrospective PO`
       - **Performed by:** AP Supervisor + Department Head
       - **Output:** Retrospective PO data object
   - **G5c — Yes, by other means (supplier correction, contract confirmation):**
     - Corrected data flows back into standard process

**A16.** `Task: Return Resolved Invoice to Normal Flow`
   - ** Rejoin at A6 or A10** (re-verification by Purchasing / re-matching by AP)

---

### **PHASE E — MANAGER APPROVAL**

**A17.** `Task: System Sends Approval Request to Department Manager`
   - **Performed by:** Accounting System (automated)
   - **Trigger:** Successful completion of three-way match (A10) AND Purchasing confirmation (A9)
   - **Recipient:** Department Manager (Carlos)
   - **Output:** Approval Notification

**A18.** `Task: Review Invoice, PO, and Confirmation Details`
   - **Performed by:** Department Manager
   - **Sub-checks:** Quantities, pricing, budget alignment, special notes

** Gateway G6: Does the invoice pass the manager's review?** *(XOR)*
   - **G6a — No (suspicious amount, unexpected items, budget concerns):**
     - **A19.** `Task: Reject or Hold Invoice for Further Investigation`
       - **Performed by:** Department Manager
       - **Recipients:** AP Clerk (Mary)
     - ** Loop back to Phase D (Exception Handling)** OR ** End Event: Invoice Rejected**
     - *Note: Carlos indicates this happens ~once every couple of months.*
   - **G6b — Yes (acceptable):**
     - Proceed to A20

**A20.** `Task: Approve Invoice in System`
   - **Performed by:** Department Manager
   - **Output:** System approval; invoice status = "Approved"

---

### **PHASE F — PAYMENT SCHEDULING**

**A21.** `Task: Schedule Invoice for Payment`
   - **Performed by:** AP Clerk
   - **Output:** Invoice added to next payment run / payment cycle

** End Event: Invoice Scheduled for Payment**

## 8. Key Process Characteristics

- **Complexity:** Medium — multiple decision points, three exception loops, and cross-functional handoffs
- **Cycle Time Drivers:** Three-way match verification, supplier responsiveness on discrepancies
- **Bottleneck Risk:** Discrepancy resolution (Phase B/C) and Manager availability (Phase E)
- **Control Mechanism:** Three-way match (Invoice + PO + Goods Receipt) is the principal financial control
- **Exception Rate:** Low for routine procurement; higher for informal/uncontracted services (Rita's domain)
