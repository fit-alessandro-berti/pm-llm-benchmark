### Possible Reasons for Anomalies

- **Systematic acceleration or manual overrides:**  
  The tight RP timing may come from an automated approval rule (e.g., small claims autoapproved after a fixed delay). The low standard deviation supports this.

- **Notification backlogs:**  
  The long PN gap could be due to a scheduled batch job (e.g., daily email run) or a manual notification step that is often delayed by adjuster workload.

- **Claim skipping / shortcut handling:**  
  The short AC and EC times indicate that claims may be closed right after assignment or evaluation, omitting approval and notification. This might happen when adjusters close claims as “duplicate” or “invalid” without following standard flow.

- **Automated evaluation  notification:**  
  The EN gap of 5 minutes is plausible for an automated notification system (e.g., system sends SMS/email instantly after evaluation), but it could also be a result of the notification being triggered before proper approval, if the process steps are not enforced in order.

- **Data quality or timestamp issues:**

#### 1. Identify claims with unusually short/long times between specified activities

```sql
-- Time from Receive to Approve (RP) for each claim
WITH rp AS (
    SELECT
        e1.claim_id,
        e1.timestamp AS r_time,
        e2.timestamp AS p_time,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (e2.timestamp - e1.timestamp)) AS seconds_diff
    FROM claim_events e1
    JOIN claim_events e2 ON e1.claim_id = e2.claim_id
    WHERE e1.activity = 'R'
      AND e2.activity = 'P'
)
SELECT claim_id, seconds_diff
FROM rp
WHERE seconds_diff < 3600*24          -- less than 1 day (too fast?)
   OR seconds_diff > 3600*24*2       -- more than 2 days (too slow?)
ORDER BY seconds_diff;
```

#### 3. Filter claims where approval to notification took excessively long and check claim amount/type

```sql
-- Approve  Notify delay per claim, with claim details
SELECT

    c.submission_date
```

#### 5. Identify if certain adjusters have a pattern of abnormal timings

```sql
-- For each adjuster, compute average and stddev of RP time
WITH adjuster_rp AS (
    SELECT
        e1.resource AS adjuster,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (e2.timestamp - e1.timestamp)) AS rp_seconds
    FROM claim_events e1
    JOIN claim_events e2 ON e1.claim_id = e2.claim_id
    WHERE e1.activity = 'R' AND e2.activity = 'P'
)
SELECT
    adjuster,
    AVG(rp_seconds) AS avg_rp,
    STDDEV(rp_seconds) AS std_rp,
    COUNT(*) AS claims
FROM adjuster_rp
GROUP BY adjuster
HAVING STDDEV(rp_seconds) < 3600   -- unusually low variation
ORDER BY avg_rp;
```
