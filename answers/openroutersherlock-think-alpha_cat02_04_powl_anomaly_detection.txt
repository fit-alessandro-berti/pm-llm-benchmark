### Identified Anomalies

The model deviates from a **standard Order-to-Cash (O2C) workflow**, which typically follows a strict sequence: `Receive_Order  Check_Credit  Approve_Order  Issue_Invoice  Receive_Payment  Close_Order`. No skips or loops are expected, as each step is mandatory and executed once to minimize risk and ensure compliance. The anomalies are:

1. **Optional Credit Check (XOR with Silent Skip)**:
   - The `xor_credit` node allows **skipping `Check_Credit` entirely** via a silent transition (`skip`), which produces no event log trace.
   - **Breaks expectation**: Credit checks are a critical gatekeeping step in O2C to assess customer solvency before proceeding.

2. **LOOP on Approval and Invoicing (`loop_approve_invoice`)**:
   - The LOOP operator permits **unbounded repetitions** of `Approve_Order  Issue_Invoice` (e.g., approve  invoice  re-approve  re-invoice  ...  payment).
   - **Breaks expectation**: Approvals and invoices should occur **exactly once** per order. Repeated approvals suggest indecision or rework (e.g., initial approval revoked), while multiple invoices per order are non-standard.

These are **structural anomalies** manifesting as **choices (XOR)** and **loops (LOOP)**, enabling traces that skip safeguards or repeat steps inefficiently.

1. **Skipping Credit Check**:
