### Rationale for Added Constraints

1.  **Existence (`BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
    *   **Why:** We enforce that every case must contain a specific "Bias Mitigation" step. This ensures that the process explicitly acknowledges fairness checks in every single application, rather than leaving it to chance.

2.  **Coexistence (`ManualReview` & `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
    *   **Why:** If a case requires a `ManualReview` (often a result of complex or flagged data), it should coexist with a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This links manual intervention with required fairness oversight.

3.  **Response & Succession (`CheckApplicantRace`/`Gender` -> `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
    *   **Why:** These constraints ensure that once sensitive attributes are accessed, the process is forced to perform a bias check. The `Succession` constraint specifically ensures the bias check eventually happens, while the `Response` ensures it is a valid follow-up option.

4.  **Chain Succession (`CheckApplicantRace` -> `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
    *   **Why:** This is the strictest constraint. It forces the bias check to happen *immediately* after checking the applicant's race. This prevents any other activity (like a quick reject) from occurring in the middle.

5.  **Non-Succession & Non-Chain Succession (`CheckApplicantRace` -> `Reject`/`Approve`)**:
    *   **Why:** This is the direct bias block. It strictly prohibits the decision (Reject or Approve) from occurring immediately after the sensitive attribute check. This forces the process to insert the `BiasMitigationCheck` (or other steps) in between, removing the correlation between "seeing race" and "immediate decision."
