This analysis provides a structured process mining framework to transform Speedy Parcels' raw event logs into actionable operational intelligence.

---

### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking
**Preprocessing and Integration:**
*   **The Challenge:** The primary hurdle is "Event Correlation." GPS data is high-frequency (continuous), while scanner data is discrete. I would use the `Case ID (Vehicle-Day)` as the primary join key.
*   **Data Cleaning:** I would implement a "State Transition" filter. For example, GPS pings at 5km/h over a specific coordinate cluster are aggregated into a single 'Traffic Delay' activity, while timestamps within a specific radius of a customer coordinate are mapped to 'Service Time'.
*   **Discovery:** Using algorithms like the *Inductive Miner*, I would visualize the "Happy Path" (Depot $\rightarrow$ Delivery $\rightarrow$ Depot). I would then layer "Performance Overlay" to visualize where time is spent (e.g., highlighting that 40% of the day is consumed by transitions between stops).

**Conformance Checking:**
*   **Deviation Analysis:** I would perform a "Petri Net" alignment against the planned route.
*   **Detection:** I would flag:
    *   **Sequence Deviations:** Stops performed in an order different from the dispatch plan.
    *   **Time Window Violations:** Arrivals occurring outside the customer-requested slot.
    *   **Structural Deviations:** Unscheduled maintenance stops or return trips to the depot that were not in the original plan.

---

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification
**KPI Definition:**
*   **Service Time Variability:** Standard deviation of time spent at a stop; helps identify drivers struggling with specific parcel types or locations.
*   **Travel-to-Service Ratio:** Total driving time divided by total time at customer locations. A high ratio indicates inefficient routing or urban congestion.
*   **First-Attempt Success Rate:** Percentage of `Delivery Success` vs. `Delivery Failed` per route.

**Bottleneck Identification:**
*   **Dwell Time Analysis:** I would use *Heuristics Miner* to identify "Hotspots." If specific geographic zones consistently show high "dwell times" (idle engine + high frequency of scanner events), I can isolate whether these are parking challenges or complex building access issues.
*   **Impact Quantification:** By calculating the "Throughput Time" of the entire fleet, I can isolate specific "time-sinks"—e.g., if traffic congestion at 16:00 is consistently causing 20% of late arrivals, that bottleneck represents a measurable cost in fuel and overtime.

---

### 3. Root Cause Analysis
*   **Variant Analysis:** I would cluster routes into "High Performers" and "Low Performers." By comparing the event logs of a top-tier driver to a struggling one in similar zones, I can pinpoint if delays are due to **route planning** (e.g., ignoring one-way streets) or **execution** (e.g., excessive dwell time at stops).
*   **Traffic Correlation:** By overlaying regional traffic data with our `Low Speed Detected` events, I can determine if the dispatcher is ignoring historical peak-hour congestion in their planning phase.
*   **Maintenance Correlation:** By correlating `Engine Warning Light` events with vehicle age and utilization, I can determine if maintenance is reactive (causing delivery failure) rather than preventive.

---

### 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies
| Strategy | Target Inefficiency | Root Cause | Expected Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dynamic Routing** | High travel-to-service ratio | Static, outdated routes | Reduction in km driven; lower fuel costs. |
| **Predictive Maintenance** | Unscheduled downtime | Reactive maintenance | Higher vehicle availability; fewer missed time windows. |
| **Time-Window Optimization** | High Failed Deliveries | Customer unavailability | Higher first-attempt success rate; lower re-delivery costs. |

*   **Logic:** For *Dynamic Routing*, if process mining shows that Driver A consistently skips stop 5 to do it later due to parking, the system should incorporate "Parking Availability" as a constraint in the next day's dispatch algorithm.

---

### 5. Operational Constraints and Monitoring
**Accounting for Constraints:**
*   The models will be constrained by **Hard Constraints** (driver mandatory rest periods, vehicle capacity limits) and **Soft Constraints** (customer preferred windows). The process mining model will act as a *Simulation Engine* to test if a new routing strategy violates these constraints before real-world implementation.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan:**
*   **Operational Dashboard:** I would implement a live *Process Cockpit* monitoring:
    *   **Live Conformance:** Alerting dispatchers when a vehicle drifts significantly from the planned path.
    *   **Delay Prediction:** Using historical process data to predict if a route will finish late, allowing for proactive customer notifications (reducing dissatisfaction).
    *   **Sustainability Tracking:** Monitoring fuel-per-package ratios to ensure cost-efficiency gains remain stable over time. 
*   **Feedback Loop:** Monthly review of "Process Drift" to update the dispatch logic, ensuring the system learns from actual performance rather than theoretical planning.
