Below are the main anomalies I see in the log compared with a “typical” Procure-to-Pay (P2P) flow, where the usual high-level order is:

**Create/approve requisition  Issue PO  Receive goods (or service)  Receive invoice  Match invoice to PO (and/or approve invoice)  Release/payment  Close case**

---

## 1) Case 1002: Invoice is received *before* requisition approval
**Sequence in Case 1002:**
- Create Purchase Requisition (2024-02-01 08:30)
- **Issue Purchase Order** (2024-02-01 08:40)
- **Vendor Invoice Received** (2024-02-03 07:00)
- Release Payment (2024-02-03 07:15)
- **Approve Purchase Requisition** (2024-02-03 08:00)   appears after payment
- Receive Goods (2024-02-04 09:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-05 08:00)
- Close Case (2024-02-06 16:00)

### Why this is anomalous/problematic
- In standard controls, the requisition is typically approved **before** PO issuance and certainly **before** payment.
- Here, **payment occurs before the requisition approval** is even recorded.
- That implies either:
  - the requisition was approved earlier but not logged,
  - or approvals/payments are happening out of control order.
- Business impact: creates **audit and compliance risk** (you may pay without having gone through approval governance).

---

## 2) Case 1002: Payment released immediately after invoice, but goods are received later (missing/late receipt-to-invoice linkage)
**Key sequence in Case 1002:**
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-03 07:00)
- Release Payment (2024-02-03 07:15)
- Receive Goods (2024-02-04 09:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-05 08:00)

### Why this is anomalous/problematic
- Common best practice: payment should follow (or at least be tightly controlled against) receiving and matching.
- Here, **goods are received after payment** and **invoice matching occurs after payment**.
- That can lead to paying for:
  - incorrect quantities,
  - unordered goods,
  - goods not yet delivered,
  - or mismatched invoice amounts.
- Business impact: increases **financial leakage**, fraud risk, and breaks core P2P control logic (receipt confirmation and three-way matching concepts).

---

## 3) Case 1002: PO issuance happens before requisition approval (control inversion)
**Key sequence in Case 1002:**
- Create Purchase Requisition (08:30)
- **Issue Purchase Order** (08:40)  before approval
- later: Approve Purchase Requisition (next day 08:00)

### Why this is anomalous/problematic
- PO issuance is typically allowed only after requisition approval.
- If the approval was “supposed to happen” earlier, it is missing from the log; if not, the authorization control is violated.
- Business impact: weakens procurement authorization, making it harder to ensure spend is permitted and budgeted.

---

## 4) Case 1003: Payment released before invoice is received and before goods are received
**Sequence in Case 1003:**
- Create Purchase Requisition (2024-02-02 09:00)
- Approve Purchase Requisition (09:10)
- Issue Purchase Order (10:00)
- **Release Payment** (2024-02-08 11:00)   before invoice and receipt
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-09 12:00)
- Receive Goods (2024-02-10 13:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (2024-02-11 14:00)
- Close Case (2024-02-12 10:00)

### Why this is anomalous/problematic
- Payment being released **before**:
  - invoice receipt, and
  - goods receipt,
  is a major deviation from standard P2P sequencing.
- It can indicate:
  - the payment is an advance payment/deposit (but then typically the process would record “advance” or a different payment reason),
  - or the events are logged inaccurately,
  - or the process is bypassing critical invoice/receipt controls.
- Business impact: very high risk of paying without validated invoice amounts and without confirming delivery.

---

## 5) Case 1004: Purchase order is issued after goods are received (receipt precedes ordering)
**Sequence in Case 1004:**
- Create Purchase Requisition (2024-02-05 10:00)
- Approve Purchase Requisition (10:15)
- **Receive Goods** (2024-02-06 09:00)
- **Issue Purchase Order** (2024-02-06 09:15)  after receipt
- Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-07 08:00)
- Match Invoice to PO (08:30)
- Release Payment (09:00)
- Close Case (2024-02-08 11:00)

### Why this is anomalous/problematic
- Receiving goods normally presumes a PO (or at least a formally authorized request) already exists.
- Getting goods and then issuing a PO shortly after could mean:
  - the PO creation was delayed but the goods arrived earlier (possible in reality),
  - “goods receipt” was recorded against an existing framework/blanket agreement not represented in the log,
  - or ordering occurred out of sequence (a form of maverick buying).
- Business impact: weakens traceability and control—harder to ensure the supplier, terms, and quantities were authorized before receipt.

---

# Summary of the largest control breaks
- **Case 1002:** approval and matching/payment order appear inverted; payment occurs before receiving and before invoice matching.
- **Case 1003:** payment occurs before invoice receipt and before goods receipt.
- **Case 1004:** goods receipt occurs before PO issuance.

These kinds of anomalies are typically problematic because they undermine core P2P controls such as **authorization before commitment**, **receipt before payment**, and **invoice matching before payment** (or at least before releasing the final settlement).
