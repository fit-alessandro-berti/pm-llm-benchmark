### 1. Standard Hire-to-Retire Baseline
A normative hiring workflow follows a strict causal sequence:
`Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case`

Key logical constraints:
- **Screening** filters candidates before interviews.
- **Interviews** must precede the hiring decision (the decision is informed by interview outcomes).
- **Onboarding & Payroll** occur only after a positive decision.
- All steps must eventually converge to a single closure point; no activity should run indefinitely or remain disconnected from the main flow.

---

### 2. Analysis of POWL Model 1

**Structure & Flow:**
- `Post  Screen`
- `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview` (parallel branches)
- `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`
- `Interview` has **no outgoing edges**.

**Identified Anomalies:**
1. **Interview & Decision Parallelism (Severe):** In a `StrictPartialOrder`, the absence of a precedence edge between `Interview` and `Decide` means they are incomparable and can execute concurrently. This allows a hiring decision to be made before, during, or completely independently of interviews, violating a core causal dependency.
2. **Dangling/Unsynchronized Branch (Severe):** `Interview` never merges back into the main flow. The process can legally proceed to `Onboard`, `Payroll`, and `Close` while `Interview` is still running, or even if `Interview` never executes. This creates a "fire-and-forget" activity that breaks process closure guarantees.
3. **Missing Screening-to-Interview Dependency (Moderate):** While screening logically precedes interviewing, the model doesn't enforce it. However, this is overshadowed by the more critical decision/interview flaw.

**Impact on Integrity:** High. The model fundamentally decouples the evaluation phase from the decision phase, making it possible to hire without interviewing and to close cases with unresolved parallel work. This violates soundness and business logic.

### 3. Analysis of POWL Model 2

- `Post  Screen` and `Post  Interview` (parallel branches)
- `Interview  Decide  loop_onboarding  xor_payroll  Close`
- `loop_onboarding = *(Onboard, skip)`  Onboard can repeat with silent iterations.
- `xor_payroll = X(Payroll, skip)`  Payroll is optional.

**Identified Anomalies:**
1. **Screening & Interview Parallelism (Moderate):** `Screen` and `Interview` can start simultaneously after posting. In practice, screening usually filters candidates before interviews. However, some organizations run initial phone screens alongside resume screening, making this a tolerable deviation rather than a fatal flaw.
2. **Optional Payroll (Moderate-High):** `X(Payroll, skip)` makes payroll setup optional. In reality, adding an employee to payroll is mandatory for legal and operational compliance. This is a policy/compliance anomaly, but structurally it's a valid control-flow choice.
3. **Onboarding Loop with Silent Transition (Low-Moderate):** `*(Onboard, skip)` allows onboarding to repeat. While onboarding is typically a one-time phase, modeling it as a loop could represent iterative checklist completion or training modules. The use of a `skip` transition makes it functionally equivalent to "Onboard can occur multiple times," which is unusual but doesn't break the control flow.
4. **Screening Disconnected from Downstream (Low):** `Screen` has no outgoing edges to `Interview`, `Decide`, or later steps. This means screening can be skipped entirely without blocking the hire. Again, suboptimal but less critical than breaking the interview-decision link.

**Impact on Integrity:** Moderate. The core decision-to-onboarding sequence remains strictly sequential and logically sound. The anomalies are primarily policy or optimization deviations rather than structural impossibilities.
