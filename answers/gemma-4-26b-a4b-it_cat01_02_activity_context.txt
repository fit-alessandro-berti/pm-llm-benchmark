### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

Based on the event log, the following activities appear multiple times within a single case, serving different functional purposes:

*   **`Verify Documents`**
    *   **Occurrence 1 (Initial):** This is the primary identity check performed at the start of the process to establish eligibility.
    *   **Occurrence 2 (Subsequent):** This is a secondary check or "re-verification" performed after new information has been provided (C001) or after internal account changes have been made (C003).
*   **`Call Customer`**
    *   **Occurrence 1 (Inquiry/Issue):** This is an outbound call triggered by a problem, such as missing documentation (C001), a discrepancy (C002), or the customer being physically unavailable (C003). It is an "investigative" or "corrective" action.
    *   **Occurrence 2 (Follow-up):** In case C002, the second call is not to solve a problem, but to confirm that the previously discussed adjustments were successful. This is a "closing" or "confirmation" action.

### 3. Justification for Naming Choices

*   **`Initial Document Verification` vs. `Document Re-verification`**: 
    The context shows that the first instance of verification is a standard gateway activity. The second instance always follows a "triggering event" (either a customer submission in C001 or an account adjustment in C003), making "Re-verification" a more accurate description of its role in the lifecycle.
*   **`Outbound Inquiry Call`**: 
    In all cases where a call occurs first (C001, C002, C003), the agent is calling to solve a specific hurdle (missing docs, discrepancy, or locating the customer). "Inquiry" captures this proactive, problem-solving nature.
*   **`Outbound Follow-up Call`**: 
    In C002, the second call is clearly a post-action confirmation. Using "Follow-up" distinguishes this from the investigative calls and correctly identifies it as a step intended to close the loop on a previously handled issue.
