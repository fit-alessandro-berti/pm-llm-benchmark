### Analysis of Each Case:

**Case ID: 1001**

**Case ID: 1002**

- Anomaly detected:
  - The sequence is: Create Purchase Requisition  Issue Purchase Order  Vendor Invoice Received  Release Payment  Approve Purchase Requisition  Receive Goods  Match Invoice to PO  Close Case.
  
  **Problematic Aspect:**
  - The approval step (Approve Purchase Requisition) occurs after the invoice has been received and payment released. Normally, approvals should occur before issuing a purchase order and receiving goods. This can lead to:
    - Unnecessary payments being made on non-approved requisitions.
    - Potential overruns due to unapproved spending.
    - Increased risk of financial mismanagement.

- Anomaly detected:

  **Problematic Aspect:**
  - The goods are received before the invoice is matched with the purchase order, which might indicate:

**Case ID: 1004**

- Anomaly detected:
  - The sequence is: Create Purchase Requisition  Approve Purchase Requisition  Receive Goods  Issue Purchase Order  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice to PO  Release Payment  Close Case.
  
  **Problematic Aspect:**
  - Goods are received before issuing a purchase order, which typically should be done before goods arrive. This can result in:
    - Incorrect inventory records and mismatched delivery notes.
    - Potential delays or confusion in invoicing due to discrepancies between expected and actual procurement activities.

### Summary of Anomalies:

1. **Case 1002**: Approval occurs after payment release, which might lead to unauthorized expenditures and financial inconsistencies.
   
2. **Case 1003**: Goods received before the invoice is processed can cause mismatches with purchase orders and potentially inaccurate billing records.

3. **Case 1004**: Receiving goods before issuing a purchase order may result in incorrect inventory management and delayed invoicing processes, affecting both cost control and supplier relationships.

These anomalies are problematic because they deviate from standard P2P practices that ensure approvals precede expenditures, maintain accurate procurement documentation, and prevent discrepancies that could lead to financial errors or operational inefficiencies. Addressing these issues involves reviewing the approval workflows and ensuring timely alignment with business processes to mitigate risks associated with unauthorized spending and operational discrepancies.
