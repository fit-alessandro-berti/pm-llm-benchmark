## Main Process Flow

### Start Event
- **Message Start Event:** Invoice received via email from Supplier.

### 1. Check for PO Number
- **Lane:** APC  
- **Task:** “Check if invoice contains a valid Purchase Order number”  
- **XOR Gateway:** PO number present?
  - **No**  APC performs “Request missing PO details from Supplier” (send email). The Supplier may respond with a revised invoice. The process loops back to the start (or a new instance of invoice received is created).  
  - **Yes**  Proceed.

### 2. Log Invoice
- **Lane:** APC  
- **Task:** “Log invoice into the accounting system” (creates a system record).

### 3. Forward to Purchasing
- **Lane:** APC  PO  
- **Task:** APC “Forward invoice to Purchasing for confirmation” (via email/system). The flow moves to the Purchasing Officer lane.

### 4. Verify Invoice Against PO and Receipt of Goods
- **Lane:** PO  
- **Task:** “Verify invoice details against Purchase Order and confirm goods/services received” (compare quantities, prices, delivery charges; check receipt).  
- **XOR Gateway:** Discrepancy found?
  - **No (Invoice matches and goods received)**  PO performs “Send confirmation email to APC”. Flow returns to APC lane for step 5.
  - **Yes (Discrepancy)**  Enter **Discrepancy Resolution Subprocess** (see below).  
    - If the discrepancy can be resolved internally (e.g., missing information corrected by Supplier), the flow loops back to reverify (step 4).  
    - If the discrepancy cannot be resolved easily, the case is escalated to the APS (see Exception Path). After APS resolution, the invoice reenters the normal flow at step 4 (reverification) or is terminated (rejected).

#### Discrepancy Resolution Subprocess (adhoc, can involve multiple roles)
- **Trigger:** Discrepancy detected by PO or later by APC.
- **Possible actions (not strictly sequential):**
  - PO directly contacts Supplier (often with assistance from SRM) to correct the invoice.
  - PO requests APC to “Hold invoice until corrected invoice arrives”. APC puts the invoice on hold; when corrected invoice arrives, the PO rechecks.
  - If the issue cannot be resolved at the PO/APC level, **escalate to APS** (see Exception Path).
- **SRM** may be consulted at any point to check contract terms or expedite supplier communication.  
- **Note:** Accounts indicate that the exact resolution path can vary; the subprocess iterates until the invoice either passes verification or is permanently rejected.

### 5. Match Invoice Line Items to PO in System
- **Lane:** APC  
- **Task:** “Match invoice line items against the PO in the accounting system” (using logged data and confirmation).  
- **XOR Gateway:** All line items match?
  - **Yes**  Proceed to step 6.
  - **No**  APC “Email Purchasing for clarification” – this triggers another round of discrepancy resolution (reenter the subprocess described in step 4). If unresolved after further attempts, **escalate to APS**. After resolution, repeat step 5.

### 6. Send for Manager Approval
- **Lane:** APC  DM  
- **Task:** APC (or the system automatically) sends a notification to the Department Manager. The system presents the invoice details, PO, and confirmation.

### 7. Review and Approve Invoice
- **Lane:** DM  
- **Task:** “Review invoice, PO, and confirmation; approve or reject/hold.”  
- **XOR Gateway:** Approved?
  - **Yes**  Flow moves back to APC lane for step 8.
  - **No (Reject or Hold for investigation)**  APC performs “Investigate and resolve issues” (may involve PO, SRM, APS). After investigation, the corrected invoice may be resubmitted for approval (return to step 7) or cancelled permanently.

### 8. Schedule for Payment
- **Lane:** APC  
- **Task:** “Schedule invoice for the next payment cycle” (add to payment run in the accounting system).

### End Event
- **End Event:** Invoice payment scheduled (normal completion) or invoice rejected (exception end).

## Exception Path: Escalation to Accounts Payable Supervisor
- **Trigger:** A discrepancy cannot be resolved through normal channels, or an invoice has no PO and no way to associate a valid one (e.g., informal services).
- **Lane:** APS  
- **Task:** APS “Resolve exception” – coordinates with departments, contacts Supplier directly or via SRM, may create a retrospective PO with the involved department head, or decide to reject the invoice.
- **Outcome:**  
  - If resolved, the invoice returns to the normal flow at step 4 (Purchasing verification) or step 5 (matching), as appropriate.  
  - If rejected, the process ends without payment.
