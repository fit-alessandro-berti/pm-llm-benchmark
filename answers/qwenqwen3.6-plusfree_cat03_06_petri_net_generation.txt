### Key Modeling Notes:
1. **Loop Structure**: The loops for `DV  RDS` and `FC  II` are modeled using intermediate places (`p_dv`, `p_rds`, `p_fc`, `p_ii`). This creates a standard XOR-split/loop pattern where a token can either trigger the re-submission/investigation transition (looping back) or proceed to the next main activity.
2. **Free-Choice Points**: Places like `p_dv` and `p_fc` have multiple outgoing arcs, representing decision points where the process either repeats a verification step or moves forward. This aligns with standard process mining Petri net semantics.
3. **Accepting Net**: The tuple `(net, im, fm)` constitutes the accepting Petri net, where `im` marks the arrival of a claim and `fm` marks its successful closure.
