### **Detailed Phase Analysis & Rationale**

#### **Phase 1: Idle / Standby (Events 1–3)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Temperature is at ambient room levels (20–21°C), Pressure is atmospheric (1 bar), Vibration and Material Flow are completely inactive (0), and the tool is at its home position (0 mm). Energy consumption is at a minimal standby baseline (~0.45 kWh).
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** The machine is powered on but completely static. No physical processing is happening yet. This represents the standby/idle state of the machine awaiting the arrival of a raw workpiece.

#### **Phase 2: Cutting Metal (Events 4–6)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Vibration spikes instantly from 0 to 30–38 Hz. Temperature rises steadily (50°C to 58°C). Material flow is high at a steady 5 units/s. The tool position is actively changing dynamically (moving from 5 mm to 10 mm), and energy consumption climbs sharply to 3.00 kWh.
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** High mechanical friction from a tool cutting through metal causes high vibration and a heat build-up. The moving tool position points to the active progression of a cutting path, while the high material flow represents raw stock feeding into the cutting head.

#### **Phase 3: Assembling Parts (Events 7–8)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Vibration drops to a low, stable 10 Hz. Temperature drops slightly and stabilizes at 40°C. Pressure increases and holds steady at 2 bar. Material flow drops to a steady 2 units/s, and the tool position is held constant at 15 mm.
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** To assemble components, parts must be aligned and pressed together. The increased pressure (2 bar) represents mechanical clamping, while the stable tool position (15 mm) holds the parts in place. The lower, steady material flow (2 units/s) represents minor auxiliary fasteners or parts being steadily fed to the assembly zone.

#### **Phase 4: Welding (Event 9)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** A sudden and massive spike in Temperature (80°C), Energy consumption (5.00 kWh—the peak of the cycle), and Vibration (50 Hz). Material flow drops to 0, pressure remains high (2 bar), and the tool is held static at 15 mm.
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** Welding is a high-energy, high-thermal bonding process. The extreme spike in power and temperature is characteristic of drawing electrical current to strike an arc or engage heating elements. Vibration spikes due to high-frequency arc noise or ultrasonic weld vibrations. Material flow is 0 because the assembly is held static under pressure (2 bar) while being permanently joined.

#### **Phase 5: Quality Inspection (Event 10)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Rapid thermal cooling down to 30°C. Pressure drops back to baseline (1 bar), meaning the clamp has released the part. Vibration falls to nearly dead silence (1 Hz). Material flow is 0, and energy drops back down to 1.00 kWh. The tool briefly stays at 15 mm.
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** After welding, the assembly must cool down and be inspected. The sudden drop in vibration and pressure creates a quiet, stable environment. Holding the tool temporarily in place allows proximity sensors, laser scanners, or cameras to verify the geometric dimensions and weld integrity before movement.

#### **Phase 6: Packaging & Outfeed (Events 11–12)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Temperature settles near ambient (25°C). The tool retracts to an output/eject limit of 20 mm. A gentle, steady vibration (5 Hz) occurs alongside a slow, steady material flow of 1 unit/s. Energy is moderate (1.50–1.60 kWh).
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** The tool retracts out of the workspace to clear the path. The low, steady vibration combined with low material flow (1 unit/s) is highly indicative of an outfeed conveyor belt transporting the finished product out of the machine workspace and onto the packaging line, drawing moderate mechanical motor energy.

#### **Phase 7: Idle / Standby (Events 13–15)**
*   **Sensor Indicators:** Complete return to initial baseline values (Temp 20–21°C, Pressure 1 bar, Vibration 0 Hz, Material Flow 0, Tool Position 0 mm, Energy ~0.45 kWh).
*   **Deduction/Rationale:** The finished part has cleared the machine. The tool has returned to its home starting position (0 mm) and all sensors have returned to ambient baselines, completing the process loop and waiting for the next raw block.
