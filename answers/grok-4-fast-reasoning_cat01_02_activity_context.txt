The parenthetical specifies the exact contextual trigger from the additional context, distinguishing it from the initial check and emphasizing process dependency.

2. **Call Customer (recurs in C002; single in C001 and C003)**:
   - For C002's first occurrence: Renamed to "Call for Account Clarification" to capture the outbound purpose tied to resolving a discrepancy, as described in the context.
   - For C002's second occurrence: Renamed to "Follow-up Confirmation Call" to indicate its sequential role after the adjustment, confirming changes per the context.
   - For single occurrences: Renamed C001's to "Call for Missing Documents" (purpose-driven by incomplete verification) and C003's to "Call to Return to Branch" (context-specific to the walk-in scenario where the customer leaves temporarily). This ensures consistency across cases, even for non-recurring instances, by using purpose-based descriptors derived from timestamps (e.g., timing relative to prior events) and additional context, preventing ambiguity in cross-case analysis.

These renames make the log more process-oriented, revealing workflow patterns (e.g., verification loops) without altering other attributes. They are concise yet descriptive, improving traceability in a complex support workflow.
