# Procure-to-Pay Event Log Analysis

## Standard P2P Process Flow (Reference)

Before identifying anomalies, let me establish the expected sequence:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition**  2. **Approve Purchase Requisition**  3. **Issue Purchase Order**  4. **Receive Goods**  5. **Vendor Invoice Received**  6. **Match Invoice to PO** (typically a 3-way match: PO, goods receipt, invoice)  7. **Release Payment**  8. **Close Case**

---

## Case-by-Case Analysis

###  Case 1001 — Normal (No Anomalies)

This case follows the standard P2P sequence perfectly. It serves as the benchmark for comparison.

---

###  Case 1002 — Multiple Serious Anomalies

| Order | Activity | Problem |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Create Purchase Requisition | OK |
| 2 | **Issue Purchase Order** |  PO issued **before** requisition approval |
| 3 | **Vendor Invoice Received** |  Invoice received **before** goods received |
| 4 | **Release Payment** |  Payment released **before** approval, goods receipt, and invoice matching |
| 5 | **Approve Purchase Requisition** |  Approval happens **after** PO, invoice, and even payment |
| 6 | Receive Goods | Late — after payment already made |
| 7 | Match Invoice to PO | Retroactive — matching done after payment |
| 8 | Close Case | — |

**Why this is problematic:**
- **Skipped approval before PO issuance:** This bypasses authorization controls, meaning purchases can be made without proper oversight — a major internal control violation that exposes the organization to unauthorized spending or fraud.
- **Payment before goods receipt:** Paying a vendor before confirming that goods were actually received creates a significant financial risk — the organization may pay for goods never delivered, or for incorrect/defective goods with no leverage to negotiate.
- **Payment before invoice matching:** The three-way match (PO  goods receipt  invoice) is a fundamental control to ensure the company pays the right amount for what was actually ordered and received. Releasing payment before this step defeats the purpose of this control entirely.
- **Retroactive approval:** Approving the requisition after the fact suggests either a rubber-stamping culture or an attempt to paper over an unauthorized purchase — both are red flags for fraud or policy non-compliance.

---

###  Case 1003 — Payment Before Invoice, Goods Receipt, and Matching

| Order | Activity | Problem |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Create Purchase Requisition | OK |
| 2 | Approve Purchase Requisition | OK |
| 3 | Issue Purchase Order | OK |
| 4 | **Release Payment** |  Payment released **before** receiving the invoice, goods, or performing a match |
| 5 | **Vendor Invoice Received** | Invoice arrives **after** payment |
| 6 | **Receive Goods** | Goods arrive **after** payment |
| 7 | Match Invoice to PO | Matching is retroactive and meaningless as a control |
| 8 | Close Case | — |

**Why this is problematic:**
- **Payment without an invoice:** There is no documented obligation to pay, yet payment was released. This is a critical control failure — it could indicate a prepayment arrangement (which would require special controls not evident here), or worse, a fictitious or fraudulent payment.
- **Payment before goods receipt:** Same risk as Case 1002 — paying without confirmation of delivery removes any leverage and risks financial loss.
- **Retroactive matching:** The three-way match performed after payment has already been released is purely ceremonial and offers no preventive control value.

---

###  Case 1004 — Goods Received Before Purchase Order Issued

| Order | Activity | Problem |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Create Purchase Requisition | OK |
| 2 | Approve Purchase Requisition | OK |
| 3 | **Receive Goods** |  Goods received **before** the PO was issued |
| 4 | **Issue Purchase Order** | PO issued **after** goods already in hand |
| 5 | Vendor Invoice Received | OK (relative to PO) |
| 6 | Match Invoice to PO | OK |
| 7 | Release Payment | OK |
| 8 | Close Case | — |

**Why this is problematic:**
- **Goods received without a PO ("maverick buying"):** Receiving goods before a purchase order exists means there was no formal, authorized commitment to the vendor. This undermines procurement controls, makes it impossible to verify that what was received matches what was agreed upon, and can lead to disputes over pricing, quantity, or terms.
- **Retroactive PO creation:** Issuing a PO after goods receipt suggests the PO was created after the fact to regularize the transaction — a common audit finding that indicates weak procurement discipline and potential policy violations.

---

## Summary of Anomalies

| Case | Key Anomaly | Risk Level | Primary Risk |
|------|-------------|------------|--------------|
| 1001 | None |  Normal | — |
| 1002 | PO before approval; payment before goods, matching, and approval |  **Critical** | Fraud, unauthorized purchases, financial loss |
| 1003 | Payment before invoice, goods receipt, and matching |  **Critical** | Fraudulent/erroneous payment, no verification |
| 1004 | Goods received before PO issued |  **Moderate** | Maverick buying, weak procurement controls |

**Cases 1002 and 1003** represent the most severe violations, as they both involve **releasing payment without proper verification controls**, which is the single most consequential failure in the P2P process from a financial risk and fraud prevention perspective. **Case 1004** reflects a procurement discipline issue that, while less immediately damaging, still weakens the control environment and should be addressed.
