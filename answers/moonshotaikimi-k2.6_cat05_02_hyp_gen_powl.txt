```sql
        LEAD(activity) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_activity
    FROM 
        claim_events
    WHERE 
        activity IN ('A', 'E', 'P', 'N', 'C')
)
SELECT DISTINCT
    claim_id
FROM 
    ordered_events
WHERE 
    activity = 'A' 
    AND next_activity = 'C';
```

### Contextual / Root-Cause Queries

**Query D.1 ñ Do looping re-evaluations correlate with adjusterñclaim mismatch?**
*Hypothesis:* The loop exists because adjusters are repeatedly rejecting claims outside their specialization.
```sql
SELECT 
    ce.claim_id,
    ce.resource AS adjuster_resource,
    a.specialization,
    c.claim_type,
    COUNT(*) AS eval_count
FROM 
    claim_events ce
JOIN 
    claims c ON ce.claim_id = c.claim_id
LEFT JOIN 
    adjusters a ON ce.resource = a.name   -- adjust if resource stores adjuster_id
WHERE 
    ce.activity = 'E'
GROUP BY 
    ce.claim_id, ce.resource, a.specialization, c.claim_type
HAVING 
    COUNT(*) > 1;
```

**Query D.2 ñ Are skipped notifications clustered by region or adjuster?**
*Hypothesis:* The XOR skip reflects a departmental or regional practice rather than a global business rule.
```sql
SELECT 
    a.region,
    ce_a.resource AS adjuster_name,
    COUNT(DISTINCT c.claim_id) FILTER (WHERE ce_n.event_id IS NULL) AS skipped_count,
    COUNT(DISTINCT c.claim_id) AS total_closed
FROM 
    claims c
JOIN 
    claim_events ce_a ON c.claim_id = ce_a.claim_id AND ce_a.activity = 'A'
JOIN 
    claim_events ce_c ON c.claim_id = ce_c.claim_id AND ce_c.activity = 'C'
LEFT JOIN 
    adjusters a ON ce_a.resource = a.name
LEFT JOIN 
    claim_events ce_n ON c.claim_id = ce_n.claim_id AND ce_n.activity = 'N'
GROUP BY 
    a.region, ce_a.resource
HAVING 
    COUNT(DISTINCT c.claim_id) > 5
ORDER BY 
    skipped_count DESC;
```

By running these queries, an analyst can move from *model-level suspicion* to *data-grounded evidence*, determining whether the POWL anomalies represent real non-conformance in the process execution or merely over-permissive modeling of legitimate exceptions.
