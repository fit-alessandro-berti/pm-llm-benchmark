### **2. Grouping Rationale & Justification**

#### **HL-01: Material Preparation & Setup**
*   **Logical Cohesion:** This group encompasses the complete "front-end" lifecycle of the raw material before value-adding assembly begins. It moves the part from *storage* (Retrieve) $\rightarrow$ *identification/traceability* (Scan) $\rightarrow$ *fixturing* (Place/Align) $\rightarrow$ *thermal conditioning* (Preheat).
*   **Resource/Location Continuity:** Events 1–3 are performed by **Operator A** at **Table T-3**. Events 4–5 handoff to automated systems (Robot, Heater) but remain at the same physical workstation.
*   **Temporal Proximity:** Events occur in a tight, unbroken chain (08:00:05 – 08:00:25).
*   **Domain Context:** In sheet metal fabrication, preheating is often a prerequisite for welding quality; grouping it here separates "material conditioning" from the actual "joining" process.

#### **HL-02: Welding Assembly**
*   **Atomic Work Package:** This represents a single "setup-to-completion" cycle for the human operator (Operator B). The sequence *Pick Tool $\rightarrow$ Weld A $\rightarrow$ Weld B* is a standard operational method sheet (OMS) step.
*   **Resource Affinity:** All three events are performed by **Operator B** using **Tool W-12**.
*   **Functional Unity:** The goal is singular: create the structural joints (Corner A & B). Splitting "Weld A" and "Weld B" into separate high-level steps would create unnecessary granularity for process mining/KPI dashboards (e.g., "Welding Cycle Time" is a standard metric).

#### **HL-03: In-Process Weld Verification**
*   **Critical Control Point (CCP):** This is a mandatory, automated quality gate (Quality Sensor #1) immediately following the high-risk joining operation.
*   **Decoupling from Assembly:** It is performed by a *different resource type* (Sensor vs. Human/Robot) and measures *output quality* (IntegrityScore) rather than executing the process.
*   **Compliance/Traceability:** Manufacturing execution systems (MES) often require this as a distinct "Hold/Release" decision point. If IntegrityScore < Threshold, the process branches to Rework—making it a necessary distinct node in a process model.

#### **HL-04: Coating & Curing**
*   **Process Chemistry Unity:** Application (Coating Unit #3) and Cross-linking/Drying (Heating Unit #2) are chemically linked steps. The coating is not "finished" until cured.
*   **Automated Cell/Handoff:** This appears to be an automated cell handoff (Unit 3 $\rightarrow$ Unit 2). The part moves through a tunnel or conveyor.
*   **Temporal Block:** Events 10 & 11 occur back-to-back (08:01:30 – 08:01:50) with no operator intervention, distinct from the manual welding phase before and the manual inspection after.

#### **HL-05: Final Quality Inspection & Release**
*   **Human-in-the-Loop Gate:** This is the final subjective assessment (Visual Check) by **Operator C** (a distinct resource from Assembly Operator B), enforcing separation of duties (Builder vs. Checker).
*   **Terminal Event:** "Check: Passed" signifies the CaseID is ready for logistics/shipment. In process mining terms, this is the **End Activity**.
