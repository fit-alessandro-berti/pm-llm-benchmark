# Analysis of the Two POWL Models

## Standard (Normative) Hire-to-Retire Sequence

The expected flow is a clear, linear progression:

**Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case**

---

## Model 1 Analysis

**Structure (precedence edges):**
- Post  Screen
- Screen  Decide
- Screen  Interview
- Decide  Onboard
- Onboard  Payroll
- Payroll  Close

**Anomalies identified:**

1. **Decision and Interviews run in parallel (severe).** After `Screen_Candidates`, both `Make_Hiring_Decision` and `Conduct_Interviews` become possible with *no ordering relation between them*. This means the hiring decision can be made **before, after, or simultaneously with** the interviews.
   - This is a **fundamental logical violation**: you cannot legitimately decide whom to hire before interviewing candidates. The interview is supposed to *inform* the decision.
   
2. **Interviews lead nowhere (severe).** `Conduct_Interviews` has an incoming edge (from Screen) but **no outgoing edge**. It is a "dangling" activity that does not feed into the decision. So interviews are conducted but their results never influence any downstream step.

**Positive aspects:** The tail of the process (Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close) is correctly ordered and complete. All activities occur exactly once.

---

## Model 2 Analysis

**Structure:**
- Post  Screen
- Post  Interview
- Interview  Decide
- Decide  loop_onboarding `*(Onboard, skip)`
- loop_onboarding  xor_payroll `X(Payroll, skip)`
- xor_payroll  Close

**Anomalies identified:**

1. **Screen_Candidates is disconnected from the decision flow (severe).** `Screen` only has an incoming edge from Post; it has no outgoing edge. Meanwhile `Conduct_Interviews` follows directly from `Post` (parallel to Screen). This means **interviews can happen without screening first**, and screening results never feed into interviews or the decision. The natural Screen  Interview dependency is broken.

2. **Onboarding loop (moderate).** `*(Onboard, skip)` allows `Onboard_Employee` to be repeated multiple times. Onboarding an employee multiple times is unusual but not catastrophic—it could loosely model re-onboarding or corrections.

3. **Payroll is optional/skippable (moderate-to-severe).** `X(Payroll, skip)` allows the process to skip `Add_to_Payroll` entirely. An employee who is onboarded but never added to payroll would not be paid—a real but recoverable deviation.

**Positive aspects:** `Interview  Decide  Onboard  Payroll/skip  Close` maintains the correct backbone order (interview informs decision). Post and Screen ordering is partially preserved.

---

## Comparison and Verdict

| Aspect | Model 1 | Model 2 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Decision informed by interviews? |  Decide parallel to Interview |  Interview  Decide |
| Interview connected to outcome? |  dead-end |  feeds decision |
| Screening properly placed? |  Screen precedes both |  Screen is dead-end |
| Optional/looping anomalies | None | Onboard loop, Payroll skippable |
| All activities mandatory & once |  |  (skips, repeats) |

### Which model is closer to normative?

**Model 2 more closely aligns with the normative process.**

**Justification:**
- Model 1's core anomaly is **fundamental**: it allows the **hiring decision to be made independently of (even before) the interviews**, and interviews produce no usable result. This breaks the very *essence* of a hiring process—the decision logic itself is corrupted.
- Model 2 preserves the **critical causal chain** `Interview  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`, meaning the decision is always properly informed by interviews. Its anomalies (a misplaced/dangling Screen, an optional payroll, a repeatable onboarding) are **structural deviations and quality issues**, but they do not invert the central decision logic of the process.

In short: Model 1 violates the *integrity of the decision-making sequence* (a severe semantic flaw), while Model 2 only introduces *optionality, repetition, and a misordered screening step* (less severe deviations). Therefore **Model 2 is the better-aligned model**, though both require correction to be fully normative.
