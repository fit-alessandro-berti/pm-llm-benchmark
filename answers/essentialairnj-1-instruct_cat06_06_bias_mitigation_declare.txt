### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Coexistence Constraint (`coexistence`):** Ensures that if a sensitive attribute check is performed, a manual review (`ManualReview`) must coexist with any sensitive demographic decision (`Approve_Minority`, `Reject_Minority`). This helps balance the performance of decision-making for sensitive demographics.

2. **Response Constraint (`response`):** Prevents immediate biased outcomes by enforcing that a `BiasMitigationCheck` precedes any decision activities (`Approve`, `Reject`). This checks for potential biases in the inputs considered for decision-making.

3. **Precedence Constraint (`precedence`):** Ensures that a manual review must precede the final decision (`FinalDecision`), helping to standardize the decision-making process and reducing the chance of biased outcomes.

4. **Succession Constraint (`succession`):** Ensures that sensitive checks are followed by certain decision activities (`Approve`, `Reject`), preventing immediate biases in conclusions based solely on sensitive attributes.

5. **Alternative Precedence Constraint (`alternative precedence`):** Ensures that for any decision event (`Approve`, `Reject`), there must have been a manual review for applicants from sensitive demographics, helping to mitigate potential biases in decision paths.

6. **Non-succession Constraint (`nonsuccession`):** Prevents an immediate biased outcome by stopping a decision event directly after a sensitive attribute event, which must have been reviewed and confirmed non-discriminatory through a manual check or alternative decision pathway.
