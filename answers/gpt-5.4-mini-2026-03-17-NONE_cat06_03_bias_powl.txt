Yes. The XOR branch here can introduce bias because it gives some applicants an extra positive signal that others do not receive.

### Where the bias comes from
In your model, after `PreliminaryScoring`, the process can either:

- perform `CheckLocalAffiliation (D)`, or
- skip it.

If `D` is only executed for applicants who are local residents or belong to a known community group, then those applicants may receive a subtle score uplift or favorable consideration before `ManualReview` and `FinalDecision`.

That means the model is not treating all applicants identically at that stage. Even if the group is not legally protected, it still receives an incremental procedural advantage.

### Why this is problematic
This kind of branching can affect fairness in several ways:

1. **Unequal informational advantage**  
   Applicants who go through `D` provide more evidence that can help them, while others do not get the same opportunity.

2. **Process-based bias**  
   The outcome may be influenced not only by creditworthiness, but also by whether the applicant falls into the group that triggers `D`.

3. **Proxy discrimination risk**  
   “Local affiliation” may correlate with socioeconomic status, geography, ethnicity, or access to community networks. Even if the group is not legally protected, it can still create indirect discrimination.

4. **Consistency and explainability issues**  
   Two applicants with similar financial profiles may receive different decisions simply because one was eligible for the extra check and the other was not.

### Implications for fairness and equity
Giving a non-legally protected group an incremental advantage can still undermine equity because fairness in lending is not only about avoiding explicit protected-class discrimination. It also requires that similarly situated applicants be evaluated similarly.

Possible consequences:

- **Systematic preference** for applicants from the favored group
- **Reduced access** for applicants outside the group
- **Distorted decision quality**, because the final decision reflects process access, not just risk
- **Trust and compliance concerns**, especially if the advantage is not justified by a legitimate, job-related, or credit-related criterion

### In short
The XOR choice embeds a selective advantage: some applicants can benefit from an extra local-affiliation check while others cannot. Even if the group is not legally protected, this can still create unfairness by amplifying differences unrelated to repayment risk and by making final loan decisions less equitable.
