**Grouping Principles Used**

1. **Temporal Contiguity**  
   Events that occur in rapid succession and precede a clear process shift (e.g., preheating ends, welding begins) were grouped together. Gaps or changes in resource type often signal a phase boundary.

2. **Functional Coherence**  
   Activities that collectively achieve a single objective (e.g., preparing a part for welding, or verifying a weld) were merged. For example, scanning and retrieving are both intake/identification tasks essential to preparation.

3. **Resource Affinity**  
   Where possible, steps performed by similar resource classes (operators for setup, dedicated machines for specific treatments) were grouped, while preserving process logic over rigid resource-based clustering.

4. **Process Dependencies**  
   Steps that must precede others (e.g., coating before drying; alignment/preheating before welding) were kept in order, with group boundaries placed where dependencies change (e.g., after QA and before finishing).

5. **Gatekeeping / Control Logic**  
   Inspection steps (automated measurement and visual check) were isolated as distinct phases because they validate outcomes rather than transform the product, and often determine whether downstream steps proceed.
