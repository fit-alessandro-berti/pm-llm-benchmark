###  **Explanation of Key Constraints**

#### **Equivalence**
- Activities that must happen the **same number of times** in a case. For example, every time you receive an order, you must also reserve items.

#### **Always Before**
- Ensures **precedence**. If activity B occurs, activity A **must have occurred earlier** in the process.

#### **Always After**
- Ensures that **eventually**, after A occurs, B will occur **later** in the trace — not necessarily immediately.

#### **Never Together**
- These activities **cannot appear in the same trace**. Useful to prevent invalid combinations like recording payment before dispatch.

#### **Activity Frequency (`activ_freq`)**
- Each activity is constrained to occur a specific number of times (e.g., exactly once per order).

#### **Directly-Follows**
- Enforces **immediate succession**. If A happens, B **must be the very next activity**.

###  **Use Case Fitting**
This Log Skeleton models a **strict, linear order fulfillment process** where each step must follow the previous one in a precise sequence. It ensures:
- No skipping of critical steps (e.g., you can't pack without picking).
- No invalid ordering (e.g., you can't invoice before dispatch).
- Each activity occurs only once per order.
- Logical grouping of activities that must occur together or in sequence.

This model can be used for **process mining**, **conformance checking**, or **simulation** in a warehouse management system.
