2. What mechanisms ensure design changes from R&D (e.g., camera or sensor specification updates) propagate reliably through procurement, manufacturing planning, and downstream distribution without creating mismatched builds?

3. In what ways do customs delays and regulatory documentation risks vary by country and shipment mode, and how are those uncertainties quantified in planning?

4. How does the 24/7 assembly environment balance throughput targets with worker safety, fatigue risk, and equipment maintenance schedules to prevent quality drift?

5. What quality assurance strategy is used to detect defects early enough to avoid expensive downstream failures, and how are “acceptable risk thresholds” defined across different markets?

6. When using just-in-time replenishment for high-demand markets, what contingencies are in place for supplier shocks (e.g., fabrication plant downtime or geopolitical disruptions)?

7. For slower-moving markets that use aggregated monthly shipments, how does the company avoid overstock obsolescence while still controlling transportation and warehousing costs?

8. How do exchange-rate volatility and multi-currency invoicing affect procurement decisions, pricing strategy, and profit margins—and what hedging or adjustment policies exist?

9. How are demand forecasts validated in practice, and what happens operationally when machine learning predictions consistently diverge from realized sales?

10. What trade-offs are considered between minimizing carbon footprint (e.g., shipping mode and routing choices) and minimizing cost or delivery time?

11. How does the company decide between shipping directly to consumers versus routing through retail and regional fulfillment centers for VIP and pre-order customers?

12. During transportation, how are insurance coverage, damage tolerance, packaging standards, and carrier performance metrics aligned to reduce warranty-impacting incidents?

13. What is the governance approach for ensuring environmental and labor compliance across all suppliers and logistics partners, especially when enforcement standards differ by country?

14. How does the company manage warranty and after-sales support planning (service parts, repair capacity, and turnaround time) based on expected defect rates and regional demand?

15. In the event of a product recall, what decisions determine the scope, speed, and logistics approach—and how is the financial impact weighed against brand and safety obligations?

16. How does the digital system reconcile data inconsistencies across time zones (e.g., order confirmations, shipment scans, production completion signals) to prevent inventory “phantom availability”?

17. What role do buffer stocks play in absorbing variability (supplier delays, demand spikes, yield changes), and where is the buffer placed—components, WIP, or finished goods?

18. How does the company measure and improve end-to-end lead time visibility—from R&D design freeze through supplier delivery to retailer shelves?

19. What incentives or performance metrics are used to align procurement, manufacturing, logistics, and sales teams so that optimization in one area doesn’t harm another (e.g., cost minimization driving long lead times)?

20. How does the organization conduct continuous improvement (process redesign, supplier collaboration, automation upgrades) while minimizing operational disruption and maintaining compliance throughout the transition?
