**The DECLARE model describes a highly structured, compliance-driven loan approval process.** It enforces a linear, auditable workflow from intake to disbursement while prohibiting shortcuts, repetitions, or illogical sequences. All constraints have support and confidence of 1.0, meaning they are treated as mandatory rules in every valid execution trace.

### Step-by-Step Description of the Process

1. **Receive_Application** (mandatory starting point)  
   The process always begins here. A customer submits a loan application, triggering intake, basic data capture, and initial logging. The `init` and `existence` constraints guarantee this is the first activity and that it always occurs.

2. **Preliminary_Credit_Check** (exactly once, immediately after application receipt)  
   An automated or manual initial credit assessment (e.g., pulling credit scores, basic risk flags) is performed. The `response` constraint from Receive_Application and the `exactly_one` constraint ensure this happens exactly once and cannot be skipped or duplicated.

3. **Gather_Additional_Documents** (must occur after the credit check)  
   The applicant is asked to provide supporting documents (pay stubs, tax returns, ID, proof of income, etc.). The `succession` constraint from Preliminary_Credit_Check forces this step to follow the credit check. The `existence` constraint further requires it to occur in every valid trace. The `coexistence` constraint links this activity with Authorize_Contract_Terms: either both happen or neither does.

4. **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  
   Using the credit data and documents, staff or the system prepares the proposed loan terms, interest rate, conditions, and documentation package.

5. **Quality_Assurance_Review** (required if the offer package is assembled)  
   An independent review verifies that the offer complies with internal policies, regulatory requirements, and risk appetite. The `responded_existence` constraint from Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package guarantees that if the package is created, a QA review must also occur somewhere in the trace. The `precedence` constraint ensures the review happens before any authorization.

6. **Authorize_Contract_Terms** (only after QA review)  
   Formal approval or finalization of the loan terms occurs. The `precedence` constraint from Quality_Assurance_Review blocks authorization until QA is complete. The `coexistence` constraint with Gather_Additional_Documents ensures that if documents were collected, authorization is permitted (and vice versa).

7. **Transfer_Funds** (disbursement)  
   Once authorized, the approved amount is sent to the customer’s account. The `chainresponse` constraint from Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package creates a tight linkage to this step (emphasizing that assembly should lead directly toward execution once other gates are passed). The `altresponse` and `altprecedence` constraints between Transfer_Funds and Notify_Customer enforce that these two activities are tightly coupled—essentially requiring them to occur together in the expected order.

8. **Notify_Customer** (final communication)  
   The customer is informed of the outcome, next steps, or any conditions. The alternating response/precedence constraints ensure notification does not occur in isolation from the fund transfer.

**Forbidden activity**: `Proceed_Without_Compliance` is explicitly prohibited by the `absence` constraint. Any attempt to bypass the above sequence (e.g., disbursing funds without documentation or QA) is invalid.

Additional constraints (`noncoexistence`, `nonsuccession`, `nonchainsuccession`) act as guardrails:
- They prevent incompatible pairs (e.g., transferring funds in a way that effectively bypasses the original application receipt or jumping directly from early steps to notification).
- They block invalid immediate successions (e.g., authorizing terms and then immediately notifying without proper sequencing through later steps).

### How the Constraints Enforce Compliant, Logical Order

- **Ordering constraints** (`response`, `precedence`, `succession`, and their chain/alternate variants) create a directed flow: Receive  Preliminary Credit Check  Gather Documents  Assemble  QA Review  Authorize  Transfer  Notify.  
- **Cardinality constraints** (`existence`, `exactly_one`, `absence`) guarantee required steps happen the right number of times (or never).  
- **Coexistence / noncoexistence** ensure logical dependencies (documents and authorization are inseparable; certain activities cannot appear together at all).  
- **Responded existence** creates “if-then” obligations (assembling an offer triggers the need for QA).  
- These rules collectively prevent both skipping steps and performing them out of sequence.

### Real-World Motivations

- **Regulatory compliance**: Banks must follow KYC/AML rules, fair lending laws, and consumer protection regulations. Credit checks and document gathering are legally required; QA and authorization create auditable records.
- **Fraud prevention**: Early credit screening and mandatory document collection reduce identity fraud and straw-borrower schemes. The prohibition on “Proceed_Without_Compliance” directly targets insider or procedural fraud.
- **Risk management**: The preliminary credit check acts as a cheap filter to reject obviously unqualified applicants before consuming expensive resources (document review, underwriting). QA before authorization prevents systemic errors in pricing or terms.
- **Operational best practices**: Exactly-one credit check avoids redundant work and conflicting data. Succession and precedence rules create a repeatable, measurable process that supports automation and process mining.
- **Customer satisfaction and fairness**: Proper sequencing ensures applicants are not surprised by sudden requests or denials. Final notification after funds transfer (or decision) reduces confusion and complaints.

### Consequences of Violations

- **Omitting Preliminary_Credit_Check**: High-risk loans are approved, increasing defaults, regulatory fines (e.g., from CFPB or OCC), and capital reserve requirements.
- **Skipping or delaying Gather_Additional_Documents**: Incomplete underwriting leads to mispriced loans, higher loss rates, and potential fair-lending violations if decisions appear arbitrary.
- **Authorizing before Quality_Assurance_Review**: Defective offers reach customers, triggering lawsuits, reputational damage, and regulatory sanctions for inadequate controls.
- **Transferring funds without proper authorization or documents**: This can constitute unauthorized disbursement or even fraud. The bank faces clawback risks, customer disputes, and severe penalties under banking regulations.
- **Using Proceed_Without_Compliance**: Immediate compliance breach. Could result in consent orders, monetary penalties, executive liability, and loss of lending licenses in extreme cases.
- **Wrong order (e.g., notifying the customer before funds transfer or QA)**: Creates customer confusion, potential privacy or disclosure violations, and operational chaos (customers acting on incorrect information).
- **Repeating or skipping exactly-one activities**: Wastes resources or leaves gaps in the audit trail, complicating regulatory examinations.

In practice, this DECLARE model produces a conservative, heavily gated process typical of regulated financial institutions. Any deviation would be logged as a compliance exception and likely blocked by workflow systems enforcing these rules. The model prioritizes risk control and auditability over speed, which is common in lending but can feel bureaucratic to customers.
