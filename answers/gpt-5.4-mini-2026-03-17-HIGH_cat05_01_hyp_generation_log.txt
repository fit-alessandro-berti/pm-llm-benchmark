**Prompt**

You are a process mining and operational anomaly analyst.

You will be given PostgreSQL tables describing order-processing event logs and reference data. Your task is to analyze the event data and identify any anomalies, undesirable behaviors, or suspicious patterns in the process execution.

### Available tables
- `order_event_log(case_id, event_id, activity, timestamp, resource, additional_info)`
- `orders(case_id, customer_id, order_type, order_value)`
- `resources(resource_id, role, department)`

### What to do
1. Inspect the event log across all cases.
2. Identify deviations from normal or expected process behavior.
3. For each anomaly you find:
   - explain what looks unusual,
   - hypothesize why it may have happened,
   - and suggest relevant SQL queries to investigate further.
4. Use the `orders` and `resources` tables when useful to enrich your analysis.
5. Keep your reasoning grounded in the data. If something is uncertain, say so.
6. Do not rely on external assumptions; infer from the tables and observed sequences only.

### Output format
Please structure your response as follows:

1. **Anomaly Summary**
   - case_id
   - observed issue
   - why it is anomalous
   - possible impact

2. **Hypotheses**
   - likely cause(s) for each anomaly
   - whether the cause seems operational, technical, or policy-related

3. **SQL Investigation Queries**
   - Provide PostgreSQL SQL queries that can test each hypothesis
   - Use clear aliases and comments where helpful

### Important
- Focus on detecting patterns such as unusual event ordering, missing or repeated activities, suspicious timing, inconsistent resource usage, or other unexpected process behavior.
- Be precise and concise.
- Write SQL that is directly usable in PostgreSQL.
