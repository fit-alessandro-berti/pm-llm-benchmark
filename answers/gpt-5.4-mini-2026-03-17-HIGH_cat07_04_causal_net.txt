The image shows a **workflow / process model** for handling a request.

### Main diagram
- On the far left, the process starts with **“a: register request.”**
- The request can then go through several possible checking activities:
  - **b: examine thoroughly**
  - **c: examine casually**
  - **d: check ticket**
- These paths feed into **e: decide**.
- After the decision, the process branches to either:
  - **g: pay compensation**
  - **h: reject request**
- Both routes eventually lead to **z: end**.

There is also a **loop/rework step**:
- **f: reinitiate request**, which suggests that some cases can be sent back for another round of processing.

### Visual style
- The **rounded rectangles** with letters are the process steps.
- The **black dots and curved arrows** show the connections and branching logic between steps.
- The layout is a bit like a **Petri net / workflow net**, with multiple possible execution paths and merges.

### Inset legend
The gray box in the bottom-right explains different routing patterns:
- **XOR-split / XOR-join**: exactly one branch is chosen or merged
- **AND-split / AND-join**: all branches are taken or synchronized
- **OR-split / OR-join**: one or more branches may be taken or merged
