### Notes on construction
- **Backbone path**: `SS  OP  RC  QI  CA  PT  PK  WS  DT  AS`, treated as the dominant control flow, so every listed pair represents activities that eventually follow each other.
- **Averages** for non-consecutive pairs are the sums of the intervening segment averages (e.g., `('SS','RC') = SSOP + OPRC = 172800 + 604800 = 777600`).
- **Standard deviations** for multi-step pairs assume approximate independence between segments, so variances add and the resulting std is `sqrt( variance_i)` — this is why long-range pairs (e.g., `('SS','AS')`) show larger spread.
- **Dominant variability** comes from `OP  RC` (supplier lead time) and `DT  AS` (after-sales horizon), which is realistic for a global supply chain.

### Example deviation check
With `ZETA = 2`, for the pair `('OP','RC')` with `(AVG, STDEV) = (604800, 172800)`:
- Lower bound: `604800 - 2*172800 = 259200` (~3 days)
- Upper bound: `604800 + 2*172800 = 950400` (~11 days)

An observed lead time below ~3 days or above ~11 days would be flagged as a temporal deviation.
