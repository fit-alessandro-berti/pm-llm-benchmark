**The biased log is Group B (Unprotected Group log).**

## Why Group B exhibits bias
Group B gives candidates with the professional association **LocalBusinessLeadersClub** a **+5 CulturalFit boost** at the CulturalFitCheck and then carries that advantage into later stages and the final decision.

That means:
- Two candidates can have similar or even lower core qualifications,
- but the ones with the favored association receive an artificial advantage,
- increasing their likelihood of being hired.

This is a form of **systematic preferential treatment** tied to a non-job-relevant attribute.

## How the bias manifests
The bias appears in these ways:

1. **Score adjustment based on association**
   - U001 and U003 receive `+5 (Association Boost)`.
   - U002, who has `None`, receives no boost.
   - This changes the effective CulturalFit score and influences the downstream decision.

2. **Different hiring outcomes for comparable candidates**
   - In Group B, candidates with the club affiliation are hired (`U001`, `U003`).
   - The unaffiliated candidate with similar scores (`U002`) is not hired.
   - This suggests the association is affecting the result beyond merit-based criteria.

3. **Non-transparent advantage**
   - The extra boost is not tied to demonstrated skill, personality, or job performance.
   - It introduces a hidden preference that may not be fair or defensible.

## Comparison with Group A
Group A does **not** apply any association-based score boost:
- Decisions appear to depend on the recorded assessment scores without an extra favoritism mechanism.
- While Group A may still reflect ordinary selection differences, it does not show the same explicit association-based preference.

## Fairness and equity implications
This bias has important consequences:

- **Fairness risk:** Candidates outside the favored association are disadvantaged, even if they are equally qualified.
- **Equity issue:** Access to hiring opportunities becomes influenced by social/network membership rather than merit.
- **Potential discrimination:** If the association correlates with protected characteristics or access to privileged networks, the bias can indirectly reinforce unequal outcomes.
- **Reduced trust:** Applicants may view the hiring process as nepotistic or opaque.
- **Legal and ethical concerns:** Hiring decisions should generally be based on job-related criteria, not club membership unless it is clearly justified and applied consistently.

## Bottom line
**Group B is biased because it gives a selection advantage to candidates affiliated with a specific professional association.**  
That bias is visible in the score boost and in the resulting hiring decisions, which undermines fairness and equity in the hiring process.
