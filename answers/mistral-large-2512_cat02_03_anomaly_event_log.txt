### **Anomalies Identified:**

#### **1. Case ID 1002: Payment Released Before Goods Received and Invoice Matched**
**Anomalous Sequence:**
- `Issue Purchase Order`  `Vendor Invoice Received`  `Release Payment`  `Approve Purchase Requisition`  `Receive Goods`  `Match Invoice to PO`

**Problems:**
- **Payment before receipt of goods:**
  The payment (`Release Payment`) was made **before the goods were received** (`Receive Goods`). In standard P2P, payment should only occur **after** goods/services are received and verified to avoid fraud or overpayment.
  - *Risk:* The company may pay for goods that are never delivered or are defective.

- **Invoice received before goods:**
  The invoice was received (`Vendor Invoice Received`) **before the goods arrived**. This is unusual because invoices typically follow the receipt of goods (or at least the PO issuance).
  - *Risk:* The vendor may invoice for undelivered goods, leading to disputes or duplicate payments.

- **Approval after PO issuance and payment:**
  The `Approve Purchase Requisition` step occurred **after** the PO was issued and payment was released. Approval should happen **before** the PO is issued to ensure compliance and budget control.
  - *Risk:* Unauthorized purchases may slip through, violating internal controls.

#### **2. Case ID 1003: Payment Released Before Invoice Received or Goods Received**
**Anomalous Sequence:**
- `Issue Purchase Order`  `Release Payment`  `Vendor Invoice Received`  `Receive Goods`  `Match Invoice to PO`

**Problems:**
- **Payment before invoice or goods:**
  Payment was released **before the invoice was received or goods were delivered**. This violates the **3-way match** principle (PO, receipt, invoice) in P2P.
  - *Risk:* The company may pay for goods not yet delivered or invoiced, leading to cash flow issues or fraud.

- **Invoice received after payment:**
  The invoice arrived **after payment**, which is highly irregular. Invoices should be received **before or at the time of payment** to ensure accuracy.
  - *Risk:* The company may pay an incorrect amount or fail to detect discrepancies.

- **Goods received after payment:**
  Goods were received **after payment**, which is a major red flag. Payment should only occur after goods are verified.
  - *Risk:* The vendor may deliver substandard or incorrect goods after receiving payment.

#### **3. Case ID 1004: Goods Received Before Purchase Order Issued**
**Anomalous Sequence:**

**Problems:**
- **Goods received before PO issuance:**
  The goods were received (`Receive Goods`) **before the PO was issued** (`Issue Purchase Order`). This is a **critical violation** of P2P best practices.
  - *Risk:*

    - The vendor may charge higher prices or deliver unauthorized items.

- **Invoice received before PO matching:**
  The invoice was received **before the PO was issued**, which is impossible in a standard process. This suggests either:
  - A data entry error (e.g., incorrect timestamps).
  - A **maverick purchase** (unauthorized buying outside the P2P process).
  - *Risk:* The company may pay for goods not formally ordered, leading to disputes or overpayment.

### **Standard P2P Process Flow (Expected Sequence):**
A typical **Procure-to-Pay** process follows this order:
1. **Create Purchase Requisition** (request for goods/services)
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition** (budget/need validation)
3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)** (formal agreement with vendor)
4. **Receive Goods/Services** (physical/logistical verification)
5. **Vendor Invoice Received** (billing from vendor)
6. **Match Invoice to PO & Receipt (3-Way Match)** (verify accuracy)
7. **Release Payment** (settle invoice)
8. **Close Case** (archive records)

---

### **Recommendations to Address Anomalies:**
1. **Enforce 3-Way Matching:**
   - Ensure payment is only released after **PO, receipt, and invoice** are matched.
   - Implement automated controls in ERP systems (e.g., SAP, Oracle) to block payments without matching.

2. **Mandate Approval Before PO Issuance:**
   - Require **purchase requisition approval** before a PO can be issued.
   - Use workflow automation to prevent PO creation without approval.

3. **Audit Trail for Goods Receipt:**
   - Ensure goods are **never received before a PO is issued**.
   - Flag cases where receipt timestamps precede PO issuance for investigation.

4. **Invoice Validation:**
   - Invoices should **only be processed after goods are received**.
   - Reject invoices received before goods or without a PO.

5. **Fraud Detection:**
   - Monitor cases where payments are made **before goods/invoices** (e.g., Case 1002, 1003).
   - Investigate vendors involved in such transactions for potential collusion.

6. **Process Mining & Monitoring:**
   - Use **process mining tools** (e.g., Celonis, Disco) to detect deviations from the standard P2P flow.
   - Set up alerts for anomalous sequences (e.g., payment before receipt).

---

### **Conclusion:**
The anomalies in **Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004** violate core P2P principles, exposing the organization to **financial, operational, and compliance risks**. Addressing these issues requires **stricter controls, automation, and continuous monitoring** to ensure adherence to best practices.
