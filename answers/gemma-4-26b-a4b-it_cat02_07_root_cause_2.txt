### 2. Root Cause Analysis
By correlating the durations with the provided attributes, we can deduce the following:

#### **A. Complexity vs. Process Loops (Primary Driver)**
There is a direct, positive correlation between **Complexity** and **Duration**. 
* **Low Complexity** cases (2001, 2004) do not trigger the "Request Additional Documents" activity and are completed in under 2 hours.
* **Medium Complexity** cases (2002) trigger the document request once, extending the time to ~26 hours.
* **High Complexity** cases (2003, 2005) trigger multiple document requests (2 and 3 times respectively), extending the duration to 48–77 hours.
* **Conclusion:** The "Request Additional Documents" activity acts as a process loop that prevents the claim from moving toward "Approve Claim."

#### **B. Resource Analysis**
While different resources handle different cases, the delay is not strictly tied to a "slow" person, but rather to the **type of work** they are assigned:
* **Adjuster_Lisa** handles both a fast case (2004) and the slowest case (2005).
* **Adjuster_Mike** handles both a fast case (2001) and a slow case (2003).
* **Conclusion:** The resource is not the root cause; rather, the resource's effectiveness is being hampered by the high frequency of document requests required for complex claims.

#### **C. Regional Analysis**
* **Region A** contains both the fastest (2001) and a slow case (2003).
* **Region B** contains both the fastest (2004) and the slowest case (2005).
* **Conclusion:** **Region** is not a significant factor in the performance variance.

### 3. Explanations and Mitigation Strategies

#### **Root Cause 1: Incomplete Initial Submissions for Complex Claims**
* **Explanation:** The "Request Additional Documents" loop suggests that the "Submit Claim" stage is not capturing enough information for high-complexity cases. This creates a "ping-pong" effect between the adjuster and the customer, where each request for a new document triggers a new wait period.
* **Mitigation:** 
    * **Smart Intake Forms:** Implement dynamic digital forms at the "Submit Claim" stage. If a user selects a "High Complexity" claim type, the system should automatically mandate the upload of specific documents (e.g., police reports, medical records) before the claim can even be submitted.
    * **Pre-validation:** Use automated tools to check if uploaded files meet the required criteria (format, completeness) before they reach the Adjuster.

#### **Root Cause 2: High "Review Lag" After Document Receipt**
* **Explanation:** Looking at Case 2005, there is a significant gap between the last "Request Additional Documents" (April 3rd, 15:00) and the "Approve Claim" (April 4th, 10:00). This indicates that once the documents are finally received, they sit in a queue before being reviewed.
* **Mitigation:**
    * **Priority Queueing:** Implement a rule where any case that has already gone through one or more "Request Additional Documents" cycles is moved to the top of the Adjuster's priority list to prevent further delays.
    * **SLA Monitoring:** Set strict Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for the "Evaluate Claim" step specifically for cases that have pending incoming documentation.

#### **Root Cause 3: Adjuster Workload Management**
* **Explanation:** Adjusters are likely spending a disproportionate amount of time managing administrative follow-ups (document requests) rather than performing actual evaluations.
* **Mitigation:**
    * **Automated Follow-ups:** Use automated email/SMS triggers to follow up with customers regarding missing documents, reducing the manual administrative burden on Adjusters like Lisa and Mike.
