**Identified Anomalies in the Event Log**

The standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) process follows a logical, controlled sequence designed to enforce internal controls, segregation of duties, prevent fraud, ensure proper authorization, and enable verification (e.g., three-way matching of PO, goods receipt, and invoice). The typical flow is:

1. Create Purchase Requisition (PR)  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Issue Purchase Order (PO) to vendor  
4. Receive Goods / Services  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Match Invoice to PO (and goods receipt)  
7. Release Payment (only after successful matching)  
8. Close Case

Deviations from this sequence can indicate control weaknesses, recording errors, bypassing of approvals, or fraudulent activity (e.g., unauthorized purchases, payments for non-received goods, or kickback schemes).

### Case 1001
**Sequence**: Normal / compliant. Follows the standard order with reasonable timestamps (PR approval  PO  goods receipt  invoice  matching  payment). No anomalies.

### Case 1002 (Multiple serious anomalies)
- **Issue PO immediately after Create PR, before Approve PR**.  
- **Release Payment occurs before Approve PR, before Receive Goods, and before Match Invoice to PO**.  
- **Approve PR occurs *after* PO issuance, invoice receipt, and even after payment**.

**Why problematic**: This completely bypasses authorization controls. Issuing a PO and making payment without PR approval violates segregation of duties and creates significant fraud risk (e.g., an employee could create and pay for fictitious orders). Paying before goods are received and before matching increases the risk of paying for undelivered or incorrect items. The late approval looks like an after-the-fact rubber-stamping, undermining the entire control framework.

### Case 1003 (Major timing violation)
- **Release Payment occurs right after Issue PO, but *before* Vendor Invoice Received, before Receive Goods, and before Match Invoice to PO** (payment on Feb 8; goods and invoice arrive days later).

**Why problematic**: Payment without confirmed receipt of goods or an invoice is highly unusual in standard P2P for goods (this would only be acceptable in rare, pre-approved prepayment scenarios with strong justification and documentation). It prevents proper three-way matching and creates risks of paying for goods that are never delivered, incorrect quantities, or poor quality. The subsequent late receipt and matching suggest the payment was made without verification, increasing financial exposure and compliance issues (e.g., with audit or SOX requirements).

### Case 1004 (Clear sequencing error)
- **Receive Goods *before* Issue Purchase Order** (goods received on Feb 6 at 09:00; PO issued at 09:15 the same day).

**Why problematic**: Receiving goods against a non-existent PO defeats the purpose of the purchase order as a formal commitment and control document. This suggests either an emergency/uncontrolled purchase (maverick buying) or backdated PO creation, which hides the lack of prior authorization. It makes proper matching difficult and increases risks of unauthorized or unfavorable pricing/terms. Vendors should not deliver without a PO in a well-controlled process.

### Summary of Risks
These anomalies (especially in Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004) point to weaknesses in process enforcement, potentially indicating:
- Lack of system-level controls (e.g., workflow that allows PO issuance or payment without prior approvals).
- Manual overrides or workarounds.
- Possible fraud or error (unauthorized purchases, payments for non-receipts).
- Increased financial, operational, and compliance risks (e.g., overpayments, unrecorded liabilities, audit findings).

In a real investigation, these cases would warrant further review, such as checking supporting documents, user IDs who performed each step, and vendor relationships. Case 1001 demonstrates that the process *can* run normally, highlighting that the issues are not universal but case-specific deviations.
