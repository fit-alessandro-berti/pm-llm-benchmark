1) Normative rule violations

Case 1  
No violations.  
Sequence followed exactly:  
Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case.  
All ordering constraints satisfied and documentation finalized only after approval.

Case 2  
Violates multiple normative rules.

• Risk Assessment before Credit Check  
  - Event order: Application Received  Risk Assessment  Credit Check  
  - Violates rule: Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment.

• Finalize Documentation after a rejection  
  - Loan Decision: Rejected  
  - Yet “Finalize Documentation” is executed.  
  - Violates rule: Documentation must not be finalized when the loan is rejected.

• Decision logic inconsistency  
  - For rejected loans the correct path should be:  
    Loan Decision (Rejected)  Inform Applicant  Close Case.  
  - Instead: Finalize Documentation  Close Case.

Case 3  
No normative violations.  
All mandatory ordering rules are respected:  
Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case.

No timing-related desiderata violations.

3) Possible interpretations or justifications

Case 1  
Represents an ideal or well-controlled process execution. Automated workflow systems or strong internal compliance controls may enforce the correct order and ensure quick communication.

Case 2  
Possible explanations:  
• Data entry or logging error where the credit check actually occurred earlier but was recorded later.  
• Parallel processing where risk analysts started evaluation prematurely before the formal credit check was logged.  
• Administrative mistake where documentation finalization was triggered automatically despite rejection.  
• Missing “Inform Applicant” step may indicate it occurred outside the tracked system (e.g., phone call not logged).

Case 3  
Behavior suggests operational delays rather than process violations.

Possible reasons:  
• Credit check delay due to backlog, external credit bureau availability, or manual verification.  
• Delay in informing the applicant due to internal approval reviews or communication scheduling.  
• Followup call omitted because the approval and documentation process proceeded smoothly and additional contact was deemed unnecessary.

Summary  
• Normative violations: Case 2.  
• Desiderata deviations: primarily Case 3 (timing delays and missing followup call), and optionally the missing followup in Case 1 and 2.  
• Case 1 is fully compliant with normative rules and mostly aligned with recommended practices.
