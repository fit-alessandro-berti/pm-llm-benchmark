### Key Insights from Aggregation

*   **Resource Handoffs:** The high-level steps clearly delineate where responsibility shifts: **Operator A** (Setup)  **Automation** (Pre-processing)  **Operator B** (Welding)  **Automation/Sensors** (Inspection/Coating)  **Operator C** (Final Check). This helps identify handoff points for potential optimization.
*   **Quality Gates:** The aggregation highlights two distinct quality gates: **Weld Quality Inspection** and **Final Inspection**. This allows for focused analysis on defect rates at specific stages.
*   **Process Duration:** By aggregating, we can analyze the cycle time of macro-steps. For example, the gap between "Preheat" and "Pick up welding tool" may represent a bottleneck or idle time that is less visible at the low level.
*   **Standardization:** This structure enables the creation of a standard process model. Deviations (e.g., a case skipping "Preheat" or adding an extra "Visual check") can be easily detected by comparing event sequences against this high-level model.

This structured representation transforms a granular, noisy log into a clear, actionable manufacturing workflow, facilitating process mining, bottleneck analysis, and operational efficiency improvements.
