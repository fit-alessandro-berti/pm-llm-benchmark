**Conclusion:** Cases **102, 104, and 105** take significantly longer than average to resolve. While Cases 101 and 103 are resolved in a matter of hours, Cases 102, 104, and 105 stretch over multiple days.

### 2. Root Causes of Performance Issues

By examining the time elapsed between specific activities in the slower cases, three primary factors are driving the delays:

**A. The "Investigate to Resolve" Overnight Bottleneck**
In all three slow cases (102, 104, 105), there is a massive gap between the "Investigate Issue" step and the "Resolve Ticket" step. 
*   Case 104: 19 hours (13:00 to next day 08:00)
*   Case 102: 19 hours (14:00 to next day 09:00)
*   Case 105: 19 hours (14:00 to next day 09:00)
This consistent 19-hour gap strongly suggests an operational bottleneck. It appears that if an issue is investigated in the afternoon, the resolution is not executed until the next business day. This could be due to agents working standard office hours and failing to resolve tickets before logging off, or a systemic batch-processing delay where resolutions are only pushed through the system the following morning.

**B. Escalation Handoff Delays**
Cases 102 and 105 involve an escalation to a Level-2 Agent, which introduces massive waiting times:
*   **Case 102:** 2 hours 30 minutes between Assign to Level-1 and Escalate to Level-2, followed by a 2.5-hour wait before the Level-2 agent begins investigation.

Delayed triage pushes the entire lifecycle of the ticket later into the day, increasing the likelihood that the ticket will hit the "Investigate to Resolve" overnight bottleneck mentioned above.

### 3. Impact on Cycle Times & Recommendations

**Impact Explanation:**
The combination of poor shift-handoff practices and unstructured escalation workflows creates a compounding effect on cycle times. A ticket that takes 2 hours of actual work can easily stretch to 24+ hours if it hits the end of a shift or gets stuck in a Level-2 queue. Furthermore, delays in triage cause a "snowball effect," pushing tickets closer to the end-of-day cutoff, which guarantees an overnight delay.

**Recommendations to Address Bottlenecks:**

1.  **Implement End-of-Day Resolution Protocols (Addressing the 19-hour gap):**
    *   *Action:* Institute a policy where agents must resolve, update, or hand off any ticket they are actively investigating before the end of their shift. Leaving tickets in an "Investigating" state overnight is the single biggest driver of excess cycle time.

3.  **Automate or Streamline Triage (Addressing initial delays):**
    *   *Action:* Utilize automated ticket routing based on keywords or customer input to bypass manual triage for common issues.
