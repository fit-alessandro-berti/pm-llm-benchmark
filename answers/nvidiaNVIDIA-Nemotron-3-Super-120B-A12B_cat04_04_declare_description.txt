**Loanapproval process as read from the DECLARE model**  
Below is a stepbystep “story” of what a typical loanapplication looks like when it is forced to obey every constraint that appears in the model.  After each step I note which constraint(s) make that step necessary or forbidden, and I explain the business reason (regulatory, riskmanagement, fraudprevention, etc.) behind the rule.  Finally I discuss what would happen if a step were moved out of its prescribed place or omitted altogether.

---

## 1.  **Receive_Application**  
*What happens:* The lender’s frontoffice (online portal, callcenter, or branch) captures the borrower’s basic loan request – amount, purpose, personal identifiers, and any attached supporting files that the borrower voluntarily uploads.  

*Why it must occur first:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `init` – Receive_Application | Marks the activity as a possible startpoint of the process. | The process cannot begin anywhere else. |
| `existence` – Receive_Application | The activity must **occur** (at least once) in every trace. | Guarantees that a loan file is opened before anything else can be done. |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | Transfer_Funds **cannot coexist** with Receive_Application (i.e., the two may not be active at the same time). | Prevents money from being sent out while the application is still being taken in – a basic fraudprevention safeguard. |
| `nonchainsuccession` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer | Authorize_Contract_Terms may **not** be a chainsuccessor of Notify_Customer. | Guarantees that the customer is not notified *before* the contract has been authorized. |

*Business rationale:*  The application is the trigger for every downstream control (creditcheck, documentation, underwriting, etc.).  Opening the file first creates an immutable audit trail, satisfies KYC/AML “knowyourcustomer” rules, and makes it impossible to disburse funds before the borrower’s identity has been captured.

---

## 2.  **Preliminary_Credit_Check**  
*What happens:* A quick, automated or semiautomated scorebased check is run against the applicant’s credit bureau data (or internal scoring model).  The result is a binary “pass/fail” (or a score band) that tells the underwriter whether the applicant merits a deeper look.  

*Why it must come **after** the application but **before** document collection:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `response` – Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check | Every time Receive_Application occurs, a Preliminary_Credit_Check must follow (directly, as the next activity in the trace). | Forces the credit check to be the *first* substantive step after the app is logged. |
| `exactly_one` – Preliminary_Credit_Check | The activity may appear **once and only once** in a trace. | Prevents the model from looping the check multiple times (which would be wasteful and could generate conflicting scores). |
| `succession` – Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents | The check must **precede** the gathering of any extra documents. | Guarantees that the underwriter does not ask for paystubs, tax returns, etc., before knowing whether the applicant’s credit score even qualifies them for the product. |
| `nonsuccession` – Notify_Customer  Preliminary_Credit_Check | Notify_Customer may **not** be a successor of the credit check. | Blocks a premature “we’ve decided” notice to the customer before the underwriter has even seen the credit result. |
| `chainprecedence` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Preliminary_Credit_Check | Contract authorization must happen **after** the credit check. | Makes sure no loan terms are signed off before the bank knows the applicant’s creditworthiness. |

*Business rationale:*  Credit bureaus are the primary source of risk information.  Running the check early saves the underwriter time (no need to collect documents for an obviously ineligible applicant) and satisfies regulatory expectations that a *preassessment* of credit risk be performed before any underwriting work begins.  The “exactly_one” rule also prevents duplicate hardpulls that could unnecessarily hurt the borrower’s score.

---

## 3.  **Gather_Additional_Documents**  
*What happens:* The loan officer (or an automated documentcollection engine) requests and receives the specific pieces of evidence required for the product – e.g., recent pay stubs, W2s, tax returns, bank statements, proof of collateral, insurance certificates, etc.  Each document is indexed and stored in the loan file.  

*Why it must follow the credit check but precede the offer assembly:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `existence` – Gather_Additional_Documents | The activity must **occur** (at least once) in every trace. | Guarantees that no loan can be offered without the required paperwork. |
| `succession` – Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents | (see above) The check must happen first. | Prevents the underwriter from wasting time on applicants who already failed the credit screen. |
| `coexistence` – Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms | The two activities may **cooccur** (i.e., they are not forced into a strict linear order by this edge). | Allows some parallelism – e.g., while the underwriter is reviewing the credit score, the ops team can be gathering documents in the background.  The model does *not* require them to be sequential, only that both happen before the next gating step (see below). |
| `chainprecedence` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Preliminary_Credit_Check | (see above) Authorization must be after the check; this indirectly forces the docs to be done before authorization because the next gating step (Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package) needs the docs. | Implements the rule “no offer can be built without the supporting evidence.” |
| `altresponse` – Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer | (see later) Ensures that the borrower is informed **after** money moves, not while docs are still being collected. | Prevents premature “your loan is approved” notices that could confuse the borrower before the file is complete. |

*Business rationale:*  Documentation is the evidentiary backbone of underwriting.  Collecting it after a positive credit check satisfies the “capacity” and “collateral” legs of the threeC’s of credit (Character, Capacity, Collateral).  It also meets regulatory documentationretention rules (e.g., SR117A, RESPA) that require the lender to keep a copy of every supporting file for the life of the loan.

---

## 4.  **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  
*What happens:* Using the data gathered so far (credit score, documents, product rules, pricing matrices), the underwriter or a ruleengine builds the concrete loan offer – interest rate, term, fees, amortization schedule, any special conditions (e.g., prepayment penalties, covenants).  The output is a **loan offer package** that will be presented to the borrower for acceptance.  

*Why it must happen after docs and before QA:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `chainresponse` – Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds | After the offer is assembled, the next *guaranteed* step is the transfer of funds (once the offer is accepted and all approvals are in place). | Prevents money from moving before the offer is even constructed. |
| `chainsuccession` – Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package | QA review must **precede** the assembly of the offer. | Guarantees that a second pair of eyes checks the offer before it is sent to the borrower. |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | (see earlier) Reinforces that no disbursement can happen while the application is still being taken. | Adds a safety net against premature funding. |
| `altprecedence` – Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds | (see later) The notification must happen **before** the funds move (i.e., the borrower is told the loan is approved **first**, then the money is sent). | Aligns with consumerprotection rules that require clear approval communication prior to disbursement. |

*Business rationale:*  The offer is the contractual manifestation of the underwriter’s decision.  Assembling it after the documents are verified ensures that the offer reflects the true risk profile.  The QA step (see next) is an internal control that catches pricing errors, missing fees, or policy violations before the borrower ever sees the offer – a key element of modelrisk management and fairlending compliance.

---

## 5.  **Quality_Assurance_Review**  
*What happens:* A separate underwriting or compliance specialist (or an automated rulechecker) reviews the assembled offer.  They verify that:  

* the interest rate complies with the bank’s pricelist and any applicable usury limits,  
* all required disclosures (APR, finance charge, total payments) are present and correct,  
* any covenants or collateral requirements are correctly documented,  
* the offer matches the producttype rules approved by the risk committee, and  
* no redflags (e.g., unusually high DTI, missing insurance) remain.  

If any issue is found, the offer is sent back to the underwriter for correction; otherwise it is marked “approved” and passed on.

*Why it must happen before assembly and before authorization:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `chainsuccession` – Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package | QA must **precede** the assembly of the offer. | Guarantees that the offer is checked *before* it is shown to the customer. |
| `precedence` – Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms | QA must **precede** contract authorization. | Stops the loan from being signed off until the QA team signs off. |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | (see earlier) Reinforces that no money moves while the application is still open – QA is a checkpoint that occurs well before any funds leave. |
| `altprecedence` – Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds | (see later) The borrower must be notified **before** funds move; the QA step occurs even earlier, so the notice is based on a fully vetted offer. | Aligns with TRID/RESPA timing rules that require the consumer to receive the Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure *before* settlement. |

*Business rationale:*  QA is the “second pair of eyes” that catches human or model error.  It satisfies internal modelrisk governance (e.g., SR117B) and external fairlending statutes (ECOA, Fair Housing) by ensuring that no discriminatory or incorrect terms slip through.  It also fulfills the consumerprotection requirement that the borrower receives a correct, compliant offer before any commitment is made.

---

## 6.  **Authorize_Contract_Terms**  
*What happens:* A senior loan officer, loan committee, or automated approval engine signs off on the final loan contract.  The authorization creates a legally binding agreement between the bank and the borrower (subject to the borrower’s acceptance).  The output is an “approved loan contract” ready for funding.  

*Why it must happen after QA and before fund transfer:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `precedence` – Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms | QA must precede authorization. | Prevents a contract from being signed off on an unverified offer. |
| `chainprecedence` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Preliminary_Credit_Check | Authorization must follow the credit check (indirectly, via the QA>assembly>authorization chain). | Guarantees that creditworthiness is evaluated *before* the contract is binding. |
| `nonchainsuccession` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer | Authorization may **not** be a successor of the customer notice. | Stops the bank from telling the customer “your loan is approved” *before* the contract has actually been authorized. |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | (see earlier) Reinforces that no money moves while the application is still open – authorization occurs well before any disbursement. | Aligns with the legal principle that a contract must be executed (signed) before performance (funding) can begin. |

*Business rationale:*  Authorization is the point at which the bank assumes the legal obligation to lend.  Doing it after QA satisfies the bank’s internal creditapproval policy (often a “foureyes” rule) and external regulations that require a formal credit decision before the loan is funded (e.g., Regulation B, SR117C).  It also prevents the bank from being liable for a loan that was never properly approved.

---

## 7.  **Transfer_Funds**  
*What happens:* The bank’s funding wire (ACH, FedWire, internal ledger move) credits the borrower’s account with the agreedupon loan principal.  The transaction is recorded, and the loan status changes from *approved* to *outstanding*.  

*Why it must happen after authorization and before customer notification:*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `chainresponse` – Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds | After the offer is assembled (and approved), the next guaranteed step is the fund transfer. | Ensures money moves only after a concrete, approved offer exists. |
| `altresponse` – Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer | The transfer **must** be followed by a customer notification. | Guarantees the borrower is told that the money has arrived (or is on its way). |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | (see earlier) Prevents any disbursement while the application is still being taken. | Stops premature funding – a basic fraud control. |
| `altprecedence` – Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds | The notice must precede the transfer (i.e., the borrower is informed **first**, then the money moves). | Matches consumerprotection rules that require the borrower to receive a “funding confirmation” *before* the funds are considered settled (e.g., Regulation Z timing for early disbursement notices). |
| `nonchainsuccession` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer | Authorization may not be a successor of the notice. | Prevents the bank from saying “your loan is approved” *after* the money has already moved (would be misleading). |

*Business rationale:*  Moving money is the point of no return for the lender.  Doing it after formal authorization satisfies Regulation B (which requires a credit decision before extension of credit) and the bank’s internal funding policy (often a dualcontrol: one party authorizes, a second party releases the wire).  Notifying the borrower before or immediately after the transfer meets TRID/RESPA and Regulation Z timing rules that require a “Closing Disclosure” and a “Funding Confirmation” to be received by the consumer no later than the business day after settlement.  It also gives the borrower a clear audit trail should they need to dispute the transaction later.

---

## 8.  **Notify_Customer**  
*What happens:* The lender sends the borrower a final communication – typically an approvedloan letter, an email, or a portal notification – that states:  

* the loan has been funded (or is scheduled to fund on a specific date),  
* the principal amount, interest rate, term, and first payment date,  
* how to access the loan account, and  
* any nextsteps (e.g., setting up autopay, receiving the first statement).  

*Why it must be the final step (or at least after the funds have moved):*  

| Constraint | Meaning | Effect on ordering |
|------------|---------|--------------------|
| `altresponse` – Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer | Transfer must be **followed** by notification. | Guarantees the borrower hears about the outcome **after** the money has moved. |
| `altprecedence` – Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds | Notification must **precede** the transfer (in some model variants). | In practice, the bank sends a prefunding “approval” notice, then the funds move, then a postfunding “confirmation” notice; the model captures both possibilities but enforces that *some* notice surrounds the transfer. |
| `nonchainsuccession` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer | Authorization may **not** be a successor of the notice. | Stops the bank from telling the customer the loan is approved *before* the contract is actually signed. |
| `noncoexistence` – Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application | (see earlier) Reinforces that no money moves while the application is still open – the notice therefore cannot appear before the app is closed. | Matches the idea that you cannot be told “your loan is funded” before you have even finished applying. |
| `chainprecedence` – Authorize_Contract_Terms  Preliminary_Credit_Check | (see earlier) Ensures that any notice about approval cannot precede the credit check. | Guarantees the borrower is not misled about their credit standing before it has been evaluated. |

*Business rationale:*  The final notice is the consumerprotection capstone.  It satisfies Regulation Z (TruthinLending) and Regulation B (which require clear, timely communication of credit terms and funding status).  It also completes the audit trail for internal compliance and external examiners – every action from application to funding is recorded, and the borrower receives a corroborating message that matches the recorded state of the loan.

### Putting It All Together – The “HappyPath” Trace

```
Receive_Application
    Preliminary_Credit_Check
           Gather_Additional_Documents
                 Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package
                       Quality_Assurance_Review
                             Authorize_Contract_Terms
                                   Transfer_Funds
                                         Notify_Customer
```

*Optional parallelism* (allowed by `coexistence` edges):  
- While the preliminary credit check is running, the ops team can start gathering documents in the background.  
- While the offer is being assembled, the QA team can begin its review of any preliminal data (e.g., verifying that the producttype rules are still current).  

*Forbidden patterns* (edges labeled **non**):  
- No Transfer_Funds while the application is still being taken (`noncoexistence`).  
- No Customer Notification before the Tertiary Credit Check or the Contract Authorization (`nonsuccession`, `nonchainsuccession`).  
- No duplicate Preliminary Credit Check (`exactly_one`).  
- No moving money before the loan contract is authorized (`nonchainsuccession` on Authorization  Notify).  
- No authorizing a contract before the QA review has signed off (`precedence`).

### Why the Constraints Make Sense – RealWorld Motivations

| Constraint Cluster | Typical Regulation / Policy | What It Prevents |
|--------------------|----------------------------|------------------|
| `init` + `existence` (Receive_Application) | KYC/AML, SR117A (recordkeeping) | Starts a trace with a verified borrower identity; prevents “ghost” applications. |
| `response` + `exactly_one` + `succession` (Credit Check  Docs) | Regulation B (credit decision before extending credit), Fair Lending (ECOA) | Guarantees a credit decision is made *once* and before any underwriting work, avoiding wasted work and discriminatory “prescreening”. |
| `coexistence` (Docs  Auth) | Internal “parallel processing” allowance | Lets front and backoffice work streams run concurrently for efficiency while still respecting downstream gates. |
| `chainresponse` + `altresponse` (Offer  Transfer  Notice) | Regulation Z (Timing of disclosures), SR117B (modelrisk) | Money moves only after a concrete offer exists and is verified; the borrower is told the outcome right after the funds move (or shortly before, per the bank’s chosen notice style). |
| `precedence` + `chainprecedence` (QA  Auth  Init) | Internal creditapproval policy (foureyes), SR117C (approval workflow) | Guarantees a senior/qualified pair of eyes signs off *after* modelrisk and compliance checks. |
| `noncoexistence` (Transfer  Receive) | Fraud prevention, ACH returnrisk rules | Stops money from leaving the lender while the borrower’s file is still incomplete – a classic “authorizedpushfraud” block. |
| `nonsuccession` + `nonchainsuccession` (Notify  Credit/Auth) | Consumerprotection (TRID/RESPA) timing | Prevents premature “approved” or “funded” messages that could confuse the borrower or induce reliance on incomplete information. |
| Alternative notification ordering (`altprecedence` / `altresponse`) | Regulatory flexibility (some banks send a prefunding approval notice, others a postfunding confirmation) | Allows the institution to pick the notice timing that matches its internal workflow and the applicable consumerprotection rule, while still guaranteeing that *some* notice surrounds the transfer. |

### What Happens If the Order Is Violated?

| Violated Step | Likely Immediate Effect | Potential Regulatory / Business Consequence |
|---------------|------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Transfer_Funds occurs before Receive_Application is completed** | Money wired to a customer who has not finished providing ID or loan purpose. | AML/KYC violation – possible Suspicious Activity Report (SAR), fines, and reputational damage. Also, the loan may be unenforceable because the contract never existed. |
| **Transfer_Funds occurs before Preliminary_Credit_Check** | Loan extended to a borrower whose credit is unknown or deficient. | Violation of Regulation B (credit decision prior to credit) – enforcement actions, civil liability if the loan later defaults and the borrower claims they were misled about their creditworthiness. |
| **Transfer_Funds occurs before Gather_Additional_Documents** | Funds disbursed without proof of income, collateral, or other required documentation. | Breaks supervisory guidance on loan file completeness (SR117A); can result in classification as “documentation exception” and higher regulatory capital addons. Also increases risk of fraud (false income statements). |
| **Transfer_Funds occurs before Quality_Assurance_Review** | An offer with a mispriced rate, missing APR disclosure, or noncompliant covenant is funded. | Modelrisk or fairlending violation – potential fair lending claim, penalty under ECOA/FHA, and the need to recoffer or buy back the loan. |
| **Transfer_Funds occurs before Authorize_Contract_Terms** | The bank loans money on an offer that has never been formally approved. | Breaks internal creditauthorization policy and could be deemed an *unauthorized extension of credit* – a serious safetyandsoundness finding, possible enforcement action, and lossgivendefault increase because the loan lacks proper underwriting. |
| **Notify_Customer occurs before Preliminary_Credit_Check** | Customer told “your loan is approved” before the bank even knows their credit score. | Misleading advertisement – violation of TRID/RESPA and BBB advertising standards; can lead to customer complaints, potential classaction alleging deceptive practices. |
| **Notify_Customer occurs before Tissue Transfer_Funds (i.e., notice sent but money not yet moved)** | Borrower believes funds are in hand when they are not yet available. | Could cause the borrower to incur overdraft fees if they spent based on a premature notice; also a breach of RegZ timing requirements for the Closing Disclosure/Funding Confirmation. |
| **Duplicate Preliminary Credit Check** | Two hard pulls on the borrower’s credit file. | Unnecessary impact on the borrower’s credit score (could lower it and affect pricing on other products) and may be viewed as a “harassing” credit inquiry under FCRA. |
| **Notify_Customer occurs after Transfer_Funds but with a contradictory message (e.g., notice says “denied” after money moved)** | blatant operational error – funds sent despite a denial. | Serious fraud event, potential for recovery action, and significant reputational harm; also a clear violation of internal control segregation of duties. |

### Bottom Line

- The DECLARE model encodes a **linear, gated flow**: *application  credit check  docs  offer assembly  QA  contract authorization  funding  customer notification*.  
- **Parallelism** is expressly limited to those pairs that the model tags as `coexistence` (e.g., docs can be gathered while the credit check runs). Every other ordering is enforced by a `succession`, `precedence`, `chainresponse`, or similar edge, which means the next activity cannot start until the previous one has finished.  
- **Prohibitions** (`non**`) make explicit the activity combinations that would break regulatory, riskmanagement, or consumerprotection rules (e.g., moving money before the borrower is identified, or telling the borrower the loan is approved before the credit decision).  
- The **business motivations** behind every edge are rooted in:  
  * KnowYourCustomer / AML rules (first step, no money before ID).  
  * Reg B / fair lending (credit decision before extending credit).  
  * Documentation and supervisory expectations (SR117A).  
  * Modelrisk / internal validation (QA before offer and authorization).  
  * Legal enforceability of the contract (authorization before performance/money).  
  * Timing and disclosure obligations under TRID/RESPA and Regulation Z (notice around funding).  

If any edge were ignored, the lender would be exposed to regulatory sanctions, heightened creditoroperational risk, potential fairlending or UDAP claims, and—most importantly—loss of trust from the borrowers whose loan journey would no longer be transparent, correct, or predictable.  The DECLARE model, therefore, is not just a bureaucratic flowchart; it is a concise expression of the control environment that keeps consumerlending safe, compliant, and sound.
