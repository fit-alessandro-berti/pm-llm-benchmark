**BPMNstyle Textual Process Model – Invoice Approval Workflow**

*The model below captures the main flow described by the interviewees (Mary–AP Clerk, Dan–Purchasing Officer, Carlos–Department Manager, Rita–AP Supervisor) and highlights known decision points, alternate/exception paths, and the roles/participants involved.  Where interview accounts differ slightly, the uncertainty is noted in brackets.*

---

### **1. Overview of Participants / Pools**

| Role (Pool)                | Primary Responsibilities in This Process |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Accounts Payable Clerk** (Mary – *AP*)   | Receives invoice, validates PO existence, logs into system, forwards to Purchasing, matches lineitems, escalates exceptions, schedules payment. |
| **Purchasing Officer** (Dan)               | Checks receipt confirmation, compares PO vs. invoice details, returns discrepancies or sends confirmation back to AP. |
| **Department Manager** (Carlos)            | Reviews final matched invoice in the system; approves or rejects based on budget/accuracy; may request further investigation. |
| **AP Supervisor** (Rita)                   | Handles exceptions that cannot be resolved at clerk level (missing PO, informal contracts, supplier disputes). Coordinates with suppliers via Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen). |
| **Supplier Relationship Manager** (Karen)  | Acts as liaison with external suppliers to resolve missingPO or pricing discrepancies; provides contractbased price guidance. |

*The BPMN diagram would be organized into these pools; the flow below shows the sequence of activities and gateways within each pool.*

### **2. Process Flow – Activities, Sequence Flows & Gateways**

```
[Start] (Invoice Arrival)
   |
   v
(1) AP Clerk: “Receive Invoice”  --[via Email]-->  
   |
   v
(2) Decision Gateway – *Has a Purchase Order?*
         Yes  Continue to step (3)
         No   Exception Path (see §4)

(When PO exists)
   |
   v
(3) AP Clerk: “Log Invoice into Accounting System”
   |
   v
(4) AP Clerk: “Forward Invoice & PO Reference”  Purchasing Department
   |
   v
(5) Purchasing Officer: “Verify Receipt Confirmation”
         If receipt confirmed  proceed to (6)
         If not confirmed  loop back:
                Purchasing informs AP (“Awaiting confirmation”) 
                AP may hold invoice until receipt is documented.
   |
   v
(6) Decision Gateway – *Invoice Matches PO?*
         Yes  Continue to step (7)
         No   Exception Path (see §4)

(When match succeeds)
   |
   v
(7) AP Clerk: “Match LineItems & Quantities” (system validation)
   |
   v
(8) System Action: “Notify Department Manager – Approval Request”
   |
   v
(9) Decision Gateway – *Manager Review*
         Approve  proceed to (10)
         Reject / Request Clarification  loop back:
                • If rejection with comments, AP Clerk contacts Purchasing/Supervisor.
                • If clarification needed, AP may rematch or request updated invoice.

(When approved)
   |
   v
(10) AP Clerk: “Schedule Invoice for Payment” (add to next payment run)
   |
   v
[End – Invoice Paid]

```

### **3. Exception / Alternate Paths**

| # | Trigger (as described)                     | Path Description (textual BPMN steps) |
|---|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **A** | *Invoice arrives without a PO* (Mary, Dan) | 1  **(2No)**  **AP Supervisor (Rita)** is notified. Rita either: <br>• Requests the missing PO from the supplier via Karen; <br>• Creates a retrospective/retroPO with the relevant Department Head; <br>• Decides to reject the invoice if no valid justification exists. After resolution, flow returns to **(2Yes)** and proceeds normally. |
| **B** | *Mismatch between PO & Invoice* (Mary “incorrect pricing / missing items”; Dan “extra units”) | 6  **(6No)**  AP Clerk sends an email to Purchasing asking for clarification; if unresolved, escalates to Rita. Rita may: <br>• Contact the supplier (via Karen) for a corrected invoice; <br>• Negotiate adjustments; <br>• Issue a retrospective PO where appropriate. Once discrepancies are cleared, flow returns to **(6Yes)** and continues. |
| **C** | *Manager rejects or flags suspicious total* (Carlos) | 9  **Reject/Clarify**  AP Clerk receives rejection reason; may: <br>• Rematch line items; <br>• Request updated invoice from supplier via Karen; <br>• Escalate to Rita for deeper investigation. After remediation, the manager is renotified and can approve. |
| **D** | *Complex or highvalue discrepancy that cannot be resolved quickly* (Rita) | Any of A–C may trigger this: Rita coordinates directly with Karen; Karen reaches out to supplier, obtains corrected invoice or contractual price data; Rita updates the accounting record; flow reenters at the appropriate gateway. |

*All exception paths loop back to a prior decision point once the issue is resolved, preserving continuity.*

---

### **4. Artifacts / Data Objects (Inputs & Outputs)**

| Artifact | Origin  Destination | Description |
|----------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Invoice Document** | Supplier  AP Clerk (email) | Primary record containing lineitems, total amount, PO reference (if any). |
| **Purchase Order (PO) Record** | Procurement System  AP Clerk & Purchasing Officer | Used for validation; may be created retroactively by Rita/Department Head in exception A. |
| **Receipt Confirmation** | Purchasing Officer  AP Clerk | Email or system flag indicating that goods/services were actually received. |
| **Matching Report** | AP Clerk (system)  Manager Notification | Autogenerated report showing lineitem comparison between PO and Invoice. |
| **Approval Request Message** | System  Department Manager | UI/Email notification with attached invoice, PO, and matching report for approval decision. |
| **Payment Schedule Entry** | AP Clerk  Finance Payment Engine | Record that places the approved invoice into the next scheduled payment run. |
| **Exception Log / Escalation Ticket** | Rita (Supervisor)  System & Karen | Tracks unresolved discrepancies; includes communication history with supplier. |

---

### **5. Summary of Logical Flow**

1. **Invoice Arrival**  AP Clerk validates PO existence.  
2. **If no PO:** Exception A handled by Supervisor (Rita) and possibly Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen).  
3. **Log & Forward** to Purchasing for receipt confirmation.  
4. **Purchasing Confirmation** leads to lineitem matching; mismatch triggers Exception B (handled by Clerk  Rita/Karen).  
5. **Successful Match** generates an automatic approval request to the Department Manager.  
6. **Manager Decision:** Approve  schedule payment; Reject/Clarify  loop back for correction (Exception C).  
7. Throughout, any unresolved or complex issue escalates to **Rita (AP Supervisor)** who may involve **Karen (Supplier Relationship Manager)**.  

*All loops return to the appropriate gateway once the underlying problem is resolved, ensuring a clean continuation of the main path.*
