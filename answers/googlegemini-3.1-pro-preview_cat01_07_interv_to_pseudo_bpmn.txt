Based on the interview transcripts provided, here is a structured, BPMN-like textual process model. It is organized into Participants/Roles, Artifacts, and the Step-by-Step Sequence Flow, including gateways and exception handling descriptions.

### 1. Pools & Lanes (Participants & Systems)
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Lane:**
    *   AP Clerk (Mary)
    *   AP Supervisor (Rita)
*   **Purchasing Lane:** Purchasing Officer (Dan / Purchasing Team)
*   **Management Lane:** Department Manager (Carlos / Department Heads)
*   **Supplier Relationship Management (SRM) Lane:** SRM Manager (Karen)
*   **System Lane:** Accounting/System Platform Tools
*   **External Pool:** Supplier

### 2. Artifacts / Inputs / Outputs
*   **Data Objects:** Invoice (via email), Purchase Order (PO), Revised Invoice
*   **Messages:** Confirmation Email, System automated approval request/notification
*   **Data Stores:** Accounting System

### 3. Step-by-Step Sequence Flow

#### **Phase 1: Invoice Receipt & Initial Logging (Accounts Payable)**
1.  **Start Event:** AP Clerk receives an Invoice (typically via email) from the Supplier.
2.  **Task:** AP Clerk reviews the invoice to confirm it is associated with a valid Purchase Order (PO) number.
3.  **Exclusive Gateway: Does the invoice have a valid PO?**
    *   *Path A (Yes):* Proceed to Step 4.
    *   *Path B (No PO - Supplier Error):* AP Clerk (or SRM) contacts the supplier to request missing details/revised invoice. **Loop back** to Start Event upon receipt.
    *   *Path C (No PO - Informal Contract):* AP Clerk escalates to AP Supervisor. AP Sup works with Dept Heads to create a retrospective PO or reject the invoice. Once a retro-PO is created, proceed to Step 4.
4.  **Task:** AP Clerk logs the invoice into the Accounting System.
5.  **Task:** AP Clerk forwards the invoice (via email/system) to the Purchasing Officer.

#### **Phase 2: Receipt Confirmation & Verification (Purchasing)**
6.  **Task:** Purchasing Officer checks invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) against the PO and confirms the physical receipt of goods/services.
7.  **Exclusive Gateway: Does the invoice align with the PO and Goods Received?**
    *   *Path A (All correct):* Proceed to Step 8.
    *   *Path B (Discrepancy found - e.g., extra units billed):* Purchasing Officer either contacts Supplier directly (or loops in SRM) OR asks AP Clerk to place the invoice on hold. Wait for a corrected invoice -> **Loop back** to Start Event.
    *   *Path C (Missing PO slipped through):* Purchasing returns the invoice to AP Clerk to obtain the PO. (*Note: This is an exception mentioned by Purchasing, implying AP's initial check might occasionally fail or bypass.*)
8.  **Task:** Purchasing Officer sends a Confirmation Email back to the AP Clerk.

#### **Phase 3: System Matching & Issue Resolution (Accounts Payable)**
9.  **Task:** Upon receiving confirmation, AP Clerk matches invoice line items against the PO in the Accounting System. (*Note: This may seem duplicative of Purchasing's check, but reflects the distinction between business/physical validation (Dan) and systemic accounting validation (Mary).*).
10. **Exclusive Gateway: Does the System Match succeed?**
    *   *Path A (Exact Match):* Proceed to Phase 4 (Step 13).
    *   *Path B (Discrepancy - e.g., system pricing differs or items missing):* AP Clerk emails Purchasing team to clarify. Proceed to Step 11.
11. **Task:** Clarification Collaboration (AP Clerk, Purchasing, SRM).
12. **Exclusive Gateway: Is the discrepancy easily resolvable?**
    *   *Path A (Resolved):* Correct details updated. Proceed to Phase 4 (Step 13).
    *   *Path B (Unresolvable / Complex):* AP Clerk escalates to AP Supervisor. AP Supervisor coordinates between departments and SRM to resolve terms discrepancy. Once resolved, proceed to Step 13. Alternatively, AP Supervisor rejects the invoice -> **End Event (Invoice Rejected).**

#### **Phase 4: Final Approval & Payment (Manager & System)**
13. **Task:** Accounting System automatically generates and sends an email notification to the Department Manager requesting sign-off.
14. **Task:** Department Manager reviews the invoice details, the PO, and Purchasing's confirmation in the system.
15. **Exclusive Gateway: Manager Approval Decision?**
    *   *Path A (Approved):* Manager clicks "Approve" in the System. Proceed to Step 16.
    *   *Path B (Suspicious / Budget Issue):* Manager rejects the invoice in the system OR requests AP Clerk to hold and investigate. **Loop back** to Exception Handling (Step 11).
16. **Task:** AP Clerk receives final approval notification and schedules the invoice for the next payment run in the system.
17. **End Event:** Invoice successfully scheduled for payment.

### 4. Special Exception / Collaboration Notes (For Process Modelers)
*   **Involvement of the SRM (Supplier Relationship Manager):** The SRM does not have formal execution tasks in the primary flow. Instead, they act as an ad-hoc support resource (attached to tasks via a **BPMN Collaboration/Message flow**). They are triggered primarily at Gateways where discrepancies occur (e.g., mismatched pricing/contracts or missing POs).
*   **Duplicate PO Checks:** The interviews indicate a conflict/overlap in responsibilities regarding missing POs. AP Clerk claims to catch missing POs immediately at step 1; however, Purchasing notes they also occasionally catch missing POs. A BPMN model should show a validation check at the AP step, with a secondary exception path at the Purchasing step to accurately model the "as-is" reality described.
