### Key points about the structure

| Constraint type | Format in pm4py | Example from above |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unary: `existence`, `absence`, `exactly_one`, `init` | `{ 'Activity': {'support': 1.0, 'confidence': x} }` | `'init': {'IG': {'support': 1.0, 'confidence': 1.0}}` |
| Binary: all others | `{ 'A': {'B': {'support': 1.0, 'confidence': x}} }` | `'precedence': {'DD': {'IG': {'support': 1.0, 'confidence': 1.0}}}` means IG must precede DD |

Note: For binary constraints, the outer key is the "target" activity and the inner key is the "source" activity. So `'response': {'IG': {'DD': ...}}` means "if IG occurs, DD must occur eventually after it".
