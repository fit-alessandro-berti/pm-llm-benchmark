# Process Mining Consultation Report: Speedy Parcels Optimization

**To:** Speedy Parcels Management Team
**From:** Process Mining Consultant
**Date:** October 26, 2024
**Subject:** Comprehensive Process Mining Strategy for Last-Mile Delivery Optimization

## 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

To effectively analyze Speedy Parcels' operations, we must first transform raw data into a structured event log and visualize the reality of operations versus the plan.

### Data Preprocessing and Integration
The primary challenge lies in harmonizing heterogeneous data sources into a single **Event Log** compatible with process mining tools (e.g., ProM, Celonis, Disco).

*   **Case ID Definition:** We will define the primary **Case ID** as `Vehicle-ID` + `Date` (e.g., `V12-20241205`). This allows us to analyze the daily operational flow of a specific van. A secondary index will link `Package-ID` to specific activities for delivery-level granularity.
*   **Activity Abstraction:** Raw GPS points (lat/lon/speed) are too granular. We must aggregate them into process activities.
    *   *Example:* A continuous stream of GPS points where `Speed < 5 km/h` for > 5 minutes becomes the activity `Traffic Delay`.
    *   *Example:* Scanner events (`Arrive Customer`, `Delivery Success`) map directly to activities.
*   **Timestamp Synchronization:** All sources must be normalized to a single time zone and format. Discrepancies between GPS clocks and Scanner clocks must be resolved (e.g., by assuming the Scanner timestamp is the ground truth for delivery milestones and adjusting GPS logs accordingly).
*   **Challenges:**
    *   **Missing Data:** GPS signal loss in urban canyons or scanner battery failure. We will use interpolation for GPS gaps but flag missing scanner logs as potential "Process Anomalies."
    *   **Granularity Mismatch:** Maintenance logs are event-based (start/end of repair), while Dispatch is plan-based. We must treat maintenance as a "Resource Constraint" or "Exception Activity" in the log.

### Process Discovery
Using **Heuristic Miners** or **Inductive Miners**, we will generate a process map representing the *actual* flow.
*   **Visualization:** The map will show nodes (e.g., `Depart Depot`, `Drive`, `Stop`, `Deliver`, `Return`) and edges representing the sequence.
*   **Expected Findings:** Unlike a linear plan, the discovered model will likely show loops (e.g., `Drive` -> `Failed Delivery` -> `Drive` -> `Retry`), parallel activities (e.g., `Idle` occurring during `Traffic Delay`), and bypasses (e.g., `Depart Depot` -> `Unscheduled Maintenance` -> `Return Depot`).
*   **Value:** This visualizes the "Happy Path" versus the "Real Path," highlighting how often deviations occur naturally.

### Conformance Checking
We will align the discovered model against the **Dispatch System Plan**.
*   **Technique:** Token-based replay or alignment-based conformance checking.
*   **Deviations to Detect:**
    1.  **Sequence Deviations:** Did the driver visit Stop 5 before Stop 3? (Indicates route optimization failure or driver discretion).
    2.  **Timing Deviations:** Did `Arrive Customer` occur outside the promised `Time Window`?
    3.  **Unplanned Activities:** Presence of `Unscheduled Stop` or `Low Speed Detected` events not in the plan.
    4.  **Resource Deviations:** Was the assigned vehicle (`V12`) actually used, or was there a swap?
*   **Output:** A conformance heatmap showing which routes or drivers deviate most frequently from the plan.

## 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

Once the process is mapped, we must quantify performance to identify where time and money are lost.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
We will calculate the following KPIs directly from the event log attributes:

1.  **On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate:** `(Count of `Delivery Success` within Time Window) / (Total `Delivery Success`)`.
2.  **Average Service Time:** `Timestamp(Depart Customer) - Timestamp(Arrive Customer)`. This isolates time spent at the location (parking, walking, interaction).
3.  **Travel Efficiency:** `Total Distance (GPS) / Total Stops`. High ratios indicate excessive driving between stops.
4.  **Fuel Cost per Stop:** Derived from `Engine Status` (Idle vs. Moving) duration and `GPS Speed` (aggressive acceleration/braking inferred from speed changes).
5.  **Failed Delivery Rate:** `(Count of `Delivery Failed`) / (Total Planned Deliveries)`.
6.  **Overtime Ratio:** `(Timestamp(End Shift) - Scheduled End Time) / Scheduled Shift Duration`.

### Bottleneck Identification Techniques
*   **Performance View:** We will generate a "Bottleneck Diagram" where edges are colored by **Average Waiting Time** or **Throughput Time**.
    *   *Example:* If the edge `Drive` -> `Arrive Customer` has high variance, it indicates unpredictable travel times.
*   **Variant Analysis:** We will group cases (Vehicle-Days) into variants based on performance (e.g., Top 20% Efficient vs. Bottom 20% Inefficient).
    *   *Comparison:* Do inefficient variants share specific characteristics (e.g., specific routes, specific times, specific drivers)?
*   **Resource Bottlenecks:** Analyze `Driver ID` performance. Does Driver D105 consistently have higher `Service Time` than Driver D106?
*   **Geospatial Bottlenecks:** Overlay `Low Speed Detected` events on a city map to identify "Traffic Hotspots" that consistently delay the process.
*   **Quantification:** We will calculate the **Cost of Delay** by multiplying `Overtime Hours` by `Driver Hourly Rate` and `Idle Fuel Consumption` by `Fuel Price`.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

Identifying bottlenecks is step one; understanding *why* they happen is step two. We will use process mining analytics to validate hypotheses.

### Potential Root Causes & Validation Methods

1.  **Suboptimal Route Planning (Static vs. Dynamic)**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Dispatch plans routes based on distance, not real-time traffic.
    *   *Analysis:* Correlate `Travel Time` (Actual) vs. `Dispatch Estimated Travel Time`. If Actual consistently exceeds Estimated during specific hours (e.g., 08:00-09:30), the planning algorithm is outdated.
    *   *Process Mining:* Variant analysis on routes taken during "Rush Hour" vs. "Off-Peak."

2.  **High Variability in Service Time**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Drivers spend too long finding parking or customers are unavailable.
    *   *Analysis:* Filter cases where `Service Time` > 15 minutes. Analyze the `Notes` field or `Location` (e.g., dense urban center vs. suburb).
    *   *Process Mining:* Process tree analysis to see if `Arrive Customer` is often followed by `Depart Customer` without `Delivery Success` (indicating parking/search time).

3.  **Vehicle Breakdowns and Maintenance**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Reactive maintenance causes mid-shift stoppages.
    *   *Analysis:* Correlate `Unscheduled Stop` events with `Vehicle Age` or `Total Mileage` (from GPS).
    *   *Process Mining:* Check if `Unscheduled Stop` occurs frequently after a specific number of hours of operation.

4.  **Failed Delivery Attempts**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Delivery windows are too tight or customers are not home.
    *   *Analysis:* Analyze the timestamp of `Delivery Failed` events. Are they clustered at the end of the day?
    *   *Process Mining:* Variant analysis comparing routes with 0 failures vs. routes with >2 failures. Check if specific drivers have higher failure rates (skill issue) or if it's location-based.

5.  **Driver Behavior**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Aggressive driving increases fuel costs and maintenance.
    *   *Analysis:* Analyze `Speed` variance. High frequency of `Speed > Limit` or rapid deceleration events correlates with higher fuel use.
    *   *Process Mining:* Compare `Fuel Consumption` KPI across `Driver ID`.

## 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

Based on the insights above, I propose the following three strategies.

### Strategy 1: Dynamic "Traffic-Aware" Routing
*   **Targeted Inefficiency:** High variance in Travel Time and Late Deliveries.
*   **Root Cause:** Static route planning that ignores historical congestion patterns.
*   **Process Mining Support:** The conformance check will reveal that `Travel Time` consistently exceeds `Plan` during specific time windows (e.g., 8:00 AM). The geospatial analysis will identify specific road segments causing delays.
*   **Implementation:** Integrate historical GPS speed data into the dispatch algorithm. If a route segment historically shows `Speed < 10 km/h` at 8:30 AM, the system should route vehicles around it or schedule that stop for later in the day.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **+15% On-Time Delivery Rate.**
    *   **-10% Fuel Consumption** (less idling in traffic).

### Strategy 2: "Smart" Failed Delivery Protocol
*   **Targeted Inefficiency:** High Cost per Delivery and Overtime due to re-deliveries.
*   **Root Cause:** Inaccurate customer availability data and rigid time windows.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Analysis of `Delivery Failed` events will likely show a pattern (e.g., 60% of failures happen between 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM). It may also reveal that certain `Customer Locations` have a 30% failure rate.
*   **Implementation:**
    1.  **Customer Communication:** Automate SMS notifications 1 hour before arrival for high-risk customers.
    2.  **Time Window Adjustment:** Shift "high risk" residential stops to evening slots (17:00-19:00) based on historical success data.
    3.  **Incentive:** Offer customers a discount for selecting a 2-hour window where historical data shows 95% success.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **-40% Failed Delivery Attempts.**
    *   **-15% Operational Cost** (reduced re-delivery mileage).

### Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance Scheduling
*   **Targeted Inefficiency:** Unscheduled Stops and Vehicle Downtime.
*   **Root Cause:** Maintenance is reactive (fix when broken) rather than predictive.
*   **Process Mining Support:** The event log shows `Unscheduled Stop` events often preceded by `Low Speed Detected` or specific engine warning patterns (if available in GPS telemetry). We can identify the "wear and tear" signature before a breakdown.
*   **Implementation:** Create a "Health Score" for each vehicle based on `Mileage` + `Idle Time` + `Warning Events`. Schedule maintenance during non-peak hours when the score hits a threshold, rather than waiting for a breakdown during a shift.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **-90% Unscheduled Mid-Shift Stops.**
    *   **-20% Maintenance Costs** (preventive is cheaper than emergency repairs).

## 5. Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring

### Operational Constraints
Any optimization must respect the physical and legal reality of logistics:
*   **Driver Working Hours:** Dynamic routing cannot schedule a route that exceeds legal driving limits (e.g., 9 hours in EU). The optimization algorithm must include a hard constraint on `Shift Duration`.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** Re-routing cannot exceed the van's package volume/weight. The system must validate `Package ID` counts against `Vehicle Capacity` before reassigning stops.
*   **Customer Time Windows:** While we can adjust windows, we cannot violate contractual commitments. Optimization should focus on *scheduling* within the window, not shifting the window itself without customer consent.

### Continuous Monitoring Plan
Process mining is not a one-time project; it requires a "Process Intelligence" dashboard.

*   **Dashboard Components:**
    1.  **Real-Time Conformance Monitor:** Alerts dispatchers if a vehicle deviates significantly from the planned route (e.g., `Deviation > 15 mins`).
    2.  **KPI Control Charts:** Track OTD and Fuel per km over time. Use statistical process control to detect "special cause" variations (e.g., a sudden spike in fuel cost on a specific route).
    3.  **Bottleneck Heatmap:** A visual map showing current congestion vs. historical norms.
    4.  **Driver Performance Scorecard:** Transparent metrics for drivers (Service Time, OTD, Fuel Efficiency) to encourage self-optimization.

*   **Sustainability Check:**
    *   **Monthly Variant Review:** Re-run the variant analysis monthly to ensure new bottlenecks haven't emerged (e.g., a new construction zone).
    *   **A/B Testing:** Pilot the "Smart Failed Delivery Protocol" on 10% of the fleet. Compare KPIs against the control group before full rollout.
    *   **Feedback Loop:** Allow drivers to input "Reason Codes" for deviations in the handheld scanner. This enriches the event log with qualitative data (e.g., "Road Closed") that GPS cannot capture, improving future model accuracy.

By implementing this process mining framework, Speedy Parcels will move from reactive problem solving to proactive process management, directly addressing the pressures of punctuality and cost.
