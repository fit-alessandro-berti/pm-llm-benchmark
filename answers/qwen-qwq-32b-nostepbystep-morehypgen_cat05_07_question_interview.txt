Here’s a list of open-ended questions designed to explore the property onboarding process thoroughly, focusing on decision criteria, roles, timing, exceptions, and hidden complexities:

---

### **Documentation & Initial Review**
1. Could you walk through the criteria you use to determine whether a document is “complete” or “outdated”? How do you handle situations where a landlord refuses to provide a document?  
2. Are there any exceptions where a property might proceed without certain documents (e.g., under urgent circumstances)? How is that approval granted?  

### **Property Manager Assignment**  
3. How does the team balance conflicting priorities when assigning a property manager (e.g., high workload vs. geographic expertise)? Are there established protocols for these trade-offs?  
4. When a property requires specialized expertise (e.g., luxury or student housing), how do you ensure the assigned manager has the necessary experience?  

### **Landlord-Manager Alignment**  
5. What happens if the landlord and property manager disagree on policies like pet rules or maintenance responsibilities? Who mediates these conflicts?  
6. Are there templates or guidelines for the introductory call, or is it left to the manager’s discretion?  

### **Inspection & Repairs**  
7. What defines a “critical” versus “non-critical” issue in an inspection report? How does this affect the timeline?  
8. Who bears the cost of repairs—landlord or the company—and how is this communicated and enforced?  

### **Marketing & Pricing**  
9. How flexible is the company in adjusting pricing if the landlord insists on a higher price than market trends suggest? What data informs this negotiation?  
10. How do regional market differences (e.g., local housing trends, competition) influence the marketing strategy for a property?  

### **Tenant Screening & Leasing**  
11. What specific criteria are non-negotiable in tenant screening, and how are these communicated to managers?  
12. In cases of multiple viable applicants, what criteria determine prioritization (e.g., lease length, payment method flexibility)?  

### **Compliance & Financial Checks**  
13. How do compliance officers stay updated on evolving fair housing laws across different cities? What happens if a lease clause is flagged as non-compliant mid-process?  
14. When a financial analyst identifies a pricing discrepancy, what adjustments are made, and who has authority to override the recommendation?  

### **Exceptions & Risk Management**  
15. How does the team handle properties requiring specialized insurance (e.g., flood-prone areas or high-end renovations)? Is there a dedicated team for such cases?  
16. What steps are taken if a landlord demands a custom lease clause that introduces legal or liability risks?  

### **Timeline & Dependencies**  
17. What is the typical timeline for each phase, and which steps are most likely to cause delays? Are there contingency plans for bottlenecks?  
18. How do you handle urgent onboarding requests (e.g., a landlord needing a property listed quickly)? Does this override standard checks?  

### **Roles & Handoffs**  
19. How is accountability tracked when responsibilities shift between teams (e.g., from the coordinator to the manager, then to the marketing team)?  
20. What happens if a property manager leaves mid-onboarding—how is continuity maintained?  

### **Communication & Feedback**  
21. How do stakeholders (landlords, tenants, support teams) receive updates during the process? Are there standardized communication protocols?  
22. How is feedback from landlords or tenants incorporated into future onboarding processes?  

### **Scalability & Adaptability**  
23. Are there scenarios where the process is streamlined or abbreviated (e.g., for long-term partners)? How is this approved?  
24. What changes would you make to the process if scaling to a new city with unfamiliar regulations or markets?  

### **Decision-Making Authority**  
25. When there’s a disagreement between teams (e.g., marketing vs. compliance on a lease clause), who has final decision-making authority?  
26. In what situations would a property be rejected from the portfolio, and who makes that call?  

### **Technical & Operational Practices**  
27. How do you ensure consistency in data entry across regional offices when updating the central database?  
28. What happens if a critical system failure (e.g., CRM outage) disrupts the onboarding process?  

### **Post-Onboarding Reflection**  
29. Are there post-mortem reviews after a property is activated to identify process improvements? What metrics are used to evaluate success?  

---

### **Rationale for Questions**  
- **Decision Criteria**: Probes into trade-offs (e.g., workload vs. expertise) and prioritization frameworks.  
- **Roles & Accountability**: Clarifies ownership of tasks and escalation paths.  
- **Exceptions & Edge Cases**: Explores how the process adapts to non-standard scenarios (e.g., legal conflicts, urgent timelines).  
- **System Interactions**: Highlights dependencies on technology and data integrity.  
- **Scalability & Adaptability**: Tests the process’s flexibility for growth or regulatory changes.  

These questions aim to expose hidden assumptions, interdependencies, and governance structures that might not be obvious in the initial description.
