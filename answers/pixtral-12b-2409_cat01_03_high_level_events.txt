To group the low-level events into meaningful, higher-level process steps, we need to identify coherent stages of the manufacturing process. Let's analyze the event log and propose high-level activities along with their justifications.

### High-Level Activities and Justifications

1. **Material Preparation**
   - **Events:**
     - Retrieve raw metal sheet
     - Scan barcode
     - Place metal sheet on table
     - Align metal sheet
     - Preheat metal sheet
   - **Rationale:** These events are all related to the initial setup and preparation of the raw material. They involve retrieving, identifying, positioning, aligning, and preheating the metal sheet, which are sequential and logically grouped activities.
   - **Name:** Material Preparation

2. **Welding**
   - **Events:**
     - Pick up welding tool
     - Weld corner A
     - Weld corner B
   - **Rationale:** These events involve the use of a welding tool and are focused on joining parts together. They are performed by the same operator and occur in close temporal proximity.
   - **Name:** Welding

3. **Quality Inspection**
   - **Events:**
     - Measure weld integrity
     - Visual check
   - **Rationale:** These events involve checking the quality of the weld and performing a visual inspection. They are quality assurance steps that ensure the product meets the required standards.
   - **Name:** Quality Inspection

4. **Coating**
   - **Events:**
     - Apply protective coating
     - Dry coating
   - **Rationale:** These events involve applying a protective coating to the product and then drying it. They are part of the finishing process to ensure durability and protection.
   - **Name:** Coating
