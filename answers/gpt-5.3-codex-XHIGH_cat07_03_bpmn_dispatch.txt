Yes — it’s a **BPMN-style swimlane process diagram** showing how a **computer tech shop dispatches goods**.

### What it shows
- **Swimlanes/roles:**  
  - **Logistics** (top)  
  - **Secretary** (middle)  
  - **Warehouse** (bottom)

### Process flow (left to right)
1. **Start:** “Ship goods.”
2. A **parallel split (+ gateway)** launches two tracks at once:
   - **Secretary track:** clarify shipment method and decide on special handling.
   - **Warehouse track:** package goods.

3. In the secretary track, an **exclusive decision (X gateway)** asks: **“Special handling?”**
   - **Yes:** get 3 logistics offers  select logistics company and place order.
   - **No:** check if insurance is required (using order/customer info).  
     Then an **inclusive split ( gateway)**:
     - If needed: **Insure parcel** (Logistics lane)
     - Always: **Write package label**
     These rejoin afterward.

4. The secretary outcomes merge and later connect to warehouse completion steps.

5. In the warehouse track, **“Package goods”** has a **48-hour timer boundary event**:
   - If delayed, **notify customer about delay**, then rejoin the main packaging flow.

6. A **parallel join (+ gateway)** synchronizes office/logistics and warehouse readiness.

7. Final steps:
   - **Fill out shipment paperwork**
   - **Move package to pickup area**
   - **End:** “Shipment prepared.”

In short, it models coordination between admin/logistics decisions and physical packaging, including branches for insurance, special handling, and delay notification.
