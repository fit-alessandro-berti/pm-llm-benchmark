### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Coexistence (`Reject_Minority` / `Approve_Minority` $\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview`)**: 
   By enforcing that any decision specifically flagged for a minority applicant must coexist with a `ManualReview`, we guarantee human oversight. This ensures that automated or systemic biases do not disproportionately affect sensitive demographics without a secondary, fair evaluation.

2. **Response (`CheckApplicantRace` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck`)**: 
   This constraint mandates that if the system or an agent checks an applicant's race, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must occur later in the trace. It ensures that the mere act of considering a sensitive attribute actively triggers a fairness evaluation protocol.

3. **Precedence (`BiasMitigationCheck` $\prec$ `Reject` / `Approve`)**: 
   This dictates that a final decision (`Reject` or `Approve`) can only be executed if a `BiasMitigationCheck` has occurred beforehand in the process. This prevents final loan decisions from being made in a vacuum without prior bias evaluation, enforcing a standardized fairness checkpoint.

4. **Non-chain Succession (`CheckApplicantRace` $\not\rightarrow\circ$ `Reject` / `Approve`)**: 
   This prevents a final decision from *immediately* following a sensitive attribute check. By blocking direct succession, the model forces at least one intermediate analytical step (such as the `BiasMitigationCheck` or `ManualReview`), effectively preventing "snap judgments" or immediate biased outcomes triggered directly by demographic data.
