## 3. Possible interpretations or justifications

- **Case 1** appears to be a clean, standard approved-loan execution. The only possible soft issue is the missing Follow-up Call, if that activity was expected to be logged.

- **Case 2** may reflect a process execution error, logging error, or mislabeling problem. For example, perhaps the Credit Check was actually performed before Risk Assessment but logged late, or “Finalize Documentation” may have been intended to mean administrative rejection paperwork. However, under the given rules, the recorded behavior is clearly non-compliant.

- **Case 3** preserves the correct strict order, so it is normatively acceptable. The delays could be due to workload, weekend/holiday effects, applicant availability, or additional internal review. Still, the case shows suboptimal performance against the recommended timing targets and lacks the recommended Follow-up Call.
