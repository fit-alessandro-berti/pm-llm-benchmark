### Detailed Justification of Each Grouping  

1. **Material Preparation**  
   *Temporal proximity*: All events occur within ~15seconds of each other (08:00:05  08:01:00).  
   *Logical flow*: Retrieval  identification (barcode)  positioning  alignment  thermal conditioning – a classic “setup” sequence for any machining or welding line.  
   *Resource pattern*: Involves both human operators and automated equipment that work together on the same physical object, indicating they belong to one preparatory subprocess.

2. **Assembly / Welding**  
   *Sequential dependency*: Picking up a tool is a prerequisite for executing weld actions; without the tool no welding can happen.  
   *Temporal cluster*: Events are only 3–5seconds apart (08:01:03  08:01:12).  
   *Resource continuity*: Same operator (OperatorB) and same tool (ToolIDW12) across the three actions, showing a single “assembly” microprocess.

3. **Quality Inspection**  
   *Distinct purpose*: The only event whose primary goal is measurement/validation rather than transformation or preparation.  
   *Temporal separation*: Occurs right after welding but before any finishing steps (08:01:20), indicating it is an interstage check.  

4. **Finishing – Protective Coating Application**  
   *Twostep nature*: Applying a coating and then drying/curing are inseparable in practice; the coating would be ineffective without curing, and curing cannot start until the liquid is on the surface.  
   *Resource shift*: Different equipment (Coating Unit  Heating Unit) but they belong to the same “finish” subprocess because both modify the part’s external condition for durability.

5. **Final Visual Verification**  
   *Human gatekeeper*: The visual check is a manual decision point that can reject or accept the product after all automated steps are complete. Its placement at the very end of each trace makes it a natural “final verification” stage.
