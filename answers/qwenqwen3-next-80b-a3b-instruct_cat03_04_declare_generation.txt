###  **Notes on Design Decisions**

1. **`exactly_one`**: Left empty because pm4py treats absence of a key as “not enforced”. If you want to enforce each activity occurs *exactly once*, you can populate it with all activities. But in many real processes, activities may repeat (e.g., if a test fails and needs rework). Since the scenario implies a *linear, gate-governed* process with no rework loops, we assume each activity occurs exactly once — **but we leave it empty** to avoid over-constraining unless explicitly required.

2. **`absence`**: Left empty — all activities are required, none are forbidden.

3. **`response` / `precedence` / `succession`**: All are included for completeness; `succession` = `response` + `precedence`. In practice, `succession` is often sufficient.

4. **`altresponse`, `altprecedence`, `altsuccession`**: Used to indicate that **either** TFC or CE must follow DD (OR path), and **either** LT or UT must precede AG. This reflects parallel branches.

5. **`chain*` variants**: Used to enforce *immediate* succession. For example, after DD, TFC *must* be the next activity — no other activity can intervene. This models strict sequential dependencies.

6. **`noncoexistence` / `nonsuccession` / `nonchainsuccession`**: Enforce that early activities cannot directly precede or coexist with late ones — prevents "skipping" stages. For example, you cannot go from IG  FL directly.

7. **Final Launch must be last**: Enforced via `succession` and `nonsuccession` rules.
