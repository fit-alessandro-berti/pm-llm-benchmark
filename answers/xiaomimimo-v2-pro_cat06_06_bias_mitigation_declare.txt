## Rationale for Added Constraints:

1. **Coexistence Constraints**: When decisions involve applicants from sensitive demographic groups (minority status, specific gender, age ranges), a `ManualReview` must always be present in the trace. This ensures human oversight for potentially vulnerable populations.

2. **Response Constraints**: After checking any sensitive attribute (`CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge`), the process must immediately perform a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This creates a procedural safeguard before any decision-making.

3. **Precedence Constraints**: All decision activities (`Approve`, `Reject`, `RequestAdditionalInfo`) must be preceded by a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This ensures that bias prevention measures are applied before any decision is finalized.

4. **Succession Constraints**: These complement the response constraints, ensuring that sensitive attribute checks are always followed by bias mitigation checks throughout the process.

5. **Non-succession Constraints**: Prevent direct succession from sensitive attribute checks to approval or rejection decisions. This eliminates the possibility of immediate discriminatory decisions based on sensitive characteristics.

These constraints collectively ensure that:
- Sensitive attributes never directly trigger decisions
- Human oversight is required for vulnerable groups  
- Bias mitigation checks are systematically applied
- The process maintains fairness and consistency across all applicant demographics
