### Explanation of Transformation Logic

**1. Case Identification:**  
Cases represent distinct user tasks centered around a specific document or communication thread. Each unique document or email thread is treated as a separate case:
- **C1**: Work on *Quarterly_Report.docx* (opened early, paused, then resumed and closed).
- **C2**: Work on *Document1.docx* (drafting, saving, switching away, returning to finalize).
- **C3**: Handling an email thread in Gmail (opening, replying, sending).
- **C4**: Reviewing *Report_Draft.pdf* (scrolling and highlighting).
- **C5**: Editing *Budget_2024.xlsx* (updating figures and saving).

This approach ensures that each case reflects a coherent unit of work with a clear start (FOCUS or SWITCH into the context) and end (CLOSE or implicit completion).

**2. Activity Naming:**  
Low-level actions are abstracted into semantically meaningful activities:
- **FOCUS**  *Open Document* (initial focus) or *Resume Editing* (if returning after a break).
- **TYPING**  *Draft Content* (for documents) or *Draft Email Content* (for emails).
- **CLICK** (email-related)  *Open Email*, *Compose Reply*, *Send Email*.
- **SCROLL**  *Review* (in PDF or email context).
- **HIGHLIGHT**  *Annotate PDF*.
- **SAVE**  *Save Document* or *Save Spreadsheet* (differentiated by app).
- **SWITCH**  *Switch to Email* (only when initiating a new case context; otherwise, used to infer case resumption).
- **CLOSE**  *Close Document*.

Redundant or overly granular actions (e.g., multiple TYPING events) are preserved as separate events but labeled consistently to reflect iterative drafting.

**3. Temporal and Contextual Reasoning:**  
- The initial FOCUS on *Quarterly_Report.docx* (C1) is brief and followed by immediate switch to *Document1.docx* (C2), suggesting C1 was paused. When the user returns to *Quarterly_Report.docx* later, it is treated as a continuation of C1.
- The SWITCH event at 09:01:45 triggers the start of the email case (C3), as it shifts context to a new application and task.
- Similarly, SWITCH to Adobe Acrobat and Excel initiates new cases (C4 and C5), as they involve distinct artifacts.

This transformation yields an event log that captures the user’s multitasking behavior while preserving the integrity of individual work units—enabling meaningful process discovery, conformance checking, or performance analysis in standard process mining tools (e.g., Disco, Celonis, or PM4Py).
