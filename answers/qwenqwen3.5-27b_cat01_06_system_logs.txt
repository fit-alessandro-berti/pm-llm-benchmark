### Explanation of Transformation Logic

**1. Case Identification (Artifact-Centric):**
Instead of treating the entire 8-minute session as a single "User Session" case, I grouped events by the **specific document or task artifact** being worked on. This aligns with the prompt's guidance to identify a "logical unit of user work."
*   **Case IDs:** `Case_Document1`, `Case_Email`, `Case_PDF`, `Case_Excel`, `Case_QuarterlyReport`.
*   **Rationale:** This allows a process analyst to analyze the lifecycle of specific artifacts (e.g., "How long does it take to draft Document1?"). It also handles interleaving naturally; for example, `Case_Document1` has events at the beginning and middle of the log, representing a paused and resumed task.

**2. Activity Standardization:**
Raw system actions (FOCUS, TYPING, CLICK) were mapped to higher-level process activities to improve readability and analysis capability.
*   **FOCUS:** Mapped to "Open Window" (indicates the start of interaction with that artifact).
*   **TYPING:** Mapped to "Edit Content" (generic) or specific actions like "Draft Reply" where context allows.
*   **SWITCH:** Mapped to "Switch Context" and assigned to the **Destination Case**. This signifies the transition into the new task.
*   **CLICK:** Mapped to specific actions (e.g., "Open Email", "Send Email") derived from the `Action` attribute to provide granular process steps.
*   **SCROLL/HIGHLIGHT:** Mapped to "Review Content" and "Annotate Document" to distinguish passive reading from active editing.
*   **SAVE/CLOSE:** Mapped to "Save Changes" and "Close Window" to mark process milestones and completion.

**3. Handling Context Switches:**
When a `SWITCH` event occurred (e.g., Word to Chrome), it was assigned to the **Destination Case ID**. This treats the switch as the entry point for the new task, ensuring every event row has a valid Case ID and the sequence of the new task begins logically.

**4. Narrative:**
The resulting log tells the story of a user working on a **Quarterly Report** workflow. They briefly checked the final report, started drafting a new document (`Document1`), paused to handle an urgent **Email**, reviewed a **PDF** draft, updated financial data in **Excel**, returned to finalize `Document1`, and finally updated the **Quarterly Report** summary before closing. This structure reveals the dependencies and context switches inherent in the user's work process.
