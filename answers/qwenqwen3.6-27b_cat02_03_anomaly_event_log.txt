Based on the event log provided and standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) best practices, here is the analysis of each case. The standard P2P flow generally requires the following logical sequence and controls:

1.  **Authorization:** Purchase Requisition (PR) must be approved before a Purchase Order (PO) is issued.
2.  **Commitment:** PO must be issued before Goods are Received (GR) to ensure the purchase is authorized and committed.
3.  **Validation & Matching:** Payment should generally only be released after Goods are Received and the Invoice is matched to the PO (and ideally the GR) to verify quantity, price, and delivery.
4.  **Timing:** Payment should not occur before the Invoice is received or before Goods are received, to mitigate fraud and overpayment risks.
