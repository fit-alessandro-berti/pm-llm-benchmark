### **Identification of Bias in the Process**

The event log reveals several potential sources of bias that could influence fairness and equity in the decision-making process. Below is a structured analysis of where and how bias manifests, the attributes involved, and their implications.

---

## **1. Community Affiliation and Geographic Bias**
### **Observed Bias:**
- **Community Group Adjustments:** Cases with a **community affiliation (e.g., "Highland Civic Darts Club")** receive a **+10 score adjustment**, while those without any affiliation do not.
  - **Example:**
    - **C001 (Affiliated):** Score adjusted from **710  720** (Approved).
    - **C002 (Unaffiliated):** Score remains **720** (Approved).
    - **C004 (Affiliated):** Score adjusted from **690  700** (Approved).
    - **C003 & C005 (Unaffiliated):** No adjustment (C003 rejected, C005 approved).

- **Implications:**
  - **Affiliated applicants** have a **structural advantage** in scoring, even if their base score is lower (e.g., C004: 690  700 vs. C002: 720).
  - **Unaffiliated applicants** (especially those without a community group) are **disadvantaged** unless their base score is already high (e.g., C005: 740 approved without adjustment).
  - **Possible Unfairness:** Individuals who are **new to the area, socially isolated, or lack access to community groups** may be systematically penalized, even if their financial risk profile is similar.

### **Potential Causes of Bias:**
- **Algorithmic Preference for "Social Capital":** The system may implicitly favor applicants who are part of organized groups, assuming they are lower-risk (e.g., community support reduces default risk).
- **Data Limitation:** The model may not account for **unobserved heterogeneity**Śe.g., an unaffiliated applicant could be equally creditworthy but lacks the "community bonus."

---

## **2. Local Resident Status and Geographic Bias**
### **Observed Bias:**
- **LocalResident = TRUE** is a **neutral attribute** in this dataset (all cases are TRUE except C003 & C005, which are FALSE).
- **However, the lack of variation makes it hard to assess its true impact.**
  - **Possible Bias:** If **non-local residents** were systematically rejected or penalized, this could indicate **geographic discrimination** (e.g., favoring long-term residents over newcomers).
  - **Example:**
    - **C003 (Non-local, rejected)** vs. **C005 (Non-local, approved)**Śboth have no community adjustment, but C005Æs higher base score (740) may have outweighed the lack of affiliation.

### **Potential Causes of Bias:**
- **Assumption of Stability:** Locals may be seen as lower-risk due to **longer credit histories** or **established ties**, while newcomers lack this track record.
- **Data Skew:** If the training data overrepresents locals, the model may **learn to favor them implicitly**.

---

## **3. Manual Reviewer Bias**
### **Observed Bias:**
- **ManualReview is performed by different reviewers (Reviewer #2, #3, #4, #5, #7), but their decision criteria are not transparent.**
  - **Possible Issues:**
    - **Subjectivity:** Reviewers may introduce **personal biases** (e.g., favoring certain communities, penalizing non-locals).
    - **Inconsistency:** Different reviewers may apply **different standards**, leading to **uneven outcomes**.
  - **Example:**
    - **C003 (Rejected by Reviewer #4)** vs. **C005 (Approved by Reviewer #5)**Śboth non-local, but different outcomes.

### **Potential Causes of Bias:**
- **Lack of Standardized Criteria:** If reviewers are not given **clear guidelines**, decisions may reflect **personal prejudices**.
- **Reviewer Fatigue:** Overworked reviewers may default to **quick judgments** (e.g., rejecting borderline cases).

---

## **4. Score Adjustment Mechanism**
### **Observed Bias:**
- **Only community-affiliated cases receive adjustments (+10), while others do not.**
  - **This creates a two-tier system:**
    - **Tier 1 (Affiliated):** Can "boost" their score via community ties.
    - **Tier 2 (Unaffiliated):** Must rely solely on base score.
  - **Example:**
    - **C001 (710  720, Approved)**
    - **C002 (720, Approved)** ¢ No adjustment, but still approved.
    - **C004 (690  700, Approved)**
    - **C003 (715, Rejected)** ¢ No adjustment, rejected despite similar base score.

### **Potential Causes of Bias:**
- **Correlation  Causation:** The model may **mistake correlation for causation**Śe.g., assuming that being in a darts club **causes** lower default risk, when in reality, it may just be a proxy for **stable social networks**.
- **Lack of Alternative Adjustments:** The system does not account for **other forms of social capital** (e.g., employment stability, family ties).

---

## **5. Final Decision Bias**
### **Observed Bias:**
- **Approvals:**
  - **All affiliated cases (C001, C004) are approved.**
  - **Unaffiliated cases (C002, C005) are approved if base score is high.**
- **Rejections:**
  - **Only C003 (unaffiliated, base score 715) is rejected.**
  - **If C003 had been affiliated, it might have been approved (715  725).**

### **Implications:**
- **Affiliation acts as a "safety net"** for lower-scoring applicants.
- **Unaffiliated applicants face a higher bar**Śthey must have a **higher base score** to compensate for the lack of adjustment.

## **Recommendations for Fairness & Equity**
1. **Remove or Standardize Community Adjustments**
   - Either **eliminate the +10 adjustment** or **apply it universally** (e.g., based on verifiable social ties, not just club membership).
   - Consider **alternative adjustments** (e.g., employment stability, rental history).

2. **Audit Manual Reviewer Decisions**
   - Implement **blind reviews** (remove applicant identifiers).
   - Use **standardized scoring rubrics** to reduce subjectivity.

3. **Expand Data Collection**
   - Include **more diverse community affiliations** (not just darts clubs).
   - Track **non-traditional social capital** (e.g., volunteer work, religious groups).

4. **Geographic Fairness Checks**
   - Ensure **non-local applicants** are not unfairly penalized.
   - Consider **rental payment history** as an alternative to residency status.

5. **Transparency & Explainability**
   - Provide **clear reasons for adjustments** (e.g., "Approved due to community ties").
   - Allow **applicants to appeal** if they believe bias occurred.

6. **Bias Testing & Model Validation**
   - Run **fairness audits** to check for disparate impact across groups.
   - Use **counterfactual testing** (e.g., "Would C003 have been approved if affiliated?").

---
### **Conclusion**
The current system **favors applicants with community affiliations**, creating a **structural bias** that disadvantages **socially isolated individuals, newcomers, and those without access to organized groups**. While the adjustments may have **some predictive value**, they risk **reinforcing existing social inequalities** in credit access. A more **equitable approach** would involve **standardizing adjustments, reducing manual reviewer discretion, and expanding the definition of "social capital"** beyond club memberships.
