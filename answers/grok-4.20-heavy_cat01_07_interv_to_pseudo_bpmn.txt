**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

**Process Name:** Supplier Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling  
**Scope:** From invoice receipt to payment scheduling (including PO validation, 3-way matching elements, managerial approval, and exception handling).  
**Overall Characteristics:** Primarily sequential with multiple decision points. Includes rework loops for discrepancies and a parallel exception/escalation path. The process reflects a standard 3-way match (PO + goods/services confirmation + invoice) with system support for notifications.

**Pools / Lanes (Participants/Roles):**
- **External:** Supplier
- **Accounts Payable:** AP Clerk (Mary – primary executor), AP Supervisor (Rita – exception handling only)
- **Purchasing:** Purchasing Officer (Dan)
- **Department Manager:** Carlos
- **Supplier Relationship:** Karen (support role in resolutions; does not execute core tasks in the system)

**Data Objects / Artifacts:**
- Incoming Invoice (email/document)
- Purchase Order (PO) reference
- Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation
- System record (logged invoice, matching results)
- Approval notification / rejection note
- Corrected invoice or retrospective PO (in exception path)

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (email to AP Clerk)

**Main Happy Path Flow:**

1. **Task (AP Clerk):** Check for Valid PO Number  
   **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Has valid PO reference?  
   - **No**  Task: Request PO/details from Supplier (AP Clerk or Karen assists in communication)  Message Flow to Supplier  Loop back to revised invoice receipt (or escalate to Rita for retrospective PO creation).  
   - **Yes**  Task: Log/Register Invoice in Accounting System

2. **Task (AP Clerk):** Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Verification/Confirmation of Receipt

3. **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Verify Invoice against PO (compare quantities, prices, delivery charges, and confirm goods/services were received)  
   **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Matches PO terms and receipt confirmed?  
   - **No**  Task: Resolve discrepancy (contact supplier directly or via Karen; request corrected invoice)  Message Flow to Supplier  Rework loop back to AP/Purchasing (or escalate).  
   - **Yes**  Task: Send Confirmation Email back to AP

4. **Task (AP Clerk):** Perform Final Invoice Matching against PO + Confirmation  
   **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Discrepancy identified (price, items, totals)?  
   - **No**  System automatically sends notification/email to Department Manager  
   - **Yes**  Task: Clarify with Purchasing or escalate to AP Supervisor (see exception path below)

5. **Task (Department Manager):** Review Invoice, PO, and Confirmations in System  
   **Exclusive Gateway (XOR):** Approve? (Manager checks for budget alignment, suspicious items, etc.)  
   - **Yes**  Task (AP Clerk): Schedule Invoice for Payment (add to payment run)  
   - **No**  Task: Reject or request further investigation  Message back to AP Clerk/Purchasing  Rework loop or end with rejection

6. **End Event:** Invoice Scheduled for Payment (or Rejected/Returned to Supplier)

**Exception / Alternative Paths (noted by multiple interviewees):**
- **Missing PO or Unresolvable Discrepancy Path** (frequent mention): Escalation from AP Clerk or Purchasing to AP Supervisor (Rita). Rita coordinates creation of retrospective PO (with Department Manager input), works with Karen to obtain corrected invoice from supplier, or decides to reject. Once resolved, the invoice re-enters the normal flow at the "Log in system" or "Purchasing verification" step.  
- **Manager Rejection/Investigation Path:** Rare (once every couple of months per Carlos). May result in hold, further supplier contact (Karen), or full rejection.  
- **Supplier Correction Loop:** Any party (AP, Purchasing, or Karen) can initiate contact with Supplier for revised invoice or missing information. This creates a loop back to the initial receipt/check steps.  
- **Escalation to Supervisor:** Triggered when discrepancies cannot be easily resolved by Mary or Dan. Rita ensures the invoice does not remain stuck indefinitely.

**Sequence Flow Notes & Ambiguities Consolidated:**
- Core sequence is consistent across interviews: PO validation  logging  Purchasing verification/confirmation of receipt  matching  managerial approval  payment scheduling.
- Slight variation exists on exact ownership of "matching" (Mary describes it as her step after Dan's confirmation; Dan describes himself as performing the detailed comparison). Consolidated here as collaborative verification primarily led by Purchasing, with AP performing logging and final system recording.
- System acts as an automated participant for logging, notifications, and routing after successful matching.
- No explicit parallel gateways identified; most decisions are exclusive (XOR).
- Rework loops are common for exceptions (missing PO, pricing/quantity issues, budget flags).

**Pain Points / Variations Highlighted:**
- Invoices without PO (common for informal services) require extra effort (retrospective PO via Rita).
- Discrepancies often require multi-party coordination (AP, Purchasing, Karen, Supplier), causing delays.
- Manager approval is usually straightforward but can trigger investigation on budget or unexpected items.
- Risk of invoices becoming stuck without supervisor intervention (Rita's role).

This model is complete enough for direct translation into a BPMN diagram (using lanes, tasks, XOR gateways, message flows to Supplier/Karen, sequence flows with loops, and data objects attached to relevant tasks). The happy path is clearly distinguished from exception paths while noting where they rejoin the main flow.
