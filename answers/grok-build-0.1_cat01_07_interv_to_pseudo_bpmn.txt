**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

### Process Overview
**Name:** Invoice Approval Process  
**Trigger:** Receipt of supplier invoice (typically via email)  
**End States:**  
- Payment scheduled (successful path)  
- Invoice rejected or returned to supplier (exception paths)  
**Primary Participants (Lanes):**  
- Accounts Payable Clerk  
- Purchasing Officer  
- Department Manager  
- Accounts Payable Supervisor (exception handling)  
- Supplier Relationship Manager (conditional support)

---

### Main Process Flow

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (Accounts Payable Clerk)

#### 1. Initial Validation (Accounts Payable Clerk)
- **Task:** Receive and Review Invoice  
- **Gateway (Exclusive):** Does the invoice contain a valid Purchase Order (PO) number?  
  - **No:**  
    - **Task:** Request missing PO/details from supplier  
    - **End Event:** Invoice returned to supplier (incomplete)  
  - **Yes:**  
    - **Task:** Log invoice into accounting system

#### 2. Purchasing Confirmation
- **Task:** Forward invoice to Purchasing Officer (with PO reference)  
- **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Verify invoice against Purchase Order (quantities, prices, delivery charges, etc.)  
- **Gateway (Exclusive):** Do invoice details match the PO?  
  - **No:**  
    - **Task:** Contact supplier for correction (or request AP to hold invoice)  
    - **Gateway:** Can discrepancy be resolved?  
      - **Yes:** Return to Purchasing verification task  
      - **No:** Escalate to Accounts Payable Supervisor  
  - **Yes:**  
    - **Task:** Send confirmation email to Accounts Payable Clerk

#### 3. Matching and Routing (Accounts Payable Clerk)
- **Task:** Perform line-item matching (invoice vs. PO)  
- **Gateway (Exclusive):** Do line items match?  
  - **No:**  
    - **Task:** Email Purchasing Officer for clarification  
    - **Gateway:** Can issue be resolved?  
      - **Yes:** Return to matching task  
      - **No:** Escalate to Accounts Payable Supervisor  
  - **Yes:**  
    - **Task:** Route invoice to Department Manager for approval (system-triggered notification)

#### 4. Managerial Approval (Department Manager)
- **Task:** Review invoice, PO, and purchasing confirmation  
- **Gateway (Exclusive):** Approve invoice?  
  - **Yes:**  
    - **Task:** Approve in system  
    - **Task (Accounts Payable Clerk):** Schedule invoice for payment run  
    - **End Event:** Payment scheduled  
  - **No:**  
    - **Task:** Reject or request further investigation  
    - **End Event:** Invoice rejected / returned for rework (or escalated)

---

### Exception / Variation Handling

**Exception Path – Accounts Payable Supervisor** (triggered by escalation from AP Clerk or Purchasing Officer)

- **Task:** Investigate escalated invoice  
- **Gateway (Exclusive):** Can exception be resolved through normal channels?  
  - **No (Missing PO or informal service):**  
    - **Task:** Coordinate creation of retrospective PO with Department Manager  
    - **Task:** Engage Supplier Relationship Manager if needed (pricing, contract terms, or missing information)  
    - **Gateway:** Retrospective PO created and accepted?  
      - **Yes:** Return to main flow (Purchasing Confirmation or Matching step)  
      - **No:** Reject invoice  
  - **Yes:** Return to appropriate point in main flow (usually before Manager approval)

**Additional Variations Noted:**
- Invoices without a PO (especially for services) may bypass initial rejection and go directly to the AP Supervisor for retrospective PO creation.
- Supplier Relationship Manager is involved only on an ad-hoc basis when contractual or relationship issues arise.
- Minor discrepancies may be resolved directly between Purchasing and the supplier without escalating to the Supervisor.

---

### Artifacts / Data Objects
- **Input:** Supplier Invoice (email)  
- **Data Object:** Purchase Order (referenced throughout)  
- **Data Object:** Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation (referenced by Purchasing)  
- **Output:** Approved Invoice (ready for payment scheduling)

---

### Notes on Model Construction
- The model represents the **consensus happy path** across all interviewees, with Purchasing verification occurring before final managerial approval.
- Escalation logic to the AP Supervisor is modeled as a separate exception lane to keep the main flow clean.
- Some ambiguity remains around exact timing of “goods receipt confirmation” vs. “PO verification” — the model treats Purchasing as performing both checks.
- The process is largely sequential but includes several exclusive gateways to reflect decision points described in the interviews.
