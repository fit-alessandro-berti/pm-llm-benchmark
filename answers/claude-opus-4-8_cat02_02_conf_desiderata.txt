# Loan Process Analysis

## 1. Normative Rule Violations (Serious)

### Case 1 —  COMPLIANT
The sequence is followed perfectly:
> Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case

All ordering rules are satisfied, and since the loan was **approved**, finalizing documentation before closing is correct. **No normative violations.**

### Case 2 —  MULTIPLE VIOLATIONS
This case violates several hard rules:

| Violation | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Order reversal** | Risk Assessment (08:20) occurred *before* Credit Check (09:00). The rule **Credit Check  Risk Assessment** is broken. |
| **Contradictory action** | The loan was **Rejected**, yet **Finalize Documentation** was performed (10:00). Documentation must NOT be finalized after a rejection. |
| **Missing activity** | There is **no "Inform Applicant"** step. The applicant was never informed of the outcome, violating the mandatory *Inform Applicant after Loan Decision* rule. |

**Verdict:** Case 2 is severely non-compliant and should be flagged for investigation/rework.

### Case 3 —  NORMATIVELY COMPLIANT
The activity **order** is fully correct:
> Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case

Since approved, documentation was finalized before closing. **No hard rule is broken** — its issues are purely timing-related (see below).

## 2. Desiderata (Soft) Violations — Suboptimal but Acceptable

### Case 1 — Meets desiderata (mostly)
- Credit Check within 30 min of application 
- Inform Applicant within 2 hours of decision  (well under 48h)
-  **No Follow-up Call** recorded — minor desideratum miss.

### Case 2 — Desiderata are secondary here
The hard violations dominate. Note it also lacks a Follow-up Call, but this is irrelevant given the serious normative failures.

### Case 3 — Multiple desiderata misses (not serious)
| Desideratum | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| Credit Check within 24h |  Took **~49 hours** (Jan 12 09:00  Jan 14 10:00) |
| Inform Applicant within 48h of decision |  Took **~75 hours** (Jan 14 12:00  Jan 16 15:00) |
| Follow-up Call before closing |  **Not performed** |

**Clarification:** None of these make Case 3 invalid. The process was logically correct; it was simply *slow* and skipped the optional courtesy call. These represent **suboptimal service quality**, not rule breaches.

## 3. Interpretations & Justifications

**Case 2 (cannot be justified):**
The reversed ordering and post-rejection documentation are genuine errors. Possible *explanations* (not justifications):
- The reversed order may reflect a **logging/timestamp error** rather than actual out-of-order execution — worth verifying with system logs.
- The "Finalize Documentation" after rejection might be a **mislabeled activity** (e.g., archiving the rejection file rather than approval paperwork). If so, the activity should be renamed; if it truly is approval documentation, it's an outright violation.
- The missing "Inform Applicant" is the most concerning — the applicant may have been left uninformed, a compliance and customer-fairness risk.

**Case 3 (justifiable delays):**
- The Credit Check delay spans a weekend-like gap (Jan 12  Jan 14), suggesting **non-working days or external dependency** (e.g., waiting on a third-party credit bureau).
- The Inform Applicant delay similarly spans several days — possibly **batch communications, applicant unavailability, or workload backlog**.
- The missing Follow-up Call is optional; its absence is reasonable if the applicant was already clearly informed.
