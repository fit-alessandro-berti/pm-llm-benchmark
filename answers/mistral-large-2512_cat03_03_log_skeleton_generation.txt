### Key Observations:
1. **Equivalence**: `Check Availability (CA)` and `Reserve Items (RI)` are equivalent because if one occurs, the other must occur the same number of times (either both occur once or neither occurs).
2. **Always Before/After**: These constraints enforce the sequential nature of the process (e.g., `PI` must occur after `RI`).
3. **Never Together**: `CA` and `RP` cannot co-occur because `RP` (recording payment) happens much later in the process.
4. **Activity Frequencies**: Most activities occur at most once per case, except for `RI` (which may not occur if items are unavailable).
5. **Directly-Follows**: Captures immediate transitions (e.g., `RO  CA  RI  PI  ...`).

### Notes:
- The `always_before` and `always_after` constraints are often redundant (e.g., `('A', 'B')` in `always_before` implies `('B', 'A')` in `always_after`). Both are included for clarity.
- `Invoice Customer (IC)` and `Record Payment (RP)` are loosely coupled with the physical fulfillment process, so their constraints are more flexible.
- Adjustments can be made based on specific business rules (e.g., if `IC` must always occur before `D`).
