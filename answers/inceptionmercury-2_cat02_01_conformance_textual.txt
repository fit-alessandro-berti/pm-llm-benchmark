## Detailed explanation of each violation

### Case2 – “Package before payment”

| Sequence | Normative rule that is broken |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Receive  Check  **Package items**  **Authorize payment**  Ship  Close | 1. *Package items* **must occur after** *Authorize payment* (the text: “Package items should only occur after authorization and before shipping”). <br>2. *Authorize payment* **must occur after** *Check inventory* **and before** packaging. The order in case2 reverses these two steps, so both constraints are violated. |

The process is therefore nonconformant because it attempts to prepare the goods before confirming that the customer’s payment method is approved.

---

### Case3 – “Cancellation after payment and shipping after cancellation”

| Sequence | Normative rule that is broken |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Receive  Check  **Authorize payment**  **Cancel order**  **Ship order**  Close | 1. When the inventory check finds insufficient stock, the **only** allowed continuation is *Cancel order*  *Close order*. Authorizing payment **before** a cancellation contradicts the rule “Authorize payment should only occur after Check inventory **and before packaging**, and only when inventory is sufficient.” <br>2. After a *Cancel order* step, **no fulfillment activities** (Package, Ship) may occur. The presence of *Ship order* after cancellation directly violates “Ship order should never occur after a cancellation.” |

Thus case3 breaches both the branching logic (cancellation should be the terminal branch for insufficient inventory) and the prohibition on postcancellation fulfillment.

---

### Case5 – “Missing packaging step”

| Sequence | Normative rule that is broken |
|----------|------------------------------|
| Receive  Check  **Authorize payment**  **Ship order**  Close | The description states: “Package items should only occur after authorization **and before shipping**.” The *Package items* activity is completely absent, so the process jumps from payment authorization straight to shipping, violating the required packaging step. |

Because packaging is a prerequisite for shipping, case5 does not conform to the expected flow.

---

## Interpretation of ambiguities

The normative text mentions that “the exact nature of these sequences and the conditions they imply may contain some room for interpretation.” However, the core constraints are explicit:

1. **Check inventory must precede any payment or fulfillment activity.**  
2. **Authorize payment is allowed only when inventory is sufficient** (i.e., before the cancellation branch).  
3. **Packaging must follow payment authorization and precede shipping.**  
4. **Shipping is forbidden after a cancellation.**  
5. **Close order is always the final activity.**

Even allowing for occasional “extraordinary circumstances,” none of the three flagged cases can be justified without breaking a fundamental rule above. Therefore they are classified as deviations.

---

**Bottom line:**  
- **Conforming cases:** 1 and 4.  
- **Nonconforming cases:** 2 (wrong order of package/authorize), 3 (cancellation after payment and shipping after cancellation), and 5 (missing packaging).
