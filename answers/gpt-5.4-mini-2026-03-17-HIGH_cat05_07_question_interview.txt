Here’s a set of targeted, open-ended questions I would ask to better understand and clarify the property onboarding process:

### Intake and document verification
1. **Can you walk me through how a new property onboarding request first enters the process, and who is responsible at that point?**
2. **What documents are considered mandatory before onboarding can move forward, and how do you determine whether a document is valid or outdated?**
3. **When documentation is missing or incomplete, what is the usual follow-up process with the landlord, and who owns that communication?**
4. **Are there any differences in the required documents depending on property type, city, or local regulations?**
5. **At what point is a property considered “ready” to move from document review into active onboarding?**

### Property data capture
6. **What property information must be captured centrally, and which details are most important for downstream teams?**
7. **How do you confirm that the property details entered into the system are accurate and complete?**
8. **Are there cases where property information changes later in the process, and if so, how are those changes handled?**

### Property manager assignment
9. **How is the decision made to assign a specific property manager to a new property?**
10. **Which factors matter most when matching a manager to a property, and how are those factors weighed against each other?**
11. **Are there situations where workload, geography, and property type expertise conflict in the assignment decision? How do you resolve that?**
12. **Who has the final say in the assignment, and can landlords request a particular manager?**
13. **How do you handle properties with unusual requirements such as furnished units, luxury tenants, or student housing?**

### Introductory meeting and expectation setting
14. **What is typically covered in the introductory call or meeting between the property manager and landlord?**
15. **What are the most common special conditions or policies that need to be clarified early in the process?**
16. **How are disagreements or misaligned expectations with the landlord handled at this stage?**
17. **Are there standard topics that must be confirmed before the process can proceed further?**

### Inspection, photography, and property readiness
18. **How do you decide when to schedule photography and inspection, and which one happens first?**
19. **What criteria does the inspector use to determine whether the property is ready to be marketed or leased?**
20. **When the inspection reveals issues, how do you decide which issues must be fixed immediately versus later?**
21. **Who coordinates repairs or contractor involvement, and how is responsibility shared with the landlord?**
22. **Are there cases where a property can be partially activated while certain issues are still being addressed?**
23. **What happens if a landlord delays repairs or declines to address inspection findings?**

### Marketing and pricing
24. **How do the marketing team and property manager coordinate once the property passes inspection?**
25. **What information is needed to create the listing and brochures, and who approves the final marketing materials?**
26. **How are pricing decisions made, and what market or internal factors influence them?**
27. **Are there situations where pricing is adjusted differently for certain property types or local conditions?**
28. **Who has authority to approve promotional discounts or special offers?**

### Activation and tenant screening
29. **What does it mean operationally when a property is “activated” in your systems?**
30. **At what point do prospective tenants become involved, and who manages that part of the process?**
31. **Can you describe the full tenant screening workflow and the criteria used to evaluate applicants?**
32. **How do you balance objective screening criteria with landlord preferences, if those differ?**
33. **What happens when multiple qualified tenants apply for the same property?**
34. **How are lease term negotiations handled when tenants want different terms than the company or landlord prefers?**

### Lease finalization and move-in
35. **What steps must be completed before a lease can be finalized and signed?**
36. **Who is responsible for collecting the security deposit, and are there any exceptions to the standard process?**
37. **How is the move-in date coordinated across the landlord, tenant, and internal teams?**
38. **Are there any required handoff steps between onboarding and ongoing property management once a tenant moves in?**

### Compliance, finance, and IT checks
39. **What kinds of compliance reviews occur after the lease is drafted or signed, and what issues are they looking for?**
40. **How does the financial review influence the onboarding process, especially if pricing or profitability assumptions change?**
41. **What does the IT validation check for, and when does that happen relative to activation and leasing?**
42. **If one of these internal reviews flags an issue, how is the resolution coordinated and who owns it?**

### Exceptions and special cases
43. **What are the most common exceptions or edge cases that arise during onboarding?**
44. **How do you handle properties that require special insurance, custom lease clauses, or unusual legal terms?**
45. **Are there any property types, locations, or landlord behaviors that tend to create the most friction in the process?**
46. **What happens when there is a conflict between landlord requests and company policy or legal requirements?**
47. **Are there any scenarios where onboarding is paused, restarted, or abandoned entirely?**

### Roles, accountability, and communication
48. **Can you describe the main roles involved in the process and what each is accountable for?**
49. **Where do responsibilities shift from one team or person to another, and are those handoffs clearly defined?**
50. **How do teams communicate across regional offices, support functions, and external stakeholders during onboarding?**
51. **What tools or channels are used to track status, approvals, and outstanding tasks?**
52. **How do you ensure that everyone involved is working from the same current information?**

### Timing, sequencing, and performance
53. **What is the typical sequence of steps from initial landlord contact through tenant move-in?**
54. **Are there any steps that can happen in parallel, and which ones must happen strictly in order?**
55. **Which stages usually take the most time, and what tends to slow them down?**
56. **Are there service-level expectations or internal targets for how quickly onboarding should move?**
57. **How do you know when the onboarding process has been successful from the company’s perspective?**

### Broader process understanding
58. **If you were to describe the ideal onboarding journey, what would it look like end to end?**
59. **What are the most important decision points in the process, and what information is needed at each one?**
60. **If you could improve one part of the onboarding process, which part would it be and why?**
