Based on the provided interview transcripts, here is the structured BPMN-like process model.

### **Process Overview: Accounts Payable Invoice Workflow**

#### **1. Roles/Participants**
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk (Mary):** Initiator, logging, reconciliation, payment scheduling.
*   **Purchasing Department (Dan):** Goods receipt confirmation, PO/Invoice matching.
*   **Department Manager (Carlos):** Final budgetary and business approval.
*   **AP Supervisor (Rita):** Exception handling, escalations, retrospective PO management.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** External communication and contract dispute resolution.

---

#### **2. Process Flow**

**Phase 1: Reception and Validation**
1.  **Task:** Receive Invoice (Email).
2.  **Gateway (Decision - Valid PO?):**
    *   *No PO:* **Task:** Contact Supplier/Return to Supplier for details. (Can involve *Supplier Relationship Manager*).
    *   *Valid PO:* **Task:** Log Invoice into Accounting System.
3.  **Flow:** Forward to Purchasing Department for validation.

**Phase 2: Reconciliation and Matching**
4.  **Task:** Verify Goods Receipt and Match Invoice to PO (Purchasing).
5.  **Gateway (Decision - Match Found?):**
    *   *Discrepancy identified:*
        *   **Task:** Resolve via Supplier/Purchasing/AP Clerk.
        *   **Escalation Path:** If unresolved, escalate to **AP Supervisor (Rita)**.
        *   *Exception:* If no PO exists (service contract), **AP Supervisor** initiates Retrospective PO creation.
    *   *Match Confirmed:* **Task:** Send Confirmation Email to AP.

**Phase 3: Approval**
6.  **Task:** System-triggered notification to Department Manager.
7.  **Gateway (Decision - Manager Approval):**
    *   *Approve:* **Task:** Manager hits "Approve" in system.
    *   *Reject/Hold:* **Task:** Return to AP Clerk for investigation.

**Phase 4: Closure**
8.  **Task:** Schedule for Payment (AP Clerk).

---

#### **3. Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs**
*   **Input:** Supplier Invoice (Digital/Email).
*   **System Objects:** Purchase Order (PO), Accounting System Log, Confirmation Email.
*   **Output:** Scheduled Payment/Processed Invoice.

---

#### **4. Exceptions and Variations**
*   **The "No PO" Exception:** Invoices arriving without a PO trigger an immediate return to the supplier or an intervention by the AP Supervisor to create a retrospective PO.
*   **The "Discrepancy" Loop:** If pricing/quantities do not match, the process enters a loop between Purchasing and AP. If the loop exceeds standard resolution time, the AP Supervisor steps in to mediate.
*   **The "Relationship" Intervention:** The Supplier Relationship Manager acts as a specialized resource for resolving contractual pricing disputes, effectively "pausing" the standard flow until they provide clarification.
*   **The "Managerial Hold":** Managers may reject/hold invoices based on budgetary variance (occurring ~once every two months).

---

#### **5. Summary Model Table (for mapping)**

| Step | Actor | Action | Condition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | AP Clerk | Receive/Validate | Check for PO |
| 2 | AP Clerk | Log to System | PO exists |
| 3 | Purchasing | Match/Verify | Compare PO vs. Invoice |
| 4 | AP Clerk/Supervisor | Handle Exceptions | Discrepancy found |
| 5 | Manager | Final Approval | System Notification |
| 6 | AP Clerk | Schedule Payment | Final sign-off received |

**Ambiguity Note:** There is slight uncertainty regarding the exact handover after Purchasing confirms the match; Mary claims she forwards it to the manager, while Dan assumes the system handles it or it goes back to Mary. The model assumes a system-triggered notification to the Manager as described by both Mary and Carlos.
