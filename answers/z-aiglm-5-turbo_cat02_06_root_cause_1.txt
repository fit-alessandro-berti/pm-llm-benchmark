By analyzing the timestamps between individual activities, three primary factors are causing these massive delays:

**A. Inefficient Escalation Handoffs (Cases 102 & 105)**
When a ticket is escalated to a Level-2 Agent, it sits dormant for an unacceptable amount of time before the specialized team even begins investigating.
*   **Case 102:** Waited 2.5 hours between "Escalate to Level-2 Agent" (11:30) and "Investigate Issue" (14:00).
*   **Case 105:** Waited a staggering **28 hours** between "Escalate to Level-2 Agent" (10:00) and "Investigate Issue" (14:00 the next day). 
*   *Root Cause:* There is no direct routing or urgency assigned to Level-2 escalations. The ticket likely sits in a general queue unseen by the specialized team.

**B. Overnight Stagnation / Shift Handoff Issues (Cases 102, 104, & 105)**
A recurring, suspicious ~19-hour gap appears between the final "Investigate Issue" and "Resolve Ticket" steps in the delayed cases.
*   **Case 102:** Investigated at 14:00 (March 1)  Resolved at 09:00 (March 2) = **19-hour gap**
*   **Case 104:** Investigated at 13:00 (March 1)  Resolved at 08:00 (March 2) = **19-hour gap**
*   **Case 105:** Investigated at 14:00 (March 2)  Resolved at 09:00 (March 3) = **19-hour gap**
*   *Root Cause:* Once an agent investigates an issue late in the afternoon, the ticket sits idle overnight. There is no after-hours resolution capability, nor is there a system in place to hand off active, investigated tickets to a following shift to apply the fix.

**C. Level-1 Agent Lag Before Investigation (Case 104)**

### 3. Insights and Recommendations
The data reveals that the delays are **not caused by the actual complexity of the issues**, but rather by *systemic waiting*. When agents actually perform tasks (Triage, Investigate, Resolve, Close), they take between 10 and 60 minutes. The cycle time is being inflated by tickets sitting in queues.
