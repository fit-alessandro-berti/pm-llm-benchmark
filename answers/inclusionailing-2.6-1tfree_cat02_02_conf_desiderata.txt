### **3. Possible Interpretations / Justifications**  

1. **Case 2 — Risk Assessment before Credit Check**  
   - *Possible reason:* Human error or system misrouting — perhaps the Risk Assessment was started before realizing Credit Check data was needed, or an exception was attempted to speed up processing but without proper authorization.  
   - *Why it matters:* This could lead to incorrect risk evaluation if credit data is missing or outdated, potentially causing bad loan decisions.  

2. **Case 2 — Finalize Documentation after Rejection**  
   - *Possible reason:* Administrative mistake — maybe the staff prepared documents assuming approval, or there was a system glitch that didn’t block the action after rejection.  
   - *Why it matters:* Wastes resources and could create confusion or legal issues if finalized documents are sent or stored improperly for a rejected case.  

3. **Case 3 — Long delays in Credit Check and Informing Applicant**  
   - *Possible reason:* High workload, missing information requiring additional checks, or batch processing on specific days (e.g., credit checks only done on certain weekdays).  
   - *Why it’s not severe:* The sequence is correct, so the loan decision is still valid; delays affect customer experience but not compliance.  

4. **Missing Follow-up Calls**  
   - *Possible reason:* Not automated, relies on staff discretion, or deemed unnecessary for straightforward cases.  
   - *Why it’s not severe:* It’s a soft recommendation for customer satisfaction and alignment, not a compliance requirement.

**Summary:**  
- **Serious normative violations** exist only in **Case 2** (wrong order of Credit Check/Risk Assessment, and illegal documentation finalization after rejection).  
- **Desiderata violations** exist in **Case 3** (delays in credit check and informing applicant) and **Case 2 & 3** (missing follow-up calls).  
- **Case 1** is fully compliant with both normative rules and desiderata.
