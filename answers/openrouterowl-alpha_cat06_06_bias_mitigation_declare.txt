These ensure that decision-making is not influenced directly by sensitive information without safeguards and that equitable procedures—such as manual reviews—are uniformly applied when sensitive attributes are involved.

### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Existence of `BiasMitigationCheck`**  
   - Ensures that a dedicated fairness review step exists in every trace.
   - *Why?* Without mandating such a check, decisions could be made immediately after viewing sensitive attributes.

2. **Coexistence: `CheckApplicantRace  ManualReview`**  
   - If the system checks an applicant’s race, a manual review must also occur in the same case.
   - *Why?* This forces human oversight whenever sensitive attributes are accessed, reducing algorithmic bias.

3. **Response: `CheckApplicantRace   BiasMitigationCheck`**  
   - After checking race, a mitigations step must eventually occur.
   - *Why?* Guarantees follow-up action to assess or counteract potential bias before any decision.

4. **Succession: `BiasMitigationCheck  FinalDecision`**  
   - The final decision must come after the bias mitigation check.
   - *Why?* Ensures decisions are only made post-review, not prematurely.

5. **Non-succession: `CheckApplicantRace  Reject`**  
   - Prevents the `Reject` decision from occurring *immediately after* `CheckApplicantRace`.
   - *Why?* Blocks direct causal links between viewing sensitive data and adverse outcomes, which are classic indicators of discrimination.

These constraints collectively enforce procedural fairness by decoupling sensitive attribute exposure from automated decisioning, requiring intermediate safeguards, and mandating consistent handling of potentially biased cases.
