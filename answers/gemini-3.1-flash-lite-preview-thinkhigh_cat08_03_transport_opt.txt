### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Integration & Preprocessing:**
*   **The Approach:** I will utilize an ETL process to create a "Unified Event Log." Using the `Vehicle-Day` as the **Case ID**, I will correlate GPS coordinates with Scanner timestamps.
*   **Challenges:** 
    *   **Clock Skew:** Scanners and GPS trackers may have unsynchronized internal clocks. I will apply time-offset normalization.
    *   **Data Granularity:** GPS events occur every few seconds, while scanner events are infrequent. I will "sample" or aggregate GPS data into logical states (e.g., *Moving, Idling, Parking*) to reduce noise while maintaining process context.

**Discovery & Conformance:**
*   **Visualization:** Using algorithms like the *Inductive Miner*, I will visualize the "Happy Path" (Depot $\rightarrow$ Delivery $\rightarrow$ Depot) versus the "Reality Path" (which may include `Unscheduled Stop` or `Failed Delivery` loops).
*   **Conformance Checking:** I will map the actual execution against the *Dispatch Plan*. I will look for:
    *   **Sequence Deviations:** Stops performed in a different order than planned.
    *   **Temporal Deviations:** Time spent at a stop exceeding the planned "service time" threshold.
    *   **Resource Deviations:** Use of a vehicle/driver combination that violates the dispatch constraint.

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
*   **On-Time Delivery Rate:** `Count(Delivery Success within Window) / Total Deliveries`.
*   **Travel vs. Service Time Ratio:** A critical metric. If this is high, it indicates inefficient routing; if low, it indicates inefficient on-site operations.
*   **Failed Delivery Rate:** Calculated by tracking `Case ID` clusters where a `Delivery Failed` event occurs, requiring return to depot.

**Bottleneck Identification:**
*   **Technique:** I will use *Dotted Chart Analysis* to visualize stop-density by time of day. 
*   **Impact Quantification:** By applying a *Bottleneck Analysis* (calculating the waiting time between `Arrive Customer` and `Depart Customer`), I can isolate whether delays are systemic (e.g., high-rise buildings take longer to service) or isolated (e.g., poor driver training or difficult parking).

### 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

To move from "what" to "why," I will use:
*   **Variant Analysis:** I will compare "Top 10% Performing Routes" vs. "Bottom 10%." Does the top-tier use a different sequence? Do they avoid specific zones?
*   **Correlation Analysis:** By overlaying the `Unscheduled Stop` events (maintenance) with `Vehicle ID` age or mileage, I can determine if specific vans are causing delays due to mechanical failure.
*   **Dwell Time Analysis:** I will analyze if failed deliveries correlate with specific time windows (e.g., 9:00 AM vs. 5:00 PM), suggesting the need for tighter customer communication for high-risk time slots.

### 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

| Strategy | Targeted Inefficiency | Root Cause Addressed | Expected Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dynamic Windowing** | Late deliveries in congested zones. | Inaccurate static travel time estimations. | +15% On-Time Delivery rate. |

### 5. Operational Constraints and Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints:**
*   The optimization algorithms will be governed by "Hard Constraints": Driver Hours of Service (HOS) and vehicle capacity. Process mining models will treat these as "Guardrails" in the discovery phase—any route violating these is flagged as an immediate operational risk.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan:**
*   **Process Mining Dashboard:** I will deploy a live dashboard that monitors:
    *   **Real-time Process Conformance:** Alerts when a driver deviates significantly from the planned sequence.
    *   **Drift Analysis:** Tracking if the process is "drifting" (e.g., are average service times slowly creeping up?).
    *   **Sustainability Metrics:** Monitoring the "Idling Time" vs. "Travel Time" ratio to provide drivers with feedback on fuel-efficient driving habits.
