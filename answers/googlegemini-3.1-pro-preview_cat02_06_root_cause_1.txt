### 2. Potential Root Causes of Performance Issues

**A. Escalation Bottlenecks (Level-2 Queue Delays)**

**B. Inefficient Triage and Routing**
*   In **Case 102**, the ticket is assigned to a Level-1 agent at 09:00, but without ever triggering an "Investigate Issue" step, it is escalated to Level-2 at 11:30. This means the ticket sat with a Level-1 agent for 2.5 hours only to be passed off. This represents a failure in the initial "Triage" phase, which should have routed it to Level-2 immediately.

**C. Level-1 Agent Capacity / Overload**
*   **Case 104** is never escalated, yet it takes 24 hours to resolve. The root cause here is a massive delay between assignment and investigation. The ticket is assigned to a Level-1 agent at 09:30, but investigation doesn't start until 13:00 (3.5 hours later). 
*   Because investigation started so late in the day, the resolution spilled over into the next morning (a 19-hour gap between investigation and resolution, likely due to the end of business hours).

**D. The "Overnight" Factor**
*   A systemic issue compounding these delays is standard business scheduling. When queues (either L1 or L2) back up and push investigation times into the afternoon, resolution is pushed to the following morning. Every time a ticket crosses a day boundary, 12-16 hours of "dead time" is added to the total lifecycle.

### 3. Explanation of Impact and Recommendations

**How these factors impact Cycle Times:**
Long waiting times between steps (queue times) are the primary culprits behind inflated resolution times, rather than the actual work being performed (touch time). Unnecessary handoffs (like in Case 102) and severe understaffing at specialized tiers (like in Case 105) cause tickets to stall. Consequently, tickets miss the window to be solved within the same business day, further ballooning the cycle time metrics.

**Insights & Recommendations to Address Bottlenecks:**

1.  **Improve Front-Line Triage (Intelligent Routing):**
    *   *Insight:* Case 102 wasted hours at Level-1 before being escalated un-investigated. 
    *   *Recommendation:* Upgrade triage criteria. Implement keyword tracking, AI pre-screening, or more thorough intake forms so complex issues are routed directly to Level-2 agents at the time of receipt, bypassing Level-1 entirely.

2.  **Review Level-2 Staffing and Capacity:**
    *   *Insight:* The 28-hour wait in Case 105 suggests the Level-2 team is severely understaffed or dealing with too much backlog.
    *   *Recommendation:* Reallocate resources, hire additional specialized agents, or cross-train senior Level-1 agents to handle lower-tier Level-2 tickets to clear the escalation pipeline faster. 

3.  **Implement Internal Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for "Time to First Action":**
    *   *Insight:* Case 104 sat for 3.5 hours after assignment before being looked at.
    *   *Recommendation:* Create internal SLAs requiring agents to begin investigating a ticket within 30–60 minutes of it being assigned to them. If an agent is at capacity, the ticket should be routed to an available agent instead of sitting in a personal queue.

4.  **Adjust SLA Reporting for Business Hours (Optional based on business model):**
    *   *Recommendation:* If the support team is not 24/7, ensure that cycle time metrics are calculated using "Business Hours" rather than strict chronological time. This will give leadership a more accurate reflection of actual queue delays versus natural overnight pauses.
