**1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

**Data Preprocessing and Integration**  
The first step is to create a unified event log where each case is defined as a **Vehicle-Day (Case ID = VehicleID + Date)**. This is the natural process instance for last-mile delivery.

**Integration steps:**
- Use timestamp + VehicleID as the primary key for merging.
- Map all events to standardized activity names (e.g., "Depart Depot", "Arrive Customer", "Delivery Success", "Delivery Failed", "Unscheduled Stop", "Arrive Depot").
- Enrich events with attributes from all sources: planned route and time windows (from Dispatch), GPS-derived travel time, speed, location, idle time, scanner-derived service time, maintenance events, driver ID, number of packages, etc.
- Handle different granularities: GPS events are high-frequency; scanner and dispatch events are sparse. Apply rule-based logic to infer missing activities (e.g., if speed drops to 0 near a customer location followed by a scanner event, infer "Parking/Approach Customer").
- Add derived attributes: travel time between stops, service time (time between Arrive and Depart Customer), waiting time, overtime flag, distance traveled (from GPS), estimated vs actual arrival time.

**Challenges:**
- Timestamp synchronization across systems (different clocks, timezone issues).
- Missing or inconsistent Case IDs — requires entity resolution.
- Ambiguous events (e.g., "Low Speed Detected" vs actual traffic jam vs parking).
- Failed deliveries requiring same-day or next-day re-delivery — deciding whether they belong to the same case.
- High volume of GPS points — requires aggregation or filtering to avoid unreadable models.

**Process Discovery**  
I would apply the **Inductive Miner** (infrequent variant) or **Split Miner** on the filtered log to discover the actual end-to-end process model. This produces a BPMN or Petri net showing the real flow, including:
- Frequent loops (return to depot mid-shift for reloads)
- Unplanned stops (maintenance, traffic, driver breaks)
- Parallel branches (multiple failed delivery attempts)
- Variants caused by failed deliveries or vehicle issues.

**Conformance Checking**  
Using **Alignments** or **Multi-alignments** (ProM or PM4Py), I would replay the event log on the *ideal process model* derived from the Dispatch System (planned route sequence and time windows). 

Deviations to detect:
- **Sequence deviations**: stops executed in different order than planned.
- **Unplanned activities**: "Unscheduled Stop", "Low Speed Detected" for long periods, extra depot returns.
- **Time deviations**: moves that exceed planned travel time by >30%, service times significantly above median.
- **Skipped activities**: planned stops completely omitted.
- **Extra cases**: vehicles returning late or with overtime.

This produces a **deviation dashboard** showing frequency and severity per route, driver, and time of day.

**2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** calculated directly from the enriched event log:

- **On-Time Delivery Rate**: % of "Delivery Success" where actual timestamp is within customer time window (from dispatch).
- **Average Service Time per Stop**: time between "Arrive Customer" and "Depart Customer".
- **Travel Time vs Service Time Ratio**: total moving time vs total customer dwell time per case.
- **Fuel Consumption Proxy**: idle time + time spent in low-speed zones (<15 km/h) per km or per package.
- **Vehicle Utilization Rate**: (number of deliveries / planned capacity) and % of shift time spent moving vs idle.
- **Traffic Delay Frequency/Duration**: cumulative time where speed < threshold in known road segments.
- **Failed Delivery Rate**: % of stops ending in "Delivery Failed".
- **Overtime Rate** and **Total Cycle Time** (Depart Depot to Arrive Depot).

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**:
- **Performance-enhanced process maps** (using DFG with time annotations) to color activities/arcs by duration.
- **Bottleneck analysis** using waiting time decomposition (between activities).
- **Case-level filtering** and **variant analysis** to compare fastest vs slowest 20% of cases.
- **Clustering** of cases by driver, route zone, time-of-day, and vehicle type.
- **Location-based analysis**: aggregate delays by GPS coordinates to identify traffic hotspots or difficult parking zones.
- **Social network analysis** on handovers or driver performance.

Bottlenecks are typically found in specific zones during peak hours, particular drivers with high variance in service time, or routes with many failed deliveries requiring rework.

**3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Process mining allows us to move from "where" to "why" through targeted analyses:

- **Variant Analysis**: Compare "happy paths" (on-time, low fuel) vs "problematic paths" (late, high idle time). Use decision trees or statistical tests on attributes (driver, zone, #packages, day of week, weather if added).
- **Dwell Time Analysis**: Correlate service time with customer type, floor level (if available), time of day, or driver. High variance usually points to parking difficulty or customer interaction issues.
- **Traffic Pattern Mining**: Overlay GPS low-speed events with time-of-day and location to discover recurring congestion patterns not accounted for in static routing.
- **Failure Cascade Analysis**: Trace failed deliveries — how often they cause route deviation, extra distance, or overtime. Calculate the "cost of failure" in additional km and time.
- **Maintenance Impact Analysis**: Identify cases with "Unscheduled Stop" and measure the downstream effect on subsequent deliveries and overtime.
- **Driver Behavior Analysis**: Compare performance metrics across drivers on the same routes. Look for differences in route adherence, speed choice, and failed delivery handling.

Typical root causes discovered in last-mile settings:
- Static routes that ignore time-of-day traffic patterns.
- Time windows that cluster too many deliveries in the same 30-minute slot.
- Inaccurate travel time estimates (especially "last 100 meters" parking time).
- High failed delivery rate due to poor customer notification.
- Certain drivers consistently taking longer service times (training opportunity).

**4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Traffic-Aware Time Windows**  
*Targets*: Travel time deviations and traffic bottlenecks.  
*Root cause*: Static planning ignores discovered recurring congestion patterns.  
*Process Mining Support*: Hotspot analysis from GPS data + conformance checking showing systematic late arrivals in specific zones/times.  
*Implementation*: Feed discovered average travel times per zone and time bucket into the routing engine. Dynamically adjust time windows and stop sequences during the day.  
*Expected Impact*: 15-25% reduction in travel time, improved On-Time Delivery Rate, lower fuel consumption.

**Strategy 2: Failed Delivery Prevention Program**  
*Targets*: High failed delivery rate and subsequent rework.  
*Root cause*: Poor timing of delivery attempts and lack of customer communication.  
*Process Mining Support*: Variant analysis showing failed deliveries as a major cause of overtime and extra distance; correlation between specific time windows and failure rate.  
*Implementation*: 
- Prioritize high-failure customers for morning slots.
- Use SMS/app notifications 30-60 min before arrival based on real-time GPS.
- Create "failed delivery prediction" model using historical patterns.  
*Expected Impact*: 30-50% reduction in failed deliveries, significant drop in overtime and total cycle time.

**Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance and Vehicle Assignment Optimization**  
*Targets*: Unscheduled stops and maintenance-related disruptions.  
*Root cause*: Maintenance needs triggered by usage patterns not visible in traditional scheduling.  
*Process Mining Support*: Analysis of "Unscheduled Stop" events correlated with high idle time, frequent short stops, or high speed variation (aggressive driving).  
*Implementation*: 
- Build usage profiles per vehicle (km/day, idle time, stop frequency).
- Implement condition-based maintenance triggers.
- Assign vehicles with known issues to shorter urban routes rather than long suburban ones.  
*Expected Impact*: 40-60% reduction in mid-shift breakdowns, improved vehicle utilization, lower emergency repair costs.

**Additional Strategy (Bonus)**: Driver Coaching Program using personalized performance dashboards derived from process mining insights (service time, route adherence, failed delivery rate per driver).

**5. Operational Constraints and Continuous Monitoring**

All strategies must respect:
- Driver working hours and legal break requirements (track "Start Shift" to "End Shift" duration).
- Vehicle capacity constraints (already in dispatch data).
- Customer time windows and delivery deadlines.
- Union agreements on overtime.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan**:
Implement a **Process Mining Dashboard** (using Celonis, UiPath Process Mining, or PM4Py + Power BI) with:

**Daily View**:
- Conformance rate vs ideal process.
- On-Time Delivery Rate and Failed Delivery Rate (with drill-down by zone/driver).
- Top 5 bottlenecks by duration.

**Weekly View**:
- Variant comparison (before/after optimization).
- Driver performance league table (anonymized for coaching).
- Traffic hotspot evolution.

**Monthly View**:
- Trend of key KPIs.
- Impact of failed deliveries on total cost.
- Predictive maintenance effectiveness.

**Alerting**:
- Automatic notification when a new variant exceeds 10% of cases.
- Significant increase in service time in a specific zone.
- Drop in conformance rate below threshold.

This creates a **closed-loop Process Mining** system where insights continuously feed the routing, scheduling, and training systems, allowing Speedy Parcels to move from reactive to proactive operations management.

The combination of process discovery, conformance checking, and performance analysis on the integrated multi-source event log provides a uniquely powerful view of last-mile delivery operations that traditional BI or GPS analytics alone cannot deliver.
