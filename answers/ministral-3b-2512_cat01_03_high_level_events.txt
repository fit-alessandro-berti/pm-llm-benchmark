### **Proposed High-Level Process Steps**

#### **1. Material Preparation**
**Low-Level Events Included:**
- Retrieve raw metal sheet
- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet

**Rationale:**
These events collectively describe the initial handling of the raw material. The sequence involves:
- **Input acquisition** (retrieving the sheet),
- **Identification** (scanning the barcode for part tracking),
- **Positioning** (placing the sheet on a stable surface),
- **Alignment** (ensuring the sheet is correctly oriented for further processing).
These steps are distinct from later phases (e.g., welding or coating) and focus on preparing the material for the next stage.

**Name Justification:**
"Material Preparation" clearly reflects the preparatory role of these actions in the manufacturing cycle.

---

#### **2. Preheating and Welding**
**Low-Level Events Included:**
- Preheat metal sheet
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B
- Measure weld integrity

**Rationale:**
This group represents the core **assembly** phase where the metal sheet is joined. The sequence follows:
1. **Preheating** (to soften the metal for easier welding).
2. **Tool preparation** (operator picks up the welding tool).
3. **Welding execution** (two sequential welds on opposite corners).
4. **Quality check** (measuring weld integrity post-welding).
The events are temporally contiguous and involve both human (operator) and machine (quality sensor) resources, indicating a critical assembly step.

**Name Justification:**
"Preheating and Welding" captures the dual nature of the process (thermal treatment + joining), while "Assembly" could also be used if the focus is on the final product formation.

#### **3. Coating and Drying**
**Low-Level Events Included:**
- Apply protective coating
- Dry coating

**Rationale:**
These events describe the **finishing** phase where the welded part is coated and cured. The sequence:
1. **Coating application** (adding a protective layer, e.g., polyurethane).
2. **Drying** (heating to cure the coating).
The events are distinct from earlier steps (preparation/welding) and serve to enhance durability or aesthetics.

**Name Justification:**
"Coating and Drying" is precise and aligns with the functional purpose of these actions (surface treatment).

---

#### **4. Quality Inspection**
**Low-Level Events Included:**

- Visual check

**Rationale:**
These events represent **quality assurance** checks. While "measure weld integrity" is part of the "Preheating and Welding" step, the **visual check** (by Operator C) is a standalone inspection step. For consistency, we split this into a separate high-level step to emphasize:

**Name Justification:**

2. **Resource Diversity:** Mixed human/machine resources (e.g., operator + robot arm) are grouped if they logically belong together (e.g., alignment in "Material Preparation").
3. **Functional Cohesion:** Steps are named to reflect their purpose (e.g., "Quality Inspection" vs. "Assembly").
4. **Flexibility:** The grouping can be adjusted if additional events (e.g., part packaging) are observed in the full log.
