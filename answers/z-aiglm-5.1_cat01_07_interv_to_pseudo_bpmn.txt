Here is a structured, BPMN-like textual process model synthesized from the interview transcripts. It is organized to clearly delineate participants, process flows, decision points, and exceptions so that it can be unambiguously translated into a visual BPMN diagram.

---

### **Process Title:** Invoice Approval Process

### **1. Pools and Lanes (Roles/Participants)**
*   **Pool: Our Organization**
    *   **Lane: Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk** (Mary)
    *   **Lane: AP Supervisor** (Rita)
    *   **Lane: Purchasing Officer** (Dan)
    *   **Lane: Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM)** (Karen)
    *   **Lane: Department Manager** (Carlos)
*   **Pool: Supplier** (External)

### **2. Data Objects / Artifacts**
*   **Invoice** (Input / Email)
*   **Purchase Order (PO)** (Referenced data)
*   **Contract** (Referenced data - for pricing verification)
*   **Accounting System Record** (System data)
*   **Payment Schedule** (Output)

---

### **3. Main Process Flow (Happy Path)**

**Start Event:** Invoice received via email.
1.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Check invoice for valid PO number.
2.  **Gateway 1 (Exclusive):** Does the invoice have a valid PO?
    *   *No:* See Exception Path A.
    *   *Yes:* Continue to Step 3.
3.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Log invoice into the accounting system.
4.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Forward invoice to Purchasing.
5.  **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Compare invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) against PO and confirm receipt of goods/services.
6.  **Gateway 2 (Exclusive):** Do details match and confirm receipt?
    *   *No:* See Exception Path B.
    *   *Yes:* Continue to Step 7.
7.  **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Send confirmation email to AP.
8.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Match invoice line items against PO in the system.
9.  **Gateway 3 (Exclusive):** Do system line items match?
    *   *No:* See Exception Path C.
    *   *Yes:* Continue to Step 10.
10. **Task (Accounting System):** Send automatic email requesting manager sign-off.
11. **Task (Department Manager):** Review invoice details, PO, and confirmation.
12. **Gateway 4 (Exclusive):** Is the invoice approved?
    *   *No:* See Exception Path D.
    *   *Yes:* Continue to Step 13.
13. **Task (Department Manager):** Hit "Approve" in the system.
14. **Task (AP Clerk):** Schedule invoice for payment run.
**End Event:** Invoice scheduled for payment.

---

### **4. Exception Paths and Variations**

**Exception Path A: Missing PO (Branches from Gateway 1)**
*   **Task (AP Clerk):** Send invoice back to Supplier requesting PO/details.
*   **Parallel Sub-process (SRM):** If needed, SRM contacts Supplier to request PO or revised invoice.
*   **Exception Resolution A1:** Supplier provides PO / Revised Invoice. Flow returns to Step 3.
*   **Exception Resolution A2 (Informal/No PO):** If no PO exists because services were contracted informally, AP Clerk escalates to AP Supervisor. See Exception Path E.

**Exception Path B: Purchasing Discrepancy (Branches from Gateway 2)**
*   **Gateway B1 (Exclusive):** Can Purchasing Officer resolve directly?
    *   *Yes:* **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Contact Supplier directly to resolve. Flow returns to Step 7.
    *   *No:* **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Ask AP Clerk to hold invoice. Involves SRM to check contracts/contact Supplier. Once corrected invoice is received, flow returns to Step 5.

**Exception Path C: System Matching Discrepancy (Branches from Gateway 3)**
*   **Task (AP Clerk):** Email Purchasing team to clarify.
*   **Gateway C1 (Exclusive):** Can issue be resolved easily?
    *   *Yes:* Issue resolved via clarification. Flow returns to Step 10.
    *   *No:* **Task (AP Clerk):** Escalate to AP Supervisor. See Exception Path E.

**Exception Path D: Manager Rejection/Hold (Branches from Gateway 4)**
*   **Gateway D1 (Exclusive):** Reject or Hold/Investigate?
    *   *Reject:* Process ends (Invoice rejected).
    *   *Hold/Investigate:* **Task (AP Clerk):** Hold invoice and investigate. (If unresolvable, likely escalates to AP Supervisor - Exception Path E). Once resolved, flow returns to Step 10.

**Exception Path E: Supervisor Escalation (Triggered by Unresolvable Discrepancies or No PO)**
*   **Task (AP Supervisor):** Coordinate between departments and/or contact Supplier.
*   **Gateway E1 (Exclusive):** Is there a missing PO due to informal contracting?
    *   *Yes:* **Task (AP Supervisor & Dept Heads):** Create a retrospective PO. Flow returns to Step 5 (Purchasing confirmation).
    *   *No (Discrepancy):* Issue resolved by Supervisor. Flow returns to Step 5 (Purchasing confirmation) or Step 10 (Manager approval), depending on where the exception originated.

### **5. Ambiguities and Conflicting Accounts**

1.  **Sequence of Matching:** Mary (AP Clerk) states she forwards the invoice to Purchasing *before* matching line items in the system. She matches *after* receiving Purchasing's confirmation. Dan (Purchasing) implies his check *is* the matching process, after which he assumes Mary moves it to the manager. The model synthesizes this by having Dan do a manual verification against the PO first, followed by Mary doing a formal system match after receiving his confirmation.
2.  **Escalation Routing:** When a Manager (Carlos) rejects or holds an invoice for investigation, he states he asks "Mary to hold it." It is unclear if Mary investigates this herself or if it automatically triggers a supervisor escalation (Rita). The model assumes Mary attempts resolution first, looping back to the manager, but notes the potential for Supervisor escalation.
3.  **SRM Involvement Trigger:** Karen (SRM) states she is looped in by AP or Purchasing when terms don't match. However, Mary and Dan both mention contacting the supplier directly in some cases. The model reflects that supplier contact can be done by Purchasing, AP, or routed through the SRM depending on the nature of the issue (e.g., relationship vs. simple data error).
