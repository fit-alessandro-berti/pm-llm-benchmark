This visualization illustrates a **proclet-based modeling approach** for representing interacting business processes, using a laboratory testing scenario as an example. It consists of three parts:

## (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels

This is the main diagram showing two parallel process flows:

**Lab Visit process (left side):**
- Begins with "Order entry" (performed by doctor)
- "Take blood sample" (lab assistant)
- "Receive result"
- "Judge results"
- Branches into "Everything fine" or "Do tests again"
- "Receive updated result"
- "Send report" (lab assistant)

**Lab Test process (right side):**
- "Perform test" (chemical analyst)
- "Make report"
- "Finish lab test"
- "Perform test again"
- "Provide updated report"

The two processes communicate through two **channels**:
- **Order system** (top channel)
- **HIS** (Hospital Information System, middle channel)

The connections include **cardinality annotations** (like `1,*`, `1,1`, `1,?`) on the channel ports, indicating multiplicity relationships between messages.

## (b) Class Diagram Containing the Two Proclet Classes

A simple UML-style class diagram showing the relationship between the two classes:
- **Lab visit** *requires* **Lab test**
- The multiplicity is **1..1** to **1..\***, meaning one lab visit requires one or more lab tests

## (c) Example of a Performative

A table showing a sample message (performative) exchanged between proclets:

| Time | Channel | Sender | Receivers | Action | Content | Scope | Direction |
|------|---------|--------|-----------|--------|---------|-------|-----------|
| 11:00 | Order system | Lab visit - John | Lab test – HGB John | Create | Can you perform a HGB test for John? | Private | OUT |

This represents a concrete communication instance, where a lab visit requests an HGB test for a patient named John.

---

This type of diagram is commonly used in **workflow management** and **business process modeling** research to show how separate but interacting process instances coordinate through message exchanges.
