### Interpretation
The **slanted connecting lines** each represent an individual process instance (a "case" or "trace" — i.e., one traffic fine). Their position shows when an event occurred for that case, the vertical placement indicates the activity, and the slope reflects the **duration/waiting time** between steps (steeper = shorter duration; shallower = longer delays). Redder/pinker areas likely highlight higher density, overlapping cases, or specific variants of the process. The overall pattern reveals:
- High initial event volume ("Send Fine" happens often).
- Fewer cases progress to later steps, with noticeable temporal clusters, batch-like behavior, and varying delays.
- Potential insights into bottlenecks, seasonality, or process performance in fine notification and credit collection.

This style of chart is commonly used in process mining to visualize event frequency, case flows, throughput, and temporal dynamics in real-world logs without aggregating away individual instance details.
