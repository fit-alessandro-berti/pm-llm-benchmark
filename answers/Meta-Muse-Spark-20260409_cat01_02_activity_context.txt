Looking at the log, two activities repeat with clearly different meanings: **Verify Documents** and **Call Customer**. The meaning shifts based on sequence, resource, and what triggered them.

### 1. Activities with changing context

**Verify Documents**
- *First occurrence*: Always the initial document check right after request initiation.
- *Second occurrence*: Happens only after new info was added — either `Submit Additional` in C001 or `Adjust Account` in C003. So this is a re-check, not a first check.

**Call Customer**  
- *C001 09:45*: Purpose is to request missing docs because verification was incomplete.
- *C002 09:00*: Purpose is to clarify an account discrepancy found during/after verification. 
- *C002 09:50*: Purpose is to confirm adjustments were completed. This is a follow-up, not a request.
- *C003 11:50*: Purpose is to get the walk-in customer to return to the branch. Logistical, not content-related.

**Initiate Request** and **Approve Request** appear once per case with consistent meaning, so no rename needed. Same for **Submit Additional** and **Adjust Account**.

### 2. Renaming approach
I renamed based on 3 clues: position in sequence, triggering event, and outcome/goal from `Additional Context`.

| Original Activity | New Name | Contextual clue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verify Documents (1st) | **Initial Document Verification** | First verification after initiation, baseline check |
| Verify Documents (2nd) | **Re-verify Documents Post-Update** | Occurs after new docs/account changes submitted |
| Call Customer (request docs) | **Call Customer - Request Missing Documents** | Triggered by incomplete verification |
| Call Customer (clarify) | **Call Customer - Clarify Account Discrepancy** | Triggered by discrepancy finding |
| Call Customer (confirm) | **Call Customer - Confirm Adjustments** | Triggered after internal adjustment completed |
| Call Customer (logistics) | **Call Customer - Request Customer Return** | Triggered by customer stepping out of branch |

### 4. Justification of naming choices

**Verify Documents → Initial Document Verification vs Re-verify Documents Post-Update**  
The key clue is sequence + dependency. The first verification always follows `Initiate Request` and establishes the baseline. The second only appears after a change event like `Submit Additional` or `Adjust Account`, per timestamps and context. Calling both "Verify Documents" hides whether we're doing first-pass compliance or closing the loop after a fix.

**Call Customer variations**  
These four instances have totally different business purposes, visible in `Additional Context`:
1. *Request Missing Documents*: Explicitly triggered by incomplete verification. Goal is data collection.
2. *Clarify Account Discrepancy*: Not about missing docs, but about conflicting info. Goal is clarification before action.
3. *Confirm Adjustments*: Happens after `Adjust Account`. Goal is confirmation/closure, not information gathering.
4. *Request Customer Return*: Purely logistical. The customer is physically absent. No data issue yet.

Without renaming, process mining would merge these and you’d lose why calls happen. With the new names, you can see patterns like "80% of cases that require Request Missing Documents also require Re-verify" vs "Confirm Adjustments calls never loop back".
