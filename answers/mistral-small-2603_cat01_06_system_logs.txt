### **Explanation of Logic**

#### **1. Case Identification**
Cases were grouped based on the **document or application context** in which activities occurred. This ensures that related events (e.g., editing, saving, closing a document) are grouped into a coherent workflow. The following cases were identified:
- **`Document1_001`**: All activities related to editing and closing `Document1.docx`.
- **`Email_001`**: All activities related to handling an email (opening, replying, sending).
- **`ReportDraft_001`**: All activities related to reviewing `Report_Draft.pdf`.
- **`Budget_2024_001`**: All activities related to editing `Budget_2024.xlsx`.
- **`QuarterlyReport_001`**: All activities related to editing and closing `Quarterly_Report.docx`.

#### **2. Activity Naming**
Raw actions were translated into higher-level activities for clarity:
- **`FOCUS`**  **`Open Document`** (when switching to a new document) or **`Switch to [Application]`** (when changing focus between applications).
- **`TYPING`**  **`Edit Document`** (for Word/Excel) or **`Edit Email`** (for Chrome).
- **`SAVE`**  **`Save Document`** or **`Save Spreadsheet`**.
- **`SWITCH`**  **`Switch to [Application]`** (with context of the source/target).
- **`CLICK`**  **`Open Email`**, **`Reply to Email`**, or **`Send Email`** (based on the action).
- **`SCROLL`** and **`HIGHLIGHT`** were kept as-is but contextualized (e.g., `Scroll Email`, `Highlight Text`).

#### **3. Additional Attributes**
- **Application** and **Document** were included to provide context for each activity.
- **Additional Info** was used to capture key details (e.g., typed text, email actions).

#### **4. Coherent Narrative**
The event log tells a story of a user working across multiple documents and applications:
1. **Document1.docx**: Editing, saving, and closing.
2. **Email**: Handling an email (replying, sending).
3. **Report_Draft.pdf**: Reviewing and highlighting.
4. **Budget_2024.xlsx**: Updating spreadsheet data.
5. **Document1.docx (again)**: Final edits and closure.
6. **Quarterly_Report.docx**: Drafting and closing.

This grouping ensures that the process mining analysis can reveal patterns in how the user switches between tasks and applications.
