### How the added constraints reduce bias

1. **Global bias control**
   - `existence["BiasMitigationCheck"]`: enforces that every application trace contains at least one dedicated fairness/bias check step.

2. **Mandatory checks after using sensitive attributes**
   - `response["CheckApplicantRace" | "CheckApplicantGender" | "CheckApplicantAge"]["BiasMitigationCheck"]`: whenever race, gender, or age is inspected, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must occur afterwards, avoiding silent use of sensitive data.

3. **Bias checks before decisions**
   - `precedence["BiasMitigationCheck"]["Approve" | "Reject" | "FinalDecision"]`: no approval/rejection/final decision can be taken unless a bias check has already been performed, preventing decisions that ignore fairness assessment.

4. **Extra scrutiny for minority applicants**
   - `responded_existence["Approve_Minority" | "Reject_Minority"]["ManualReview"]` and
     `coexistence["Approve_Minority" | "Reject_Minority"]["ManualReview"]`:
     whenever a case involves a minority-specific approval or rejection, the trace must also contain `ManualReview`, ensuring consistent human oversight for sensitive groups.
   - `precedence["ManualReview"]["Approve_Minority" | "Reject_Minority"]`: manual review must occur before those minority decisions, ensuring oversight influences the decision.

5. **No immediate decisions after sensitive-attribute checks**
   - `nonchainsuccession["CheckApplicantRace" | "CheckApplicantGender" | "CheckApplicantAge"]["Approve" | "Reject"]`:
     forbids a decision step to occur immediately after reading a sensitive attribute, forcing at least one intervening step (typically `BiasMitigationCheck` or `ManualReview`), reducing the chance of snap, attribute-driven bias.
