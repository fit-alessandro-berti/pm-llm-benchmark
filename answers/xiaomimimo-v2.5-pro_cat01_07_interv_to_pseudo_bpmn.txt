### **Process: Invoice Approval and Payment**

**Participants / Pools & Lanes:**
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Department**
    *   *Lanes:* AP Clerk (Mary), AP Supervisor (Rita)
*   **Purchasing Department**
    *   *Lane:* Purchasing Officer (Dan)
*   **Department Management**
    *   *Lane:* Department Manager (Carlos)
*   **Supplier Relationship Management**
    *   *Lane:* Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)
*   **External Entity**
    *   *Lane:* Supplier

---

### **Process Flow (Sequence Flow with Gateways)**

**Start Event:** Invoice received (typically via email).

**Task 1: Initial Validation (AP Clerk)**
*   AP Clerk (Mary) checks the invoice for a Purchase Order (PO) number.
*   **Gateway 1 (PO Present?):**
    *   **Path A (No PO):** Invoice is returned to the Supplier (via email/request) with a request for more details (specifically a PO). This creates a **rework loop** to the start.
    *   **Path B (Yes PO):** Proceed to Task 2.

**Task 2: Log Invoice in System (AP Clerk)**
*   AP Clerk logs the invoice into the accounting system.
*   **Data Object Created:** System Record of Invoice.

**Task 3: Verify Goods/Services Receipt (Purchasing)**
*   AP Clerk forwards the invoice to the Purchasing Department (Dan).
*   Purchasing Officer (Dan) compares the invoice details (quantities, prices, charges) against the PO.
*   **Gateway 2 (Invoice Matches PO?):**
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Found):**
        *   *Sub-task:* Purchasing contacts the Supplier (directly or via AP) to request a corrected invoice.
        *   This creates a **rework loop** back to the start or to Task 3.
        *   **Exception Path:** If discrepancy cannot be resolved, Purchasing may ask AP to hold the invoice. This may trigger **Task 8: Escalate to Supervisor**.
    *   **Path B (Match Confirmed):** Purchasing Officer sends a confirmation email/notification back to AP Clerk. Proceed to Task 4.

**Task 4: Three-Way Match (AP Clerk)**
*   AP Clerk performs a final system match of invoice details against the PO and the receipt confirmation.
*   **Gateway 3 (Final Match Successful?):**
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Found):**
        *   *Sub-task:* AP Clerk emails Purchasing for clarification.
        *   **Exception Path:** If discrepancy cannot be easily resolved, this may trigger **Task 8: Escalate to Supervisor**.
    *   **Path B (All Match):** Proceed to Task 5.

**Task 5: Manager Approval (System Triggered)**
*   The system automatically sends an approval request/notification to the Department Manager (Carlos).
*   **Data Object:** Approval Request.
*   Manager reviews invoice, PO, and confirmation details.
*   **Gateway 4 (Manager Decision):**
    *   **Path A (Approve):** Manager approves the invoice in the system. Proceed to Task 6.
    *   **Path B (Reject / Hold for Investigation):**
        *   *Sub-task:* Manager may request AP (Mary) to investigate further.
        *   This creates a **rework loop** back to earlier tasks (e.g., Task 4) for investigation, potentially triggering **Task 8**.

**Task 6: Schedule for Payment (AP Clerk)**
*   Upon final manager approval, the invoice is scheduled for payment in the next payment run.

**End Event:** Invoice paid (outside the scope of this approval process).

---

### **Exception Handling & Alternate Paths**

**Task 8: Escalate to Supervisor (AP Supervisor)**
*   **Trigger:** AP Clerk (Mary) cannot resolve a discrepancy after contacting Purchasing, or the issue is complex.
*   **Action:** AP Supervisor (Rita) investigates. This may involve:
    *   Contacting the Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen) to resolve disputes.
    *   Coordinating with department heads to create a **retrospective PO** for "informal" service invoices.
    *   Making a final decision on rejection if issues are unresolvable.
*   **Outcome:** Once resolved, the invoice is returned to the **appropriate point in the normal flow** (e.g., back to Task 3 or Task 4).

**Interaction with Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)**
*   **Trigger:** Purchasing (Dan) or AP (Mary/Supervisor) encounter a contract or pricing dispute.
*   **Action:** Karen contacts the Supplier, consults internal contracts, and provides guidance to AP/Purchasing to resolve the issue.
*   **Outcome:** Facilitates resolution so the invoice can re-enter the main flow (typically back to Task 3 or Task 4).

---

### **Data Objects & Artifacts (Mentioned)**
*   **Invoice:** The primary input document.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** The key reference document for validation.
*   **System Record of Invoice:** Logged in the accounting system.
*   **Confirmation Email/Notification:** From Purchasing to AP confirming receipt/validation.
*   **Approval Request:** System-generated notification to the Manager.
*   **Contract:** Referenced by Karen for pricing disputes.

### **Notes on Ambiguity & Consolidation**
1.  **Trigger for Manager Approval:** Both Mary and Carlos state the manager is notified after the PO match and purchasing confirmation. The model reflects this as a system-triggered step.
2.  **Return after Exceptions:** Both Rita and Karen indicate that resolved exception invoices re-enter the main flow. The model assumes they go back to the verification stage (Task 3) but could potentially re-enter at the matching stage (Task 4).
3.  **Retroactive PO Creation:** Rita mentions this as a solution for "informal" service invoices. This is captured as a special action within the Supervisor escalation task.
4.  **Supplier Communication:** There is a slight ambiguity on whether Purchasing contacts the Supplier directly or always through AP. The model notes both possibilities within the discrepancy paths.
