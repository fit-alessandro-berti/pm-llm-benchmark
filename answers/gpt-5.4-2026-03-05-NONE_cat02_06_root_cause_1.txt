- unresolved tickets are not being completed within the same shift,

## Summary of likely root causes

### 1. Slow handoffs during escalation
Especially visible in **102** and **105**.
- Tickets sit too long after being escalated.
- Level-2 appears to have a backlog or delayed response.

### 2. Long queue times before investigation
Visible in **104**, and also partly in **102**.
- Assigned tickets are not picked up promptly.
- This may indicate overloaded agents or poor work allocation.

### 3. Delayed completion after investigation
Visible in **102**, **104**, and **105**.
- Investigation happens, but resolution is postponed until much later or next day.
- This suggests either batching, dependency on another team, or insufficient authority/resources to resolve immediately.

## Recommendations

### 1. Reduce escalation delays
- Set an SLA for **escalated ticket pickup** by Level-2.
- Monitor time from **Escalate to Level-2**  **next Investigate**.
- Increase Level-2 capacity if backlog is high.
- Improve triage quality so only necessary tickets are escalated.

### 2. Speed up assignment-to-investigation time
- Track **Assign to Agent  Investigate** as a key KPI.
- Rebalance workload across Level-1 agents.
- Introduce alerts for tickets sitting too long without investigation.

### 3. Minimize overnight waiting after investigation
- Prioritize tickets already under investigation for same-day completion.
- Identify whether approvals, customer responses, or technical dependencies are causing hidden waits.
- If possible, create end-of-shift handoff rules so active tickets are not left idle overnight.

### 4. Separate delay analysis by process variant
Useful variants:
- **Standard path**: Receive  Triage  Assign L1  Investigate  Resolve  Close
- **Escalation path**: Receive  Triage  Assign L1  Escalate L2  Investigate  Resolve  Close

This helps compare baseline cycle time versus escalation cycle time and quantify the escalation penalty.
