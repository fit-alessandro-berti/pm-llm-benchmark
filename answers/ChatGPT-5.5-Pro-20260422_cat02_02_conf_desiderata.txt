## Case-by-case normative analysis

### Case 1 — Normatively compliant

Case 1 follows the required approved-loan path:

> Application Received → Credit Check → Risk Assessment → Loan Decision Approved → Inform Applicant → Finalize Documentation → Close Case

There is no skipped step, no reversal of order, and no contradiction between the decision and later activities. Since the loan is approved, finalizing documentation before closing the case is correct.

**Conclusion:** No normative violation.

---

### Case 2 — Normative violations

Case 2 contains multiple strict violations.

Observed sequence:

> Application Received → Risk Assessment → Credit Check → Loan Decision Rejected → Finalize Documentation → Close Case

#### Violation 1: Risk Assessment occurs before Credit Check

The strict rule says:

> Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment.

But in Case 2:

* Risk Assessment: **2024-01-11 08:20**
* Credit Check: **2024-01-11 09:00**

So the risk assessment was done **40 minutes before** the credit check. This directly violates the required order.

#### Violation 2: Applicant is not informed after the loan decision

The normative process requires:

> Loan Decision → Inform Applicant

In Case 2, after the rejected decision at **09:30**, there is no recorded **Inform Applicant** event. The case proceeds to documentation finalization and closure instead.

That means the case appears to skip a required post-decision activity.

#### Violation 3: Documentation is finalized after rejection

The rule says:

> If the loan is rejected, the case should be closed without finalizing documentation.

But in Case 2:

* Loan Decision Rejected: **2024-01-11 09:30**
* Finalize Documentation: **2024-01-11 10:00**
* Close Case: **2024-01-11 10:30**

Finalizing documentation after a rejection is explicitly not allowed under the normative model.

#### Violation 4: Closing does not follow the correct rejected-case path

For a rejected case, the expected path is:

> Loan Decision Rejected → Inform Applicant → Close Case

Instead, Case 2 does:

> Loan Decision Rejected → Finalize Documentation → Close Case

So the closing logic is also incorrect because the process includes an activity that should not occur after rejection and omits the applicant notification.

**Conclusion:** Case 2 is the only clear normative violation, and it has several severe deviations.

---

### Case 3 — Normatively compliant

Case 3 follows the required approved-loan path:

> Application Received → Credit Check → Risk Assessment → Loan Decision Approved → Inform Applicant → Finalize Documentation → Close Case

The activity order is correct. Since the loan is approved, finalizing documentation before closing the case is also correct.

The issue in Case 3 is timing, not strict control-flow compliance.

**Conclusion:** No normative violation.

# 2. Desiderata violations

The desiderata are recommended best practices, not hard compliance rules. Violating them indicates suboptimal behavior, but not a serious process violation.

The relevant recommendations are:

1. Credit Check should ideally occur within **24 hours** of Application Received.
2. Applicant should ideally be informed within **48 hours** of Loan Decision.
3. A Follow-up Call is encouraged sometime before closing the case.

---

| Case       |                                      Desiderata status | Explanation                                                                                                                                       |
| ---------- | -----------------------------------------------------: | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Case 1** |                                    Mostly satisfactory | Credit check and applicant notification are prompt. No follow-up call is recorded, depending on how strictly absence from the log is interpreted. |
| **Case 2** | Hard to assess cleanly because of normative violations | Credit check occurs within 24 hours, but the process is already normatively broken. No applicant notification is recorded.                        |
| **Case 3** |                                    Violates desiderata | Credit check is late, applicant notification is late, and no follow-up call is recorded.                                                          |

## Case-by-case desiderata analysis

### Case 1 — Desiderata mostly satisfied

* Application Received: **2024-01-10 09:00**
* Credit Check: **2024-01-10 09:30**

The credit check happens **30 minutes** after the application is received, well within the recommended 24-hour window.

* Loan Decision Approved: **2024-01-10 11:00**
* Inform Applicant: **2024-01-10 13:00**

The applicant is informed **2 hours** after the decision, well within the recommended 48-hour window.

No Follow-up Call is shown in the log. Since the follow-up call is only recommended, its absence would be a minor desideratum deviation, not a normative violation. However, the table only explicitly flags the missing follow-up call for Case 3. So there are two possible readings:

* If absence from the log means the activity did not happen, then Case 1 also missed the recommended follow-up call.
* If the explicit note applies only to Case 3, then Case 1 has no stated desideratum issue.

**Conclusion:** Case 1 is strong overall. At most, it may have a minor missed follow-up-call desideratum.

---

### Case 2 — Desiderata are overshadowed by normative violations

For timing:

* Application Received: **2024-01-11 08:00**
* Credit Check: **2024-01-11 09:00**

The credit check occurs **1 hour** after the application, which satisfies the 24-hour recommendation.

However, this does not save the case because the risk assessment was performed before the credit check. That is a strict normative violation.

There is no recorded **Inform Applicant** event after the rejection. This is not merely a desideratum issue, because informing the applicant after the decision is required by the normative process.

No Follow-up Call is recorded. If absence from the log means the call did not occur, then Case 2 also misses that recommended best practice. But because Case 2 already violates strict rules, the follow-up issue is comparatively minor.

**Conclusion:** Case 2 does not mainly fail because of desiderata; it fails because of strict normative violations.

Case 3 follows the required activity order, but violates several recommendations.

The log explicitly states that there was **No Follow-up Call**.

Since the follow-up call is encouraged but not mandatory, this is a soft deviation only. It indicates less-than-ideal customer handling, but it does not make the case non-compliant under the strict normative process.

# 3. Possible interpretations or justifications

## Case 1

Case 1 appears to be a clean execution of the intended process. The quick credit check and quick applicant notification suggest an efficient, well-controlled case.

If no follow-up call occurred, a possible justification is that the case progressed quickly, the applicant was informed promptly, and final documentation was completed soon after. The organization may have judged a follow-up call unnecessary because the process was straightforward.

Still, from a desiderata perspective, a short follow-up call would have improved customer alignment before closure.

---

## Case 2

Case 2 is problematic as logged, but there are a few possible interpretations that might reduce the severity if additional context were available.

The early **Risk Assessment** could have been a preliminary screening rather than the formal risk assessment. If so, the activity label may be inaccurate. Under the strict model, however, it is recorded as **Risk Assessment**, so it counts as a violation.

The **Finalize Documentation** event after rejection might also be a labeling issue. Perhaps the team was finalizing rejection paperwork rather than finalizing loan approval documentation. If the activity means “finalize closing/rejection documents,” then it may not be contradictory. But under the stated process definition, **Finalize Documentation** belongs only to approved loans, so it is non-compliant as recorded.

The missing **Inform Applicant** event could mean the applicant was informed outside the system, for example by phone or email without being logged. But based strictly on the event log, the required notification step is missing.

So the best interpretation is:

> Case 2 may reflect logging ambiguity or informal workarounds, but under the stated normative rules it is non-compliant.

---

## Case 3

Case 3 is a good example of a case that is legally or procedurally acceptable under the strict process model but not ideal from a service-quality perspective.

The late credit check could be justified by backlog, missing applicant data, manual verification, weekend delay, system downtime, or the need for additional documentation. Since the credit check still happened before the risk assessment, the core compliance logic was preserved.

The late applicant notification could similarly be due to staff workload, internal approval confirmation, or communication delays. Since the applicant was still informed before documentation finalization and case closure, the normative order remains intact.

The missing follow-up call may be justifiable if the applicant had already received enough information, if the approval was straightforward, or if written communication was considered sufficient. However, skipping it still means the case falls short of the recommended best-practice behavior.
