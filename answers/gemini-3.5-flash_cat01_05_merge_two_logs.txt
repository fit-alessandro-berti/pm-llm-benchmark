### **1. Integration Methodology & Reasoning**

#### **Timestamp Alignment Strategy**
* **The "System of Record" Rule**: Log A's timestamps represent the time the event was *received* by the central system, whereas Log B's timestamps record the actual *execution* time at the source (the warehouse system). Therefore, for matched events, **System B’s timestamp is treated as the primary execution timestamp** (`unified_timestamp`), as it is closest to the physical action.
* **Tolerance Window**: While a general matching window of $\le 2$ seconds is used, business context allows us to expand this window. For example, the payment step has a 5-second offset, but because both events represent the unique payment action for this order and System B contains a note specifically mentioning a *"Payment gateway delay"*, they are confidently matched.
