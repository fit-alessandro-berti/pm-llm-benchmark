# Loan Process Conformance Analysis

I'll analyze each case against both normative rules and desiderata, then provide interpretations for the observed behaviors.

## **Case 1: Fully Compliant**

**Normative Compliance:** 
- Sequence follows: Application  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering rules respected
- Approved loan properly includes documentation finalization before closure
- No skipped steps or contradictory actions

**Desiderata Compliance:** 
- Credit Check: 30 minutes after application (well within 24-hour window)
- Inform Applicant: 2 hours after decision (well within 48-hour window)
- Missing Follow-up Call: This is a minor desideratum violation, but not problematic

**Interpretation:** This appears to be an ideal, well-executed loan processing case with efficient turnaround times. The only deviation from recommendations is the absence of a follow-up call, which is optional.

## **Case 2: Multiple Normative Violations**

**Normative Violations:** 
1. **Out-of-order Risk Assessment:** Risk Assessment (08:20) occurred before Credit Check (09:00)
2. **Unauthorized Documentation Finalization:** Documentation was finalized (10:00) despite loan rejection (09:30)
3. **Missing Inform Applicant step:** The process jumps from Loan Decision to Finalize Documentation without informing the applicant

**Desiderata Compliance:**  (but irrelevant given normative violations)
- Credit Check: 40 minutes after application (within 24-hour window)
- Missing Follow-up Call: Minor violation
- Cannot assess Inform Applicant timing since step was skipped entirely

**Interpretation:** This case exhibits serious process breakdowns. Possible explanations:
- **System/workflow error:** The Risk Assessment may have been triggered prematurely due to a software bug or misconfiguration
- **Human error:** An agent may have accidentally selected "Risk Assessment" instead of "Credit Check," or confused the sequence
- **Process misunderstanding:** Staff may not understand that documentation finalization is conditional on approval
- **Procedural shortcut:** Someone may have attempted to expedite by doing assessments in parallel, violating the required sequence

The most concerning violation is finalizing documentation after rejection, as this creates legal and compliance risks.

## **Case 3: Fully Normative with Desiderata Violations**

**Normative Compliance:** 
- Sequence follows all requirements
- All ordering rules respected
- Approved loan properly includes documentation finalization before closure
- No skipped steps or contradictory actions

**Desiderata Violations:** 
1. **Credit Check Delay:** Occurred 49 hours after application (exceeds 24-hour recommendation)
2. **Inform Applicant Delay:** Occurred 51 hours after decision (exceeds 48-hour recommendation)
3. **Missing Follow-up Call:** Minor violation

**Interpretation:** While fully compliant with normative rules, this case shows operational inefficiency. Possible explanations:
- **Resource constraints:** Credit check department may be understaffed, causing backlog
- **External dependencies:** Waiting for third-party credit bureau responses
- **Prioritization issues:** This application may have been deprioritized for manual review
- **Process bottlenecks:** The gap between decision and notification suggests communication workflow issues

These are process optimization issues rather than compliance problems.

## **Recommendations**

**For Case 2:** Immediate process review and retraining. The out-of-order steps suggest either system design flaws or staff training gaps. The documentation finalization after rejection needs urgent correction to prevent legal exposure.

**For Case 3:** Process optimization focused on reducing time-to-credit-check and time-to-notification. Could implement SLAs with credit bureaus, automated notification systems, or workload rebalancing.

**Overall:** The process works correctly when followed (Case 1 & 3), but shows vulnerability to human/system errors in sequencing (Case 2). Consider implementing system-enforced workflow controls rather than relying on procedural compliance alone.
