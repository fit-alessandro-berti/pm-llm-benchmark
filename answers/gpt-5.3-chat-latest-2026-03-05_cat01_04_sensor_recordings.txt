**1. Idle / Machine Standby (Events 1–3, 13–15)**

- Temperature: ~20–21°C (ambient)
- Pressure: 1 bar
- Vibration: 0 Hz
- Material Flow: 0
- Tool Position: 0
- Energy: ~0.4–0.5 kWh

Reasoning:
These readings indicate the machine is powered but not performing work. No vibration, no material flow, and the tool is parked at position 0. Low energy consumption suggests standby mode. The same pattern appears again at the end, indicating the process cycle completed and the machine returned to idle.

Label: **Machine Idle / Standby**

**2. Cutting Metal (Events 4–6)**  
Characteristics:
- Temperature rises quickly (50  58°C)
- High vibration (30–38 Hz)
- Continuous material flow (5 units/s)
- Tool position increasing (5  10 mm)
- High energy usage (2.0–3.0 kWh)

Reasoning:
Cutting or machining operations typically generate significant vibration due to tool-material interaction. Heat generation and high power consumption also support this. Continuous material flow suggests chips or removed material. The moving tool position indicates active machining.

Label: **Cutting Metal**

**3. Assembling / Positioning Parts (Events 7–8)**  
Characteristics:
- Moderate temperature (~40°C)
- Moderate pressure (2 bar)
- Low vibration (10 Hz)
- Reduced material flow (2 units/s)
- Tool position stable at 15 mm
- Moderate energy (~2 kWh)

Reasoning:
Lower vibration compared to cutting suggests less aggressive mechanical interaction. Stable tool position and moderate flow could indicate components being aligned or inserted. Increased pressure might represent pneumatic assembly mechanisms.

Label: **Assembling Parts / Component Positioning**

---

**4. Welding (Event 9)**  
Characteristics:
- Very high temperature (80°C)
- Highest vibration (50 Hz)
- No material flow
- Tool position fixed (15 mm)
- Very high energy consumption (5 kWh)

Reasoning:
Welding processes require intense heat and power but typically do not involve material flow like machining. The spike in temperature and energy strongly suggests a welding arc or similar heat-based joining process.

Label: **Welding**

---

**5. Quality Inspection (Event 10)**  
Characteristics:
- Temperature dropping (30°C)
- Pressure back to baseline (1 bar)
- Very low vibration (1 Hz)
- No material flow
- Tool remains positioned
- Low energy use (1 kWh)

Reasoning:
After welding, systems often perform inspection or measurement. Very low vibration and no material flow indicate sensing rather than manufacturing. The tool staying in position suggests a probe, camera, or sensor verification step.

Label: **Quality Inspection**

**6. Packaging / Transfer (Events 11–12)**

- Moderate vibration (5 Hz)
- Small material flow (1 unit/s)

- Moderate energy (~1.5–1.6 kWh)
