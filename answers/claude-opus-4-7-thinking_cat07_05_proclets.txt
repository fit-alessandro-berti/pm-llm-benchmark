This visualization depicts a **proclet-based process model** for a laboratory testing workflow, consisting of three interconnected parts:

## (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels

This is the main process diagram showing two parallel workflows:

**Left side - "Lab visit" proclet** (performed by doctor and lab assistant):
- Starts with **Order entry** (doctor)
- **Take blood sample** (lab assistant)
- **Receive result**
- **Judge results** — branching into either:
  - **Everything fine** (end state), or
  - **Do tests again**
- **Receive updated result**
- **Send report** (end)

**Right side - "Lab test" proclet** (performed by chemical analyst):
- Starts with **Perform test**
- **Make report**
- **Finish lab test**, or alternatively
- **Perform test again**  **Provide updated report**

The two proclets communicate through two channels (shown as dashed boxes):
- **Order system** channel (connects order entry to test performance)
- **HIS** (Hospital Information System) channel (connects results and updates)

Cardinalities like `1..*` and `1..?` annotate the message exchanges between proclets.

A simple UML-style diagram showing the relationship between the two proclet classes:

## Summary

Overall, the figure illustrates how **proclets** (lightweight, interacting workflow processes) can model complex, multi-perspective processes like medical lab testing, where different actors (doctors, lab assistants, chemical analysts) participate in related but distinct workflows that communicate via shared channels.
