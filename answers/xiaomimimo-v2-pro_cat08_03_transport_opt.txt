Another challenge is handling missing data (e.g., a driver forgetting to scan).

**Process Discovery:**
Using algorithms like the **Heuristic Miner** or **Fuzzy Miner** on the integrated log, we can discover the *actual* process map. This model will visually represent:
*   The most frequent flow: `Depart Depot -> Travel -> Arrive Customer -> Delivery Activity -> Depart Customer -> ... -> Arrive Depot`.

*   This model quantifies frequencies and timings, showing, for example, that 15% of stops result in a failed delivery, or that the path including a traffic jam adds an average of 25 minutes to a route segment.

**Conformance Checking:**

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) & Calculation:**
*   **On-Time Delivery Rate:** % of `Delivery Success` events where `Actual Arrival Time` <= `Customer Requested Time Window End`. (Calculated from Scanner and Dispatch data).
*   **Average Time per Delivery Stop:** `(Depart Customer Timestamp - Arrive Customer Timestamp)` averaged across all successful stops. (Scanner data).
*   **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio:** Total `Travel Time` (between stops) / Total `Service Time` (time at customer location). High ratios indicate inefficient routing or traffic.
*   **Failed Delivery Rate:** `# Delivery Failed` / (`# Delivery Success` + `# Delivery Failed`). (Scanner data).
*   **Vehicle Utilization Rate:** `(Total Time Moving or Servicing) / (Total Shift Time - Maintenance Time)`. (GPS + Scanner + Maintenance logs).
*   **Dwell Time at Bottlenecks:** Average time spent in predefined traffic hotspot locations. (GPS data).

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques:**
*   **Performance Spectrum Analysis:** We will analyze the **sojourn time** (time between two consecutive activities) across the process map. This instantly highlights the slowest steps. For example, we might find the average time between `Depart Customer A` and `Arrive Customer B` is 20 minutes, but the 90th percentile is 50 minutes, indicating a severe bottleneck on certain routes or at certain times.
*   **Resource-based Analysis:** We can compare KPIs across `Driver ID` and `Vehicle ID`. Is Driver D105 consistently slower? Does Vehicle V12 (an older model) have more unscheduled stops?
*   **Attribute-based Analysis:** We will correlate bottlenecks with case attributes like `Route Area` (urban vs. suburban), `Time of Day`, and `Weather Data` (if integrated). This reveals if bottlenecks are systemic or context-specific.
