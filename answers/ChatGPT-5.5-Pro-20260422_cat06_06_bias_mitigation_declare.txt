### Rationale for the added constraints

The new **`response`** constraints require a `BiasMitigationCheck` after any sensitive-attribute activity such as `CheckApplicantAge`, `CheckApplicantGender`, or `CheckApplicantRace`. This prevents the process from observing protected information without triggering a fairness-control step.

The new **`precedence`** constraints require `BiasMitigationCheck` before decision activities such as `Approve`, `Reject`, `RequestAdditionalInfo`, and `FinalDecision`. This means a decision cannot validly occur unless the fairness check has already happened.

The new **`coexistence`** constraints require `ManualReview` whenever the trace contains a sensitive-group-specific decision, such as `Reject_Minority`, `Approve_ProtectedAgeGroup`, or `RequestAdditionalInfo_UnderrepresentedGender`. This adds human oversight for cases where protected-group status is explicitly involved.

The new **`ManualReview` precedence** constraints strengthen that rule by requiring the manual review to occur before sensitive-group-specific decisions, not merely somewhere in the same trace.

The new **`nonchainsuccession`** constraints prevent a sensitive-attribute check from being immediately followed by a decision. For example, `CheckApplicantRace` cannot be directly followed by `Reject`. I used `nonchainsuccession` instead of `nonsuccession` because the goal is to prevent an immediate biased transition, while still allowing a decision later after `BiasMitigationCheck` and, where needed, `ManualReview`.

Together, these constraints reduce the chance that protected attributes directly influence loan decisions without fairness safeguards.
