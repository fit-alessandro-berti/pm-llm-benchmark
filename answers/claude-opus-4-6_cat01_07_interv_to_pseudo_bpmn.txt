

# BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

---

## 1. Process Overview

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Process Name** | Invoice Approval Process |
| **Trigger** | Receipt of a supplier invoice (typically via email) |
| **End Event** | Invoice scheduled for payment |
| **Pools/Participants** | Accounts Payable (Mary, Rita), Purchasing Department (Dan), Department Manager (Carlos), Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen), External Supplier |

---

## 2. Roles / Lanes

| Lane | Role(s) | Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| **Accounts Payable (AP)** | AP Clerk (Mary) | Receive invoice, validate PO, log invoice, match invoice to PO, forward for approvals, schedule payment |
| **AP Supervision** | AP Supervisor (Rita) | Handle escalated exceptions, coordinate cross-department resolution, create retrospective POs |
| **Purchasing** | Purchasing Officer (Dan) | Verify goods/services receipt, confirm invoice-to-PO match on quantities/prices/charges |
| **Management** | Department Manager (Carlos) | Final approval of matched and confirmed invoices; reject or request investigation for anomalies |
| **Supplier Relations** | Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen) | Resolve supplier-side discrepancies (missing PO, pricing issues) by consulting contracts and contacting suppliers |
| **External** | Supplier | Provide invoices, respond to requests for corrections or additional information |

---

## 3. Artifacts / Data Objects

| Artifact | Description |
|---|---|
| **Invoice** | The incoming document from the supplier (email attachment) |
| **Purchase Order (PO)** | Internal document recording the authorized purchase; used for matching |
| **Accounting System Record** | The logged invoice entry in the accounting/ERP system |
| **Supplier Contract** | Agreement on file with pricing/terms (consulted by Karen when discrepancies arise) |
| **Confirmation Email** | Purchasing's confirmation that goods/services were received and invoice details match |
| **Manager Approval Notification** | System-generated email/notification requesting the manager's sign-off |
| **Retrospective PO** | A PO created after the fact for informally contracted services (exception path) |

---

## 4. Process Model — Step-by-Step Textual Description

---

### **START EVENT:** Invoice Received (via email from Supplier)

---

### **TASK 1: Check for Valid Purchase Order Number**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)
- **Input:** Incoming Invoice
- **Description:** AP Clerk examines the invoice to determine if it references a valid PO number.

---

### **GATEWAY 1 (Exclusive — XOR): Does the invoice have a valid PO number?**

#### **Branch A — NO PO Number Present:**

> **TASK 2a: Request PO / Additional Details from Supplier**
> - **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)
> - **Output:** Request sent to Supplier
> - **Description:** Mary sends the invoice back to the supplier requesting the missing PO number or additional details.
> - **Note:** Dan (Purchasing) also indicated he would tell Mary to obtain this information if it reaches him without a PO. Karen (Supplier Relations) may be looped in to contact the supplier for the PO or a revised invoice.

> **SUB-GATEWAY 1a (Exclusive — XOR): Can the supplier provide a valid PO?**
>
> - **YES:** Supplier provides corrected/revised invoice with PO  Flow returns to **TASK 1** (re-check) or proceeds to **TASK 3**.
> - **NO / Invoice is for informally contracted services (no PO exists):**
>
>   > **TASK 2b: Escalate to AP Supervisor for Exception Handling**
>   > - **Lane:** AP Supervision (Rita)
>   > - **Description:** Rita coordinates with department heads to either:
>   >   - **Create a Retrospective PO** (TASK 2c) — a PO is generated after the fact to formalize the purchase  Flow then proceeds to **TASK 3**.
>   >   - **Reject the Invoice**  Proceed to **END EVENT (Invoice Rejected)**.
>   > - **Ambiguity Note:** Rita's account suggests this decision involves department heads, but exact approval criteria for retrospective POs are not detailed in the interviews.

#### **Branch B — Valid PO Present:**

> Proceed to **TASK 3**.

---

### **TASK 3: Log Invoice into Accounting System**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)
- **Input:** Invoice with confirmed valid PO number
- **Output:** Accounting System Record created
- **Description:** Mary enters the invoice details into the accounting/ERP system.

---

### **TASK 4: Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)  Purchasing (Dan)
- **Input:** Logged invoice and associated PO reference
- **Description:** Mary forwards the invoice to Dan in Purchasing to verify that the goods or services described on the invoice were actually received, and that invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) align with the PO.

---

### **TASK 5: Verify Invoice Against Purchase Order**
- **Lane:** Purchasing (Dan)
- **Input:** Invoice, PO details
- **Description:** Dan opens the PO and compares it line-by-line against the invoice — checking quantities, unit prices, delivery charges, and any other terms.

---

### **GATEWAY 2 (Exclusive — XOR): Do invoice details match the PO?**

#### **Branch A — MATCH (Everything is correct):**

> **TASK 6a: Send Confirmation to Accounts Payable**
> - **Lane:** Purchasing (Dan)
> - **Output:** Confirmation email to AP
> - **Description:** Dan sends a confirmation email to Mary indicating that the invoice is verified — goods/services were received and details match the PO.
>
> Proceed to **TASK 8**.

#### **Branch B — DISCREPANCY Found (incorrect pricing, extra units, missing items, etc.):**

> **TASK 6b: Investigate and Resolve Discrepancy**
> - **Lanes involved:** Purchasing (Dan), Accounts Payable (Mary), and potentially Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)
> - **Description:** Dan may contact the supplier directly **or** ask Mary to hold the invoice until a corrected version is received. If the issue relates to contractual pricing, Karen is looped in to check the supplier contract and communicate the correct terms.
>
> **SUB-GATEWAY 2a (Exclusive — XOR): Can the discrepancy be resolved at this level?**
>
> - **YES — Resolved:**
>   - Supplier sends a corrected invoice, or the discrepancy is explained/accepted.
>   - Flow returns to **TASK 5** (re-verification) or proceeds to **TASK 6a** (confirmation).
>
> - **NO — Cannot be resolved easily:**
>
>   > **TASK 7: Escalate to AP Supervisor**
>   > - **Lane:** AP Supervision (Rita)
>   > - **Description:** Mary escalates the unresolved discrepancy to Rita. Rita coordinates between departments and/or contacts the supplier to resolve the issue. Once resolved, the invoice re-enters the normal flow.
>   > - **Output:** Resolution directive; invoice returns to **TASK 5** for re-verification or proceeds to **TASK 6a** if cleared.
>   > - **Note:** Rita stated that she ensures "problem invoices don't get stuck indefinitely," implying an active follow-up/monitoring responsibility.

---

### **TASK 8: Perform Invoice-to-PO Line Item Matching in System**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)
- **Input:** Confirmation from Purchasing, invoice record in accounting system, PO
- **Description:** After receiving Dan's confirmation that goods/services were received, Mary performs a formal matching of invoice line items against the PO in the accounting system (three-way match: invoice  PO  receipt confirmation).

---

### **GATEWAY 3 (Exclusive — XOR): Do line items match in the system?**

- **MATCH:** Proceed to **TASK 9**.
- **DISCREPANCY:** Loop back to **TASK 6b** (Investigate and Resolve Discrepancy) or **TASK 7** (Escalate to Supervisor) as described above.

> **Ambiguity Note:** Mary describes matching *after* receiving purchasing confirmation, while Dan describes his verification *as* the matching step. It is possible that there are two matching activities — one informal (by Purchasing) and one formal/system-based (by AP). The model represents both. In some cases, these may be collapsed into a single verification depending on organizational practice.

---

### **TASK 9: Submit Invoice for Manager Approval**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary) / System-automated
- **Input:** Fully matched and confirmed invoice
- **Output:** System-generated approval notification to Department Manager
- **Description:** The accounting system automatically sends an email notification to the Department Manager (Carlos) requesting sign-off. The notification includes the invoice details, PO, and purchasing confirmation.

---

### **TASK 10: Review and Approve/Reject Invoice**
- **Lane:** Department Manager (Carlos)
- **Input:** Invoice details, PO, purchasing confirmation (displayed in system)
- **Description:** Carlos reviews the invoice for reasonableness — checking quantities, pricing, budget alignment, and any special notes.

---

### **GATEWAY 4 (Exclusive — XOR): Manager's Decision**

#### **Branch A — APPROVED:**

> Proceed to **TASK 11**.

#### **Branch B — REJECTED or HELD FOR INVESTIGATION:**

> **TASK 10a: Reject or Request Investigation**
> - **Lane:** Department Manager (Carlos)  Accounts Payable (Mary)
> - **Description:** Carlos rejects the invoice in the system or asks Mary to hold it and investigate further. This may occur due to unexpected items, significant budget variances, or suspicious totals.
> - **Frequency:** Carlos noted this happens approximately once every couple of months.
> - **Flow:** Invoice returns to **TASK 6b** / **TASK 7** (investigation/escalation path). Once resolved, it re-enters the flow at **TASK 9** for re-submission to the manager.

---

### **TASK 11: Schedule Invoice for Payment**
- **Lane:** Accounts Payable (Mary)
- **Input:** Manager-approved invoice
- **Description:** Mary adds the approved invoice to the next payment cycle/payment run.

---

### **END EVENT: Invoice Scheduled for Payment**

---

### **ALTERNATE END EVENT: Invoice Rejected**
- **Trigger:** Invoice cannot be matched to any PO, retrospective PO is denied, or discrepancy cannot be resolved.
- **Lane:** AP Supervision (Rita) or Department Manager (Carlos)

---

## 6. Noted Ambiguities and Uncertainties

| # | Ambiguity | Source | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Two matching steps vs. one.** Mary describes matching invoice line items *after* receiving purchasing confirmation, while Dan describes his review *as* the matching step. It is unclear whether these are two distinct activities or a single activity viewed from two perspectives. | Mary (Interview 1) vs. Dan (Interview 2) | Modeled as two steps (informal verification by Purchasing + formal system match by AP). Validate with process owner whether these are truly separate. |
| 2 | **Who contacts the supplier during discrepancy resolution?** Dan says he sometimes contacts the supplier directly; Mary implies she communicates through email; Karen says she is looped in for key suppliers. | Dan (Interview 2), Mary (Interview 1), Karen (Interview 5) | Modeled as a collaborative task with multiple possible actors. Clarify organizational policy on supplier communication authority. |
| 3 | **Retrospective PO creation authority.** Rita mentions working with "department heads" to create retrospective POs, but it is not specified who formally authorizes this or what criteria apply. | Rita (Interview 4) | Flag for further investigation. May require a sub-process model for exception handling. |
| 4 | **Manager rejection frequency and re-entry point.** Carlos says rejections are rare (~bimonthly). The exact re-entry point after investigation of a manager-rejected invoice is not explicitly stated. | Carlos (Interview 3) | Assumed to re-enter at TASK 9 after resolution. Confirm with stakeholders. |
| 5 | **Invoice rejection as a terminal outcome.** Rita mentions invoices may be rejected, but no formal rejection notification process to the supplier is described by any interviewee. | Rita (Interview 4) | Modeled as an end event. Consider adding a supplier notification task. |
| 6 | **Karen's involvement trigger.** It is not entirely clear whether Karen is engaged only for key/strategic suppliers or for all supplier discrepancies. | Karen (Interview 5) | Modeled as conditional involvement. Clarify threshold or criteria for engaging Supplier Relations. |

---

## 7. Recommended Next Steps

1. **Validate with process owner** whether the two matching activities (Purchasing verification + AP system match) are indeed separate steps.
2. **Clarify the escalation sub-process** — Rita's exception-handling path could be modeled as a separate BPMN sub-process with its own internal logic.
3. **Define explicit business rules** for Gateway decisions (e.g., discrepancy thresholds, budget variance tolerances for Carlos's review).
4. **Add timer events** — consider modeling SLA/timeout boundaries (e.g., how long before an unresolved discrepancy is auto-escalated to Rita).
5. **Map to a formal BPMN 2.0 diagram** using a tool (e.g., Camunda Modeler, Bizagi, Signavio) based on the structure above.
