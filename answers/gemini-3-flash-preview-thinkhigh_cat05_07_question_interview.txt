### 1. Initial Documentation and Verification
*   **Threshold for Rejection:** "Are there specific 'red flag' issues in the initial documentation (e.g., specific compliance failures or deed discrepancies) that would lead to an immediate rejection of the property rather than a request for updates?"
*   **Data Integrity:** "When the property coordinator records details into the central database, what is the source of truth for 'expected rental price range' before the professional inspection and market analysis occur?"

### 2. Property Manager Assignment Logic
*   **Load Balancing:** "How is a manager’s 'current workload' quantified? Is it based purely on the number of units, or does it account for the complexity/tier of the properties currently in their portfolio?"
*   **Conflict of Interest:** "In cases where a property manager is a specialist in a specific geographic area but is currently at capacity, what is the secondary protocol for assignment?"

### 3. Inspection and Remediation
*   **Disagreement Handling:** "If a landlord disagrees with the inspector’s findings or refuses to fund a necessary repair, what is the escalation path? Is there a point where the company will decline to represent the property?"
*   **Parallel Processing:** "Can the marketing team begin creating listings and brochures while repairs are still underway, or must the property pass the final inspection before any marketing assets are produced?"

### 4. Marketing and Pricing Dynamics
*   **Pricing Authority:** "The process mentions updating pricing models based on market trends. Who holds the final authority to set the 'live' rental price—the property manager, the digital marketing team, or the landlord?"
*   **Internal vs. External:** "Does the 'activation' in the rental portal happen simultaneously across all external platforms (like Zillow or apartments.com), or is there a tiered release strategy?"

### 5. Tenant Screening and Selection
*   **The Final Say:** "Once a suitable tenant is identified through the screening process, does the property manager have the delegated authority to sign the lease, or is the landlord required to give final approval on the specific applicant?"
*   **Multiple Applicant Logic:** "When dealing with multiple qualified applicants, you mentioned prioritizing longer leases or negotiating terms. Are there formalized 'weighting' criteria to ensure these decisions remain objective and compliant with fair housing standards?"

### 6. Compliance, Audits, and Quality Control
*   **Post-Lease Rectification:** "If the compliance officer or financial analyst identifies an error during the final audit (after the lease is signed and the security deposit is collected), what mechanisms are in place to rectify the agreement?"
*   **IT Integration:** "What specific 'data triggers' signify that a property has moved from the 'onboarding' phase to the 'active management' phase within your IT systems?"

### 7. Exceptions and Variations
*   **Custom Clauses:** "How do you manage the risk and operational overhead associated with 'custom lease clauses' requested by landlords? Is there a standardized library of approved alternative clauses, or is each one reviewed by legal?"
*   **Geographic Variation:** "Given that this process spans multiple cities, how do you adapt the workflow for regions with significantly different local housing regulations or inspection requirements?"

### 8. Communication and Tools
*   **The 'Golden Thread':** "With communication flowing across emails, CRMs, and project tools, how do you ensure a single, cohesive 'audit trail' exists if a dispute arises months after onboarding?"
*   **Stakeholder Visibility:** "Does the landlord have visibility into the onboarding pipeline (e.g., a portal showing current status), or is the property coordinator/manager responsible for providing manual updates?"
