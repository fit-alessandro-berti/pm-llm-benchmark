# BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

Based on the interview transcripts, the following model captures the standard flow, decision points, roles, and exceptions in the invoice approval process.

## Process Overview
**Process Name:** Invoice Approval & Payment Scheduling  
**Start Event:** Invoice Received (via email)  
**End Event:** Payment Scheduled or Invoice Rejected/Returned

---

## **Process Participants (Roles & Systems)**
1. **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)** – Primary processor
2. **Purchasing Officer (Dan)** – Confirmation of goods/services receipt and PO matching
3. **Department Manager (Carlos)** – Final approval
4. **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)** – Exception handling
5. **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** – Supplier-side issue resolution (indirect)
6. **Supplier** – External entity
7. **Accounting System** – System of record for logging and tracking
8. **Email System** – Communication channel

---

## **Process Flow (Activities & Sequence)**

### **Main Path (Standard Scenario)**
1. **Start Event:** Invoice Received (via email to Accounts Payable).
2. **Task:** [AP Clerk] Check for Valid Purchase Order (PO) Number.
   - **Data Object:** Incoming Invoice
3. **Gateway (Exclusive):** PO Number Present?
   - **Yes (PO Valid):** Proceed to Task 4.
   - **No (PO Missing/Invalid):** Follow **Exception Path 1** (see below).
4. **Task:** [AP Clerk] Log Invoice into Accounting System.
   - **Data Object:** Logged Invoice Record (created)
5. **Task:** [AP Clerk] Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation.
   - **Data Object:** Invoice & PO Reference
6. **Task:** [Purchasing Officer] Confirm Goods/Services Receipt.
   - Purchasing verifies delivery against the PO.
   - **Data Object:** Receipt Confirmation (email)
7. **Gateway (Exclusive):** Receipt Confirmed?
   - **Yes:** Proceed to Task 8.
   - **No:** Follow **Exception Path 2** (see below).
8. **Task:** [Purchasing Officer] Match Invoice Line Items to PO.
   - Compare quantities, prices, delivery charges.
9. **Gateway (Exclusive):** Invoice Matches PO?
   - **Yes (Perfect Match):** Proceed to Task 10.
   - **No (Discrepancy Found):** Follow **Exception Path 3** (see below).
10. **Task:** [AP Clerk] Send Invoice for Manager Approval.
    - System sends automatic email notification to Department Manager.
    - **Data Object:** Approval Request (with invoice, PO, and confirmation)
11. **Task:** [Department Manager] Review & Approve Invoice.
    - Manager checks details, budget alignment, and any special notes.
12. **Gateway (Exclusive):** Manager Approves?
    - **Yes:** Proceed to Task 13.
    - **No (Rejects or Requests Investigation):** Follow **Exception Path 4** (see below).
13. **Task:** [AP Clerk] Schedule Invoice for Payment.
    - Invoice added to next payment run.
14. **End Event:** Payment Scheduled.

---

## **Exception Paths & Variations**

### **Exception Path 1: Missing/Invalid PO Number**
- **Trigger:** Invoice lacks a valid PO number.
- **Path:**
  1. [AP Clerk] Return Invoice to Supplier, requesting PO details.
  2. [Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)] (if involved) Contacts Supplier to obtain PO or revised invoice.
  3. Once PO is provided, re-enter main flow at Task 4.
- **Alternative (Informal Service Invoices):** [AP Supervisor (Rita)] coordinates with department heads to create a retrospective PO or reject the invoice.
  - If retrospective PO is created, re-enter main flow at Task 4.
  - If rejected, process ends (Invoice Rejected).
- **Note:** According to Mary, if PO is missing, the invoice is sent back to the supplier. Dan confirms that he cannot proceed without a PO reference.

### **Exception Path 2: Receipt Not Confirmed**
- **Trigger:** Purchasing cannot confirm goods/services receipt.
- **Path:**
  1. [Purchasing Officer] Notifies AP Clerk that receipt is unconfirmed.
  2. [AP Clerk] May hold invoice and follow up with supplier/shipping.
  3. Resolution may involve [Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)] if delivery issue.
  4. Once receipt is confirmed, re-enter main flow at Task 8 (matching).
  5. **Uncertainty:** Rita mentioned that persistent issues may be escalated to her; Dan did not specify this step.

### **Exception Path 3: Invoice-PO Discrepancy**
- **Trigger:** Invoice does not match PO (pricing, quantity, etc.).
- **Path:**
  1. [Purchasing Officer] Identifies discrepancy.
  2. **Gateway:** Can discrepancy be resolved quickly?
     - **Yes (e.g., minor issue):** [Purchasing Officer] contacts Supplier directly for clarification/corrected invoice.
     - **No (e.g., complex issue):** [Purchasing Officer] notifies [AP Clerk] to hold invoice; [AP Supervisor (Rita)] may be escalated to for coordination.
  3. [AP Supervisor (Rita)] or [Purchasing Officer] may involve [Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)] to verify contract terms.
  4. Once resolved (via corrected invoice or agreement), re-enter main flow at Task 10 (manager approval).
  - **Note:** Mary mentioned that if discrepancy can't be resolved, she escalates to supervisor.

### **Exception Path 4: Manager Rejection/Investigation Request**
- **Trigger:** Department Manager suspects error or budget mismatch.
- **Path:**
  1. [Department Manager] Rejects approval or requests investigation.
  2. [AP Clerk] is notified to hold invoice and investigate.
  3. Investigation may involve [Purchasing Officer] or [AP Supervisor (Rita)].
  4. After investigation, re-enter main flow at Task 10 or reject invoice.
- **Note:** Carlos indicated this happens rarely.

## **Artifacts/Data Objects**

- **Purchase Order (PO)** (in system)
- **Logged Invoice Record** (in Accounting System)
- **Receipt Confirmation** (email)
- **Approval Request** (system notification with embedded data)
- **Payment Schedule Entry** (in Accounting System)

## **Clarifications & Uncertainties**
1. **Manager Approval Trigger:** Mary stated it's triggered automatically after both receipt confirmation and matching; Carlos receives a system notification. The model assumes both conditions must be met before sending for approval.
2. **Escalation to Supervisor:** Mary and Rita both mentioned supervisor involvement, but the exact escalation threshold is not fully defined. The model places it within exception paths.
3. **Role of Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** Her involvement is indirect and occurs only during exceptions, primarily in Paths 1 and 3. She does not perform system tasks.
4. **Non-PO Invoices:** Rita highlighted a separate handling for invoices without a PO (retrospective PO creation or rejection), which is captured in Exception Path 1.
5. **Sequence Variations:** Some steps may overlap (e.g., receipt confirmation and PO matching might occur concurrently by Purchasing), but the textual model follows a sequential logic for clarity.

## **Conversion to BPMN Diagram Notes**
- Use **lanes** for roles: AP Clerk, Purchasing Officer, Department Manager, AP Supervisor, Supplier (external), and a general "Accounting System" lane for automated steps.
- **Gateways** should be clearly labeled with conditions (e.g., "PO Present?", "Receipt Confirmed?").
- **Data Objects** can be represented as documents/attachments on the relevant tasks or flows.
- **Exception paths** should be modeled as alternative flows from gateways, potentially leading to a **sub-process** for exception handling that returns to the main flow.
