Here’s a targeted list of open-ended questions designed to deepen your understanding of the property onboarding process:

### Roles and Responsibilities
1. Can you clarify the distinction between the **property coordinator** and the **property manager**? Specifically, at what point does responsibility transfer from one to the other, and do their responsibilities ever overlap during the onboarding process?

2. How are **leasing agents** involved in the process? Are they always involved in tenant screening, or only under certain conditions?

### Decision-Making and Assignment Criteria
3. When assigning a property manager, what happens when two or more managers appear equally qualified based on the stated criteria? Is there a formal tie-breaking process or escalation path?

4. How does the company weigh the different assignment factors (workload, geography, experience, etc.) when they conflict? For example, how would you decide between a manager with high relevant experience but a heavy current workload versus one with lighter workload but less experience?

### Timing and Sequencing
5. After the introductory meeting with the landlord, what typically determines the timeline for scheduling the photographer and inspector? Are these activities usually done in sequence or can they happen in parallel?

6. Once the property passes inspection and marketing materials are created, how much time typically passes before the property is activated in the rental portal? What factors might accelerate or delay this step?

### Inspection, Repairs, and Remediation
7. When the inspector identifies issues, how is it decided whether the landlord must address them before onboarding can continue? Are there thresholds or categories of issues that automatically pause the process?

8. If repairs are needed, how are contractors selected, and who is ultimately responsible for ensuring the repairs meet the required standards?

### Marketing and Pricing
9. What level of input does the property manager have over the marketing strategy or initial rental price? Can they override recommendations from the digital marketing team or pricing models?

10. How are market trends incorporated into the pricing model, and how frequently is this information updated during the onboarding process?

### Tenant Selection and Lease Negotiation
11. When multiple qualified tenants apply for the same property, what criteria or process is used to prioritize one over the others? Are there formal policies, or does this vary by property manager?

12. How are requests for non-standard lease terms (such as custom clauses from the landlord or special tenant requests) evaluated and approved? Who has the final authority to accept or reject these requests?

### Exceptions and Special Cases
13. What kinds of properties or landlord requirements typically trigger the need for special insurance or additional approvals? How is this identified early in the process rather than later?

14. Are there different onboarding paths or additional steps for certain property types (e.g., student housing, furnished rentals, or multi-unit buildings)? If so, how are these variations triggered?

### Quality Assurance and Governance
15. At what points in the process are the compliance officer and financial analyst reviews conducted? Can their findings cause the process to loop back to an earlier stage?

16. If a quality check reveals a problem (such as a lease that doesn’t meet fair housing standards or pricing that falls outside profitability targets), how is that issue resolved and who is responsible for fixing it?

### Communication and Coordination
17. How does the company ensure that information about special conditions or landlord preferences discussed in the introductory meeting is consistently communicated to all downstream teams (marketing, leasing, compliance, etc.)?

18. When issues or exceptions arise that require input from multiple stakeholders, what mechanisms exist to track decisions and prevent miscommunication between regional offices, property managers, and support teams?

These questions focus on uncovering the logic, decision rights, variations, and coordination mechanisms that are only lightly described in the current explanation.
