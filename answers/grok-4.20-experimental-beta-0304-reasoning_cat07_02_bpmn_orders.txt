**Description of the BPMN Diagram**

This diagram is a **BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation)** model showing an **Order-to-Cash** process with material defect handling. It is divided into four horizontal **swimlanes** (pools/lanes):

---

### 1. **Sales** (Top Lane)
This lane handles the initial order validation and administrative tasks:

- **Start Event**: "Monday morning"
- **Task**: *Check order for completeness*
- **Exclusive Gateway (XOR)**: "Check if customer is already in the database"
  - **No** branch  Task: *Add customer in the database*
- After customer validation, the process sends **"Order details"** (message flow) to the **Production** lane.
- Later in the process, Sales creates a **"Draft invoice"** once the product is sent.

---

### 2. **Production** (Second Lane)
Responsible for manufacturing the ordered product:

- Receives the **Order details** from Sales.
- **Task**: *Plan production*
- **Task**: *Manufacture product*
- After manufacturing, it sends a **"Product sent"** message to the **Warehouse**.
- There is an error loop labeled **"Material is faulty"** that triggers the **Material Compensation Subprocess**.

---

### 3. **Material Compensation Subprocess** (Third Lane)
This is a **subprocess** triggered when faulty materials are detected during production:

- **Start Event**: Triggered by "Material is faulty"
- **Task**: *Order materials* (01)
- **Task**: *Return material* (02)
- **Task**: *Check materials*
- An **exclusive gateway** handles the outcome of the material check/order confirmation.
- This subprocess loops back to Production once materials are resolved.

---

### 4. **Warehouse** (Bottom Lane)
Handles final delivery:

- Receives the **"Product sent"** message from Production.
- **Task**: *Send product*
- Ends with a decision gateway related to successful shipment.

---

### Overall Process Flow

The diagram depicts a realistic end-to-end order fulfillment process with exception handling:

1. Sales receives and validates a customer order.
2. Production plans and manufactures the product.
3. If materials are faulty  the **Material Compensation Subprocess** is triggered (order new materials + return defective ones).
4. Once production is complete, the product is sent to the Warehouse for shipping.
5. Sales then finalizes the invoice.

---

**Key BPMN Elements Used:**
- Swimlanes (Sales, Production, Material Compensation, Warehouse)
- Start/End events
- Tasks (rounded rectangles)
- Exclusive Gateways (diamonds) for decision points
- Message flows (dashed arrows between lanes)
- Looping behavior for material defects
- A subprocess (the Material Compensation section)
