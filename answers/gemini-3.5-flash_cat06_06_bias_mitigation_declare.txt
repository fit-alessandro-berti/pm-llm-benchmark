### Rationale for the Added Constraints

The newly added constraints introduce guardrails to prevent discrimination and guarantee procedural justice when sensitive demographic attributes are accessed:

1. **`coexistence` (`CheckApplicantRace` $\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview`)**:
   - *Rationale*: If a sensitive demographic attribute is accessed during the workflow (represented by `CheckApplicantRace`), a `ManualReview` must also occur during the process. This ensures "human-in-the-loop" oversight and prevents fully automated systems from making unvetted decisions when sensitive information is present.

2. **`response` (`CheckApplicantRace` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
   - *Rationale*: If the applicant's race is checked, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must eventually follow. This forces the system or agent to log and execute fairness checks to ensure that the evaluation criteria remain objective and unbiased.

3. **`precedence` (`BiasMitigationCheck` $\rightarrow$ `FinalDecision`)**:
   - *Rationale*: A `FinalDecision` cannot be reached unless a `BiasMitigationCheck` has already occurred in the trace. This guarantees that all loans undergo systematic bias auditing before any final approval or rejection is rendered.

4. **`nonchainsuccession` (`CheckApplicantRace` $\nrightarrow$ `FinalDecision`)**:
   - *Rationale*: This constraint prevents a `FinalDecision` from *immediately* following a `CheckApplicantRace` event. By ensuring that a decision cannot directly succeed a sensitive attribute check, we eliminate the possibility of immediate, unmediated bias-driven outcomes (e.g., instant rejection upon revealing minority status), forcing intermediary validation or mitigation steps instead.
