This matters for fairness and equity because professional association membership may reflect access to networks, local social capital, socioeconomic privilege, or insider connections rather than actual job ability. Even if the rule is not explicitly based on a protected characteristic, it can still create **indirect discrimination** if members of certain demographic groups are more likely to belong to the club. It may also disadvantage candidates from the protected group if they do not have access to the same association-based boost.

In short, **Group B’s hiring process is biased because it gives preferential treatment to candidates with a specific professional association affiliation.** The bias manifests through score inflation at the cultural fit stage and leads to hiring decisions that favor connected candidates over comparable or stronger candidates without that affiliation. A fairer process would remove the association boost, define cultural fit using job-relevant criteria, audit outcomes across groups, and ensure that equivalent scores lead to equivalent decisions.
