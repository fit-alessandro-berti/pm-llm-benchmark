Below is a structured explanation of the pseudo-BPMN process, broken down by business purpose, control flow, and branching logic.

---

## 1) Overall business purpose

This process appears to model a **customer order handling workflow** with two major request types:

- **Standard requests**: handled through a mostly routine validation and fulfillment process.
- **Custom requests**: require additional feasibility analysis and may end in a custom quote or rejection.

After the request is assessed, the process may require **manager approval** before finalization. If approval is denied, the process can loop back for rework and reassessment. If everything is accepted, the process ends with a confirmation to the customer.

---

## 2) Step-by-step explanation of each activity

### Start Event
This marks the beginning of the workflow when a customer request is received.

---

### Task A: ōReceive Customer Requestö
The business process starts by collecting the customerÆs request. This could include order details, product/service type, quantity, custom requirements, deadlines, etc.

**Purpose:**  
To formally capture the incoming request and place it into the fulfillment/processing pipeline.

---

### XOR Gateway: ōCheck Request Typeö
This gateway routes the process based on the type of request.

- **XOR (exclusive gateway)** means only **one** path is taken.
- In this case, the request is categorized as either:
  - **Standard**
  - **Custom**

This is a decision point, not a parallel split.

---

## 3) Standard request path

### Task B1: ōPerform Standard Validationö
For a standard request, the business performs a routine validation.

**Purpose:**  
To confirm that the request meets basic business rules, completeness requirements, and eligibility criteria.

Examples might include:
- Required fields are filled in
- The customer is eligible
- The request is within standard product/service limits

---

### AND Gateway: ōRun Parallel Checksö
After standard validation, two checks run in parallel.

- **AND gateway** means the process splits into **both** branches simultaneously.
- Both branches must complete before the process can continue.

#### Task C1: ōCredit Checkö
Evaluates whether the customer is financially acceptable or has sufficient credit.

**Purpose:**  
To reduce financial risk before fulfilling the request.

#### Task C2: ōInventory Checkö
Verifies whether the requested product or service capacity is available.

**Purpose:**  
To ensure the order can actually be fulfilled.

---

### Join after parallel checks: ōAll Parallel Checks Completedö
This is the synchronization point.

- The process waits until **both** the credit check and inventory check are finished.
- Only then can the workflow move forward.

**Purpose:**  
To ensure no downstream decision is made until all required verification steps are complete.

---

### Task D: ōCalculate Delivery Dateö
Once validation and parallel checks are complete, the system calculates when the customer can expect delivery or completion.

**Purpose:**  
To generate a realistic promised date based on availability, capacity, lead time, and any internal constraints.

---

## 4) Custom request path

### Task B2: ōPerform Custom Feasibility Analysisö
For a custom request, the process is more complex.

**Purpose:**  
To assess whether the requested customization is technically, operationally, and economically possible.

This may involve:
- Technical review
- Engineering effort estimate
- Resource availability
- Cost and timeline feasibility

---

### XOR Gateway: ōIs Customization Feasible?ö
This decision determines whether the request can move forward.

- **If Yes**
- **If No**

Again, only one outcome is chosen.

---

### If Yes  Task E1: ōPrepare Custom Quotationö
If the custom request is feasible, a special quotation is prepared.

**Purpose:**  
To provide pricing, timelines, and conditions tailored to the custom request.

This is different from the standard flow because custom work often requires:
- Non-standard pricing
- Special lead times
- Extra terms and conditions

---

### If No  Task E2: ōSend Rejection Noticeö  End Event
If the customization is not feasible, the customer is informed that the request cannot be fulfilled.

**Purpose:**  
To formally reject the request and stop the process.

This branch ends immediately, so:
- No approval step
- No invoice
- No confirmation for fulfillment

---

## 5) Convergence after standard or custom work

The diagram then says:

> ōAfter Standard or Custom Path Tasks Completedö

This indicates that the process converges after either:
- the standard path reaches its post-processing stage, or
- the custom path successfully prepares a quotation

In BPMN terms, this is a **merge/convergence point** after alternative branches.

However, note an important nuance:

- On the **standard path**, the last task before this point is **Calculate Delivery Date**
- On the **custom path**, the last task before this point is **Prepare Custom Quotation**

So the process rejoins after the path-specific preparation is complete.

---

## 6) Approval logic

### XOR Gateway: ōIs Approval Needed?ö
At this stage, the process decides whether managerial approval is required.

**Purpose:**  
To enforce internal governance based on factors such as:
- Order value
- Discount level
- Risk level
- Non-standard terms
- Margin thresholds
- Customization complexity

This gateway is exclusive:
- If approval is needed, go to manager approval.
- If not, proceed directly.

---

### If Yes  Task F: ōObtain Manager Approvalö
A manager reviews the request or quote.

**Purpose:**  
To authorize the transaction before it becomes final.

This is a control step used to ensure policy compliance and reduce risk.

---

### XOR Gateway: ōIs Approval Granted?ö
After review, the manager makes a decision.

#### If Yes  Task G: ōGenerate Final Invoiceö
If the manager approves, the system generates the final invoice.

**Purpose:**  
To formalize the sale and initiate billing.

This typically means the request is now considered accepted and ready for customer notification.

---

#### If No  Task H: ōRe-evaluate Conditionsö
If approval is denied, the process goes into rework.

**Purpose:**  
To reassess the request and possibly modify it so it can be approved later.

This could involve:
- Adjusting price
- Revising scope
- Changing delivery expectations
- Rechecking feasibility or fulfillment conditions

---

## 7) Loop-back behavior

The diagram says:

> ōLoop back to Task E1 (for Custom Path) or Task D (for Standard Path)ö

This means the process does not simply end on rejection of approval. Instead, it returns to an earlier step depending on the request type:

### For Custom Path:
Loop back to **Task E1: Prepare Custom Quotation**

This suggests the quotation may be revised based on manager feedback.

### For Standard Path:
Loop back to **Task D: Calculate Delivery Date**

This suggests the delivery date or associated conditions may need to be recalculated after conditions are adjusted.

**Purpose of the loop:**  
To support rework and refinement until the request becomes acceptable or a valid outcome is reached.

---

## 8) No approval needed path

### If No Approval Needed  Task G: ōGenerate Final Invoiceö
If the request does not require managerial approval, the process skips the approval steps and goes straight to invoicing.

**Purpose:**  
To complete the financial side of the order efficiently when no extra authorization is required.

---

## 9) Final customer communication

### Task I: ōSend Confirmation to Customerö
After the final invoice is generated, a confirmation is sent to the customer.

**Purpose:**  
To notify the customer that the request/order has been accepted and finalized.

This may include:
- Invoice details
- Delivery date
- Next steps
- Reference numbers

---

### End Event
The process ends after the customer has been informed.

---

# 10) Meaning of the gateways

HereÆs a quick summary of each gateway type in the process:

## XOR Gateway
Used for **decision making** where only one path can be taken.

Examples:
- Request type: Standard vs Custom
- Customization feasible? Yes vs No
- Approval needed? Yes vs No
- Approval granted? Yes vs No

## AND Gateway
Used for **parallel execution** where multiple tasks happen at the same time.

Example:
- Credit check and inventory check run simultaneously

The AND join waits until all parallel tasks finish before proceeding.

---

# 11) How the paths differ

## Standard request path
- Routine validation
- Parallel operational checks:
  - credit
  - inventory
- Delivery date calculation
- Possible approval
- Invoice generation
- Customer confirmation

## Custom request path
- Feasibility analysis
- Decision on whether customization is possible
- If feasible:
  - custom quotation
- If infeasible:
  - rejection notice and process ends
- Possible approval
- Invoice generation
- Customer confirmation

### Key difference
The custom path has an **extra feasibility and quotation layer**, while the standard path focuses on **routine validation and fulfillment readiness**.

---

# 12) When approvals or rework are triggered

## Approval is triggered when:
The request meets conditions that require managerial oversight. The pseudo-BPMN does not define the exact conditions, but typically these are business rules such as:
- High-value order
- Discount above threshold
- Custom terms
- Risk-related cases
- Exception handling

## Rework is triggered when:
Manager approval is **not granted**.

Then the process loops back to:
- **Custom path:** revise the quotation
- **Standard path:** recalculate delivery conditions

This implies the organization wants to adjust the request until it aligns with policy or approval criteria.

# 13) Important modeling note

There is one practical BPMN nuance worth mentioning:

- The line ōAfter Standard or Custom Path Tasks Completedö suggests the two branches merge before approval logic.
- In a formal BPMN model, this would usually be represented with a proper **merge gateway** or a well-structured convergence point to avoid ambiguity.

Also, the loop-back from Task H is conditional on the branch type, so in a formal diagram there would likely be explicit sequence flows or process variables indicating whether the process came from the standard or custom route.

# 14) In simple business terms

This process says:

1. Receive the request.
2. Determine whether it is standard or custom.
3. Process it according to the request type.
4. If needed, run parallel checks for standard requests.
5. If custom, verify feasibility and possibly reject.
6. Decide whether approval is required.
7. If approval is required, get it.
8. If approval is denied, revise and retry.
9. If approved or not needed, generate the invoice.
10. Notify the customer.
11. End.
