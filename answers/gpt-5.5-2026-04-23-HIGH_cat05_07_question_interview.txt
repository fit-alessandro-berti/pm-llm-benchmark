Below are targeted, open-ended questions I would ask to clarify and deepen understanding of the property onboarding process.

## 1. Process Scope and Starting Point

1. What event officially starts the property onboarding process: the landlord’s first inquiry, receipt of documentation, a signed service agreement, or something else?

2. What types of properties are included in this onboarding process, and are there different paths for single-family homes, multi-unit buildings, luxury rentals, student housing, or commercial-adjacent properties?

3. How do you determine whether a landlord or property is eligible for onboarding before investing time in documentation review?

4. What information is typically collected from the landlord during the initial contact?

5. Are there situations where the company declines to onboard a property? If so, what are the main reasons?

## 2. Documentation Review

6. Which documents are mandatory before the process can move forward?

7. Which documents are optional, conditional, or dependent on the city, property type, or landlord arrangement?

8. How does the coordinator determine whether a document is current, valid, and acceptable?

9. What happens when documentation is incomplete, outdated, inconsistent, or legally questionable?

10. Who is responsible for following up with the landlord when documents are missing, and how often does that follow-up occur?

11. Are there deadlines for landlords to provide missing documentation before the onboarding is paused or cancelled?

12. How are local housing regulations identified and verified for properties in different cities or regions?

## 3. Property Data Capture

13. What specific property details must be recorded before the property can proceed to the next stage?

14. How do you validate property details such as square footage, unit count, amenities, zoning, or occupancy limits?

15. Are property details provided only by the landlord, or are they independently verified through inspections, public records, or other sources?

16. What happens if the landlord’s description of the property differs from the inspector’s findings or public records?

17. Who owns the accuracy of the property record once it is entered into the central database?

## 4. Property Manager Assignment

18. How is the dedicated property manager selected in practice?

19. Which assignment factor carries the most weight: workload, geography, property type experience, landlord needs, or something else?

20. Who makes the final decision on manager assignment?

21. What happens if no available property manager is an ideal fit for the property?

22. Can a property manager assignment change during onboarding, and what would trigger that change?

23. How are conflicts handled if multiple managers could reasonably be assigned to the property?

## 5. Landlord Introduction and Expectation Setting

24. What topics must be covered during the introductory call or meeting with the landlord?

25. Is there a standard agenda or checklist for this meeting?

26. What decisions are expected to be finalized during this conversation?

27. How are special landlord requirements captured and approved?

28. What types of landlord requests are considered routine versus exceptional?

29. Who decides whether custom terms, special policies, or unusual landlord requirements can be accepted?

30. What happens if the landlord’s expectations conflict with company policy or local regulations?

## 6. Inspection, Photography, and Property Readiness

31. At what point are the photographer and inspector scheduled, and who coordinates those appointments?

32. Are photography and inspection done in parallel, or must one happen before the other?

33. What criteria determine whether a property has passed inspection?

34. How are inspection issues categorized, such as minor, major, safety-related, or legally blocking?

35. Who decides whether inspection findings must be resolved before marketing can begin?

36. What is the process when repairs are required?

37. Who selects and manages contractors when repair work is needed?

38. How are repair costs approved, and who is responsible for payment?

39. What happens if the landlord refuses to make required repairs?

40. Is a re-inspection always required after repairs, or only for certain types of issues?

## 7. Marketing Preparation

41. What inputs does the marketing team need before creating listings and brochures?

42. Who approves the final listing content, photos, rental description, and advertised price?

43. How is the expected rental price determined or adjusted based on market conditions?

44. What role does the property manager play in setting or approving the rental price?

45. How are listing claims reviewed to ensure they are accurate and compliant?

46. Are there restrictions on what can be included in marketing language due to fair housing or local advertising rules?

## 8. Property Activation

47. What does it mean for a property to be “activated” in the rental portal and internal systems?

48. What conditions must be satisfied before activation can occur?

49. Who has authority to activate the property?

50. Can a property be partially activated, such as internally visible but not publicly listed?

51. What are the most common reasons activation is delayed?

52. How is the landlord notified that the property is live and available to prospective tenants?

## 9. Tenant Application and Screening

53. When does tenant screening begin relative to property activation?

54. Who is responsible for managing tenant inquiries and applications: the property manager, leasing agent, or another team?

55. What are the standard tenant screening steps?

56. What criteria are used to determine whether an applicant is suitable?

57. How are credit history, employment verification, income, rental history, and references weighted in the decision?

58. What happens when an applicant meets some criteria but not others?

59. How are screening decisions documented to ensure consistency and compliance?

60. How do you handle multiple qualified applicants for the same property?

61. What role does the landlord have, if any, in selecting the tenant?

62. How do you ensure tenant selection complies with fair housing requirements?

## 10. Lease Negotiation and Finalization

63. At what point does lease negotiation begin?

64. Which lease terms are negotiable, and which are standardized or non-negotiable?

65. Who has authority to approve promotional discounts, custom lease clauses, or unusual lease durations?

66. How are landlord-specific policies, such as pets, maintenance responsibilities, or payment channels, reflected in the lease?

67. What review steps occur before the lease is sent for signature?

68. What happens if the tenant requests changes to the lease?

69. What happens if the landlord requests custom clauses that are not part of the standard agreement?

70. When is the security deposit collected, and what must happen before it is considered complete?

71. Who schedules and confirms the move-in date?

## 11. Compliance, Financial, and IT Reviews

72. At what stages do compliance officers, financial analysts, and IT teams become involved?

73. Are these reviews performed sequentially, in parallel, or only after lease finalization?

74. What specifically does the compliance officer review?

75. What types of issues commonly cause a lease or onboarding record to fail compliance review?

76. What does the financial analyst check beyond confirming the rental price?

77. How are exceptions handled when a rental price is below the recommended pricing model?

78. What does the IT review involve from a business-process perspective?

79. What happens if the online listing, property record, or internal system data is incorrect after activation?

80. Who has final sign-off that onboarding is complete?

## 12. Timing, Sequencing, and Service Levels

81. What is the typical timeline from landlord inquiry to property activation?

82. What is the typical timeline from property activation to signed lease?

83. Which steps usually take the longest?

84. Are there target turnaround times or service-level expectations for each phase?

85. Which steps can happen in parallel, and which must happen in a strict sequence?

86. What are the most common bottlenecks in the process?

87. How do you prioritize onboarding when multiple properties are entering the pipeline at the same time?

88. Are urgent or high-value properties handled differently?

## 13. Roles and Responsibilities

89. Can you walk me through the main roles involved and what each role is responsible for?

90. Where do handoffs occur between the coordinator, property manager, marketing team, leasing agent, compliance officer, financial analyst, IT team, and landlord?

91. Which role is accountable for the overall success of the onboarding process?

92. Who monitors progress across the full lifecycle?

93. How are responsibilities divided between regional offices and central support teams?

94. What decisions can regional teams make independently, and what requires corporate approval?

## 14. Exception Handling

95. What are the most common exceptions that occur during onboarding?

96. How are properties requiring special insurance handled?

97. How are custom landlord requirements evaluated and approved?

98. What happens when a property fails inspection multiple times?

99. What happens if a landlord becomes unresponsive during onboarding?

100. What happens if market conditions change significantly before the property is activated or leased?

101. How do you handle disputes between landlords and property managers during onboarding?

102. Are there formal escalation paths for legal, financial, compliance, or operational issues?

103. What criteria determine whether onboarding should be paused, escalated, or cancelled?

## 15. Communication and Tracking

104. How is communication with landlords typically managed throughout the process?

105. Which communications are considered important enough to formally record?

106. How do internal teams stay aligned on the current status of an onboarding property?

107. What tools or channels are used for coordination, and what purpose does each serve?

108. How do you prevent important decisions from being lost across email threads, CRM notes, or project management tools?

109. Are landlords given visibility into onboarding status, upcoming tasks, or outstanding blockers?

110. How are status changes communicated internally and externally?

## 16. Completion Criteria and Success Measurement

111. What exact conditions must be met for the property onboarding process to be considered complete?

112. Does onboarding end at property activation, signed lease, tenant move-in, or after all internal audits are complete?

113. What metrics do you use to evaluate whether onboarding was successful?

114. Do you track cycle time, landlord satisfaction, tenant placement speed, compliance issues, repair delays, or pricing accuracy?

115. How are lessons learned from difficult onboardings captured and used to improve the process?

116. What parts of the process are most in need of improvement today?
