### **Process Model: Invoice Approval Workflow**

**Process Name:** Standard Invoice Approval  
**Key Participants (Roles):** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary), Purchasing Officer (Dan), Department Manager (Carlos), Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita), Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)  

---

#### **Activities/Tasks & Sequence Flow**
1. **Receive Invoice**  
   *Triggered by:* Supplier email.  
   *Participants:* Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary).  
   *Output:* Invoice document.

2. **Check for Valid Purchase Order (PO) Reference**  
   *Action:* Mary verifies if the invoice includes a valid PO number.  
   *Participants:* Mary.  
   *Decision Point:*  
   - **If PO exists ** Proceed to Step 3.  
   - **If PO missing ** Email Karen (Supplier) for PO number. *Note: Dan (Purchasing) may request PO from Mary in some cases.*  

3. **Log Invoice in Accounting System**  
   *Action:* Mary logs the invoice in the system if PO is valid.  
   *Participants:* Mary.  
   *Output:* Logged invoice entry.  

4. **Purchase Order Confirmation**  
   *Action:* Dan (Purchasing) reviews the invoice against the PO to confirm quantities/pricing.  
   *Participants:* Dan.  
   *Decision Point:*  
   - **If discrepancies found ** Email Mary with clarification request or send corrected invoice. *Note: Escalations to Rita (Supervisor) or Karen (Supplier) may occur in unresolved cases.*  
   - **If no discrepancies ** Email Mary confirmation of PO match.  

5. **Department Manager Approval**  
   *Action:* Carlos (Manager) reviews the invoice (via system email) and approves/rejects.  
   *Participants:* Carlos.  
   *Decision Point:*  
   - **If approval ** Proceed to Step 6.  
   - **If rejection ** Hold invoice; escalate to Rita for investigation.  

6. **Payment Scheduling**  
   *Action:* Mary schedules payment after approval.  
   *Participants:* Mary.  

---

#### **Gateways/Decision Points**  
- **Discrepancy Handling Gateway**  
  - If invoice discrepancies exist after Step 4, path splits:  
    - **Path A:** Email Purchasing (Dan) for clarification  (if unresolved, escalate to Rita).  
    - **Path B:** Email Supplier (Karen) to resolve PO/price issues  retry Steps 4/5.  

- **PO Availability Gateway**  
  - If PO missing at Step 1, path splits:  
    - **Path A:** Email Supplier (Karen) for PO  retry Step 2.  
    - **Path B:** Email Purchasing (Dan) to request PO  retry Step 2 (per Dan’s account).  

---

#### **Roles & Responsibilities**  
- **Mary (Accounts Payable Clerk):** Initial invoice reception, PO checks, system logging, payment scheduling.  
- **Dan (Purchasing Officer):** PO verification, confirmation emails, supplier clarification requests.  
- **Carlos (Department Manager):** Final approval/rejection, rare rejections for budget/item mismatches.  
- **Rita (Accounts Payable Supervisor):** Escalates unresolved discrepancies (e.g., missing POs, supplier negotiations).  
- **Karen (Supplier Relationship Manager):** Resolves PO/price issues with suppliers upon request.  

---

#### **Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs**  
- **Inputs:** Supplier invoice, Purchase Order (PO).  
- **Outputs:** Approved invoice (scheduled for payment), rejected invoice (escalated for investigation).  
- **Supporting Artifacts:** Confirmation email (Dan  Mary), system notifications (Carlos), supplier communication logs.  

---

#### **Exceptions/Variations**  
- **Missing PO:** Delays Step 4; requires supplier or purchasing intervention.  
- **Discrepancy Resolution Failure:** May cause rework loops (e.g., supplier corrections, PO creation).  
- **Rare Manager Rejection:** Triggered by budget/item mismatches (e.g., unexpected charges).  
- **Retrospective PO Creation:** For informal service contracts (Rita handles this).  

---

**Ambiguities/Uncertainties Noted:**  
- Timing of Karen’s involvement (indirect resolution vs. direct escalation).  
- Whether Dan always requires a PO before proceeding (conflicting accounts).  
- Frequency of Carlos’s rejections (rare, per his account).  

This model synthesizes the interviews into a coherent flow, highlighting exceptions and role dependencies while acknowledging conflicting details.
