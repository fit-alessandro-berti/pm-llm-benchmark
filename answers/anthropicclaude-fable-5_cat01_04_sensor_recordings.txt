# Process Mining Analysis: Manufacturing Event Log

## Step 1: Pattern Detection

Scanning the log for distinct "regimes" in sensor behavior, I identified **six distinct phases** based on simultaneous shifts in multiple sensor readings:

| Phase | Events | Key Signature |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| A | 1–3 | All readings at baseline (~20°C, 0 Hz, 0 flow, ~0.45 kWh) |
| B | 4–6 | High vibration (30–38 Hz), rising temp (50–58°C), strong flow (5), tool advancing |
| C | 7–8 | Moderate stable readings, elevated pressure (2 bar), tool stationary |
| D | 9 | Temperature spike (80°C), max vibration (50 Hz), max energy (5 kWh), zero flow |
| E | 10 | Sharp cooldown (30°C), near-zero vibration, low energy |
| F | 11–12 | Low temp, light vibration (5 Hz), gentle flow (1), tool extended to 20mm |
| G | 13–15 | Return to baseline — identical to Phase A |

## Step 2: Activity Labeling & Rationale

###  Events 1–3: **Idle / Machine Startup**
- Ambient temperature (~20°C), zero vibration, zero flow, minimal energy draw (~0.45 kWh).
- This is a machine at rest, consuming only standby power. The process has not yet begun.

###  Events 4–6: **Cutting Metal**
- **High vibration (3038 Hz)** is the strongest indicator — cutting tools produce intense mechanical oscillation.
- **Rising temperature (5058°C)** from friction between tool and material.
- **Steady material flow (5 units/s)** — raw material being fed through the cutter.
- **Tool position advancing (510mm)** — the tool progressively moving into the workpiece.
- Energy ramps up (2.03.0 kWh), consistent with active machining.

###  Events 7–8: **Assembling Parts**
- **Stable, moderate conditions**: temperature drops to 40°C, vibration falls sharply to 10 Hz.
- **Pressure increases to 2 bar** — consistent with clamping/pressing components together.
- **Reduced but steady flow (2 units/s)** — parts being positioned rather than machined.
- The *repetition of identical readings* across two events suggests a controlled, steady-state operation rather than aggressive material removal.

###  Event 9: **Welding**
- **Temperature spike to 80°C** (highest in the log) — localized heat application is the hallmark of welding.
- **Maximum energy consumption (5.0 kWh)** — welding arcs/torches are extremely power-hungry.
- **Vibration peak (50 Hz)** consistent with arc activity.
- **Zero material flow** — no material moving; heat is applied to a fixed joint.
- Tool position unchanged (15mm) — the tool holds steady at the weld point.

###  Event 10: **Quality Inspection (Cooldown & Check)**
- **Sharp drop in all readings**: temperature falls to 30°C, vibration nearly zero (1 Hz), energy drops to 1.0 kWh.
- Zero flow, tool still positioned at the workpiece (15mm).
- This quiet, low-activity pause immediately after welding fits an inspection step — sensors/cameras checking the weld require minimal energy and no mechanical action.

###  Events 11–12: **Packaging**
- **Light, gentle activity**: low vibration (5 Hz), low flow (1 unit/s), moderate energy (~1.5 kWh).
- **Tool position extends to 20mm** — consistent with a handler/pusher moving the finished product off the line.
- Cool temperatures (25°C) confirm no thermal processing — just material handling.

###  Events 13–15: **Idle / Cycle Complete**
- All readings return to baseline, identical to Events 1–3.
- The tool retracts to 0mm — the machine resets, ready for the next production cycle.

## Step 3: Discovered Process Model

```
[Idle]  [Cutting Metal]  [Assembling Parts]  [Welding] 
        [Quality Inspection]  [Packaging]  [Idle]
```

## Summary Table

| Events | Activity | Primary Evidence |
|--------|----------|------------------|
| 1–3 | Idle/Startup | Baseline readings, standby energy |
| 4–6 | Cutting Metal | High vibration + friction heat + material flow + advancing tool |
| 7–8 | Assembling Parts | Stable moderate readings, increased pressure (clamping) |
| 9 | Welding | Peak temperature + peak energy + zero flow |
| 10 | Quality Inspection | Sudden quiescence post-weld, minimal energy, tool in place |
| 11–12 | Packaging | Gentle handling, tool extension, cool temps |
| 13–15 | Idle/Reset | Return to baseline, tool retracted |

## Methodological Note

This analysis follows classic process mining principles: **change-point detection** (identifying where multiple sensors shift simultaneously), **regime clustering** (grouping events with similar multivariate profiles), and **semantic labeling** (mapping clusters to domain knowledge — e.g., welding = heat + energy, cutting = vibration + flow). The cyclical return to baseline (Events 13–15  Events 1–3) confirms we've captured one complete process instance (case) lasting ~75 seconds.
