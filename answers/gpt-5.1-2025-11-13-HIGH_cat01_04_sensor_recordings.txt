## 2. Rationale for Each Labeled Activity

### A. Machine Idle / Standby (Events 1–3 and 13–15)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature ~20–21 °C (near ambient)
  - Pressure = 1 bar (baseline)
  - Vibration = 0 Hz
  - Material Flow = 0 units/s
  - Tool Position = 0 mm
  - Low, steady energy (~0.4–0.5 kWh)
- **Interpretation:**
  - All process-related signals are essentially off; only minimal energy is used.
  - Tool is at home position (0 mm).
  - This fits an “Idle/Standby” state before and after a processing cycle.

---

### B. Cutting Metal (Material Removal) (Events 4–6)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature jumps from ~21 to 50–58 °C.
  - Vibration rises sharply to 30–38 Hz.
  - Material Flow = 5 units/s (continuous removal or feed of material).
  - Tool Position increases from 5  7  10 mm (tool engaged and moving through the workpiece).
  - Energy increases significantly (2.0  3.0 kWh).
  - Slight rise in pressure (1  1.5 bar).
- **Interpretation:**
  - High temperature + high vibration + continuous flow + moving tool are characteristic of **cutting, milling, or drilling**.
  - The gradual position change while all other metrics stay elevated is typical of a cutting pass.

---

### C. Part Positioning / Assembly Setup (Events 7–8)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature drops to 40 °C (cooler than during cutting but still above ambient).
  - Pressure increases to 2 bar.
  - Vibration lowers to 10 Hz (less intense than cutting).
  - Material Flow decreases to 2 units/s.
  - Tool Position = 15 mm and held constant.
  - Energy ~2.0 kWh (moderate).
- **Interpretation:**
  - Machine is no longer aggressively removing material: lower vibration and lower flow.
  - Increased pressure and a fixed tool position suggest **clamping, aligning, or bringing parts together**.
  - This fits an **“Assembly Setup / Part Positioning”** phase, where parts are pressed/positioned for the next operation, rather than actively machined.

---

### D. Welding / High-Heat Joining (Event 9)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature spikes to 80 °C (highest in the log).
  - Vibration increases to 50 Hz (very high).
  - Material Flow = 0 units/s (no bulk material being moved).
  - Tool Position remains at 15 mm (engaged point).
  - Energy jumps to 5.0 kWh (largest energy draw).
  - Pressure remains at 2 bar.
- **Interpretation:**
  - High temperature + high vibration + high energy with **no material flow** and a fixed position strongly suggests a **localized, energy-intensive operation**.
  - This closely matches **welding or similar high-heat joining** at a specific joint.

---

### E. Cooling / Post-Process Stabilization (Event 10)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature drops from 80  30 °C.
  - Vibration falls from 50  1 Hz.
  - Material Flow = 0 units/s.
  - Tool Position remains at 15 mm.
  - Energy drops sharply to 1.0 kWh.
- **Interpretation:**
  - After the intense welding step, the process appears to pause while conditions return toward normal.
  - This is consistent with a **cooling or stabilization** step, letting the welded joint solidify and heat dissipate.

---

### F. Quality Inspection / Measurement (Events 11–12)

- **Patterns:**
  - Temperature further drops to 25 °C (near ambient).
  - Pressure returns to 1 bar.
  - Vibration at a low but nonzero 5 Hz.
  - Material Flow = 1 unit/s (small, intermittent movement).
  - Tool Position = 20 mm (different from cutting/assembly positions) and held.
  - Energy modest and stable (1.5–1.6 kWh).
- **Interpretation:**
  - Conditions are calm, with only light motion and low energy usage.
  - Small flow and slight vibration at a dedicated position suggest **probing, scanning, or measuring** rather than machining.
  - This fits a **“Quality Inspection / Measurement”** stage, verifying the part after welding and cooling.

**Idle  Cutting Metal  Part Positioning / Assembly Setup  Welding  Cooling  Quality Inspection  Idle**
