### Standard / normative Hire-to-Retire flow
A typical Hire-to-Retire process is roughly:

**Post Job Ad  Screen Candidates  Conduct Interviews  Make Hiring Decision  Onboard Employee  Add to Payroll  Close Case**

So the key expectations are:

- **screening happens before interviews**
- **interviews happen before the hiring decision**
- **onboarding and payroll follow the decision**
- **payroll is not optional**
- **closing the case should be last**

---

## Model 1 analysis
### What it gets right
- It preserves the main backbone:
  - **Post  Screen**
  - **Decision  Onboard  Payroll  Close**
- Screening is at least placed before the decision and interview.

### Anomalies
- **Conduct_Interviews and Make_Hiring_Decision are unordered / parallel**
  - This means the decision can be made **before the interview**
  - Or the interview could even happen very late, potentially after later steps
- The interview is **not used as a prerequisite** for the hiring decision, which is logically odd in a hiring process

### Severity
- This is a **moderate anomaly**
- The process is still structurally close to a normal Hire-to-Retire flow, but the causal logic between interview and decision is broken

---

## Model 2 analysis
### What it gets right
- It does keep:
  - **Interview  Decision**
  - **Decision  Onboarding  Payroll/Close**
- So part of the hiring logic is present

### Anomalies
- **Screening and interviewing are not properly ordered**
  - Post leads to both **Screen** and **Interview**
  - So the interview may happen **before screening**
- **Screening is detached from the main decision path**
  - It does not influence the decision at all
- **Onboarding is inside a loop**
  - This allows repeated onboarding, which is atypical
- **Payroll is optional**
  - Because of the XOR with a silent transition, the case can close **without adding the employee to payroll**
  - This is a major violation of the Hire-to-Retire logic

### Severity
- These are **more serious anomalies**
- Especially the possibility of **skipping payroll** and the **wrong order of screening/interviewing**
- The model is much less faithful to the intended business process

---

## Which model is closer to the normative process?
### **Model 1 is closer**
Why:

- It still preserves the main sequence of the process
- Screening happens before the hiring decision
- Payroll and closure are mandatory and correctly ordered
- Its main problem is that **interview and decision are not properly sequenced**

In contrast, **Model 2** has multiple structural problems:
- interview can happen before screening
- screening is not part of the core decision logic
- onboarding can repeat unexpectedly
- payroll can be skipped entirely

---

## Bottom line
- **Model 1**: closer to a realistic Hire-to-Retire process, but with a serious interview/decision ordering flaw
- **Model 2**: significantly less normative, with more fundamental violations of process logic and integrity

**Therefore, Model 1 more closely aligns with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**
