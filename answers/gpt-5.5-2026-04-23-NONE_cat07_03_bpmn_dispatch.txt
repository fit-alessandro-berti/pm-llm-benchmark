The visualization is a BPMN-style process diagram for **“Dispatch of goods”** at **Computer Hardware Shop**, organized into three swimlanes:

- **Logistics** (top)
- **Secretary** (middle)
- **Warehouse** (bottom)

The process starts with **“Ship goods”** in the Secretary lane.

A parallel split follows, sending work into two main paths:

1. **Secretary path**
   - The secretary **clarifies the shipment method**.
   - A decision gateway asks: **“Special handling?”**
     - If **yes**, the secretary:
       - Gets **3 offers from logistic companies**
       - Selects a logistics company and places the order
     - If **no**, the secretary:
       - Checks whether **insurance is required**, using **customer information** and **order details**
       - A gateway then branches:
         - If insurance is necessary, the task moves to the Logistics lane to **insure the parcel**
         - In all cases, the process continues to **write the package label**
   - The insurance/label path and the logistics company order path later merge through gateways.

2. **Warehouse path**
   - The warehouse **packages the goods**.
   - There is a timer/event annotation indicating **48 hours**.
   - If delayed, the warehouse **notifies the customer about the delay**.
   - The packaging path then continues toward synchronization with the secretary/logistics path.

After both the shipment preparation and warehouse packaging-related activities are complete, the process joins at a parallel gateway. Then the warehouse:

- **Fills out shipment paperwork**
- **Moves the package to the pickup area**

The process ends with the final event: **“Shipment prepared.”**
