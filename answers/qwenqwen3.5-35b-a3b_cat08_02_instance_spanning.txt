# Strategic Process Optimization Plan: Fulfillment Center

## 1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact

To address the specific challenges posed by instance-spanning constraints, we must move beyond standard process discovery (which maps the flow of a single order) to **Process Mining techniques that analyze inter-case dependencies**. The event log provided contains the necessary attributes (`Resource`, `Order Type`, `Requires Cold Packing`, `Hazardous Material`, `Timestamp`) to perform this analysis.

### Identification and Quantification Techniques

*   **Shared Cold-Packing Stations (Resource Contention):**
    *   **Technique:** Perform a **Resource Performance Analysis**. We will filter the log for `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE` and map the `Packing` activity to `Station C2` (Cold) vs. `Station S7` (Standard).
    *   **Metric:** **Resource Utilization Rate** and **Queue Time per Case**. We calculate the average time an order waits in the queue before `Packing START` specifically for Cold Stations compared to Standard Stations.
    *   **Impact:** High utilization (>85%) on Cold Stations combined with high queue time indicates a bottleneck. If `Express` orders consistently have shorter wait times than `Standard` orders on Cold Stations, it confirms priority pre-emption is occurring, potentially starving standard perishable goods.

*   **Shipping Batching (Inter-Case Waiting):**
    *   **Technique:** **Inter-Case Waiting Time Analysis**. We analyze the time delta between `Packing COMPLETE` and `Shipping Label Gen. COMPLETE` for orders sharing the same `Destination Region`.
    *   **Metric:** **Batch Wait Time**. This is calculated as: `Time(Shipping Label Gen.) - Time(Packing Complete)`.
    *   **Impact:** If this delta is consistently high (e.g., >1 hour) for specific regions (e.g., "North"), it indicates orders are completing processing but are held up artificially to form a batch. This inflates the "End-to-End Lead Time" metric without adding value.

*   **Priority Order Handling (Interruption):**
    *   **Technique:** **Interruption and Pre-emption Analysis**. We correlate the `START` of an `Express` order at a resource (e.g., QC Staff A) with the `COMPLETE` status of a `Standard` order at the same resource.
    *   **Metric:** **Interruption Frequency** and **Standard Order Delay**. We measure how many times a `Standard` order's activity is paused or delayed by the arrival of an `Express` order on the same resource.
    *   **Impact:** High interruption frequency suggests the "Express" priority rule is causing instability in the standard flow, potentially increasing the total time for standard orders due to context-switching or resource re-allocation.

*   **Regulatory Compliance (Concurrent Instance Limits):**
    *   **Technique:** **Concurrency Analysis**. We reconstruct the timeline of the facility. At any given timestamp $t$, we count the number of active cases where `Activity` is `Packing` OR `Quality Check` AND `Hazardous Material = TRUE`.
    *   **Metric:** **Constraint Violation Rate** and **Peak Concurrent HazMat**.
    *   **Impact:** If the count exceeds 10, we have a compliance risk. If the count frequently hits 9 or 10, we have a "capacity saturation" risk where new HazMat orders must wait, creating a backlog specific to this order type.

### Differentiating Waiting Time: Within-Instance vs. Between-Instance

Process mining tools (e.g., Celonis, UiPath, Disco) allow us to decompose the **Flow Time** (End-to-End Duration) into **Processing Time** and **Waiting Time**.

*   **Within-Instance Factors (Processing Time):** This is the duration between `Activity START` and `Activity COMPLETE` for a specific Case ID. This represents the actual work done (e.g., how long it takes to pack an item).
*   **Between-Instance Factors (Waiting Time):** This is the duration between `Previous Activity COMPLETE` and `Next Activity START` for a Case ID.
    *   **Resource Contention:** If `Waiting Time` at `Packing` is high, and the `Resource` column shows high utilization by *other* Case IDs at that timestamp, the wait is due to **resource contention** (an instance-spanning constraint).
    *   **Batching:** If `Waiting Time` at `Shipping Label Gen.` is high, but the resource is `System (Batch B1)`, the wait is due to **batch formation logic** (an instance-spanning constraint).
    *   **Method:** We will use **Event Correlation** to match the `START` time of Order A with the `COMPLETE` time of Order B on the same resource. If Order A starts immediately after Order B, the wait was zero. If there is a gap, we investigate the `Resource` status during that gap.

## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

Optimizing one constraint in isolation can inadvertently worsen another due to the interconnected nature of the fulfillment center. Understanding these interactions is critical for a holistic solution.

### Interaction 1: Priority Handling vs. Cold-Packing Scarcity
*   **Scenario:** `Express` orders often contain perishables (`Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`).
*   **Conflict:** If the facility prioritizes `Express` orders for the limited 5 Cold Stations, `Standard` orders that also need cold packing will face longer queues.
*   **Risk:** While `Express` SLAs are met, `Standard` perishable orders may spoil or miss their delivery windows because they are pushed to the back of the line for the scarce resource.
*   **Implication:** A pure "Express First" policy at the Cold Station level is suboptimal if it creates a massive backlog of standard perishables that eventually clogs the system during peak times.

### Interaction 2: Batching vs. Hazardous Material Limits
*   **Scenario:** Orders going to the "North" region are batched. Some of these orders are `Hazardous Material`.
*   **Conflict:** The safety regulation limits 10 HazMat orders in Packing/QC simultaneously.
    *   If a batch of 15 HazMat orders is destined for "North," the batching logic wants to process them together.
    *   The safety regulation says only 10 can be processed at once.
*   **Risk:** The system might wait for the batch to fill up before releasing them, but the safety limit prevents them from moving through Packing simultaneously. This creates a "double wait": waiting for the batch *and* waiting for the HazMat slot to open up.
*   **Implication:** Batching logic must be aware of the HazMat capacity limit. A batch cannot be released if it would violate the concurrent limit.

### Interaction 3: Priority Handling vs. HazMat Limits
*   **Scenario:** An `Express` HazMat order arrives.
*   **Conflict:** If the system allows the Express order to preempt a `Standard` HazMat order currently in Packing to meet the 10-limit, does it create a compliance gap?
*   **Risk:** If the Express order is inserted into a slot, it might temporarily push the count to 11 if the Standard order isn't removed instantly.
*   **Implication:** Priority rules must be subordinate to hard safety constraints. The system must ensure that pre-emption does not violate the "Max 10" rule at any micro-second.

**Why this matters:** If we optimize for Speed (Priority), we risk Safety (HazMat). If we optimize for Safety (HazMat), we risk Speed (Batching delays). The strategy must find the Pareto frontier where safety is guaranteed, but flow is maximized.

## 3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies

Here are three concrete strategies designed to mitigate the identified instance-spanning constraints.

### Strategy 1: Dynamic "Cold-Station" Resource Pooling & Priority Triage
*   **Constraint Addressed:** Shared Cold-Packing Stations & Priority Handling.
*   **Specific Changes:**
    *   **Flexible Allocation:** Instead of reserving Cold Stations exclusively for Express or Standard, implement a **Dynamic Allocation Algorithm**.
    *   **Triage Logic:** At the `Packing START` decision point, the system checks the `Destination Region` and `Order Type`.
        *   If `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`: Check if a Cold Station is free.
        *   If multiple orders wait: Prioritize based on a weighted score: `(Time in Queue * 0.6) + (IsExpress * 0.4)`. This prevents "Starvation" of Standard perishables while still prioritizing Express.
        *   **Cross-Utilization:** If `Station C2` (Cold) is busy with a Standard order, but `Station S7` (Standard) is free, can a Standard order with `Requires Cold Packing = FALSE` use `S7` while a Cold order uses `C2`? (Yes, for Standard). But if a Cold order needs `C2`, it must wait.
    *   **Data Leverage:** Use historical data to predict peak "Cold Demand" hours. If the log shows 80% of Cold demand comes between 14:00-16:00, schedule temporary staff or open overflow capacity during these windows.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Reduced queue time for Cold Orders by 20%. Prevents starvation of Standard perishables while maintaining Express SLAs. Increases Cold Station utilization from 60% to 85%.

### Strategy 2: Constraint-Aware Batching Logic (HazMat-Integrated Batching)
*   **Constraint Addressed:** Shipping Batching & Regulatory Compliance.
*   **Specific Changes:**
    *   **Revised Batching Trigger:** Change the batching rule from "Wait for Region" to "Wait for Region **OR** HazMat Threshold."
    *   **HazMat Buffer:** If a batch is forming for the "North" region and contains 8 HazMat orders, the system should **not** wait for more orders to complete the batch if the next incoming order is also HazMat.
    *   **Action:** Release the batch of 8 HazMat orders immediately to Packing (if <10 limit allows) and send remaining North orders via a different carrier or delayed batch.
    *   **Decoupling:** For HazMat orders, decouple `Packing` from `Batching`. HazMat orders can generate Shipping Labels individually to ensure they clear the Packing/QC bottleneck faster, even if they don't form a full geographical batch.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Reduces "Batch Wait Time" for HazMat orders by 40%. Ensures 100% compliance with the 10-order limit by preventing the system from attempting to batch more than 10 HazMat orders simultaneously.

### Strategy 3: Staging-Based HazMat Capacity Management
*   **Constraint Addressed:** Regulatory Compliance (Max 10 Concurrent HazMat).
*   **Specific Changes:**
    *   **Physical/Digital Staging:** Create a designated "HazMat Holding Zone" (physical or logical in the WMS).
    *   **Token System:** Implement a "HazMat Token" logic. There are 10 tokens. When an order enters `Packing` or `QC`, it consumes a token.
    *   **Flow Control:** If 10 tokens are in use, new HazMat orders entering `Item Picking` are routed to the Holding Zone. They are *not* allowed to start Packing until a token is released (i.e., an order finishes QC).
    *   **Priority Integration:** If an `Express` HazMat order arrives, it can "borrow" a token from a `Standard` order currently in the Holding Zone (if available), but cannot preempt an order currently in the `Packing` activity (to avoid safety violations).
*   **Expected Outcome:** Eliminates the risk of regulatory violation (0% non-compliance). Smooths the flow of HazMat orders by preventing them from queuing up at the Packing door, which causes congestion.
