### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

By reviewing the process flow, we see three specific activities that carry different meanings depending on their role in the workflow:

*   **Verify Documents:**
    *   *Initial occurrence:* Standard identity verification (automated or initial screening).
    *   *Subsequent occurrence:* Remediation verification (validating new info provided after a roadblock).
*   **Call Customer:**
    *   *Occurrence A (Clarification):* Call to request missing documentation or resolve account discrepancies.
    *   *Occurrence B (Status Update/Follow-up):* A call made to inform the customer that the process is moving forward or that internal corrections were successful.
    *   *Occurrence C (Logistical):* A call to bring a customer back to a service point (branch scenario).
*   **Initiate Request:** While this appears once per case, the "Additional Context" reveals a need to distinguish between digital (Portal) and physical (Branch) intake for process mining analytics.

### 2. Renaming Strategy

| Original Activity | Context-Specific Name |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Initiate Request** | `Intake: Online Portal` / `Intake: Branch Walk-in` |
| **Verify Documents** | `Verify: Identity Screening` / `Verify: Remediation Review` |
| **Call Customer** | `Call: Request Missing Info` / `Call: Adjustment Follow-up` / `Call: Logistical Recall` |

### 4. Justification of Naming Choices

*   **Intake:** Distinguishing by channel (Portal vs. Branch) is critical for operational efficiency analysis, as branch processes likely have different Service Level Agreements (SLAs) than digital ones.
*   **Verify:** Splitting into "Identity Screening" vs. "Remediation Review" clarifies that the latter is a conditional step that only occurs if the first step fails or requires an update, allowing for better identification of process bottlenecks.
*   **Call Customer:** These names now differentiate between **proactive data collection** (Request Missing Info), **customer service/reassurance** (Adjustment Follow-up), and **physical workflow management** (Logistical Recall). This prevents the process model from incorrectly grouping all calls as "outbound solicitation" and helps measure the specific intent of frontline labor.
