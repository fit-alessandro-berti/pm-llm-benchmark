# Analysis of Bias in the Two Event Logs

## Identifying the Biased Log

**Group B (Unprotected Group)** exhibits bias. The event log reveals a systematic +5 "Association Boost" applied to the Cultural Fit score of candidates affiliated with the *LocalBusinessLeadersClub*. Group A (the protected group) receives no such adjustment, regardless of any comparable factors.

## How the Bias Manifests

### 1. Differential Scoring Based on Group Membership
The bias is explicit in the `ScoreAdjustment` column. Candidates in Group B who are members of the LocalBusinessLeadersClub (U001, U003) receive an automatic +5 Cultural Fit boost, while no candidate in Group A receives any adjustment.

### 2. Outcome Divergence Driven by the Boost
The effect on hiring decisions is particularly visible in **case U003**:
- Raw Cultural Fit score: **58** (likely below the hiring threshold)
- Adjusted score after boost: **63**  **Hired**

Compare this to **case P002** in Group A:
- Skill: 78, Personality: 75, Cultural Fit: 60 (unadjusted)  **Not Hired**

U003 has a *lower* Skill Score (75 vs. 78), a *lower* Personality Score (65 vs. 75), and a *lower* raw Cultural Fit (58 vs. 60) than P002 Ś yet U003 is **hired** and P002 is **not**. The only differentiator is membership in the LocalBusinessLeadersClub.

### 3. Correlation with a Protected Attribute
The `LocalResident` attribute is **TRUE** for all Group B members and **FALSE** for all Group A members. Because the LocalBusinessLeadersClub is inherently tied to local residency, the "professional association" boost functions as a **proxy for local residency status**. This transforms an apparently neutral criterion (association membership) into a mechanism of indirect discrimination against non-local candidates.

## Implications for Fairness and Equity

### Disparate Treatment
Two candidates with essentially identical qualifications can receive different decisions solely because one has access to a local network. This violates the principle of **equal treatment of equals**.

### Disparate Impact
Even if association membership is framed as a legitimate "cultural fit" signal, non-local residents are systematically excluded from opportunities to join the LocalBusinessLeadersClub. The boost therefore produces a statistically disparate impact on non-locals Ś a classic pattern of indirect discrimination.

### Erosion of Merit-Based Evaluation
The boost undermines the integrity of the Cultural Fit assessment. Scores no longer reflect the candidate's actual fit but rather their access to local social capital. Candidates with superior Skill and Personality scores (like P002) can be passed over in favor of weaker candidates (like U003) who benefit from the adjustment.

### Proxy Discrimination Risk
Using professional associations as a scoring factor is legally and ethically risky when those associations correlate with protected or demographically sensitive attributes (residency, socioeconomic status, national origin, etc.). Courts and regulators increasingly treat such proxies as equivalent to direct discrimination.

### Loss of Transparency and Trust
Although the adjustment is *logged* (which is commendable from a process-mining perspective), the existence of a hidden scoring lever that benefits one group erodes candidate trust and exposes the organization to reputational and legal risk under frameworks such as the EU AI Act, GDPR automated-decision provisions, and equal-employment legislation.

## Recommended Remediations

1. **Remove the Association Boost** or apply equivalent, neutral criteria uniformly across all candidates.
2. **Audit Cultural Fit scoring** to ensure it measures job-relevant behaviors rather than in-group familiarity.
3. **Test for disparate impact** using statistical parity or equal-opportunity metrics across the LocalResident attribute.
4. **Document scoring rules** transparently and subject them to fairness review before deployment.
5. **Separate merit signals from network signals** Ś if networks matter, capture them as a distinct, non-scored data point rather than embedding them in evaluation scores.

## Summary

Group B's event log is biased because it grants a +5 Cultural Fit adjustment to members of a locally-exclusive association, producing different hiring outcomes for candidates of comparable or even superior merit. Because association membership correlates with local residency, the boost functions as proxy discrimination against non-local applicants, compromising both procedural fairness and substantive equity in the hiring process.
