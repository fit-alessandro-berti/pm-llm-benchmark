Bias appears to enter the process through **non-credit attributes** that affect scoring and/or final approval: **CommunityGroup membership** and possibly **LocalResident status**.

## 1. Community affiliation receives a direct positive adjustment

The clearest bias is the **+10 “Community” score adjustment** given to applicants affiliated with the **Highland Civic Darts Club**.

Examples:

| Case | LocalResident | CommunityGroup | PreliminaryScore | Adjustment | Final/Adjusted Score | Decision |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| C001 | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 710 | +10 | 720 | Approved |
| C004 | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 690 | +10 | 700 | Approved |
| C003 | FALSE | None | 715 | 0 | 715 | Rejected |

This means membership in a specific community organization improves the applicant’s score even though it is not obviously related to creditworthiness. That creates a preference for applicants who have that affiliation.

The most concerning comparison is:

- **C004** had a preliminary score of **690**, received a +10 community adjustment, and was **approved**.
- **C003** had a higher preliminary score of **715**, received no adjustment, and was **rejected**.

So an applicant with lower underlying creditworthiness but the favored community affiliation was approved, while an applicant with a higher underlying score but no affiliation was rejected.

## 2. Local residency may also be favored

All applicants with the Highland Civic Darts Club affiliation are also marked as **LocalResident = TRUE**. The log suggests that local status may be interacting with the community adjustment or final decision rule.

For example:

- **C002**: Local resident, no group, score 720  Approved.
- **C003**: Non-local, no group, score 715  Rejected.
- **C005**: Non-local, no group, score 740  Approved.

This pattern suggests that non-local applicants may need a higher score to be approved, while local applicants or applicants with favored local affiliations may be approved at lower effective scores.

The log is small, so it does not prove that LocalResident status is explicitly used as a decision rule. But the pattern raises concern that geography is influencing outcomes.

## 3. The bias manifests at the PreliminaryScoring stage and carries through to FinalDecision

The explicit adjustment occurs during **PreliminaryScoring**:

- CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club  **+10 adjustment**
- CommunityGroup = None  **0 adjustment**

That adjusted score is then visible in **ManualReview** and appears to influence the **FinalDecision**.

This means the bias is not merely incidental; it is embedded into the workflow and propagated through later stages.

## 4. Applicants without the favored affiliation are disadvantaged

Applicants who are not members of the Highland Civic Darts Club receive no comparable benefit. This can disadvantage:

- Non-members of the club.
- New residents who have not had time to join local groups.
- People who are socially isolated or less connected.
- People who cannot participate in community organizations because of work schedules, disability, caregiving responsibilities, transportation barriers, language barriers, or cultural exclusion.
- Non-local applicants, if local residency is being favored.

Even if these applicants have similar or better underlying creditworthiness, they may receive worse outcomes.

## 5. Fairness concern: non-credit characteristics may be acting as proxies

Community membership and local residency may correlate with protected or sensitive characteristics such as race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, age, disability, nationality, or neighborhood. Even if the system does not directly use protected traits, using community affiliation or geography can create **proxy discrimination** or **disparate impact**.

For example, if the Highland Civic Darts Club is mostly composed of certain demographic groups, then the +10 adjustment could indirectly favor those groups.

## 6. Key fairness issue

The process appears to violate the principle that applicants with similar creditworthiness should receive similar treatment.

A particularly strong example:

- **C003**: Preliminary score 715, no affiliation, non-local  Rejected.
- **C001**: Preliminary score 710, club member, local  Adjusted to 720  Approved.
- **C004**: Preliminary score 690, club member, local  Adjusted to 700  Approved.

This suggests that community affiliation and/or local residency can outweigh the applicant’s actual preliminary credit score.

## Conclusion

Bias appears to manifest through the **+10 community adjustment** and possibly through preferential treatment for **local residents**. These factors favor applicants connected to a specific local community group and may disadvantage otherwise creditworthy applicants who lack that affiliation or geographic status. For fairness and equity, the organization should verify whether these attributes are genuinely predictive of repayment risk; if not, they should be removed from scoring and decision-making or carefully audited for disparate impact.
