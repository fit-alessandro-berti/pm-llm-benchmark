### How the new constraints reduce bias in the loanapplication process

| Constraint type | New rule(s) | Why it helps fairness |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------------------|
| **existence** ¢ `BiasMitigationCheck`, `ManualReview`, `CheckSensitiveAttributes` | Each trace must contain at least one biasmitigation check, a manual review, and a sensitiveattribute check. | Guarantees that the process never reaches a decision without first performing a dedicated fairness step. |
| **coexistence** ¢ `FinalDecision  ManualReview` and `Reject  ManualReview` | A final decision (or any rejection) can only appear together with a manual review. | Prevents ōunreviewedö outcomes that could hide biased shortcuts. |
| **response** ¢ `CheckSensitiveAttributes  BiasMitigationCheck` | After any activity that reveals a protected attribute, a biasmitigation activity must occur. | Ensures that newly exposed sensitive information is immediately processed through a fairness lens. |
| **precedence** ¢ `ManualReview` before `Approve`, `Reject`, `FinalDecision` | Manual review must occur **before** any decision is made. | Makes it impossible to decide on an application before a human has examined the case for potential discrimination. |
| **chainresponse** ¢ `CheckSensitiveAttributes  BiasMitigationCheck` (immediate) | The **very next** activity after a sensitiveattribute check must be the mitigation step. | Closes the window for a biased decision to be taken in the same step as the attribute check. |
| **chainprecedence** ¢ `ManualReview  FinalDecision` (immediate) | A final decision can only be issued **directly** after a manual review. | Eliminates any intermediate steps that could bypass the required human review. |
| **noncoexistence** ¢ `Reject` & `Approve` cannot coexist | A trace cannot contain both a rejection and an approval. | Forces a single, unambiguous outcome, preventing contradictory decisions that might hide bias. |
| **nonsuccession** / **nonchainsuccession** ¢ `CheckSensitiveAttributes  Reject` | A sensitiveattribute check may never be followed (directly or later) by a rejection without a mitigation step. | Stops the pattern ōsee protected attribute  reject immediatelyö, a classic source of disparate impact. |

**Overall effect:**  
These constraints embed a *fairbyconstruction* workflow: every time a protected attribute is observed, a biasmitigation activity is forced to intervene; every decision (approve/reject/final) is forced to be preceded by a manual review; and contradictory or unreviewed outcomes are prohibited. The result is a process model that mathematically limits the ability to discriminate based on `ApplicantAge`, `ApplicantGender`, `ApplicantRace`, or any other sensitive attribute.
