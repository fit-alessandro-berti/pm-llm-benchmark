1. **First triggers & intake**
   - What specifically initiates onboarding—does the landlord submit a formal request packet, and is there a standard intake form or checklist used by the regional office?

2. **Document verification scope**
   - Which documents are *required* versus *recommended*, and how do you decide whether something is “outdated” or “missing”?
   - Do you ever accept temporary documentation while you wait for renewals?

3. **Ownership of the verification work**
   - Who has the final say on whether a property can move forward after the document review—the coordinator, a compliance team, or another function?
   - What happens if the coordinator can’t get a clear answer from the landlord?

4. **Central database data model**
   - When you record property details, what fields are most critical to downstream teams (pricing, marketing, leasing, compliance)?
   - How do you handle cases where property facts are uncertain (e.g., exact unit counts, square footage discrepancies)?

5. **Manager assignment decision criteria**
   - How do you weigh factors like workload, geography, and specialization—are there any hard constraints (e.g., only certain managers can onboard certain property types)?
   - What historical signals help predict a “good fit” between the manager and this landlord/property?

6. **SLAs and timing**
   - What are the typical time targets for each stage (document review, manager assignment, scheduling photos/inspection, marketing launch, tenant onboarding)?
   - Where do you most often see delays, and what “recovery” steps do you take?

7. **First landlord-manager alignment meeting**
   - What are the most important topics covered in the initial meeting, and how do you ensure expectations are documented consistently?
   - Are there common areas of disagreement (pets, maintenance responsibilities, lease customization, payment terms)?

8. **Special conditions and exceptions early on**
   - If a landlord requests custom lease clauses or has unique requirements, at what point in the process do those requests get evaluated?
   - Who evaluates them, and what kinds of requests are automatically rejected?

9. **Photographer and inspector scheduling**
   - How do you decide when to schedule the photography and inspection relative to document verification and manager assignment?
   - Are there quality thresholds for photography and inspection (e.g., acceptable defect lists, photo standards)?

10. **Inspection outcomes and remediation**
   - What are the possible inspection outcomes (pass, conditional pass, fail), and what is the decision rule for “property can proceed to marketing”?
   - Who funds and coordinates repairs—property owner, company-managed budget, or shared responsibility?
   - How do you handle situations where repairs take longer than marketing timelines?

11. **Marketing readiness requirements**
   - Are there specific “go/no-go” criteria for launching listings (inspection must pass, pricing must be approved, portal activation requires compliance sign-off)?
   - How do you ensure accuracy of listing details (amenities, availability date, pricing range)?

12. **Pricing model update**
   - What inputs drive your pricing decisions besides market trends (property condition, neighborhood demand, seasonality, lease length, incentives)?
   - When a landlord insists on a particular rent, how do you resolve that?

13. **Portal activation & internal system integration (conceptually)**
   - What must be true before a property is visible to applicants (data completeness, approvals, deposit policies, availability calendar)?
   - Who verifies that everything is ready from a business perspective (not technical)?

14. **Tenant screening workflow**
   - What is the step-by-step screening process, and what criteria are “must-have” versus “soft factors”?
   - How do you treat incomplete applications or applicants with limited credit history?

15. **Reference checking depth**
   - How are prior landlord references obtained and evaluated—what do you do when references conflict with applicant-provided information?

16. **Decision points when there are multiple applicants**
   - How do you rank applicants—purely by eligibility/credit, or do you also consider stability, lease preferences, and timing?
   - What’s the approach for short-term versus long-term leaseholders when demand is high?

17. **Negotiation authority**
   - Who has authority to negotiate lease terms or offer promotions/discounts?
   - What guardrails exist to prevent inconsistent or unprofitable concessions?

18. **Lease finalization and deposit collection**
   - What are the typical lease phases (initial agreement, final signing, deposit handling), and what compliance checks occur before signing?
   - Are there scenarios where you sign a lease but delay move-in due to pending repairs or documentation?

19. **Move-in readiness**
   - How do you confirm move-in readiness—are there pre-move inspections, final checklists, or last-mile approvals?

20. **Quality checks & audit responsibilities**
   - What does each oversight function (compliance officer, financial analyst, IT/operations) specifically look for?
   - Are audits done only at the end, or do you have checkpoints throughout onboarding?

21. **Common failure modes**
   - What are the most common reasons onboarding stalls or needs to restart (missing docs, inspection failure, pricing disagreement, lease clause issues)?
   - What corrective actions are used to prevent recurrence?

22. **Exception handling playbook**
   - Can you describe a recent “high-friction” onboarding case with major exceptions (e.g., insurance needed, legal clauses requested, major repair delays)?
   - What decisions had to be made, by whom, and what outcome resulted?

23. **Communication and accountability**
   - How are communications structured so all parties are aligned (landlord, property manager, leasing agent, compliance, support)?
   - When something falls through the cracks, how do you detect it and who escalates?

24. **Documentation and traceability**
   - What ensures that key decisions and approvals are traceable (for compliance, dispute resolution, or internal audits)?
   - How do you capture “why” a decision was made, not just “what” was decided?

25. **Post-onboarding feedback loop**
   - Once the property is active and leased, do you review outcomes (tenant retention, maintenance issues, audit findings) to improve future onboarding decisions?
