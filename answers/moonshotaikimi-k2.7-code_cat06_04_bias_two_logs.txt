## Short answer

The **Group B (Unprotected Group) log reveals the biased mechanism**: a `+10 Community Boost` is applied to applicants who are both `LocalResident = TRUE` and members of the `Highland Civic Darts Club`. Because **Group A (Protected Group) never receives this boost**, the same—or even lower—preliminary scores can produce worse outcomes for protected applicants. In other words, the **overall decision process is biased *against* Group A**, and the evidence of that bias is recorded in Group B’s `ScoreAdjustment` column.

---

## How the bias manifests

### 1. Score adjustments are based on group membership, not merit
In Group A, every case has:

- `LocalResident = FALSE`
- `CommunityGroup = None`
- `ScoreAdjustment = 0`

In Group B, the `ScoreAdjustment` is `0` **unless** the applicant is a local resident **and** belongs to the `Highland Civic Darts Club`, in which case they receive `+10`.

| Case | Group | PreliminaryScore | ScoreAdjustment | Adjusted Score | Decision |
|------|-------|------------------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| P001 | A (Protected) | 720 | 0 | 720 | Approved |
| U001 | B (Unprotected) | 720 | **+10** | **730** | Approved |
| P002 | A (Protected) | 710 | 0 | 710 | Rejected |
| U002 | B (Unprotected) | 710 | 0 | 710 | Rejected |
| P003 | A (Protected) | 740 | 0 | 740 | Approved |
| U003 | B (Unprotected) | 695 | **+10** | **705** | Approved |

### 2. Lower-scoring unprotected applicants can be approved while higher-scoring protected applicants are rejected
The clearest sign of bias is the **P002 vs. U003** comparison:

- **P002** (Group A): preliminary score **710**  no boost  **Rejected**
- **U003** (Group B): preliminary score **695**  `+10` boost  **705**  **Approved**

A protected applicant with a higher preliminary score is rejected, while an unprotected applicant with a lower preliminary score is approved solely because of the community boost.

### 3. The decision threshold is effectively lower for favored community members
Without the boost, the cutoff seems to fall between 710 and 720. With the boost, an applicant with a 695 score can clear the threshold. This shifts the bar for approval depending on whether the applicant belongs to the `Highland Civic Darts Club`.

### 4. `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` act as gatekeepers to the boost
- Being a `LocalResident` alone is **not enough**: U002 is a local resident but has `CommunityGroup = None` and receives no boost, ending up rejected like P002.
- The boost requires **both** local residency **and** membership in a specific community group.
- Group A is excluded by definition (`LocalResident = FALSE`, `CommunityGroup = None`), so it can never benefit.

## Why this is problematic

| Factor | Concern |
|--------|---------|
| **`ScoreAdjustment`** | It is not tied to creditworthiness, income, risk, or any business-relevant feature. It is tied to community/residency status. |
| **`LocalResident`** | If protected-status applicants are disproportionately non-local, this becomes a proxy for the protected attribute. |
| **`CommunityGroup`** | Favoring one specific club (e.g., `Highland Civic Darts Club`) can encode ethnic, cultural, or social affiliations that correlate with protected characteristics, leading to **disparate impact**. |
| **Final decisions** | The boost systematically raises approval rates for Group B and lowers them for Group A, producing unequal outcomes for equally or less qualified applicants. |

## Conclusion

The **Group B log exhibits the biased rule** (`+10 Community Boost`), but the **harm falls on Group A**, the protected group. The bias is a form of **systemic preferential treatment**: local residents who belong to a favored community group receive a score increase that protected applicants cannot obtain, causing protected applicants with equal or better preliminary scores to be rejected more often. This is unfair because the adjustment is based on group identity rather than on legitimate risk or merit criteria.
