**Bias manifests clearly and systematically in the ScoreAdjustment stage.**

### Where and How Bias Appears

The process introduces an explicit **+10 (Community)** adjustment exclusively for applicants associated with "Highland Civic Darts Club" (all of whom are also marked `LocalResident: TRUE`). This adjustment is applied in the PreliminaryScoring/Scoring Engine phase and carried through to ManualReview and FinalDecision.

Key evidence from the log:

- **C004** (LocalResident TRUE, Highland Civic Darts Club): Preliminary score **690**  +10 adjustment  **700**  **Approved**.
- **C003** (LocalResident FALSE, no group): Preliminary score **715**  **no adjustment**  **715**  **Rejected**.

A non-affiliated applicant needs a materially higher preliminary score than an affiliated one to reach approval. C004 is approved at an adjusted 700 while C003 is rejected at 715 — a 15-point disadvantage for the non-affiliated applicant despite a higher raw score. 

Additional patterns:
- C001 (710 +10 = 720) is approved, while C003 (715, no adjustment) is rejected.
- C002 (LocalResident TRUE but no CommunityGroup) receives **no adjustment** at 720 and is approved, showing the bias is specifically tied to the named community affiliation rather than LocalResident status alone.
- Non-local applicants (C003, C005) receive zero adjustment and face what appears to be a higher effective threshold (715 rejected, 740 approved).

The adjustment is not justified by any other risk factor in the log. It is explicitly labeled "(Community)" and applied based on the CommunityGroup attribute.

### Attributes Driving the Bias

- **CommunityGroup** ("Highland Civic Darts Club" vs None): This is the clearest proxy. Only members of this specific group receive the +10 boost.
- **LocalResident** (TRUE/FALSE): Strongly correlated with the community adjustment. All recipients of the +10 are LocalResident = TRUE. Non-locals never receive it in this sample.
- These attributes are used to modify the score *after* the PreliminaryScore is calculated, meaning the model is overriding its own assessment of creditworthiness with a group-membership bonus.

### Impact on Fairness and Equity

This is **disparate treatment** based on group membership rather than individual creditworthiness. The preliminary score presumably reflects objective factors (income, credit history, debt load, etc.). By adding a community bonus, the system lowers the bar for members of one specific social group while holding others to a stricter standard.

**Concrete inequity**:
- An applicant with a preliminary score of 690 who belongs to the favored darts club is approved.
- An applicant with a preliminary score of 715 who lacks the affiliation is rejected.

This cannot be justified as "similar risk" treatment. The process effectively creates two different approval thresholds: one for "Highland Civic Darts Club + Local" applicants and a higher one for everyone else.

### Implications for Non-Affiliated Individuals

People without the favored community affiliation or local status must achieve significantly higher preliminary scores to overcome the built-in disadvantage. This creates systemic barriers for:
- Newcomers to the area
- Residents who do not participate in the specific social/civic organizations that receive preferential treatment
- Groups who are statistically less likely to join such organizations (due to cultural, ethnic, age, or socioeconomic reasons)

Even if the preliminary score is an accurate measure of creditworthiness, the adjustment introduces a non-merit factor that penalizes unaffiliated individuals. This is the textbook definition of **algorithmic bias** introduced through proxy variables (community membership and residency) that correlate with protected characteristics.

The process as shown is neither fair nor equitable. It demonstrably favors one narrow social group at the expense of others with equal or superior objective qualifications.
