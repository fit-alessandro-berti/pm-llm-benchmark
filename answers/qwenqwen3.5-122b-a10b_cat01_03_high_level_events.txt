#### 2. Rationale for Groupings

*   **Logic:** These events represent the initial state change of the raw material before any value-added manufacturing occurs.
*   **Resource Consistency:** Primarily driven by **Operator A**, supported by automation (Robot Arm) and initial heating.
*   **Temporal Proximity:** These events occur in rapid succession (within 15 seconds) with no significant gaps, indicating a continuous setup workflow.
*   **Function:** The goal is to ensure the material is identified (Scan), positioned (Align), and thermally conditioned (Preheat) to receive the assembly work.

**HL-02: Assembly & Inline Verification**
*   **Logic:** This is the core value-add phase where the product is physically constructed.
*   **Resource Consistency:** Driven by **Operator B** and validated by **Quality Sensor #1**.
*   **Grouping Rationale:** While "Measure weld integrity" is a quality check, it is grouped here because it acts as an **inline gate**. It validates the specific welding action immediately after completion. If this check fails, the process should not proceed to coating. Therefore, it is functionally part of the Assembly validation loop rather than a separate downstream inspection phase.
*   **Temporal Proximity:** Occurs as a tight cluster (20 seconds).

**HL-03: Protective Finishing**
*   **Logic:** These events occur after the structural assembly is complete. They focus on surface protection rather than structural integrity.
*   **Resource Consistency:** Dedicated automated units (**Coating Unit #3** and **Heating Unit #2**).
*   **Function:** Applying a chemical layer (Coating) and curing it (Drying) are chemically and physically linked processes. The drying cannot happen without the coating, making them a single logical step.

**HL-04: Final Quality Assurance**

*   **Resource Consistency:** Performed by **Operator C**, a different human resource than the assembly operator (Operator B), ensuring segregation of duties.

#### 4. Summary of Transformation Rules

To apply this abstraction to the full event log, the following rules should be applied:

1.  **Start Trigger:** A high-level step begins when a `Retrieve raw metal sheet` or equivalent material intake event occurs.
2.  **Resource Switching:** A new high-level step is triggered when the `Resource` changes to a new primary agent (e.g., Operator A  Operator B  Operator C) or a new functional machine cluster (e.g., Welding Robot  Coating Unit).
3.  **Inline Quality Inclusion:** Low-level sensor checks occurring within 30 seconds of a manufacturing action (e.g., Welding) are aggregated into that manufacturing action.
4.  **Final Check Isolation:** A manual `Visual check` performed by a different resource at the end of the timeline is always classified as the final step.
