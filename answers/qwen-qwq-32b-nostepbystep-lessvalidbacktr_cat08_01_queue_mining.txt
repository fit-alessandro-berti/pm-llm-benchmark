### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization  

#### Calculating Waiting Times  
To identify waiting times between consecutive activities, we analyze the **time gap between the completion of one activity and the start of the next activity** for each patient case (e.g., the gap between "Registration COMPLETE" and "Nurse Assessment START" for a given patient). Waiting time is defined as the time a patient spends idle between activities due to resource unavailability, process inefficiencies, or dependencies.  

#### Key Metrics  
- **Average Waiting Time**:** The mean delay between two activities across all cases.  
- **Median Waiting Time:** Robust measure of central tendency, less skewed by outliers.  
- **90th/95th Percentile Waiting Time:** Highlights extreme delays that affect vulnerable cases.  
- **Maximum Waiting Time:** Identifies extreme bottlenecks.  
- **Queue Frequency:** Number of cases experiencing waits exceeding a threshold (e.g., >15 minutes).  
- **Patient Impact:** Proportion of urgent cases or specific patient types (e.g., new patients) experiencing long waits.  

#### Prioritizing Critical Queues  
Prioritize queues based on:  
- **Impact on Overall Visit Duration:** Queues between early activities (e.g., Registration  Nurse) disproportionately affect total visit time.  
- **Resource Scarcity:** Queues where the subsequent activity depends on a bottleneck resource (e.g., a single ECG machine).  
- **Patient Satisfaction:** Queues with high variability or long waits for urgent cases.  

### 2. Root Cause Analysis  

#### Potential Root Causes Identified via Process Mining:  
1. **Resource Bottlenecks:**  
   - Example: A single nurse handling 100% of "Nurse Assessment" tasks might create backlogs during peak hours. Process mining’s **resource profile analysis** would reveal overutilized staff or rooms.  
   - Example: A "Doctor Consultation" activity might have long service times due to under-staffing or complex cases, causing downstream delays.  

2. **Activity Variability:**  
   - High variability in activity durations (e.g., inconsistent registration times due to staff inconsistency) can cause unpredictable waiting times. **Throughput analysis** would highlight activities with high standard deviations.  

3. **Handover Delays:**  
   - Inefficient handoffs between activities (e.g., poor communication between nurses and doctors) might cause idle time. Process mining’s **case timeline analysis** can identify gaps in activity transitions.  

4. **Scheduling Policies:**  
   - Overbooking in certain time slots (e.g., morning rush) could be identified via **calendar-based log analysis**, showing patient arrival patterns.  

5. **Patient Type Differences:**  
   - New patients might have longer registration times due to more paperwork, leading to longer queues. **Variant analysis** by patient type (e.g., "New" vs. "Follow-up") could highlight disparities.  

#### Process Mining Techniques:  
- **Bottleneck Analysis:** Identify resources (e.g., specific nurses or rooms) with high utilization or long service times.  
- **Variant Analysis:** Compare process variants (e.g., patients taking a "Registration  Nurse  Doctor" path vs. an alternative path).  
- **Case Timeline Clustering:** Group cases with similar waiting patterns to identify systemic issues (e.g., daily peaks in nurse assessment waits).

### 3. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies  

#### Strategy 1: **Dynamic Nurse Staffing Adjustments**  
- **Targets:** Queues between Registration and Nurse Assessment (e.g., average wait of 15 minutes, but 90th percentile at 30 minutes.  
- **Root Cause:** Understaffing during peak hours (e.g., 9–10 AM).  
- **Solution:** Use log data to identify peak demand periods and reallocate nurses or add temporary staff during those times.  
- **Impact:** Reduces Registration  Nurse wait by 30–40% during peak hours, validated by comparing wait times before/after staffing changes.  

#### Strategy 2: **Pre-Visit Digital Check-In**  
- **Targets:** Registration queue delays caused by manual form-filling.  
- **Root Cause:** New patients (50% of cases) spend excessive time at registration due to paperwork.  
- **Solution:** Implement a digital check-in system for patients to complete forms beforehand.  
- **Impact:** Reduces Registration completion time by 20%, cutting Registration  next activity waits by 25%.  

#### Strategy 3: **Parallelize Non-Critical Activities**  
- **Targets:** Queues between Doctor Consultation and Diagnostic Tests (e.g., ECG).  
- **Root Cause:** The doctor must complete their portion before tests can begin, creating a sequential bottleneck.  
- **Solution:** Allow nurses to initiate non-critical tests (e.g., ECG) while the doctor is still with the patient, provided no medical dependency.  
- **Impact:** Reduces wait between Doctor and Test by 50% for non-urgent cases.

### 4. Trade-offs and Constraints  

#### Trade-offs of Proposed Strategies:  
1. **Dynamic Nurse Staffing:**  
   - *Risk:* Increased labor costs during peak hours.  
   - *Mitigation:* Use part-time staff or cross-trained staff to minimize cost.  

2. **Pre-Visit Check-In:**  
   - *Risk:* Reduced patient satisfaction if the digital system is cumbersome.  
   - *Mitigation:* Pilot the system with a subset of patients and iterate based on feedback.  

3. **Parallel Processing:**  
   - *Risk:* Overloading technicians if tests are started prematurely.  
   - *Mitigation:* Implement a "soft parallel" approach (e.g., pre-allocating slots but only start tests after doctor sign-off.  

#### Balancing Objectives:  
- Use a **cost-benefit analysis** to ensure that staffing adjustments do not overburden staff (e.g., monitor nurse workload using resource utilization metrics).  
- Prioritize interventions where the cost of resolving a bottleneck (e.g., hiring an extra nurse) is justified by reduced patient wait time and dissatisfaction costs.
