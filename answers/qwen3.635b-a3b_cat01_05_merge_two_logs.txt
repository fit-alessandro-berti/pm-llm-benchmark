### Reasoning & Methodology

#### 1. Matching Strategy
Events were aligned based on three criteria:
*   **Order ID:** All events belong to `Order#1234`.
*   **Semantic Name Mapping:** Event names were mapped based on functional equivalence (e.g., `Order Received`  `OrderReceived`, `Payment Processed`  `PaymentCheck`).
*   **Temporal Proximity:** A tolerance threshold was applied. Strictly, a <2s difference is ideal, but semantic confidence allowed for a slightly wider window where system behavior justified the drift (e.g., payment gateway delays).

#### 2. Detailed Matching Decisions
*   **Order Received:** Matched `Order Received` (A) with `OrderReceived` (B). Timestamps differ by **2 seconds**. Given the exact name match and minimal drift, these are merged. Log B's timestamp is 2 seconds earlier, likely reflecting the actual capture time at the warehouse system before propagation to System A.
*   **Order Validated:** Matched `Order Validated` (A) with `OrderValidation` (B). Timestamps differ by **1 second**. Confident merge.
*   **Payment Processed:** Matched `Payment Processed` (A) with `PaymentCheck` (B). Timestamps differ by **5 seconds**. While this exceeds the strict 2s tolerance, the semantic match is strong, and Log B's note (`"Payment gateway delay"`) explains the latency. The payment was likely processed at 10:02:00 (Log A received time), but the check/log entry occurred at 10:02:05. Merged to preserve the complete workflow view.
*   **Quality Check:** `Quality Check` (B) at `10:03:00` has no counterpart in Log A. Log A skips from Payment to Shipping. This represents a value-added step in System B not tracked by System A. Included as a unique event.
*   **Item Shipped:** Matched `Item Shipped` (A) with `Shipping` (B). Timestamps differ by **2 seconds**. Confident merge.
*   **Item Delivered:** `Item Delivered` (A) at `10:20:00` has no counterpart in Log B. Log B ends at `10:05:02`. This event is likely outside System B's scope. Included as a unique event from Log A.

#### 3. Timestamp Handling
*   **Primary Timestamp:** Log A is designated as the primary timeline. Merged events use Log A's timestamp as the canonical time to maintain consistency with the primary system's view.
*   **Discrepancy Preservation:** Log B timestamps are retained in the `Match Details` column. This allows downstream analysis to detect sync issues or processing delays (e.g., the 5s payment gap).

#### 4. Attribute Enrichment
*   Merged records combine all available attributes. If `user_id` or `resource_id` exists in Log B, it is added to the merged record.
*   Unique events retain their original attributes.
*   Where Log A lacks attributes (e.g., `Item Delivered`), the enriched fields are marked as unavailable rather than leaving blank values, to avoid ambiguity.
