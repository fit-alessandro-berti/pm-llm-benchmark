This textual BPMN model represents the "Invoice-to-Payment" process based on the synthesized accounts of the five stakeholders.

### **Process Overview**
*   **Pool:** Accounts Payable Process
*   **Lanes:** Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk/Supervisor, Purchasing Department, Department Manager, Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM).

---

### **1. Activities / Tasks**
*   **[AP Clerk] Receive Invoice:** Invoice arrives via email.
*   **[AP Clerk] Check for PO:** Validate presence of a PO number.
*   **[AP Clerk] Log Invoice:** Record invoice in the accounting system.
*   **[Purchasing] Verify Receipt & Match:** Confirm goods/services received; compare invoice against PO (quantity, price, delivery).
*   **[Manager] Review & Approve:** Final system sign-off based on budget/details.
*   **[AP Clerk] Schedule Payment:** Final entry into payment cycle.
*   **[AP/Purchasing/SRM] Resolve Discrepancy:** Communicate with supplier or internal parties to fix errors (missing PO, price mismatch, missing info).
*   **[Supervisor] Exception Handling:** High-level intervention for complex blocks.
*   **[SRM] Contract Verification:** Check contract terms for pricing disputes.

---

### **2. Sequence Flow & Gateways**

1.  **Start:** Invoice Received.
2.  **Gateway 1 (PO Presence):**
    *   *No PO:* Go to **Resolve Discrepancy** (Contact supplier/create retrospective PO).
    *   *Has PO:* Proceed to **Log Invoice**.
3.  **Task:** Log Invoice into System $\rightarrow$ Forward to Purchasing.
4.  **Gateway 2 (Matching Check):**
    *   *Match Success:* Proceed to **Manager Review**.
    *   *Match Failure (Discrepancy):* Go to **Resolve Discrepancy**.
5.  **Gateway 3 (Manager Decision):**
    *   *Approve:* Proceed to **Schedule Payment**.
    *   *Reject/Hold:* Go to **Resolve Discrepancy**.
6.  **End:** Payment Scheduled.

---

### **3. Roles & Participants**
*   **AP Clerk (Mary):** Primary executor, gatekeeper, and payment scheduler.
*   **Purchasing Officer (Dan):** Technical validator of goods/services received.
*   **Department Manager (Carlos):** Final authority for financial sign-off.
*   **AP Supervisor (Rita):** Escalation point for stuck/complex invoices.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** External liaison for contract-related disputes.

---

### **4. Artifacts**
*   **Invoice (Digital):** Primary input.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Required reference document.
*   **Accounting System:** Central repository for logging, matching, and approval logs.
*   **Confirmation Email:** Automated trigger for Manager approval.

---

### **5. Exceptions & Variations**
*   **Missing PO:** Can lead to a rejection or the creation of a "retrospective PO" (handled by Supervisor).
*   **Pricing/Quantity Discrepancy:** Loops back to Purchasing or SRM. Purchasing may request a corrected invoice from the supplier.
*   **Manager Rejection:** If the manager sees unexpected items or budget variances, the invoice is pulled out of the flow for manual investigation.
*   **Escalation Path:** If the AP Clerk cannot resolve an issue, the task is escalated to the **AP Supervisor**, who acts as the process stabilizer.
*   **Informal Contracts:** A specific exception where invoices for services lack a PO, requiring the Supervisor to coordinate with Department Heads.

---

### **Ambiguities / Notes for Implementation**
*   **Iteration:** The process flow suggests that the "Resolve Discrepancy" activity is a circular sub-process that can be triggered at three different points: initially (by AP), during matching (by Purchasing), and during final review (by Manager).
*   **System Ownership:** While the Manager and Purchasing use the system, the AP Clerk acts as the primary "process owner" who tracks the invoice through these handoffs.
*   **Uncertainty:** There is a slight discrepancy regarding the "Reject" workflow; while the Manager can reject, it is unclear if the system forces a hard stop or if it simply returns to the AP queue for modification. The model assumes a return to the "Resolve Discrepancy" loop.
