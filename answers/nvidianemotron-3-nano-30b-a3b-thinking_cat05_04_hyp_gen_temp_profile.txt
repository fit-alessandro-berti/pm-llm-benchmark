**2. Hypotheses for the Observed Anomalies**

- **Rigid Scheduling / Scripted Workflows**  
  The tight RP and AC timings may be driven by automated scripts that force approval or closure after a fixed interval, ignoring realworld workload fluctuations.

- **Premature Closure**  
  The short AC interval suggests some claims are being closed immediately after assignment, possibly bypassing required evaluation or approval steps.

- **Bottleneck in Notification**  
  The wide variance and long mean for PN could reflect a backlog of approvals waiting for a notification step, or manual handoffs between teams that introduce unpredictable delays.

- **Skipped Quality Checks**  
  The nearzero interval EN points to a possible omission of a separate “Communicate Decision” step that should involve multiple reviewers or documentation.

- **SystemGenerated TimeStamps vs. Human Action**  
  Large STDEV values (e.g., EC, RE) may stem from asynchronous processing where timestamps record system events rather than actual human activity, creating apparent outliers.

- **Resource Constraints by Segment**  
  Certain adjusters, regions, or claim types might experience higher load, leading to irregular timing patterns that aggregate into the overall profile anomalies.

**a. Claims where the time between specific activities deviates from the expected range (using a ±3 threshold).**  

```sql
WITH event_diffs AS (
    SELECT
        c.claim_id,
        ce1.timestamp   AS ts_R,
        ce2.timestamp   AS ts_A,
        ce3.timestamp   AS ts_E,
        ce4.timestamp   AS ts_P,
        ce5.timestamp   AS ts_N,
        ce6.timestamp   AS ts_C,
        -- calculate deltas in seconds
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce2.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))   AS delta_RA,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce3.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))   AS delta_RE,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce4.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))   AS delta_RP,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))   AS delta_PN,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce5.timestamp))   AS delta_NC,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce3.timestamp))   AS delta_EC,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce2.timestamp))   AS delta_AC
    FROM claim_events ce1
    JOIN claim_events ce2 ON ce2.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'A'
    JOIN claim_events ce3 ON ce3.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce3.activity = 'E'
    JOIN claim_events ce4 ON ce4.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce4.activity = 'P'
    JOIN claim_events ce5 ON ce5.claim_id = ce4.claim_id AND ce5.activity = 'N'
    JOIN claim_events ce6 ON ce6.claim_id = ce5.claim_id AND ce6.activity = 'C'
    JOIN claims c ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id
    WHERE ce1.activity = 'R'
)
SELECT
    claim_id,
    delta_RA,
    delta_RE,
    delta_RP,
    delta_PN,
    delta_NC,
    delta_EC,
    delta_AC
FROM event_diffs
WHERE
    -- anomaly thresholds (±3)
    delta_RP   NOT BETWEEN 90000 - 3*3600 AND 90000 + 3*3600   -- RP
    OR delta_PN  NOT BETWEEN 604800 - 3*172800 AND 604800 + 3*172800   -- PN
    OR delta_AC  NOT BETWEEN 7200 - 3*3600 AND 7200 + 3*3600   -- AC
    OR delta_EC  NOT BETWEEN 300 - 3*60 AND 300 + 3*60;      -- EN
```

**b. Correlation of anomalies with adjusters, claim types, or regions.**  

```sql
WITH anomalies AS (
    SELECT
        claim_id,
        delta_RP   AS rp_seconds,
        delta_PN   AS pn_seconds,
        delta_AC   AS ac_seconds,
        delta_EC   AS ec_seconds,
        c.claim_type,
        a.adjuster_id,
        a.region
    FROM (
        -- reuse the same delta calculation as above, limited to the key pairs
        SELECT
            claim_id,
            EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce4.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))   AS rp_seconds,
            EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))   AS pn_seconds,
            EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce2.timestamp))   AS ac_seconds,
            EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce3.timestamp - ce1.timestamp))   AS ec_seconds
        FROM claim_events ce1
        JOIN claim_events ce2 ON ce2.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce2.activity = 'A'
        JOIN claim_events ce3 ON ce3.claim_id = ce1.claim_id AND ce3.activity = 'E'
        JOIN claim_events ce4 ON ce4.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce4.activity = 'P'
        JOIN claim_events ce5 ON ce5.claim_id = ce4.claim_id AND ce5.activity = 'N'
        JOIN claim_events ce6 ON ce6.claim_id = ce5.claim_id AND ce6.activity = 'C'
        JOIN claims c ON c.claim_id = ce1.claim_id
        JOIN adjusters a ON a.adjuster_id = ce4.resource::int   -- assuming resource stores adjuster_id
        WHERE ce1.activity = 'R'
    ) d
    JOIN claims c          ON c.claim_id = d.claim_id
    JOIN adjusters a       ON a.adjuster_id = d.resource::int
)
SELECT
    claim_type,
    region,
    adjuster_id,
    COUNT(*)               AS anomaly_cnt,
    AVG(rp_seconds)        AS avg_rp,
    AVG(pn_seconds)        AS avg_pn,
    AVG(ac_seconds)        AS avg_ac,
    AVG(ec_seconds)        AS avg_ec
FROM anomalies
GROUP BY claim_type, region, adjuster_id
HAVING COUNT(*) > 5        -- optional filter for statistically meaningful groups
ORDER BY anomaly_cnt DESC;
```

**c. Claims that close immediately after assignment (possible premature closure).**  

```sql
SELECT
    claim_id,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce2.timestamp)) AS seconds_A_to_C,
    c.claim_type,
    a.adjuster_id,
    a.region
FROM claim_events ce2
JOIN claim_events ce6 ON ce6.claim_id = ce2.claim_id AND ce6.activity = 'C'
JOIN claims c          ON c.claim_id = ce2.claim_id
JOIN adjusters a       ON a.adjuster_id = ce2.resource::int
WHERE ce2.activity = 'A'
  AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce6.timestamp - ce2.timestamp)) <= 7200   -- 2hours
  AND NOT EXISTS (
        SELECT 1 FROM claim_events ce3
        WHERE ce3.claim_id = ce2.claim_id
          AND ce3.activity IN ('E','P','N')
          AND ce3.timestamp BETWEEN ce2.timestamp AND ce6.timestamp
      );
```

**d. Checks for unusually long gaps between Approval and Notification per region or claim type.**  

```sql
SELECT
    region,
    claim_type,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))) AS avg_pn_seconds,
    STDDEV(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))) AS stddev_pn_seconds,
    COUNT(*) AS cnt
FROM claim_events ce4
JOIN claim_events ce5 ON ce5.claim_id = ce4.claim_id AND ce5.activity = 'N'
JOIN claims c           ON c.claim_id = ce4.claim_id
JOIN adjusters a        ON a.adjuster_id = ce4.resource::int
GROUP BY region, claim_type
HAVING AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))) > 604800   -- >7days
   OR STDDEV(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce5.timestamp - ce4.timestamp))) > 172800;
```
