- Picking events are granular per item to capture individual warehouse activities, as timestamps differ and picker_ids vary.
- Dispatch and arrival events are per delivery record to handle split shipments (e.g., order 1002).
- Invoice and payment events are per record, linked via invoice_id to order_id.
- Order 1004 lacks delivery, invoice, and payment data, so its process instance ends after picking; assumed incomplete or non-billable (e.g., internal order).
- Resource for payments is marked as N/A, as no actor is specified in the Payments table.
- Carriers are assigned as resources for dispatch and arrival events, assuming they handle these logistics steps.
- Timestamps are used as provided; all events are chronologically ordered within each case.
- No additional attributes (e.g., amounts, quantities) were included in the table to keep it minimal, but they could be added as extra columns if needed for mining (e.g., invoice_amount for "Issue Invoice" events).
- Resource names are mapped from the Resources table for readability.
