### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization  
#### Calculating Waiting Times:  
- **Waiting Time Definition**: The time between the completion of one activity and the start of the next activity for the same patient (e.g., the time between "Registration COMPLETE" and "Nurse Assessment START").  
- **Calculation Method**:  
  - For each patient’s case log, compute the **inter-activity waiting times** by subtracting the completion timestamp of Activity *A* from the start timestamp of Activity *B*.  
  - Exclude waiting times where the next activity starts immediately after the previous one (e.g., no queue).  

#### Key Metrics:  
- **Core Queue Metrics**:  
  - Average, median, and 90th percentile waiting times between each activity pair.  
  - Maximum waiting time (to identify outliers) and the percentage of cases exceeding a threshold (e.g., >15 minutes).  
  - **Queue Frequency**: How often a queue occurs between two activities (e.g., 80% of patients wait between Registration and Nurse Assessment).  
- **Impact Metrics**:  
  - **Total Visit Duration**: Sum of activity durations + waiting times per visit.  
  - **Critical Path Identification**: Which queues contribute most to total visit duration (e.g., a 30-minute wait before a 10-minute activity could be more impactful than a 5-minute wait before a 1-hour activity).  

#### Prioritizing Critical Queues:  
- **Criteria for Criticality**:  
  1. **High Impact on Visit Duration**: Focus on queues contributing the most to total visit times (e.g., a 20-minute wait before a 5-minute activity).  
  2. **Frequency and Severity**: Queues that occur often (e.g., 90% of patients wait >15 minutes between Nurse Assessment and Doctor Consultation.  
  3. **Patient Type/Urgency**: Prioritize queues affecting urgent patients (e.g., "Urgent" cases waiting longer than "Normal" cases.  
  4. **Resource Constraints**: Queues where the downstream activity is a bottleneck (e.g., a single doctor handling all consultations).

### 2. Root Cause Analysis  
#### Potential Root Causes:  
- **Resource Bottlenecks**:  
  - Example: A single nurse handles all assessments, causing delays if they’re overbooked.  
  - Process Mining Insight: Resource utilization heatmaps showing nurses or doctors at 100% capacity during peak hours.  
- **Activity Dependency**:  
  - Example: Nurse Assessment cannot start until Registration is complete; if Registration has long service times, it cascades into delays.  
  - Process Mining Insight: Concurrency analysis to detect whether dependencies are necessary or avoidable.  
- **Service Time Variability**:  
  - Example: Doctor Consultations vary widely (e.g., 10–45 minutes), causing unpredictable delays.  
  - Process Mining Insight: Distribution of service times and their correlation with waiting times.  
- **Scheduling Policies**:  
  - Example: Appointments are booked at 15-minute intervals, but actual service times average 25 minutes, creating backlogs.  
  - Process Mining Insight: Throughput analysis comparing scheduled vs. actual activity durations.  
- **Patient Type Differences**:  
  - Example: New patients take twice as long for Registration due to paperwork, causing a bottleneck.  
  - Process Mining Insight: Activity duration stratified by Patient Type (New vs. Follow-up).  

#### Process Mining Techniques for Root Cause Identification:  
- **Resource Profiles**: Identify idle times or overutilization of key resources.  
- **Bottleneck Analysis**: Identify activities with consistently long waiting times and their associated resources.  
- **Frequency Profiles**: Highlight activities with high variability in service times (e.g., Doctor Consultations).  
- **Case Duration Analysis**: Compare total visit durations across patient types or urgency levels to identify systemic delays.
