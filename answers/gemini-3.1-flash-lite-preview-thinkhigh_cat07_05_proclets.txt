This image illustrates the **"Proclet" modeling approach**, which is a technique used to model complex, interacting business processes by breaking them into smaller, loosely coupled process components.

The visualization is divided into three parts:

### (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels
This is the primary flowchart showing how two separate processes—"Lab visit" (left) and "Lab test" (right)—interact via two software systems: the **Order system** and the **HIS** (Hospital Information System).
*   **Process Flow:** The "Lab visit" process includes steps like "Order entry," "Take blood sample," "Judge results," and "Send report." The "Lab test" process includes "Perform test," "Make report," and "Finish lab test."
*   **Interaction:** The dashed boxes represent the communication channels. When a step in the "Lab visit" process needs to trigger an action in the "Lab test" process, it sends a message through one of these channels (e.g., ordering a test or receiving a result).
*   **Roles:** The arrows on the left and right sides labeled "doctor," "lab assistant," and "chemical analyst" indicate which human roles are responsible for performing specific tasks within the flow.

### (b) Class Diagram Containing the Two Proclet Classes
This is a high-level structural model showing the relationship between the two processes:
*   It defines a **1..1 to 1..*** relationship, signifying that one "Lab visit" requires one or more "Lab tests." This establishes the cardinality or business rule governing the interaction between the two processes.

### (c) Example of a Performative
This table provides a concrete example of an individual message (a "performative") sent between these processes:
*   It records a specific transaction occurring at **11:00**.
*   It details the **Channel** (Order system), **Sender** (Lab visit - John), **Receiver** (Lab test - HGB John), the **Action** (Create), and the **Content** ("Can you perform a HGB test for John?").
*   This table demonstrates how the abstract flows in part (a) translate into actual data exchanges in a technical system.

### Summary
Overall, the visualization explains how to decompose a large, monolithic medical laboratory process into smaller, manageable "proclets" that communicate through defined software channels, maintain specific business relationships, and exchange actionable messages.
