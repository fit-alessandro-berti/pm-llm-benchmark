# Conformance Analysis: Loan Application Process

## 1. Normative Rule Violations (Hard Constraints)

###  Case 1 — FULLY COMPLIANT
The sequence follows all normative rules perfectly:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- Credit Check precedes Risk Assessment 
- Approved  Finalize Documentation before Close Case 

**No normative violations.**

---

###  Case 2 — MULTIPLE NORMATIVE VIOLATIONS
This case contains **two serious violations**:

**Violation A — Ordering Error (Credit Check / Risk Assessment reversed):**
- Risk Assessment occurred at **08:20**, but Credit Check happened at **09:00**.
- This breaks the mandatory rule: *Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment.*
- The risk evaluation was performed without the prerequisite credit information, undermining the integrity of the assessment.

**Violation B — Contradictory Action (Finalize Documentation after Rejection):**
- Loan Decision was **Rejected** (09:30), yet **Finalize Documentation** was performed at 10:00.
- This directly violates: *If the loan is rejected, do not finalize documentation.*

**Additional Note — Missing Activity:**
- "Inform Applicant" appears to be **absent** from the log. If truly skipped, this is an additional violation of the mandatory sequence (*Inform Applicant must occur after Loan Decision*).

**Verdict:** Case 2 is **non-conformant** and represents the most serious deviation in the log.

---

###  Case 3 — NORMATIVELY COMPLIANT
Despite timing issues (see Part 2), the **order and logic are correct**:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- Credit Check before Risk Assessment 
- Approved  Finalize Documentation before Close Case 

**No normative (hard-rule) violations.** All deviations are desiderata-level only.

## 3. Interpretations & Justifications

### Case 2 (Hard Violations — Requires Corrective Action)
- **Reversed order:** This may indicate a **logging error**, a **parallel-processing system** that timestamped activities out of true sequence, or genuine procedural error. *Recommendation:* Investigate whether the credit data actually fed into the risk assessment, or whether the timestamps were mislabeled.
- **Finalize Documentation after rejection:** Possible **misclassification** — perhaps documentation here means "archiving rejection paperwork" rather than "loan finalization." If so, the activity label should be corrected (e.g., "Archive Rejection File"). If it genuinely finalized loan documents, this is a **compliance/control failure** needing escalation.
- **Recommendation:** Case 2 should be flagged for **manual review and remediation.**

### Case 3 (Soft Deviations — Acceptable with Context)
- The multi-day delays (Jan 12  Jan 14  Jan 16) suggest the application spanned a **weekend or holiday period**, or required additional manual verification.
- These delays harmed **customer experience and SLA targets** but not process **validity**.
- The missing Follow-up Call is a minor courtesy gap.
- **Recommendation:** No corrective action required; consider operational improvements (e.g., staffing, automation) to meet timing targets and reinstate follow-up calls.

### Case 1 (Best Practice Benchmark)
- Aside from the optional Follow-up Call, Case 1 is the **model example** of conformant, timely processing and can serve as the reference standard.
