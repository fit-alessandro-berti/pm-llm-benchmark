This Log Skeleton represents the typical flow and constraints of an order fulfillment process.

### Analysis and Constraint Mapping

**1. Activity Occurrences (`activ_freq`):**
*   **Receive Order (RO):** Happens exactly once per case (order).
*   **Check Availability (CA):** Must happen at least once (if the order is processed).
*   **Reserve Items (RI):** Must happen once per order.
*   **Pick Items (PI):** Must happen once per order.
*   **Quality Check (QC):** Typically happens once per order.
*   **Pack Order (PO):** Must happen once per order.
*   **Generate Shipping Label (GSL):** Must happen once per order.
*   **Dispatch (D):** Must happen at least once (if the order is shipped).
*   **Invoice Customer (IC):** Happens once per order.
*   **Record Payment (RP):** Happens once per order (assuming successful payment processing).

**2. Sequential Flow (Always Before/Always After/Directly Follows):**
*   RO $\rightarrow$ CA $\rightarrow$ RI $\rightarrow$ PI $\rightarrow$ QC $\rightarrow$ PO $\rightarrow$ GSL $\rightarrow$ D.
*   Payment and Billing (IC, RP) usually happen after dispatch or concurrently with billing cycles. We will place them after Dispatch (D).

**3. Specific Constraints:**
*   **Equivalence:** None of the activities are stated to be equivalent in terms of occurrence count or necessity (e.g., if A happens, B must also happen exactly once).
*   **Always Before:** CA must happen after RO. RI must happen after CA. ... D must happen after GSL.
*   **Always After:** This is generally covered by the flow. If PO happens, GSL must happen after it.
*   **Never Together:** Usually, activities that are mutually exclusive (e.g., if the order is cancelled, PI does not occur). In this general model, we assume all necessary activities can coexist in a successful case.
