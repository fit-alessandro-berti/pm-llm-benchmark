### 1) Distinct phases detected in the numeric signals

Looking across time, the log naturally breaks into segments where multiple sensors change together (temperature/energy/vibration/flow/tool position). Those coordinated shifts are typical “activity boundaries” in process mining.

I see **6 phases**:

1. **Idle / standby (baseline)**: low temp (~20–21°C), no vibration, no flow, tool at 0, low energy.
2. **Cutting / machining**: sudden rise in vibration (30–38 Hz), increased material flow (5 units/s), tool position advancing (510 mm), temperature rising (5058°C), energy rising (2.03.0 kWh).
3. **Assembly / placement / clamping**: moderate, stable conditions (temp ~40°C, pressure 2 bar, low vibration 10 Hz, flow 2 units/s, tool position held at 15 mm, steady energy 2.0).
4. **Welding / heat-join operation**: very high temperature (80°C) and highest vibration (50 Hz), **no material flow**, high energy (5.0 kWh) while tool position stays fixed (15 mm).
5. **Cooldown / transition**: drop back in temp (30°C), near-zero vibration (1 Hz), no flow, energy reduced (1.0).
6. **Quality inspection / light handling / final feed**: low temperature (25°C), low but present vibration (5 Hz), small flow (1 unit/s), tool position changes to 20 mm, moderate energy (1.5–1.6).

Then the system returns to **idle/standby** again.

### 3) Rationale for each label (why these sensor combinations imply the activity)

- **Idle / Standby (Events 1–3, 13–15)**  
  All “work” indicators are off: **zero vibration**, **zero flow**, **tool at home position (0 mm)**, and **minimal energy**. This is a classic machine-wait state before/after a cycle.

- **Cutting Metal / Machining (Events 4–6)**  
  Cutting typically produces:
  - **Higher vibration** (tool-work contact),
  - **Increasing temperature** (friction),
  - **Material flow** present (coolant/chip evacuation or feed),
  - **Tool position moving** (engaging deeper/advancing),
  - **Higher energy draw**.
  The combination of rising temp + rising vibration + high flow + advancing tool strongly suggests machining/cutting.

- **Assembling Parts / Positioning & Clamping (Events 7–8)**  
  Assembly/positioning often looks like:
  - **Pressure increases** (pneumatic clamps/actuators),
  - **Vibration decreases** vs cutting (less aggressive contact),
  - **Tool position held constant** (part held/aligned),
  - **Some material flow** may remain (feed/handling), but lower than cutting.
  The stable plateau (two identical readings) also suggests a “hold while something is placed/secured” step.

- **Welding / Joining (Event 9)**  
  Welding is characterized by:
  - **High temperature spike**,
  - **High energy consumption**,
  - Often **little/no material flow** during the actual weld,
  - Tool stays fixed while the weld is applied.
  Event 9 is a clear outlier peak in temp (80) and energy (5.0), matching a concentrated heat/energy operation like welding.

- **Cooldown / Transition (Event 10)**  
  Immediately after welding: temperature and vibration collapse, flow is still zero, and energy drops—this looks like a short **post-operation stabilization** (cooldown, retract/transition, controller step).

- **Quality Inspection / Final Handling (Events 11–12)**  
  Inspection/verification or light finishing often shows:
  - **Low temperature** (no heavy heat process),
  - **Small vibration** (probing, scanning, minor actuator movement),
  - **Small material flow** (e.g., conveying to a sensor station),
  - **Moderate energy** (sensors/actuators active but not heavy load),
  - **Tool position moves to a new setpoint** (20 mm) and holds.
  The stable repetition at 11–12 suggests a brief “measure/confirm” dwell.
