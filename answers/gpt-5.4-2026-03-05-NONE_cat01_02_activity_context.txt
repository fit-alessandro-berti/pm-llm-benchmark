## 1) Repeated activities and how their meanings differ

The activities that recur within the same case are:

- **Verify Documents**
- **Call Customer**

Their meanings change by sequence position and surrounding events.

### Verify Documents
- When it appears first, it is the **initial document/identity check**.
- When it appears later in the same case, it is a **re-verification** after new information, documents, or corrections were provided.

### Call Customer
- In **C001**, the call is made to **request missing documents**.
- In **C002**, the first call is to **clarify an account discrepancy**, while the second is a **follow-up confirmation call**.
- In **C003**, the call is a **return-to-branch / resume-processing call** because the customer stepped out.

## 3) Brief justification for naming choices

### C001
- **Initial Document Verification**: first occurrence of document checking.
- **Call Customer for Missing Documents**: context explicitly says the purpose is to obtain missing docs.
- **Document Re-Verification**: happens after **Submit Additional**, so this is clearly a second-pass verification.

### C002
- **Initial Document Verification**: first verification step in the case.
- **Call Customer to Clarify Discrepancy**: context says the call is about an account discrepancy.
- **Follow-Up Customer Confirmation Call**: occurs after **Adjust Account** and is explicitly to confirm changes.

### C003
- **Initiate Request at Branch**: same base activity as initiation, but branch context matters here because this case is in-person rather than via portal.
- **Initial In-Person Document Verification**: differs from other verification events because it is physical, branch-based verification.
- **Call Customer to Return to Branch**: purpose is not clarification or document collection, but bringing the customer back to continue processing.
- **Post-Adjustment Document Re-Verification**: this second verification occurs after **Adjust Account**, so the name reflects both sequence and trigger.

## 4) Observations on the disambiguation strategy

The renaming is based on:
- **Order in the case**: first occurrence vs later occurrence.
- **Preceding event**: e.g. after submitting extra documents or after account adjustment.
- **Stated purpose in context**: missing docs, discrepancy clarification, confirmation, return to branch.
- **Channel/resource context**: web portal vs branch, back office vs branch officer.
