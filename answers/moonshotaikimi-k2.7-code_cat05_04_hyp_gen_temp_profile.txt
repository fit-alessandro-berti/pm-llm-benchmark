## 1. Identified Anomalies

| Transition | Avg. Time | Std. Dev. | Concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R  P** (Receive  Approve) | ~25 h | 1 h | Very tight distribution around a fixed elapsed time, suggesting an artificial or batched schedule rather than organic workflow timing. |
| **P  N** (Approve  Notify) | 7 days | 2 days | Unusually long and highly variable wait before the customer is notified, indicating a likely bottleneck or batching point. |
| **A  C** (Assign  Close) | 2 h | 1 h | Closure can occur very quickly after assignment, potentially bypassing Evaluate and Approve steps. |

| **E  C** (Evaluate  Close) | 1 h | ~50 min | High relative variability around a short close time, suggesting inconsistent handling practices. |

## 2. Hypotheses for the Anomalies

- **Rigid approval scheduling (R  P):** Claims may be approved by an overnight batch job or a scheduled process that always runs close to the same clock time, producing a low-variance 25-hour pattern.
- **Notification backlog or batching (P  N):** The 7-day average with wide spread may reflect a weekly notification batch, periodic mail/e-mail runs, or an understaffed notification team.
- **Premature or bypassed closures (A  C):** Claims closed shortly after assignment may be duplicate claims, customer withdrawals, fraud cases, or incorrectly processed claims that skip evaluation and approval.
- **Automated notification without manual review (E  N):** The 5-minute E  N interval may mean the system auto-generates a notification immediately upon evaluation, possibly before a proper approval gate.
- **Inconsistent workload or ad-hoc intervention (E  C):** Large relative variation around the close time suggests some adjusters close claims immediately while others wait, possibly due to unclear policies or special-case handling.
- **Data-quality issues:** Clock skew, timezone mismatches, or event timestamps recorded by batch ETL jobs rather than actual user actions could also compress or inflate apparent durations.

### A. Generic anomaly detector

Identify every claim whose transition time falls outside a chosen sigma band.

```sql
WITH profile AS (
    SELECT * FROM (VALUES
        ('R','A',3600,600),
        ('R','E',86400,28800),
        ('R','P',90000,3600),
        ('A','C',7200,3600),
        ('E','N',300,60),
        ('E','C',3600,3000),
        ('P','N',604800,172800),
        ('N','C',1800,300)
    ) AS t(from_act, to_act, avg_sec, stdev_sec)
),
transitions AS (
    SELECT
        e1.claim_id,
        e1.activity AS from_act,
        e2.activity AS to_act,
        e1.timestamp AS t_from,
        e2.timestamp AS t_to,
        e1.resource AS resource_from,
        e2.resource AS resource_to,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (e2.timestamp - e1.timestamp)) AS duration_sec
    FROM claim_events e1
    JOIN claim_events e2
        ON e1.claim_id = e2.claim_id
        AND e1.activity <> e2.activity
        AND e2.timestamp > e1.timestamp
)
SELECT
    t.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    t.from_act || ' -> ' || t.to_act AS transition,
    t.t_from,
    t.t_to,
    t.duration_sec,
    p.avg_sec,
    p.stdev_sec,
    (t.duration_sec - p.avg_sec) / NULLIF(p.stdev_sec, 0) AS z_score,
    t.resource_from,
    t.resource_to
FROM transitions t
JOIN profile p
    ON t.from_act = p.from_act
    AND t.to_act = p.to_act
JOIN claims c ON c.claim_id = t.claim_id
WHERE ABS(t.duration_sec - p.avg_sec) > 3 * p.stdev_sec
ORDER BY ABS((t.duration_sec - p.avg_sec) / NULLIF(p.stdev_sec, 0)) DESC;
```

### D. Correlate anomalies with claim type, adjuster region, and specialization

```sql
WITH profile AS (
    SELECT * FROM (VALUES
        ('R','A',3600,600),
        ('R','E',86400,28800),
        ('R','P',90000,3600),
        ('A','C',7200,3600),
        ('E','N',300,60),
        ('E','C',3600,3000),
        ('P','N',604800,172800),
        ('N','C',1800,300)
    ) AS t(from_act, to_act, avg_sec, stdev_sec)
),
transitions AS (
    SELECT
        e1.claim_id,
        e1.activity AS from_act,
        e2.activity AS to_act,
        e1.resource AS resource_from,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (e2.timestamp - e1.timestamp)) AS duration_sec
    FROM claim_events e1
    JOIN claim_events e2
        ON e1.claim_id = e2.claim_id
        AND e1.activity <> e2.activity
        AND e2.timestamp > e1.timestamp

anomalies AS (
    SELECT t.*, p.avg_sec, p.stdev_sec
    FROM transitions t
    JOIN profile p
        ON t.from_act = p.from_act
        AND t.to_act = p.to_act

    c.claim_type,
    COALESCE(a.region, 'Unknown') AS region,
    COALESCE(a.specialization, 'Unknown') AS specialization,
    an.from_act || ' -> ' || an.to_act AS transition,

    ROUND(AVG(an.duration_sec), 2) AS avg_actual_sec,

    ON a.adjuster_id = an.resource_from::int
```
