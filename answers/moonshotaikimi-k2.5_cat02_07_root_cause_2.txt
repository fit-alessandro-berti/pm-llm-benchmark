## 3. Explanations & Mitigation Strategies

### **Root Cause 1: Iterative Document Request Loops**
**Why:** High-complexity claims trigger multiple "Request Additional Documents" activities, creating stop-and-start cycles. Each iteration includes:
- Customer notification delay
- Customer gathering time (often 24+ hours)
- Re-evaluation queue time
- Potential misalignment between what was requested and what was provided

**Mitigation:**
- **Front-loading protocol**: For high-complexity claims, mandate a comprehensive document checklist at first evaluation (single contact strategy) rather than incremental requests
- **Digital document portals**: Implement real-time document upload with automated validation to eliminate email/postal delays
- **SLA enforcement**: Set 24-hour customer response expectations with automated reminders; auto-escalate if documents aren't received within 48 hours

### **Root Cause 2: Resource Skill/Workload Variation**
**Why:** Adjuster_Lisa's cases consistently take longer, suggesting either skill gaps in initial evaluation (leading to more document iterations) or excessive workload causing queue delays.

**Mitigation:**
- **Peer review program**: Have Manager_Ann (associated with faster approvals) mentor Adjuster_Lisa on efficient evaluation techniques
- **Workload balancing**: Redistribute high-complexity claims between adjusters; consider pairing Adjuster_Lisa with a documentation specialist to reduce iterations
- **First-Time-Right metrics**: Implement KPIs tracking document request accuracy (ratio of requests to approvals) to identify training needs

### **Root Cause 3: Regional Process Friction**
**Why:** Region B shows particular strain with complex cases, possibly due to Manager_Bill's approval queue or Finance_Carl's payment processing schedule.

**Mitigation:**
- **Regional capacity audit**: Investigate if Region B is understaffed relative to claim volume

- **Payment batching optimization**: Finance_Carl appears to process payments in batches (notice the 4-hour delay in Case 2005 vs. 15-45 minutes in other cases). Implement same-day payment processing for claims approved before 2 PM

### **Root Cause 4: Cross-Handoff Delays**
**Why:** Overnight gaps appear between "Request Documents  Approve" and "Approve  Pay," suggesting the process pauses outside business hours.

**Mitigation:**
- **Case ownership model**: Assign dedicated case managers who track claims across shifts rather than functional handoffs

- **Process automation**: Auto-approve claims meeting specific criteria (e.g., document completeness score >90%) to bypass manual queues
