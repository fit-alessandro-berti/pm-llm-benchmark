### Overall flow
The process starts with **“Ship goods”** and ends with **“Shipment prepared.”**

### Main structure
After the start, the workflow splits into **two parallel branches**:

1. **Administrative/shipping preparation branch**

---

## 1) Administrative branch
In the **Secretary** lane, the process goes to **“Clarify shipment method.”**

Then there is a decision: **“Special handling?”**

- **If yes:**  
  - **Get 3 offers from logistic companies**
  - **Select logistic company and place order**

- **If no:**  
  - **Check if insurance is required**
    - This step references **customer information** and nearby **order details**
  - Then the flow splits again:
    - **Always**: **Write package label**
    - **If insurance is necessary**: in the **Logistics** lane, **Insure parcel**
  - These two paths rejoin afterward

The normal path and the special-handling path then merge into one branch.

---

## 2) Warehouse branch
In parallel, the **Warehouse** lane performs **“Package goods.”**

There is also a **48-hour timer** attached to this task:
- if packaging is delayed that long, the process triggers **“Notify customer about delay.”**

Those warehouse-related paths then rejoin.

---

## Final steps
Once both major branches are complete, they synchronize and continue to:

- **Fill out Shipment paperwork**
- **Move package to pickup area**

Then the process ends at **“Shipment prepared.”**

### In plain English
The diagram shows how a shop prepares an order for shipment by:
- deciding how it should be shipped,
- arranging insurance or outside logistics if needed,
- packaging the goods,
- notifying the customer if packaging is delayed,
- and finally completing paperwork and moving the package for pickup.
