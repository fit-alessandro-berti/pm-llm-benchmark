**Analysis of the Event Log**

### 1. Normative Rule Violations (Strict/Non-Negotiable)

**Case 2 is the only case that violates the normative rules.**

**Specific violations in Case 2:**
- **Order violation**: Risk Assessment was performed at 08:20, *before* the Credit Check (09:00). This directly breaches the mandatory sequence "Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment."
- **Missing mandatory activity**: The process completely skipped the "Inform Applicant" step after the Loan Decision. The normative model requires that the applicant must be informed after every decision.
- **Prohibited action after rejection**: After a "Rejected" decision, the process performed "Finalize Documentation" (10:00). This is explicitly forbidden. For rejected applications, the case should proceed directly to "Close Case" without finalization.

**Cases 1 and 3** fully comply with all normative constraints:
- They follow the correct sequence.
- Credit Check precedes Risk Assessment, which precedes Loan Decision, which precedes Inform Applicant.
- Case 1 and Case 3 were both approved, and both correctly performed Finalize Documentation before Close Case.
- Case 2 was rejected and should not have finalized documentation — it did so anyway.

### 2. Desiderata Violations (Recommended, Not Strict)

**Only Case 3 fails the desiderata** (these are *not* serious violations):

- Credit Check occurred ~49 hours after Application Received (well beyond the recommended 24-hour window).
- Inform Applicant occurred ~51 hours after the Loan Decision (exceeding the recommended 48-hour window).
- No Follow-up Call was performed before closing the case.

These deviations represent **suboptimal but non-critical behavior**. They do not break the required process integrity, but they fall short of the recommended best practices for timeliness and customer communication.

**Case 1** fully satisfies the desiderata (Credit Check within 30 minutes, Inform Applicant within 2 hours, and while a Follow-up Call is not explicitly logged, the overall execution is efficient).

**Case 2** had a prompt Credit Check (~1 hour after application), but its normative violations are so severe that desiderata compliance is largely irrelevant.

### 3. Suggested Interpretations/Justifications

- **Case 3 (Delays)**: The delayed Credit Check (spanning a weekend) could plausibly result from waiting for external credit bureau responses or internal workload bottlenecks. The late notification might reflect additional internal approval layers or resource constraints. While not ideal, these are understandable operational frictions rather than intentional process defiance. The missing Follow-up Call may indicate the team judged it unnecessary given the straightforward approval.

- **Case 2 (Serious deviations)**: The most likely explanation is **human error or inadequate process controls**. A processor may have mistakenly started the Risk Assessment based on incomplete information or bypassed the system-enforced order. The performance of Finalize Documentation after rejection strongly suggests either:
  - A misunderstanding of the decision outcome, or
  - Lack of system-level guardrails preventing prohibited actions.

  The complete skipping of "Inform Applicant" further supports the hypothesis of weak process enforcement or training gaps.

**Summary**:
- **Normatively compliant**: Cases 1 and 3
- **Normatively non-compliant**: Case 2 (multiple severe violations)
- **Desiderata compliant**: Case 1
- **Desiderata non-compliant (minor)**: Case 3
