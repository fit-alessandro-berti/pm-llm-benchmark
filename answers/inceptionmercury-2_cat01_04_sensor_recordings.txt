## 3.  How the inference was made – a short “processmining” rationale  

1. **Similarity clustering** – Events were grouped by looking for contiguous blocks where the multivariate sensor vector (T,P,V,Flow,Tool,Energy) stayed within a narrow band.  
2. **Changepoint detection** – Sharp jumps in temperature, vibration, and flow (e.g., 20°C  50°C at event4) were treated as transition points between phases.  
3. **Domain knowledge mapping** –  
   * High temperature+high vibration+material flowcutting.  
   * Moderate temperature+low vibration+stillsome flowassembly/positioning.  
   * Very high temperature+high vibration+zero flowwelding (heat source, no feed).  
   * Low temperature+tiny vibration+nearzero flowinspection or idle.  
4. **Energy corroboration** – Energy consumption mirrors the intensity of the operation (cutting 23kWh, welding 5kWh, idle 0.40.5kWh).  
5. **Toolposition trend** – The tool moves from 0mm  10mm during cutting, then to 15mm for assembly, stays at 15mm for welding, and finally moves to 20mm during inspection – a logical progression of a workpiece through the line.
