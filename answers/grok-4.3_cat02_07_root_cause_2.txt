**Analysis of Insurance Claims Processing Performance Issues**

### 1. Case Durations (Lead Time Analysis)

Here are the calculated durations from **Submit Claim** to **Close Claim**:

| Case ID | Complexity | Region | Duration          | Status          |
|---------|------------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 2001    | Low        | A      | 1h 30m            | Normal          |
| 2004    | Low        | B      | 1h 25m            | Normal          |
| 2002    | Medium     | B      | ~26 hours         | Delayed         |
| 2003    | High       | A      | ~48 hours         | Significantly Delayed |
| 2005    | High       | B      | ~77 hours         | **Critical**    |

**Key Finding**: Cases **2003** and **2005** (both High complexity) are the clear performance outliers. Case 2005 is particularly problematic, taking over 3 days.

### 2. Root Cause Analysis

#### Primary Root Cause: **Claim Complexity + Multiple Document Requests**

The strongest correlation is between **Complexity** and the number of "Request Additional Documents" activities:

- **Low Complexity** (2001, 2004): No document requests  Completed in ~1.5 hours.
- **Medium Complexity** (2002): 1 document request  ~26 hours.
- **High Complexity** (2003, 2005): 2–3 document requests  48–77 hours.

**Observation**: High-complexity claims trigger repeated document requests, creating significant waiting time between events. This is the dominant driver of long lead times.

#### Secondary Patterns by Attributes

| Attribute     | Observation                                                                 | Impact |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| **Complexity** | Strongest predictor. High complexity = multiple document iterations        | **High** |
| **Region**     | Region B high-complexity cases perform worse than Region A (2005 vs 2003)  | Medium |
| **Resource**   | Adjuster_Lisa (Region B) handles all document requests in 2002 & 2005      | Medium |
| **Resource**   | Manager_Bill appears only in high-complexity cases (2003 & 2005)           | Low    |

**Resource Analysis**:
- **Adjuster_Lisa** (Region B) is involved in both delayed high-complexity cases and appears to trigger multiple document requests.
- **Adjuster_Mike** (Region A) also triggered two requests in Case 2003, but the overall case completed faster than 2005.
- This suggests that **Adjuster_Lisa** may be less efficient at gathering complete information upfront or has higher workload.

#### Region Analysis:
- Region B shows worse performance on high-complexity claims (2005: 77h vs 2003: 48h in Region A).
- Low and medium complexity cases perform similarly across regions.

### 3. Root Cause Summary

| Rank | Root Cause | Evidence | Strength |
|------|------------|----------|----------|
| 1    | High complexity claims require multiple rounds of additional documentation | 2003 & 2005 have 2–3 document requests | **Very High** |
| 2    | Inefficient information collection by Adjuster_Lisa (Region B) | Multiple document requests in Cases 2002 & 2005 | High |
| 3    | Possible capacity or process issues in Region B for complex claims | 2005 significantly worse than 2003 | Medium |

### 4. Recommendations to Mitigate Performance Issues

**Immediate Actions:**

1. **Improve Upfront Data Collection**
   - Create a **standardized checklist** for high-complexity claims.
   - Implement a pre-submission validation step to reduce the need for follow-up document requests.

2. **Resource Optimization**
   - Assign more experienced adjusters (or create a "High Complexity" specialist team) to handle High complexity cases.
   - Investigate Adjuster_Lisa’s workload and performance on complex claims.

3. **Process Redesign**
   - Introduce **parallel processing** where possible (e.g., start gathering certain documents while evaluation is ongoing).
   - Set **SLA timers** on document requests to prevent cases from stalling for days.

4. **Long-term Improvements**
   - Develop a **complexity scoring model** at submission to automatically route high-complexity claims to senior resources.
   - Analyze why Region B underperforms on high-complexity cases (workload, training, or system access issues).

**Summary**: The performance issues are primarily driven by **high complexity** claims requiring repeated document requests. While Region B (particularly Adjuster_Lisa) appears to exacerbate the problem, the root issue is the **lack of complete information at the start** of complex claims.
