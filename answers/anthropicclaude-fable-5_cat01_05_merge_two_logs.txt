## Step 3: Reasoning & Conflict Resolution

**Naming variations.** I normalized names by removing spaces/casing and comparing stems: `Order Received  OrderReceived` (identical), `Order Validated  OrderValidation` (same root), `Item Shipped  Shipping` (clear semantic equivalent). For the unified event label I kept Log A's naming convention for consistency, since A is the primary system.

**Timestamp tolerance.** With a 2-second tolerance:
- Events 1, 2, and 5 fall at or within the tolerance (t = 2s, 1s, 2s)  confidently merged.
- **Event 3 (Payment) is the exception**: t = 5s exceeds the strict tolerance. However, I merged it anyway for three reasons: (a) it is the *only* payment-related event in either log for this order, so there is no risk of mismatching; (b) Log B's note explicitly says *"Payment gateway delay,"* which directly explains the larger offset; (c) the events are semantically aligned and sandwiched between two confidently matched pairs in both logs (validation before, shipping after), preserving sequence consistency. I flagged it with **Medium** confidence rather than High to make this judgment call auditable. (A stricter pipeline could keep these as two records — I documented the choice so it can be reversed.)

**Timestamp selection.** Both timestamps are retained rather than picking one, because the systems measure different things (A = receipt time, B  occurrence time). For chronological ordering I used Log A's timestamp where available, and Log B's timestamp for the B-only event — note that Quality Check (10:03:00 in B) slots cleanly between Payment (10:02) and Shipping (10:05) on both timelines, so ordering is unambiguous.

**Unmatched events.**
- **Quality Check** exists only in Log B — System A likely doesn't track internal inspections. Included as-is with origin `B only`; no A timestamp fabricated.
- **Item Delivered** exists only in Log A — System B's recording apparently ends at courier handoff (consistent with its "warehouse system" note). Included with origin `A only`; user/resource attributes left null rather than imputed.

**Attribute integration.** Merged records carry the union of attributes: A contributes the primary timestamp and canonical event type; B contributes `user_id`, `resource_id`, and `notes`. No attribute conflicts occurred (order_id matched in all cases), so no value-level conflict resolution was needed beyond timestamps.
