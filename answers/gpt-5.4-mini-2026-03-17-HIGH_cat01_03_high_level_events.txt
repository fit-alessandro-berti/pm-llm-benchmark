Below is a sensible aggregation of the low-level events into higher-level manufacturing stages.  
The same grouping applies to both cases **A1** and **B2**, since their sequences follow the same pattern.

## Proposed high-level process steps

| Step | High-level activity name | Low-level events included | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Raw Material Preparation & Workpiece Setup** | Retrieve raw metal sheet  Scan barcode  Place metal sheet on table  Align metal sheet  Preheat metal sheet | These events are tightly connected, happen at the beginning of the case, and prepare the part for production. They form a single setup phase before any value-adding work begins. The resources also reflect setup roles: Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1. |
| 2 | **Welding / Assembly** | Pick up welding tool  Weld corner A  Weld corner B | These are the core joining operations. The tool pickup is immediately followed by the actual welding actions, performed by the same operator, so they naturally belong to one assembly stage. |
| 3 | **In-Process Weld Inspection** | Measure weld integrity | This is a clear verification step directly after welding, using a quality sensor. It checks the result of the preceding assembly work before moving on to finishing, so it is best treated as a separate inspection sub-stage. |
| 4 | **Surface Finishing & Curing** | Apply protective coating  Dry coating | These events belong together as a finishing phase: coating is applied, then cured/dried using heat. They are temporally close and share the same post-processing purpose. |
| 5 | **Final Visual Inspection** | Visual check | This is the last quality gate in the sequence. It is performed after finishing and confirms the completed product meets visual standards. |

## Why this grouping works

The grouping is based on three main signals:

- **Temporal proximity:** the events occur back-to-back with no meaningful interruption.
- **Resource specialization:** different tools/resources indicate a phase change, e.g., Operator A for setup, Operator B for welding, Quality Sensor for inspection, Coating Unit for finishing.
- **Process logic:** each group represents a coherent manufacturing intention:
  - prepare the material,
  - join parts,
  - verify weld quality,
  - finish/protect the product,
  - perform final inspection.
