### **Analysis of Recurring Activities and Contextual Differences**

The event log contains three activities that appear multiple times within the same case:
1. **Verify Documents**
2. **Call Customer**
3. **Adjust Account**

Each of these activities has different meanings depending on **when they occur** and **what additional context is provided**. Below is a breakdown of their contextual variations:

---

### **1. "Verify Documents"**
- **First Occurrence (Initial Verification):**
  - Happens right after the request is initiated.
  - Focuses on **checking identity documents** for the first time.
  - Example:
    - *C001*: "Agent checks identity documents."
    - *C002*: "Identity verification."
    - *C003*: "Physical verification of ID."

- **Second Occurrence (Re-verification):**
  - Happens **after** some corrective action (e.g., customer submits missing docs, account adjustments).
  - Focuses on **re-checking documents** after updates.
  - Example:
    - *C001*: "Agent re-checks documents now that ID scan is provided."
    - *C003*: "Re-verify docs after account adjustment."

**Renamed Activities:**
- **Initial Document Verification** (first occurrence)
- **Re-Verify Documents** (second occurrence)

**Justification:**
- The first verification is a **standard check** of submitted documents.
- The second verification is a **follow-up check** after corrections or submissions, ensuring completeness.

---

### **2. "Call Customer"**
- **First Occurrence (Outbound Call for Clarification):**
  - Happens when the agent needs **more information** from the customer.
  - Example:
    - *C001*: "Verification incomplete, agent calls to request missing docs."
    - *C002*: "Outbound call to clarify account discrepancy."
    - *C003*: "Customer stepped out. Officer calls to have them return."

- **Second Occurrence (Follow-up Call):**
  - Happens **after an internal adjustment** to confirm changes with the customer.
  - Example:
    - *C002*: "Follow-up call to confirm adjustments made."

**Renamed Activities:**
- **Request Missing Information** (first occurrence)
- **Confirm Adjustments** (second occurrence)

**Justification:**
- The first call is **proactive** (agent reaches out for missing info).
- The second call is **reactive** (agent confirms changes after internal work).

---

### **3. "Adjust Account"**
- **Only appears in C002 and C003**, but in different contexts:
  - **C002**: Happens **after a clarification call** to fix an account issue.
  - **C003**: Happens **after a customer was called back** due to missing info.

**Renamed Activity:**
- **Correct Account Details** (generalized, since the exact adjustment type isn㦙 specified)

**Justification:**
- The activity is **internal** (no customer interaction), so the name should reflect **systemic corrections** rather than a specific action.

### **Summary of Naming Choices**
| Original Activity | Renamed Activity | Reasoning |
|-------------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Verify Documents** | **Initial Document Verification** / **Re-Verify Documents** | Distinguishes between first-time and follow-up checks. |
| **Call Customer** | **Request Missing Information** / **Confirm Adjustments** | Differentiates between proactive (missing info) and reactive (confirmation) calls. |
| **Adjust Account** | **Correct Account Details** | Generalizes the internal correction step without assuming specifics. |

This renaming ensures that **each activity name is unique in meaning** while maintaining clarity in the process flow. The new names reflect **when and why** an activity occurs, making the log easier to analyze for process improvements.
