#### C. Cross-Day / Off-Hours Idle Time
The largest contributor to calendar time is work stopping overnight. All delayed cases span multiple days with long idle periods:

- **Case 102:** Investigate at 14:00 on Mar 1  Resolve at 09:00 on Mar 2 (**19-hour gap**).

### 3. Impact on Cycle Time and Recommendations

#### How These Factors Increase Cycle Time
1. **Escalations multiply queue time:** Each handoff (L1  L2) introduces a new waiting period for the specialized team. In Case 105, the escalation added a **28-hour delay** before Level-2 even began work.
2. **Pre-investigation queues waste productive hours:** In Case 104, the 3.5-hour lag after assignment consumed nearly an entire business morning, pushing the investigation into the afternoon and guaranteeing an overnight wait.
3. **Overnight gaps dominate calendar time:** A ticket that takes only 2–3 hours of actual work can consume 24+ hours of calendar time simply because it missed an arbitrary end-of-day cutoff. These idle periods are **non-value-added time** and are the primary reason Cases 102 and 104 exceed 24 hours.

#### Recommendations

| Issue | Recommended Action |
