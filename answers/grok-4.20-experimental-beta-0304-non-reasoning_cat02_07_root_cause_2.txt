**Analysis of the Insurance Claims Process Event Log**

### 1. Case Durations and Identification of Performance Issues

I calculated the total duration for each case (from "Submit Claim" to "Close Claim"):

* **Case 2001**: 1.5 hours (Low complexity, Region A)  **Fast**
* **Case 2004**: 1 hour 25 min (Low complexity, Region B)  **Fast**
* **Case 2002**: ~1 day 1 hour 55 min (Medium complexity, Region B)  **Slow**
* **Case 2003**: ~2 days 30 min (High complexity, Region A)  **Very Slow**
* **Case 2005**: ~3 days 5 hours (High complexity, Region B)  **Extremely Slow**

**Cases with clear performance issues**: **2002, 2003, and especially 2005**.

### 2. Root Cause Analysis û Correlation with Attributes

#### a) Complexity Level û Strongest Correlation
There is a very clear pattern:
* **Low complexity** (2001, 2004): No "Request Additional Documents" activity. Average duration  1.25 hours.
* **Medium complexity** (2002): 1 request for documents. Duration > 1 day.
* **High complexity** (2003, 2005): Multiple requests for documents (2û3 times). Durations of 2û3+ days.

**Conclusion**: High-complexity claims are the primary driver of long durations. They trigger repeated "Request Additional Documents" loops, which introduce significant waiting time (often overnight or over multiple days).

#### b) Region û Secondary Effect
* **Region B** appears slightly slower than Region A for similar complexity levels:
  * High complexity: Region B (2005) = 3+ days vs Region A (2003) = 2 days.
  * Low complexity: Region B (2004) is actually slightly faster than Region A (2001), but sample size is small.

The difference is modest compared to the complexity effect.

#### c) Resource Analysis
* **Adjusters**: Adjuster_Lisa (mostly Region B) handles both medium and high complexity cases and is involved in all document requests. She appears in the two slowest cases (2002 and 2005).
* **Managers**: Manager_Bill only approves high-complexity claims (2003, 2005) and these approvals happen later (sometimes the next day), whereas Manager_Ann handles low/medium claims very quickly.
* **Finance**: No clear bottleneck ù payment usually follows approval quickly.

**Main bottleneck resource**: The combination of **Adjuster_Lisa + High Complexity** leads to multiple document requests with long delays between them.

### 3. Root Causes and Explanations

**Primary Root Cause: Claim Complexity**
- High-complexity claims require multiple iterations of "Request Additional Documents."
- Each request introduces significant waiting time (likely because the customer must respond, and the case is not actively worked on until documents arrive).
- In the log, we see requests spaced 6û24+ hours apart, suggesting either customer delay or the adjuster not following up promptly.

**Secondary Root Cause: Resource Allocation & Specialization**
- High-complexity claims are routed to Manager_Bill, who seems to have lower availability or processes them in batches (approvals occur late in the day or next day).
- Adjuster_Lisa handles a high volume of complex/medium cases in Region B and appears to be a bottleneck when multiple document requests are needed.

**Tertiary Factor: Region B**
- Slightly longer durations, possibly due to higher average complexity or resource constraints in that region.

### 4. Recommendations to Mitigate These Issues

1. **Complexity-Based Process Design**
   - Create a dedicated fast-track for Low complexity claims (already working well).
   - Introduce a structured "High Complexity" pathway with clearer SLAs for document requests (e.g., follow-up within 4 hours if no response).

2. **Resource Optimization**
   - Assign more experienced adjusters or create a specialized high-complexity team to reduce the number of document request loops.
   - Balance workload between Adjuster_Mike and Adjuster_Lisa.
   - Review Manager_BillÆs capacity ù high-complexity claims should not wait until the next day for approval.

3. **Improve Document Request Process**
   - Implement better customer communication (e.g., digital portal with clear instructions, automated reminders).
   - Allow parallel document collection instead of sequential requests.
   - Set a maximum of 2 document requests before escalating to a senior adjuster or manager.

4. **Monitoring & Analytics**
   - Track "number of document requests" as a key performance indicator.
   - Monitor duration by complexity Î region Î resource combination.
   - Set automated alerts when a high-complexity case exceeds 1 day without progress.

**Summary**: The main performance issue is not the process steps themselves, but the **high complexity of certain claims** combined with **repeated document requests** and **limited capacity on specialized resources** (especially Adjuster_Lisa and Manager_Bill). Addressing the document collection loop for complex claims will yield the biggest improvement in overall cycle time.
