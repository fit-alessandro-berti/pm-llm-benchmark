### 1. Identified Anomalies in Temporal Profile

Based on the provided temporal profile, the following anomalies indicate deviations from a stable, human-driven process flow:

*   **`R`  `P` (Receive to Approve): Suspiciously Low Variance**
    *   **Observation:** Average time is ~25 hours with a standard deviation of only 1 hour.
    *   **Anomaly:** The coefficient of variation is extremely low (~4%). In a manual or semi-automated process, approval timing usually correlates with evaluation complexity, leading to higher variance. A rigid 25h ± 1h window suggests a fixed schedule, batch processing, or an automated trigger that is decoupled from actual claim content.
    *   **Cross-Check:** The average time `R`  `E` is ~24 hours with a high standard deviation (8 hours). Mathematically, if `P` depended on `E`, the variance of `R`  `P` should be at least as large as `R`  `E`. The fact that `R`  `P` has *lower* variance suggests `P` may be generated independently of `E`, or `P` occurs *before* `E` in some cases, violating process logic.

*   **`P`  `N` (Approve to Notify): Excessive Delay and High Variance**
    *   **Observation:** Average delay is 7 days with a standard deviation of 2 days.
    *   **Anomaly:** A week-long delay between approval and customer notification is unusually long and inconsistent. This indicates a severe bottleneck, likely due to manual notification queues, email server issues, or a lack of integration between the approval system and the notification module.

*   **`A`  `C` (Assign to Close): Premature Closure**
    *   **Observation:** Average time is only 2 hours.
    *   **Anomaly:** Claims are being closed very shortly after assignment. Given the process flow, claims should undergo Evaluation (`E`) and Approval (`P`) before closure. A 2-hour average suggests claims are being closed prematurely, possibly due to system errors, auto-closure of rejected claims, or incorrect logging of the `C` activity.

*   **`E`  `N` (Evaluate to Notify): Rapid Transition**
    *   **Observation:** Average time is 5 minutes.
    *   **Anomaly:** Notification occurs almost immediately after evaluation. This low latency suggests an automated notification triggered instantly upon evaluation completion, potentially bypassing manual review steps or approval gates.

### 2. Hypotheses for Anomalies

*   **Hypothesis 1: Automated/Rigid Approval Logic (`R`  `P`)**
    The system may be generating an `Approve` event at a fixed offset from submission (e.g., 25 hours) regardless of claim status. Alternatively, `P` might be a system artifact or a batch job that overwrites timestamps, explaining the low variance. The variance discrepancy with `R`  `E` strongly suggests `P` may not be causally linked to `E`, or `P` precedes `E` in some executions.

*   **Hypothesis 2: Process Violation – Approval Before Evaluation (`R`  `P` vs `R`  `E`)**
    Due to the low variance of `R`  `P` compared to `R`  `E`, it is possible that for some claims, the `Approve` event is timestamped before the `Evaluate` event. This indicates a data integrity issue or a workflow bug where approvals are generated prematurely.

*   **Hypothesis 3: Premature Closure or Error Logging (`A`  `C`)**
    The short `A`  `C` interval suggests that claims are being closed without completing the core workflow. This could be caused by:
    *   Auto-closure of clearly invalid or duplicate claims.
    *   A bug where the `Close` activity is triggered immediately after `Assign`.
    *   Incorrect mapping of activities (e.g., `C` being used for "Cancel" or "Reject" in the database).

*   **Hypothesis 4: Notification Backlog (`P`  `N`)**
    The 7-day delay and high variance indicate that notifications are not sent automatically upon approval. A manual process or a separate notification service with a large backlog is likely causing delays. The variance suggests resource constraints vary significantly over time.

*   **Hypothesis 5: Automated Evaluation Notifications (`E`  `N`)**
    The 5-minute interval suggests that notifications are triggered automatically by the evaluation module, possibly sending status updates to the system or customer without requiring human intervention or approval.

#### Query 1: Detect `R`  `P` Anomalies and Check for `P` Before `E`
Verifies the rigid timing of `R`  `P` and checks if `P` occurs before `E` (which would explain the variance anomaly).

```sql
WITH r_p_times AS (
    SELECT 
        ce_r.claim_id,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_p.timestamp - ce_r.timestamp)) AS time_diff_seconds,
        (EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_p.timestamp - ce_r.timestamp)) - 90000) / 3600 AS z_score
    FROM claim_events ce_r
    JOIN claim_events ce_p 
        ON ce_r.claim_id = ce_p.claim_id
    WHERE ce_r.activity = 'R' 
      AND ce_p.activity = 'P' 
      AND ce_r.timestamp < ce_p.timestamp
),
r_e_times AS (
    SELECT 
        ce_r.claim_id,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_e.timestamp - ce_r.timestamp)) AS time_diff_seconds
    FROM claim_events ce_r
    JOIN claim_events ce_e 
        ON ce_r.claim_id = ce_e.claim_id
    WHERE ce_r.activity = 'R' 
      AND ce_e.activity = 'E' 
      AND ce_r.timestamp < ce_e.timestamp
),
p_before_e AS (
    SELECT 
        r_p.claim_id,
        r_p.time_diff_seconds AS r_p_time,
        r_e.time_diff_seconds AS r_e_time,
        r_e.time_diff_seconds - r_p.time_diff_seconds AS p_before_e_delta
    FROM r_p_times r_p
    JOIN r_e_times r_e ON r_p.claim_id = r_e.claim_id
    WHERE r_p.time_diff_seconds < r_e.time_diff_seconds
)
SELECT 
    'R_P_ANOMALY' AS anomaly_type,
    claim_id,
    time_diff_seconds,
    z_score
FROM r_p_times
WHERE ABS(z_score) > 2.0  -- More than 2 standard deviations from mean

UNION ALL

SELECT 
    'P_BEFORE_E' AS anomaly_type,
    claim_id,
    r_p_time,
    p_before_e_delta AS z_score  -- Using delta instead of Z for this check
FROM p_before_e
WHERE p_before_e_delta < 0;   -- P happened before E
```

#### Query 3: Analyze `P`  `N` Delays by Resource
Identifies resources (adjusters/systems) associated with excessive notification delays.

```sql
WITH p_n_times AS (
    SELECT 
        ce_p.claim_id,
        ce_p.resource AS notify_resource,
        EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_n.timestamp - ce_p.timestamp)) AS time_diff_seconds,
        (EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_n.timestamp - ce_p.timestamp)) - 604800) / 172800 AS z_score
    FROM claim_events ce_p
    JOIN claim_events ce_n 
        ON ce_p.claim_id = ce_n.claim_id
    WHERE ce_p.activity = 'P' 
      AND ce_n.activity = 'N' 
      AND ce_p.timestamp < ce_n.timestamp
)
SELECT 
    notify_resource,
    COUNT(*) AS claim_count,
    AVG(time_diff_seconds) AS avg_delay_seconds,
    STDDEV(time_diff_seconds) AS stdev_delay_seconds,
    MAX(time_diff_seconds) AS max_delay_seconds
FROM p_n_times
WHERE ABS(z_score) > 2.0  -- Focus on extreme delays
GROUP BY notify_resource
HAVING AVG(time_diff_seconds) > 604800 + (2 * 172800)  -- Average delay > Mean + 2
ORDER BY avg_delay_seconds DESC;
```
