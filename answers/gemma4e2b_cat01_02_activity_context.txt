## 3. Justification of Naming Choices

The renaming strategy focuses on making the activity name explicitly state the *action*, the *target*, or the *outcome*, making the log easier to query and understand the specific flow within each case.

1. **`Verify Documents` (Renamed to `Check Required Docs`, `Verify Identity`, `Physical Document Check`, `Re-Verify Documents`):**
    * **Justification:** This disambiguates the type of verification. Whether the agent is checking submitted paperwork, performing a physical check, or validating the submitted file (re-verification) is crucial information that was previously hidden under a generic name.

2. **`Call Customer` (Renamed to `Request Missing Docs`, `Clarify Discrepancy Call`, `Follow-up Confirmation Call`):**
    * **Justification:** This clarifies the *purpose* of the communication. Was the call initiated to request missing information, or is it a subsequent call designed to confirm that an internal process (like an account adjustment) has been completed?

3. **`Adjust Account` (Renamed to `Internal Account Adjustment`):**
    * **Justification:** This specifies that the action is an internal operational correction performed by the BackOfficeAgent, rather than an external communication or customer-facing action. This separates system-side work from external interaction.

4. **`Initiate Request` / `Finalize Request` (Renamed to `Initiate Request` / `Finalize Request`):**
    * **Justification:** These remain largely unchanged as they represent clear, terminal starting and ending points of the workflow, respectively.
