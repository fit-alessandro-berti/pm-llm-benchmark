### 3. Impact on Cycle Time & Recommendations

The identified factors lead to increased cycle times by introducing **queue time** (waiting for the next agent/shift) and **complexity handling time** (escalation overhead).

#### How Factors Increase Cycle Time:
1.  **Escalation Overhead:** Escalating a ticket moves it from a high-throughput Level-1 queue to a potentially smaller or slower Level-2 queue. The log shows that Level-2 tickets often wait overnight for the next investigation activity.
2.  **Lack of Immediate Availability:** The gaps between "Investigate" and "Resolve" (e.g., Case 105) suggest that while work is being done, the active resolution phase is not happening in real-time due to staffing constraints.
3.  **Operational Gaps:** Cases resolving over multiple days indicate that the support team may be operating on a single shift model, missing opportunities for overnight or weekend resolution.

#### Recommendations:
1.  **Optimize Level-2 Queue Capacity:**
    *   Ensure Level-2 agents are available to take escalated tickets immediately during business hours (08:00–17:00).
    *   If possible, implement an automated triage system that routes complex issues to Level-2 overnight or on weekends to prevent the 24+ hour waits seen in Case 105.
2.  **Review Escalation Criteria:**
    *   Analyze why tickets are being escalated so quickly (e.g., Case 105 escalated only 10 minutes after initial investigation). If a ticket is complex, it should be routed to Level-2 immediately upon triage rather than after a short Level-1 investigation.
3.  **Standardize SLAs for Investigation:**
    *   Enforce a strict Service Level Agreement (SLA) on the time between "Assign" and "Investigate." Case 104 shows a 3.5-hour gap which is acceptable, but combined with multi-day resolution times, it suggests process inefficiencies.
4.  **Cross-Shift Coverage:**
    *   Given that Cases 102, 104, and 105 all span multiple days, implementing a rotating shift model or on-call support for Level-2 agents would significantly reduce the "overnight" resolution times.

### Summary Conclusion
The primary driver of performance issues is **Case Complexity requiring Escalation**, compounded by **insufficient Level-2 availability** during non-business hours. Cases 102 and 105 clearly demonstrate that escalation correlates with longer durations, but Case 104 proves that delays can also occur at the Level-1 stage or due to operational gaps without an explicit escalation flag. Addressing staffing coverage for overnight shifts is the most critical immediate action.
