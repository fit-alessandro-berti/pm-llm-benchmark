### 4. Justification for Naming Choices

*   **`Verify Documents` renamed to `Verify Documents - Initial` and `Verify Documents - Post-Input/Post-Adjustment`:**
    *   **Clue**: The "Additional Context" column explicitly states the reason for the re-verification. The first verification is the standard check. The second one is a direct consequence of a preceding event (new customer input or an internal adjustment). The suffixes `-Post-Input` (for C001) and `-Post-Adjustment` (for C003) make this dependency clear and highlight the different process paths.

*   **`Call Customer` renamed to `Call Customer - Investigate Issue` / `Request Info` / `Request Return` and `Call Customer - Confirm Resolution`:**
    *   **Clue**: The purpose of the call, as described in the context, changes fundamentally. The first call is always a proactive step to *gather information* or *resolve a blockage* ("request missing docs," "clarify discrepancy," "have them return"). The second call in C002 is reactive and serves to *close the loop* ("confirm adjustments"). Using `-Investigate Issue` (or similar) and `-Confirm Resolution` clearly separates the exploratory phase from the confirmation phase.

*   **`Adjust Account` renamed to `Adjust Account Details`:**
    *   **Justification**: While this activity does not recur within a single case in the provided log, the new name `Adjust Account Details` is more specific and descriptive of the action being taken, as mentioned in the context of C002. This improves clarity overall, even if it doesn't resolve an ambiguity within a single case trace. If this activity were to appear multiple times in a more complex case, future suffixes could be added (e.g., `-Correct Error` vs. `-Update Preference`).

These new names transform the log from a sequence of generic steps into a clear narrative for each case, making the process logic, dependencies, and variations immediately apparent to anyone analyzing it.
