## Case grouping and activity naming logic

**Case identification logic**
- I used the main work object in the window title as the case anchor:
  - `Document1.docx`  `CASE_DOC_DOCUMENT1`
  - `Quarterly_Report.docx`  `CASE_DOC_QUARTERLY_REPORT`
  - `Budget_2024.xlsx`  `CASE_XLS_BUDGET_2024`
  - `Report_Draft.pdf`  `CASE_PDF_REPORT_DRAFT`
  - the email about Annual Meeting  `CASE_EMAIL_ANNUAL_MEETING`
- This is analyst-friendly because each case represents a coherent unit of work on a specific business object.

**Activity abstraction logic**
- Raw UI events were mapped to higher-level business activities:
  - `FOCUS` on a file/window  `Open ...` or `Resume ...`
  - `TYPING` in Word/Excel  `Edit document` / `Edit spreadsheet`
  - `SAVE`  `Save ...`
  - `CLICK` in email context  `Open email`, `Start email reply`, `Send email reply`
  - `SCROLL` in email/PDF  `Review email` / `Review PDF`
  - `HIGHLIGHT` in PDF  `Highlight PDF content`
  - `CLOSE`  `Close ...`

**Narrative of work**
- The user briefly had `Quarterly_Report.docx` in focus, then worked on `Document1.docx`.
- They paused document editing to handle an email about the annual meeting.
- Next, they reviewed and highlighted a PDF.
- Then they updated a budget spreadsheet.
- After that, they returned to `Document1.docx` to insert a budget reference and closed it.
- Finally, they resumed work on `Quarterly_Report.docx`, saved it, and closed it.
