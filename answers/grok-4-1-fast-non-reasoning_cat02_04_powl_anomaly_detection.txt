### Identified Anomalies

Two structural anomalies deviate from a **standard Order-to-Cash workflow** (expected: R  C  A  I  P  O, strictly sequential, no skips/loops):

1. **XOR Choice to Skip Credit Check (`xor_credit = XOR(C, skip)`)**:
   - **Breaks Expectation**: Credit checks are mandatory in standard OTC to assess customer risk before approval. Here, `skip` (silent transition) allows bypassing C entirely, enabling direct flow from R  loop_approve_invoice.
   - **Unusual Pattern**: This introduces a "workaround" for risky customers, possibly to rush orders without due diligence.

2. **LOOP on Approval and Invoicing (`loop_approve_invoice = LOOP([A, I])`)**:
   - **Breaks Expectation**: Approval (A) and Invoicing (I) are typically **one-time activities** post-credit check. A loop allows *arbitrary repetitions* of A  I (e.g., approve  invoice  re-approve  re-invoice).
   - **Unusual Pattern**: This models "retry" behavior (e.g., invoice corrections requiring re-approval), but unbounded loops suggest inefficiency or error-prone handling (e.g., repeated invoices for one order).
