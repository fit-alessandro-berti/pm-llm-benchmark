## Rationale for Each Added Constraint

### 1. Existence Constraints: `BiasMitigationCheck` and `ManualReview`
- **Why:** By mandating that every single process trace contains both a `BiasMitigationCheck` and a `ManualReview`, we ensure that no loan application is processed without passing through fairness verification (algorithmic) and human oversight. This is a blanket safeguard Ś regardless of applicant demographics, every case receives the same structural protections.

### 2. Responded Existence: Sensitive Attribute Checks  `BiasMitigationCheck`
- **Why:** If any activity that reveals a sensitive attribute (`CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge`) appears in the trace, then a `BiasMitigationCheck` must also appear. This guarantees that whenever sensitive information is accessed, the process includes a fairness check somewhere in the trace.

### 3. Coexistence: Demographic-Specific Decisions  `ManualReview`
- **Why:** Activities like `Reject_Minority`, `Approve_Minority`, `Reject_Female`, `Approve_Female`, `Reject_Elderly`, and `Approve_Elderly` represent decisions explicitly tagged with sensitive demographic attributes. Requiring coexistence with `ManualReview` ensures that any decision associated with a protected group undergoes human review. Importantly, this applies to **both** approvals and rejections, ensuring consistent treatment and preventing scenarios where, e.g., minority rejections slip through without oversight.

### 4. Response: Sensitive Attribute Checks  `BiasMitigationCheck`; `BiasMitigationCheck`  `ManualReview`
- **Why:** These response constraints enforce a **temporal ordering**: after a sensitive attribute is checked, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must *eventually* follow. After the `BiasMitigationCheck`, a `ManualReview` must *eventually* follow. This creates a mandatory pipeline: **sensitive data access  algorithmic fairness check  human review**, ensuring layered protection against bias.

### 5. Precedence: `FinalDecision` and `ManualReview` Require Prior `BiasMitigationCheck`
- **Why:** No `FinalDecision` can be issued unless a `BiasMitigationCheck` has already occurred in the trace. Similarly, `ManualReview` requires a preceding `BiasMitigationCheck`. This prevents any decision path that bypasses the algorithmic fairness check.

### 6. Succession: `BiasMitigationCheck`  `ManualReview`
- **Why:** Succession combines response and precedence: `BiasMitigationCheck` must be followed by `ManualReview`, and `ManualReview` must be preceded by `BiasMitigationCheck`. This tightly couples the two fairness activities, ensuring they always appear together in the correct order.

### 7. Chain Response: `ManualReview`  `FinalDecision` (immediately next)
- **Why:** After a human reviewer completes `ManualReview`, the very next activity must be `FinalDecision`. This prevents any intervening activity from tampering with or overriding the human reviewer's assessment. The decision is made *immediately* after the human check, preserving the integrity of the review.

### 8. Chain Precedence: `FinalDecision` must be immediately preceded by `ManualReview`
- **Why:** This is the mirror of the chain response above. It ensures that the last activity before any `FinalDecision` is always `ManualReview`. No automated step, attribute check, or other potentially biasing activity can sneak in between the human review and the final decision.

### 9. Non-Succession: Sensitive Attribute Checks  `Reject`
- **Why:** This constraint prohibits a pattern where `CheckApplicantRace` (or gender/age) is eventually followed by `Reject` without the mandatory intervening activities. Combined with the response constraints that require `BiasMitigationCheck` after sensitive checks, this effectively blocks any pathway from sensitive attribute disclosure directly to rejection.

### 10. Non-Chain-Succession: Sensitive Attribute Checks  `FinalDecision`/`Reject` (immediately next)
- **Why:** Even stronger than non-succession, these constraints prevent sensitive attribute checks from being *immediately* followed by a decision or rejection. This eliminates the most egregious form of bias Ś where seeing a sensitive attribute directly triggers a decision without any intervening analysis, fairness check, or review.

### Summary of the Fairness Architecture

The constraints collectively enforce this process flow for any trace involving sensitive attributes:

```
StartApplication  ...  CheckApplicantRace/Gender/Age  ...  BiasMitigationCheck  ...  ManualReview  FinalDecision
```

**Key guarantees:**
1. **Universal coverage**: Every trace has both `BiasMitigationCheck` and `ManualReview` (existence constraints).
2. **Triggered protection**: Accessing sensitive data triggers mandatory fairness checks (responded existence + response).
3. **Correct ordering**: Fairness checks happen before decisions (precedence + succession).
4. **Last-mile integrity**: The final decision is immediately preceded by human review (chain constraints).
5. **Prohibited shortcuts**: No direct path from sensitive attribute disclosure to rejection (non-succession + non-chain-succession).
6. **Demographic consistency**: Both approval and rejection of protected-group applicants receive identical oversight (coexistence with ManualReview for all demographic-tagged decisions).
