Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify the property onboarding process, organized by the phase of the workflow they address.

### **Initial Documentation and Verification**
*   **Handling Missing Information:** You mentioned that the coordinator requests updates if documents are missing or outdated. Is there a defined timeline or SLA (Service Level Agreement) for the landlord to provide these updates before the process is paused or cancelled?
*   **Data Entry Standards:** When recording property details into the central database, are there specific validation rules or standardized taxonomies used (e.g., standard definitions for "luxury" vs. "standard" amenities), or is this largely subjective based on the coordinator's discretion?
*   **Risk Assessment:** Does the initial documentation review include a preliminary risk assessment of the property or the owner that might trigger different onboarding paths, or does every property follow the same standard workflow regardless of condition?

### **Property Manager Assignment**
*   **Assignment Decision Logic:** When assigning a property manager, how do you weigh conflicting criteria? For example, if the most geographically specialized manager is at full capacity, is the workload prioritized over geographic expertise?
*   **The "Handshake":** regarding the introductory meeting, are specific contractual terms or service level agreements signed at this stage to formally align expectations, or is it primarily a casual conversation to establish rapport?
*   **Fail-safes:** What happens if a property manager is identified but the introductory meeting reveals a mismatch in personality or approach with the landlord? Is there a process for reassignment?

### **Inspection and Repairs**
*   **The "Pass" Threshold:** You mentioned the property must pass inspection. Could you elaborate on the specific "go/no-go" criteria? Are there minor issues that can be waived, or does the property have to be 100% compliant before marketing can begin?
*   **Repair Logistics:** When repairs are required, does the property manager have the authority to unilaterally hire contractors up to a certain budget, or does every repair invoice need explicit approval from the landlord or the regional office?
*   **Scheduling Dependencies:** How is the scheduling coordinated between the photographer and the inspector to ensure the photos accurately reflect the post-repair condition of the property?

### **Marketing and Pricing**
*   **Pricing Authority:** There seems to be a potential overlap between the digital marketing team updating pricing models and the financial analyst verifying profitability targets later. Who has the final authority to set the initial listing price before the property goes live?
*   **Marketing Approval:** Does the landlord have an opportunity to review and approve the marketing materials (brochures, online descriptions) before they are published, or is this handled entirely internally by the marketing team?

### **Tenant Screening and Leasing**
*   **Selection Criteria:** When multiple tenants are interested, you mentioned negotiating lease terms or prioritizing longer commitments. Is there a standardized scoring matrix used to rank applicants, or does the property manager rely primarily on intuition?
*   **Exception Handling in Screening:** How do you handle situations where a highly qualified tenant fails a specific screening metric (e.g., a slightly lower credit score) but the landlord wants to proceed? Does this require a special waiver or escalation?
*   **Lease Customization:** regarding the lease agreement, are the "custom clauses" mentioned by landlords reviewed by legal counsel before the tenant signs, or is the property manager empowered to insert standard addendums without legal review?

### **Quality Checks and Audits**
*   **Timing of Audits:** The description places compliance and financial audits after the lease is finalized. Do these audits happen *before* the tenant moves in, or can they occur concurrently? If a compliance issue is found after the lease is signed but before move-in, what is the remediation procedure?
*   **System Integration:** If the IT department finds that data entries are incorrect during their check, does this prevent the tenant from moving in, or is it treated as a backlog ticket to be fixed later?
*   **Feedback Loops:** If the compliance officer or financial analyst consistently finds errors with a specific regional office or property manager, is there a mechanism to trigger a review of that individual's onboarding procedures?

### **Process Exceptions and General Workflow**
*   **Complex Exceptions:** You mentioned properties requiring special insurance. How early in the process are these special requirements flagged? Does this create a parallel workflow that runs alongside the standard onboarding?
*   **Communication Ownership:** With communication flowing between regional offices, landlords, and support teams across various platforms (email, CRM, etc.), who owns the "single source of truth" for the status of the onboarding process?
*   **Cancellation:** Is there a formal "kill switch" or offboarding process if the landlord decides to withdraw the property halfway through the onboarding? If so, how are the sunk costs (e.g., photographer fees, inspection costs) handled?
