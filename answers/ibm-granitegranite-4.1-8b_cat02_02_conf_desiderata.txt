#### **3. Possible Interpretations or Justifications**

- **Case 2 – Risk Assessment before Credit Check:**  
  - *Interpretation:* The system may have autogenerated a preliminary risk assessment based on application data before the formal credit check was completed. The subsequent manual *Credit Check* might have been intended to replace the preliminary assessment, but the timing and logging order suggest an oversight.  
  - *Justification:* The team could have mistakenly assumed the risk assessment could proceed without a credit check, or the credit check was delayed and the workflow allowed a provisional assessment to proceed. Regardless, this is a clear breach of the defined sequence that must be corrected.

- **Case 2 – Finalize Documentation after Rejection:**  
  - *Interpretation:* The staff might have inadvertently followed a template that defaulted to finalizing documentation for any decision, failing to apply the conditional logic specific to rejections.  
  - *Justification:* This underscores a need for stricter enforcement checks or automation to ensure that documentation steps are conditional on the loan decision outcome.

- **Case 3 – Delayed Credit Check (24hour window):**  
  - *Interpretation:* The delay may reflect operational constraints (e.g., staffing, system load) or an intentional strategic choice to gather additional supporting information before performing the credit check.  
  - *Justification:* While suboptimal, such delays are permissible as long as they do not jeopardize the overall process integrity. However, regular monitoring could ensure that such deviations are minimized.

- **Case 3 – Inform Applicant Timing:**  
  - *Interpretation:* The exact window of 48 hours is a guideline; arriving exactly at 48 hours still satisfies the rule technically. If the organization prefers faster communication, further training or workflow adjustments (e.g., automatic notifications) could be implemented.  

- **Case 3 – Missing Followup Call:**  
  - *Interpretation:* The absence of a followup call may reflect operational prioritization or a temporary policy deviation. Since the call is nonessential, its omission does not impair the core loan decision process but could be revisited for customer experience improvements.
