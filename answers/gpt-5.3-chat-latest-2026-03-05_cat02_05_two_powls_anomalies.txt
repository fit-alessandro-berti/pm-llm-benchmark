Model 1

Main structure  
Post  Screen  {Interview and Decide in parallel}  Onboard  Payroll  Close

Anomalies  
• Hiring decision may occur before interviews.  
  Because both edges originate from Screen (Screen  Decide and Screen  Interview) but there is no constraint between Interview and Decide, the model allows the decision to be taken before interviews are conducted. This contradicts the normal logic where interviews inform the decision.

• Interviews are not required for the decision.  
  Since Decide does not depend on Interview, the process can legally execute: Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard…, completely skipping interviews.

Severity  
This is a logical error in the decision-making step. The hiring decision should normally follow candidate evaluation (including interviews). However, the rest of the process (onboarding  payroll  closing) is structurally correct and mandatory once a hire is made.

---

Model 2

Main structure  
Post  Screen  
Post  Interview  
Interview  Decide  (loop Onboard)  (XOR Payroll or skip)  Close

Anomalies  
• Interviews may occur before screening.  
  Because Interview only depends on Post, the model allows Interview to start before Screen. In a typical hiring process, screening should filter candidates before interviews.

• Screening is not required for the hiring decision.  
  Decide only depends on Interview, meaning the process could execute Post  Interview  Decide without screening candidates.

• Onboarding can repeat indefinitely.  
  The loop *(Onboard, skip)* allows multiple onboarding executions. In reality, onboarding normally happens once.

• Payroll is optional.  
  The XOR between Payroll and skip means an employee can be onboarded but never added to payroll, which fundamentally contradicts the purpose of employment administration.

Severity  
Two anomalies are severe:
- optional payroll after onboarding  
- repeated onboarding  
Both contradict core HR process logic.

---

Which model is closer to a normative HiretoRetire process?

Model 1 is closer to the normative process.

Reasoning  
• Model 1 preserves the essential HR backbone: once a hiring decision is made, onboarding, payroll registration, and case closure always occur in the correct order.  
• Its main flaw is that interviews are not guaranteed to precede the decision.

In contrast, Model 2 introduces more serious structural violations:  
• onboarding can repeat  
• payroll can be skipped entirely  
• screening is not required before interviews or decisions

Skipping payroll or repeating onboarding undermines the fundamental semantics of the HiretoRetire lifecycle.

Conclusion  
Model 1 is the closer approximation to a standard HiretoRetire workflow because its anomaly only affects the ordering of candidate evaluation activities, while Model 2 violates core employment administration steps such as mandatory payroll registration.
