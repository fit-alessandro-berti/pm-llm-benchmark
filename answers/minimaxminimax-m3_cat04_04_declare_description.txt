# Loan Approval Process: A DECLARE-Constrained Workflow

## Overview

The DECLARE model encodes a highly regulated, risk-averse loan approval process where every step is deliberately ordered to prevent fraud, ensure regulatory compliance, and protect both the institution and the customer. The constraints act as guardrails that enforce a logical progression from initial intake to final disbursement. Below is a step-by-step reconstruction of how the process unfolds in practice.

---

## Step-by-Step Process Description

### Step 1: Application Intake
**Activity:** `Receive_Application`

The process **must** begin with receiving the customer's loan application (enforced by the `init` constraint). This is the gateway activity without which the entire process cannot legally or operationally proceed. The `existence` constraint guarantees that this activity occurs in every valid case, establishing the contractual and documentary foundation of the loan request. In the real world, this is when the bank captures applicant identity, loan purpose, amount requested, and basic demographic data.

### Step 2: Initial Creditworthiness Assessment
**Activity:** `Preliminary_Credit_Check`

Once the application is received, a **response** constraint mandates that a `Preliminary_Credit_Check` must eventually follow. The `exactly_one` constraint is particularly significant: it ensures the credit check happens **exactly once**—preventing both the omission of this critical risk assessment and redundant, wasteful re-checks that could delay the process. This step typically involves querying credit bureaus, verifying income signals, and flagging high-risk applicants early. Doing this before gathering documents prevents the bank from wasting resources on applicants who would not qualify.

### Step 3: Document Collection
**Activity:** `Gather_Additional_Documents`

A `succession` constraint dictates that `Gather_Additional_Documents` follows the credit check. The logic is clear: only after confirming preliminary eligibility does the bank invest effort in collecting pay stubs, tax returns, identification, and collateral documentation. The `existence` constraint ensures document gathering cannot be skipped—even if some documents were provided at intake, the bank must formally complete this verification stage. This supports **Know Your Customer (KYC)** and **Anti-Money Laundering (AML)** obligations.

### Step 4: Quality Assurance Review
**Activity:** `Quality_Assurance_Review`

After documents are gathered, a `Quality_Assurance_Review` must occur (via `altsuccession` from document gathering). The `responded_existence` constraint ties this to the `Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package`—wherever the loan offer package is assembled, a quality review must also be present in the trace. This is the "four-eyes principle" in action: a second pair of eyes validates the work before it advances. The `precedence` constraint explicitly requires that `Quality_Assurance_Review` occurs **before** `Authorize_Contract_Terms`, preventing premature authorization of terms that have not been vetted for errors, regulatory compliance, or internal policy adherence.

### Step 5: Assembling the Loan Offer
**Activity:** `Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package`

The `chainsuccession` constraint is strict here: `Quality_Assurance_Review` must be **immediately followed by** `Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package`. There is no room for intermediate steps or delays—once the QA review concludes, the offer package preparation happens next. This activity consolidates interest rates, repayment terms, fees, and conditions into a formal offer document. The `chainresponse` constraint then mandates that `Transfer_Funds` immediately follows assembly, which we'll address in the disbursement phase.

### Step 6: Authorizing Contract Terms
**Activity:** `Authorize_Contract_Terms`

The `coexistence` constraint ensures that wherever `Gather_Additional_Documents` occurs, `Authorize_Contract_Terms` must also occur (and vice versa). This is a powerful safeguard: it guarantees that the bank never finalizes loan terms without having first collected the supporting documentation. Combined with the `precedence` rule (QA Review before authorization), this creates a multi-layered approval gate. The `chainprecedence` constraint specifies that authorization immediately precedes a final verification step, acting as a final checkpoint before funds move. The `nonchainsuccession` constraint prevents authorization from being immediately followed by customer notification, ensuring the customer is not told the loan is approved before the final verification and disbursement machinery is engaged.

### Step 7: Fund Disbursement
**Activity:** `Transfer_Funds`

The `chainresponse` constraint dictates that `Transfer_Funds` occurs **immediately after** `Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package`. This tight coupling ensures that once the offer is formalized, disbursement happens in a controlled, traceable sequence—no manual delays or opportunities for tampering. The `noncoexistence` constraint between `Transfer_Funds` and `Receive_Application` is a fascinating safeguard: it ensures these two critical bookend activities are not treated as interchangeable or duplicated in a way that could obscure the audit trail. In practice, this prevents scenarios where a disbursement record could be confused with or mask an application record.

### Step 8: Customer Notification
**Activity:** `Notify_Customer`

The `altresponse` and `altprecedence` constraints together govern the notification step. The customer must be informed of the outcome—typically after disbursement (so they know funds are available), and potentially in preliminary form before final disbursement. This dual-touch approach supports **customer satisfaction** and **transparency**, reducing call-center volume and disputes. The `nonsuccession` constraint prevents notification from being followed by another credit check, ensuring the process does not retroactively reopen risk assessment after the customer has been informed.

### Forbidden Activity
**Activity:** `Proceed_Without_Compliance`

The `absence` constraint is a hard prohibition: this activity must **never** occur. It represents any shortcut, workaround, or override that bypasses compliance protocols. In regulatory terms, this protects the institution from sanctions, fines, and reputational damage.

## Real-World Motivations Behind the Rules

1. **Regulatory Compliance**: Banks operate under frameworks like the **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)**, **Fair Lending regulations**, **Truth in Lending Act (TILA)**, and **Basel III** capital requirements. Constraints like `Quality_Assurance_Review` before `Authorize_Contract_Terms` ensure documented compliance with fair-lending and disclosure rules.

2. **Fraud Prevention**: The mandatory `Preliminary_Credit_Check` and `Gather_Additional_Documents` stages are frontline defenses against synthetic identity fraud and income misrepresentation. The `absence` of `Proceed_Without_Compliance` closes the door on insider overrides that have historically been a source of fines (e.g., Wells Fargo's account fraud scandal).

3. **Risk Management**: The `exactly_one` constraint on the credit check ensures a single, authoritative risk grade is used—avoiding conflicting assessments. The sequencing prevents "offering" loans to applicants who would be rejected, which would expose the bank to reputational and legal risk.

4. **Operational Best Practices**: Chain constraints reduce cycle time by eliminating ambiguity about what happens next. The `coexistence` rule ensures that the loan officer's work (document gathering) and the underwriter's work (authorization) are tightly linked, preventing orphaned files.

5. **Customer Satisfaction**: Notification constraints ensure the customer is kept informed, reducing anxiety and the volume of inbound inquiries. The structured flow prevents the chaotic experience of repeated requests for the same information or contradictory updates.

## Consequences of Constraint Violations

| Violation | Potential Consequence |
|---|---|
| **Skipping Preliminary_Credit_Check** | Disbursing funds to high-risk applicants; increased default rates; potential fair-lending violations if applied inconsistently |
| **Gathering documents before credit check** | Wasted operational effort on ineligible applicants; potential privacy violations from collecting data unnecessarily |
| **Authorizing terms without QA review** | Errors in interest rates, fees, or terms going uncorrected; customer disputes; regulatory findings during audits |
| **Transferring funds without proper sequencing** | Funds released to the wrong account, duplicate disbursements, or breach of internal controls—potentially triggering **SOX** (Sarbanes-Oxley) compliance failures |
| **Notifying customer before credit check completion** | Premature commitments that must be walked back, damaging trust; potential violations of communication-timing regulations |
| **Allowing Proceed_Without_Compliance** | Catastrophic: regulatory sanctions, loss of charter, executive liability, and criminal exposure in cases of willful violation |
| **Omitting the QA review** | Undetected errors propagate to the offer package; downstream rework costs increase; audit findings escalate |
| **Reordering authorization and credit check** | The chainprecedence ensures a final risk gate before funds move; violating this could mean disbursing funds to an applicant whose creditworthiness has materially changed |
