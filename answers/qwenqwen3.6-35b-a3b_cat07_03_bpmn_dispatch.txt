**Overview**
This is a **BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) swimlane diagram**. It illustrates the workflow for "Dispatch of goods" at a "Computer Hardware Shop." The diagram is divided into three horizontal lanes representing different departments or roles responsible for specific tasks: **Logistics**, **Secretary** (Computer Hardware Shop), and **Warehouse**.

**The Workflow Process**

1.  **Start:** The process begins with a start event labeled **"Ship goods"** in the Secretary lane.
2.  **Parallel Split:** Immediately after starting, the flow splits into two simultaneous paths (indicated by a Plus sign gateway): one going down to the Warehouse and one continuing right in the Secretary lane.

**Lane 1: Warehouse (Bottom)**
*   **Task:** The warehouse team begins to **"Package goods."**
*   **Timer Event:** A timer icon labeled **"48 hours"** monitors the process. If the goods aren't ready in time, it triggers a task to **"Notify customer about delay."**
*   Both the normal packaging path and the delay notification path converge at a gateway.

**Lane 2: Secretary (Middle)**
*   **Task:** The secretary **"Clarify shipment method."**
*   **Decision (X):** A gateway asks, **"Special handling?"**

        *   **Labeling:** Regardless of insurance, the flow proceeds to **"Write package label."**
*   These paths eventually merge back into the main flow.

**Lane 3: Logistics (Top)**
*   This lane is only active if the "Special handling?" path determined insurance was needed. The task is simply **"Insure parcel."**

**Final Convergence and End**
*   The outputs from the Secretary lane (selecting a company/writing labels) and the Warehouse lane (packaging goods) meet at a **Parallel Gateway (+)**. This indicates that both administrative and physical tasks must be complete before proceeding.
*   **Final Tasks:**
