To construct the DECLARE model for this manufacturing scenario, we define the logical flow of the process. We assume a primary sequence of events (the backbone) and use coexistence and precedence rules to handle the parallel tasks (like Cost Evaluation and Testing) and their dependencies.

In the dictionary below, for relational rules (like `succession` or `precedence`), the keys are represented as **tuples of activities** `(activity_a, activity_b)` to correctly map the relationship to its support and confidence, as a single activity cannot represent a relationship on its own.

### Logic Summary:
1.  **The Backbone (Succession):** The primary path follows `IG` $\rightarrow$ `DD` $\rightarrow$ `TFC` $\rightarrow$ `PC` $\rightarrow$ `LT` $\rightarrow$ `AG` $\rightarrow$ `MP` $\rightarrow$ `FL`.
2.  **Parallelism (Coexistence):** `CE` (Cost Evaluation) is modeled to coexist with `TFC`, and `UT` (User Testing) is modeled to coexist with `LT`.
3.  **Constraints (Precedence):** Even though `CE` and `UT` coexist with other tasks, they have strict precedence requirements (e.g., `CE` must be done before `PC` can begin, and `UT` must be finished before the `AG` gate).
