#### **1. "Verify Documents"  Disambiguated as "Initial Document Verification" and "Re-Verification of Documents"**
- **Contextual Clues:**
  - **First Occurrence ("Initial Document Verification")**:
    - Represents the *initial* check of documents (e.g., identity verification, physical ID check).
    - In **C001**, the agent checks documents but finds them incomplete (leading to a subsequent call for missing docs).
    - In **C002**, it𠏋 a standard initial identity verification.
    - In **C003**, it𠏋 a physical verification at the branch.
    - **Key Insight**: All first verifications share the common trait of being the *initial* document check, regardless of outcome.

  - **Second Occurrence ("Re-Verification of Documents")**:
    - Represents a *re-check* after an intervening step (e.g., customer submission of additional docs or account adjustment).
    - In **C001**, the re-verification occurs after the customer uploads the requested ID scan.
    - In **C003**, it happens after an internal account adjustment.
    - **Key Insight**: The prefix "Re-" clearly indicates this is a *repeat* verification, distinguishing it from the initial check.

#### **2. "Call Customer"  Disambiguated as "Call Customer (Discrepancy Clarification)" and "Call Customer (Follow-up Confirmation)"**
- **Contextual Clues (C002 only, as other cases have only one call):**
  - **First Call ("Call Customer (Discrepancy Clarification)")**:
    - Triggered by an *account discrepancy* (e.g., unclear transaction history, mismatched details).
    - The Additional Context explicitly states: *"Outbound call to clarify account discrepancy."*
    - **Key Insight**: The purpose is *clarification*, so the suffix "(Discrepancy Clarification)" captures the intent.

  - **Second Call ("Call Customer (Follow-up Confirmation)")**:
    - Occurs *after* internal adjustments (e.g., account corrections) to confirm changes with the customer.
    - The Additional Context states: *"Follow-up call to confirm adjustments made."*
    - **Key Insight**: The purpose is *confirmation*, so the suffix "(Follow-up Confirmation)" distinguishes it from the initial call.

### **Why This Approach Works**
- **Consistency**: The renaming applies the same logic across all cases (e.g., "Initial" vs. "Re-" for verifications, purpose-based suffixes for calls).
- **Clarity**: New names explicitly describe *why* the activity occurred (e.g., "Discrepancy Clarification" vs. "Follow-up Confirmation"), eliminating ambiguity.
- **Process Transparency**: The revised names highlight the *flow* of the process (e.g., initial verification  re-verification after submission/adjustment; clarification call  follow-up confirmation).
