### **Supply Chain Design & Strategy**

2. What criteria are used to decide whether to use air freight vs. sea freight for transporting components and finished goods, and how might geopolitical tensions or fuel price fluctuations alter these decisions?
3. How does the company evaluate the long-term sustainability of its supply chain, particularly in terms of carbon footprint, ethical sourcing, and compliance with evolving environmental regulations (e.g., EU’s Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)?
4. What strategies could the company adopt to reduce dependency on a single region (e.g., Taiwan for semiconductors) without significantly increasing costs or lead times?
5. How might the company restructure its supply chain to better handle "black swan" events (e.g., pandemics, trade wars, natural disasters) while maintaining profitability?

### **Operational Efficiency & Risk Management**
6. What are the biggest bottlenecks in the current supply chain, and how could automation, AI, or process redesign alleviate them?

8. What are the hidden costs of just-in-time (JIT) inventory strategies in this supply chain, and under what conditions might a shift to safety stock or hybrid models be justified?
9. How does the company handle discrepancies between sales forecasts and actual demand, and what role do machine learning models play in reducing forecast errors?
10. What are the most common quality control failures in the assembly process, and how could real-time monitoring (e.g., IoT sensors, computer vision) reduce defects before shipment?

### **Technology & Data-Driven Decision Making**
11. How does the company integrate data from suppliers, logistics partners, and retailers into a unified system, and what challenges arise from inconsistent data formats or latency?
12. What predictive analytics use cases (e.g., demand forecasting, supplier risk scoring, transportation delays) have the highest ROI in this supply chain, and which remain underutilized?
13. How could blockchain or distributed ledger technology improve transparency and trust in this supply chain, particularly for tracking raw materials, verifying ethical sourcing, or managing warranties?
14. What are the limitations of current AI/ML models in supply chain planning, and how might emerging technologies (e.g., digital twins, generative AI) address these gaps?
15. How does the company ensure cybersecurity across its global supply chain, especially when sharing sensitive data (e.g., production schedules, IP) with third-party suppliers and logistics providers?

### **Global Trade, Compliance & Stakeholder Management**
16. How do fluctuating exchange rates, tariffs, and trade policies (e.g., U.S.-China tensions, Brexit) impact sourcing decisions, and what hedging strategies are in place?
17. What are the biggest compliance risks in this supply chain (e.g., labor violations, conflict minerals, data privacy laws), and how does the company audit and mitigate them?
18. How does the company manage relationships with suppliers to ensure alignment on quality, cost, and innovation, while avoiding over-reliance on a few key partners?
19. What role do free trade agreements (e.g., USMCA, CPTPP, RCEP) play in shaping the company’s supply chain strategy, and how might future geopolitical shifts alter these dynamics?
20. How does the company balance the need for speed in product launches with the complexities of regulatory approvals (e.g., FCC, CE, RoHS) across different markets?
