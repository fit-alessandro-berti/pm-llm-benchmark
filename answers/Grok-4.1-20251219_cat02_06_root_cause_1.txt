**Identified factors causing delays**:
- **Presence of escalation**: All escalated cases (102 and 105) have significantly longer durations. Escalation introduces handoffs and appears to trigger long idle periods (likely waiting for Level-2 availability).
- **Long waiting times between activities**, especially:
  - After assignment or escalation to investigation.
  - Large overnight gaps before "Resolve Ticket" (seen in 102, 104, 105).
- **Delays in starting investigation**:
  - Non-escalated case 104 has a 3.5-hour wait from assignment to investigation despite no escalation.
  - Escalated cases have even longer post-escalation waits.
- **Overnight/weekend idle time**: Resolution steps for delayed cases consistently resume the next morning, suggesting work stops outside business hours.

### 3. Explanation and Recommendations

**How these factors increase cycle times**:
- **Escalations** create bottlenecks because Level-2 agents likely have limited capacity or higher prioritization queues. The handoff itself adds coordination overhead, and the data shows substantial idle time immediately after escalation (e.g., 28 hours in case 105).
- **Long waits before investigation** indicate resource constraints during peak morning hours (all tickets received around 08:00–08:25). Level-1 agents may be overloaded, preventing prompt investigation starts.
- **Overnight gaps** reflect a policy or operational reality where issues are not worked on outside standard business hours. When investigation starts late in the day (e.g., after 13:00), resolution inevitably spills into the next day(s), multiplying cycle time.

**Insights and Recommendations**:
1. **Reduce unnecessary escalations**: Analyze why cases 102 and 105 required Level-2 (e.g., issue complexity). Improve Level-1 training, knowledge base, or diagnostic tools to handle more issues at the first level.
2. **Improve resource allocation during peak intake**: The morning rush (all tickets arrive ~08:00–08:25) overloads triage/assignment. Consider staffing ramps or automated initial triage to free agents faster.
3. **Minimize post-escalation delay**: Implement a priority queue or dedicated escalation handover process (e.g., immediate notification + SLA for Level-2 pickup within 1–2 hours).
4. **Extend coverage or enable progress outside business hours**: For critical tickets, introduce on-call Level-2 support or allow partial remote investigation to prevent full-day delays.
5. **Monitor and alert on idle time**: Add monitoring for tickets idle >2 hours after assignment/escalation to trigger proactive reassignment or management alerts.

Addressing escalations and idle periods after handoffs/resource assignments would likely bring the longer cases (102, 104, 105) closer to the efficient performance seen in 101 and 103.
