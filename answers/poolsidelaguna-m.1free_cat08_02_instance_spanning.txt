## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

### Critical Interaction Scenarios

**Express + Cold-Packing Synergy Effect:**
When an express order requiring cold-packing preempts a standard order, the cascading impact includes:
- Extended standard order cycle time beyond simple preemption duration
- Increased cold-station queue due to disrupted workflow
- Potential violation of express SLA if other express orders also compete for same station

**Batch Formation + Hazardous Material Constraints:**
Regional batching of hazardous orders creates a compound bottleneck:
- 10-order hazardous limit may prevent optimal batch formation for that region
- Batch completion delayed until enough compliant orders accumulate
- Risk of suboptimal routing due to mixed hazardous/non-hazardous batches

**Regulatory Limits + Priority Handling:**
Express orders containing hazardous materials create maximum complexity:
- Must satisfy both priority escalation AND hazardous material constraints
- May require dynamic re-allocation of QC resources to maintain compliance
- Could necessitate express order batching exceptions, undermining regional optimization

### Strategic Importance

Understanding these interactions is crucial because:
1. **Sequential optimization fails**: Addressing constraints in isolation creates sub-optimal solutions
2. **Emergent behaviors**: Combined effects can amplify delays exponentially rather than additively
3. **Resource allocation complexity**: Multi-dimensional optimization requires holistic scheduling approaches
4. **Compliance risk**: Interacting constraints can create regulatory violations not apparent in individual analysis
