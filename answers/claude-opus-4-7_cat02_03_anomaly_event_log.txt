# Procure-to-Pay Process Anomaly Analysis

## Standard P2P Reference Flow
The typical, well-controlled P2P sequence is:
1. Create Purchase Requisition  2. Approve Purchase Requisition  3. Issue Purchase Order  4. Receive Goods  5. Vendor Invoice Received  6. Match Invoice to PO (3-way match)  7. Release Payment  8. Close Case

Case **1001** follows this sequence perfectly and serves as the benchmark. The other three cases each contain anomalies.

---

## Case 1002 Ś Multiple Control Violations

**Observed sequence:** Create PR  Issue PO  Invoice Received  **Release Payment**  Approve PR  Receive Goods  Match Invoice to PO  Close

**Anomalies:**
- **PO issued before PR approval.** The Purchase Requisition was never approved prior to the PO being issued; approval occurred *two days later*. This bypasses spend authorization controls.
- **Payment released before goods were received and before invoice matching.** Payment was made on 02-03 at 07:15, but goods weren't received until 02-04 and the 3-way match didn't happen until 02-05.
- **Approval performed *after* payment.** Approving a requisition after money has already left the organization is effectively a rubber-stamp and defeats the purpose of segregation of duties.

**Why problematic:** This is the most severe case. It exposes the company to paying for goods never received, paying incorrect amounts, fraud, and duplicate payments. It violates segregation of duties and basic internal control principles (SOX-style controls).

---

## Case 1003 Ś Payment Before Invoice and Goods Receipt

**Observed sequence:** Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  **Release Payment**  Invoice Received  Receive Goods  Match Invoice to PO  Close

**Anomalies:**
- **Payment released before the vendor invoice was even received.** There is no invoice to pay againstŚwhat amount was paid?
- **Payment released before goods arrived.** No verification that the vendor fulfilled the order.
- **Invoice matching performed *after* payment.** The 3-way match (PO, Goods Receipt, Invoice) is a post-hoc formality here, so any discrepancy (wrong quantity, wrong price, missing items) cannot be corrected before money leaves.

**Why problematic:** Prepayment without an invoice or receipt is a major financial control weakness. It could indicate fraudulent disbursement, a vendor financing arrangement being handled outside proper channels, or a clerical error that cannot be caught by normal controls.

---

## Case 1004 Ś Goods Received Before PO Issued

**Observed sequence:** Create PR  Approve PR  **Receive Goods**  Issue PO  Invoice Received  Match  Release Payment  Close

**Anomaly:**
- **Goods were received before the Purchase Order was issued.** On 02-06 at 09:00 goods arrived, but the PO was only issued 15 minutes later at 09:15.

**Why problematic:** This pattern is characteristic of **"maverick buying"** or **retroactive PO creation**Śwhere someone orders (or accepts) goods informally and the PO is created after the fact to legitimize the transaction. Consequences include:
- Loss of pre-approved pricing / negotiation leverage.
- Inability to validate that the goods were actually ordered by the company.
- Risk of accepting unauthorized or unwanted items.
- Distortion of procurement KPIs and budget controls.

## Key Takeaway
The recurring theme across the anomalies is the **breakdown of the 3-way match control** (PO  Goods Receipt  Invoice) and **payments being released prematurely**. These are precisely the controls that P2P processes exist to enforce, and their violation typically warrants investigation for error, control failure, or fraud.
