## Where bias appears in the process

The main bias signal is the use of **LocalResident** and **CommunityGroup** as decision-relevant attributes. The clearest mechanism is the **ScoreAdjustment** field, where membership in the **Highland Civic Darts Club** receives a **+10 “Community” adjustment**.

That adjustment appears at the **PreliminaryScoring** stage, carries into **ManualReview**, and is reflected again at **FinalDecision**. So the bias is not just informational; it becomes operationalized in the scoring and decision process.

## Attribute-based advantage

The process appears to favor applicants who are:

1. **Local residents**
2. **Members of the Highland Civic Darts Club**
3. Potentially people with stronger local social or community affiliations

The most explicit advantage is the **+10 Community adjustment**:

| Case | LocalResident | CommunityGroup            | Base Score | Adjustment | Adjusted Score | Decision |
| ---- | ------------: | ------------------------- | ---------: | ---------: | -------------: | -------- |
| C001 |          TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club |        710 |        +10 |            720 | Approved |
| C002 |          TRUE | None                      |        720 |          0 |            720 | Approved |
| C003 |         FALSE | None                      |        715 |          0 |            715 | Rejected |
| C004 |          TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club |        690 |        +10 |            700 | Approved |
| C005 |         FALSE | None                      |        740 |          0 |            740 | Approved |

The concerning comparison is **C003 versus C001 and C004**.

C003 has a preliminary score of **715**, which is higher than C001’s original **710** and much higher than C004’s original **690**. Yet C003 is **rejected**, while C001 and C004 are **approved** after receiving or benefiting from community-linked treatment.

That suggests the process may not be evaluating applicants solely on underlying creditworthiness.

## How the bias manifests

### 1. Direct preferential scoring

The **+10 Community adjustment** gives Highland Civic Darts Club members an advantage unrelated to the apparent base credit score.

C001 moves from **710 to 720**.

C004 moves from **690 to 700**.

This matters because final approval appears sensitive to small score differences. If someone receives a community-based boost, they may cross an approval threshold that a non-affiliated applicant cannot cross.

### 2. Unequal treatment of similarly or more creditworthy applicants

C003 is especially important:

* C003 is **not local**
* C003 has **no community group**
* C003 has a score of **715**
* C003 is **rejected**

Meanwhile:

* C001 starts at **710**, gets a community boost, and is approved.
* C004 starts at **690**, gets a community boost, and is approved.

This creates a fairness concern because an applicant with **better underlying creditworthiness than some approved applicants** receives a worse outcome, apparently because they lack the favored local or community affiliation.

### 3. Geographic favoritism

The **LocalResident** attribute may also be influencing decisions, even apart from formal score adjustments.

C002 is local, unaffiliated, has a score of **720**, and is approved.

C003 is non-local, unaffiliated, has a score of **715**, and is rejected.

C005 is non-local, unaffiliated, but has a much higher score of **740**, and is approved.

This pattern suggests that non-local applicants may need a higher score to achieve the same outcome as local applicants, although the sample is small and the log does not reveal the full decision rule.

### 4. Community affiliation as a proxy for social advantage

The Highland Civic Darts Club membership may seem neutral on its face, but it could act as a proxy for:

* Local social networks
* Socioeconomic status
* Long-term residency
* Age
* Ethnicity or cultural affiliation
* Access to community institutions
* Insider status within a small geographic area

Even if the system does not explicitly use protected characteristics, a community-group boost can still produce inequitable outcomes if the group is not equally accessible to all applicants.

## Manual review concern

The **ManualReview** stage also sees the adjusted score and the community-related information. That creates a risk of human confirmation bias.

For example, a reviewer seeing that an applicant is a local civic club member might perceive them as more trustworthy, stable, or socially embedded. Conversely, an applicant with no local affiliation may appear less connected or less reliable, even when their base score is comparable or stronger.

The log does not prove that individual reviewers acted unfairly, but the process exposes reviewers to attributes that could influence judgment.

## Fairness and equity implications

The process appears to reward **community embeddedness** rather than just creditworthiness. That can disadvantage people who:

* Recently moved to the area
* Are not local residents
* Do not belong to favored clubs
* Lack time or money to participate in civic groups
* Are socially isolated
* Come from communities not represented by recognized organizations
* Have equivalent or better financial qualifications but weaker local ties

The most troubling equity issue is that **lack of affiliation becomes a disadvantage**, even though it may have little or no legitimate relationship to repayment ability, eligibility, or risk.

## Bottom line

Bias appears to manifest through a **community-based score adjustment** and possibly through **local-resident favoritism** in final decisions. The **+10 adjustment for Highland Civic Darts Club membership** favors applicants with a specific local affiliation and may allow them to receive approvals that similarly or more creditworthy unaffiliated applicants do not receive.

The clearest red flag is:

> **C003 has a base score of 715 and is rejected, while C004 has a base score of 690, receives a community adjustment to 700, and is approved.**

That suggests the process may be giving material advantage to certain geographic or social groups, raising fairness concerns for applicants who lack those affiliations despite similar or stronger underlying creditworthiness.
