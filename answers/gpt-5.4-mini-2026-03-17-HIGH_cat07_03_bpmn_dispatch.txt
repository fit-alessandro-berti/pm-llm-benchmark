This is a BPMN-style process diagram showing how a shipment is prepared and sent, split across three swimlanes:

- **Logistics**
- **Secretary / Dispatch of goods to customer through logistics**
- **Warehouse**

### Overall flow
The process starts with **“Ship goods”** and then branches into parallel work:

1. **Shipment method is clarified**
2. **Goods are packaged in the warehouse**

### Main decision points
After the shipment method is clarified, there is a decision:

- **Special handling?**
  - **Yes**  get **3 offers from logistics companies**, then **select a logistics company and place the order**
  - **No**  **check if insurance is required**

If insurance is needed, the process goes to **“Insure parcel”** in the Logistics lane, then continues to **“Write package label.”**  
If insurance is not needed, it goes directly to **“Write package label.”**

### Warehouse activity
In parallel, the warehouse performs **“Package goods.”**  
There is a **48-hour timer** indicating delay monitoring, and if packaging takes too long, the warehouse **notifies the customer about the delay**.

### Final steps
The administrative and warehouse paths are synchronized, then the process continues with:

- **Fill out shipment paperwork**
- **Move package to pickup area**
- End event: **“Shipment prepared”**

### Visual elements
The diagram uses common BPMN symbols:
- **Circles** for start/end events
- **Diamonds** for gateways/decisions
- **Rounded rectangles** for tasks
- **A document icon** for order details
- **A database icon** for customer information
- **A timer marker** on the packaging task

In short, it’s a **shipment preparation workflow** that shows how dispatch, logistics, and warehouse activities are coordinated before a package is ready for pickup.
