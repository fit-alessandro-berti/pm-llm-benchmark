Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify the property onboarding process, categorized by key areas of inquiry:

### **Process Flow & Timing**
1.  **Sequencing Dependencies:** Are there any steps in this process that can occur in parallel? For example, can the marketing team begin drafting listings before the property manager has completed the introductory meeting with the landlord?
2.  **Turnaround Times:** What is the typical duration for the documentation verification phase, and are there specific Service Level Agreements (SLAs) in place that dictate how quickly a property must move from "contacted" to "active" in the portal?
3.  **Activation Triggers:** What specific criteria must be met before the property is officially "activated" in the rental portal? Is activation contingent solely on a passing inspection, or does it require financial sign-off as well?

### **Roles & Responsibilities**
4.  **Ownership of Stalls:** If a process stalls during the documentation review (e.g., the landlord is slow to provide deeds), who is responsible for following up, and at what point does the regional office step in to escalate the issue?
5.  **Manager Assignment:** You mentioned workload, geography, and experience as factors for assigning a property manager. Is there a formal algorithm or scorecard used for this assignment, or is it primarily a manual decision made by a team lead?
6.  **Lease Authority:** During the tenant screening and negotiation phase, what is the property manager's limit of authority regarding lease terms and discounts? At what dollar amount or clause complexity does the decision require higher-level approval?

### **Decision Criteria & Rules**
7.  **Documentation Standards:** How do you define "outdated" inspection reports or compliance certificates? Is there a specific timeframe (e.g., within the last 12 months) that triggers a requirement for a new report?
8.  **Pricing Strategy:** When the marketing team updates internal pricing models based on market trends, how is that reconciled with the landlord's "expected rental price range" recorded earlier? If the market suggests a lower price, who has the final say?
9.  **Inspection Thresholds:** What constitutes a "critical" issue versus a "minor" issue during the certified inspection? Does a minor issue prevent the property from being activated, or does it simply get added to a maintenance backlog?

### **Exception Handling**
10. **Refusal of Repairs:** If an inspector finds safety violations and the landlord refuses to authorize the necessary repairs, does the onboarding process halt indefinitely, or is there a mechanism to onboard the property with a disclaimer or holdback on funds?
11. **Custom Clauses:** You mentioned landlords who insist on custom lease clauses. What is the review process for these non-standard terms? Does the legal team review every custom clause, or is there a pre-approved library of exceptions?
12. **Failed Tenant Screening:** If a prospective tenant fails a credit check but the landlord insists on renting to them, what is the procedure for overriding the standard screening protocol?

### **Communication & Stakeholder Management**
13. **Information Silos:** Since communication flows through email, internal CRMs, and project management tools, how do you ensure that a critical update (like a change in move-in date) is synchronized across all three systems to prevent miscommunication between the IT department and the leasing agent?
14. **Landlord Visibility:** How are landlords kept informed of the status of their property during the marketing and inspection phases? Do they have a portal view, or is communication strictly one-on-one via the property coordinator?
15. **Feedback Loops:** After the quality checks and audits are completed, is there a mechanism to feed insights back to the initial property coordinator? For example, if the financial analyst frequently flags pricing discrepancies, does that change how the coordinator records the initial expected price range?

### **Success Metrics & Continuous Improvement**
16. **Definition of Success:** Beyond the property being "active" and rented, how does the company measure the success of the onboarding process? Is it based on speed-to-market, first-month occupancy, or landlord satisfaction scores?
17. **Bottleneck Identification:** In your experience, which stage of this lifecycle most frequently causes delays or requires the most rework?
18. **Scalability:** As the company expands into new cities, are there specific steps in this process that are currently manual and would need to be standardized or automated to handle higher volume?
