Below is a context-aware renaming of the **repeated activities within the same case**. I kept the non-repeated activities mostly unchanged, and only refined the ones whose meaning shifts by position and situation.

## 1) Contextual interpretation of recurring activities

### `Verify Documents`
- **First occurrence**: an initial identity/document check.
- **Second occurrence**: a re-check after something was missing or corrected.
- In C003, the first check is specifically a **physical** ID verification at the branch.

### `Call Customer`
- **C001**: call to request missing documents.
- **C002**: first call clarifies an account discrepancy; second call confirms internal adjustments.
- **C003**: call asks the customer to return to the branch.

## 3) Brief justification for the naming choices

- **ōVerify Documentsö** was renamed based on whether it was:
  - an **initial check**,
  - a **re-check after missing information was supplied**, or
  - a **physical branch verification**.
- **ōCall Customerö** was renamed to reflect the actual purpose of each call:
  - requesting missing documents,
  - clarifying an account discrepancy,
  - confirming adjustments,
  - asking the customer to return to the branch.

These labels make the process flow clearer and distinguish repeated actions by their role in the case, not just by their generic activity name.
