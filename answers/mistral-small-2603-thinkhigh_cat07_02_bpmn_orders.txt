The diagram is a BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) representation of an order-to-delivery process, divided into four sections: Sales, Production, Procurement, and Warehouse. The Sales section starts with checking the completeness of the customer's order and verifying if the customer is already in the database. If the customer is not in the database, they are added. Once the order details are sent, a draft invoice is prepared. The Production section begins with planning production and checking material availability. If materials are faulty, a subprocess in the Procurement section is triggered to order materials, check them, and potentially return them. Once materials are confirmed to be faultless, production proceeds, and the completed product is sent to the Warehouse. The Warehouse section handles sending the product. The diagram illustrates the interaction between different departments and subprocesses, emphasizing the flow of information and materials across the order-to-delivery process.
