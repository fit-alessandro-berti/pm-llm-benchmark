# **Data-Driven Scheduling Optimization for Precision Parts Inc. Using Process Mining**

## **1. Analyzing Historical Scheduling Performance and Dynamics**

To reconstruct and analyze the actual flow of jobs and task execution sequences, we leverage **process mining** techniques on the MES event logs. The goal is to quantify key performance metrics and identify inefficiencies.

### **1.1 Process Discovery & Flow Analysis**
- **Technique:** **Alpha Miner, Heuristics Miner, or Inductive Miner** to extract the **as-is process model** (e.g., a Petri net or BPMN diagram) from the event logs.
  - This reveals the **actual routing** of jobs across machines, including deviations from planned sequences.
  - **Variant Analysis:** Identify common vs. rare job routings to detect inefficiencies (e.g., unnecessary rework loops).
- **Performance Spectrum Analysis:** Overlay timing data (e.g., task durations, waiting times) on the process model to visualize bottlenecks.

### **1.2 Key Metrics & Process Mining Techniques**

| **Metric** | **Process Mining Technique** | **Implementation** | **Insight** |
|------------|-----------------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Job Flow Time / Lead Time** | **Performance Analysis (Token Replay, Alignment-Based)** | Track time from job release to completion for each case. | Distribution of lead times, variability, and outliers. |
| **Task Waiting Times (Queue Times)** | **Queue Mining, Bottleneck Analysis** | Measure time between `Queue Entry` and `Task Start` for each machine. | Identify machines with excessive idle/WIP buildup. |
| **Resource Utilization** | **Resource Profile Analysis** | Aggregate `Task Start`/`Task End` events per machine/operator. | % productive time vs. idle/setup time. |
| **Sequence-Dependent Setup Times** | **Setup Time Matrix Extraction** | For each machine, group `Setup Start`/`Setup End` events by **previous job  current job** pairs. | Quantify setup time variability based on job sequence. |
| **Tardiness & Schedule Adherence** | **Conformance Checking (Alignments, Fitness)** | Compare planned vs. actual task durations and due dates. | Frequency of late jobs, average tardiness, and root causes. |
| **Disruption Impact (Breakdowns, Hot Jobs)** | **Deviation Mining, Impact Analysis** | Correlate `Breakdown Start` events with subsequent delays in job completion. | Quantify how disruptions propagate through the system. |

### **1.3 Sequence-Dependent Setup Time Analysis**
- **Method:**
  1. Extract all `(Previous Job ID, Current Job ID, Setup Duration)` tuples for each machine.
  2. Cluster jobs by **similarity attributes** (e.g., material, geometry, tooling requirements) to group setups.
  3. Build a **setup time matrix** (or regression model) predicting setup time based on job pair characteristics.
- **Output:** A lookup table or ML model estimating setup times for new job sequences.

### **1.4 Disruption Impact Analysis**
- **Technique:** **Causal Footprint Analysis** (from process mining) to trace how a breakdown in one machine affects downstream tasks.
- **Example:**
  - If `MILL-02` breaks down, how many jobs are delayed?
  - Do urgent jobs (`Priority=High`) get expedited at the expense of others?

## **2. Diagnosing Scheduling Pathologies**

Using process mining, we identify systemic inefficiencies in the current scheduling approach.

### **2.1 Key Pathologies & Evidence from Process Mining**

| **Pathology** | **Evidence from Process Mining** | **Impact** |
|--------------|----------------------------------|------------|
| **Bottleneck Machines** | **Bottleneck Analysis (Token Replay):** Machines with long queues and high utilization. | Delays propagate to downstream tasks, increasing WIP. |
| **Poor Prioritization** | **Variant Analysis:** Late high-priority jobs (`Priority=High`) stuck behind low-priority ones. | Missed due dates, customer penalties. |
| **Suboptimal Sequencing** | **Setup Time Matrix:** Frequent high-setup-time job pairs at critical machines. | Excessive setup time  reduced throughput. |
| **Downstream Starvation** | **Resource Profile:** Some machines idle while others are overloaded. | Unbalanced workload  inefficient capacity use. |
| **Bullwhip Effect in WIP** | **WIP Trend Analysis:** Spikes in queue lengths due to upstream variability. | Unpredictable lead times, high inventory costs. |

### **2.2 Diagnostic Techniques**
- **Bottleneck Detection:** Use **Token Replay** to identify machines where jobs spend the most time waiting.
- **Conformance Checking:** Compare actual job flows against optimal routes to detect unnecessary detours.
- **Deviation Mining:** Identify jobs that frequently miss due dates and trace their path to root causes (e.g., long setups, breakdowns).
- **Resource Contention Analysis:** Overlay machine utilization with job priorities to see if high-priority jobs are delayed by low-priority ones.

## **3. Root Cause Analysis of Scheduling Ineffectiveness**

### **3.1 Root Causes**
| **Root Cause** | **Process Mining Evidence** | **Explanation** |
|---------------|----------------------------|----------------|
| **Static Dispatching Rules** | **Rule-Based vs. Actual Flow:** Jobs follow FCFS/EDD blindly, ignoring dynamic shop floor state. | No real-time adaptation to breakdowns or urgent jobs. |
| **Lack of Real-Time Visibility** | **Queue Length Variability:** Machines alternate between starvation and overload. | No centralized view of machine availability or WIP levels. |
| **Inaccurate Time Estimations** | **Planned vs. Actual Duration Mismatch:** Large deviations in `Task Duration (Planned)` vs. `Actual`. | Schedules are based on unreliable estimates. |
| **Poor Setup Time Management** | **High Setup Time Variability:** Some job sequences take 2x longer than others. | No optimization for setup reduction. |
| **Uncoordinated Work Centers** | **Handovers & Starvation:** Downstream machines idle while upstream is overloaded. | No synchronization between workstations. |
| **Reactive Disruption Handling** | **Breakdown Impact:** Long recovery times after unplanned downtime. | No predictive rescheduling. |

### **3.2 Differentiating Scheduling vs. Capacity Issues**
- **Process Mining Approach:**
  - **If bottlenecks persist even with optimal sequencing  Capacity constraint.**
  - **If tardiness reduces when using better dispatching rules  Scheduling issue.**
  - **If setup times vary widely based on sequence  Poor sequencing strategy.**
  - **If breakdowns cause prolonged delays  Need for predictive maintenance + rescheduling.**

## **4. Advanced Data-Driven Scheduling Strategies**

### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Multi-Factor Dispatching Rules**
**Core Logic:**
- Replace static FCFS/EDD with **weighted, adaptive rules** considering:
  - **Due date urgency** (time to due date / remaining processing time).
  - **Job priority** (High > Medium > Low).
  - **Estimated setup time** (from historical matrix).
  - **Downstream machine load** (avoid overloading next bottleneck).
  - **Critical ratio** (`(Due Date - Current Time) / Remaining Processing Time`).

**Process Mining Inputs:**
- Historical setup time matrix.
- Machine utilization trends.
- Tardiness patterns by job type.

**Expected Impact:**
- ** Tardiness** (better prioritization of urgent jobs).
- ** Setup Times** (sequencing to minimize transitions).
- ** Utilization** (balanced workload across machines).

---

### **Strategy 2: Predictive Scheduling with Simulation-Based Optimization**
**Core Logic:**
- Use **Monte Carlo simulation** with:
  - **Task duration distributions** (from process mining).
  - **Breakdown probabilities** (from MES logs).
  - **Setup time predictions** (from historical data).
- Generate **stochastic schedules** and select the one with the **lowest expected tardiness**.
- **Dynamic Rescheduling:** Re-optimize when disruptions occur (e.g., breakdowns, new hot jobs).

**Process Mining Inputs:**
- Probability distributions of task times.
- Machine failure rates.
- Historical disruption patterns.

**Expected Impact:**
- ** Lead Time Variability** (more accurate predictions).
- ** WIP** (better synchronization between workstations).
- ** Robustness to Disruptions** (proactive rescheduling).

---

### **Strategy 3: Setup Time Minimization via Intelligent Batching**
**Core Logic:**
- **Group similar jobs** (based on material, tooling, geometry) to **minimize setup times** at bottleneck machines.
- **At bottleneck machines:**
  - **Batch jobs with identical setup requirements.**
  - **Use a Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) approach** to find the optimal sequence minimizing total setup time.
- **At non-bottleneck machines:** Use simpler rules (e.g., EDD).

**Process Mining Inputs:**
- Setup time matrix (job pair  setup duration).
- Bottleneck machine identification.

**Expected Impact:**
- ** Total Setup Time** (by 20-40%, based on historical data).
- ** Throughput** (more jobs processed in the same time).
- ** WIP** (faster turnaround at bottlenecks).

**Approach:**

  - **Process models** (from process mining).
  - **Task time distributions** (fitted from logs).
  - **Breakdown rates** (from MES).
  - **Setup time models** (from historical data).
- **Test Scenarios:**
  1. **Baseline (Current Rules)**  Measure KPIs (tardiness, WIP, utilization).
  2. **Strategy 1 (Dynamic Dispatching)**  Compare improvement.
  3. **Strategy 2 (Predictive Scheduling)**  Test under high load & disruptions.
  4. **Strategy 3 (Setup Optimization)**  Validate setup time reduction.
  5. **Stress Tests:**
     - High job arrival rate.
     - Frequent breakdowns.
     - Mix of high/low-priority jobs.

**Key Metrics to Compare:**
- **Average Tardiness**
- **% Jobs On-Time**
- **WIP Levels**
- **Machine Utilization**
- **Makespan**

   - **Dashboard** showing:
     - Current WIP levels.

     - Predicted tardiness.

     - Update the **setup time matrix** and **task duration models** monthly using new MES data.
