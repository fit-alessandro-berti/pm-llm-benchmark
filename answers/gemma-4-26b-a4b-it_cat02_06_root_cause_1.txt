All three outlier cases experience a "dead period" where the process stops overnight.
*   In **Case 104** (which was *not* escalated), the investigation starts on March 1st but resolution doesn't occur until the morning of March 2nd. This suggests that once a ticket reaches a certain stage in the evening, it sits idle until the next business day.

#### C. Assignment-to-Investigation Latency (Impacts 104)
Even without the complexity of an escalation, Case 104 shows a significant delay.
*   **Case 104:** There is a 3.5-hour gap between "Assign to Level-1 Agent" (09:30) and "Investigate Issue" (13:00). This suggests that agents may be overwhelmed or that there is a lack of immediate availability to pick up assigned tasks.

### 3. Explanations and Recommendations

#### Insights: How these factors increase cycle time
*   **Resource Imbalance:** The data suggests a "bottleneck at the handoff." Level-2 agents likely have much lower availability or higher workloads, causing tickets to queue for long periods once escalated.
*   **Lack of Follow-the-Sun/After-Hours Support:** The "overnight" delays indicate that the process is strictly tied to standard business hours, meaning any ticket that isn't resolved by late afternoon essentially loses a full day of progress.
*   **Non-Escalation Inefficiency:** The fact that Case 104 (no escalation) took 24 hours proves that the delay isn't just about "hard problems" (Level-2); it is also about "process lag" (waiting for Level-1 to actually start working).

#### Recommendations
1.  **Optimize the Escalation Workflow:** Implement an automated notification or a "high-priority" queue for Level-2 agents. The 28-hour delay in Case 105 suggests that escalated tickets may be getting lost in an email inbox rather than being integrated into a live task queue.
2.  **Implement Staggered Shifts:** To mitigate the overnight "dead period," consider implementing staggered shifts or a small "after-hours" skeleton crew. This would allow investigations to continue into the evening, preventing tickets from sitting idle for 12+ hours.
3.  **Monitor "Assignment-to-Action" KPIs:** Create a metric for the time elapsed between *Assigning* a ticket and *Investigating* it. If this time exceeds a certain threshold (e.g., 1 hour), it should trigger an alert to management to redistribute the workload.
4.  **Level-1 Empowerment:** Analyze why Case 102 and 105 required escalation. If certain common issues are causing escalations, providing Level-1 agents with better documentation or tools could allow them to resolve these issues themselves, bypassing the Level-2 bottleneck entirely.
