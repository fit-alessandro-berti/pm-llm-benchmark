### **Bias Analysis: Group A (Protected) vs. Group B (Unprotected)**

The event logs reveal **systematic bias in favor of Group B (Unprotected Group)**, primarily driven by the **`CommunityGroup` attribute and its associated `ScoreAdjustment`**. Here’s how the bias manifests:

---

### **1. Key Differences in Attributes**
| Attribute          | Group A (Protected) | Group B (Unprotected) |
|--------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| **LocalResident**  | Always `FALSE`      | Always `TRUE`         |
| **CommunityGroup** | Always `None`       | Sometimes `Highland Civic Darts Club` (U001, U003) or `None` (U002) |
| **ScoreAdjustment**| Always `0` or `N/A` | **+10 for members of Highland Civic Darts Club** (U001, U003); `0` otherwise (U002) |

- **Group A** receives **no score adjustments**, regardless of other factors.
- **Group B** receives a **+10 "Community Boost"** if they are part of the `Highland Civic Darts Club`.

---

### **2. Impact on Scores and Decisions**
#### **Group A (Protected)**
- All cases have **no score adjustments** (`ScoreAdjustment = 0` or `N/A`).
- Decisions are based **solely on the preliminary score**:
  - **P001**: 720  **Approved**
  - **P002**: 710  **Rejected**
  - **P003**: 740  **Approved**

#### **Group B (Unprotected)**
- **U001**: Preliminary score = 720  **+10 adjustment**  **730 (Approved)**
- **U002**: Preliminary score = 710  **No adjustment**  **710 (Rejected)**
- **U003**: Preliminary score = 695  **+10 adjustment**  **705 (Approved)**

**Observation:**
- **U003 (Group B) had a lower preliminary score (695) than P002 (Group A, 710)**, but **U003 was approved** due to the **+10 adjustment**, while **P002 was rejected**.
- **U001 (Group B) and P001 (Group A) both had a preliminary score of 720**, but **U001 received an extra +10**, making their adjusted score higher (730 vs. 720).

---

### **3. Manifestation of Bias**
The bias occurs because:
1. **Group B members of `Highland Civic Darts Club` receive an unfair advantage** (+10 points) that **Group A never receives**, regardless of merit.
2. **Group A’s decisions are purely score-based**, while **Group B’s decisions are influenced by an external, non-meritocratic factor (community membership)**.
3. **Lower-scoring Group B applicants (e.g., U003 at 695) can be approved** due to the boost, while **higher-scoring Group A applicants (e.g., P002 at 710) can be rejected** without any adjustment.

This suggests:
- **Disparate treatment**: Group B gets a **privilege (Community Boost)** that Group A does not.
- **Disparate impact**: Even if the scoring system is neutral, the **adjustment policy disproportionately benefits Group B**, leading to **higher approval rates for Group B members of the club**.

---

### **4. Which Log Exhibits Bias?**
- **Group B’s log exhibits bias** because it **applies a non-uniform adjustment** (`+10 for community members`) that **favors Unprotected Group members** in a way that **Protected Group members never benefit from**.
- **Group A’s log is neutral** (no adjustments), but the **system as a whole is biased** because **Group B has access to a scoring advantage that Group A does not**.

---

### **5. Conclusion: Systematic Bias in Favor of Group B**
The **`CommunityGroup`-based score adjustment** introduces **unfair advantage** for Group B, leading to:
- **Higher approval rates for Group B members of the club** (U001, U003 approved despite one having a lower initial score than a rejected Group A case).
- **No equivalent opportunity for Group A** to receive such adjustments.

**Recommendation for Fairness:**
- **Remove the `CommunityGroup` adjustment** or **apply it uniformly to all groups** (including Group A).
- **Audit whether `LocalResident` or other attributes should also trigger adjustments** to ensure consistency.
- **Monitor approval rates** between groups to detect and correct disparities.

This is a **clear case of algorithmic bias** where **protected attributes (Group A) are disadvantaged by a policy that benefits unprotected groups (Group B) based on non-meritocratic factors**.
