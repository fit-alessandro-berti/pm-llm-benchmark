**Step4 – Brief justification of the naming choices**

- **Initial Document Verification** vs. **PostSubmission / PostAdjustment Document Verification** – The qualifier “Initial” marks the first identity check that occurs immediately after the request is logged. The later checks happen *after* the customer has supplied extra information (C001) *or* after an internal account correction (C003), therefore they are prefixed with “PostSubmission” or “PostAdjustment” to signal the trigger that prompted the recheck.

- **Clarification Call – Account Discrepancy** vs. **Confirmation Call – Adjustment Verification** – Both are outbound calls, but the first is prompted by a need to *clarify* an unclear account detail uncovered during verification. The second occurs *after* the backoffice has performed an adjustment; its purpose is to *confirm* with the customer that the change is correct. The added descriptors make the intent of each call explicit.

These contextspecific names eliminate ambiguity when the same activity label appears more than once within a single case, allowing analysts (or automated discovery techniques) to distinguish the different process steps based on when and why they occur.
