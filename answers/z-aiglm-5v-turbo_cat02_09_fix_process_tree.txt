**1. Issues with the Current Model**

The current process tree uses a `PARALLEL` operator (`AND`) for the five middle activities (`create_po`, `receive_goods`, `receive_invoice`, `match_invoice`, `pay_invoice`). This means:

- **No enforced order among those five activities** – they can occur in any interleaving or concurrently.
- **`create_po` is not guaranteed to run before** `receive_goods` or `receive_invoice`.  
  (In reality, the purchase order must exist before goods can be received against it and before an invoice referencing it is processed.)
- **`match_invoice` may execute before** both `receive_goods` and `receive_invoice` have happened, or even **before the invoice is received**, which breaks the business rule that matching requires both the goods receipt and the invoice.
- **`pay_invoice` can occur before matching**, before receiving goods, or before receiving the invoice – clearly invalid.
- **`close_case` runs only after all five activities finish**, but the allowed internal disorder makes many traces nonconforming to the standard ProcuretoPay flow.

In short, the model is **underfitting**: it admits too many behavior patterns, including ones that violate the logical dependencies of the domain.

root = ProcessTree(

**3. How the Fixed Model Enforces Correct Behavior**

The updated tree preserves all original activities but restructures their relationships to reflect the true ProcuretoPay logic:

| Step | Operator / Position | Enforced Constraint |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|
| `Create_Purchase_Requisition` | First child of the outer sequence | Must be the very first activity. |
| `Approve_Purchase_Requisition` | Second child | Immediately follows requisition creation. |
| `Create_Purchase_Order` | Third child | Runs **after approval** and **before anything else** (goods receipt, invoice receipt). |
| `Receive_Goods` & `Receive_Invoice` | Inside a `PARALLEL` block placed fourth | Both **must** occur, but their relative order is free (they can interleave or run concurrently). | `Match_Invoice_to_PO` | Fifth child | Executes **only after** both goods and invoice have been received. |

- Any activity occurring out of the prescribed logical order  blocked by the combination of sequencing and the parallel synchronisation point.
