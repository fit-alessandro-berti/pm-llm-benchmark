### 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

Instance-spanning constraints rarely act in isolation; they interact, often creating compounding bottlenecks.

*   **Priority Handling + Shared Cold-Packing:** If an Express order requires cold packing, it will preempt or jump the queue for the already scarce C-stations. *   **Priority Handling + Batching:** If Express orders are batched alongside Standard orders, the urgency of the Express order might force the batch to dispatch before it reaches optimal size, reducing shipping efficiency for the Standard orders left behind.

Understanding these interactions is crucial because **local optimization can cause global degradation**. For example, increasing batch sizes to improve shipping truck utilization might inadvertently cause more frequent HazMat cap violations, paralyzing the Packing floor.

#### Strategy 2: Segregated Virtual Queuing and "Breakable" Reservations for Cold-Packing

*   **Expected Outcomes:** Eliminates the hidden waste of context switching, guarantees SLA for Express Cold orders without completely starving Standard orders, and significantly reduces the variance in processing times.
