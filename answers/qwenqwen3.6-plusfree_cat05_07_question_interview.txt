Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to clarify roles, decision criteria, sequencing, timing, and exception handling within the property onboarding process:

**Documentation & Initial Intake**
1. Could you walk me through how your team defines “outdated” versus “missing” documentation, and what the typical follow-up cadence looks like when requesting updates from landlords?
2. When a landlord is unable or unwilling to provide required compliance documents, what escalation path or alternative approval process exists before deciding whether to pause or decline onboarding?

**Data Entry & Validation**
3. Once the coordinator records property details into the central database, what validation or review steps occur before that information is passed to marketing, leasing, or compliance teams?
4. Are there specific data attributes or property characteristics that frequently cause misalignment between regional offices and central systems, and how do you resolve those discrepancies?

**Property Manager Assignment & Handoff**
5. How does the organization weigh workload, geography, experience, and landlord-specific requirements when assigning a property manager, and is there a formal scoring or decision framework used?
6. What happens if the assigned property manager becomes unavailable, or if the introductory meeting reveals a mismatch in expectations or communication style?

**Inspection, Repairs & Readiness**
7. When an inspector identifies issues, how do you determine which items must be resolved before marketing can begin versus those that can be addressed later or disclosed to tenants?
8. Who holds the authority to approve repair scopes, contractor selection, and associated costs, and how is that financial and operational decision communicated back to the landlord?

**Marketing, Pricing & Activation**
9. How do you navigate situations where the internal pricing model’s recommended range differs from the landlord’s expectations or current market realities?
10. What specific checklist or set of conditions must be satisfied before a property is officially “activated” in the rental portal, and can activation ever occur in a phased or conditional manner?

**Tenant Screening & Lease Negotiation**
11. Beyond standard credit and employment checks, what qualitative or quantitative thresholds define a “suitable” tenant, and who ultimately makes the final selection decision?
12. When multiple qualified applicants are competing for the same unit, how do you balance promotional incentives, lease-term preferences, and fair housing compliance during the negotiation phase?

**Compliance, Audits & Quality Assurance**
13. Are the compliance, financial, and IT audits conducted sequentially or in parallel, and what is the remediation process if an audit flags an issue after the lease has already been executed?
14. How are custom lease clauses or special insurance requirements evaluated for legal, financial, and operational risk, and which stakeholders must formally approve them before signing?

**Timing, Sequencing & Communication**
15. What does the typical end-to-end timeline look like from initial landlord contact to tenant move-in, and where do you most frequently encounter bottlenecks or rework?
16. Given the number of internal teams, external vendors, and landlords involved, what mechanisms do you use to ensure seamless handoffs and maintain a single source of truth across email, CRM, and project management tools?

**Exceptions & Process Evolution**
17. Could you describe a recent edge case or exception that significantly deviated from the standard onboarding flow, and how the team adapted to manage it without disrupting the broader portfolio?
18. How does the organization capture feedback from landlords, property managers, and support teams to iteratively refine this onboarding process over time?
