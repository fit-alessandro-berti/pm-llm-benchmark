## 1. Recurring activities with changed meaning

The main repeated activity names within the same case are:

| Case ID | Original Activity | Occurrence | Contextual Meaning | Renamed Activity |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| C001 | Verify Documents | 1st | Initial identity-document check after online request submission | **Verify Documents — Initial Identity Check** |
| C001 | Verify Documents | 2nd | Re-check after the customer uploads the missing ID scan | **Verify Documents — Recheck After ID Upload** |
| C002 | Call Customer | 1st | Call made to clarify an account discrepancy | **Call Customer — Clarify Account Discrepancy** |
| C002 | Call Customer | 2nd | Follow-up call after internal account adjustments | **Call Customer — Confirm Account Adjustments** |
| C003 | Verify Documents | 1st | Initial physical ID verification for walk-in customer | **Verify Documents — Initial In-Branch ID Check** |
| C003 | Verify Documents | 2nd | Re-verification after internal account correction | **Verify Documents — Recheck After Account Correction** |

I also renamed some non-repeated but context-sensitive activities, such as different types of customer calls and request initiation channels, to make the revised log more semantically clear.

## 3. Brief justification of naming choices

- **“Verify Documents” in C001** was split into an initial check and a later recheck because the second occurrence happens only after the customer submits the missing ID scan.
- **“Call Customer” in C002** was split into a discrepancy-clarification call and a confirmation call because the first call triggers the account adjustment, while the second validates that the adjustment is acceptable.
- **“Verify Documents” in C003** was split into an initial in-branch physical ID check and a post-adjustment recheck because the second verification occurs after back-office correction.
- **Customer calls were renamed by purpose** because “Call Customer” covers very different actions: requesting documents, clarifying an account issue, confirming adjustments, or asking a customer to return to the branch.
- **Request initiation was renamed by channel** because online portal submissions and branch walk-in intake represent different operational contexts.
- **Approval remained essentially unchanged** because all approval events represent the same final supervisory approval step.
