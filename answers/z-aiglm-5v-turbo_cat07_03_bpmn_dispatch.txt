This is a **BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation)** diagram illustrating the **"Dispatch of Goods" process** for a Computer Hardware Shop. The visualization depicts a cross-functional workflow involving three departments (swimlanes): **Logistics**, **Secretary**, and **Warehouse**.

## Key Components & Flow

### 1. Process Initiation (Secretary Lane)
- **Start Event**: "Ship goods" triggers the process
- **Parallel Split (AND Gateway)**: The process immediately divides into two concurrent workstreams:
  - *Administrative track* (remains in Secretary lane)
  - *Physical operations track* (moves to Warehouse lane)

### 2. Administrative Track (Secretary)
The secretary handles documentation and carrier coordination:

- **Clarify shipment method**: Determines shipping requirements
- **Decision Point - Special Handling?** (XOR Gateway):
  - **NO**: Proceeds to verify insurance requirements
    - Accesses **"Order details"** document and **"Customer information"** database
    - **Check if insurance is required**  Conditional branch:
      - *If needed*: Routes to **Logistics** lane for **"Insure parcel"** execution, then returns
      - *Always*: **"Write package label"** (occurs regardless of insurance status)
  - **YES**: Requires specialized logistics procurement
    - **"Get 3 offers from logistic companies"**  **"Select logistic company and place order"**

### 3. Physical Operations Track (Warehouse)
Concurrent with administrative tasks, the warehouse prepares the shipment:

- **"Package goods"**: Physical packing of items
  - **Exception Handling**: Attached **Timer Event (48 hours)** — If packaging exceeds 48 hours, the system automatically triggers **"Notify customer about delay"** before proceeding
- Flows to the final synchronization point

### 4. Synchronization & Completion
- **Parallel Join (AND Gateway)**: Both tracks (administrative/packaging) must complete before continuing
- **Final Steps**:
  - **Fill out Shipment paperwork**
  - **Move package to pickup area**
- **End Event**: "Shipment prepared" (terminal state)

## Notable Features
- **Cross-functional collaboration**: Handoffs between Secretary (coordination), Logistics (insurance), and Warehouse (physical handling)
- **Conditional routing**: Insurance is optional based on value/risk assessment; special handling requires vendor bidding
- **Exception management**: Automated customer notification for delays exceeding 48-hour SLA threshold
- **Data integration**: References external data stores (customer info) and artifacts (order documents) during decision-making

This process ensures that administrative preparation (labeling, insurance, carrier selection) occurs in parallel with physical warehousing activities, optimizing cycle time while maintaining quality controls through mandatory synchronization checkpoints before final handoff.
