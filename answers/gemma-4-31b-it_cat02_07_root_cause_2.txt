### 2. Root Cause Analysis

By correlating the durations with the attributes (Resource, Region, Complexity), the following patterns emerge:

#### A. Complexity and the "Documentation Loop"
There is a direct linear correlation between the **Complexity** level and the number of times the "Request Additional Documents" activity is performed:
*   **Low Complexity:** 0 requests $\rightarrow$ Duration: $\approx$ 1.5 hours.
*   **Medium Complexity:** 1 request $\rightarrow$ Duration: $\approx$ 26 hours.
*   **High Complexity:** 2–3 requests $\rightarrow$ Duration: 48–77 hours.

**Root Cause:** The primary driver of delay is not the processing time of the activities themselves, but the **rework loop** created by requesting additional documentation. Each single request for documents adds roughly 24+ hours to the total lead time.

#### B. Regional Performance Variance
Comparing the two High-complexity cases:
*   **Case 2003 (Region A):** High Complexity $\rightarrow$ 2 requests $\rightarrow$ 48 hours.
*   **Case 2005 (Region B):** High Complexity $\rightarrow$ 3 requests $\rightarrow$ 77 hours.

**Root Cause:** Region B appears to be performing worse than Region A. Not only did Case 2005 require more document requests than Case 2003, but the time elapsed between those requests was longer.

#### C. Resource Analysis
**Adjuster_Lisa** handled both Case 2002 (Medium) and Case 2005 (High). Both involved multiple document requests and extended durations. While **Adjuster_Mike** also requested documents in Case 2003, Lisa's cases show a higher frequency of requests per case.

---

### 3. Explanations and Proposed Mitigations

#### Why is this happening?
1.  **Inefficient Intake:** The fact that High-complexity claims require multiple separate requests for documents suggests that the initial "Submit Claim" phase is not capturing all necessary data. The adjuster is discovering missing information piecemeal rather than identifying all requirements upfront.
2.  **Regional Friction:** The longer delays in Region B could be due to several factors: slower customer response times in that geographic area, a lack of digital submission tools in that region, or a lack of training for resources (like Adjuster_Lisa) on how to request all documents in one go.

#### Proposed Mitigations:
*   **Implement Dynamic Intake Forms:** Instead of a generic submission form, implement a smart form that changes based on the claim type. If a claim is flagged as "High Complexity," the system should mandate the upload of all known required documents before the claim can be submitted.
*   **Standardize "Documentation Checklists":** Provide Adjusters with a comprehensive checklist for Medium and High-complexity claims. This ensures that when they perform the "Evaluate Claim" step, they request **all** missing documents in one single communication rather than multiple iterative requests.
*   **Targeted Training for Region B:** Conduct a process review in Region B to determine why document turnaround is slower. If the issue is the resource, provide training to Adjuster_Lisa on "First-Time-Right" evaluation techniques.
*   **SLA Alerts:** Implement automated alerts for any case that enters the "Request Additional Documents" loop more than once, triggering a manager review to see if the claim can be expedited or if the customer needs assistance.
