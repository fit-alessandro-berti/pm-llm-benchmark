### Business Process Explanation:

---

#### **1. Initiation Phase:**
- **Start Event  Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**  
  The process begins when a customer submits a request (e.g., an order or inquiry). The system captures the request details here.

---

#### **2. Request Type Determination (XOR Gateway):**  
The first decision point (XOR Gateway) routes the request into one of two paths based on the **request type**:  
- **Standard Request**: Typically predefined products/services with known parameters (e.g., off-the-shelf items).  
- **Custom Request**: Tailored solutions requiring additional analysis (e.g., bespoke products or complex services).  

---

#### **3. Standard Request Path**  
**Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**  
- Validates the request against predefined criteria (e.g., customer eligibility, order limits).  

**AND Gateway  Parallel Checks (Tasks C1 & C2):**  
- **Task C1: "Credit Check"**: Assesses the customer’s creditworthiness.  
- **Task C2: "Inventory Check"**: Confirms availability of required inventory.  
- The **AND Gateway (Join)** ensures both checks complete before proceeding.  

**Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**  
- Determines the expected delivery timeline based on inventory status and production lead times.  

---

#### **4. Custom Request Path**  
**Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**  
- Evaluates technical, logistical, or cost feasibility of fulfilling the custom request.  

**XOR Gateway: "Is Customization Feasible?"**  
- **Yes**: Proceed to **Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"**, which creates a detailed offer including costs, timelines, and terms.  
- **No**: Execute **Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"**  **End Event**. The process terminates here for unfeasible requests.  

---

#### **5. Convergence Point: Approval Check (XOR Gateway)**  
After completing either the Standard or Custom path, the process routes to a second **XOR Gateway** to determine:  
- **"Is Approval Needed?"**  
  - **Yes**: Triggers **Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"** (e.g., for high-value orders or complex customizations).  
    - **Approval Granted**: Proceed to **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**.  
    - **Approval Denied**:  
      - **Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"** adjusts the proposal (e.g., revises quotation or delivery date).  
      - **Loop Back**: Returns to either:  
        - **Task E1** (for Custom paths to rework the quotation) **OR**  
        - **Task D** (for Standard paths to reassess delivery timelines or terms).  
  - **No Approval Needed**: Bypasses Task F and directly proceeds to **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**.  

---

#### **6. Finalization and Closure**  
- **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**: Creates the invoice for the customer.  
- **Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**: Notifies the customer of approval, delivery, or next steps.  
- **End Event**: Process concludes successfully unless rejected earlier.  

---

### **Key Components and Logic**  
#### **Gateways Explained**  
- **XOR (Exclusive) Gateways**:  
  - Route the process into **one of two mutually exclusive paths** (e.g., Standard vs. Custom, Approval Granted vs. Denied).  
- **AND Gateway**:  
  - Splits the process into **parallel paths** (Tasks C1 and C2 run simultaneously). The Join merges them afterward.  

#### **Divergence and Convergence**  
- **Divergence**: The process splits into Standard/Custom paths initially and again for approvals.  
- **Converge**: Both paths merge at the **Approval Needed?** Gateway before finalizing.  

#### **Loops and Revisions**  
- **Re-evaluation Loop (Task H  E1/D)**:  
  - Triggers when approval is denied. It allows iterative adjustments until the proposal meets requirements.  
  - Ensures alignment with managerial expectations or constraints.

### **Task Differentiation Between Standard and Custom**

| **Validation/Analysis**       | Standard Validation (B1)                  | Custom Feasibility Analysis (B2)                 |  
| **Critical Checks**          | Parallel Credit + Inventory Checks (C1/C2) | Feasibility Check (B2) followed by a binary outcome |  
| **Deadline Calculation**      | Calculated post-checks (Task D)            | Not required; replaced by quotation (E1)         |  
| **Rejection Handling**        | No direct rejection path; only via approval denial | Direct rejection (E2) if feasibility fails upfront |

### **Approval and Rework Triggers**  
- **Approval Needed?** depends on predefined criteria (e.g., order value exceeding a threshold, complexity).  
- **Rework (Task H)** occurs only if approval is denied. It loops back to adjust:  
  - **Custom**: Revise the quotation (E1) to reduce costs or scope.  
  - **Standard**: Adjust delivery dates (Task D) or terms to secure approval.

1. **Input**: Customer request.  
2. **Analyze Type  Validate/Feasibility Check  Parallel Execution (Standard) or Binary Outcome (Custom)**.

4. **Output**: Invoice and confirmation sent if successful; rejection notice if failed.  

This structure balances efficiency (parallel checks for standards) with rigorous analysis for custom requests while maintaining managerial oversight via approvals.
