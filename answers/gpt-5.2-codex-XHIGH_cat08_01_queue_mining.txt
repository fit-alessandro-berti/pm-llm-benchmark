# 1. Queue Identification & Characterization

### **How to calculate waiting times**
Using the event log’s **START** and **COMPLETE** timestamps:

- **Service time** for activity *A*:  
  \[
  Service(A) = Complete(A) - Start(A)
  \]
- **Waiting time (queue time)** before activity *A* for a given visit:  
  \[
  Queue(A) = Start(A) - Complete(Previous\ Activity)
  \]

If activities can occur in parallel, the waiting time is computed from the time the patient becomes *ready* for the next activity (e.g., completion of predecessor) to the start of that activity.  
For the first activity (Registration), waiting time is ideally **Start(Registration) – Arrival/Appointment time** (if recorded); otherwise queue time before registration cannot be computed directly.

### **Key metrics to characterize queues**
For each activity (and optionally each resource), compute:

- **Average waiting time**
- **Median waiting time**
- **90th/95th percentile waiting time**
- **Maximum waiting time**
- **Standard deviation** (variability)
- **% of visits exceeding SLA thresholds** (e.g., >15 min)
- **Queue frequency** (how often patients wait before the activity)
- **Contribution to total visit duration** (queue time share of throughput)
- **Queue length / Work-In-Process (WIP)** over time (using arrival/service timestamps)

### **Identify critical queues**
Rank queues using a combined priority score such as:

- **High average or P90 waiting time**
- **High frequency** (affects many patients)
- **High impact on total visit duration**
- **High SLA violations**
- **High impact on urgent/new patients**

A simple prioritization metric might be:

\[
Priority = (Average\ Wait \times Frequency) \times Severity\ Weight
\]

# 2. Root Cause Analysis

Once critical queues are identified, use processmining methods to identify **why** they occur.

### **Potential Root Causes**
1. **Resource bottlenecks**
   - Staff or equipment at >85–90% utilization
   - Long queues when specific nurses/doctors or rooms are unavailable

2. **Activity dependencies & handovers**
   - Delays between nurse assessment and doctor consult
   - Handover inefficiencies or waiting for approvals

3. **Service time variability**
   - New patients or urgent cases have much longer consultations
   - High variance destabilizes queues

4. **Appointment scheduling issues**
   - Overbooking or clustering of arrivals (e.g., 9–10 a.m.)
   - Insufficient slots for highcomplexity patients

5. **Arrival patterns**
   - Early-morning surges vs low midday demand

6. **Patient segmentation**
   - New vs followup vs urgent show very different queue profiles

### **Process Mining Techniques to Pinpoint Causes**
- **Performance overlay on process model** (highlight long queues)
- **Resource utilization analysis** (staff/room workloads by hour)
- **Handover analysis** (delays between roles)
- **Variant analysis** (different flow patterns by specialty/patient type)
- **Timeofday heatmaps** (queue peaks)
- **Conformance analysis** (detect deviations/rework loops)

# 3. DataDriven Optimization Strategies

Below are **three concrete, distinct strategies**, each tied to specific queues and root causes:

---

### **Strategy 1: DemandDriven Staffing & Resource Pooling**
**Target queues:** Registration, Nurse Assessment, Checkout  
**Root cause:** Undercapacity during peak arrival hours  
**Data evidence:** Queue spikes 9–11 a.m.; staff utilization >90%

**Actions**
- Add or shift clerks/nurses into peak hours
- Crosstrain staff to flex between registration and checkout
- Open extra exam rooms in peak windows

**Expected impact**
- 30–60% reduction in average registration/nurse wait
- Lower P90 waits during peak periods

---

### **Strategy 2: Appointment Scheduling Optimization & Patient Segmentation**
**Target queues:** Doctor Consultation, Diagnostics  
**Root cause:** Poor match between scheduled slot length and actual service time; clustered arrivals  
**Data evidence:** New patients take 2× consult time; high wait for doctor in early morning

**Actions**
- Use historical logs to predict consultation time by patient type
- Allocate longer slots for new/urgent; shorter for followups
- Stagger arrivals rather than batch on the hour
- Create a **“fasttrack”** for lowcomplexity followups

**Expected impact**
- 20–30% reduction in doctor queue wait
- More consistent flow, fewer extreme waits

---

### **Strategy 3: Process Redesign & Parallelization**
**Target queues:** Registration  Nurse; Doctor  Diagnostics  
**Root cause:** Sequential task structure and coordination delays  
**Data evidence:** Long waits between doctor consult and ECG/Lab even when equipment idle

**Actions**
- **Preregistration online** (insurance, history)
- **Self checkin kiosks** to reduce clerical time
- Allow labs/ECG **before** consult for certain visit types
- Centralized diagnostic scheduling with realtime queue visibility

**Expected impact**
- Reduce registration service time by 25–40%
- Cut diagnostic waiting by 15–20 minutes per visit

---

# 4. Tradeoffs & Constraints

| Strategy | Potential Tradeoffs |
|----------|----------------------|
| Extra staffing / flexible shifts | Higher labor cost, potential idle time if demand misestimated |
| Fasttrack segmentation | Perceived inequity, risk of delaying complex cases |
| Parallelization / previsit tasks | More process complexity, IT cost, risk of incomplete patient data |
| Scheduling changes | Patient convenience impacts, resistance from clinicians |
| Technology (kiosks, portals) | Upfront cost, digital divide concerns |

**Balancing Objectives**
- Use **simulation/whatif analysis** to test options
- Apply **costbenefit analysis** (wait reduction vs extra staffing cost)
- Pilot changes in one specialty before full rollout
- Maintain clinical quality by tracking medical outcomes

---

# 5. Measuring Success (KPIs & Monitoring)

### **Primary KPIs**
- **Average/median/P90 waiting time** per activity
- **Total visit duration**
- **% of patients meeting wait-time SLAs**
- **Queue length / WIP levels**
- **Resource utilization & overtime**
- **Patient satisfaction scores / complaints**
- **Noshow and cancellation rates**
- **Clinical quality indicators** (rework, repeat tests, safety events)

### **Ongoing Monitoring**
- Use processmining dashboards with live event logs
- Compare **pre vs post** implementation with statistical tests
- Use control charts to detect drift
- Segment KPIs by patient type, urgency, specialty, timeofday
- Trigger alerts when SLA thresholds are exceeded
