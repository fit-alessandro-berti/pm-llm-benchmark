### 2. Potential Root Causes of Performance Issues

I analyzed intra-case activity durations, waiting times (gaps between consecutive activities), and process deviations. Key patterns in long cases:

- **Escalations to Level-2 (L2) Agents** (present in 102, 105; absent in 101, 103, 104):
  - Case 102: 150-min wait after **Assign to Level-1 (L1)** (09:00–11:30) before **Escalate**, then 150-min wait (11:30–14:00) before L2 **Investigate**, followed by overnight delay to **Resolve** (14:00 to next day 09:00, ~19 hours).
  - Case 105: Quick L1 **Investigate** (09:10–10:00, 50 min) leads to **Escalate**, but then massive gap to L2 **Investigate** (03-01 10:00 to 03-02 14:00, ~28 hours including overnight), followed by another overnight to **Resolve** (14:00 to 03-03 09:00, ~19 hours).
  - **Impact**: Escalations introduce handoff delays (L1  L2 availability) and often trigger off-hours spillover.

- **Long Waiting/Idle Times Before Key Steps** (all long cases):
  - Case 102: Post-**Assign** idle (09:00–11:30, 150 min); post-**Escalate** idle (11:30–14:00, 150 min).
  - Case 104 (no escalation): Extreme post-**Assign** idle before **Investigate** (09:30–13:00, **210 min** or 3.5 hours), then overnight to **Resolve** (13:00 to next day 08:00, ~19 hours).
  - Case 105: Dominated by post-**Escalate** idle (~28 hours).
  - Quick cases (101, 103): Minimal waits (<60 min between any steps).

- **Overnight/Off-Hours Spans** (102, 104, 105):
  - All long cases cross into evenings/nights/weekends, with **Resolve** or **Investigate** deferred to next business day(s). No evidence of 24/7 coverage; activities halt after ~14:00–15:00.

- **Other Factors**:
  - No unnecessary loops/duplicates, but L1 **Investigate**  **Escalate** (105) suggests complex issues overwhelming L1.
  - Early steps (Receive  Triage  Assign) are consistently fast (<40 min) across all cases—no bottlenecks there.
  - **Investigate** and **Resolve** are the pain points, averaging >10 hours in long cases vs. <1 hour in quick ones.

### 3. How Factors Lead to Increased Cycle Times + Recommendations

- **Escalation Impact**: Adds ~20–28 hours per case due to L2 queueing/handoffs and off-hours gaps. Complex tickets (requiring escalation) aren't contained at L1, inflating totals by 10–20x.
- **Idle Times Impact**: Post-assignment/escalation waits (150–210 min) indicate agent unavailability, poor prioritization, or workload overload, directly adding hours before progress.
- **Overnight Spans Impact**: Business-hours-only processing turns minor delays into full-day multipliers (e.g., 14:00 end-of-day  next 09:00 = +19 hours). This compounds with other factors, turning 2–3 hour processes into multi-day ones.
- **Overall**: Long cases show a "delay cascade"—early waits  escalation/off-hours  exponential growth. Quick cases prove the baseline process is efficient (~2 hours) when unhindered.

**Insights & Recommendations**:
1. **Reduce Escalations (Target: <20% of cases)**:
   - Enhance L1 training/tools for common issues (e.g., knowledge base, AI triage).
   - Implement "escalation gates" requiring L1 justification to discourage overuse.

2. **Minimize Idle Times**:
   - Auto-assign tickets post-triage with SLAs (e.g., L1 start Investigate within 30 min; L2 within 60 min post-escalation).
   - Monitor agent workload dashboards to redistribute during peaks (e.g., 09:00–11:00 backlog in 102/104).

3. **Address Off-Hours Bottlenecks**:
   - Introduce on-call rotations or partial 24/7 L2 coverage for escalated tickets.
   - Set urgency-based SLAs (e.g., escalated = resolve within 24 hours total).

4. **Broader Process Improvements**:
   - **Bottleneck Heatmap**: Visualize waits (e.g., via process mining tools) to quantify Assign  Investigate as #1 hotspot.
   - **Pilot Self-Service**: Divert simple tickets pre-Receive to reduce volume.
   - **Metrics to Track**: Escalation rate, mean wait post-assign/escalate, % resolved same-day. Aim: Reduce average cycle to <4 hours.
   - **Quick Win**: Alert system for tickets idle >60 min post-assign.

Implementing these could cut long-case times by 50–70%, aligning all cases closer to the 101/103 baseline. If more logs are available, segment by ticket type/agent for deeper causality.
