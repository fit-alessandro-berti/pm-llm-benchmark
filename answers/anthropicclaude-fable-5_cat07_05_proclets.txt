# Description of the Visualization

This figure illustrates the **proclet framework** for modeling inter-process communication in a healthcare scenario (laboratory testing). It consists of three parts:

## (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels

The main diagram shows two Petri-net–style process models side by side:

**Lab visit proclet (left side):**
- Starts with a start node, followed by tasks: **Order entry** (performed by a doctor), **Take blood sample** (lab assistant), **Judge results** (lab assistant), then branches into **Everything fine** or **Do tests again**, with **Receive result** and **Receive updated result** as communication tasks, ending with **Send report** (lab assistant) and a final node.

**Lab test proclet (right side):**
- Starts with a start node, followed by: **Perform test** (chemical analyst), **Make report** (chemical analyst), then either **Finish lab test** or a loop through **Perform test again** and **Provide updated report** (both chemical analyst), ending at a final node.

**Two channels** (dashed rounded rectangles) connect the proclets:
- **Order system** — carries the test order from Lab visit to Lab test
- **HIS** (Hospital Information System) — carries results and re-test requests between the two processes

The small envelope-like ports on the channels are annotated with **cardinality/multiplicity labels** such as `*,1`, `1,*`, `1,?`, indicating how many instances send/receive messages. Human figures (icons) denote the roles responsible for each task (doctor, lab assistant, chemical analyst).

## (b) Class Diagram

A simple UML-style class diagram on the upper right shows the relationship between the two proclet classes:
- **Lab visit** *requires* **Lab test**
- Cardinalities: **1..1** on the Lab visit side and **1..\*** on the Lab test side (one lab visit can require one or more lab tests).

## (c) Example of a Performative

A table at the bottom shows a sample message (performative) exchanged via a channel, with columns:

| Time | Channel | Sender | Receivers | Action | Content | Scope | Direction |
|------|---------|--------|-----------|--------|---------|-------|-----------|
| 11:00 | Order system | Lab visit - John | Lab test – HGB John | Create | Can you perform a HGB test for John? | Private | OUT |

This exemplifies how one proclet instance (John's lab visit) creates and communicates with another proclet instance (an HGB blood test for John) through the Order system channel.
