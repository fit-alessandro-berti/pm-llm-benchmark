**2. Handling Interruptions and Context Switches**
The workflow shows clear multi-tasking: the user began Document1, switched to email, reviewed a PDF, updated budget figures, and returned to Document1. To preserve this narrative:
- **SWITCH events** were assigned to the case being *left* (e.g., "Suspend for Research" in DOC-001) to mark the pause in that workflow
- **FOCUS events** after a switch were treated as "Resume Editing" or "Open Document" for the target case
- This creates a "ping-pong" pattern visible in DOC-001, showing the document was worked on in two distinct sessions with a research gap in between

**3. Activity Naming Hierarchy**
Low-level system actions were abstracted into business-meaningful activities using three layers:

- **Context-specific editing**: Rather than generic "Typing," I used "Draft Introduction," "Insert References," "Update Financial Data," and "Draft Executive Summary" based on the content attributes and document context
- **Navigation/Review**: "Review Content" for scrolling, "Annotate Document" for highlighting, and "Suspend for [X]" for context switches
