     The machine is inactive, with no operational activity. This represents startup, cooldown, or reset phases between cycles.

#### 3. **Quality Inspection (Events 7–8)**

     - Energy holds at 2 kWh (moderate power for sensors/scanners).  
   - **Why this label?**  
     The moderate vibration and energy suggest active but non-destructive testing (e.g., vision systems or sensors verifying part dimensions). The drop in temperature and material flow indicates a transition from cutting to a less intensive phase.

#### 4. **Welding (Event 9)**

     - Temperature spikes to 80°C (localized heating for fusion).  
     - Vibration peaks at 50 Hz (high-frequency welding tool action).  
     - Material flow drops to 0 (no new material; focus on joining).  
     - Tool position holds at 15 mm (fixed welding position).  
     - Energy surges to 5 kWh (intensive power for heating/joining).

     The extreme heat, vibration, and energy are hallmark signs of welding. Pressure remains at 2 bar, likely for clamping or shielding gas, confirming a fusion process.

#### 5. **Assembling Parts (Events 10–12)**

### Process Flow Summary
The inferred process sequence is:  
**Idle  Cutting Metal  Quality Inspection  Welding  Assembling Parts  Idle**.  

- **Key Transitions**:  
  - Cutting ends with a temperature/vibration drop (Events 67), transitioning to inspection.  
  - Inspection leads to welding via a material flow drop (Events 89).  
  - Welding shifts to assembly with tool repositioning (Events 910).  
- **Why not other labels?**  
  - "Packaging" would show material flow without tool movement/energy spikes (not observed).  
  - "Material Handling" isn't needed; tool movement in assembly covers repositioning.  

This labeling aligns with real-world manufacturing: cutting shapes parts, inspection ensures quality, welding joins components, and assembly adds final elements. The idle phases bookend the cycle.
