**High-Level Process Steps**

Here is a structured mapping of the low-level events into five coherent, higher-level process stages. The groupings are based on **temporal sequence**, **functional purpose**, **resource type**, and **logical manufacturing flow**.

### 1. Material Preparation
**Low-level events included:**
- Retrieve raw metal sheet
- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet
- Preheat metal sheet

**Rationale:**  
These events represent the initial handling and conditioning of the raw material. They are performed at the start of the process, involve material identification and positioning (Operator A + Robot Arm), and prepare the metal sheet for the subsequent joining operation. The preheating step marks the transition from mechanical handling to thermal preparation.

**High-Level Activity Name:** `Material Preparation`

### 2. Welding Assembly
**Low-level events included:**
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B

**Rationale:**  
This group captures the core joining operation. The sequence begins with tool acquisition and proceeds directly to the physical welding tasks. All events are performed by the same resource (Operator B) and share the same functional goal of permanently joining the metal components.

**High-Level Activity Name:** `Welding Assembly`

### 3. Weld Integrity Verification
**Low-level events included:**
- Measure weld integrity

**Rationale:**  
This is a dedicated quality gate immediately following the welding operation. It uses an automated sensor to validate the structural quality of the welds before moving to surface treatment. Separating this check prevents defects from proceeding to later stages.

**High-Level Activity Name:** `Weld Integrity Verification`

### 4. Surface Protection
**Low-level events included:**
- Apply protective coating
- Dry coating

**Rationale:**  
These events form a distinct finishing subprocess focused on surface treatment. The coating application is followed directly by a curing/drying step using a heating unit. This phase protects the product and occurs after structural work is complete.

**High-Level Activity Name:** `Surface Protection`

### 5. Final Quality Inspection
**Low-level events included:**
- Visual check

**Rationale:**  
This represents the final human validation step. It occurs after all manufacturing and treatment operations are complete and serves as the last quality gate before the product is released. It differs from the earlier weld-specific check by being a holistic visual assessment.

**High-Level Activity Name:** `Final Quality Inspection`

---

**Summary of Grouping Logic**

| High-Level Step              | Key Grouping Criteria                          | Primary Resources       | Phase Type      |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------|-------------------------|-----------------|
| Material Preparation        | Initial handling + conditioning                | Operator A, Robot Arm   | Preparation     |
| Welding Assembly            | Tool use + joining operations                  | Operator B              | Assembly        |
| Weld Integrity Verification | Automated quality check post-welding           | Quality Sensor          | Quality Gate    |
| Surface Protection          | Coating + curing sequence                      | Coating Unit, Heater    | Finishing       |
| Final Quality Inspection    | Human final validation                         | Operator C              | Quality Gate    |

This abstraction reduces the 12 low-level events into 5 meaningful stages while preserving the chronological and logical flow of the manufacturing process. The structure is consistent across both cases (A1 and B2).
