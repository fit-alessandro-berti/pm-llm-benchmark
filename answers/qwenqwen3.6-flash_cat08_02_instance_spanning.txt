*   **Regulatory Compliance (HazMat):**

    *   *Impact:* Identify bottlenecks where HazMat orders pile up because the facility is at the 10-order limit, causing downstream delays.

#### **B. Differentiating Within-Instance vs. Between-Instance Waiting Time**

To isolate the root cause of delays, we calculate **Total Waiting Time** and decompose it:

$$ \text{Waiting Time} = T_{\text{Start}} - \max(T_{\text{PrevComplete}}, T_{\text{ResourceFree}}, T_{\text{BatchReady}}) $$

*   **Within-Instance Factor:** If $T_{\text{Start}} - T_{\text{PrevComplete}}$ is small, but the activity duration is long, the delay is internal (e.g., slow picker).
*   **Between-Instance Factor:**
    *   If $T_{\text{ResourceFree}}$ dominates, the wait is due to **Resource Contention** (e.g., other orders occupying the station).
    *   If $T_{\text{BatchReady}}$ dominates, the wait is due to **Batching Dependencies**.
    *   *Analysis:* We will generate a **Waiting Time Sankey Diagram** visualizing the flow of waiting time sources, explicitly separating flow delays from constraint-induced waits.

### **2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions**

Constraints do not exist in isolation; their interactions can create compounding bottlenecks or compliance risks.

*   **Cold-Packing + Priority Interaction:**
    *   *Risk:* **Starvation of Standard Cold Orders.** If Express orders with cold requirements arrive frequently, the limited Cold-Packing stations may be perpetually occupied by Express orders. Standard cold orders, which may have longer shelf-life requirements but lower priority, could face delays that violate product integrity.
    *   *Analysis:* We must quantify the queue depth of Standard Cold orders during peak Express periods.

*   **Batching + HazMat Interaction:**
    *   *Risk:* **Compliance-Induced Batch Fragmentation.** Batching logic based solely on region might group 12 HazMat orders destined for the "West" region. However, the facility cannot process more than 10 HazMat orders simultaneously in Packing/QC. This forces the batch to split, causing some orders to wait for the batch to dissolve or for capacity to free up, increasing $WCT_{Batch}$ and potentially violating the HazMat limit if the system attempts to push the full batch.
    *   *Analysis:* We will analyze the correlation between Batch Size and HazMat Count to identify "Violation-Prone Batches."

*   **Priority + Batching Interaction:**
    *   *Risk:* **Batch Disruption.** An Express order might arrive late to a batch. If the batching rule is strict, the Express order waits for the batch. If the rule allows preemption of the batch, the Express order breaks the batch, potentially delaying other orders in that batch and increasing their waiting time.
    *   *Analysis:* Evaluate the trade-off between Express delivery speed and the delay impact on batch-mates.

**Cruciality:** Understanding these interactions prevents **local optima**. For example, increasing batch size might improve shipping efficiency but could trigger HazMat violations or increase Express wait times, negating gains.

### **3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies**

I propose three concrete strategies that leverage process mining insights to mitigate negative impacts.

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Cold-Packing Queue Manager with Priority-Aware Routing**
*   **Constraints Addressed:** Shared Cold-Packing, Priority Handling.
*   **Proposed Changes:**
    *   Implement a **Smart Routing Algorithm** at the "Item Picking" stage. The system will predict the expected arrival time at the Packing station based on historical picking durations.
    *   **Policy:** If an Express Cold Order is predicted to arrive within $X$ minutes of a Standard Cold Order, and the queue for Cold Stations exceeds a threshold, the Standard order is routed to a "Deferred Queue" or a non-cold packing station if packaging allows, or the Standard order is processed with a lower priority flag.
    *   **Preemption Rule:** Formalize preemption only if the Express order's SLA breach risk > Standard order's SLA breach risk * Switch Cost.
*   **Data Leveraging:** Use **Prediction Process Mining** to forecast picking durations and arrival times. Analyze historical preemption costs to tune thresholds.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Reduced $WCT_{Cold}$ for Express orders; eliminated Standard Cold order starvation; smoother resource utilization.

#### **Strategy 2: Compliance-Aware Adaptive Batching**
*   **Constraints Addressed:** Shipping Batching, HazMat Limits.
*   **Proposed Changes:**
    *   Revise batching logic from `Region`-only to **`Region + HazMat-Count`**.
    *   **Dynamic Batch Trigger:** A batch is formed only if `HazMat_Count_Batch <= 10`. If a region has >10 HazMat orders, the system triggers **Sub-Batching** or **Time-Shifting**.
    *   **Slack Utilization:** Non-HazMat orders in a mixed batch are prioritized for label generation to clear capacity, allowing HazMat orders to cycle through the limit more frequently.
*   **Data Leveraging:** Analyze the distribution of HazMat orders by region and time to optimize batch sizes. Use **What-If Analysis** to determine the optimal batch size that maximizes throughput without violating the limit.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Zero HazMat violations; reduced batch formation delays for HazMat orders; improved compliance without sacrificing shipping efficiency.

#### **Strategy 3: HazMat Capacity Buffering & Time-Shifted Processing**
*   **Constraints Addressed:** HazMat Limits, Peak Season Overload.
*   **Proposed Changes:**
    *   Introduce a **HazMat Buffer Area** or "Staging Zone" specifically for compliance management.
    *   **Time-Shifting:** During peak seasons, if the HazMat limit is consistently hit, shift the processing of non-urgent HazMat orders to off-peak hours (e.g., night shift) where QC staff might be dedicated or less utilized by standard orders.
    *   **Resource Decoupling:** If data shows QC is the bottleneck for HazMat, cross-train staff to create a **HazMat-Experienced QC Pool** that operates independently of the general QC flow, effectively increasing capacity for HazMat without affecting standard throughput.
*   **Data Leveraging:** Analyze **Load Profiles** to identify peak HazMat arrival times. Correlate HazMat throughput with QC staff availability.
*   **Expected Outcome:** Decoupling of HazMat flow from general congestion; reduced risk of limit breaches; improved overall throughput during peaks.
