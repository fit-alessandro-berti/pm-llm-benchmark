# Clarifying Questions for the Property Onboarding Process

## Initial Intake & Documentation
1. What specific criteria does the property coordinator use to determine whether a document is "outdated"𡟙s there a standard expiration window, or does it vary by document type and jurisdiction?
2. When documentation is missing, how long does the coordinator typically wait for the landlord to provide updates before the onboarding request is paused, escalated, or canceled?
3. Are there cases where a property is rejected outright at the documentation stage? What would cause that?
4. Who has the authority to approve exceptions when a landlord cannot produce a required document?

## Property Database & Data Entry
5. Who verifies the accuracy of the property details entered into the central database, and is there a second set of eyes on this data?
6. How is the "expected rental price range" determined at this early stage, before the formal pricing models are applied later in the process?
7. What happens if details collected early (e.g., square footage) turn out to differ from what the inspector later reports?

## Property Manager Assignment
8. How are the assignment factors (workload, geography, experience, track record) weighted against each other? Is there a formal scoring system or is it discretionary?
9. Who actually makes the final assignment decision𡟙s it automated, done by a supervisor, or chosen by the coordinator?
10. What happens if no property manager is a good fit (e.g., all qualified managers are overloaded)? Is the onboarding delayed, or is a less-ideal match made?
11. Can a property manager refuse an assignment, and under what conditions?
12. How are reassignments handled if a property manager leaves or becomes unavailable mid-process?

## Landlord Meeting & Expectations
13. Is the introductory meeting documented formally (e.g., meeting notes, signed agreements), or is it informal?
14. What happens if the landlord's expectations fundamentally conflict with company policy during this meeting?
15. Are there standard policies that cannot be negotiated, versus ones that are flexible per landlord?

## Photography & Inspection
16. Are the photographer and inspector typically scheduled in parallel or sequentially, and does one depend on the other?
17. Who chooses the photographer and inspector㻡re these internal staff, preferred vendors, or selected per job?
18. How are inspection failures categorized (minor vs. major issues), and does this affect whether the property can still be marketed conditionally?
19. Who pays for repairs鍟he landlord, the company, or is it negotiated? How are disputes resolved?
20. Is there a typical timeframe between scheduling and completion of inspection/photography?

## Marketing & Activation
21. How does the digital marketing team coordinate with the pricing model team㻡re these sequential steps or done in parallel?
22. Who has final approval on the listing before it goes live?
23. What triggers the "activation" step specifically? Is there a formal checklist that must be complete?
24. Can a listing be deactivated and, if so, by whom and under what circumstances?

## Tenant Screening & Lease Negotiation
25. What's the distinction between when a property manager handles screening versus when a leasing agent does?
26. Are there specific thresholds (credit score, income-to-rent ratio) for auto-approving or auto-rejecting applicants?
27. How are ties broken when multiple equally qualified tenants apply?
28. Who has authority to approve promotional discounts or deviate from standard lease terms?
29. How are fair housing concerns prevented during the "prioritization" of tenants?
30. What happens when a landlord wants to reject a tenant the property manager has recommended?

## Lease Finalization & Move-In
31. Is the security deposit collected before or after the compliance review of the lease?
32. What happens if the compliance officer finds issues with a lease after the tenant has already signed?
33. How far in advance must the move-in date be scheduled, and who coordinates key handover?

## Quality Checks & Audits
34. Are the compliance, financial, and IT reviews performed in parallel or sequentially, and are they blocking or non-blocking?
35. What happens if the financial analyst finds the negotiated rent doesn't meet profitability targets after the lease is signed?
36. Is there a single checkpoint where all three audits are reconciled, or are they independent?
37. How often do these audits actually uncover problems, and what's the typical remediation path?

## Exceptions & Edge Cases
38. How are properties requiring special insurance flagged early, and who handles procurement?
39. When landlords insist on custom lease clauses, who reviews and approves these𡐤egal counsel, compliance, or the property manager?
40. Are there property types (e.g., luxury, student housing) that follow a materially different onboarding workflow?
41. What's the process if a landlord withdraws mid-onboarding? Are there costs recovered?

## Communication & Tracking
42. Is there a single system of record for the onboarding status, or is the process tracked across multiple tools?
43. Who is responsible for ensuring communication across email, CRM, and project management tools stays synchronized?
44. How is handoff between stages communicated㻡re there formal triggers, or does it rely on individuals notifying each other?

## Timing & Overall Metrics
45. What's the typical end-to-end duration from landlord contact to property activation? To first tenant move-in?
46. Are there SLAs or target durations for each stage, and what happens when they're missed?
47. What are the most common bottlenecks or points where onboarding stalls?
48. How is the success of an onboarding measured after the fact鍟enant retention, landlord satisfaction, time-to-lease, or something else?
