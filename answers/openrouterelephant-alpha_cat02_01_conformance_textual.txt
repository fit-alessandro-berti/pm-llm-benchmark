**Analysis of Deviations from the Normative Process**

---

### **Case 1**
- **Sequence:** Receive  Check  Authorize Payment  Package  Ship  Close  
- **Compliant with normative process**   
All steps follow the expected flow when inventory is sufficient:  
- “Receive” starts the process  
- “Check inventory” precedes payment authorization  
- Authorization occurs before packaging  
- Packaging occurs before shipping  
- Shipping is followed by closing  
No violations.

---

### **Case 2**
- **Sequence:** Receive  Check  Package  Authorize Payment  Ship  Close  
- **Violations:**  
  1. **Packaging before payment authorization**  
     - The normative process states: *“after confirming inventory, the process should include the authorization of payment”* and importantly, *“Authorize payment… before packaging items”*.  
     - Payment authorization must occur *before* packaging. Here, “Package items” happens before “Authorize payment,” which reverses the required order.  
  2. **Shipping without explicit payment authorization (implicit)**  
     - Since payment authorization did not occur before packaging, it’s also missing entirely before shipping. The normative process expects authorization prior to any fulfillment activity including shipping.  
- **Explanation of deviation:** Case 2 violates the strict ordering constraint that payment authorization must precede packaging and shipping. Packaging before authorization may indicate that payment was assumed sufficient without formal authorization or that the process allowed fulfillment steps to proceed prematurely.

---

### **Case 3**
- **Sequence:** Receive  Check  Authorize Payment  Cancel order  Ship order  Close  
- **Violations:**  
  1. **Shipping after cancellation**  
     - The normative process explicitly states: *“it would be illogical to continue with activities intended for fulfillment (such as packaging or shipping) after a cancellation.”*  
     - “Ship order” occurs after “Cancel order”, which is a clear violation.  
  2. **Unnecessary authorization before cancellation**  
     - Although not explicitly forbidden, the normative description implies that if inventory is insufficient, payment authorization should not occur; instead, the process should go directly to cancellation after inventory check.  
     - Here, payment authorization happens *before* cancellation, which is only justifiable if inventory was sufficient — but then cancellation afterwards contradicts that assumption unless inventory changed (but no such re-check is indicated).  
- **Explanation of deviation:** The process allows both payment authorization and shipping after cancellation, which defies logical and normative expectations. Shipping should never follow cancellation.

---

### **Case 4**
- **Sequence:** Receive  Check  Cancel  Close  
- **Compliant with normative process**   
This matches the “insufficient inventory” path exactly:  
- After check, if inventory is insufficient, cancel order and then close.  
No extra activities (payment, packaging, shipping) occur. This is correct.

---

### **Case 5**
- **Sequence:** Receive  Check  Authorize Payment  Ship order  Close  
- **Violations:**  
  1. **Missing “Package items” before shipping**  
     - The normative process states: *“Only after items have been properly packaged is it appropriate to proceed with ‘Ship order.’”*  
     - Here, “Ship order” occurs directly after payment authorization, skipping the packaging step entirely.  
  2. **Implied risk of shipping unpackaged items**  
     - This violates the explicit requirement that packaging must occur before shipping.  
- **Explanation of deviation:** The case omits the necessary packaging step, which is a prerequisite for shipping according to the normative flow.

---

### **Summary of Violations**

| Case ID | Violations |
|---------|------------|
| 2       | Payment authorization occurs *after* packaging; packaging before authorization violates ordering. |
| 3       | Shipping occurs *after* cancellation; logically invalid per normative rules. Also, payment authorization before cancellation may be premature. |
| 5       | “Package items” activity is missing before shipping; violates the required sequence. |

**Correct cases:** 1 and 4 follow the normative process as intended.
