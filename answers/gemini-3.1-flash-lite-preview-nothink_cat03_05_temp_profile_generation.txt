To represent the temporal profile of the global supply chain process, we must account for the accumulation of time across consecutive and non-consecutive steps. In this model, the average time between non-consecutive activities (e.g., `SS` to `RC`) is the sum of the average times of the intermediate steps, while the variance (the square of the standard deviation) for independent steps is the sum of the individual variances.

The following Python dictionary estimates these parameters in seconds, assuming a logical flow from procurement through after-sales support.

```python
temporal_profile = {

    ('RC', 'QI'): (14400, 3600),        # Receiving to Inspection
    ('QI', 'CA'): (86400, 10800),       # Inspection to Assembly (1 day)
    ('CA', 'PT'): (43200, 7200),        # Assembly to Testing
    ('PT', 'PK'): (18000, 3600),        # Testing to Packaging
    ('PK', 'WS'): (3600, 900),          # Packaging to Warehouse
    ('WS', 'DT'): (172800, 43200),      # Warehouse to Distribution (approx 2 days)
    ('DT', 'AS'): (2592000, 604800),    # Distribution to After-Sales (approx 30 days)

    # Distant successors (accumulated time and propagated variance)
    ('SS', 'RC'): (612000, 86418),      # SS -> OP -> RC
    ('OP', 'QI'): (619200, 86475),      # OP -> RC -> QI
    ('RC', 'CA'): (100800, 11384),      # RC -> QI -> CA
    ('QI', 'PT'): (129600, 13000),      # QI -> CA -> PT
    ('CA', 'PK'): (61200, 8118),        # CA -> PT -> PK

    # Long-range dependencies

    ('CA', 'AS'): (2779200, 604842)      # Accumulated from CA to AS

```

### Explanation of Estimates:
1.  **Procurement Phases (`SS` to `RC`)**: These are dominated by logistics and supplier lead times, resulting in a high average time and a larger standard deviation due to shipping volatility.
2.  **Manufacturing & Quality (`QI` to `PK`)**: These are internal operational steps with relatively high consistency (lower standard deviation).
3.  **Distribution and Support (`WS` to `AS`)**: These involve external factors and customer behavior, leading to significantly higher average times and standard deviations, reflecting the uncertainty of post-sale support requirements.
4.  **Calculations**: 
    *   **Average**: Calculated as the sum of expected durations between subsequent steps.
    *   **Standard Deviation**: Calculated as the square root of the sum of the variances (where $\sigma^2 = \text{std}^2$) for independent time segments.
