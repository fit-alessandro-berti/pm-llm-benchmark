### 3. Explanation of Impact and Risks

These anomalies represent "workarounds" or "smells" in the process design that have serious implications for process quality, compliance, and risk management.

#### Impact of Skipping Credit Check
*   **Financial Risk:** The primary function of a credit check is risk assessment. Allowing the process to bypass this step (via the `skip` option) exposes the company to significant financial risk. It could lead to selling goods to customers who cannot pay, resulting in bad debt and revenue loss.
*   **Compliance and Control Failure:** In most regulated financial environments, credit checks are mandatory controls. Having a model that explicitly allows bypassing this control suggests a lack of rigor in the process design or a workaround used to process "urgent" orders that fail credit checks, effectively circumventing policy.

#### Impact of the Approval-Invoice Loop
*   **Operational Inefficiency:** Repeatedly approving the same order or issuing multiple invoices for a single transaction causes confusion and administrative overhead. It bloats the event log with redundant activities, making performance analysis difficult.
*   **Billing Errors and Customer Disputes:** If multiple invoices are generated for the same order (e.g., Invoice #1001, Invoice #1001-2, Invoice #1001-3), the customer is likely to dispute the charges or delay payment. This impacts cash flow and damages customer relationships.
*   **Systemic Issues:** This loop often indicates that the process is not handling exceptions properly. For example, if an invoice is issued with an error, the correct process is to issue a *credit note* and a *corrected invoice*, not to simply loop the entire approval and issuance steps. This design suggests a "retry" mechanism that is destructive to the process state.
