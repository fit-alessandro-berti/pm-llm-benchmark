This guarantees that these fairness-critical steps are mandatory in every valid loan application trace, preventing them from being skipped during automated or rushed processing.

2. **`succession` (Mandatory Fairness Pipeline):** 
   - `CheckApplicantRace`  `BiasMitigationCheck`: Ensures that whenever sensitive demographic data is accessed, a dedicated fairness evaluation must occur next.
   - `BiasMitigationCheck`  `Approve`/`Reject`: Forces all final decisions to be preceded by the bias mitigation step. This breaks the pattern where sensitive attributes directly influence outcomes without intermediate oversight.

3. **`coexistence` (Mandatory Oversight for Decisions):** 
   - `Approve`  `ManualReview` and `Reject`  `ManualReview`: Enforces that any final decision (approve or reject) must co-occur with a manual review in the same process trace. This ensures that sensitive cases always receive human-in-the-loop verification, reducing algorithmic or implicit bias in automated decisions.

4. **`nonsuccession` (Block Direct Bias Pathways):** 
   - `CheckApplicantRace`  `Reject`: Explicitly forbids a decision to reject from immediately following the disclosure of race data. This constraint targets the most common pattern of automated discrimination by forcing a temporal gap and mandatory intermediate steps between sensitive data exposure and adverse outcomes.

Together, these constraints structurally enforce fairness by mandating oversight, enforcing sequential bias-checkpoints, and blocking direct causal links between sensitive attributes and negative decisions, thereby aligning the process model with equitable lending standards.
