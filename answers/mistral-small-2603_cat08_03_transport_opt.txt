# **Process Mining Approach for Speedy Parcels: Optimizing Last-Mile Delivery Operations**

## **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

### **Data Preprocessing and Integration**
To analyze Speedy Parcels' operations, we must first integrate and preprocess data from multiple sources into a **unified event log** suitable for process mining. The key steps are:

#### **Data Sources and Challenges:**
| **Source**            | **Key Data Fields** | **Challenges in Integration** |
|-----------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Vehicle GPS Trackers** | Timestamp, Lat/Lon, Speed, Vehicle Status (idle/moving), Ignition on/off | High-frequency data (e.g., every 10 sec) requires aggregation to avoid noise. Missing data due to GPS signal loss in tunnels or urban canyons. |
| **Driver Handheld Scanners** | Timestamp, Activity (Arrive/Depart Customer, Delivery Success/Failure), Package ID, Location (optional) | Manual entries may have errors or delays. Some stops may lack precise location data. |
| **Dispatch System** | Planned route, Assigned packages, Vehicle capacity, Driver assignments, Customer time windows | Static routes may not reflect real-world deviations. Time windows may be unrealistic. |
| **Maintenance Logs** | Start/End time of maintenance, Vehicle ID, Reason (scheduled/unscheduled) | Maintenance events may not align with GPS data (e.g., vehicle parked vs. in use). |

#### **Event Log Construction:**
- **Case ID:** Use a composite key like `VehicleID-Date` (e.g., `V12-20241205`) to track a vehicle’s operations for a single shift.
- **Activity:** Standardize event labels (e.g., "Depart Depot," "Arrive Customer," "Delivery Failed," "Engine Warning Light").
- **Timestamp:** Ensure all events are synchronized (e.g., GPS data may need interpolation for missing timestamps).
- **Additional Attributes:**
  - **Traffic Conditions:** Enrich with external traffic data (e.g., TomTom, Google Maps API) to correlate delays.
  - **Driver/Route Performance:** Link to dispatch data (e.g., planned vs. actual stops).
  - **Vehicle Health:** Tag maintenance-related stops (e.g., "Unscheduled Stop: Engine Warning").

**Challenges & Mitigations:**
- **Temporal Misalignment:** GPS and scanner timestamps may not match. Use **fuzzy matching** (e.g., events within 2 minutes are considered related).
- **Missing Data:** Impute missing GPS points using **map-matching algorithms** (e.g., OpenStreetMap road network alignment).
- **Noise in Data:** Filter out unrealistic speeds (e.g., >120 km/h in urban areas) or idle times >30 minutes (unless justified by maintenance).

### **Process Discovery: Visualizing the Actual Delivery Process**
Using **process discovery algorithms** (e.g., **Heuristics Miner, Inductive Miner, or Disco**), we can reconstruct the **as-is process model** from the event log.

#### **Expected Process Flow:**
1. **Start Shift** (Depot)  **Depart Depot**  **Travel to Customer**  **Arrive Customer**  **Delivery Attempt** (Success/Failure)  **Depart Customer**  ...  **Return to Depot**  **End Shift**.
2. **Deviations:**
   - **Unplanned Stops:** Traffic jams, vehicle breakdowns, customer unavailability.
   - **Failed Deliveries:** Requiring re-attempts or customer redirection.
   - **Maintenance Stops:** Unexpected repairs during shifts.
   - **Overtime:** Shifts exceeding planned duration.

#### **Conformance Checking: Comparing Planned vs. Actual Routes**
- **Planned Process Model:** Derived from the dispatch system (e.g., a linear sequence of 35 stops).
- **Actual Process Model:** Discovered from event logs (may show loops, skipped stops, or out-of-order deliveries).
- **Deviations to Investigate:**
  | **Deviation Type** | **Example** | **Process Mining Technique** |
  |--------------------|------------|-----------------------------|
  | **Sequence Deviations** | Stop 10 was skipped due to traffic. | **Alignment-based conformance checking** (e.g., using the **Alignments algorithm** in ProM). |
  | **Timing Deviations** | Planned 15-min stop took 45 mins. | **Performance analysis** (e.g., comparing planned vs. actual timestamps). |
  | **Unplanned Activities** | Vehicle stopped for 20 mins due to engine warning. | **Variant analysis** (identify rare but costly paths). |
  | **Skipped/Added Stops** | Driver missed a stop due to capacity issues. | **Trace clustering** (group similar deviations). |

**Tools:**
- **ProM, Disco, or Celonis** for process discovery and conformance checking.
- **Python (PM4Py library)** for custom analysis (e.g., filtering high-deviation traces).

## **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
To measure efficiency, we define KPIs aligned with Speedy Parcels' goals:

| **KPI** | **Definition** | **Calculation** | **Target** |
|---------|---------------|----------------|------------|
| **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR)** | % of deliveries completed within customer’s time window. | `(Successful Deliveries within Window) / (Total Deliveries)` | >90% |
| **Average Time per Delivery Stop** | Mean time spent per customer (travel + service). | ` (Depart Customer - Arrive Customer) / (Total Stops)` | <15 mins |
| **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio** | Proportion of time spent driving vs. at customer. | ` Travel Time /  Service Time` | <2:1 (ideally <1.5:1) |
| **Fuel Consumption per km/package** | Efficiency of fuel usage. | `Total Fuel Used / (Total km + Total Packages)` | Reduce by 10% |
| **Vehicle Utilization Rate** | % of time vehicle is in use vs. idle. | `(Total Driving Time) / (Shift Duration)` | >70% |
| **Traffic Delay Impact** | Extra time lost due to congestion. | ` (Actual Travel Time - Planned Travel Time)` | <10% of total shift |
| **Failed Delivery Rate** | % of deliveries requiring redelivery. | `(Failed Deliveries) / (Total Delivery Attempts)` | <5% |
| **Maintenance Downtime** | % of shift lost to unscheduled repairs. | `(Maintenance Duration) / (Shift Duration)` | <2% |

---

### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
Using **process mining**, we can pinpoint inefficiencies:

1. **Performance Spectrograph (Disco/Celonis):**
   - Visualize **waiting times** (e.g., long idle periods before departures).
   - Highlight **high-variance activities** (e.g., service times vary from 5 to 45 mins).

2. **Social Network Analysis (SNA):**
   - Identify if certain **drivers or routes** consistently underperform.
   - Example: Driver D105 has 3 failed deliveries in a row  potential training need.

3. **Queueing Analysis:**
   - Model customer service times as **queues** (e.g., parking difficulty causes delays).
   - Use **Little’s Law** to estimate optimal service time.

4. **Traffic Correlation Analysis:**
   - Overlay **GPS speed data** with external traffic APIs to identify **hotspots**.
   - Example: Route A has 20% longer travel time due to a recurring traffic jam at 8:30 AM.

5. **Variant Analysis:**
   - Compare **high-performing vs. low-performing routes**:
     - **Good Route:** 95% OTDR, 12 mins/service time.
     - **Bad Route:** 70% OTDR, 25 mins/service time.
   - Identify **root causes** (e.g., dense urban area, poor parking).

**Example Findings:**
- **Bottleneck:** Failed deliveries in Zone B (15% failure rate vs. 5% company avg.).
- **Root Cause:** Customers in Zone B often request "leave at door," increasing service time variability.

## **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

### **Potential Root Causes & Process Mining Insights**

| **Inefficiency** | **Possible Root Cause** | **Process Mining Analysis** |
|------------------|------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Late Deliveries** | Traffic congestion, suboptimal routing, long service times. | **Conformance checking** reveals frequent delays on Route C. **Performance analysis** shows high service time variance in Zone D. |
| **High Fuel Costs** | Inefficient routing, excessive idling, aggressive driving. | **GPS speed analysis** identifies frequent braking/accelerating. **Maintenance logs** show 5% of shifts have engine issues causing extra idling. |
| **Failed Deliveries** | Customer unavailability, incorrect time windows, poor communication. | **Variant analysis** shows Zone E has 3x failed deliveries due to "customer not home." **Scanner data** reveals 40% of failures occur between 12-2 PM (lunch hours). |
| **High Maintenance Costs** | Aggressive driving, lack of predictive maintenance. | **Social network analysis** links Driver D201 to 3x more maintenance stops. **GPS data** shows excessive speeding on Route F. |
| **Overtime Shifts** | Poor time estimation, traffic delays, inefficient stops. | **Process discovery** shows 60% of shifts exceed planned duration by >30 mins. **Traffic overlay** reveals Route G has chronic delays. |

### **Advanced Root Cause Techniques**
1. **Correlation Analysis:**
   - **Traffic + Delays:** Use **regression models** to quantify how much delay is due to traffic vs. service time.
   - **Driver Behavior:** Compare **speed profiles** of top vs. bottom performers.

2. **Predictive Process Mining:**
   - Train a **machine learning model** (e.g., Random Forest) to predict:
     - **Failed deliveries** based on time of day, zone, customer type.
     - **Maintenance needs** based on driving patterns (e.g., harsh braking).

3. **Dwell Time Analysis:**
   - **Customer Interaction Time:** Long service times may indicate:
     - Difficulty finding parking.
     - Customer inquiries (e.g., "Where’s my package?").
   - **Solution:** Use **sensor data** (e.g., door-open events) to distinguish between service and idle time.

## **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Real-Time Traffic Adaptation**
**Target Inefficiency:** Late deliveries due to traffic congestion.
**Root Cause:** Static routes don’t account for real-time traffic.
**Process Mining Insight:**
- **Conformance checking** shows 30% of delays occur on 5% of routes.
- **Traffic overlay** reveals recurring jams at 8:30 AM and 4:00 PM.

**Implementation:**
- **Integrate live traffic data** (e.g., via HERE Maps API) into dispatch system.
- **Use reinforcement learning** to adjust routes dynamically (e.g., reroute if congestion >20%).
- **Pilot in high-delay zones** (e.g., Route C) and measure OTDR improvement.

**Expected Impact:**
- **OTDR:** Increase from 85% to 92%.
- **Travel Time:** Reduce by 10-15%.
- **Fuel Costs:** Decrease by 5-8%.

---

### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance to Reduce Unplanned Stops**
**Target Inefficiency:** Maintenance-related downtime (e.g., Engine Warning Light events).
**Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance instead of predictive.
**Process Mining Insight:**
- **Maintenance logs** show 60% of unscheduled stops could have been predicted.
- **GPS data** reveals harsh braking/acceleration patterns before breakdowns.

**Implementation:**
- **Train a predictive model** using:
  - **Vehicle telemetry** (speed, RPM, fuel efficiency).
  - **Maintenance history** (time since last service).
  - **Driver behavior** (aggressive vs. smooth driving).
- **Schedule preemptive maintenance** during low-demand periods (e.g., overnight).
- **Alert drivers** if engine warning signs appear (e.g., via telematics).

**Expected Impact:**
- **Maintenance Downtime:** Reduce from 2% to <0.5% of shift time.
- **Fuel Efficiency:** Improve by 3-5% (fewer breakdowns = less idling).
- **Customer Satisfaction:** Fewer missed deliveries due to vehicle issues.

---

### **Strategy 3: Optimized Delivery Territories & Time Windows**
**Target Inefficiency:** High failed delivery rates in certain zones.
**Root Cause:** Poorly designed delivery territories and unrealistic time windows.
**Process Mining Insight:**
- **Variant analysis** shows Zone E has 15% failed deliveries vs. 5% company avg.
- **Scanner data** reveals 40% of failures occur between 12-2 PM (lunch hours).

**Implementation:**
1. **Redesign Territories:**
   - Use **clustering algorithms** (e.g., k-means) to group customers by:
     - **Geographic proximity** (reduce travel time).
     - **Delivery success rate** (avoid high-failure zones).
   - **Dynamic time windows:** Adjust based on historical success rates (e.g., no deliveries between 12-2 PM in Zone E).
2. **Customer Communication:**
   - Send **SMS/email alerts** 1 hour before delivery with a 1-hour time slot.
   - Offer **alternative delivery options** (e.g., pickup at partner store).

**Expected Impact:**
- **Failed Delivery Rate:** Reduce from 8% to <3%.
- **Service Time:** Decrease by 10-15% (fewer failed attempts).
- **Driver Productivity:** Increase stops per hour by 5-10%.
