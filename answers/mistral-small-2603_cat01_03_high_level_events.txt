### **Proposed High-Level Process Steps for Manufacturing Workflow**

Based on the event log, the manufacturing process can be divided into **five distinct high-level steps**, each representing a coherent stage in the production of a metal component. Below is the structured grouping of low-level events into these steps, along with justifications and proposed names.

---

## **1. Material Preparation**
**Low-Level Events Grouped:**
- Retrieve raw metal sheet
- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet
- Preheat metal sheet

**Rationale:**
These events represent the initial stage where the raw material (metal sheet) is retrieved, verified (via barcode scan), positioned, and prepared for processing. The activities are temporally close, involve the same resource (Operator A) for retrieval/placement, and are followed by a machine-driven alignment and preheating step. This ensures the material is correctly set up before any fabrication begins.

**Proposed Name:**
**"Material Preparation"**

---

## **2. Assembly (Welding & Joining)**
**Low-Level Events Grouped:**
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B

**Rationale:**
This step focuses on the core fabrication process where the metal sheet is assembled into a component. The sequence starts with tool pickup (a preparatory action) followed by welding operations (corner A and B). These events are logically grouped because they involve the same operator (Operator B) and tool (W-12), representing a distinct phase of joining parts.

**Proposed Name:**
**"Assembly (Welding)"**

---

## **3. Quality Inspection (Post-Welding)**
**Low-Level Events Grouped:**
- Measure weld integrity (Quality Sensor #1)
- Visual check (Operator C)

**Rationale:**
After welding, the component undergoes quality checks to ensure structural integrity. The "Measure weld integrity" event (automated via Quality Sensor #1) and the "Visual check" (manual inspection by Operator C) are grouped because they serve the same purpose: verifying that the welding process was successful and the component meets standards. These checks are temporally close and occur after assembly.

**Proposed Name:**
**"Quality Inspection"**

---

## **4. Finishing (Coating & Drying)**
**Low-Level Events Grouped:**
- Apply protective coating (Coating Unit #3)
- Dry coating (Heating Unit #2)

**Rationale:**
This step involves applying a protective layer (e.g., polyurethane) to the welded component and then drying it. The events are grouped because they are sequential, involve different but complementary resources (Coating Unit and Heating Unit), and represent a distinct phase where the component is prepared for final use or further processing.

**Proposed Name:**
**"Finishing (Coating & Drying)"**

---

## **5. Final Verification**
**Low-Level Events Grouped:**
- Visual check (Operator C)

**Rationale:**
The final visual check by Operator C ensures that the entire process (from material prep to coating) was completed correctly. While this could be grouped under "Quality Inspection," it is separated here because it is the last step in the observed sequence, serving as a final approval before the component moves to the next stage (e.g., packaging or shipping).

**Proposed Name:**
**"Final Verification"**

---

### **Structured Representation of High-Level Process Steps**

| **High-Level Step**          | **Low-Level Events Included**                                                                 | **Resources Involved**               | **Purpose**                                                                 |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1. Material Preparation**  | Retrieve raw metal sheet, Scan barcode, Place on table, Align, Preheat                     | Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1 | Prepare and position raw material for fabrication.                          |

| **4. Finishing (Coating)**   | Apply protective coating, Dry coating                                                       | Coating Unit #3, Heating Unit #2      | Apply and cure protective coating.                                         |
| **5. Final Verification**    | Visual check                                                                               | Operator C                           | Final approval before next stage (e.g., packaging).                        |

---

### **Key Observations & Justifications**
1. **Temporal Clustering:**
   - Events are grouped based on their timestamps, ensuring logical sequencing (e.g., preheating before welding).
   - Overlapping or parallel events (e.g., Operator A retrieving material while Robot Arm #2 aligns) are still grouped under the same high-level step if they contribute to the same stage.

2. **Resource-Based Grouping:**
   - Events performed by the same operator or machine type (e.g., Operator B for welding) are grouped together.
   - Automated checks (e.g., Quality Sensor #1) are grouped with manual inspections (Operator C) under "Quality Inspection" because they serve the same purpose.

3. **Domain-Specific Logic:**
   - The process follows a clear **preparation  fabrication  inspection  finishing  final check** workflow, which is typical in manufacturing.
   - Coating and drying are grouped separately from welding because they are distinct subprocesses with different resources and purposes.

4. **Flexibility for Expansion:**
   - If additional steps (e.g., packaging, shipping) were included in the log, they could be added as subsequent high-level steps (e.g., "Packaging" or "Shipping Preparation").
