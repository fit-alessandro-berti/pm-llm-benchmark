Below is an analysis of the four cases against standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) best practices and typical control objectives (e.g., segregation of duties, proper authorization, three-way matching, and paying only for goods/services actually received).

---

**Key P2P expectations used for comparison**
- Requisition should be created and approved before issuing a purchase order (PO).
- PO should normally be issued before goods are received and before an invoice is paid.
- Goods should typically be received before (or at least in alignment with) the vendor invoice, and a three-way match (PO, goods receipt, invoice) should occur before payment.
- Payment should not be released before proper authorization or before key validations (e.g., invoice matching).
- Approval of a requisition should not occur after payment or after the goods receipt in a way that defeats authorization controls.
- Paying before receiving an invoice, or before matching the invoice to the PO and goods receipt, is unusual and risky.

---

### Case 1001 – Largely standard
- Sequence: Requisition  Approval  PO  Goods receipt  Invoice received  Match Invoice to PO  Payment  Close.
- Assessment: This follows a conventional, controlled P2P flow. No major anomalies.

---

### Case 1002 – Multiple significant anomalies
Unusual sequence highlights:
- Requisition created, then PO issued **before** requisition approval.
- Invoice received and **payment released** before requisition is approved and before goods are received.
- Goods received **after** payment.
- Invoice-to-PO matching occurs **after** payment.

Why this is problematic:
- **Lack of prior authorization:** Issuing a PO without an approved requisition undermines internal controls and may lead to unauthorized or unnecessary purchases.
- **Premature payment:** Releasing payment before matching the invoice to the PO and before confirming goods receipt increases risk of paying for goods not received, paying incorrect amounts, or paying fraudulent invoices.
- **Three-way match after payment:** Defeats the purpose of the match, which is to validate that the invoice corresponds to what was ordered and received before funds are released.
- **Segregation of duties concerns:** The ordering, approval, receiving, and payment activities appear out of logical order, increasing the potential for errors or fraud to go undetected.

---

### Case 1003 – Major anomaly: payment before receiving goods and before invoice
Unusual sequence highlights:
- PO issued after proper requisition approval (good).
- **Payment released** before goods receipt and before the vendor invoice is received.
- Invoice received **after** payment.
- Goods received **after** payment.
- Matching occurs after both goods receipt and invoice receipt (as expected), but only after payment is already made.

Why this is problematic:
- **Paying before receiving goods/services:** This is highly unusual and risky. If goods are never received or are unsatisfactory, recovering funds is difficult.
- **Paying without an invoice:** Payment typically requires a vendor invoice to validate amount due and to perform matching. Paying without it weakens controls and increases fraud risk.
- **Three-way match rendered irrelevant for payment decision:** Since payment precedes the match, the organization cannot use the match to prevent erroneous or fraudulent payments.
- **Cash flow inefficiency:** Paying earlier than necessary without a contractual or operational reason unnecessarily ties up cash.

---

### Case 1004 – Anomaly: goods received before PO issued
Unusual sequence highlights:
- Requisition created and approved.
- **Goods received before PO issued.**
- PO issued after receipt of goods.
- Invoice received, matched, and paid afterward.

Why this is problematic:
- **Unauthorized procurement risk:** Receiving goods without a PO suggests a lack of purchase authorization prior to commitment. This can lead to maverick spending and budget overruns.
- **Matching and pricing risks:** Without a PO in place beforehand, there is no agreed price, quantity, or specification to validate against when the goods arrive and when the invoice is processed.
- **Operational inefficiency:** Vendors may not have a PO to reference, potentially leading to disputes, delayed invoicing, or missed discounts.
- While the three-way match eventually occurs, the fact that goods arrived before the PO indicates a breakdown in purchasing controls.

---

### Summary of high-priority anomalies
- **Case 1002:** PO issued without prior requisition approval; payment before goods receipt and before invoice matching.
- **Case 1003:** Payment released before receiving goods and before receiving/validating the invoice.
- **Case 1004:** Goods received before PO issued, indicating uncontrolled procurement.

These anomalies are problematic primarily because they circumvent core P2P controls designed to prevent fraud, ensure proper authorization, confirm that goods/services were received as expected, and verify that payments are accurate and legitimate. Addressing them would typically involve reinforcing process discipline, system-enforced approval workflows, and payment holds until three-way matching is complete.
