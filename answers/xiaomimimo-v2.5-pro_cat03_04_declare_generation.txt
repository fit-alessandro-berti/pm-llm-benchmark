## Explanation of the Model

This DECLARE model captures the typical workflow and constraints of the product design process:

1. **Init Constraint**: The process always starts with Idea Generation (IG).

2. **Existence Constraints**: Every activity must occur at least once in a complete process instance.

3. **Precedence Constraints**: These define the mandatory order of activities:
   - IG must precede DD
   - DD must precede both TFC and CE
   - Both TFC and CE must precede PC
   - PC must precede both LT and UT
   - Both LT and UT must precede AG
   - AG must precede MP
   - MP must precede FL

4. **Response Constraints**: If an activity occurs, then its corresponding subsequent activity must eventually occur (similar to precedence but focused on causality).

5. **Succession Constraints**: Combine both precedence and response - if one activity occurs, the other must occur before or after it respectively.

6. **Chain Response Constraints**: Similar to response but with immediacy - the response activity must occur immediately after the triggering activity (no other activities in between).

7. **Non-coexistence Constraints**: Certain activity pairs cannot occur together in the same process instance:
   - Early-stage activities (IG, DD, TFC, CE, PC, LT, UT, AG) cannot co-occur with the final launch (FL)
   - Idea Generation (IG) cannot co-occur with late-stage activities (MP, AG, LT, UT, PC)
   - Marketing Plan (MP) cannot co-occur with Idea Generation (IG)

## Note
All support and confidence values are set to 1.0, indicating perfect compliance with these rules in the ideal process flow. In real-world scenarios, these values would typically be between 0 and 1, reflecting process variability and exceptions.
