Explanation of how the added constraints reduce bias:

- **Mandatory mitigation and review:** `existence`, `responded_existence`, `precedence`, `response`, and `chainsuccession` constraints force every trace with a decision (Approve/Reject for any group) to include both `BiasMitigationCheck` and `ManualReview`. This builds a standard, auditable mitigation and human-review step into every decision.

- **Separation from sensitive attributes:** `chainresponse`, `nonsuccession`, and `nonchainsuccession` ensure that decisions cannot be taken directly after reading sensitive attributes. There must be at least one intervening `BiasMitigationCheck` (and then `ManualReview`), reducing the risk that the raw attribute directly drives the outcome.

- **Symmetric treatment across groups:** Coexistence, responded existence, precedence, and other constraints are imposed both on `_Minority` and `_NonMinority` variants of decisions. This enforces that whatever fairness safeguards apply to minority applicants apply equally to non-minority applicants, promoting procedural parity.

- **Consistent multi-step fairness pattern:** The combination of response/succession/chainprecedence constraints enforces a canonical pattern:
  `CheckSensitiveAttributes  BiasMitigationCheck  ManualReview  Decision`.
  This makes it harder for ad hoc, potentially biased shortcuts to bypass mitigation or review.
