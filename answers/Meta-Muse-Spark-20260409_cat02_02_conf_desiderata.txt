### **3. Interpretations / likely justifications**

**Case 1**: Textbook execution. Likely an automated or well-monitored workflow with SLAs enforced. No issues.

**Case 2**: The two normative violations suggest either system error or manual override. Possible interpretations:  
- *Risk Assessment before Credit Check*: An analyst may have run a preliminary risk model using application data alone, then backfilled the credit check. This breaks process integrity because risk scores should incorporate credit data.  
- *Finalize Documentation after Rejection*: Could be a data entry error where “Rejected” was recorded but staff proceeded with approval paperwork by mistake, or the activity label was misapplied. Either way it contradicts the decision logic and creates audit risk.

**Case 3**: Normatively clean but operationally slow. Possible interpretations:  
- *49h delay to Credit Check*: Application may have been received late Friday/holiday, or external credit bureau had downtime. Delay doesn’t break compliance but impacts customer experience and conversion.  
- *51h delay to Inform Applicant*: Decision made but notification workflow batched or required manual approval. Still within reasonable business bounds if no SLA was breached.  
- *No Follow-up Call*: May be skipped for low-value loans or when applicant was already contacted via email during “Inform Applicant”. Skipping is allowed, though follow-ups tend to reduce post-decision inquiries.

**Key takeaway**: Only Case 2 has severe, non-compliant behavior that would fail an audit. Cases 1 and 3 satisfy all hard rules. Case 3 flags process efficiency issues you’d want to track in KPIs, but it’s not a violation.
