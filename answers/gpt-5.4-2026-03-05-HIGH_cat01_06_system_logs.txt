### Case grouping and activity naming logic

**Case identification**
- I used an **artifact-centric case notion**: each case represents work on one main business object.
- This produced 5 cases:
  - `DOC_DOCUMENT1`  editing `Document1.docx`
  - `DOC_QUARTERLY_REPORT`  editing `Quarterly_Report.docx`
  - `EMAIL_ANNUAL_MEETING`  handling the email about the annual meeting
  - `PDF_REPORT_DRAFT`  reviewing `Report_Draft.pdf`
  - `XLS_BUDGET_2024`  updating `Budget_2024.xlsx`

This is more analyst-friendly than one big “session” case, because the raw log clearly shows the user multitasking across separate work items.

**Activity naming**
- `FOCUS` and `SWITCH` to a target window were normalized to **Access ...** activities.
- `TYPING` was translated by application context:
  - Word  **Edit document**
  - Excel  **Update workbook**
  - Email reply  **Draft email reply**
- `SAVE` became **Save document/workbook**
- `SCROLL` became **Review email/PDF**
- `HIGHLIGHT` became **Annotate PDF**
- `CLICK` actions in email were mapped to **Open email**, **Start email reply**, and **Send email**
- `CLOSE` became **Close document**

**Narrative**
- The user briefly accessed the quarterly report, then worked on `Document1.docx`, paused to handle an annual meeting email, reviewed a PDF, updated the budget workbook, returned to `Document1.docx` to add a budget reference, and finally resumed and completed work on `Quarterly_Report.docx`.
