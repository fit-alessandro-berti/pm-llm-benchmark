## Why these anomalies matter in a realworld P2P environment  

| Problem type | Business impact |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Missing/late approval** | • Unauthorized spend  budget overruns. <br>• Lack of audit trail  compliance violations (e.g., SOX, internal policies). |
| **Payment before receipt / before invoice** | • Overpayment or payment for nondelivered goods  financial loss. <br>• Missed earlypayment discounts  higher cost of goods. <br>• Increased fraud risk (e.g., “phantom” invoices). |
| **Receipt before PO** | • Vendor may ship at a higher price than the organization would have negotiated. <br>• Inventory may be stocked without cost justification  waste of storage and working capital. |
| **Invoicefirst processing** | • Difficult to prove that the invoice matches a valid PO and received goods  higher chance of duplicate or inflated payments. |
| **Extended matching latency** (as in 1001) | • Cashflow inefficiency, missed discount windows, and potential accrual errors in financial statements. |

---

## Recommendations for remediation  

1. **Enforce strict activity sequencing in the workflow engine** – the system should not allow “IssuePO” until the requisition is approved, nor “ReleasePayment” until both “ReceiveGoods” and “MatchInvoicetoPO” are completed.  
2. **Add validation rules**:  
   * *Payment*  *Invoice*  *Receipt*  *Match*  *Close*  
   * *PO* must exist before *Receipt* and *Invoice* can be logged.  
3. **Implement exception handling & alerts** – any case that deviates from the canonical flow should raise a realtime alert for manual review.  
4. **Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs)** such as “average time between invoice receipt and matching” and “percentage of payments released before goods receipt”.  
5. **Periodic audit of outoforder cases** – use processmining tools to flag and investigate patterns like those seen in cases10021004.  

By tightening the control points and ensuring the canonical “3way match” (POGoodsInvoice) is respected, the organization can reduce fraud risk, improve cashflow management, and stay compliant with internal and external regulations.
