###  Transformation Logic & Explanation

#### 1. Case Identification Strategy
The raw log represents a multi-tasking workflow. Following process mining best practices for desktop activity tracking, I grouped events by the **primary digital artifact** (document, email, or file) being manipulated. This creates five distinct, coherent process instances:
- `CASE_01_Document1`: Drafting and finalizing a working document.
- `CASE_02_Email`: Handling an incoming meeting request.
- `CASE_03_PDF_Review`: Reviewing and annotating a draft report.
- `CASE_04_Budget`: Updating financial figures.
- `CASE_05_QuarterlyReport`: Working on the final quarterly deliverable.

*Note on `SWITCH` events:* Context switches were assigned to the **target case** (the application/file the user navigated *to*). This accurately captures the initiation of a new task within the event log structure, which is essential for calculating cycle times and identifying bottlenecks.

#### 2. Activity Naming Standardization
Low-level UI interactions (`FOCUS`, `TYPING`, `CLICK`, `SCROLL`) were abstracted into standardized, analyst-friendly process steps:
- `FOCUS`  `Open Document` / `Switch Context`
- `TYPING`  `Edit Content`, `Draft Response`, or `Update Data` (context-dependent but semantically aligned)
- `SAVE`  `Save Document`
- `CLICK` (Open/Reply/Send)  `Open Message`, `Compose Reply`, `Send Message`
- `SCROLL` / `HIGHLIGHT`  `Review Content`, `Annotate Document`
- `CLOSE`  `Close Document`

This abstraction removes UI noise (e.g., key-by-key typing or mouse scrolling) and focuses on **value-adding process steps**, making the log immediately compatible with conformance checking, variant analysis, and process discovery algorithms in tools like Celonis, Disco, or ProM.

#### 3. Coherent Narrative
The transformed log tells a clear story of a focused morning work session:
1. The user briefly opens the `Quarterly Report` before switching to a new `Document1` to draft an introduction.
2. After saving, they pivot to email, read a meeting notice, draft a quick reply, and send it.
3. They then review a `PDF draft`, highlighting key findings.
4. Next, they update Q1/Q2 figures in a `Budget spreadsheet`.
5. Finally, they return to `Document1` to insert budget references, save it, close it, and resume work on the `Quarterly Report` to draft the executive summary before closing out the session.

This structure enables direct calculation of metrics like **task duration**, **context-switch frequency**, **document lifecycle stages**, and **cross-task dependencies** without manual parsing of raw UI logs.
