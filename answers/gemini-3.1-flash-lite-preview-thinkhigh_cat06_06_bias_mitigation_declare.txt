To mitigate bias in the loan application process, I have introduced several new constraints. These ensure that sensitive attributes are not used for immediate decision-making and that oversight (Manual Review) is enforced for high-stakes decisions involving potentially sensitive demographics.

### Rationale for Constraints

1.  **Existence (`ManualReview`, `BiasMitigationCheck`):** I added these as mandatory activities to the process model to ensure that human oversight and formal bias evaluation are always available components of the lifecycle.
2.  **`nonsuccession` (CheckApplicantDemographics -> FinalDecision):** This is the primary anti-bias constraint. It explicitly prevents a `FinalDecision` from occurring immediately after the system checks applicant demographics, preventing the automation of bias based on sensitive data.
3.  **`response` (CheckApplicantDemographics -> BiasMitigationCheck):** This forces the process to trigger a formal bias check whenever demographic information is retrieved. The process cannot proceed to a final decision until this check has occurred.
4.  **`precedence` (BiasMitigationCheck -> FinalDecision):** This enforces a strict order of operations: the bias check must occur before a final decision is reached, ensuring that the decision is informed by the mitigation step.
5.  **`responded_existence` & `coexistence` (CheckApplicantDemographics -> ManualReview):** These constraints ensure that whenever the process involves sensitive demographic data, a `ManualReview` is triggered. This forces human accountability when sensitive attributes are present in the trace.
