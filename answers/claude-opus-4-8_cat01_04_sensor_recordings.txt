# Process Mining Analysis: Manufacturing Activity Inference

## Step 1: Data Overview & Approach

I analyzed the event log by tracking how each sensor reading changes across time, looking for **state transitions** that signal a shift from one activity to another. Key indicators:
- **Temperature spikes**  thermal processes (cutting, welding)
- **Vibration**  mechanical contact/movement
- **Material flow**  active processing of raw material
- **Tool position changes**  tool engagement/movement
- **Energy consumption**  process intensity
- **Baseline/idle states**  setup, transitions, completion

---

## Step 2: Identified Phases & Labels

###  Phase 1 — IDLE / STARTUP (Events 1–3)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 20–21°C (ambient) |
| Vibration | 0 Hz |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 0 mm |
| Energy | 0.40–0.50 kWh (standby) |

**Rationale:** All readings sit at baseline. Ambient temperature, no vibration, no material moving, tool retracted to zero, and minimal energy draw. This is the machine **powered on but not yet working** — a warm-up/standby state.

---

###  Phase 2 — CUTTING METAL (Events 4–6)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 50  58°C (rising) |
| Vibration | 30  38 Hz (high) |
| Material Flow | 5 units/s (high, steady) |
| Tool Position | 5  10 mm (advancing) |
| Energy | 2.0  3.0 kWh (rising) |

**Rationale:** A sharp jump in **vibration (030+ Hz)** combined with **high material flow** and **rising temperature** is the classic signature of a cutting operation. The blade/tool generates friction heat, mechanical chatter, and consumes material as it advances steadily through the workpiece. The progressive tool advance (510 mm) shows it cutting deeper.

---

###  Phase 3 — ASSEMBLING PARTS (Events 7–8)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 40°C (dropped, stable) |
| Pressure | 2 bar (increased, stable) |
| Vibration | 10 Hz (low) |
| Material Flow | 2 units/s (reduced) |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (held constant) |
| Energy | 2.0 kWh (steady) |

**Rationale:** Conditions become **stable and moderate** — temperature cools from cutting, vibration drops substantially, and tool position holds steady at 15 mm. The **increased pressure (2 bar)** suggests parts being pressed/fitted together. Two identical consecutive readings indicate a controlled, repeatable mechanical joining step rather than aggressive material removal.

---

###  Phase 4 — WELDING (Event 9)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 80°C (peak — highest in log) |
| Vibration | 50 Hz (peak) |
| Material Flow | 0 (none) |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (stationary) |
| Energy | 5.0 kWh (peak — highest in log) |

**Rationale:** This single event shows the **maximum temperature, vibration, and energy** of the entire log, with **zero material flow**. High heat + high energy + no material consumption is the definitive welding signature: intense localized thermal energy fuses parts together without feeding new material through. The stationary tool confirms a fixed-point operation.

---

###  Phase 5 — QUALITY INSPECTION (Event 10)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 30°C (cooling down) |
| Vibration | 1 Hz (near zero) |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (held) |
| Energy | 1.0 kWh (low) |

**Rationale:** Immediately after welding, all activity nearly halts — vibration drops to ~1 Hz, no material flow, low energy, temperature cooling. The tool remains at the workpiece (15 mm) but is essentially idle. This **brief, low-activity pause** at the part location is consistent with a **measurement/inspection step**, where sensors/probes check the welded assembly without performing physical work.

---

###  Phase 6 — PACKAGING (Events 11–12)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 25°C (near ambient) |
| Vibration | 5 Hz (gentle) |
| Material Flow | 1 unit/s (slow, steady) |
| Tool Position | 20 mm (extended further) |
| Energy | 1.5–1.6 kWh (low-moderate) |

**Rationale:** Low temperature and gentle vibration rule out machining/welding. A **slow material flow (1 unit/s)** with the **tool extended to its furthest position (20 mm)** suggests a handling/conveying motion — moving the finished part outward (e.g., onto packaging/conveyor). Low, steady energy reflects light mechanical transport rather than processing.

---

###  Phase 7 — IDLE / SHUTDOWN (Events 13–15)
| Indicator | Reading |
|-----------|---------|
| Temperature | 20–21°C (ambient) |
| Vibration | 0 Hz |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 0 mm (retracted) |
| Energy | 0.40–0.50 kWh (standby) |

**Rationale:** Readings return **exactly to the Phase 1 baseline** — tool retracts to 0 mm, ambient temperature, no vibration or flow. This marks **cycle completion** and a return to idle, ready for the next part.

## Step 3: Discovered Process Model

```
[IDLE/STARTUP]  [CUTTING]  [ASSEMBLING]  [WELDING]  [INSPECTION]  [PACKAGING]  [IDLE/SHUTDOWN]
   (1–3)          (4–6)        (7–8)          (9)          (10)          (11–12)        (13–15)
```

## Summary Table

| Phase | Events | Activity | Key Distinguishing Signal |
|-------|--------|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | 1–3 | Idle / Startup | All baseline, energy at standby |
| 2 | 4–6 | Cutting Metal | High vibration + high flow + rising temp |
| 3 | 7–8 | Assembling Parts | Stable moderate values + raised pressure |
| 4 | 9 | Welding | Peak temp + peak energy + zero flow |
| 5 | 10 | Quality Inspection | Near-zero activity, low energy pause |
| 6 | 11–12 | Packaging | Slow flow + max tool extension, low temp |
| 7 | 13–15 | Idle / Shutdown | Return to baseline, tool retracted |

## Methodological Notes
- **Transitions** were detected at points of abrupt multi-sensor change (e.g., Event 34 vibration jump; Event 67 temperature drop; Event 89 energy spike).
- **Repeated identical rows** (e.g., 7–8, 11–12) reinforced phase stability, confirming these weren't transient spikes.
- The **symmetry** between Phase 1 and Phase 7 (identical baselines) validates a complete production cycle for a single workpiece.
- One caveat: Events 9 (Welding) and 10 (Inspection) are each represented by only a single reading, so these labels carry lower confidence than the multi-event phases. Additional data would strengthen the inference.
