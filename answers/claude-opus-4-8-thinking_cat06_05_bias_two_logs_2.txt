## Which Log Exhibits Bias

**Group B's process is biased.** The bias is introduced through the `ScoreAdjustment` mechanism—the "+5 Association Boost" granted to members of the *LocalBusinessLeadersClub*. Group A's process applies no such adjustment and is internally consistent.

## How the Bias Manifests

### 1. The boost is a proxy for the protected attribute

The two groups are distinguished primarily by `LocalResident` status (Group A = FALSE, Group B = TRUE). The only people eligible for the boost belong to the **"Local**BusinessLeadersClub"—an association that, by its very name, is gated behind local residency.

This is classic **proxy (indirect) discrimination**: the system never explicitly says "favor local residents," but it rewards a credential (club membership) that non-local applicants (Group A) structurally cannot obtain. Every Group A applicant shows `ProfessionalAssociation = None`, not by coincidence but by exclusion.

### 2. The boost changes outcomes, not just scores

Tracing the decisions reveals the effective hiring bar is roughly CulturalFit  65:

| Case | Group | Raw CF | Adjusted CF | Decision |
|------|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| P001 | A | 65 | 65 | **Hired** |
| P002 | A | 60 | 60 | Not Hired |
| P003 | A | 65 | 65 | **Hired** |
| U001 | B | 60 | **65** (+5) | **Hired** |
| U002 | B | 60 | 60 | Not Hired |
| U003 | B | 58 | **63** (+5) | **Hired** |

Two findings stand out:

- **U001 was hired only because of the boost.** Its raw CF of 60 is *identical* to P002 and U002, both of whom were rejected. The +5 is the sole reason for the different outcome.
- **U003 is the weakest candidate in either log** (Skill 75, Personality 65, raw CF 58) yet was hired—over Group A's P002, who had stronger raw scores (Skill 78, Personality 75, CF 60) and was rejected.

So a less-qualified member of the unprotected group was hired over a more-qualified member of the protected group, purely due to the boost.

### 3. The "control" cases prove the disparity is caused by the boost

When the boost is *not* triggered, the two processes treat candidates identically:

- **P002 (Group A)** and **U002 (Group B)** have near-identical profiles and no adjustment  both **Not Hired**.

This is the key analytical point: the underlying evaluation pipeline is neutral. The inequity enters *exclusively* through the association adjustment available to only one group.

## Implications for Fairness and Equity

- **Disparate impact / indirect discrimination:** A facially neutral rule ("reward club members") produces systematically unequal outcomes along the local-resident line. In many legal frameworks this is actionable even without discriminatory intent.

- **Merit is undermined:** The boost inflates a *subjective* attribute (cultural fit) rather than objective competence (skills). It effectively lets affiliation override demonstrated ability, eroding the legitimacy of the hiring decision.

- **Compounding/structural disadvantage:** Local insiders are already more likely to know about and join a local club; the boost layers an additional advantage on top of pre-existing access inequality, creating a self-reinforcing in-group preference.

- **Lack of transparency and auditability:** Because the adjustment is logged as a vague "Association Boost," its discriminatory effect is easy to overlook in routine reporting—exactly why process mining of these logs is valuable.

- **Inconsistency even within the favored group:** U003 was hired with an adjusted score (63) that sits *below* the apparent threshold (65) that gated Group A. This suggests the boost may be applied loosely or with extra discretion, an additional fairness risk.

## Recommendations

1. **Remove or justify the adjustment.** Eliminate the association boost unless club membership is a *bona fide, job-related* qualification—which a social/business club almost never is.
2. **Test for proxy correlation.** Audit whether `ProfessionalAssociation` correlates with `LocalResident` (here it clearly does) before allowing it to influence scores.
3. **Decision based on raw, comparable criteria** applied uniformly across all candidates, with consistent thresholds.
4. **Log all adjustments transparently** with explicit rationale, and run regular bias audits comparing outcomes for matched candidates across groups (as done above with P002/U002).
5. **Reduce reliance on subjective "cultural fit,"** which is the most manipulable score and the entry point for the bias here.

**Bottom line:** Group B is biased. The bias operates not by altering the core process but by injecting a residency-correlated "boost" that flips hiring outcomes—hiring weaker favored-group candidates while rejecting stronger protected-group ones.
