## Rationale for Added Constraints:

### 1. **Existence Constraints**
- Added `FairnessReview` and `ManualReview` as mandatory activities to ensure oversight mechanisms are always present in the process.

### 2. **Absence Constraints**
- Added `DirectRejectAfterDemographics` to prevent any pattern where rejections immediately follow demographic checks without proper review.

### 3. **Responded Existence Constraints**
- When sensitive attributes are checked (`CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge`), a `FairnessReview` must occur somewhere in the process to ensure oversight.

### 4. **Coexistence Constraints**
- Sensitive attribute checks must coexist with `ManualReview` to ensure human oversight when demographics are considered.
- Any `Reject` decision must coexist with `DocumentedJustification` to ensure transparency and accountability.

### 5. **Response Constraints**
- After checking sensitive attributes, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must eventually occur.
- `BiasMitigationCheck` must be followed by `ObjectiveCriteriaAssessment` to ensure decisions are based on objective factors.

### 6. **Precedence Constraints**
- `ObjectiveCriteriaAssessment` must precede `FinalDecision` to ensure decisions are based on objective criteria.
- `ManualReview` must precede any `Reject` decision.
- `FairnessReview` must precede both `Approve` and `Reject` decisions.

### 7. **Alternative Response Constraints**
- After checking race or gender, a `SecondReviewer` must be involved before the next activity, ensuring multiple perspectives.

### 8. **Non-coexistence Constraints**
- Sensitive attribute checks cannot coexist with `ImmediateReject` in the same trace, preventing snap judgments based on demographics.

### 9. **Non-succession Constraints**
- Prevents direct succession from demographic checks to approval/rejection decisions, requiring intermediate review steps.

### 10. **Non-chain Succession Constraints**
- Ensures that `FinalDecision` cannot immediately follow demographic checks, requiring at least one intermediate activity.

These constraints collectively ensure that:
- Decisions cannot be made solely based on demographic information
- Multiple review layers are enforced when sensitive attributes are involved
- Transparency and documentation requirements prevent hidden biases
- Objective criteria must be assessed before final decisions
- Human oversight is mandatory for sensitive cases
