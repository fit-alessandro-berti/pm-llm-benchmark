**Risk and Resilience**
1. Given the reliance on highly specialized suppliers in specific regions (e.g., Taiwan for microprocessors), how would the company maintain operations if a geopolitical crisis or natural disaster incapacitated a primary sourcing hub?

3. How does the company balance the cost-efficiency of centralized assembly in Malaysia against the risk of having a single point of failure for global production?
4. What mechanisms are in place to detect and mitigate "the bullwhip effect," where small fluctuations in consumer demand lead to massive over-ordering at the raw material supplier level?

**Operational Efficiency and Strategy**
5. How could the company optimize the trade-off between the speed of air freight and the cost-effectiveness of cargo ships to maximize profit margins without sacrificing customer satisfaction?

**Technology and Data Analytics**

11. In what ways could the implementation of blockchain technology improve transparency and trust in the tracking of raw materials and compliance with labor regulations?
12. How does the company determine the threshold at which a predictive insight from an AI model is trusted enough to trigger a significant change in procurement orders?

**Sustainability and Ethics**
13. Considering the carbon footprint of global air and sea freight, what strategic shifts could the company make to align its delivery goals with aggressive corporate sustainability targets?
14. How does the company ensure that the "strict labor regulations" mentioned are actually enforced at the tier-2 or tier-3 supplier level, where visibility is typically lower?

16. How do the differing environmental regulations in Europe versus Latin America affect the way products are packaged or the materials used in the assembly process?

**Customer Experience and Market Dynamics**
17. How does the decision to offer direct-to-consumer shipping for VIPs complicate the logistics flow and inventory management at regional distribution centers?
18. In the event of a global product recall, how would the company coordinate the reverse logistics across so many different regulatory frameworks and shipping partners?

20. To what extent does the complexity of the multinational supply chain limit the company's ability to pivot quickly to a new design or a different set of materials in response to a competitor's innovation?
