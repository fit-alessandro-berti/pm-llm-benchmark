### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization  
**Calculating Waiting Times:**  
To identify waiting times between consecutive activities, calculate the **time difference** between the *completion timestamp* of one activity and the *start timestamp* of the subsequent activity for the same patient. This interval constitutes the "waiting time" in the queue for the next activity. For example, the waiting time between Registration and Nurse Assessment for Visit V1001 is:  
\[ \text{Wait Time} = \text{Start of Nurse Assessment} - \text{Completion of Registration} = 09:15:20 - 09:08:45 = 6 minutes 35 seconds. \]  

**Key Metrics:**  
- **Average/Median Waiting Time:** Standard measures of central tendency.  
- **90th/95th Percentile Waiting Time:** Identifies extreme delays.  
- **Maximum Waiting Time:** Highlights worst-case scenarios.  
- **Queue Frequency:** How often a queue occurs (e.g., 80% of patients wait >15 minutes between Doctor Consultation and Check-out).  
- **Impact on Total Visit Duration:** Calculate the contribution of each queue to the overall visit time (e.g., if 30% of the total visit time is spent waiting between Nurse Assessment and Doctor Consultation).  

**Identifying Critical Queues:**  
Prioritize queues using a **multi-criteria scoring system**:  
1. **Severity:** Higher weight on queues with longer average waits (e.g., >30 minutes).  
2. **Frequency:** Queues affecting >50% of patients.  
3. **Impact:** Queues occurring on critical path activities (e.g., delays before Doctor Consultation prolong the entire visit).  
4. **Patient Type/Urgency:** Queues affecting urgent patients or vulnerable groups (e.g., new patients needing lengthy registration).

- **Cost vs. Efficiency:** Adding staff reduces waits but increases labor costs. Solution: Use part-time staff during peaks instead of full-time hires.

- **Bottleneck Shifting:** Reducing one queue (e.g., registration) may shift pressure to the next step (e.g., nurses). Solution: Use **bottleneck analysis** to ensure resources are balanced end-to end.
