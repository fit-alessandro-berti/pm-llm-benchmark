# Insurance Claims Process — Performance Analysis

## 1. Case Durations (Lead Times)

| Case ID | Start | End | Total Duration | Complexity | Region |
|---------|-------|-----|---------------|------------|--------|
| 2001 | 04-01 09:00 | 04-01 10:30 | **1.5 hours** | Low | A |
| 2004 | 04-01 09:20 | 04-01 10:45 | **~1.4 hours** | Low | B |
| 2002 | 04-01 09:05 | 04-02 11:00 | **~26 hours** | Medium | B |
| 2003 | 04-01 09:10 | 04-03 09:30 | **~48 hours** | High | A |

**Problem cases:** Cases **2003** and **2005** are clear outliers (2–3 days vs. ~1.5 hours for the fast cases). Case **2002** is moderately slow.

---

## 2. Root Cause Analysis

###  Root Cause #1: Complexity drives "Request Additional Documents" loops (strongest signal)

| Complexity | # Doc Requests | Avg. Duration |
|------------|---------------|---------------|
| Low (2001, 2004) | 0 | ~1.5 h |
| Medium (2002) | 1 | ~26 h |
| High (2003, 2005) | 2–3 | ~48–77 h |

There is an almost perfect correlation: every additional document request adds roughly a full day to the case. The doc-request loop itself is the bottleneck:
- **Case 2003:** two requests (11:00  17:00  next-day approval) — ~29 hours spent in the documentation phase.
- **Case 2005:** three requests spanning **April 1–3** — over 48 hours just collecting documents.

###  Root Cause #2: Manager_Bill's approval delays on high-complexity claims

| Approver | Cases | Time from last doc request/evaluation  Approval |
|----------|-------|------------------------------------------------|
| Manager_Ann | 2001, 2002, 2004 | 20–30 min (2002: overnight, but next morning) |
| Manager_Bill | 2003, 2005 | **23 hours** (2003), **19 hours** (2005) |

Manager_Bill consistently takes ~1 day to approve, while Manager_Ann approves within minutes to hours. This may reflect workload, availability, or that he is assigned only difficult claims — but it's a measurable per-resource delay.

###  Root Cause #3: Region B mildly slower — but confounded

Region B holds the slowest case (2005, 77h) and the medium case (2002, 26h), but also a fast case (2004, 1.4h). Region A also has a slow case (2003). **Region is likely not a true root cause** — the apparent regional effect is explained by complexity mix and Adjuster_Lisa handling the worst case.

###  Secondary observation: Adjuster_Lisa on high-complexity claims
Adjuster_Lisa issued **3 separate document requests** on case 2005 (vs. Adjuster_Mike's 2 on case 2003), each ~1 day apart. Sequential, piecemeal requests rather than one consolidated request directly multiply the cycle time.

---

## 3. Explanations & Mitigation Recommendations

### Why these issues arise
1. **Incomplete intake for complex claims:** High-complexity claims arrive without all required documentation, triggering reactive, iterative requests.
2. **Sequential (drip-feed) document requests:** Adjusters discover missing items one at a time instead of reviewing requirements holistically up front.
3. **Approval bottleneck:** Complex claims route to Manager_Bill, who appears overloaded or batches approvals once daily.

### Recommendations
| Issue | Mitigation |
|-------|-----------|
| Iterative doc requests | **Complexity-based intake checklists** — require a complete document list at submission for high-complexity claims; validate completeness before evaluation begins. |
| Multiple sequential requests | **Consolidate requests** — train adjusters to identify *all* missing documents in one pass; track "# of doc requests per case" as a KPI. |
| Manager_Bill delay | **Rebalance approval workload** or add a delegation/escalation rule (e.g., auto-escalate approvals pending > 4 business hours). Investigate whether he needs decision-support tools for complex claims. |
| High-complexity flow generally | **Fast-track / triage lanes:** keep low-complexity claims on the current efficient path; assign high-complexity claims to a dedicated team with SLAs per phase. |
| Monitoring | Implement dashboards tracking lead time segmented by complexity, resource, and rework loops to catch outliers early. |

**Bottom line:** The dominant root cause is **high complexity  repeated document-request loops**, compounded by **slow approvals from Manager_Bill**. Region is a spurious correlate. Fixing intake completeness and approval turnaround would reduce the worst-case duration from ~77 hours toward the ~1.5-hour baseline achieved by clean cases.
