# Clarifying Questions About the Property Onboarding Process

## Documentation and Initial Review

1. Can you walk me through how the regional office initially receives and logs a landlord's request for services? Is this done through a standardized form, email, phone call, or some combination of methods?

2. When a property coordinator reviews the documentation you mentioned—deeds, compliance certificates, inspection reports—are there specific checklists or criteria they use to determine whether documents are complete and current? What happens if a document is partially complete but not fully satisfactory?

3. How long does the documentation verification phase typically take, and what is the average turnaround time when coordinators need to request additional or updated documents from landlords?

## Database and Property Recording

4. You mentioned recording property details into a central database. What specific fields or information are mandatory versus optional during this entry process? Are there any property types or features that require additional documentation at this stage?

5. How does the database handle properties that fall into multiple categories—for example, a building that contains both residential units and commercial space?

## Property Manager Assignment

6. When assigning a property manager, you mentioned several factors: workload, geographic specialization, experience with similar property types, and track record with demanding landlords. How are these factors weighted against each other? Is there a formal scoring system, or is it more intuitive?

7. Who makes the final decision on property manager assignment—the coordinator, a regional manager, or an automated system? Are landlords ever consulted or given preference in this selection?

8. What happens if all qualified property managers are at capacity? Is there a queue or priority system, and how does this affect the overall onboarding timeline?

## Introductory Meetings and Expectation Alignment

9. You described an introductory call or meeting between the property manager and landlord. What specific topics or agenda items are typically covered? Are there any mandatory discussion points that must be documented?

10. How is the format of this meeting determined—is it always in-person, or are video calls and phone conversations equally acceptable? What factors influence this decision?

11. What happens if the landlord and property manager have significant disagreements during this meeting about policies, expectations, or responsibilities? Is there an escalation path?

## Photography, Inspection, and Remediation

12. How are photographers and inspectors selected and contracted? Are they internal staff members, or does the company work with external vendors? How is quality controlled?

13. When issues are found during inspection, what is the typical process for addressing them? Does the property manager coordinate repairs directly, or does the landlord handle contractor selection and payment?

14. Is there a maximum number of inspection cycles a property can go through before the onboarding is paused or rejected? How does the company balance thoroughness with timely activation?
