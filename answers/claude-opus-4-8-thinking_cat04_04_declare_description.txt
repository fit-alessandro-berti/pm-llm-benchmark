# Reading the DECLARE Model as a Loan Approval Process

Below I (1) lay out the intended "happy path," (2) walk through it step by step, (3) explain how each family of constraints shapes that path, (4) connect the rules to real-world motivations, and (5) discuss what happens when steps are skipped or reordered — including a few places where the constraints actually fight each other.

---

## 1. The Intended End-to-End Flow

Reading the constraints together, the process the model is *trying* to describe looks roughly like this:

> **Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents  Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Authorize_Contract_Terms  Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer**

with **Proceed_Without_Compliance** strictly forbidden at any point.

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Practical Narrative

1. **Receive_Application** — Every case *must* start here (`init` + `existence`). The application is logged and a file is opened.
2. **Preliminary_Credit_Check** — Triggered after intake (`response: Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check`). It happens **exactly once** (`exactly_one`): the bank pulls credit, scores the applicant, and decides whether to continue.
3. **Gather_Additional_Documents** — Required once a credit check is done (`succession: Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents`). Pay stubs, IDs, proof of income, etc. This step is mandatory (`existence`).
4. **Quality_Assurance_Review** — Documents feed into a compliance/quality gate (`altsuccession: Gather_Additional_Documents  Quality_Assurance_Review`).
5. **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package** — The reviewed material is turned into a concrete offer. The model tightly couples this with QA (`chainsuccession: Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package`), so assembling an offer must sit *immediately* next to the review.
6. **Authorize_Contract_Terms** — Final approval of terms. It is gated by QA (`precedence: Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms`) and is linked to document gathering (`coexistence`).
7. **Transfer_Funds** — Disbursement. The model wants it tightly bound to the offer package (`chainresponse: Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds`).
8. **Notify_Customer** — The customer is informed *after* funds move (`altprecedence: Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds`, and `altresponse: Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer`), and never before the relevant prior steps (`nonsuccession`, `nonchainsuccession`).

---

## 3. How Each Constraint Shapes the Process

**Cardinality & boundary rules**
- `init: Receive_Application` — guarantees a single, well-defined entry point; no case begins mid-stream.
- `existence: Receive_Application, Gather_Additional_Documents` — these two are non-negotiable; you cannot have a valid loan case without intake and document collection.
- `exactly_one: Preliminary_Credit_Check` — the credit check happens once and only once (no skipping, no repeated pulls that could harm the applicant's score or signal manipulation).
- `absence: Proceed_Without_Compliance` — a hard prohibition; the system must *never* take the non-compliant shortcut.

**Existence (co-)dependencies**
- `responded_existence: Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Quality_Assurance_Review` — if you ever build an offer, a QA review must exist in that case.
- `coexistence: Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms` — you either do both or neither; you can't authorize terms without documentation, and gathering documents implies the case is meant to reach authorization.
- `noncoexistence: Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application` — formally, these two must **never** appear in the same case (more on this tension below).

**Simple ordering**
- `response: Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check` — every application is eventually credit-checked.
- `precedence: Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms` — authorization can only occur if a QA review came before it.
- `succession: Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents` — the credit check both *triggers* and *precedes* document gathering.

**Alternating ordering** (no repeats sneaking in between)
- `altresponse: Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer` — each disbursement is followed by a notification before any further transfer.
- `altprecedence: Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds` — each notification is preceded by a transfer, with no second notification slipping in unmatched.
- `altsuccession: Gather_Additional_Documents  Quality_Assurance_Review` — documents and QA alternate cleanly.

**Chain (immediate adjacency) ordering**
- `chainsuccession: Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package` — QA must be *immediately* followed by assembling the package (nothing in between).
- `chainresponse: Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds` — assembling the package is *immediately* followed by the transfer.
- `chainprecedence: Authorize_Contract_Terms / Preliminary_Credit_Check` — a chain-precedence coupling between authorization and the credit check.

**Negative ordering (forbidden sequences)**
- `nonsuccession: Notify_Customer  Preliminary_Credit_Check` — you must never run a credit check *after* notifying the customer (notification is a terminal, not a mid-process, event).
- `nonchainsuccession: Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer` — authorization may not be *immediately* followed by notification; intervening steps (funding) must occur first.

---

## 4. Real-World Motivations Behind the Rules

- **Regulatory compliance:** `exactly_one` credit check and the mandatory `Quality_Assurance_Review` before `Authorize_Contract_Terms` mirror "four-eyes" and audit requirements (e.g., fair-lending, KYC/AML). The `absence` of `Proceed_Without_Compliance` is the explicit "no shortcuts" rule.
- **Fraud prevention:** Forcing the credit check and document gathering *before* any offer or funding (`succession`, `coexistence`) prevents disbursing money on unverified identities or income.
- **Risk management:** `precedence` and the chain couplings ensure no money moves without a vetted, QA-approved offer package. The order is the risk control.
- **Operational best practice:** Alternating constraints keep one-in/one-out discipline — every transfer matched to exactly one notification, no orphaned or duplicated events.
- **Customer satisfaction:** `altresponse/altprecedence` between `Transfer_Funds` and `Notify_Customer` guarantees the customer is always told what happened, right after it happens — never left in the dark, never notified about money that didn't move.

---

## 5. Consequences of Violations — and Internal Tensions in the Model

**If steps were skipped or reordered:**
- Omitting `Preliminary_Credit_Check` (forbidden by `exactly_one`) would mean lending blind — regulatory and credit-risk failure.
- Authorizing terms before `Quality_Assurance_Review` (blocked by `precedence`) risks issuing non-compliant or mispriced offers.
- Running a credit check after `Notify_Customer` (blocked by `nonsuccession`) signals a broken, out-of-sequence process and possible re-decisioning after the customer was already told an outcome.
- Triggering `Proceed_Without_Compliance` would, by definition, void the case's legitimacy.

**Where the model itself is over- or contradictorily specified (worth flagging):**

This model reads like a richly generated specification, and a few constraints conflict with the practical "happy path" — a useful illustration of how DECLARE rules can become *jointly unsatisfiable*:

- **`noncoexistence: Transfer_Funds  Receive_Application`** says the two can never appear together. But `Receive_Application` is mandatory (`init` + `existence`). Taken literally, this means **`Transfer_Funds` can never occur** — i.e., no loan could ever be funded. That defeats the process's purpose and is almost certainly a mis-specified rule (perhaps intended as "don't transfer in the same breath as intake," not "never coexist").
- **`chainresponse: Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds`** demands the package be *immediately* followed by funding, with **no `Authorize_Contract_Terms` in between** — yet authorization is logically and legally required before disbursement (and is implied by `coexistence`). The chain rule and the authorization step compete for the same slot.
- **`chainprecedence` involving `Authorize_Contract_Terms` and `Preliminary_Credit_Check`** imposes an immediate adjacency between authorization and the single credit check; combined with `response (Receive  Credit)` and `init (Receive)`, this forces awkward or impossible orderings, since the credit check naturally belongs near the start while authorization belongs near the end.

**Takeaway:** The *intent* of the model is a textbook, compliance-first loan pipeline — verify, document, review, approve, fund, notify — with negative constraints fencing off shortcuts. But the literal combination of some chain/non-coexistence constraints would either freeze the process (no funding possible) or demand contradictory adjacencies. In practice, before deploying such a model, you would run a **consistency/conformance check** to detect exactly these conflicts, then relax the offending constraints (e.g., loosen `noncoexistence` to `nonsuccession`, and replace the rigid `chainresponse` with a plain `response` so authorization can legitimately sit between offer assembly and funding).
