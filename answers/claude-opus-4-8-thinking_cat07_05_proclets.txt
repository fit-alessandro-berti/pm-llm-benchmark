This visualization illustrates a **proclet-based modeling approach** for representing interacting processes, using a laboratory testing scenario as an example. It consists of three parts:

## (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels

This is the main diagram on the left, showing two parallel process flows:

**Lab Visit (left flow):**
- Starts with "Order entry" (performed by a doctor)
- "Take blood sample" (lab assistant)
- "Receive result"
- "Judge results" (lab assistant)
- Branches into "Everything fine" or "Do tests again"
- "Receive updated result"
- "Send report" (lab assistant)

**Lab Test (right flow):**
- Starts with "Perform test" (chemical analyst)
- "Make report"
- "Finish lab test" or "Perform test again"
- "Provide updated report"

The two flows communicate through two channels:
- **Order system** (top channel)
- **HIS** (Hospital Information System, middle channel)

The boxes with cardinalities (e.g., `*,1`, `1,1`, `1,*`, `1,?`) represent **ports** that specify the multiplicities of message exchanges between proclets.

## (b) Class Diagram Containing the Two Proclet Classes

A simple UML-style class diagram showing the relationship between the two classes:
- **Lab visit** and **Lab test**
- The association "requires" with cardinalities **1..1** to **1..\***, meaning one lab visit requires one or more lab tests.

## (c) Example of a Performative

A table showing the structure of a message (performative) exchanged between proclets:

| Time | Channel | Sender | Receivers | Action | Content | Scope | Direction |
|------|---------|--------|-----------|--------|---------|-------|-----------|
| 11:00 | Order system | Lab visit – John | Lab test – HGB John | Create | Can you perform a HGB test for John? | Private | OUT |

This example demonstrates how a message requesting a test is formatted with its various attributes.

## Overall Purpose
The figure demonstrates **proclets**—lightweight, interacting workflow processes—and how they communicate via channels using performatives, useful for modeling complex, multi-entity processes such as healthcare workflows.
