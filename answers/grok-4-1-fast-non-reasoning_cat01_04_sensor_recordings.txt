### Rationale for Labels

I deduced these labels by clustering events with similar sensor profiles and interpreting them through common manufacturing physics:

1. **Idle/Setup (Events 1-3, 13-15)**  
   - **Patterns**: All sensors at baseline/idle levels (low temp, no vibration/flow, tool at 0mm, minimal energy). Slight temp rise to 21°C may indicate machine warm-up. This repeats exactly at the end, signaling process completion and reset.  
   - **Why this label?** Matches a machine in standby or preparing for a cycle—no active work, just readiness. Common in assembly lines before starting operations.

2. **Cutting Metal (Events 4-6)**  
   - **Patterns**: Sudden temp jump (2050°C), high vibration (30+ Hz), steady material flow (5 units/s), advancing tool position (010mm), and rising energy. Pressure mildly increases.  
   - **Why this label?** High vibration and flow suggest friction-based cutting (e.g., saw or laser on metal), generating heat. Tool advancement indicates material processing. This is a classic "start-up intensive" phase post-idle.

3. **Welding (Events 7-9)**  
   - **Patterns**: Temp peaks at 80°C, pressure doubles to 2 bar, vibration spikes to 50 Hz, flow drops (less material needed), tool stable at 15mm, energy surges to 5 kWh.  
   - **Why this label?** Extreme heat/pressure and vibration spike align with arc/plasma welding (melting metal). Stable tool position suggests holding pieces together. Flow taper indicates transition from feeding to fusing. High energy is typical for electrical welding arcs.

4. **Assembling Parts & Quality Inspection (Events 10-12)**  
   - **Patterns**: Cooling temp/declining energy post-weld peak, low/stabilizing vibration (1-5 Hz), minimal flow (0-1 units/s), slight tool adjustment to 20mm, pressure normalizing to 1 bar.  
   - **Why this label?** Post-heating cooldown with low activity suggests fitting parts (assembly) and checking for defects (inspection)—no heavy loads, just precision adjustments and monitoring. Steady low vibration/flow fits non-destructive verification before reset.

### Process Flow Summary
The inferred process is cyclic: **Idle/Setup  Cutting Metal  Welding  Assembling Parts & Quality Inspection  (repeat)**. This forms a logical assembly line for metal products (e.g., fabricating a part: cut  weld  assemble/inspect). Patterns are consistent across the log, with clear transitions driven by tool position resets and sensor baselines. No packaging phase is evident (no high flow or conveyor-like patterns), possibly occurring off-machine. This process mining reveals a 55-second cycle per part.
