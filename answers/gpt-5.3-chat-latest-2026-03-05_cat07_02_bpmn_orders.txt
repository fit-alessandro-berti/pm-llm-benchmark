It’s a **BPMN-style business process diagram** showing how a customer order moves through several departments: **Sales, Production, Procurement, and Warehouse**.

Overview of the flow:

1. Sales lane
- The process starts **Monday morning** in Sales.
- Steps:
  - Check order for completeness.
  - Check if the **customer already exists in the database**.
- Decision:
  - If the customer is **not in the database**, Sales **adds the customer to the database**.
  - If the customer **exists**, the process continues.
- Then Sales **sends order details** to other departments and later **drafts the invoice** after the product is sent.

2. Production lane
- Receives **order details**.
- Steps:
  - Plan production.
  - Manufacture product.
- There is a check for **material availability/quality**.
- If materials are fine, manufacturing continues until **product complete**.

3. Procurement lane
- Also receives **order details**.
- Contains a **Material Compensation Subprocess**:
  - Order materials.
  - If necessary, **return material**.
- After ordering, procurement **checks materials**.
- If materials cannot be resolved, the process may lead to **order cancellation**.

4. Warehouse lane
- Waits for the **product complete** signal from production.
- Then **sends the product** to the customer.
- This triggers a **product sent message** back to Sales.

5. Finalization
- After the **product is sent**, Sales completes the process by **drafting the invoice**.

Key elements in the diagram:
- **Swimlanes** represent departments.
- **Gateways (diamonds)** show decisions such as customer existence or material condition.
- **Message events (envelope icons)** show communication between departments.
- **Dashed lines** indicate message flows.
- **Subprocess block** handles material ordering and returns.

In short: the visualization models the **endtoend order fulfillment process**, from order verification and customer validation, through production and procurement, to product shipment and invoicing.
