Brief explanation of logic:
1) Case identification: A case represents work on a specific artifact/thread within an application, derived from App + Window (and action details when needed). This yields:
- CASE-Word-Document1-1 for Document1.docx across multiple interruptions/resumptions.
- CASE-Email-AnnualMeeting-1 for the Annual Meeting email thread.
- CASE-PDF-ReportDraft-1 for reviewing Report_Draft.pdf.
- CASE-Excel-Budget2024-1 for editing Budget_2024.xlsx.
- CASE-Word-QuarterlyReport-1 for Quarterly_Report.docx.
2) Activity naming: Low-level actions were mapped to standardized, analyst-friendly activities:
- FOCUS or SWITCH leading into an artifact became Open document/Open workbook (first time) or Resume work; SWITCH away became Suspend work; CLOSE became Close document.
- TYPING in Word/Docs became Edit content; in Excel, data vs structure edits were separated into Edit data vs Edit structure; SAVE became Save document/workbook.
- Email actions mapped to Open email thread, Reply to email, Compose reply, Send email, Browse mailbox.
- PDF actions mapped to Open document, Review document (scroll), Annotate document (highlight).
3) Timestamps: Original timestamps preserved per event. Additional attributes include Application, Window, and Details for typed text or action context.
4) Narrative: The user drafts Document1, pauses to handle an email, reviews a PDF, updates a budget in Excel, resumes and finalizes Document1, then drafts and saves the Quarterly Report. This structure supports process mining with clear cases and standardized activities.
