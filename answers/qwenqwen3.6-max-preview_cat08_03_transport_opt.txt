### **Process Mining Analysis & Optimization Plan for Speedy Parcels**

**To:** Speedy Parcels Management Team  
**From:** Process Mining Consultant  
**Subject:** Data-Driven Approach to Improve Punctuality and Reduce Operational Costs  

This document outlines a comprehensive process mining strategy to analyze Speedy Parcels' last-mile operations. By leveraging your multi-source event data, we will move beyond anecdotal evidence to uncover the ground truth of your delivery processes, identify root causes of inefficiency, and implement targeted optimizations.

---

### **1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

#### **Data Preprocessing and Integration**
The foundation of this analysis is creating a high-quality, unified event log. Your data resides in disparate systems with varying granularities.

*   **Integration Strategy:**
    *   **Case ID Definition:** We will adopt a multi-dimensional approach. The primary Case ID will be **`Vehicle-Day`** (e.g., `V12-20241205`) to analyze route efficiency. However, to analyze customer-centric metrics, we will link **`Package ID`** as a secondary case identifier, enabling **Object-Centric Process Mining (OCPM)** insights where packages and vehicles interact.
    *   **Eventization of GPS Streams:** GPS data is a high-frequency stream, not discrete events. We will apply **stop-detection algorithms** to convert GPS traces into events. For example, a cluster of coordinates with speed < 2 km/h for > 3 minutes near a customer location generates an `Inferred Stop` event. This bridges the gap between GPS and Scanner data.
    *   **Timestamp Synchronization:** We must align clocks across GPS units, scanners, and the dispatch server to prevent false duration calculations.
    *   **Geocoding & Enrichment:** Locations will be enriched with geofences (Depot, Customer Zones, Known Traffic Hotspots) to categorize events contextually.

*   **Challenges:**
    *   **Noisy GPS Data:** Urban canyons may cause signal drift. We will use smoothing filters and map-matching algorithms.
    *   **Missing Scans:** Drivers may forget to scan "Arrive" but scan "Delivery Success." We will implement imputation rules to infer missing milestones based on GPS proximity and subsequent events.
    *   **Data Volume:** Six months of high-frequency GPS data is massive. We will use sampling or aggregation techniques for discovery while retaining full resolution for performance analysis.

#### **Process Discovery**
*   **Algorithm Selection:** We will use the **Inductive Miner** to discover a process model that balances fitness and precision, handling the noise inherent in logistics data. For a granular view of frequencies, a **Directly-Follows Graph (DFG)** with performance overlays will be generated.
*   **Visualization:** The discovered model will visualize the actual flow:
    *   *Standard Flow:* `Start Shift`  `Depart Depot`  `Travel`  `Arrive Customer`  `Service`  `Depart Customer`  ...  `Arrive Depot`  `End Shift`.
    *   *Deviations:* The model will highlight loops for `Delivery Failed`  `Retry Later`, branches for `Unscheduled Maintenance`, and self-loops indicating `Idling` or `Search for Parking`.

#### **Conformance Checking**
*   **Reference Model:** The **Dispatch System** provides the "To-Be" model, including the planned sequence of stops, assigned time windows, and expected durations.
*   **Alignment Analysis:** We will perform alignment-based conformance checking to compare the `Vehicle-Day` event logs against the planned routes.
*   **Key Deviations to Detect:**
    *   **Sequence Swaps:** Drivers visiting Stop B before Stop A despite the plan. Frequent swaps suggest the routing algorithm ignores practical constraints (e.g., one-way streets, parking availability).
    *   **Unplanned Activities:** Detection of `Unscheduled Stops` or long `Idle` periods not associated with deliveries.
    *   **Timing Violations:** Calculating the delta between `Planned Arrival Window` and `Actual Arrival`.
    *   **Rework Loops:** Quantifying how often `Delivery Failed` leads to a second attempt on the same day vs. a return to depot.

### **2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

#### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
We will calculate the following KPIs directly from the enriched event log:

| KPI Category | Metric | Calculation Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Punctuality** | **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTD)** | % of deliveries where `Arrive Customer` falls within `Dispatch Time Window`. |
| | **Window Adherence Deviation** | Average minutes early/late per stop. |
| **Efficiency** | **Service Time** | `Timestamp(Depart Customer)` - `Timestamp(Arrive Customer)`. |
| | **Travel Time per Stop** | `Timestamp(Arrive Next)` - `Timestamp(Depart Current)`. |
| | **Stop Density** | Number of successful deliveries per hour of shift time. |
| **Cost/Asset** | **Idle Time Ratio** | Total duration of `Speed=0` & `Ignition=On` outside of customer geofences / Total Shift Time. |
| | **Fuel Efficiency Proxy** | Correlation of `High Idle Time` + `Aggressive Acceleration` (from GPS speed deltas) with maintenance/fuel logs. |
| | **Vehicle Utilization** | `Time Moving` / `Total Shift Duration`. |
| **Quality** | **First-Attempt Success Rate** | 1 - (`Delivery Failed` count / Total Delivery Attempts). |

#### **Bottleneck Identification Techniques**
*   **Performance Overlay:** We will project average durations onto the process model. "Red" edges will highlight segments with excessive travel times; "Red" nodes will highlight activities with high service times.
*   **Dotted Charts:** Visualizing events over time for all vehicles will reveal systemic bottlenecks, such as a "morning rush" cluster where all vehicles experience delays between 08:00–09:00, or specific vehicles that consistently finish later than others.
*   **Variant Analysis:** We will compare the **"Happy Path"** (variants with high OTD and low cost) against **"Problem Variants"** (late deliveries, high idle).
*   **Quantification:**
    *   *Geographic Bottlenecks:* Heatmaps linking `Low Speed` and `High Service Time` to specific coordinates. This identifies traffic hotspots or areas with difficult parking.
    *   *Activity Bottlenecks:* If `Search for Parking` (inferred from GPS stop to Scanner scan gap) averages 4 minutes in Zone A vs. 1 minute in Zone B, Zone A is a quantified bottleneck adding ~3 minutes per stop.

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Using process mining diagnostics, we will validate hypotheses regarding root causes:

*   **Suboptimal Route Planning:**
    *   *Analysis:* If conformance checking reveals that **>40% of drivers consistently swap the same sequence of stops**, the root cause is likely the routing algorithm lacking local knowledge or realistic constraints.
    *   *Validation:* Compare `Planned Travel Time` vs. `Actual Travel Time`. Systematic underestimation indicates the dispatch system uses average speeds that don't reflect urban reality.

*   **Traffic and Congestion Patterns:**
    *   *Analysis:* Correlate `Low Speed Detected` events with time-of-day and location.
    *   *Validation:* If delays spike predictably between 16:00–17:00 in specific suburbs, the root cause is static routing failing to account for temporal traffic patterns.

*   **Service Time Variability & Parking:**
    *   *Analysis:* Analyze the distribution of Service Times. High variance often points to external factors.
    *   *Validation:* Segment service time by `Location Type` (e.g., Apartment vs. House) or `Driver`. If variance is high across all drivers in a specific zone, the root cause is likely infrastructure (parking/access), not driver skill.

*   **Failed Deliveries:**
    *   *Analysis:* Use **Decision Mining** on the `Delivery Failed` activity.
    *   *Validation:* The algorithm may reveal rules such as: `IF Time > 17:30 AND Customer_Type = Residential THEN Failure_Probability = 65%`. This points to inaccurate time windows or customers not being home late in the day.

*   **Vehicle Maintenance & Breakdowns:**
    *   *Analysis:* Link `Unscheduled Stop` events with `Maintenance Logs`.
    *   *Validation:* Identify if specific vehicles have recurring breakdowns or if breakdowns correlate with high `Idle Time` or aggressive driving patterns (inferred from GPS speed variance), suggesting driver behavior impacts vehicle health.

*   **Driver Behavior:**
    *   *Analysis:* Compare drivers on similar routes/vehicles.
    *   *Validation:* Identify outliers. Driver X may have 20% higher idle time than the fleet average, indicating behavioral inefficiency or unauthorized breaks.

### **4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

Based on the anticipated insights, I propose three concrete strategies:

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic "Learning-Based" Routing Optimization**
*   **Target:** Sequence deviations, travel time underestimation, traffic delays.
*   **Root Cause:** Static routing algorithms rely on theoretical maps and do not adapt to driver knowledge, parking reality, or temporal traffic patterns.
*   **PM Insight Support:** Conformance data shows drivers frequently optimize sequences manually. Performance data reveals specific zones/times with predictable delays.
*   **Action:**
    *   Feed actual travel times and driver-preferred sequences back into the routing engine.
    *   Implement **Time-Dependent Routing** that adjusts estimated travel times based on the time-of-day heatmaps derived from GPS data.
    *   Add "Parking/Search Time" buffers to service time estimates for zones identified as high-variance bottlenecks.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   Increase **OTD by 10-15%** by setting realistic expectations.
    *   Reduce **Travel Time per Stop by 5-8%** by aligning plans with reality, reducing driver cognitive load and re-routing.

#### **Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance & Driver Coaching Program**
*   **Target:** Unscheduled stops, fuel consumption, vehicle wear.
*   **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance leads to breakdowns during shifts. Aggressive driving and excessive idling increase fuel costs and maintenance frequency.
*   **PM Insight Support:** Correlation analysis links `Unscheduled Stops` to specific vehicle usage patterns. GPS data identifies drivers with high idle ratios or harsh acceleration/braking events.
*   **Action:**
    *   **Predictive Maintenance:** Use process data (mileage, idle hours, engine warning events) to trigger maintenance *before* failure. Schedule repairs during low-utilization windows identified in the process model.
    *   **Targeted Coaching:** Generate individual driver scorecards based on `Idle Time`, `Smooth Driving Index`, and `Adherence to Route`. Coach outliers on fuel-efficient driving and reducing unauthorized idling.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   Reduce **Unscheduled Downtime by 30%**, improving vehicle availability.
    *   Decrease **Fuel Costs by 5-10%** through reduced idling and smoother driving.
    *   Extend vehicle lifespan.

#### **Strategy 3: Smart Time Window Management & Customer Communication**
*   **Target:** Failed deliveries, re-delivery costs, low first-attempt success rate.
*   **Root Cause:** Delivery attempts occur when customers are unavailable; time windows are too broad or inaccurate.
*   **PM Insight Support:** Decision mining reveals high failure rates for specific time slots (e.g., late evening) or customer segments. Analysis shows re-delivery attempts add significant cost and delay.
*   **Action:**
    *   **Dynamic Time Windows:** Adjust offered time windows based on historical success probabilities. Avoid offering 17:00-18:00 slots for residential areas if failure rates are high.
    *   **Proactive Alerts:** Integrate real-time GPS tracking with customer notifications. Send "Driver is 10 minutes away" alerts to increase the chance the customer is home.
    *   **Route Prioritization:** Prioritize "High Risk of Failure" stops earlier in the route when customers are more likely to be available, or cluster them for efficient re-attempts.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   Increase **First-Attempt Success Rate by 12-15%**.
    *   Reduce **Cost per Package** by eliminating re-delivery miles and stops.
    *   Improve **Customer Satisfaction** through reliable windows and communication.

*   **Customer Time Windows:** While we aim to optimize windows, hard constraints from key accounts will be respected. The routing engine will treat these as fixed constraints while optimizing flexible stops around them.
*   **Union/Labor Rules:** Driver coaching will be framed as supportive improvement, not punitive, respecting labor agreements. Data privacy regarding driver tracking will be strictly maintained.

    *   *Deviation Alerts:* Notify dispatch if a vehicle deviates significantly from route or idles >15 minutes.

    *   Monthly reviews with operations teams to analyze new variants.
