# BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Verification & Approval Process

##  Process Metadata
- **Process Name:** Invoice Verification & Approval Process
- **Primary Trigger:** Receipt of supplier invoice (typically via email)
- **Process Goal:** Validate invoice against purchase order, secure approvals, resolve discrepancies, and schedule payment
- **Modeling Notation:** Standard BPMN 2.0 textual conventions (`<<Start/End Event>>`, `<<Task>>`, `<<Exclusive/Parallel Gateway>>`, `<<Data Object>>`, `<<Participant>>`)

---

##  Swimlanes / Participants
| Lane/Pool | Roles/Systems Involved |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Accounts Payable** | AP Clerk, AP Supervisor |
| **Purchasing** | Purchasing Officer |
| **Department Management** | Department Manager |
| **Supplier Relations** | Supplier Relationship Manager |
| **External** | Suppliers |
| **System** | Accounting/ERP System (auto-triggers, logs, notifications) |

---

##  Artifacts & Data Objects
- `Invoice` (Input: Email attachment; Output: Matched/Approved/Rejected)
- `Purchase Order (PO)` (Reference document)
- `PO Verification Record` (System-generated log)
- `Receipt Confirmation` (Email/System status)
- `Contract Terms/Agreement` (Reference for pricing disputes)
- `Retrospective PO` (Exception artifact)
- `Payment Schedule` (Output)

---

##  Sequence Flow (Main Path)

1. `<<Start Event>>` **Invoice Received** (Email)
2. `<<Participant>>` **Accounts Payable Clerk**
3. `<<Task>>` **Validate PO Presence**
   - *Checks if invoice references a valid PO number*
4. `<<Exclusive Gateway>>` **PO Valid?**
   - **No**  Routes to Exception Path (see below)
   - **Yes**  `<<Task>>` **Log Invoice into Accounting System**
5. `<<Task>>` **Forward to Purchasing for Verification**
   - *System/Email notification sent to Purchasing Officer*
6. `<<Participant>>` **Purchasing Officer**
7. `<<Task>>` **Compare Invoice vs. PO Details**
   - *Verifies quantities, unit prices, delivery charges, and delivery confirmation*
8. `<<Exclusive Gateway>>` **Details Match PO?**
   - **No**  Routes to Exception Path (see below)
   - **Yes**  `<<Task>>` **Send Confirmation to AP**
9. `<<Participant>>` **Accounts Payable Clerk**
10. `<<Task>>` **System Auto-Matches Line Items & Triggers Approval Request**
    - *System generates email/notification to Department Manager*
11. `<<Participant>>` **Department Manager**
12. `<<Task>>` **Review Invoice, PO, & Confirmation**
13. `<<Exclusive Gateway>>` **Approval Decision?**
    - **Approve**  `<<Task>>` **Record Approval in System**
    - **Reject/Question**  Routes to Exception Path (see below)
14. `<<Participant>>` **Accounts Payable Clerk**
15. `<<Task>>` **Schedule Invoice for Payment Cycle**
16. `<<End Event>>` **Payment Scheduled & Process Complete**

---

##  Gateways & Decision Logic (Consolidated)

| Gateway | Condition | Path Yes | Path No |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **PO Valid?** | Invoice contains valid PO reference | Log & proceed | Request PO/correction from Supplier |
| **Details Match PO?** | Quantities, prices, charges align with PO | Confirm to AP | Contact Supplier / Request correction / Escalate |
| **Approval Decision?** | Manager validates budget, items, & terms | Record & schedule payment | Reject or return to AP for investigation |

---

##  Exception & Variation Paths

###  Path A: Missing PO / Informal Services
- **Trigger:** `<<Task>>` Validate PO Presence  **No**
- **Actions:**
  - AP Clerk or SRM contacts Supplier for PO number or revised invoice.
  - *If informal service:* AP Supervisor coordinates with Department Heads to create `Retrospective PO` or decides on rejection.
- **Merge:** Returns to `<<Task>>` Log Invoice into Accounting System

###  Path B: Purchasing Discrepancy (Pricing/Quantity/Charges)
- **Trigger:** `<<Exclusive Gateway>>` Details Match PO?  **No**
- **Actions:**
  - Purchasing Officer contacts Supplier directly or requests AP to hold invoice.
  - *If pricing/terms unclear:* SRM consulted to verify contract terms  feeds back to Purchasing/AP.
  - *If unresolvable:* Escalated to AP Supervisor.
- **Merge:** Supplier provides corrected invoice/details  Purchasing re-verifies  `<<Task>>` Send Confirmation to AP

###  Path C: Manager Rejection / Investigation
- **Trigger:** `<<Exclusive Gateway>>` Approval Decision?  **Reject/Question**
- **Actions:**
  - Manager rejects or flags invoice in system.
  - Invoice routed back to AP Clerk for investigation.
  - If root cause is PO mismatch or supplier error  loops to Path A or B.
  - If internal budget/authorization issue  Supervisor mediates or rejects permanently.
- **Merge:** Resolved  returns to `<<Task>>` Send Confirmation to AP or `<<End Event>>` Rejected

##  Modeling Notes & Consolidation of Interview Accounts

| Aspect | Interview Accounts | Consolidation & Modeling Decision |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **PO Validation** | Mary checks first; Dan says "never approve without confirmed PO"; Rita handles informal services. | PO validation placed at start. Missing PO routes to Supplier request. Informal services explicitly routed to Supervisor for retrospective PO creation. |
| **Matching Responsibility** | Mary matches line items in system; Dan checks quantities/prices/delivery. | Split logically: Purchasing verifies commercial/delivery details  AP Clerk performs system line-item match  System auto-triggers approval. |
| **Approval Trigger** | Mary: "System sends automatic email after receipt confirmation & match." Dan: "Assume Mary moves to manager." Carlos: "Get notification after match & confirmation." | Modeled as system-automated trigger post-confirmation to eliminate manual handoff ambiguity. |
| **Exception Handling** | Rita handles escalations & retrospective POs; Karen handles contract/pricing disputes; Dan contacts suppliers directly. | Consolidated into two parallel exception lanes: (1) Purchasing/SRM for commercial/terms disputes, (2) AP Supervisor for systemic/escalated issues. Both feed back into verification loop. |
| **Payment Scheduling** | Mary schedules; Carlos assumes it goes back to AP. | Placed as final AP Clerk task post-approval. No ambiguity. |
| **Uncertainties Noted** | Exact timing of system auto-matching vs. manual AP entry; whether Karen touches the system or only communicates externally; frequency of manager rejections. | Modeled as system-assisted with manual override points. SRM activity marked as external consultation (no system task). Rejection path explicitly loops back for investigation rather than assuming rejection rate. |

##  BPMN Conversion Guidance
- **Pools/Lanes:** Create 5 lanes (AP, Purchasing, Dept Mgmt, SRM, Suppliers) + 1 System lane for automated tasks.
- **Gateways:** Use `Exclusive (XOR)` gateways for all decision points.
- **Data Objects:** Attach `Invoice`, `PO`, `Contract`, `Retrospective PO`, and `Payment Schedule` as data objects linked to relevant tasks.
- **Message Flows:** Use dashed message flows between AP, Purchasing, SRM, and Suppliers for emails/notifications.
- **Subprocesses:** Consider grouping "Discrepancy Resolution" and "Supplier Coordination" as called subprocesses if diagram complexity requires it.
- **Annotations:** Add notes for system-triggered tasks (`<<Task>>` auto-match & approval request) to clarify automation vs. manual steps.

This textual model captures the end-to-end workflow, decision logic, role responsibilities, artifact flow, and exception handling as described across all five interviews, while explicitly resolving overlaps and noting areas of empirical uncertainty. It is structured for direct translation into any BPMN 2.0-compliant diagramming tool.
