**The bias is present in the overall system and manifests as systematic disadvantage to Group A (the Protected Group).**

### Key Evidence from the Logs

**Group A (Protected)** receives *no* score adjustments. All decisions are based strictly on the PreliminaryScore:
- P001: 720  Approved
- P002: 710  Rejected
- P003: 740  Approved

**Group B (Unprotected)** receives favorable ScoreAdjustments when members belong to the "Highland Civic Darts Club":
- U001: 720  +10  730  Approved
- U002: 710  0  710  Rejected (identical outcome to P002)
- U003: 695  +10  705  Approved

### How the Bias Manifests

1. **Disparate Treatment via CommunityGroup**  
   The `ScoreAdjustment` column explicitly adds +10 for CommunityGroup membership. In this dataset, this benefit is only available to Group B members. Group A has `CommunityGroup = None` in all cases and receives 0 adjustments. This creates an artificial advantage for Group B.

2. **Lower Effective Approval Threshold for Group B**  
   - A Protected Group applicant with 710 (P002) is rejected.
   - An Unprotected Group applicant with 695 (U003) is *approved* after the +10 boost (effective score 705).  
   This means the system applies a lower bar to people who can claim the community boost.

3. **LocalResident as a Correlated Factor**  
   All Group B cases have `LocalResident = TRUE`, while all Group A cases have `LocalResident = FALSE`. However, the data shows the actual adjustment is tied to `CommunityGroup`, not LocalResident status directly (see U002, which is LocalResident=TRUE but has no community group and receives no boost). LocalResident may function as a proxy or gating factor for access to the community organizations that trigger the boost.

### Systematic Impact

This design produces **disparate impact**:
- Group B members can cross the approval threshold with weaker underlying qualifications because of the community adjustment.
- Group A members are held to a stricter standard with no equivalent pathway to improve their score.
- The "Highland Civic Darts Club" boost effectively functions as an unstated advantage that correlates with Group B membership in these logs.

If the community organizations that trigger the +10 boost are not equally accessible to the Protected Group (due to geography, culture, socioeconomic factors, or historical exclusion), the adjustment becomes a mechanism that systematically favors the Unprotected Group.

**Bottom line**: The bias is not in the individual decisions per se, but in the *rules* (particularly the CommunityGroup-based ScoreAdjustment) that create unequal treatment between the two groups. Group A is disadvantaged by the absence of any equivalent adjustment mechanism.
