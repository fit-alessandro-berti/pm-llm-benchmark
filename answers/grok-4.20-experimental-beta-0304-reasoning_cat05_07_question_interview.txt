**Questions to Ask the Interviewee:**

**Initial Documentation & Verification**
1. What specific red flags or common deficiencies do property coordinators look for when reviewing the initial documentation package? How do you distinguish between minor issues that can be resolved quickly versus those that would delay or prevent onboarding?
2. Who is ultimately responsible for approving the documentation as "complete," and is there a formal sign-off process or checklist that the coordinator follows?

**Property Manager Assignment**
3. Can you walk me through the decision-making process for assigning a property manager? How are the different factors (workload, geography, experience, landlord type) weighted, and what happens when there's a conflict between these factors?
4. What happens if the ideal property manager is not available? Is there a backup assignment process or escalation path?

**Introductory Meeting & Expectation Setting**
5. What are the most important topics that must be covered in the introductory meeting with the landlord, and how do you ensure both parties are aligned on non-standard requirements (e.g., pet policies, maintenance responsibilities, or custom clauses)?
6. How are decisions or agreements from that meeting captured and made visible to other stakeholders who will interact with the property later?

**Inspection & Remediation**
7. After the inspector identifies issues, how do you determine which repairs must be completed before activation versus those that can be addressed post-move-in? Who makes that final determination?
8. Can you describe the typical scenarios where contractors are brought in versus when the landlord handles repairs themselves? How are timelines and quality of repairs monitored?

**Marketing & Activation**
9. What market data or factors does the digital marketing team consider when updating pricing models, and at what point in the process does this pricing get locked in versus remaining flexible?
10. Are there any final gate checks that must be completed before a property is "activated" in the rental portal, or is activation primarily based on the inspection passing?

**Tenant Screening & Lease Finalization**
11. How does the process handle situations with multiple strong applicants? What criteria are used to make the final selection, and how much flexibility does the property manager have in offering concessions or different lease terms?
12. What role does the property manager play versus a dedicated leasing agent in the screening and selection process?

**Quality Assurance & Compliance**
13. Can you describe the quality assurance process in more detail? At what point do the compliance officer and financial analyst get involved, and what happens if they flag an issue after a lease has been prepared or signed?
14. How do you ensure that custom lease clauses requested by landlords don't violate fair housing regulations or company policy?

**Overall Process Flow & Exceptions**
15. What are the most common exceptions or non-standard scenarios you encounter during onboarding (e.g., special insurance requirements, unique property types, difficult landlords), and how are these typically routed or handled differently?
16. Looking at the entire process from initial contact to tenant move-in, what are the major decision points or "handoff moments" where the process is most likely to stall or require rework?
17. How visible is the status of a property's onboarding to the landlord throughout the process? Do they receive regular updates, or only when there's an issue or next step required from them?

These questions are designed to reveal decision criteria, clarify roles, uncover sequencing dependencies, identify exception paths, and understand the "why" behind various steps without touching on technical implementation.
