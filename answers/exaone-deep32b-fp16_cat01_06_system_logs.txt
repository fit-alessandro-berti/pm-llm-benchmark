### Explanation of Logic:
1. **Case Identification**:  
   - Cases are grouped by the **document/email being actively used**. For example:  
     - `Quarterly_Report.docx`, `Document1.docx`, `Report_Draft.pdf`, and `Budget_2024.xlsx` are treated as separate document cases.  
     - The email handling is a single case (`Annual_Meeting_Email`) derived from the email subject in the action description ("Open Email about Annual Meeting").  
   - A case starts with the first interaction (e.g., `FOCUS` or `OPEN`) and ends with a `CLOSE` or transition to another case (implicitly via `SWITCH`).  

2. **Activity Naming**:  
   - Raw actions like `FOCUS`, `TYPING`, and `SAVE` are mapped to higher-level activities (e.g., "Opened Quarterly Report", "Edited Document1 Content", "Sent Email").  
   - The email’s "Reply" and "Send" actions are standardized to "Started Reply to Email" and "Sent Email".  

3. **Temporal Context**:  
   - Events are ordered chronologically within their cases. Transitions between cases (e.g., `SWITCH` to a new app) are excluded from the event log but determine case boundaries.  

4. **Narrative**:  
   The log reflects a work session where the user:  
   - Started drafting a quarterly report,  
   - Switched to editing a document (Document1),  
   - Handled an email about an annual meeting (opened, replied, sent),  
   - Reviewed a PDF report,  
   - Updated a budget spreadsheet,  
   - Returned to Document1 to finalize it,  
   - and finally completed the quarterly report.  

This structure ensures the log is analyst-friendly and aligns with process mining conventions.
