**Final Query:**

```sql
WITH ordered_events AS (
  SELECT 
    *,
    LAG(activity) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS prev_activity,
    LEAD(activity) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_activity,
    LEAD(timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_timestamp
  FROM event_log
),
bad_cases AS (
  SELECT DISTINCT case_id
  FROM ordered_events
  WHERE activity = 'Create Order'
    AND prev_activity = 'Approve Purchase Requisition'
    AND next_activity = 'Confirm Order'
    AND (next_timestamp - timestamp) > INTERVAL '5' DAY
)
SELECT e.*
FROM event_log e
WHERE e.case_id NOT IN (SELECT case_id FROM bad_cases);
```

**Explanation:**

1. The `ordered_events` CTE adds three window columns per `case_id` (ordered by `timestamp`):
   - `prev_activity`: the activity immediately before the current row.
   - `next_activity`: the activity immediately after the current row.
   - `next_timestamp`: the timestamp of the next row.

2. The `bad_cases` CTE identifies cases that contain the exact sequence  
   `'Approve Purchase Requisition'`  `'Create Order'`  `'Confirm Order'`  
   with no other events in between (ensured by checking the immediate neighbors) **and** where the time between `'Create Order'` and `'Confirm Order'` exceeds 5 days.

3. The final `SELECT` returns all events from `event_log` except those belonging to the `case_id`s listed in `bad_cases`. This meets the requirement to exclude cases that satisfy both the sequence and timing conditions.
