**Labeled Manufacturing Process Activities**

After analyzing the sensor patterns, I identified four distinct phases that repeat. The sequence is: **Idle  Cutting  Assembly/Positioning  Welding  Cooldown/Idle**.

### 1. Idle / Standby
**Events:** 1, 2, 3, 13, 14, 15 (and the return to this state at the end)

**Sensor signature:**
- Temperature  20–21 °C
- Pressure = 1 bar
- Vibration = 0 Hz
- Material Flow = 0
- Tool Position = 0 mm
- Energy  0.40–0.50 kWh

**Rationale:** All values are at baseline. No material is moving, the tool is fully retracted, vibration is absent, and energy consumption is minimal. This is clearly the machine at rest between production cycles.

### 2. Cutting / Material Removal
**Events:** 4, 5, 6

**Sensor signature:**
- Temperature rapidly rises (50  58 °C)
- Vibration jumps to 30–38 Hz
- Material Flow steady at 5 units/s
- Tool Position moving from 5  10 mm
- Energy consumption high (2.0  3.0 kWh)
- Pressure slightly elevated (1.5 bar)

**Rationale:** High vibration + increasing temperature + continuous material flow + tool advancing into the workpiece is the classic signature of a cutting, milling, or machining operation. Heat and vibration are generated by the tool removing material.

### 3. Assembly / Part Positioning & Holding
**Events:** 7, 8, 11, 12

**Sensor signature:**
- Temperature drops or stabilizes around 40 °C or 25 °C
- Vibration low (5–10 Hz)
- Material Flow reduced (1–2 units/s)
- Tool Position advanced and stable (15–20 mm)
- Moderate energy (1.5–2.0 kWh)
- Pressure stable at 2 bar

**Rationale:** After cutting, the tool moves to a new position and holds. Vibration and flow drop significantly while the tool remains extended. This pattern matches positioning parts, clamping, or performing light assembly/alignment steps where the machine is holding position rather than aggressively removing material.

### 4. Welding / High-Heat Joining
**Events:** 9, 10

**Sensor signature:**
- Sharp spike in temperature (80 °C) and vibration (50 Hz)
- Material Flow drops to 0
- Tool Position stationary at 15 mm
- Very high energy consumption (5.0 kWh in event 9, then 1.0 kWh cooldown in event 10)
- Pressure remains at 2 bar

**Rationale:** The highest temperature and vibration of the entire log, combined with zero material flow and peak energy use, strongly indicates a welding, brazing, or heat-joining operation. The machine is applying intense localized energy while the parts are held stationary.

### Summary of Process Sequence (one complete cycle)

1. Idle (events 1–3)
2. Cutting phase (events 4–6)
3. Assembly/Positioning (events 7–8)
4. Welding (event 9)
5. Cooldown / Repositioning (event 10)
6. Light positioning / final alignment (events 11–12)
7. Return to Idle (events 13–15)

This sequence is consistent with a typical manufacturing cell that cuts material, positions or assembles components, then performs a weld or heat-based joining step before resetting.

The labels I assigned are:
- **Idle/Standby**
- **Cutting/Machining**
- **Assembly/Positioning**
- **Welding/Heat Joining**

These interpretations are derived purely from the numeric sensor patterns and standard manufacturing process signatures.
