**Analysis of POWL Models**

### Standard Hire-to-Retire Process
A normative Hire-to-Retire process follows a logical sequence: **Post  Screen  Interview  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close**. Screening should precede interviewing, interviewing should inform the hiring decision, and payroll registration must follow onboarding (as an employee cannot be onboarded without being added to the payroll system).

### Anomalies in Model 1
Model 1 maintains the core sequential backbone (`Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`), but has one significant structural flaw:

*   **Interview is disconnected from the decision logic**: While `Screen  Interview` exists, `Interview` has no outgoing edges to `Decide`. This means the hiring decision can be made without conducting interviews, and interviews can occur in parallel or even after the decision. This violates the fundamental purpose of interviews in recruitment.

### Anomalies in Model 2
Model 2 contains several severe anomalies that fundamentally violate process logic:

*   **Screening is irrelevant to decision-making**: `Post  Screen` exists, but `Screen` has no outgoing edges. The path to `Decide` runs through `Interview` only. This means screening results do not influence who gets interviewed or hired, rendering the screening activity meaningless.
*   **Interview precedes Screening**: `Post  Interview` allows interviews to occur before or parallel to screening. This reverses the logical order (you cannot interview candidates you have not yet screened).
*   **Payroll is optional**: The `XOR` operator between `Payroll` and `skip` allows the process to complete without adding the employee to the payroll system. This is a critical business violation—an onboarded employee must be paid.
*   **Onboarding is repeatable**: The `LOOP` operator allows `Onboard` to be executed multiple times. While some onboarding activities may be iterative, modeling the entire onboarding phase as a repeatable loop (rather than a single milestone) is semantically incorrect for this process.

### Which Model is More Normative?

**Model 1 is significantly more normative.**

While Model 1 has a clear flaw (the interview activity is poorly integrated), it preserves the **essential control-flow skeleton** of the process. The sequence from job posting through to case closure follows a logical progression, and the only deviation is that one activity (Interview) is not properly constrained by precedence relations.

Model 2, by contrast, violates the **semantic integrity** of the process at multiple critical points:
1.  It allows hiring decisions without screening (`Screen` is a dead-end activity).
2.  It permits onboarding without payroll registration (a fundamental business rule violation).
3.  It inverts the screening-interview order.

**Conclusion**: Model 1 represents a process with a missing constraint (interviews should precede decisions), whereas Model 2 represents a process with inverted logic and optional critical steps. The anomalies in Model 2 are more severe because they break the causal and temporal dependencies that define the Hire-to-Retire process, whereas Model 1 merely has an incomplete ordering constraint.
