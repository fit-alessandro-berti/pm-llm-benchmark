**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

### Process Overview
**Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Preparation  
**Trigger:** Receipt of supplier invoice (usually via email)  
**End Event:** Invoice scheduled for payment (or rejected/cancelled)  
**Scope:** From invoice receipt to payment scheduling, including normal flow and identified exception paths.

---

### Pools & Lanes
- **Accounts Payable** (Mary + Rita)
- **Purchasing** (Dan)
- **Department Manager** (Carlos)
- **Supplier Relationship** (Karen) – used as supporting role in exception path
- **Supplier** (external/black-box pool)

---

### Main Process Flow (Textual BPMN Representation)

```text
Start Event: "Invoice Received" (Email to AP)

 [Accounts Payable] Task: "Check for Valid PO Reference"
   
Exclusive Gateway: "Has Valid PO?"
    No  Task: "Request PO/Details from Supplier"
              (Mary/Karen/Rita may be involved; Supplier sends corrected invoice)
              
           Loop back to "Check for Valid PO Reference"
   
    Yes  Task: "Log Invoice in Accounting System"
               
            Task: "Forward to Purchasing for Goods/Service Confirmation"
               
            [Purchasing] Task: "Verify Invoice vs PO" 
               (Compare quantities, prices, delivery charges, etc.)
               
            Exclusive Gateway: "Matches PO & Expectations?"
                No  Task: "Resolve Discrepancy"
                          (Dan contacts supplier or escalates to Mary/Karen)
                          
                       (Possible loop back to "Verify Invoice vs PO" after correction)
               
                Yes  Task: "Send Confirmation to AP"
                           
                        [Accounts Payable] Task: "Match Invoice Line Items to PO"
                           
                        Exclusive Gateway: "Perfect Match?"
                            No  Task: "Handle Discrepancy"
                                      (Mary emails Purchasing  if unresolved, escalate to Rita)
                                      
                                   Subprocess: "Exception Handling" (see below)
                           
                            Yes  Task: "Route to Department Manager" 
                                       (System automatically sends notification)
                                       
                                    [Department Manager] Task: "Review & Approve/Reject"
                                       
                                    Exclusive Gateway: "Manager Approves?"
                                        Yes  [Accounts Payable] Task: "Schedule for Payment"
                                                      
                                                   End Event: "Invoice Scheduled for Payment"
                                       
                                        No  Task: "Request Further Investigation"
                                                   
                                                Return to "Exception Handling" or "Match Invoice"
```

---

### Exception Subprocess: "Exception Handling" (Rita primarily)

```text
Triggered from:
- Missing PO
- Unresolvable discrepancies
- Manager rejection

[Accounts Payable Supervisor - Rita] Task: "Investigate Exception"
   
   • Contact Supplier (often via Karen)
   • Create Retrospective PO (when service was informally contracted)
   • Coordinate between Purchasing, Manager, and Supplier
   
Exclusive Gateway: "Issue Resolved?"
    Yes  Return to main flow at appropriate point 
              (usually "Match Invoice Line Items" or "Route to Manager")
   
    No  Task: "Reject Invoice / Return to Supplier"
               
            End Event: "Invoice Rejected"
```

### Key Observations & Ambiguities Noted

1. **Core Happy Path (most consistent across interviews):**
   Receive  Check PO  Log  Purchasing Verification  AP Matching  Manager Approval  Schedule Payment.

2. **Decision Points (Gateways):**
   - PO exists?
   - Invoice matches PO (Purchasing view)?
   - Invoice matches after detailed matching (AP view)?
   - Manager approves?

3. **Roles & Responsibilities:**
   - Mary handles the majority of operational work.
   - Dan is the main control for goods/services confirmation.
   - Carlos provides business approval and budget check.
   - Rita acts as escalation point and exception resolver.
   - Karen is a supporting actor for supplier communication.

4. **Minor Inconsistencies Across Interviews:**
   - Mary sometimes sends invoice to Purchasing *before* full matching; Dan implies he receives it after logging.
   - The exact moment the system triggers the manager notification is slightly fuzzy (after both Purchasing confirmation *and* AP matching).
   - Treatment of completely PO-less invoices varies (return to supplier vs. retrospective PO creation by Rita).

5. **Artifacts/Data Objects:**
   - Invoice (email/PDF)
   - Purchase Order (in system)
   - Goods Receipt Confirmation (from Purchasing)
   - Manager Approval (system flag)

---

This textual model captures the consensus process while explicitly showing the main exception paths and decision points described by the stakeholders. It is structured so a BPMN modeler could directly translate it into a diagram with pools, lanes, tasks, exclusive gateways, and loops.
