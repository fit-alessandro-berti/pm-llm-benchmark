### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization

**Calculating Waiting Times:**
- **Waiting Time Definition:** In the context of this clinic, *waiting time* (queue time) is the duration between the completion timestamp of one activity and the start timestamp of the subsequent activity for the same patient visit. For instance, the waiting time for a patient moving from registration to nurse assessment is the interval from the completion of registration to the start of nurse assessment.
- **Data Utilization:** Using the provided event log, for each case (visit), we sequentially pair consecutive activities. For each pair, the waiting time is calculated as `Start_Timestamp_Next_Activity - Completion_Timestamp_Current_Activity`.

**Key Metrics for Queue Characterization:**
- **Average Waiting Time (AWT):** Mean of all waiting times for each activity transition.
- **Median Waiting Time (MWT):** Median of waiting times to understand typical experience, less influenced by outliers.
- **Maximum Waiting Time (MXT):** Longest observed wait, crucial for identifying extreme cases.
- **90th Percentile Waiting Time (P90WT):** Highlights the wait experienced by the top 10% of patients, useful for setting service standards.
- **Queue Frequency (QF):** Count of how often a particular queue (e.g., registration to nurse assessment) occurs across all visits.
- **Excessive Wait Frequency (EWF):** Number of cases where waiting time exceeds a predefined threshold (e.g., 30 minutes), indicating problematic intervals.

**Identifying Critical Queues:**
- **Criteria for Criticality:** 
  - **Longest AWT:** Queues with the highest average waiting times are prioritized as they impact the majority of patients.
  - **High QF:** Frequent queues, even with slightly lower average waits, can collectively cause significant patient experience degradation.
  - **EWF Impact:** High frequency of extreme waits suggests systemic issues rather than isolated incidents.
