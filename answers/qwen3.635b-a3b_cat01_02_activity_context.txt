### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities and Contextual Differences

The following activities appear multiple times within cases or across cases with distinct meanings based on the **Resource**, **Additional Context**, and **Process Position**:

*   **`Initiate Request`**:
    *   *C001/C002*: Submitted via `WebPortal` by the customer. Represents a digital submission.
    *   *C003*: Submitted via `BranchReception` by a walk-in customer. Represents a physical, in-branch intake.
    *   *Distinction*: **Digital Submission** vs. **Branch Intake**.

*   **`Verify Documents`**:
    *   *First Occurrence (C001, C002, C003)*: Performed by the agent/branch officer as the initial check after initiation.
    *   *Second Occurrence (C001, C003)*: Performed after an intermediate step (e.g., `Submit Additional` or `Adjust Account`) to validate new or corrected information.
    *   *Distinction*: **Initial Verification** vs. **Re-verification**.

*   **`Call Customer`**:
    *   *C001*: `FrontlineAgent` calls because verification is incomplete; purpose is to request missing documents.
    *   *C002 (First)*: `FrontlineAgent` calls to clarify an account discrepancy.
    *   *C002 (Second)*: `FrontlineAgent` calls to confirm that internal adjustments have been made.
    *   *C003*: `BranchOfficer` calls because the customer stepped out; purpose is to summon the customer back.
    *   *Distinction*: Four distinct call types: **Request Missing Docs**, **Clarify Discrepancy**, **Confirm Adjustments**, and **Summon Customer**.

*   **`Approve Request`** and **`Adjust Account`**:
    *   `Approve Request` appears in all cases with identical context ("Final approval given") and resource (`Supervisor`). No renaming required.
    *   `Adjust Account` appears in C002 and C003 with identical context ("Internal correction/adjustment") and resource (`BackOfficeAgent`). No renaming required.

### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

| Original Activity | Renamed Activity | Justification & Contextual Clues |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `Initiate Request` | `Digital Request Submission` | **Resource:** `WebPortal`. **Context:** Online submission. Distinct from physical intake. |
| `Initiate Request` | `Branch Intake` | **Resource:** `BranchReception`. **Context:** Walk-in customer. Distinct from digital submission. |
| `Verify Documents` | `Initial Document Verification` | **Position:** First verification step. **Context:** Standard identity check. |
| `Verify Documents` | `Re-verification of Documents` | **Position:** Second verification step. **Context:** Triggered by `Submit Additional` or `Adjust Account`. Indicates a loop/re-work scenario. |
| `Call Customer` | `Call for Missing Information` | **Context:** "Verification incomplete... request missing docs." Purpose is information gathering. |
| `Call Customer` | `Call for Discrepancy Clarification` | **Context:** "Clarify account discrepancy." Purpose is resolving an error/issue. |
| `Call Customer` | `Follow-up Confirmation Call` | **Context:** "Confirm adjustments made." Purpose is closing the loop/notification. |
| `Call Customer` | `Call to Return Customer` | **Context:** "Customer stepped out... have them return." Purpose is physical summoning. |

**Summary of Improvements:**
*   **Disambiguation:** The revised log eliminates ambiguity by ensuring every activity name is unique within the process flow or clearly distinguishes between different semantic types (e.g., distinguishing between an initial check and a re-check).
*   **Process Mining Readiness:** These names allow for accurate process discovery. For example, a mining tool can now correctly identify the `Re-verification of Documents` as a loop back to verification, rather than treating it as a separate, unrelated activity.
*   **Contextual Richness:** The names capture the *why* and *how* of the event (e.g., `Call for Missing Information` vs. `Call to Return Customer`), enabling better root-cause analysis and bottleneck identification.
