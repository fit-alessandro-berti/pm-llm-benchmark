To address the challenges faced by Speedy Parcels, I will implement a structured process mining framework. Unlike traditional BI, which tells us *what* happened (e.g., "deliveries are late"), process mining will tell us *how* the process unfolded and *why* it deviated from the intended path.

---

### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Preprocessing and Integration:**
The primary challenge is that the data resides in silos with different granularities. GPS data is continuous (streaming), scanner data is discrete (event-based), and dispatch data is static (master data).
*   **Integration Strategy:** I would create a unified **Event Log** using a "Case ID" representing a unique **Vehicle-Day combination**. I would use the `Timestamp` as the primary anchor.
*   **Transformation:** 
    *   *GPS to Events:* Convert continuous GPS streams into discrete events like "Vehicle Moving," "Vehicle Idling," or "Low Speed Detected" based on speed thresholds.
    *   *Alignment:* Use the `Package ID` to link Scanner events to specific `Route IDs` from the Dispatch system.
*   **Challenges:** 
    *   **Clock Drift:** Ensuring the handheld scanner and the vehicle GPS are time-synchronized.
    *   **Data Noise:** Filtering out "GPS jitter" (erroneous location jumps) to avoid false "unplanned stop" events.
    *   **Data Volume:** Managing the massive volume of GPS data by aggregating it into meaningful intervals.

**Process Discovery:**
Using algorithms such as the **Inductive Miner**, I will visualize the *As-Is* process. This will produce a flow diagram showing the typical sequence of events: *Depot Departure $\rightarrow$ Travel $\rightarrow$ Arrival $\rightarrow$ Delivery $\rightarrow$ Departure $\rightarrow$ Next Stop*. 
Crucially, this model will reveal "hidden" paths, such as loops (returning to a customer due to a failed attempt) or side-tracks (unscheduled maintenance stops), which are often invisible in high-level reports.

**Conformance Checking:**
I will compare the *As-Is* model against the *To-Be* model (the planned routes from the Dispatch system).
*   **Sequence Deviations:** Did the driver skip a stop and return to it later? Did they deviate from the intended order?
*   **Unplanned Activities:** Identifying events present in the logs but absent in the plan (e.g., long idle periods in non-delivery zones, or mid-route engine warnings).
*   **Temporal Deviations:** The sequence might be correct, but the *timing* is wrong. I will look for "Time-Lags" where the duration between `Arrive Customer` and `Delivery Success` exceeds the historical average for that specific zone.

---

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
I will derive the following from the integrated log:
*   **On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate:** % of `Delivery Success` events occurring within the `Customer-Requested Window`.
*   **Service vs. Travel Ratio:** $\frac{\sum \text{Time at Customer}}{\sum \text{Time in Transit}}$. A low ratio suggests inefficient routing; a very high ratio might suggest difficult delivery locations.
*   **Dwell Time (Service Time):** Duration between `Arrive Customer` and `Depart Customer`.
*   **Failed Delivery Rate:** $\frac{\text{Count(Delivery Failed)}}{\text{Total Delivery Attempts}}$.
*   **Fuel Efficiency Proxy:** Correlation of `Idling Time` and `Low Speed` events with total distance traveled.

**Bottleneck Identification:**
Using **Resource and Time-based Bottleneck Analysis**, I will identify where the process "stalls."
*   **Spatial Bottlenecks:** Mapping "Low Speed" and "High Travel Time" events to specific coordinates to identify traffic hotspots.
*   **Activity Bottlenecks:** Using **Token Replay** to see if the "Delivery Success" step is consistently delayed. Is the delay caused by the driver (searching for the house) or the customer (not being home)?
*   **Resource Bottlenecks:** Identifying if specific vehicle types (e.g., older vans) correlate with higher `Unscheduled Stop` events, causing cascading delays for the rest of the route.

---

### 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

Once bottlenecks are located, I will use advanced process mining techniques to find the "Why":

*   **Variant Analysis (High vs. Low Performers):** I will group "Cases" (Vehicle-Days) into "Gold Standard" (on-time, low fuel) and "Outlier" (late, high fuel) groups. By comparing their process maps, we can see if high performers use a specific sequence or avoid certain zones during peak hours.
*   **Correlation Analysis (Traffic vs. Delay):** I will overlay GPS speed data with dispatch arrival times. If "Arrival Delays" correlate highly with "Low Speed" events in specific corridors, the root cause is **static routing** that fails to account for time-of-day traffic patterns.
*   **Dwell Time Decomposition:** By analyzing the time between `Arrive Customer` and `Delivery Success`, I can distinguish between **Service Variability** (e.g., apartment buildings take longer than houses) and **Driver Behavior** (e.g., excessive breaks).
*   **Maintenance Correlation:** I will correlate `Unscheduled Stops` with the `Vehicle ID` and `Maintenance Logs`. If certain vehicles show frequent "Engine Warning" events shortly after a "Scheduled Maintenance" event, the root cause may be **poor maintenance quality**.

### 5. Operational Constraints and Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints:**
Any optimization must respect the "hard constraints" of the physical world:
*   **Driver Hours:** Optimization algorithms will be constrained by the `Start Shift` and `End Shift` timestamps found in the logs to prevent illegal overtime.
*   **Capacity:** Dispatch constraints (package volume) will be used as a boundary condition when proposing new route sequences.
*   **Time Windows:** Optimization will prioritize "Tight Windows" identified by the scanner/dispatch data.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan:**
Post-implementation, I will deploy **Process Mining Dashboards** featuring:
1.  **Conformance Dashboard:** A real-time view of how many active routes are currently deviating from the optimized plan (e.g., "Alert: Route V12 is 20 minutes behind schedule").
2.  **KPI Trend Lines:** Tracking OTD and Fuel Efficiency daily to ensure the "Gold Standard" routes are becoming the norm.
3.  **Drill-Down Capability:** Allowing managers to click on a "Late Delivery" and instantly see the GPS trace, the scanner events, and the traffic conditions at that moment to prevent "drift" back to old, inefficient habits.
