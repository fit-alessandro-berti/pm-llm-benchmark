**Trace that the model accepts but the domain forbids**

```
Receive_Order  Check_Inventory  Ship_Order  Package_Items  Authorize_Payment  Close_Order
```

**Why this trace is problematic**

1. **Domain constraint** – In a correct OrdertoCash flow, payment must be **authorized** *before* the goods are shipped.  
   The canonical sequence for the “successful” branch is:  

   ```
   Authorize_Payment  Package_Items  Ship_Order  Close_Order
   ```

   (Some variations allow packaging before payment authorization, but **shipping** is never allowed to precede payment authorization.)

2. **What the model allows** – The `xor_node` choose the *parallel* subtree  
   `PARALLEL[Authorize_Payment, Package_Items, Ship_Order]`.  
   A parallel operator only requires that **all** its children finish before the flow can continue; it does **not** impose any ordering among the children. Consequently, any permutation of the three activities (including `Ship_Order` before `Authorize_Payment`) is a legal trace of the model, and the final `Close_Order` (the last child of the root SEQUENCE) is executed after the parallel block finishes.

3. **Resulting violation** – In the trace above, `Ship_Order` occurs **before** `Authorize_Payment`.  
   From a business perspective this means the company would ship the product to the customer without having verified that the customer can pay (or without having actually captured the payment). This can lead to:

   * Unpaid shipments and revenue loss.  
   * Increased risk of fraud or bad debt.  
   * Breach of contractual or paymentgateway requirements that demand authorization prior to fulfillment.

Hence, while the trace syntactically fits the overlypermissive processtree model, it violates a fundamental business rule of the OrdertoCash process and should be prohibited. The model’s underfitting (using a PARALLEL where a SEQUENCE is needed) allows such illegal behaviour to appear as a valid trace.
