### **3. Proposed Explanations and Mitigation Strategies**

**Why are these attributes contributing to issues?**
1.  **High Complexity & Document Loops:** High complexity claims likely have missing or unclear initial data. This necessitates multiple back-and-forth interactions (Requests for Additional Documents). Without automated pre-screening, this manual loop creates significant waiting periods.
2.  **Resource Bottleneck (Adjuster_Lisa):** Adjuster_Lisa is likely the "weakest link" in the chain. Whether due to time management issues, lack of capacity, or a preference for simpler cases, her delays in processing "Evaluate" and "Request" steps are cascading into total process delays of 24-77 hours.

**Suggestions to Mitigate Root Causes:**

1.  **Resource Allocation & Workload Management:**
    *   **Immediate Action:** Review Adjuster_Lisa's workload. If she is overloaded, redistribute cases to other resources (e.g., Adjuster_Mike in Region A performed Case 2003 at a faster rate than Adjuster_Lisa in Case 2005).
    *   **Skill Matching:** Ensure High Complexity cases are not assigned to resources who are not specialized or available to handle the wait times, unless the process is streamlined to handle it.

2.  **Process Automation & Front-End Triage:**
    *   **Automated Validation:** Implement software that automatically checks documents against requirements during the "Submit" phase. This would reduce the likelihood of the "Request Additional Documents" step being triggered multiple times, significantly cutting down the lead time for Complex cases.
    *   **Pre-Approvals:** Establish clear criteria for what constitutes "Complete" data for Complex claims to avoid the iterative process seen in Case 2005.

3.  **Performance SLAs (Service Level Agreements):**
    *   **Strict Turnaround:** Set an internal SLA for the "Request Additional Documents" step to prevent gaps longer than 2-4 hours (similar to the gap seen in Case 2002). If a resource cannot respond within this window, the case must be escalated or assigned to a backup resource.
