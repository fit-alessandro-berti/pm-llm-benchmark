1.  **Request:** Create Requisition $\rightarrow$ Approve Requisition.

### **Anomaly Analysis**

#### **Case 1002: Severe Control Violations**
This case exhibits the most critical deviations from standard practice.

*   **Anomaly 1: Purchase Order Issued Before Approval.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Issue Purchase Order` (08:40) occurs before `Approve Purchase Requisition` (08:00 next day).
    *   *Problem:* A PO is a legally binding contract. Issuing it without approval means the company committed to a purchase without managerial authorization. This bypasses budget controls and spending limits.
*   **Anomaly 2: Payment Released Before Goods Received.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Release Payment` (02-03 07:15) occurs before `Receive Goods` (02-04 09:00).
    *   *Problem:* Paying before receiving goods creates a high risk of financial loss. If the vendor fails to deliver, the company must initiate a difficult refund process rather than simply withholding payment.
*   **Anomaly 3: Payment Released Before Invoice Matching.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Release Payment` (02-03 07:15) occurs before `Match Invoice to PO` (02-05 08:00).
    *   *Problem:* This violates the "Three-Way Match" principle (matching PO, Goods Receipt, and Invoice). Paying without verification means the company might overpay or pay for incorrect items without realizing it.
*   **Anomaly 4: Approval Happened After Payment.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Approve Purchase Requisition` happens two days *after* payment was released.
    *   *Problem:* This renders the approval process retroactive and meaningless for control purposes.

#### **Case 1003: Premature Payment**
This case follows the correct ordering logic for the first half but fails critically in the payment phase.

*   **Anomaly 1: Payment Before Goods and Invoice.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Release Payment` (02-08) occurs before `Receive Goods` (02-10) and `Vendor Invoice Received` (02-09).
    *   *Problem:* Similar to Case 1002, this indicates the company paid for goods they had not yet physically verified and for an invoice they had not yet seen. This is a classic indicator of potential fraud or a breakdown in accounts payable discipline.
*   **Anomaly 2: Payment Before Matching.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Release Payment` occurs before `Match Invoice to PO`.
    *   *Problem:* Without a match, there is no verification that the amount paid aligns with what was ordered and received.

#### **Case 1004: Goods Received Before Purchase Order**
This case follows a mostly logical flow but violates the procurement ordering sequence.

*   **Anomaly 1: Goods Received Before PO Issued.**
    *   *Sequence:* `Receive Goods` (02-06 09:00) occurs before `Issue Purchase Order` (02-06 09:15).
    *   *Problem:* This is often referred to as a "blind receipt" or "maverick buying."

        *   **Contractual Risk:** Receiving goods without a PO can lead to disputes regarding warranty, delivery terms, or liability.
        *   **Inventory Risk:** The warehouse accepts items that may not have been budgeted or authorized, potentially leading to inventory bloat.
