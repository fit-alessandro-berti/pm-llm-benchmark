The visualization is a multi-stage business process flow (drawn like a BPMN diagram) showing how an order moves from **Sales** through **Production**, **Procurement/Material handling**, and finally to **Warehouse/Shipping**.

### 1) Sales (top lane)
- The process starts in the **“Monday morning”** timing marker.
- **“Check customer’s order completeness”** (gateways are used to branch/validate).
- It then checks whether the **customer is already in the database**:
  - If not, it **“Add customer to the database.”**
  - After customer verification, the **order is sent** (an output labeled like *Send order* / *Order data to next step*).
- An invoice step is shown later: **“Draft invoice”** / **“Send draft invoice.”**
- There are also decision points indicating duplicate/validity checks (diamond symbols with plus-like markers).

### 2) Production (second lane)
- Triggered by **“Order details arrive.”**
- **“Plan production”** runs first.
- Then it proceeds to **“Manufacture product.”**
- A decision checks whether the **product is completed**:
  - There is a branch labeled conceptually as **“Material not fully …”**, indicating production may be interrupted or require correction if materials are insufficient.

### 3) Procurement / Material Compensation (third lane)
- Labeled as **“Material Compensation Subprocess.”**
- Triggered when materials are missing or faulty.
- Steps shown:
  - **“Order materials”**
  - **“Check materials”**
  - A loop/branch for **returning materials** (**“Return material”**) if needed.
  - After the compensation workflow resolves, the process continues toward production/progress (there’s also an *order cancellation* indicator on the right side).

### 4) Warehousing / Shipping (bottom lane)
- After completion, there is a step **“Send product”**.
- It includes a final handoff where the **product is sent** externally (to the customer), shown by the final output arrow labeled like **“Product sent.”**
- The workflow also shows intermediate transitions (dashed connectors and intermediate states) related to confirmation/dispatch.

**Overall:** it’s an end-to-end order fulfillment process: validate order + customer  send order  plan and manufacture  if materials are insufficient, run a material compensation loop (order/check/possibly return)  produce final product  ship to the customer.
