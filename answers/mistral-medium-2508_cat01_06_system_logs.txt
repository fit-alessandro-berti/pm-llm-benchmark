### **Explanation of Transformation Logic**

#### **1. Case Identification**
Cases were grouped based on **document/window focus** and **logical work units**:
- **Document1.docx**: All events related to editing, saving, and closing this Word document were grouped under `Draft_Document1`.
- **Email - Inbox**: Events from opening the email to sending a reply were grouped as `Email_AnnualMeeting`.
- **Report_Draft.pdf**: PDF review actions (scrolling, highlighting) formed `Review_ReportDraft`.
- **Budget_2024.xlsx**: Excel edits and saves were grouped as `Update_Budget2024`.
- **Quarterly_Report.docx**: A separate case (`Draft_QuarterlyReport`) was created for this document.

**Key Heuristics**:
- A new case starts when the user **focuses on a new document/window** (e.g., switching from Word to Chrome).
- Cases continue until the document is **closed** or the user **switches to an unrelated task** (e.g., closing `Document1.docx` ends that case).
- **Temporal proximity** was used to merge related actions (e.g., typing and saving in Word).

#### **2. Activity Naming**
Raw actions (e.g., `TYPING`, `CLICK`) were mapped to **higher-level business activities**:
- **Typing in Word/Excel**: Mapped to `"Edit Content"` or `"Edit Data"` with the typed keys as context.
- **Saving files**: Standardized as `"Save Document"`/`"Save Spreadsheet"`.
- **Email interactions**: Split into `"Open Email"`, `"Review Email"`, `"Reply to Email"`, and `"Send Email"`.
- **PDF actions**: `"Review Document"` (scrolling) and `"Highlight Key Section"`.
- **Application switches**: Implicitly handled by case transitions (not always explicit in the event log).

#### **3. Additional Attributes**
- **Application**: Retained to show tool usage (e.g., Word vs. Excel).
- **Document/Window**: Critical for case grouping and context.
- **Additional Context**: Preserved raw details (e.g., typed keys, email actions) for drill-down analysis.

#### **4. Coherent Narrative**
The event log tells a story of a user’s morning work session:
1. **Drafting a document** (`Document1.docx`) with references to a budget.
2. **Handling an email** about an annual meeting (opening, replying, sending).
3. **Reviewing a PDF report** (scrolling, highlighting key sections).
4. **Updating a budget spreadsheet** (Excel edits).
5. **Returning to the draft** to add budget references, then saving/closing.
6. **Starting a new document** (`Quarterly_Report.docx`) for an executive summary.

This structure allows process mining tools to discover patterns like:
- Common sequences (e.g., editing  saving  switching apps).
- Bottlenecks (e.g., time spent reviewing emails).
- Frequently co-occurring activities (e.g., Excel updates followed by Word edits).
