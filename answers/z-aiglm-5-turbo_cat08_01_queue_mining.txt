### 1. Queue Identification and Characterization

**Calculating and Defining Waiting Time**
In the context of this event log, "waiting time" (queue time) is defined as the exact duration a patient is idle between the completion of one activity and the start of the next. It is strictly calculated using the timestamps:
*   *Formula:* `Waiting Time (Activity A to B) = START Timestamp (Activity B) - COMPLETE Timestamp (Activity A)`
*   *Exclusion:* Processing time (the difference between START and COMPLETE for a single activity) is strictly separated from waiting time. 

**Key Metrics for Characterization**
To build a statistically sound profile of each queue, I would calculate the following metrics for the transition between every pair of consecutive activities (e.g., Registration $\rightarrow$ Nurse Assessment):
*   **Central Tendency:** Mean and Median waiting times (Median is crucial here as wait times are typically right-skewed by outliers).
*   **Dispersion & Extremes:** Standard Deviation, Maximum wait time, and the **90th Percentile** (critical for patient experience, as the "worst cases" disproportionately drive dissatisfaction).
*   **Frequency & Volume:** Total number of cases passing through the queue.
*   **Excessive Wait Rate:** The percentage of cases where the wait time exceeds a clinically or administratively defined threshold (e.g., >15 minutes for Nurse Assessment, >30 minutes for Diagnostics).

**Identifying Critical Queues**
I would not simply rank queues by "longest average wait." A 45-minute wait for a rare test impacts fewer people than a consistent 20-minute wait before Nurse Assessment. I would identify critical queues using a composite scoring model:
1.  **Total Wait Burden:** (Average Wait Time $\times$ Queue Frequency). This highlights bottlenecks that drain the most aggregate time from the system.
2.  **Pain Point Index:** Multiplying the 90th percentile wait by a weighting factor based on patient complaints/survey data (if available) or patient vulnerability.
3.  **Target Segment Impact:** Prioritizing queues that severely impact high-value or high-risk segments (e.g., "Urgent" patients experiencing long waits, or "New" patients whose first impression is degraded).
