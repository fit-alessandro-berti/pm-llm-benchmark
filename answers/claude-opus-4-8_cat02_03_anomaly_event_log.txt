# Procure-to-Pay Anomaly Analysis

## Standard P2P Reference Flow
The expected sequence for a healthy P2P process is:
1. Create Purchase Requisition
2. Approve Purchase Requisition
3. Issue Purchase Order
4. Receive Goods
5. Vendor Invoice Received
6. Match Invoice to PO (3-way match: PO, Goods Receipt, Invoice)
7. Release Payment
8. Close Case

---

## Case 1001 —  NORMAL (Baseline)

| Order | Activity |
|-------|----------|
|  | Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Invoice  Match  Pay  Close |

This case follows the canonical flow perfectly. It serves as the control/reference case.

---

## Case 1002 —  MULTIPLE SERIOUS ANOMALIES

| Issue | Observation | Why It's Problematic |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Missing approval before PO** | PO issued (08:40) before any approval | PO committed financially **without authorization** — control breakdown |
| **Approval occurs AFTER the fact** | "Approve Purchase Requisition" appears on 02-03, *after* payment already released | This is a **rubber-stamp / retroactive approval** — defeats the purpose of pre-spend control |
| **Payment before Goods Receipt** | Pay (02-03 07:15) before Receive Goods (02-04) | Paying for goods **not yet received** — high risk of fraud/loss |
| **Payment before Invoice Matching** | Pay before Match Invoice to PO (02-05) | **No 3-way match performed before payment** — bypasses verification of price/quantity/receipt |

**Verdict:** This is the most severely anomalous case. Nearly every control is violated or out of sequence. The ordering (PO  Invoice  **Pay**  Approve  Receive  Match  Close) suggests either a bypassed approval workflow, fraud, or a system error. Payment was authorized with no validation whatsoever.

---

## Case 1003 —  SERIOUS SEQUENCE ANOMALY

| Issue | Observation | Why It's Problematic |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Payment before everything downstream** | Pay (02-08) occurs *before* Invoice Received (02-09), Receive Goods (02-10), and Match (02-11) | Classic **"pay-first" anomaly** — money disbursed with no invoice, no goods, no match |
| **Invoice received after payment** | Cannot validate what you're paying for | No documentation existed to support payment |
| **No 3-way match before payment** | Match happens *after* payment | The matching control is rendered useless |

**Verdict:** Payment was released with **no invoice, no goods receipt, and no matching** completed beforehand. While the PR was properly created and approved, the back half of the process is completely inverted. This pattern is a strong indicator of **maverick payments or potential fraud**.

---

## Case 1004 —  MODERATE ANOMALY

| Issue | Observation | Why It's Problematic |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Goods received before PO issued** | Receive Goods (02-06 09:00) precedes Issue PO (02-06 09:15) | Goods arrived **without a formal purchase order** — indicates informal/maverick procurement |

**Verdict:** Aside from the PO/Goods inversion, the rest of the flow (Approve  ...  Invoice  Match  Pay  Close) is correct. This is the **least severe** anomaly. It may reflect:
- An **emergency/rush purchase** where goods came before paperwork
- A **retroactive PO** created to cover an existing delivery
- A timing/system entry error (only 15 minutes apart)

While less dangerous than 1002/1003, it still undermines the principle that **no goods should be received without an authorized PO**, weakening spend control and budget tracking.

## Summary Table

| Case | Severity | Key Anomaly |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1001 |  None | Compliant reference case |
| 1002 |  Critical | Payment before approval/receipt/match; retroactive approval |
| 1003 |  Critical | Payment before invoice, goods, and matching |
| 1004 |  Moderate | Goods received before PO issued |

## Overarching Risks Identified
1. **Broken segregation of duties / authorization controls** (1002, 1003) — payments without proper approval invite fraud.
2. **Violation of the 3-way match principle** (1002, 1003) — paying without verifying PO, goods, and invoice agreement risks overpayment, duplicate payment, or paying for undelivered goods.
3. **Maverick / unauthorized procurement** (1004) — receiving goods without a PO erodes budget control and contract compliance.

**Recommendation:** Cases 1002 and 1003 warrant immediate audit/investigation. The system should enforce *hard stops* preventing payment release until approval, goods receipt, and invoice matching are completed.
