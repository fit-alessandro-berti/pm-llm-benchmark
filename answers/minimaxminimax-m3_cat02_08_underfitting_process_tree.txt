## A Trace Accepted by the Model but Invalid in the Domain

**Trace:**

```
Receive_Order  Check_Inventory  Ship_Order  Authorize_Payment  Package_Items  Close_Order
```

### Why the model accepts it

The three activities `Authorize_Payment`, `Package_Items`, and `Ship_Order` are grouped under an **`Operator.PARALLEL`** node. The semantics of the parallel (interleaving / concurrency) operator is that any interleaving of its children is allowed, as long as every child appears exactly once. Therefore, every permutation of these three activities — including `Ship  Authorize  Package` shown above — is technically a valid trace according to the model.

### Why this trace is problematic from a domain perspective

The real Order-to-Cash process imposes a strict causal order on these three activities:

1. **Authorize_Payment** must occur *before* anything is physically prepared — you must confirm the customer can be charged before the warehouse starts working.
2. **Package_Items** must occur *before* **Ship_Order** — you cannot ship goods that have not yet been packed; doing so would mean dispatching (possibly empty or incorrect) packages and bypassing the packing quality check.

The trace `Ship_Order  Authorize_Payment  Package_Items` violates **both** of these constraints:

- **Shipping before authorization:** The order is dispatched before the payment has been approved, creating the risk of sending goods to a customer whose payment fails.
- **Shipping before packaging:** The shipment is sent out before the items have been packed, which is operationally impossible in any real warehouse process.

### Root cause in the model

The underfitting comes from using **`Operator.PARALLEL`** where the domain actually requires a **`SEQUENCE`** (Authorize  Package  Ship). The XOR/SEQUENCE structure of the rest of the tree is fine, but the internal ordering of the "successful order" sub-process is too permissive. Replacing the `PARALLEL` operator with `SEQUENCE` would eliminate this kind of incorrect behavior.
