The visualization is a BPMN-style business process diagram for handling a customer order, organized into four swimlanes: **Sales**, **Production**, **Procurement**, and **Warehouse**.

### Overall flow

1. **Sales**
   - The process starts on **Monday morning**.
   - Sales checks the customer order for completeness.
   - Sales checks whether the customer already exists in the **database**.
   - If the customer is not in the database, they are added.
   - Once the order is validated, Sales sends the **order details** to both Production and Procurement.
   - Later, after receiving confirmation that the product has been sent, Sales drafts the invoice and the process ends.

2. **Production**
   - Production receives the order details.
   - It begins production planning.
   - Production also waits for Procurement to confirm that the material is not faulty.
   - Once planning is complete and materials are approved, Production manufactures the product.
   - When the product is complete, Production notifies the Warehouse.

3. **Procurement**
   - Procurement receives the order details.
   - It runs a **Material Compensation Subprocess**, where materials are ordered.
   - If needed, there is a compensation action to **return material**.
   - Procurement then checks the materials.
   - If the materials are acceptable, Procurement sends a “material not faulty” message to Production.
   - If there is a problem, an **order cancellation** path is triggered.

4. **Warehouse**
   - The Warehouse receives notification that the product is complete.
   - It sends the product to the customer.
   - It then sends a “product sent” message back to Sales.

### Notation used

- **Solid arrows** show the normal sequence of work.
- **Dashed arrows** show message flows between departments.
- **Diamonds** represent decision or synchronization gateways.
- **Rounded rectangles** are tasks or subprocesses.
- **Circles** represent events such as starts, messages, timers, and process endings.
- A **database cylinder** represents the customer database.
