### 1) Material Receiving & Preparation
**Low-level events included:**

- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet
- Preheat metal sheet

**Why this grouping makes sense:**
These events are all part of getting the raw material ready for production. The sequence is clearly preparatory: identify the part, register it in the system, position it for processing, physically align it, and condition it thermally before fabrication begins. They occur consecutively at the start of the case and are performed by preparation-oriented resources.

**Suggested high-level name:**
- `Material Preparation`

### 2) Welding / Assembly
**Low-level events included:**
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B

**Why this grouping makes sense:**
These events form a single fabrication step: the operator acquires the required tool and performs the welding operations that join the metal sheet components. The tool pickup is not a separate business step; it is an enabling action for the assembly work. The welding actions are tightly temporally connected and logically belong to the same assembly phase.

**Suggested high-level name:**
- `Assembly and Welding`

---

### 3) In-Process Quality Verification
**Low-level events included:**
- Measure weld integrity

**Why this grouping makes sense:**
This is a distinct inspection/checkpoint after welding and before finishing. It serves as immediate validation of the assembly result. Even though only one event appears in this sample, it represents a clear quality control stage.

**Suggested high-level name:**
- `Weld Inspection`

---

### 4) Surface Finishing
**Low-level events included:**
- Apply protective coating
- Dry coating

**Why this grouping makes sense:**
These events are clearly part of the finishing phase. The coating application and subsequent drying are sequential sub-activities of the same process step: protecting and finalizing the product surface. They are technologically dependent and should be grouped together.

**Suggested high-level name:**
- `Protective Finishing`

---

### 5) Final Quality Check
**Low-level events included:**
- Visual check

**Why this grouping makes sense:**
This is the final validation before the item leaves the process. It is a classic end-of-line inspection, distinct from the in-process weld integrity measurement because it checks the overall result after finishing.

**Suggested high-level name:**
- `Final Inspection`

## General Grouping Rules Used

1. **Temporal adjacency**  
   Events occurring back-to-back with no intervening unrelated activity are likely part of the same high-level step.

2. **Shared purpose**  
   Actions that collectively accomplish one business goal were grouped together, such as all steps needed to prepare raw material.

3. **Resource and role consistency**  
   Events performed by related resources or in a natural handoff sequence were grouped, e.g., operator setup followed by machine alignment.

4. **Process phase logic**  
   The process naturally moves through phases:
   - Preparation
   - Assembly/Fabrication
   - Quality Control
   - Finishing
   - Final Inspection
