**Explanation of modeling choices:**

- **Equivalence**: Each activity in this ordered process occurs exactly once per case, so they all have matching occurrence counts.
- **Always Before / Always After**: Because the process has a linear sequence (order receipt  availability check  reservation  picking  QC  packing  label  dispatch, and separately invoicing  payment), each activity has a predecessor and successor it must precede/follow.
- **Never Together**: Empty, since no activities are mutually exclusive in a successful fulfillment flow.
- **Activity Occurrences**: Each activity happens exactly once per customer order.
- **Directly Follows**: Models the strict sequential steps in the physical fulfillment chain (RO  CA  RI  PI  QC  PO  GSL  D). Invoicing (IC) and Record Payment (RP) are not placed in `directly_follows` because they may occur in parallel or at a different point relative to the physical dispatch flow—only their ordering relative to RO and each other is constrained via `always_before`/`always_after`.
