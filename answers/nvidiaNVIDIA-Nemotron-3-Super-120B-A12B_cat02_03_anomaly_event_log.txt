### Why these patterns are problematic in a ProcuretoPay context

| Control principle | How the anomaly violates it |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Approval before commitment** (requisition  PO) | Cases1002 &1004 issue a PO (or receive goods) without prior requisition approval. |
| **Threeway match** (PO  Goods Receipt  Invoice) before payment | Cases1002 &1003 release payment before any matching step; case1002 even pays before the invoice exists. |
| **Segregation of duties** (requester  approver  payer) | Early payment and outoforder activities concentrate too much authority in a single role, weakening checks and balances. |
| **Timing logic** (goods receipt  invoice  payment) | Invoices received before goods (1002) or after payment (1003) and goods receipt before PO (1004) break the causal chain that ensures you only pay for what you actually ordered and received. |
| **Auditability & traceability** | Outofsequence timestamps make it impossible to reconstruct a legitimate procurement trail, leading to failed internal/external audits. |

### Bottom line

- **Case1001** follows the textbook P2P flow – no concerns.
- **Cases1002,1003,** and **1004** each contain one or more clear deviations that undermine core procurement controls, expose the organization to financial loss, fraud, inventory inaccuracies, and compliance violations.  
Remediation would involve enforcing systemlevel validations (e.g., block PO creation until requisition is approved, block payment until a successful threeway match, enforce goods receipt before invoicing) and reinforcing procedural training and segregation of duties.
