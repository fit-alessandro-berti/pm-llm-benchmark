Here are the main anomalies in the log, based on a standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) flow:

## Expected “normal” P2P order
A typical P2P sequence is roughly:

1. Create Purchase Requisition  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Issue Purchase Order  
4. Receive Goods  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Match Invoice to PO / 3-way match  
7. Release Payment  
8. Close Case  

Some companies may vary slightly, but the key control idea is that:
- the requisition should be approved before the PO is issued,
- goods should be received before payment,
- the invoice should usually be matched before payment,
- and ideally invoice/payment should not happen before receipt of goods unless there is a special prepayment arrangement.

## Case 1002
Sequence:
- Create Purchase Requisition
- Issue Purchase Order
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Release Payment
- Approve Purchase Requisition
- Receive Goods
- Match Invoice to PO
- Close Case

### Anomalies
1. **Purchase Order issued before requisition approval**
   - The PO is issued immediately after requisition creation, before approval.
   - Problem: This bypasses approval controls and can violate authorization policy.

2. **Payment released before goods receipt**
   - Payment occurs before the goods are received.
   - Problem: This creates risk of paying for goods not yet delivered, or never delivered.

3. **Payment released before invoice matching**
   - Invoice matching happens after payment.
   - Problem: Standard controls require matching before payment to detect quantity/price discrepancies or duplicate invoices.

4. **Approval happens after payment**
   - Approval is performed very late, after the financial commitment and payment.
   - Problem: This defeats the purpose of approval as a preventive control.

### Why problematic
This case suggests serious control violations and possibly fraud risk, such as:
- unauthorized purchasing,
- premature payment,
- weak invoice validation,
- poor segregation of duties or process enforcement.

## Case 1003
Sequence:
- Create Purchase Requisition
- Approve Purchase Requisition
- Issue Purchase Order
- Release Payment
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Receive Goods
- Match Invoice to PO
- Close Case

### Anomalies
1. **Release Payment before Vendor Invoice Received**
   - Payment happens before invoice arrival.
   - Problem: Normally the invoice is the basis for payment processing.

2. **Release Payment before Receive Goods**
   - Payment is made before goods receipt.
   - Problem: This breaks the standard “pay only after receipt” control.

3. **Release Payment before invoice matching**
   - Matching occurs after payment.
   - Problem: Controls are being applied too late to prevent overpayment or payment for nonconforming goods.

### Why problematic
This looks like a major ordering issue. Unless this is an intentional prepayment or advance-payment scenario, it is highly unusual and risky.

Potential concerns:
- paying without evidence of receipt,
- inability to stop incorrect or duplicate payments,
- weak financial controls.

---

## Case 1004
Sequence:
- Create Purchase Requisition
- Approve Purchase Requisition
- Receive Goods
- Issue Purchase Order
- Vendor Invoice Received
- Match Invoice to PO
- Release Payment
- Close Case

### Anomalies
1. **Receive Goods before Purchase Order**
   - Goods are received before the PO is issued.
   - Problem: In normal P2P, the PO authorizes the supplier to ship goods. Receiving before PO suggests an off-process delivery or missing procurement authorization.

2. **Receive Goods before PO approval/issuance**
   - Even more broadly, goods are received before the formal purchasing commitment is made.
   - Problem: This indicates lack of purchasing control and makes matching difficult.

### Why problematic
This may indicate:
- unauthorized or emergency purchasing,
- “free goods” or unplanned deliveries,
- poor process discipline,
- potential mismatch between receipt and purchasing documents.

In some organizations, emergency purchases can happen, but they should still be documented and controlled. As logged, this sequence is unusual.

# Overall problematic patterns

The main issues across anomalous cases are:

- **Approval after PO issuance**: weak authorization control
- **Payment before goods receipt**: risk of paying for undelivered goods
- **Payment before invoice matching**: weak invoice validation
- **Goods receipt before PO issuance**: lack of procurement authorization
- **Invoice/payment before normal checkpoints**: increased fraud and error risk
