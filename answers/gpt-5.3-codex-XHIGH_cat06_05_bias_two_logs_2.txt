The **biased log is Group B** (and, more importantly, the overall process when comparing A vs. B).

### Why Group B is biased
Group B applies a **+5 CulturalFit boost** for membership in `LocalBusinessLeadersClub`.  
That is a **different scoring rule** based on a social affiliation, not core candidate performance.

This is bias because:

- It is **disparate treatment**: candidates are treated differently due to association status.
- The association appears only in the **unprotected group**, so it creates a structural advantage for that group.
- It can act as a **proxy** for privilege/network access (local ties, class, etc.), not merit.

### How it shows up in decisions
The boost changes outcomes near decision thresholds:

- **U001**: Cultural fit goes from 60 to 65  hired.
- **U003**: Cultural fit goes from 58 to 63  hired.
- Similar non-boosted candidates (e.g., **U002**, **P002**) are not hired.

So affiliation is materially influencing who gets hired.

### Fairness & equity implications
- Violates **equal opportunity** and consistency of evaluation.
- Undermines **merit-based hiring**.
- Creates potential **legal/compliance risk** (disparate impact against protected candidates).
- Reinforces existing social/network advantages and can reduce diversity.

So, even if top-line hire rates might look similar in this tiny sample, the **process itself is inequitable**.
