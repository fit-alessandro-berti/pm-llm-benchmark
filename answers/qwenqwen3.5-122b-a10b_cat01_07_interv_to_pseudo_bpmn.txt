# Process Model: Invoice Approval and Payment Workflow

## 1. Process Overview
This model describes the "Invoice Approval and Payment" process based on stakeholder interviews. The process begins with the receipt of an invoice and ends with the scheduling of payment. It involves a "Happy Path" (standard flow) and several exception paths for discrepancies, missing data, or budget issues.

## 2. Participants (Swimlanes)
The process is divided into the following roles/departments:
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk:** Primary process owner (e.g., Mary).
*   **Purchasing Department:** Validates receipt and order details (e.g., Dan).
*   **Department Manager:** Final budgetary approval (e.g., Carlos).
*   **AP Supervisor:** Handles escalations and complex exceptions (e.g., Rita).
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM):** Assists with supplier-side resolution (e.g., Karen).
*   **Supplier (External):** Source of invoices and information.

## 3. Artifacts & Data Objects
*   **Invoice:** Input document (email/PDF).
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference document for validation.
*   **Goods Receipt Note:** Confirmation of service/product delivery.
*   **Payment Schedule:** Output artifact for finance run.
*   **System Log:** Digital record of the invoice status.

---

## 4. Process Flow Description

### Phase 1: Intake and Validation
1.  **Start Event:** Invoice received (typically via email).
2.  **Task (AP Clerk):** **Check PO Number.**
    *   *Input:* Invoice.
    *   *Action:* Verify if a valid Purchase Order (PO) number is present on the invoice.
3.  **Exclusive Gateway:** **Is PO Valid?**
    *   **NO:**
        *   **Task (AP Clerk):** **Request Details from Supplier.**
        *   *Flow:* Return invoice to supplier.
        *   *Loop:* Wait for revised invoice. Return to **Start Event**.
    *   **YES:**
        *   **Task (AP Clerk):** **Log Invoice in Accounting System.**
        *   *Output:* System record created.
        *   **Task (AP Clerk):** **Forward to Purchasing.**
        *   *Action:* Send invoice to Purchasing Department for receipt confirmation.

### Phase 2: Verification and Matching
4.  **Task (Purchasing):** **Verify Goods/Services Receipt.**
    *   *Input:* Invoice, PO.
    *   *Action:* Compare invoice quantities/prices against PO and confirm delivery.
5.  **Exclusive Gateway:** **Is Receipt Confirmed?**
    *   **NO:**
        *   **Task (Purchasing):** **Hold Invoice / Contact Supplier.**
        *   *Note:* If PO is missing at this stage, Purchasing notifies AP Clerk to retrieve PO.
        *   *Flow:* Return to **Task: Check PO Number**.
    *   **YES:**
        *   **Task (Purchasing):** **Send Confirmation to AP.**
        *   *Output:* Confirmation email/System update.
6.  **Task (AP Clerk):** **Match Invoice to PO.**
    *   *Input:* Invoice, PO, Receipt Confirmation.
    *   *Action:* Match line items (price, quantity, delivery charges).
7.  **Exclusive Gateway:** **3-Way Match Successful?**
    *   **NO (Discrepancy Found):**
        *   **Task (AP Clerk):** **Flag Discrepancy.**
        *   *Action:* Email Purchasing to clarify OR Escalate to **AP Supervisor**.
        *   *Sub-Process:* **Exception Resolution** (See Section 5).
    *   **YES:**
        *   **Task (System):** **Generate Manager Approval Request.**
        *   *Trigger:* Automatic email/notification sent to Department Manager.

### Phase 3: Approval and Payment
8.  **Task (Department Manager):** **Review and Approve.**
    *   *Input:* Invoice details, PO, Confirmation, Budget Context.
    *   *Action:* Review quantities, pricing, and budget alignment.
9.  **Exclusive Gateway:** **Manager Approval Status?**
    *   **REJECT / HOLD:**
        *   **Task (Manager):** **Return to AP for Investigation.**
        *   *Reason:* Suspicious amounts, budget variance, unexpected items.
        *   *Flow:* Return to **Task: Match Invoice to PO** (or Exception Resolution).
    *   **APPROVE:**
        *   **Task (AP Clerk):** **Schedule for Payment.**
        *   *Action:* Add invoice to next payment cycle.
        *   **End Event:** Payment Scheduled.

---

## 5. Exception Handling (Sub-Processes)

### Exception A: Unresolved Discrepancy
*   **Trigger:** AP Clerk cannot resolve pricing/item mismatch with Purchasing.
*   **Task (AP Supervisor):** **Escalate and Coordinate.**
    *   *Action:* Contact Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM) or Supplier directly.
    *   *Role (SRM):* **Verify Contract Terms.**
        *   *Action:* Check agreed pricing/terms; inform AP/Purchasing of correct data.
    *   *Resolution:* If resolved, return to **Task: Match Invoice to PO**.
    *   *Rejection:* If unresolved, invoice rejected.

### Exception B: Informal Services (No PO)
*   **Trigger:** Invoice received for services contracted informally (no prior PO).
*   **Task (AP Supervisor):** **Create Retrospective PO.**
    *   *Action:* Work with Department Head to generate PO retroactively.
    *   *Gateway:* **Is Retrospective PO Approved?**
        *   **NO:** Reject Invoice.
        *   **YES:** Return to **Phase 1 (Log Invoice)**.

## 6. Process Notes & Ambiguities
*   **Purchasing Timing:** Mary (AP Clerk) states she matches items *after* Purchasing confirmation. Dan (Purchasing) states he checks details when the invoice hits his inbox.
    *   *Modeling Decision:* The model harmonizes this as a sequential flow: AP Logs -> Purchasing Confirms Receipt -> AP Matches. This ensures the "Three-Way Match" (PO, Receipt, Invoice) is complete before Manager Approval.
*   **Manager Rejection:** Carlos (Manager) mentions rejecting invoices if budget differs. Mary (AP) does not explicitly mention the rejection loop.
    *   *Modeling Decision:* The model includes a rejection loop from Manager back to AP Clerk to reflect Carlos's input, as this is a critical control point.
*   **SRM Involvement:** Karen (SRM) describes an indirect role.
    *   *Modeling Decision:* SRM is modeled as a resource within the "Exception Resolution" lane, only triggered when discrepancies cannot be resolved by standard AP/Purchasing communication.
*   **Payment Trigger:** All parties agree AP schedules payment, but the specific timing (e.g., "next payment run") is vague.
    *   *Modeling Decision:* Modeled as a final task "Schedule for Payment" without specific date logic, as interview data did not specify payment run frequency.
