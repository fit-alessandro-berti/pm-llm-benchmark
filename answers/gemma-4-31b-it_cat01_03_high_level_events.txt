Based on the analysis of the event logs for Case A1 and Case B2, I have aggregated the low-level events into five distinct high-level process steps. The patterns are consistent across both cases, suggesting a standardized manufacturing workflow.

### Proposed High-Level Process Mapping

| High-Level Activity | Included Low-Level Activities | Resource(s) Involved | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Material Preparation** | Retrieve raw metal sheet $\rightarrow$ Scan barcode $\rightarrow$ Place metal sheet on table $\rightarrow$ Align metal sheet $\rightarrow$ Preheat metal sheet | Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1 | This phase encompasses all logistics and setup tasks. It moves the raw material from storage to the workstation, ensures traceability (barcode), and prepares the physical and thermal state of the material for fabrication. |
| **2. Welding & Assembly** | Pick up welding tool $\rightarrow$ Weld corner A $\rightarrow$ Weld corner B | Operator B | This is the core value-adding step where the physical transformation occurs. It groups the tool acquisition and the actual joining of the material into a single assembly phase. |
| **3. Weld Validation** | Measure weld integrity | Quality Sensor #1 | This is a technical checkpoint. It occurs immediately after assembly to verify that the structural integrity of the welds meets specifications before the part is permanently coated. |
| **4. Surface Finishing** | Apply protective coating $\rightarrow$ Dry coating | Coating Unit #3, Heating Unit #2 | These events represent the finishing stage. The activities are logically linked: the application of a chemical agent followed by the thermal process required to cure/dry that agent. |
| **5. Final Inspection** | Visual check | Operator C | This is the final quality gate. It is a holistic check performed by a different resource (Operator C) to ensure the overall quality of the finished product. |

### Summary of Process Workflow

By aggregating the data, the complex log is simplified into the following linear workflow:

**Material Preparation** $\rightarrow$ **Welding & Assembly** $\rightarrow$ **Weld Validation** $\rightarrow$ **Surface Finishing** $\rightarrow$ **Final Inspection**

### Logic used for Aggregation:
1.  **Functional Cohesion:** I grouped events that contribute to a single goal (e.g., "getting the sheet ready" or "applying a coating").
2.  **Resource Transitions:** Changes in the primary resource (e.g., switching from Operator B to Quality Sensor #1) often signal a transition from a "doing" phase to a "checking" phase.
3.  **Temporal Sequence:** Events that occur in rapid succession and depend on the immediate previous action (like applying a coating and then drying it) were grouped to avoid fragmented process steps.
4.  **Domain Logic:** I applied manufacturing principles where preparation, fabrication, validation, and finishing are standard distinct stages of a production lifecycle.
