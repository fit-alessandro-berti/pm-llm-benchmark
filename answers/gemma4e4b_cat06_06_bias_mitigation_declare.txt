### Rationale for Bias Mitigation Constraints

The added constraints impose regulatory and procedural safeguards on the loan review process, ensuring that sensitive attributes are processed through mandatory review steps before a final decision is made.

1. **`coexistence` Constraint:**
    * **Added Link:** `CheckApplicantRace` $\rightarrow$ `ManualReview`
    * **Rationale:** This constraint mandates that whenever the process reaches the point of assessing a sensitive attribute (`CheckApplicantRace`), a human intervention (`ManualReview`) must *always* occur alongside the decision process. This prevents automated systems from making decisions solely based on raw sensitive data inputs.

2. **`noncoexistence` Constraints (The 'Gatekeeping' Rules):**
    * **Constraint 1 (Implicit):** `CheckApplicantRace` $\not\leftrightarrow$ `FinalDecision`
    * **Constraint 2:** `CheckApplicantRace` $\not\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview` (Used here to illustrate that skipping the review is forbidden).
    * **Rationale:** By adding `noncoexistence` rules that relate sensitive checks to final decisions or required reviews, we enforce that the path is highly constrained. For example, simply detecting a sensitive attribute (`CheckApplicantRace`) cannot instantaneously proceed to a `FinalDecision` without the mitigation steps being engaged.

3. **`nonsuccession` Constraint:**
    * **Added Link:** `CheckApplicantRace` $\not\rightarrow$ `Reject`
    * **Rationale:** This is a critical anti-bias measure. It prevents the most immediate and potentially most biased outcome: a direct path from assessing a sensitive attribute (e.g., `CheckApplicantRace`) straight to an adverse action (`Reject`). Any rejection must now be preceded by the required `BiasMitigationCheck` or `ManualReview`, ensuring the rejection is justifiable through documented fairness steps, not just correlation.

**Summary of Bias Reduction:**
The constraints elevate the role of `BiasMitigationCheck` and `ManualReview` from optional steps to mandatory checkpoints. They establish that the sensitive attribute data (`CheckApplicantRace`) cannot lead directly to a final adverse outcome (`Reject` or `FinalDecision`). Instead, the flow is forced through a human-reviewed, checked path, drastically reducing the chance that decisions are made based on algorithmic biases related to sensitive demographics.
