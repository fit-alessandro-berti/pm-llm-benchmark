### **1. Case 1002: Out-of-Order Approval**
**Anomaly:**
- **Issue Purchase Order (2024-02-01 08:40)** occurs **before** **Approve Purchase Requisition (2024-02-03 08:00)**.
- The purchase order is issued without prior approval of the requisition, which violates the standard sequence:
  **Create Requisition  Approve Requisition  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Invoice  Match  Pay**.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Compliance Risk:** Purchase orders should only be issued after requisitions are approved to ensure alignment with budgetary controls and procurement policies.
- **Fraud Risk:** Unapproved requisitions could lead to unauthorized spending or vendor exploitation.
- **Operational Inefficiency:** If the requisition is later rejected, the PO and subsequent steps (e.g., payment) may need reversal, causing delays and errors.

### **2. Case 1003: Invoice Received After Payment Release**
**Anomaly:**
- **Release Payment (2024-02-08 11:00)** occurs **before** **Vendor Invoice Received (2024-02-09 12:00)**.
- Payment is released without an invoice, which is a critical control in P2P to prevent overpayments or duplicate payments.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Financial Risk:** Payments should only be released after verifying the invoice (3-way match: PO, Goods Receipt, Invoice). Paying without an invoice could lead to:
  - Overpayments (if the invoice amount is higher than expected).
  - Disputes with vendors (if goods were never received or invoiced).
  - Violations of internal controls (e.g., SOX compliance).
- **Audit Issues:** Auditors may flag this as a lack of proper authorization or segregation of duties.

### **3. Case 1004: Purchase Order Issued After Goods Receipt**
**Anomaly:**
- **Receive Goods (2024-02-06 09:00)** occurs **before** **Issue Purchase Order (2024-02-06 09:15)**.
- Goods are received without a corresponding PO, which is unusual in P2P.

**Why it’s problematic:**
- **Lack of Traceability:** Without a PO, it’s unclear whether the goods were ordered, approved, or budgeted for. This could lead to:
  - Unauthorized purchases (e.g., "maverick spending").
  - Difficulty matching invoices to POs (critical for 3-way matching).
- **Vendor Risk:** Vendors may exploit this by sending unordered goods, leading to disputes or payment for undelivered items.
- **Compliance Violations:** Many organizations require POs for all purchases to enforce spend controls.

### **Recommendations for Process Improvement**
1. **Enforce Sequential Controls:**
   - Require **requisition approval before PO issuance** (Case 1002).
   - Mandate **3-way matching (PO  GR  Invoice)** before payment (Cases 1003, 1004).
2. **Automate Validations:**
   - Use workflow tools to block payments until all prerequisites (GR, invoice) are met.
3. **Monitor Timing Anomalies:**
   - Flag cases where goods receipt or invoice arrival deviates significantly from expected lead times (e.g., Case 1003’s 8-day delay).
4. **Segregate Duties:**
   - Ensure approvals (requisitions, POs) and payments are handled by separate roles to prevent collusion.
5. **Vendor Performance Tracking:**
   - Investigate why goods receipts are delayed (Case 1003) to identify vendor or internal inefficiencies.

---
### **Standard P2P Flow for Reference**
A typical P2P process follows this order:
1. **Create Purchase Requisition**  2. **Approve Requisition**  3. **Issue PO**  4. **Receive Goods**  5. **Vendor Invoice Received**  6. **Match Invoice to PO/GR**  7. **Approve Payment**  8. **Release Payment**  9. **Close Case**.

Any deviation from this sequence should be investigated for risks.
