## Rationale for Added Constraints:

1. **Coexistence constraints** for demographic-specific decisions: These ensure that if a decision is made for applicants from sensitive groups (Minority, Female, Elderly), a `ManualReview` must also occur in the process. This creates oversight for potentially biased decisions.

2. **Response constraints** for sensitive attribute checks: When the system checks applicant race, gender, or age, it must subsequently perform a `BiasMitigationCheck`. This forces the process to address bias before making decisions.

3. **Precedence constraints** for decisions: All approval/rejection decisions must be preceded by a `BiasMitigationCheck`, ensuring fairness considerations are addressed before final decisions.

4. **Succession constraints** for oversight activities: The `ManualReview` and `BiasMitigationCheck` activities must lead to `FinalDecision`, creating a complete workflow that includes fairness checks.

5. **Chain response constraints** for proper workflow: These enforce a specific sequence: `StartApplication`  `RequestAdditionalInfo`  `ManualReview`  `FinalDecision`, ensuring required fairness checks occur in the proper order.

6. **Non-succession constraints**: These prevent direct transitions from sensitive attribute checks to rejection decisions, eliminating the possibility of immediate biased decisions without mitigation steps.

These constraints collectively create a process where:
- Decisions are never made in isolation when sensitive attributes are involved
- Multiple checks and balances exist before final decisions
- Oversights activities are systematically included for vulnerable groups
- The workflow must include fairness mitigation steps when demographic data is accessed
