This is a **swimlane flowchart** (likely BPMN-style) depicting the end-to-end dispatch/shipping process for a **Computer Hardware Shop**. It is divided into three horizontal lanes:

- **Logistics** (top lane)
- **Secretary** (middle lane – labeled “Dispatch of goods Computer Hardware Shop”)
- **Warehouse** (bottom lane)

### Overall Flow Summary
The diagram shows how an order moves from the initial decision to ship goods through packaging, insurance/labeling, carrier selection, paperwork, and final preparation for pickup. It includes decision points, parallel activities, and a delay notification step.

### Step-by-Step Process

**1. Start & Initial Routing (Secretary)**
- Process begins at the left with a start event (“Ship goods”).
- It immediately hits a parallel gateway (+) and splits into two main paths:
  - One path goes to the **Secretary** for administrative steps.
  - The other path drops into the **Warehouse** to begin packaging.

**2. Secretary Path – Shipment Clarification**
- “Clarify shipment method”
- Decision diamond: **Special handling required?**
  - **Yes**  “Get 3 offers from logistic companies”  “Select logistic company and place order”  proceeds to a merge point.
  - **No**  continues to insurance check.

**3. Insurance & Labeling (Secretary + Logistics)**
- “Check if insurance is required” (pulls from a “Customer information” database).
- Decision: **If insurance necessary?**
  - **Yes**  “Insure parcel” (Logistics lane)  then “Write package label”.
  - Otherwise  directly to “Write package label”.
- After labeling, the flow merges and connects downward into the Warehouse lane.

**4. Warehouse Path – Packaging & Preparation**
- “Package goods” (with a 48-hour timer attached).
- Decision point after packaging:
  - If delayed beyond 48 hours  “Notify customer about delay” (loops back to the decision).
  - Once ready  flows into a merge gateway (+).
- After the merge:
  - “Fill out Shipment paperwork”
  - “Move package to pickup area”
- Ends at a final event: **“Shipment prepared”**

**5. Cross-Lane Connections**
- The selected logistics company/order placement (from Secretary) feeds into the merge point before paperwork.
- The package label and insurance steps also converge into the final warehouse steps.
- There are multiple merge points (diamonds with + or X) to synchronize the parallel secretary and warehouse activities.

### Key Elements
- **Documents/Data**: “Order details” document and “Customer information” database.
- **Timer**: 48-hour packaging duration with a delay notification.
- **Decisions**: Special handling, insurance requirement, and delay checks.
- **End State**: Package is ready in the pickup area with all paperwork and carrier arranged.

The diagram illustrates a coordinated process between administrative (Secretary), operational (Warehouse), and external (Logistics carriers) roles to prepare and ship hardware orders.
