### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

Upon analyzing the event log, three specific activity names appear more than once within a single case (C001, C002, or C003), indicating different process stages rather than identical actions:

*   **`Verify Documents`:**
    *   **Context A (Initial):** Occurs immediately after the request is initiated. The goal is to validate the authenticity of submitted data before proceeding.
    *   **Context B (Re-Verification):** Occurs after a customer action (uploading new docs) or an internal adjustment. The goal is to ensure the integrity of the data following a change.
*   **`Call Customer`:**
    *   **Context A (Clarification):** Occurs when there is a discrepancy or missing information requiring explanation or instruction from the agent.
    *   **Context B (Confirmation):** Occurs after an adjustment has been made internally to ensure the customer acknowledges the change.

### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

The renaming strategy was driven by the temporal and functional context of each activity instance within a case, rather than just the raw action performed.

*   **`Verify Documents` Split:**
    *   **Initial Document Verification:** This name highlights that this is the *first* gate in the workflow where data integrity is established. It implies a static check against initial submission criteria (e.g., checking original ID scans).
    *   **Re-Verification of Documents:** This name distinguishes itself by indicating a *dynamic* check. It occurs after a state change (uploading new docs or internal adjustment) and ensures the process remains compliant with updated information. Without this distinction, it would be unclear if the system was checking for fraud or just updating records.

*   **`Call Customer` Split (C002):**
    *   **Customer Clarification Call:** This name reflects the purpose of gathering missing context or resolving a specific issue ("clarify account discrepancy"). It is an information-gathering phase.
    *   **Post-Adjustment Confirmation Call:** This name reflects the closure phase, ensuring the customer acknowledges the change made in `Adjust Account`. It shifts from "gathering info" to "validating action."

*   **Consistency Across Cases:**
    *   Even though `Call Customer` only recurs in C002, and `Verify Documents` recurs in C001 & C003, applying the specific naming convention (Initial vs. Re-Verification) across all applicable cases ensures that the workflow logic is clear regardless of the entry point (WebPortal vs. Branch). This allows for better process mining and automation rules (e.g., "If `Re-Verification` is triggered, require Supervisor approval").
