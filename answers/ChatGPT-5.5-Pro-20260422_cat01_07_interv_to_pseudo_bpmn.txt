## BPMN-like textual process model: Invoice approval and payment scheduling

### 1. Process scope

**Process name:** Purchase-order-based invoice approval process
**Primary outcome:** Invoice is approved and scheduled for payment, rejected, or held pending investigation.
**Start trigger:** Supplier sends an invoice, usually by email.
**End states:**

1. Invoice scheduled for payment.
2. Invoice returned to supplier for correction or missing information.
3. Invoice rejected.
4. Invoice held pending investigation or exception resolution.

---

## 2. Pools / lanes / participants

### Lane: Supplier

Provides invoice, PO number, missing information, revised invoice, or corrected pricing/details.

### Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk

Main operational coordinator. Receives invoices, checks for PO number, logs invoices, forwards to Purchasing, performs invoice-to-PO matching, requests clarifications, sends invoices for approval, and schedules payment after approval.

### Lane: Accounting System

Stores logged invoices and PO references, supports matching, and sends automatic manager approval notifications.

### Lane: Purchasing Department / Purchasing Officer

Confirms that goods or services were received and checks invoice details against the purchase order, including quantities, prices, and delivery charges.

### Lane: Department Manager

Performs final business approval after PO validation, receipt confirmation, and invoice matching.

### Lane: Accounts Payable Supervisor

Handles unresolved exceptions, missing information, non-matching invoices, and invoices without valid POs.

### Lane: Supplier Relationship Manager

Supports supplier-facing issue resolution, especially missing PO numbers, contract/pricing discrepancies, or supplier communication problems.

---

## 3. Key artifacts / data objects

| Artifact / Data Object                  | Description                                                                                                 |
| --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Supplier invoice**                    | Invoice received by Accounts Payable, usually by email.                                                     |
| **Purchase order number**               | Required reference for standard processing.                                                                 |
| **Purchase order record**               | Used to compare invoice details against ordered goods/services.                                             |
| **Goods/services receipt confirmation** | Confirmation from Purchasing that the goods or services were received.                                      |
| **Invoice line-item match result**      | Result of AP’s invoice-to-PO comparison.                                                                    |
| **Purchasing confirmation email**       | Email from Purchasing confirming invoice/PO details and receipt status.                                     |
| **Corrected/revised invoice**           | Supplier-issued replacement invoice when information or pricing is wrong.                                   |
| **Contract / supplier terms**           | Used by Supplier Relationship Manager to validate pricing discrepancies.                                    |
| **Manager approval notification**       | System-generated request for manager sign-off.                                                              |
| **Manager approval/rejection decision** | Final approval decision before payment scheduling.                                                          |
| **Payment schedule / payment run**      | Output created by AP after manager approval.                                                                |
| **Retrospective PO**                    | Exception artifact created when an invoice lacks a PO but may correspond to informally contracted services. |

---

# 4. Main happy-path process flow

### Start Event E1: Invoice received

**Trigger:** Supplier sends invoice by email.

---

### Task T1: Receive invoice

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Supplier invoice
**Output:** Invoice ready for initial validation

The AP Clerk receives the invoice and begins the validation process.

---

### Gateway G1: Does the invoice contain a valid PO number?

**Type:** Exclusive gateway

#### If **No PO number is present**

Proceed to exception flow **E-PO-MISSING**.

#### If **PO number is present**

Continue to Task T2.

---

### Task T2: Log invoice in accounting system

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Invoice with PO number
**System:** Accounting System
**Output:** Logged invoice record

The AP Clerk records the invoice in the accounting system.

---

### Task T3: Forward invoice to Purchasing for receipt and order confirmation

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Logged invoice, PO number
**Output:** Invoice sent to Purchasing

AP forwards the invoice to the Purchasing Department to confirm that the goods or services were received and that the invoice aligns with the PO.

---

### Task T4: Check invoice against purchase order and receipt status

**Lane:** Purchasing Department / Purchasing Officer
**Input:** Invoice, PO record
**Output:** Confirmation, discrepancy, or request for correction

Purchasing checks whether the invoice details match what was ordered and received. This includes:

* Quantities
* Prices
* Delivery charges
* PO reference
* Goods/services receipt status

---

### Gateway G2: Does Purchasing confirm that invoice details and receipt are valid?

**Type:** Exclusive gateway

#### If **Yes**

Continue to Task T5.

#### If **No, discrepancy exists**

Proceed to exception flow **E-DISCREPANCY**.

#### If **PO is missing or invalid at this stage**

Proceed to exception flow **E-PO-MISSING-LATE**.

---

### Task T5: Send confirmation to Accounts Payable

**Lane:** Purchasing Department / Purchasing Officer
**Input:** Validated invoice and PO comparison
**Output:** Purchasing confirmation email

Purchasing sends a confirmation email back to AP stating that the invoice corresponds to the PO and that the goods or services were received.

---

### Task T6: Match invoice line items against PO in accounting system

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Invoice, PO record, Purchasing confirmation
**System:** Accounting System
**Output:** Match result

AP performs or finalizes the invoice-to-PO matching step in the accounting system.

**Note:** Purchasing and AP both describe comparison activities. In the consolidated model, Purchasing performs operational confirmation against the PO and receipt status, while AP performs the formal accounting-system match before manager approval.

---

### Gateway G3: Does the invoice match the PO?

**Type:** Exclusive gateway

#### If **Yes**

Continue to Task T7.

#### If **No**

Proceed to exception flow **E-AP-MATCH-ISSUE**.

---

### Task T7: Send manager approval request

**Lane:** Accounting System
**Input:** Matched invoice, PO, Purchasing confirmation
**Output:** Manager approval notification

The system automatically sends an approval notification to the relevant Department Manager.

**Trigger condition:** Manager approval is triggered only after both of the following are complete:

1. Purchasing confirms receipt of goods/services.
2. AP confirms that the invoice matches the PO.

---

### Task T8: Review invoice, PO, and confirmation

**Lane:** Department Manager
**Input:** Invoice details, PO, Purchasing confirmation, system notification
**Output:** Approval, rejection, or hold/investigation request

The Department Manager reviews the invoice details, PO, confirmation, quantities, pricing, and any special notes.

---

### Gateway G4: Does the Department Manager approve the invoice?

**Type:** Exclusive gateway

#### If **Approved**

Continue to Task T9.

#### If **Rejected or suspicious**

Proceed to exception flow **E-MANAGER-REJECT-HOLD**.

---

### Task T9: Schedule invoice for payment

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Manager-approved invoice
**Output:** Invoice added to next payment cycle

After manager approval, AP schedules the invoice for payment.

---

### End Event E2: Invoice scheduled for payment

The invoice has completed the approval process and is queued for payment.

---

# 5. Exception and variation flows

---

## E-PO-MISSING: Invoice received without PO number

### Trigger

At initial AP review, the invoice does not contain a PO number.

---

### Task E1.1: Request missing PO details from supplier

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Invoice without PO number
**Output:** Request for missing PO or revised invoice

AP sends the invoice back to the supplier or contacts the supplier requesting the missing PO number or a revised invoice.

---

### Optional Task E1.2: Supplier Relationship Manager assists with supplier contact

**Lane:** Supplier Relationship Manager
**Input:** Missing PO issue
**Output:** Supplier provides PO number, revised invoice, or further explanation

The Supplier Relationship Manager may contact the supplier, especially for key suppliers or recurring supplier issues.

---

### Gateway E1.G1: Does the supplier provide a valid PO number or revised invoice?

#### If **Yes**

Return to main flow at **Task T2: Log invoice in accounting system**.

#### If **No**

Continue to Gateway E1.G2.

---

### Gateway E1.G2: Is the invoice related to informal services or a non-standard purchase?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to Task E1.3.

#### If **No**

Proceed to End Event E1.E1.

---

### Task E1.3: Escalate missing-PO exception to AP Supervisor

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk → AP Supervisor
**Input:** Invoice without valid PO
**Output:** Exception case opened

Mary escalates the issue to Rita when the missing PO cannot be resolved through standard supplier follow-up.

---

### Task E1.4: Determine whether retrospective PO can be created

**Lane:** AP Supervisor, Department Heads
**Input:** Invoice, supplier information, service/purchase context
**Output:** Retrospective PO created or invoice rejected

The AP Supervisor coordinates with department heads to determine whether the invoice corresponds to a legitimate purchase and whether a retrospective PO should be created.

---

### Gateway E1.G3: Can a retrospective PO be created?

#### If **Yes**

Create retrospective PO and return to main flow at **Task T2: Log invoice in accounting system**.

#### If **No**

Proceed to End Event E1.E1.

---

### End Event E1.E1: Invoice rejected or remains on hold due to missing PO

The invoice cannot proceed without a valid PO or approved retrospective PO.

---

## E-PO-MISSING-LATE: PO issue discovered by Purchasing

### Trigger

Purchasing receives an invoice from AP but determines that the PO number is missing, invalid, or insufficient.

This variation appears in Dan’s account, even though Mary describes AP as checking the PO before forwarding the invoice. The model treats this as a secondary validation exception.

---

### Task E2.1: Notify AP that PO information is missing or invalid

**Lane:** Purchasing Department
**Input:** Invoice with missing/invalid PO reference
**Output:** Notification to AP

Purchasing tells AP that the invoice cannot be processed without a confirmed PO reference.

---

### Task E2.2: AP obtains correct PO information

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Purchasing notification
**Output:** Corrected PO details or unresolved issue

AP requests the missing or corrected PO details from the supplier or relevant internal party.

---

### Gateway E2.G1: Is valid PO information obtained?

#### If **Yes**

Return to **Task T3: Forward invoice to Purchasing for receipt and order confirmation**.

#### If **No**

Route to **E-PO-MISSING**, beginning at **Task E1.3: Escalate missing-PO exception to AP Supervisor**, if the issue cannot be resolved through routine follow-up.

---

## E-DISCREPANCY: Purchasing identifies discrepancy

### Trigger

Purchasing finds that the invoice does not match the PO, ordered items, received goods/services, agreed pricing, quantities, or delivery charges.

Examples:

* Extra units billed
* Incorrect pricing
* Missing items
* Unexpected delivery charges
* Missing information
* Invoice terms do not match supplier agreement

---

### Task E3.1: Determine discrepancy type

**Lane:** Purchasing Department
**Input:** Invoice, PO, receipt information
**Output:** Discrepancy classification

Purchasing identifies whether the issue is related to quantity, price, missing goods/services, delivery charges, missing information, or supplier terms.

---

### Gateway E3.G1: Can Purchasing resolve the issue directly with supplier?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to Task E3.2.

#### If **No**

Proceed to Task E3.3.

---

### Task E3.2: Contact supplier for clarification or corrected invoice

**Lane:** Purchasing Department
**Input:** Discrepant invoice
**Output:** Supplier clarification or corrected invoice

Purchasing may contact the supplier directly to resolve the issue.

---

### Task E3.3: Ask AP to hold invoice pending correction

**Lane:** Purchasing Department → Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Discrepancy details
**Output:** Invoice placed on hold

Purchasing may ask AP to hold the invoice until the supplier sends a corrected version.

---

### Optional Task E3.4: Supplier Relationship Manager checks contract or supplier terms

**Lane:** Supplier Relationship Manager
**Input:** Pricing discrepancy, supplier terms, contract
**Output:** Correct price or supplier clarification

The Supplier Relationship Manager may review the supplier contract and inform AP or Purchasing of the correct pricing or terms.

---

### Gateway E3.G2: Is the discrepancy resolved?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to Task E3.5.

#### If **No**

Proceed to Task E3.6.

---

### Task E3.5: Receive corrected invoice or clarification

**Lane:** Supplier / Purchasing / AP
**Input:** Supplier response
**Output:** Corrected invoice or accepted clarification

Once the discrepancy is resolved, the corrected or clarified invoice re-enters the normal process.

**Return to:** **Task T4: Check invoice against purchase order and receipt status** or **Task T6: Match invoice line items against PO**, depending on whether Purchasing or AP needs to repeat validation.

---

### Task E3.6: Escalate unresolved discrepancy to AP Supervisor

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk → AP Supervisor
**Input:** Unresolved discrepancy
**Output:** Escalated exception

If the issue cannot be resolved easily, AP escalates it to the AP Supervisor.

---

### Task E3.7: Coordinate exception resolution

**Lane:** AP Supervisor
**Input:** Discrepant invoice, PO, supplier information, department input
**Output:** Resolution, corrected invoice, retrospective action, rejection, or continued hold

The AP Supervisor contacts suppliers, coordinates between departments, and ensures the invoice does not remain stuck indefinitely.

---

### Gateway E3.G3: Is the exception resolved after supervisor intervention?

#### If **Yes**

Return to **Task T4** or **Task T6**, depending on where the correction is needed.

#### If **No**

Proceed to End Event E3.E1.

---

### End Event E3.E1: Invoice rejected or held unresolved

The invoice cannot continue until the discrepancy is resolved.

---

## E-AP-MATCH-ISSUE: AP cannot match invoice to PO

### Trigger

After Purchasing confirmation, AP attempts the formal invoice-to-PO match in the accounting system and identifies a discrepancy.

---

### Task E4.1: Email Purchasing for clarification

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Invoice match issue
**Output:** Clarification request

AP asks Purchasing to clarify incorrect pricing, missing items, quantity differences, or other mismatch issues.

---

### Gateway E4.G1: Can Purchasing clarify or resolve the match issue?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to Task E4.2.

#### If **No**

Proceed to Task E4.3.

---

### Task E4.2: Update invoice record or receive corrected invoice

**Lane:** AP / Purchasing / Supplier
**Input:** Clarification or corrected invoice
**Output:** Updated invoice information

Once clarified, AP updates the accounting record or receives a corrected invoice.

**Return to:** **Task T6: Match invoice line items against PO in accounting system**.

---

### Task E4.3: Escalate unresolved matching issue to AP Supervisor

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk → AP Supervisor
**Input:** Unresolved match issue
**Output:** Escalated exception

AP escalates to the Supervisor when the discrepancy cannot be resolved easily.

---

### Optional Task E4.4: Supplier Relationship Manager supports pricing or supplier-term resolution

**Lane:** Supplier Relationship Manager
**Input:** Contract terms, supplier relationship context
**Output:** Correct price or supplier clarification

The Supplier Relationship Manager may help resolve pricing discrepancies or obtain revised documents from the supplier.

---

### Gateway E4.G2: Is the matching issue resolved?

#### If **Yes**

Return to **Task T6: Match invoice line items against PO in accounting system**.

#### If **No**

End at **Invoice rejected or held unresolved**.

---

## E-MANAGER-REJECT-HOLD: Manager rejects or requests investigation

### Trigger

The Department Manager reviews the invoice and sees something suspicious or unacceptable.

Examples:

* Large difference between invoice amount and budget
* Unexpected items
* Unusual quantities
* Pricing concern
* Special notes that require review

---

### Task E5.1: Reject invoice or request AP hold/investigate

**Lane:** Department Manager
**Input:** Invoice approval package
**Output:** Rejection or investigation request

The manager either rejects the invoice or asks AP to hold it while the issue is investigated.

---

### Task E5.2: AP holds invoice and investigates

**Lane:** Accounts Payable Clerk
**Input:** Manager rejection/hold request
**Output:** Investigation case

AP investigates the issue with Purchasing, the supplier, the AP Supervisor, or the Supplier Relationship Manager as needed.

---

### Gateway E5.G1: Can the issue be resolved?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to Task E5.3.

#### If **No**

Proceed to End Event E5.E1.

---

### Task E5.3: Correct issue and resubmit for manager approval

**Lane:** AP / Accounting System
**Input:** Corrected invoice package
**Output:** New manager approval notification

The corrected or clarified invoice is resubmitted to the manager.

**Return to:** **Task T7: Send manager approval request** or **Task T8: Review invoice, PO, and confirmation**.

---

### End Event E5.E1: Invoice rejected or remains on hold after manager review

The invoice does not proceed to payment scheduling.

---

# 6. Consolidated BPMN sequence in compact textual notation

```text
START: Supplier sends invoice by email
  -> AP Clerk receives invoice
  -> AP Clerk checks for valid PO number

XOR Gateway: PO number present?
  YES:
    -> AP Clerk logs invoice in accounting system
    -> AP Clerk forwards invoice to Purchasing
    -> Purchasing checks PO, quantities, prices, delivery charges, and receipt status

    XOR Gateway: Purchasing confirms invoice and receipt?
      YES:
        -> Purchasing sends confirmation email to AP
        -> AP Clerk matches invoice line items against PO in accounting system

        XOR Gateway: Invoice matches PO?
          YES:
            -> Accounting System sends manager approval notification
            -> Department Manager reviews invoice, PO, and confirmation

            XOR Gateway: Manager approves?
              YES:
                -> AP Clerk schedules invoice for next payment cycle
                -> END: Invoice scheduled for payment

              NO:
                -> Department Manager rejects or requests hold/investigation
                -> AP Clerk investigates with Purchasing / Supervisor / Supplier Relationship Manager / Supplier
                -> XOR Gateway: Issue resolved?
                    YES -> Resubmit for manager approval
                    NO  -> END: Invoice rejected or held unresolved

          NO:
            -> AP Clerk asks Purchasing for clarification
            -> XOR Gateway: Issue resolved?
                YES -> Repeat invoice-to-PO match
                NO  -> Escalate to AP Supervisor
                       -> Optional Supplier Relationship Manager support
                       -> XOR Gateway: Resolved?
                           YES -> Repeat invoice-to-PO match
                           NO  -> END: Invoice rejected or held unresolved

      NO, discrepancy:
        -> Purchasing contacts supplier directly OR asks AP to hold invoice
        -> Optional Supplier Relationship Manager checks contract/pricing terms
        -> XOR Gateway: Corrected invoice or clarification received?
            YES -> Return to Purchasing confirmation / AP matching
            NO  -> Escalate to AP Supervisor
                   -> AP Supervisor coordinates resolution
                   -> XOR Gateway: Resolved?
                       YES -> Return to normal flow
                       NO  -> END: Invoice rejected or held unresolved

      NO, missing/invalid PO discovered:
        -> Purchasing notifies AP
        -> AP obtains correct PO information
        -> XOR Gateway: Valid PO obtained?
            YES -> Return to Purchasing confirmation
            NO  -> Escalate missing-PO exception

  NO:
    -> AP Clerk requests PO number or revised invoice from supplier
    -> Optional Supplier Relationship Manager assists supplier follow-up

    XOR Gateway: Supplier provides valid PO/revised invoice?
      YES:
        -> AP Clerk logs invoice in accounting system
        -> Continue normal flow

      NO:
        -> AP Clerk escalates to AP Supervisor

        XOR Gateway: Informal service / retrospective PO possible?
          YES:
            -> AP Supervisor works with department heads to create retrospective PO
            -> Return to AP logging and normal flow

          NO:
            -> END: Invoice rejected or held due to missing PO
```

# 7. Business rules captured from the interviews

1. **No standard invoice approval occurs without a confirmed PO reference.**
   Purchasing explicitly states that they do not approve anything without a confirmed PO.

2. **Manager approval is only triggered after PO matching and receipt confirmation.**
   The system sends the manager approval request once AP has confirmation from Purchasing and the invoice matches the PO.

3. **Purchasing validates operational correctness before manager approval.**
   Purchasing checks whether the invoice aligns with ordered and received goods/services.

4. **AP performs the formal accounting-system match.**
   Even though Purchasing checks quantities and prices, AP is responsible for matching invoice line items against the PO in the accounting system.

5. **The Department Manager provides final business approval, not exception resolution.**
   The manager approves, rejects, or requests investigation but does not appear to resolve supplier or PO discrepancies directly.

6. **The AP Supervisor handles unresolved exceptions.**
   The supervisor becomes involved when AP or Purchasing cannot resolve missing information, unmatched invoices, or problematic discrepancies.

7. **The Supplier Relationship Manager is an optional support role.**
   This role does not approve invoices or use the approval system directly but helps resolve supplier communication, missing PO, and pricing/contract issues.

8. **Resolved exceptions return to the normal flow.**
   After correction, clarification, or retrospective PO creation, invoices re-enter the process at the relevant validation step.

# 8. Ambiguities and reconciled differences across interviews

### Ambiguity 1: Who performs the invoice-to-PO comparison?

Both AP and Purchasing describe comparison activities.

**Reconciled model:**
Purchasing performs an operational check against the PO and receipt status, while AP performs the formal invoice-to-PO match in the accounting system.

---

### Ambiguity 2: Can invoices without PO numbers reach Purchasing?

Mary says AP checks for a PO before forwarding the invoice. Dan says that sometimes he tells Mary she needs to obtain the PO number before Purchasing can proceed.

**Reconciled model:**
The process includes both an initial AP PO check and a secondary PO validation by Purchasing. Missing or invalid PO information can therefore be caught either before or after forwarding to Purchasing.

---

### Ambiguity 3: Who contacts the supplier during discrepancies?

Dan says Purchasing may contact the supplier directly. Mary says AP emails Purchasing for clarification. Rita says the AP Supervisor may contact suppliers. Karen says the Supplier Relationship Manager may contact suppliers.

**Reconciled model:**
Supplier contact depends on issue type and severity:

* Routine PO/detail discrepancy: Purchasing may contact supplier directly.
* Missing PO or revised invoice request: AP may contact supplier.
* Contract/pricing issue: Supplier Relationship Manager may assist.
* Unresolved or complex exception: AP Supervisor coordinates supplier and internal follow-up.

---

### Ambiguity 4: What happens after manager rejection?

Carlos says he may reject the invoice or ask Mary to hold it and investigate. The exact downstream path after rejection is not fully specified.

**Reconciled model:**
A manager rejection or hold request sends the invoice into an investigation loop. If resolved, the invoice is resubmitted for manager approval. If unresolved, it is rejected or remains on hold.
