The visualization is a **flowchart** (business/process diagram) showing how a company handles **shipping goods and preparing a package**, including decision points and handoffs between departments.

Key elements and flow:

- **Swimlanes/areas:** Labeled vertically on the left as **Contract Department / Operations / Secretary / Logistics / Warehouse** (wording appears as those department lanes).
- **Start:** The process begins with **“Ship goods”**.
- **Packaging/dispatch decision chain:**
  - A box **“Clarify shipment method”** leads into a decision **“Special handling?”**
    - If **no**, it goes to checking **“Check if insurance is required”** (involves customer info).
    - If **yes**, it still routes toward insurance/packaging steps via the same general flow.
  - **Decision “Insurance required?”**
    - If required, the step **“Insure parcel”** happens; otherwise it skips to packaging.
  - **“Write package label”** follows (with the label step occurring before logistics selection).
- **Logistics selection:**
  - A decision **merge point** then goes to **“Select logistic company and place order”**.
  - Then another decision/merge leads to the next stage where package movement is prepared.
- **Warehouse/work order side:**
  - **“Package goods”** is shown with a timing note **“48 hours”**.
  - **“Notify customer about delay”** is connected to the flow (for the delayed condition).
  - Then **“Fill out shipment paperwork”** is performed.
  - Finally **“Move package to pickup area”** and the process ends at **“Shipment prepared.”**

Overall, it depicts a **shipping fulfillment workflow**: determine shipment method and special handling, verify whether insurance is needed using customer information, label and choose a logistics provider, package and (if needed) notify of delays, complete paperwork, and move the parcel for pickup.
