### Rationale for the added constraints

- **`FinalDecision` coexists with `BiasMitigationCheck`**: every loan application trace that reaches a final decision must include a fairness-oriented bias mitigation check.

- **`BiasMitigationCheck` precedes decision activities**: decisions such as `Approve`, `Reject`, `RequestAdditionalInfo`, and their protected-group-specific variants cannot occur unless a bias mitigation step has already happened.

- **`CheckApplicantAge`, `CheckApplicantGender`, and `CheckApplicantRace` chain-respond to `BiasMitigationCheck`**: whenever a sensitive attribute is checked, the next activity must be a bias mitigation check. This prevents the process from moving directly from sensitive attribute inspection to a decision.

- **`nonchainsuccession` from sensitive-attribute checks to decisions**: a decision cannot immediately follow a sensitive attribute check. This directly blocks suspicious patterns such as `CheckApplicantRace -> Reject`.

- **`ManualReview` required for protected-group-specific outcomes**: if the process records outcomes such as `Reject_Minority`, `Approve_Minority`, or `RequestAdditionalInfo_Minority`, then `ManualReview` must exist and must precede those outcomes. This adds a human or secondary review safeguard for decisions involving sensitive demographics.
