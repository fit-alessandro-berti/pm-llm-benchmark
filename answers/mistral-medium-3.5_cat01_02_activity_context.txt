### **Analysis of Repeating Activities and Contextual Differences**

From the event log, the following activities appear multiple times within the same case or across cases with different meanings:

1. **Verify Documents**
   - Occurs in **C001, C002, C003** at different stages:
     - **Initial verification** (first occurrence in each case).
     - **Re-verification after additional documents are submitted** (e.g., C001 after "Submit Additional").
     - **Re-verification after account adjustments** (e.g., C003 after "Adjust Account").

2. **Call Customer**
   - Occurs in **C001, C002, C003** with different purposes:
     - **Request missing documents** (C001).
     - **Clarify account discrepancy** (C002).
     - **Request customer to return** (C003, since they stepped out).
     - **Follow-up confirmation** (C002, after adjustments).

3. **Adjust Account**
   - Only appears once per case where it occurs (C002, C003), but the **context differs**:
     - In **C002**, it follows a call to clarify a discrepancy.
     - In **C003**, it is an internal correction due to missing info.

### **Renamed Activities with Contextual Clarity**

We will disambiguate the activities as follows:

| **Original Activity**  | **New Activity Name**                     | **Justification** |
|------------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------|
| Verify Documents (1st) | **Initial Document Verification**        | First check of documents in the process. |
| Verify Documents (2nd) | **Reverify Documents After Submission**  | Happens after customer submits missing docs (C001) or after adjustments (C003). |
| Call Customer (1st in C001) | **Call Customer for Missing Documents** | Explicitly states the purpose. |
| Call Customer (1st in C002) | **Call Customer to Clarify Discrepancy** | Different from the follow-up call. |
| Call Customer (2nd in C002) | **Follow-Up Call to Confirm Adjustments** | Confirms changes made. |
| Call Customer (in C003) | **Call Customer to Return to Branch**    | Unique to in-person cases. |
| Adjust Account         | **Adjust Account (Discrepancy Resolution)** (C002) | Clarifies it’s due to a discrepancy. |
| Adjust Account         | **Adjust Account (Missing Info Correction)** (C003) | Different reason for adjustment. |

*(Note: "Initiate Request" and "Approve Request" are unique in meaning per occurrence, so they do not need renaming.)*

### **Key Justifications for Renaming**

1. **Verify Documents  Initial/Reverify**
   - The first occurrence is always the **initial check**, while later ones are **reverifications** triggered by new information (submitted docs or adjustments).

2. **Call Customer  Purpose-Specific Names**
   - The **reason for the call** varies (missing docs, discrepancy clarification, follow-up, or recalling a customer). Explicitly stating the purpose removes ambiguity.

3. **Adjust Account  Reason-Specific Names**
   - While this activity doesn’t repeat in a single case, the **context differs between cases** (discrepancy vs. missing info). Adding the reason helps distinguish them in cross-case analysis.

### **Benefits of the New Naming Scheme**
- **Clearer process mining**: Helps in identifying loops (e.g., "Reverify after Submission" indicates a rework loop).
- **Better traceability**: Each activity’s role in the workflow is explicit.
- **Reduced ambiguity**: No two identical activity names have different meanings.

This approach ensures that the event log can be accurately analyzed for process improvement, bottleneck detection, and compliance checks.
