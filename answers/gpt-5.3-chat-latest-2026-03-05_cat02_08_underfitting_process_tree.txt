One invalid but **accepted** trace is:

Receive_Order  Check_Inventory  Ship_Order  Package_Items  Authorize_Payment  Close_Order

Why the model accepts it:
- The root is a **SEQUENCE**: Receive_Order  Check_Inventory  XOR  Close_Order.
- If the XOR chooses the **successful_subtree**, it executes a **PARALLEL** block containing:
  - Authorize_Payment
  - Package_Items
  - Ship_Order
- A **PARALLEL** operator allows **any ordering (interleaving)** of its activities, as long as each happens once.

Therefore the model allows:
Ship_Order  Package_Items  Authorize_Payment  
or any other permutation.

Why this is problematic in the real OrdertoCash domain:
- **Shipping an order before it is packaged** is impossible.
- **Shipping before payment authorization** is also typically prohibited.
- The real business logic requires a strict dependency such as:
  Authorize_Payment  Package_Items  Ship_Order.

Because the model uses **PARALLEL**, it fails to enforce these dependencies, allowing logically impossible behavior like shipping items before they are packaged or before payment is authorized.
