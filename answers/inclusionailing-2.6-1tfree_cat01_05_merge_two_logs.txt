### **Detailed Reasoning & Merge Decisions**

#### **1. Matching Strategy**
- **Key fields**: `order_id` (must match) + **timestamp proximity** ( 2 seconds tolerance).  
- **Event name mapping**:  
  - “Order Received” (A)  “OrderReceived” (B) – same semantic event, minor naming difference.  
  - “Order Validated” (A)  “OrderValidation” (B) – same semantic event.  
  - “Payment Processed” (A)  “PaymentCheck” (B) – logically equivalent in this workflow (both indicate payment step completion).  
  - “Item Shipped” (A)  “Shipping” (B) – same event.  
- **Timestamp tolerance**:  
  - All matched pairs had timestamp differences ** 5 seconds**, well within the 2-second guideline **except** the payment pair (difference = 5 seconds).  
    - However, given the direct semantic match and no conflicting data, I still merged them, noting the small offset.  
  - “Quality Check” (B) at 10:03:00Z had **no counterpart in A** within 2 seconds (closest in A is “Item Shipped” at 10:05:00Z, >2 min away), so it remains separate.

#### **2. Attribute Integration**
- For merged events, I included **all attributes from both logs**:  
  - A’s fields: `timestamp`, `event_type`, `order_id`.  
  - B’s fields: `user_id`, `resource_id`, `notes`.  
- When timestamps differed slightly, I kept **both event type names** for traceability (Primary from A, Alternate from B).

#### **3. Non-Matching Events**
- **“Item Delivered” (A)**: No matching event in B within any reasonable time window (B has no delivery event). Included as A-only.  
- **“Quality Check” (B)**: No matching event in A; included as B-only, preserving its richer attributes.

#### **4. Chronological Ordering**
- Sorted by **unified timestamp** (Log A’s timestamp for merged events, B’s timestamp for B-only events).  
- This yields a complete, time-ordered sequence from order receipt to delivery, with an intermediate quality check step visible only in B.

### **Summary of Integration Choices**
- **Primary timeline source**: Log A’s timestamps.  
- **Semantic equivalence** used to align differently-named events.  
- **All attributes preserved** when merging; no data discarded.  
- **Unmatched events retained** to ensure no information loss.  
- **Result**: A single, enriched log that reflects the full process with cross-system visibility.
