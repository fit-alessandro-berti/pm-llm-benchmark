# Process Mining Diagnostic & Investigation Playbook

## 1. Case-by-Case Diagnostic Analysis

By mapping the sample case data against the **Assumed Normal Process Flow**:
$$\text{Register} \rightarrow \text{Credit Check} \rightarrow \text{Validate Stock} \rightarrow \text{Confirm Shipment} \rightarrow \text{Ship Goods} \rightarrow \text{Issue Invoice} \rightarrow \text{Receive Payment}$$

We can pinpoint significant process conformance issues:

### **Case 1001 (Standard Order - Value: 1,250.00)**
*   **Path:** Register $\rightarrow$ Credit Check $\rightarrow$ Validate Stock $\rightarrow$ Confirm Shipment $\rightarrow$ Ship Goods $\rightarrow$ Issue Invoice $\rightarrow$ Receive Payment.
*   **Conformance:** **Perfect Conformance.** It serves as our control baseline.

### **Case 1002 (Priority Order - Value: 2,000.00)**
*   **Path:** Register $\rightarrow$ **Confirm Shipment** $\rightarrow$ **Ship Goods** $\rightarrow$ *Credit Check* $\rightarrow$ *Validate Stock* $\rightarrow$ Issue Invoice $\rightarrow$ Receive Payment.
*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Severe Out-of-Sequence Execution:** Goods were confirmed and physically shipped **before** a credit check was run or inventory was validated.
    *   **Risk:** Highly critical. If the credit check had failed (score $\le$ threshold) or stock was unavailable, the company would have already dispatched the items, creating credit exposure and potential inventory stockouts.

### **Case 1003 (Standard Order - Value: 1,500.00)**
*   **Path:** Register $\rightarrow$ Credit Check $\rightarrow$ **Ship Goods** $\rightarrow$ Issue Invoice $\rightarrow$ **Confirm Shipment** $\rightarrow$ Receive Payment.
*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Skipped Step:** The critical step `Validate Stock` was completely bypassed.
    *   **Retroactive/Late Confirmation:** `Confirm Shipment` occurred **after** the goods were already shipped (`Ship Goods`).
    *   **Risk:** Skipping stock validation leads to unexpected delivery delays if items are out of stock. Late confirmation indicates that system logging is decoupled from physical operations, degrading data integrity.

### **Case 1004 (Standard Order - Value: 3,000.00)**
*   **Path:** Register $\rightarrow$ **Receive Payment** $\rightarrow$ Issue Invoice $\rightarrow$ Confirm Shipment $\rightarrow$ Ship Goods.
*   **Anomalies Identified:**
    *   **Pre-Payment Flow & Skipped Steps:** Credit check and stock validation were bypassed. Payment was collected 5 minutes after registration, and the invoice was generated *after* payment was received.
    *   **Risk:** While receiving payment early eliminates credit risk (justifying bypassing the `Credit Check`), shipping goods without `Validate Stock` remains a major operational risk.

## 2. Hypotheses & Business Context Analysis

To understand *why* these process exceptions occur, we must formulate hypotheses linking process deviations to operational realities:

### **Hypothesis A: "Priority Bypass" System Workarounds**
*   **Theory:** Priority orders (like Case 1002) bypass standard validation loops because logistics personnel prioritize speed over governance. 
*   **Indicator:** Priority orders will have a high frequency of "Ship Goods" preceding "Perform Credit Check" or "Validate Stock."

### **Hypothesis B: Post-Hoc / Retroactive System Logging**
*   **Theory:** Logistical personnel perform physical work (packing/shipping) first, then batch-log steps like "Confirm Shipment" retrospectively in the system.
*   **Indicator:** Negative or near-zero duration between shipment validation and actual shipping, or "Confirm Shipment" timestamps occurring *after* "Ship Goods."

### **Hypothesis C: Digital Pre-Payment Loop**
*   **Theory:** Online channels or specific order profiles allow instant customer pre-payment. In these cases, the standard Credit Check is redundant, but the system lacks a structured pathway to bypass it cleanly without breaking conformance dashboards.
*   **Indicator:** Orders with `Receive Payment` occurring immediately after `Register Order` bypass `Perform Credit Check` but still proceed to shipment.

### **Hypothesis D: Segregation of Duties (SoD) Violations**
*   **Theory:** Internal controls are compromised because individual resources are executing sequential tasks that should be decoupled to prevent fraud or manual errors (e.g., registering an order and validating its credit check).

## 3. SQL Investigation Playbook (PostgreSQL)

To test these hypotheses across the entire database, execute the following diagnostic SQL queries.

### **Query 1: Process Path Conformance (The "Process Map" Generator)**
This query reconstructs the end-to-end path for every case, allowing you to instantly isolate and count non-conforming process patterns.

```sql
SELECT 
    actual_path,
    COUNT(*) as frequency,
    ROUND(AVG(o.order_value), 2) as avg_order_value
FROM (
    SELECT 
        case_id,
        STRING_AGG(activity, ' -> ' ORDER BY timestamp, event_id) as actual_path
    FROM order_event_log
    GROUP BY case_id
) path_analysis
JOIN orders o ON path_analysis.case_id = o.case_id
GROUP BY actual_path
ORDER BY frequency DESC;
```

---

### **Query 2: Out-of-Sequence Shipments (Testing Hypothesis A)**
This query identifies cases where physical shipping occurred before the required financial gatekeeper activities (Credit Check, Stock Validation) were executed.

```sql
WITH activity_timestamps AS (
    SELECT 
        case_id,
        MIN(CASE WHEN activity = 'Perform Credit Check' THEN timestamp END) as credit_check_time,
        MIN(CASE WHEN activity = 'Validate Stock' THEN timestamp END) as stock_val_time,
        MIN(CASE WHEN activity = 'Ship Goods' THEN timestamp END) as ship_time
    FROM order_event_log
    GROUP BY case_id
)
SELECT 
    at.case_id,
    o.order_type,
    o.order_value,
    at.credit_check_time,
    at.stock_val_time,
    at.ship_time,
    CASE 
        WHEN at.ship_time < at.credit_check_time THEN 'Shipped before Credit Check'
        WHEN at.ship_time < at.stock_val_time THEN 'Shipped before Stock Validation'
        WHEN at.credit_check_time IS NULL THEN 'Bypassed Credit Check completely'
        ELSE 'Other Deviation'
    END as violation_type
FROM activity_timestamps at
JOIN orders o ON at.case_id = o.case_id
WHERE at.ship_time < at.credit_check_time 
   OR at.ship_time < at.stock_val_time
   OR at.credit_check_time IS NULL;
```

### **Query 3: Retroactive/Post-Hoc Confirmation Auditing (Testing Hypothesis B)**
This query finds cases where "Confirm Shipment" was logged *after* the goods were physically dispatched, calculating the delay interval.

```sql
WITH ship_sequence AS (
    SELECT 
        case_id,
        MAX(CASE WHEN activity = 'Ship Goods' THEN timestamp END) as ship_time,
        MAX(CASE WHEN activity = 'Confirm Shipment' THEN timestamp END) as confirmation_time
    FROM order_event_log
    GROUP BY case_id
)
SELECT 
    s.case_id,
    o.order_type,
    s.ship_time,
    s.confirmation_time,
    (s.confirmation_time - s.ship_time) as retroactive_delay
FROM ship_sequence s
JOIN orders o ON s.case_id = o.case_id
WHERE s.confirmation_time > s.ship_time
ORDER BY retroactive_delay DESC;
```

### **Query 4: Bypassed Process Steps Analysis (Testing Hypothesis C)**
This query identifies which critical steps are skipped most often, grouped by `order_type` and registration channel (extracted from `additional_info`).

```sql
WITH case_activity_matrix AS (
    SELECT 
        case_id,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN activity = 'Perform Credit Check' THEN 1 END) as has_credit_check,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN activity = 'Validate Stock' THEN 1 END) as has_stock_validation,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN activity = 'Receive Payment' THEN 1 END) as has_payment
    FROM order_event_log
    GROUP BY case_id
)
SELECT 
    o.order_type,
    COUNT(o.case_id) as total_orders,
    SUM(CASE WHEN cam.has_credit_check = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) as missing_credit_check,
    SUM(CASE WHEN cam.has_stock_validation = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) as missing_stock_val,
    ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN cam.has_credit_check = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) * 100.0 / COUNT(o.case_id), 2) as credit_bypass_rate
FROM orders o
JOIN case_activity_matrix cam ON o.case_id = cam.case_id
GROUP BY o.order_type;
```

### **Query 5: Segregation of Duties (SoD) Violations (Testing Hypothesis D)**
This query flags internal control weaknesses where the same employee or team registered the order and approved its credit check, or generated the invoice and logged the payment.

```sql
SELECT 
    e1.case_id,
    e1.activity as activity_1,
    e1.resource as resource_1,
    r1.role as role_1,
    e2.activity as activity_2,
    e2.resource as resource_2,
    r2.role as role_2
FROM order_event_log e1
JOIN order_event_log e2 ON e1.case_id = e2.case_id
LEFT JOIN resources r1 ON e1.resource = r1.resource_id
LEFT JOIN resources r2 ON e2.resource = r2.resource_id
WHERE e1.case_id = e2.case_id
  -- Pair 1: Order Maker vs Credit Checker
  AND ((e1.activity = 'Register Order' AND e2.activity = 'Perform Credit Check' AND e1.resource = e2.resource)
   OR  -- Pair 2: Billing Officer vs Cashier (Invoice Issuer vs Payment Receiver)
       (e1.activity = 'Issue Invoice' AND e2.activity = 'Receive Payment' AND e1.resource = e2.resource));
```

## 4. Key Recommendations based on Preliminary Analysis
1. **Enforce System Hard Gates:** Configure the ERP / Order Management system to programmatically lock the "Confirm Shipment" and "Ship Goods" options until "Perform Credit Check" and "Validate Stock" are completed in the system.
2. **Review Priority Routing Rules:** Establish a legal/operational policy that explicitly defines whether "Priority" orders are allowed to bypass credit checks, or if they should instead undergo automated instant credit scoring.
3. **Align Logging Interfaces:** Eliminate the manual logging lag for logistics by introducing real-time handheld scanners for warehouse staff. This ensures "Confirm Shipment" is recorded automatically upon scanning, preventing retroactive logging entries.
