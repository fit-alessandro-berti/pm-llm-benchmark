**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

### Process Overview
**Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Preparation  
**Trigger:** Receipt of supplier invoice (primarily via email)  
**End Event:** Invoice scheduled for payment (or rejected/returned to supplier)

### Participants (Pools / Lanes)
- **Accounts Payable (AP)** ñ Mary (Clerk) + Rita (Supervisor)
- **Purchasing** ñ Dan (Purchasing Officer)
- **Department Manager** ñ Carlos
- **Supplier Relationship Manager** ñ Karen (support role, not a formal lane)
- **Supplier** (external black-box participant)

### Textual BPMN Representation

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (Email)

**Lane: Accounts Payable**

- **Task:** Log Invoice & Validate PO Reference
  - If **no PO number**  
    - **Task:** Request missing PO / revised invoice from Supplier (via email or Karen)
    - **Loop back** to Log Invoice & Validate PO Reference (or end in ìRejected/On Holdî)

  - If **PO exists**  Proceed

- **Task:** Record Invoice in Accounting System

- **Message:** Forward Invoice + PO to Purchasing for Goods Receipt Confirmation

**Lane: Purchasing**

- **Task:** Verify Invoice against PO (quantities, prices, delivery charges, etc.)

  - **Exclusive Gateway: Match?**
    - **No (Discrepancy)** 
      - **Task:** Resolve Discrepancy (contact supplier directly or via Karen, or ask AP to hold)
      - **Message:** Send corrected invoice or clarification back to AP
      - **Rework loop**  back to APís ìLog & Validateî or ìMatch Invoiceî step
    - **Yes** 
      - **Task:** Send Confirmation of Receipt & Match to AP
      - **Message:** Confirmation email + updated status in system

**Lane: Accounts Payable**

- **Task:** Perform 3-Way Match (Invoice ñ PO ñ Confirmation)

  - **Exclusive Gateway: Match?**
    - **Yes**  Proceed to Manager Approval
    - **No** 
      - **Task:** Investigate / Request Clarification (may involve Karen)
      - **Exclusive Gateway: Can be resolved quickly?**
        - **Yes**  Rework loop back to Match step
        - **No**  
          - **Task:** Escalate to AP Supervisor (Rita)

**Lane: AP Supervisor (Rita)** ñ Exception Path

- **Task:** Handle Exception / Escalate Issue
  - Activities: Contact supplier (via Karen), create retrospective PO, coordinate between departments, or decide to reject
  - After resolution  **Message** back to normal flow (re-enter at ìPerform 3-Way Matchî)

**Lane: Department Manager (Carlos)**

- **Task:** Review & Approve Invoice
  - Trigger: System notification after successful 3-way match and Purchasing confirmation
  - **Exclusive Gateway: Approved?**
    - **Yes**  Invoice Approved
    - **No / Suspicious** 
      - **Task:** Reject or Request Further Investigation (message to AP)
      - **Rework loop**  back to AP for investigation (may involve Rita/Karen)

**Lane: Accounts Payable**

- **Task:** Schedule Invoice for Payment
  - **End Event:** Invoice Scheduled for Payment Run

### Additional Notes & Ambiguities

- **Main Happy Path:**  
  Invoice Received  Validate PO  Log in System  Purchasing Verification  3-Way Match  Manager Approval  Schedule for Payment

- **Major Exception Paths:**
  1. Missing PO  Request from Supplier (Karen assists)
  2. Mismatch between Invoice/PO/Receipt  Resolution loop involving Purchasing, AP, Karen, and possibly Rita
  3. Manager Rejection  Investigation loop
  4. Unresolvable issues  Escalation to Rita  possible retrospective PO or rejection

- **System Behavior:** The accounting system automatically sends approval requests to the Department Manager after Purchasing confirmation and successful matching.

- **Uncertainties / Variations:**
  - Exact sequence when a retrospective PO is created is not fully detailed.
  - Some interviewees suggest Purchasing may contact the supplier directly; others route everything through AP or Karen.
  - Frequency of exceptions is low (Manager rejection ~once every couple of months).

- **Artifacts/Data Objects:**
  - Supplier Invoice
  - Purchase Order (PO)
  - Goods Receipt Confirmation
  - System-matched invoice record
  - Approval status in accounting system

This textual model can be directly translated into a standard BPMN diagram with one main pool (Accounts Payable) and collaboration pools for Purchasing and Department Manager, plus clear exception gateways and rework loops.
