- **Data Granularity**: GPS data at frequent intervals may create noise; aggregation into meaningful activities (e.g., "en route," "parking search") is needed.  

#### **Process Discovery**

#### **Conformance Checking**  
- **Reference Model**: Use dispatch-planned routes as the "ideal" process model.  
- **Deviation Detection**:

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification  

#### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**  
1. **On-Time Delivery Rate**:**  
   - Calculated as % of deliveries completed within the customer’s time window (from dispatch logs).  
2. **Average Time per Delivery Stop**:  
   - Time between "Arrive Customer" and "Depart Customer" (includes parking search, interactions, etc.).  
3. **Travel vs. Service Time Ratio**:  
   - Ratio of time "en route" (GPS speed > 20 km/h) vs. service time (speed 0–10 km/h).  
4. **Fuel Consumption per km/Package**:  
   - Estimated using GPS distance (calculate distances between GPS points) and total packages delivered.  
5. **Vehicle Utilization Rate**:  
   - % of shift time spent on productive activities (e.g., deliveries, vs. idle time or maintenance.  
6. **Traffic Impact**:  
   - Average speed drops (e.g., <15 km/h for >10 mins) in specific zones.  
7. **Failed Delivery Rate**:  
   - % of "Delivery Failed" events per route or driver.  

#### **Bottleneck Identification**  
- **Process Mining Techniques**:  
  - **Cycle Time Analysis**: Identify activities with high variance or delays (e.g., "Delivery Failed" causing repeated stops).  
  - **Frequency and Performance Perspectives**: Highlight paths with frequent deviations (e.g., drivers repeatedly taking long routes to avoid traffic, leading to overtime.  
  - **Resource Profiles**: Compare drivers’ performance (e.g., Driver D105’s "Idle Time" vs. others.  

**Bottleneck Sources**:  
- **Spatial Bottlenecks**: Frequent traffic jams in specific corridors (e.g., GPS data showing repeated low-speed zones).  
- **Temporal Bottlenecks**: Morning rush hour congestion delaying departures.  
- **Resource Bottlenecks**: Drivers with high "Service Time" per stop (e.g., poor navigation causing parking delays.  
- **Operational Bottlenecks**: High vehicle downtime due to unscheduled maintenance (e.g., frequent engine warnings leading to unscheduled stops.
