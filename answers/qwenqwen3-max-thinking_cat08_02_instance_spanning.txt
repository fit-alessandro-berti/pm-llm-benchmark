### **1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact**

To address the fulfillment center’s challenges, we must first isolate and quantify how each *instance-spanning constraint* (i.e., dependencies between orders) contributes to delays and inefficiencies. Process mining provides the analytical foundation for this diagnosis.

#### **Approach Using Event Log and Process Mining Techniques**

- **Resource-Centric Analysis (Cold-Packing Stations):**  
  Use **resource-aware process mining** (e.g., resource performance analysis in tools like Disco, Celonis, or PM4Py) to reconstruct resource timelines. Filter logs for activities requiring cold-packing (`Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`) and map them to the 5 Cold-Packing stations. Compute:
  - **Queue length over time** per station.
  - **Waiting time before Packing START** for cold-pack orders.
  - **Utilization rate** of cold stations vs. standard stations.

- **Batching Delays (Shipping Label Generation):**  
  Analyze the **time delta between Quality Check COMPLETE and Shipping Label Generation COMPLETE**. Orders with identical `Destination Region` that complete QC close in time but experience significant gaps before label generation likely indicate batching. Use **cohort analysis** by region to:
  - Identify average **batch formation time**.
  - Measure **maximum wait time** for the last order in a batch.

- **Priority Handling (Express Orders):**  
  Compare processing timelines of **Express vs. Standard orders** at shared resources (e.g., packing stations, QC staff). Use **variant analysis** to detect:
  - Premature termination or extended idle time of Standard orders when Express orders arrive.
  - **Preemption events**: e.g., a Standard order’s Packing START is followed by a long gap before COMPLETE, coinciding with an Express order’s Packing START at the same station.

- **Hazardous Material (HazMat) Limits:**  
  Reconstruct a **real-time concurrency counter** for HazMat orders in Packing or QC. For each timestamp, count concurrent `Hazardous Material = TRUE` orders in those states. Identify:
  - **Throttling events**: periods where new HazMat orders are delayed despite resource availability due to the 10-order cap.
  - **Throughput loss**: compare theoretical vs. actual HazMat order completion rates.

#### **Key Metrics to Quantify Impact**

| Constraint | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Cold-Packing | Avg. waiting time before Packing START (cold orders) | Cold station utilization, queue length |
| Batching | Avg. wait time in “post-QC, pre-label” state by region | Batch size, batch formation time |
| Priority Handling | % of Standard orders delayed by >X mins due to Express preemption | Express order cycle time vs. SLA |
| HazMat Limits | % of time HazMat orders are queued despite free resources | Concurrent HazMat orders, throughput reduction |

#### **Differentiating Within- vs. Between-Instance Waiting**

- **Within-instance waiting**: Caused by long activity durations (e.g., Packing takes 15 mins due to complexity). Detected via **activity duration analysis**—consistent long durations across similar order types.
- **Between-instance waiting**: Caused by external dependencies. Detected by:
  - **Resource contention**: Resource is busy with *another* case (verified via resource timeline).
  - **Batch dependency**: No resource conflict, but label generation only occurs after peer orders complete QC (verified via cohort analysis).
  - **Regulatory blocking**: Resource is free, but system enforces wait due to HazMat cap (verified via concurrency counter).

Use **enhanced process maps** with color-coded waiting reasons (e.g., red = resource contention, blue = batching) to visually separate causes.

### **2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions**

Constraints do not operate in isolation; their interactions amplify bottlenecks:

- **Express + Cold-Packing**: An Express order requiring cold-packing may **preempt** a Standard cold-pack order, increasing queue length for cold stations. During peak times, this can cascade—multiple Express cold orders monopolize limited stations, starving Standard cold orders and increasing their cycle time beyond SLA.
  
- **Batching + HazMat Limits**: If a region (e.g., South) receives many HazMat orders, batching logic may **delay batch closure** until all orders are ready—but HazMat limits cap concurrent processing. This creates a **double delay**: orders wait for HazMat slots *and* then wait for batch peers, inflating end-to-end time.

- **Priority + HazMat**: An Express HazMat order may **bypass queueing** but still be blocked by the 10-order cap. If the system lacks visibility into near-future HazMat completions, it may hold the Express order unnecessarily.

**Why This Matters**:  
Optimizing one constraint in isolation (e.g., adding cold stations) may worsen another (e.g., increase HazMat concurrency if cold stations also handle HazMat). Effective solutions must be **holistically modeled**, respecting all interdependencies.

### **3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies**

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Cold-Packing Allocation with Priority-Aware Queuing**

- **Constraints Addressed**: Shared Cold-Packing + Priority Handling.
- **Proposed Changes**:
  - Implement a **priority-weighted queue** for cold stations: Express orders get immediate access if station free; if busy, Standard orders are paused after a grace period (e.g., 2 mins).
  - Introduce **real-time queue visibility** on warehouse floor displays to manage picker expectations.
- **Data Leverage**:
  - Use historical data to predict **peak cold-pack demand windows** (e.g., 2–4 PM daily) and pre-allocate staff.
  - Apply **machine learning** (e.g., regression) to forecast cold-pack order arrival rates by hour.
- **Expected Outcomes**:
  - Reduce Express cold-pack cycle time by 25–40%.
  - Limit Standard order preemption to <5% of cases, balancing fairness and priority.

#### **Strategy 2: Adaptive Batching with Time- and Volume-Based Triggers**

- **Constraints Addressed**: Shipping Batching + HazMat Limits (indirectly).
- **Proposed Changes**:
  - Replace fixed-region batching with **dynamic triggers**:
    - **Time cap**: Close batch if no new order arrives within 15 mins.
    - **Volume cap**: Close batch at 20 orders (or 8 HazMat orders to stay under regulatory cap).
    - **SLA override**: Force-close batch if any order exceeds 90% of its delivery SLA.
- **Data Leverage**:
  - Analyze historical **inter-arrival times** by region to set optimal time caps.
  - Use **cluster analysis** to identify regions with high HazMat density and apply stricter batch caps.
- **Expected Outcomes**:
  - Reduce average batch wait time by 30–50%.
  - Prevent HazMat batching bottlenecks by capping hazardous orders per batch.

#### **Strategy 3: Constraint-Aware Scheduling Engine for Packing & QC**

- **Constraints Addressed**: All four constraints via centralized orchestration.
- **Proposed Changes**:
  - Deploy a **real-time scheduling engine** that:
    - Tracks available cold stations, HazMat concurrency count, and batch status.
    - Assigns orders to resources using a **multi-objective scoring function**:
      ```
      Score = w1·(1/priority) + w2·(time_to_SLA) + w3·(hazmat_flag·hazmat_buffer)
      ```
    - Reserves HazMat capacity by **reserving future slots** based on predicted completions.
- **Data Leverage**:
  - Use **process simulation models** (calibrated with event log) to train the scheduler.
  - Integrate **real-time WMS data** for live resource status.
- **Expected Outcomes**:
  - Increase overall throughput by 15–20%.
  - Maintain HazMat compliance while reducing idle time.
  - Minimize preemption by scheduling Express orders during low-utilization windows.

### **4. Simulation and Validation**

Before implementation, **discrete-event simulation (DES)** models—parameterized with process mining insights—will validate strategies.

#### **Simulation Model Requirements**

- **Accurate Resource Modeling**:
  - 5 cold stations, N standard stations, M QC staff—with shift patterns.
  - Resource preemption rules for Express orders.
- **Constraint Enforcement**:
  - Hard cap of 10 concurrent HazMat orders in Packing/QC.
  - Batching logic with configurable time/volume triggers.
- **Demand Injection**:
  - Use **replayed event log traces** or **statistical distributions** (fitted to real data) for order arrivals, types, and attributes.

#### **Validation Focus Areas**

- **KPI Comparison**: Simulate “as-is” vs. “to-be” for:
  - End-to-end cycle time (by order type).
  - Resource utilization and queue lengths.
  - % of orders meeting delivery SLA.
- **Stress Testing**:
  - Simulate peak-season demand (e.g., 2× order volume).
  - Inject surge of Express or HazMat orders to test robustness.
- **Constraint Compliance**: Verify HazMat cap is never violated and batching rules are correctly applied.

Tools like **AnyLogic**, **Simul8**, or **Python SimPy** (integrated with PM4Py) can execute these simulations.

### **5. Monitoring Post-Implementation**

Continuous monitoring ensures sustained improvement and rapid detection of new bottlenecks.

#### **Key Metrics & Dashboards**

- **Constraint-Specific Dashboards**:
  1. **Cold-Packing Dashboard**:
     - Real-time queue length per station.
     - Avg. wait time (Standard vs. Express).
     - Preemption rate (% of Standard orders interrupted).
  2. **Batching Dashboard**:
     - Avg. batch formation time by region.
     - % of orders delayed >10 mins due to batching.
     - Batch size distribution.
  3. **HazMat Compliance Dashboard**:
     - Real-time concurrent HazMat count.
     - Throttling events per hour.
     - Throughput vs. theoretical max.
  4. **Priority Effectiveness Dashboard**:
     - Express order SLA compliance rate.
     - Impact on Standard order cycle time (delta vs. baseline).

- **Process Mining-Powered Alerts**:
  - Trigger alerts if:
    - Cold station queue > 8 orders.
    - Batch wait time exceeds 20 mins.
    - HazMat concurrency > 9 for >5 mins.
  - Use **conformance checking** to detect deviations from new scheduling rules.

- **Feedback Loop**:
  - Monthly **process variant analysis** to identify emerging patterns (e.g., new bottleneck at QC due to cold-pack delays).
  - Retrain prediction models quarterly with fresh event log data.

By embedding these dashboards into daily operations reviews, the fulfillment center can maintain agility and continuously refine its constraint-aware workflows.
