# Analysis of POWL Models for Hire-to-Retire Process

## Normative Process Flow
The standard Hire-to-Retire sequence should be:
**Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case**

---

## Model 1 Analysis

**Structure:** A strict partial order with the following edges:
- Post  Screen
- Screen  Decide
- Screen  Interview
- Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close

**Anomalies Identified:**
1. **Parallel/unordered Interview and Decide:** `Screen` leads to both `Decide` and `Interview`, but there is **no ordering edge between Interview and Decide**. This means the hiring decision could be made *before*, *after*, or *in parallel with* the interviews.
   - **Severity: HIGH** — This fundamentally violates process logic. A hiring decision should logically be informed by interview outcomes. Making the decision before interviewing defeats the purpose of interviewing.

**Otherwise:** The rest of the flow (Onboard  Payroll  Close) is correct and complete.

---

## Model 2 Analysis

**Structure:** A strict partial order with:
- Post  Screen, Post  Interview
- Interview  Decide  loop(Onboard, skip)  xor(Payroll, skip)  Close

**Anomalies Identified:**
1. **Screen_Candidates is disconnected from the downstream flow:** Screen follows Post but has no outgoing edge to Interview or Decide. Screening results don't feed into interviewing—Interview can happen independently (even in parallel with Screen).
   - **Severity: HIGH** — Candidates could be interviewed without being screened first.

2. **Loop on Onboarding:** `*(Onboard, skip)` allows the employee to be onboarded repeatedly.
   - **Severity: MEDIUM** — Repeated onboarding is nonsensical for a single hire; you onboard once.

3. **Optional Payroll (XOR with skip):** The employee may never be added to payroll.
   - **Severity: HIGH** — An employee who is hired but never added to payroll cannot be paid. This fundamentally breaks the Hire-to-Retire process integrity.

---

## Comparison and Conclusion

| Aspect | Model 1 | Model 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Missing/skippable critical steps | None | Payroll can be skipped |
| Incorrect parallelism | Interview vs Decide | Screen vs Interview |
| Unjustified repetition | None | Onboarding loop |
| Core flow completeness | Complete | Broken (payroll optional) |

### **Model 1 more closely aligns with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

**Justification:**
- Model 1 has **one structural anomaly** (unordered Interview/Decide), which, while serious, still guarantees that all essential activities execute exactly once in the expected overall progression.
- Model 2 contains **three compounding anomalies**: 
  - Screening is effectively decoupled from its purpose,
  - Onboarding can loop indefinitely,
  - Payroll—a mandatory regulatory and operational step—can be entirely skipped.
- The ability to skip payroll in Model 2 is particularly damaging: it means the process can "complete" with an employee hired but not compensated, which violates the fundamental essence of a Hire-to-Retire workflow.

Thus, although Model 1 is imperfect, it preserves the mandatory execution of all critical activities, whereas Model 2 allows traces that entirely subvert the purpose of the process.
