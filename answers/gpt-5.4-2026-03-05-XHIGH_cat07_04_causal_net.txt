Yes — it’s a workflow/process diagram, not a chart.

### What it shows
The visualization models a **request-handling / compensation decision process** using rounded rectangles for activities and arrows for flow.

### Main flow, left to right
- **a — register request**
  - This is the starting activity on the far left.

- From there, the process can go into review steps:
  - **b — examine thoroughly**
  - **c — examine casually**
  - **d — check ticket**

- These review activities feed into:
  - **e — decide**

- After the decision, there are three possible outcomes:
  - **g — pay compensation**
  - **h — reject request**
  - **f — reinitiate request**

- If the request is **reinitiated** at **f**, the flow loops back to the earlier examination steps and then returns to **e — decide**.

- The process finishes at:
  - **z — end**

### Visual structure
- The boxes are arranged mostly **left to right**:
  - start/request registration on the left,
  - examination steps in the middle-left,
  - decision in the center,
  - outcomes on the right,
  - end at the far right.
- **f — reinitiate request** sits lower left and creates a **feedback loop** back into the review stage.

### What the black dots mean
The black dots represent **split/join logic** in the workflow.

The inset legend in the lower-right explains the notation:
- **XOR-split / XOR-join** = exclusive choice / one path
- **AND-split / AND-join** = parallel all paths
- **OR-split / OR-join** = one or more possible paths

### Overall interpretation
In plain language, the diagram says:

> A request is registered, examined in one or more ways, then a decision is made.  
> The request is either approved for compensation, rejected, or sent back to be reinitiated and reviewed again before another decision.
