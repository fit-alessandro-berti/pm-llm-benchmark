Here are the main anomalies in the log, using a typical Procure-to-Pay flow as reference:

Typical P2P sequence:
1. Create Purchase Requisition
2. Approve Purchase Requisition
3. Issue Purchase Order
4. Receive Goods
5. Vendor Invoice Received
6. Match Invoice to PO

## Case 1001
This case looks normal.
- Requisition is created and approved before PO issuance.
- Goods are received before payment.
- Invoice is matched before payment.
- No obvious anomaly.

## Case 1002
This case has several anomalies:

### 1. Purchase Order issued before requisition approval
- `Issue Purchase Order` occurs at 08:40
- `Approve Purchase Requisition` occurs later at 08:00 on 2024-02-03

Why problematic:
- A PO should normally only be issued after the requisition is approved.
- This bypasses approval controls and may indicate unauthorized purchasing.

### 2. Payment released before requisition approval
- `Release Payment` happens before `Approve Purchase Requisition`

Why problematic:
- Payment is being made before internal authorization is completed.
- This is a major control weakness and increases fraud/compliance risk.

### 3. Payment released before goods receipt
- `Release Payment` on 2024-02-03 07:15
- `Receive Goods` on 2024-02-04 09:00

Why problematic:
- Standard practice is usually to pay after confirming receipt of goods/services.
- Paying before receipt increases risk of paying for items not delivered.

### 4. Payment released before invoice matching
- `Release Payment` occurs before `Match Invoice to PO`

Why problematic:
- Matching is a core control to verify invoice, PO, and receipt consistency.
- Paying before matching can result in overpayment, duplicate payment, or payment of invalid invoices.

## Case 1003
This case is clearly anomalous:

### 1. Payment released before invoice receipt
- `Release Payment` on 2024-02-08
- `Vendor Invoice Received` on 2024-02-09

Why problematic:
- A company should not normally pay before receiving an invoice.
- There is no formal basis for validating the payment amount.

### 2. Payment released before goods receipt
- `Release Payment` happens before `Receive Goods`

Why problematic:
- Payment should usually follow confirmation that goods were delivered.
- This creates financial and operational risk.

### 3. Payment released before invoice matching
- `Release Payment` occurs before `Match Invoice to PO`

Why problematic:
- This bypasses three-way match controls.
- Suggests a serious deviation from standard accounts payable practice.

Overall, case 1003 shows a very weak control sequence, with payment occurring much too early.

## Case 1004
This case has an important anomaly:

### 1. Goods received before purchase order issued
- `Receive Goods` at 2024-02-06 09:00
- `Issue Purchase Order` at 2024-02-06 09:15

Why problematic:
- Normally the PO should be issued before goods are received.
- Receiving goods before a PO suggests a retrospective or after-the-fact PO.
- This can indicate maverick buying, weak procurement discipline, or attempts to formalize an unauthorized purchase after delivery.

The rest of the flow is relatively consistent after that, but this ordering is still suspicious.

# Summary of anomalous cases

- **1001:** No obvious anomaly.
- **1002:** Multiple anomalies:
  - PO before approval
  - Payment before approval
  - Payment before goods receipt
  - Payment before matching
- **1003:** Multiple severe anomalies:
  - Payment before invoice receipt
  - Payment before goods receipt
  - Payment before matching
- **1004:** Goods received before PO issuance

# Why these anomalies matter
These deviations are problematic because they undermine standard P2P controls designed to ensure:
- authorized spending,
- proper segregation of duties,
- verification of delivery,
- validation of invoice correctness,
- prevention of fraud, duplicate payments, and unauthorized purchases.

In short, the most concerning pattern is any case where **payment is made before approval, receipt, or invoice matching**, because that defeats the core control logic of the P2P process.
