### **Overview**
This is a timeline-based process visualization (often used in process mining) that tracks the flow and duration of events for traffic fines or financial penalties over a 13-year period. It maps individual cases as lines connecting three key milestones (activities) represented by horizontal axes.

*   **Timeframe (X-axis):** The timeline runs horizontally from left to right, spanning from **January 25, 2000** (left) to **September 9, 2006** (middle) to **April 24, 2013** (right).
*   **Activities (Y-axis levels):** There are three labeled horizontal lines representing stages in the process:
    1.  **Send Fine** (Top)

    3.  **Send for Credit Collection** (Bottom)

### **Visual Patterns and Process Behavior**

#### **1. Top to Middle Stage ("Send Fine" to "Insert Fine Notification")**
*   **Appearance:** This section is densely packed with nearly vertical, straight grey lines.
*   **Meaning:** Because the lines are vertical, it indicates that the transition from "Send Fine" to "Insert Fine Notification" happens almost instantaneously or with very little delay. This activity occurs continuously and frequently throughout the entire 13-year period.

#### **2. Middle to Bottom Stage ("Insert Fine Notification" to "Send for Credit Collection")**
*   **Appearance:** Unlike the top section, the lines here are highly slanted and group together into distinct, inverted "fan" or "funnel" shapes that converge at specific, single points on the bottom line.
*   **Meaning (Batch Processing):** This pattern indicates **batch processing**. Fines that accumulated over months or years (the wide top of the fans) were processed and sent to credit collection all at once on specific dates (the convergence points at the bottom). There are roughly 10 major batch events visible on the bottom timeline.

#### **3. Color Coding of the Connections**
*   **Grey/Light Pink Lines:** Represent standard transitions. The faint pink/light-grey lines show the typical flow of cases over time.
*   **Bright Red/Orange Lines:** A cluster of sharp red lines is highly visible in the first half of the timeline (roughly between 2002 and 2006). These lines are highly slanted, indicating cases that had a very long delay between the notification stage and being sent to credit collection.

---

### **Summary of the Process Story**
The visualization reveals that while sending a fine and inserting its notification is a rapid, continuous daily operation, sending cases to credit collection is a highly delayed, periodic batch operation that only occurs a few times a year.
