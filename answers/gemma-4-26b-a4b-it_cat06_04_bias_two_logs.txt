1.  **The Mechanism:** When a member of Group B belongs to the `"Highland Civic Darts Club"`, a $+10$ point adjustment is added to their `PreliminaryScore`.
2.  **The Impact on Outcomes:** This adjustment directly alters the final decision by artificially inflating the applicant's standing.

    *   However, because of the "Community Boost," their score is adjusted to **705**, which leads to an **"Approved"** decision.

### Influence of Key Attributes

The bias is driven by the interaction of three specific columns:

*   **`CommunityGroup` (The Trigger):** This is the primary driver of the bias. The presence of a specific value (`Highland Civic Darts Club`) acts as a conditional trigger for the adjustment. If this attribute is `"None"` (as seen in Case U002), no boost is applied, even though the applicant is a `LocalResident`.
*   **`ScoreAdjustment` (The Vehicle):** This column serves as the mathematical vehicle for the bias. It transforms a subjective social attribute (`CommunityGroup`) into a quantitative advantage (`+10`).
*   **`LocalResident` (The Contextual Variable):** While all members of Group B are marked as `TRUE` for `LocalResident`, this attribute alone does not trigger the boost. Instead, it appears to create a baseline environment where community affiliation is rewarded, whereas Group A (all `FALSE`) is excluded from this benefit entirely.

The logs reveal a fundamental disparity in how "success" is achieved between the two groups:

1.  **Merit-Based vs. Affiliation-Based Approval:** In **Group A**, approval is strictly tied to the `PreliminaryScore`. High scores (720, 740) are approved, and lower scores (710) are rejected. The process is consistent and transparent.
2.  **Threshold Manipulation:** In **Group B**, the threshold for approval is effectively lowered for members of the Highland Civic Darts Club. This creates a "two-tier" system where one subset of the Unprotected Group is held to a higher standard (U002, who had no community group and was rejected at 710), while another subset receives preferential treatment (U003, who was approved at a boosted 705).

By allowing `CommunityGroup` to dictate `ScoreAdjustment`, the system ceases to be an objective evaluator of the `PreliminaryScore` and instead becomes a mechanism that rewards social connectivity, systematically disadvantaging those without specific community ties (including all members of Group A).
