### **BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Workflow**

---

#### **Process Name:**  
Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling

#### **Primary Goal:**  
To validate, approve, and schedule payment for incoming supplier invoices in accordance with procurement policies and financial controls.

---

### **Roles Involved:**
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)** – Initiates and manages the process; performs matching, verification, and escalation.
- **Purchasing Officer (Dan)** – Confirms receipt and accuracy of goods/services against PO.
- **Department Manager (Carlos)** – Final approval authority for payment.
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)** – Handles exceptions and unresolved issues.
- **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** – Assists in resolving supplier-related discrepancies.
- **Supplier (External)** – Provides invoice and supporting documentation.

---

### **Key Artifacts (Data Objects / Documents):**
- **Invoice (PDF/Email attachment)** – Incoming document from supplier.
- **Purchase Order (PO)** – Reference document linking order to delivery.
- **PO Confirmation (Email or system entry)** – From Purchasing confirming receipt.
- **Invoice Match Report** – System-generated or manual verification record.
- **Exception Log / Escalation Ticket** – Track unresolved issues.
- **Corrected Invoice (Revised)** – Updated invoice from supplier (if needed).
- **Retrospective PO** – Created for non-PO invoices (via Rita’s intervention).

---

### **Main Process Flow (Sequence of Activities):**

1. **Receive Invoice**  
   - *Role:* Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - *Trigger:* Email or system upload of invoice  
   - *Input:* Invoice (with or without PO number)  
   - *Description:* Mary receives the invoice. Checks for PO number.

2. **Check for Valid Purchase Order (PO)**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Decision Point (Gateway):*  
     - **Yes  Proceed to Step 3**  
     - **No  Proceed to Step 4**  
   - *Condition:* PO number present and valid in system  
   - *Output:* If missing, initiate request for PO.

3. **Log Invoice into Accounting System**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Input:* Invoice + PO  
   - *Output:* System record created; status = “In Review”

4. **Request Missing PO or Clarify Information**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Action:* Sends email to supplier requesting PO number or clarification  
   - *Trigger:* PO missing or ambiguous details  
   - *Output:* Supplier response (PO or revised invoice)  
   - *Note:* If no response within 3 business days, escalate to Rita (per Rita’s account).

5. **Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Action:* Sends invoice and PO details to Purchasing Officer (Dan)  
   - *Input:* Invoice + PO  
   - *Output:* Confirmation from Dan (via email or system)

6. **Purchasing Confirm Receipt and Match Details**  
   - *Role:* Dan  
   - *Action:* Compares invoice line items (quantities, prices, delivery charges) with PO  
   - *Decision Point (Gateway):*  
     - **Match  Proceed to Step 7**  
     - **Mismatch  Proceed to Step 8**  
   - *Condition:* All items, quantities, and prices align with PO  
   - *Output:* Confirmation email or system status update (“Confirmed”)

7. **Send Confirmation to Accounts Payable**  
   - *Role:* Dan  
   - *Action:* Notifies Mary that invoice is confirmed and ready for approval  
   - *Trigger:* System auto-notifies Mary and Carlos

8. **Discrepancy Identified – Notify Accounts Payable**  
   - *Role:* Dan  
   - *Action:* Emails Mary with specific mismatch details (e.g., extra units, incorrect pricing)  
   - *Trigger:* Discrepancy found during comparison  
   - *Output:* Mary initiates resolution process (see Step 10)

9. **Invoice Matching and Discrepancy Resolution**  
   - *Role:* Mary (primary), Rita (if unresolved), Karen (if needed)  
   - *Actions:*  
     - Mary attempts to resolve via email with Dan or supplier.  
     - If unresolved after 2 attempts, Mary escalates to Rita.  
     - Rita may contact Karen to verify contract terms or supplier agreement.  
     - Karen provides guidance on correct pricing or terms.  
     - If needed, Rita creates a **retrospective PO** for non-PO invoices (e.g., ad-hoc services).  
   - *Output:* Resolved discrepancy, corrected invoice, or approved retrospective PO.

10. **Re-initiate Matching After Resolution**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Action:* Re-checks invoice against PO (or retrospective PO) after resolution  
   - *Decision Point (Gateway):*  
     - **Match  Proceed to Step 11**  
     - **Still Mismatched  Escalate to Rita (again)**  
   - *Note:* Rework loop possible if resolution fails.

11. **Send to Department Manager for Approval**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Action:* System automatically triggers approval request to Carlos  
   - *Input:* Invoice, PO, Purchase Confirmation  
   - *Output:* Notification sent to Carlos

12. **Manager Review and Approval**  
   - *Role:* Carlos  
   - *Action:* Reviews invoice details, PO, and confirmation in system  
   - *Decision Point (Gateway):*  
     - **Approve  Proceed to Step 13**  
     - **Reject  Proceed to Step 14**  
   - *Condition:*  
     - Approve: All details correct, no red flags (e.g., budget overrun, unusual item).  
     - Reject: Suspicious variance (e.g., 20%+ above budget), missing documentation, or unclear justification.  
   - *Output:* Approval or rejection notification in system

13. **Schedule for Payment**  
   - *Role:* Mary  
   - *Action:* Adds invoice to next available payment run based on schedule  
   - *Output:* Payment scheduled (status updated to “Scheduled”)

14. **Rejection and Investigation**  
   - *Role:* Carlos  Mary  Rita (if needed)  
   - *Action:*  
     - Carlos rejects invoice and specifies reason.  
     - Mary notifies supplier and internal team.  
     - If reason is unclear or dispute persists, Mary escalates to Rita.  
     - Rita investigates (e.g., checks contract, contacts Karen, reviews budget).  
     - Decision: Reopen for correction, reject permanently, or approve after exception handling.  
   - *Output:* Either re-submission to approval or final rejection

15. **Exception Handling (Escalation to Supervisor)**  
   - *Role:* Rita (Supervisor)  
   - *Trigger:*  
     - Invoice lacks PO and cannot be resolved.  
     - Discrepancy persists despite multiple attempts.  
     - Need to create retrospective PO.  
     - Supplier disputes or contract ambiguity.  
   - *Actions:*  
     - Coordinates with Karen to validate terms.  
     - Works with department heads to justify retrospective PO.  
     - Documents exception and approves resumption of process.  
   - *Output:* Exception cleared; process resumes at Step 6 or Step 10

---

### **Alternate Paths & Variations:**

| **Path** | **Trigger** | **Description** |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Non-PO Invoice** | Invoice received without PO | Requires retrospective PO creation (Rita’s role); may involve contract review (Karen). |
| **Persistent Discrepancy** | Multiple failed resolution attempts | Escalates to Rita; possible creation of new PO or rejection. |
| **Manager Rejects** | Budget anomaly, unclear item | Triggers investigation loop (Carlos  Mary  Rita  Karen). |
| **Supplier Response Delay** | No reply within 3 days | Automatic escalation to Rita; possible payment delay. |
| **Internal Disagreement** | Dan and Mary disagree on match | Rita mediates; may require Karen’s input. |

---

### **Ambiguities & Uncertainties:**
- **Who creates retrospective PO?** Rita explicitly states she does; no other role confirms this.  
- **Is the PO check before or after logging?** Mary says “check before logging,” but Dan implies PO is needed *before* any action. Consistent, but wording suggests logging only if PO exists.  
- **What happens if manager rejects?** Carlos says he may ask Mary to investigate further, but no formal rework loop is described. Model assumes rework via Mary  Rita  supplier.  
- **System automation level:** All notifications are described as “system sends email,” implying automation, but no confirmation of real-time updates or dashboards.  
- **Payment scheduling rules:** Not specified (e.g., batch runs, payment terms). Assumed to be handled by Mary based on standard cycle.
