### Explanation

**1. Case Identification Logic:**
I grouped events based on the **Window Title** attribute, as this provides a clear semantic boundary for a specific task.
*   **Case_001:** Focused on the lifecycle of `Document1.docx` (Drafting, editing, updating with budget info, closing).
*   **Case_002:** Focused on communication via `Email - Inbox`.
*   **Case_003:** Focused on the review of `Report_Draft.pdf`.
*   **Case_004:** Focused on financial record keeping in `Budget_2024.xlsx`.
*   **Case_005:** Focused on finalizing the `Quarterly_Report.docx`.

*Note: The initial `FOCUS` event on `Quarterly_Report.docx` at 08:59:50 was treated as noise/background activity since the user immediately switched to `Document1` to begin active work.*

**2. Activity Naming Logic:**
I mapped raw inputs to high-level business actions to make the log suitable for process discovery algorithms:
*   `TYPING` + `CLICK` combinations were simplified into business-friendly terms like **Content Editing**, **Data Entry**, or **Compose Email**.
*   `SAVE`/`CLOSE` events were standardized to explicitly reflect the state transition of the object (Document/File).
*   Interaction with non-editable content (like `SCROLL` or `HIGHLIGHT`) was classified under **Review** or **Annotate** to capture the "Read/Analyze" portion of the process.
