The event log for **Group B (Unprotected Group)** exhibits bias. ---

### Implications for Fairness and Equity
1. **Undermines Meritocracy**:  
   - The boost rewards candidates for **irrelevant affiliations** rather than job-related qualifications. This contradicts the principle that hiring should be based on skills, experience, and cultural fit (as measured objectively).  

2. **Discriminatory Impact**:  
   - The "LocalBusinessLeadersClub" may have **exclusionary membership criteria** (e.g., socioeconomic status, networking access). Boosting scores for members indirectly discriminates against candidates who lack such affiliations.  
   - This violates **equal opportunity principles**, as candidates are advantaged/disadvantaged based on social capital rather than merit.  

3. **Compounded Bias**:  
   - The adjustment is applied at multiple stages (CulturalFitCheck and FinalInterview), **amplifying its influence** on the decision.  

4. **Lack of Transparency**:  
   - The rationale for the boost ("Association Boost") is vague and not tied to job requirements, making the process **opaque and unaccountable**.  

---

### Conclusion
Group B’s practice of granting a **+5 cultural fit boost** to "LocalBusinessLeadersClub" members introduces **systemic bias** by favoring candidates with specific social affiliations. This:  
- **Reduces fairness** by overriding objective scores with subjective advantages.  
- **Exacerbates inequity** by potentially excluding qualified candidates who lack access to elite networks.  
- **Violates ethical hiring standards**, which require decisions to be based solely on job-relevant criteria.
