Let’s analyze the event log for each **Case ID** to identify anomalies in the **Procure-to-Pay (P2P)** process. The standard P2P process flow typically follows this sequence:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition** 
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition** 
3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)** 
4. **Receive Goods** 
5. **Vendor Invoice Received** 
6. **Match Invoice to PO** 
7. **Release Payment** 
8. **Close Case**

Any deviation from this logical order or missing steps can indicate inefficiencies, compliance risks, or fraud.

### **Case 1002: Multiple Anomalies**
**Sequence:**
1. Create Purchase Requisition
2. **Issue Purchase Order** *(before approval!)*
3. Vendor Invoice Received
4. **Release Payment** *(before receiving goods or matching invoice!)*
5. **Approve Purchase Requisition** *(after PO and payment!)*
6. Receive Goods
7. Match Invoice to PO
8. Close Case

#### **Anomalies & Risks:**
1. **PO Issued Before Approval (Step 2)**
   - **Problem:** A PO should **never** be issued before the purchase requisition is approved. This violates **internal controls** and **compliance policies**, as it allows unauthorized purchases.
   - **Risk:** Potential for **fraud, maverick spending, or non-compliant procurement**.

2. **Payment Released Before Receiving Goods (Step 4)**
   - **Problem:** Payment was made **before goods were received** and **before invoice matching**. This is a **major red flag** in P2P.
   - **Risk:**
     - **Overpayment** (if goods are never delivered).
     - **Fraud** (vendor could be paid for undelivered goods).
     - **Cash flow issues** (paying for something not yet received).
     - **Violation of 3-way matching** (PO, receipt, invoice should align before payment).

3. **Approval After PO and Payment (Step 5)**
   - **Problem:** The requisition was approved **after** the PO was issued and payment was made. This is **backward** and defeats the purpose of approvals.
   - **Risk:** **No proper authorization** for the purchase, leading to **audit failures** and **compliance violations**.

 **Overall Assessment:** This case is **highly anomalous** and suggests **serious control failures**, possibly **fraud or collusion** between an employee and vendor.

   - **Risk:**

     - **Non-compliance** with standard P2P controls.

 **Overall Assessment:** This case is **highly irregular** and suggests **poor financial controls**, possibly **fraud or process bypassing**.
