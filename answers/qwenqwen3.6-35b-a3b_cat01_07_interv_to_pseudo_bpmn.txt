### BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

**Process Name:** Invoice Approval & Payment Scheduling  
**Primary Trigger:** Receipt of Invoice (via Email)  
**Process Goal:** Validate invoice against PO, resolve discrepancies, obtain managerial approval, and schedule payment.

---

#### 1. Roles & Participants (Swimlanes)
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk:** Initial intake, logging, matching, escalation, payment scheduling.
*   **Purchasing Officer:** Verification of goods/services receipt, price/quantity validation, supplier coordination.
*   **Department Manager:** Financial/budgetary approval.
*   **AP Supervisor:** Exception handling, escalation resolution, retrospective PO creation.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM):** Contract verification, supplier liaison for discrepancies.
*   **Supplier (External):** Provider of invoices, POs, and corrections.
*   **Accounting System:** Automated logging, matching, notifications.

#### 2. Artifacts & Data Objects
*   **Invoice:** Incoming document.
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference document.
*   **Receipt Confirmation:** Evidence of goods/services received.
*   **System Record:** Digital entry in accounting system.
*   **Contract:** Reference for pricing/terms.
*   **Retrospective PO:** Created for informal contracts.
*   **Email Notifications:** System-generated alerts for approvals/holds.

---

#### 3. Process Flow Description

**Pool: Invoice Approval Process**

**Lane: Accounts Payable (Clerk)**
1.  **Start Event:** Invoice Received.
2.  **Task:** Validate PO Presence.
    *   *Check:* Does invoice contain a valid PO number?
3.  **Gateway:** PO Valid?
    *   **No:**  **Task:** Send Invoice to Supplier requesting details/PO.
        *   *Flow:* Supplier provides PO  Loop back to **Task: Validate PO Presence**.
    *   **Yes:**  **Task:** Log Invoice in Accounting System.
4.  **Task:** Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation.
5.  **Task:** Receive Confirmation from Purchasing.
6.  **Task:** Match Invoice Line Items vs. PO in System.
7.  **Gateway:** Match Successful?
    *   **No (Discrepancy):**  **Task:** Email Purchasing to Clarify.
        *   *Flow:* Purchasing responds  Loop back to **Task: Match Invoice Line Items**.
        *   *Exception:* If discrepancy unresolvable  **Task:** Escalate to AP Supervisor.
    *   **Yes:**  **Task:** Request Manager Approval.
        *   *System Action:* System sends automatic email to Manager.
8.  **Task:** Receive Manager Decision.
    *   *Condition:* If Manager Rejects/Holds  **Task:** Investigate/Hold Invoice.
        *   *Flow:* Loop back to **Task: Request Manager Approval** (or investigate root cause).
    *   *Condition:* If Manager Approves  **Task:** Schedule for Payment.
9.  **End Event:** Payment Scheduled.

**Lane: Purchasing Officer**
1.  **Task:** Receive Invoice from AP.
2.  **Task:** Verify Details vs. PO.
    *   *Check:* Quantities, prices, delivery charges, receipt of goods.
3.  **Gateway:** Discrepancy Found?
    *   **Yes:**  **Task:** Contact Supplier OR **Task:** Ask AP to Hold Invoice.
        *   *Sub-Process:* Supplier sends corrected invoice  Loop back to **Task: Verify Details**.
        *   *Note:* Purchasing may also email AP to clarify based on context.
    *   **No:**  **Task:** Send Confirmation Email to AP.
        *   *Constraint:* Never confirm without valid PO reference.
4.  **Task:** Respond to AP Clarification Requests.
    *   *Flow:* Triggered by AP email regarding discrepancies.
    *   *Output:* Resolution/Correction  Loop back to AP **Task: Match Invoice**.

**Lane: Department Manager**
1.  **Task:** Receive System Notification.
2.  **Task:** Review Invoice Details.
    *   *Check:* Quantities, pricing, special notes, budget variance.
3.  **Gateway:** Suspicious/Issues Detected?
    *   **Yes:**  **Task:** Reject Invoice OR **Task:** Request Hold/Investigation.
        *   *Flow:* Hold request  Loop back to AP for investigation.
    *   **No:**  **Task:** Approve in System.
        *   *Output:* Approval Signal  Loop back to AP **Task: Receive Manager Decision**.

**Lane: AP Supervisor**
1.  **Task:** Receive Escalation.
    *   *Triggers:* Unresolvable discrepancy, Purchasing issue, Missing info, No PO cases.
2.  **Task:** Coordinate Resolution.
    *   *Actions:* Contact suppliers, coordinate between departments.
3.  **Gateway:** Exception Type?
    *   **Informal Contract/No PO:**  **Task:** Work with Dept Head to Create Retrospective PO OR **Task:** Reject Invoice.
        *   *Post-Action:* If Retrospective PO created  Return to **Lane: Purchasing** for confirmation (Normal Flow).
    *   **Other Exception:**  **Task:** Resolve Issue.
        *   *Post-Action:* Return to Normal Flow (Confirmation from Purchasing  Manager Approval).
4.  **Task:** Ensure Invoice Re-enters Flow or is Closed.
    *   *Note:* Supervisor does not perform final approvals.

**Lane: Supplier Relationship Manager**
1.  **Task:** Receive Loop-In Request.
    *   *Triggers:* AP/Purchasing flags mismatched terms or missing POs.
2.  **Task:** Resolve Supplier Issue.
    *   *Actions:* Contact supplier for PO/correction; Check contract for pricing/terms; Advise AP/Purchasing.
    *   *Output:* Resolution info  Support Purchasing/AP tasks.

---

#### 4. Exceptions & Variations Summary
*   **Missing PO:** Invoice returned to supplier. If supplier cannot provide or contract is informal, Supervisor creates Retrospective PO or Rejects.
*   **Discrepancies (Pricing/Items):**
    *   *Minor/Resolvable:* AP emails Purchasing; Purchasing contacts Supplier or asks AP to hold. Loop until resolved.
    *   *Major/Unresolvable:* Escalation to Supervisor.
*   **Manager Rejection:** Invoice held for investigation or rejected. Investigation may trigger re-approval.
*   **Informal Contracts:** Supervisor intervention to validate via Dept Head and create Retrospective PO.
*   **Supplier Terms Mismatch:** SRM engaged to verify contract and advise correct pricing.

#### 5. Ambiguities & Conflicts Resolved
*   **Matching vs. Checking:** Mary (AP) describes "matching in system" after Purchasing confirmation, while Dan (Purchasing) describes "checking details." The model reflects this as a two-step validation: Purchasing validates business reality (receipt/quantities), then AP performs the system match against the PO before escalation.
*   **Discrepancy Handling:** Mary mentions emailing Purchasing; Dan mentions contacting the supplier. The model allows Purchasing discretion to either contact the supplier directly or collaborate with AP, acknowledging both viewpoints.
*   **Retrospective POs:** Mary states all invoices without POs are sent back. Rita clarifies that informal contracts may require a Retrospective PO. The model prioritizes Rita's exception handling for cases where the standard "send back" loop fails or is inappropriate.
*   **SRM Involvement:** Karen is not in the main sequence but is a support resource. The model places her in a "Loop-In" path triggered by specific discrepancies.

#### 6. Visual Mapping Guide
*   **Tasks:** Represented as rounded rectangles.
*   **Gateways:** Represented as diamonds (Exclusive/XOR) for decisions like "PO Valid?", "Discrepancy?", "Match Successful?".
*   **Sequence Flow:** Arrows indicating direction.
*   **Loops:** Arrows returning to previous tasks for rework (e.g., Discrepancy resolution).
*   **Swimlanes:** Vertical or horizontal lanes for each Role.
*   **Artifacts:** Dashed lines connecting tasks to data objects (e.g., Invoice  Task: Log Invoice).

This textual model provides a complete specification for converting into a BPMN diagram, capturing the standard flow, all identified exceptions, role responsibilities, and decision points derived from the interview data.
