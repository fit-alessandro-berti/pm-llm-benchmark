**Key design notes:**

1. **Temporal Progression Logic**: Times increase systematically with process distance:
   - Immediate transitions: Hours to days (e.g., inspection takes 4h ｱ 1h)
   - Intermediate spans: Days with moderate variability (e.g., assembly to testing: 2d ｱ 4h)
   - Full process: ~19.7 days total with substantial variability (ｱ2.5 days)

2. **Realistic Supply Chain Dynamics**:
   - **Procurement variability**: High standard deviation for supplier-related steps (ｱ1-2 days)
   - **Manufacturing consistency**: Lower variability in controlled factory processes
   - **Distribution uncertainty**: Significant variability in final delivery stages

3. **Mathematical Consistency**:
   - For separated activities: `average = sum of intermediate averages`
   - `std_dev = sqrt(sum of squared intermediate std_devs)` (assuming independence)
   - Example: `('SS', 'RC') = 86400+604800 = 691200`, `std = sqrt(3600ｲ+86400ｲ)  86472`

4. **Process Bottlenecks Identified**:
   - **Longest single transition**: Order to Receive Components (7 days)
   - **Highest variability**: Distribution to After-Sales Support (ｱ2 days)
   - **Critical path**: Supplier Selection to After-Sales Support spans nearly 3 weeks

This profile enables deviation detection where `ZETA` thresholds can flag:
- Components arriving faster than expected (supply chain efficiency)
- Testing taking longer than normal (quality issues)
- Distribution delays exceeding predicted variability

The dictionary structure supports both direct monitoring of adjacent activities and detection of systemic delays across the entire supply chain.
