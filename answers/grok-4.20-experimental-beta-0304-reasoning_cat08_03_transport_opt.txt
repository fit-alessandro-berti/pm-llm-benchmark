**1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

**Data Preprocessing and Integration**  
The first step is to create a unified event log where each case represents a meaningful unit of analysis. I recommend using **Vehicle-Day (Case ID = VehicleID-Date)** as the primary case identifier, as it captures a complete operational cycle from shift start to end.  

Integration steps:  
- **Timestamp standardization**: Convert all timestamps to UTC with millisecond precision and handle timezone differences between systems.  
- **Event correlation**: Use Vehicle ID + Timestamp proximity + Driver ID as matching keys. GPS events provide continuous movement data, scanner events provide discrete delivery milestones, dispatch events provide planned context, and maintenance logs provide interruption events.  
- **Activity labeling**: Create a standardized activity taxonomy (e.g., "Depart Depot", "Travel", "Arrive Customer", "Service Delivery", "Delivery Failed", "Unscheduled Stop - Maintenance", "Return to Depot").  
- **Attribute enrichment**: Attach contextual attributes to each event (planned vs actual route sequence, number of remaining stops, traffic level if derivable from speed/dwell time, driver experience, vehicle type, weather if available).  

**Challenges**:  
- Granularity mismatch (GPS events occur every few seconds, scanner events are sporadic). Solution: aggregate GPS data into meaningful activities ("Travel", "Idle > 3min", "Traffic Delay").  
- Missing or conflicting data (scanner events without GPS fix, or GPS without scanner confirmation). Use probabilistic matching and imputation where reasonable.  
- Defining start and end events clearly to avoid partial traces.  

**Process Discovery**  
I would apply the **Inductive Miner** (infrequent variant) or **Heuristics Miner** to discover the *actual* process model from the integrated event log. These algorithms handle noisy real-world logistics data better than the classic Alpha algorithm. The resulting model would visualize the end-to-end flow including:  
- Frequent loops (multiple failed delivery attempts)  
- Parallel paths (maintenance events interrupting delivery sequences)  
- Highly variable travel-service-travel patterns  

**Conformance Checking**  
Using the planned routes from the dispatch system as the normative model, I would perform **alignments-based conformance checking**. This identifies:  
- **Sequence deviations**: Vehicles skipping planned stops or changing order significantly.  
- **Unplanned activities**: "Unscheduled Stop", "Low Speed Detected > 5min", "Delivery Failed" not in the original plan.  
- **Timing deviations**: Activities exceeding planned time windows (e.g., arrival at customer >15min late).  
- **Fitness and precision metrics** at both process and trace levels, with drill-down by route, driver, and time of day.  

This comparison typically reveals that 30-50% of cases in last-mile logistics deviate meaningfully from the planned route.

**2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** (calculated directly from the event log):

- **On-Time Delivery Rate**: % of "Delivery Success" events occurring within the customer-requested time window (from dispatch data).
- **Average Time per Delivery Stop**: Time between "Arrive Customer" and "Depart Customer" (service time).
- **Travel Time vs Service Time Ratio**: Total time with speed >5km/h vs total time at customer locations.
- **Fuel Consumption Proxy**: Derived from idle time, average speed, and harsh acceleration/braking patterns from GPS (or integrate with actual fuel data if available).
- **Vehicle Utilization Rate**: Active delivery time vs total shift time (excluding depot waiting).
- **Traffic Delay Frequency**: Number and duration of "Low Speed Detected" events in specific geographic areas/times.
- **Failed Delivery Rate**: % of stops ending in "Delivery Failed", plus second-attempt success rate.
- **Route Completion Time Variance**: Standard deviation of total case duration.

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**  
- **Performance-enhanced process maps** (using tools like Celonis, Disco, or PM4Py) with waiting time overlays.  
- **Bottleneck analysis** focusing on activities with highest waiting time or longest duration.  
- **Case-level filtering** and **variant analysis** to compare fastest vs slowest 20% of cases.  
- **Geographic performance analysis**: Aggregate delays by GPS coordinates to identify traffic hotspots or parking difficulty zones.  
- **Time-based analysis**: Heatmaps showing performance by hour of day and day of week.  

Typical bottlenecks in this domain: morning congestion on specific arterial roads, high service time variability at certain customer types (apartments vs houses), and cascading delays after the first failed delivery.

**3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

Process mining enables moving from symptoms to root causes through targeted analyses:

- **Variant Analysis**: Compare "happy paths" (on-time, low fuel) vs "unhappy paths" (late, high cost). This often reveals that certain route sequences or stop orders dramatically increase delay probability.
- **Dwell Time Analysis**: Break down time at customer locations. High variance usually points to parking difficulty, customer interaction issues, or failed deliveries requiring notes/reattempts.
- **Correlation Analysis**: Link "Low Speed Detected" events to specific times and locations to validate traffic patterns. Cross-reference with external traffic data if available.
- **Driver/Route Performance Matrix**: Identify statistically significant performance differences between drivers on similar routes (suggesting skill or behavior differences).
- **Maintenance Impact Analysis**: Measure the downstream effect of unscheduled stops on same-day delivery performance and subsequent days (vehicle availability).
- **Failed Delivery Cascade Analysis**: Quantify how one failed delivery increases total route time and failure probability for subsequent stops.

Common root causes likely to emerge:  
- Static route planning that doesn't account for real-world traffic patterns or delivery time variability.  
- Inaccurate estimated arrival times in the dispatch system.  
- High failed delivery rates due to poor time window management or lack of customer pre-notification.  
- Driver-specific behaviors (e.g., inefficient parking or customer approach).  
- Vehicle maintenance patterns correlated with high-utilization routes.

**4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Route Re-sequencing Based on Real-Time Insights**  
*Target*: Sequence deviations and cascading delays from early delays.  
*Root Cause*: Static planning that doesn't reflect actual travel and service time distributions discovered in the process map.  
*Process Mining Support*: Discovered process models show optimal stop sequences for different times of day and traffic conditions. Create "digital route twins" based on historical successful variants.  
*Expected Impact*: 15-25% reduction in total route time, improved On-Time Delivery Rate, lower Travel Time vs Service Time ratio.  

**Strategy 2: Predictive Failed Delivery Prevention and Time Window Optimization**  
*Target*: High failed delivery rate and subsequent re-delivery costs.  
*Root Cause*: Poor correlation between assigned time windows and actual customer availability patterns visible in the scanner data.  
*Process Mining Support*: Analysis of "Delivery Failed" events by customer type, time of day, and previous delivery history. Cluster customers by successful delivery patterns.  
*Expected Impact*: 20-40% reduction in failed deliveries, significant reduction in overtime and fuel waste from re-delivery loops. Implement pre-delivery SMS notifications for high-risk customers.

**Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance and Vehicle Assignment Optimization**  
*Target*: Unscheduled stops during delivery shifts and associated downtime.  
*Root Cause*: Maintenance needs triggered by usage patterns not visible in traditional scheduling (high idling, frequent short stops, specific route stress).  
*Process Mining Support*: Correlate vehicle event patterns (frequent low-speed/high-idle periods, harsh braking) with subsequent maintenance events. Identify "high stress" route patterns.  
*Expected Impact*: 30-50% reduction in unscheduled breakdowns during shifts, better vehicle utilization, lower overall maintenance costs through condition-based scheduling.

**5. Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring**

All strategies must respect hard constraints:  
- Driver working hours (maximum shift length, mandatory breaks detectable in the event log).  
- Vehicle capacity and package compatibility.  
- Customer time windows (cannot be completely ignored).  
- Regulatory requirements for delivery in urban areas.  

Implementation should use **constraint-aware optimization** algorithms that take the process mining-derived time distributions as input rather than static estimates.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan**  
Deploy a **process mining dashboard** with:  
- Real-time conformance checking (daily fitness score vs baseline).  
- KPI trend monitoring with automated alerts when On-Time Delivery drops below threshold or failed delivery rate spikes.  
- **Variant drift detection** to identify when new process patterns emerge (e.g., new traffic patterns or construction impacts).  
- Driver/vehicle performance scorecards updated weekly.  
- Geographic bottleneck heatmaps refreshed daily.  

Review cadence: Weekly operational review of top 5 deviations, monthly deep-dive root cause analysis, and quarterly strategy recalibration. This creates a closed-loop system where process mining continuously validates whether optimizations are delivering expected KPI improvements and rapidly identifies emerging issues.

This approach transforms Speedy Parcels' raw event data into a powerful operational intelligence capability, moving from reactive firefighting to proactive, data-driven logistics optimization.
