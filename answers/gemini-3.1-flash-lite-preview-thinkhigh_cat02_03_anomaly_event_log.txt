Based on an analysis of the provided event log against standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) best practices, Case 1001 represents a standard, compliant process. However, Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004 exhibit significant anomalies. 

Below is an analysis of these deviations and why they pose risks to business operations and financial integrity.

---

### 1. Case 1002: Lack of Segregation of Duties and Premature Payment
**Anomalies:**
*   **Approval after Issuance:** The Purchase Requisition (PR) was approved *after* the Purchase Order (PO) was issued and payment was released.
*   **Pre-delivery Payment:** Payment was released on Feb 3, but goods were not received until Feb 4.

**Why it is problematic:**
*   **Control Failure:** Approving a PR after the PO is issued suggests the process is merely a "rubber stamp" exercise. It bypasses internal controls designed to verify the business need for a purchase before committing company funds.
*   **Financial Risk:** Paying for goods before they are received (or confirmed as quality-compliant) exposes the company to the risk of "pay and pray." If the vendor fails to deliver or delivers damaged goods, the company has lost its primary leverage (withholding payment) to resolve the issue.

### 2. Case 1003: Payment without Verification
**Anomalies:**
*   **Payment before Invoice and Receipt:** The payment was released on Feb 8, but the Vendor Invoice was not received until Feb 9, and the Goods were not received until Feb 10.

**Why it is problematic:**
*   **Control/Audit Failure:** This is a severe deviation known as "unauthorized payment." Paying before receiving an invoice prevents the company from verifying that the amount, tax, and terms match the PO. 
*   **Fraud Risk:** Paying before receiving goods removes the "Three-Way Match" (PO vs. Receipt vs. Invoice), which is the cornerstone of P2P fraud prevention. This scenario could indicate a fictitious vendor or embezzlement, as no evidence of goods or a valid invoice existed at the time of payment.

### 3. Case 1004: Receipt before Purchase Order
**Anomalies:**
*   **Receipt before PO:** The Goods were received on Feb 6, but the Purchase Order was not issued until Feb 6 (15 minutes later).

**Why it is problematic:**
*   **"Maverick Buying":** This indicates that goods were ordered or acquired without a formal, approved purchase order in place. This is a classic indicator of "Maverick Spending," where employees circumvent the procurement department.
*   **Lack of Spend Visibility:** By ordering goods before a PO is issued, the organization loses the ability to negotiate prices, enforce contract terms, or ensure that the purchase is within budget. It also creates a compliance gap where the organization has effectively accepted a liability (the goods) before the legal obligation (the PO) was formally established.
