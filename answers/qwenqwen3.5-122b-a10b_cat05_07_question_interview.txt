Here is a list of targeted, open-ended questions designed to deepen your conceptual understanding of the property onboarding process. They are categorized by process phase to help structure the conversation.

### **Phase 1: Documentation & Initial Intake**
1.  **Regarding documentation thresholds:** You mentioned that documentation must be verified before proceeding. What is the specific protocol if a landlord is unable to provide a specific compliance certificate due to local jurisdiction issues? Is there a waiver process, or does the onboarding halt indefinitely?
2.  **Regarding data entry:** When the coordinator records property details into the central database, is there a validation step where the landlord confirms the accuracy of that data before the property moves to the assignment phase?
3.  **Regarding timelines:** What is the typical Service Level Agreement (SLA) or expected turnaround time for the coordinator to review and approve the initial documentation packet?

### **Phase 2: Property Manager Assignment**
4.  **Regarding assignment logic:** You noted that assignments depend on workload, geography, and experience. How is "current workload" quantitatively measured? Is there a cap on the number of units a manager can handle, and who authorizes an exception to that cap?
5.  **Regarding continuity:** If the assigned property manager leaves the company or goes on extended leave during the onboarding process, what is the handover protocol to ensure the landlord and inspection teams are not left without a point of contact?
6.  **Regarding the introductory meeting:** Is the introductory call between the manager and landlord mandatory for all properties, or are there scenarios where this step is bypassed based on the landlord’s existing relationship with the company?

### **Phase 3: Inspection, Repairs, & Marketing**
7.  **Regarding repair authority:** When the inspector identifies issues, the manager works with the landlord to address them. Who has the final authority to approve repair costs, and is there a financial threshold that requires additional sign-off from a regional director or finance team?
8.  **Regarding pricing strategy:** You mentioned the marketing team updates internal pricing models based on market trends. How is the final rental price agreed upon? Is it a collaborative decision between the property manager and the landlord, or does the company have a set margin they must maintain?
9.  **Regarding marketing activation:** Is the property listing published to the public portal immediately after the inspection passes, or is there a "soft launch" period where the listing is visible only to internal agents for quality assurance?

### **Phase 4: Tenant Screening & Lease Finalization**
10. **Regarding tie-breakers:** In scenarios where multiple tenants are equally qualified, what specific criteria does the property manager use to prioritize one applicant over another? Is it strictly first-come-first-served, or do factors like credit score or lease length take precedence?
11. **Regarding lease negotiations:** You mentioned the possibility of offering promotional discounts. What is the approval hierarchy for authorizing these discounts? Does a manager have autonomy, or does it require regional approval?
12. **Regarding security deposits:** Once the deposit is collected, is there a specific timeline for when it must be transferred to a holding account or escrow to ensure regulatory compliance?

### **Phase 5: Audits, Compliance, & Exceptions**
13. **Regarding audit timing:** The description mentions compliance and financial audits happen toward the end. Do these audits occur *before* the lease is signed, or do they run concurrently? If a discrepancy is found during the audit after the lease is signed, what is the remediation process?
14. **Regarding custom clauses:** You mentioned landlords may insist on custom lease clauses. How are these exceptions tracked to ensure they don't conflict with standard company policies or fair housing regulations?
15. **Regarding communication tracking:** Since communication flows through email, CRMs, and project management tools, how do you ensure that a critical decision made in an email thread is accurately reflected in the official project record within the CRM?

### **Phase 6: Process Flow & Metrics**
16. **Regarding process blockers:** What are the most common reasons a property gets stuck in the "onboarding" status for an extended period? Is it usually due to landlord delays, inspection failures, or internal resource constraints?
17. **Regarding success metrics:** How does the company measure the success of this onboarding process? Are you tracking time-to-revenue (how long it takes from initial contact to first rent collection), or are there other KPIs used to evaluate the efficiency of this workflow?
