**1.  What the “standard” HiretoRetire workflow looks like**

| Typical step | Normal ordering (precedence) |
|--------------|------------------------------|
| Post_Job_Ad  | First activity               |
| Screen_Candidates | After posting, before any interview |
| Conduct_Interviews | After screening, before the hiring decision |
| Make_Hiring_Decision | After the interview(s) |
| Onboard_Employee | After the decision (only once) |
| Add_to_Payroll | After onboarding (once) |
| Close_Case | Final step, after payroll |

In a normative model the flow is essentially a **linear chain** with only one parallelism point (e.g., several interviews can run concurrently, but they are all completed before the decision).  

---

**2.  Modelbymodel analysis**

### Model1  

```text
Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close
          
            Interview
```

| Observation | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview` are both present. | Screening must happen first; both interview and decision are allowed after screening. |
| No edge *Interview  Decide*. | The decision **may be taken before the interview is finished** (or even without any interview at all). |
| No edge *Interview  Onboard* (or *Interview  Payroll*). | If the decision is taken first, the interview can be completely ignored for the rest of the process. |
| No loops, no silent transitions. | The process is forced to execute each activity exactly once. |
| All “core” activities (Post, Screen, Interview, Decide, Onboard, Payroll, Close) are present. | No activity is omitted. |

**Anomalies**

1. **Decision before interview** – violates the logical requirement that a hiring decision be based on interview outcomes.  
2. **Parallel decision / interview** – technically allowed by the partial order, but it creates a *risk* that the decision is made without interview evidence.  
3. **No repetition** – while not a strict error, real hiring often involves rescreening or additional interview rounds; the model cannot express that.

*Severity*: The most serious anomaly is the **ordering violation** (decision before interview). The other issues are relatively mild (lack of loops, parallelism that could be avoided).

---

### Model2  

```text
Post  Screen
Post  Interview  Decide  Loop(Onboard/skip)  XOR(Payroll/skip)  Close
```

| Observation | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| `Post  Screen` **and** `Post  Interview` are both present, but **no edge from Screen to Decide**. | The decision can be taken **without any screening** having been performed. |
| `Interview  Decide` is the only path that leads to the decision. | The interview is the *only* prerequisite for the decision; screening is optional. |
| `Loop(Onboard, skip)` – the onboarding step can be repeated arbitrarily or completely skipped. | This allows **multiple onboarding cycles** or **no onboarding at all**, which is not a normal business rule. |
| `XOR(Payroll, skip)` – payroll can be performed **once** or **skipped**. | Skipping payroll is a serious breach of the “hiretopay” part of the process. |
| No explicit edge from `Decide` to `Onboard` (the loop is the only successor). | If the loop is skipped, the process jumps straight to the XORpayroll node, potentially bypassing onboarding. |
| Silent transitions are used to model “skip”. | The model deliberately encodes *optional* execution of critical activities. |

**Anomalies**

1. **Screening optional** – violates the normative requirement that candidates be screened before interviewing.  
2. **Payroll optional** – a hiring process that does not add the employee to payroll is fundamentally incomplete.  
3. **Onboarding loop / optional** – allows repeated onboarding or none at all; the process should enforce a single onboarding after the decision.  
4. **Decision depends only on interview** – while acceptable, the lack of a required screening step makes the decision logic weak.  
5. **Parallel Post  Screen / Post  Interview** – both can start simultaneously, which is unusual because you normally screen before scheduling interviews.

*Severity*: The anomalies are **more fundamental**. Skipping payroll or onboarding breaks the *essence* of “HiretoRetire”. Making screening optional undermines the quality gate of the process. The loop construct introduces uncontrolled repetition, which is rarely desired in a hiring context.

---

**3.  Which model is closer to a normative HiretoRetire process?**

**Model1** aligns better with the standard workflow.

*Why?*

| Criterion | Model1 | Model2 |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **All core activities present** | Yes (Post, Screen, Interview, Decide, Onboard, Payroll, Close) | Yes, but payroll and onboarding can be skipped |
| **Logical ordering** | Only one ordering violation (decision before interview) | Multiple violations (screening optional, payroll optional, onboarding optional) |
| **Use of loops / skips** | None – each activity executed exactly once | Loops and XORskip introduce optionality for critical steps |
| **Severity of anomalies** | Moderate (ordering only) | Severe (critical activities may be omitted) |
| **Ease of correction** | Adding an edge `Interview  Decide` would restore the proper order | Requires removing loops, removing XORskip, and adding missing precedence edges – a much larger redesign |

Even though Model1 permits the decision to be taken before the interview, the **core sequence of activities is still enforced** (post  screen  …  close) and **no essential step can be omitted**. Model2, by contrast, deliberately allows the process to bypass screening, onboarding, or payroll, which would render the “HiretoRetire” lifecycle incomplete and noncompliant with typical HR policies.

**Conclusion:**  
- **Model1** is the more normative representation, albeit with a single ordering anomaly that can be fixed by adding the edge `Interview  Decide`.  
- **Model2** contains several highimpact deviations (optional screening, optional payroll, looping onboarding) that fundamentally compromise the integrity of the hiring process.  

Therefore, Model1 should be preferred as the baseline for a normative HiretoRetire workflow.
