## Analysis of Each Case

**Reference flows derived from the normative description:**
- **Sufficient inventory:** Receive  Check  Authorize Payment  Package  Ship  Close
- **Insufficient inventory:** Receive  Check  Cancel  Close

---

###  Case 1 — Compliant
Sequence: Receive  Check  Authorize  Package  Ship  Close.
This matches the standard "sufficient inventory" flow exactly. No violations.

---

###  Case 2 — Violation
Sequence: Receive  Check  **Package  Authorize**  Ship  Close.

**Violation:** "Package items" occurs *before* "Authorize payment."
The norm explicitly states: *"Package items should only occur after authorization and before shipping."* Here packaging happens before payment is authorized, reversing the required order. This is exactly the cited example of "packaging items before authorization."

---

###  Case 3 — Multiple Violations
Sequence: Receive  Check  Authorize  **Cancel  Ship**  Close.

**Violations:**
1. **Ship after cancellation:** The norm states *"Ship order should occur... never after a cancellation."* Once an order is canceled, fulfillment activities must stop. Shipping a canceled order is the explicitly forbidden case of "shipping after cancellation."
2. **Cancellation after authorization in a fulfillment path:** A cancellation should follow directly from an insufficient-inventory check (Check  Cancel). Here payment was authorized first (implying inventory was sufficient), then the order was canceled — mixing the two mutually exclusive branches.
3. **Shipping without packaging:** "Package items" never occurred, yet the order was shipped, violating *"Ship order should only occur after packaging."*

---

###  Case 4 — Compliant
Sequence: Receive  Check  Cancel  Close.
This matches the "insufficient inventory" branch perfectly. No violations.

---

###  Case 5 — Violation
Sequence: Receive  Check  Authorize  **Ship**  Close (no Package step).

**Violation:** "Ship order" occurs without a preceding "Package items" activity. The norm requires: *"Only after items have been properly packaged is it appropriate to proceed with Ship order"* and *"Ship order should only occur after packaging."* The mandatory packaging step is missing.

---

## Summary

| Case | Status | Violation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 |  Compliant | — |
| 2 |  Violation | Packaging before payment authorization |
| 3 |  Violation | Shipping after cancellation; cancel mixed with fulfillment path; shipping without packaging |
| 4 |  Compliant | — |
| 5 |  Violation | Shipping without packaging (missing step) |

**Violating cases: 2, 3, and 5.**

The core principle violated across these is the mandatory ordering of fulfillment activities (Authorize  Package  Ship) and the rule that shipping must never follow a cancellation. Cases 1 and 4 correctly represent the two legitimate process variants.
