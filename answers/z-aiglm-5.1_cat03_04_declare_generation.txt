### Explanation of the Constraints:
- **Init / Existence:** Every product idea strictly begins at Idea Generation (`IG`), and the lifecycle guarantees that standard steps exist.
- **Exactly One:** To avoid redundancies in the process tracking, an idea is generated exactly once (`IG`), approved once (`AG`), and launched exactly one time (`FL`).
- **Precedence / Response / Succession:** These map the natural forward progression of the product lifecycle (e.g. `IG` precedes `DD`, `AG` is followed by `MP`).
- **Chain Precedence / Chain Response:** Denotes tightly coupled sequential steps. For example, user testing `UT` immediately follows lab testing `LT`, and marketing `MP` immediately follows approval `AG`.
- **Non-Succession / Non-Chain-Succession:** Prevents backward stepping or illogical skipping. For instance, after an Approval Gate `AG`, you cannot revert to an earlier Design Draft `DD`. Similarly, Technical Feasibility `TFC` cannot be *immediately* followed by Prototype Creation `PC` because Cost Evaluation `CE` must happen in between.
