### **Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling Process

### **1. Pools & Lanes (Roles/Participants)**

### **2. Data Objects / Artifacts**
*   **Incoming Invoice:** Received via email (Input)
*   **Purchase Order (PO):** Referenced in system (Input)
*   **Logged Invoice:** Invoice logged into the accounting system (Data Object)
*   **Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation:** Email from Purchasing (Data Object)
*   **System Approval Notification:** Auto-generated email with invoice, PO, and confirmation details (Data Object)
*   **Retrospective PO:** Created for informal services (Data Object, Exception path)

---

### **3. Process Flow (Main Path & Exceptions)**

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (via Email)

**1. Initial Validation (AP Clerk)**
*   `(Task)` **Check for valid PO number** [Lane: AP Clerk]
*   `[XOR Gateway 1: Valid PO Present?]`
    *   `{No}` -> `(Task)` **Request details/PO from Supplier** [Lane: AP Clerk] -> `(Message End Event)` **Invoice Returned to Supplier** *(Note: Process ends here, assuming a new invoice will be submitted).*
    *   `{Yes}` -> `(Task)` **Log Invoice into Accounting System** [Lane: AP Clerk] *(Produces Data Object: Logged Invoice)*

**2. Purchasing Verification**
*   `(Task)` **Forward Invoice to Purchasing** [Lane: AP Clerk]
*   `(Task)` **Verify Invoice against PO** [Lane: Purchasing Officer] *(Checking quantities, prices, delivery charges)*
*   `[XOR Gateway 2: Details Match?]`
    *   `{No}` -> `(Task)` **Hold Invoice & Investigate Discrepancy** [Lane: Purchasing Officer]
        *   `[XOR Gateway 2a: Discrepancy Resolution Path]`
            *   `{Option A: Contact Supplier directly}` -> `(Task)` **Request Corrected Invoice** [Lane: Purchasing Officer] -> *(Loops back to Task 2.2)*
            *   `{Option B: Loop in SRM}` -> `(Task)` **Check Contract/Contact Supplier** [Lane: SRM] -> `(Task)` **Provide Correct Price/PO Info to AP or Purchasing** [Lane: SRM] -> *(Loops back to Task 2.2)*
    *   `{Yes}` -> `(Task)` **Send Confirmation Email to AP** [Lane: Purchasing Officer] *(Produces Data Object: Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation)*

**3. Three-Way Matching (AP Clerk)**
*   `(Task)` **Match Invoice Line Items against PO & Confirmation** [Lane: AP Clerk]
*   `[XOR Gateway 3: System Match Successful?]`
    *   `{No}` -> `(Task)` **Email Purchasing to Clarify** [Lane: AP Clerk]
        *   `[XOR Gateway 3a: Easily Resolved?]`
            *   `{Yes}` -> *(Loops back to Task 3.1)*
            *   `{No}` -> **(Flow to Exception Handling Sub-Process - See Section 4 below)**
    *   `{Yes}` -> *(Proceed to Step 4)*

**4. Manager Approval**
*   `(Task)` **Trigger Manager Approval Request** [Lane: System] *(System sends automatic email notification with invoice, PO, and confirmation attached)*
*   `(Task)` **Review Invoice Details in System** [Lane: Department Manager]
*   `[XOR Gateway 4: Invoice Approved?]`
    *   `{No (Suspicious/Budget Mismatch)}` -> `(Task)` **Reject or Request Hold & Investigation** [Lane: Department Manager] -> *(Flows back to Task 3.1 for AP Clerk to investigate)*
    *   `{Yes}` -> `(Task)` **Approve Invoice in System** [Lane: Department Manager]

**5. Payment Scheduling**
*   `(Task)` **Schedule Invoice for Payment Run** [Lane: AP Clerk]

**End Event:** Payment Scheduled

*   `(Task)` **Coordinate Resolution** [Lane: AP Supervisor]

### **5. Noted Ambiguities & Conflicting Accounts**
*The following observations should be considered when finalizing the diagram, as the interviewees provided slightly conflicting views on certain steps:*

1.  **Duplicate Verification (Mary vs. Dan):**
    *   *Mary (AP)* states that after receiving confirmation from Dan, *she* matches the invoice line items against the PO. If there's a discrepancy, she emails Purchasing.
    *   *Dan (Purchasing)* states that *he* compares quantities and prices when the invoice first hits his inbox, and if something is off, he contacts the supplier or asks AP to hold it.
    *   *Resolution:* The model reflects a two-stage check to satisfy both accounts: Dan checks upon receipt (Step 2), and Mary does a formal system match after receiving his confirmation (Step 3). In practice, this may be redundant, but it accurately reflects the described behavior.
2.  **Missing PO Routing (Mary vs. Dan):**
    *   *Mary* explicitly handles missing POs at the very beginning of the process, returning them to the supplier before logging them.
    *   *Dan* mentions that "if the PO number is missing, I tell Mary she needs to get that information."
    *   *Resolution:* The model follows Mary's account as the standard sequence, treating Dan's comment as a potential variation (e.g., a PO number is present but invalid/incorrect, prompting Dan to reject it back to Mary).
3.  **SRM Involvement Trigger:**
    *   *Karen (SRM)* notes she is looped in by either AP or Purchasing for missing POs or pricing discrepancies.
    *   *Resolution:* The model places Karen's involvement primarily in the Purchasing verification phase (Step 2) and as an optional consult for the AP Supervisor during escalations (Step 4), covering both possibilities.
