### 2. Attribute Analysis & Root Causes

By analyzing the attributes (Resource, Region, Complexity), we can deduce the following root causes:

#### A. Complexity and the "Rework Loop"
There is a direct correlation between **High Complexity** and the frequency of the **"Request Additional Documents"** activity.
*   **Low Complexity:** 0 requests.
*   **Medium Complexity:** 1 request.
*   **High Complexity:** 2–3 requests.
*   **Root Cause:** The "Submit Claim" step is likely not capturing enough detail for complex claims. This forces Adjusters into a "ping-pong" communication style with claimants, where information is gathered piecemeal rather than all at once.

#### B. Managerial Approval Bottleneck (Manager_Bill)
*   **Manager_Ann** (Region A & B) handles Low and Medium claims. Her approval usually happens within 30–60 minutes of the previous step.
*   **Manager_Bill** (Region A & B) handles High complexity claims.
    *   In Case 2003: There is a **23-hour gap** between the last document request and Bill’s approval.
    *   In Case 2005: There is a **19-hour gap** between the last document request and Bill's approval.
*   **Root Cause:** Manager_Bill appears to be a bottleneck. This could be due to a high workload, a specific once-a-day review schedule for complex claims, or the fact that complex claims require a much deeper (and thus slower) manual audit.

#### C. Regional and Resource Variance
*   **Case 2003 (Region A / Adjuster_Mike):** 2 requests for documents.
*   **Case 2005 (Region B / Adjuster_Lisa):** 3 requests for documents.
*   **Root Cause:** While both are high complexity, Region B (or Adjuster_Lisa) requires more iterations to finalize a claim. This suggests either a lack of standardized documentation checklists in Region B or that high-complexity claims in Region B are fundamentally more difficult than those in Region A.

### 3. Proposed Explanations & Mitigations

#### Explanation 1: Information Gap at Intake
High-complexity claims likely involve specialized forms or proof of loss that the standard "Submit Claim" process does not demand upfront.
*   **Suggestion:** Implement **Dynamic Intake Forms**. If a customer selects a category that triggers "High Complexity," the system should automatically require the additional documents that Adjusters Mike and Lisa are currently requesting manually. This "shifts left" the effort and reduces the document request loops.

#### Explanation 2: Expert Resource Overload
Manager_Bill is only seen handling high-complexity cases. These cases likely require specialized knowledge, and Bill may be the only person qualified to sign off on them.
*   **Suggestion:** **Resource Leveling.** Train "Manager_Ann" or another supervisor on high-complexity protocols to share the load. Alternatively, introduce a "Pre-Approval" checklist for Adjusters to ensure that when the claim reaches Bill, it is "ready to sign," reducing his review time.

#### Explanation 3: Communication Inefficiency
The time between "Request Additional Documents" and the next step suggests the process is waiting on the customer.
*   **Suggestion:** **Automated Follow-ups and Portals.** Instead of manual emails/calls, use a customer portal where claimants can see exactly which documents are missing. For Case 2005, the three separate requests suggest the adjuster kept finding new things missing. A **standardized checklist** for Adjusters would ensure they request all missing items in one single request rather than three separate ones.
