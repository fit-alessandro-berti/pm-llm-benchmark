# BPMN-Like Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## 1. Process Overview
This model describes the end-to-end invoice approval workflow, from invoice receipt via email through payment scheduling. The process involves five roles across multiple departments, with several decision points and exception-handling paths.

---

## 2. Roles / Participants (Lanes)
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)** Ś Primary process owner; logs and routes invoices.
- **Purchasing Officer (Dan)** Ś Verifies receipt of goods/services and matches invoice to PO.
- **Department Manager (Carlos)** Ś Provides final approval.
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)** Ś Handles exceptions and escalations.
- **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** Ś Liaises with suppliers to resolve issues.
- **Supplier** (external participant) Ś Source of invoice; responds to clarification requests.
- **Accounting System** (system participant) Ś Logs invoices, routes notifications, schedules payments.

---

## 3. Artifacts / Data Objects
- **Invoice** (email / PDF)
- **Purchase Order (PO)**
- **Goods Receipt Confirmation**
- **Manager Approval Record**
- **Payment Schedule Entry**
- **Retrospective PO** (for informal/contracted services Ś exception artifact)

---

## 4. Process Flow (Sequence)

### 4.1 Start Event
- **Trigger:** Invoice received by email (in AP Clerk's inbox).

### 4.2 Main Flow

**Task 1 Ś Check PO Reference on Invoice** *(AP Clerk)*  
- Input: Incoming invoice.

**Gateway 1 (XOR) Ś Does the invoice have a valid PO number?**
- **No ** Go to *Task 1a Ś Request PO/Details from Supplier*
- **Yes ** Go to *Task 2 Ś Log Invoice in Accounting System*

**Task 1a Ś Request Missing Info from Supplier** *(AP Clerk, optionally supported by Supplier Relationship Manager)*  
- Output: Clarification request sent to supplier.  
- After supplier responds with PO or revised invoice, process loops back to **Task 1**.  
- *Exception branch:* If no PO can be obtained (e.g., informal service), escalate to **Rita (AP Supervisor)**  see Section 5.

**Task 2 Ś Log Invoice in Accounting System** *(AP Clerk)*  
- Output: Invoice record created.

**Task 3 Ś Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation** *(AP Clerk  Purchasing Officer)*

**Task 4 Ś Verify Receipt & Match Invoice to PO** *(Purchasing Officer)*  
- Compares quantities, prices, delivery charges against PO.

**Gateway 2 (XOR) Ś Does the invoice match the PO?**
- **Yes (match confirmed) ** Go to *Task 5*
- **No (discrepancy detected) ** Go to *Task 4a Ś Resolve Discrepancy*

**Task 4a Ś Resolve Discrepancy** *(Purchasing Officer and/or AP Clerk)*  
Possible sub-actions (parallel or alternative):
  - Contact supplier directly for corrected invoice, OR
  - Ask AP Clerk to hold invoice pending supplier correction, OR
  - Loop in **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** to verify contract pricing or obtain revised invoice.

**Gateway 2a (XOR) Ś Discrepancy resolved?**
- **Yes ** Return to *Task 4* (re-verify)  *Task 5*
- **No / unresolvable ** Escalate to **AP Supervisor (Rita)**  see Section 5.

**Task 5 Ś Send PO-Match Confirmation to AP** *(Purchasing Officer  AP Clerk)*  
- Output: Confirmation email logged in system.

**Task 6 Ś System Notifies Department Manager** *(Accounting System, automated)*  
- Trigger: Both receipt confirmed AND invoice-PO match confirmed.  
- System auto-sends approval request email to Carlos.

**Task 7 Ś Manager Reviews Invoice** *(Department Manager)*  
- Reviews invoice, PO, confirmation, notes.

**Gateway 3 (XOR) Ś Manager's Decision**
- **Approve ** Go to *Task 8*
- **Reject / Request Investigation ** Return to **AP Clerk** to hold and investigate (may trigger escalation to Rita). Process re-enters at an appropriate earlier task depending on the issue (e.g., back to Task 4a).
  - *Note:* Carlos reports this happens roughly once every 1¢2 months.

**Task 8 Ś Schedule Invoice for Payment** *(AP Clerk)*  
- Output: Invoice added to next payment cycle.

### 4.3 End Event
- **Invoice scheduled for payment** Ś process complete.

---

## 5. Exception Handling Sub-Process Ś "Exception Resolution" (Rita's Lane)

**Trigger events for escalation:**
- Unresolved PO mismatch / discrepancy (from Task 4a).
- Invoice with no PO that cannot be obtained (e.g., informal/contracted services).
- Manager-initiated hold for investigation.

**Exception Task E1 Ś Assess Exception** *(AP Supervisor)*

**Gateway E1 (XOR) Ś Type of exception?**
- **Missing/invalid PO for informal service ** *Task E2a*
- **Unresolved discrepancy ** *Task E2b*

**Task E2a Ś Coordinate with Department Head to Create Retrospective PO** *(AP Supervisor + Department Head)*
- **Sub-decision:** Should the invoice be accepted?
  - Yes  Create retrospective PO  rejoin main flow at **Task 2 or Task 4**.
  - No  Reject invoice  **End Event: Invoice Rejected**.

**Task E2b Ś Coordinate Between Supplier, Purchasing, and Karen** *(AP Supervisor + Supplier Relationship Manager)*
- Resolve pricing or quantity issue via contract review or supplier negotiation.
- Once resolved  rejoin main flow at **Task 4** (re-verify match).

---

## 6. Supplier Interaction Sub-Process (Karen's Lane)

**Triggered from:** Task 1a, Task 4a, or Exception Task E2b.

- **Task K1 Ś Contact Supplier** *(Supplier Relationship Manager)*
- **Task K2 Ś Obtain missing PO / revised invoice / contract price verification**
- **Task K3 Ś Relay correct information back to AP or Purchasing**
- No direct system-approval action; re-enters the main flow via the requesting party.

---

## 7. Gateways Summary

| Gateway | Location | Type | Branches |
|---|---|---|---|
| G1 | After invoice receipt | XOR | PO present / PO missing |
| G2 | After receipt & match check | XOR | Match / Discrepancy |
| G2a | After discrepancy resolution attempt | XOR | Resolved / Unresolved ( escalate) |
| G3 | Manager review | XOR | Approve / Reject or Hold |
| GE1 | Exception entry | XOR | No-PO case / Discrepancy case |

---

## 8. Noted Ambiguities and Uncertainties

1. **Task 3 / Task 4 ordering:** Mary describes forwarding to Purchasing for *receipt confirmation* first, then matching line items herself. Dan describes Purchasing performing the match. It is unclear whether matching is done by AP Clerk, Purchasing, or both. The model assumes Purchasing performs the authoritative match (per Dan), with AP Clerk's matching as a preliminary check.
2. **Who resolves discrepancies first:** Both Dan and Mary describe initiating discrepancy resolution Ś the interviews do not specify a strict rule. Modeled as a joint/either task.
3. **Manager rejection path destination:** Carlos says rejection leads to Mary "holding and investigating," but the exact re-entry point in the flow is not explicit.
4. **Karen's involvement trigger:** Karen can be pulled in at multiple points (missing PO, pricing issues). The model represents this as a reusable sub-process callable from several tasks.
5. **Payment execution:** The process ends at *scheduling* for payment; actual payment execution (payment run) is mentioned but not described in detail and is therefore out of scope for this model.

## 9. Textual BPMN Summary (for direct diagram conversion)

```
[Start: Invoice Received]
    (AP Clerk) Check PO Reference
    <XOR G1: PO valid?>
        -- No   (AP Clerk, Karen) Request PO from Supplier  loop to Check PO
                 (if unresolved  Escalate to Rita  Exception Subprocess)
        -- Yes  (AP Clerk) Log Invoice in System
                  (AP Clerk) Forward to Purchasing
                  (Purchasing) Verify Receipt & Match to PO
                  <XOR G2: Match?>
                     -- No   (Purchasing/AP/Karen) Resolve Discrepancy
                              <XOR G2a: Resolved?>
                                 -- Yes  back to Verify Receipt & Match
                                 -- No   Escalate to Rita  Exception Subprocess
                     -- Yes  (Purchasing) Send Confirmation to AP
                              (System) Notify Manager
                              (Manager) Review Invoice
                              <XOR G3: Approve?>
                                 -- Reject/Hold  (AP Clerk) Investigate  possible Rita escalation
                                 -- Approve     (AP Clerk) Schedule Payment
                                               [End: Invoice Scheduled]

[Exception Subprocess Ś Rita]
    Assess Exception
    <XOR GE1>
        -- No-PO / informal service  Coordinate Retrospective PO
               Accept  rejoin main flow
               Reject  [End: Invoice Rejected]
        -- Unresolved discrepancy    Coordinate with Supplier/Karen  rejoin main flow
```

This structured description provides sufficient detail for a BPMN tool (e.g., Camunda Modeler, Signavio, bpmn.io) to render swimlanes, gateways, tasks, and exception subprocesses without further interpretation.
