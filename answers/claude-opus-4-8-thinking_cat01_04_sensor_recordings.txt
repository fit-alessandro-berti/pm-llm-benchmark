# Process Mining Analysis: Manufacturing Assembly Line

## Methodology

Since no explicit labels exist, I clustered events by looking at **co-occurring sensor signatures**. The key insight is that each manufacturing activity has a characteristic "fingerprint" across the six sensors. I tracked **transitions** (sudden jumps/drops) to mark phase boundaries, then matched each cluster's signature to a plausible physical operation.

---

## Identified Phases & Labels

### Phase A — **Idle / Standby (Startup)**
**Events 1–3**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 20–21 °C (ambient) |
| Vibration | 0 Hz |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 0 mm (home) |
| Energy | ~0.4–0.5 kWh (baseline draw) |

**Rationale:** Everything sits at ambient/zero. The tool is at home position, no material moving, and energy reflects only "machine powered but idle." This is the line waiting to start a cycle.

---

### Phase B — **Cutting Metal**
**Events 4–6**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 50  58 °C (rising) |
| Vibration | 30  38 Hz (high) |
| Material Flow | 5 units/s (high) |
| Tool Position | 5  10 mm (advancing) |
| Energy | 2.0  3.0 kWh (climbing) |

**Rationale:** The hallmark of cutting is **high vibration + high material flow + rising temperature**, all simultaneously. Friction from the blade against stock generates heat and vibration, while raw material feeds through steadily. The tool advances progressively (510 mm) as it cuts deeper.

---

### Phase C — **Assembling Parts**
**Events 7–8**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 40 °C (cooling, moderate) |
| Pressure | 2 bar (elevated, steady) |
| Vibration | 10 Hz (low) |
| Material Flow | 2 units/s (reduced) |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (fixed) |
| Energy | 2.0 kWh (stable) |

**Rationale:** The dead-stable readings across two consecutive events signal a controlled, mechanical operation. **Elevated but constant pressure** suggests parts being pressed/fitted together, while **low vibration** rules out machining. Temperature is dropping from the cutting heat. Stability is the key marker of assembly.

---

### Phase D — **Welding**
**Event 9**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 80 °C (peak — highest in log) |
| Vibration | 50 Hz (peak) |
| Material Flow | 0 (stopped) |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (fixed) |
| Energy | 5.0 kWh (peak — highest in log) |

**Rationale:** The **simultaneous peaks** in temperature, energy, and vibration—combined with **zero material flow**—are the unmistakable signature of welding. Heat fusion requires maximum localized energy and produces intense heat with no material throughput (the parts are stationary while joined). The fixed tool position confirms a single-point operation.

---

### Phase E — **Quality Inspection (Cool-down)**
**Event 10**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 30 °C (rapid drop) |
| Vibration | 1 Hz (near zero) |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 15 mm (held) |
| Energy | 1.0 kWh (low) |

**Rationale:** A sharp drop in all active parameters following the welding peak. The part is held in place (tool at 15 mm) while the machine cools and remains nearly idle—consistent with a **passive inspection/measurement step** where sensors verify the part without active processing.

---

### Phase F — **Packaging**
**Events 11–12**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 25 °C (near ambient) |
| Vibration | 5 Hz (gentle) |
| Material Flow | 1 unit/s (slow output) |
| Tool Position | 20 mm (max — ejection/transfer) |
| Energy | 1.5–1.6 kWh (modest) |

**Rationale:** **Low, gentle vibration with a small material flow** indicates light mechanical handling rather than processing. The tool reaching its **maximum extension (20 mm)** suggests an ejector/transfer arm pushing the finished part out for packaging. Temperature is back near ambient (part is finished and cool).

---

### Phase G — **Idle / Shutdown (Cycle Reset)**
**Events 13–15**

| Signature | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature | 20–21 °C (ambient) |
| Vibration | 0 Hz |
| Material Flow | 0 |
| Tool Position | 0 mm (returned home) |
| Energy | ~0.4–0.5 kWh (baseline) |

**Rationale:** Readings return to the exact baseline of Phase A, with the tool back at home (0 mm). This marks the **end of the production cycle**, ready to repeat.

---

## Discovered Process Model

```
[Idle/Standby]  [Cutting Metal]  [Assembling Parts]  [Welding]
                                                             
[Idle/Shutdown]  [Packaging]  [Quality Inspection] 
```

**Full sequence:** Idle  Cut  Assemble  Weld  Inspect  Package  Idle

---

## Summary Table

| Events | Activity | Strongest Indicators |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| 1–3 | Idle / Standby | All ambient/zero |
| 4–6 | Cutting Metal | High vibration + high flow + rising temp |
| 7–8 | Assembling Parts | Stable readings + elevated steady pressure |
| 9 | Welding | Peak temp + peak energy + zero flow |
| 10 | Quality Inspection | Rapid drop, part held, low energy |
| 11–12 | Packaging | Gentle vibration + max tool position (eject) |
| 13–15 | Idle / Shutdown | Return to baseline, tool home |

## Key Analytical Principles Used
- **Vibration + flow together**  active machining (cutting)
- **Energy/temperature peaks with no flow**  fusion process (welding)
- **Stability over time**  controlled assembly
- **Return to baseline**  idle/transition boundaries
- **Tool position trajectory** (0  advancing  max 20 mm  0) traced the part's physical journey through the cell, reinforcing the phase ordering.
