# Description of the Visualization

This is a **performance spectrum** (also known as a performance spectrum miner chart), a visualization technique used in **process mining** to show how individual cases flow through a sequence of process steps over time.

## Structure

**Three horizontal axes** (process activities), arranged top to bottom:
1. **Send Fine** (top)
2. **Insert Fine Notification** (middle)
3. **Send for Credit Collection** (bottom)

**Time axis (horizontal):** spans roughly 13 years, from **2000-01-25** to **2013-04-24**, with a midpoint marker at **2006-09-09**.

## What the Lines Represent

Each diagonal/vertical line is a single case (e.g., one traffic fine) moving from one activity to the next:

- The line's **starting x-position** is when the first activity occurred, and the **ending x-position** is when the next activity occurred.
- **Steep, nearly vertical lines** indicate short durations between steps; **strongly slanted lines** indicate long waiting times.

## Key Observations

- **Send Fine  Insert Fine Notification (upper segment):** Lines are mostly vertical and gray, indicating the notification typically follows the fine quickly and consistently.
- **Insert Fine Notification  Send for Credit Collection (lower segment):** Lines are heavily slanted to the right, forming repeating "fan" or sawtooth patterns. This suggests credit collection is performed in **periodic batches** — many cases accumulate and are sent for collection together at certain points in time.
- **Color coding (gray vs. red/pink):** likely encodes performance classes — e.g., red lines highlight cases with notably longer (or outlier) durations between the notification and credit collection steps.
- **Gaps** in the pattern (white vertical bands) indicate periods of inactivity or no new cases.

This dataset appears to be the well-known **Road Traffic Fine Management** event log commonly used in process mining research.
