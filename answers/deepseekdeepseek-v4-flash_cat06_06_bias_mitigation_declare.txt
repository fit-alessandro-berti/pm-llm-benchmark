## Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Coexistence (`ManualReview`, `FinalDecision`)**  
   *Why:* Guarantees that every trace containing a final decision also contains a manual review (and vice versa). This prevents a decision from being made without a human oversight or extra fairness check, reducing the chance of biased outcomes based on sensitive attributes.

2. **Response (`CheckApplicantRace`  `ManualReview`)**  
   *Why:* When a race check activity occurs (which might trigger bias), the process must eventually perform a manual review. This ensures that any potential bias introduced by the attribute check is reviewed before a final decision is reached.

3. **Precedence (`ManualReview`  `FinalDecision`)**  
   *Why:* Forces the manual review to happen *before* the final decision is made. Combined with the coexistence constraint, this enforces that a manual review is not only present but also temporally precedes the decision, preventing biased snap judgments.

4. **Nonsuccession (`CheckApplicantRace`  `FinalDecision`)**  
   *Why:* Explicitly prohibits a direct succession from a sensitiveattribute check to a final decision. This eliminates the possibility of an immediate, unchecked decision after learning a sensitive attribute, which would be a clear avenue for discrimination.

Together, these constraints embed fairness checks into the process model. They ensure that whenever sensitive information is accessed, a manual review is mandatory, and that a final decision cannot be made either without that review or immediately after the sensitive check. This reduces the risk of biased decisionmaking in the loan application pipeline.
