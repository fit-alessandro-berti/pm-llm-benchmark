### **1. Out-of-Order Activity Sequences**
**Observations:**

  - *Sequence*: Register  Confirm Shipment  Ship Goods  Credit Check  Validate Stock  ...
- **Case 1003**: `Ship Goods` occurs **before** `Validate Stock` and `Confirm Shipment`.
  - *Sequence*: Register  Credit Check  Ship Goods  Issue Invoice  Confirm Shipment  ...
- **Case 1004**: `Receive Payment` occurs **before** `Issue Invoice`, `Confirm Shipment`, and `Ship Goods`.
  - *Sequence*: Register  Receive Payment  Issue Invoice  Confirm Shipment  Ship Goods.

**Hypotheses:**
- **Policy Violations**: Sales/Logistics teams are **bypassing mandatory checks** (e.g., credit/stock validation) to expedite orders, especially for **priority orders** (Case 1002 is `priority`).
- **System Workarounds**: The system allows **out-of-sequence actions** (e.g., Logistics can confirm shipments before Finance approves credit).
- **Training Gaps**: Employees may not understand **dependencies** (e.g., `Ship Goods` requires `Confirm Shipment` first).
- **Fraud Risk (Case 1004)**: Early payment receipt could indicate **fake transactions** to inflate revenue metrics.

### **2. Missing Critical Steps**

- **Case 1004**: Missing **both** `Perform Credit Check` and `Validate Stock`.

**Hypotheses:**

- **Manual Overrides**: Employees may **skip steps** due to time pressure or lack of oversight.
- **Data Entry Errors**: Steps may have been **omitted during logging** (but assume data is accurate per prompt).

**Hypotheses:**

## ** Proposed SQL Queries for Investigation**

### ** 1. Detect All Cases with Out-of-Order Activities**
**Purpose**: Find cases where activities occur **before their dependencies** (e.g., `Ship Goods` before `Confirm Shipment`).
**Hypothesis Test**: Validates if employees are **skipping steps** or the system lacks **enforcement**.

```sql
WITH activity_sequence AS (
  SELECT 'Register Order'         AS activity, 1 AS seq
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Perform Credit Check', 2
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Validate Stock',       3
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Confirm Shipment',      4
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Ship Goods',            5
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Issue Invoice',         6
  UNION ALL SELECT 'Receive Payment',      7
),
events_with_seq AS (
  SELECT
    oel.case_id,
    oel.activity,
    asq.seq,
    oel.timestamp
  FROM order_event_log oel
  JOIN activity_sequence asq ON oel.activity = asq.activity
),
ordered_events AS (
  SELECT
    case_id,
    activity,
    seq,
    timestamp,
    LAG(seq) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS prev_seq
  FROM events_with_seq
)
SELECT DISTINCT case_id
FROM ordered_events
WHERE prev_seq IS NOT NULL AND seq <= prev_seq
ORDER BY case_id;
```
**Expected Output**: `1002, 1003, 1004`
**Interpretation**: These cases have **at least one activity out of sequence**.
