Low vibration and energy, combined with controlled tool motion, are consistent with **Quality Inspection** or **Final Adjustment**, where the part is verified or fine-tuned before completion.

---

### Summary of Inferred Process Flow:

1. **Idle / Standby**  
2. **Material Feeding / Initial Setup**  
3. **Assembling Parts**  
4. **Welding**  
5. **Quality Inspection / Final Adjustment**  
6. **Return to Idle**

This sequence reflects a logical manufacturing workflow: prepare material, assemble components, join them (weld), inspect the result, and reset.

By mapping sensor patterns—especially spikes in temperature/energy (welding), steady flow with motion (feeding), stable low-vibration states (assembly), and cooldown with minor adjustments (inspection)—we can meaningfully reconstruct the underlying process activities from unlabeled numerical data, fulfilling the goals of process mining.
