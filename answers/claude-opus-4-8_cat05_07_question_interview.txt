# Clarifying Questions for the Property Onboarding Process

## Initial Intake & Documentation

1. When a landlord first contacts the regional office, are there any pre-qualification criteria that determine whether the company will even consider onboarding their property? Or do you accept all inquiries?
2. How does the property coordinator decide which documents are "outdated"? Is there a defined shelf-life or standard for each document type?
3. When documents are missing or outdated, how long does the company typically wait for the landlord to provide updates before pausing or canceling the onboarding? Is there a formal follow-up cadence?
4. Who is responsible for the accuracy of the data entered into the central property database? Is there a verification or double-entry step?

## Property Manager Assignment

5. You mentioned several factors influence manager assignment (workload, geography, experience, track record). How are these factors weighted when they conflict—for example, when the most experienced manager for a property type is already overloaded?
6. Who actually makes the assignment decision—is it automated, made by a supervisor, or negotiated among managers?
7. What happens if no available manager has the right specialization for a unique property type (e.g., student housing in a new city)?
8. Is there a backup or escalation path if the assigned manager becomes unavailable mid-process?

## Landlord Alignment Meeting

9. Are the outcomes of the introductory call/meeting formally documented? If so, where, and who is accountable for capturing special conditions?
10. What happens if the landlord and property manager cannot agree on key policies (e.g., pet policy or maintenance responsibilities) during this meeting?

## Inspection & Photography

11. Are the photographer and inspector scheduled in parallel or sequentially? Does one need to precede the other?
12. Who pays for repairs when the inspector finds issues—the landlord, the company, or is it negotiated case by case?
13. Is there a maximum number of re-inspections, or a threshold of issues, beyond which the company would decline to onboard the property?
14. Roughly how long does the inspection-to-passing phase typically take, and what is the longest you've seen it stretch?

## Marketing & Activation

15. Who has final approval over the listing price—the property manager, the marketing team, or the financial analyst? What happens if their pricing recommendations differ?
16. Can a property be "activated" before all marketing materials are complete, or must everything be finalized first?
17. How current are the "market trends" used in the pricing models—are they updated in real time, monthly, or otherwise?

## Tenant Screening & Selection

18. What are the specific disqualifying criteria during tenant screening (e.g., credit score thresholds, employment requirements)?
19. When multiple tenants apply, what criteria take priority—lease length, financial strength, application timing? Is this at the manager's discretion or governed by policy?
20. Who has authority to approve promotional discounts or negotiate non-standard lease terms, and are there limits on what a manager can offer independently?
21. How do you ensure fair housing compliance *during* tenant selection, rather than only in the post-selection audit?

## Quality Checks & Audits

22. Are the compliance, financial, and IT reviews conducted in parallel or in sequence? Do they happen before or after the tenant moves in?
23. What happens if the compliance officer or financial analyst flags a problem after the lease has already been signed and the deposit collected?
24. Is any of the final audit blocking (i.e., must pass before move-in), or are they retrospective checks?

## Exceptions & Edge Cases

25. For properties needing special insurance or custom lease clauses, who is brought in, and at what stage does this typically surface?
26. How are exceptions documented and tracked so that they don't get lost across email, CRM, and project management tools?
27. Can you walk me through the most common reasons an onboarding gets stalled or abandoned entirely?

## Roles, Timing & Communication

28. End to end, what's a typical timeline for onboarding a property, and which stages are the most common bottlenecks?
29. With communication spread across email, CRM, and project management tools, how do you maintain a single source of truth about a property's status?
30. Are there defined hand-off points or sign-offs between roles (coordinator  manager  leasing agent  audit team), or do responsibilities sometimes overlap?
31. Who "owns" the overall process end-to-end and is accountable if something falls through the cracks between teams?
