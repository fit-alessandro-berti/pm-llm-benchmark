## 3. Explanation of how these factors increase cycle time

### Pattern 1: Escalated tickets take longer
Escalation introduces handoff delays. When a Level-1 agent cannot resolve the issue, the ticket is transferred to Level-2. This often adds waiting time due to:

- Queueing for a specialized team
- Lack of immediate Level-2 availability
- Rework or repeated investigation
- Poor handoff information from Level-1 to Level-2
- Prioritization conflicts

This is most visible in **Case 105**, where the ticket waits **28 hours** after escalation before the next investigation.

---

### Pattern 2: Late-day investigation leads to next-day resolution
Cases investigated in the afternoon are not resolved until the next morning or later:

| Case ID | Investigation Time | Resolution Time |
|---|---:|---:|
| 102 | Mar 1, 14:00 | Mar 2, 09:00 |
| 104 | Mar 1, 13:00 | Mar 2, 08:00 |
| 105 | Mar 2, 14:00 | Mar 3, 09:00 |

This suggests that once investigation is delayed until early afternoon, resolution may slip to the next day. That creates a large overnight waiting period and sharply increases total cycle time.

---

### Pattern 3: Idle time before investigation is a major bottleneck
Cases that begin investigation quickly are resolved quickly:

| Case ID | Time from Receive to Investigation | Total Cycle Time |
|---|---:|---:|
| 103 | **35m** | **1h 20m** |
| 101 | **1h** | **2h 15m** |
| 104 | **4h 40m** | **24h 10m** |
| 102 | **5h 55m** | **25h 10m** |

Cases **101** and **103** move into investigation quickly and are closed the same morning. Cases **102** and **104** wait much longer before investigation, and their resolution is pushed into the next day.

## Recommendations and insights

### 1. Reduce queue time before investigation
The organization should focus on reducing the time between assignment and investigation, especially for cases like **102** and **104**.

Possible actions:
- Set a maximum allowed wait time after assignment.
- Use workload balancing across agents.
- Add alerting when tickets remain assigned but uninvestigated.
- Prioritize tickets received early in the day to prevent afternoon investigation and overnight delay.

---

### 2. Improve escalation handling
Escalated cases appear vulnerable to long delays, especially Case **105**.

Recommended improvements:
- Introduce an escalation SLA, e.g., Level-2 must review within 1 hour.
- Create a dedicated Level-2 triage queue.
- Ensure Level-1 agents provide complete investigation notes before escalation.
- Automatically notify Level-2 when escalation occurs.
- Track “Escalate to Level-2” to “Level-2 Investigation” time as a key metric.

---

### 3. Address overnight resolution delays
The repeated **19-hour gap** between investigation and resolution suggests a structural issue.

Possible actions:
- Add end-of-day resolution reviews.
- Ensure investigations completed by early afternoon are acted on before day end.
- Provide limited after-hours support for high-priority tickets.
- Separate “waiting for external input” from internal processing time in the log.
- Add more granular activities, such as “Waiting for Customer,” “Waiting for Vendor,” or “Pending Approval,” to better explain idle periods.

---

### 4. Monitor process performance using key metrics

Useful metrics include:

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Receive to Triage time | Measures initial responsiveness |
| Triage to Assignment time | Measures routing speed |
| Assignment to Investigation time | Detects agent queue delays |
| Escalation to Level-2 Investigation time | Detects escalation bottlenecks |
| Investigation to Resolution time | Detects technical or dependency delays |
| Total cycle time | Measures overall customer impact |

---

## Conclusion

The main delayed cases are **102, 104, and 105**, with **105** being the most severe outlier.

The most likely root causes are:

1. **Escalation-related delays**, especially long waits for Level-2 handling.
2. **Long idle periods before investigation**, particularly in Cases 102 and 104.
3. **Repeated overnight delays between investigation and resolution**, suggesting insufficient same-day closure capacity or dependency delays.
4. **Possible workload or prioritization issues**, causing tickets investigated later in the day to roll over to the next day.

To reduce cycle times, the support process should focus on faster investigation start times, stricter escalation SLAs, better Level-2 capacity management, and improved handling of tickets that risk slipping past the end of the business day.
