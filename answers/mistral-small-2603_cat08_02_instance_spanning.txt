# **Optimizing E-Commerce Fulfillment with Instance-Spanning Constraints: A Process Mining Approach**

## **1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact**

To systematically identify and quantify the impact of instance-spanning constraints, we will use **process mining techniques**—particularly **conformance checking, performance analysis, and bottleneck detection**—on the event log. Below is a structured approach for each constraint:

---

### **A. Shared Cold-Packing Stations**
**Objective:** Identify contention for the limited cold-packing stations and measure its impact on order flow.

#### **Process Mining Techniques & Metrics:**
1. **Resource Utilization Analysis**
   - **Tool:** Disco (Fluxicon) or ProM for resource performance analysis.
   - **Method:**
     - Filter the log for orders requiring cold packing (`Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`).
     - Analyze the **resource-activity matrix** to see how often cold-packing stations (e.g., `Station C1-C5`) are used.
     - Calculate **resource contention time** = Time between when an order is ready for cold packing and when it actually starts packing.
   - **Key Metrics:**
     - **Average waiting time per order** for cold-packing.
     - **Resource utilization rate** (e.g., % of time stations are busy).
     - **Queue length** at cold-packing stations over time.

2. **Bottleneck Detection**
   - **Tool:** Celonis or PM4Py for bottleneck analysis.
   - **Method:**
     - Use **throughput time breakdown** to see if delays occur specifically at the `Packing` stage for cold-packing orders.
     - Apply **sequence clustering** to identify patterns where orders wait excessively due to cold-packing unavailability.
   - **Key Metrics:**
     - **Percentage of orders delayed** due to cold-packing contention.
     - **Correlation between demand spikes** (e.g., holiday seasons) and cold-packing delays.

3. **Impact Quantification**
   - **Waiting time due to cold-packing contention** = Total time spent waiting for a cold-packing station.
   - **Opportunity cost:** Delayed cold-packing orders may miss shipping deadlines, leading to **late deliveries** or **express shipping upgrades** (increased costs).

---

### **B. Shipping Batches (Geographical Batching)**
**Objective:** Measure delays caused by batching dependencies and assess their impact on order lead time.

#### **Process Mining Techniques & Metrics:**
1. **Batching Pattern Analysis**
   - **Tool:** Custom Python/Pandas analysis or Celonis for batching detection.
   - **Method:**
     - Group orders by `Destination Region` and `Timestamp` to identify when batches form.
     - Check if orders **wait at `Shipping Label Generation`** until the batch is full.
   - **Key Metrics:**
     - **Average waiting time per order** in batch formation.
     - **Batch size distribution** (e.g., how many orders wait for a batch of 10?).
     - **Correlation between batch size and delay** (e.g., larger batches  longer waits).

2. **Dynamic Batching vs. Fixed Batching**
   - Compare **fixed batching rules** (e.g., "batch every 30 minutes") vs. **dynamic batching** (e.g., "batch when 5 orders are ready").
   - Use **simulation** (see Section 4) to test optimal batching thresholds.

3. **Impact Quantification**
   - **Batching delay** = Time between `Packing Complete` and `Shipping Label Generation`.
   - **Trade-off:** Smaller batches  faster individual orders but higher shipping costs; larger batches  slower individual orders but lower shipping costs.

---

### **C. Priority Handling (Express Orders)**
**Objective:** Assess how express orders disrupt standard order flow and measure their impact on throughput.

#### **Process Mining Techniques & Metrics:**
1. **Priority Interruption Analysis**
   - **Tool:** ProM for **inter-case dependency mining**.
   - **Method:**
     - Identify cases where an **express order preempts a standard order** at a shared resource (e.g., cold-packing station, QC staff).
     - Use **sequence mining** to find patterns like:
       - Express order arrives  standard order is paused  express order jumps queue.
   - **Key Metrics:**
     - **Number of preemptions per day** (how often standard orders are interrupted).
     - **Average delay imposed on standard orders** due to express order preemption.
     - **Resource idle time** while express orders are prioritized.

2. **Impact Quantification**
   - **Express order benefit:** Faster delivery for high-value customers.
   - **Standard order cost:** Increased waiting time, potential SLA violations.
   - **Throughput impact:** If express orders dominate resources, standard orders may accumulate, leading to **backlogs**.

---

### **D. Hazardous Material (HazMat) Regulatory Limits**
**Objective:** Ensure compliance with the **10 simultaneous HazMat orders in Packing/QC** while minimizing throughput impact.

#### **Process Mining Techniques & Metrics:**
1. **Regulatory Compliance Checking**
   - **Tool:** Custom SQL query or Celonis for **conformance checking**.
   - **Method:**
     - Filter for orders where `Hazardous Material = TRUE`.
     - Check if **>10 HazMat orders** are active in `Packing` or `QC` simultaneously.
     - If violations occur, analyze **which orders were delayed** due to the limit.
   - **Key Metrics:**
     - **Number of regulatory violations per day.**
     - **Average delay per HazMat order** due to the 10-order cap.
     - **Correlation between HazMat volume and delays.**

2. **Impact Quantification**
   - **Compliance risk:** Fines or shutdowns if limits are exceeded.
   - **Throughput reduction:** If HazMat orders are capped, other orders may proceed faster, but **HazMat orders face delays**.

---

### **Differentiating Within-Instance vs. Between-Instance Delays**
To isolate **between-instance delays** (caused by dependencies) from **within-instance delays** (e.g., slow packing), we use:

| **Delay Type**               | **Detection Method**                                                                 | **Example**                                                                 |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Within-Instance Delay**    | Compare actual activity duration vs. expected duration (from historical data).      | A standard order takes 20 min to pack (normal), but 40 min due to complex items. |
| **Between-Instance Delay**   | Check if delay occurs **after** an order is ready but **before** the next step starts. | Order is ready for cold-packing but waits 15 min for a station to free up. |
| **Batching Delay**           | Orders wait at `Shipping Label Generation` until batch is full.                     | 3 orders wait 20 min for a batch of 5.                                     |
| **Priority Preemption Delay**| Express order interrupts a standard order’s processing.                              | Standard order is paused for 10 min while an express order jumps queue.    |
| **Regulatory Delay**         | Order waits because HazMat limit is reached.                                         | HazMat order waits 30 min until another HazMat order completes QC.          |

**Key Technique:**
- **Time-between-activities analysis** (e.g., in Disco) to see if delays occur **between steps** rather than **during steps**.
- **Social network analysis (SNA)** to identify resource contention (e.g., if `Station C2` is a bottleneck, orders pile up before it).

## **2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions**

The constraints do **not operate in isolation**—they interact in complex ways that can **amplify delays** or **create new bottlenecks**. Below are key interactions and their implications:

---

### **A. Express Orders + Cold-Packing Contention**
**Interaction:**
- An **express order requiring cold packing** may **preempt a standard order** at a cold-packing station.
- If multiple express orders arrive, they **compete for the same limited stations**, leading to **longer queues**.

**Impact:**
- **Standard orders** (even those needing cold packing) face **longer waits**.
- **Express orders** may still be delayed if cold-packing stations are saturated.

**Example:**
- `ORD-5002` (Express, Cold-Packing) arrives  preempts `ORD-5001` (Standard, Cold-Packing).
- If another express order arrives, both may wait, defeating the purpose of express shipping.

---

### **B. Batching + Hazardous Material Limits**
**Interaction:**
- If multiple **HazMat orders** are destined for the **same region**, they may be **batched together** for shipping.
- However, the **HazMat limit (10)** may force some orders to **wait longer** before batching.

**Impact:**
- **HazMat orders** face **double delays**:
  1. Waiting for HazMat limit to free up (`Packing/QC`).
  2. Waiting for batch formation (`Shipping Label Generation`).
- **Non-HazMat orders** in the same region may proceed faster, but **HazMat orders become bottlenecks**.

**Example:**
- 12 HazMat orders for the **South region** arrive.
- Only 10 can be packed at once  2 orders wait.
- The batch forms only when **all 12 are ready**, delaying all of them.

---

### **C. Priority Handling + Regulatory Limits**
**Interaction:**
- An **express HazMat order** may need **both priority handling and cold packing**.
- If the **HazMat limit is reached**, the order may be **delayed despite its priority**, leading to **customer dissatisfaction**.

**Impact:**
- **Express orders lose their advantage** if regulatory limits block them.
- **Standard HazMat orders** may suffer less (since they don’t have tight deadlines).

**Example:**
- `ORD-5003` (Standard, HazMat) is in `QC`.
- An **express HazMat order** arrives but must wait because the HazMat limit is reached.
- The express order’s **SLA is violated**, hurting customer trust.

---

### **D. Cold-Packing + Batching**
**Interaction:**
- Orders requiring **cold packing** may be **geographically dispersed**, making batching difficult.
- If cold-packing stations are a bottleneck, **batching is delayed**, leading to **later shipping**.

**Impact:**
- **Cold-packing orders** face **longer lead times** due to both **resource contention and batching delays**.
- **Shipping costs may increase** if batches are smaller (due to cold-packing delays).

**Example:**
- 5 cold-packing orders for the **North region** arrive.
- Only 2 cold-packing stations are free  3 orders wait.
- The batch forms **30 min later** than planned, delaying all 5 orders.

---

### **Why Interaction Analysis is Critical**
- **Optimization must be holistic**—fixing one constraint may **worsen another**.
- **Trade-offs must be managed** (e.g., increasing cold-packing capacity may reduce batching delays but increase costs).
- **Dynamic policies** (e.g., adjusting batching thresholds based on HazMat volume) may be needed.

---

## **3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies**

Below are **three concrete strategies** to mitigate the negative impacts of instance-spanning constraints, along with their expected benefits.

---

### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Resource Allocation & Preemption Policies**
**Primary Constraints Addressed:**
- **Cold-Packing Contention**
- **Priority Handling (Express Orders)**
- **Hazardous Material Limits**

#### **Proposed Changes:**
1. **Tiered Priority System for Cold-Packing Stations**
   - **Express orders** get **reserved slots** (e.g., 2 out of 5 stations always available for express).
   - **Standard orders** use the remaining stations, but with **time-sharing** (e.g., max 15 min per order if express orders are waiting).
   - **HazMat orders** get **priority in QC** to free up stations faster.

2. **Predictive Resource Allocation**
   - Use **machine learning** (e.g., XGBoost) to predict **demand for cold-packing** based on:
     - Historical order volumes.
     - Seasonality (e.g., holidays).
     - Express order arrival patterns.
   - **Pre-allocate stations** during predicted peak times.

3. **Regulatory-Aware Scheduling for HazMat**
   - **Cap HazMat orders at 8** (instead of 10) to **prevent last-minute violations**.
   - **Reserve 2 stations** for non-HazMat orders to **avoid blocking standard flow**.

#### **How It Leverages Data:**
- **Process mining** identifies **peak cold-packing demand times**.
- **Predictive models** adjust station allocation **before bottlenecks form**.
- **Conformance checking** ensures HazMat limits are **never exceeded**.

#### **Expected Outcomes:**
 **Reduced cold-packing waiting time** by **30-40%** (fewer express order preemptions).
 **Faster express order processing** (meets SLA 95% of the time).
 **Compliance maintained** with HazMat limits.
 **Potential trade-off:** Standard orders may see **slightly longer waits** if express orders dominate.

---

### **Strategy 2: Dynamic Batching with Regional & Priority-Based Triggers**
**Primary Constraints Addressed:**
- **Shipping Batching Delays**
- **Hazardous Material Limits**

#### **Proposed Changes:**
1. **Dynamic Batching Thresholds**
   - Instead of **fixed batch sizes**, use **regional demand-based triggers**:
     - **High-demand regions (e.g., North, West):** Batch when **5 orders** are ready (faster delivery).
     - **Low-demand regions (e.g., South):** Batch when **10 orders** are ready (lower shipping costs).
   - **Express orders** are **batched immediately** (even if batch size = 1) to meet SLAs.

2. **HazMat-Specific Batching Rules**
   - **HazMat orders** are **batched separately** from non-HazMat orders to:
     - Avoid **contaminating non-HazMat batches**.
     - Ensure **HazMat batches form faster** (since they have fewer orders).
   - **Minimum batch size for HazMat = 3** (to balance speed and cost).

3. **Real-Time Batch Monitoring Dashboard**
   - Track **batch formation time** and **order wait times** in a **Celonis dashboard**.
   - Alert managers if **batches are taking too long** (e.g., >2 hours).

#### **How It Leverages Data:**
- **Historical batching data** identifies **optimal thresholds per region**.
- **Priority order logs** ensure **express orders are never delayed by batching**.
- **HazMat order patterns** help set **regional batching rules**.

#### **Expected Outcomes:**
 **Reduced batching delays** by **25-35%** (faster order completion).
 **Lower shipping costs** (optimized batch sizes per region).
 **HazMat orders processed faster** (separate batches).
 **Potential trade-off:** **Slightly higher shipping costs** for low-demand regions (but offset by faster delivery).

---

### **Strategy 3: Decoupled Packing & QC with Buffer Zones**
**Primary Constraints Addressed:**
- **Cold-Packing Contention**
- **Hazardous Material Limits**
- **Priority Handling**

#### **Proposed Changes:**
1. **Introduce a "Buffer Zone" After Packing**
   - After `Packing Complete`, orders move to a **buffer storage** (instead of immediately to `QC`).
   - **Buffer Zone Rules:**
     - **Express orders** skip the buffer and go straight to **priority QC**.
     - **HazMat orders** go to a **dedicated HazMat QC lane** (to avoid mixing with non-HazMat).
     - **Standard orders** wait in buffer until a **QC staff member is free**.

2. **Dynamic Buffer Management**
   - Use **IoT sensors** to track buffer levels.
   - If buffer **exceeds 20 orders**, **temporarily assign extra QC staff**.
   - If **HazMat buffer is full**, **pause non-HazMat packing** to free up HazMat slots.

3. **Cold-Packing Station Optimization**
   - **Dedicate 1 station exclusively to express orders**.
   - **Use AI to predict cold-packing demand** and **pre-warm stations** (e.g., turn on extra stations 30 min before peak).

#### **How It Leverages Data:**
- **Buffer analytics** identify **QC bottlenecks**.
- **IoT + process mining** enable **real-time buffer adjustments**.
- **Predictive models** optimize **cold-packing station allocation**.

#### **Expected Outcomes:**
 **Reduced QC waiting time** by **40%** (buffer absorbs fluctuations).
 **Express orders processed in <10 min** (meets SLA).
 **HazMat compliance maintained** (dedicated lane).
 **Potential trade-off:** **Slightly higher storage costs** (buffer zone requires space).

## **4. Simulation and Validation**

Before implementing changes, **simulation** is critical to **test strategies under realistic constraints**.

### **Simulation Approach:**
1. **Model the Current Process (Baseline)**
   - **Input:** Event log + constraints (cold-packing stations, HazMat limits, batching rules).
   - **Tool:** AnyLogic, SimPy, or PM4Py for discrete-event simulation.
   - **Key Components:**
     - **Resource pools** (pickers, packers, QC staff, cold-packing stations).
     - **Batching logic** (fixed vs. dynamic).
     - **Priority rules** (express vs. standard).
     - **Regulatory limits** (HazMat cap).

2. **Test Optimization Strategies in Simulation**
   - Run **each strategy (or combination)** under:
     - **Peak load scenarios** (e.g., Black Friday).
     - **Random order arrivals** (to test robustness).
   - **Key KPIs to Track:**
     - **Average order lead time** (end-to-end).
     - **% of orders meeting SLAs** (especially express).
     - **Resource utilization** (avoid over/under-allocation).
     - **Regulatory compliance** (HazMat limit adherence).
     - **Batching efficiency** (orders per batch).

3. **Sensitivity Analysis**
   - Test **how changes in parameters** affect outcomes:
     - What if **cold-packing stations increase from 5 to 7?**
     - What if **HazMat limit changes from 10 to 8?**
     - What if **batching threshold changes from 5 to 8?**

4. **Validation Against Real Data**
   - Compare **simulated KPIs** with **historical event log metrics**.
   - Adjust model parameters until **simulation matches reality**.

### **Ensuring Realistic Constraints in Simulation:**
- **Resource contention:** Model **queues** at cold-packing, QC, and packing.
- **Batching delays:** Simulate **order arrivals** and **batch formation time**.
- **Priority preemption:** Implement **express order jumps** in the queue.
- **Regulatory limits:** Enforce **HazMat cap** in the simulation.

**Expected Outcome:**
- **Identify the best strategy** before costly real-world changes.
- **Optimize parameters** (e.g., best batching threshold).
- **Predict failure modes** (e.g., if HazMat limit is too strict).
