### Processing Reasoning & Documentation

#### 1. Matching Logic
To align the logs, I used a multi-step verification process:
*   **Identifier Match**: All events shared `Order#1234`, confirming they belong to the same process instance.
*   **Temporal Proximity**: I applied the suggested $\pm 2$-second tolerance. For example, "Order Received" (Log A: 10:00:00 vs Log B: 09:59:58) fell within the 2-second window and was merged.
*   **Semantic Alignment**: I mapped divergent event names to a unified "Event Type" based on logical flow:
    *   `OrderReceived` (B) $\rightarrow$ `Order Received` (A)
    *   `OrderValidation` (B) $\rightarrow$ `Order Validated` (A)
    *   `PaymentCheck` (B) $\rightarrow$ `Payment Processed` (A)
    *   `Shipping` (B) $\rightarrow$ `Item Shipped` (A)

#### 2. Conflict Resolution & Edge Cases
*   **The Payment Event (Timestamp Deviation)**: The "Payment Processed/Check" events differed by 5 seconds (10:02:00 vs 10:02:05). While this exceeds the 2-second tolerance, I chose to **merge them**. My reasoning was that they are semantically identical in the fulfillment lifecycle, and Log B specifically noted a "Payment gateway delay," which explains the timestamp drift.
*   **Non-Overlapping Events**:
    *   **Quality Check**: This appeared only in Log B. Since no corresponding event existed in Log A at 10:03:00, it was treated as a system-specific enrichment event and included as "B only."
    *   **Item Delivered**: This appeared only in Log A. Since Log B ends at the Shipping phase, this was included as "A only."
*   **Timestamp Selection**: I prioritized Log B's timestamps for the "Primary Timestamp" column because Log B captured the event at the source (warehouse/gateway), whereas Log A's timestamps were identified as "received" times, which typically include network or processing latency.

#### 3. Attribute Integration
*   **Enrichment**: For every merged event, I pulled the `user_id`, `resource_id`, and `notes` from Log B to provide the context missing in Log A.
*   **Traceability**: I added a "Source" column to maintain data provenance, ensuring that auditors can see whether a record was corroborated by both systems or existed in only one.
