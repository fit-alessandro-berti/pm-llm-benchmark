### Rationale for Bias Mitigation Constraints:

To reduce bias in the loan application process, I have introduced a set of safeguards that ensure transparency, human oversight, and auditing before a final outcome is reached:

1.  **Mandatory Audit (`existence` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck`):** By adding `BiasMitigationCheck` to the existence constraint, the model mandates that every single trace must include a bias audit. This ensures that fairness is not an optional step but a requirement of the process.
2.  **Human Oversight for Sensitive Data (`coexistence` $\rightarrow$ `CheckSensitiveAttributes` & `ManualReview`):** To prevent algorithmic bias (where a model might automatically penalize a specific demographic), I added a coexistence constraint. This ensures that if the process accesses sensitive attributes (Race, Gender, Age), a `ManualReview` activity must also occur in that trace to provide human judgment and accountability.
3.  **Ordered Fairness Check (`precedence` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck` before `FinalDecision`):** Simply having a bias check is not enough; it must happen *before* the decision is finalized. This precedence constraint ensures that the `FinalDecision` cannot be reached until the bias audit has been completed.
4.  **Prevention of Immediate Decisioning (`nonsuccession` $\rightarrow$ `CheckSensitiveAttributes` to `FinalDecision`):** This constraint prevents the process from moving directly from viewing sensitive attributes to issuing a final decision. By forbidding this immediate succession, the model forces the inclusion of intermediate steps (like `ManualReview` or `BiasMitigationCheck`), thereby reducing the likelihood of "snap judgments" based on sensitive attributes.
