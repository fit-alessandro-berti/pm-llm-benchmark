### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Coexistence Constraint (`ManualReview` with `FinalDecision`)**  
   - **Purpose:** Ensures that when a `FinalDecision` is made for applications involving sensitive demographics (e.g., minority applicants), a `ManualReview` activity must also be present in the process trace.  
   - **Bias Reduction:** Guarantees that decisions based on sensitive attributes are accompanied by a fairness check, reducing the chance of immediate, biased outcomes without additional scrutiny.

2. **Response Constraint (`CheckApplicantRace` to `BiasMitigationCheck`)**  
   - **Purpose:** Inserts a systematic `BiasMitigationCheck` activity immediately following any sensitive attribute check (e.g., `CheckApplicantRace`).  
   - **Bias Reduction:** Prevents a direct leap from recognizing a sensitive attribute to a decision (e.g., rejection) without a mitigation step, thereby curtailing immediate discriminatory decisions.

3. **Succession Constraint (`BiasMitigationCheck` to `FinalDecision`)**  
   - **Purpose:** Enforces that a `BiasMitigationCheck` must precede any `FinalDecision` after sensitive attribute checks have been performed.  
   - **Bias Reduction:** Acts as a safeguard layer, ensuring that any decision influenced by potentially biased information passes through a fairness review before being finalized.

4. **Non-succession Constraint (`CheckApplicantRace` to `FinalDecision`)**  
   - **Purpose:** Explicitly blocks a direct succession from identifying a sensitive attribute (e.g., `CheckApplicantRace`) to making a final decision (`FinalDecision`).  
   - **Bias Reduction:** Eliminates the possibility of immediate, biased decisions (e.g., rejection) right after a sensitive attribute is assessed, compelling the process to include intermediary fairness checks.

By integrating these constraints, the model now incorporates mechanisms that actively monitor and mitigate bias in the loan application review process, promoting equitable treatment for all applicants regardless of sensitive attributes.
