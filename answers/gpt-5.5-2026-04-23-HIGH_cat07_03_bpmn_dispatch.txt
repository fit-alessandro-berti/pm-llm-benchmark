The visualization is a BPMN-style swimlane process diagram for **dispatching goods** in a **computer hardware shop**. It is divided into three lanes:

- **Logistics**
- **Secretary**
- **Warehouse**

The process starts with **“Ship goods”** and ends with **“Shipment prepared.”**

Key flow:

1. The process begins and splits into parallel work:
   - The **Secretary** clarifies the shipment method.
   - The **Warehouse** packages the goods.

2. In the Secretary lane:
   - After **“Clarify shipment method,”** a decision checks whether **special handling** is needed.
   - If **yes**, the Secretary:
     - Gets three offers from logistic companies.
     - Selects a logistic company and places the order.
   - If **no**, the Secretary:
     - Checks whether insurance is required, using **customer information**.
     - Always writes the package label.
     - If insurance is necessary, the parcel is sent to **Logistics** to be insured.
   - These administrative paths then merge.

3. In the Warehouse lane:
   - The Warehouse performs **“Package goods.”**
   - If packaging takes more than **48 hours**, a timer event triggers **“Notify customer about delay.”**
   - The packaging path then continues toward final synchronization.

4. Once both the administrative shipping arrangements and warehouse packaging are complete, the flows join.
5. The Warehouse then:
   - Fills out shipment paperwork.
   - Moves the package to the pickup area.
6. The process ends with **“Shipment prepared.”**

Overall, the diagram models the coordinated steps needed to prepare goods for shipment, including shipment method clarification, optional special handling, optional insurance, packaging, delay notification, paperwork, and final pickup preparation.
