Brief explanation of the logic

1. Case identification:
   - Cases are defined by the primary work object:
     - Case_Document1_Report: Work on Document1.docx.
     - Case_AnnualMeeting_Email: Handling the specific “Email about Annual Meeting” thread.
     - Case_ReportDraft_PDF: Reviewing Report_Draft.pdf.
     - Case_Budget2024: Editing Budget_2024.xlsx.
     - Case_QuarterlyReport: Working on Quarterly_Report.docx.
   - Switch events that clearly move from one object to another are used as boundaries/links but kept in the originating or target case when meaningful.

2. Activity naming (abstraction from low-level logs):
   - FOCUS on a document/email  “Start editing …”, “Start handling email”, or “Start reviewing PDF”.
   - TYPING in Word/Excel  “Write content” or “Edit data”.
   - TYPING in email  “Compose reply”.
   - CLICK Reply  “Start reply”.
   - CLICK Send  “Send email”.
   - SAVE  “Save document”.
   - SCROLL in email/PDF  “Read email” or “Read/scroll document”.
   - HIGHLIGHT in PDF  “Highlight key content”.
   - SWITCH between apps  high-level “Switch to …” activities to reflect cross-artifact navigation.

3. Narrative coherence:
   - The log now represents distinct, analyzable workflows:
     - Drafting Document1, referencing the budget, and saving/closing.
     - Handling an annual meeting email from open to send.
     - Reviewing a draft report PDF.
     - Updating the 2024 budget.
     - Editing and finalizing the quarterly report.
