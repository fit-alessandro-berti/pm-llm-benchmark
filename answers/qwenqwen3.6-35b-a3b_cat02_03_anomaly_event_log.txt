### Analysis of Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log

Below is an analysis of the provided event log. First, we establish the **Standard P2P Process Flow** for reference:
1.  **Create & Approve Requisition:** Requester creates a need; management approves budget and necessity.
2.  **Issue Purchase Order (PO):** A formal contract is sent to the vendor based on the approved requisition.
3.  **Receive Goods:** The company confirms receipt of goods/services (Goods Receipt Note).
4.  **Receive Invoice:** The vendor submits a bill.
5.  **Match Invoice to PO:** Finance validates the invoice against the PO and Goods Receipt (Three-Way Match).
6.  **Release Payment:** Payment is authorized and executed.
7.  **Close Case:** The process is finalized.

**Case 1001** follows this standard flow perfectly and represents a healthy process instance. The anomalies are identified in Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004.

### **Overall Recommendations**

1.  **Immediate Investigation:** Cases 1002 and 1003 require immediate audit investigation. The sequences suggest potential fraud, system configuration errors allowing bypasses, or compromised user credentials.
2.  **System Controls:** Implement hard stops in the ERP system:
    *   PO cannot be issued without an Approved PR.
    *   Payment cannot be released without a completed Three-Way Match.
    *   Payment cannot be released before Goods Receipt and Invoice.
    *   Goods Receipt cannot be posted without a valid PO.
3.  **Segregation of Duties:** Review user roles to ensure no single user can perform conflicting activities (e.g., creating POs and releasing payments).
4.  **Process Training:** Case 1004 suggests operational confusion or workarounds. Staff training on the necessity of POs before receiving goods is required.

**Conclusion:** Only **Case 1001** adheres to standard P2P best practices. Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004 contain significant anomalies that violate fundamental internal controls, exposing the organization to financial loss, fraud, and compliance risks.
