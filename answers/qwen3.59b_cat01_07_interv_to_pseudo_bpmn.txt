# Process Model: Invoice Approval & Payment Workflow

Based on the provided interview transcripts, the following structured textual model describes the **Invoice Approval and Payment Process**. This model synthesizes the accounts of the Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary), Purchasing Officer (Dan), Department Manager (Carlos), AP Supervisor (Rita), and Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen).

---

## 1. Process Overview
*   **Process Name:** Invoice to Payment Workflow
*   **Goal:** Validate incoming invoices against Purchase Orders (POs) and receipts, resolve discrepancies, and schedule payment.
*   **Primary Participants:** Accounts Payable Clerk, Purchasing Officer, Department Manager, AP Supervisor, Supplier Relationship Manager, Suppliers, Dept Heads.
*   **Key Artifacts:** Invoice, Purchase Order (PO), Goods Receipt Confirmation, Payment Schedule.

---

## 2. Process Flow Description

### Start Event: `Invoice Received`
*   **Trigger:** An invoice arrives via email or system upload.
*   **Actor:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary).
*   **Artifact:** Invoice Document.

### Task 1.1: Initial Validation (PO Check)
*   **Action:** The Clerk checks if the invoice is associated with a valid Purchase Order number.
*   **Decision Gateway: Is PO Number Present?**
    *   **Path A (No):** Send back to supplier for details / Request missing info.
        *   *Note:* If the invoice is for services contracted informally, this path may lead to an exception handling loop involving the AP Supervisor or Supplier Relationship Manager.
    *   **Path B (Yes):** Proceed to Logging.

### Task 1.2: System Logging & Routing
*   **Action:** Log the invoice into the accounting system. Forward the invoice to the Purchasing Department for receipt confirmation.
*   **Actor:** Accounts Payable Clerk.

### Task 1.3: Goods Receipt & Detail Verification
*   **Trigger:** Invoice forwarded to Purchasing.
*   **Actor:** Purchasing Officer (Dan).
*   **Action:** Open the PO and compare invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) against ordered items. Verify goods/services were received.
*   **Decision Gateway: Do Details Match?**
    *   **Path A (Mismatch):**
        *   *Condition:* Extra units, wrong price, or missing info (e.g., PO number).
        *   *Action:* Contact Supplier directly OR Request AP to hold invoice until supplier sends corrected version.
        *   *Exception Note:* If the issue cannot be resolved easily by Purchasing, it escalates to the AP Supervisor (Rita) or Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen) for contract verification.
    *   **Path B (Match):** Send confirmation email to Accounts Payable.

### Task 1.4: Three-Way Match
*   **Actor:** Accounts Payable Clerk.
*   **Action:** Match invoice line items against the PO and Receipt Confirmation in the system.
*   **Decision Gateway: Are All Discrepancies Resolved?**
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Exists):** Email Purchasing Team to clarify. If unresolved, escalate to AP Supervisor (Rita).
        *   *Exception Note:* Karen (Supplier RM) may be looped in here to check contract pricing or negotiate terms with the supplier before returning the invoice to flow.
    *   **Path B (Resolved/Matched):** Proceed to Manager Approval.

### Task 1.5: Manager Approval
*   **Trigger:** System notification sent to Department Manager.
*   **Actor:** Department Manager (Carlos).
*   **Action:** Review invoice details, PO, and budget impact.
*   **Decision Gateway: Is Invoice Approved?**
    *   **Path A (Reject/Exception):**
        *   *Condition:* Suspicious items, significant budget variance, or unexpected charges.
        *   *Action:* Reject in system OR Ask AP to hold for further investigation.
        *   *Flow:* If held/investigated by Supervisor (Rita) and cleared, return to Task 1.4 (Match).
    *   **Path B (Approve):** Proceed to Payment Scheduling.

### Task 1.6: Payment Scheduling
*   **Actor:** Accounts Payable Clerk / System.
*   **Action:** Schedule invoice for the next payment run.
*   **End Event:** `Invoice Paid` (or queued for payment).

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

| Role | Primary Responsibility | Secondary/Exception Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AP Clerk** | Initial validation, logging, matching, routing to manager. | Escalating unresolved issues to Supervisor. |
| **Purchasing Officer** | Verifying goods received and PO details; confirming receipt status. | Blocking flow if PO info is missing or mismatch exists. |
| **Department Manager** | Final budget/accuracy approval. | Rejecting suspicious invoices; delegating investigation to AP. |
| **AP Supervisor** | Exception handling; coordinating with suppliers/depts. | Resolving cases where Clerk/Purchasing cannot fix discrepancies. |
| **Supplier RM** | External liaison; verifying contract terms/pricing. | Negotiating price corrections or missing PO info from suppliers. |

---

## 4. Exceptions and Variations

The following paths diverge from the standard flow based on interview data:

1.  **Missing PO Number:**
    *   *Scenario:* Invoice arrives without a PO reference.
    *   *Action:* Purchasing tells AP to get info; if informal service, Supervisor creates retrospective PO or rejects invoice.
2.  **Unresolved Discrepancy:**
    *   *Scenario:* Pricing or quantity mismatch cannot be resolved by standard correspondence.
    *   *Action:* Escalation to AP Supervisor (Rita). If the discrepancy relates to contract terms, Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen) is contacted.
3.  **Manager Rejection:**
    *   *Scenario:* Budget variance or suspicious items detected during review.
    *   *Action:* Invoice is rejected or held. It loops back to Purchasing/AP for investigation. If cleared by Supervisor, it re-enters the flow at Task 1.4.
4.  **Informal Services:**
    *   *Scenario:* Invoice does not match any PO (e.g., utility bills, consulting).
    *   *Action:* AP Supervisor coordinates with Dept Heads to create a retrospective PO or decide on rejection.

---

## 5. Notes on Ambiguities and Conflicts

*   **Matching Timing:** There is a slight variation in when the "Three-Way Match" occurs. Mary describes matching *after* receiving confirmation from Purchasing regarding receipt. Dan implies he checks details when the invoice hits his inbox.
    *   *Model Resolution:* The model sequences this as `Logging -> Forward to Purchasing (Receipt Confirm) -> AP Matching`. This assumes Purchasing validates physical/logistical receipt first, followed by AP validating financial accuracy before Manager approval.
*   **Escalation Paths:** Interviews suggest both Mary and Dan can escalate issues, but Rita specifically handles "exceptions."
    *   *Model Resolution:* The model distinguishes between a standard mismatch (resolved by Purchasing/AP) and an exception (resolved by Supervisor).
*   **Karen’s Role:** Karen is involved indirectly.
    *   *Model Resolution:* Her role is mapped to the "External Coordination" sub-activity within the Exception Handling path, rather than a direct internal process step.
