# 20 Thought-Provoking Questions on the Smartphone Supply Chain Process

## Rationale Behind Process Flows

1. **Why does the process source components from four different geographic regions (Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, Germany/USA), and what trade-offs between cost, quality, and supply chain resilience does this multi-sourcing strategy reflect?**

2. **What is the strategic rationale for centralizing assembly in a single plant in Malaysia rather than establishing regional assembly facilities closer to end markets, and under what conditions might this decision need to be revisited?**

3. **Why are component procurement activities modeled as parallel tasks rather than sequential ones, and what coordination challenges arise when components arrive at the assembly plant at different times?**

4. **What justifies the decision to split outbound logistics between air freight and cargo ships, and how should the organization determine which markets qualify as "high-demand" enough to warrant the cost premium of air transport?**

## Risk Management

5. **How vulnerable is this process to geopolitical disruptions (e.g., tensions affecting Taiwan's semiconductor exports), and what contingency mechanisms could be embedded into the process to mitigate single-region dependencies?**

6. **What happens to the entire downstream flow if one component supplier in the parallel procurement gateway experiences a significant delay, and should the process include timeout or escalation paths at the parallel join?**

7. **How does the rework loop at the quality check gateway affect overall throughput, and what threshold of failure rate should trigger a deeper root-cause investigation rather than continued rework?**

8. **What risks does the 24/7 assembly operation introduce regarding workforce fatigue, equipment maintenance windows, and quality consistency, and how should these be balanced against throughput demands?**

9. **How should the process handle customs clearance failures or regulatory non-compliance in specific countries, given that this task currently appears as a single point through which all shipments must pass?**

## Potential Improvements

10. **Could earlier quality inspection of incoming components (before assembly) reduce the volume of failed units at the post-assembly quality check, and what would be the cost-benefit profile of such a shift-left approach?**

11. **Would introducing buffer inventory or safety stock at the Malaysian assembly plant improve resilience against component delays, or would the carrying costs and obsolescence risk in fast-moving consumer electronics outweigh the benefits?**

12. **How might digital technologies (IoT tracking, blockchain for provenance, AI demand forecasting) be integrated into specific tasks in this process to improve visibility and reduce cycle time?**

13. **Is the placement of "Monitor Inventory & Sales Forecast" near the end of the process optimal, or should demand sensing occur continuously and feed back into earlier stages like component procurement and assembly scheduling?**

## Decision-Making

14. **What criteria and data sources should inform the exclusive gateway decision between "Adjust Orders to Suppliers" and "Proceed to Next Sales Cycle," and who should hold the authority to make this call?**

15. **How should the organization decide the allocation of finished goods across the four distribution channels (warehousing, retail, e-commerce, VIP/pre-orders) when supply is constrained, and what prioritization rules should apply?**

16. **At what point in the rework loop should a failed unit be scrapped rather than reworked, and how should the cost of rework, brand reputation risk, and sustainability considerations factor into this decision?**

## Stakeholder Communication

17. **How should information about production delays or quality issues be communicated across the chain—from suppliers to assembly to regional distribution centers—and what service-level agreements should govern these communications?**

18. **What mechanisms should exist for retailers, e-commerce platforms, and VIP customers to provide demand signals and feedback that influence the "Adjust Orders to Suppliers" decision, and how can conflicting signals from different channels be reconciled?**

## Performance Measurement

19. **What key performance indicators (KPIs) should be defined for each major stage of this process—procurement lead time, assembly yield rate, logistics cycle time, customs clearance duration, forecast accuracy—and how should they be weighted in an overall supply chain scorecard?**

20. **How should the organization measure the end-to-end cash-to-cash cycle time of this process, and which stages offer the greatest opportunity to reduce working capital tied up in the supply chain?**
