**Analysis of POWL Models vs. Normative Hire-to-Retire Process**

A normative “Hire-to-Retire” (essentially a successful-hire variant here) follows this core logic:  
**Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case**.  
All activities are mandatory and occur exactly once in (roughly) this sequence. Early filtering steps (screening) must precede evaluation steps (interviews, decision), the decision must be informed by interviews, and post-hire steps (onboarding then payroll) must follow a positive decision. Real processes often include rejection branches, but neither model provides them; both assume the “hire” path.

**Anomalies in Model 1**  
- The `StrictPartialOrder` only enforces `Post  Screen`, `Screen  Decide`, `Screen  Interview`, and `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`.  
- There is **no ordering constraint between Interview and Decide** (or between Interview and the later activities Onboard/Payroll/Close). Linear extensions therefore allow traces in which `Make_Hiring_Decision` occurs before `Conduct_Interviews`, or `Conduct_Interviews` occurs *after* onboarding, payroll, or even closing the case. This violates core process logic: a hiring decision cannot be made without interviews, and interviews should not occur after the employee has already been onboarded.  
- All activities remain mandatory (no skips or loops), and the core chain `Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close` is correctly ordered. The only real flaw is the “floating” Interview activity attached only to Screen.

**Anomalies in Model 2**  
- The partial order only enforces `Post  Screen`, `Post  Interview`, `Interview  Decide`, `Decide  loop_onboarding  xor_payroll  Close`.  
- **Screen has no outgoing precedence** beyond being after Post. Consequently `Screen_Candidates` can occur *after* `Conduct_Interviews`, after `Make_Hiring_Decision`, after onboarding, or even after `Close_Case`. This is a severe violation—screening is an early filtering step; deciding to hire or onboarding without it (or screening at the very end) makes no sense.  
- `loop_onboarding = LOOP(Onboard, skip)` allows repeated execution of onboarding (semantics of `*(Onboard, skip)` typically mean “do Onboard, then zero or more do-nothing redos,” resulting in one or more onboardings). Onboarding is not an activity that should be repeated in a normal hire flow.  
- `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)` makes `Add_to_Payroll` entirely optional. A hired/onboarded employee can finish the process without ever being added to payroll, which fundamentally breaks the “hire” objective.  
- Introduction of silent transitions and control-flow operators (LOOP, XOR) adds deviations not present in the standard linear sequence.

**Which Model More Closely Aligns with the Normative Version?**  
**Model 1 aligns more closely with a normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

**Justification**  
- Model 1 preserves the mandatory nature of every activity and maintains the correct ordering of the core chain (`Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`). Its anomaly is limited to an ordering flaw for a single activity (Interview can be misplaced relative to Decide and later steps). While this is a clear deviation from good practice, the process still guarantees that every step occurs.  
- Model 2’s anomalies are more severe and more numerous. In addition to a comparable ordering flaw (Screen can be executed too late), it introduces control-flow constructs that allow *skipping a critical activity* (payroll) and *repeating an activity* (onboarding). Allowing an employee to be onboarded but never added to payroll violates the fundamental integrity of a hire process; the same is true of gratuitous repetition of onboarding. These are not minor deviations—they change the essence of what the process accomplishes.  

In summary, Model 1’s flaw is an incomplete partial order (missing precedence edges for Interview), which is a modeling inaccuracy but keeps all steps mandatory. Model 2’s combination of incomplete ordering *plus* optionality and repetition makes it deviate further from the standard sequence and logic of the Hire-to-Retire process. Therefore Model 1 is the closer (though still imperfect) representation.
