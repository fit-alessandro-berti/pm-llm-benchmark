### Rationale summary (why these groupings make sense)
- **Idle vs active:** Phases with **zero vibration + zero flow + tool at 0 mm + low energy** (events 1–3, 13–15) are strongly indicative of **standby/reset**.
- **Cutting/machining signature (events 4–6):** The combination of **high vibration**, **non-zero flow**, **rising tool position**, and **higher energy** is typical of **mechanical material removal**.
- **Thermal joining signature (event 9):** The **maximum temperature (80°C)** and **largest energy spike (5.0 kWh)** alongside **flow dropping to 0** and stable tool position strongly suggests a **welding-like** step.
- **Cooling/end phase (event 10):** Rapid drop in temperature/energy/vibration matches **post-weld cooling or retract**.
- **Post-process follow-up (events 11–12):** Moderate energy with **small flow** and **tool advancing** at relatively low temperature fits a **secondary minor mechanical step** (e.g., deburr/rework) rather than the main cutting or welding extremes.
