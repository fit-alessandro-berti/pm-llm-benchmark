This is a timeline-based process flow visualization tracking fine-related administrative cases from **January 25, 2000, to April 24, 2013**:

1.  **Three sequential process stages, top to bottom**:
    -   **Send Fine**: This initial step is represented by dense, uniformly spaced gray vertical lines, showing that fines were issued consistently and evenly across the entire 13-year period, with no major gaps in activity.
    -   **Insert Fine Notification**: This middle step uses pink-to-red lines, with visible clusters of brighter red indicating batches of cases moving to this step. The volume here varies over time, unlike the steady first step, with waves of activity.
    -   **Send for Credit Collection**: The final step has the most concentrated, pointed pink/red flows, showing this escalation happens in distinct, periodic batches, not evenly over time. Gaps between these clusters indicate lulls where fewer cases were sent to credit collection.

2.  Overall pattern:
    While fines are issued at a steady, consistent rate, the follow-up processes—notification, then credit collection—follow an episodic, batch-driven pattern, suggesting administrative backlogs, scheduled cycles, or periodic policy-driven efforts to escalate unpaid fines over the years.
