## Matching approach used

- **Primary timestamp:** Log A timestamp when a Log A event is present; otherwise Log B timestamp.
- **Matching keys:** same `order_id` + normalized/similar event name + close timestamp.
- **Default tolerance:** approximately **2 seconds** for high-confidence matches.
- **Exception:** I merged `Payment Processed` from Log A with `PaymentCheck` from Log B even though the timestamps differ by 5 seconds, because:
  - both are payment-related,
  - they share the same `order_id`,
  - there is no other competing payment event,
  - Log B explicitly notes a “Payment gateway delay.”

Both source timestamps are retained so the offset is visible.

## Reasoning Summary

- `Order Received` and `OrderReceived` were merged because the names are equivalent and timestamps differ by only 2 seconds.
- `Order Validated` and `OrderValidation` were merged because they are semantically equivalent and differ by 1 second.
- `Payment Processed` and `PaymentCheck` were merged with **medium confidence** due to the 5-second gap. The semantic similarity and “Payment gateway delay” note support treating them as the same payment-related occurrence.
- `Quality Check` appears only in Log B, so it was included as a separate B-only event.
- `Item Shipped` and `Shipping` were merged because they describe the same fulfillment step and differ by 2 seconds.
- `Item Delivered` appears only in Log A, so it was retained as an A-only event.

If a stricter rule of “only merge events within 2 seconds” were required, the payment events should be left as two separate records.
