## **Justification of Naming Choices**

### 1. **Verify Documents**

This activity appears up to two times per case, but serves different purposes based on context:

- **First Occurrence**: Always occurs early in the process as part of standard identity verification.  
   Renamed to: **Verify Documents (Initial Check)**  
  *Justification*: It's the first step after request initiation, focused on gathering or validating initial documentation.

- **Second Occurrence**: Only appears after corrective actions (e.g., document submission or account adjustment), indicating re-verification.  
   C001: Following "Submit Additional", it's a re-check  **Verify Documents (Post-Submission)**  
   C003: Following "Adjust Account", it's re-verification after internal changes  **Verify Documents (Post-Adjustment)**  
  *Justification*: These are not redundant—they validate completeness or accuracy after an intermediate action, distinguishing them from the initial check.

---

### 2. **Call Customer**

This activity occurs multiple times **within the same case** (C002 and C003 have two calls each), with different purposes:

- **C001**: One call – to request missing documents.  
   Already unique; retained as **Call Customer for Documents** for consistency.

- **C002**: Two calls:  
  - First: To clarify a discrepancy  **Call Customer for Clarification**  
  - Second: To confirm adjustments  **Call Customer for Confirmation**  
  *Justification*: The "Additional Context" explicitly states the purpose—clarifying vs. confirming—enabling clear disambiguation.

- **C003**: One call – to ask the customer to return to the branch.  
   Renamed to **Call Customer to Return**  
  *Justification*: This is operationally distinct—it's not about information gathering but physical presence.

---

### 3. **Adjust Account**

Appears once in C002 and C003, always involving internal corrections due to discovered issues.  
- Kept as **Adjust Account** because its meaning is consistent across contexts (always an internal fix post-call or post-verification failure). No ambiguity with other activities.

---

### 4. **Initiate Request & Approve Request**

These remain unchanged because:
- They occur exactly once per case.
- Their meaning is consistent: start and end of the workflow.
- No contextual variation suggests a need for renaming.

---

## **Summary of Renaming Logic**

| Original Activity        | New Name(s)                                      | Disambiguation Basis |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Verify Documents         | (Initial Check), (Post-Submission), (Post-Adjustment) | Timing and trigger: first verification vs. re-verification after corrective input |
| Call Customer            | for Documents, for Clarification, for Confirmation, to Return | Purpose stated in context: requesting info, clarifying, confirming, or recalling presence |

These changes ensure that **identical activity names no longer mask functional differences**, improving traceability, process mining accuracy, and stakeholder understanding of workflow complexity.
