### **Process Mining Analysis & Optimization Plan for TechSolve Solutions**

**To:** TechSolve Solutions Management  
**From:** Process Improvement Consultant (ITSM & Process Mining Specialist)  
**Subject:** Data-Driven Optimization of Resource Assignment and Incident Management  

Based on the operational challenges and the available event log data, I have developed a comprehensive approach to diagnose inefficiencies and optimize resource assignment. This plan leverages process mining techniques to move beyond anecdotal management views and establish a factual, data-driven foundation for improvement.

---

### **1. Analyzing Resource Behavior and Assignment Patterns**

To understand the current state, we must transform the raw event log into actionable insights regarding agent performance, tier behavior, and assignment logic.

#### **Metrics and Performance Analysis**
Using the event log attributes (`Resource`, `Agent Tier`, `Agent Skills`, `Required Skill`, `Timestamp`), we will calculate the following metrics:

*   **Workload Distribution:**
    *   *Case Volume per Agent:* Histogram of ticket counts to identify outliers (overloaded vs. underutilized agents).
    *   *Effective Workload:* Weighted cases based on `Ticket Priority` and `Category` complexity, as handling a P1 Network ticket carries more weight than a P4 Software-App ticket.
*   **Processing Efficiency:**
    *   *Activity Duration:* Time between `Work L1 Start` and `Work L1 End` per agent. This reveals if certain agents take disproportionately long, potentially due to skill gaps.
    *   *Queue Wait Times:* Time between `Assign L1/L2` and `Work Start`. High wait times indicate bottlenecks in specific tiers or skill pools.
*   **First Contact Resolution (FCR) & Escalation Rates:**
    *   *L1 FCR:* Percentage of tickets where `Work L1 End` is followed by `Ticket Resolved` rather than `Escalate L2`.
    *   *Escalation Ratio:* Volume of escalations per agent. High escalation rates may indicate lack of skill, poor assessment, or "throw-over-the-wall" behavior.
*   **Skill Utilization & Match Rate:**
    *   *Skill Match Ratio:* Compare `Agent Skills` vs. `Required Skill`. Calculate the percentage of assignments where the agent possesses the required skill.
    *   *Specialist Underutilization:* Analyze L2/L3 agents handling tickets with `Required Skill` that match basic L1 capabilities or are unrelated to their specialization.

#### **Process Mining Techniques for Pattern Discovery**
*   **Resource Interaction & Social Network Analysis (SNA):**
    *   Construct a network graph where nodes are agents and edges represent handovers (`Reassign`, `Escalate`).
    *   *Insight:* Identify "Handover Hubs" (agents who constantly reassign tickets, indicating poor initial judgment) and "Bottleneck Nodes" (agents where tickets pile up before resolution).
    *   *Comparison:* Map actual handovers against the intended hierarchy. Frequent lateral reassignments within L2 suggest the round-robin logic is failing to match skills.
*   **Role Discovery:**
    *   Analyze behavior clusters rather than static titles.
    *   *Insight:* The log may reveal "Hidden Roles." For example, some L1 agents might consistently resolve `Software-App` tickets without escalation (effectively acting as L2 specialists), while some L2 agents might only handle low-complexity tasks. This challenges the rigid tier structure.
*   **Skill Matrix Mining:**
    *   Create a dynamic skill matrix derived from successful resolutions. If Agent B12 consistently resolves `Database-SQL` tickets quickly with high quality, their effective skill profile is validated and weighted higher than the static list.

### **2. Identifying Resource-Related Bottlenecks and Issues**

By correlating resource data with process outcomes, we can pinpoint specific inefficiencies and quantify their impact.

#### **Pinpointing Problems**
*   **Skill-Based Bottlenecks:**
    *   *Analysis:* Filter cases where `Required Skill` = 'Networking-Firewall'. Analyze the wait time distribution.
    *   *Finding:* If wait times for this skill are significantly higher than the average, it indicates a resource scarcity. The log may show tickets queuing in L2 for hours while L1 agents are idle, proving the dispatcher cannot route directly to the scarce resource.
*   **Reassignment Impact:**
    *   *Analysis:* Calculate the time delta between `Reassign` and the subsequent `Work Start`.
    *   *Quantification:* *"Average delay per reassignment is 45 minutes. Tickets with 2 reassignments have a 300% higher probability of SLA breach compared to tickets with 0 reassignments."*
*   **Incorrect Initial Assignments:**
    *   *Analysis:* Identify cases where `Assign L1` occurs, but `Agent Skills` does not include `Required Skill`, leading to immediate `Escalate` or `Reassign`.
    *   *Quantification:* *"20% of L1 assignments result in reassignment within 30 minutes due to skill mismatch, wasting L1 capacity and delaying resolution."*
*   **SLA Breach Correlation:**
    *   *Analysis:* Perform a correlation analysis between SLA status (Breach/No Breach) and resource attributes.
    *   *Finding:* *"65% of P2 SLA breaches are associated with tickets that underwent at least one reassignment. P3 breaches are strongly correlated with assignments to agents with >10 concurrent cases."*
*   **Uneven Workload:**
    *   *Analysis:* Coefficient of variation in agent workload.
    *   *Finding:* *"Top 20% of agents handle 50% of the volume, while bottom 20% handle <10%, indicating the round-robin logic ignores agent availability and capacity."*

### **3. Root Cause Analysis for Assignment Inefficiencies**

We will use advanced mining techniques to determine *why* these issues occur.

#### **Potential Root Causes**
*   **Deficient Assignment Rules:** The round-robin approach treats all tickets and agents as homogeneous, ignoring `Required Skill` and current workload.
*   **Skill Profile Inaccuracy:** The static `Agent Skills` list may be outdated. Agents may have acquired new skills or lost proficiency, leading to mismatches.
*   **Poor Ticket Classification:** L1 agents or users may misidentify `Required Skill` or `Category`, causing the dispatcher to route to the wrong pool.
*   **Lack of Real-Time Visibility:** Dispatchers and agents cannot see who is available *and* skilled, leading to manual workarounds and delays.

#### **Analytical Techniques**
*   **Variant Analysis:**
    *   Compare the "Happy Path" (Create  Assign  Resolve) against "Reassignment Variants."
    *   *Insight:* Analysis may reveal that tickets with `Category=Network` and `Priority=P2` have a 90% reassignment rate when assigned to Agent A02, suggesting A02 lacks the necessary network skills or assessment capability.
*   **Decision Mining:**
    *   Build a predictive model using ticket attributes (`Priority`, `Category`, `Required Skill`) and assignment outcomes (`Reassigned?`, `SLA Breach?`).
    *   *Feature Importance:* This will highlight the strongest predictors of failure. For example, the model might show that *"Missing Skill Match"* contributes 40% to reassignment probability, while *"Agent Workload > 8"* contributes 25%. This quantifies the root causes.
*   **Conformance Checking:**
    *   Compare the event log against the ideal process model (e.g., "Tickets requiring 'Database-SQL' must go to L2 agents with that skill").
    *   *Insight:* Quantify the deviation rate. High deviation confirms the current system cannot enforce skill-based rules.

### **4. Developing Data-Driven Resource Assignment Strategies**

Based on the analysis, I propose three distinct strategies to optimize assignment. Each addresses specific issues identified via process mining.

#### **Strategy 1: Dynamic Skill-Based Routing (SBR) with Proficiency Weighting**
*   **Issue Addressed:** Skill mismatches, frequent reassignments, specialists handling low-level tasks.
*   **Leveraging Insights:** Uses the mined skill match rates and variant analysis showing reassignment drivers. Incorporates "Role Discovery" to identify hidden proficiencies.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Replace round-robin with a routing engine that matches `Required Skill` to `Agent Skills`.
    *   **Proficiency Weighting:** Assign scores to agents based on historical performance (e.g., FCR, resolution time) for specific skills. An agent with `App-CRM` who resolves tickets faster gets a higher weight.
    *   **Tier Flexibility:** Allow L1 agents with high proficiency in specific skills to receive those tickets directly, bypassing L2 queues for simple variations.
*   **Data Required:** `Agent Skills`, `Required Skill`, Historical resolution times, FCR per agent/skill.
*   **Expected Benefits:** Reduce reassignment rate by ~40%; Increase L1 FCR by 15%; Improve SLA compliance for P2/P3 by reducing routing errors.

#### **Strategy 2: Workload-Aware Assignment with Real-Time Capacity Balancing**
*   **Issue Addressed:** Uneven workload, overloaded agents causing delays, underutilization.
*   **Leveraging Insights:** Uses workload distribution analysis and correlation between agent load and SLA breaches.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Implement an assignment algorithm that considers `Current Active Cases`, `Average Handling Time (AHT)` per agent, and `Skill Availability`.
    *   **Capacity Scoring:** Calculate a "Capacity Score" for each agent in real-time. Assign tickets to the agent with the best skill match who has the highest capacity score.
    *   **Thresholds:** Prevent assignment to agents exceeding a dynamic threshold (e.g., >10 concurrent cases or >8 hours of estimated work).
*   **Data Required:** Real-time queue status, `Resource` active cases, `Timestamp` data for AHT calculation, Agent shift schedules.
*   **Expected Benefits:** Reduce workload variance by 50%; Decrease burnout; Stabilize SLA performance by preventing queue spikes at overloaded agents.

#### **Strategy 3: Predictive Assignment & Skill Inference Engine**
*   **Issue Addressed:** Poor initial categorization, incorrect `Required Skill` identification, manual assessment delays.
*   **Leveraging Insights:** Decision mining reveals that misclassification is a predictor of reassignment. Variant analysis shows specific keywords/categories that consistently require specific skills.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Deploy a machine learning model that analyzes ticket attributes (`Category`, `Notes`, `Description`) to predict the true `Required Skill` and `Complexity` at the moment of creation.
    *   **Auto-Enrichment:** The system suggests or auto-fills `Required Skill` based on historical patterns. For example, if a ticket mentions "CRM login timeout," the model predicts `App-CRM` and `Security-IAM` with 95% confidence.
    *   **Pre-Assignment:** Route the ticket to the optimal agent pool immediately upon creation, reducing dispatcher intervention.
*   **Data Required:** Ticket text (`Notes`), `Category`, `Required Skill`, Resolution outcomes, Agent performance data.
*   **Expected Benefits:** Reduce manual categorization time; Improve routing accuracy by 30%; Enable faster initial response times; Reduce reliance on dispatcher judgment.
