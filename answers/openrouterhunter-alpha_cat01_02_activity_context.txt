# Analysis of Event Log & Activity Renaming

## Identified Recurring Activities and Contextual Differences

After examining the event log, I found that three activity names recur within individual cases, each carrying distinct contextual meanings:

### 1. **"Verify Documents"** (appears twice in C001 and C003)
- **First occurrence**: Initial identity/document verification process
- **Second occurrence**: Re-verification after modifications or additional submissions

### 2. **"Call Customer"** (appears twice in C002 and C003)
- **First occurrence**: Outbound call for clarification or to request missing information
- **Second occurrence**: Follow-up or confirmation call after adjustments are made

### 3. **"Adjust Account"** (appears once each in C002 and C003, but represents similar contextual patterns)
- Both occur after initial customer contact and before re-verification/approval

## Justification for Naming Changes

### **1. "Verify Documents"  "Initial Document Verification" / "Re-verification of Documents" / "In-Person ID Verification" / "Post-Adjustment Document Check"**
- **Contextual clues**: The "Additional Context" column distinguishes between first-time verification ("Agent checks identity documents"), re-verification after additional documents are submitted ("Agent re-checks documents"), physical verification ("Physical verification of ID"), and verification following account changes ("Re-verify docs after account adjustment").
- **Position in process**: First verification always occurs early in the workflow, while subsequent verifications follow specific triggers (document submission or account adjustment).

### **2. "Call Customer"  "Missing Documents Request Call" / "Discrepancy Clarification Call" / "Adjustment Confirmation Call" / "Return Request Call"**
- **Contextual clues**: Each call has a distinct purpose articulated in the context: requesting missing documents, clarifying account discrepancies, confirming adjustments, or asking a customer to return.
- **Position in process**: Initial calls seek information or action from customers, while later calls confirm completed actions or provide updates.

### **3. "Initiate Request"  "Initiate Request (Walk-in)" for C003**
- **Contextual clues**: C003's context specifies "Walk-in customer at branch," indicating a different initiation channel than the web portal submissions in C001 and C002.
- **Resource**: The resource "BranchReception" confirms this is an in-person initiation rather than digital.

### **4. "Adjust Account"  "Internal Account Adjustment"**
- **Contextual clues**: The context describes this as an internal action ("Adjust account details internally," "Internal correction") performed by back-office agents, not customer-facing adjustments.
- **Consistency**: Both instances (C002 and C003) involve similar internal adjustments following customer contact, making "Internal Account Adjustment" an accurate descriptor.

## Key Insights from This Analysis

1. **Same activity names mask process variations**: The original log's repeated activity names concealed important process differences that would affect process mining accuracy, performance metrics, and bottleneck analysis.

2. **Context determines meaning**: Activities that appear identical in name serve different functions based on:
   - Their position in the workflow sequence
   - The specific trigger that initiated them
   - The resource performing them
   - The explicit purpose described in the context column

3. **Renaming improves process clarity**: The revised names make it immediately clear what each activity accomplishes, enabling better process understanding, compliance monitoring, and optimization efforts.

This disambiguation is crucial for accurate process modeling in financial services, where compliance requirements demand precise tracking of verification steps, customer communications, and approval workflows.
