### Explanation of Logic

**1. Case Identification:**
All the logged activities were grouped into a single case, `Case_001`. This decision was based on the temporal proximity and the logical flow of user actions. The sequence of events suggests a continuous work session dedicated to preparing a "Quarterly Report." The user moves sequentially between different applications and documents (Word, Chrome for email, Acrobat, Excel) that are commonly related to producing such a report. Instead of splitting the case by each document opened, which would break the context of the overall task, treating this entire sequence as one case provides a coherent narrative of a single, overarching "Report Preparation" process.

**2. Activity Naming:**
Raw system actions were translated into higher-level, more meaningful process steps:
*   **`FOCUS`**: Was translated to `Start Editing Document` when the focus shifts to a document for the first time within a session, indicating the beginning of work on that specific artifact.
*   **`TYPING`**: Was translated to `Edit Document` for text-based applications (Word, Excel) and `Compose Email` for the email client, representing the core content creation or modification step.
*   **`SAVE`**: Was standardized to `Save Document` for all documents.
*   **`SWITCH`**: Was translated to `Switch Application`, indicating a change in the user's focus from one application to another.
*   **`CLICK` (Email actions)**: These were translated to specific, intuitive activities: `Open Email`, `Reply to Email`, and `Send Email`.
*   **`SCROLL`**: Was translated to `View Email` or `View PDF` depending on the application, representing the act of reviewing content.
*   **`HIGHLIGHT`**: Was translated to `Annotate PDF`, reflecting the specific action of marking content within a PDF.
*   **`CLOSE`**: Was translated to `Close Document` when a document window was closed.

**3. Additional Attributes:**
*   **`Application`**: The original application name is retained for context.
*   **`Resource`**: Derived from the `Window` title, this attribute specifies the exact document, email inbox, or PDF file being interacted with. This is crucial for understanding the scope and context of each activity within the case.
