**Bias in the lending decision**

By giving a non-legally protected group an incremental advantage in the lending decision, the POWL model introduces a subtle bias that may favor certain applicants. The incremental advantage is encoded in the Decision (F), where issuing the final decision after passing the XOR branch may be influenced by the XOR outcome. 

This bias can arise from the XOR structure, where:

*   If the applicant passes the XOR check (i.e., they are checked for local affiliation, `D`), they receive a subtle score uplift. This implies that certain individuals within the scope of the XOR (those with local affiliation) are given a marginal advantage over others, potentially disrupting the fairness of the loan decision.
*   If they opt to bypass the local affiliation check by choosing `skip`, they do not receive this affinity score. This absence can create a disparate impact on applicants who are not part of the checked non-legally protected group but receive the benefit, indirectly strengthening the application.

While marginal advantages may seem non-significant, these small biases can exacerbate and widen existing socioeconomic disparities in access to credit for individuals without local affiliation or other excluded groups. These unjustified credits may prefer applicants or certain classes ultimately resulting in economically damaging money distribution.
