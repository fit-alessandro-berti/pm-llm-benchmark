**Identified Anomalies in the Temporal Profile Model:**

The temporal profile reveals several irregularities in the average times and variations between activities:

- The interval from Receive (R) to Approve (P) averages approximately 25 hours but with an unusually low standard deviation of only 1 hour, indicating extremely consistent timing that lacks expected natural variation.
- From Approve (P) to Notify (N), there is a long average delay of 7 days with a high standard deviation of 2 days, pointing to both extended waits and significant inconsistency.
- The time from Assign (A) to Close (C) averages only 2 hours, which appears excessively short for a full claim handling lifecycle.
- Evaluate (E) to Notify (N) shows an average of just 5 minutes with low deviation, suggesting transitions that may be unrealistically rapid.

**Hypotheses on Potential Causes:**

These anomalies could arise from several factors in the process:
- Highly consistent R to P timing might result from automated approval rules or batch processing that applies uniform schedules regardless of claim details, reducing variability but potentially overlooking complex cases.
- Prolonged and variable P to N delays may stem from backlogs in notification queues, resource constraints among staff, or integration issues between approval and communication systems that cause unpredictable wait times.
- Rapid A to C transitions could indicate premature closures, possibly for low-value or straightforward claims, data entry errors, or cases where intermediate steps like evaluation are bypassed to meet internal metrics.
- The near-immediate E to N sequence might reflect automated notification triggers that fire right after evaluation, or logging practices that combine steps, potentially skipping required reviews or compliance checks.

Other contributing elements could include differences by claim type, specific adjusters/resources, or regional workload variations.

**Proposed Verification Approaches with SQL Queries:**

The following PostgreSQL queries on the `claim_events` table (joined with `claims` and `adjusters` where relevant) can help identify affected claims and correlate patterns:

```sql
-- Query for claims with unusually consistent or specific R to P timing, correlated with claim type
SELECT 
    c.claim_type,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) AS avg_rp_seconds,
    STDDEV(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) AS stdev_rp,
    COUNT(*) AS claim_count,
    MIN(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) AS min_rp,
    MAX(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) AS max_rp
FROM claim_events r
JOIN claim_events p ON r.claim_id = p.claim_id
JOIN claims c ON r.claim_id = c.claim_id
WHERE r.activity = 'R' 
  AND p.activity = 'P' 
  AND p.timestamp > r.timestamp
GROUP BY c.claim_type
HAVING COUNT(*) > 5
ORDER BY stdev_rp;
```

```sql
-- Identify claims with long P to N delays and link to adjusters/regions
SELECT 
    p.claim_id,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (n.timestamp - p.timestamp)) AS pn_seconds,
    c.claim_type,
    c.claim_amount,
    a.name AS adjuster_name,
    a.region
FROM claim_events p
JOIN claim_events n ON p.claim_id = n.claim_id
JOIN claims c ON p.claim_id = c.claim_id
LEFT JOIN adjusters a ON p.resource = a.name OR p.resource = a.adjuster_id::VARCHAR
WHERE p.activity = 'P' 
  AND n.activity = 'N' 
  AND n.timestamp > p.timestamp
  AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (n.timestamp - p.timestamp)) > 432000  -- > 5 days
ORDER BY pn_seconds DESC
LIMIT 50;
```

```sql
-- Detect quick A to C closures and count intervening activities
WITH event_times AS (
    SELECT 
        claim_id,
        MAX(CASE WHEN activity = 'A' THEN timestamp END) AS assign_ts,
        MAX(CASE WHEN activity = 'C' THEN timestamp END) AS close_ts,
        MAX(CASE WHEN activity = 'A' THEN resource END) AS assigned_resource
    FROM claim_events
    GROUP BY claim_id
)
SELECT 
    et.claim_id,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (et.close_ts - et.assign_ts)) AS ac_seconds,
    c.claim_type,
    COUNT(DISTINCT ce.activity) AS activities_between,
    et.assigned_resource,
    a.region
FROM event_times et
JOIN claim_events ce ON et.claim_id = ce.claim_id 
    AND ce.timestamp > et.assign_ts 
    AND ce.timestamp < et.close_ts
JOIN claims c ON et.claim_id = c.claim_id
LEFT JOIN adjusters a ON et.assigned_resource = a.name
WHERE EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (et.close_ts - et.assign_ts)) < 10800  -- < 3 hours
GROUP BY et.claim_id, et.assign_ts, et.close_ts, c.claim_type, et.assigned_resource, a.region
ORDER BY ac_seconds ASC;
```

These queries allow filtering for outliers, calculating actual deviations, and correlating with claim attributes or resources to test the hypotheses above. Additional filters or window functions can be added to focus on specific Z-score deviations from the profile averages.
