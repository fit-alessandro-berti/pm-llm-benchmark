The observed process follows a mandatory sequence of steps, with a highly reusable and optional loop structure in the middle section responsible for handling deficiencies in the application.

The process tree model is:

$$
\text{RA} \rightarrow \text{VF} \rightarrow \text{AE} \rightarrow * (\text{RMI} \rightarrow \text{VF}) \rightarrow \text{AD} \rightarrow \text{SC} \rightarrow \text{AC}
$$

### Explanation of the Structure:

1.  **$\text{RA} \rightarrow \text{VF} \rightarrow \text{AE}$ (Receive Application $\rightarrow$ Validate Form $\rightarrow$ Assess Eligibility):**
    *   This establishes the mandatory initial sequence common to all cases.

2.  **$* (\text{RMI} \rightarrow \text{VF})$ (Loop for Missing Info and Re-validation):**
    *   This represents the core loop. It models the repetitive nature observed in cases 1 and 3.
    *   **$*$ (Loop):** Indicates that the entire cycle inside can be executed zero or more times.
    *   **$\text{RMI} \rightarrow \text{VF}$ (Sequence):** Represents the flow: Missing information is requested ($\text{RMI}$), and subsequently, the forms are re-validated ($\text{VF}$) based on the new information, before the process attempts to exit the loop.
    *   *If the loop executes 0 times (Case 2), the process skips directly to $\text{AD}$.*
    *   *If the loop executes $N$ times (Case 3, where $N \ge 1$), it repeats the cycle $\text{RMI} \rightarrow \text{VF}$ until the system is satisfied.*

3.  **$\rightarrow \text{AD} \rightarrow \text{SC} \rightarrow \text{AC}$ (Approval Decision $\rightarrow$ Send Confirmation $\rightarrow$ Archive Case):**
    *   This represents the final, mandatory sequential steps that close the process instance, occurring only after the looping condition is resolved (i.e., enough information has been provided).
