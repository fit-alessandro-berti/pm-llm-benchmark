### Explanation of Logic
Events were grouped into **5 coherent cases** based on the primary **Window/Document** (logical unit of work, e.g., editing a specific doc or handling a particular email), ensuring analyst-friendly separation: `Document1.docx`, `Quarterly_Report.docx`, `Budget_2024.xlsx`, `Report_Draft.pdf`, and `Annual_Meeting_Email` (Email - Inbox renamed for specificity due to the targeted "Annual Meeting" actions). **SWITCH** events were assigned to the **ToWindow** case as they signal the start/resumption of work there. **Activity names** were elevated from raw actions (e.g., TYPING  "Draft Intro Paragraph" using Keys for context; CLICK  "Open Annual Meeting Email" using Action; generic like SCROLL  "Review [Content]") into descriptive, consistent steps (e.g., repeated "Save Document", "Activate Document") that reveal process patterns like drafting  saving  closing, while preserving granularity for discovery/conformance checking. Cases interleave naturally (e.g., Document1 paused for support tasks like Budget, which precedes its budget-reference edit), telling a story of multitasking in a report-preparation session. This structure supports standard PM analysis (e.g., process maps showing switches, bottlenecks in editing).
