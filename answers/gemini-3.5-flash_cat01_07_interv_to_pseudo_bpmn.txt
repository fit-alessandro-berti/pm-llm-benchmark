# BPMN-Like Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## 1. Roles & Participants (Lanes)
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk (Mary):** Initiates the process, logs invoices, matches line items, handles initial exceptions, and schedules payments.
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Supervisor (Rita):** Handles escalated exceptions, unresolved discrepancies, and missing PO issues for informal services.
*   **Purchasing Officer (Dan):** Verifies invoice details against the PO and confirms receipt of goods/services.
*   **Department Manager (Carlos):** Conducts final review and grants system approval.
*   **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** Provides support for contract verification and direct supplier communication during dispute resolution.
*   **Supplier (External Actor):** Sends invoices, revised invoices, and PO information.

---

## 2. Process Artifacts & Data Objects
*   **Invoice** (Input, received usually via Email)
*   **Purchase Order (PO)** (Reference document)
*   **Confirmation Email** (Sent from Purchasing to AP)
*   **System Notification / Email Request** (Automated trigger for Manager approval)
*   **Retrospective PO** (Created for informal service invoices)
*   **Supplier Contract** (Used for pricing disputes)
*   **Corrected Invoice** (Request from supplier in case of errors)

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process Flow

### **Phase 1: Initial Receipt & Triaging**
1.  **Start Event:** Invoice received by email (AP Clerk - Mary).
2.  **Gateway 1 [Is there a valid PO number?]:**
    *   **Path A [No PO / Informal Services]:** 
        *   *Standard Exception:* Mary sends the invoice back to the Supplier requesting more details. (End/Loop)
        *   *Alternative Exception (Rita's Variant):* If the invoice is for informal services, Mary escalates to AP Supervisor (Rita). Rita works with the Department Head to create a **Retrospective PO**. Once created, proceed to Step 3.
    *   **Path B [Yes, valid PO]:** Proceed to Step 3.
3.  **Activity:** Log Invoice into the Accounting System (Mary).
4.  **Activity:** Forward Invoice to Purchasing Department (Mary).

---

### **Phase 2: Purchasing Verification**
5.  **Activity:** Verify Invoice against PO (Purchasing Officer - Dan). Dan compares quantities, prices, and delivery charges.
6.  **Gateway 2 [Do details match the order?]:**
    *   **Path A [No / Discrepancy]:** 
        *   *Sub-process (Exception Handling):* Dan or Mary contacts the Supplier directly, or AP holds the invoice waiting for a **Corrected Invoice**. 
        *   *Escalation:* If pricing is disputed, Karen (Supplier Relationship Manager) checks the Contract and advises on the correct price.
        *   *Resolution:* Once corrected/clarified, repeat Step 5.
    *   **Path B [Yes / Correct]:** Dan sends a **Confirmation Email** to AP (Mary).

---

### **Phase 3: AP Matching**
7.  **Activity:** Match Invoice Line Items against the PO in the system (Mary).
8.  **Gateway 3 [Do line items match exactly?]:**
    *   **Path A [No / Discrepancy]:** Mary emails the Purchasing team to clarify.
        *   *Escalation:* If unresolved easily, Mary escalates to AP Supervisor (Rita). Rita coordinates with suppliers/departments to resolve.
        *   *Resolution:* Once resolved, return to Step 7.
    *   **Path B [Yes / Matches]:** Proceed to Step 9.

---

### **Phase 4: Manager Approval**
9.  **System Trigger:** The accounting system automatically sends an email notification to the Department Manager (Carlos) with the invoice, PO, and purchasing confirmation.
10. **Activity:** Review Invoice Details (Department Manager - Carlos).
11. **Gateway 4 [Manager Decision]:**
    *   **Path A [Reject / Hold]:** Carlos rejects or asks Mary to hold and investigate (e.g., due to budget discrepancies or unexpected items).
        *   *Resolution:* Loops back to AP Clerk (Mary) / AP Supervisor (Rita) for escalation and dispute resolution.
    *   **Path B [Approve]:** Carlos clicks "Approve" in the system.

---

### **Phase 5: Payment Scheduling**
12. **Activity:** Schedule Invoice for Payment in the next payment cycle (Mary).
13. **End Event:** Invoice paid and closed.

---

## 4. Key Exceptions & Rework Loops

| Exception Trigger | Handling Role | Resolution Path |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Missing PO Number** | AP Clerk (Mary) / AP Supervisor (Rita) | Send back to supplier OR create retrospective PO with department head. |
| **Discrepancy (Quantities/Pricing)** | Purchasing Officer (Dan) / SRM (Karen) | Dan requests corrected invoice from supplier, or Karen verifies against supplier contract. |
| **Unresolved Matching Discrepancy** | AP Supervisor (Rita) | Rita coordinates between departments and suppliers to clear discrepancies before routing back to normal flow. |
| **Manager Rejection (Budget/Suspicious items)** | Dept Manager (Carlos) | Invoice held; Mary/Rita must investigate the variance before it can be re-submitted. |

---

## 5. Ambiguities & Stakeholder Discrepancies (For Process Miner's Note)

*   **PO Check Timing (Mary vs. Dan):** Mary states she checks for a valid PO number *before* logging the invoice and forwarding it to purchasing. However, Dan mentions, *"If the PO number is missing, I tell Mary she needs to get that..."* This indicates that either:
    1. Some invoices bypass Mary's initial triage and go directly to Dan, or
    2. Mary sometimes misses the missing PO, requiring Dan to act as a secondary gatekeeper.
*   **Supplier Contact (Dan vs. Mary vs. Karen):** There is overlapping responsibility regarding supplier contact. Dan says Purchasing contacts suppliers directly. Mary says AP contacts suppliers. Karen (SRM) notes she is looped in to contact suppliers for key accounts. In a BPMN diagram, this should be modeled as a shared/collaborative subprocess for "Supplier Dispute Resolution."
