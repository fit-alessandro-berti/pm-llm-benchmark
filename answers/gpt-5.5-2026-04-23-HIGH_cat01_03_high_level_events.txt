## Proposed High-Level Process Steps

The low-level events can be grouped into the following higher-level manufacturing stages:

| Step # | High-Level Activity Name | Low-Level Events Included | Rationale |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Material Preparation** | Retrieve raw metal sheet; Scan barcode; Place metal sheet on table; Align metal sheet; Preheat metal sheet | These events prepare the raw sheet for processing. They include material retrieval, identification, positioning, alignment, and thermal preparation before welding. |
| 2 | **Welding / Assembly** | Pick up welding tool; Weld corner A; Weld corner B | These events represent the actual joining or assembly operation. Picking up the welding tool is included because it is the immediate setup action required for welding. |
| 3 | **Weld Quality Inspection** | Measure weld integrity | This is a quality-control step specifically focused on verifying the weld quality before downstream processing. It uses a quality sensor and produces an integrity score. |
| 4 | **Protective Coating and Curing** | Apply protective coating; Dry coating | These events form a surface treatment stage. The coating is applied and then dried/cured using heat, so they are logically part of the same finishing operation. |
| 5 | **Final Visual Inspection** | Visual check | This is the final manual quality check after processing and coating are complete. It validates the finished item before release or further handling. |

---

## Grouping Logic

The grouping is based on three main principles:

1. **Process intent**  
   Events that contribute to the same manufacturing objective are grouped together.  
   For example, retrieval, barcode scanning, placement, alignment, and preheating all serve to prepare the sheet for welding.

2. **Temporal and sequential proximity**  
   Events that occur close together in time and follow a stable sequence across cases are likely part of the same process stage.

3. **Resource and function similarity**  
   Events performed by related resources or serving a related operational function are grouped.  
   For example, welding actions by Operator B are grouped as assembly, while inspection events by sensors or inspectors are separated as quality checks.

## Resulting High-Level Process Model

The manufacturing workflow can therefore be summarized as:

```text
Material Preparation
     Welding / Assembly
     Weld Quality Inspection
     Protective Coating and Curing
     Final Visual Inspection
```

This abstraction preserves the main business and manufacturing meaning of the process while hiding unnecessary low-level operational detail.
