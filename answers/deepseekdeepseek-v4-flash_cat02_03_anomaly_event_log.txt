Based on the standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) process flow, the expected sequence of activities is:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition** (PR)
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition**
3. **Issue Purchase Order** (PO)
4. **Receive Goods**
5. **Vendor Invoice Received**
6. **Match Invoice to PO**
7. **Release Payment**
8. **Close Case**

Any significant deviation from this order can indicate operational inefficiencies, control weaknesses, or potential fraud. Below is an analysis of each case.

---

### Case 1001 – No Anomaly
The sequence follows the standard order exactly. All steps occur in the expected timeline. This case is normal and compliant.

---

### Case 1002 – Multiple Anomalies
**Observed sequence:**
1. Create PR  
2. **Issue PO**  
3. **Vendor Invoice Received**  
4. **Release Payment**  
5. Approve PR  
6. Receive Goods  
7. Match Invoice to PO  
8. Close Case  

**Issues:**
- **PO issued before PR approval** – The purchase requisition should be approved before a purchase order is created to ensure budget authorization and necessity. Issuing a PO without approval bypasses internal controls.
- **Invoice received and payment released before PR approval and goods receipt** – Paying an invoice before goods are received and before the invoice is matched to the PO is a severe internal control failure. This could lead to paying for undelivered goods, incorrect quantities, or fraudulent invoices.
- **Payment occurs before the matching step** – The three-way match (PO, goods receipt, invoice) is a key fraud prevention mechanism. Skipping it by paying first defeats the purpose.

**Why problematic:**  
These actions expose the organization to financial loss, duplicate payments, and potential collusion. They also indicate a breakdown in segregation of duties and approval workflows.

---

### Case 1003 – Premature Payment
**Observed sequence:**
1. Create PR  
2. Approve PR  
3. Issue PO  
4. **Release Payment**  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Receive Goods  
7. Match Invoice to PO  
8. Close Case  

**Issues:**
- **Payment released before receiving the invoice and before goods receipt** – A payment should only be made after the goods are received and the invoice has been matched to the PO and receipt. Here, payment occurs entirely out of sequence, meaning the organization paid for goods that may not yet exist, and without verifying the invoice.
- **Invoice and goods arrive after payment** – This creates reconciliation issues and increases the risk of overpayment or paying for undelivered items.

**Why problematic:**  
This violates the fundamental principle of “pay after receipt and verification.” It can lead to cash flow inefficiencies, loss of leverage in disputes, and higher fraud risk.

---

### Case 1004 – Goods Received Before Purchase Order
**Observed sequence:**
1. Create PR  
2. Approve PR  
3. **Receive Goods**  
4. Issue PO  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Match Invoice to PO  
7. Release Payment  
8. Close Case  

**Issues:**
- **Goods received before a purchase order is issued** – A PO is a formal commitment to purchase and serves as the basis for receiving and invoicing. Accepting goods without an active PO means the organization may not have a contractual agreement on price, terms, or specifications.
- The PO is issued *after* receipt, which essentially turns the PO into a “post-facto” approval. This can cause problems with inventory valuation, vendor disputes, and unauthorized procurement.

**Why problematic:**  
Receiving unordered goods can result in liability for payment without proper authorization, potential overstocking of unwanted items, and difficulty in matching invoices later. It also bypasses standard procurement controls.

---

## Summary of Anomalies

| Case ID | Anomaly Description | Root Problem |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| 1002 | PO before PR approval; payment before goods receipt, invoice, and matching | Bypassed approvals and payments controls; high fraud risk |
| 1003 | Payment before invoice and goods receipt | Fundamental violation of pay-after-receipt principle |
| 1004 | Goods receipt before PO issuance | Unauthorized receipt; lack of contractual commitment |

These anomalies should be investigated further, as they indicate process violations that could lead to financial losses, compliance issues, and operational inefficiencies.
