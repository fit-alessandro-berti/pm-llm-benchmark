**Explanation of the model:**

- **Existence/Init/Exactly_one:** Every product launch must begin with Idea Generation (IG) and end with Final Launch (FL). Key milestones like IG, AG, and FL happen exactly once.
- **Responded existence & Coexistence:** Technical Feasibility Check and Cost Evaluation always appear together (both needed before prototyping). Laboratory and User testing always coexist. Marketing Plan and Final Launch go hand-in-hand.
- **Response/Precedence/Succession:** Enforces the logical order — design must follow ideation, prototyping requires prior design/feasibility/cost checks, testing must precede approval, approval precedes marketing, and marketing precedes launch.
- **Alternate constraints:** Between DDPC and PCAG, no repetition of the same activity is allowed before reaching the next stage.
- **Chain constraints:** IG is immediately followed by DD; AG is immediately followed by MP; MP is immediately followed by FL — ensuring no interruptions in the final phases.
- **Nonsuccession/Nonchainsuccession:** Prevents logically invalid orders, e.g., Final Launch cannot precede Idea Generation or Design Draft, and Marketing cannot precede Prototyping.
