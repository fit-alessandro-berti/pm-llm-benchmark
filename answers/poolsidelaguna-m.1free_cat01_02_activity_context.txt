## Justification for Naming Choices

**1. "Initiate Web Request" vs. "Initiate Branch Request"**
- *Context*: Different entry channels (web portal vs. physical branch)
- *Naming rationale*: The activity type is the same (initiating a request) but the entry point affects downstream processing

**2. "Initial Document Verification" vs. "Re-verify Documents After [Action]"**
- *Context*: First verification (checking original documents) vs. subsequent verification (after corrections/updates)
- *Naming rationale*: The purpose differs - initial check vs. validating updated/correction documents

**3. "Call to Request Missing Documents"**
- *Context*: Used when verification fails due to incomplete documentation
- *Naming rationale*: Specific purpose of the call is retrieving missing items

**4. "Call to Clarify Account"**
- *Context*: Used to resolve account discrepancies identified during verification
- *Naming rationale*: Purpose is investigation/clarification rather than document collection

**5. "Follow-up Call for Confirmation"**
- *Context*: Post-correction confirmation that customer is satisfied with changes
- *Naming rationale*: Purpose is confirmation and closure verification

**6. "Call Customer to Return"**
- *Context*: Physical branch scenario where customer left mid-process
- *Naming rationale*: Specific purpose of getting customer back to continue service

**7. "Internal Account Adjustment"**
- *Context*: Backend corrections made after customer interactions
- *Naming rationale*: Indicates internal system modification rather than customer-facing action
