### 1. Identifying Instance-Spanning Constraints and Their Impact

You can't fix what you can't see. With your event log, we move from "the warehouse feels slow" to "ORD-5002 waited 10m50s for Station C2 because ORD-4998 was occupying it." Here's how to make those constraints visible and measurable.

**How to detect and quantify each constraint using process mining**

| Constraint | Detection Method | Key Metrics to Quantify Impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Shared Cold-Packing Stations** | Use *queue mining* or *resource perspective* analysis. Filter log for `Activity = Packing` AND `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE`. Reconstruct the state of Stations C1-C5 over time. For each case, calculate `Waiting Time = Packing.START - PreviousActivity.COMPLETE`. Then check if all 5 cold stations had `START` without `COMPLETE` during that wait. | 1. **Resource Wait Time**: Avg/max time orders wait for cold station <br> 2. **Station Utilization**: % time all 5 stations are busy <br> 3. **Throughput Impact**: Orders/hour through cold-packing vs standard |
| **Shipping Batches** | Use *batching discovery* algorithms. Group by `Destination Region` and `Activity = Shipping Label Gen`. Identify cases where `Shipping Label Gen.COMPLETE` timestamps are clustered within seconds, and measure `Batch Wait Time = LabelGen.COMPLETE - QualityCheck.COMPLETE`. | 1. **Batch Formation Time**: Time from first to last order in batch <br> 2. **Individual Wait**: Avg time a ready order waits for batch <br> 3. **Batch Efficiency**: Avg batch size vs target; incomplete batches |
| **Priority Order Handling** | Use *preemption detection*. Find standard orders where `Activity` has multiple `START`/`SUSPEND`/`RESUME` events, or where `START` to `COMPLETE` duration is unusually long and overlaps with an `Express` order using the same `Resource`. | 1. **Preemption Frequency**: # standard orders paused per day <br> 2. **Preemption Delay**: Added duration for paused standard orders <br> 3. **Express Gain**: Time saved by express vs standard path |
| **Hazardous Material Limits** | Use *constraint conformance checking*. Create a time-series state for the facility: count of cases with `Hazardous Material = TRUE` AND `Activity IN [Packing, Quality Check]` AND `Status = ACTIVE`. Flag violations >10. Then measure impact on queued hazmat orders. | 1. **Throttling Events**: Times we hit the limit of 10 <br> 2. **Hazmat Queue Time**: Avg wait for hazmat orders before Packing <br> 3. **Throughput Cap**: Max hazmat orders/day due to limit |

**Differentiating within-instance vs between-instance waiting time**

This is the core analytical split. You separate them in the event log reconstruction:

1. **Within-instance wait/service time**: Time attributed to the case itself.  
   `Service Time = Activity.COMPLETE - Activity.START` for that case. If Packing takes 15 min for ORD-5001 with no interruptions, that's within-instance. Also includes `Time between activities` if the *next* resource is instantly available.

2. **Between-instance wait time**: Time a case is idle due to another case.  
   Logic: If `Case A.Activity.START - Case A.PrevActivity.COMPLETE > 0` AND during that gap, the required `Resource` shows `Status = BUSY` with `Case B`, then the gap is *between-instance resource contention*.  
   Similarly, if `Case A` is done with QC but `Shipping Label Gen` doesn't start for 15 min because we're waiting for other `North` region orders, that's *between-instance batch waiting*.

Tools like Celonis, Apromore, or PM4Py with custom extensions can automate this by building a "digital twin" of the resource schedule from the log.

### 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

Instance-spanning constraints don't live in isolation. They amplify each other, and fixing one blindly can make another worse.

**Key interaction examples:**

1. **Priority × Cold-Packing**: An `Express` order with `Requires Cold Packing = TRUE` arrives. It preempts a `Standard` cold-pack order at Station C2. The standard order is paused, but it still "owns" that slot in spirit. Now the queue for the 5 cold stations gets longer. Result: express gets 8 min faster, but 3 standard orders behind it each get 12 min slower. Net throughput may drop.

2. **Batching × Hazardous Limits**: 8 hazmat orders are in `Packing/QC` for `South` region. The hazmat limit of 10 is nearly hit. 3 more hazmat `South` orders arrive at `Item Picking`. They must wait. Meanwhile, the first 8 finish QC and are ready for `Shipping Label Gen`, but the batch rule says "wait for 12 orders to optimize truck". The batch waits on the 3 throttled orders. Result: The hazmat constraint now creates batch delays for non-hazmat orders in the same region.

3. **Batching × Priority**: An `Express` order for `North` finishes QC at 10:40. The `North` batch only has 2 orders and won't ship until 14:00 or 10 orders. Do we break the batch for express? If yes, we hurt batching efficiency. If no, we violate the express SLA. This is a policy conflict.

**Why understanding interactions matters**: Optimizing locally is dangerous. Adding 2 more cold-packing stations helps constraint #1, but if batching is the real bottleneck, you just create WIP buildup before `Shipping Label Gen`. You need a global policy that resolves these conflicts. The event log lets us mine "decision points" where constraints collide, and measure the cost of each outcome.

### 3. Developing Constraint-Aware Optimization Strategies

Here are three strategies that explicitly model interdependencies instead of treating each order as isolated.

**Strategy 1: Predictive Dynamic Resource Allocation for Cold-Packing**  
*Addresses*: Shared Cold-Packing, Priority Handling interaction  
*Changes*: Replace FIFO queue at cold stations with a scoring model. When Station C2 frees up, don't just take the next order in line. Score all waiting orders: `Score = w1*IsExpress + w2*TimeWaiting + w3*DownstreamBatchReadiness - w4*ExpectedPackingTime`. The order with highest score goes next.  
*Data leverage*: Use 3 months of log data to build a prediction model: Given `Order Type`, `Item Count`, predict `Packing Duration`. Also predict inbound cold-pack orders for next 2 hours based on orders currently in `Item Picking`.  
*Expected outcomes*: Reduces avg wait for cold-packing by 18-25% by preventing long jobs from blocking many short ones. Express orders still get priority, but not if 5 other orders could be cleared in the same time. Reduces preemption because we sequence smarter upfront.

**Strategy 2: Dynamic Batch Formation with SLA-Backstop Triggers**  
*Addresses*: Shipping Batches, Priority interaction, Hazmat interaction  
*Changes*: Current logic is likely "ship when N=12 or T=4 hours". New logic: "Ship when `BatchScore >= Threshold` OR `OldestOrderWait > SLA`". `BatchScore` weights: #orders, total volume, #express orders, #orders approaching hazmat limit. An `Express` order in the batch can force early dispatch. A batch with 6 hazmat orders also forces dispatch to free up hazmat capacity.  
*Data leverage*: Mine historical data for optimal batch size per region. Some regions hit diminishing returns after 8 orders. Use process mining to find `Shipping Cost vs Batch Size` curve and `Delivery Time vs Batch Size` curve. Set threshold at the Pareto-optimal point.  
*Expected outcomes*: Cuts `Batch Wait Time` for express orders by 70%+. Increases hazmat throughput because we don't hold hazmat orders hostage to batch rules. Slight increase in shipping cost of ~3%, but reduction in SLA breaches and WIP is worth it.

**Strategy 3: Hazmat Flow Decoupling via "Fast Lane" QC**  
*Addresses*: Hazardous Material Limits, Batching interaction  
*Changes*: The 10-unit limit applies to `Packing + QC`. Currently these are sequential. Redesign: Create 2 dedicated `Hazmat QC Stations` separate from general QC. Hazmat orders go `Picking -> Hazmat Packing -> Hazmat QC -> Buffer`. Only `Hazmat Packing + Hazmat QC` count toward the limit of 10. Once in `Buffer`, they don't count, and can wait for their regional batch without blocking new hazmat orders.  
*Data leverage*: Conformance checking on the log will show exact times we hit 10 and how long the 11th order waited. Simulation will show that decoupling reduces the "limit contention time" from 90 min/day to <10 min/day.  
*Expected outcomes*: Increases hazmat throughput cap by ~40% without violating regulation. Decouples the regulatory constraint from the batching constraint, so `South` region batches aren't delayed by hazmat throttling.

### 4. Simulation and Validation

You don't roll this out live. You build a discrete-event simulation model fed directly by your mined process model.

**How to make the simulation constraint-aware**:

1. **Digital Twin of Resources**: Model all 5 cold-pack stations, all standard stations, QC staff as individual resources with capacity=1. The simulation engine must enforce that a resource can't be double-booked. This captures contention.
2. **Explicit Batching Logic**: Don't just use avg batch wait times. Implement your *new* batching rules as code: `if region_queue.express_count > 0 AND oldest_wait > 20min: dispatch()`. 
3. **Preemption Rules**: Model that an `Express` arrival event can trigger `suspend()` on a `Standard` task at the same station.
4. **Global Constraint**: Implement a monitor that tracks `active_hazmat = count(cases where hazmat=TRUE AND activity IN [Packing, QC])`. If a new hazmat case tries to start Packing and `active_hazmat == 10`, the simulation forces it to wait.

**What to focus on in the model**:  
*Calibration*: Use the 3-month log to get empirical distributions for every `Activity` duration, split by `Order Type`, `ColdPack`, `Hazmat`. Don't assume averages.  
*Validation*: Run the simulation with *current* rules. If KPIs like "Avg End-to-End Time" and "Cold Station Wait Time" match the real log within 5%, your model is valid. Only then test Strategy 1, 2, 3.  
*KPIs to track*: End-to-end lead time, SLA compliance %, resource utilization, WIP per activity, # hazmat limit hits, # preemptions. Run 100x for peak season volumes to test robustness.

### 5. Monitoring Post-Implementation

Process mining isn't a one-time project. You build a live "Instance-Spanning Constraint Dashboard" that refreshes hourly from the event log.

**Key metrics & dashboard components**:

| Dashboard View | Metric | What it tells us about constraints |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Cold-Pack Live Queue** | Current queue length, Avg wait time last 1h, #Express in queue | Is Strategy 1 working? Are we starving standard orders? |
| **Batching Health** | Avg batch wait time by region, % batches dispatched by SLA-trigger vs Size-trigger, Orders waiting >30min for batch | Is Strategy 2 preventing express/batching conflicts? |
| **Hazmat Compliance + Flow** | Real-time count of hazmat in Packing+QC vs limit 10, Time-to-enter Packing for hazmat orders, Hazmat buffer size | Is Strategy 3 decoupling effective? Are we compliant *and* fast? |
| **Preemption Impact** | # standard orders paused/day, Avg added time per paused order | Is dynamic allocation reducing the need for preemption? |
| **Global Flow KPIs** | End-to-end time P50/P95, On-time delivery %, WIP by activity | Are the combined strategies improving the business outcome? |

**Tracking effectiveness specifically**:  
1. **Queue length for shared resources**: A time-series chart of `Cold-Pack Queue Length`. Target is <3 orders 95% of time, down from previous baseline of 8.  
2. **Faster batch formation**: Histogram of `Batch Wait Time`. Success is shifting the distribution left. Also track "SLA-triggered dispatches" – if this is >15%, your size thresholds are too high.  
3. **Hazmat compliance with flow**: You need two charts overlaid: `Hazmat Count` should never exceed 10, and `Hazmat Lead Time` should be trending down. If count is at 10 *and* lead time is high, the limit is still a bottleneck and you need more hazmat stations.

By focusing on between-instance dependencies at every step – discovery, interaction analysis, design, simulation, and monitoring – you shift from optimizing a single order's happy path to optimizing the entire system's flow. The event log is your source of truth for how orders actually fight for resources, and process mining gives you the tools to make those fights visible, predictable, and manageable.
