## Analysis of Event Log

### 1. Normative Rule Violations

**Case 2** violates multiple strict process rules:

- **Order violation:** The credit check (08:20) was performed *after* risk assessment (09:00), contrary to the required sequence (Credit Check  Risk Assessment).
- **Missing mandatory activity:** After the loan decision (Rejected), the applicant was not informed. The rule "Inform Applicant must occur after Loan Decision" was ignored.
- **Illegal activity after rejection:** Finalize Documentation was performed despite the loan being rejected, which is explicitly forbidden.

**Case 1** and **Case 3** comply with all normative rules. Their activity sequences follow the prescribed order and decision logic.

### 3. Interpretation and Justification

- **Case 1** is nearly perfect; the missing follow-up call may be due to resource constraints or a policy that considers it optional. The process is efficient and meets all hard rules.
- **Case 2** shows serious process drift: an employee likely performed risk assessment prematurely (perhaps based on incomplete data) and then tried to “fix” the order by doing the credit check later. The rejection triggered a mistaken finalization of documentation, possibly because the same staff member was unaware of the no-finalization rule. The complete absence of informing the applicant is a critical oversight.
- **Case 3** exhibits timing delays, likely due to weekends (Jan 12–14 includes a Saturday and Sunday) or backlog. The inform step (51 h vs. 48 h) is only slightly over the recommendation and might be considered acceptable in practice. The lack of a follow-up call is a common concession when caseload is high. Despite these, the core sequence and decision logic are correct, so the case is still valid under the strict rules.
