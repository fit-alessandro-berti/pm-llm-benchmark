### **Overview of the Business Process**

This process models how a company handles incoming **customer requests** for products or services, which may be either **standard** (off-the-shelf) or **custom** (tailored to specific customer needs). The workflow ensures appropriate validation, feasibility checks, approvals (if needed), and final invoicing before confirming the request to the customer.

---

### **Step-by-Step Breakdown**

#### **1. Start Event  Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**
- **Purpose**: The process begins when a customer submits a request.
- This is the entry point for all incoming orders or inquiries.

---

#### **2. Gateway (XOR): "Check Request Type"**
- **Type**: Exclusive (XOR) gateway – only **one** outgoing path is taken based on the request type.
- **Logic**:
  - If the request is **Standard**  follow the **Standard Path**.
  - If the request is **Custom**  follow the **Custom Path**.

---

### **Standard Request Path**

#### **3a. Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
- **Purpose**: Validate basic details of a standard request (e.g., product ID, quantity, customer info).
- Assumes the product exists in the catalog and follows predefined rules.

#### **4a. Gateway (AND): "Run Parallel Checks"**
- **Type**: Parallel (AND) gateway – **all** outgoing paths are executed **simultaneously**.
- **Purpose**: Conduct independent but required checks in parallel to improve efficiency.

##### **Task C1: "Credit Check"**
- Verify the customer’s creditworthiness or payment history.

##### **Task C2: "Inventory Check"**
- Confirm product availability in stock.

#### **5a. Join (AND Gateway – implicit synchronization)**
- **Purpose**: Wait for **both** checks (C1 and C2) to complete before proceeding.
- Ensures no step is missed before moving forward.

#### **6a. Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**
- **Purpose**: Based on inventory status and logistics, determine the expected delivery date.
- Only possible after validation and checks are complete.

---

### **Custom Request Path**

#### **3b. Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**
- **Purpose**: Assess whether the requested customization is technically and operationally feasible (e.g., engineering constraints, resource availability, cost).

#### **4b. Gateway (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**
- **Exclusive decision**:
  - **If Yes**  Proceed to prepare a quotation.
  - **If No**  Notify the customer of rejection.

##### **Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"**
- Generate a detailed price and timeline proposal for the custom request.

##### **Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"  End Event**
- Inform the customer that the request cannot be fulfilled.
- **Process ends here** for infeasible custom requests.

---

### **Convergence Point: Post-Validation/Quotation**

After either:
- **Standard path**: Task D ("Calculate Delivery Date"), **or**
- **Custom path**: Task E1 ("Prepare Custom Quotation")

… the process **converges** to a shared decision point regarding **approval requirements**.

> **Note**: The pseudo-diagram implies that both paths feed into the same subsequent gateway, even though it’s not explicitly shown as a merge. This is a logical convergence in the business flow.

### **Approval Decision Gateway**

#### **7. Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**
- **Purpose**: Determine if the request (standard or custom) requires managerial approval.
- **Triggers for approval might include**:
  - High value/order amount
  - Special terms (e.g., extended payment)
  - Custom complexity
  - Policy thresholds

##### **If Approval IS Needed**:
- **Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"**
  - Submit the validated request/quotation to a manager for review.

- **Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**
  - **If Yes**  Proceed to **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**.
  - **If No**  **Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"**

    - **Task H** leads to a **loop back**:
      - For **Custom requests**: Return to **Task E1** ("Prepare Custom Quotation") – e.g., to revise pricing or scope.
      - For **Standard requests**: Return to **Task D** ("Calculate Delivery Date") – e.g., to adjust terms or find alternatives.

    > **Important**: The loop implies that the process must retain context (e.g., request type) to route correctly upon rework. This is a critical design consideration in actual BPMN modeling (often handled via data objects or process variables).

##### **If Approval IS NOT Needed**:
- Skip approval steps and go directly to **Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**.

---

### **Final Steps (Common to All Paths)**

#### **8. Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**
- Create the official invoice based on validated/quoted terms.

#### **9. Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**
- Notify the customer of order confirmation, including invoice, delivery date (for standard), or custom terms.

#### **10. End Event**
- Process concludes successfully.

> **Note**: The **rejection path** (Task E2) ends earlier and **does not** reach the final confirmation steps.

### **Key Observations & Clarifications**

1. **Path Divergence & Convergence**:
   - The process cleanly splits based on **request type** (standard vs. custom).
   - After initial handling, both paths **converge** before the approval decision, enabling unified downstream logic.

2. **Parallelism in Standard Path**:
   - Credit and inventory checks run **in parallel** to reduce cycle time—common in order fulfillment.

3. **Approval Logic is Unified**:
   - Regardless of request type, the **same approval rules** apply, promoting consistency.

4. **Rework Loop**:
   - Rejection at approval **does not terminate** the process but triggers **re-evaluation**.
   - The loop-back targets differ by path:
     - Custom  revise quotation (E1)
     - Standard  revise delivery/terms (D)
   - This requires the process engine to **remember the original path**, typically via a data flag (e.g., `requestType`).

5. **End States**:
   - **Successful completion**: After sending confirmation.
   - **Early termination**: Only in the custom-infeasible path (E2  End).
