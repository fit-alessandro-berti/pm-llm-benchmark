### General Approach Used

1. **Canonical Event Mapping**  
   - Created a mental mapping table linking the two naming schemes:  
     - `Order Received`  `OrderReceived`  
     - `Order Validated`  `OrderValidation` (but kept separate due to namedifference policy)  
     - `Payment Processed`  `PaymentCheck` (different semantics  keep separate)  
     - `Item Shipped`  `Shipping` (different wording  keep separate unless timestamps are *strictly* identical).  

2. **Timestamp Tolerance**  
   - Defined a strict “<2seconds” window for considering two events the same occurrence.  
   - This avoids accidental merging of duplicate captures that might legitimately be recorded twice by different subsystems.

3. **Attribute Aggregation**  
   - When two events were deemed identical (none in this data set), all attributes from both logs would have been combined, with a note indicating source duplication.  
   - In the merged log above each row lists attributes originating from its primary source; missing fields are shown as “—” to make clear which system supplied them.

4. **Chronological Ordering**  
   - Primary timestamps were chosen perrow: if an event appears in both logs within tolerance, SystemA’s timestamp (the “primary timeline”) is used for ordering because the prompt states LogA is considered primary even though its times reflect *receipt* rather than start.  
   - When a row originates from only one log, that log’s timestamp becomes the ordering key.

5. **Documentation of Ambiguities**  
   - Every borderline case (exact 2second differences) was explicitly noted in the reasoning table to show why we kept events separate despite nearmatches. This transparency is important for downstream analytics or audits.
