The visualization is a workflow/process model for handling a request.

It begins on the left with activity **a: “register request.”** From there, the process branches into possible assessment steps:

- **b: “examine thoroughly”**
- **c: “examine casually”**
- **d: “check ticket”**

These activities feed into **e: “decide.”** After the decision, the process can either:

- go to **g: “pay compensation”**, then to **z: “end”**
- go to **h: “reject request”**, then to **z: “end”**
- loop back through **f: “reinitiate request”**, which sends the case back into the examination/checking part of the process before another decision is made

The black dots on the arrows represent routing or synchronization points between activities. The inset legend in the lower-right explains the notation for different control-flow patterns:

- **XOR-split / XOR-join**: exclusive choice, where only one branch is taken or merged
- **AND-split / AND-join**: parallel branching or synchronization
- **OR-split / OR-join**: inclusive branching, where one or more branches may be taken or merged

Overall, the diagram shows a request-processing workflow with conditional branching, possible rework/reinitiation, and two final outcomes: compensation paid or request rejected.
