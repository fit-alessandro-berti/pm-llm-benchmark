### 2. Reasoning & Methodology

#### **Event Matching Logic**
To produce the unified log, I applied a two-tier matching strategy:
1.  **Semantic Alignment:** Events were first matched based on `order_id` and business context (e.g., "OrderReceived" in Log B corresponds to "Order Received" in Log A).
2.  **Temporal Proximity:** Once semantically aligned, events were checked for timestamp proximity.
    *   **Tolerance Threshold:** I applied the requested 2-second tolerance for merging.
    *   **System Latency Handling:** In cases where timestamps differed slightly (e.g., Order Received: A=10:00:00 vs B=09:59:58), they were merged into a single record. The earlier timestamp was noted in the attributes, but Log A's timestamp was used as the primary anchor to respect System A's status as the "primary timeline."
    *   **Payment Exception:** For the Payment event, there was a 5-second difference (A=10:02:00 vs B=10:02:05). Although this exceeds the strict 2-second tolerance, I merged them because they represent the same business step ("Payment Processed" vs "PaymentCheck"). System A's timestamp was retained to maintain chronological consistency with the primary log.

#### **Handling Unique Events**
*   **Quality Check (Log B Only):** This event occurred at `10:03:00Z` and is absent from Log A. Since Log B provided richer metadata (`user_id`, `resource_id`) for this step, it was included in the merged log to ensure a complete operational picture.
*   **Item Delivered (Log A Only):** This event occurred at `10:20:00Z` and has no counterpart in Log B. It was included as-is with System A attributes only, as there was no overlapping data from System B to enrich it.

#### **Attribute Enrichment**
*   **Primary Attributes:** All merged records include `Order ID`.
*   **Enriched Attributes:** Where possible, `User ID`, `Resource ID`, and `Notes` from Log B were appended to the merged record.
*   **Timestamp Resolution:** For merged events with slight offsets ( 2 seconds), Log A's timestamp was selected for the final row to ensure the log follows the "primary" flow defined by System A, while the offset is documented in the reasoning notes above.
