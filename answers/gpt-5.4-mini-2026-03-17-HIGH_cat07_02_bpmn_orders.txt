This is a **BPMN-style business process diagram** showing how an order moves through four departments arranged in swimlanes:

- **Sales**
- **Production**
- **Procurement**
- **Warehouse**

### Overall flow
The diagram starts in **Sales** on **Monday morning**. Sales:

1. **Checks whether the order is complete**
2. **Checks if the customer already exists in the database**
   - If not, the customer is **added to the database**
3. Then Sales **sends the order details** to the other departments
4. After the product is shipped, Sales **drafts the invoice**

### Production lane
Production receives the order details and:

1. **Plans production**
2. Waits for material status / confirmation
3. **Manufactures the product**
4. Marks the product as complete

### Procurement lane
Procurement also receives the order details and handles materials:

1. A **material compensation subprocess** appears inside this lane
   - **Order materials**
   - **Return material** if needed
2. Then it **checks materials**
3. If materials are fine, it notifies Production
4. If there is a problem, it can lead to **order cancellation**

### Warehouse lane
Warehouse receives the finished product:

1. Gets the **product complete** message
2. **Sends the product**
3. Sends back a **product sent** message

### Visual conventions
- **Rounded rectangles** = tasks
- **Diamonds** = decision points / gateways
- **Circles** = start/end or intermediate events
- **Dashed arrows** = message flows between departments
- **Cylinder icon** = database

### In short
The visualization shows an **end-to-end order fulfillment process**: Sales validates the order and customer, Production makes the product, Procurement manages materials, Warehouse ships the product, and Sales then creates the invoice.
