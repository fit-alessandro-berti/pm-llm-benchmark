# BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## **Process Overview**  
The Invoice Approval Process begins when an invoice is received and concludes with scheduling payment. The process includes validation, matching, approval, and exception handling. Multiple roles participate, including Accounts Payable (AP), Purchasing, Department Management, and Supplier Relationship Management.

---

## **1. Activities/Tasks**  
| Task ID | Activity Name                          | Description                                                                 |
|---------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| T1      | Receive Invoice                        | Invoice is received via email or other means.                               |
| T2      | Check for PO Number                    | Verify if the invoice is associated with a valid Purchase Order (PO).        |
| T3      | Send Back to Supplier (if no PO)       | Request additional details or corrected invoice if PO is missing.           |
| T4      | Log Invoice into System                | Record the invoice in the accounting system.                                |
| T5      | Confirm Receipt of Goods/Services      | Purchasing verifies that goods/services were received as per PO.            |
| T6      | Match Invoice to PO                    | Compare invoice line items with the PO details (quantities, prices, etc.).  |
| T7      | Identify Discrepancy                   | Detect mismatches between invoice and PO (e.g., pricing, quantity).         |
| T8      | Resolve Discrepancy                    | Contact supplier or purchasing to clarify or correct issues.                |
| T9      | Escalate to Supervisor                 | Forward unresolved discrepancies to AP Supervisor (Rita).                   |
| T10     | Manager Approval                       | Department Manager reviews and approves/rejects the invoice.                |
| T11     | Schedule Payment                       | Add approved invoice to the payment cycle.                                  |
| T12     | Create Retrospective PO (Exception)    | For informal contracts, create a PO after the fact.                       |
| T13     | Reject Invoice (Exception)             | Discard invoice if issues cannot be resolved.                               |

---

## **2. Sequence Flow**  
1. **Start**: Invoice is received (T1).  
2. **Decision Gateway 1**: Is there a valid PO number?  
   - **No**  T3 (Send back to supplier)  T1 (Return to start).  
   - **Yes**  T4 (Log invoice)  T5 (Confirm receipt)  T6 (Match invoice to PO).  
3. **Decision Gateway 2**: Does the invoice match the PO?  
   - **No**  T7 (Identify discrepancy)  T8 (Resolve discrepancy).  
     - If resolved: Proceed to T5.  
     - If unresolved: T9 (Escalate to supervisor).  
   - **Yes**  T10 (Manager approval).  
4. **Decision Gateway 3**: Is the invoice approved by the manager?  
   - **No**  T13 (Reject invoice).  
   - **Yes**  T11 (Schedule payment).  
5. **End**: Payment is scheduled.  

---

## **3. Gateways/Decision Points**  
- **Gateway 1 (PO Check)**: Determines if the invoice has a valid PO.  
- **Gateway 2 (Match Check)**: Validates alignment between invoice and PO.  
- **Gateway 3 (Manager Approval)**: Final approval decision by the department manager.  

---

## **4. Roles/Participants**  
| Role                         | Responsible Tasks                                                                 |
|------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Accounts Payable Clerk**   | T1, T2, T3, T4, T6, T8, T9, T11                                                  |
| **Purchasing Officer**       | T5, T8                                                                            |
| **Department Manager**       | T10                                                                               |
| **AP Supervisor**            | T9, T12                                                                           |
| **Supplier Relationship Mgr**| T8 (indirectly, via supplier communication)                                     |

---

## **5. Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs**  
- **Inputs**: Invoice, Purchase Order (PO), Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation.  
- **Outputs**: Approved Invoice, Rejected Invoice, Payment Schedule.  
- **Documents**:  
  - Invoice (primary document).  
  - PO (linked to invoice).  
  - Confirmation Email (from Purchasing to AP).  
  - Escalation Ticket (for supervisor intervention).  

---

## **6. Exceptions/Variations**  
- **Missing PO**: Invoice is returned to supplier for correction (T3).  
- **Discrepancies**:  
  - Resolved via communication with supplier/purchasing (T8).  
  - Escalated to AP Supervisor if unresolved (T9).  
- **Unresolved Issues**: Supervisor may create a retrospective PO (T12) or reject the invoice (T13).  
- **Manager Rejection**: Invoice is discarded if suspicious (T13).  

---

## **7. Ambiguities/Conflicting Accounts**  
- **Escalation Path**: Mary mentions escalating to her supervisor, while Rita (AP Supervisor) handles exceptions. These roles may overlap or report differently.  
- **Payment Scheduling**: Carlos assumes AP schedules payment after approval, but Rita’s role in exceptions suggests potential delays or manual intervention.
