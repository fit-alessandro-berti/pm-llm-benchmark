### 5.Recommendations to eliminate the bias  

1. **Remove the +5 boost** – If professionalassociation membership does not directly relate to job performance, the adjustment should be eliminated entirely.  

2. **If the association is genuinely relevant**, require **documented, jobrelated justification** (e.g., the club provides leadership training that is directly applicable to the role) and apply the same **objective criteria** to *all* candidates (e.g., a certification, a specific training program).  

3. **Standardize scoring** – Keep CulturalFit as a pure assessment, with no discretionary “adjustments.” Any modifications must be:  
   - **Predefined** in the hiring policy,  
   - **Transparent**, and  
   - **Consistently applied** to every candidate.  

4. **Blind scoring** – Strip identifiers (e.g., name, address, club membership) from the data that assessors see until after the scoring is complete. This reduces the chance that unconscious bias influences the evaluation.  

5. **Audit & monitor** – Periodically compare hiring outcomes across groups (e.g., local vs. nonlocal, club member vs. nonmember). If a statistical disparity appears, investigate and adjust the process.  

6. **Legal review** – Have the policy examined by legal counsel to ensure it does not run afoul of equalopportunity regulations, especially if the club membership can be linked to protected characteristics.  

7. **Training for recruiters** – Educate hiring managers and HR staff on implicit bias, fair scoring, and the importance of basing decisions on **jobrelevant criteria** only.
