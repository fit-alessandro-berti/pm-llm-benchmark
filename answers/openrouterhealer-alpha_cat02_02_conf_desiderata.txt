# Analysis of Loan Application Process Cases

## Case 1: Fully Compliant

**Normative Rules:**  All constraints satisfied
- Sequence: Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering rules respected
- Approved loan properly finalized documentation before closing

**Desiderata:**  One recommendation missed
-  Credit check within 24 hours (completed 30 minutes after application)
-  Inform applicant within 48 hours (completed 2 hours after decision)
-  No Follow-up Call recorded before case closure

**Interpretation:** This case represents optimal process execution. The 30-minute credit check suggests efficient resource allocation. The missing follow-up call is understandable珦ince all documentation was finalized promptly and the applicant was already informed, a follow-up might have been deemed redundant.

## Case 2: Multiple Serious Violations

**Normative Rules:**  Multiple violations
1. **Incorrect sequence:** Risk Assessment performed before Credit Check (violation of ordering rule: "Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment")
2. **Missing required activity:** No "Inform Applicant" step (violates "Inform Applicant must occur after Loan Decision")
3. **Contradictory action:** Finalize Documentation performed after loan rejection (explicitly forbidden: "do not finalize documentation before closing the case" for rejected loans)

**Desiderata:** 
-  Credit check timing technically within 24 hours (though out of sequence)
-  Cannot assess "inform applicant" timing due to missing activity
-  No Follow-up Call (though process was already non-compliant)

**Interpretation:** This appears to be a systemic process failure. The risk assessment occurring before the credit check suggests either:
- An urgent need to assess risk without complete information, possibly due to time pressure
- A process design flaw where parallel activities were incorrectly implemented
- A data entry error where timestamps were reversed

The missing "Inform Applicant" step combined with finalizing documentation after rejection suggests either confusion about requirements or intentional deviation to appear more "efficient" while actually violating core fairness principles.

## Case 3: Normatively Compliant with Timing Issues

**Normative Rules:**  All constraints satisfied
- Correct sequence maintained throughout
- Proper decision-based branching (approved  finalized documentation)
- All required activities present

**Desiderata:**  Multiple recommendations not met
-  Credit check took 49 hours (exceeds recommended 24 hours)
-  Applicant notification took 51 hours (exceeds recommended 48 hours)
-  No Follow-up Call recorded

**Interpretation:** The timing delays suggest possible resource constraints or backlog. The 49-hour credit check delay might indicate:
- A weekend or holiday period between Jan 12-14
- Limited credit analysis resources
- A backlog of applications needing processing

The extended notification time (51 hours) after decision may have been due to:
- Weekend scheduling affecting communications
- The need to prepare additional information for the applicant
- Internal review processes before external communication

## Summary

**Process Health Assessment:**
- **Case 1:** Excellent execution (minor recommendation missed)
- **Case 2:** Critical failures requiring immediate attention
- **Case 3:** Process-compliant but suboptimal timing

**Recommendations:**
1. For Case 2: Investigate why the standard sequence was bypassed and why documentation was finalized after rejection. This represents serious compliance and potential regulatory issues.
2. For Case 3: Analyze the root cause of timing delays to determine if they're due to resource constraints or process inefficiencies.
3. Consider implementing automated alerts for sequencing violations (like Case 2's reversed activities) to prevent similar issues.
4. While Follow-up Calls are only recommended, their absence across all cases suggests this recommendation may not be valued or understood in the current process culture.

The most concerning aspect is Case 2's violations, which represent not just inefficiency but potential regulatory non-compliance and unfair treatment of applicants. The timing issues in Case 3, while not violating strict rules, indicate opportunities for process improvement.
