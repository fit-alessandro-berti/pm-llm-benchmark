The **bias is exhibited in the Group B log**, but its effect is a **systematic advantage for Group B and a corresponding disadvantage for Group A**, the protected group.

### Key difference between the logs

Group A applicants are all:

- `LocalResident = FALSE`
- `CommunityGroup = None`
- `ScoreAdjustment = 0`
- Final decisions appear to depend mostly on the unadjusted preliminary score.

Group B applicants are all:

- `LocalResident = TRUE`
- Some have `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`
- Those community-group members receive a `+10 Community Boost`
- The boost carries through to manual review and final decision.

### How the bias manifests

The clearest bias mechanism is the **ScoreAdjustment column** in Group B.

| Case | Group | LocalResident | CommunityGroup | Preliminary Score | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
|---|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| P002 | A | FALSE | None | 710 | 0 | 710 | Rejected |
| U002 | B | TRUE | None | 710 | 0 | 710 | Rejected |
| U003 | B | TRUE | Highland Civic Darts Club | 695 | +10 | 705 | Approved |

The concerning case is **U003**. U003 starts with a lower preliminary score, `695`, than P002 and U002, both of whom have `710`. Yet U003 receives a community-based boost and is approved, while the 710-score applicants without the community affiliation are rejected.

That suggests the process is not relying only on the underlying score. Instead, membership in a particular community group — and possibly local-resident status — is giving certain Group B applicants preferential treatment.

### Role of `LocalResident`

`LocalResident` is perfectly aligned with group membership in this data:

- Group A: all `FALSE`
- Group B: all `TRUE`

That makes `LocalResident` a potential proxy for protected/unprotected status. If local residents are eligible for favorable treatment and protected-group applicants are never local residents, the system can indirectly discriminate even if it does not explicitly use the protected-group label.

### Role of `CommunityGroup`

The `CommunityGroup` attribute is especially important. In Group B:

- U001 belongs to `Highland Civic Darts Club` and receives `+10`
- U003 belongs to `Highland Civic Darts Club` and receives `+10`
- U002 has no community group and receives no adjustment

So the favorable adjustment appears tied to community-group membership. Since this community group appears only in the unprotected group, the adjustment benefits Group B applicants but not Group A applicants.

### Conclusion

The process shows evidence of **bias in favor of Group B**, the unprotected group. The bias manifests through a **Community Boost** applied only to certain Group B applicants based on `CommunityGroup`, with `LocalResident` acting as a possible proxy or enabling condition.

This can create systematic decision differences: protected Group A applicants receive no comparable adjustment, while unprotected Group B applicants with the favored community affiliation can be approved despite having lower underlying preliminary scores.
