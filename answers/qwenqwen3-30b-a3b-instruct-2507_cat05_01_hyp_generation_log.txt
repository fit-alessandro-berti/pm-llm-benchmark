Here is a **fully self-contained prompt** designed to guide a target LLM toward identifying anomalies, hypothesizing root causes, and generating relevant SQL queries for investigation—without providing any hints or scaffolding:

---

You are a process mining and operational analytics expert analyzing a real-world order fulfillment process using event log data. Your task is to **identify anomalies and undesirable behaviors** in the provided event log (`order_event_log`) that deviate from the expected normal process flow.  

The expected process flow is:
1. Register Order (Sales)  
2. Perform Credit Check (Finance)  
3. Validate Stock (Warehouse)  
4. Confirm Shipment (Logistics)  
5. Ship Goods (Logistics)  
6. Issue Invoice (Finance)  
7. Receive Payment (Finance)

Each case (`case_id`) should follow this sequence in chronological order. However, deviations may indicate process issues such as:
- Out-of-sequence activities
- Missing steps
- Duplicate or redundant events
- Early or late execution (timing anomalies)
- Resource misassignment
- Illogical event dependencies (e.g., payment before credit check)

---

### Your Task:

1. **Analyze the event log data** (including `order_event_log`, `orders`, and `resources`) to **identify specific anomalies** in the workflow execution.  
2. For each anomaly detected, **hypothesize plausible root causes** (e.g., system bug, incorrect process design, training gap, policy violation, data entry error, etc.).  
3. **Propose one or more targeted SQL queries** (using PostgreSQL syntax) that can be run against the `order_event_log`, `orders`, and `resources` tables to **investigate your hypotheses further**.  
   - Queries must be **precise, executable, and logically grounded** in the data structure.
   - Avoid generic or overly broad queries (e.g., `SELECT * FROM order_event_log`).
   - Include filtering by `case_id`, `activity`, `timestamp`, `resource`, or `additional_info` where relevant.
   - Use window functions, joins, or aggregations only when necessary for the analysis.

Do **not** provide explanations of the queries unless they are essential for understanding the anomaly detection logic. Focus on **clear, actionable, and insightful SQL** that supports deeper investigation.

---

> **Note**: The LLM should **not** assume the data is complete or correct. It must treat the log as potentially flawed and act as an investigator uncovering patterns, inconsistencies, and risks.
