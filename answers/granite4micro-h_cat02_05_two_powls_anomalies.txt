### Standard Hire-to-Retire Process Overview

The typical “Hire-to-Retire” workflow involves several key stages:
1. **Post Job Advertisement** - Advertising the position to attract candidates.
2. **Screen Candidates** - Initial filtering based on resumes or application criteria.
3. **Conduct Interviews** - Evaluating shortlisted candidates in person or virtually.
4. **Make Hiring Decision** - Deciding whether to extend an offer to any candidate(s).
5. **Onboard Employee** - Preparing the new hire for their role, including paperwork and training.
6. **Add to Payroll System** - Officially enrolling the employee into the organization’s payroll system.

Typically, these steps follow a linear sequence without revisiting earlier stages unless there's an error or special condition (e.g., rejecting a candidate).

### Analysis of POWL Models

#### Model 1 Analysis

```python
model1 = StrictPartialOrder(nodes=[Post, Screen, Decide, Interview, Onboard, Payroll, Close])
model1.order.add_edge(Post, Screen)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Decide)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Interview)
model1.order.add_edge(Decide, Onboard)
model1.order.add_edge(Onboard, Payroll)
model1.order.add_edge(Payroll, Close)
```

**Observations:**
- **Edges `Screen -> Decide` and `Screen -> Interview`:** The model allows for two divergent paths after screening. This could mean that candidates can either be sent directly to the decision stage or move on to interviews without a clear preference or additional criterion-based filtering.
- **Lack of explicit looping mechanisms:** There is no indication of any re-entry into previous stages (e.g., if a candidate fails an interview, they may not return for another round).
  
**Anomalies Identified:**
1. **Parallel Paths after Screening:** The inclusion of `Screen -> Decide` and `Screen -> Interview` deviates from the norm by suggesting multiple potential routes without clear criteria on when one path should be chosen over the other.
2. **Absence of Revisiting or Looping Constructs:** There’s no mechanism to revisit earlier stages (e.g., a failed interview might allow for re-interviews), which is typical in robust hiring processes.

### Comparison and Decision

**Which Model Aligns More Closely with Normative Hire-to-Retire?**

**Model 1** appears to align more closely with a normative Hire-to-Retire process for several reasons:
- **Linear Sequence:** It maintains a largely linear sequence of activities, reflecting the typical progression from posting a job ad through hiring and onboarding.
- **Absence of Unnecessary Loops or Parallel Paths Without Justification:** The only deviation is in branching at screening, which could be explained by criteria-based routing (e.g., some candidates may proceed directly to interviews based on certain qualifications). This does not fundamentally alter the expected order but rather represents a more flexible variant.
- **Lack of Revisiting Mechanisms:** Though Model 1 lacks explicit looping mechanisms for error correction or re-interviews, these are rare exceptions in most standard processes. The absence is less detrimental than introducing new loops that could obscure process clarity.

**Model 2**, while innovative with its use of `XOR` and looping constructs, introduces anomalies such as:
- **Exclusive Payroll Decision (`XOR`):** This deviates significantly from normative practice by limiting the process to either finalize payroll or close out without it. In real-world scenarios, there are usually additional checks (e.g., HR approval) before closing a case.
- **Looping Onboarding:** The looping onboarding construct suggests that any issue in this stage must be manually addressed outside standard operational flow. This is less efficient and can lead to ambiguity regarding when the process truly closes.

### Conclusion

**Model 1** aligns more closely with the normative Hire-to-Retire process due to its adherence to a linear sequence, minimal deviations (such as branching at screening), and overall simplicity that reflects typical hiring procedures. While both models incorporate some innovative approaches—Model 1 with parallel pathways after screening and Model 2 with `XOR` and looping onboarding—the former’s deviations are more aligned with common practice variations, making it a better representation of the standard Hire-to-Retire workflow.
