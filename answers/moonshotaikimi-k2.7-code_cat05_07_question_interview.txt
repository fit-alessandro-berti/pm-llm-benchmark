Here are the targeted, open-ended questions I would ask, organized by theme, to build a clearer picture of the onboarding process.

### 1. Process Intake & Documentation
1. How does a landlord typically first engage with the company—by phone, email, a web form, or in person—and what information is captured at that very first touchpoint?
2. What does the property coordinator actually check when reviewing documents? Are there standard checklists or compliance thresholds?
3. What happens when documentation is missing or outdated? Who follows up with the landlord, how often, and is there a deadline or SLA before the process is paused or rejected?
4. Is there a formal “acceptance” or “go/no-go” gate before the property details are entered into the central database?

### 2. Property Data & Systems
5. Who is responsible for entering the property details, and how is the accuracy of that data verified?
6. What is considered the single source of truth for a property record—the central database, the CRM, or the rental portal—and how do those systems stay in sync?
7. What property attributes are *required* before the process can move forward, and which are optional?

### 3. Assigning the Property Manager
8. Can you walk me through how a property manager is actually selected? Is it automated, a committee decision, or up to a regional supervisor?
9. How is “current workload” measured—by number of properties, units, tenants, or some weighted score?
10. What counts as a manager’s “geographic specialization,” and how are territory boundaries defined?
11. When you say a manager has a track record with “unique requirements,” what specific experience or past performance data do you look at?

### 4. Landlord Alignment Meeting
12. Is the introductory meeting always required, or can it be skipped in some cases?
13. What decisions or agreements are expected to come out of that meeting, and are they formally documented or signed off?
14. How are disagreements resolved if the landlord’s expectations—such as rental price, pet policy, or tenant responsibilities—conflict with company policy?

### 5. Inspections, Repairs & Readiness
15. How are the photographer and inspector scheduled relative to each other—sequentially, in parallel, or independently?
16. What happens if the inspector finds issues? How do you decide whether repairs are mandatory versus recommended?
17. Who pays for repairs: the landlord, the company, or is it negotiable? Are there cost thresholds that require approval?
18. What is the criteria for a property to “pass” inspection, and who has the authority to sign off on it?

### 6. Marketing, Pricing & Activation
19. How does the digital marketing team decide which channels to use for a given property?
20. What inputs go into the internal pricing models, and who has final say on the listed rental price?
21. Can you describe how short-term promotional discounts or incentives are approved?
22. What exactly triggers “activation” in the rental portal—inspection sign-off, marketing completion, pricing approval, or something else?
23. Are marketing and activation ever done before all quality checks are complete?

### 7. Tenant Screening & Lease Finalization
24. What are the standard tenant screening criteria, and how do you ensure fair housing compliance throughout that process?
25. When multiple tenants are interested, how do you prioritize among them? Are there objective scoring rules, or is it largely judgment-based?
26. What is the approval authority for negotiating lease terms or offering concessions?
27. Who drafts or finalizes the lease agreement, and under what circumstances does it go to legal review?
28. How is the security deposit collected and held, and who is responsible for recording the move-in date?

### 8. Quality Checks, Audits & Handoffs
29. At what point in the process do the compliance officer, financial analyst, and IT checks typically occur—before or after activation, before or after lease signing?
30. What does each of those reviewers specifically look for, and what happens if one of them rejects or flags something?
31. Is there a formal sign-off or approval gate that says the property is fully onboarded, and who gives it?

### 9. Exceptions, Escalations & Edge Cases
32. Can you give me examples of properties that require special insurance, and how that changes the process?
33. How are custom lease clauses handled? Is there a limit to what can be customized, and who approves exceptions?
34. What happens if a landlord refuses to make required repairs or provide updated documents?
35. Have you encountered situations where a property is de-activated or removed from the portfolio after onboarding? What triggers that?

### 10. Communication, Tracking & Timing
36. How do the regional office, property manager, landlord, and support teams communicate with each other—email, a project management tool, a CRM, or a combination?
37. How is the overall timeline tracked, and what are the typical SLAs or expected durations for each major phase?
38. Which steps in the process are the biggest sources of delay or back-and-forth, and how are those bottlenecks managed?
39. Is there a dedicated owner or process manager who monitors the end-to-end onboarding, or does accountability shift entirely from one role to the next?
40. How do you know the process is working well—what metrics, feedback loops, or retrospective reviews are in place?

These questions should help surface the implicit rules, handoffs, decision points, and pain points that make the process complex.
