### 3. How These Factors Increase Cycle Times and Recommendations

**Why delays occur:**
- **Handoff and queue waiting:** Tickets that require L2 sit idle while waiting for an available specialist. If L2 capacity is low or they react only after a delay, the ticket’s lead time grows quickly.
- **Afternoon investigations:** Any ticket that reaches the investigation phase after midday risks not being finished before endofshift, incurring an automatic overnight penalty.
- **Process inefficiency:** Separate L1 and L2 investigations imply a lack of integrated diagnostics or effective handover, forcing the customer issue to restart in the specialist queue.

**Recommendations to address the bottlenecks:**

1. **Rightsize L1 and L2 capacity:**  
   Analyse workload and occupancy rates. If L1 agents are too busy to start investigations promptly (as in Case 104), add L1 staff or redistribute tasks. If L2 queues are long (as in Case 105), consider adding L2 resources or providing L1 with better tools/support to resolve more cases without escalation.

2. **Set timebased SLAs for internal handoffs:**  
   Define targets, e.g., “Investigation must start within 60 minutes of assignment” and “Escalation must be acknowledged by L2 within 1 hour”. Alert supervisors when thresholds are breached.

3. **Introduce a “followthesun” or lateshift handover:**  
   For tickets that enter investigation late in the day, a planned handover to another shift or region could avoid the hard overnight stop and dramatically reduce cycle time.

4. **Streamline the escalation process:**  
   - Enable collaborative solves where L1 works with L2 directly instead of throwing the ticket over the fence.  
   - Ensure L1 provides complete diagnostic notes so L2 does not reinvestigate from scratch.  
   - Use intelligent routing to assign complex tickets to L2 earlier, avoiding the “L1 investigates then escalates” doubleeffort pattern.

5. **Monitor and visualise key time intervals:**  
   Track “Assign  Investigation Start”, “Escalation  L2 Pickup”, and “Investigation  Resolve” as leading indicators of process health. A dashboard with these metrics will help managers detect bottlenecks before they become systematic.
