The visualization is a **BPMN-style process flow diagram** for preparing a shipment, organized into **three horizontal swimlanes**:

- **Logistics** (top)
- **Secretary / Dispatch of goods in the computer hardware shop** (middle)
- **Warehouse** (bottom)

### Main flow
1. **Start event:** “Ship goods”
2. The process begins with a **parallel gateway** splitting work.
3. In the middle lane, the shipment method is **clarified**.
4. A decision is made: **“Special handling?”**
   - **Yes**  get **3 offers from logistics companies**, then **select logistics company and place order**
   - **No**  check whether **insurance is required**
5. If insurance is needed, the flow goes to **“Insure parcel”** in the logistics lane, then rejoins the process.
6. The package label is **written**, and the paths merge again.
7. Meanwhile, in the warehouse lane, **goods are packaged**.
   - A **timer event** indicates **48 hours**
   - If delayed, the process can go to **“Notify customer about delay”**
8. The warehouse flow rejoins the main path.
9. The process continues with **filling out shipment paperwork**.
10. Then the package is **moved to the pickup area**.
11. **End event:** “Shipment prepared”

### What the diagram shows
It illustrates how shipping preparation is handled across departments, with:
- **decision points** for special handling and insurance,
- **parallel activities** between packaging and administrative/logistics tasks,
- and a **timed delay handling step** in the warehouse.
