### Rationale for Added Constraints

1.  **New Activities Introduced**: 
    *   `CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge`: Explicit activities representing the retrieval/viewing of sensitive attributes.
    *   `BiasMitigationCheck`: A mandatory activity (e.g., algorithmic fairness test, rule validation) that must occur after sensitive attributes are accessed.
    *   `ManualReview`: A human-in-the-loop activity required for high-stakes decisions.
    *   `Approve`, `Reject`: Explicit decision outcomes (refining `FinalDecision`).

2.  **Coexistence Constraints** (`coexistence`):
    *   **`Approve`/`Reject`  `ManualReview`**: Ensures that no final decision is made without human oversight, preventing fully automated biased rejections/approvals.
    *   **`CheckApplicant...`  `BiasMitigationCheck`**: Guarantees that if the process inspects a sensitive attribute, a fairness verification step is also present in the trace.

3.  **Response Constraints** (`response`):
    *   **`CheckApplicant...`  `BiasMitigationCheck`**: Enforces that accessing sensitive data *triggers* a subsequent fairness check eventually.
    *   **`BiasMitigationCheck`  `Approve`/`Reject`**: Ensures the mitigation check is not a dead-end; a decision must follow.

4.  **Precedence Constraints** (`precedence`):
    *   **`BiasMitigationCheck`  `Approve`/`Reject`**: **Critical for bias mitigation.** A decision (especially `Reject`) is only valid if a `BiasMitigationCheck` has *already occurred* earlier in the trace. This prevents decisions based *solely* on the sensitive attribute check.
    *   **`ManualReview`  `Approve`/`Reject`**: Ensures human review happens *before* the decision, not after.

5.  **Succession Constraints** (`succession`):
    *   Combines Response and Precedence for `BiasMitigationCheck` and Decisions, enforcing a strict "Check -> Decide" lifecycle where the check is a mandatory prerequisite and the decision is a mandatory consequence.

6.  **Chain Response Constraints** (`chainresponse`):
    *   **`CheckApplicant...`  `BiasMitigationCheck` (Immediate)**: Forces the `BiasMitigationCheck` to be the *very next activity* after a sensitive attribute is checked. This eliminates the possibility of any intermediate processing (e.g., a risky heuristic calculation) occurring between seeing the sensitive data and the fairness guard.

7.  **Non-Succession Constraints** (`nonsuccession`):
    *   **`CheckApplicant...`  `Approve`/`Reject` (Direct)**: Explicitly forbids a direct transition from viewing a sensitive attribute to making a decision. This acts as a hard syntactic guard against "if Race==X then Reject" patterns encoded directly in the control flow.
