**Explanation of the Transformation Logic**

1. **Case Identification:**  
   The log was segmented into logical work sessions (cases) based on changes in application focus and distinct workflow phases:
   - **Case 1**: Editing *Document1.docx* – a continuous editing session.
   - **Case 2**: Handling an email related to the Annual Meeting – from opening the inbox to sending the reply.
   - **Case 3**: Reviewing/revising the PDF report draft (*Report_Draft.pdf*) – includes navigation and highlighting activities specific to reviewing content.
   - **Case 4**: Updating financial data in *Budget_2024.xlsx* – a focused spreadsheet editing session.
   - **Case 5**: Further editing of *Document1.docx* after returning from the email task, adding a reference section.

2. **Activity Naming:**  
   Lowlevel actions were mapped to higherlevel process steps that are meaningful for analysis:
   - **Focus**  identifies the start/end of an edit session.
   - **Typing**  descriptive of content creation or correction.
   - **Save**  captures document persistence points.
   - **Switch**, **Click**, **Scroll**, **Highlight**  capture navigation and interaction steps that contribute to workflow understanding (e.g., switching applications, opening specific items).

3. **Attribute Inclusion:**  
   For each event we kept the original timestamp as the “Timestamp” attribute; a unique **Case ID** was assigned based on logical segmentation described above.

This transformed event log provides a clear narrative of the user’s work flow across different applications and documents, suitable for process mining analysis to identify bottlenecks, efficiency gaps, or typical activity durations.
