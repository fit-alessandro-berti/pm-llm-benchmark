To determine which model more closely aligns with a normative version of the "Hire-to-Retire" process, we will first analyze the structure of both models, identify their anomalies, and then compare their impact on process integrity.

---

### **Normative Hire-to-Retire Process Sequence**
Typically, a standard Hire-to-Retire process should follow this logical sequence:
1. **Post_Job_Ad** $\rightarrow$ 2. **Screen_Candidates** $\rightarrow$ 3. **Conduct_Interviews** $\rightarrow$ 4. **Make_Hiring_Decision** $\rightarrow$ 5. **Onboard_Employee** $\rightarrow$ 6. **Add_to_Payroll** $\rightarrow$ 7. **Close_Case**.

---

### **1. Analysis of POWL Model 1**

#### **Structure & Flow:**
* `Post` must occur before `Screen`.
* After `Screen`, the model splits:
  * One path executes `Screen -> Decide -> Onboard -> Payroll -> Close` in a strict linear sequence.
  * The other path executes `Screen -> Interview`. Crucially, `Interview` has no outgoing edges (no successors).

#### **Identified Anomalies:**
1. **Decision Before Interview:** Because there is no causal link (edge) from `Interview` to `Decide`, the hiring decision (`Make_Hiring_Decision`) can happen concurrently with or even **before** the interviews are conducted. 
2. **Out-of-Order/Dangling Interview:** Since `Interview` has no successor constraints, a candidate can be onboarded, added to payroll, and the case can be closed *before* the interview is actually marked as complete. The interview can theoretically happen at any point after screening, even after case closure.

---

### **2. Analysis of POWL Model 2**

#### **Structure & Flow:**
* After `Post`, the process splits:
  * One path executes `Post -> Screen` (with `Screen` having no successor).
  * The other path executes `Post -> Interview -> Decide -> loop_onboarding -> xor_payroll -> Close`.
* `loop_onboarding` is a loop operator: `*(Onboard, skip)`.
* `xor_payroll` is an exclusive choice (XOR) operator: `X(Payroll, skip)`.

#### **Identified Anomalies:**
1. **Interview/Decision Before Screening:** Because `Screen` has no outgoing edges and runs in parallel with the rest of the workflow, the system allows the HR department to conduct interviews, make hiring decisions, onboard the employee, and close the case *before* the screening step even begins.
2. **Skipping Payroll (Critical Compliance Violation):** The XOR operator allows the process to choose the `skip` path instead of `Add_to_Payroll`. This means an employee can be hired, onboarded, and the case closed without ever being added to the payroll system.
3. **Infinite Onboarding Loop:** The loop structure `*(Onboard, skip)` means that after onboarding once, the process can choose to loop and onboard the same employee multiple times. While onboarding might occasionally require minor follow-ups, repeating the entire onboarding activity infinitely is a process design flaw.

---

### **3. Decision: Which Model is Closer to the Normative Process?**

**POWL Model 1 aligns much more closely with the normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

#### **Justification:**

* **Process Integrity and Compliance:** 
  The most severe anomaly in either model is the **optional payroll** (`xor_payroll`) in Model 2. In any real-world HR process, adding a hired employee to the payroll is legally and operationally mandatory. Model 1 ensures that `Add_to_Payroll` is a strictly mandatory step that *must* occur before the case can be closed. 
  
* **Correctness of Core Sequence:** 
  In Model 1, the core sequence of `Post -> Screen -> Decide -> Onboard -> Payroll -> Close` is strictly preserved. Every single hired employee is guaranteed to go through screening, onboarding, and payroll setup. The only flaw is that `Interview` is decoupled and can run in parallel, which—while a process anomaly—does not break the transactional integrity of the hire itself (e.g., it could represent a fast-tracked candidate where the interview step is updated asynchronously in the system).

* **Severity of Model 2's Flaws:** 
  Model 2 allows for a highly dysfunctional workflow where a candidate is interviewed, decided on, onboarded multiple times, and the case is closed without ever being screened or put on the payroll. This represents a fundamental breakdown of the process.

**Conclusion:**  
While both models have anomalies, **Model 1** preserves the mandatory, linear flow of the critical milestones (Screening $\rightarrow$ Decision $\rightarrow$ Onboarding $\rightarrow$ Payroll $\rightarrow$ Closure), making it the superior and more normative model.
