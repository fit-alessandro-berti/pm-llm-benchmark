### 3. Explanation of Gateways

In BPMN, gateways control the flow of the process. Here is how they function in this specific model:

*   **XOR Gateway (Exclusive Gateway):**
    *   *Symbolism:* Represented in your text as `Gateway (XOR)`.
    *   *Meaning:* Only **one** path can be taken out of the gateway. It represents a decision where conditions are mutually exclusive.
    *   *Usage in this process:*
        *   **Request Type:** A request cannot be both Standard and Custom simultaneously.
        *   **Feasibility:** Customization is either Feasible or Not Feasible.
        *   **Approval Needed:** Either approval is required, or it is not.
        *   **Approval Granted:** Approval is either Yes or No.
*   **AND Gateway (Parallel Gateway):**
    *   *Symbolism:* Represented as `Gateway (AND)`.
    *   *Meaning:* The flow splits into multiple paths that happen **concurrently**, or multiple paths must complete before the flow continues (Join).
    *   *Usage in this process:*
        *   **Run Parallel Checks:** The Credit Check (C1) and Inventory Check (C2) happen at the same time. The process cannot calculate the delivery date (Task D) until **both** checks are finished.
