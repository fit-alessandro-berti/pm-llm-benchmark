# Anomaly Analysis: Procure-to-Pay Event Log

## Standard P2P Process Flow (Reference)
The canonical Procure-to-Pay sequence with key control points:
1. **Create Purchase Requisition**  2. **Approve Purchase Requisition**  3. **Issue Purchase Order**  4. **Receive Goods**  5. **Vendor Invoice Received**  6. **Match Invoice to PO (3-way match)**  7. **Release Payment**  8. **Close Case**

**Critical Control Principles:**
- **Authorization before commitment**: PR must be approved before PO issuance
- **Receipt before payment**: Goods must be verified received before payment
- **Three-way match**: Invoice must match PO and Goods Receipt before payment
- **Segregation of duties**: No single actor should control the full cycle

###  Case 1001 — **COMPLIANT**

###  Case 1002 — **SEVERE MULTIPLE CONTROL FAILURES**

| Timestamp | Activity | Anomaly |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| 2024-02-01 08:40 | Issue Purchase Order | **PO issued BEFORE PR approval** (approval occurs 2 days later at 08:00 on Feb 3) |
| 2024-02-03 07:15 | Release Payment | **Payment released BEFORE**: goods receipt, invoice matching, AND PR approval |
| 2024-02-03 08:00 | Approve Purchase Requisition | **Retroactive approval** — after PO issued AND payment released |
| 2024-02-04 09:00 | Receive Goods | Goods received **after payment already released** |
| 2024-02-05 08:00 | Match Invoice to PO | Three-way match performed **after payment** |

**Why this is critical:**
- **Financial risk**: Payment made for goods not yet received, invoice not verified, PR not authorized
- **Fraud vulnerability**: Enables fictitious vendor schemes, duplicate payments, unauthorized purchases
- **Audit failure**: No evidence of approval at time of commitment; payment precedes all verification
- **Segregation of duties breakdown**: Same actor appears to control requisition, ordering, and payment

###  Case 1003 — **PREMATURE PAMENT RELEASE**

| Timestamp | Activity | Anomaly |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| 2024-02-08 11:00 | Release Payment | **Payment released 1 day BEFORE invoice received**, 2 days BEFORE goods receipt, 3 days BEFORE matching |

**Why this is critical:**
- **Three-way match bypassed entirely**: Payment occurs without any verification of price, quantity, or receipt
- **Cash flow risk**: Funds disbursed with no evidence of obligation validity
- **Vendor dispute exposure**: No leverage if goods are damaged, short-shipped, or never delivered
- **Suggests**: Manual override, emergency payment without controls, or system misconfiguration

###  Case 1004 — **SEQUENCE VIOLATION (Goods Before PO)**

| Timestamp | Activity | Anomaly |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| 2024-02-06 09:00 | Receive Goods | **Goods received 15 minutes BEFORE PO issued** |

**Why this is problematic (moderate severity):**
