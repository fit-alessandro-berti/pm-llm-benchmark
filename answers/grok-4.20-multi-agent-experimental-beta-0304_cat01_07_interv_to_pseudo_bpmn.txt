**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

**Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Preparation  
**Scope:** From invoice receipt to scheduling for payment (excluding actual payment execution)  
**Trigger:** Receipt of supplier invoice (typically by email)

### Participants (Pools/Lanes)
- **Accounts Payable (AP) Clerk** (Mary) – primary process owner
- **Purchasing** (Dan)
- **Department Manager** (Carlos)
- **AP Supervisor** (Rita) – exception handling only
- **Supplier Relationship Manager** (Karen) – supporting role for supplier communication

### Artifacts/Data Objects
- Supplier Invoice
- Purchase Order (PO)
- Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation
- Matching Report (system-generated)
- Approval Record

---

### Process Flow (Main Happy Path)

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (email to AP)

**AP Clerk Lane:**
1. **Task: Validate PO Reference**  
   - Check if invoice contains a valid PO number.

**Exclusive Gateway: Has Valid PO?**
   - **No**  Send request for PO/revised invoice to supplier (Karen assists)  **Rework Loop** back to "Validate PO Reference" once response received.
   - **Yes**  **Task: Log Invoice in Accounting System**

2. **Task: Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation**

**Purchasing Lane:**
3. **Task: Verify Invoice against PO** (compare quantities, prices, delivery charges, etc.)
   
**Exclusive Gateway: Matches PO & Receipt Confirmed?**
   - **Yes**  **Task: Send Confirmation** to AP (email + system update)
   - **No**  **Task: Resolve Discrepancy** (contact supplier directly or via Karen, or return to AP)  **Rework Loop** back to AP Clerk for re-matching or supplier correction

**AP Clerk Lane:**
4. **Task: Perform Three-Way Match** (Invoice + PO + Confirmation)

**Exclusive Gateway: Match Successful?**
   - **Yes**  System automatically triggers notification to Department Manager
   - **No**  **Task: Clarify Discrepancy** with Purchasing  if unresolved  **Escalation** to AP Supervisor

**AP Supervisor Lane (Exception Path):**
5. **Task: Handle Exception** (Rita)
   - Coordinate with Purchasing, Department Manager, and/or Karen
   - For non-PO invoices: create retrospective PO or decide to reject
   - Once resolved  Return invoice to normal flow (back to Step 3 or 4)

**Department Manager Lane:**
6. **Task: Review & Approve Invoice**
   - Reviews invoice, PO, confirmation, and any notes
   - System shows all supporting documents

**Exclusive Gateway: Manager Approves?**
   - **Yes**  Invoice status updated; returns to AP
   - **No** (suspicious amount, unexpected items, budget issue)  **Task: Reject or Request Investigation**  returns to AP Clerk or Supervisor (rework loop)

**AP Clerk Lane:**
7. **Task: Schedule for Payment** (add to next payment run)

**End Event:** Invoice Approved and Scheduled for Payment

---

### Key Exceptions & Variations (Noted in Interviews)

- **Missing PO (common)**: Handled initially by AP Clerk  escalated to Karen for supplier follow-up  may require Rita to create retrospective PO.
- **Pricing/Quantity Discrepancies**: Can be resolved at Purchasing level or escalated. Karen is frequently involved for contract interpretation.
- **Unresolvable Issues**: Loop back to supplier for corrected invoice; in rare cases, invoice may be rejected entirely (not explicitly modeled as a separate end event due to low frequency).
- **Manager Rejection**: Occurs infrequently ("once every couple of months"). Leads to investigation rather than automatic rejection.
- **System Automation**: After successful matching + confirmation, the system sends automatic approval request to the manager.

### Consolidated Notes on Ambiguities
- All interviewees largely agree on the core sequence: PO validation  Purchasing confirmation  Matching  Manager approval  Payment scheduling.
- Slight differences in who initiates supplier contact (Mary, Dan, or Karen) depending on the issue — modeled as flexible involvement of Karen.
- Rita’s role is purely exception-driven; she does not perform routine tasks.
- No interviewee mentioned parallel activities or multiple approvers; the model assumes a single Department Manager approver.

This textual model can be directly translated into a BPMN diagram with:
- One main pool (AP) and collaboration pools for Purchasing and Manager
- Multiple exclusive gateways
- A clear rework loop from the "Match Successful?" and "Has Valid PO?" gateways
- An exception sub-process or attached event for the AP Supervisor lane

The model captures the "as-is" process as described by the stakeholders with high fidelity while maintaining logical coherence.
