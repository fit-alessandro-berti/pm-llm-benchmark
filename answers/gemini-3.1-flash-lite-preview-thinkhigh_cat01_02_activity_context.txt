### 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

By reviewing the process flow, we see three specific activities that recur with distinct purposes:

*   **Verify Documents:**
    *   *Initial occurrence:* Standard identity check (Onboarding).
    *   *Subsequent occurrences:* Remediation or validation check (triggered by additional information or account adjustments).
*   **Call Customer:**
    *   *Initial occurrence:* Requesting missing information or clarifying discrepancies.
    *   *Subsequent occurrences:* Status update or follow-up communication.
*   **Initiate Request:** While not recurring *within* a case, its source varies (WebPortal vs. BranchReception). While the prompt asks for contextual renaming of recurring activities, differentiating the initiation based on the source adds significant value for process mining.

### 4. Justification
*   **Verification:** Splitting into "Initial" and "Re-Verification" clarifies that the second check is a quality control gate triggered by exceptions (like document uploads or internal account fixes), rather than a redundant repetition of the first step.
*   **Calling:** By distinguishing "Clarification" (information gathering) from "Follow-up" (status communication), we enable management to measure how many customer interactions are due to friction (missing info) versus proactive service (updating the customer).
*   **Initiation:** Distinguishing between "Digital" and "In-Person" allows the business to analyze the performance of different intake channels separately.
