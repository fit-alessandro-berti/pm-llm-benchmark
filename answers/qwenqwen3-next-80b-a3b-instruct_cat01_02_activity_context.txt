###  **Justification for Renaming Choices**

#### 1. **“Verify Documents”  “Verify Initial Documents” / “Verify Resubmitted Documents”**
- **Contextual Clue**:  
  - First occurrence always follows *Initiate Request* and precedes any customer interaction or correction. It is the **first pass** of document validation.
  - Second occurrence always follows a customer-provided update (e.g., upload or return) and is a **re-verification** after correction.
- **Naming Logic**:  
  - “Initial” signals the first validation attempt.  
  - “Resubmitted” explicitly ties the re-verification to a prior customer action, distinguishing it from the initial check.  
  - This avoids ambiguity and clearly separates *first-time* from *post-correction* validations.

#### 2. **“Call Customer”  Three distinct names**
The original “Call Customer” appears in three distinct contexts. Each requires a unique name reflecting the **purpose** and **stage** of the call:

- **“Call Customer for Missing Docs”** (C001):  
  - Trigger: Incomplete documents  Call is **requesting additional evidence**.  
  - Distinct from other calls because it’s **pre-correction** and **document-centric**.

- **“Call Customer for Clarification”** (C002):  
  - Trigger: Account discrepancy  Call is **investigative**, not document-related.  
  - Purpose: To *understand* an issue, not to request paperwork.  
  - This is a **diagnostic call**, distinct from document requests or confirmations.

- **“Call Customer for Confirmation”** (C002, C003):  
  - Trigger: After an internal adjustment (e.g., account correction or document resubmission).  
  - Purpose: To **confirm resolution** and ensure customer awareness/agreement.  
  - This is a **closure-oriented** call, signaling the process is nearing completion.

- **“Call Customer to Return”** (C003):  
  - Unique context: Customer physically present at branch but stepped away.  
  - This is an **operational reminder**, not a support or verification call.  
  - Naming reflects **logistics**, not content validation.  
  - Important to distinguish because the customer is already on-site — this is a **branch workflow** nuance.

#### 3. **“Adjust Account” — Left unchanged**
- This activity occurs only once per case and is always associated with **internal corrections** triggered by verification failures or clarifications.
- No ambiguity in context — always occurs *after* a call and *before* re-verification.
- No need to rename — its role is consistently defined.

#### 4. **“Initiate Request” and “Approve Request” — Left unchanged**
- These are **unique, unambiguous endpoints** of the process.
- “Initiate” always starts the case; “Approve” always ends it.
- No repetition or contextual variation  no renaming needed.

###  **Summary of Naming Principles Applied**
| Principle | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Temporal Position** | First vs. second occurrence of verification  “Initial” vs. “Resubmitted” |
| **Trigger Source** | Call triggered by missing docs vs. account discrepancy vs. post-adjustment  different verbs and objects |
| **Agent Intent** | “Clarification” (investigate), “Confirmation” (validate resolution), “Return” (logistics) |
| **Channel Consistency** | “Call Customer to Return” reflects branch-specific workflow; others reflect remote support |
| **Avoid Ambiguity** | Each unique context gets a unique label, even if same verb is used |
