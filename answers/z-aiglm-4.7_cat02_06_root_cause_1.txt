### 2. Root Cause Analysis of Performance Issues

#### A. The "Level-2 Escalation" Bottleneck
The most significant driver of delays is the escalation process to specialized teams (Level-2 Agents).

*   **Evidence:** Compare **Case 105** (Escalated) vs. **Case 101** (Not Escalated).

*   **Cause:** It appears that once a ticket is escalated to Level-2, it enters a queue with a very low priority or severe resource shortage, leading to massive idle times before a specialist even looks at the issue.

#### B. "End-of-Day" Stalling (Overnight Delay)
A consistent pattern appears in all "Slow" cases (102, 104, 105) regarding the final stages of the process.

*   **Evidence:** Look at the time between *Investigate Issue* and *Resolve Ticket*.
    *   **Fast Cases:** Resolution happened immediately after investigation (e.g., Case 101: 1 hour; Case 103: 30 mins).
    *   **Slow Cases:** There is a consistent gap of approximately **19 hours** between Investigation and Resolution.
        *   Case 102: Investigated at 14:00  Resolved next day at 09:00.
        *   Case 104: Investigated at 13:00  Resolved next day at 08:00.
*   **Cause:** If an issue is not investigated and resolved by early afternoon, it sits idle overnight. This suggests that the workflow lacks a "same-day closure" protocol for tickets that reach the investigation stage later in the day.

#### C. Inefficient Level-1 Triage/Assignment
Even before escalation, there are noticeable inefficiencies in how Level-1 agents handle tickets that eventually become "slow" cases.

*   **Evidence:**
    *   In **Case 104** (which did not escalate), the agent waited **3.5 hours** between *Assign to Level-1 Agent* and *Investigate Issue*. In contrast, fast cases like 101 and 103 had waits of 40 minutes or less.
