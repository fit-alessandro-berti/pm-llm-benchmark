### Summary and Logic

#### Case Identification:

- **Cases** were identified based on the logical grouping of activity sequences across applications and documents. They represent user sessions revolving around a particular task, such as:
	- Editing specific documents (e.g., "Quarterly_Report.docx" or "Document1.docx").
	- Responding to an email.
	- Reviewing a PDF file.
	- Updating a budget or cross-referencing data between files.

#### Activity Naming:

- **Activities** were derived by mapping low-level actions to meaningful, consistent higher-level descriptions. Examples:
	- `FOCUS`  "Edit [document/application]".
	- `SWITCH`  "Switch to [application/window]".
	- `TYPING`  "Draft/Add/Insert [specific action context]".
	- `SAVE`  "Save [document/application]".
	- `CLOSE`  "Close [document/application]".

#### Event Attributes:

- **Case ID**: Generated based on logical user tasks.
- **Activity Name**: Represents the semantics of the user's action at a higher level.
- **Timestamp**: Original event timestamp.
- **Additional Details**: Context-specific details (e.g., typed text or clicked actions).

#### Coherent Narrative:

The transformed log depicts a user session involving multiple interrelated work activities: editing documents, responding to emails, reviewing a PDF, and updating a budget. Events are logically grouped into coherent cases to provide an analyst-friendly view suitable for process mining. This structure allows identifying workflows, inefficiencies, and task transitions across applications.
