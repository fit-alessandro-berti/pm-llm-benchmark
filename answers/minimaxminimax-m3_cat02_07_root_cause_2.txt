## 4. Recommendations to Mitigate

### A. Address the core complexity loop
- **Front-load information gathering**: Use an intelligent intake checklist at "Submit Claim" to capture the full set of documents upfront for high-complexity claims.
- **Define a "completeness" rule** so the first evaluation already knows exactly what's needed, instead of discovering it across multiple iterations.
- **One comprehensive request instead of multiple partial requests** — bundle all document needs into a single, well-structured request.

### B. Customer-side acceleration
- **Customer self-service portal** to upload requested documents immediately, with email/SMS reminders at 24h and 48h.
- **Pre-defined templates** for common document types so customers know exactly what to send.
- **SLA on customer response** (e.g., 48h) with automatic escalation.

### C. Resource & workflow balancing
- **Distribute complex cases** between adjusters (avoid concentration on Lisa) to prevent workload bottlenecks.
- **Skill-pair high-complexity work** — assign a senior adjuster to the first evaluation of complex claims to reduce iteration count.
- **Investigate Manager_Bill's throughput** — if Bill is slower than Ann, provide additional support or training for high-complexity approvals.

### D. Process automation
- **Auto-reminders / nudges** when a document request has been open > 24h.
- **Dashboards per region** to monitor average iterations per case — flag Region B if it consistently requires more requests.
- **KPI targets**: Cap document requests at 1 for Medium and 2 for High complexity; trigger escalation if exceeded.

### E. Region-level improvement
- **Benchmark Region A vs. Region B** on high-complexity cases and identify procedural differences (e.g., intake forms, escalation rules, adjuster experience).
- **Standardize the document-request template and follow-up cadence** across both regions.

---

##  Bottom Line
The performance problem is **not** in payment, approval, or closing — those are fast. The real bottleneck is the **document-request loop on high-complexity claims**, exacerbated by **workload concentration on specific resources** and potentially **weaker practices in Region B**. Reducing iterations through better upfront evaluation, bundling requests, and enforcing follow-up SLAs should cut case durations for Cases 2002, 2003, and 2005 by 60–80%.
