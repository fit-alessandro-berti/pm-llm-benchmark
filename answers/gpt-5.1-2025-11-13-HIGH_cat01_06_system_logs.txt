### How cases and activities were defined

**1. Case identification (case_id)**  
Cases are defined by the “work object” the user is interacting with:

- `DOC_Quarterly_Report` – working on `Quarterly_Report.docx` in Word (open, edits, save, close across time).
- `DOC_Document1` – working on `Document1.docx` in Word.
- `MAIL_Annual_Meeting` – handling the specific “Annual Meeting” email (open inbox, open email, read, reply, send).
- `PDF_Report_Draft` – reviewing and annotating `Report_Draft.pdf` in Adobe Acrobat.
- `XLS_Budget_2024` – updating `Budget_2024.xlsx` in Excel.

Even when events are interleaved in time (e.g., switching from a Word document to email and back), all events related to the same artifact are grouped into the same case.

**2. Activity naming (activity)**  

Low-level system actions are mapped to higher-level, standardized activities:

- `FOCUS` or `SWITCH` (target window)  “Open/focus Word document”, “Open/focus spreadsheet”, “Open/focus PDF document”, “Open/focus email application (Inbox)” depending on the application.
- `TYPING`  
  - In Word  “Edit document content”.  
  - In Excel  “Update spreadsheet data”.  
  - In Chrome (email)  “Compose email reply”.
- Email-related `CLICK` events:  
  - `Action=Open Email about Annual Meeting`  “Open specific email”.  
  - `Action=Reply to Email`  “Start email reply”.  
  - `Action=Send Email`  “Send email”.
- `SCROLL`  
  - In email  “Read email”.  
  - In PDF  “Review PDF content”.
- `HIGHLIGHT` in PDF  “Annotate PDF (highlight text)”.
- `SAVE`  “Save document” (Word) or “Save spreadsheet” (Excel).
- `CLOSE`  “Close document”.

Raw attributes (`raw_event_type`, `raw_details`) are preserved so you can refine activity definitions further if needed.

**3. Narrative of work sessions**

Using these cases and activities, each case now tells a coherent mini-process:

- Drafting and refining documents (`DOC_Document1`, `DOC_Quarterly_Report`), including interruptions and returns.
- Handling a specific email from arrival to reply and send (`MAIL_Annual_Meeting`).
- Reviewing and annotating a supporting PDF (`PDF_Report_Draft`).
- Updating supporting financial data in a spreadsheet (`XLS_Budget_2024`).

This structure should be directly usable for discovery, conformance, and performance analysis in standard process mining tools.
