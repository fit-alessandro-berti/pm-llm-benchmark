# Conformance Analysis of the Loan Application Event Log

---

## 1. Normative Rule Violations (Strict)

###  Case 1 — **Fully Conformant**
- Sequence: Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering rules are respected, and the "Approved" branch logic is correctly followed (documentation finalized before closing).
- **No normative violations.**

###  Case 2 — **Multiple Severe Violations**
Three distinct normative breaches:

1. **Ordering violation:** Risk Assessment (08:20) occurs **before** Credit Check (09:00). The rule "Credit Check must precede Risk Assessment" is broken — the risk evaluation was performed without credit information available.
2. **Contradictory action:** Finalize Documentation (10:00) is performed **after a rejection** (09:30). The rules explicitly forbid finalizing documentation for rejected loans; rejected cases must be closed without finalization.
3. **Skipped mandatory activity:** **Inform Applicant never occurs.** The applicant was never notified of the rejection before the case was closed — a clear skip of a required step in the sequence.

**Case 2 is the only case violating the normative model, and it does so in three ways (wrong order, forbidden activity, and skipped activity).**

###  Case 3 — **Normatively Conformant**
- All required activities occur in the correct order, and the "Approved" path is correctly handled (Finalize Documentation before Close Case).
- The issues in Case 3 are timing/optionality issues only (see below). **No strict violations.**

## 2. Desiderata Violations (Suboptimal but Not Severe)

| Case | Credit Check  24h? | Inform  48h after Decision? | Follow-up Call? |
|------|---------------------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 |  Yes (30 min) |  Yes (2 hours) |  Missing |
| 2 |  Yes (1 hour, though out of order) | — (never informed; normative issue) |  Missing |
| 3 |  No (~49 hours) |  No (~51 hours) |  Missing |

- **Case 1:** Excellent timing throughout; only the **optional Follow-up Call** was skipped. This is the mildest possible deviation — the process is otherwise exemplary.
- **Case 2:** The Follow-up Call is missing, but this is dwarfed by its normative violations. (Note: the "Inform Applicant within 48h" desideratum cannot even be evaluated, since the activity never happened — that absence is a *strict* violation, not merely a desideratum issue.)
- **Case 3:** **Three desiderata violations**: Credit Check ~49 hours after receipt (recommended 24h), Inform Applicant ~51 hours after the decision (recommended 48h), and no Follow-up Call. The case is *correct* but *slow and suboptimal* — service quality concerns rather than compliance failures.

---

## 3. Possible Interpretations and Justifications

### Case 2 (the problematic case)
- **Risk Assessment before Credit Check:** May reflect a parallelization attempt or workload pressure — an analyst started the risk assessment while waiting for credit bureau results. Alternatively, it could be a **logging artifact** (activities recorded out of order). Either way, the assessment may have been performed on incomplete information, which is exactly the risk the rule guards against.
- **Finalize Documentation after rejection:** Possibly a misunderstanding of procedure (e.g., a clerk believed *rejection paperwork* counted as "Finalize Documentation"), or a default workflow template was followed mechanically regardless of decision outcome. This suggests the process system does not enforce decision-dependent branching.
- **Missing Inform Applicant:** Could be an unlogged phone call (data quality issue) or a genuine omission. If genuine, it is a serious customer-facing and possibly regulatory failure — the case was closed without notifying the applicant of rejection.

### Case 3 (the slow case)
- The 2-day delay before the Credit Check spans **Jan 12 (Friday)  Jan 14**, suggesting a **weekend effect** — a reasonable real-world explanation, though the SLA recommendation was still missed. Adjusting desiderata to business hours/days might be appropriate.
- The delayed Inform Applicant (~51h vs. 48h) is a marginal miss, possibly caused by attempting to reach the applicant unsuccessfully, or batching of notifications.
- The skipped Follow-up Call is common to all cases, suggesting it may not be embedded in the standard workflow at all.

### Process-Level Observations
- **The Follow-up Call is universally absent (0/3 cases).** This points to a systemic gap: the recommendation likely isn't operationalized (no task, no reminder). If management values it, it should be added as an explicit (still optional) workflow step.
- **Recommendation:** Introduce automated control-flow enforcement (e.g., block Risk Assessment until Credit Check completes; conditionally disable Finalize Documentation on rejection; require Inform Applicant before Close Case) to prevent the Case 2 pattern, and add SLA timers/alerts to address the Case 3 pattern.

---

### Summary
- **Normative violations:** Case 2 only (wrong ordering, forbidden documentation after rejection, skipped applicant notification).
- **Desiderata violations:** Case 3 (both timing recommendations missed) and all three cases lack the recommended Follow-up Call — undesirable but tolerable deviations indicating suboptimal, not non-compliant, behavior.
- **Best-performing case:** Case 1, which fully conforms to the strict rules and meets all timing desiderata, missing only the optional follow-up.
