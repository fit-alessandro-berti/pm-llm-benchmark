A typical **Procure-to-Pay** flow is usually:

**Create Purchase Requisition → Approve Purchase Requisition → Issue Purchase Order → Receive Goods → Receive Vendor Invoice → Match Invoice to PO / goods receipt → Release Payment → Close Case**

In practice, **invoice receipt and goods receipt can sometimes occur in either order**, depending on the business, but **payment should normally happen only after invoice receipt, PO matching, and required approval controls**.

## Detailed observations

### Case 1001: Standard behavior

Case **1001** appears to follow the expected flow:

1. Purchase requisition created
2. Purchase requisition approved
3. Purchase order issued
4. Goods received
5. Vendor invoice received
6. Invoice matched to PO
7. Payment released
8. Case closed

This is the reference example of a healthy P2P process.

---

### Case 1002: Payment before controls are complete

Case **1002** contains several control violations:

The purchase order was issued at **08:40**, but the purchase requisition was not approved until **2024-02-03 08:00**. This means the PO was created before formal authorization.

Even more concerning, the payment was released at **2024-02-03 07:15**, before:

* purchase requisition approval,
* goods receipt,
* invoice-to-PO matching.

This is a major issue because the company paid the vendor before confirming that the purchase was properly approved, before confirming that goods were received, and before completing invoice matching.

This could indicate:

* control bypass,
* unauthorized procurement,
* premature payment,
* risk of paying for goods not received,
* risk of duplicate or fraudulent invoices,
* poor segregation of duties or weak approval enforcement.

---

### Case 1003: Payment before invoice and goods receipt

Case **1003** is highly suspicious because the payment was released before the basic documents and confirmations existed in the process log.

The sequence was:

1. PR created
2. PR approved
3. PO issued
4. **Payment released**
5. Invoice received
6. Goods received
7. Invoice matched
8. Case closed

This is not standard P2P behavior. Payment should normally happen after the invoice is received and matched against the PO and goods receipt. Here, payment occurred before there was even a vendor invoice in the log.

This could point to:

* advance payment without proper controls,
* manual override,
* system error,
* fraudulent or unauthorized payment,
* missing invoice documentation,
* incorrect event timestamps,
* failure of 3-way matching controls.

Advance payments can be legitimate in some industries, but they usually require a special process, explicit approval, and supporting documentation. In this log, there is no such activity shown.

---

### Case 1004: Goods received before PO issuance

Case **1004** mostly follows a controlled payment process after the PO is eventually issued, but the early part is unusual:

* Goods received: **2024-02-06 09:00**
* PO issued: **2024-02-06 09:15**

Receiving goods before issuing a purchase order suggests that the supplier may have delivered goods before a formal order existed in the system.

This may indicate:

* retrospective PO creation,
* off-system purchasing,
* maverick buying,
* urgent procurement handled outside normal controls,
* inaccurate timestamps,
* weak enforcement of “no PO, no pay” policy.

This is less severe than cases **1002** and **1003** because payment still occurred after invoice matching. However, it is still problematic because the organization may have committed to a purchase before formal authorization and procurement documentation were in place.

## Overall conclusion

The anomalous cases are **1002, 1003, and 1004**.

| Severity   | Case ID  | Main Issue                                                   |
| ---------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| High       | **1003** | Payment released before invoice, goods receipt, and matching |
| High       | **1002** | PO and payment occurred before proper approval and matching  |
| Medium     | **1004** | Goods received before PO issuance                            |
| Low / None | **1001** | Normal process flow                                          |

The most critical concern is **premature payment**, especially in cases **1002** and **1003**. In standard P2P controls, payment should normally be the result of a verified obligation: an approved purchase, a valid PO, confirmed goods or services receipt, and a matched invoice. When payment happens before those controls, the organization faces increased risk of financial loss, fraud, compliance issues, and inaccurate accounting.
