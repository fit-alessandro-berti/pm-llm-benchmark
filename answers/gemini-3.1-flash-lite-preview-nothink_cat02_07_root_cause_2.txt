### 3. Explanations and Recommendations

#### **Explanations:**
1.  **Information Asymmetry:** The high rate of "Request Additional Documents" suggests that CSRs (Jane, Paul, Mary) may not have clear guidelines on the documentation required for "High" complexity claims, leading to incomplete submissions.
2.  **Resource Overload:** `Adjuster_Lisa` handles multiple requests for the same high-complexity files over several days, suggesting she may be balancing too many tasks or lacks the authority to resolve issues in a single interaction.
3.  **Process Rework:** The repetition of "Request Additional Documents" is a classic sign of "ping-pong" process behavior, which significantly increases cycle time.

#### **Mitigation Suggestions:**
*   **Standardized Checklists:** Implement mandatory documentation checklists based on "Complexity" levels at the "Submit Claim" stage. This would prevent the need for multiple follow-up requests.
*   **Adjuster Empowerment/Training:** Provide `Adjuster_Lisa` (and other adjusters) with more autonomy or specialized training on high-complexity claims to reduce the iterative document requests.
*   **Workload Balancing:** If `Manager_Bill` is the only approver for high-complexity claims, consider delegating high-complexity approval authority to other managers to prevent a bottleneck.
*   **Proactive Notification:** For High-Complexity cases, automate a "documentation requirements" notification to the claimant immediately upon submission to ensure all necessary data is captured in the first interaction.
