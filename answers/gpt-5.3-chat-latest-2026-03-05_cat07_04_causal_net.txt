The visualization shows a **process/workflow model** (similar to a Petri net or workflow net) describing how a **request is handled and decided**.

Main flow:

- **a – register request:** The process starts when a request is registered.
- The request is then examined in different possible ways:
  - **b – examine thoroughly**
  - **c – examine casually**
  - **d – check ticket**

- After these checks, the process moves to **e – decide**, where a decision about the request is made.

Possible outcomes:

- **g – pay compensation**  then **z – end**
- **h – reject request**  then **z – end**

Rework loop:

- If more information or correction is needed, the process can go to **f – reinitiate request**, which loops back to earlier examination steps before returning to the decision stage.

Notation elements:

- **Rounded rectangles** represent **activities/tasks**.
- **Black circles** represent **routing nodes (gateways)** controlling splits and joins in the flow.
- **Arrows** show the direction of the process flow.

Legend (bottom right):

- **XOR-split / XOR-join:** only one path is taken or merged.
- **AND-split / AND-join:** multiple paths run in parallel and must all complete.
- **OR-split / OR-join:** one or more paths may be taken depending on conditions.

Overall, the diagram models a **request evaluation workflow** with optional checks, a decision point, possible rejection or compensation, and the possibility to **reinitiate the request and repeat the evaluation cycle**.
