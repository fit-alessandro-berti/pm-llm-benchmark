Based on standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) best practices, a standard process flow should follow this logical sequence:
**Create Requisition $\rightarrow$ Approve Requisition $\rightarrow$ Issue Purchase Order (PO) $\rightarrow$ Receive Goods $\rightarrow$ Receive Invoice $\rightarrow$ Match Invoice to PO $\rightarrow$ Release Payment $\rightarrow$ Close Case.**

After analyzing the event log, Case 1001 follows the standard "happy path." However, **Cases 1002, 1003, and 1004 contain significant anomalies.**

---

### Case 1002: Critical Process Breakdown
**Anomalies Identified:**
1.  **PO issued before Approval:** The Purchase Order was issued on Feb 1st, but the requisition wasn't approved until Feb 3rd.
2.  **Payment before Approval, Receipt, and Matching:** Payment was released on Feb 3rd (07:15), *before* the requisition was approved (08:00), *before* the goods were received (Feb 4th), and *before* the invoice was matched (Feb 5th).

**Why this is problematic:**
*   **Lack of Financial Control:** Paying a vendor before an official approval is granted bypasses the organization's budgetary controls.
*   **Financial Risk:** Releasing payment before receiving goods or matching the invoice means the company has paid for items it hasn't verified as received or correct. This opens the door to paying for damaged goods, incorrect quantities, or fraudulent invoices.
*   **Compliance Failure:** This is a major audit red flag, indicating a complete failure of the "Three-Way Match" (PO, Receipt, and Invoice).

---

### Case 1003: Premature Payment
**Anomalies Identified:**
1.  **Payment before Invoice and Receipt:** The payment was released on Feb 8th, but the invoice was not received until Feb 9th and the goods were not received until Feb 10th.

**Why this is problematic:**
*   **Cash Flow Inefficiency:** Paying vendors before the goods are delivered or the invoice is received negatively impacts the company's working capital.
*   **Risk of Non-Delivery:** If the vendor fails to deliver the goods or the invoice contains errors, the company has already surrendered the funds, losing all leverage to resolve the dispute.
*   **Process Circumvention:** This suggests a "pre-payment" happened outside of a formal agreement, which deviates from standard accounting principles.

---

### Case 1004: Retroactive Documentation ("Maverick Spending")
**Anomalies Identified:**
1.  **Goods received before PO issued:** The goods were recorded as received on Feb 6th at 09:00, but the Purchase Order was not issued until 09:15.

**Why this is problematic:**
*   **Maverick Spending:** This is a classic sign of "Maverick Spending," where an employee orders goods directly from a vendor and only creates the paperwork afterward to "fix" the system.
*   **Loss of Negotiating Power:** Because the goods were already received, the company lost the ability to negotiate terms, pricing, or quality standards through the PO process.
*   **Audit Trail Integrity:** The timestamps suggest the PO was created specifically to justify a receipt that had already happened, which undermines the purpose of a PO as a controlling document.
