**BPMN-like Textual Process Model:**

---

### **Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Workflow

---

### **1. Start Event:**  
- **Invoice Receipt**  
  - Trigger: Invoice arrives via email or system upload (typically from supplier).  
  - Artifact: Invoice (digital document).

---

### **2. Task: Validate Invoice and PO Association**  
- **Activity:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary) checks whether the invoice contains a valid Purchase Order (PO) number.  
- **Decision:**  
  - **If NO PO number:**  
    - **Action:** Send back to supplier requesting PO or additional details.  
    - **Artifact:** Communication (email/request for PO).  
    - **End of path until PO is provided.**  
  - **If PO number is valid:**  
    - **Next Step:** Log invoice into accounting system.  
    - **Artifact:** Invoice, PO (linked in system).

---

### **3. Task: Confirm Goods/Services Received**  
- **Activity:** Purchasing Officer (Dan) reviews invoice against PO to confirm that goods or services were received.  
- **Note:** This step may be initiated by Mary forwarding the invoice; Dan independently verifies.  
- **Artifact:** PO, invoice, delivery confirmation (if applicable).

---

### **4. Task: Match Invoice to PO (Three-Way Match)**  
- **Activity:** Mary (Accounts Payable) performs line-item matching between invoice, PO, and delivery confirmation (if available).  
- **Decision:**  
  - **If match is successful (quantities, prices, terms align):**  
    - **Next Step:** Send invoice for managerial approval.  
  - **If discrepancy found (e.g., incorrect pricing, missing items, extra units):**  
    - **Action:** Email Purchasing team (Dan) to clarify or resolve.  
    - **Possible Sub-Process:**  
      - **If issue resolved quickly:** Proceed to manager approval.  
      - **If unresolved or significant:** Escalate to Supervisor (Rita).  
    - **Artifact:** Communication (clarification request), revised invoice (if supplier responds).

---

### **5. Task: Manager Approval**  
- **Activity:** Department Manager (Carlos) reviews invoice in system after notification from AP or Purchasing.  
- **Decision:**  
  - **If all details are correct and within budget:**  
    - **Action:** Approve invoice in system.  
  - **If suspicious or out of scope (e.g., pricing anomaly, unexpected item):**  
    - **Action:** Reject or request further investigation.  
    - **Escalation Path:** May involve Mary and/or Rita to resolve before re-approval.  
- **Artifact:** System notification, invoice, PO.

---

### **6. Task: Payment Scheduling**  
- **Activity:** Accounts Payable (Mary) schedules invoice for payment once approved.  
- **Trigger:** Final approval from manager.  
- **Artifact:** Invoice, payment run schedule.

---

### **7. Exception Handling (Re-work / Alternate Paths):**  
- **Exception 1: Missing or Invalid PO Number**  
  - **Path:** Invoice returned to supplier  supplier resubmits with PO  process restarts at Invoice Receipt.  
- **Exception 2: Discrepancy in Matching (Three-Way Match Fails)**  
  - **Sub-Path A (Resolvable):** Purchasing clarifies  match succeeds  proceed to manager approval.  
  - **Sub-Path B (Unresolvable):** Escalate to Supervisor (Rita)  may involve contract review or PO creation  then re-initiate matching.  
- **Exception 3: Suspicious or Out-of-Policy Invoice**  
  - **Path:** Manager rejects  AP investigates  may involve department head or Supervisor  possible retrospective PO creation  re-submit for approval.

---

### **8. Roles & Participants:**  
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary):** Logs, matches, forwards, schedules.  
- **Purchasing Officer (Dan):** Confirms receipt and PO alignment.  
- **Department Manager (Carlos):** Final approval authority.  
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita):** Resolves exceptions, escalates issues, supports cross-functional resolution.  
- **Suppliers (via Karen):** Provide correct invoices, PO numbers, and pricing; respond to inquiries.  
- **Systems:** Accounting system (triggers approval workflow, logs actions), email (communication channel).

---

### **9. Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs:**  
- **Input:** Invoice, Purchase Order, delivery confirmation (if available).  
- **Output:** Approved invoice, payment schedule, communication logs, resolved discrepancies.

---

### **10. Sequence Flow Summary (Linear Path with Decisions):**  
1. Invoice Receipt   
2. Validate PO  (Decision: PO valid?)  No  Send to supplier  Wait  (Re-try)  Yes   
3. Log in system   
4. Confirm receipt (Purchasing)   
5. Three-way match (AP)  (Decision: Match successful?)  No  Escalate or resolve  Yes   
6. Send for manager approval   
7. Manager approves (or rejects)   
8. If approved  Schedule payment  End.

---

### **11. Notes on Ambiguity & Variability:**  
- **Overlap in Roles:** Dan (Purchasing) and Mary (AP) both verify PO-invoice alignment, but Dan focuses on order details, Mary on system matching.  
- **Trigger for Manager Approval:** Confirmed by both Mary and Carlos — after PO match and receipt confirmation.  
- **Exception Frequency:** Most exceptions are rare (e.g., manager rejection ~once every few months).  
- **System Role:** The accounting system appears to automate notifications and track matching, but human judgment is central in exceptions.

---

### **12. Suggested BPMN Representation (for diagram conversion):**  
- **Start Event:** Invoice Received  
- **Exclusive Gateway:** PO Valid?  
  - No  Send Request (Task)  Wait for Response  Exclusive Gateway (PO received?)  Yes  Log in System  
  - Yes  Log in System  
- **Task:** Confirm Receipt (Purchasing)  
- **Task:** Three-Way Match (AP)  
- **Exclusive Gateway:** Match Successful?  
  - No  Escalate (Task: Contact Purchasing / Contact Supervisor)  Resolve  Loop back to Match  
  - Yes  Send for Approval  
- **Task:** Manager Approval  
  - Gateway: Approved?  
    - No  Reject / Investigate  Loop back  
    - Yes  Schedule Payment  
- **End Event:** Payment Scheduled

---

This textual model is structured, unambiguous, and designed for direct translation into a BPMN diagram. It integrates all key elements from the interviews while transparently representing points of coordination, decision, and exception.
