### Justification of Groupings

The events were grouped based on **Functional Intent** and **Phase Dependency**:

1.  **Preparation & Staging:** These events represent the "setup" phase. They involve moving the raw material into the workspace, establishing its identity (scanning), and performing mechanical/thermal conditioning required before permanent modifications (welding) can occur.
2.  **Assembly:** This step represents the active fabrication phase. It encompasses the interaction between the operator/tool and the material to permanently alter the product's structure. These events are grouped together because they rely on the same tool (Welding Tool) and directly result in the physical union of parts.
3.  **Finishing & Treatment:** These events involve applying a secondary layer/substance to the material and subjecting it to environmental curing. They are distinct from assembly because they change the chemical or protective state of the surface rather than the physical structure.
4.  **Quality Assurance:** These events are grouped because their primary function is diagnostic rather than productive. They serve to validate the results of the previous two steps (Assembly and Finishing) against predefined integrity and visual standards.
