The image is a **flowchart (BPMN-style) of a parcel shipping process**, organized in horizontal “lanes” for responsibilities:

### Lanes / roles
- **Top lane: Logistics**
- **Middle lane: Dispatch of goods / Secretary**
- **Bottom lane: Warehouse**

### Main flow (step-by-step)
1. **Ship goods** (start) from **Dispatch of goods**.
2. **Clarify shipment method**.
3. A decision **“Special handling?”**:
   - **No**: the flow skips the special-handling steps.
   - **Yes**: the process requests offers:
     - **Get 3 offers from logistic companies**
     - **Select logistic company and place order**
4. In parallel (or as a conditional path) the flow checks **insurance**:
   - Uses **Customer information** to **check if insurance is required**.
   - If required: **Insure parcel**.
5. Regardless, the next major step is **“Write package label”**, after which the flow continues toward the shipping/warehouse handoff.
6. In the **warehouse**:
   - **Package goods** (noted with **48 hours**)
   - If needed, **notify customer about delay**
7. Then the process:
   - **Fill out shipment paperwork**
   - **Move package to pickup area**
8. Ends at **“Shipment prepared”**.

Overall, it models how an order is prepared for shipment, including **optional insurance**, **conditional special handling via selecting a logistics provider**, **labeling**, and **warehouse packaging with potential customer notification for delays**.
