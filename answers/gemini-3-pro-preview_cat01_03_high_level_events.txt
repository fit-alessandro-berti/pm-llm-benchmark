### 2. Rationale & Justification

The logic for grouping these specific events is derived from resource handovers, physical state changes, and functional dependencies.

#### **Activity 1: Material Preparation**
*   **Grouping Logic:** This phase encompasses all actions required to take the raw material from storage and condition it for the core manufacturing process.
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Resource Continuity:** It begins with **Operator A** performing manual logistics and identification (Retrieving, Scanning, Placing).
    *   **Functional Logic:** The subsequent automated steps (Aligning by **Robot Arm** and Preheating by **Heating Unit**) are not value-add "assembly" steps yet; they are setup requirements to ensure the material is in the correct state (position and temperature) for welding.
    *   **Breakpoint:** The phase ends when the material is fully prepped, and control is handed over to **Operator B** for active assembly.

#### **Activity 2: Welding Assembly**
*   **Grouping Logic:** This phase represents the primary structural alteration of the product (joining parts).
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Task Cohesion:** Picking up the tool and welding the corners are inseparable parts of the manual assembly task performed by **Operator B**.
    *   **Quality Integration:** I have included "Measure weld integrity" (**Quality Sensor #1**) in this step rather than a separate QC step. In modern manufacturing, automated in-line quality checks are often considered the "closing bracket" of the assembly task. If this sensor fails, the welding phase is incomplete or failed; thus, it belongs logically to the assembly block.

#### **Activity 3: Surface Treatment**
*   **Grouping Logic:** This phase involves chemical and thermal applications to finish the product's surface.
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Distinct Process:** This is a distinct departure from metalworking (welding) to finishing (coating).
    *   **Resource Type:** Both steps are fully automated machine steps (**Coating Unit** and **Heating Unit**) without human intervention, distinguishing them from the manual welding before and the manual check after.
    *   **Dependency:** Drying is functionally dependent on the application of the coating; they form a single "Finishing" transaction.

#### **Activity 4: Final Inspection**
*   **Grouping Logic:** A manual gatekeeping step to release the product.
*   **Justification:**
    *   **Process Separation:** This occurs after the product is physically complete and dry. No further alterations are made to the product.
    *   **Resource Change:** The process hands over to a new resource, **Operator C**, who has not been involved in previous steps. This represents an independent "four-eyes" principle validation before the case is closed.
