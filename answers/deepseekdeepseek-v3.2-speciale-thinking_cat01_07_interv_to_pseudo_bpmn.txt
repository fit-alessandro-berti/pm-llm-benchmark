## Process Flow

### Start: Invoice Received (via email)

1. **Check PO Validity** [AP Clerk]  
   - Examine invoice for a valid purchase order number (exists in system).  
   - *Gateway: Valid PO?*  
     - **Yes**  proceed to step 2.  
     - **No**  proceed to step 1a.

   *Step 1a – Missing PO*  
   - *Common path*: **Return Invoice to Supplier** [AP Clerk] – send back requesting PO details. After supplier provides details, the invoice is effectively resubmitted (go back to Start).  
   - *Alternative path (informal services)*: **Escalate to AP Supervisor** [AP Clerk  AP Supervisor]. AP Supervisor handles by contacting department heads to **Create Retrospective PO** or **Reject Invoice**. If retrospective PO is created, treat as valid PO  proceed to step 2. If rejected  **End (Invoice Rejected)**.

2. **Log Invoice** [AP Clerk] – Enter invoice details into accounting system.

3. **Forward to Purchasing** [AP Clerk] – Send invoice (via email/system) to Purchasing for confirmation of receipt and validation.

4. **Confirm Receipt & Validate** [Purchasing Officer]  
   - Verify that goods/services have been received.  
   - Compare invoice line items (quantities, prices, charges) to PO.  
   - *Gateway: Everything correct?*  
     - **Yes**  **Send Confirmation** to AP Clerk  proceed to step 5.  
     - **No**  proceed to step 4a.

   *Step 4a – Resolve Discrepancy*  
   - This subprocess may involve:  
        - Purchasing Officer contacting supplier directly,  
        - Involving Supplier Relationship Manager to contact supplier,  
        - Requesting AP Clerk to hold invoice until corrected.  
   - After obtaining clarification/correction, loop back to step 4 to re-validate.  
   - If resolution cannot be reached  **Escalate to AP Supervisor** (step 4b).

   *Step 4b – Handle Unresolved Discrepancy* [AP Supervisor]  
   - Supervisor coordinates with departments/suppliers, decides to adjust PO, reject invoice, or take other action. If invoice is to proceed, it returns to step 4 (or step 3) as appropriate.

5. **Receive Confirmation** [AP Clerk] – (Implicit; may be system update).

6. **Match Invoice in System** [AP Clerk] – Perform system matching of invoice line items against PO.  
   - *Gateway: Match successful?*  
     - **Yes**  proceed to step 7.  
     - **No**  proceed to step 6a.

   *Step 6a – Clarify with Purchasing*  
   - **Email Purchasing for Clarification** [AP Clerk]. Typically this loops back to step 4 (or 4a) to resolve the mismatch. If repeated attempts fail  escalate to AP Supervisor (step 4b).

7. **Send for Manager Approval** [System/AP Clerk] – System generates an approval request to Department Manager.

8. **Review & Approve** [Department Manager]  
   - Manager examines invoice details, PO, confirmation, and matching result.  
   - *Gateway: Approve?*  
     - **Yes**  proceed to step 9.  
     - **No** (reject or request hold)  proceed to step 8a.

   *Step 8a – Return for Resolution*  
   - Invoice is sent back to AP (Clerk or Supervisor) for further investigation. This may involve looping back to earlier steps (e.g., step 4, step 6) or escalating to AP Supervisor (step 4b). If ultimately resolved, return to step 7 (or appropriate step). If rejected  **End (Invoice Rejected)**.

9. **Schedule Payment** [AP Clerk] – Add invoice to next payment run.

10. **End: Invoice Paid**.

## Additional Notes
- **Supplier Relationship Manager** is a support role that may be engaged during any discrepancy resolution step (4a, 4b, 8a) to facilitate communication with suppliers.
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor** handles escalations when normal resolution fails, and also special cases of missing POs for informally contracted services.
- Data objects flow: Invoice is the main artifact; PO is referenced; Confirmation and matching results are generated; Payment schedule is updated.

- Where accounts conflict (e.g., handling missing PO), the model includes both described possibilities, noting the alternative path.
