# Analysis of the Support Workflow Event Log

## 1. Identification of Recurring Activities

Looking across the three cases, the following activities recur **within the same case** with different contextual meanings:

| Activity | Recurs In | Contextual Variation |
|---|---|---|
| **Verify Documents** | C001, C003 | Initial verification vs. re-verification after additional info |
| **Call Customer** | C002 | Clarification call vs. follow-up confirmation call |

Additionally, across cases, activities like **Initiate Request**, **Call Customer**, and **Adjust Account** occur in very different contexts (online vs. walk-in; outbound clarification vs. callback request; post-call adjustment vs. missing-info correction). These should also be disambiguated for clarity.

## 3. Justification of Naming Choices

### **Initiate Request  "Online" vs. "Walk-in"**
- In C001/C002, the request originates from `WebPortal` (digital channel).
- In C003, the resource is `BranchReception` (physical channel).
- Channel distinction is critical in financial workflows for SLAs and compliance, so the channel is encoded in the name.

### **Verify Documents  "Initial" vs. "Re-Verification"**
- The **first** occurrence in every case is a baseline verification.
- The **second** occurrence (C001, C003) always follows a remediation step (document upload in C001, account adjustment in C003). It is functionally different: the agent re-checks against *new* information rather than performing the original review.
- C003's first verification is also specifically *physical* (branch), whereas C001/C002 are back-office document checks ó reflected in the naming.

### **Call Customer  "Outbound/Request Missing Docs" vs. "Clarify Discrepancy" vs. "Follow-up" vs. "Callback"**
- **C001:** The call is triggered by incomplete verification  purpose is to *request missing documents*.
- **C002 (first call):** Purpose is to *clarify a discrepancy* detected during verification.
- **C002 (second call):** Explicitly a *follow-up* after an internal adjustment ó confirmation in nature, not investigative.
- **C003:** The customer physically left; the call's purpose is a *callback to return to the branch* ó a totally different intent from the phone-based clarifications in C001/C002.

### **Adjust Account  "Post-Call Adjustment" vs. "Missing-Info Correction"**
- **C002:** Occurs *after* the clarification call ó it is a direct consequence of the customer interaction.
- **C003:** Triggered by *missing info* discovered internally, not by a customer call.
- Same activity label, different triggers  different process semantics.

### **Submit Additional  "Customer Submits Additional Documents"**
- Renamed to a full subject-verb-object form to make clear this is a **customer-side** action (self-service upload), distinguishing it from agent-initiated activities.

### **Approve Request  "Final Approval"**
- Appears only once per case, but prefixing with "Final" reinforces that this is the workflow terminator (important for process mining end-event detection).

---

## Key Takeaway

The original log suffers from **activity-name overloading**: identical labels mask differing intents, triggers, and channels. By incorporating the **trigger** (e.g., post-call, missing info), **sequence role** (initial vs. re-verification), **channel** (online vs. walk-in), and **purpose** (clarify vs. follow-up vs. callback) into activity names, downstream process mining can correctly distinguish rework loops, customer-interaction patterns, and channel-specific variants ó rather than collapsing them into a single misleading node.
