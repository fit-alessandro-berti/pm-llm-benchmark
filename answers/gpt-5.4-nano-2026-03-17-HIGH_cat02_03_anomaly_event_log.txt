Below are the main **anomalous sequences** compared with a typical Procure-to-Pay (P2P) flow, and why theyÆre problematic.

## Expected (typical) P2P order
1. Create Purchase Requisition (PR)
2. Approve PR
3. Issue Purchase Order (PO)
4. Receive Goods
5. Vendor Invoice Received
6. Match Invoice to PO (and/or goods receipt)
7. Release/Approve Payment
8. Close Case

## Case 1002 Ś PO issued and payment released out of order (major compliance risk)
**Observed sequence (key events):**
- 2024-02-01 08:30 Ś Create Purchase Requisition  
- 2024-02-01 08:40 Ś **Issue Purchase Order**  *(before PR approval)*
- 2024-02-03 07:00 Ś Vendor Invoice Received  
- 2024-02-03 07:15 Ś **Release Payment** *(before receiving goods and before invoice/PO matching)*
- 2024-02-03 08:00 Ś **Approve Purchase Requisition** *(approval occurs after PO was issued and after payment was released)*
- 2024-02-04 09:00 Ś Receive Goods  
- 2024-02-05 08:00 Ś Match Invoice to PO  
- 2024-02-06 Ś Close Case  

**Why this is anomalous / problematic:**
- **PO issued before PR approval**: Authorization controls are bypassed (youÆre buying without approved budget/intent).
- **Payment released before goods are received**: You pay without verifying delivery/receipt.
- **Payment released before invoice matching**: You pay without ensuring the invoice corresponds to the correct PO (quantity, price, terms).
- **Invoice match happens after payment**: So matching canÆt protect the payment decision that already occurred.

## Case 1003 Ś Payment released before invoice and before receiving goods (high fraud/waste risk)
**Observed sequence (key events):**
- 2024-02-02 09:10 Ś Approve PR (ok)
- 2024-02-02 10:00 Ś Issue PO (ok)
- 2024-02-08 11:00 Ś **Release Payment**  
- 2024-02-09 12:00 Ś Vendor Invoice Received  
- 2024-02-10 13:00 Ś Receive Goods  
- 2024-02-11 14:00 Ś Match Invoice to PO  

**Why this is anomalous / problematic:**
- **Payment occurs before invoice is received**: ThereÆs no validated basis (invoice) to pay.
- **Payment occurs before goods receipt**: No confirmation that the vendor delivered what was ordered.
- **Invoice matching occurs after payment**: Any discrepancy would be detected too lateŚtoo late to prevent financial loss.

## Case 1004 Ś Goods received before PO issued (operational/audit inconsistency)
**Observed sequence (key events):**
- 2024-02-05 10:00 Ś Create PR  
- 2024-02-05 10:15 Ś Approve PR  
- 2024-02-06 09:00 Ś **Receive Goods**  
- 2024-02-06 09:15 Ś **Issue Purchase Order**  

**Why this is anomalous / problematic:**
- **Receiving goods before PO issuance**: YouÆre recording receipt of goods that (according to the log) were not formally authorized yet via a PO.
- This breaks traceability: it becomes harder to prove what was ordered, at what price/terms, and whether receipt corresponds to an approved order.
- It may indicate **backdating**, **missing events**, or **process circumvention**, each of which undermines auditability and internal control.

## Case 1001 Ś Appears consistent with standard P2P flow
**Observed sequence:**
PR created  approved  PO issued  goods received  invoice received  invoice matched  payment released  case closed.

This case does not show obvious ordering anomalies.

### Overall conclusion
The strongest issues are in **cases 1002 and 1003**, where **payment is released before key control steps** (PR approval, goods receipt, invoice receipt/matching). **Case 1004** has an ordering violation between **receiving goods** and **issuing the PO**. These patterns typically indicate **control bypass**, **potential data quality problems**, or **fraud/waste risk**, and they directly weaken the P2P processÆs ability to ensure correct authorization, delivery verification, and accurate payment.
