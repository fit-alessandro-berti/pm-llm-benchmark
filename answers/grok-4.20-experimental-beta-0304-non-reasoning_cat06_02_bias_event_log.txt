**Bias manifests primarily through the "CommunityGroup" attribute and the associated "+10 (Community)" ScoreAdjustment, which functions as a non-transparent, non-financial booster.**

### Where and How Bias Manifests

1. **Community Affiliation Bonus**:
   - Applicants affiliated with "Highland Civic Darts Club" (C001, C004) receive an automatic +10 adjustment during PreliminaryScoring.
   - This pushes marginal cases across approval thresholds: 
     - C001: 710  720 (Approved)
     - C004: 690  700 (Approved)
   - Non-affiliated applicants (C002, C003, C005) receive 0 adjustment regardless of their preliminary score.

2. **Correlation with LocalResident**:
   - Every applicant receiving the community bonus is also marked `LocalResident: TRUE`.
   - Rejected case C003 is `LocalResident: FALSE` and has no community group.
   - This suggests the system is effectively giving preferential treatment based on local ties and specific social affiliations.

3. **Inconsistent Threshold Application**:
   - Approved cases show final adjusted scores ranging from 700 to 720.
   - C003 was rejected at 715 (no adjustment).
   - C005 was approved at 740 (no adjustment).
   - This implies the effective approval threshold is lower (~700 adjusted) for community-affiliated locals than for non-affiliated applicants (~730–740 unadjusted). A non-local with 710–720 would likely be rejected, while a darts-club local with 690–710 gets approved.

4. **ManualReview Appears to Rubber-Stamp the Adjustment**:
   - The community bonus is applied in PreliminaryScoring by the "Scoring Engine" and carried through ManualReview and FinalDecision without apparent scrutiny or justification. The "Underwriter" stage does not remove or challenge the +10 (Community) adjustment.

### Attributes and Adjustments That Favor Certain Groups

- **CommunityGroup = "Highland Civic Darts Club"**: Acts as a proxy for social capital or insider status. This is not a legitimate credit risk factor.
- **LocalResident = TRUE**: Strongly correlated with receiving the bonus. Non-residents appear systematically disadvantaged.
- **ScoreAdjustment = "+10 (Community)"**: This is the mechanism of bias. It's arbitrary, non-transparent, and not based on objective risk data.

These factors create a **group-based preference** rather than individual merit. The darts club affiliation functions as a membership perk that improves lending outcomes.

### Implications for Fairness and Equity

- **Individuals without community affiliations** (especially non-locals) face a higher effective bar for approval even when their preliminary scores are similar or better. C003 (715, no bonus, rejected) vs. C001 (710 +10 = 720, approved) illustrates this directly.
- This violates core principles of credit scoring fairness: decisions should be based on predictive, explainable, and non-discriminatory factors. Community membership is not a valid proxy for creditworthiness.
- It creates **disparate impact** on people who:
  - Are new to the area
  - Don't participate in the specific local social club
  - Belong to different cultural or recreational groups
  - Lack the social networks that grant access to such affiliations

- The bias is particularly problematic because it favors a narrow, arbitrary group ("Highland Civic Darts Club") rather than broadly defined community involvement. This resembles old-fashioned cronyism or "old boys' club" lending practices disguised in an automated scoring system.

### Recommendations for Mitigation (if the goal is fairness)

- Remove the community bonus entirely or base any adjustment on verifiable, inclusive, and relevant factors (e.g., years at current address, verified stable local employment).
- Make all score adjustments fully transparent and auditable.
- Test for disparate impact by local residency and community group status.
- Ensure the final approval threshold is consistent regardless of community affiliation.

In summary, the process embeds **social affiliation bias** through the community adjustment mechanism. It systematically advantages a specific local subgroup while disadvantaging equally creditworthy outsiders, undermining both fairness and the goal of objective risk assessment. This is a clear example of how seemingly neutral "community support" features in scoring models can conceal protected-class or geographic discrimination.
