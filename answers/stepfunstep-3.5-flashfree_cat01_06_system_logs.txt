### Explanation of Logic

**Case Identification:**  
Cases were defined based on the `Window` attribute (e.g., document names or application context). Each unique window title represents a distinct work item (e.g., a specific document or email thread). For the email, the case ID `AnnualMeetingEmail` was inferred from the action "Open Email about Annual Meeting" to capture a specific email handling instance, as the generic window title "Email - Inbox" could encompass multiple emails. SWITCH events were excluded because they represent transitions between cases rather than meaningful activities within a case.

**Activity Naming:**  
Raw actions were mapped to higher-level, standardized process steps:
- **FOCUS**: Mapped to `Open Document` if it is the first event for that case (indicating initial activation), otherwise `Activate Document` (returning to a previously opened but inactive window).
- **TYPING**: Generalized to `Edit Content` across all applications (Word, Excel, Chrome) to represent content modification.
- **SAVE**: `Save Document`.
- **CLOSE**: `Close Document`.
- **CLICK**: Specific actions in Chrome were mapped:
  - "Open Email about Annual Meeting"  `Open Email`
  - "Reply to Email"  `Reply to Email`
  - "Send Email"  `Send Email`
- **SCROLL**: `Review` (a generic activity for scrolling through content, applicable to both email and PDF).
- **HIGHLIGHT**: `Annotate` (for marking up content in a PDF).

This mapping creates a coherent narrative of user work sessions:  
1. **Quarterly_Report.docx**: Opened early but not worked on until later; after activating, edited, saved, and closed.  
2. **Document1.docx**: Edited in two sessions (initial typing/save, then later addition referencing the budget, followed by save and close).  
3. **AnnualMeetingEmail**: A complete email handling sequence (open, review, reply, compose, send).  
4. **Report_Draft.pdf**: Briefly reviewed and annotated.  
5. **Budget_2024.xlsx**: Updated and saved without closing.
