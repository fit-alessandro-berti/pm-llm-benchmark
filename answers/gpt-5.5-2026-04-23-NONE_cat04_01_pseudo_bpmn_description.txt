The pseudo-BPMN describes a customer request handling process that branches depending on whether the customerÆs request is **standard** or **custom**, then later converges into a common approval, invoicing, and confirmation flow.

At a high level, the process is:

1. Receive the customer request.
2. Determine whether it is a standard or custom request.
3. Process the request according to its type.
4. Decide whether approval is required.
5. If approval is granted or not required, generate the final invoice.
6. Send confirmation to the customer.
7. End the process.

---

## 1. Start Event

The process begins with a **Start Event**.

This indicates that a new instance of the process is triggered, most likely when a customer submits a request, order, inquiry, or quotation request.

---

## 2. Task A: Receive Customer Request

**Purpose:**  
The organization receives and records the customerÆs request.

This step may involve:

- Capturing customer details
- Recording requested products or services
- Identifying delivery requirements
- Reviewing initial request information
- Creating a case, order, ticket, or opportunity in a system

This is the entry point into the business process.

---

## 3. XOR Gateway: Check Request Type

After the request is received, the process reaches an **exclusive gateway**, also called an **XOR gateway**.

### Meaning of the XOR Gateway

An XOR gateway means that **only one path can be taken** based on a condition.

Here, the condition is the request type:

- If the request is **standard**, the process follows the standard request path.
- If the request is **custom**, the process follows the custom request path.

The two paths are mutually exclusive. A request is treated either as standard or custom, not both.

---

# Standard Request Path

If the request type is **Standard**, the process follows the standard processing route.

---

## 4. Task B1: Perform Standard Validation

**Purpose:**  
This task checks whether the standard customer request is valid and can be processed according to normal rules.

Validation may include:

- Checking that the requested product or service exists
- Confirming required customer information is complete
- Verifying standard pricing rules
- Checking basic order eligibility
- Ensuring the request complies with internal policies

Because the request is standard, the organization does not need to perform detailed customization analysis. The focus is on confirming that the request fits predefined offerings and business rules.

---

## 5. AND Gateway: Run Parallel Checks

After standard validation, the process reaches an **AND gateway**, also called a **parallel gateway**.

### Meaning of the AND Gateway

An AND gateway means that **multiple paths are started at the same time**, and all must complete before the process can continue.

In this case, two parallel checks are launched:

- Credit Check
- Inventory Check

These checks can be performed independently and simultaneously.

---

## 6. Task C1: Credit Check

**Purpose:**  
This task verifies the customerÆs financial or credit status.

It may include:

- Checking credit limit
- Reviewing outstanding balances
- Confirming payment history
- Determining whether the customer is financially eligible for the order

This step helps reduce financial risk before the organization commits to fulfilling the request.

---

## 7. Task C2: Inventory Check

**Purpose:**  
This task verifies whether the requested product or service resources are available.

It may include:

- Checking stock availability
- Confirming warehouse quantities
- Reviewing resource capacity
- Identifying whether backordering is required

This ensures the organization can fulfill the standard request within expected constraints.

---

## 8. AND Join: All Parallel Checks Completed

The parallel paths converge at a joining point.

### Meaning of the AND Join

The AND join waits until **all parallel checks are completed** before moving forward.

That means:

- The credit check must be completed.
- The inventory check must be completed.

Only after both tasks finish can the process proceed to calculating the delivery date.

---

## 9. Task D: Calculate Delivery Date

**Purpose:**  
Once validation, credit checking, and inventory checking are complete, the business calculates a delivery date.

The delivery date may depend on:

- Inventory availability
- Shipping lead times
- Customer location
- Internal processing time
- Production or fulfillment capacity
- Results of previous checks

This step produces an expected delivery or fulfillment date for the customerÆs request.

At this point, the standard path has completed its request-specific processing and can move into the shared downstream flow.

---

# Custom Request Path

If the request type is **Custom**, the process follows a different route because custom requests require special analysis.

---

## 10. Task B2: Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis

**Purpose:**  
This task determines whether the requested customization can be delivered.

This may involve:

- Technical feasibility review
- Cost estimation
- Resource assessment
- Engineering or design input
- Legal or compliance checks
- Delivery timeline assessment
- Supplier or production capability review

Unlike a standard request, a custom request cannot simply be validated against predefined offerings. It must be analyzed to determine whether the organization can realistically fulfill it.

---

## 11. XOR Gateway: Is Customization Feasible?

After the feasibility analysis, another XOR gateway decides whether the custom request can proceed.

### Meaning of the XOR Gateway

Again, the XOR gateway allows only one outgoing path.

There are two possible outcomes:

1. **Yes, customization is feasible**
2. **No, customization is not feasible**

---

## 12. If Feasible: Task E1 Ś Prepare Custom Quotation

**Purpose:**  
If the customization is feasible, the organization prepares a custom quotation.

This may include:

- Custom pricing
- Estimated delivery timeline
- Special terms and conditions
- Required resources
- Scope of customization
- Assumptions and exclusions
- Approval conditions

This task produces a formal offer or proposal for the customer based on the custom work required.

After this step, the custom path proceeds into the common downstream process, where the system checks whether approval is needed.

---

## 13. If Not Feasible: Task E2 Ś Send Rejection Notice

**Purpose:**  
If the customization is not feasible, the organization informs the customer that the request cannot be fulfilled.

This may include:

- Explaining the reason for rejection
- Suggesting alternatives
- Offering standard options
- Closing the request

After the rejection notice is sent, the process reaches an **End Event**.

In this branch, the process ends early because there is no valid offer, order, or invoice to continue with.

---

# Convergence of Standard and Custom Paths

After the standard path completes **Task D: Calculate Delivery Date**, or the feasible custom path completes **Task E1: Prepare Custom Quotation**, the process converges into a shared flow.

This means both acceptable request types eventually arrive at the same decision point:

## 14. XOR Gateway: Is Approval Needed?

This gateway determines whether management approval is required before final invoicing.

### Meaning of the XOR Gateway

Only one of two paths is selected:

- Approval is needed.
- Approval is not needed.

### When Approval May Be Needed

Approval may be triggered by business rules such as:

- High order value
- Large discount
- Unusual delivery commitment
- Low customer credit rating
- Custom pricing
- Non-standard terms
- Strategic or high-risk customer
- Exceptions identified during validation
- Custom work with significant resource impact

The approval requirement can apply to either standard or custom requests, although custom requests may be more likely to require approval because they often involve special pricing, scope, or risk.

---

# Approval Path

If approval is needed, the process proceeds to manager review.

---

## 15. Task F: Obtain Manager Approval

**Purpose:**  
A manager or authorized approver reviews the request, quotation, delivery date, pricing, and relevant conditions.

The manager may evaluate:

- Profitability
- Risk
- Resource availability
- Customer importance
- Credit exposure
- Proposed delivery commitment
- Discount level
- Custom terms
- Compliance with policy

This step ensures that requests requiring oversight are formally reviewed before final commitment to the customer.

---

## 16. XOR Gateway: Is Approval Granted?

After the manager review, another XOR gateway determines the outcome of the approval decision.

There are two possible paths:

1. Approval granted
2. Approval not granted

---

## 17. If Approval Granted: Task G Ś Generate Final Invoice

**Purpose:**  
If approval is granted, the process moves forward to final invoice generation.

The invoice reflects the validated request, delivery terms, pricing, and any approved special conditions.

---

## 18. If Approval Not Granted: Task H Ś Re-evaluate Conditions

**Purpose:**  
If approval is not granted, the request is not immediately rejected. Instead, it enters a rework or re-evaluation step.

This task may involve:

- Revising pricing
- Adjusting discount levels
- Changing delivery dates
- Modifying scope
- Rechecking availability
- Revising the custom quotation
- Updating terms and conditions
- Reducing risk factors

This step gives the business an opportunity to correct or adjust the request so that it may become approvable.

---

# Loop Back Behavior

After **Task H: Re-evaluate Conditions**, the process loops back to an earlier task depending on the original request type.

## For a Custom Request

The loop returns to:

### Task E1: Prepare Custom Quotation

This means the custom quotation is revised after re-evaluating the conditions.

For example:

- The price may be increased.
- The scope may be reduced.
- The delivery timeline may be extended.
- Special conditions may be added.
- Approval-sensitive terms may be changed.

After the quotation is updated, the process again checks whether approval is needed and, if necessary, goes through approval again.

## For a Standard Request

The loop returns to:

### Task D: Calculate Delivery Date

This means the standard request is recalculated or adjusted based on the re-evaluation.

For example:

- The delivery date may be changed.
- Inventory allocation may be reconsidered.
- Fulfillment assumptions may be updated.
- Terms may be modified to satisfy approval concerns.

After recalculating the delivery date, the process again proceeds to the approval-needed decision.

---

# No Approval Needed Path

If the gateway **Is Approval Needed?** determines that no approval is required, the process skips manager approval and goes directly to:

## 19. Task G: Generate Final Invoice

**Purpose:**  
The final invoice is created based on the completed standard or custom processing.

For standard requests, the invoice may be generated using standard prices and delivery terms.

For custom requests, the invoice may reflect the prepared custom quotation.

---

# Final Common Path

Once the invoice has been generated, both approval and no-approval paths converge.

---

## 20. Task I: Send Confirmation to Customer

**Purpose:**  
The organization sends the customer a confirmation.

This may include:

- Final invoice
- Confirmed delivery date
- Order confirmation number
- Quotation acceptance information
- Payment instructions
- Terms and conditions
- Next steps

This is the final communication confirming that the request has been accepted and processed.

---

## 21. End Event

The process ends after the confirmation is sent to the customer.

This indicates successful completion of the process for accepted standard or feasible custom requests.

The process may also end earlier if a custom request is determined to be infeasible and a rejection notice is sent.

---

# Summary of Standard vs. Custom Handling

| Aspect | Standard Request | Custom Request |
|---|---|---|
| Initial processing | Standard validation | Custom feasibility analysis |
| Main checks | Credit and inventory checks in parallel | Feasibility decision |
| If successful | Calculate delivery date | Prepare custom quotation |
| If unsuccessful | Not explicitly shown; likely exception handling needed | Send rejection notice and end |
| Approval possibility | May require approval depending on rules | May require approval depending on rules |
| Rework loop | Returns to delivery date calculation | Returns to custom quotation preparation |
| Final output | Invoice and confirmation | Invoice and confirmation if feasible; rejection if infeasible |

---

# Gateway Summary

| Gateway | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Check Request Type | XOR | Selects either standard or custom path |
| Run Parallel Checks | AND split | Starts credit check and inventory check simultaneously |
| All Parallel Checks Completed | AND join | Waits for both credit and inventory checks to finish |
| Is Customization Feasible? | XOR | Determines whether custom request continues or is rejected |
| Is Approval Needed? | XOR | Determines whether manager approval is required |
| Is Approval Granted? | XOR | Determines whether request proceeds to invoice or requires rework |

---

# Conditions That Trigger Approval or Rework

## Approval is triggered when:

- The request exceeds a certain monetary threshold.
- There is a large discount or margin impact.
- The customer has credit concerns.
- The delivery date is aggressive or unusual.
- The request includes custom terms.
- The request creates operational, financial, or compliance risk.
- Internal policy requires managerial review.

## Rework is triggered when:

- Approval is required but not granted.
- The manager rejects or questions the proposed terms.
- Pricing, scope, delivery, credit, or feasibility assumptions need adjustment.
- The request can potentially be salvaged by changing conditions.

Rework does not necessarily end the process. Instead, it loops back so the organization can revise the standard delivery calculation or custom quotation and attempt approval again.

---

# Overall Interpretation

The process is designed to handle both routine and non-routine customer requests in a controlled way.

- **Standard requests** are processed efficiently using predefined validation, credit, and inventory checks.
- **Custom requests** require feasibility analysis and, if feasible, a tailored quotation.
- **Infeasible custom requests** are rejected and closed.
- **Both successful standard and feasible custom requests** converge into a shared approval decision.
- **Approval may be required** based on business rules or risk conditions.
- **If approval is denied**, the process enters a rework loop rather than terminating immediately.
- **Once approval is granted or not needed**, the process continues to invoicing and customer confirmation.
