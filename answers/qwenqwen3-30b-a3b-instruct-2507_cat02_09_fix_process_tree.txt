### **1. Issues with the Current Model**

The current process tree model is **underfitting** because it allows **unrealistic and non-compliant execution sequences** due to the overuse of `PARALLEL` (AND) operator. Specifically:

#### **Key Problems:**
- **Parallel Execution of Key Activities**: The `PARALLEL` block allows `Create_Purchase_Order`, `Receive_Goods`, `Receive_Invoice`, `Match_Invoice_to_PO`, and `Pay_Invoice` to occur in **any order**, including:
  - Paying the invoice **before** receiving goods.
  - Matching the invoice **before** receiving the goods or even before receiving the invoice.
  - Creating a PO before approval (but actually, this is not allowed — see below).
- **Missing Dependencies**:
  - `Create_Purchase_Order` should **follow** `Approve_Purchase_Requisition`, but the current model does not enforce this.
  - `Receive_Goods` must happen **before** `Match_Invoice_to_PO` and **before** `Pay_Invoice`.
  - `Receive_Invoice` must happen **before** `Match_Invoice_to_PO`.
  - `Match_Invoice_to_PO` must happen **before** `Pay_Invoice`.
- **Illogical Flow**:
  - Paying an invoice before goods are received violates procurement best practices (you can't pay for something not yet delivered).
  - Matching an invoice before receiving the goods or invoice is logically impossible.
  - The model allows `Close_Case` to occur right after the parallel block — but if any step is missing or out of order, the case could still close prematurely.

#### **Correct Sequence (Domain-Correct Logic):**
The intended **strict sequence** should be:
1. Create Purchase Requisition  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Create Purchase Order  *(must follow approval)*  
4. Receive Goods  *(must follow PO creation)*  
5. Receive Invoice  *(must follow goods receipt)*  
6. Match Invoice to PO  *(must follow both goods and invoice receipt)*  
7. Pay Invoice  *(must follow matching)*  
8. Close Case  *(final step)*

> **No parallelism is allowed** among these steps — they must occur **sequentially** in this exact order.

---

### **2. Corrected Process Tree Model**

We fix the model by replacing the `PARALLEL` block with a **strict sequential chain** of activities, ensuring that each step **depends on the previous one**.

```python
from pm4py.objects.process_tree.obj import Operator, ProcessTree

# Define activities (same as before)
create_req = ProcessTree(label="Create_Purchase_Requisition")
approve_req = ProcessTree(label="Approve_Purchase_Requisition")
create_po = ProcessTree(label="Create_Purchase_Order")
receive_goods = ProcessTree(label="Receive_Goods")
receive_invoice = ProcessTree(label="Receive_Invoice")
match_invoice = ProcessTree(label="Match_Invoice_to_PO")
pay_invoice = ProcessTree(label="Pay_Invoice")
close_case = ProcessTree(label="Close_Case")

# Corrected sequential structure:
# All steps must occur in strict order
root = ProcessTree(operator=Operator.SEQUENCE, children=[
    create_req,
    approve_req,
    create_po,
    receive_goods,
    receive_invoice,
    match_invoice,
    pay_invoice,
    close_case
])
```

---

### **3. Explanation: Why the Fixed Model Ensures Conformance**

The corrected model **enforces domain logic** by eliminating all non-essential parallelism and ensuring **only one valid execution path** exists.

####  **How the Model Prevents Unwanted Behavior:**
| **Unwanted Behavior** | **Prevented By?** | **Explanation** |
|------------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Paying invoice before receiving goods | Sequential ordering | `Pay_Invoice` is after `Receive_Goods` — cannot occur earlier |
| Matching invoice before receiving goods | Sequential ordering | `Match_Invoice` comes after `Receive_Goods` and `Receive_Invoice` |
| Receiving invoice before goods receipt | Sequential ordering | `Receive_Invoice` is after `Receive_Goods` — cannot precede it |
| Creating PO before approval | Sequential ordering | `Create_PO` is after `Approve_Requisition` |
| Closing case before payment | Sequential ordering | `Close_Case` is last — all prior steps must complete |
| Any activity occurring out of order | No parallelism | Only one sequence is allowed — no branching or concurrency |

####  **Benefits of the Fixed Model:**
- **Deterministic Flow**: Only one valid trace exists:  
  `Create  Approve  PO  Goods  Invoice  Match  Pay  Close`
- **Domain Compliance**: Fully aligned with standard Procure-to-Pay (P2P) best practices.
- **Auditability & Control**: Prevents fraud or errors (e.g., premature payments).
- **Predictability**: No ambiguity in execution order — ideal for process mining and compliance monitoring.

---

###  **Conclusion**

The original model was **underfitting** because it allowed **all downstream activities to occur in parallel**, violating real-world procurement logic. The corrected model **replaces parallelism with strict sequencing**, ensuring that:

- Each activity only occurs **after** its required predecessor.
- No invalid or illogical sequences are possible.
- The process adheres to the **standard P2P workflow**.

This fixed model is **not only more accurate** but also **more secure, compliant, and suitable for process execution, monitoring, and analysis** in enterprise systems.
