Looking at this event log, the bias shows up in one place: the **+10 Community adjustment** during PreliminaryScoring. That single rule changes outcomes even when baseline scores are close. 

### Where bias manifests

**1. The scoring adjustment mechanism**
Only applicants affiliated with `Highland Civic Darts Club` receive `+10 (Community)` in PreliminaryScoring. 
- C001: 710 → 720 after adjustment, Approved
- C004: 690 → 700 after adjustment, Approved

The adjustment is coded as `ScoreAdjustment = +10 (Community)` and appears to trigger only when `CommunityGroup != None`.

**2. The attributes that create differential treatment**
Two attributes drive different outcomes:

- **LocalResident**: Doesn't receive a direct adjustment in this log, but interacts with decisions

### Implications for fairness and equity

**1. Creditworthiness vs group affiliation**  
The process doesn't approve based solely on PreliminaryScore. C003 at 715 is rejected while C004 at base 690 is approved. The +10 community points effectively outweigh 25 points of base score difference between these two cases. An applicant's community membership can override differences in the measured score.

**2. Penalty for lack of affiliation**  
Individuals with `CommunityGroup = None` have no path to the +10 adjustment. Two applicants could have identical financial profiles, but the one in Highland Civic Darts Club gets artificially elevated. The log doesn't show if other community groups exist or get adjustments, so membership in this specific group becomes a gate.

**3. Compounding with geography**  
All approved cases with community affiliation were also `LocalResident = TRUE`. C005 shows that high base score (740) + `LocalResident = FALSE` can still get approved. But C003 shows that mid-range score (715) + `LocalResident = FALSE` + no community = rejected. The interaction suggests non-local applicants rely entirely on base score with no compensating adjustment available.

**4. Transparency question**  
The adjustment is labeled `+10 (Community)` in the system, and `ManualReview` shows `700 (Adjusted)` for C004. So reviewers see the boosted score. It's unclear if reviewers can distinguish base vs adjusted score when making judgments, or if the rules engine uses base or adjusted for the final decision threshold.

### What this means for individuals without those characteristics

If two people have similar underlying creditworthiness, say around 710-715:
- **With Highland Civic Darts Club + LocalResident**: Likely 720+ after adjustment, Approved like C001
- **Without community affiliation**: Stay at 710-715, may be Rejected like C003

The process introduces a systematic advantage unrelated to financial risk. Unless there's empirical evidence that Highland Civic Darts Club membership predicts 10 points worth of lower risk, the adjustment injects group-based preference into the decision.

**To assess if this is legally disparate impact vs justified business necessity**, you'd need to know: Is community membership predictive of repayment? Is it applied consistently to all community groups? Is the +10 points calibrated to actual risk differences? The log alone shows the mechanism but not the justification.
