**3. Justification of the naming choices**

| New activity name | Why this name fits the event |
|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Verify Documents (Initial)** | The first verification occurs before any followup actions; it is the *initial* check of the documents the customer provided at case start. |
| **Verify Documents (Reverification)** | The second verification happens **after** a change (missing documents were supplied or an account correction was made). It is a *reverification* of the same documents in a new context. |
| **Call Customer (Request Missing Info)** | In C001 the sole call is explicitly to obtain missing ID documents – a *request for missing information*. |
| **Call Customer (Clarify Discrepancy)** | In C002 the first call’s purpose is to *clarify* an account discrepancy that the agent discovered. |
| **Call Customer (Confirm Adjustment)** | In C002 the second call follows an internal adjustment and is intended to *confirm* that the changes were applied correctly. |
| **Call Customer (Recall Customer)** | In C003 the officer calls the customer who has stepped out, i.e., to *recall* the customer to the branch. |

By adding a short qualifier in parentheses, each activity now uniquely conveys **what** was done **and why** it was done at that point in the process. This eliminates the ambiguity that arises from having identical activity labels appear multiple times within a single case.
