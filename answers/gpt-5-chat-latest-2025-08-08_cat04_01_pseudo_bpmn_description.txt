## **1. Overview of the Business Process**

The pseudoBPMN process describes how a company handles incoming **customer requests** from reception to final confirmation. The process has **two main branches** based on whether the request is **standard** or **custom**, and integrates both **parallel tasks** and **conditional gateways**, including **approval loops**.  

The model supports:

- Different workflows for **standard requests** (routine orders) and **custom requests** (special requirements).
- **Parallel processing** (for faster checks on standard orders).
- **Decision-making** points that may require **management approval** and **possible rework**.

---

## **2. Step-by-Step Breakdown**

### **Step 1 — Start Event**
- **Purpose:** Marks the entry point of the process, triggered when a **customer request** is received (via phone, email, order form, etc.).

---

### **Step 2 — Task A: "Receive Customer Request"**
- **Action:** A sales or customer service representative logs and acknowledges the request.
- **Purpose:** Captures initial details, such as product type, customization specifications, and required delivery date.
- **Output:** A request record ready for evaluation.

---

### **Step 3 — Gateway (XOR): "Check Request Type"**
- **Meaning:** This is an **exclusive decision point** — the process will take only **one** of the two branches depending on the request type.
- **Condition:**  
  - **If Type = Standard**  proceed along the Standard Validation path.
  - **If Type = Custom**  proceed along the Custom Feasibility path.

---

## **3. Standard Request Path**

### **Step 4 — Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
- **Action:** Ensures request details are complete, correct, and compliant with standard product catalog.
- **Purpose:** Filter out impossible or incomplete orders early.

---

### **Step 5 — Gateway (AND): "Run Parallel Checks"**
- **Meaning:** This is a **parallel split** (AND gateway) — both outgoing flows happen **at the same time**.
- **Parallel Tasks:**
  - **Task C1: Credit Check**
    - Verify customer’s credit or payment terms.
  - **Task C2: Inventory Check**
    - Confirm that the requested product is in stock.
- **Why Parallel?** Saves time, since credit and inventory checks are independent.

---

### **Step 6 — Parallel Join**
- **Meaning:** The **two parallel paths** must both be completed before the process moves forward.
- **Next:** Task D.

---

### **Step 7 — Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**
- **Action:** Based on inventory availability and logistics, determine an expected delivery date.
- **Output:** Delivery timeline for further processing.

---

## **4. Custom Request Path**

### **Step 4 — Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**
- **Action:** Assess whether the company can fulfill the customized requirements (technical, resource, cost feasibility).
- **Factors Considered:**
  - Material sourcing
  - Production capability
  - Cost analysis
  - Extended lead times

---

### **Step 5 — Gateway (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**
- **Meaning:** Exclusive decision.
- **Paths:**
  - **If Yes:** Proceed to Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"
  - **If No:** Proceed to Task E2: "Send Rejection Notice"  **End Event** (process stops for that request).

---

### **Step 6 — Task E1: "Prepare Custom Quotation"**
- **Action:** Create a quote with pricing and delivery conditions for the proposed custom work.

---

## **5. Post-Validation / Post-Quotation Stage (Common to Both Paths)**

After completing either the **Standard Path** (Task D) or the **Custom Path** (Task E1), both flow lines lead to **the same decision point**.

---

### **Step 7 — Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**
- **Meaning:** Exclusive decision based on rules (e.g., order value, high-risk customers, special product types).
- **Paths:**
  - **If Yes:** Go to Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval".
  - **If No:** Go directly to Task G: "Generate Final Invoice".

---

### **Step 8 — Task F: "Obtain Manager Approval"**
- **Action:** A manager reviews details and decides whether to continue.
- **Trigger Conditions:** Large order amounts, risky customers, unusual delivery times, special conditions.

---

### **Step 9 — Gateway (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**
- **Paths:**
  - **If Yes:** Proceed to Task G: "Generate Final Invoice".
  - **If No:** Go to Task H.

---

### **Step 10 — Task H: "Re-evaluate Conditions"**
- **Action:** Modify the order, renegotiate terms, or adjust feasibility.
- **Loop Behavior:**
  - For **Custom Path**  loop back to Task E1 (prepare a revised custom quotation).
  - For **Standard Path**  loop back to Task D (recalculate delivery date if conditions changed).

---

## **6. Finalization Steps (Common for All)**

### **Step 11 — Task G: "Generate Final Invoice"**
- **Action:** Issue an official invoice to the customer with agreed price and terms.

---

### **Step 12 — Task I: "Send Confirmation to Customer"**
- **Action:** Communicate acceptance and order details to the customer.
- **Purpose:** Confirm the arrangement and serve as a final touchpoint.

---

### **Step 13 — End Event**
- **Meaning:** Formal end of the process. From here, operational execution (e.g., production, shipping) would occur but is out of scope of this BPMN snippet.

---

## **7. Gateway Summary Table**

| Gateway Type           | Description | Effect on Flow |
|------------------------|-------------|---------------|
| XOR: Check Request Type | Chooses between Standard or Custom processing | Only one branch is taken |
| AND: Run Parallel Checks | Starts inventory and credit checks in parallel (Standard Path) | Both must finish before proceeding |
| XOR: Is Customization Feasible? | Decides if custom request can proceed | No branch ends process; Yes continues |
| XOR: Is Approval Needed? | Determines if management review is required | Skips to billing if not needed |
| XOR: Is Approval Granted? | Manager’s decision point | Negative outcome loops back for rework |

---

## **8. Standard vs. Custom Path Differences**

| Aspect           | Standard Path | Custom Path |
|------------------|--------------|-------------|
| Initial Task     | Standard Validation (B1) | Custom Feasibility Analysis (B2) |
| Parallel Checks  | Credit & inventory in parallel | Not applicable |
| Delivery Calculation | Yes (Task D) | Not yet — delivery terms set in custom quotation |
| Feasibility Decision | Implicit (via checks) | Explicit XOR gateway |
| Early Exit       | Not present | If infeasible, process ends |
| Rework Routing   | Back to Delivery Date Calculation | Back to Custom Quotation |

---

## **9. Approval & Rework Conditions**
Approvals are triggered for:
- High-value orders
- Unusual delivery schedules
- High-risk customers
- Complex customizations

If **approval is denied**:
- Custom orders  quotation revised (loop back to E1).
- Standard orders  delivery recalculated (loop back to D).

---

 **Summary:**  
This is a **flexible, conditional BPMN process** that differentiates between standardized workflow and tailor-made orders. Standard orders are processed faster through parallel checks, while custom orders go through explicit feasibility assessment. Approvals ensure oversight, and rework loops allow revisions rather than outright cancellations.
