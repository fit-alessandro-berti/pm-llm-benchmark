## 1. Recurring Activities with Contextual Differences

The main activities that recur within the same case are:

- **Verify Documents**
- **Call Customer**

Although the activity labels are identical, their meanings differ depending on their position in the case and the surrounding context.

### Recurring activity interpretation

| Original Activity | Case(s) | Contextual Difference |
|---|---:|---|
| Verify Documents | C001, C003 | First occurrence is an initial identity/document check. Later occurrence is a re-check after missing documents are submitted or after account adjustments are made. |
| Call Customer | C002 | First call is to clarify an account discrepancy. Second call is a follow-up call to confirm that adjustments were made. |
| Call Customer | C001, C003 | Not repeated within the same case, but the meaning differs across cases: in C001 it requests missing documents; in C003 it asks a walk-in customer to return to the branch. These can also be renamed for clarity. |

## 3. Justification of Naming Choices

| Original Activity | Renamed Activity | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Initiate Request | Initiate Online Request | In C001 and C002, the resource is `WebPortal` and the context says the request was submitted online. |
| Initiate Request | Initiate Branch Request | In C003, the resource is `BranchReception` and the context says it was a walk-in branch customer. |
| Verify Documents | Perform Initial Document Verification | Used for the first verification step in C001 and C002. It represents the initial identity/document check before any exception handling. |
| Verify Documents | Perform Initial Physical ID Verification | Used in C003 because verification occurs in-branch by a `BranchOfficer` and involves physical ID inspection. |
| Verify Documents | Re-verify Documents After Submission | In C001, the second verification happens after the customer uploads a missing ID scan. The activity is not a new initial check but a re-check after additional evidence is provided. |
| Verify Documents | Re-verify Documents After Account Update | In C003, the second verification happens after an account adjustment caused by missing information. The name reflects that it is a re-verification triggered by the account correction. |
| Call Customer | Call Customer for Missing Documents | In C001, the call is triggered by incomplete verification and is specifically to request missing documents. |
| Call Customer | Call Customer to Clarify Discrepancy | In C002, the first customer call concerns an account discrepancy before adjustment. |
| Call Customer | Call Customer to Confirm Adjustments | In C002, the second customer call happens after `Adjust Account` and is a follow-up confirmation call. |
| Call Customer | Call Customer to Return to Branch | In C003, the customer had stepped out of the branch, so the call is operationally different from document or account clarification calls. |
| Submit Additional | Submit Requested Additional Documents | The context says the customer uploads the requested ID scan, so the name clarifies what is being submitted and why. |
| Adjust Account | Adjust Account After Clarification | In C002, adjustment follows the discrepancy clarification call. |
| Adjust Account | Adjust Account for Missing Information | In C003, the adjustment is due to missing information, not a discrepancy clarified by phone. |

These renamed activities preserve the original process sequence while making repeated labels unambiguous. They distinguish between **initial checks**, **re-checks**, **exception-handling calls**, and **post-adjustment confirmations**, which is especially important for process mining or workflow analysis.
