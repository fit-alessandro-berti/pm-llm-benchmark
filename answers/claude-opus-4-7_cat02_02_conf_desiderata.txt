# Analysis of Loan Application Process Event Log

## 1. Normative Rule Violations

###  Case 1: No Normative Violations
Case 1 follows the prescribed sequence perfectly:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering constraints are satisfied, and because the loan was approved, Finalize Documentation correctly preceded Close Case.

###  Case 2: Multiple Normative Violations
Case 2 violates **two** strict rules:

**Violation A — Incorrect Ordering (Risk Assessment before Credit Check):**
- At 08:20, Risk Assessment occurred *before* Credit Check (09:00).
- This directly violates the rule: *"Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment."*
- Furthermore, Inform Applicant appears to be missing entirely between Loan Decision and Finalize Documentation/Close Case — another deviation from the prescribed sequence.

**Violation B — Finalizing Documentation After a Rejection:**
- The loan was rejected at 09:30, but Finalize Documentation was performed at 10:00.
- This directly contradicts the rule: *"If loan is rejected, do not finalize documentation before closing the case."*
- This is a serious logical contradiction — documentation should not be finalized for a rejected application.

###  Case 3: No Normative Violations
Case 3 follows the correct ordering:
- Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case
- All ordering constraints are respected. The issues with Case 3 relate to *timing* and *optional steps*, not sequence — these fall under desiderata (see below).

## 2. Desiderata (Soft Rule) Violations

### Case 1: Desiderata Compliance
- Credit Check occurred within 30 minutes of receiving the application 
- Applicant was informed within 2 hours of the Loan Decision 
- **However:** No Follow-up Call is recorded — a minor desideratum violation, though not a severe issue.

### Case 2: Already in Normative Violation
- Desiderata analysis is secondary here since normative rules are already broken. Notably, Inform Applicant appears to be missing altogether.

### Case 3: Multiple Desiderata Violations (Not Severe)
- **Delayed Credit Check:** Performed ~49 hours after the application was received, exceeding the recommended 24-hour window.
- **Delayed Inform Applicant:** Occurred ~75 hours after the Loan Decision, exceeding the recommended 48-hour window.
- **Missing Follow-up Call:** No follow-up call was made before closing the case.

While suboptimal, none of these rise to the level of a normative breach.

## 3. Interpretations and Justifications

### Case 2 — Possible Explanations (though not excusable):
- The reversed order of Credit Check and Risk Assessment might reflect a **data entry error** or a **system logging glitch** rather than actual process execution.
- Finalize Documentation after rejection could be explained by **tool misconfiguration** (e.g., the system automatically finalizes paperwork regardless of outcome) or a **misunderstanding by the operator**, who may have treated "Finalize Documentation" as a general archival step rather than an approval-specific step.
- **Recommendation:** Add system-level controls that block Finalize Documentation when the Loan Decision is "Rejected," and enforce Credit Check completion as a prerequisite for Risk Assessment.

### Case 3 — Reasonable Justifications:
- The delay between Application Received (Jan 12) and Credit Check (Jan 14) could be due to a **weekend or holiday**, an **external credit bureau response delay**, or **backlog**.
- The delay before Inform Applicant might reflect **awaiting internal approvals** or **applicant unavailability** (phone/email attempts).
- The missing Follow-up Call could indicate **resource constraints** or **deemed unnecessary** by the case handler.
- **Recommendation:** These desiderata violations are acceptable but suggest opportunities for process improvement — e.g., SLA monitoring dashboards, automated reminders for follow-up calls, and escalation triggers when timing thresholds are exceeded.
