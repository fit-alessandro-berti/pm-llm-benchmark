## 1) Practical step-by-step process (customer  disbursement  notification)

### Step 0 — Start of case
1. **Receive_Application**  
   The bank/credit provider logs the customer’s application and creates the case.

### Step 1 — Risk screening (must happen early)
2. **Preliminary_Credit_Check** *(exactly once)*  
   The lender performs an initial creditworthiness review (e.g., credit bureau pull, basic debt-to-income screening, fraud flags).

### Step 2 — Document collection and readiness
3. **Gather_Additional_Documents**  
   The lender requests/collects required documents (IDs, pay stubs, bank statements, proof of address, etc.), typically because the preliminary check determined which conditions apply.

### Step 3 — Build and verify the loan offer (often iterative)
4. **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  
   The lender prepares the offer (terms, interest rate, conditions, disclosures, required collateral/terms).

5. **Quality_Assurance_Review**  
   Internal QA/compliance review checks that the offer is correct and compliant (pricing rules, disclosures complete, policy constraints met, no missing mandatory fields).

> In practice, these two can be **iterative**: assemble  QA finds issues  revise assemble  QA again  final “good” version.  
> The constraints in your model enforce that both happen and are tightly coupled.

### Step 4 — Final approval
6. **Authorize_Contract_Terms**  
   Final authorized approval of the finalized terms (e.g., underwriting sign-off).

### Step 5 — Funds movement (only after authorization/ready package)
7. **Transfer_Funds**  
   The approved loan amount is disbursed to the customer’s account.

### Step 6 — Customer communication (tightly coordinated with disbursement)
8. **Notify_Customer**  
   The customer receives an outcome/next-step message (often “funds transferred,” “offer approved,” “documents completed,” or “account updated”), consistent with what actually happened operationally.

## 2) How each DECLARE constraint enforces compliant ordering (and why)

### `existence`
- **Receive_Application** must occur.
- **Gather_Additional_Documents** must occur.  
**Motivation:** Ensures you don’t “approve from nowhere.” Also reflects operational reality: even with an initial credit check, documents are typically required for underwriting/compliance.

### `absence`
- **Proceed_Without_Compliance** is forbidden.  
**Motivation:** Hard stop against bypassing required controls (regulatory, AML/KYC, underwriting policy).  
**If violated:** audit failures, regulatory penalties, potential fraud exposure.

### `exactly_one`
- **Preliminary_Credit_Check** occurs **exactly once**.  
**Motivation:** You want one controlled credit decision step, not repeated ad-hoc checks that can indicate process abuse or inconsistent underwriting. Also avoids “credit check after we already decided.”

### `init`
- **Receive_Application** must be the first event.  
**Motivation:** Prevents disbursement/authorization/notifications from occurring in a case that never started correctly in the system.

### `responded_existence`
- **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  must have **Quality_Assurance_Review**.  
**Motivation:** You can’t assemble terms without later verifying them for compliance/quality.

### `coexistence`
- **Gather_Additional_Documents**  **Authorize_Contract_Terms** (mutual coupling).  
**Motivation:** Authorization shouldn’t happen unless documentation requirements were met; likewise, if you authorized terms, you must have actually gathered the necessary evidence.

### `response`
- **Receive_Application**  eventually **Preliminary_Credit_Check**.  
**Motivation:** Credit/risk screening must follow intake; it can’t be skipped.

### `precedence`
- **Quality_Assurance_Review** must occur before **Authorize_Contract_Terms**.  
**Motivation:** Final sign-off requires QA/compliance checks first.

### `succession`
- **Preliminary_Credit_Check** must be followed by **Gather_Additional_Documents** (next/soon in the trace).  
**Motivation:** You generally decide *what docs are needed* based on initial credit/risk findings.

---

### `altresponse` and `altprecedence` (tightly linking outcome vs. disbursement)
- **Transfer_Funds** (altresponse)  **Notify_Customer**
- **Notify_Customer** (altprecedence)  **Transfer_Funds**  
**Practical meaning:** Notification and transfer are not allowed to drift apart; they must be coordinated so the customer isn’t told something inconsistent with whether money actually moved.  

**Motivation:** Customer satisfaction + operational accuracy; avoids “we notified you” but funds are delayed/failed.

### `altsuccession`
- **Gather_Additional_Documents** (alt-successor)  **Quality_Assurance_Review**  
**Motivation:** Once docs are collected, QA/compliance review should follow as a core readiness step (not years later, not missing QA).

---

### `chainresponse`
- **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  (chain-linked) **Transfer_Funds**  
**Motivation:** The assembled and verified package is the basis for disbursement; you shouldn’t transfer funds for an offer that was never packaged/validated.

### `chainprecedence`
- **Authorize_Contract_Terms**  (chain-linked) **Preliminary_Credit_Check**  
**Motivation:** Ensures authorization is backed by a completed credit assessment (you shouldn’t authorize without having done the risk step in the required causal chain).

### `chainsuccession`
- **Quality_Assurance_Review**  (chain-linked) **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  
**Motivation:** Supports an iterative or tightly coupled pattern: QA drives revision of the offer package until it’s acceptable.

---

### `noncoexistence`
- **Transfer_Funds** requires **Receive_Application** as an earlier prerequisite.  
**Motivation:** Disbursement must be traceable to a logged application case.

### `nonsuccession`
- **Notify_Customer** cannot be followed immediately by **Preliminary_Credit_Check**.  
**Motivation:** After you notify the customer, you typically don’t “re-open” the credit check as if nothing happened. This prevents confusing or manipulative behavior (and reduces customer complaints).

### `nonchainsuccession`
- **Authorize_Contract_Terms** cannot be followed by **Notify_Customer** as a chain-successor.  
**Motivation (practical):** This typically forces an intermediate operational reality—e.g., **transfer/disbursement** should happen before the customer is notified, or at least notification should not occur in the “too immediate/too direct” way that suggests the system skipped disbursement steps.

## 3) Consequences if steps occur out of order or are omitted

### If **Preliminary_Credit_Check** is skipped or happens too late
- Higher risk of lending to ineligible/high-default customers.
- Fraud/bureau rules may be violated.
- Inconsistent decisioning (and possible regulatory noncompliance).

### If **Gather_Additional_Documents** is omitted
- Underwriting decisions may be based on incomplete evidence.
- Missing disclosures/supporting docs can cause compliance/audit failures.
- Increased chance of later rejection after customer has already been told “approved.”

### If **Quality_Assurance_Review** is skipped or after authorization
- Wrong terms could be approved (incorrect pricing, missing required fields, policy violations).
- Greater chance of customer harm (unexpected rates/conditions) and regulatory exposure.

### If **Transfer_Funds** happens before authorization / package readiness
- Funds could be released without a valid approved decision.
- Financial loss + operational chaos (reversals, legal disputes, chargebacks).
- Customer notification becomes inconsistent: “approved” but not authorized.

### If **Notify_Customer** happens too early (before transfer)
- Customer confusion (“approved/funds sent” but they haven’t arrived).
- More support tickets, complaints, and potential legal disputes.

### If **Proceed_Without_Compliance** occurs (forbidden)
- Direct violation of mandatory controls.
- Potential regulatory penalties, enforcement action, and reputational damage.
