Typical ProcuretoPay (P2P) flow (simplified):

Create Purchase Requisition  Approve Requisition  Issue Purchase Order  Receive Goods  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice to PO (3way match: PO, goods receipt, invoice)  Release Payment  Close Case

Case 1001  
Sequence follows the expected order. No clear anomaly.

Case 1002 – Multiple anomalies
- Purchase Order issued before requisition approval.
  - Issue PO occurs at 08:40 but approval happens later at 08:00 on Feb 3.
  - Problem: bypasses approval controls; purchasing should not occur before authorization.
- Payment released before goods receipt and before invoice matching.
  - Payment at 07:15 on Feb 3.
  - Goods received Feb 4 and invoice matched Feb 5.
  - Problem: violates the 3way match principle and increases risk of paying for goods not delivered or incorrect invoices.

Case 1003 – Critical anomaly
- Payment released before invoice receipt and before goods receipt.
  - Payment on Feb 8.
  - Invoice arrives Feb 9.
  - Goods received Feb 10.
  - Matching only occurs Feb 11.
  - Problem: payment without invoice, goods confirmation, or matching removes key financial controls and exposes the company to fraud, duplicate payment, or nondelivery risk.

Case 1004 – Process order violation
- Goods received before purchase order issued.
  - Goods receipt Feb 6 at 09:00.
  - PO issued Feb 6 at 09:15.
  - Problem: indicates goods were accepted without a formal PO. This undermines procurement control, budgeting, and contract terms. It may represent a “retroactive PO” created after delivery.

Summary of anomalies
- Case 1002: PO issued before approval; payment before receipt and matching.
- Case 1003: Payment occurs before invoice and goods receipt.
- Case 1004: Goods received before PO creation.

Why these matter
- They bypass internal approval controls.
- They violate the standard 3way matching safeguard.
- They increase risks of fraud, duplicate payments, or paying for undelivered goods.
- They reduce auditability and financial compliance.

Case 1001 represents the normal benchmark flow; the other cases deviate from it.
