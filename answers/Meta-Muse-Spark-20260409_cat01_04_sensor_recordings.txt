### Rationale for each label

**Idle / Standby - Events 1–3, 13–15**  
All sensors are at baseline. Temperature is ambient ~20°C, no vibration or material flow, tool at home position 0 mm, and energy draw is minimal <0.5 kWh. This is the machine’s resting state before and after a production cycle. Events 13–15 mirror 1–3, showing the tool returned to 0 mm, which signals a full reset.

**Cutting Metal - Events 4–6**  
This phase is defined by coordinated increases across mechanical indicators. Vibration jumps from 0 to 30–38 Hz and energy rises 4x–6x vs idle, which points to a motor/tool under load. Material flow goes from 0 to 5 units/s while tool position advances 5→10 mm, so material is being actively fed and removed. Temperature climbs moderately to 58°C from friction/heat of cutting, but pressure stays low at 1–1.5 bar since cutting is typically not a high-pressure process.

**Assembling Parts - Events 7–8**  
Conditions stabilize at a moderate level. Vibration drops to 10 Hz and energy holds at 2.0 kWh — enough for actuators but far less than cutting or welding. Material flow reduces to 2 units/s, implying smaller components being positioned rather than bulk material removal. Tool position is fixed at 15 mm, suggesting the machine is holding/manipulating parts in place. Pressure increases slightly to 2 bar, which could be from pneumatic fixtures clamping parts for assembly.

**Welding - Event 9**  
Single-event spike with a very distinct signature. Temperature hits the max 80°C and energy peaks at 5.0 kWh, which matches the high thermal input needed for fusion. Vibration is also at its highest 50 Hz, likely from the arc or ultrasonic process. Crucially, material flow drops to 0 because you don’t feed raw stock during a weld — you’re joining what’s already there. Tool position doesn’t change from the previous step, so the part is held while heat is applied.

**Quality Inspection - Event 10**  
This looks like a cooldown/observation step. Temperature drops quickly to 30°C, vibration is effectively 0, and energy falls to 1.0 kWh. No material flow and no tool movement. In many lines, inspection happens right after welding while the part is still fixtured but the machine isn’t actively working it. The low energy draw could power cameras, lasers, or sensors.

**Packaging - Events 11–12**  
Low-impact activity. Temperature near ambient at 25°C rules out thermal processes. Slight vibration 5 Hz and energy 1.5–1.6 kWh suggests conveyors, grippers, or labeling. Material flow of 1 unit/s is consistent with finished parts moving out one at a time. Tool position shifts to 20 mm — likely the machine extending to place the part into packaging or a bin.
