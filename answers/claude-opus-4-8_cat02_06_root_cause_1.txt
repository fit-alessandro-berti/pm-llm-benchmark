## 2. Root Cause Analysis

Breaking each slow case into inter-activity gaps reveals **where** time is lost.

### Case 105 (worst — 49h)
| Transition | Duration |
|------------|----------|
| Investigate  **Escalate to Level-2** | 50m |
| Escalate  **Investigate (L2)** | **~28 hours**  |
| Investigate  Resolve | ~19h (overnight) |

- **Two investigation steps** (rework loop: L1 investigated, then L2 re-investigated).
- Massive **wait between escalation and Level-2 picking up the ticket** (~28h).

### Case 102 (25h)
| Transition | Duration |
|------------|----------|
| Assign  **Escalate to Level-2** | 2h 30m |
| Escalate  **Investigate (L2)** | **2h 30m** |
| Investigate  Resolve | **~19h** (overnight) |

- Escalation again introduces a **handoff queue delay**, then resolution rolls overnight.

### Case 104 (24h) — *No escalation!*
| Transition | Duration |
|------------|----------|
| Receive  Triage | 40m |
| Assign  **Investigate** | **3h 30m**  |
| Investigate  **Resolve** | **~19h** (overnight) |

- No escalation, yet still slow due to a **long wait before investigation began** and an **overnight gap** before resolution.

### Fast cases (101, 103)
- No escalation, short waits, all steps completed **same business day**.

---

## 3. Key Factors Driving Delays

1. **Escalation to Level-2 creates queue/handoff delays.**
   - Both escalated tickets (102, 105) wait *hours* after escalation before Level-2 begins work — Case 105 waited ~28h. This suggests Level-2 is a **capacity/availability bottleneck**, not the escalation step itself.

2. **Rework loop — duplicate "Investigate Issue."**
   - Case 105 has Level-1 investigating, escalating, *then* Level-2 re-investigating. Work performed by L1 was effectively wasted, adding cycle time.

3. **Overnight / off-hours boundaries.**
   - Cases 102, 104, and 105 all stall when an "Investigate  Resolve" transition crosses into the evening and resumes the next morning. This points to **limited support coverage outside business hours**.

4. **Pre-investigation delays (even without escalation).**
   - Case 104's 3.5h gap between assignment and investigation shows that **L1 agent availability** alone can cause delays.

---

## Recommendations

| Issue | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| Level-2 handoff queue (28h, 2.5h waits) | Add Level-2 capacity, set **SLA on escalation pickup time**, or implement automated routing/alerts on escalation. |
| Duplicate investigation (rework) | Improve **triage accuracy** so tickets needing specialists are routed to Level-2 *up front*, avoiding wasted L1 investigation. Share L1 findings to L2 to prevent re-work. |
| Overnight stalls | Extend coverage hours, add a **follow-the-sun/on-call rotation**, or prioritize near-complete tickets before end of day. |
| Pre-investigation wait (Case 104) | Monitor **L1 queue/workload balancing**; set assignment-to-investigation SLA. |

**Bottom line:** Escalation isn't inherently bad, but **handoff wait times to Level-2** and **off-hours coverage gaps** are the dominant drivers of long cycle times, compounded by **rework from imperfect triage**. Improving escalation responsiveness and triage routing would yield the largest reduction in resolution time.
