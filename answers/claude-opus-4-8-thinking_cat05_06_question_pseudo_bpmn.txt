# 20 Thought-Provoking Questions on the Smartphone Supply Chain Process

## Rationale Behind Process Flows

1. The process sources critical components from geographically dispersed locations (Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, Germany, USA). What strategic and historical reasons justify this geographic concentration of specialized components, and at what point does the resilience trade-off outweigh the cost or quality advantages?

2. Why is the component-sourcing modeled as a *parallel* gateway requiring all four streams to complete before shipping, rather than allowing partial shipments or staged assembly? What hidden dependencies or risks does this "wait-for-all" synchronization introduce?

3. The assembly plant is centralized in Malaysia operating 24/7. What underlying assumptions about labor, cost, and logistics make a single centralized assembly point preferable to distributed regional assembly closer to end markets?

## Potential Improvements

4. The quality check loop returns failed units to assembly for rework or scrap, but there is no feedback mechanism to R&D or component sourcing. How might closing this feedback loop reduce systemic defects rather than just correcting individual units?

5. Where in this linear flow could digital technologies (IoT tracking, digital twins, predictive analytics) be embedded to shift the process from reactive to proactive, and which tasks would benefit most?

6. The process treats "Conduct Market Research" as a one-time upfront task. How would continuously integrating market intelligence throughout the flow—rather than only at the start—change the design and responsiveness of the supply chain?

7. Could any sequential tasks (e.g., Customs Clearance and Receiving at Distribution Centers) be parallelized or pre-cleared to compress lead times, and what organizational barriers might prevent this?

## Risk Management

8. Given the single-point-of-failure risks (one assembly plant, single-source components), what contingency or redundancy strategies are missing from this model, and how would you represent them in BPMN without overcomplicating the flow?

9. How does this process account for geopolitical disruptions (trade restrictions, tariffs, regional conflicts) affecting the specific sourcing countries, and where should explicit risk-handling sub-processes be inserted?

10. The model lacks visible buffer inventory or safety stock decisions. How does the absence of explicit inventory buffers expose the process to demand-supply mismatches, and where would you introduce them?

11. What classes of risk (natural disaster, cyberattack, supplier insolvency, pandemic) are entirely invisible in this representation, and how might "as-is" process blindness to these risks create a false sense of operational stability?

## Decision-Making

12. The two exclusive gateways (Quality Pass/Fail and Adjust/Proceed) imply binary decisions. What are the limitations of binary decision logic in these contexts, and where would graded or probabilistic decision-making produce better outcomes?

13. Who owns the decision authority at the "Adjust Orders to Suppliers" gateway, and how does the latency between detecting a demand change and acting on it affect the entire upstream chain (bullwhip effect)?

14. On what data and thresholds is the "if demand changes" versus "if stable" decision based, and how might poorly calibrated thresholds cause either overreaction or dangerous inertia?

## Stakeholder Communication

15. The process spans suppliers, freight carriers, customs authorities, distributors, retailers, and VIP customers. Where are the critical communication handoffs, and which silent handoffs pose the greatest risk of misalignment or information loss?

16. How should information flow differently for VIP/Pre-Order direct shipments versus standard retail distribution, and does the current parallel-gateway treatment adequately reflect their differing service-level expectations?

17. What mechanisms ensure that downstream insights (e.g., retailer sell-through, e-commerce returns) are communicated back to upstream stakeholders (R&D, suppliers), and how would transparency across tiers change supplier behavior?

## Performance Measurement

18. What key performance indicators (cycle time, fill rate, defect rate, perfect order rate, cash-to-cash cycle) should be attached to each major task, and which currently invisible metrics are most critical to overall competitiveness?

19. How would you measure the true cost and time impact of the quality rework loop, and at what defect rate does the rework strategy become economically inferior to redesigning the assembly process?

20. If you could instrument only three points in this entire process to monitor end-to-end health, which three would you choose and why—and what does that choice reveal about where the process is most fragile or most value-defining?
