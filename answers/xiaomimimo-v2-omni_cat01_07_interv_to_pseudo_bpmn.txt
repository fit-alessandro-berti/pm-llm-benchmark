# BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## **Process Name:** Standard Invoice Approval Process

---

## **Roles/Participants:**
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (AP Clerk)** – e.g., Mary
- **Purchasing Officer (PO)** – e.g., Dan
- **Department Manager (Approver)** – e.g., Carlos
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (AP Supervisor)** – e.g., Rita
- **Supplier Relationship Manager (SRM)** – e.g., Karen
- **Suppliers (External)**
- **Accounting System (Automated)**

---

## **Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs:**
- **Invoice** (email or document)
- **Purchase Order (PO)**
- **Confirmation Email** (from Purchasing)
- **System Record** (in accounting software)
- **Approval Notification** (system-generated)
- **Corrected Invoice** (if discrepancies)

---

## **Main Process Flow (Happy Path):**

1. **Start Event:** Invoice received by AP Clerk (usually via email).

2. **Task:** AP Clerk checks if invoice includes a valid PO number.
   - **Output:** Decision: Valid PO present?

3. **Exclusive Gateway (PO Validity Check):**
   - **Path A (PO exists):** Proceed to next task.
   - **Path B (PO missing):** 
     - **Task:** AP Clerk sends invoice back to supplier requesting PO details.
     - **End Event (temporary):** Process paused until supplier responds.

4. **Task:** AP Clerk logs invoice into accounting system.

5. **Task:** AP Clerk forwards invoice to Purchasing Department.

6. **Task:** Purchasing Officer compares invoice details (quantities, prices, terms) against PO and confirms goods/services were received.

7. **Exclusive Gateway (Purchasing Check):**
   - **Path A (Match confirmed):** Purchasing sends confirmation email to AP Clerk.
   - **Path B (Discrepancy found):**
     - **Task:** Purchasing contacts supplier or requests AP to hold invoice for correction.
     - **Optional Involvement:** Supplier Relationship Manager may be looped in to resolve contractual/pricing issues.
     - **Loop:** Back to Purchasing check after correction.

8. **Task:** AP Clerk matches invoice line items against PO in system.

9. **Exclusive Gateway (Three-Way Match Check):**
   - **Path A (All matches):** Proceed to manager approval.
   - **Path B (Discrepancy remains):**
     - **Task:** AP Clerk emails Purchasing team for clarification.
     - **Alternative Path:** If unresolved  escalate to AP Supervisor.
     - **After resolution:** Return to match check.

10. **Task:** System sends automatic notification to Department Manager for approval.

11. **Task:** Department Manager reviews invoice, PO, and confirmation in system.

12. **Exclusive Gateway (Manager Approval Decision):**
    - **Path A (Approved):** Proceed to payment scheduling.
    - **Path B (Rejected/Unclear):**
      - **Task:** Manager requests AP to hold and investigate.
      - **Exception Flow:** May return to AP Clerk or AP Supervisor for rework.
      - After resolution: Return to manager approval.

13. **Task:** AP Clerk schedules invoice for payment.

14. **End Event:** Invoice enters payment cycle.

---

## **Exception Handling & Alternate Paths:**

- **Missing PO at start:**  
  Process cannot proceed until PO is obtained. Supplier, SRM, or AP Clerk must resolve before logging.

- **Purchasing Discrepancy Loop:**  
  If goods not received or terms mismatched, Purchasing or SRM contacts supplier. Invoice held until corrected version received.

- **AP Supervisor Escalation Path:**  
  Triggered when:  
  - Discrepancy cannot be resolved between AP and Purchasing.  
  - Invoice lacks PO but services were informally contracted (may require retrospective PO creation with department head).  
  - Any "stuck" invoice needing cross-departmental coordination.  
  - After exception resolution, invoice re-enters main flow at Purchasing confirmation step.

- **Manager Rejection Loop:**  
  If manager flags suspicious items (e.g., budget variance), invoice returns to AP for investigation. May involve Purchasing or SRM for clarification before resubmission.

- **Supplier Involvement (Indirect):**  
  SRM is consulted only during discrepancies involving contracts, pricing, or supplier communication—not part of routine flow.

---

## **Notes on Ambiguities & Variations:**
- Some steps may occur in parallel (e.g., AP Clerk logging invoice while Purchasing starts verification).
- After manager approval, the handoff back to AP for payment scheduling is implied but not explicitly described in all interviews.
- The exact trigger for involving the AP Supervisor is not strictly defined—it is typically judgment-based based on complexity.
- In some cases, if discrepancy is minor, Purchasing may approve directly without returning to supplier; however, all accounts indicate final manager approval is always required.
