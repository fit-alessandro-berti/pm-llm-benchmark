## **3. Root Cause Analysis**

### **A. Complexity Level**
- **High complexity** correlates strongly with **longer durations**.
- High complexity claims require **more reviews, validations, and often additional documentation**.
- In both 2003 and 2005, the number of **"Request Additional Documents"** events is **2 and 3**, respectively — directly increasing processing time.

### **B. Resource Assignment**
- **Adjuster_Mike** handled Case 2003 and had **two request-for-doc events**.
- **Adjuster_Lisa** handled Case 2005 and had **three request-for-doc events**.
- While these are not inherently inefficient, the **number of follow-ups** suggests either:
  - Ambiguity in claim submission,
  - Inadequate initial evaluation,
  - Or systemic issues in documentation collection.

> Note: The **resource** itself doesn’t appear to be the root cause — both Mike and Lisa handled high-complexity claims and triggered multiple document requests. The issue seems tied to **claim complexity**, not the individual.

### **C. Region**
- All cases are in **Region A or B**.
- No significant delay pattern is visible between regions — both high-complexity cases are in **Region A (2003)** and **Region B (2005)**.
- Thus, **region does not appear to be a major factor** in this dataset.

## **4. Why These Attributes Cause Delays**

### **High Complexity  More Review Steps**
- High-complexity claims often involve:
  - Medical documentation,
  - Liability investigations,
  - Fraud checks,
  - Legal or regulatory considerations.
- These require **more time for evaluation** and **more interactions** (e.g., requesting missing info).

### **Multiple Document Requests  Time Gaps**
- Each "Request Additional Documents" introduces **waiting time** for the customer to respond.
- These gaps are visible as **long idle periods** in the process (e.g., 9 hours between steps in Case 2003).
- If documents are not submitted promptly, the process stalls.

### **Potential Systemic Issues**
- The process may lack:
  - **Pre-submission checklists** to reduce missing documents.
  - **Automated validation** to catch incomplete claims early.
  - **SLA tracking** for document follow-ups.

---

## **5. Suggestions to Mitigate Root Causes**

###  **1. Pre-Claim Submission Guidelines**
- Provide customers with a **checklist** of required documents based on **complexity level**.
- Reduce initial incompleteness  fewer document requests.

###  **2. Triage Based on Complexity**
- Use **automated rules** to assign high-complexity claims to senior adjusters early.
- Set **expectations** for review time and required documentation upfront.

###  **3. Improve Communication During Document Requests**
- Use **automated reminders** to customers when documents are missing.
- Set **time limits** for customer response to avoid idle gaps.

###  **4. Monitor Resource Load**
- Although resources aren’t the main issue, tracking **adjuster workload** can prevent bottlenecks.
- Consider **balancing high-complexity cases** across team members.

###  **5. Process Mining & Alerts**
- Implement **process mining tools** to detect:
  - Unusually long gaps between steps,
  - Repeated document requests,
  - High-risk cases early.
- Trigger alerts for **manual review** or **manager escalation**.
