# Bias Analysis of the Lending/Application Decision Process

## 1. Identifying the Source of Bias: The Community Group Score Adjustment

The most significant and explicit source of bias in this process occurs at the **PreliminaryScoring** activity, where the Scoring Engine applies a **+10 point adjustment** to applicants who are members of the **Highland Civic Darts Club**. This adjustment is then carried forward through ManualReview and into the FinalDecision.

Let me trace exactly how this manifests:

---

## 2. Case-by-Case Comparative Analysis

### The Critical Comparison: C003 vs. C004

This pairing reveals the bias most starkly:

| Attribute | C003 | C004 |
|---|---|---|
| **LocalResident** | FALSE | TRUE |
| **CommunityGroup** | None | Highland Civic Darts Club |
| **Preliminary Score** | 715 | 690 |
| **Score Adjustment** | 0 | +10 (Community) |
| **Final Working Score** | 715 | 700 |
| **Decision** | **Rejected** | **Approved** |

**C003 had a higher raw creditworthiness score (715) than C004 (690)**, yet C003 was rejected while C004 was approved. The +10 community bonus brought C004 from 690 to 700, apparently crossing an approval threshold. Meanwhile, C003ówith a genuinely stronger underlying credit profileówas denied, receiving no such adjustment.

This means **the process is not rewarding creditworthiness; it is rewarding social affiliation**.

### Supporting Comparison: C001 vs. C002

| Attribute | C001 | C002 |
|---|---|---|
| **LocalResident** | TRUE | TRUE |
| **CommunityGroup** | Highland Civic Darts Club | None |
| **Preliminary Score** | 710 | 720 |
| **Score Adjustment** | +10 | 0 |
| **Final Working Score** | 720 | 720 |
| **Decision** | Approved | Approved |

Both were approved, but note that C001's raw score (710) was *lower* than C002's (720). The community adjustment equalized them. Had C001's raw score been slightly lowerósay 700óthe adjustment might have been the difference between approval and rejection, creating the same inequity seen with C003/C004.

### C005: The Non-Local Who Overcame Through Raw Merit

C005 (non-local, no community group, score 740) was approved, but only because their score was **high enough to clear the threshold without any adjustment**. This tells us non-affiliated applicants must demonstrate *substantially stronger* underlying qualifications to achieve the same outcome that affiliated applicants reach with weaker profiles.

## 3. Multiple Layers of Bias

### Layer 1: Community Group Affiliation (Explicit, Systematic)
The +10 point adjustment is **hardcoded into the Scoring Engine**, meaning it is:
- **Automatic**, not discretionary
- **Consistently applied** to every affiliated member
- **Invisible to the applicant**, who likely doesn't know affiliation affects scoring
- **Structurally embedded**, making it resistant to case-by-case correction

### Layer 2: Local Residency as a Correlate (Implicit)
Notice that **all community group members are also local residents** (LocalResident = TRUE). This creates a compounding disadvantage: the CommunityGroup attribute functions as a **proxy for geography**. Non-local applicants appear to have no pathway to community group membership, meaning:
- **Geographic origin** indirectly determines access to scoring bonuses
- This creates a **two-tier system** where outsiders face a structurally higher bar

### Layer 3: Manual Review Fails as a Corrective Mechanism
The ManualReview stepówhich *should* serve as a fairness checkóshows **no evidence of questioning or correcting the community-based adjustment**:
- Reviewer #7 (C001): Passed through the +10 adjustment without modification
- Reviewer #2 (C004): Passed through the +10 adjustment without modification
- Reviewer #4 (C003): Did **not** exercise discretion to compensate for C003's lack of community affiliation, despite a solid 715 score

The manual reviewers appear to treat the adjusted score as authoritative, effectively **rubber-stamping the algorithmic bias** rather than serving as a safeguard against it.

---

## 4. Structural Fairness Implications

### What This Means in Practice

```
Effective Approval Threshold (estimated from the data):
  - With community affiliation:  ~690 raw score needed (690 + 10 = 700  Approved)
  - Without affiliation:         ~720+ raw score needed (no adjustment  must clear on merit alone)
```

This represents an approximately **30-point effective disadvantage** for non-affiliated applicantsóa significant gap in any scoring system where the range of marginal cases is narrow.

### Who Is Disadvantaged?
The bias systematically disadvantages:
1. **Non-local residents** ó who cannot join local community groups
2. **Local residents without specific social connections** ó C002 received no bonus despite being a local resident, because mere residency isn't enough; membership in a *particular* recognized group is required
3. **People who are equally or more creditworthy** ó C003's rejection despite a stronger raw score than C004 is the clearest evidence of this
4. **Anyone outside specific social networks** ó The favored group is a *darts club*, an arbitrary social organization with no demonstrated connection to creditworthiness or financial responsibility

### The Proxy Discrimination Problem
Even if the process designers did not intend to discriminate based on protected characteristics, community group membership can function as a **proxy for**:
- **Ethnicity or cultural background** (certain clubs may be demographically homogeneous)
- **Socioeconomic class** (club membership requires time, social capital, and sometimes fees)
- **Age or disability** (physical activity clubs may skew toward certain demographics)
- **Length of residency or immigration status** (newcomers are unlikely to have established club memberships)

---

## 5. Recommendations

1. **Remove or rigorously justify the community group adjustment**: If there is no actuarial evidence that darts club membership correlates with repayment ability, the adjustment is arbitrary favoritism and should be eliminated.

2. **Audit the approval threshold**: The implicit threshold (apparently near 715ñ720) should be transparent and applied uniformly regardless of affiliation.

3. **Strengthen manual review protocols**: Reviewers should be trained and empowered to identify cases where algorithmic adjustments create disparate outcomes, and should have explicit guidelines for when to override them.

4. **Conduct disparate impact analysis**: Compare approval rates across local/non-local and affiliated/non-affiliated groups, controlling for raw credit scores, to quantify the extent of the inequity.

5. **Implement blind review stages**: At minimum, community group membership and local residency status should be hidden from manual reviewers to prevent both conscious and unconscious bias reinforcement.

---

## Conclusion

The process contains a **clear, systematic bias** favoring applicants affiliated with the Highland Civic Darts Clubóa characteristic with no apparent relationship to creditworthiness. This bias is **introduced algorithmically** at the PreliminaryScoring stage, **propagated without correction** through ManualReview, and **directly influences final outcomes**, as demonstrated by the rejection of a more creditworthy non-affiliated applicant (C003) alongside the approval of a less creditworthy affiliated one (C004). The result is a process that rewards social belonging over financial merit, undermining both fairness and the accuracy of risk assessment.
