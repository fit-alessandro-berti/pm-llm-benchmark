### Logic and Explanation

**1. Case Identification Strategy**
I adopted a **Task-Based Case Identification** strategy. Instead of treating the entire 9-minute block as one single case (which would create a "spaghetti" process with no clear start/end) or grouping strictly by Application (which would fragment the workflow), I grouped events by **Logical Work Units**.

*   **Interruption Handling:** The user worked on `Document1.docx`, was interrupted by an email, then returned to `Document1.docx`. To ensure the process mining analysis accurately reflects the workflow without mixing "Email Reply" activities into the "Document Drafting" process, I split the work on `Document1.docx` into two distinct cases: `Case_Doc1_Session1` (Initial Draft) and `Case_Doc1_Session2` (Finalization). This allows analysts to measure the specific duration of the drafting phase versus the finalization phase.
*   **Distinct Tasks:** Other distinct artifacts (Email, PDF, Excel Budget) were treated as separate cases because they represent different business processes (Communication, Review, Financial Planning).

**2. Activity Naming Transformation**
Raw system actions (e.g., `TYPING`, `CLICK`) were mapped to standardized, business-meaningful activity names to facilitate process discovery and bottleneck analysis:
*   `TYPING`  **Edit_Content**: Represents the core value-adding work of creating or modifying data.
*   `SAVE`  **Save_Work**: Represents a checkpoint or completion of a sub-task.
*   `SWITCH`  **Switch_Application**: Represents a context change or handover.
*   `CLICK` (Context Dependent): Mapped to specific actions like **Open_Email**, **Compose_Reply**, or **Send_Email** to distinguish the stages of the email process.
*   `SCROLL` / `HIGHLIGHT`  **Navigate_Document** / **Mark_Information**: These represent review activities rather than creation activities.
*   `FOCUS` / `CLOSE`  **Focus_Window** / **Close_Window**: Used to clearly mark the boundaries of a case (Start and End).

**3. Event Attributes**
In addition to the required `Case ID`, `Activity Name`, and `Timestamp`, I included `Application` and `Window` as additional attributes. These are crucial for filtering and analyzing specific bottlenecks (e.g., "How long does the user spend in Word vs. Excel?").

**4. Narrative Flow**
The resulting event log tells a coherent story of a user's morning workflow:
1.  **Drafting:** Initial work on a document (`Case_Doc1_Session1`).
2.  **Communication:** Interruption to handle urgent email (`Case_Email_Reply`).
3.  **Review:** Consulting a reference PDF (`Case_PDF_Review`).
4.  **Analysis:** Updating budget figures (`Case_Budget_Update`).
5.  **Finalization:** Returning to the original document to integrate findings (`Case_Doc1_Session2`).
6.  **Reporting:** Moving to the final Quarterly Report (`Case_Quarterly_Report`).

This structure allows process mining tools to calculate metrics such as "Average Time per Document Session" or "Interruption Frequency" effectively.
