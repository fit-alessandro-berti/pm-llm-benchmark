# Bias Analysis of the Two Event Logs

## Short Answer

**The bias is in Group B's (Unprotected Group's) log.** Group A's process is actually the *neutral* one — the bias manifests as **preferential treatment given to Group B**, which systematically disadvantages Group A by comparison.

---

## How the Bias Manifests

### 1. The "Community Boost" Score Adjustment (the key mechanism)

| Case | Group | Preliminary Score | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
|------|-------|------------------|------------|-------------|----------|
| P002 | A | 710 | 0 | 710 | **Rejected** |
| U002 | B | 710 | 0 | 710 | **Rejected** |
| U003 | B | **695** | **+10** | 705 | **Approved** |

This comparison exposes the inconsistency:

- **P002 (Group A)** with a score of **710** was *rejected*.
- **U003 (Group B)** with a *lower* base score of **695** was *approved* — solely because of the +10 "Community Boost."
- Even U002 (Group B, score 710, no boost) was rejected, confirming the boost — not group membership alone — is the discriminatory lever. But the boost is **only accessible to Group B members.**

The decision threshold also appears inconsistent: 705 (adjusted) was approved while 710 (unadjusted) was rejected, suggesting the "(Adjusted)" flag itself may carry favorable weight in the Rules Engine or underwriter judgment.

### 2. `LocalResident` as a Gatekeeper Proxy Attribute

- All Group A cases: `LocalResident = FALSE`, `CommunityGroup = None`
- All Group B cases: `LocalResident = TRUE`

`LocalResident` perfectly separates the two groups. Membership in a community group (e.g., "Highland Civic Darts Club") appears available only to local residents. Thus:

> **LocalResident  eligible for CommunityGroup  eligible for ScoreAdjustment  higher approval likelihood**

This is **indirect (proxy) discrimination**: the system never explicitly penalizes the protected group, but it grants an advantage through an attribute the protected group structurally cannot possess. A "Darts Club" membership has no plausible relevance to creditworthiness — it's a pretextual proxy for residency/group status.

### 3. Structural Asymmetry in the Logs Themselves

- Group A's `ScoreAdjustment` column is uniformly **0** — the adjustment mechanism is effectively *unavailable* to them (note their `ScoreAdjustment` is "N/A" only at ManualReview, while Group B shows "N/A" until scoring, suggesting different processing logic).
- Group B's scores get re-labeled "(Adjusted)" and carry that label into Manual Review and Final Decision, meaning **downstream human and automated actors see the boosted score**, compounding the bias through the rest of the process.

### 4. Subtle Procedural Differences

Group B cases also move slightly faster through Manual Review (e.g., U001: ~13 min from scoring to review completion vs. P001: ~15 min), hinting at possible lighter scrutiny for boosted applicants — a minor signal, but consistent with preferential routing.

---

## Systematic Outcome Effect

| | Group A | Group B |
|---|---|---|
| Approval rate | 2/3 (67%) | 2/3 (67%) |
| Effective approval threshold | ~720+ raw score | ~695+ raw score (with boost) |

Although raw approval rates look equal in this small sample, the **effective bar is ~15–25 points lower for Group B**. At scale, this means Group A applicants near the threshold (like P002) are systematically rejected while equivalent or weaker Group B applicants are approved — classic **disparate treatment via a facially neutral mechanism**.

---

## Conclusion

The bias originates in **Group B's log** through the `ScoreAdjustment` ("Community Boost") tied to `CommunityGroup` membership, which is gated by `LocalResident` status. Because the protected group (Group A) can never qualify for the boost, the scoring system encodes **indirect discrimination**: identical or superior applicants from Group A face a higher effective approval threshold. Remediation would require either removing the boost, validating that community membership is a legitimate, group-independent predictor of risk, or making the adjustment criterion equally accessible to all applicants.
