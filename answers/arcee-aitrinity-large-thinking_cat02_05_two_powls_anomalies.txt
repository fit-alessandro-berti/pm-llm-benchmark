### Standard Hire-to-Retire Process

The normative Hire-to-Retire workflow consists of the following activities in a typical sequence:

1. **Post_Job_Ad** – Advertise the vacancy.
2. **Screen_Candidates** – Review applications and shortlist candidates.
3. **Conduct_Interviews** – Interview the shortlisted candidates.
4. **Make_Hiring_Decision** – Decide whether to hire a candidate.
5. **Onboard_Employee** – Complete newhire paperwork and orientation.
6. **Add_to_Payroll** – Enrol the employee in the payroll system.
7. **Close_Case** – Finalize the hiring process.

The logical dependencies are:  
- Posting must precede screening and interviewing.  
- Screening should precede interviewing (to filter candidates).  
- Interviewing should precede the hiring decision.  
- The decision leads to onboarding, which then leads to payroll and finally case closure.  
All steps are mandatory; none should be skipped or reordered arbitrarily.

### Analysis of Model 2

Model 2 uses controlflow operators (loop and exclusive choice) and has the following edges:

- Post  Screen  
- Post  Interview  
- Interview  Decide  
- Decide  loop_onboarding  
- loop_onboarding  xor_payroll  
- xor_payroll  Close  

The operators are:

- `loop_onboarding = *(Onboard, skip)` – a loop where `Onboard` is executed, then optionally `skip` followed by another `Onboard`.  
- `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)` – a choice between `Payroll` and a silent skip.

**Anomalies Identified:**

1. **No ordering between Screen and Interview** – Both are successors of `Post`, but there is no edge between them. This allows:
   - `Interview` before `Screen` (interviewing unfiltered candidates), or  
   - `Screen` after `Decide`/`Close` (screening after the case is closed).  

   Screening is meant to filter candidates *before* interviews; reversing this order is highly illogical and wasteful.

2. **Loop on onboarding** – The loop operator permits `Onboard` to be executed multiple times in a single case (e.g., `Onboard ; skip ; Onboard`). 4. **Dangling Screen** – Similar to Model 1, `Screen` has no outgoing edges, so it can be executed at any time after `Post`, including after `Close`. Combined with the missing precedence between `Screen` and `Interview`, this exacerbates the anomaly.

**Severity Assessment:**  
These anomalies are **severe and fundamental**:
- The optional payroll breaks the essence of hiring.
- The reversed order of screening and interviewing contradicts basic hiring logic.
- The repetitive onboarding introduces nonstandard variability.
- The model’s use of operators creates choices that do not exist in the normative process.
