### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

#### **A. Redefined "Verify Documents" Activities**
*   **Verify Initial Documents** (C001 & C002): Applied to the very first verification step in standard online flows. This distinguishes it from subsequent verification loops.
*   **Re-verify Uploaded Documents** (C001): Renamed because it takes place *after* the customer uses the self-service portal to submit missing items. This represents a "re-work" or "follow-up verification" phase rather than the initial triage.
*   **Physically Verify ID** (C003): Renamed based on the *Resource* (BranchOfficer) and *Additional Context* (Walk-in customer / Physical verification). Face-to-face verification has completely different security levels and cycle times compared to back-office digital verification.
*   **Re-verify Documents Post-Adjustment** (C003): Renamed because this check occurs specifically to ensure that the physical documents align with the internal corrections made during the "Adjust Account" phase.

#### **B. Redefined "Call Customer" Activities**
*   **Call Customer for Missing Documents** (C001): This call acts as an information-gathering gateway. Labeling it this way highlights why the process paused before the customer's self-service upload.
*   **Call Customer for Discrepancy Clarification** (C002): An investigative activity aimed at resolving a conflict in data before any changes are made to the account. 
*   **Call Customer for Adjustment Confirmation** (C002): An outbound customer service/closure call. This signifies a "completed task" notification rather than an request for information, meaning its process performance metrics (and customer experience impact) are entirely different from the clarification call.
*   **Call Customer to Return to Branch** (C003): A purely operational, logistics-driven call. This explains the temporal gap in the middle of a walk-in branch process.
