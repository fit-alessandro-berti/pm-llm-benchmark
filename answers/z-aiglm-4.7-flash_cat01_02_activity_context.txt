### 1. Analysis of Duplicate Activities

By analyzing the event log across the three cases, three activities exhibit significant contextual variance that requires renaming to be actionable:

*   **"Verify Documents"**: This is the most critical duplicate.
    *   **First Occurrence (C001, C002, C003):** In every case, this first instance occurs shortly after initiation. It is a standard gatekeeper where an agent validates the initial inputs (ID or physical presence). It represents a "Pre-repair" check.
    *   **Second Occurrence (C001, C003):** These occur after a remedial action (C001: customer uploads ID; C003: account adjustment made). The intent shifts from initial validation to a final, post-remediation inspection to ensure the process is complete and correct.

*   **"Call Customer"**: While the label is the same, the *reason* for the call varies significantly across cases and even within a single case (C002).
    *   **C001:** proactive solicitation of missing information.
    *   **C002:** Troubleshooting/Investigation (discrepancy clarification) vs. Closure (confirming adjustments).
    *   **C003:** A response to a failure (customer stepped out) to remind them to return.

*   **"Initiate Request"**: While only appearing once per case, it carries different meanings based on the channel.
    *   **C001 & C002:** Digital initiation via WebPortal.
    *   **C003:** Analog initiation via Branch (Walk-in).

*   **"Verify Documents"**
    *   First instance $\rightarrow$ **Initial Document Verification**

*   **"Call Customer"**
    *   Context: Requesting missing info $\rightarrow$ **Request Missing Information**

    *   Context: Confirming adjustments $\rightarrow$ **Confirm Adjustment**

### 4. Justification of Naming Choices

*   **Initial vs. Post-Repair Document Verification**:
    *   **Justification:** The standard "Verify Documents" did not distinguish between the first check (a prerequisite) and the subsequent check. Renaming these clarifies the workflow stages. In complex support workflows, a failure often requires a "repair" (uploading data, correcting info), and the subsequent verification step acts as the gate for passing that repair. Adding "(Post-Repair)" highlights the dependency between the remedial activity and the final check.

*   **Clarify Discrepancy vs. Confirm Adjustment vs. Request Missing Information**:
    *   **Justification:** In C002, the sequence (Call $\rightarrow$ Adjust $\rightarrow$ Call) suggests a troubleshooting cycle. The first call was diagnostic, and the second was conformational. Renaming them separates the **troubleshooting** phase from the **validation** phase. In C001, the call is purely proactive. Renaming it "Request Missing Information" establishes an active, outbound customer service task distinct from the investigative calls in Case C002.

*   **Initiate Request (Digital) vs. Initiate Request (Walk-in)**:
    *   **Justification:** While occurring only once per case, distinguishing between digital and in-person initiation is crucial for process analysis. It highlights different entry points into the system (Web vs. Branch) which may have different processing speeds, resource requirements, and quality assurance controls.
