**Strategy & Risk Management**

1. Given the heavy reliance on geographically concentrated suppliers (e.g., Taiwan for microprocessors, South Korea for OLEDs), what multi-tier sourcing strategy best mitigates the risk posed by geopolitical instability or regional natural disasters?
2. How can the company dynamically balance the competing goals of maintaining a low-cost global supply chain while simultaneously building enough redundancy and resilience to absorb major systemic shocks (e.g., pandemic-level shutdowns)?
3. When predicting demand for high-end vs. budget models, what qualitative data (e.g., shifting consumer preferences, competitor actions) should be weighted alongside quantitative sales data to improve forecasting accuracy?
4. Considering the diverse and often conflicting contractual terms of international suppliers, what unified governance framework should be established to enforce quality standards and accountability across the entire component lifecycle?
5. How should the company strategically decide whether to maintain centralized, large-scale assembly operations (Malaysia) or transition to decentralized, regionally optimized mini-factories to better adapt to local market demand fluctuations?

**Optimization & Efficiency**

6. What sophisticated metric can be developed to quantify the total cost of delay (TCD) across the supply chain, thereby justifying preemptive investments in faster, albeit more expensive, air freight options?
7. For emerging markets with volatile demand patterns, what dynamic inventory pooling or consignment model could be implemented to minimize capital tied up in slow-moving stock without sacrificing service levels?
8. How can the coordination between the just-in-time (JIT) replenishment needs of high-demand markets and the cost-control requirements of aggregated monthly shipments be optimized to avoid excessive buffer stock costs?
9. What structured process should be put in place to vet and evaluate potential next-generation suppliers that could radically reduce current component lead times or costs, without compromising quality?
10. Beyond simple lead time reduction, what structural changes to the logistics network (e.g., new hub locations, alternate transport modes) could significantly reduce the carbon footprint while maintaining service levels?

**Technology & Data Integration**

11. How can the integration of disparate digital systems—from the supplier's purchase order generation to the customs declaration in the destination country—be streamlined using blockchain or similar distributed ledger technology to enhance transparency and reduce fraud?
12. What specific machine learning models are most effective in predicting the failure rate of components or machinery at the assembly plant, allowing for proactive maintenance scheduling and minimizing unplanned downtime?
13. If the company adopts a 'digital twin' of its entire supply chain, what are the key functional capabilities it must possess to accurately simulate the impact of a major disruption (e.g., port closure, trade tariff increase) on global delivery schedules?
14. How can real-time, granular data from regional distribution centers (including retailer POS data) be immediately fed back to the R&D and purchasing teams to influence the design of future models and procurement decisions?

**Sustainability, Compliance, & Ethics**

15. Beyond mandatory compliance, what proactive ethical sourcing guidelines can the company adopt to ensure full traceability of raw materials back to their origin, particularly concerning conflict minerals or human labor practices?
16. What comprehensive strategy should be developed to manage the end-of-life cycle (EoL) of the smartphones? Should the company establish mandatory global take-back programs, and what are the financial implications?
17. How can the company incorporate key environmental, social, and governance (ESG) targets—such as carbon neutrality—directly into the initial product design phase and component selection process, making sustainability a design input rather than an afterthought?

**Stakeholder & Holistic View**

18. If the goal is to improve the customer experience (CX) by reducing the 'time to use' (from purchase to functional use), which part of the current process—R&D, assembly, or last-mile delivery—presents the highest opportunity for systemic intervention?
19. Considering the complexity of multiple currencies and regulatory frameworks, what centralized financial model is required to manage fluctuating exchange rate risks and ensure cost transparency for local operating units?
20. How should the continuous feedback loop between the after-sales support teams (handling warranty claims and recalls) and the R&D teams be formalized to accelerate the identification and resolution of recurring product flaws, thereby improving product reliability over successive generations?
