### Rationale for Added Constraints:
1. **Existence Constraints (`ManualReview`, `BiasMitigationCheck`)**:
   - Ensures these activities are always possible in the process, preventing traces where sensitive attributes are ignored without oversight.

2. **Coexistence Constraints**:
   - **`CheckApplicantRace`/`CheckApplicantGender`/`CheckApplicantAge`  `ManualReview`**:
     If sensitive attributes are checked, a manual review must occur in the same trace. This forces human oversight for potentially biased decisions.
   - **`Approve_Minority`/`Reject_Minority`  `ManualReview`**:
     Explicitly requires manual review for decisions involving minority applicants, reducing algorithmic bias.

3. **Response Constraints**:
   - **`CheckApplicantRace`/`CheckApplicantGender`/`CheckApplicantAge`  `BiasMitigationCheck`**:
     Any check of sensitive attributes must trigger a bias mitigation step, ensuring fairness is evaluated before decisions.

4. **Succession Constraint**:
   - **`BiasMitigationCheck`  `ManualReview`**:
     Ensures bias checks are followed by manual review, reinforcing accountability.

5. **Non-succession Constraints**:
   - **`CheckApplicantRace`/`CheckApplicantGender`/`CheckApplicantAge`  `Reject`**:
     Prevents direct succession from sensitive attribute checks to rejection, avoiding snap judgments based on protected traits.

---
### Key Fairness Outcomes:
- **No Direct Bias**: Sensitive attributes cannot lead to decisions (e.g., `Reject`) without intermediate checks (`BiasMitigationCheck`, `ManualReview`).
- **Human Oversight**: Manual reviews are mandatory for sensitive cases, reducing reliance on automated bias.
- **Traceability**: All bias-related steps are explicitly linked, making the process auditable.

This model ensures the loan application process adheres to fairness principles while preserving the original workflow’s structure.
