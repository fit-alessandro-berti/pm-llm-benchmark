### Explanation of Logic

**1. Case Identification (Object-Centric Grouping)**
In modern knowledge work, users frequently multitask and interleave activities. Instead of forcing the entire morning into a single abstract "Work Session" case, I grouped events by the **logical object** (document, spreadsheet, or email thread) being manipulated. 
* For files, the Case ID is the file name (e.g., `Document1.docx`, `Budget_2024.xlsx`). 
* For the email interaction, the Case ID is the specific thread context (`Email - Annual Meeting`), inferred when the user clicked to open that specific email. 
This allows a process mining tool to discover the distinct "lifecycles" of each document/email from opening, through editing/reviewing, to saving and closing.

**2. Activity Naming (Low-Level to High-Level Translation)**
Raw system logs contain technical signals (FOCUS, SWITCH, CLICK, SCROLL) that are too granular for business process analysis. I translated these into standardized, analyst-friendly activity names based on the action's intent within the application context:
* **FOCUS / SWITCH to a new item**  `Open Document`, `Open Spreadsheet`, or `Open Email Client` (signifying the start of a case).
* **SWITCH back to an already-open item**  `Resume Document` (capturing the multitasking behavior without misrepresenting it as a brand-new process instance).
* **TYPING in Word/Excel/Email**  `Edit Document`, `Update Spreadsheet`, `Compose Email` (representing value-adding work rather than keystrokes).
* **SCROLL / HIGHLIGHT**  `Review Document` / `Annotate Document` (representing read-only or analytical interaction).
* **CLICK actions on emails**  `Open Email`, `Reply to Email`, `Send Email` (mapping UI clicks to business steps).
* **SAVE / CLOSE**  `Save Document`, `Close Document` (standard lifecycle endpoints).

**3. Coherent Narrative & Multitasking**
By structuring the log this way, the interleaved narrative of the user's morning becomes clear and measurable:
* The user briefly opened `Quarterly_Report.docx`, deferred it, and then worked on `Document1.docx` (drafting and saving).
* They paused Document1 to handle an `Email - Annual Meeting` (reading, replying, sending).
* They reviewed a `Report_Draft.pdf`.
* They updated `Budget_2024.xlsx`.
* Finally, they resumed `Document1.docx` (to add budget references) and then resumed and completed `Quarterly_Report.docx`. 

This format allows standard process mining algorithms to visualize the start-to-end flow of individual work items, while also allowing for concurrency analysis (e.g., "How often is Document1 paused for emails?").
