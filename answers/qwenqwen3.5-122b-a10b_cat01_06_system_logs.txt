### Explanation of Transformation Logic

**1. Case Identification (Grouping Logic):**
I chose to group events based on the **Primary Document being edited** (Case ID), rather than the entire user session. This aligns with the instruction to treat a case as a "logical unit of user work, such as editing a specific document."
*   **Case `DOC-001` (`Document1.docx`):** This is the central process instance. It captures the lifecycle of the main document being drafted. Crucially, I assigned the intermediate activities (Email, PDF Review, Budget Update) to this Case ID. In process mining terms, these are **support activities** or **resource dependencies** required to complete the main task (`Document1`). Assigning them here allows an analyst to see the full workflow of "How long does it take to finalize Document1 including research?"
*   **Case `DOC-002` (`Quarterly_Report.docx`):** This represents a secondary, distinct task. It was briefly opened at the start, abandoned, and then resumed and completed at the end. It is treated as a separate case because it represents a distinct business object with its own lifecycle (Open -> Close).

**2. Activity Naming (Standardization):**
Raw system actions (e.g., `TYPING`, `CLICK`, `SCROLL`) were translated into higher-level business activities to make the log analyzable:
*   **`FOCUS`** $\rightarrow$ **`Start Work`**: Indicates the beginning of interaction with a specific artifact.
*   **`TYPING`** $\rightarrow$ **`Edit Content`**: Represents the core value-adding activity.
*   **`SAVE`** $\rightarrow$ **`Save Document`**: A checkpoint in the process.
*   **`CLICK` (Email)** $\rightarrow$ **`Read/Write/Send Correspondence`**: Distinguished based on the specific email action to show communication steps.
*   **`SCROLL` / `HIGHLIGHT`** $\rightarrow$ **`Review Content`**: Represents information consumption rather than creation.
*   **`SWITCH`** $\rightarrow$ **`Context Switch`**: Explicitly tracks application switching, which is valuable for analyzing multitasking overhead.
*   **`CLOSE`** $\rightarrow$ **`End Work`**: Marks the completion of the case instance.

**3. Narrative & Coherence:**
The resulting log tells the story of a user preparing a report (`Document1`). The process flow shows that the user did not work in isolation; they had to **Read Correspondence** (Email), **Review Content** (PDF), and **Edit Content** (Excel) before they could finalize their **Edit Content** (Document1) and **Save Document**. This view is far more valuable for process optimization than a raw list of system clicks, as it highlights dependencies and potential bottlenecks (e.g., time spent switching contexts).
