### 3. Why These Anomalies Matter (Business Impact)

These structural anomalies are significant because they represent "process leakage" or "workarounds" that introduce risk and inefficiency.

#### Impact of the Optional Credit Check:
*   **Financial Risk:** Skipping a credit check allows orders from high-risk or bankrupt customers to be approved and processed. This increases the likelihood of bad debt and financial loss.
*   **Compliance Failure:** In many industries, credit checks are a mandatory internal control requirement (e.g., for SOX compliance). A model that allows skipping this step indicates a failure in regulatory conformance.

#### Impact of the Approval/Invoice Loop:
*   **Operational Inefficiency:** Repeatedly approving and invoicing the same order suggests a "rework loop." This could be caused by data entry errors, where an invoice is issued incorrectly, cancelled, and re-issued.
*   **Customer Experience Issues:** From the customer's perspective, receiving multiple invoices for a single order is confusing and unprofessional, likely leading to increased customer service inquiries.
*   **Potential for Fraud:** A loop that allows multiple approvals and invoice issuances could be exploited to create "ghost" invoices or manipulate financial records to inflate revenue figures artificially.
