### 2. Root Cause Analysis
By correlating the attributes with the duration, we can identify the following drivers of delay:

*   **Complexity Level:** There is a direct, positive correlation between claim complexity and duration. Low-complexity claims follow a "happy path" (no document requests), while Medium and High claims trigger the "Request Additional Documents" activity.
*   **Repetitive Rework (Document Requests):** The primary driver for the longest cases (2003 and 2005) is not just the initial request for documents, but the **repetition** of the request.
    *   **Case 2003:** Two requests for documents.
    *   **Case 2005:** Three requests for documents (an escalation of the bottleneck).
*   **Resource/Efficiency Factors:** 
    *   **Adjuster_Lisa** handles cases 2002 and 2005. While she is efficient with low-complexity claims (via other adjusters), the high volume of document requests associated with her assigned cases suggests either a lack of clear documentation requirements for the claimant at the start, or a recurring issue with documentation quality in Region B.
    *   **Manager_Bill** is involved in both high-duration cases (2003 and 2005). This suggests that high-complexity cases are funnelled to him, potentially creating a bottleneck at the "Approve Claim" stage.

---

### 3. Proposed Explanations and Mitigations

#### **Explanations:**
1.  **Information Asymmetry:** The high number of "Request Additional Documents" events suggests that customers are not being clearly informed of what is required at the time of submission, leading to a back-and-forth communication cycle.
2.  **Complexity Bottleneck:** The process design seems to lack a specialized "fast track" for high-complexity claims, forcing them into a standard workflow that isn't equipped to handle multiple iterations of document gathering.
3.  **Resource Loading:** Manager_Bill may be overloaded, as all high-complexity approvals are routed to him, causing the delay between the final document request and the approval.

#### **Suggested Mitigations:**
*   **Implement "Checklist" Automation:** For "High" complexity claims, trigger an automated, comprehensive list of required documents immediately upon submission. This should reduce the need for multiple follow-up "Request Additional Documents" events.
*   **Front-loading Validation:** Empower "CSR" roles (like Jane or Paul) to perform a basic completeness check on documentation at the "Submit Claim" stage to prevent incomplete files from reaching the Adjusters.
*   **Load Balancing/Resource Training:** 
    *   Evaluate if other managers can be trained to handle "High" complexity approvals to reduce the dependency on Manager_Bill.
    *   Investigate if "Adjuster_Lisa" requires additional training or better documentation guidelines for the cases in Region B to minimize repeat requests.
*   **Dynamic Routing:** Introduce a triage step after "Submit Claim" that automatically flags specific documents required based on "Complexity," ensuring that high-complexity cases get the necessary oversight immediately rather than waiting for an adjuster to request them one by one.
