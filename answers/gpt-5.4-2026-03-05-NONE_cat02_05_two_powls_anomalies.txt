A normative Hire-to-Retire flow is roughly:

**Post job ad  Screen candidates  Conduct interviews  Make hiring decision  Onboard employee  Add to payroll  Close case**

Possible minor variants exist, but the core logic is:

- interviews should normally happen **before** the hiring decision,
- onboarding should happen only **after** a positive hiring decision,
- payroll should normally be done for a hired/onboarded employee,
- closing the case should come at the end.

## Model 1 analysis

### Structure
Key constraints in Model 1:

- `Post -> Screen`
- `Screen -> Decide`
- `Screen -> Interview`
- `Decide -> Onboard -> Payroll -> Close`

Important observation: there is **no order between `Interview` and `Decide`** except that both happen after `Screen`.

So after screening, the model allows:

- `Interview` before `Decide`, or
- `Decide` before `Interview`, or
- potentially interleaving as long as both follow `Screen`.

### Anomalies in Model 1
The main anomaly is:

1. **Hiring decision can be made before interviews are conducted**
   - This is a clear deviation from the usual hiring logic.
   - In a standard process, interviews are typically an input to the decision.
   - The model permits `Screen -> Decide -> ...` while `Interview` may happen later.

Less severe point:

2. **Interview is detached from the decision logic**
   - It exists, but it is not required to precede the decision.
   - That weakens process correctness, but the activity is still present.

### Severity
This is a **moderate anomaly**. The process still preserves the overall backbone:

- post,
- screen,
- some evaluation,
- decision,
- onboarding,
- payroll,
- close.

The flaw is mostly in the ordering between interview and decision.

## Model 2 analysis

### Structure
Key constraints in Model 2:

- `Post -> Screen`
- `Post -> Interview`
- `Interview -> Decide`
- `Decide -> loop_onboarding`
- `loop_onboarding -> xor_payroll`
- `xor_payroll -> Close`

Where:

- `loop_onboarding = LOOP(Onboard, skip)`
- `xor_payroll = XOR(Payroll, skip)`

Interpretation:

- onboarding must occur **at least once**, and may repeat,
- payroll is **optional**,
- screening and interview are both after posting, but **screening is not required before interview or decision** except indirectly decision depends only on interview.

### Anomalies in Model 2
There are several anomalies:

1. **Interview can occur before screening is completed**
   - Since both depend only on `Post`, the model allows `Post -> Interview` before `Screen`.
   - That is usually illogical: screening should precede interviews.

2. **Hiring decision does not depend on screening**
   - `Decide` depends on `Interview`, but not on `Screen`.
   - So the model allows a hiring decision without candidate screening having happened first.
   - This is a strong violation of the intended process logic.

3. **Onboarding is modeled as a loop**
   - Repeated onboarding is usually not a normal Hire-to-Retire behavior.
   - It could represent rework, but as a normative model this is odd.
   - This is a weaker anomaly than the decision-order issue, but still a deviation.

4. **Payroll is optional**
   - `XOR(Payroll, skip)` means the employee may be onboarded and then the case closed without adding them to payroll.
   - For a standard hiring process, that is a serious business inconsistency.

### Severity
Model 2 contains **multiple substantial anomalies**, especially:

- decision can happen without screening,
- interview can precede screening,
- payroll can be skipped.

These affect the core integrity of the process, not just a local ordering issue.

## Which model is closer to the normative Hire-to-Retire process?

**Model 1** is closer to the normative process.

### Why Model 1 is better
- It includes all essential activities.
- It preserves the main downstream sequence:
  - decision  onboarding  payroll  close.
- Its main problem is that **interviews are not forced to occur before the hiring decision**.

### Why Model 2 is worse
- It breaks more fundamental logic:
  - screening is not properly required before decision,
  - interview may happen before screening,
  - payroll may be skipped entirely,
  - onboarding may repeat unnecessarily.
- These are not just minor sequencing issues; they undermine whether the process still reliably represents ōhire-to-retire.ö

## Final judgment

- **Model 1 anomaly:** decision may occur before interview.
- **Model 2 anomalies:** interview may occur before screening, decision may occur without screening, onboarding may repeat, and payroll may be skipped.

**Conclusion:** **Model 1** more closely aligns with a normative Hire-to-Retire process, because it only has one major sequencing defect, whereas **Model 2** violates several core business rules of hiring and employee setup.
