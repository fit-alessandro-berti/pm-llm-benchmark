## Rationale Behind the Estimates

| Segment | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| `SS  OP` | Negotiation/decision time before order placement (~2 days, variable). |
| `OP  RC` | Supplier lead time dominates the process (~7 days, high variance due to logistics). |
| `RC  QI` | Inspection scheduled quickly after receipt (~1 day). |
| `QI  CA` | Components released to manufacturing (~2 days). |
| `CA  PT` | Assembly-to-test handoff including curing/calibration (~3 days). |
| `PT  PK` | Packaging soon after passing tests (~1 day). |
| `PK  WS` | Internal warehouse transfer is fast (~half a day). |
| `WS  DT` | Storage duration depends on demand/shipping schedules (~4 days, high variance). |
| `DT  AS` | After-sales interactions typically begin weeks after delivery (~14 days, very high variance). |

**Key modeling properties:**
- **Averages are roughly additive** along the chain (e.g., `('SS','RC')`  `('SS','OP')` + `('OP','RC')`).
- **Standard deviations grow sub-additively** for multi-step pairs (variances compound, not standard deviations), reflecting realistic uncertainty accumulation.
- With a given `ZETA`, a deviation is flagged for pair `(X, Y)` when the observed time `t` satisfies `t < AVG - ZETA * STDEV` or `t > AVG + ZETA * STDEV` — e.g., with `ZETA = 2`, a `('OP','RC')` observation under ~2.5 days or over ~11.5 days would be flagged as anomalous (unusually fast or delayed supplier delivery).
