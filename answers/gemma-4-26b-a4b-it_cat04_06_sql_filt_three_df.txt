To solve this problem, we can use window functions to inspect the sequence of activities and their timestamps within each case. We will then identify the `case_id`s that meet the specific "bad" criteria and use that list to filter the original table.

Here is the DuckDB SQL query:

```sql
WITH sequence_analysis AS (
    -- Step 1: Use LEAD to look ahead at the next two activities and timestamps 
    -- within the same case to check for direct succession.
    SELECT 
        case_id,
        activity,
        timestamp,
        LEAD(activity, 1) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_activity,
        LEAD(activity, 2) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_next_activity,
        LEAD(timestamp, 1) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_timestamp,
        LEAD(timestamp, 2) OVER (PARTITION BY case_id ORDER BY timestamp) AS next_next_timestamp
    FROM event_log
),
flagged_cases AS (
    -- Step 2: Identify the case_ids that meet BOTH the sequence and timing conditions.
    -- The sequence must be 'Approve Purchase Requisition' -> 'Create Order' -> 'Confirm Order'.
    -- The time difference between 'Create Order' and 'Confirm Order' must be > 5 days.
    SELECT DISTINCT case_id
    FROM sequence_analysis
    WHERE activity = 'Approve Purchase Requisition'
      AND next_activity = 'Create Order'
      AND next_next_activity = 'Confirm Order'
      AND (next_next_timestamp - next_timestamp) > INTERVAL '5 days'
)
-- Step 3: Return all events from the original table, excluding those 
-- belonging to the flagged cases.
SELECT 
    case_id, 
    activity, 
    timestamp
FROM event_log
WHERE case_id NOT IN (SELECT case_id FROM flagged_cases)
ORDER BY case_id, timestamp;
```

### Explanation:

1.  **`sequence_analysis` (CTE):**
    *   We use the `LEAD` window function, partitioned by `case_id` and ordered by `timestamp`. 
    *   `LEAD(activity, 1)` looks at the very next event in the case. By checking if the current activity is 'Approve Purchase Requisition' and the *next* is 'Create Order', we ensure **direct succession** (no other activity exists between them).
    *   `LEAD(activity, 2)` looks two steps ahead to find the 'Confirm Order' activity.
    *   We also pull the timestamps for the 1st and 2nd subsequent events to perform the time duration calculation.

2.  **`flagged_cases` (CTE):**
    *   We filter the results of the first CTE for the exact sequence requested.
    *   We apply the temporal constraint: `(next_next_timestamp - next_timestamp) > INTERVAL '5 days'`. In DuckDB, subtracting two timestamps results in an interval, which we compare against a 5-day interval.
    *   `SELECT DISTINCT case_id` ensures we have a unique list of "bad" cases.

3.  **Final `SELECT`:**
    *   We query the original `event_log` table.
    *   We use `WHERE case_id NOT IN (...)` to perform the exclusion. This ensures that if a case is flagged, **all** its associated events (even those not part of the specific sequence) are removed from the final output.
    *   We add an `ORDER BY` clause to ensure the returned event log is readable and chronologically organized per case.
