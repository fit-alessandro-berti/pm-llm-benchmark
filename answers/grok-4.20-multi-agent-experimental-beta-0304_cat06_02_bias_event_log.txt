**Bias manifests primarily in the ScoreAdjustment attribute and the PreliminaryScoring stage**, with downstream effects on ManualReview and FinalDecision.

### Specific Attributes and Mechanisms
- **CommunityGroup = "Highland Civic Darts Club"**: This triggers an explicit **+10 (Community)** adjustment (seen in C001 and C004). No other cases receive any positive adjustment; others receive 0. This is not applied based on the PreliminaryScore itself but on group affiliation.
- **LocalResident = TRUE**: All cases receiving the +10 adjustment are LocalResident=TRUE. Non-local cases (C003, C005) receive no adjustment regardless of their PreliminaryScore. The system tracks and correlates LocalResident with the community bonus.
- **Effective approval threshold inconsistency**: 
  - C004: Preliminary 690 + 10 = **700 Adjusted  Approved**.
  - C003: Preliminary **715 (no adjustment, Local=FALSE)  Rejected**.
  - C001: 710 + 10 = 720  Approved.
  - C002: 720 (Local=TRUE, no group)  Approved.
  - C005: 740 (Local=FALSE, no adjustment)  Approved.

This shows the adjustment effectively lowers the bar for affiliated local residents by 10ñ15+ points relative to unaffiliated or non-local applicants. The PreliminaryScore (presumably reflecting core creditworthiness factors like payment history, debt-to-income, etc.) is overridden by these group-based factors.

### How Bias Influences Fairness and Equity
The bias enters algorithmically at scoring (Scoring Engine) and is not corrected during ManualReview (different reviewers across cases do not appear to override the adjustment) or FinalDecision (Rules Engine). It treats **group membership and geographic/local status as positive signals** independent of objective risk metrics. 

This creates **disparate impact**:
- Affiliated local residents can be approved with meaningfully lower underlying scores (e.g., the 690 in C004 would almost certainly be rejected without the +10, while the 715 non-affiliated applicant is rejected).
- Unaffiliated or non-local individuals must achieve higher preliminary scores to compensate for the missing adjustment, even when their creditworthiness is comparable or superior on the base metrics.

### Implications for Individuals Lacking Community Affiliations or Geographic Characteristics
People without membership in favored groups like the "Highland Civic Darts Club" (or similar organizations) or who are not marked as LocalResident face systematically higher hurdles. This disadvantages:
- Newcomers, non-locals, or people from other geographic areas.
- Individuals who do not participate in specific social/community organizations (which may correlate with ethnicity, culture, age, socioeconomic status, or social networks).
- Applicants whose "creditworthiness" (PreliminaryScore) is similar to approved club members but who lack the affiliation proxy.

Such a system can act as a proxy for protected characteristics or unrelated social capital, reducing **fairness** (decisions should be based on relevant risk factors, not group membership) and **equity** (equal opportunity for similarly situated individuals). It risks perpetuating exclusion, limiting access to credit/loans for underrepresented or mobile populations, and potentially violating anti-discrimination principles in automated decision-making. The community adjustment appears arbitrary and unrelated to financial risk, undermining trust in the process. 

In short, the process encodes favoritism toward a specific local social group at the expense of merit-based, consistent evaluation.
