## BPMN-Like Process Model: Invoice Approval Workflow

**Process Name:** Invoice Lifecycle and Approval
**Overall Goal:** Ensure invoices are validated against purchase orders (POs) and approved before payment is scheduled.

### 1. Participants (Pools/Lanes)

| Role/Department | Key Responsibilities Described |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)** | Initial invoice handling, PO matching, communicating discrepancies, initiating escalations. |
| **Purchasing Officer (Dan)** | PO verification, matching invoice data, handling supplier discrepancies, confirming PO validity. |
| **Department Manager (Carlos)** | Final authorization/rejection of invoices, oversight of budgetary concerns. |
| **AP Supervisor (Rita)** | Handling exceptions, coordinating inter-departmental resolution, managing complex discrepancies. |
| **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** | Contract review, facilitating communication with suppliers regarding pricing/POs. |

### 2. Process Flow Description (Sequence and Activities)

#### Phase 1: Invoice Receipt and PO Verification (AP Clerk & Purchasing)

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (via email)

**Task 1: PO Check (Mary/AP Clerk)**
*   *Action:* Check if the invoice is associated with a valid Purchase Order (PO) number.
*   **Gateway 1: PO Found?** (Decision)
    *   **Path A (No PO):**
        *   *Task:* Request missing details/PO from Supplier. (Interaction with Supplier/Karen)
        *   *Flow:* Return to Invoice Receipt/Wait for updated invoice.
    *   **Path B (PO Found):**
        *   *Task:* Log invoice into Accounting System.
        *   *Task:* Forward invoice to Purchasing Department.

**Task 2: PO Comparison and Validation (Purchasing Officer - Dan)**
*   *Action:* Compare invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) against the PO.
*   **Gateway 2: Discrepancy Found?** (Decision)
    *   **Path A (Discrepancy Exists):**
        *   *Task:* Handle discrepancy (Choose one: Contact Supplier OR Request AP to hold invoice).
        *   *Action:* (If discrepancy requires external resolution): Loop to Interaction with Supplier/Karen.
    *   **Path B (No Discrepancy):**
        *   *Task:* Send Confirmation Email back to Accounts Payable (AP).
        *   *Flow:* Proceed to Approval phase.

#### Phase 2: Manager Approval and Exception Handling (Manager & Supervisor)

**Task 3: Manager Approval (Carlos - Department Manager)**
*   *Trigger:* Notification received (Invoice matched to PO and confirmation obtained).
*   *Action:* Review invoice details, PO, and confirmation.
*   **Gateway 3: Approval Status?** (Decision)
    *   **Path A (Acceptable):**
        *   *Action:* Approve Invoice in System.
        *   *Flow:* Proceed to Payment Scheduling.
    *   **Path B (Suspicious/Rejection):**
        *   *Action:* Reject Invoice OR Request Hold for Investigation (Involve AP Clerk for review).

**Task 4: Exception Resolution (AP Supervisor - Rita & All Stakeholders)**
*   *Trigger:* Invoice rejection (from Manager) or unresolved discrepancy (from Dan/Mary).
*   *Action:* Coordinate resolution.
    *   *Sub-Task:* If invoice lacks PO or has unresolvable discrepancy:
        *   *Action:* Coordinate with Department Heads to create a retrospective PO or formally reject the invoice.
        *   *Action:* Coordinate with Suppliers (Karen) to correct pricing or provide necessary documentation.
    *   *Flow:* After resolution, route the flow back to Task 2 (Confirmation) or the start, ensuring the invoice reaches a validated state.

#### Phase 3: Payment Scheduling (Accounts Payable)

**Task 5: Payment Scheduling (Accounts Payable)**
*   *Trigger:* Manager Approval (Task 3) + Successful Exception Resolution (Task 4).
*   *Action:* Schedule invoice for payment run.

**End Event:** Invoice Paid.

### 3. Data Flows and Artifacts

*   **Input:** Invoice (Email)
*   **Artifacts:** Purchase Order (PO), System Logs, Confirmation Emails, Retrospective POs.
*   **Data Flow:** Information/Requests flow between AP, Purchasing, the Manager, and external Suppliers (via the SRM).

### 4. Summary of Key Gateways and Loops

1.  **Gateway 1 (PO Found?):** Determines if the process immediately hits an external request (Path A) or proceeds to internal matching (Path B).
2.  **Gateway 2 (Discrepancy Found?):** Determines the action required by Purchasing (contacting supplier vs. holding the invoice).
3.  **Gateway 3 (Approval Status?):** Determines if the invoice moves forward (Approval) or triggers the complex exception workflow (Rejection/Hold).
4.  **Loop/Exception:** The flow loops back from Task 4 (Exception Resolution) back into the validation process (Task 2/3) to ensure that problematic invoices are corrected before reaching the final payment stage.
