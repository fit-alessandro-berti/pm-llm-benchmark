### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Preprocessing and Integration**

To enable process mining, we must first construct a single, unified event log from heterogeneous sources:

- **Event Identification and Mapping:**
  - *GPS data*  events such as “Depart Depot”, “Travel Segment”, “Arrive Depot”, with coordinates, speed, and idle/low-speed flags.
  - *Scanner data*  explicit milestones: “Scan at Depot”, “Arrive Customer”, “Delivery Success”, “Delivery Failed”, “Depart Customer”.
  - *Dispatch data*  planned route, stops, time windows, vehicle capacity, and driver assignments (used for planning baseline).
  - *Maintenance logs*  unscheduled stops labeled “Maintenance/Repair” with start/end times.

- **Common Key and Temporal Alignment:**
  - Use **Case ID** (Vehicle-Day) to group events per operational shift.
  - Synchronize timestamps across systems (GPS may have higher frequency; scanner events are discrete actions).
  - Handle missing data (e.g., GPS pings lost) via interpolation or flagging.

- **Attribute Enrichment:**
  - Add semantic attributes: `Location` (from GPS), `Planned Stop Order` (from dispatch), `Delivery Outcome` (from scanner), `Vehicle Status` (engine warning/offline).
  - Derive new events: e.g., “Low Speed Detected”  possible traffic; “Engine Warning”  unscheduled stop.

**Challenges:**
- **Data Silos & Formats:** GPS coordinates vs. depot geofence zones; scanner text vs. structured status codes.
- **Temporal Misalignment:** GPS logs every few seconds; scanner events occur at point-in-time actions.
- **Missing or Noisy Data:** GPS dropouts, unrecorded short stops, incorrect package IDs.
- **Semantic Gap:** “Low Speed Detected” may mean traffic, parking, or driver behavior—needs contextual interpretation.

**Process Discovery and Visualization**

Using algorithms like **Alpha Miner**, **Inductive Miner**, or **Heuristic Miner** (robust to noise):

- Discover the **actual end-to-end process model** per vehicle-day:
  - Start: “Start Shift”  “Depart Depot”
  - Core loop: For each package: “Arrive Customer”  (Success/Failed)  “Depart Customer”
  - Branches: “Unscheduled Stop” (maintenance, traffic), “Delivery Failed”  potential re-scheduling or reattempt
  - End: “Arrive Depot”  “End Shift”

- Visualize:
  - **Control-flow maps** showing frequency of paths (e.g., 70% of routes follow planned order; 20% include uns stops).
  - **Multiview analysis**: overlay GPS speed heatmaps, time durations, and failure rates on the model.

**Conformance Checking (Alignment with Planned Routes)**

Use **Align-ETCONA** or **Conformance Checking** techniques:

- Compare discovered traces against **dispatch-planned process model** (sequence of stops, time windows).
- Detect deviations:
  - **Sequence deviations**: e.g., Stop 3 visited before Stop 2.
  - **Unplanned activities**: unscheduled stops not in dispatch list.
  - **Divergence in timing**: actual time between “Depart Customer” and next “Arrive Customer” exceeds planned travel + service time.
  - **Skipped stops**: failed deliveries not reattempted within shift.
- Quantify deviation frequency and impact on schedule.

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

Derived directly from event log timestamps and attributes:

| KPI | Definition | Calculation from Event Log |
|-----|------------|---------------------------|
| **On-Time Delivery Rate** | % of deliveries within customer time window | (Successful deliveries in window) / (Total successful deliveries) |
| **Avg. Time per Stop** | Time from “Arrive Customer” to “Depart Customer” | Avg. (Depart - Arrive) per successful delivery |
| **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio** | Travel time / (Travel + Service) | Sum travel segments / total stop time |
| **Fuel Consumption per km/Package** | Estimated via speed/idle patterns | Integrate speed over GPS segments; correlate with packages delivered |
| **Vehicle Utilization Rate** | % of shift time actively delivering | (Total active driving + service time) / Shift duration |
| **Traffic Delay Frequency** | Occurrences of low speed (<10 km/h) outside depots | Count “Low Speed Detected” events per km traveled |
| **Failed Delivery Rate** | % of deliveries with “Failed” outcome | Failed attempts / total attempts |
| **Unscheduled Stop Duration** | Time spent in maintenance/traffic | Sum duration of unscheduled stop events |

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**

- **Performance Horizon Analysis**: Split process into phases (depotfirst stop, inter-stop travel, customer service, return). Measure time per phase.
- **Heatmap Analysis**: Overlay GPS coordinates with delay frequency to identify chronic traffic hotspots.
- **Activity Frequency Analysis**: Identify rarely used but time-consuming activities (e.g., “failed delivery” leading to reroutes).
- **Resource Performance Analysis**: Group by driver or vehicle to find outliers (e.g., Driver D105 consistently has 20% longer service times).
- **Queueing Analysis**: Use idle times at customer locations (zero speed + “Arrive” to “Depart” gap) to infer parking difficulty or customer availability.

### 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

Beyond “where” delays happen, we investigate “why”:

**Potential Root Causes & Validation via Process Mining**

1. **Suboptimal Route Planning**
   - *Evidence*: Frequent sequence deviations or longer-than-expected travel segments between stops.
   - *Analysis*: Compare planned vs. actual stop order; measure extra distance/time per deviation.

2. **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations**
   - *Evidence*: Consistent underestimation in planned vs. actual travel time between specific stops.
   - *Analysis*: Regression on historical travel times per route segment; identify systematic bias.

3. **Traffic Congestion Patterns**
   - *Evidence*: Recurrent low-speed events at same times/locations (e.g., 8–9 AM near business districts).
   - *Analysis*: Correlate GPS speed with time-of-day and day-of-week; build congestion profiles.

4. **High Service Time Variability**
   - *Evidence*: Large standard deviation in “Service Time” per stop; some stops take 2x longer.
   - *Analysis*: Cluster stops by duration; investigate outliers (e.g., high-rise buildings requiring stairs).

5. **Vehicle Breakdowns / Maintenance Needs**
   - *Evidence*: Unscheduled stops with “Engine Warning” or recurring maintenance logs.
   - *Analysis*: Link unscheduled stops to prior vehicle health events; calculate downtime cost.

6. **Driver Behavior Differences**
   - *Evidence*: Significant KPI variation across drivers on similar routes.
   - *Analysis*: Variant analysis comparing driver-specific process models; identify inefficient habits (e.g., excessive idling).

7. **Failed Delivery Attempts**
   - *Evidence*: “Delivery Failed” events leading to later reattempts or rescheduling.
   - *Analysis*: Analyze failure reasons (e.g., “Customer Not Home”); calculate re-delivery rate and added travel.

### 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Real-Time Traffic Integration**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Static dispatch routes ignore real-time traffic, causing delays.
- **Root Cause**: Outdated travel time estimates and lack of adaptive rerouting.
- **Solution**: Integrate GPS speed and traffic APIs into dispatch system to reroute around congestion (e.g., avoid zone X between 8–10 AM).
- **Evidence**: Process mining identifies recurrent low-speed segments; rerouting reduces travel time variance.
- **KPI Impact**:  On-Time Delivery Rate;  Travel Time Ratio;  Fuel Consumption.

**Strategy 2: Customer Time-Window Optimization & Proactive Communication**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: High failed delivery rate due to mismatched time windows.
- **Root Cause**: Inflexible scheduling; customers unavailable during narrow windows.
- **Solution**: Use historical “Failed” patterns to reschedule deliveries to broader windows; send SMS/APP alerts 1 hour prior.
- **Evidence**: Variant analysis shows certain time windows have 30% higher failure rates.
- **KPI Impact**:  Failed Delivery Rate;  Vehicle Utilization;  Re-delivery Travel.

**Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance & Fleet Allocation**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Unscheduled stops due to breakdowns.
- **Root Cause**: Reactive maintenance schedules.
- **Solution**: Analyze vehicle usage patterns (miles driven, idle time, warning events) to predict maintenance needs; assign healthier vehicles to long routes.
- **Evidence**: Process models show unscheduled stops cluster around vehicles with prior warnings.
- **KPI Impact**:  Unscheduled Stop Duration;  Vehicle Utilization;  Emergency Repairs.

### 5. Operational Constraints and Continuous Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints**

- **Driver Hours**: Optimization must respect legal driving limits; routes planned within max shift duration.
- **Vehicle Capacity**: Package assignments per route must not exceed load limits; confirmed via dispatch data.
- **Customer Windows**: Re-routing must still honor feasible time windows; use constraint-aware optimization.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Parking restrictions, low-emission zones mapped into routing logic.

**Monitoring Plan with Process Mining Dashboards**

Deploy a **real-time process mining dashboard** tracking:

- **Live Conformance**: Overlay current day’s trace on planned model; flag deviations instantly.
- **KPI Trends**: Daily On-Time Rate, Avg. Stop Time, Traffic Delay Frequency.
- **Bottleneck Alerts**: Trigger alerts if service time at a stop exceeds 90th percentile.
- **Variant Monitoring**: Track emergence of new frequent paths (e.g., new traffic hotspots).
- **Maintenance Predictors**: Monitor vehicle warning event frequency to preempt failures.

**Sustainability Loop:**
- Weekly review of process deviations  refine dispatch rules.
- Monthly variant analysis  update routing policies.
- Quarterly root cause review  prioritize fleet upgrades or driver training.
