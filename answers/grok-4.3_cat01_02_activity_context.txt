### Identification of Recurring Activities and Contextual Differences

The following activities appear multiple times within individual cases:

- **Verify Documents** (appears in C001 and C003):
  - First occurrence: Initial check of identity documents immediately after request submission.
  - Second occurrence: Re-check performed after the customer has provided additional information or after an internal adjustment.

- **Call Customer** (appears twice in C002):
  - First occurrence: Outbound call triggered by an account discrepancy found during initial processing.
  - Second occurrence: Follow-up call to confirm that the previously discussed adjustments were correctly applied.

### Naming Justification

- **Initial Document Verification**: Used for the first verification activity in C001 and C003. This name reflects that it is the primary identity/document check performed early in the process before any additional actions or submissions occur.
- **Re-verify Documents**: Applied to the second verification activity. The prefix “Re-” clearly signals that this is a subsequent check triggered by new information (additional upload in C001 or account adjustment in C003).
- **Call to Clarify Discrepancy**: Used for the first call in C002. It captures the specific purpose of resolving an account discrepancy identified after the initial verification.
- **Follow-up Confirmation Call**: Used for the second call in C002. This name distinguishes it as a verification step that occurs after an internal adjustment has been made.

These renames preserve the original intent while eliminating ambiguity caused by repeated activity labels within the same case.
