This textual model is structured to facilitate easy conversion into a visual BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) diagram. It is organized by **Pools/Lanes** to represent roles and follows the logical sequence of the process, including the "Happy Path" and various "Exception Paths."

---

### **BPMN Process Model: Accounts Payable & Invoice Approval**

#### **1. Participants (Lanes/Pools)**
*   **External Entity:** Supplier
*   **Accounts Payable (AP) Department:**
    *   AP Clerk (Mary)
    *   AP Supervisor (Rita)
*   **Purchasing Department:**
    *   Purchasing Officer (Dan)
*   **Management:**
    *   Department Manager (Carlos)
*   **Support/Consultative:**
    *   Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)
*   **System:**
    *   Accounting/ERP System (Automated triggers/logging)

---

#### **2. Process Flow**

**Phase 1: Invoice Receipt & Initial Validation**
1.  **Start Event:** Invoice received via email.
2.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Check invoice for valid Purchase Order (PO) number.
3.  **Gateway (PO Exists?):**
    *   **[No]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (AP Clerk):** Request missing details from Supplier $\rightarrow$ *Return to Start Event.*
        *   *Note: If the invoice is for an informal service, see "Exception: No PO/Informal Service" below.*
    *   **[Yes]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (AP Clerk):** Log invoice into Accounting System.
4.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Forward invoice/details to Purchasing Department for receipt confirmation.

**Phase 2: Purchasing Verification**
5.  **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Compare invoice details (quantities, prices, delivery charges) against the PO.
6.  **Gateway (Details Match?):**
    *   **[No]** $\rightarrow$ **Sub-Process (Discrepancy Resolution):**
        *   *Path A:* **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Contact Supplier directly for correction.
        *   *Path B:* **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Instruct AP Clerk to hold the invoice until a corrected version is received.
        *   *Path C (Escalation):* **Task (SRM/Supervisor):** If terms/contracts are disputed, **SRM (Karen)** reviews contract and informs AP/Purchasing.
    *   **[Yes]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (Purchasing Officer):** Send confirmation email to AP Clerk.

**Phase 3: Matching & Internal Discrepancy Handling**
7.  **Task (AP Clerk):** Match invoice line items against the PO in the Accounting System.
8.  **Gateway (Line Items Match?):**
    *   **[No]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (AP Clerk):** Email Purchasing Team to clarify discrepancy.
    *   **Gateway (Resolvable?):**
        *   **[Yes]** $\rightarrow$ *Return to Task: Match invoice line items.*
        *   **[No]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (AP Clerk):** Escalate to AP Supervisor (Rita).
            *   **Task (AP Supervisor):** Coordinate between departments/suppliers to resolve.
            *   *Once resolved $\rightarrow$ Return to Phase 3 (Matching).*
    *   **[Yes]** $\rightarrow$ **System Trigger:** Automatically send approval request email to Department Manager.

**Phase 4: Managerial Approval**
9.  **Task (Department Manager):** Review invoice details, PO, and Purchasing confirmation via System.
10. **Gateway (Approval Status?):**
    *   **[Rejected/Suspicious]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (Department Manager):** Reject invoice **OR** instruct AP Clerk to hold/investigate.
        *   *If investigation is required $\rightarrow$ AP Clerk investigates $\rightarrow$ Return to Task: Managerial Approval.*
    *   **[Approved]** $\rightarrow$ **Task (Department Manager):** Approve in Accounting System.

**Phase 5: Payment Execution**
11. **Task (AP Clerk):** Schedule invoice for the next payment run/cycle.
12. **End Event:** Invoice processed for payment.

---

#### **3. Exception & Variation Log (Special Logic)**

*   **Exception: No PO / Informal Services:**
    *   If an invoice arrives without a PO but is for a valid informal service:
    *   **Task (AP Supervisor):** Coordinate with Department Heads to create a retrospective PO.
    *   **Gateway:** Once PO is created $\rightarrow$ Proceed to Phase 1 (Logging).
    *   **Alternative:** If no retrospective PO can be created $\rightarrow$ Reject invoice.

*   **Uncertainty/Ambiguity Notes:**
    *   *Hand-off direction:* While Mary (AP) usually initiates the match, Dan (Purchasing) occasionally provides feedback that forces Mary to "hold" the invoice. The model treats these as loops back to the matching/verification stage.
    *   *SRM Involvement:* Karen (SRM) does not have a dedicated system task but acts as a "Resource" triggered by discrepancies in pricing or terms.

---

#### **4. Summary of Artifacts/Data Objects**
*   **Input:** Invoice (Email).
*   **Reference Data:** Purchase Order (PO), Contract/Agreed Terms.
*   **Outputs:** Confirmation Email, Approval Notification, Scheduled Payment.
