### Analysis of Bias in Event Logs

**Bias Identification:**
The **Group B (Unprotected Group)** log exhibits bias. While Group A shows consistent scoring and decision-making without adjustments, Group B introduces a systematic **ScoreAdjustment** based on the `CommunityGroup` attribute, which creates a disparate advantage for specific applicants.

**Manifestation of Bias:**
The bias manifests through the **`ScoreAdjustment`** column, which applies a **+10 boost** to applicants affiliated with the **"Highland Civic Darts Club."** This adjustment alters the final score used for decision-making, allowing applicants with lower preliminary scores to achieve approval outcomes that would be denied to applicants in Group A (or Group B applicants without the community affiliation).

**Key Evidence of Systematic Discrepancy:**
The most compelling evidence of bias is found by comparing **Case U003** (Group B) with **Case P002** (Group A):

| Case | Group | Preliminary Score | Community Group | Score Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **P002** | A | 710 | None | 0 | **710** | **Rejected** |
| **U003** | B | 695 | Highland Civic Darts Club | +10 | **705** | **Approved** |

*   **Disparate Thresholds:** Case P002 is rejected with a final score of **710**, while Case U003 is approved with a lower final score of **705**. This demonstrates that the effective approval threshold for Group B members with the community affiliation is significantly lower than for Group A.
*   **Mechanism of Advantage:** The +10 boost allows U003 to cross the approval threshold despite having a preliminary score **15 points lower** than P002. Without this adjustment, U003's final score would be 695, which would likely result in rejection consistent with the baseline threshold observed in Group A.

**Influence of Attributes:**
1.  **`CommunityGroup`:** This is the direct driver of the bias. The presence of "Highland Civic Darts Club" triggers the `ScoreAdjustment`. Applicants in Group B with `CommunityGroup` = `None` (e.g., **U002**) receive no adjustment and are treated identically to Group A (U002 has a score of 710 and is Rejected, matching P002). This confirms the bias is specific to the community affiliation, not just group membership.
2.  **`LocalResident`:** In these logs, `LocalResident` is perfectly correlated with Group membership (`FALSE` for A, `TRUE` for B). While `LocalResident` itself may not be the direct input for the adjustment, it serves as a proxy for the unobserved variable that determines community eligibility. The bias effectively advantages `LocalResident`=TRUE applicants who also belong to the specific community group.

**Conclusion:**
The system exhibits **preferential bias** in favor of Group B applicants affiliated with the "Highland Civic Darts Club." This results in **systematic differences in final decisions**, where applicants with the community attribute can secure approval with lower preliminary scores than their Group A counterparts. Group A is systematically disadvantaged as they receive no score adjustments and face a stricter effective threshold, leading to potential disparate impact where qualified Group A applicants may be rejected while less qualified Group B applicants are approved.
