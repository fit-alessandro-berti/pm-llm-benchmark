## Case 1 — Conformant 
**Trace:** Receive  Check  Authorize  Package  Ship  Close

This matches the normative "sufficient inventory" path exactly. No issues.

## Case 2 — Violation 
**Trace:** Receive  Check  **Package  Authorize**  Ship  Close

**Violated rule:** *"Package items should only occur after authorization and before shipping"* and *"Authorize payment should occur ... before packaging."*

Here, items are packaged **before** the payment is authorized. The description acknowledges some ambiguity ("one might question whether it's ever acceptable to authorize payment before certain conditions are fully confirmed"), but that ambiguity concerns authorizing *earlier*, not packaging before authorization. The text explicitly lists "packaging items before authorization" as a violation, so this case is non-conformant regardless of interpretation. Note that the rest of the trace (Ship after Package, Close as final activity) is fine — the deviation is the swapped Authorize/Package order.

## Case 3 — Violation  (most severe)
**Trace:** Receive  Check  Authorize  **Cancel  Ship**  Close

Two distinct problems:

1. **Shipping after cancellation** — the most explicit violation in the description: *"Ship order should only occur after packaging and never after a cancellation"* and *"Once an order is canceled, it would be illogical to continue with activities intended for fulfillment."* This trace ships a canceled order.
2. **Shipping without packaging** — even ignoring the cancellation, no "Package items" activity occurs, violating *"Only after items have been properly packaged is it appropriate to proceed with Ship order."*

A milder, more debatable issue is **authorizing payment before cancellation**: the cancellation branch prescribes Check  Cancel directly. One could charitably interpret this as the inventory initially appearing sufficient (hence authorization), followed by a later cancellation. Under that lenient reading, the authorization alone might be excusable — but the subsequent shipment makes the case unambiguously non-conformant.

## Case 4 — Conformant 
**Trace:** Receive  Check  Cancel  Close

This matches the normative "insufficient inventory" path exactly: *"Receive, Check, Cancel, Close."* No fulfillment activities follow the cancellation, and Close is the final activity.

## Case 5 — Violation 
**Trace:** Receive  Check  Authorize  **Ship**  Close

**Violated rule:** *"Ship order should only occur after packaging"* — the **"Package items" activity is missing entirely**, yet the order is shipped.

This is a *missing-activity* deviation rather than an ordering deviation: every recorded event is in a plausible relative order, but the mandatory precondition for shipping (packaging) was never satisfied. The normative description allows no interpretation under which shipping unpackaged items is acceptable.

---

## Conclusion

- **Cases 2, 3, and 5 deviate** from the normative process.
- Case 2 exhibits an **ordering violation** (packaging before payment authorization).
- Case 3 exhibits the gravest violations: **fulfillment (shipping) after cancellation** and **shipping without packaging**.
- Case 5 exhibits a **skipped mandatory activity** (shipping without ever packaging).
- Cases 1 and 4 follow the two prescribed paths (fulfillment and cancellation, respectively) precisely.
