### 2. Correlation Analysis of Attributes

We analyze how **Complexity**, **Region**, and **Resource** correlate with the performance issues.

#### A. Complexity (Strong Correlation)
*   **Low Complexity:** Cases 2001 and 2004 complete in ~1.5 hours with **zero** requests for additional documents.
*   **Medium Complexity:** Case 2002 takes ~26 hours and has **one** request.
*   **High Complexity:** Cases 2003 and 2005 take ~48 and ~77 hours and have **two and three** requests respectively.
*   **Conclusion:** Complexity is the **primary driver** of performance issues. High complexity claims trigger iterative loops of document requests, which drastically extend lead times. Case 2005 is the worst performer due to having the highest number of requests (3).

#### B. Region (No Correlation)
*   **Region A:** Contains Case 2001 (Fast, Low) and Case 2003 (Slow, High).
*   **Region B:** Contains Case 2004 (Fast, Low), Case 2002 (Moderate, Medium), and Case 2005 (Slow, High).
*   **Conclusion:** Both regions have fast and slow cases. Region is **not** a root cause. Performance is independent of geography in this dataset.

#### C. Resource (Confounding with Complexity)
*   **Adjusters:**
    *   *Adjuster_Mike* handles Case 2003 (High, Slow).
    *   *Adjuster_Lisa* handles Case 2002 (Medium, Moderate) and Case 2005 (High, Slow).
    *   *Observation:* Adjusters for High complexity cases generate multiple document requests. This suggests the delay is process-driven (multiple loops) rather than individual slowness. Adjuster_Lisa handles both Medium and High cases; the duration jumps significantly only when Complexity is High, reinforcing Complexity as the cause.
*   **Managers:**
    *   *Manager_Ann* handles approvals for Low/Medium cases (Fast/Moderate).
    *   *Manager_Bill* handles approvals for High cases (Slow).
    *   *Observation:* Manager_Bill is only assigned to High complexity claims. While Manager_Bill appears in slow cases, this is likely due to **specialization** (Manager_Bill is the designated approver for complex claims) rather than personal inefficiency. The delay originates from the document request loops, not the approval step itself.

### 3. Root Causes and Mitigation Strategies

Based on the analysis, the performance issues are driven by **process complexity** and **iterative loops**, not by regional disparities or specific resource inefficiencies.

#### Root Cause 1: High Complexity Triggers Iterative Document Loops
*   **Explanation:** High complexity claims require multiple rounds of information gathering. Each "Request Additional Documents" activity introduces a gap in the process (likely waiting for the customer to respond). Case 2005 illustrates this perfectly: 3 requests extend the duration to nearly 4 days.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Front-load Documentation:** Implement a pre-submission checklist or automated validation tool to ensure customers provide complete documentation for High complexity claims upfront.
    *   **Single-Request Policy:** Train adjusters to consolidate all document requests into a single communication rather than issuing them incrementally.

#### Root Cause 2: Lack of SLAs for Customer Response
*   **Explanation:** The event log shows gaps between requests and the next activity (e.g., Case 2003 has a gap of ~23 hours between requests). These gaps represent waiting time, likely due to lack of urgency or follow-up.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Automated Reminders:** Send automated notifications to customers with strict deadlines for submitting requested documents.
    *   **Escalation Rules:** If a document is not received within X hours, escalate the case to a senior adjuster or trigger a follow-up workflow.

#### Root Cause 3: Potential Bottleneck on Specialized Resources
*   **Explanation:** Manager_Bill and specific adjusters handle High complexity claims. If these resources are overwhelmed, the process could stall further. While the current data shows the delay is due to requests, the specialization creates a risk of resource contention.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Workload Monitoring:** Track the queue depth for Manager_Bill and High-complexity adjusters.
    *   **Cross-Training:** Cross-train Manager_Ann to handle High complexity approvals to increase capacity and reduce approval wait times.

#### Root Cause 4: Inefficient Handling of Medium Complexity
*   **Explanation:** Case 2002 (Medium) takes ~26 hours, which is significantly longer than Low complexity cases. While faster than High, it indicates a gap in the process for Medium claims.
*   **Mitigation:**
    *   **Triage Optimization:** Review Medium complexity claims to see if they can be standardized or automated more effectively, similar to Low complexity claims.

### Summary Recommendation
The data indicates that **Complexity** is the root cause of performance issues, not Region or individual Resource slowness. The primary inefficiency is the **multiple document request loops** associated with High complexity claims. Focus improvement efforts on **reducing the number of requests** through better intake processes and **reducing wait times** through automated customer follow-ups. Region A and B require no specific intervention, and resources are performing as expected given their complexity assignments.
