### **BPMN-Like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

---

#### **1. Start Event**  
- **Trigger:** Invoice received (via email).  

---

#### **2. Activities/Tasks**  
1. **Check for Valid Purchase Order (PO)**  
   - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - **Description:** Verify if the invoice includes a valid PO number.  

2. **Log Invoice in System**  
   - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - **Description:** Record the invoice in the accounting system if a valid PO is present.  

3. **Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation**  
   - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - **Description:** Send the invoice to the Purchasing Department to confirm goods/services were received.  

4. **Compare Invoice Details with PO**  
   - **Role:** Purchasing Officer (Dan)  
   - **Description:** Validate quantities, pricing, and delivery charges against the PO.  

5. **Contact Supplier for Corrections (if needed)**  
   - **Role:** Purchasing Officer (Dan) or Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)  
   - **Description:** Resolve discrepancies (e.g., incorrect pricing, missing items) by contacting the supplier.  

6. **Match Invoice Line Items with PO**  
   - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - **Description:** Ensure invoice details align with the PO after Purchasing confirmation.  

7. **Request Manager Approval**  
   - **Role:** System (automated)  Department Manager (Carlos)  
   - **Description:** System notifies the manager to review and approve the invoice.  

8. **Approve/Reject Invoice**  
   - **Role:** Department Manager (Carlos)  
   - **Description:** Manager approves or rejects based on alignment with PO and budget.  

9. **Schedule Payment**  
   - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
   - **Description:** Add approved invoice to the next payment cycle.  

10. **Escalate to Supervisor for Exception Handling**  
    - **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)  
    - **Description:** Unresolved discrepancies or missing POs are escalated.  

11. **Coordinate Retrospective PO Creation**  
    - **Role:** Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)  
    - **Description:** For invoices without POs (e.g., informal contracts), create a retrospective PO with department heads.  

12. **Investigate Rejected Invoices**  
    - **Role:** Department Manager (Carlos) or Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
    - **Description:** Investigate invoices rejected due to suspicious items or discrepancies.  

---

#### **3. Sequence Flow**  
1. **Start**  **Check for Valid PO**  
   - **If No PO:**  **Send Back to Supplier** (loop back to start if revised invoice received).  
   - **If Valid PO:**  **Log Invoice in System**  **Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation**.  

2. **Purchasing Confirmation**  
   - **If Discrepancy Found:**  **Contact Supplier for Corrections**  **Hold Invoice** (loop until resolved).  
   - **If No Discrepancy:**  **Send Confirmation to Accounts Payable**  **Match Invoice Line Items with PO**.  

3. **Matching & Approval**  
   - **If Mismatch Found:**  **Escalate to Supervisor** (Rita)  **Resolve Issues**  **Reattempt Matching**.  
   - **If Matched:**  **Request Manager Approval**  **Approve/Reject Invoice**.  
     - **If Approved:**  **Schedule Payment**.  
     - **If Rejected:**  **Investigate Rejected Invoices**  **Loop Back to Matching** (if corrected).  

4. **Exception Handling**  
   - **Missing PO:**  **Escalate to Supervisor**  **Coordinate Retrospective PO**  **Rejoin Main Flow**.  

---

#### **4. Gateways/Decision Points**  
1. **Valid PO Check** (Exclusive Gateway)  
   - **Paths:**  
     - **No PO:** Send back to supplier.  
     - **Valid PO:** Proceed to log and forward.  

2. **Purchasing Confirmation** (Exclusive Gateway)  
   - **Paths:**  
     - **Discrepancy Found:** Contact supplier/hold invoice.  
     - **No Discrepancy:** Proceed to matching.  

3. **Invoice Matching** (Exclusive Gateway)  
   - **Paths:**  
     - **Mismatch Found:** Escalate to supervisor.  
     - **Matched:** Proceed to manager approval.  

4. **Manager Approval** (Exclusive Gateway)  
   - **Paths:**  
     - **Approved:** Schedule payment.  
     - **Rejected:** Investigate and loop back.  

---

#### **5. Roles/Participants**  
- **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary):** Initial PO check, logging, forwarding, matching, payment scheduling.  
- **Purchasing Officer (Dan):** Receipt confirmation, discrepancy resolution.  
- **Department Manager (Carlos):** Final approval/rejection.  
- **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita):** Exception handling, retrospective PO creation.  
- **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen):** Supplier coordination for corrections.  

---

#### **6. Artifacts/Inputs/Outputs**  
- **Invoice:** Input document.  
- **Purchase Order (PO):** Reference for validation.  
- **System Notifications:** Automated emails for approval requests.  
- **Payment Schedule:** Output document.  

---

#### **7. Exceptions/Variations**  
1. **Missing PO:**  
   - Invoice is sent back to the supplier or escalated to the supervisor for retrospective PO creation.  

2. **Discrepancies in Pricing/Quantities:**  
   - Involves Purchasing, Accounts Payable, and/or Supplier Relationship Manager to resolve.  

3. **Rejected by Manager:**  
   - Requires investigation and potential rework (e.g., corrected invoice).  

4. **Unresolved Issues:**  
   - Escalated to the Accounts Payable Supervisor for coordination across departments.  

---

#### **8. Notes on Ambiguities**  
- **Unclear Handoff Timing:** Mary mentions forwarding to Purchasing after logging, while Dan states he receives it directly from AP. These steps are assumed to align sequentially.  
- **Retrospective PO Process:** Rita’s role in creating retrospective POs is a parallel path for invoices without initial POs.  
- **Supplier Involvement:** Karen’s role is triggered only when supplier communication is needed, not in the standard flow.  

---

This model consolidates the interview accounts into a coherent flow, highlighting decision points, roles, and exceptions while noting areas of ambiguity. It can be directly translated into a BPMN diagram with clear swimlanes for roles and gateways for decision logic.
