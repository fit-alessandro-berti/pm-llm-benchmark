- Precedence constraints for decisions: Approve or Reject decisions cannot occur unless a BiasMitigationCheck happened earlier in the trace, preventing decisions without fairness verification.

- Succession from BiasMitigationCheck to FinalDecision: After fairness checks are performed, the process is expected to move toward the final decision, ensuring the check is integrated into the decision pipeline.

- Non-succession and non-chain-succession constraints: These prevent sensitive attribute checks (e.g., CheckApplicantRace) from being directly followed by Reject decisions. This reduces the risk of immediate discriminatory decisions triggered by protected attributes.

Together, these constraints introduce procedural safeguards: mandatory fairness checks, required manual reviews for sensitive cases, and structural barriers preventing decisions immediately after observing sensitive attributes. This reduces the likelihood that the workflow encodes or executes biased decision patterns.
