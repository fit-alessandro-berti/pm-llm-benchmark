### **Merged Event Log and Reasoning**

To merge the logs, we use **order_id** as the primary key and apply a **timestamp tolerance of ▒2 seconds** to identify matching events. When events align, we merge all attributes from both logs, prioritizing **Log Aĺs timestamp** as the primary timeline. Events without matches are included as-is, tagged with their origin. Conflicts are resolved by preserving both timestamps and attributes.

### **Key Decisions & Conflict Resolution**
1. **Timestamp Tolerance**: ▒2 seconds used for matching.  
   - *Payment Processed* (5s gap) was merged with caution due to semantic alignment.  
   - All other matches were within tolerance.  

2. **Attribute Conflicts**:  
   - Conflicting `resource_id` (e.g., `r99` vs. missing in Log A) was preserved from Log B.  
   - `user_id` was prioritized from Log B where available (Log A omitted this field).  

3. **Event Naming**:  
   - Log Aĺs `event_type` (e.g., "Order Received") was prioritized for consistency, but Log Bĺs `event_name` was retained in `notes` for context.  

4. **Non-Overlapping Events**:  
   - `Quality Check` (Log B) and `Item Delivered` (Log A) were included as-is, tagged with their origin.  

5. **Primary Timestamp**: Log Aĺs timestamps were used as the chronological backbone to maintain consistency with the "primary timeline" requirement.
