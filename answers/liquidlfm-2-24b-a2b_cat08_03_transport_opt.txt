# Comprehensive Process Mining Approach for Speedy Parcels

## 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Preprocessing and Integration**

To create a unified event log for process mining, I would follow these steps:

1. **Data Extraction and Cleaning**: Extract raw data from each source system (GPS trackers, scanner logs, dispatch system, maintenance records). Clean the data by:
   - Standardizing timestamps to a common timezone and format.
   - Handling missing values (e.g., imputing missing location data using interpolation or flagging as "unknown").
   - Removing duplicates or erroneous entries (e.g., impossible speeds > 200 km/h).
   - Normalizing vehicle and driver IDs across systems.

2. **Event Log Construction**: Build a unified event log where each row represents a single event with attributes:
   - **Case ID**: Composite key (Vehicle + Date) to group events per vehicle per day.
   - **Timestamp**: Event time.
   - **Event Type**: Categorized (e.g., "Start Shift", "Arrive Customer", "Delivery Success").
   - **Activity/Status**: Specific action or status.
   - **Vehicle ID / Driver ID**: Assigned to each event.
   - **Package ID**: When applicable.
   - **Location**: Latitude/Longitude or descriptive address.
   - **Speed**: km/h (if available).
   - **Notes**: Free-text fields for anomalies or context.

3. **Integration Challenges**:
   - **Temporal Alignment**: GPS and scanner events may not be perfectly synchronized; need to define tolerance windows (e.g., 5 minutes).
   - **Data Granularity**: GPS provides high-frequency location data; scanner events are discrete; need to align them meaningfully.
   - **Schema Heterogeneity**: Different systems use different terminology (e.g., "Depart Customer" vs. "Leave Customer").
   - **Missing Context**: Some events (e.g., "Low Speed Detected") lack direct customer interaction.

**Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

- **Process Discovery**: Apply algorithms like **Alpha Miner**, **Heuristic Miner**, or **Inductive Miner** to the unified log to reconstruct the *actual* delivery process. This will reveal:
  - Typical sequences: Depot  Route  Stops  Return.
  - Variations: Detours, re-routes, multiple stops per customer, failed delivery attempts.
  - Temporal patterns: Average time per stop, idle time at depot, travel duration.

- **Conformance Checking**: Compare discovered models against the *planned* routes from the dispatch system. Look for:
  - **Sequence Deviations**: Unexpected order of stops or detours.
  - **Unplanned Stops**: Engine warnings, customer not home, parking issues.
  - **Timing Deviations**: Delays beyond planned arrival/departure times (e.g., >15% over schedule).
  - **Resource Violations**: Vehicle capacity exceeded, driver overtime beyond allowed hours.

This reveals where operational reality diverges from plan, highlighting inefficiencies.

## 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

1. **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR)**: % of deliveries completed within promised window.
2. **Average Time per Delivery Stop**: Mean time from arrival to departure (includes dwell time).
3. **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio**: Travel time / Service time; indicates inefficiency in movement vs. action.
4. **Fuel Consumption per km/package**: Estimated from GPS distance and fuel logs (if available).
5. **Vehicle Utilization Rate**: % of time vehicle is moving vs. idle.
6. **Traffic Delay Frequency/Duration**: % of trips with delays >10 minutes, average delay duration.
7. **Rate of Failed Deliveries**: % of attempts requiring re-delivery.
8. **Idle Time per Vehicle/Day**: Time spent at depot or waiting.
9. **Route Deviation Frequency**: Number of unplanned route changes per vehicle/day.

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**

- **Process Mining Visualizations**: Use Petri nets or process trees to spot long loops (idle time), bottlenecks (high frequency of certain activities), or frequent rework (failed deliveries).
- **Variant Analysis**: Compare high-performing vs. low-performing routes/drivers to isolate patterns.
- **Time-based Analysis**: Aggregate KPIs by time of day to detect peak congestion periods.
- **Correlation Analysis**: Link delays to traffic data, weather, or specific locations (e.g., downtown zones).
- **Resource-based Analysis**: Group events by vehicle type, driver, or depot to find underperforming segments.

Quantify impact via:
- **Cycle Time Analysis**: Average time per stop or route.
- **Throughput Metrics**: Deliveries per hour per vehicle.
- **Cost per Delivery**: Fuel + maintenance + labor per successful delivery.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

**Potential Root Causes**

1. **Suboptimal Route Planning**:
   - Static routes ignoring real-time traffic or customer-specific constraints.
   - Lack of dynamic re-routing during delays.

2. **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations**:
   - Overly optimistic planning leading to missed windows.

3. **Traffic Congestion Patterns**:
   - High-traffic corridors causing delays during peak hours.

4. **High Variability in Service Time**:
   - Inconsistent customer interactions (e.g., some customers take longer to load/unload).

5. **Vehicle Breakdowns**:
   - Unplanned maintenance causing delays and forcing rerouting.

6. **Driver Behavior/Skill Differences**:
   - Inefficient parking, route deviation, or poor time management.

7. **Failed Delivery Attempts**:
   - Customer not home, leading to re-delivery costs and time.

**Process Mining Validation**

- **Variant Analysis**: Compare routes/drivers with high vs. low OTDR to identify best practices.
- **Correlation with Traffic Data**: Overlay GPS traces with congestion maps to quantify delay impact.
- **Dwell Time Analysis**: Analyze time spent at stops; outliers may indicate parking issues or customer delays.
- **Failure Pattern Mining**: Cluster failed deliveries to detect common causes (e.g., specific neighborhoods, times).

## 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Real-Time Traffic Integration**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Inaccurate travel time estimations and static routing.
- **Root Cause**: Reliance on historical averages ignores real-time congestion.
- **Insights**: Process mining reveals frequent delays in specific corridors; variant analysis shows peak-hour slowdowns.
- **Implementation**: Integrate GPS and traffic APIs to adjust routes dynamically; reroute around congestion.
- **Expected Impact**: Reduced travel time variance, higher OTDR, lower fuel consumption per km.

**Strategy 2: Optimized Delivery Territories and Route Sequencing**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Suboptimal route planning and high idle time.
- **Root Cause**: Poorly balanced workloads and inefficient stop sequences.
- **Insights**: Process mining identifies routes with excessive backtracking; clustering shows high-density zones.
- **Implementation**: Use historical performance data to redesign territories; sequence stops by proximity and time windows.
- **Expected Impact**: Increased deliveries per route, reduced idle time, improved vehicle utilization.

**Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance and Driver Performance Coaching**

- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Vehicle breakdowns and driver variability.
- **Root Cause**: Unplanned maintenance due to usage patterns; inconsistent driving behavior.
- **Insights**: Maintenance logs correlate breakdowns with high-mileage periods; driver logs show time variance.
- **Implementation**: Develop predictive models using vehicle usage data; provide targeted training for underperforming drivers.
- **Expected Impact**: Fewer breakdowns, more consistent service times, lower maintenance costs.

## 5. Operational Constraints and Continuous Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints**

- **Driver Working Hours**: Ensure routing respects legal limits; avoid overloading shifts.
- **Vehicle Capacities**: Enforce load constraints during route planning.
- **Customer Time Windows**: Prioritize deliveries with tight windows; adjust schedules proactively.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan**

- **Dashboard Metrics**: Track OTDR, average stop time, fuel efficiency, delay frequency, and maintenance alerts.
- **Process Views**: Monitor real-time deviations, route adherence, and driver performance trends.
- **Alerting System**: Trigger notifications for KPI breaches (e.g., >15% delay) or emerging bottlenecks.
- **Feedback Loop**: Regularly update models with new data; refine strategies based on observed outcomes.

By embedding process mining into daily operations, Speedy Parcels can sustain improvements, adapt to changing conditions, and drive long-term cost savings and customer satisfaction.
