The visualization shows a **proclet-based process model** for a medical lab scenario. A proclet is a small process model for one type of object or case. Here, the process is split into two interacting proclet classes: **Lab visit** and **Lab test**.

## Main idea

Instead of drawing one large end-to-end workflow, the diagram separates the process into two lifecycles:

**Lab visit**
Represents the patient’s visit and administrative/clinical handling.

**Lab test**
Represents the lifecycle of an individual laboratory test.

These two lifecycles communicate through channels such as the **Order system** and the **HIS** — likely the hospital information system.

---

## (a) Two proclet classes connected through two channels

The large diagram on the left is the main process model.

### Lab visit proclet

This is the left-hand process. It begins with a lab visit and proceeds roughly as follows:

1. **Order entry**
   A doctor enters an order.

2. **Take blood sample**
   A lab assistant takes the patient’s sample.

3. The lab visit sends a request through the **Order system** channel to create or trigger a corresponding lab test.

4. **Receive result**
   The lab visit receives the lab test result through the **HIS** channel.

5. **Judge results**
   The result is evaluated.

6. There are then two possible paths:

   * If **everything is fine**, the process continues toward sending the final report.
   * If the test must be repeated, the lab visit performs **Do tests again**, which sends another request through the HIS channel.

7. **Receive updated result**
   If a repeat test was requested, an updated result is received.

8. **Send report**
   The final report is sent and the lab visit ends.

---

### Lab test proclet

This is the right-hand process. It begins when a lab test is created or requested.

Its basic flow is:

1. **Perform test**
   A chemical analyst performs the test.

2. **Make report**
   The analyst creates the initial report.

3. The report is sent back to the lab visit through the **HIS** channel.

4. If the lab visit requests a repeat, the lab test process continues with:

   * **Perform test again**
   * **Provide updated report**

5. Finally, the lab test is finished.

---

## Channels: Order system and HIS

The dashed boxes are **communication channels** between proclets.

### Order system

This channel connects the lab visit to the lab test at the point where a new test is ordered.

In plain language:

> After the blood sample is taken, the lab visit asks the lab test process to perform a test.

So the **Lab visit** proclet initiates the creation of a **Lab test** proclet instance.

---

### HIS

The HIS channel handles communication after the test exists.

It is used for things like:

* sending the lab result back to the visit,
* requesting that a test be performed again,
* sending an updated report back.

So the HIS channel coordinates the back-and-forth between the visit and the lab test after the initial order.

---

## Multiplicities

The small boxes near the channel connections contain multiplicities such as **1..1**, **1..***, and similar values.

These indicate how many proclet instances may participate in a communication.

For example, the class diagram on the right says:

> A **Lab visit** requires one or more **Lab tests**.

That means one patient visit can require several lab tests, such as an HGB test, glucose test, cholesterol test, and so on. Each individual lab test, however, belongs to a specific lab visit.

---

## (b) Class diagram

The small diagram on the upper right summarizes the relationship between the two proclet classes.

It shows:

**Lab visit**
requires
**Lab test**

With multiplicities:

* One **Lab visit** may require **one or more Lab tests**.
* Each **Lab test** is associated with exactly **one Lab visit**.

So the model is not just about activities; it also models the relationship between process objects.

## (c) Example of a performative

The table at the bottom shows an example communication event, called a **performative**.

The row says:

| Field     | Meaning                                                                   |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Time      | The communication happens at 11:00.                                       |
| Channel   | It uses the Order system.                                                 |
| Sender    | The sender is the Lab visit instance for John.                            |
| Receiver  | The receiver is the Lab test instance, specifically an HGB test for John. |
| Action    | The action is Create.                                                     |
| Content   | “Can you perform a HGB test for John?”                                    |
| Scope     | Private communication.                                                    |
| Direction | OUT, meaning it is outgoing from the sender’s perspective.                |

So this table gives a concrete example of what happens when the lab visit creates or requests a lab test.

## Overall interpretation

The visualization models a healthcare process where a patient’s lab visit and the individual lab tests are treated as separate but connected processes.

The benefit of this representation is that it shows both:

1. the internal workflow of each object type, and
2. the communication between those object types.

In simple terms:

> A patient has a lab visit. During that visit, one or more lab tests are ordered. Each lab test is performed and reported back. If the result is not satisfactory, the visit can request a repeat test, after which an updated result is sent and the final report is completed.
