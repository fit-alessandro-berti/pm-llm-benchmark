# Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Anomaly Analysis

## Standard P2P Process Flow (Expected)

The typical, well-controlled P2P process follows this logical sequence:

**Create Purchase Requisition  Approve Purchase Requisition  Issue Purchase Order  Receive Goods  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice to PO (3-way match)  Release Payment  Close Case**

Key control principles include:
- Approval must occur **before** a Purchase Order is issued
- Payment should occur **after** goods are received and the invoice is matched
- Payment should **never** precede goods receipt or invoice verification

###  Case 1002 — **SEVERE ANOMALIES (5 violations)**

| Timestamp | Activity | Anomaly? | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 08:30 | Create PR | — | Normal |
| 08:40 | Issue PO |  **ANOMALY 1** | PO was issued **immediately** after PR creation without any approval step recorded at this point. The approval is logged **later in time (08:00 the next day)**, which is physically impossible if taken at face value, or indicates a **temporal/logic inversion**. |
| 07:00 (Day 3) | Vendor Invoice Received |  **ANOMALY 2** | Invoice received **before goods were received** (goods not received until Day 4). You should not have an invoice for goods that haven't arrived. This suggests either a billing error or manipulation. |
| 07:15 (Day 3) | Release Payment |  **ANOMALY 3** | **Payment released before goods were received.** This is one of the most serious control failures. It means the company paid for something it hadn't verified receiving, exposing it to fraud and loss. |
| — | — |  **ANOMALY 4** | **Payment released before invoice-PO matching.** There was no three-way match performed before payment, bypassing a critical financial control. |
| 08:00 (Day 4) | Approve Purchase Requisition |  **ANOMALY 5** | **PR approval occurred AFTER PO issuance, goods receipt, invoice receipt, AND payment.** This is a complete reversal of the proper authorization flow. It appears retroactive/retrofitted, which suggests weak or overridden controls. |
| 09:00 (Day 4) | Receive Goods | — | Occurs after payment — confirms ANOMALY 3 |
| 08:00 (Day 5) | Match Invoice to PO | — | Occurs after payment — confirms ANOMALY 4 |
| 16:00 (Day 6) | Close Case | — | |

**Summary of Case 1002:** This case represents a **critical breakdown of the entire P2P control framework**. It exhibits characteristics consistent with fraudulent behavior or severely deficient internal controls — payment was made to a vendor without proper authorization, without goods receipt, and without invoice verification.

###  Case 1003 — **MODERATE ANOMALIES (2 violations)**

| Timestamp | Activity | Anomaly? | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 09:00 (Day 2) | Create PR | — | Normal |
| 09:10 (Day 2) | Approve PR |  **ANOMALY 1** | PR was approved only **10 minutes** after creation. While not impossible, this raises a red flag about whether any meaningful review or scrutiny occurred, particularly if the approver was required to verify budget codes, vendor qualifications, or business justification. This could indicate a rubber-stamp approval process. |
| 10:00 (Day 2) | Issue PO | — | Normal |
| 11:00 (Day 8) | Release Payment |  **ANOMALY 2 (CRITICAL)** | **Payment released before goods receipt, invoice receipt, AND invoice-PO matching.** This is the same critical failure as Case 1002 — the company released funds to the vendor without any verification that goods were received or that the invoice was valid. This represents a significant financial risk. |
| 12:00 (Day 9) | Vendor Invoice Received | — | Invoice arrived after payment |
| 13:00 (Day 10) | Receive Goods | — | Goods arrived after payment |
| 14:00 (Day 11) | Match Invoice to PO | — | Matching done after payment |
| 10:00 (Day 12) | Close Case | — | |

**Summary of Case 1003:** The most concerning anomaly is the **pre-payment anomaly** — all downstream verification activities happened *after* payment was released. Combined with the suspiciously fast approval, this case warrants investigation.

## Recommendations

1. **Immediate investigation** of Cases 1002 and 1003 — these exhibit patterns consistent with **fraud or severe control deficiencies**, particularly around pre-payment without verification.
2. **Implement system-enforced controls**: Configure the ERP system to block (a) PO issuance without prior approval, (b) payment release without completed three-way match, and (c) payment without goods receipt confirmation.
3. **Review the ~10-minute approval pattern** in Cases 1003 and 1004 for potential rubber-stamp behavior.
4. **Investigate the goods-before-PO** scenario in Case 1004 to determine if this was a legitimate emergency procurement or a process bypass.
5. **Segregation of duties review**: Ensure that the person who can create/approve PRs is not the same person who can release payments.
