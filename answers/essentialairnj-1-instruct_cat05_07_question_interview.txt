Certainly! Here's a list of targeted, open-ended questions for the interviewee to help clarify their property onboarding process:

1. **Decision Criteria and Roles:**
   - "When you mentioned that the property manager assignment depends on several factors, could you give some examples of these factors?"
   - "How is the expertise of property managers utilized, for instance, in managing furnished versus luxury units versus student housing?"
   - "Could you provide more detail about the collaboration process between property coordinators and property managers?"

2. **Documentation Process:**
   - "How are frequent cases of missing or outdated documents typically handled? Are there standard procedures for requesting updates?"
   - "Could you describe how disputes over missing or outdated documents are resolved?"

3. **Property Inspection and Updates:**
   - "How do you prioritize the scheduling of property inspections, and who takes part in the decision-making process about necessary updates?"
   - "What is the typical timeframe between receiving updates and the property manager returning to inspect the property?"

4. **Marketing Process:**
   - "Could you elaborate on how pricing models are updated based on current market trends?"
   - "What factors, besides property owner requests, guide your marketing campaigns?"

5. **Tenant Screening:**
   - "To simplify multi-tenant processes, how do you balance negotiation leverage, lease prioritization, and profit margins?"
   - "How do you handle potential discrepancies in rental history references during tenant screening?"

6. **Quality Checks and Internal Audits:**
   - "Could you walk me through a typical internal audit? Who leads it, and how is compliance determined?"
   - "How often are compliance officers, financial analysts, and IT departments involved in the onboarding process, and what are their roles?"

7. **Communication and Timelines:**
   - "How is communication typically managed across departments and roles to avoid delays?"
   - "Could we agree to a realistic timeline for an average onboarding process without exceptional issues?"

8. **Exceptions and Ad-hoc Requests:**
   - "Can you give some examples of scenarios where special insurance, custom lease clauses, or other unique requests arose, and how you managed these situations?"

9. **Tools and Technologies Used:**
   - "How do you leverage your CRM, project management tools, and other software to improve the efficiency of your onboarding process?"

10. **Continuous Improvement:**
    - "Are there current processes or areas for improvement that the company is aware of and is actively working on enhancing?"

These questions are crafted to delve into aspects of the process that could potentially be unclear, underground, or complex without additional context, helping you and the interviewee build a more comprehensive understanding of the onboarding process.
