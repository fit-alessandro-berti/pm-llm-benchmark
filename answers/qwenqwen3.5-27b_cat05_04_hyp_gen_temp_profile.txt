# Temporal Profile Analysis and Anomaly Detection

## 1. Identified Anomalies in the Temporal Profile

Based on the provided temporal profile model, the following statistical irregularities indicate potential process deviations:

*   **Receive (R) to Approve (P):**
    *   **Observation:** Average duration is ~25 hours with a Standard Deviation (STDEV) of only 1 hour.
    *   **Anomaly:** The extremely low STDEV relative to the mean suggests a rigid, non-organic timing pattern. In a human-driven process, approval times typically vary significantly based on claim complexity. This consistency implies an automated batch process or artificial data entry.

*   **Approve (P) to Notify (N):**
    *   **Observation:** Average duration is 7 days with a high STDEV of 2 days.
    *   **Anomaly:** A one-week delay between approval and customer notification is operationally inefficient. The high variance indicates inconsistency, suggesting that some notifications are sent promptly while others sit in a backlog, potentially due to resource shortages or system queue failures.

*   **Assign (A) to Close (C):**
    *   **Observation:** Average duration is 2 hours with a STDEV of 1 hour.
    *   **Anomaly:** This timeframe is suspiciously short for a complete lifecycle that should include Evaluation (E) and Approval (P). It suggests that claims may be closing prematurely without undergoing necessary review steps.

*   **Evaluate (E) to Notify (N):**
    *   **Observation:** Average duration is 5 minutes with a STDEV of 1 minute.
    *   **Anomaly:** Notification occurring almost immediately after evaluation implies the Approval (P) step might be bypassed, or the notification is an automated trigger that does not wait for formal approval.

## 2. Hypotheses for Anomalies

*   **Automated or Batched Approvals (R  P):**
    *   The rigid timing suggests an automated script runs approvals at fixed intervals (e.g., every 24 hours) rather than on a case-by-case basis. Alternatively, it could indicate data fabrication where timestamps are generated algorithmically rather than recorded from actual events.

*   **Notification Backlog or Manual Bottleneck (P  N):**
    *   The 7-day average delay suggests a decoupling of the approval system from the communication system. Notifications might require manual intervention by a specific team that is understaffed, leading to a queue where claims pile up for days before being contacted.

*   **Process Skipping or Fraudulent Closure (A  C):**
    *   Claims closing within 2 hours of assignment likely bypass the Evaluation and Approval stages. This could happen for low-value claims under a "fast-track" policy, or conversely, it could indicate fraudulent activity where claims are closed without proper assessment to hide losses or meet performance metrics.

*   **System Trigger Errors (E  N):**
    *   The near-instant notification after evaluation suggests a system configuration error where the "Notify" trigger is bound to the "Evaluate" event rather than the "Approve" event, causing customers to be informed before a final decision is made.

## 3. Verification Approaches using SQL

To validate these hypotheses, the following PostgreSQL queries can be executed against the `claim_events` and `adjusters` tables.

### Query 1: Identify Claims with Excessive Approval-to-Notification Delays
This query isolates claims where the gap between Approval ('P') and Notification ('N') exceeds 5 days (432,000 seconds), investigating the PN anomaly.

```sql
SELECT 
    ce_approve.claim_id,
    ce_approve.timestamp AS approval_time,
    ce_notify.timestamp AS notification_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_notify.timestamp - ce_approve.timestamp)) AS delay_seconds,
    c.claim_type
FROM claim_events ce_approve
JOIN claim_events ce_notify ON ce_approve.claim_id = ce_notify.claim_id
JOIN claims c ON ce_approve.claim_id = c.claim_id
WHERE ce_approve.activity = 'P' 
  AND ce_notify.activity = 'N'
  AND ce_notify.timestamp > ce_approve.timestamp
  AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_notify.timestamp - ce_approve.timestamp)) > 432000
ORDER BY delay_seconds DESC;
```

### Query 2: Detect Premature Closures (Skipping Evaluation/Approval)
This query finds claims where 'Close' ('C') occurs within 3 hours of 'Assign' ('A') without an intervening 'Evaluate' ('E') or 'Approve' ('P') event.

```sql
SELECT 
    ce_assign.claim_id,
    ce_assign.timestamp AS assign_time,
    ce_close.timestamp AS close_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_close.timestamp - ce_assign.timestamp)) AS duration_seconds,
    ce_assign.resource AS assigned_adjuster
FROM claim_events ce_assign
JOIN claim_events ce_close ON ce_assign.claim_id = ce_close.claim_id
WHERE ce_assign.activity = 'A' 
  AND ce_close.activity = 'C'
  AND EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_close.timestamp - ce_assign.timestamp)) < 10800
  AND NOT EXISTS (
      SELECT 1 
      FROM claim_events ce_mid
      WHERE ce_mid.claim_id = ce_assign.claim_id
        AND ce_mid.activity IN ('E', 'P')
        AND ce_mid.timestamp BETWEEN ce_assign.timestamp AND ce_close.timestamp
  );
```

### Query 3: Correlate Anomalies with Specific Adjusters
This query checks if specific adjusters are responsible for the rigid RP timing or the premature closures by grouping events by the resource performing the activity.

```sql
SELECT 
    ce.resource AS adjuster_name,
    COUNT(*) AS total_events,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (
        LEAD(ce.timestamp) OVER (PARTITION BY ce.claim_id ORDER BY ce.timestamp) 
        - ce.timestamp
    ))) AS avg_time_to_next_step
FROM claim_events ce
WHERE ce.activity IN ('A', 'P')
GROUP BY ce.resource
HAVING COUNT(*) > 50
ORDER BY avg_time_to_next_step ASC;
```

### Query 4: Verify RP Timing Consistency
This query calculates the exact duration between Receive ('R') and Approve ('P') for a sample of claims to verify if the durations cluster tightly around the 25-hour mark, confirming the low STDEV anomaly.

```sql
SELECT 
    ce_receive.claim_id,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (ce_approve.timestamp - ce_receive.timestamp)) AS duration_seconds
FROM claim_events ce_receive
JOIN claim_events ce_approve ON ce_receive.claim_id = ce_approve.claim_id
WHERE ce_receive.activity = 'R'
```
