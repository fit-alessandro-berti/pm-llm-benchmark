**Explanation of Design Choices:**

1. **Cumulative delays**: Activities further apart in the process chain naturally accumulate more average time and variance (e.g., `('SS', 'AS')` has a much larger average than `('SS', 'OP')`).

2. **High variability segments**: Steps involving external parties (suppliers, distribution, after-sales) carry higher standard deviations reflecting real-world unpredictability.

3. **Low variability segments**: Internal controlled operations like `('PK', 'WS')` or `('RC', 'QI')` exhibit smaller standard deviations due to standardized warehouse/inspection procedures.

4. **Long-tail for After-Sales**: The `AS` activity typically occurs weeks or months after the core process completes, which is reflected in the large averages for any pair ending in `AS`.

5. **Warehouse storage variability**: `('WS', 'DT')` has a relatively high stdev compared to its average because storage duration depends heavily on demand cycles.
