Below is an explanation of what the described pseudoBPMN is doing as a business process, step by step, including the intent of each task, what the gateways mean, how the control-flow paths converge, and when approvals/rework happen.

---

## 1) Start and initial intake

### **Start Event  Task A: ōReceive Customer Requestö**
**Purpose:** The process begins when a customer submits an order/service request (e.g., through a form, sales channel, or support workflow).

**Output/role in the process:** The request data (especially the ōtypeö) becomes the basis for later decisions and validations.

---

## 2) First decision: standard vs. custom

### **Gateway (XOR): ōCheck Request Typeö**
**Meaning of XOR gateway:** Exactly **one** outgoing path is taken based on a mutually exclusive condition.

**Condition:** *Request Type* is evaluated:
- **If Type = Standard**  follow the ōstandardö branch
- **If Type = Custom**  follow the ōcustomö branch

This gateway cleanly separates two different processing regimes because standard requests can be validated using existing rules, while custom requests require feasibility and quotation preparation.

---

## 3) Standard path

### **If Type = Standard  Task B1: ōPerform Standard Validationö**
**Purpose:** Apply predefined checks for standard offerings. Typical examples:
- completeness of required fields
- whether requested items/services are allowed
- basic compliance rules
- adherence to standard configuration limits

**Why it exists:** Ensures the request is eligible and can proceed without the more expensive custom feasibility work.

---

### **Then Gateway (AND): ōRun Parallel Checksö**
**Meaning of AND gateway:** The process splits into **parallel branches**, and execution continues only after **all** parallel branches complete.

It indicates multiple checks must be done concurrently to save time and ensure all critical constraints are satisfied.

---

#### Parallel tasks under the AND split:
- **Task C1: ōCredit Checkö**
  **Purpose:** Assess customer creditworthiness or payment risk to determine if they can be billed or must pay upfront.
- **Task C2: ōInventory Checkö**
  **Purpose:** Verify availability of products/components and delivery feasibility based on stock and/or lead times.

---

### **Join: ōAll Parallel Checks Completed (Join)ö**
**Meaning of the join:** This is the synchronization point of the AND gateway: it waits for both C1 and C2 to finish before moving on.

---

### **Task D: ōCalculate Delivery Dateö**
**Purpose:** Based on outputs from validations/parallel checks (especially inventory availability), compute the promised delivery date.

**Why itÆs here:** Delivery date depends on operational constraints (inventory and possibly scheduling rules).

---

## 4) Custom path

### **If Type = Custom  Task B2: ōPerform Custom Feasibility Analysisö**
**Purpose:** For custom configurations, determine whether the requested customization can be delivered given constraints such as:
- engineering/design feasibility
- supplier/manufacturing capability
- technical limitations
- lead time impact
- cost implications

**Why itÆs different from standard validation:** Custom requests typically require a more complex ōcan we do this at all?ö analysis, not just rule-based validation.

---

### **Gateway (XOR): ōIs Customization Feasible?ö**
**Meaning of XOR gateway:** Choose one path based on a yes/no decision.

#### If **Yes**:
- **Task E1: ōPrepare Custom Quotationö**
  **Purpose:** Create a quotation reflecting custom scope, price, delivery, and assumptions (often including costs derived from feasibility findings).

#### If **No**:
- **Task E2: ōSend Rejection Noticeö  End Event**
  **Purpose:** Inform the customer that the customization cannot be fulfilled, and terminate the process for that request.

So the custom branch can end early if feasibility fails.

---

## 5) Convergence after standard/custom computations

### **ōAfter Standard or Custom Path Tasks Completedö  Gateway (XOR): ōIs Approval Needed?ö**
This means that once either:
- the standard path completes (after Task D), or
- the custom path completes the successful feasibility/quotation path (after Task E1),

control flows into a common approval decision.

**Important nuance:** This convergence depends on success of the respective branch:
- Standard path always reaches this point (assuming it doesnÆt throw exceptions not shown).
- Custom path reaches this point only if customization was feasible (because if not, Task E2 ends the process).

---

## 6) Approval decision and potential rework loop

### **Gateway (XOR): ōIs Approval Needed?ö**
**Meaning of XOR:** Either approval is required or it isnÆt.

#### If **Yes**:
1. **Task F: ōObtain Manager Approvalö**
   **Purpose:** Route the case to a manager when certain criteria require human authorization (e.g., pricing thresholds, unusual conditions, credit risk, exceptions, high complexity).

2. **Gateway (XOR): ōIs Approval Granted?ö**
   **Meaning:** Another yes/no decision.

   - If **Yes**:
     - **Task G: ōGenerate Final Invoiceö**
     - then continue to confirmation (Task I).

   - If **No**:
     - **Task H: ōRe-evaluate Conditionsö**
     - **Loop back**:
       - to **Task E1 (for Custom Path)** or
       - to **Task D (for Standard Path)**

#### If **No Approval Needed**:
- **Task G: ōGenerate Final Invoiceö** directly

---

### How the loop-back works (rework trigger)
**Rework is triggered when**:
- approval was required, and
- manager approval was explicitly denied.

**What ōRe-evaluate Conditionsö likely implies:**
- adjust pricing/quotation terms,
- revisit feasibility assumptions (custom),
- re-check delivery date calculations (standard),
- propose revised conditions that could be acceptable for approval.

**Why the loop goes back to different tasks:**
- If the request is **custom**, revisions likely change quotation content  so it loops back to **E1: Prepare Custom Quotation**.
- If the request is **standard**, revisions likely affect delivery promises and feasibility based on computed logistics  so it loops back to **D: Calculate Delivery Date**.

After revising, the flow returns to the approval decision region (conceptually) and proceeds again to approval or invoice generation.

---

## 7) Finalization: invoice and customer confirmation

### **Task G: ōGenerate Final Invoiceö**
**Purpose:** Create the final billing document using the accepted conditions (standard or custom, approved or not required).

### **Task I: ōSend Confirmation to Customerö**
**Purpose:** Notify the customer with confirmation details. This may include:
- invoice information,
- delivery date,
- payment terms.

### **End Event**
The process terminates after confirmation is sent.

---

## Summary of key behaviors

### Task differences: Standard vs. Custom
- **Standard**
  - **B1: Standard validation** (rule-based eligibility checks)
  - **AND checks:** credit + inventory in parallel
  - **D: delivery date calculation** based on operational data
- **Custom**
  - **B2: feasibility analysis** (engineering/constraint check whether customization is possible)
  - **XOR feasibility outcome**
    - if feasible  **E1: custom quotation**
    - if not feasible  **E2: rejection notice and end**

### Gateway meanings
- **XOR (ōCheck Request Typeö)**: choose exactly one branch (standard or custom)
- **AND (ōRun Parallel Checksö)**: run multiple tasks simultaneously and wait for all
- **XOR (ōIs Customization Feasible?ö)**: yes/no choice; either proceed to quotation or reject and end
- **XOR (ōIs Approval Needed?ö / ōIs Approval Granted?ö)**: yes/no decisions that control whether manager approval is sought and whether invoice can be issued

### Approval and rework triggers
- **Approval is triggered when** the process decides ōapproval neededö (not specified by rules, but typically thresholds/exceptions).
- **Rework is triggered when** approval is **denied**:
  - re-evaluate conditions,
  - loop back to the relevant point:
    - custom  back to **E1**
    - standard  back to **D**
  - then attempt again to reach invoice generation and confirmation.
