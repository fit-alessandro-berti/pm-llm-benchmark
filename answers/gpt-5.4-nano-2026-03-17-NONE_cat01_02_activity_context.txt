## 1) Recurring activities and how their meaning changes
The activities that recur across cases (and can represent different moments) are:

- **Verify Documents**  
  - In **C001**, it happens first as an initial identity/document check, then later as a **re-check after the customer submits additional documents**.
  - In **C003**, it’s an initial physical verification by the branch officer, then later a **re-verification after internal account adjustment**.

- **Call Customer**  
  - In **C001**, the call is to request **missing documents** (because verification is incomplete).
  - In **C002**, it clarifies an **account discrepancy**.
  - In **C003**, it is to get the customer to **return** after stepping out.

So the same activity name (“Verify Documents”, “Call Customer”) actually covers different intent depending on where it occurs and what preceded/followed it.

## 3) Brief justification for the naming choices
- **Verify Documents**
  - Renamed to distinguish **initial verification** vs **re-verification**:
    - In **C001**, the second “Verify Documents” occurs after **“Submit Additional (ID Scan Upload)”**  “Re-check After Upload”.
    - In **C003**, the second “Verify Documents” occurs after **“Adjust Account (Internal Correction)”**  “Re-verification After Correction”.
  - Also specialized by channel/context:
    - “Initial ID Check” (BackOfficeAgent) vs “Physical ID Check” (BranchOfficer).

- **Call Customer**
  - Renamed based on the call’s explicit purpose in **Additional Context**:
    - **Missing docs request** (C001)  “Call Customer (Request Missing Docs)”
    - **Account discrepancy clarification** (C002 first call)  “Clarify Account Discrepancy”
    - **Follow-up confirmation** (C002 second call)  “Confirm Adjustments”
    - **Request customer return** (C003)  “Request Customer Return”

- **Initiate Request**
  - Disambiguated by entry channel:
    - Portal submission (C001, C002) vs branch walk-in (C003).

- **Approve Request**
  - Standardized as “Final Approval” since the context always says the supervisor’s decision is the final approval.
