# Clarifying Questions for the Property Onboarding Process

## Initial Intake & Documentation
1. When a landlord first contacts the regional office, is there a standardized intake form or checklist the coordinator uses, or does it vary by region and property type?
2. How do you define "outdated" documentation—are there specific time thresholds (e.g., inspection reports older than 12 months) that trigger a request for updates?
3. When documents are missing or outdated, how long do landlords typically have to provide updates, and what happens if they don't respond? Is the request abandoned, paused, or escalated?
4. Who is responsible for verifying the authenticity of property deeds and compliance certificates—is this done by the coordinator alone, or does legal/compliance get involved at this stage?

## Property Manager Assignment
5. You mentioned several factors influence manager assignment (workload, geography, experience, track record). When these factors conflict, how are they prioritized or weighted?
6. Who actually makes the assignment decision—is it automated, made by a regional supervisor, or determined by the coordinator?
7. What happens if no manager is a good fit (e.g., all qualified managers are at capacity)? Is there a waitlist, a reassignment process, or external support?
8. Can a property manager decline an assignment, and if so, what's the process?

## Stakeholder Alignment & the Introductory Meeting
9. Is the introductory call/meeting mandatory, or can it be skipped for straightforward properties?
10. What happens if the landlord and manager fundamentally disagree during this meeting—say, on pet policies or maintenance responsibilities? Is there an escalation path or a point where onboarding is halted?
11. Are the outcomes of this meeting (special conditions, policies) formally documented and shared, or do they live in email threads and individual notes?

## Inspection, Photography & Remediation
12. Are the photographer and inspector scheduled simultaneously or sequentially? Does one need to complete before the other begins?
13. When the inspector identifies issues, who bears the cost of repairs—the landlord, the company, or is it negotiated case by case?
14. Is there a severity threshold for inspection failures? For example, do minor issues block activation, or only major safety violations?
15. How many re-inspection cycles are typical, and is there a point where a property is deemed unfit and onboarding is terminated?

## Marketing & Pricing
16. The pricing model is updated based on market trends—who has final authority over the listed rental price, and can it override the landlord's expected range?
17. What happens if the company's pricing strategy and the landlord's expectations are far apart? Is this resolved before or after marketing materials are created?
18. Is marketing created in parallel with inspection, or strictly after the property passes? Could marketing assets be wasted if remediation fails?

## Activation & Tenant Screening
19. What specific criteria define a "suitable tenant"? Are there hard requirements (credit score thresholds, income-to-rent ratios) versus discretionary judgments?
20. When multiple tenants apply, who decides between them, and what guardrails exist to ensure fair housing compliance during that prioritization?
21. Who has authority to approve promotional discounts or non-standard lease terms—the property manager, or does this require sign-off from finance or a supervisor?
22. How is the screening handoff managed when a leasing agent (rather than the property manager) handles applications? Who owns the final decision?

## Quality Checks & Audits
23. Do the compliance, financial, and IT reviews happen sequentially or in parallel? Must they all complete before move-in, or can they occur afterward?
24. If a compliance officer or financial analyst flags a problem after a lease is signed, what's the remediation process—can a finalized lease be unwound?
25. Are these audits performed on every property, or are they sample-based or triggered only by certain risk factors?

## Exceptions, Timing & Communication
26. For exceptions like special insurance requirements or custom lease clauses, is there a defined approval workflow, or are these handled ad hoc by the manager?
27. Roughly how long does the end-to-end onboarding take, and where do the most common delays or bottlenecks occur?
28. With communication spread across email, CRM, and project management tools, how do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks—is there a single source of truth for a property's status?
29. Are there service-level agreements (SLAs) or deadlines at any stage, and what happens when they're missed?
30. Can stages overlap or run concurrently to save time, or is the process strictly linear from intake to move-in?

## Roles, Ownership & Accountability
31. Throughout this lifecycle, who is ultimately accountable if a property fails to onboard successfully—the coordinator, the property manager, or the regional office?
32. At what points does ownership formally transfer between roles (coordinator  property manager  leasing agent), and how are those handoffs documented?
33. Are there any roles you mentioned that overlap in responsibility, and if so, how are conflicts or duplicated efforts resolved?
