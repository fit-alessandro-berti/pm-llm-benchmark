## **1. Queue Identification and Characterization**

### **1.1 Calculating Waiting Times from Event Logs**
In the clinic’s event log, **waiting time (queue time)** is defined as the *idle time a patient spends between the completion of one activity and the start of the next consecutive activity in their care pathway*. This is **not** the service time (duration of an activity itself) but the **gap** where the patient is waiting for the next step to begin.

- **Calculation Method**:
  For a given patient (Case ID), waiting time between **Activity A** and **Activity B** =  
  `Timestamp (START of B) - Timestamp (COMPLETE of A)`.
  
  *Example (from snippet)*:  
  For **V1001**, waiting time between **Registration** and **Nurse Assessment** =  
  `09:15:20 (Nurse START) - 09:08:45 (Registration COMPLETE) = **6 minutes 35 seconds**`.  
  Similarly, waiting time between **Nurse Assessment** and **Doctor Consultation** =  
  `09:45:55 (Doctor START) - 09:25:10 (Nurse COMPLETE) = **20 minutes 45 seconds**`.

- **Key Distinction**:
  - **Service Time** = `COMPLETE - START` (e.g., Registration took 6m30s for V1001).
  - **Waiting Time** = `START(next) - COMPLETE(previous)` (e.g., 6m35s between Registration and Nurse).
  - **Total Visit Duration** = `COMPLETE(last activity) - START(first activity)`.

---

To profile each queue (e.g., *Registration  Nurse*, *Nurse  Doctor*), we calculate:

| **Metric**               | **Purpose**                                                                 | **Example Calculation**                                                                 |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Average Wait Time**    | Baseline performance for the queue.                                        | Sum of all wait times for the queue / number of cases.                                  |
| **Median Wait Time**     | Robust to outliers (e.g., a single 2-hour wait skewing the average).       | Middle value of sorted wait times.                                                     |

| **% of Total Visit Time**| Quantifies the queue’s contribution to overall delay.                      | (Sum of wait times for queue / Sum of all wait times) × 100.                           |

| **Clinical Outcomes**  | Time-to-treatment for urgent cases  | 15 mins (NurseDoctor)              | Avg wait time for *Urgency = "Urgent"* cases in *Nurse  Doctor* queue.                 | Event log (filtered)          |
| **Operational Cost**   | Cost per patient visit              | 2% increase                         | `(Total operational cost) / (Number of patients)`.                                     | Finance + event log           |

### **5.2 Ongoing Process Monitoring**
**Tools & Methods**:
1. **Real-Time Dashboards**:
   - **Queue Length Monitor**: Live count of patients waiting for each activity (updated every 5 mins).
   - **Wait Time Heatmap**: Color-coded grid showing avg wait times by hour/day (e.g., red = >20 mins).
   - **Bottleneck Alerts**: Automated notifications if a queue exceeds 90th percentile threshold (e.g., "NurseDoctor wait >30 mins for 5+ patients").

2. **Automated Process Mining**:
   - **Weekly Reports**: Generated via `pm4py` or Celonis, including:
     - Top 3 queues with **increasing wait times** (trend analysis).
     - **Resource utilization outliers** (e.g., Clerk A has 30% longer queues than peers).
     - **New process variants** (e.g., unexpected skips in workflow).
   - **Anomaly Detection**: Flag cases where wait time > **3 from mean** (investigate root cause).

3. **Closed-Loop Feedback**:
   - **A/B Testing**: Compare KPIs before/after implementing a strategy (e.g., dynamic scheduling in Cardio vs. control specialty like Dermatology).
   - **Staff Feedback Integration**: Monthly surveys to identify **unmeasured bottlenecks** (e.g., "Patients are now waiting longer for check-out because registration is faster").

4. **Continuous Improvement**:
   - **PDCA Cycle**: Plan (identify queue)  Do (implement fix)  Check (monitor KPIs)  Act (refine or scale).
   - **Root Cause Re-analysis**: Every 3 months, re-run bottleneck analysis to detect **new bottlenecks** (e.g., if *Nurse  Doctor* is fixed, *Doctor  Check-out* may become the new issue).

**Example Monitoring Workflow**:
1. **Morning**: Dashboard shows *Nurse  Doctor* wait time spiking to 25 mins (vs. target 15 mins).
2. **Investigation**: Process mining reveals Dr. Smith is out sick, and Dr. Lee is handling 1.5× normal caseload.
3. **Action**: Temporarily assign a nurse practitioner to assist Dr. Lee.
4. **Validation**: KPIs return to baseline within 2 hours; incident logged for future capacity planning.
