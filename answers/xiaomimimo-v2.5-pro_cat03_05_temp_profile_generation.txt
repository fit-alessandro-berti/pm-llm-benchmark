## Key Design Rationale

| Characteristic | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Adjacent pairs** | Standard deviations are tightest (e.g., 7,200s for PTPK) because fewer factors compound the delay. |
| **Non-adjacent pairs** | Standard deviations grow with distance due to **compounding variability** — e.g., SSAS spans the entire chain and has the highest  (259,200s  3 days). |
| **Supplier-dependent steps** | Pairs involving OPRC have high  (86,400s) because supplier lead times are inherently variable. |
| **Internal manufacturing steps** | Pairs like CAPT or QIPK have low  (14,400–28,800s) since internal processes are more controllable. |
| **Asymmetric variability** | The  for SSAS (259,200s) is ~15× larger than adjacent pairs, reflecting real-world supply chain volatility. |
| **All 45 pairs** | For 10 activities, $\binom{10}{2} = 45$ unique pairs are included, covering every possible eventuality relationship. |
