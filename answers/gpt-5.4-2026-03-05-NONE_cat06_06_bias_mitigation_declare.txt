### Brief rationale for the added constraints

- **`existence["BiasMitigationCheck"]`**  
  Ensures every compliant process includes an explicit fairness-control step.

- **`responded_existence` and `coexistence` between sensitive-attribute checks and `BiasMitigationCheck`**  
  If the process inspects race, gender, or age, a bias-mitigation step must also appear somewhere in the same trace.

- **`response` / `succession` from `CheckApplicantRace`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantAge` to `BiasMitigationCheck`**  
  Forces the process to follow sensitive-attribute access with a fairness-oriented control step, reducing the risk of using such information unchecked.

- **`precedence` for `Approve` and `Reject` requiring `BiasMitigationCheck` first**  
  Prevents key decisions from occurring before the fairness review has happened.

- **`coexistence`, `responded_existence`, `precedence`, and `chainprecedence` involving `Approve_Minority`, `Reject_Minority`, `RequestAdditionalInfo_Minority` and `ManualReview`**  
  Ensures decisions affecting sensitive groups are accompanied by additional human oversight.

- **`response["BiasMitigationCheck"]["ManualReview"]` and `succession["ManualReview"]["FinalDecision"]`**  
  Makes the fairness check operational by linking it to a manual review and then to a final decision.

- **`nonsuccession` and `nonchainsuccession` from sensitive checks to decisions**  
  Blocks immediate or tightly coupled decisions right after observing protected attributes, reducing the chance of direct discriminatory outcomes.

Overall, these additions make the model less permissive toward traces where sensitive attributes are checked and then used directly to drive outcomes, while requiring review and mitigation steps before decisions are finalized.
