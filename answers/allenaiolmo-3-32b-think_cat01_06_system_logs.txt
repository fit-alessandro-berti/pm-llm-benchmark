### Explanation of Logic

#### **Case Identification**  
Cases represent **document-centric workflows**. A new case is initiated when a document is first focused on (e.g., `Document1.docx` at 09:00:00 or `Quarterly_Report.docx` at 09:07:15). Events are grouped into the same case if they occur while interacting with the **same document or its related tasks** (e.g., switching to email/Adobe/Excel while editing `Document1.docx`). A case ends when the primary document is closed (e.g., `Document1.docx` closed at 09:07:00).  

- **Excluded Event**: The initial `FOCUS` on `Quarterly_Report.docx` at 08:59:50 is omitted because it was brief and no further activity occurred until 09:07:15.  
- **Nested Activities**: Actions in other apps (e.g., email, PDF, Excel) are part of the parent document’s case if contextually related (e.g., researching for `Document1.docx`).

---

#### **Activity Naming**  
Activities are derived by combining the **base action** (e.g., `Type`, `Save`) with the **application context** to reflect the task:  
- `Type Text (Word)`: Typing in Microsoft Word.  
- `Open Email`: Clicking to open an email in Chrome.  
- `Annotate PDF`: Highlighting text in Adobe Acrobat.  
- `Edit Spreadsheet`: Typing in Excel.  

This approach avoids generic terms like `FOCUS` or `SWITCH` and instead captures **meaningful process steps** (e.g., `Save Document (Word)` clarifies the action and tool).

1. The user worked on `Document1.docx`, then returned to `Quarterly_Report.docx`.  
2. Cross-application tasks (email, PDF, Excel) were sub-processes of editing `Document1.docx`.  
3. `Quarterly_Report.docx` was worked on in two sessions (09:07:15–09:08:15), treated as separate cases (`Quarterly_Report_1`).
