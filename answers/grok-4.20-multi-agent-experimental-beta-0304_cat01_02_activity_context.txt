**Justification of Naming Choices**

- **Initial Document Verification** vs. **Re-Verification of Documents**: "Verify Documents" occurs twice in C001 and C003. The first instance is always the upfront check right after initiation (basic identity/ID validation, often revealing gaps). The second instance occurs *after* additional input (submitted documents or an account fix) and serves as a follow-up/re-check. Position and context clearly differentiate an initial gate from a post-correction validation step.

- **Call Customer** variants: This activity has the most contextual variation. In C001 it is specifically to request missing documents. In C002 the first call clarifies a discrepancy while the second is a follow-up after internal changes. In C003 it is a practical "recall" because the customer physically left the branch. Specific names ("Call for Additional Documents", "Clarification Call on Account", "Confirmation Call After Adjustment", "Call to Recall Customer") capture the distinct intent and trigger for each communication.

- Minor standardization ("Internal Account Adjustment", "Submit Additional Documents"): These make the meaning more precise without overcomplicating, while "Initiate Request" and "Approve Request" were left unchanged as they serve consistent start/end roles despite minor channel differences.

These changes eliminate label ambiguity, making the log more suitable for process analysis (e.g., discovering clearer process variants or conformance checking). The new names directly incorporate clues from timestamps, sequence position, and the provided Additional Context column.
