### **3. Interpretations & Justifications of Observed Behaviors**

#### **Justifying Case 3 (Timing Delays): The Weekend Factor**
If we look at the calendar for January 2024:
*   **January 12** (Application Received) was a **Friday**.
*   **January 13 & 14** were **Saturday & Sunday**.

This explains the timing delays:
*   **Credit Check delay:** Since the application came in on Friday morning, standard business processing may have paused over the weekend, pushing the Credit Check to Sunday/Monday (Jan 14).
*   **Notification delay:** The decision was reached on Sunday, Jan 14. The 51-hour gap meant the applicant was notified on Tuesday afternoon, Jan 16, which is only about 1.5 standard business days later.
*   *Recommendation:* Adjust the desiderata rules in the process monitoring system to count **business hours** rather than absolute hours to avoid flagging false-positive delays over weekends.

#### **Interpreting Case 2's Failures (Process Breakdown)**
The severe errors in Case 2 point toward a few potential operational scenarios:
1.  **System/Data Entry Bypass:** The operator may have initiated the **Risk Assessment** early because they already possessed the applicant's credit data from a very recent past application, forgetting that the system mandates a fresh **Credit Check** log entry first.
2.  **Accidental Rejection Misclick:** The operator might have accidentally selected "Rejected" instead of "Approved" in the system. If they actually intended to approve the loan, it would explain why they went on to **Finalize Documentation**.
3.  **Automation/UI Flow Issue:** The system might lack hard validation constraints. A robust workflow management system should programmatically lock the "Finalize Documentation" button if the "Rejected" status is active, and physically prevent starting a "Risk Assessment" until the "Credit Check" event is timestamped.
