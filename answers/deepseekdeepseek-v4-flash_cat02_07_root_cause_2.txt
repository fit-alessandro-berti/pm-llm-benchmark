### Analysis of Performance Issues in Insurance Claims Processing

#### 1. Identification of Cases with Extended Durations

By calculating the total processing time from "Submit Claim" to "Close Claim" for each case:

- **Case 2001**: 1.5 hours (Low complexity, Region A) – **Fast**
- **Case 2002**: ~26 hours (Medium complexity, Region B) – **Moderate delay**
- **Case 2003**: ~48.3 hours (High complexity, Region A) – **Significant delay**
- **Case 2004**: 1.4 hours (Low complexity, Region B) – **Fast**
- **Case 2005**: ~77.1 hours (High complexity, Region B) – **Severe delay**

**Performance issues** are evident in **Cases 2002, 2003, and 2005**, with Case 2005 being the longest.

---

#### 2. Root Cause Analysis

##### a) Complexity Level
- **Low complexity** (Cases 2001, 2004)  No “Request Additional Documents” activity  Quick straight-through processing.
- **Medium complexity** (Case 2002)  One document request  Duration ~1 day.
- **High complexity** (Cases 2003, 2005)  Multiple document requests (2 in 2003, 3 in 2005)  Duration increases to 2–3 days.

**Conclusion**: Complexity is the primary driver of extended durations because additional document requests create waiting loops. Each request introduces delays due to customer response time and internal re-evaluation.

##### b) Resource (Adjuster)
- **Adjuster_Mike** (Region A) handled Case 2001 (Low) and Case 2003 (High). In 2003, two document requests occurred within the same day (11:00 and 17:00), leading to approval the next day.
- **Adjuster_Lisa** (Region B) handled Cases 2002 (Medium), 2004 (Low), and 2005 (High). In 2005, three document requests were spread across three days (04-01 11:30, 04-02 17:00, 04-03 15:00) with long intervals between them (29.5h and 22h).

The significantly longer intervals in Case 2005 compared to Case 2003 (where requests were on the same day) suggest that **Adjuster_Lisa** may be slower to process interim steps, or that her handling of document requests leads to more extended wait times. However, this is confounded by region and claim specifics.

##### c) Region
- **Region A**: High-complexity case (2003) completed in ~2 days with two requests.
- **Region B**: High-complexity case (2005) took ~3 days with three requests.

While both high-complexity claims are lengthy, the Region B case experienced an extra document request and longer gaps. This could indicate:
- Differences in customer responsiveness (Region B customers may take longer to provide documents).
- Regional variation in adjuster workload or efficiency.
- Underlying claim characteristics (e.g., more complex documentation requirements in Region B).

##### d) Manager Approval
- All low/medium cases were approved by **Manager_Ann**; both high-complexity cases were approved by **Manager_Bill**. The time from last document request to approval was similar (23h in 2003 vs. 19h in 2005), so manager is not a major contributor to delays.

---

#### 3. Proposed Explanations and Mitigations

##### Root Cause Explanations
1. **High Complexity  Multiple Document Requests**: Claims that require iterative information gathering inherently take longer. Each loop adds at least a partial day due to customer response time and adjuster re-evaluation.
2. **Adjuster Work Style or Workload**: Adjuster_Lisa shows longer intervals between requests, possibly because she processes multiple cases simultaneously, is less proactive in follow-ups, or because the specific claims in Region B have tighter document requirements.
3. **Regional Factors**: Region B may have a higher incidence of complex claims or slower document submission from claimants (e.g., due to legal or administrative differences).

##### Mitigation Suggestions
- **Standardize Document Request Timelines**: Set service-level agreements (SLAs) for when adjusters must follow up on pending documents. Automate reminders to claimants after a set number of days.
- **Assign Dedicated Resources for High-Complexity Claims**: Create a specialized team of adjusters and managers for high-complexity cases to streamline communication and reduce hand-offs.
- **Monitor Adjuster Efficiency**: Track time between requests and provide coaching or load balancing if certain adjusters consistently experience longer gaps.
- **Regional Process Harmonization**: Investigate if Region B has different documentation standards or customer demographics that lead to longer cycles. If so, consider region-specific process improvements (e.g., pre-filled forms, clearer guidelines).
- **Limit Iterations**: In cases where additional documents are requested multiple times, consider a fixed number of allowable requests before escalation or mandatory manager review to expedite closure.

By addressing these root causes, the organization can reduce the duration of medium- and high-complexity claims, improving overall process performance.
