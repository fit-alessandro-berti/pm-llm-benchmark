### Explanation of Transformation Logic

**Case Identification (Grouping into Cases):**
- I defined each **case** as a logical unit of work centered on a specific document or task artifact:
  - `DOC-Document1.docx`: All interactions with Document1.docx in Word, including the initial focus, editing sessions (paused and resumed), and final close.
  - `EMAIL-AnnualMeeting`: The complete handling of the annual meeting email (opening, reading, replying, sending).
  - `PDF-Report_Draft`: Brief review of the PDF (open, scroll, highlight).
  - `XLS-Budget_2024`: Editing the budget spreadsheet.
  - `DOC-Quarterly_Report`: Final editing session on the quarterly report.
- Switches between applications/documents mark the end of one case's active phase and the start of another, except when the user explicitly returns to a previous document (e.g., back to Document1.docx after Excel, so resumed under the same case).

**Activity Naming:**
- Raw actions (FOCUS, TYPING, SAVE, SWITCH, CLICK, SCROLL, HIGHLIGHT, CLOSE) were abstracted into higher-level, standardized process activities that are meaningful for process mining:
  - "Start Editing Document", "Resume Editing Document", "Edit Content", "Save Document", "Complete Editing Document" for Word documents.
  - "Open Email Inbox", "Open Specific Email", "Scroll Email", "Start Reply", "Type Reply", "Send Email" for email handling.
  - "Open PDF for Review", "Scroll PDF", "Highlight Text" for PDF review.
  - "Open Spreadsheet", "Edit Cells", "Save Spreadsheet" for Excel.
- This abstraction reduces noise and creates consistent activity labels across similar actions, making the log suitable for discovery of process models (e.g., in tools like PM4Py, Celonis, or Disco).

**Additional Notes:**
- Timestamps are preserved exactly as provided.
- The "Resource (Application)" column is added as a useful attribute for resource-based analysis.
- SWITCH events are either used as boundaries (pause/resume) or translated into the start of the next case's first activity to avoid redundant low-level events.
- The resulting log tells a coherent story: the user works on drafting Document1.docx, interrupts to handle an urgent email and quickly check a PDF and budget data (likely for reference), then returns to finalize Document1.docx before moving to the Quarterly_Report.docx. This creates analyst-friendly traces for process discovery and conformance checking.
