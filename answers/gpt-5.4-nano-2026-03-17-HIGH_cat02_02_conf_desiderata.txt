## 1) Normative process violations (strict rules)

### **Case 1 — No violations**
Sequence matches exactly:
Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case.

---

### **Case 2 — Violates multiple normative rules**
1. **Ordering violation: Risk Assessment before Credit Check**
   - Log shows: **Risk Assessment (08:20)** occurs **before** **Credit Check (09:00)**.
   - This directly violates: **Credit Check must occur before Risk Assessment**.

2. **Contradictory action after rejection: Finalize Documentation after Rejected decision**
   - Log shows: **Loan Decision (Rejected) (09:30)**, then **Finalize Documentation (10:00)**.
   - This violates: **If rejected, do not finalize documentation before closing the case.**

#### Plausible interpretations/justifications (only “best-effort,” not excusing the rule)
- **Timestamp/log-order mismatch:** Sometimes event timestamps represent *start* vs *completion*. If the “Credit Check” was actually completed before the “Risk Assessment” started, the ordering might be misleading. However, under the strict interpretation of the log, the violation stands.
- **Documentation finalization as an automated/administrative step:** It’s possible the system auto-completed documentation artifacts upon closure workflow even though the decision was rejected. Still, per the stated normative rule, “finalize documentation after rejection” is not allowed.

---

### **Case 3 — No normative violations**
The strict ordering is respected:
Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case.

So Case 3 is **normatively compliant**, though it has timing/best-practice issues (see next section).

## 2) Desiderata failures (recommended but not strict)

> These are **not severe normative breaches**, but they indicate suboptimal process behavior.

### **Case 1 — Meets desiderata**
- Credit check within 24 hours: **Yes** (30 minutes).
- Inform applicant within 48 hours of decision: **Yes** (2 hours after the decision).
- Follow-up call before closing: **Not shown**, but the log doesn’t explicitly show it—however there’s no requirement that it appears in the log for this task.

---

### **Case 2 — Desiderata are irrelevant/secondary due to normative failure**
Even if timing/best-practice expectations were considered, Case 2 already fails hard on ordering and decision-based documentation rules. Still, it also appears to be poor timing around the decision/informing/document steps, but the primary issues are normative.

---

### **Case 3 — Fails multiple desiderata**
1. **Credit Check timing recommendation violated**
   - Application received: **2024-01-12 09:00**
   - Credit check: **2024-01-14 10:00**
   - Delay is roughly **49 hours**, exceeding the “within 24 hours” preference.

2. **Inform Applicant timing recommendation violated**
   - Loan decision (Approved): **2024-01-14 12:00**
   - Inform applicant: **2024-01-16 15:00**
   - Delay is roughly **51 hours**, exceeding the “within 48 hours” preference.

3. **Follow-up call not performed (recommended best practice)**
   - Log explicitly notes: **“*(No Follow-up Call)*”**
   - This is suboptimal per the recommendation (not a hard rule).

#### Plausible interpretations/justifications
- **Credit check delayed by external dependency:** Waiting on bureau results, staffing, or queue/backlog can easily push the credit check past 24 hours.
- **Notification delayed by approvals/eligibility verification:** Even after a decision timestamp, generating and sending the applicant communication can be delayed if compliance steps or document preparation runs long.
- **Follow-up call skipped due to resource constraints:** Some workflows skip calls if the outcome is already clearly documented or if there’s limited capacity.
