### Standard P2P Process Flow
In a typical P2P process, the logical sequence aims for control, accuracy, and compliance. The core steps usually follow this order:
1. **Create Purchase Requisition** ¢ An internal request for goods or services.
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition** ¢ Authorization from the appropriate budget holder or manager.
3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)** ¢ A formal document sent to the vendor, legally binding the order.
4. **Receive Goods** ¢ Confirmation that the ordered items have arrived.
5. **Vendor Invoice Received** ¢ The vendor's bill for the goods or services.
6. **Match Invoice to PO** ¢ Often a three-way match (PO, goods receipt, and invoice) to verify accuracy.
7. **Release Payment** ¢ Processing payment after validation.
8. **Close Case** ¢ Finalizing the transaction in records.

This sequence ensures that purchases are authorized, documented, and verified before money leaves the organization. Deviations can introduce financial, operational, or compliance risks.
