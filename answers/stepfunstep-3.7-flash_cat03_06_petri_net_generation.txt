To solve this problem, we construct an **accepting Petri net** that models the insurance claim management process, including the specified loops for document re-submission and in-depth investigation, as well as the conditional approval step. The Petri net uses places to represent states between activities, transitions labeled with activity names to represent actions, and arcs to define the flow of the process.

### Step-by-Step Explanation of the Petri Net Structure
1. **Places (States):** We define places to represent the state of the claim between activities. The initial place `p_start` holds the initial token (representing a new claim), and the final place `p_closed` represents the closed claim (final marking).
2. **Transitions (Activities):** Each transition is labeled with the corresponding activity name from the scenario. A silent (unlabeled) transition is used to model the optional approval step (when no senior manager approval is required).
3. **Loops:**
   - **Document Re-submission Loop:** After `Document Verification (DV)`, if documents are incomplete, the process moves to `Re-Document Submission (RDS)` and returns to `DV` for re-verification.
   - **In-depth Investigation Loop:** After `Fraud Check (FC)`, if doubts are raised, the process moves to `In-depth Investigation (II)` and returns to `FC` for re-checking.
4. **Conditional Approval:** After `Claim Assessment (CA)`, the process either proceeds to `Approval (AP)` (if the claim amount exceeds the threshold) or skips approval directly to `Payment (P)`.
