## Analysis

**Normative Hire-to-Retire sequence:**
Post_Job_Ad  Screen_Candidates  Conduct_Interviews  Make_Hiring_Decision  Onboard_Employee  Add_to_Payroll  Close_Case

---

## Model 1 — Anomalies

```
Post  Screen  {Decide, Interview}  ;  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close
```

**Identified anomalies:**

1. **Decision decoupled from interviews (ordering anomaly).** `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview` are defined separately, but there is **no edge from Interview to Decide**. This means `Conduct_Interviews` and `Make_Hiring_Decision` run **in parallel**. Logically, the hiring decision should *depend on* the interview outcome; here a decision can be reached before/without the interviews actually informing it.

2. **Interview is a dangling branch.** `Conduct_Interviews` has no outgoing edge — it never feeds anything downstream. It is performed but its result is never consumed.

**Severity:** Moderate. The backbone (Post  Screen  …  Onboard  Payroll  Close) is intact, all activities are present and mandatory, and the sequence is otherwise correct. The only flaw is the misordered/parallel placement of interview vs. decision.

---

## Model 2 — Anomalies

```
Post  {Screen ; Interview  Decide  *(Onboard)  X(Payroll, skip)  Close}
```

**Identified anomalies:**

1. **Screening is disconnected from the decision (severe ordering anomaly).** `Post  Screen` exists, but Screen has **no outgoing edge**. Meanwhile `Post  Interview  Decide`. So screening runs in parallel and never feeds into interviewing or the decision. Effectively, **interviews can happen without screening**, defeating the purpose of screening.

2. **Onboarding loop `*(Onboard, skip)`.** Onboarding can be repeated multiple times. Onboarding a single hire repeatedly is not meaningful and represents a control-flow anomaly.

3. **Payroll can be skipped `X(Payroll, skip)` — most severe.** An employee can be fully hired and onboarded but **never added to payroll**. This fundamentally violates the essence of Hire-to-Retire: a hired person who is never paid breaks the core integrity of the process.

**Severity:** High. Two structural distortions (disconnected screening, optional payroll) attack the *essence* of the process, plus a meaningless loop.

---

## Verdict: **Model 1 is closer to the normative process.**

**Justification:**

- **Model 1** preserves a complete, mostly-correct totally-ordered spine. Every required activity occurs exactly once, in (almost) the right order, and the final segment `Onboard  Payroll  Close` is fully intact. Its single real flaw is the parallelism between *interview* and *decision* — an ordering imperfection, not a violation of what the process fundamentally must guarantee.

- **Model 2** contains **fundamental integrity violations**:
  - Payroll being *optional* means hired employees may never be paid — a critical correctness failure.
  - Screening being disconnected means candidates can be interviewed and hired without ever being screened — undermining the selection logic.
  - The onboarding loop introduces meaningless repetition.

Because Model 2's anomalies break the *guarantees* the process must enforce (every hire is paid; candidates are screened before being evaluated), while Model 1's anomaly is merely a misplacement in ordering, **Model 1 aligns more closely with a normative Hire-to-Retire process.**
