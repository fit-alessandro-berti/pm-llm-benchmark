## 1. Normative Hire-to-Retire reference

A standard Hire-to-Retire process is usually expected to follow a causal chain such as:

1. **Post Job Ad**  
2. **Screen Candidates**  filters the applicant pool  
3. **Conduct Interviews**  evaluates shortlisted candidates  
4. **Make Hiring Decision**  uses interview (and screening) outcomes  
5. **Onboard Employee**  after a positive hiring decision  
6. **Add to Payroll**  after the employee is on board  
7. **Close Case**

Thus, the key integrity constraints are:

- Screening precedes interviews.
- Interviews precede the hiring decision.
- The decision precedes onboarding.
- Onboarding precedes adding to payroll.
- Payroll precedes closing the case.

## 2. Analysis of POWL Model 1

```python
model1.order.add_edge(Post, Screen)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Decide)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Interview)
model1.order.add_edge(Decide, Onboard)
model1.order.add_edge(Onboard, Payroll)
model1.order.add_edge(Payroll, Close)
```

### Anomalies

| # | Anomaly | Severity |
|---|---------|----------|
| **A1** | **Decision can be made before / without interviews.** The model has `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview`, but **no** `Interview  Decide`. Therefore `Decide` and `Interview` are concurrent after `Screen`. | **Critical** |
| **A2** | **Onboarding, payroll, and closure can happen before interviews.** Because `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close` and `Interview` is not ordered before `Decide`, a case can proceed all the way to `Close` and only afterwards execute `Interview`. | **Critical** |
| **A3** | **Hiring decision is based only on screening.** Since `Interview` does not feed into `Decide`, the decision ignores interview results. | **Critical** |
| **A4** | All activities are mandatory (no silent skips or XORs), which is actually fine in principle, but the ordering makes this a moot point. | Minor |

**Overall:** Model 1 breaks the very essence of hiring: it allows an organization to hire, onboard, pay, and close a case **before** (or without) ever interviewing candidates.

## 3. Analysis of POWL Model 2

```python
model2.order.add_edge(Post, Screen)
model2.order.add_edge(Post, Interview)
model2.order.add_edge(Interview, Decide)
model2.order.add_edge(Decide, loop_onboarding)
model2.order.add_edge(loop_onboarding, xor_payroll)
model2.order.add_edge(xor_payroll, Close)

loop_onboarding = *(Onboard, skip)
xor_payroll     = X(Payroll, skip)
```

### Anomalies

| # | Anomaly | Severity |
|---|---------|----------|
| **B1** | **Screening does not have to precede interviews.** `Screen` and `Interview` are both successors of `Post` but unrelated to each other, so interviews can occur before or in parallel with screening. | Moderate |
| **B2** | **Onboarding can be repeated indefinitely.** `*(Onboard, skip)` allows executing `Onboard` one or more times. A single onboarding is the normative expectation. | Low–Moderate |
| **B3** | **Adding to payroll is optional.** `X(Payroll, skip)` means a case can close without ever adding the employee to payroll. | **High** |
| **B4** | The interview result does feed into the decision, and onboarding follows the decision. | Good |

**Overall:** Model 2 preserves the core causal chain:

```text
Interview  Decide  Onboard  (optional Payroll)  Close
```

Its main flaws are the optional payroll step and the fact that screening and interviewing are not properly ordered.

## 4. Which model is closer to the normative process?

**Model 2 is closer to a normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

### Justification

The most important property of a Hire-to-Retire process is that **candidates are evaluated before a hiring decision is made**, and that **the hiring decision precedes onboarding and payroll**. Model 2 respects this causal chain:

```text
Interview  Decide  Onboard  Payroll/Close
```

Even though Model 2 allows payroll to be skipped and allows interviews before screening, these deviations are less fundamental than Model 1’s inversion:

- **Model 1** allows a trace such as:
  ```text
  Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close  Interview
  ```
  Here the employee is hired, paid, and the case closed **before** interviews occur. This violates the very purpose of the process.

- **Model 2**, even in its worst valid trace, still follows the logical flow of evaluation  decision  onboarding:
  ```text
  Post  Interview  Screen  Decide  Onboard  (skip Payroll)  Close
  ```
  The only major business issue is the skipped payroll, which is a serious administrative gap but does not invert the hiring logic.
