### **Analysis and Renaming of Recurring Activities**

Several activities appear multiple times within individual cases (especially `Verify Documents`, `Call Customer`, and `Adjust Account`), but their **contextual meaning differs** based on timing, purpose, and preceding actions. Below is a breakdown of each recurring activity and its contextual evolution.

---

## **Step 1: Identify Contextual Differences**

###  **1. `Verify Documents`**
- **First occurrence**: Initial check of customer’s identity documents (e.g., ID, proof of address).
- **Second occurrence**: Re-verification **after** missing documents were submitted or corrections were made.
- **Contextual difference**: The first is **initial verification**, the second is **re-verification post-resolution of missing data**.

>  *Different purpose: initial vs. follow-up validation after input gap was closed.*

---

###  **2. `Call Customer`**
- **C001**: Called because documents were **missing** — outbound call to request additional info.
- **C002**: Called to **clarify account discrepancy** — explanatory, not for document collection.
- **C003**: Called because the customer **left the branch** — to bring them back; not related to data or discrepancies.

>  *Different purposes: request missing docs (C001), clarify issue (C002), retrieve customer (C003).*

---

###  **3. `Adjust Account`**
- Only appears in C002 and C003.
- In **C002**: Adjustment made **after** a call to clarify a discrepancy.
- In **C003**: Adjustment made **after** a missing info issue was identified (post-branch visit).
- Both are internal corrections triggered by prior communication or verification gaps.

>  *Contextual role: internal correction based on new information from customer interaction.*

---

## **Step 2: Rename Activities with Contextual Clarity**

| Original Activity | Renamed To | Justification |
|-------------------|------------|-------------|
| `Verify Documents` (1st occurrence) | `Initial Document Verification` | First-time check of submitted documents; foundational step. |
| `Verify Documents` (2nd occurrence) | `Re-Verify Documents (Post-Submission)` | Occurs only after customer provided missing documents; confirms resolution. |
| `Call Customer` (C001) | `Request Missing Documents` | Clear intent: to collect missing ID scan. |
| `Call Customer` (C002) | `Clarify Account Discrepancy` | Focus is on explaining or resolving inconsistency in account data. |
| `Call Customer` (C003) | `Retrieve Customer (Post-Departure)` | Specific to a walk-in customer who left the branch. |
| `Adjust Account` | `Correct Account Details (Post-Review)` | Reflects that this is an internal correction based on findings from prior interaction. |

## **Step 4: Justification Summary**

- **`Initial Document Verification` vs `Re-Verify Documents (Post-Submission)`**  
   Distinguishes between the first check and the recheck after missing data is supplied. Critical for modeling retry logic and process loops.

- **`Request Missing Documents`**  
   Clearly identifies the purpose: to collect missing inputs. Avoids ambiguity with other calls.

- **`Clarify Account Discrepancy`**  
   Reflects investigative intent, not document collection. Different from C001’s call.

- **`Retrieve Customer (Post-Departure)`**  
   Specific to branch context; highlights operational nuance (physical presence).

- **`Correct Account Details (Post-Review)`**  
   Emphasizes that this is an **internal correction** based on prior findings, not a new request.

- **Follow-up call in C002 renamed**  
   Adds temporal and functional clarity: it’s a confirmation call, not a new inquiry.

The revised log now:

- Enables more accurate process mining, conformance checking, and model discovery.
- Supports better understanding of **looping, retries, and corrective actions**.

This renaming transforms the log from a flat sequence into a **semantically rich, context-aware event stream**.
