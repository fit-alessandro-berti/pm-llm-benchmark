**Description of the Visualization**

This image is a **three-part academic figure** illustrating the concept of **Proclets** — a modeling approach for flexible, artifact-centric business processes (often associated with research by Wil van der Aalst and others).

### (a) Two Proclet Classes Connected Through Two Channels

This is the main diagram. It shows two interacting **proclet classes**:

- **Lab Visit** (left side)
- **Lab Test** (right side)

The diagram depicts a laboratory workflow involving:
- A doctor initiating an order
- A lab assistant taking a blood sample
- Results being judged
- Chemical analysts performing tests
- The possibility of repeating tests ("Do tests again" / "Perform test again")

The two proclets communicate through **multiple channels** (shown as dashed rectangles with labels like 1:1, 1:3, 1:4, 1:5, etc.). These numbers represent **cardinalities/multiplicities** — indicating how many instances of one proclet can relate to the other at each interaction point. This is a key feature of proclets: they allow complex, dynamic relationships between different types of process instances (objects).

### (b) Class Diagram

This is a simplified UML-style class diagram showing the structural relationship between the two proclet classes:
- One **Lab Visit** *requires* one or more **Lab Test** instances
- It shows the multiplicities: `1..1` (exactly one) on the Lab Visit side and `1..*` (one or more) on the Lab Test side

### (c) Example of a Performative

This table shows a concrete **message** (called a "performative" in proclet theory) exchanged between the proclets:

| Time  | Channel       | Sender              | Receivers                     | Action  | Content                                | Scope   | Direction |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|---------|----------------------------------------|---------|---------|
| 11:00 | Order system  | Lab visit - John    | Lab test - HGB, Lab test - John | Create  | Can you perform a HGB test for John?   | Private | OUT     |

This demonstrates how proclets communicate through structured messages that carry intent, content, and scoping rules.

---

**Summary**: The visualization is explaining how traditional process models struggle with scenarios involving multiple interacting object types (a patient visit and multiple lab tests), and how **Proclets** provide a more natural way to model these complex, multi-object workflows using channels and performatives.
