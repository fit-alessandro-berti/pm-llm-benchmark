# Analysis of Contextual Activity Recurrences

## 1. Identifying Recurring Activities

Looking at each case, several activities repeat with different meanings:

- **Initiate Request** — occurs once per case but via different channels (online vs. walk-in), justifying channel-specific names.
- **Verify Documents** — appears twice in C001 and C003 (initial vs. re-verification).
- **Call Customer** — appears twice in C002 (clarification call vs. follow-up call); also differs in C001 (missing docs) and C003 (request return).
- **Submit Additional** — only once, but context-specific (customer-triggered).
- **Adjust Account** — appears in C002 and C003 but at different stages (post-call vs. mid-process correction).
- **Approve Request** — terminal activity, consistent in meaning across cases.

## 4. Justification of Naming Choices

### Initiate Request  channel-specific
- **Online** (C001, C002): `WebPortal` resource signals self-service initiation.
- **Branch Walk-in** (C003): `BranchReception` implies physical presence — a different onboarding flow.

### Verify Documents  role in the verification lifecycle
- **Initial Document Verification**: the first check after intake.
- **Re-Verification of Documents** (C001): triggered specifically because the customer supplied missing ID scans.
- **Post-Adjustment Re-Verification** (C003): triggered because a back-office account change required a sanity re-check — a different cause than C001.

These names distinguish *why* verification recurs (missing docs vs. data adjustment).

### Call Customer  purpose of the call
- **Request Missing Docs** (C001): verification was blocked pending documents.
- **Clarify Discrepancy** (C002, first call): agent is investigating account issues.
- **Confirm Adjustment** (C002, second call): confirmatory/closure communication after the back-office change.
- **Request Return** (C003): operational — asking a walk-in customer to come back.

Each call plays a distinct process role (gathering, clarifying, confirming, recalling).

### Submit Additional  initiator-specific
- Renamed **Customer Uploads Missing Docs** to emphasize that the customer, not staff, advances the case here — important for process mining (handoff, waiting times).

### Adjust Account  trigger context
- **Internal Account Adjustment** (C002): follows a clarification call — expected, planned outcome.
- **Corrective Account Adjustment** (C003): triggered by missing information uncovered mid-flow — a corrective, unplanned loop.

### Approve Request  **Final Approval**
- Activity is consistent; renamed only for clarity and consistency in terminal step naming.

## Why This Matters for Process Mining

Without disambiguation, a process-mining tool would collapse *Verify Documents* and *Call Customer* into single nodes, hiding loops and the reasons behind them. The renamed log exposes:
- **Rework loops** (re-verification paths),
- **Communication intents** (requesting, clarifying, confirming),
- **Channel variants** (web vs. branch),
- **Trigger causality** (customer-driven vs. back-office-driven adjustments).

This leads to more accurate process models, fitness metrics, and bottleneck discovery.
