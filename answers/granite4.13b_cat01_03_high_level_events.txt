**Proposed High-Level Process Steps and Justifications:**

1. **Material Preparation**
   - **Justification:** This step encompasses all activities required to prepare the raw material for subsequent processing. It includes retrieving the metal sheet (PartID), scanning its barcode, placing it on a designated table, aligning it precisely, and preheating it to ensure optimal conditions for welding.
   - **Rationale:** These events are temporally close and logically precede any assembly operations. They set up the material in a condition ready for further manipulation without altering its identity or properties.

2. **Assembly**
   - **Justification:** This step groups activities directly related to forming the product structure, such as welding both corners of the metal sheet. The picking of the welding tool and subsequent welding actions are sequential processes that build the structural integrity of the component.
   - **Rationale:** It represents a core manufacturing operation where individual parts come together into a cohesive assembly stage.

3. **Quality Inspection**
   - **Justification:** Post-assembly, the weld integrity must be verified to ensure adherence to quality standards. Measuring weld integrity and conducting visual checks are critical steps ensuring that the product meets required specifications.
   - **Rationale:** These activities follow assembly operations as they assess whether the assembled components meet design and safety criteria before proceeding to final finishing.

4. **Finishing**
   - **Justification:** Once the quality inspection is passed, applying a protective coating (e.g., Polyurethane) and drying it at specified temperature ensures durability and resistance to environmental factors such as corrosion or abrasion.
   - **Rationale:** Finishing steps are essential for enhancing product longevity and aesthetic appeal. They follow quality checks as they add protective layers that maintain functionality over time.
