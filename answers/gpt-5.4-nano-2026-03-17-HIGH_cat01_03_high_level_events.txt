## Proposed High-Level Process Steps (Aggregated from Low-Level Events)

Below is a coherent set of higher-level stages inferred from the event sequence pattern shown in cases **A1** and **B2**. The same ordering appears in both cases, so the grouping is based on **(1) logical manufacturing phase**, **(2) temporal adjacency**, and **(3) functional resource roles** (operator vs robot vs heating/coating vs quality).

---

### 1) Material Identification & Staging
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Retrieve raw metal sheet* — `Operator A` (PartID: M-xxx)
- *Scan barcode* — `Operator A` (Barcode: …)
- *Place metal sheet on table* — `Operator A` (TableID: T-3)

**Rationale for grouping:**
These actions all occur at the very beginning and are part of preparing the workpiece for processing:
- retrieve the correct material,
- verify/trace it via barcode,
- physically stage it on the correct table.

**High-level step name:**
- **Material Identification & Staging**

---

### 2) Workpiece Setup & Preheating
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Align metal sheet* — `Robot Arm #2` (Alignment: Perfect)
- *Preheat metal sheet* — `Heating Unit #1` (Temperature: 200°C)

**Rationale for grouping:**
This phase transitions from “material staged” to “ready for joining”:
- alignment is required before welding,
- preheating prepares the metal so welding proceeds under correct thermal conditions.
These steps occur shortly after staging and use different equipment but serve a single setup goal.

**High-level step name:**
- **Workpiece Setup & Preheating**

---

### 3) Welding / Joining Operation
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Pick up welding tool* — `Operator B` (ToolID: W-12)
- *Weld corner A* — `Operator B` (WeldType: Spot)
- *Weld corner B* — `Operator B` (WeldType: Spot)

**Rationale for grouping:**
All events are directly tied to performing the joining operation:
- acquiring the correct tool,
- executing the welds on specified locations.
They are contiguous and share a common purpose: completing the weld.

**High-level step name:**
- **Welding / Joining**

---

### 4) Weld Quality Measurement
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Measure weld integrity* — `Quality Sensor #1` (IntegrityScore: 95/93)

**Rationale for grouping:**
This is a distinct quality-control action performed after welding and before finishing/coating:
- it measures a weld-specific attribute (integrity score),
- it likely determines whether the part meets requirements before proceeding.

**High-level step name:**
- **Weld Integrity Inspection**

---

### 5) Protective Coating Application & Curing
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Apply protective coating* — `Coating Unit #3` (CoatingType: Polyurethane)
- *Dry coating* — `Heating Unit #2` (Temperature: 120°C)

**Rationale for grouping:**
These actions are two steps of the same protective finishing process:
- apply the coating material,
- then cure/dry it under controlled heating.
They follow immediately after weld inspection, matching a typical workflow: join  inspect  protect.

**High-level step name:**
- **Protective Coating & Curing**

---

### 6) Final Visual Verification
**Included low-level events (typical):**
- *Visual check* — `Operator C` (Check: Passed)

**Rationale for grouping:**
This is the concluding check, performed after coating curing:
- it’s a human verification step,
- it provides a final “release” decision for the processed part.

**High-level step name:**
- **Final Visual Inspection**
