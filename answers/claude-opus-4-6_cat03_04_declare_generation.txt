This DECLARE model captures the following key process semantics:

1. **Unary constraints**: All core activities must exist; the process always starts with Idea Generation; Idea Generation, Final Launch, and Approval Gate occur exactly once.

2. **Responded existence / Coexistence**: Creating a prototype requires both lab and user testing to exist; design drafts require feasibility and cost checks; testing types co-occur; marketing and launch co-occur.

3. **Response / Precedence / Succession**: Enforce the logical ordering — ideas lead to designs, designs lead to prototypes, prototypes lead to testing, testing leads to approval, approval leads to marketing and launch. Precedence ensures nothing happens out of order (e.g., no prototype before feasibility check).

4. **Alternate and Chain constraints**: Enforce stricter ordering — prototype is *immediately* followed by lab testing; marketing plan is *immediately* followed by final launch. Alternate constraints ensure no duplicate approvals or launches without proper sequencing.

5. **Negative constraints**: Prevent process regression — no going back to design after approval, no restarting after final launch, and no immediate backward jumps (e.g., approval gate cannot immediately lead to prototyping).
