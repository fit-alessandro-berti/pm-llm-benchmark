### **Anomalies Identified**

From the temporal profile model, I've identified several suspicious timing patterns:

1. **RP (Receive to Approve) - Suspiciously Low Variance**  
   - **Pattern:** ~25-hour average with only 1-hour standard deviation  
   - **Issue:** Such low variability for a 25-hour process suggests artificial, fixed-time delays rather than natural workflow

2. **PN (Approve to Notify) - Extreme Delay**  
   - **Pattern:** 7-day average wait with 2-day variability  
   - **Issue:** Notification shouldn't take a week after approval; suggests broken process or manual bottleneck

3. **AC (Assign to Close) - Premature Closure**  
   - **Pattern:** Claims closing within 2 hours of assignment  
   - **Issue:** Skips Evaluation (E) and Approval (P) steps entirely

4. **EN (Evaluate to Notify) - Instant Notification**  
   - **Pattern:** 5-minute average with 1-minute variability  
   - **Issue:** Suspiciously fast; may indicate auto-notifications or skipped validation

### **Hypotheses for Anomalies**

**For RP (Fixed Timing):**
- **Batch Processing:** Claims might be approved in scheduled batches every 25 hours
- **Timer-Based Auto-Approval:** System might automatically approve after fixed period regardless of evaluation
- **Data Entry Backdating:** Employees entering timestamps with artificial delays

**For PN (7-Day Delay):**
- **Notification Backlog:** Manual notification queue with limited staff
- **Batch Mailing:** Physical letters sent weekly instead of digital notifications
- **Manual Review Requirement:** Additional post-approval verification step

**For AC (Immediate Closure):**
- **Fraud Detection:** Quick closure of obviously fraudulent claims
- **Missing Information:** Claims closed when documentation incomplete
- **System Error:** Automated closure triggers misconfigured

**For EN (Instant Notification):**
- **Auto-Notification System:** Automated emails immediately after evaluation
- **Error in Activity Sequencing:** Notification mistakenly logged before evaluation completion
- **Process Shortcut:** Workers skipping intermediate steps

### **Verification SQL Queries**

**Query 1: Identify RP Pattern Consistency**
```sql
-- Check if RP timing varies by claim type or adjuster
SELECT 
    c.claim_type,
    COUNT(*) as claim_count,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) as avg_seconds,
    STDDEV(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) as stdev_seconds,
    MIN(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) as min_seconds,
    MAX(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))) as max_seconds
FROM claim_events r
JOIN claim_events p ON r.claim_id = p.claim_id 
    AND r.activity = 'R' 
    AND p.activity = 'P'
    AND r.timestamp < p.timestamp
JOIN claims c ON r.claim_id = c.claim_id
GROUP BY c.claim_type
ORDER BY avg_seconds;
```

**Query 2: Find Extreme PN Delays**
```sql
-- Identify claims with >7 day delays between P and N
SELECT 
    p.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    c.claim_amount,
    p.timestamp as approval_time,
    n.timestamp as notification_time,
    EXTRACT(DAY FROM (n.timestamp - p.timestamp)) as days_to_notify,
    a.name as adjuster_name,
    a.region as adjuster_region
FROM claim_events p
JOIN claim_events n ON p.claim_id = n.claim_id 
    AND p.activity = 'P' 
    AND n.activity = 'N'
    AND p.timestamp < n.timestamp
JOIN claims c ON p.claim_id = c.claim_id
LEFT JOIN adjusters a ON p.resource = a.adjuster_id
WHERE EXTRACT(DAY FROM (n.timestamp - p.timestamp)) > 7
ORDER BY days_to_notify DESC;
```

**Query 3: Detect Premature Closures (AC without E/P)**
```sql
-- Find claims assigned and closed within 2 hours with no evaluation
SELECT 
    a_event.claim_id,
    c.claim_type,
    c.claim_amount,
    a_event.timestamp as assign_time,
    c_event.timestamp as close_time,
    EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (c_event.timestamp - a_event.timestamp))/60 as minutes_to_close,
    a_event.resource as assigner,
    c_event.resource as closer
FROM claim_events a_event
JOIN claim_events c_event ON a_event.claim_id = c_event.claim_id 
    AND a_event.activity = 'A' 
    AND c_event.activity = 'C'
    AND a_event.timestamp < c_event.timestamp
JOIN claims c ON a_event.claim_id = c.claim_id
WHERE EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (c_event.timestamp - a_event.timestamp)) < 7200  -- 2 hours
AND NOT EXISTS (
    SELECT 1 FROM claim_events mid 
    WHERE mid.claim_id = a_event.claim_id
    AND mid.activity IN ('E', 'P')
    AND mid.timestamp BETWEEN a_event.timestamp AND c_event.timestamp
)
ORDER BY minutes_to_close;
```

**Query 4: Correlate Quick Notifications with Adjuster Patterns**
```sql
-- Check if instant notifications are concentrated with specific adjusters
SELECT 
    e.resource as evaluator,
    COUNT(*) as instant_notification_count,
    AVG(c.claim_amount) as avg_claim_amount,
    STRING_AGG(DISTINCT c.claim_type, ', ') as claim_types_handled
```

**Query 5: Check for Temporal Clustering**
```sql
-- Detect if approvals cluster at specific times (e.g., always at 5 PM)
SELECT 
    EXTRACT(HOUR FROM p.timestamp) as approval_hour,
    COUNT(*) as approvals_at_hour,
    ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / SUM(COUNT(*)) OVER(), 2) as percent_of_total,
    AVG(EXTRACT(EPOCH FROM (p.timestamp - r.timestamp))/3600) as avg_hours_from_receive
```

**Query 6: Detect Missing Process Steps**
```sql
-- Find claims with skipped steps in the expected sequence
WITH expected_sequence AS (
    SELECT 
        claim_id,
        activity,
        timestamp,
        LAG(activity) OVER (PARTITION BY claim_id ORDER BY timestamp) as prev_activity
    FROM claim_events
)
SELECT 
    claim_id,
    STRING_AGG(activity, ' -> ' ORDER BY timestamp) as actual_sequence
FROM expected_sequence
WHERE (prev_activity = 'A' AND activity != 'E')  -- Skip from A to non-E
   OR (prev_activity = 'E' AND activity != 'P')  -- Skip from E to non-P
   OR (prev_activity = 'P' AND activity != 'N')  -- Skip from P to non-N
GROUP BY claim_id
HAVING COUNT(*) > 5  -- Claims with multiple deviations
ORDER BY claim_id
LIMIT 20;
```
