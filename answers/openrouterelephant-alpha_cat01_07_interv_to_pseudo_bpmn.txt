Based on the interview transcripts, the following BPMN-like textual process model captures the invoice approval process, integrating perspectives from Accounts Payable, Purchasing, Management, and Supplier Relations while noting ambiguities and exceptions.

**Process Name:** Invoice Approval and Payment Process  
**Participants:** Accounts Payable (Mary, Rita), Purchasing (Dan), Department Manager (Carlos), Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen), Suppliers  

---

### **Main Process Flow**
1. **Start Event:** Invoice Received (via email)  
2. **Task:** Validate PO Association  
   - *If no valid PO:*  
     - Send invoice back to supplier for clarification (loop to **Supplier**).  
     - *Note: Rita may escalate to resolve missing PO.*  
   - *If valid PO:* Proceed to logging.  
3. **Task:** Log Invoice in Accounting System  
4. **Task:** Forward to Purchasing for Confirmation  
   - Purchasing compares invoice details with PO (quantities, prices, delivery charges).  
   - *If discrepancies found:*  
     - Contact supplier directly **or** request Accounts Payable to hold invoice (loop to **Supplier** via Karen).  
   - *If PO missing:* Request PO from Accounts Payable (loop to **Task 2**).  
   - *If correct:* Send confirmation to Accounts Payable.  
5. **Gateway:** Confirm Receipt and PO Match  
   - *If confirmed:* Trigger manager approval notification (system-generated).  
   - *If unresolved:* Escalate to Rita for exception handling (loop to **Exception Handling**).  
6. **Task:** Manager Approval (Carlos)  
   - *If suspicious (e.g., budget mismatch):* Reject or request investigation (loop to **Exception Handling**).  
   - *If approved:* Proceed to payment scheduling.  
7. **Task:** Schedule Payment (Accounts Payable)  
8. **End Event:** Payment Processed  

---

### **Exception Handling & Variations**
- **Rework Loops:**  
  - *Supplier clarification loops:* Missing PO, pricing discrepancies, or mismatched quantities trigger rework with suppliers (Karen may intervene).  
  - *Discrepancy resolution:* Rita may coordinate between departments for unresolved issues.  
- **Escalation Path:**  
  - If invoice cannot be matched or resolved, Rita escalates to supervisor for decision (reject or create retrospective PO).  
- **Informal Services:**  
  - Invoices without POs for informal services may be rejected or require retrospective PO creation.  

---

### **Artifacts/Data Objects**
- Purchase Order (PO)  
- Invoice (email/electronic)  
- System confirmation notifications  
- Corrected invoices or supplier communications  

---

### **Notes on Ambiguities**
- **Sequence Uncertainty:** In some cases, after Purchasing confirmation (Task 4), Accounts Payable may directly escalate to the supervisor if discrepancies are severe (bypassing standard manager approval).  
- **Role Overlap:** Rita and Mary may handle rework loops interchangeably, depending on exception severity.  
- **Trigger Assumptions:** Manager approval is assumed to follow system notification, but manual checks by Carlos may occur (not explicitly stated).  

---

### **Textual BPMN Representation (Simplified)**
```
[Start]  (Validate PO)  {PO Valid?}  
   No  (Send to Supplier)  [Supplier Clarification]  (Update Invoice)  (Validate PO)  
     (Escalate to Rita if unresolved)  
   Yes  (Log in System)  (Forward to Purchasing)  (Compare with PO)  
           {PO Match?}  
             No  (Contact Supplier/Request Hold)  (Revalidate)  
             Yes  (Notify Manager)  {Manager Approval}  
                       Suspicious  (Escalate to Rita/Reject)  [Exception Handling]  
                       Approved  (Schedule Payment)  [End]  
```
