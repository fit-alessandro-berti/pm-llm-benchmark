## 2. How the Constraints Shape a RealLife LoanApproval Scenario  

### 2.1. Guaranteeing a Proper Trigger and Termination  
* **Receive_Application** as **init** and **existence** means every case starts with a genuine borrower request and cannot be spontaneous.  
* **Proceed_Without_Compliance** being **absent** guarantees that the process cannot be hijacked by a rogue step that sidesteps policy.

### 2.2. Ensuring a Single, Reliable Credit Check  
* **exactly_one** on B eliminates duplicate scoring (which could be manipulated to “shop” for a better score) and guarantees that the risk assessment is a single point of truth.  
* The **response** from A to B and the **succession** from B to C force the credit check to happen early, before any effort is spent on document gathering or offer preparation.

### 2.3. Mandating Supporting Evidence Before Commitment  
* **existence** on C forces the bank to actually collect documents.  
* **coexistence** between C and F ensures that the contract cannot be signed unless the documents exist somewhere in the case (even if they were gathered earlier).  
* **chainresponse** from D to G and **chainprecedence** from F to D create a tightlycoupled subsequence: *Assemble Offer  (QA)  Authorize  Transfer*.  This prevents the bank from authorizing terms before an offer is even drafted, or moving money before the offer is finalized.

### 2.4. Embedding Quality Assurance as a GateKeeper  
* **responded_existence** from D to E and its inverse make QA a mandatory downstream step after any offer is assembled.  
* The **altsuccession/chainsuccension** pair between E and C (and D) builds a **rework loop**: if QA finds missing or insufficient documentation, the case is sent back to document gathering; otherwise it proceeds forward.  
* **precedence** from E to F guarantees that **no contract can be authorized without a successful QA review** — a direct mapping to regulatormandated underwriting checks (e.g., the “foureyes principle”, SR117, Basel III’s internalratingsbased approach).

### 2.5. Controlling Customer Communication  
* **nonsuccession** from B to H stops the bank from telling the applicant “your credit looks good” before the file is complete, managing expectations and avoiding misrepresentation.  
* **nonchainsuccession** from F to H ensures the customer only sees a notification **after** the contract has been formally authorized — i.e., after the bank is legally bound.  
* **altprecedence** between G and H acknowledges that firms may choose to notify before or after funding, reflecting differing customerservice policies while still keeping the notification tied to the final outcome.

### 2.6. Preventing FundsBeforeRequest Scenarios  
* **noncoexistence** between A and G is a simple causality guard: you cannot disburse money without an application.  This is a basic antifraud control (no “phantom loans”).  
* **altresponse** from G to H allows a postfunding notification, but the **chainresponse** from D to G guarantees that the offer package (and thus the approved terms) existed before any money moved.

### 2.7. Summary of the Flow Enforced by the Model  

A typical compliant trace (one of many possible because of altbranches) would look like:

```
A (Receive_Application)
B (Preliminary_Credit_Check)
C (Gather_Additional_Documents)
D (Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package)
E (Quality_Assurance_Review)   <-- may loop back to C if deficiencies found
D (Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package) <-- after rework, offer is refined
F (Authorize_Contract_Terms)
G (Transfer_Funds)
H (Notify_Customer)            <-- may occur before or after G per altprecedence
```

If QA passes on the first attempt, the ED loop is skipped and the flow goes straight from E to F.  If the applicant is lowrisk and the policy allows a “fasttrack”, the **altsuccession** from C to E can be bypassed, letting the case go straight from C to D (offer assembly) after a light review.

## 3. Business Goals & Regulatory Drivers Behind the Rules  

| Goal / Driver | How it maps onto specific DECLARE constraints |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| **Risk Management – prevent excessive credit exposure** | *exactly_one* on B (single, authoritative credit score); *succession* BC (ensure docs are gathered before committing); *precedence* EF (QA must approve before contract). |
| **Regulatory KYC/AML & Documentation Requirements** | *existence* on C (mandatory document collection); *coexistence* CF (documents must exist before contract); *noncoexistence* AG (no money without request). |
| **Consumer Protection – transparent, nonmisleading communication** | *nonsuccession* BH (no premature creditcheck feedback); *nonchainsuccession* FH (notify only after final authorization); *altprecedence* GH (flexibility but still tied to outcome). |
| **Operational Efficiency – avoid rework and duplication** | *exactly_one* B (single credit check); *altsuccession* EC (controlled rework loop only when needed); *chainprecedence* FD (offer assembled directly before authorization, reducing handoffs). |
| **Auditability & Traceability** | Every activity has either an **existence**, **init**, **absence**, or a response/precedence/succession link, making it easy to verify that each step happened in the right order or that a required step is present. |
| **Fraud Prevention – block “ghost” loans** | *absence* of Z (prohibits any compliancebypass activity); *noncoexistence* AG (funds cannot appear without a request). |
| **Policy Flexibility – allow fasttrack vs. fullreview paths** | *altsuccession* (EC) and *altresponse* (GH) give the model alternative branches, mirroring realworld policy options (e.g., straightthrough processing for lowrisk applicants). |
| **Alignment with Internal Governance (e.g., FourEyes Principle, Segregation of Duties)** | *precedence* EF (different roles: QA vs. authorization); *chainprecedence* FD (different roles: offer assembly vs. authorization). |

In essence, the model encodes a **controloriented loanorigination pipeline** where:

1. **Initiation** is strictly tied to a customer request.  
2. **Risk assessment** happens once, early, and is never repeated.  
3. **Evidence gathering** is mandatory and linked to every downstream commitment.  
4. **Quality assurance** acts as a mandatory gate that can trigger rework but never be bypassed.  
5. **Authorization and disbursement** are tightly sequenced after the offer is built and reviewed.  
6. **Customer communication** is constrained to avoid premature or misleading messages.  
7. **Any deviation that would sidestep compliance** is expressly forbidden.

These constraints collectively deliver a **transparent, auditable, and regulatorfriendly process** while still leaving room for businesslevel decisions (fasttrack vs. full review, pre vs. postfunding notification).
