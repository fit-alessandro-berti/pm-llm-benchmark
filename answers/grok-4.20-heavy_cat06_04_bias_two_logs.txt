The most glaring disparity is that a **695 preliminary score** in Group B (U003) is boosted to 705 and approved, while a **higher preliminary score of 710** in the Protected Group (P002) is rejected with no adjustment available. This shows the boost effectively lowers the approval threshold for favored applicants.

The attributes **LocalResident** and **CommunityGroup** act as triggers for preferential treatment in Group B only. This introduces a systematic advantage for unprotected-group members with specific community ties, even when their raw creditworthiness (preliminary score) is lower. In contrast, the protected group is evaluated strictly on the unadjusted preliminary score.

In fairness and bias-detection terms, this is an example of **disparate treatment** or **disparate impact** based on group membership. The community boost functions as a proxy that favors one demographic segment, leading to inconsistent and potentially discriminatory final decisions across the two groups. The event log for Group B clearly encodes this biased logic in the ScoreAdjustment and its downstream effect on the Decision column.
