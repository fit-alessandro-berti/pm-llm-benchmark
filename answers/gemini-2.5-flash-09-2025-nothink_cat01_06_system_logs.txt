## Explanation of Transformation Logic

### 1. Case Identification Logic

The primary heuristic used for defining a **Case ID** is the **logical unit of work defined by the main application and the specific document/context**. A case begins when the user intentionally focuses on a new, distinct work item (document, email thread, or budget sheet) and performs sustained interaction, and ends when the user switches away or closes the item after a save/send action.

Four distinct cases were identified:

1.  **Case\_Draft\_Document\_1:** Focuses on the creation and editing of `Document1.docx`. This case is interrupted by other tasks but resumes when the user returns to `Document1.docx` (at 09:06:00) to complete the drafting, linking the initial drafting and the subsequent data incorporation into one logical process.
2.  **Case\_Email\_Handling:** Focuses on interacting with the inbox to open, read, reply to, and send an email about the Annual Meeting.
3.  **Case\_Document\_Review:** Focuses on reviewing and annotating the `Report_Draft.pdf`.
4.  **Case\_Budget\_Update:** Focuses on opening, typing data into, and saving the `Budget_2024.xlsx`.
5.  **Case\_Quarterly\_Report:** Focuses on the final task involving the `Quarterly_Report.docx`.

### 2. Activity Naming Strategy

The raw actions (FOCUS, TYPING, CLICK, SAVE, SWITCH, SCROLL, HIGHLIGHT) are too granular for high-level process analysis. They were aggregated and standardized into meaningful process steps based on the application and the action's intent:

| Raw Action(s) | Context/Application | Derived Activity Name | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| FOCUS (followed by TYPING) | Document/Application | Start [Context] | Marks the initiation of work on that specific item. |
| TYPING (multiple events) | Document/Email | Draft [Content] / Update Figures and Structure | Grouped consecutive typing events into one cohesive step focused on content generation. |
| SAVE / CLOSE | Document/Application | Save Draft Progress / Final Save and Close | Represents a committed completion point or checkpoint. |
| CLICK, SCROLL | Browser (Email) | Read/Review Email Content / Send Email Reply | Aggregated navigational and reading actions. |
| HIGHLIGHT, SCROLL | PDF Reader | Annotate/Highlight Key Information | Specific review actions on a finalized document. |
| SWITCH (followed by TYPING) | Word (after Excel) | Incorporate Budget Data into Document | Used the context to infer the reason for returning to the document. |

This transformation provides an event log where process analysts can clearly trace the flow of the user's work session, identify resource utilization across different work items, and analyze throughput times for specific tasks like "Email Handling" or "Budget Update."
