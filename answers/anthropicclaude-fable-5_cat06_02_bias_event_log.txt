# Bias Analysis of the Loan Decision Event Log

## Where Bias Manifests

### 1. The Community Group Score Adjustment (+10)

The most explicit bias appears in the **PreliminaryScoring** step. Applicants affiliated with the "Highland Civic Darts Club" receive a **+10 score adjustment** purely for membership in a community group:

- **C001**: 710  720 (Adjusted)  **Approved**
- **C004**: 690  700 (Adjusted)  **Approved**

This adjustment has nothing to do with creditworthiness. Club membership is a proxy for social capital, local connections, and potentially demographic characteristics — not repayment ability.

### 2. The Critical Comparison: C002 vs. C003

This pair reveals the decision boundary problem most starkly:

| Case | Resident | Score | Adjustment | Decision |
|------|----------|-------|------------|----------|
| C002 | TRUE | 720 | 0 | **Approved** |
| C003 | FALSE | 715 | 0 | **Rejected** |

C003 was rejected with a score of 715 — yet **C004, a non-adjusted 690**, was approved after the community boost pushed them to 700. This means:

- A *less creditworthy* applicant (690 raw) was approved because of club membership.
- A *more creditworthy* applicant (715 raw) was rejected, apparently because they lacked both residency and affiliation.

If the +10 adjustment were available to C003, they would have reached 725 — well above the apparent ~720 approval threshold.

### 3. Geographic (Residency) Bias

The `LocalResident` attribute correlates suspiciously with outcomes:

- **C003** (non-resident, 715): Rejected
- **C005** (non-resident, 740): Approved — but only with a substantial score buffer

Non-residents appear to need a *higher* effective score to be approved. C002 (resident, 720) was approved while C003 (non-resident, 715) was rejected — a 5-point gap producing opposite outcomes suggests either a hard threshold near 720, or that residency itself influences the decision. Either way, non-residents who can't access local community groups face a structural disadvantage.

### 4. Compounding/Intersectional Effects

The bias mechanisms stack:
- Local residents **can** join local community groups  eligible for +10
- Non-residents realistically **cannot**  permanently locked out of the adjustment

So `LocalResident = FALSE` applicants face a double penalty: no geographic advantage *and* no practical access to the affiliation bonus. The "community adjustment" is effectively a residency bonus in disguise.

## Fairness and Equity Implications

1. **Disparate treatment**: Similarly situated applicants (C003 at 715 vs. C004 at 690 raw) receive opposite outcomes based on non-financial attributes.

2. **Proxy discrimination risk**: Community group membership and residency often correlate with race, age, income, religion, and national origin. This adjustment could constitute illegal redlining-adjacent behavior under fair lending laws (e.g., ECOA, Fair Housing Act).

3. **Opaque accountability**: The manual review step (Reviewers #2–#7) appears to rubber-stamp the adjusted scores — no reviewer flagged or reversed the community adjustment, so human oversight provides no bias correction.

4. **Self-reinforcing exclusion**: Newcomers, immigrants, renters, and socially unaffiliated individuals — groups already facing credit access barriers — are systematically scored lower despite identical underlying risk profiles.

## Recommendations

- **Remove** the community affiliation adjustment, or validate it rigorously against actual repayment outcomes.
- **Audit** the decision threshold to confirm residency is not directly or indirectly influencing the Rules Engine.
- **Test for disparate impact**: compare approval rates between resident/non-resident and affiliated/non-affiliated cohorts at matched score levels.
- **Empower manual reviewers** to flag non-financial adjustments rather than passively confirming them.

The core problem: **decisions diverge for applicants of similar creditworthiness based on who they know and where they live, not how likely they are to repay.**
