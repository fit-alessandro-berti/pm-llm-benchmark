Looking at the two logs side by side, **Group B (Unprotected Group) exhibits structural bias** introduced through the `CommunityGroup` and `ScoreAdjustment` attributes. Group A shows no adjustments tied to those attributes.

### How the bias manifests

**1. Differential score adjustment mechanism**
Group A never receives a `ScoreAdjustment`. All 3 cases have `ScoreAdjustment = 0` throughout.  
Group B receives `+10 (Community Boost)` during `PreliminaryScoring` if `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`. This changes the working score before `FinalDecision`.

So an application in Group B with a raw score 15 points lower than a Group A application still received `Approved` while the higher-scoring Group A application was `Rejected`. The `+10 Community Boost` moved U003 across the apparent decision threshold that sits between 705 and 710.

**3. Coupling of attributes**  
In Group B, `LocalResident = TRUE` for all cases, and `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club` for 2 of 3 cases. The `ScoreAdjustment` only applies when `CommunityGroup` is not `None`. In Group A, `LocalResident = FALSE` and `CommunityGroup = None` for all cases, so the adjustment rule never triggers.

This creates a compound effect: Group B applicants who are local residents AND members of the specified community group get a systematic +10. Group A applicants lack both attributes, so they never qualify.

### How these factors lead to systematic differences in final decisions

**Direct mechanism: Score inflation at decision time**  
The `Scoring Engine` injects `+10 (Community Boost)` before `ManualReview` and `FinalDecision`. Since approvals seem to correlate with score thresholds, the boost directly increases approval probability for Group B members who have the favored `CommunityGroup`. 

**Indirect mechanism: Attribute gating**  
The boost is gated by two attributes:  
1. `LocalResident`: All Group B = TRUE, all Group A = FALSE  
2. `CommunityGroup`: Only some Group B cases have the qualifying value

Because Group A is defined as `FALSE` for `LocalResident` and `None` for `CommunityGroup`, the rule as implemented excludes the entire protected group from the adjustment. If membership in Group A is correlated with those attributes, then the scoring rule produces disparate impact even if the rule itself only mentions `CommunityGroup`.

**Systematic outcome**  
Across just 3 cases per group:  
- Group A: 2 Approved, 1 Rejected. Min approved raw score = 720  
- Group B: 2 Approved, 1 Rejected. Min approved raw score = 695  

The effective approval threshold is 25 points lower for Group B members with the community boost. Over many cases, this would produce a higher approval rate for Group B at any given raw score distribution, specifically for the subset with `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`.

### Key point
The bias isn't in the `FinalDecision` logic itself. The `Rules Engine` appears to apply the same threshold to the adjusted score for both groups. The bias is introduced upstream in `PreliminaryScoring` where `ScoreAdjustment` is applied based on `CommunityGroup`. Because `CommunityGroup` and `LocalResident` partition the groups completely here, the protected group cannot access the boost.
