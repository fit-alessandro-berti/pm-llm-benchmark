## Anomaly Analysis

A typical **Procure-to-Pay (P2P)** flow is:

1. Create Purchase Requisition  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Issue Purchase Order (PO)  
4. Receive Goods / Services  
5. Vendor Invoice Received  
6. Match Invoice to PO (three-way match)  
7. Release Payment  
8. Close Case  

Below is the evaluation of each case against this standard sequence.

---

### Case 1001 — **Normal / Standard**

| Sequence | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice  Release Payment  Close | Follows the expected P2P order. |

No anomalies. This is the control-compliant reference case.

---

### Case 1002 — **Multiple Serious Anomalies**

Event sequence:

```
Create PR  Issue PO  Vendor Invoice Received  Release Payment  
Approve PR  Receive Goods  Match Invoice to PO  Close
```

| Anomaly | Why It Is Problematic |
|---|---|
| **PO issued before PR approval** | A purchase order should not be sent to a vendor until the requisition is approved. Issuing it early risks unauthorized or unnecessary spending. |
| **Invoice received before goods received** | Invoices should generally be tied to a verified receipt. Processing an invoice before receipt raises the risk of paying for undelivered or incorrect goods. |
| **Payment released before PR approval and before receiving goods** | Payment is made with no evidence that the purchase was authorized or that the goods/services were received. This bypasses key financial controls and increases fraud and duplicate-payment risk. |
| **PR approval occurs after payment** | Approval is meant to be a pre-control. If it happens after the money has already left the organization, it cannot prevent improper spending. |

**Overall risk:** Very high — this case reflects a near-total breakdown of segregation of duties and three-way matching.

---

### Case 1003 — **Payment Before Receipt and Before Invoice Matching**

Event sequence:

```
Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Release Payment  
Vendor Invoice Received  Receive Goods  Match Invoice  Close
```

| Anomaly | Why It Is Problematic |
|---|---|
| **Payment released before goods received** | The organization has paid for goods it has not yet verified as received. If the delivery is late, damaged, or incorrect, recovering funds becomes difficult. |
| **Payment released before invoice matching** | Standard best practice requires matching the PO, goods receipt, and vendor invoice **before** payment (three-way match). Paying first eliminates this critical control and can lead to overpayments or payments for invalid invoices. |
| **Invoice received after payment** | The invoice should normally trigger the matching and payment process, not follow it. |

**Note:** Prepayments can exist in some contracts, but they should be explicitly authorized and documented. In a standard P2P log, this sequence is anomalous and weakens financial controls.

---

### Case 1004 — **Goods Received Before PO Issued**

Event sequence:

```
Create PR  Approve PR  Receive Goods  Issue PO  
Vendor Invoice Received  Match Invoice  Release Payment  Close
```

| Anomaly | Why It Is Problematic |
|---|---|
| **Goods received before the PO is issued** | A purchase order is the formal authorization sent to the vendor. Receiving goods before the PO exists suggests the order was placed informally (“maverick buying”), bypassing procurement controls. |
| **Weakens the three-way match** | The PO, receipt, and invoice are supposed to support one another. If the PO is created after receipt, it may be back-dated or manipulated just to enable payment, reducing its control value. |
| **Pricing and terms uncertainty** | Without an approved PO in place before delivery, pricing, quantities, and terms may not have been formally agreed, exposing the company to vendor disputes or inflated invoices. |
