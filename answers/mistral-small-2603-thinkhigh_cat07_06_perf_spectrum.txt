The diagram is a Sankey diagram, which is a type of flow diagram illustrating the movement and transformation of data over time. It shows the flow of data from one state to another, with the width of the flow representing the quantity of data. The diagram is divided into three sections, each representing different time periods, with the first section on the left, the second in the middle, and the third on the right. Each section contains vertical lines representing different states or categories, and the flows between these lines represent the transitions of data from one state to another. Labels such as 'Send Fine,' 'Insert Fine Notification,' and 'Send for Credit Collection' indicate specific actions or states within the process. The flows are color-coded, with different colors representing different categories or types of data. The overall concept of the diagram is to visualize the dynamic changes and transitions of data over time, highlighting how data moves through various states and the volume of data involved in each transition.
