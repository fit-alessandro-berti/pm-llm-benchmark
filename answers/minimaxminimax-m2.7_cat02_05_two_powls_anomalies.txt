The **loop_onboarding** and **xor_payroll** operators embed *skip* (a silent transition).  
* In a **LOOP(body, exit)** the exit transition may be taken at any time, effectively making the body **optional**.  
* In an **XOR** the process chooses **exactly one** of its children – here either the real activity **or** the silent  (i.e. *skip*).

**Consequences**

| Operator | What it allows | Consequence for the HiretoRetire process |
|----------|----------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **LOOP(Onboard, skip)** | The body **Onboard** can be executed **zero or more times**, and the loop can be exited via *skip* without ever running Onboard. | **Onboarding may be completely omitted** – a critical, illegal situation. |
| **XOR(Payroll, skip)** | Either **Add_to_Payroll** or the silent  may be taken. | **Payroll entry may be omitted**, which violates legal and financial compliance. |
| **Missing ScreenInterview ordering** | Only **Post  Screen** and **Post  Interview** are defined; no edge between Screen and Interview. | Interview could occur **before** any screening, breaking the logical flow (candidates may be interviewed without even being screened). |
| **Missing ScreenDecide ordering** | After the Interview  Decide edge, there is no constraint that Screen must precede Decide. | A hiring decision could be made **without any screening**. |
| **No explicit Onboard  Payroll prerequisite** | Although the loop is placed before the xor_payroll, the xor allows Payroll **without** Onboard ever being executed. | Even if Onboard is taken, Payroll may still be skipped, or Payroll may happen while Onboard never happened. |
