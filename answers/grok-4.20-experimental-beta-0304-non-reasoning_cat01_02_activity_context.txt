**Justification of Renaming Decisions**

**1. "Initiate Request"**
- Renamed to **Initiate Online Request** or **Initiate Branch Request**.
- Context: The channel (WebPortal vs BranchReception) fundamentally changes the meaning and starting point of the process.

**2. "Verify Documents" (appears up to 2 times per case)**
- Split into:
  - **Initial Document Verification** (always the first occurrence)
  - **Final Document Verification** or **Post-Adjustment Document Verification** (second occurrence)
- Context: The second verification occurs *after* additional documents are submitted or after an account adjustment. It serves a different purpose (closure/verification-of-corrections vs initial screening).

**3. "Call Customer" (appears up to 2 times per case)**
- This was the most contextually overloaded activity. Renamed based on intent and position:
  - **Request Missing Documents** (C001) — purpose is to request more evidence.
  - **Clarify Account Discrepancy** (C002 first call) — focused on understanding an account issue.
  - **Confirm Adjustments with Customer** (C002 second call) — confirmation/closure call.
  - **Recall Customer to Branch** (C003) — logistical call to bring the customer back physically.

**4. "Adjust Account"**
- Renamed to **Perform Account Adjustment**.
- This name better reflects that it is an *internal correction activity* rather than a customer-facing one, and it consistently occurs after a call that reveals an issue.

**5. "Submit Additional"**
- Expanded to **Submit Additional Documents** for clarity and to make the customer action explicit.

**6. "Approve Request"**
- Left unchanged. This activity has consistent meaning across all cases (final supervisory sign-off).

---

These renames transform the event log from having ambiguous, repeated activity names into a much more semantically accurate log that clearly distinguishes between different process steps that happen to share the same generic label in the original system. This is crucial for accurate process mining, conformance checking, and process improvement analysis.
