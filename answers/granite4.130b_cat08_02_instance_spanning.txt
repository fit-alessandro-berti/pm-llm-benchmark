## 1. Identifying InstanceSpanning Constraints & Their Impact  

### **a. Leveraging Event Log & Process Mining**  

| Constraint | How to Identify (ProcessMining) | Quantification Metric |
|------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------|
| **Shared ColdPacking Stations** | • Build a *resourceaware* process model (e.g., using the *Resource Aware* extension of Petri nets or BPMN with resource lanes).<br>• Trace each “Packing” event to its `Requires Cold Packing` flag and assign it to one of the 5 coldpacking stations (`Station C1 … C5`). | • **ColdPack Queue Length** – number of orders waiting for a cold station at any timestamp.<br>• **Average Waiting Time (W\_cold)** – time from *Packing* start request  actual assignment.<br>• **Utilization %** per cold station. |
| **Shipping Batching** | • Use the “Shipping Label Generation” activity as a *batching gate*. Group all orders whose timestamps fall into the same batch identifier (e.g., `Batch B1`).<br>• Extract “waiting for batch completion” by comparing the earliest start of Packing/QC to the labelgeneration timestamp. | • **Batch Wait Time (W\_batch)** – difference between an order’s readyforshipping time and actual label generation.<br>• **Average Batch Size** and its variance.<br>• **Throughput per batch** (orders processed per unit time). |
| **Priority (Express) Handling** | • Tag events with `Order Type = Express`. Compute *preemption* instances: when an express order’s activity start overlaps a standard order using the same resource. <br>• Use deviation analysis to detect “priority interruptions”. | • **StandardtoExpress Delay (D\_stdexp)** – additional time added to a standard order because its resource was seized by an express order.<br>• **Express Completion Time** vs SLA target. |
| **Hazardous Material Limit** | • Filter events where `Hazardous Material = TRUE`. For each timestamp, count active Packing/QC instances across the whole plant. Flag any moment when >10 concurrent hazardous activities occur (violations). | • **OverLimit Incidents** – number of timestamps breaching the 10order cap.<br>• **Average Hazardous Queue Length** before a slot opens.<br>• **Throughput Reduction Factor** = (desired rate) / (actual rate under limit). |

### **b. Differentiating WithinInstance vs BetweenInstance Waiting**

1. **WithinInstance Factors**  
   * Measured by the *duration* of each activity (`COMPLETE` – `START`). High variance or long durations are intrinsic to that order’s complexity, not caused by other orders.  

2. **BetweenInstance (CrossOrder) Waiting**  
   * Identify when an activity’s start is **postponed** despite its predecessor being finished: e.g., “Packing” event timestamp lags behind the preceding “Item Picking” completion because a resource is occupied.  
   * Use *resource contention graphs*: for each resource, plot occupancy over time; any gap between demand and availability signals crossinstance waiting.  

3. **Implementation**  
   * In the log, compute `Packing_Start – Picking_Complete`. If >0, investigate resource assignment (cold vs standard).  
   * For batching, compare `Label_Gen_Start` with the earliest “ready” timestamp of all batch members; lag indicates interinstance waiting.

## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions  

### **a. Express + Cold Packing**  
*An express order that needs cold packing competes for a limited resource.*  
- When an express arrives, it may *preempt* a standard order already queued at a cold station  the standard order’s waiting time spikes (captured by `D_stdexp`).  
- Simultaneously, because cold stations are scarce, the overall **ColdPack Queue Length** grows faster during peak express demand, potentially causing more orders to be delayed for batching if they must wait until packing finishes.  

### **b. Batching + Hazardous Limit**  
*If several hazardous orders share a destination region, they may be batched together.*  
- The batch can only proceed once all members finish Packing/QC; however, the plant-wide cap of 10 concurrent hazardous activities could force some members to wait even after others are ready, inflating **Batch Wait Time (W_batch)**.  
- Conversely, a large batch with many nonhazardous orders may *dilute* the hazard count, allowing more hazardous items to be processed concurrently—illustrating how batching composition directly influences regulatory compliance pressure.

### **c. Why Interaction Awareness Matters**  

Understanding these crosseffects prevents **singleconstraint optimization** that inadvertently worsens another. For example:  
- Adding extra cold stations *only* reduces expressorder waiting but may increase overall hazardous queue if those new stations are also used for nonhazardous orders, pushing the plant closer to the 10order cap.  
- Dynamic batching rules must consider both **region affinity** and **hazard count**, otherwise a batch could stall due to regulatory blocking while other regions suffer from underutilized capacity.

## 3. Developing ConstraintAware Optimization Strategies  

### **Strategy1 – Adaptive ColdPack Resource Allocation (Dynamic Prioritization)**  

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Constraints Addressed** | Shared ColdPacking stations, Express priority, Hazardous limit (indirect) |
| **Proposed Changes** | • Implement a *realtime resource scheduler* that assigns cold stations based on a weighted score: <br>`Score = w1·PriorityWeight + w2·HazardPenalty – w3·QueueAge`. <br>• `PriorityWeight = 5` for Express, `=1` for Standard.<br>• `HazardPenalty = -2` if order is hazardous (to discourage crowding the limited slots with noncritical items).<br>• `w2` tuned via simulation to keep concurrent hazardous 10. <br>• When a cold station becomes free, pick the highestscoring pending order. |
| **Data Leveraged** | • Historical log of arrival rates (peak vs offpeak)  forecast demand for each hour.<br>• Realtime queue lengths and hazard counts fed into the scheduler. |
| **Expected Outcomes** | • Reduced `W_cold` especially for express orders while keeping hazardous overflow low.<br>• Balanced utilization across stations, lowering overall variance in waiting times. |

### **Strategy2 – Dynamic Batch Formation with HazardAware Trigger**  

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Constraints Addressed** | Shipping Batching delays, Hazardous Material limit |
| **Proposed Changes** | • Replace fixed “batch size = N” rule with a *triggerbased* approach: start a batch when either (a) a configurable time window expires **or** (b) the accumulated set reaches a *hazardadjusted capacity* (`H_adj = min(N, 10 – current_hazard_count)`). <br>• For regions with many hazardous orders, enforce a maximum of `H_adj` per batch to guarantee compliance. |
| **Data Leveraged** | • Log analysis of historical batch sizes and corresponding wait times.<br>• Realtime count of active hazardous Packing/QC instances (via the resource model). |
| **Expected Outcomes** | • Shorter `W_batch` because batches form sooner when capacity is available.<br>• Zero violations of the 10order hazard rule, preventing artificial stalls. |

### **Strategy3 – PriorityPreemptive Scheduling with Compensation Slots**  

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Constraints Addressed** | Express priority interruptions, overall throughput |
| **Proposed Changes** | • When an express order requests a shared resource (cold station or generic packing), *preempt* the currently executing standard order **only if** the standard’s remaining processing time  a threshold `T_preempt` (e.g., 2min). <br>• The displaced standard order is instantly requeued with a “priority compensation” token that grants it a *fasttrack slot* in the next available cycle, ensuring its total delay does not exceed a bounded value (`D_max`). |
| **Data Leveraged** | • Activity duration distributions from the log to set realistic `T_preempt` and `D_max`. <br>• Realtime queue monitoring to detect when compensation slots are idle (to reallocate). |
| **Expected Outcomes** | • Express orders meet SLA without causing unbounded delays for standards.<br>• Overall cycle time (`Picking  Shipping`) improves because the system avoids long, unnecessary holds of standard work while express waits. |

## 4. Simulation & Validation  

### **Simulation Framework**  

1. **DiscreteEvent Simulation (DES)** built on a *resourceaware* process model derived from the mined log (Petri net with resource places).  
2. **Input Parameters**:  
   - Arrival distributions for order types, regions, coldpack need, hazardous flag (derived via statistical fitting of historic data).  
   - Resource capacities: 5 cold stations, unlimited standard packing stations, 1 QC staff per shift, batch engine logic as above.  
   - Policy settings for each strategy (weights, thresholds, batch triggers).  

### **Key Simulation Focus Areas**  

| Aspect | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Resource Contention Graphs** | Verify that `W_cold`, queue lengths, and preemption counts align with analytical targets. |
| **Batch Formation Dynamics** | Track *time to batch start* (`T_batch_start`) and *batch completion time* under varying hazard loads; ensure no >10 concurrent hazardous activities. |
| **Priority Interruption Impact** | Measure `D_stdexp` before/after compensation slot policy; confirm express SLA compliance (<Xmin) while keeping standard delay  `D_max`. |
| **Regulatory Compliance Checks** | Continuous audit of active hazardous activity count; trigger alerts if >10. |
| **Throughput & Utilization** | Compare overall orders/day, resource utilization % (cold stations, QC staff) across baseline vs each strategy. |

### **Validation Process**  

- Run *MonteCarlo* experiments for each strategy under peak and offpeak scenarios.  
- Compute KPI confidence intervals: average endtoend time, 95th percentile waiting times, violation rates of hazard limit.  
- Perform *sensitivity analysis* on key parameters (e.g., `w1`, `T_preempt`) to identify robust settings.

## 5. Monitoring PostImplementation  

### **Dashboards & Metrics**  

| Dashboard | KPI | Calculation | Alert Threshold |
|-----------|-----|-------------|-----------------|
| **ColdPack Resource Health** | Avg`W_cold`, Queue Length, Utilization % | Realtime from event log (Packing start – request) | Queue > 3 orders *or* Utilization <70%  investigate bottleneck |
| **Batch Efficiency** | `W_batch`, Batch Size Distribution, Hazardous Count per Batch | Difference between earliest ready timestamp and label gen; count hazardous flags in batch | Batch wait >5min or hazard count breach  autoadjust trigger |
| **Priority Performance** | Express Completion Time, Standard Delay (`D_stdexp`) | Timestamp diff for express vs SLA; standard delay after preemption | Express >SLA (e.g., 2h) or Standard delay >`D_max`  flag |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Concurrent Hazardous Activities | Realtime count of active Packing/QC with `Hazardous = TRUE` | Exceeds 10  immediate halt of new hazardous admissions, trigger compensation |
| **Overall Throughput** | Orders/Day, Resource Utilization (overall) | Aggregated daily totals | Drop >10% vs baseline for >3 consecutive days  review capacity |

### **ProcessMining Continuous Mining Pipeline**  

1. **NearRealTime Log Ingestion** – Stream events into an ELK/Kafka pipeline.  
2. **Replay Against Updated Model** – Each new event updates the state of the resourceaware model, producing live performance indicators.  
3. **Anomaly Detection** – Use *conformance checking* (variant analysis) to spot deviations from expected behavior (e.g., unexpected long waits).  
4. **Feedback Loop** – If a KPI crosses its threshold, automatically adjust policy parameters (e.g., increase `w1` for coldpack priority) via a rule engine; log the change for auditability.

### **Ensuring Effective Constraint Management**  

- **Queue Lengths**: Monitor decline in average and 95thpercentile lengths for both cold stations and hazardous activity slots.  
- **Batch Formation Time**: Track reduction in `T_batch_start` after dynamic trigger adoption.  
- **Compliance Audits**: Zero incidents of >10 concurrent hazardous activities confirm regulatory adherence while throughput rises.

### **Conclusion**  

By systematically *identifying* each instancespanning constraint through resourceaware process mining, quantifying its impact with targeted waitingtime metrics, and then *modeling* their interactions, we can devise **constraintaware optimization strategies** that respect the interdependencies of shared resources, batching logic, priority handling, and regulatory limits. Simulation validates these interventions under realistic load patterns, while a robust set of dashboards and continuous mining ensures ongoing compliance and performance improvement once changes are live. This integrated approach positions the fulfillment center to meet delivery deadlines reliably, even during peak seasons.
