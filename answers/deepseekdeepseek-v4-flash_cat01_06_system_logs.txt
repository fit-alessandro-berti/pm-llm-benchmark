## Explanation of Transformation Logic

### Case Identification
Each **case** represents a distinct logical unit of work that the user performed:
- **Quarterly_Report** – a Word document (started, edited, saved, closed).
- **Document1** – another Word document (opened, edited, saved, closed after a second edit session).
- **Email_AnnualMeeting** – handling a specific email (opening, reading, replying, sending). Although all email actions occurred in the same Chrome window (“Email – Inbox”), the sequence of actions (open email  reply  send) suggests a coherent task centered on that single email thread.
- **Report_Draft** – a PDF document that was read and annotated.
- **Budget_2024** – an Excel spreadsheet that was edited and saved.

Events were assigned to a case based on the **window** attribute (for FOCUS, TYPING, SAVE, CLICK, SCROLL, HIGHLIGHT, CLOSE) or the **target window** of a SWITCH event. This ensures that every event belongs to the document or task with which it is directly associated.

### Activity Naming
Raw actions were mapped to standardized, analystfriendly activity names:
- **FOCUS** and **SWITCH**  `Activate Document` (captures the moment a window becomes the focus of work).
- **TYPING**  `Edit Document` (general text editing; for the email reply it becomes `Edit Reply` to preserve subtask distinction).
- **SAVE**  `Save Document` (or `Save Spreadsheet` for Excel).
- **CLICK**  domainspecific names: `Open Email`, `Reply to Email`, `Send Email`.
- **SCROLL**  `Read Document` (or `Read Email`) – indicates passive consumption.
- **HIGHLIGHT**  `Highlight Text` (annotation).
- **CLOSE**  `Close Document`.

This naming scheme transforms lowlevel system events into meaningful process steps that can be directly used for process discovery, conformance checking, and performance analysis.

### Temporal Order
All events are listed in chronological order as they occurred in the log. No events were removed; SWITCH actions were retained as `Activate Document` events to preserve the context switches. The resulting event log tells a coherent story of a user moving between multiple tasks, returning to earlier documents, and eventually closing them.
