### 3. Insights and Recommendations

The data indicates that the process is efficient for standard tickets (Level-1 only) but breaks down during handovers and end-of-day procedures.

#### Insight 1: The Level-2 Queue is a Bottleneck
The most severe delay (Case 105) occurred while waiting for a Level-2 agent to acknowledge an escalated ticket. The 28-hour wait suggests Level-2 capacity is insufficient or the notification system for escalations is ineffective.
*   **Recommendation:** Implement a stricter Service Level Agreement (SLA) for Level-2 escalations (e.g., "First response within 4 hours"). Consider implementing an alert system that notifies Level-2 team leads if a ticket sits in the "Escalated" status for more than 2 hours.

#### Insight 2: "Park and Close" Behavior
The consistent 19-hour gap between afternoon investigation and morning resolution suggests agents are "parking" tickets. They may solve the issue late in the day but wait to document the resolution until the next morning, or they lack the authority to close complex tickets without a supervisor's morning review.
*   **Recommendation:** Encourage "Same-Day Close" policies. If an investigation is completed before a specific cutoff time (e.g., 15:00), the agent should be required to resolve and close the ticket immediately rather than carrying it over to the next day.

#### Insight 3: Inconsistent Level-1 Workload
Case 104 shows that even without escalation, tickets can stall if Level-1 agents do not begin work immediately after assignment.
*   **Recommendation:** Monitor the "Assign to Investigate" metric for individual agents. If specific agents consistently show high lag times (like the 3.5 hours in Case 104 vs. 15 mins in Case 103), provide additional training or adjust workload distribution to ensure tickets are picked up promptly.

#### Summary of Action Plan
1.  **Reduce Escalation Wait:** Audit Level-2 staffing levels and implement escalation alerts.
2.  **Eliminate Overnight Gaps:** Incentivize closing tickets on the same day investigation is completed.
3.  **Standardize Start Times:** Enforce a target time (e.g., <30 mins) for agents to begin investigation after ticket assignment.
