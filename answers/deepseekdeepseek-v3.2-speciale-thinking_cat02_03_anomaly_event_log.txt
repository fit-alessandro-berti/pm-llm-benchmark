**Analysis of Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log**

The standard Procure-to-Pay process follows a well-defined sequence to ensure proper authorization, receipt verification, and financial control. The typical flow is:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition** – request for goods/services.
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition** – authorization of the request.
3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)** – formal order to the supplier.
4. **Receive Goods** – physical or service delivery.
5. **Vendor Invoice Received** – supplier’s invoice.
6. **Match Invoice to PO** (often also to goods receipt) – validation of quantities, prices, and receipt.
7. **Release Payment** – after all checks are satisfied.
8. **Close Case** – completion.

Deviations from this order can indicate control weaknesses, increased risk of fraud, duplicate payments, or inefficiencies. The event log shows several cases that depart from this expected flow.
