# Process Mining Consultation: Speedy Parcels Optimization Plan

**To:** Management Team, Speedy Parcels
**From:** Process Mining Consultant
**Subject:** Strategic Analysis and Optimization of Last-Mile Delivery Operations

## 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

To transform raw data into actionable process insights, we must first construct a unified event log. This is the foundation of all subsequent analysis.

### Data Preprocessing and Integration
The raw data comes from heterogeneous sources with different frequencies and granularities. We will integrate them into a standard **XES (eXtensible Event Stream)** format or a SQL-based event log structure suitable for tools like Celonis, UiPath Process Mining, or Microsoft Process Mining.

*   **Case Definition:** The primary `Case ID` will be `Vehicle-Day` (e.g., `V12-20241205`). This represents one complete instance of the "Daily Delivery Route" process.
*   **Event Aggregation:**
    *   **GPS Data:** High-frequency GPS points (e.g., every 30 seconds) create "noise." We will aggregate these into **Travel Segments** (e.g., `Depart Stop A`, `Arrive Stop B`) using geofencing around customer locations and the depot.
    *   **Scanner Data:** These are high-value discrete events (`Arrive Customer`, `Delivery Success`). They will serve as the anchors for the process flow.
    *   **Maintenance/Dispatch:** These will be added as attributes or specific event types linked to the `Vehicle-Day` case.
*   **Challenges:**
    *   **Clock Synchronization:** GPS and Scanner clocks may drift. We will normalize timestamps to a central server time.
    *   **Spatial Alignment:** GPS coordinates may not perfectly match customer addresses. We will apply a geofencing buffer (e.g., 50 meters) to associate a GPS stop with a specific `Package ID` or `Stop Number`.
    *   **Missing Data:** If a scanner battery dies, we must infer "Delivery Success" based on GPS dwell time at a customer location combined with package weight data (if available) to maintain log integrity.

### Process Discovery
We will apply **Inductive Miner** or **Heuristic Miner** algorithms to the integrated log.
*   **Visualization:** The output will be a **Petri Net** or **Process Map**. This visualizes the *actual* flow: `Start Shift`  `Depart Depot`  `[Travel/Stop Cycle]`  `Return Depot`  `End Shift`.
*   **Variants:** The discovery will highlight the "Happy Path" (Planned Sequence) versus "Exception Paths." For example, a frequent loop where a vehicle returns to the depot mid-shift (refueling or maintenance) or a sequence where `Delivery Failed` triggers a `Return to Depot` for package re-assignment.

### Conformance Checking
We will compare the **Discovered Model** (Actuals) against the **Dispatch Model** (Planned Routes).
*   **Sequence Deviations:** Did the driver visit Stop 5 before Stop 4? This indicates route optimization failure or driver discretion.
*   **Unplanned Activities:** Identification of stops not in the dispatch plan (e.g., `Unscheduled Stop` due to engine warning, or `Fuel Stop`).
*   **Timing Deviations:** Comparing `Planned Arrival Time` (from Dispatch) vs. `Actual Arrival Time` (from GPS/Scanner).
*   **Skip/Revisit Analysis:** Detecting if a planned stop was skipped (requiring a second trip) or revisited unnecessarily.

## 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

Once the process flow is mapped, we quantify performance using specific Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) derived directly from the event log attributes.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
| KPI | Definition & Calculation | Source Data |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate** | % of `Delivery Success` events occurring within the `Customer Time Window`. | Scanner, Dispatch |
| **Stop Efficiency** | Average duration from `Arrive Customer` to `Depart Customer`. | Scanner |
| **Travel vs. Service Ratio** | Total GPS moving time vs. Total Scanner dwell time. Ideal ratio indicates balanced routes. | GPS, Scanner |
| **First Attempt Success Rate** | % of packages delivered without a `Delivery Failed` event. | Scanner |
| **Vehicle Utilization** | % of `Shift Duration` where `Vehicle Status` is `Moving` or `Active Service`. | GPS, Driver Logs |
| **Cost per Km** | Estimated fuel usage (based on GPS distance/speed) + Maintenance costs / Total Distance. | GPS, Maintenance, Fuel Logs |

### Bottleneck Identification Techniques
We will use **Process Mining Bottleneck Analysis** to pinpoint where value is lost.
*   **Cycle Time Analysis:** We will calculate the distribution of time spent per activity. A high standard deviation in `Arrive Customer` to `Depart Customer` suggests inconsistent service times (e.g., parking issues vs. quick drop-offs).
*   **Heatmap Visualization:** Overlaying delay data on a map. If specific geographic zones show consistently high `Travel Time` or `Unscheduled Stops`, these are spatial bottlenecks (e.g., traffic congestion zones or difficult access areas).
*   **Resource Analysis:** Filtering logs by `Driver ID` or `Vehicle ID`.
    *   *Example:* If Driver D105 consistently has a higher `Travel Time` than Driver D106 on the same route, the bottleneck is driver-specific (navigation skills or speed).
    *   *Example:* If Vehicle V12 has frequent `Unscheduled Stops` for maintenance, the bottleneck is asset reliability.
*   **Queue Analysis:** Identifying where vehicles wait. In logistics, this often manifests as long dwell times at the Depot (`Start Shift` to `Depart Depot`) or long dwell times at specific customer clusters.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

Identifying *where* the delay happens is insufficient; we must determine *why*. We will use **Decision Mining** and **Variant Analysis**.

### Potential Root Causes & Validation Methods
1.  **Suboptimal Route Planning (Static vs. Dynamic):**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Dispatch plans routes based on historical averages, ignoring real-time traffic.
    *   *Validation:* Correlate `Travel Time` variance with time-of-day. If morning routes consistently underperform compared to afternoon routes due to congestion, the planning algorithm is static.
    *   *Analysis:* Compare `Planned Distance` vs. `Actual GPS Distance`. Significant variance suggests drivers are deviating to avoid traffic, implying the original route was poor.

2.  **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* The system assumes 5 mins between stops, but reality is 15 mins.
    *   *Validation:* **Deviation Analysis**. Calculate the delta between `Dispatch Estimated Time` and `Actual Travel Time` for every segment.
    *   *Impact:* This causes cascading delays; a 5-minute delay at Stop 1 becomes a 10-minute delay at Stop 5.

3.  **Traffic Congestion Patterns:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Specific roads cause predictable delays.
    *   *Validation:* Cluster `Low Speed Detected` events by `Location` and `Time`. If a specific highway segment consistently drops below 10 km/h between 08:00–09:00, this is a known bottleneck.

4.  **High Variability in Service Time:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Some deliveries take longer due to parking or customer interaction.
    *   *Validation:* Analyze `Arrive Customer` to `Depart Customer` duration by `Package Type` or `Customer Location Type` (e.g., Residential vs. Commercial).
    *   *Insight:* If Commercial stops take 2x longer, they should be weighted differently in route optimization.

5.  **Failed Delivery Attempts:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Deliveries fail because customers are not home during the assigned window.
    *   *Validation:* Correlate `Delivery Failed` events with `Time of Day`. If failures peak at 10:00 AM, customers are likely at work.
    *   *Impact:* This creates a "Re-delivery Loop," doubling fuel and labor costs for that package.

6.  **Vehicle Breakdowns:**
    *   *Hypothesis:* Maintenance is reactive, not proactive.
    *   *Validation:* Link `Unscheduled Stop` (Engine Warning) with `Vehicle Mileage` and `Age`. If breakdowns correlate with specific mileage thresholds, maintenance is overdue.

## 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

Based on the analysis, I propose three concrete strategies to address the core issues of punctuality and cost.

### Strategy 1: Dynamic Route Adjustment & Traffic-Aware Scheduling
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Late deliveries due to traffic congestion and static route planning.
*   **Root Cause:** Dispatch system uses static travel times that do not account for real-time or historical traffic patterns.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Use the **Travel Time Variance** analysis to build a "Traffic Heatmap." Identify routes where actual travel time consistently exceeds planned time by >20%.
*   **Implementation:** Integrate the discovered traffic patterns into the routing algorithm. Re-sequence stops dynamically during the day based on real-time GPS data.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **OTD Rate:** Increase by 15-20%.
    *   **Fuel Consumption:** Decrease by reducing idling in traffic (smoother flow).

### Strategy 2: Predictive Maintenance & Asset Health Monitoring
*   **Target Inefficiency:** Unscheduled stops and high repair costs.
*   **Root Cause:** Reactive maintenance schedule; vehicles are driven until they fail.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Analyze the sequence of `Engine Warning` events leading to `Unscheduled Stop`. Identify precursor patterns (e.g., specific RPM ranges, specific routes, or mileage thresholds) that precede breakdowns.
*   **Implementation:** Move from time-based maintenance to **Usage-Based Maintenance**. Schedule service during low-volume periods *before* the predicted failure point based on the log data.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **Maintenance Cost:** Reduce emergency repair premiums by 30%.
    *   **Vehicle Utilization:** Increase by reducing downtime during peak delivery hours.

### Strategy 3: "Smart Window" Customer Communication
*   **Target Inefficiency:** High rate of `Delivery Failed` and re-delivery costs.
*   **Root Cause:** Mismatch between assigned delivery time windows and customer availability.
*   **Process Mining Support:** Analyze `Delivery Failed` events by `Time Window`. If data shows 40% of residential failures occur between 08:00–12:00, we know customers are away.
*   **Implementation:** Implement a **Customer Preference System**. Use the data to suggest time windows to customers that align with historical success rates. If a driver is running late (detected via GPS), trigger an automated SMS to the customer to "Expect 30 min delay" or "Confirm availability," preventing a failed attempt.
*   **Expected Impact:**
    *   **First Attempt Success Rate:** Increase by 25%.
    *   **Cost per Package:** Decrease significantly by eliminating re-delivery loops.

## 5. Considering Operational Constraints and Monitoring

Optimization cannot occur in a vacuum; it must respect the physical and legal constraints of the operation.

### Operational Constraints
*   **Driver Working Hours:** Any route re-optimization must comply with labor laws (e.g., maximum driving hours). The process mining model must include a constraint check to ensure `End Shift` events do not violate legal limits.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** Dynamic routing must not exceed `Vehicle Capacity` (weight/volume). We will use the `Package ID` weight data from the Dispatch system to validate this constraint in real-time.
*   **Customer Time Windows:** While we aim to optimize, we cannot violate hard promises. The optimization engine will prioritize "Hard Windows" over "Soft Windows" when re-sequencing stops.

### Continuous Monitoring Plan
To ensure sustainability, we will deploy a **Process Mining Dashboard (Control Tower)**.

*   **Key Metrics to Track:**
    *   **Real-Time Deviation:** % of active routes currently behind schedule.
    *   **Bottleneck Alerts:** Immediate notification if a specific zone or vehicle hits a threshold of delays.
    *   **KPI Trends:** Weekly trends of OTD, Fuel Cost, and Failed Delivery Rate.
*   **Process Views:**
    *   **Variant Monitor:** Track if new process variants (unexpected behaviors) emerge after changes are made.
    *   **Compliance View:** Ensure drivers are adhering to the new optimized routes and not reverting to old habits.
*   **Feedback Loop:**
    *   The dashboard will feed data back into the Dispatch System weekly.
    *   **Continuous Improvement:** Every month, we will re-run the **Process Discovery** algorithm. If the "Actual Process" diverges from the "Optimized Process," we investigate why (e.g., new traffic laws, road closures) and update the routing logic accordingly.

By implementing this structured Process Mining approach, Speedy Parcels will move from reactive management to proactive, data-driven optimization, directly addressing the pressures of punctuality and cost control.
