# 20 Open-Ended Questions on the Multinational Smartphone Supply Chain Process

### Rationale Behind Process Flows

1. What strategic considerations likely drove the decision to source components from geographically dispersed regions (Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, Germany, and the USA) rather than consolidating suppliers in one region, and what trade-offs does this introduce?

2. Why might the process designers have chosen Malaysia as the single assembly hub, and under what circumstances would it make sense to decentralize assembly across multiple countries?

3. The process uses a parallel gateway for component sourcing but a sequential flow from assembly to packing. What factors determine whether a step should run in parallel or sequentially, and are there additional steps in this process that could be parallelized?

### Potential Improvements

4. How could the integration of digital twins or AI-driven simulation tools transform the "R&D – Design Smartphone Prototype" phase, and what downstream effects would this have on component sourcing and assembly?

5. Where in the current process do you see the greatest opportunities to embed sustainability practices (e.g., circular economy principles, carbon tracking, ethical sourcing), and how would these change the flow?

6. How might adopting a "postponement strategy" — delaying final configuration until closer to the customer — reshape the assembly, packing, and distribution stages?

7. The Quality Check step only loops back to Assembly upon failure. What additional feedback loops (e.g., back to Design, Suppliers, or Market Research) could strengthen continuous improvement?

### Risk Management

8. Given that components come from politically and economically diverse regions, how should the organization structure contingency plans for geopolitical disruptions, tariffs, or export restrictions?

9. What risks emerge from relying on a single 24/7 assembly plant in Malaysia, and how could the process be redesigned to build resilience without dramatically increasing cost?

10. How should the company balance the speed advantage of air freight with the cost efficiency of ocean shipping when sudden demand spikes or supply shocks occur?

11. What cybersecurity and data integrity risks exist across this global supply chain (from supplier systems to distribution centers), and where should controls be embedded in the BPMN flow?

### Decision-Making

12. The final Exclusive Gateway chooses between "Adjust Orders to Suppliers" and "Proceed to Next Sales Cycle." What quantitative and qualitative signals should trigger each decision, and who owns that decision?

13. How should trade-offs between inventory holding costs at regional distribution centers and service-level commitments to retailers, e-commerce customers, and VIP clients be governed?

14. What criteria should determine whether a defective unit at Quality Check is reworked versus scrapped, and how should this decision reflect environmental and economic considerations?

### Stakeholder Communication

15. How should information flow between suppliers, the Malaysian assembly plant, freight carriers, customs authorities, and distribution centers to ensure end-to-end visibility — and what role could a control tower play?

16. What communication protocols should exist between Marketing/Sales teams (who inform the Market Research step) and Operations (who execute assembly and distribution) to ensure demand signals are acted upon quickly?

17. How should the organization transparently communicate supply chain disruptions (e.g., component shortages, shipping delays) to retailers, pre-order customers, and investors without damaging trust?

### Performance Measurement

18. What KPIs would best capture the efficiency and effectiveness of each major stage (sourcing, assembly, quality, distribution, post-sales), and how should they be balanced so that optimizing one doesn't harm another?

19. How could the company measure the "agility" of this supply chain — its ability to respond to demand surges, product recalls, or new market entries — beyond traditional lead-time and cost metrics?

20. In what ways could customer-centric performance indicators (e.g., perfect-order rate, time-to-customer, NPS for delivery experience) be integrated upstream into supplier and assembly performance evaluations to align the entire chain with end-user outcomes?
