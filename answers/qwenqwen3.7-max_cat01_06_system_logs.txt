### Explanation and Logic

#### 1. Case Identification Logic
In knowledge work process mining, a "case" is best defined by the primary digital artifact or logical task the user is processing, rather than a strict chronological session. Therefore, I grouped the events by the specific file or email thread being interacted with:
*   **DOC_QuarterlyReport:** Interactions with `Quarterly_Report.docx`.
*   **DOC_Document1:** Interactions with `Document1.docx`.
*   **EMAIL_AnnualMeeting:** Interactions regarding the Annual Meeting email in the inbox.
*   **PDF_ReportDraft:** Interactions with `Report_Draft.pdf`.
*   **XLS_Budget2024:** Interactions with `Budget_2024.xlsx`.

*Note on Context Switching:* Raw `SWITCH` (from) events were omitted as standalone events to prevent log clutter. Instead, the arrival at a new artifact (`SWITCH` to / `FOCUS`) triggers a `Start Task` or `Resume Task` event for the respective case. This allows process mining tools to accurately calculate active processing time and context-switching overhead without creating artificial "idle" events.

#### 2. Activity Naming Logic
Raw system verbs (e.g., `TYPING`, `CLICK`, `FOCUS`) are too low-level for business process analysis. I translated them into standardized, higher-level activity names based on the application context:
*   **Task Lifecycle:** `FOCUS` and `SWITCH` (to an app) were mapped to **Start Task** (for the first interaction with an artifact) or **Resume Task** (when returning to an already opened artifact). `CLOSE` was standardized to **Close Task**.
*   **Content Creation:** `TYPING` was contextualized. In Word, it became **Draft Content**; in Excel, it became **Update Data**; in the Email client, it became **Draft Email Reply**.
*   **Communication:** Email-specific clicks and scrolls were mapped to a logical communication flow: **Read Email**, **Start Email Reply**, and **Send Email**.
*   **Review & Annotation:** In the PDF viewer, `SCROLL` was elevated to **Read Document**, and `HIGHLIGHT` was mapped to **Annotate Document**.
*   **Persistence:** `SAVE` was universally standardized to **Save File**.

#### 3. Coherent Narrative of the Work Session
