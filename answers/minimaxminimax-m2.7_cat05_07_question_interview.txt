Below are a series of openended, probing questions you can use to deepen your understanding of the endtoend propertyonboarding process.  They are grouped by the major phases of the workflow, but feel free to jump across groups as the conversation evolves.

---

## 1. Documentation & Verification  
1. **What criteria do you use to decide whether a document (e.g., a certificate of compliance) is “required” versus “optional” for a particular property type?**  
2. **When a landlord supplies an outdated or incomplete set of papers, how is the request for clarification communicated, and what deadline (if any) is set for the landlord to respond?**  
3. **Are there any regional or cityspecific regulatory documents that must be captured beyond the standard set you described?**  
4. **How does the property coordinator track the status of each document—manual checklist, CRM flags, or something else—and how is the landlord notified of any gaps?**  

---

## 2. Data Capture & System Integration  
5. **Which fields are mandatory when entering a new property into the central database, and which are optional?**  
6. **How do you handle “approximate” data (e.g., square footage that the owner only knows roughly) – do you store both the estimate and the source?**  
7. **When the property details are entered, is there an immediate validation step that checks for duplicates or missing required fields? If so, what happens if validation fails?**  

---

## 3. PropertyManager Assignment  
8. **Can you walk me through the decision matrix you use to assign a manager? How do you weight factors such as current workload, geographic expertise, propertytype experience, and past landlordrelationship performance?**  
9. **Is there a “secondary” manager who can step in if the primary manager becomes overloaded, and how is that backup identified?**  
10. **How do you handle assignments when a property straddles two geographic zones that are each managed by different specialists?**  

---

## 4. Introductory Meeting & Expectation Alignment  
11. **What is the typical agenda for the introductory call/meeting between the manager and the landlord, and who is responsible for setting it up?**  
12. **How do you capture the outcomes of that meeting (e.g., special policies on pets, maintenance responsibilities) so that they are accessible to later teams?**  
13. **If the landlord’s expectations conflict with the company’s standard policies, what escalation path is followed?**  

---

## 5. Scheduling & Execution of Inspection & Photography  
14. **Who initiates the scheduling of the photographer and inspector, and how are their availabilities coordinated with the landlord’s availability?**  
15. **Are there preferred vendors for photography and inspection, or do you rotate providers based on location?**  
16. **What is the expected turnaround time for receiving the final photos and the inspection report, and what happens if they are delayed?**  

---

## 6. Repair & Issue Resolution After Inspection  
17. **When an inspector flags deficiencies, how does the property manager decide whether to (a) request the landlord to fix them, (b) hire a contractor on behalf of the landlord, or (c) postpone activation until issues are resolved?**  
18. **What approval workflow is used for authorizing repair expenditures, and who signs off before work begins?**  
19. **If the landlord disputes the inspector’s findings, how is that dispute mediated?**  

---

## 7. Marketing Creation & Pricing  
20. **How does the digital marketing team determine the “marketbased” pricing model you mentioned—do they pull comparable listings, use algorithmic tools, or rely on the regional manager’s input?**  
21. **Who has the final say on the advertised price and any promotional discounts (e.g., “first month free”)—the marketing team, the property manager, or the regional director?**  
22. **How quickly can a property move from “inspection passed” to “live” in the rental portal, and what steps are required before activation?**

## 8. Tenant Screening & Selection  
23. **What are the core elements of your tenantscreening checklist, and which of these are mandatory versus optional (e.g., credit score thresholds, employment verification, reference calls)?**  
24. **When multiple qualified applicants are interested, what decision framework do you use to select the “best” tenant—are there tiebreaker criteria?**  
25. **How do you handle applicants who have nonstandard circumstances (e.g., selfemployment, international tenants) – is there a separate review process?**  

---

## 9. Lease Finalization & MoveIn  
26. **Who drafts the lease agreement, and how are custom clauses requested by the landlord incorporated while still complying with company standards?**  
27. **What is the typical timeline from lease signing to movein date, and what contingencies are in place if the tenant needs to delay?**  
28. **How is the security deposit collected and recorded, and who is responsible for returning it at the end of the tenancy?**  

---

## 10. Quality Assurance, Compliance & Internal Audits  
29. **At what points in the onboarding workflow does the compliance officer review the paperwork, and what specific regulations are being verified (fairhousing, local rentcontrol, etc.)?**  
30. **What does the financial analyst check when validating the rental price—profit margin, market comparables, internal pricing guidelines?**  
31. **How does the IT department verify that the listing data in the portal matches the source database, and what happens if discrepancies are found?**

37. **What role does the internal CRM play in moving a property from one stage to the next—does it trigger automatic alerts or require manual updates?**

40. **When a step exceeds the expected timeline, what corrective actions are taken, and who is responsible for initiating them?**

### Tips for Conducting the Interview
- **Listen for gaps:** If the interviewee mentions a step but not the owner of that step, ask “Who is responsible for X?”  
- **Probe for exceptions:** When a process seems linear, ask “What happens if …?” to uncover alternative paths.  
- **Capture decision criteria:** Whenever a choice is made (e.g., manager assignment, price setting), ask how that choice is justified and whether it’s documented.  
- **Note the tools:** Even though you’re not asking for technical specs, knowing which CRM, projectmanagement, or communication platforms are used can clarify workflow integration.  

These questions should give you a comprehensive, nuanced picture of the propertyonboarding process, its decision points, its handoffs, and its handling of edge cases. Happy interviewing!
