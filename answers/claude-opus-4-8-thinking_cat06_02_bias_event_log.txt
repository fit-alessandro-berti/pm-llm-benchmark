# Bias Analysis of the Credit Decision Process

## Where Bias Manifests

The core bias is the **"+10 (Community)" ScoreAdjustment** applied during PreliminaryScoring, which is awarded exclusively to applicants affiliated with the "Highland Civic Darts Club." This adjustment directly alters outcomes.

### The Critical Evidence: C004

This case is the smoking gun. Compare it to the threshold logic implied by the data:

| Case | Local | Community | Preliminary | Adjusted | Decision |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| C001 | TRUE | Darts Club | 710 | **720** | Approved |
| C004 | TRUE | Darts Club | 690 | **700** | Approved |
| C003 | FALSE | None | 715 | 715 | **Rejected** |

- **C003 scored 715 on its own merit and was rejected.**
- **C004 scored only 690 — 25 points lower — but was approved**, because the +10 community bonus pushed it across the line.

An applicant with genuinely *higher* creditworthiness was denied while a *lower-scoring* applicant was approved, purely due to group membership.

## How the Bias Operates

**1. Proxy for an arbitrary, non-creditworthy attribute**
Membership in a recreational darts club has no logical relationship to credit risk. It functions as an arbitrary in-group marker. This is a textbook example of a feature that may serve as a **proxy** for protected or correlated characteristics (geography, social network, ethnicity, class).

**2. Compounding with geographic status**
Notice that *every* community-affiliated applicant is also `LocalResident = TRUE`, and the club is geographically named ("Highland"). The community bonus likely correlates tightly with local residency, layering a **geographic preference** on top of the affiliation preference. Non-locals (C003, C005) have no pathway to this advantage.

**3. The Manual Review is non-corrective**
Human reviewers (Underwriters) carry the *already-adjusted* score forward. The manual review does not catch or reverse the boost — it launders and ratifies it, giving the adjustment a veneer of human legitimacy.

## Fairness and Equity Implications

- **Disparate treatment:** Similarly creditworthy individuals receive different outcomes based on group membership rather than risk. C003 vs. C004 demonstrates outcome reversal at near-identical or even unfavorable score differentials.

- **Exclusionary by design:** People who lack the affiliation — newcomers, non-locals, those outside the social circle, or anyone who simply doesn't play darts — face a systematically higher effective threshold. They must score ~10+ points higher to achieve the same result.

- **Self-reinforcing inequity:** Community/geographic affiliations often track with existing socioeconomic and demographic advantages. Rewarding them tends to entrench the position of already-favored groups and disadvantage outsiders, potentially implicating fair-lending principles (e.g., disparate impact).

- **Opacity:** The adjustment is embedded in the scoring engine and propagated silently. An applicant would not know that an unrelated social factor determined their fate.

## Recommendations

1. **Remove the community-membership adjustment** unless a validated, causal link to creditworthiness can be demonstrated (it almost certainly cannot for a darts club).
2. **Audit for proxy effects** — test whether `CommunityGroup` and `LocalResident` correlate with protected classes.
3. **Apply a consistent threshold** to unadjusted, merit-based scores.
4. **Run counterfactual tests** (like the C003/C004 comparison) routinely to detect outcome reversals.
5. **Document and justify any adjustment** with statistical evidence and disclose it to applicants.

**Bottom line:** The process grants an unearned advantage to one socially/geographically defined in-group, demonstrably overriding actual creditworthiness — the C004 approval over the higher-scoring C003 rejection is the clearest proof of inequitable outcomes.
