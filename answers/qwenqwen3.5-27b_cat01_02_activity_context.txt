### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

**1. Verify Documents $\rightarrow$ `Verify Documents (Initial)` & `Verify Documents (Re-Check)`**
*   **Clues:** In C001 and C003, this activity occurs twice. The first instance always follows `Initiate Request` immediately. The second instance occurs after a trigger event (`Submit Additional` in C001, `Adjust Account` in C003).
*   **Reasoning:** The first instance represents the standard intake control. The second represents a remediation step. Distinguishing these is crucial for process mining to understand that the second verification is an exception path (loop) rather than a standard parallel task.

**2. Call Customer $\rightarrow$ `Call Customer (Request Info)`, `Call Customer (Clarify Discrepancy)`, `Call Customer (Confirm Adjustment)`, `Call Customer (Recall Customer)`**
*   **Clues:**
    *   **C001:** Context states "request missing docs."
    *   **C002 (1st):** Context states "clarify account discrepancy."
    *   **C002 (2nd):** Context states "confirm adjustments made."
    *   **C003:** Context states "Customer stepped out... calls to have them return."
*   **Reasoning:** While the base activity is a phone call, the *process intent* varies significantly.
    *   In C002, the two calls represent distinct stages: Investigation (1st) vs. Resolution (2nd). Merging these would hide the fact that the agent had to make a second contact to close the loop.
    *   In C003, the call is not about data verification but physical logistics.
    *   Renaming these prevents the process model from assuming all calls serve the same purpose (e.g., data collection).

**3. Consistency Application**
*   Even in Case C002, where `Verify Documents` only appears once, it was renamed to `Verify Documents (Initial)` to maintain schema consistency with C001 and C003. This ensures that downstream analytics can treat the activity as the same semantic step across all cases.
