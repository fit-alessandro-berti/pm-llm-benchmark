### Rationale for the added constraints

- **`existence["BiasMitigationCheck"]`**  
  Ensures every case contains an explicit fairness-control step.

- **`coexistence["BiasMitigationCheck"]["FinalDecision"]`**  
  Prevents completed cases from bypassing bias mitigation. If there is a final decision, there must also be a bias check.

- **`response` from `CheckApplicantAge`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantRace` to `BiasMitigationCheck`**  
  Whenever sensitive information is examined, a mitigation step must follow.

- **`response` from `CheckApplicantAge`, `CheckApplicantGender`, `CheckApplicantRace` to `ManualReview`**  
  If sensitive attributes are used, the case must receive additional human oversight.

- **`precedence["BiasMitigationCheck"]` to `RequestAdditionalInfo`, `Approve`, `Reject`, `FinalDecision`**  
  No important outcome-affecting activity can happen before the fairness check.

- **`responded_existence` and `precedence` involving `ManualReview` for `Approve` and `Reject`**  
  High-impact decisions must be accompanied by manual review, and that review must occur before the decision.

- **`nonchainsuccession` from sensitive-attribute checks to `RequestAdditionalInfo`, `Approve`, `Reject`, `FinalDecision`**  
  Blocks immediate jumps from seeing sensitive data to making an outcome decision, reducing the risk of direct discriminatory behavior.

Overall, these additions reduce bias by forcing:
1. **a fairness checkpoint**,  
2. **human review for sensitive or high-impact decisions**, and  
3. **a separation between sensitive-attribute inspection and decisions**.
