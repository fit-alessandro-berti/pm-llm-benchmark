### 1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking

**Data Integration and Preprocessing:**
To create a "Single Source of Truth," we must merge disparate datasets using the `Case ID (Vehicle-Day)` as the primary process instance.
*   **Challenges:** 
    *   *Granularity Mismatch:* GPS data is high-frequency (pings every few seconds), while Scanner data is discrete. We must "abstract" GPS data into meaningful events like "In Traffic" or "Moving" to match the scanner events.
    *   *Data Cleaning:* Removing GPS drift (phantom movements) and handling missing scanner logs where a driver forgot to scan.
*   **Integration Strategy:** We link the `Dispatch System` (The Plan) with the `GPS/Scanner/Maintenance` logs (The Reality). This creates an event log where each activity has an "Expected Timestamp" and an "Actual Timestamp."

**Process Discovery:**
Using algorithms like the **Inductive Miner** or **Heuristic Miner**, we will visualize the actual flow. 
*   **The Discovered Model:** This will show the "Spaghetti" of real-world logistics—loops where drivers go back to a missed street, long "Idle" blocks in traffic, and the interruption of the delivery flow by "Unscheduled Maintenance" (as seen in the snippet with the Engine Warning Light).

**Conformance Checking:**
We map the discovered process against the `Planned Route`.
*   **Sequence Deviations:** Did the driver follow the optimized stop order (Stop 1 -> 2 -> 3) or jump around? Jumping often indicates the driver knows a better route or is avoiding a localized issue not captured by dispatch.
*   **Unplanned Stops:** Identifying stops where the vehicle was stationary for >3 minutes but no scanner event occurred (e.g., unauthorized breaks or hidden traffic bottlenecks).
*   **Time Window Violations:** Flagging deliveries where `Arrive Customer` occurs after the `Requested Window` in the dispatch system.

### 2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
*   **On-Time Delivery (OTD) Rate:** (Deliveries within window / Total Deliveries).
*   **Service Time vs. Travel Time:** Ratio of time spent at the customer’s door versus time spent driving.
*   **First-Time Hit Rate:** Percentage of 'Delivery Success' vs. 'Delivery Failed'.
*   **Stop Duration Variance:** Measuring if "Stop 5" consistently takes 10 minutes longer than "Stop 10."

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques:**
*   **Temporal Analysis (Dotted Charts):** We will plot events over a 24-hour axis. If we see a "cluster" of "Low Speed" events between 08:00 and 09:00 for all vehicles in a specific zone, we identify a systemic traffic bottleneck.
*   **Activity Duration Analysis:** By calculating the time between `Arrive Customer` and `Depart Customer`, we can see if specific apartment complexes or commercial zones cause delays due to parking difficulties or long walking distances to the door.
*   **Resource Analysis:** Comparing `Driver IDs`. Are some drivers consistently faster at scanning but slower at driving? This helps differentiate between "Skill Gaps" and "Route Difficulty."

### 3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies

We will use **Variant Analysis** to compare the "Happy Path" (on-time, low cost) vs. "Problematic Path" (late, high fuel).

*   **Suboptimal Planning:** If "Success" routes have 20% less travel distance than "Failed" routes for the same number of packages, the dispatch algorithm's static maps are likely outdated.
*   **Service Time Variability:** Analysis may reveal that "Delivery Failed" events take *longer* than "Success" events because the driver spends 10 minutes trying to contact the customer before logging a failure.
*   **Maintenance Correlation:** By cross-referencing `Maintenance Logs` with `Fuel Consumption` (calculated via GPS speed/distance), we can prove that vehicles nearing their service interval consume 15% more fuel, justifying more frequent proactive maintenance.
*   **Traffic Sensitivity:** We will correlate `Low Speed Detected` events with specific time-of-day attributes. If certain routes are always hit by "Unscheduled Stops" at 11:00 AM, the root cause is likely a local congestion pattern (e.g., school zones or market days).

### 4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies

#### Strategy A: Dynamic "Buffer" Time Windows
*   **Target:** Late deliveries due to inaccurate travel estimations.
*   **Insight:** Process mining shows that actual travel time in Sector 4 is 30% higher than Dispatch estimates during rainy weather or Friday afternoons.
*   **Proposal:** Feed discovered "Actual Travel Durations" back into the Dispatch System to create dynamic, reality-based delivery windows.
*   **Impact:** Higher OTD Rate and improved customer satisfaction.

#### Strategy B: Predictive Maintenance via Telematics Integration
*   **Target:** High operational costs and "Unscheduled Stops."
*   **Insight:** The log snippet shows an "Engine Warning Light" forcing an unscheduled stop, which delays 30 subsequent packages. Analysis shows these events often follow a pattern of "High Idle Time" or "High Speed" events in the preceding week.
*   **Proposal:** Use GPS data to trigger maintenance *before* a failure occurs (e.g., if a vehicle shows 10+ hours of idling in a week, schedule a filter check).
*   **Impact:** Reduced breakdown costs and fewer interrupted delivery days.

#### Strategy C: Failed Delivery "Hotspot" Mitigation
*   **Target:** High rate of "Delivery Failed (Customer Not Home)."
*   **Insight:** Variant analysis shows that 40% of failures occur in a specific residential zone between 10:00 AM and 12:00 PM.
*   **Proposal:** Shift the delivery sequence for these specific zones to 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM or implement a "Pre-Arrival SMS" triggered by the GPS geofence (2km away).
*   **Impact:** Lower fuel waste (no re-delivery), lower cost-per-package, and higher First-Time Hit Rate.

### 5. Operational Constraints and Monitoring

**Accounting for Constraints:**
*   **Labor Laws:** The process model will include "Hard Stops" for driver breaks to ensure optimization doesn't violate legal working hours.
*   **Vehicle Capacity:** The Dispatch-to-Actual comparison will flag cases where "Capacity Overload" led to slower speeds or longer loading times at the depot.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan:**
*   **Process Dashboard:** A real-time dashboard showing "Conformance Drift." If a driver deviates from the optimized path, the dispatcher is alerted immediately.
*   **Weekly Variant Benchmarking:** Comparing this week’s "Average Stop Duration" against the 6-month baseline.
*   **Sustainability Metrics:** Tracking "Idle Time" and "Empty Kilometers" (distance driven after the last delivery back to the depot) to monitor fuel reduction and carbon footprint.
