Below is a context-aware renaming of the recurring activities. The main recurrent activities within cases are:

- **Verify Documents**
- **Call Customer**
- **Adjust Account** appears in multiple cases, but not multiple times within the same case in this log
- **Initiate Request** and **Approve Request** also recur across cases, but their meaning is stable and does not need disambiguation within a case

## 1) Contextual interpretation of recurring activities

### Verify Documents
This activity appears twice in cases **C001** and **C003**.

- **First occurrence**: initial verification of submitted identity/documentation
- **Second occurrence**: re-verification after missing information or an internal adjustment

### Call Customer
This activity appears twice in case **C002**, and once in **C001** and **C003**.

- **First occurrence in C001/C002/C003**: outbound contact triggered by an issue, missing information, or discrepancy
- **Second occurrence in C002**: follow-up call to confirm a correction/adjustment

### Adjust Account
Appears in **C002** and **C003**.

- In both cases it is an internal correction step after a customer contact or missing information issue
- The meaning is similar, so no need to split it unless you want finer granularity by case stage

## 2) Renamed activities

To disambiguate, I rename the recurring activities as follows:

- **Verify Documents** 
  - **Initial Document Verification**
  - **Reverification of Documents**
- **Call Customer** 
  - **Issue Clarification Call**
  - **Follow-up Confirmation Call** (only where it clearly occurs as a follow-up)
- **Adjust Account** 
  - **Internal Account Adjustment**  
    (kept consistent across cases since its contextual role is essentially the same)

## 4) Brief justification of naming choices

- **Initial Document Verification** vs. **Reverification of Documents**  
  The first occurrence is clearly the baseline check on newly submitted documents. The second occurrence happens only after missing information is resolved or the account is adjusted, so “reverification” better captures the retry/validation-after-correction nature.

- **Issue Clarification Call**  
  In C001, C002, and C003, the first call is triggered by a problem: missing docs, discrepancy, or needing the customer to return. This is more specific than simply “Call Customer.”

- **Follow-up Confirmation Call**  
  In C002, the second call occurs after an internal adjustment and is explicitly meant to confirm that the correction was made. That makes it distinct from the earlier issue-driven call.

- **Internal Account Adjustment**  
  This is an internal back-office correction step. The same label fits both cases because the role is consistent: it is a remediation action after a discrepancy or missing information.
