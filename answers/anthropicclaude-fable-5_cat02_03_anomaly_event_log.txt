# P2P Event Log Anomaly Analysis

## Baseline: The Standard P2P Flow

A typical compliant Procure-to-Pay case follows this sequence:

**Create Requisition  Approve Requisition  Issue PO  Receive Goods  Receive Invoice  Match Invoice to PO (3-way match)  Release Payment  Close Case**

---

## Case 1001 —  Normal (Reference Case)

All activities occur in the expected order with plausible time gaps. This case serves as the conformant baseline.

---

## Case 1002 —  Multiple Severe Anomalies

| Anomaly | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PO issued before requisition approval** | PO issued at 08:40 on Feb 1; approval not recorded until Feb 3, 08:00 — *two days after the PO and after payment was already released*. |
| **Payment released before goods receipt** | Payment at Feb 3, 07:15; goods received Feb 4. |
| **Payment released before invoice matching** | Match occurs Feb 5 — *two days after payment*. |
| **Retroactive approval** | The approval appears to be a "rubber stamp" recorded after the fact. |

**Why problematic:**
- **Approval bypass** undermines internal controls and segregation of duties — the purchase commitment was made without authorization. This is a classic red flag for fraud or maverick buying.
- **Paying before receiving goods and before matching** defeats the entire purpose of the 3-way match control (PO  goods receipt  invoice). If goods never arrived or the invoice was wrong, the money is already gone.
- Also notable: invoice received only 15 minutes before payment — unusually fast, suggesting no real verification took place.

---

## Case 1003 —  Payment Before Invoice and Goods

| Anomaly | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Payment released before invoice received** | Payment Feb 8; invoice Feb 9. Paying with no invoice on file is highly irregular. |
| **Payment before goods receipt** | Goods arrived Feb 10 — two days after payment. |
| **Matching performed after payment** | Match on Feb 11 is purely retrospective and cannot prevent an erroneous payment. |

**Why problematic:**
- Paying **without an invoice** raises questions about what the payment was based on — this pattern is associated with duplicate payments, fraudulent disbursements, or advance payments outside policy.
- A post-payment match offers no preventive control; any discrepancy found at that point requires costly recovery (clawback from vendor).

---

## Case 1004 —  Goods Received Before PO Issued

| Anomaly | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Receive Goods (Feb 6, 09:00) precedes Issue PO (Feb 6, 09:15)** | Goods arrived before any purchase order existed. |

**Why problematic:**
- This indicates a **retrospective/after-the-fact PO** — the order was likely placed informally (phone, email) and the PO created only to legitimize the transaction ("maverick buying" / "PO after invoice" pattern).
- It bypasses procurement controls: pricing, vendor selection, contract terms, and budget checks were not validated before commitment.
- The remainder of the case (invoice  match  payment) is sequenced correctly, which makes this look like a process worker "fixing the paperwork" rather than a data-entry timestamp issue — though a 15-minute gap could also warrant checking for logging errors.

---

## Summary of Findings

| Case | Severity | Key Issues |
|---|---|---|
| 1001 | None | Conformant |
| 1002 | **High** | Unapproved PO, payment before receipt/match, retroactive approval |
| 1003 | **High** | Payment before invoice and goods; match after payment |
| 1004 | Medium | Goods received before PO (retrospective PO / maverick buying) |

## Recommended Actions
1. **Investigate Cases 1002 and 1003** for potential fraud or duplicate/erroneous payment; verify goods were actually received and invoice amounts matched.
2. **Enforce system controls**: block PO issuance without approved requisitions and block payment release until a successful 3-way match.
3. **Review Case 1004's vendor relationship** for off-system ordering practices and retrain the requester on procurement policy.
4. Consider continuous conformance checking on the P2P process to flag these violations in real time rather than retrospectively.
