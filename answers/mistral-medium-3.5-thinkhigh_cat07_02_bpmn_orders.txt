The diagram presents a comprehensive overview of a business process, divided into four main sections: Sales, Production, Procurement, and Warehouse. The Sales section starts on Monday morning with checking the order for completeness, followed by verifying if the customer is in the database. If not, the customer is added to the database before sending order details and drafting an invoice. The Production section begins upon receiving order details, with planning and manufacturing the product. If materials are faulty, the Material Compensation Subprocess in the Procurement section is initiated, where materials are ordered, checked, and potentially returned. If the order is canceled, the process loops back. Once the product is complete, it moves to the Warehouse section where the product is sent out. The diagram illustrates the flow of activities, decisions, and interactions between different departments, emphasizing the sequence and conditions required to progress through each stage of the process.
