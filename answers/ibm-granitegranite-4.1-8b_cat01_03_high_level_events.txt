**Proposed HighLevel Activities and Rationale**

| HighLevel Activity | Example Events (subset) | Grouping Rationale |
|---------------------|------------------------|--------------------|
| **1. Material Preparation** | `Retrieve raw metal sheet`, `Scan barcode`, `Place metal sheet on table`, `Align metal sheet`, `Preheat metal sheet` | All events occur **early** in the case, **consecutively**, and involve acquiring, identifying, positioning, and preparing the raw part for the next stage. They are performed by different resources (Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1) but logically belong to the same phase of getting the material ready for assembly. |
| **2. Assembly** | `Pick up welding tool`, `Weld corner A`, `Weld corner B` | These events follow directly after material preparation. The **tool pickup** triggers the welding actions, which are performed by the same operator (Operator B) and involve physically joining parts. Together they constitute the **assembly of the metal sheet into a welded component**. |
| **3. Quality Inspection** | `Measure weld integrity`, `Apply protective coating`, `Dry coating`, `Visual check` | After assembly, the process shifts to **assessing and enhancing quality**. The measurement of weld integrity checks the correctness of the weld; the coating and drying steps add a protective finish; the visual check confirms the final appearance. All are **qualityassurance steps** that occur in sequence after assembly. |
| **4. Completion / Release** (optional, inferred from the end of each case) | Implicit – no explicit “release” events in the sample, but the final visual check (`Visual check`) typically signals that the product is ready for downstream processing or shipment. | The sequence terminates with a **visual acceptance check**, which is commonly used as the **release decision** in manufacturing workflows. If more explicit events (e.g., “Mark as finished”, “Package”, “Dispatch”) were present, they would be grouped here. |

**How to Use This Model**

- **From lowlevel events  highlevel steps:** Scan each `CaseID` and map its chronological event list to the appropriate highlevel bucket based on the activity names and description order.
- **Monitoring & Analysis:** Higherlevel activities provide a concise view of process flow, enabling KPI calculation per stage (e.g., average time for Material Preparation, defect rate in Quality Inspection).
- **Process Optimization:** Identifying bottlenecks at a high level (e.g., prolonged Material Preparation) directs resources to specific phases without needing to analyze every granular event.

This structured aggregation transforms a dense sequence of detailed actions into a clear, domainrelevant workflow that can be visualized, measured, and improved.
