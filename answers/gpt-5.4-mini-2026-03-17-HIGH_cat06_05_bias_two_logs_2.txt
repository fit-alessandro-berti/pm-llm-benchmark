**Group B exhibits bias.**

### Why Group B is biased
In Group B, candidates affiliated with the **LocalBusinessLeadersClub** receive a **+5 cultural fit boost** at the CulturalFitCheck and that boost carries through to the final hiring decision.

That means:
- **U001** and **U003** get improved scores because of association membership.
- **U002**, who has similar scores but **no association**, does not get the boost and is not hired.

So the process is not treating candidates consistently: membership in a particular professional association is influencing outcomes beyond the candidate’s actual qualifications.

### How the bias manifests
The bias appears in two ways:

1. **Unequal scoring**
   - The cultural fit score is adjusted upward only for candidates in one specific association.
   - This gives them an advantage unrelated to core job performance.

2. **Unequal decision outcomes**
   - Candidates with the boost are hired even when their underlying scores are not clearly superior.
   - Candidates without the boost can be rejected despite comparable qualifications.

### Comparison with Group A
Group A does **not** apply this association-based adjustment. Its hiring decisions appear to rely more directly on the candidates’ stated scores, without a special preference tied to group membership.

That makes Group A **more neutral** in this respect, while Group B shows **preferential treatment**.

### Fairness and equity implications
This bias can create several problems:

- **Unfair advantage** for candidates who belong to the favored association.
- **Indirect discrimination** if access to that association is easier for some demographic groups than others.
- **Reduced equity** because hiring outcomes depend partly on network membership rather than merit alone.
- **Lower trust** in the hiring process, especially among applicants who are not part of the favored network.

### Bottom line
**Group B is biased because it rewards association membership with a score boost that affects hiring decisions.**  
For a fair and equitable hiring process, adjustments should be based on job-relevant criteria and applied consistently to all candidates.
