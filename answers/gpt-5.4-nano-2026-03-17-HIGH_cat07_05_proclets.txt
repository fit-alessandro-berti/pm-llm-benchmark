The visualization is a UML-style interaction and message-passing diagram showing how **two proplets/procedures/classes communicate through two channels**, plus a small class diagram and an example message table.

### Main interaction diagram (a)
- **Left side (Order/Results workflow):**
  - A **doctor** visits, enters an **order**.
  - The **lab** receives the sample (“Take blood sample” / “Send report” flow).
  - The proplet shows a sequence including:
    - **Order entry**  **Order system**
    - **Receive result**  **Judge results**
    - **Do tests** (possibly repeated)
    - **Send report** back outward (with green “Everything is fine” vs. “Do tests again” loop).

- **Right side (Testing workflow):**
  - A **chemical analyst** is involved in:
    - **Perform test** (chemical test)
    - **Make report** / **Finish test**
  - There are steps for updating and providing results:
    - **Provide updated result** (shown on the channel to the other proplet)
    - **Make/perform test again** if needed.

- **Two communication channels are highlighted by dashed arrows:**
  - One channel carries **results/updated outcomes** (e.g., *Receive result  Judge results* and *Provide updated result*).
  - Another channel carries **test/control or ordering information** (connecting the order/test sides via intermediate “1..1” / “1..1” message nodes).
  - Message arrows include labels like **“1..1”**, indicating a one-to-one interaction pattern for those message types.

### Class diagram (b)
- On the upper-right, a small class diagram shows two classes/procelets:
  - **Lab visit**
  - **Lab test**
- Multiplicity relationships are indicated:
  - **Lab visit “requires” Lab test**
  - **Lab test** has multiplicity **1..*** relative to **Lab visit** (a single visit can require one or more lab tests).

### Example performative table (c)
- At the bottom, there’s a table labeled **“Example of a performative”** with columns:
  - **Time** (e.g., 11:00)
  - **Channel**
  - **Sender** (e.g., Lab visit, the sender is “John”)
  - **Receivers** (e.g., Lab test team “HBGH” / “John”)
  - **Action** (e.g., **Create**)
  - **Content** (e.g., “Can you perform a HGB test for John?”)
  - **Scope** (private)
  - **Direction** (OUT)

**In short:** it visualizes a laboratory process where a doctor’s lab visit/order triggers chemical testing, results are exchanged over two distinct channels, testing may repeat until conditions are satisfied, and reports are sent—supported by a class diagram indicating the relationship between “Lab visit” and “Lab test.”
