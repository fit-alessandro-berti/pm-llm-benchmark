The visualization is a BPMN-style swimlane process diagram for **“Dispatch of goods”** at a **Computer Hardware Shop**. It is divided into three lanes: **Logistics**, **Secretary**, and **Warehouse**.

The process starts with **“Ship goods”** and immediately splits into parallel work:

- In the **Secretary** lane, the secretary **clarifies the shipment method**.
  - A decision gateway asks **“Special handling?”**
    - If **yes**, the secretary gets **3 offers from logistic companies**, then **selects a logistic company and places the order**.
    - If **no**, the secretary **checks whether insurance is required**, using **customer information** and order details.
      - The package label is always written.
      - If insurance is necessary, the parcel is sent to **Logistics** to **insure the parcel**.
      - The insurance and label-writing paths then rejoin.

- In the **Warehouse** lane, the warehouse begins **packaging the goods** in parallel.
  - A timer event indicates a **48-hour** threshold.
  - If delayed, the warehouse **notifies the customer about the delay** before continuing.

The administrative/shipping branch and the warehouse packaging branch synchronize at a parallel gateway. After both are complete, the warehouse:

1. **Fills out shipment paperwork**
2. **Moves the package to the pickup area**
3. Ends the process with **“Shipment prepared”**

Overall, the diagram shows how order dispatch is coordinated between administrative shipment planning, logistics insurance handling, and warehouse packaging/preparation.
