### 3. Justification of Naming Choices

| Original Activity | New Name(s) | Contextual Clue & Rationale |
|-------------------|-------------|-----------------------------|
| `Verify Documents` | `Verify Documents - Initial Review`<br>`Verify Documents - Secondary Review` | **Position & Trigger:** The first occurrence always follows request initiation and serves as a baseline compliance check. The second occurrence explicitly follows new input (`Submit Additional` or `Adjust Account`) and is described as a "re-check" or "re-verify." Splitting by sequence (`Initial` vs. `Secondary`) preserves process flow logic while disambiguating the two validation stages. |
| `Call Customer` | `Call Customer - Request Missing Docs`<br>`Call Customer - Clarify Discrepancy`<br>`Call Customer - Confirm Adjustments`<br>`Call Customer - Recall Customer` | **Purpose-Driven Differentiation:** The activity name alone masks four distinct operational intents. Each new suffix is extracted directly from the `Additional Context` column and reflects the call's objective in the workflow: gathering data, resolving ambiguity, closing the loop on internal changes, or logistical coordination. This prevents process mining tools from grouping unrelated communication events. |
| `Adjust Account` | `Adjust Account - Post-Clarification`<br>`Adjust Account - Missing Info Correction` | **Trigger-Based Differentiation:** Though not recurring within a single case, the activity serves different process branches. C002's adjustment is a direct consequence of a clarification call, while C003's adjustment fixes a data gap discovered during verification. The suffixes capture the causal link to the preceding event, enabling accurate path analysis. |

**Naming Convention Applied:** `[Base Activity] - [Contextual Modifier]`  
This structure maintains traceability to the original process model, supports automated log parsing, and explicitly encodes the "why" and "when" behind each step, which is critical for performance analysis, compliance auditing, and process optimization in financial services workflows.
