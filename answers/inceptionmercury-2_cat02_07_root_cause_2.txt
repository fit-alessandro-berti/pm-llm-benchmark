### Key correlations

1. **Complexity  Duration** – All highcomplexity claims (2003,2005) and the only mediumcomplexity claim (2002) exceed 1day, whereas lowcomplexity claims finish within 2h.
2. **Multiple “Request Additional Documents”  Longer lead time** – Every slow case contains at least one such request; the more requests, the longer the total duration (2005 = 3 requests  longest overall).
3. **Adjuster_Lisa  higher request frequency** – In the two Bregion cases (2002,2005) the same adjuster (Lisa) issues the additionaldocument requests, suggesting a possible bottleneck or a stricter documentation policy for her workload.
4. **RegionB shows a slight tendency toward slower cases** – Both Bregion slow cases (2002,2005) involve high or mediumcomplexity claims and multiple document requests. RegionA has one slow case (2003) but also two fast cases (2001,2003). The sample is small, so region alone is not decisive, but it may interact with resource allocation.

## 3.  Why these attributes cause performance problems  

| Root cause | How the data supports it | Why it hurts performance |
|------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **High claim complexity** | 2003 & 2005 (High) take 48h and 77h, respectively. | Complex claims need more verification, often trigger additionaldocument cycles, and require senior manager approval (Manager_Bill). |
| **Repeated “Request Additional Documents” loops** | 2005 has three requests spread over three days; 2003 has two requests within one day. | Each request forces the claim to wait for the claimant to supply missing info, creating idle time for downstream resources. |
| **Adjusterspecific behavior (Adjuster_Lisa)** | In both Bregion slow cases, Adjuster_Lisa is the adjuster who initiates the additionaldocument requests. | If a particular adjuster applies a stricter “completedocumentationfirst” rule, the claim stays longer in the “awaiting documents” state. This can also reflect workload imbalance (Lisa may be overloaded). |
| **Regionrelated workload or staffing** | The two longest cases (2002,2005) are in RegionB, where the same finance and CSR resources (Finance_Carl, CSR_Paul) appear. | RegionB may have fewer staff or slower handoffs, causing longer waiting periods between steps. |
| **Manager approval path** | Highcomplexity claims are approved by Manager_Bill (instead of Manager_Ann). | Manager_Bill may have a higher approval queue or stricter criteria, extending the “Approve Claim” latency. |

## 4.  Recommendations to mitigate the identified root causes  

| Recommendation | Targeted root cause | Practical actions |
|----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **1. Prioritise and fasttrack highcomplexity claims** | Complexity & multiple document loops | • Create a “HighComplexity” queue with dedicated adjusters and managers.<br>• Set SLA “e.g., 24h) for completing all “Request Additional Documents” cycles.<br>• Use a checklist to precollect likelyneeded documents at submission. |
| **2. Reduce the number of documentrequest iterations** | Repeated “Request Additional Documents” | • Implement a **presubmission validation portal** that prompts claimants for all required items based on complexity.<br>• Allow adjusters to **batchrequest** all missing items in a single communication rather than incremental requests. |
| **3. Balance adjuster workload & standardise request criteria** | Adjuster_Lisa bottleneck | • Analyse Adjuster_Lisa’s workload (cases per day) and redistribute cases to other adjusters when load >threshold.<br>• Develop a **standardised requestcriteria matrix** so all adjusters request the same set of documents for a given complexity level. |
| **4. Strengthen RegionB staffing or crossregion handoff** | Regionrelated delay | • Review staffing levels in RegionB; add a backup adjuster/approver for peak periods.<br>• Enable **crossregion escalation**: if a claim stays >12h in “Awaiting Docs” in RegionB, autoassign to a RegionA adjuster with spare capacity. |
| **5. Accelerate seniormanager approval** | Manager_Bill queue | • Give Manager_Bill a **dedicated approval window** (e.g., 2h per day) for highcomplexity claims.<br>• Introduce a **parallelreview** model where a junior manager can provisionally approve, with final signoff later. |
| **6. Automate statusmonitoring & alerts** | All root causes | • Deploy a **processmining dashboard** that flags cases with >2days elapsed or >1 documentrequest cycle.<br>• Send automatic reminders to claimants and to the responsible resource when a step exceeds its SLA. |
| **7. Capture and analyse additional attributes** | Limited data insight | • Record **claimant response time**, **adjuster workload**, **queue length**, and **system latency** to refine the rootcause model. |

## 5.  Summary  

* **Slow cases:** 2002 (26h), 2003 (48h), 2005 (77h).  
* **Root causes:** High complexity, multiple “Request Additional Documents” cycles, adjusterspecific behavior (Adjuster_Lisa), possible regionB staffing constraints, and seniormanager approval bottlenecks.  
* **Mitigation:** Prioritise highcomplexity claims, collect required documents upfront, standardise adjuster request rules, balance workload across adjusters and regions, streamline seniormanager approvals, and use realtime monitoring/alerts.

Implementing these measures should bring the majority of cases back into the sub2hour window observed for lowcomplexity claims, while still preserving the necessary diligence for more complex situations.
