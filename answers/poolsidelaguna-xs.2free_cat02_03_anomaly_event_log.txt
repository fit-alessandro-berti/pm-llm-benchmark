### **Summary of Key Issues**  
1. **Process Flow Violations**  
   - All three anomalous cases (1002, 1003, 1004) violate the standard P2P sequence by performing activities out of order (e.g., issuing POs without approvals, releasing payments before matching).  

2. **Control Weaknesses**

3. **Authorization Gaps**  
   - Case 1002 and 1004 skip or delay critical approvals, exposing the organization to unauthorized spending risks.  

4. **Accounting and Compliance Risks**  
   - Payments made without proper documentation (invoice/goods receipt) could lead to incorrect financial reporting or compliance violations.

### **Recommendations**

- Strengthen internal controls to ensure adherence to the three-way match and approval hierarchies.
