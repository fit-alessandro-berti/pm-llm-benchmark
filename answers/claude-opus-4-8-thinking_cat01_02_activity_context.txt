## 1. Analysis of Recurring Activities

Looking across the three cases, two activity names recur **within the same case** while carrying different meanings:

| Activity | Cases where it repeats | Why the meaning differs |
|----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Verify Documents** | C001, C003 | First occurrence is the *initial* verification; the second occurs *after* new info/docs arrive — it's a *re-verification*. |
| **Call Customer** | C002 | First call is to *clarify a problem*; the second is a *confirmation/follow-up* after work was done. |

`Call Customer` also appears once each in C001 and C003, but with distinct **intents** (request missing docs vs. ask customer to return), so it benefits from contextual renaming too — even though it isn't repeated in those cases — to keep names consistent across the log.

## 4. Justification of Naming Choices

**Verify Documents  Initial vs. Re-check**
- The *first* occurrence always immediately follows initiation, marking the **baseline verification**.
- The *second* occurrence always follows a triggering event (document upload in C001, account adjustment in C003). Naming the trigger (`Post-Submission`, `Post-Adjustment`) disambiguates *why* the re-check happens. In C003 I also flagged **(Physical)** because the resource (`BranchOfficer`) and context indicate an in-person verification rather than a back-office one.

**Call Customer  intent-based names**
- C001's call requests **missing documents** (verification incomplete).
- C002 has two calls: the first **clarifies a discrepancy** (a problem-investigation call), the second **confirms an adjustment** (a closure/follow-up call). The timestamp ordering relative to `Adjust Account` is the key clue — clarify *before*, confirm *after*.
- C003's call asks the customer to **return** to the branch (they "stepped out").

**Initiate Request  channel-based names**
- The `Resource`/context reveals the intake channel (`WebPortal` = Online vs. `BranchReception` = Branch Walk-in). This isn't a within-case duplicate, but distinguishing the channel adds analytical value for routing/SLA analysis.

**Contextual clues used overall:** activity position in the sequence, the preceding/following activity, the resource performing it, and the free-text "Additional Context" describing intent.
