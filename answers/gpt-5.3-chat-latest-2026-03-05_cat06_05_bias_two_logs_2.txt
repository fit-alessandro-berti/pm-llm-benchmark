The **biased log is Group B (Unprotected Group)**.

**How the bias manifests**
- In Group B, candidates who belong to **LocalBusinessLeadersClub** receive a **+5 CulturalFit score adjustment** during the CulturalFitCheck.
- This adjustment is unrelated to measured competencies (skill or personality) and is based on **membership in a specific professional association**.
- The boost directly affects downstream decisions:
  - **U001**: Cultural fit increases from 60  65  **Hired**.
  - **U003**: Cultural fit increases from 58  63  **Hired**.
- Candidates without the association (e.g., **U002**) receive **no adjustment**, even with comparable scores, and are **not hired**.

**Why this is bias**
- The hiring outcome is influenced by **network affiliation rather than objective performance metrics**.
- Membership in the association likely correlates with **local social networks, socioeconomic status, or demographic groups**, which can indirectly privilege certain candidates.
- This creates **systematic advantage** for candidates connected to the association.

**Contrast with Group A**
- Group A applies **no score adjustments** based on external affiliations.
- Decisions are based only on **evaluated attributes (skill, personality, cultural fit)**.

**Implications for fairness**
- Introduces **structural favoritism / nepotism-like effects**.
- Violates principles of **equal opportunity and merit-based hiring**.
- Could lead to **indirect discrimination** if the association is dominated by certain demographics.
- Reduces **transparency and trust** in the hiring process.

In summary, **Group B’s score adjustment tied to professional association membership introduces bias**, because it systematically advantages candidates with certain social connections rather than evaluating them solely on merit.
