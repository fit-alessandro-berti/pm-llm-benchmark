**Why this is systematic bias**

1. **Disparate treatment by protected attributes**  
   - The model uses `LocalResident` and `CommunityGroup` *only* for the unprotected group. These attributes are not considered for the protected group, meaning any policy or rule that gives advantage based on residency or community membership is applied asymmetrically.

2. **ScoreAdjustment as a conduit for bias**  
   - The `ScoreAdjustment` column is the mechanism by which the aforementioned attributes influence the outcome. By adding a fixed +10 whenever `CommunityGroup` is nonnull (and only for GroupB), the model inflates the final score for a subset of the unprotected group. Because the adjustment is never applied to GroupA, the distribution of final scores is shifted upward for GroupB *independently* of the underlying creditworthiness captured by `PreliminaryScore`.

3. **Impact on downstream decisions**  
   - The final decision column shows that two of the three unprotected cases (U001, U003) are approved *solely* because of the +10 boost (their preliminary scores 720 and 695 would otherwise be below the approval threshold, assuming the same threshold that approved P001 at 720 and rejected P002 at 710). The protected group never experiences this boost, so a protected applicant with the same preliminary score as U001 (720) would be judged on the raw score alone—potentially leading to a different outcome if the threshold lies between 720 and 730.

4. **Resulting systematic difference**  
   - Over a larger population, we would expect:
     - **Higher approval rates** for unprotected applicants who are local residents and members of the favored community club, even if their underlying credit score is modest.
     - **Lower or unchanged approval rates** for protected applicants, who cannot receive the community boost regardless of residency or club membership.
   - This creates a *disparate impact* where the protected group is systematically disadvantaged relative to the unprotected group, purely because of the way the model treats `LocalResident`, `CommunityGroup`, and `ScoreAdjustment`.

**Summary**

- The bias resides in GroupB’s log.
- It arises because the model grants a **+10 community boost** *only* to unprotected applicants who are local residents and belong to a specific community club.
- The boost changes the final score and thus the approval decision, while the protected group never receives any analogous adjustment.
- Consequently, protected applicants are evaluated on a stricter baseline, leading to systematic differences in approval rates that are unrelated to their actual creditworthiness (as captured by `PreliminaryScore`).  

Mitigation would require either:  
1. Removing the community-based adjustment (or applying it equally to both groups), or  
2. Ensuring that any residency/communityrelated factors are either excluded from the model or justified, transparent, and tested for disparate impact before deployment.
