## BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

### 1. Participants / Lanes

**Pool: Company Invoice Approval Process**

**Lanes:**
1. **Supplier**
2. **Accounts Payable Clerk / AP Team**
3. **Accounting System**
4. **Purchasing Department / Purchasing Officer**
5. **Department Manager**
6. **Accounts Payable Supervisor**
7. **Supplier Relationship Manager**
8. **Department Heads** *(used only for exceptional retrospective PO cases)*

---

## 2. Main Process Flow

### Start Event

**Start Event: Invoice Received**  
- Trigger: Supplier sends invoice, usually by email, to Accounts Payable.

---

### Step A: Receive and Review Invoice

**Task A1: Receive invoice**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Input:** Supplier invoice  
- **Output:** Invoice ready for initial validation

**Task A2: Check whether invoice contains a valid PO number**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Input:** Invoice  
- **Output:** PO validation result

---

### Gateway G1: Does the invoice have a valid PO number?

#### If **No PO number / invalid PO number**

**Task A3: Request missing PO information or revised invoice from supplier**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Input:** Invoice without valid PO  
- **Output:** Request sent to supplier for PO details or revised invoice

**Optional Task A4: Supplier Relationship Manager assists supplier follow-up**  
- **Role:** Supplier Relationship Manager  
- **Condition:** Used when AP or Purchasing needs support resolving supplier-related missing information  
- **Input:** Invoice, supplier contact details, contract/supplier relationship information  
- **Output:** Supplier provides PO number, corrected invoice, or clarification

**Intermediate Event: Supplier responds with PO details or corrected invoice**

Then return to:

**Task A2: Check whether invoice contains a valid PO number**

#### If **No PO can be identified / invoice may relate to informal service arrangement**

**Task E1: Escalate missing PO exception**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Output:** Exception sent to Accounts Payable Supervisor

**Task E2: Investigate missing PO issue**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Supervisor  
- **Participants:** Department Heads, possibly Supplier Relationship Manager  
- **Input:** Invoice without PO, supplier details, contract/service information  
- **Output:** Decision on whether to create retrospective PO or reject invoice

**Gateway G1a: Can a valid retrospective PO be created or confirmed?**

- **If Yes:**  
  **Task E3: Create or confirm retrospective PO**  
  - **Role:** Accounts Payable Supervisor / Department Heads  
  - **Output:** Valid PO reference created or confirmed  
  - Return to **Task A2 / Task B1**

- **If No:**  
  **End Event: Invoice rejected due to missing or invalid PO**

#### If **Yes, valid PO exists**

Proceed to:

---

### Step B: Log Invoice

**Task B1: Log invoice in accounting system**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **System:** Accounting System  
- **Input:** Invoice with valid PO  
- **Output:** Invoice record created in accounting system

---

### Step C: Purchasing Confirmation

**Task C1: Forward invoice to Purchasing for confirmation**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Recipient:** Purchasing Department  
- **Input:** Logged invoice, PO number  
- **Output:** Invoice sent to Purchasing for review

**Task C2: Compare invoice against purchase order and receipt information**  
- **Role:** Purchasing Officer / Purchasing Department  
- **Input:** Invoice, PO, delivery/receipt information  
- **Checks Performed:**  
  - Quantities ordered vs. invoiced  
  - Prices ordered vs. invoiced  
  - Delivery charges  
  - Whether goods/services were received  
  - Whether billed items correspond to the original order  
- **Output:** Purchasing confirmation or discrepancy notice

---

### Gateway G2: Do invoice details match the PO and receipt information?

#### If **Yes**

**Task C3: Send confirmation to Accounts Payable**  
- **Role:** Purchasing Officer  
- **Output:** Confirmation email or system confirmation sent to AP

Proceed to:

---

### Step D: AP Invoice Matching

**Task D1: Match invoice line items against PO in accounting system**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **System:** Accounting System  
- **Input:** Invoice, PO, Purchasing confirmation  
- **Output:** AP match result

---

### Gateway G3: Does the invoice match the PO in AP review?

#### If **Yes**

Proceed to:

---

### Step E: Manager Approval

**Task E4: Trigger manager approval request**  
- **Role:** Accounting System  
- **Trigger Condition:** Goods/services receipt confirmed and invoice successfully matched to PO  
- **Output:** Automatic approval notification sent to Department Manager

**Task E5: Review invoice, PO, and confirmation**  
- **Role:** Department Manager  
- **Input:** Invoice details, PO, Purchasing confirmation, special notes, budget/context information  
- **Output:** Approval decision

---

### Gateway G4: Does the Department Manager approve the invoice?

#### If **Yes**

**Task F1: Approve invoice in system**  
- **Role:** Department Manager  
- **System:** Accounting System  
- **Output:** Approved invoice

**Task F2: Schedule invoice for payment**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk / AP Team  
- **Input:** Approved invoice  
- **Output:** Invoice added to next payment cycle/payment run

**End Event: Invoice scheduled for payment**

---

## 3. Exception and Rework Flows

### Exception Flow 1: Purchasing Finds Discrepancy

At **Gateway G2: Do invoice details match the PO and receipt information?**

#### If **No**

**Task X1: Notify AP of discrepancy / request invoice hold**  
- **Role:** Purchasing Officer  
- **Input:** Invoice discrepancy details  
- **Output:** AP informed that invoice should be held or corrected

**Task X2: Place invoice on hold**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **System:** Accounting System  
- **Output:** Invoice status changed to “On Hold” or equivalent

**Task X3: Resolve discrepancy**  
- **Role:** Purchasing Officer and/or Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Optional Participant:** Supplier Relationship Manager  
- **Possible Actions:**  
  - Contact supplier directly  
  - Ask supplier for corrected invoice  
  - Check contract pricing  
  - Clarify quantities, prices, delivery charges, or missing items  
  - Confirm whether billed items were actually ordered or received  
- **Output:** Clarification, corrected invoice, or unresolved exception

---

### Gateway XG1: Is the discrepancy resolved?

#### If **Yes**

**Task X4: Update invoice information or receive corrected invoice**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Input:** Supplier clarification or corrected invoice  
- **Output:** Updated invoice record

Return to:

**Task C2: Purchasing compares invoice against PO and receipt information**  
or, if Purchasing has already confirmed the corrected details, return to:  
**Task D1: AP matches invoice line items against PO**

#### If **No**

**Task X5: Escalate discrepancy to AP Supervisor**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Output:** Exception escalated

**Task X6: Coordinate exception resolution**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Supervisor  
- **Participants:** Purchasing, Supplier, Supplier Relationship Manager, Department Heads if needed  
- **Output:** Resolution decision

---

### Gateway XG2: Can the supervisor resolve the discrepancy?

#### If **Yes**

Return to normal flow at either:

- **Task C2: Purchasing review**, or  
- **Task D1: AP invoice matching**, depending on whether a new Purchasing confirmation is required.

#### If **No**

**End Event: Invoice rejected / not approved due to unresolved discrepancy**

---

### Exception Flow 2: AP Match Fails After Purchasing Confirmation

At **Gateway G3: Does the invoice match the PO in AP review?**

#### If **No**

**Task X7: Email Purchasing for clarification**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Input:** Invoice, PO, discrepancy details  
- **Output:** Clarification request sent to Purchasing

Then follow the same discrepancy resolution path:

**Task X3: Resolve discrepancy**

If resolved, return to:

**Task D1: Match invoice line items against PO**

If unresolved, escalate to:

**Task X5: Escalate discrepancy to AP Supervisor**

---

### Exception Flow 3: Manager Rejects or Requests Investigation

At **Gateway G4: Does the Department Manager approve the invoice?**

#### If **Manager sees suspicious issue and requests investigation**

**Task X8: Ask AP to hold and investigate invoice**  
- **Role:** Department Manager  
- **Output:** Invoice returned to AP for investigation

**Task X9: Place or keep invoice on hold**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **System:** Accounting System  
- **Output:** Invoice held pending investigation

**Task X10: Investigate manager concern**  
- **Role:** Accounts Payable Clerk  
- **Participants:** Purchasing, AP Supervisor, Supplier Relationship Manager as needed  
- **Typical Issues:**  
  - Unexpected items  
  - Total amount significantly different from budget  
  - Pricing concerns  
  - Quantity concerns  
  - Special notes requiring clarification  
- **Output:** Clarification, corrected invoice, or unresolved issue

---

### Gateway XG3: Is the manager concern resolved?

#### If **Yes**

Return to:

**Task E4: Trigger manager approval request**  
or directly to:  
**Task E5: Manager reviews invoice**, if the same approval request remains active.

#### If **No**

**End Event: Invoice rejected due to unresolved manager concern**

#### If **Manager explicitly rejects invoice**

**End Event: Invoice rejected by Department Manager**

---

## 4. Textual Sequence Flow Summary

```text
Start: Invoice received by AP
 AP checks for valid PO number
 [Gateway: Valid PO?]

If No:
     AP requests PO details/revised invoice from Supplier
     Optional Supplier Relationship Manager assists
     Supplier responds
     AP rechecks PO
     If still no valid PO and informal/non-standard case:
         AP escalates to AP Supervisor
         Supervisor works with Department Heads/Supplier/SRM
         [Gateway: Retrospective PO possible?]
            If Yes  create/confirm PO  return to PO validation/logging
            If No  Reject invoice

If Yes:
     AP logs invoice in accounting system
     AP forwards invoice to Purchasing
     Purchasing compares invoice to PO and receipt/goods-services confirmation
     [Gateway: Purchasing confirms match and receipt?]

        If No:
             Purchasing/AP place invoice on hold
             Purchasing/AP/SRM contact supplier or check contract
             [Gateway: Discrepancy resolved?]
                If Yes  update/correct invoice  return to Purchasing review or AP matching
                If No  escalate to AP Supervisor
                     [Gateway: Supervisor resolves?]
                        If Yes  return to normal review/matching flow
                        If No  Reject invoice

        If Yes:
             Purchasing sends confirmation to AP
             AP matches invoice line items against PO in accounting system
             [Gateway: AP match successful?]

                If No:
                     AP asks Purchasing for clarification
                     follow discrepancy resolution path

                If Yes:
                     Accounting system sends automatic approval request to Department Manager
                     Manager reviews invoice, PO, confirmation, budget/special notes
                     [Gateway: Manager approves?]

                        If Yes:
                             Manager approves in system
                             AP schedules invoice for next payment run
                             End: Invoice scheduled for payment

                        If Manager requests investigation:
                             AP holds invoice
                             AP/Purchasing/Supervisor/SRM investigate
                             [Gateway: Issue resolved?]
                                If Yes  return to Manager approval
                                If No  Reject invoice

                        If Manager rejects:
                             End: Invoice rejected
```

## 5. Key Data Objects / Artifacts

| Artifact / Data Object | Description | Used By |
|---|---|---|
| **Supplier Invoice** | Original invoice received by email | AP, Purchasing, Manager, Supervisor |
| **Purchase Order / PO** | Required reference for normal invoice processing | AP, Purchasing, Manager |
| **Invoice Record in Accounting System** | Logged representation of the invoice | AP, Accounting System, Manager |
| **Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation** | Confirmation that ordered goods/services were received | Purchasing, AP, Manager |
| **Purchasing Confirmation Email/System Note** | Confirmation from Purchasing that invoice aligns with PO and receipt | Purchasing, AP, Manager |
| **Corrected Invoice** | Revised invoice from supplier if original was incorrect | Supplier, AP, Purchasing |
| **Contract / Agreed Terms** | Used to resolve pricing discrepancies | Supplier Relationship Manager, Purchasing, AP |
| **Manager Approval Notification** | Automatic system-generated approval request | Accounting System, Manager |
| **Approval Decision** | Manager’s approve/reject/hold decision | Manager, AP |
| **Payment Schedule / Payment Run Entry** | Final AP output after approval | AP |

---

## 6. Business Rules Captured

1. **Invoices normally require a valid PO before processing can continue.**
2. **Invoices without a PO are returned to the supplier or otherwise investigated.**
3. **Purchasing must confirm that goods/services were received and that invoice details align with the PO.**
4. **AP performs invoice-to-PO matching before triggering manager approval.**
5. **Manager approval is triggered only after receipt confirmation and successful invoice matching.**
6. **Discrepant invoices are placed on hold until clarified or corrected.**
7. **Unresolved discrepancies are escalated to the AP Supervisor.**
8. **The Supplier Relationship Manager may assist with supplier communication or contract/pricing clarification but does not approve invoices in the system.**
9. **The AP Supervisor handles exceptions but does not perform final approval.**
10. **After manager approval, AP schedules the invoice for payment.**
11. **Invoices may be rejected if no valid PO can be established, discrepancies cannot be resolved, or the manager rejects the invoice.**

## 7. Noted Ambiguities / Variations from Interviews

1. **Timing of detailed invoice matching:**  
   - Mary describes AP matching invoice line items after Purchasing confirms receipt.  
   - Dan describes Purchasing also comparing quantities, prices, and charges.  
   - The model treats Purchasing review and AP matching as two related but distinct checks.

2. **Who contacts the supplier during discrepancies:**  
   - Dan says Purchasing may contact the supplier directly or ask AP to hold the invoice.  
   - Mary mentions emailing Purchasing for clarification.  
   - Karen may be looped in for supplier relationship or contract issues.  
   - The model allows AP, Purchasing, or the Supplier Relationship Manager to participate depending on the discrepancy.

3. **Missing PO handling:**  
   - Mary says invoices without a PO are sent back to the supplier.  
   - Rita says some no-PO invoices may relate to informally contracted services and may require retrospective PO creation or rejection.  
   - The model includes both the standard “request missing PO” path and an escalated retrospective PO path.

4. **Manager rejection vs. investigation:**  
   - Carlos may reject an invoice outright or ask Mary to hold and investigate.  
   - The model includes both an immediate rejection end event and an investigation/rework loop.
