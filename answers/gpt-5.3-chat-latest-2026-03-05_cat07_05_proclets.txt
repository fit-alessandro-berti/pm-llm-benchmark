The visualization shows a **process model of a medical laboratory workflow** and how different participants and systems communicate during a lab visit and testing process.

Key parts of the diagram:

1. **Main process flow (left and center)**  
   - The process starts with a **Lab visit** where a **doctor enters an order** in an **Order entry system**.  
   - A **lab assistant takes a blood sample** from the patient.  
   - The sample triggers a **Lab test process**, where a **chemical analyst performs the test** and **creates a report**.  
   - Results are sent through a **Health Information System (HIS)** channel back to the lab side where results are **received and reviewed**.  
   - If the results are **not satisfactory**, the process loops: **tests are performed again**, a **new report is produced**, and **updated results are sent**.  
   - When everything is fine, the **lab assistant sends the final report** and the process ends.

2. **Communication channels (dashed boxes)**  
   - Two system channels connect the processes:  
     - **Order system** – used to send the initial lab test request.  
     - **HIS (Health Information System)** – used to exchange results and updates between the lab and testing process.

3. **Participants (roles)**  
   - **Doctor** – creates the lab order.  
   - **Lab assistant** – collects samples, checks results, and sends reports.  
   - **Chemical analyst** – performs the lab tests and prepares reports.

4. **Class relationship diagram (top right)**  
   - Shows that a **Lab visit requires one or more Lab tests**.  
   - Multiplicity:  
     - A lab visit is associated with **1..*** lab tests.  
     - Each lab test belongs to **exactly one lab visit (1..1)**.

5. **Performative message example (bottom table)**  
   - An example communication message:  
     - **Time:** 11:00  
     - **Channel:** Order system  
     - **Sender:** Lab visit – John  
     - **Receiver:** Lab test – HGB  
     - **Action:** Create  
     - **Content:** “Can you perform an HGB test for John?”  
     - **Scope:** Private  
     - **Direction:** Outgoing

Overall, the visualization illustrates **how a lab visit triggers lab tests, how results flow between systems and actors, and how the workflow handles retesting and reporting**.
