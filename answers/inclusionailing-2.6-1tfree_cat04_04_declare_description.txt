Below is a practical, step-by-step description of the loan approval process encoded in the DECLARE model, followed by an explanation of how each constraint enforces compliant, logically ordered behavior, the real-world motivations for these rules, and the likely consequences if the process were violated.

---

## 1. End-to-end process in practical terms

1) **Receive_Application**  
The customer’s journey begins when a loan application is formally accepted by the institution. This is an *init* and *existence* activity, meaning every valid process instance must start here and it must occur at least once.

2) **Preliminary_Credit_Check**  
Immediately after the application is received, a *response* constraint requires that a preliminary credit check be performed. This is also marked as *exactly_one*, meaning it must occur once and only once per application. Its purpose is to screen for obvious credit risk before investing effort in document collection.

3) **Gather_Additional_Documents**  
A *succession* constraint enforces that document collection follows the credit check. This sequencing reflects operational good sense: why request sensitive documents from a customer who is already disqualified by credit risk? Document collection is also *coexistent* with **Authorize_Contract_Terms**, meaning these two activities must both occur in the same case and neither can be omitted.

4) **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package**  
As documents come in, the institution prepares the offer (rates, terms, disclosures). A *responded_existence* rule links this activity to **Quality_Assurance_Review**: whenever an offer is assembled, a QA review must also occur. A *chain* relationship then ensures that QA review is directly followed by assembling the offer, creating a tight, sequential “prepare–review–finalize” loop.

5) **Quality_Assurance_Review**  
QA checks that the assembled offer complies with internal policies and external regulations. It is *preceded* by **Authorize_Contract_Terms** (in a precedence rule), meaning authorization cannot happen until QA has already taken place. A *chainsuccession* from QA to offer assembly reinforces the order: QA must not be skipped, and it must directly precede assembling the final package.

6) **Authorize_Contract_Terms**  
Formal approval of the loan terms occurs after QA and in alignment with **Gather_Additional_Documents** (coexistence) and **Preliminary_Credit_Check** (chain precedence). This ensures authorization is grounded in both verified documents and an earlier credit assessment.

7) **Transfer_Funds**  
Funds are disbursed only after **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package** (chain response), meaning the offer must be fully prepared and routed for approval before any money moves. Critically, a *noncoexistence* rule forbids **Transfer_Funds** from occurring in the same case as **Receive_Application**; this prevents premature or automatic disbursements before checks and reviews.

8) **Notify_Customer**  
The customer is informed of the decision and next steps. An *altresponse/altprecedence* pair governs **Notify_Customer** and **Transfer_Funds**: either the customer is notified immediately before funds are transferred or in an alternative, tightly linked sequence. A *nonsuccession* constraint also prevents notification from directly following the preliminary credit check, ensuring customers are not given premature or misleading signals.

Throughout the process, **Proceed_Without_Compliance** is *absent* by rule, acting as a hard constraint that any violation of compliance protocols must never occur.

---

## 2. How constraints enforce compliant, logical ordering

- **Init and existence (Receive_Application)**  
  Guarantee every process instance has an auditable starting point and is not missing intake.

- **Exactly_one (Preliminary_Credit_Check)**  
  Prevents duplicate or redundant checks that waste resources or cause contradictory decisions.

- **Response and succession (Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents)**  
  Enforce a sensible early sequence: qualify the customer before requesting documents, preserving customer experience and operational efficiency.

- **Coexistence (Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms)**  
  Ensures authorization always relies on gathered evidence and that evidence gathering is never pointless or incomplete.

- **Precedence and chainsuccession (QA  Authorize, QA  Assemble_Offer  QA)**  
  Make sure nothing is formally approved before being vetted, and that reviews loop tightly into offer finalization.

- **Responded existence (Assemble_Offer  QA)**  
  Prevents “ghost” offers being created without review, reducing compliance and quality failures.

- **Chainresponse and noncoexistence (Assemble_Offer  Transfer_Funds; not with Receive_Application)**  
  Ensure funds move only after a complete, reviewed offer exists—and never at the very start of a case, which would bypass all controls.

- **Altresponse/altprecedence and nonsuccession (Notify  Transfer; not after Preliminary_Credit_Check)**  
  Regulate communication timing so customers are not misled by early notifications and are always informed in coordination with actual disbursement events.

- **Absence (Proceed_Without_Compliance = forbidden)**  
  Acts as a hard stop against any non-compliant shortcut.

---

## 3. Real-world motivations behind these rules

- **Regulatory compliance**  
  Many jurisdictions require documented credit assessments, full disclosure before funding, and separation of duties between preparation, review, and approval. QA and precedence rules operationalize these requirements.

- **Fraud prevention**  
  Sequencing checks (credit check  documents  QA  approval) limits opportunities for insiders to fast-track risky or fraudulent loans without oversight.

- **Risk management**  
  Early credit screening reduces wasted effort; coexistence between documents and authorization ensures decisions are evidence-based; QA reduces model, pricing, or legal errors before funds move.

- **Operational best practices**  
  Exactly-once constraints reduce rework; chain constraints enforce predictable handovers and reduce queue times; absence and noncoexistence rules eliminate costly rework from procedural violations.

- **Customer satisfaction**  
  Logical ordering avoids requesting sensitive documents unnecessarily, prevents confusing or premature notifications, and ensures that once a customer receives an offer, it is complete and reliable.

---

## 4. Consequences of violating order or omitting steps

- **Skipping Preliminary_Credit_Check**  
  High risk of processing applications that are unlikely to be approved, wasting operational capacity and potentially exposing the institution to avoidable defaults or fair-lending scrutiny.

- **Gathering documents before any credit check**  
  Poor customer experience and unnecessary data privacy exposure; may breach internal policies requiring proportionality in data collection.

- **Omitting or bypassing QA**  
  Defective offers (incorrect rates, missing disclosures) may reach customers, causing legal exposure, reputational harm, and potential regulatory sanctions.

- **Authorizing terms before QA**  
  Approvals based on unchecked or incorrect information could lead to enforcement actions and financial losses.

- **Transferring funds before assembling a reviewed offer**  
  Funds could move on incomplete or incorrect terms, creating reconciliation nightmares, potential fraud losses, and immediate regulatory breach.

- **Notifying customers prematurely (e.g., right after credit check)**  
  Customers may be told they are “approved” before terms are finalized or QA is complete, leading to confusion, expectation gaps, and potential disputes or complaints.

- **Proceeding without compliance (absence violation)**  
  Represents a breakdown in controls, likely triggering audit findings, fines, or loss of license in regulated environments.

- **Duplicating or missing exactly-once steps**  
  Duplicates waste time and create contradictory records; omissions create gaps that undermine audit trails and decision integrity.

---

## Summary
The DECLARE model encodes a disciplined, compliance-first loan approval workflow. It requires intake, credit qualification, evidence collection, offer preparation and review, formal approval, controlled disbursement, and coordinated customer communication—each in a permissible order and with no tolerance for compliance shortcuts. These constraints directly mirror real-world needs: regulatory adherence, fraud and risk controls, operational reliability, and a trustworthy customer experience. Deviations from the model tend to produce regulatory, financial, or reputational harm, underscoring why the encoded constraints exist.
