**BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval Process**

**Process Name:** Invoice Approval Process  
**Process Owner:** Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)  
**Participants (Lanes):**  
- Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)  
- Purchasing Officer (Dan)  
- Department Manager (Carlos)  
- Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)  
- Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)  

---

### **Main Process Flow**

**Start Event:** Invoice Received (via email)

**Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk**  
1. **Task:** Check for Valid Purchase Order (PO) Number  
   **Exclusive Gateway: PO Number Present?**  
   - **No**  **Task:** Request PO/details from Supplier (via Karen or direct contact)  
      **End Event (Variant):** Invoice Returned to Supplier (or held pending correction)  
   - **Yes**  **Task:** Log Invoice into Accounting System  

2. **Task:** Forward Invoice to Purchasing Department for Confirmation  

**Lane: Purchasing Officer**  
3. **Task:** Verify Invoice against PO (quantities, prices, delivery charges, and receipt confirmation)  
   **Exclusive Gateway: Invoice Details Match PO?**  
   - **No**  **Task:** Request Correction from Supplier (directly or via Karen)  
      **Intermediate Event:** Hold Invoice until corrected version received  
      Loop back to Verification task  
   - **Yes**  **Task:** Send Confirmation Email to Accounts Payable Clerk  

**Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk**  
4. **Task:** Perform Line-Item Matching (Invoice vs. PO)  
   **Exclusive Gateway: Matching Successful?**  
   - **No**  **Task:** Email Purchasing for Clarification  
     **Exclusive Gateway: Discrepancy Resolved?**  
     - **No**  **Escalate to Accounts Payable Supervisor** (see Exception Handling)  
     - **Yes**  Proceed to Manager Approval  
   - **Yes**  **Task:** Route Invoice to Department Manager for Approval (system-triggered notification)  

**Lane: Department Manager**  
5. **Task:** Review Invoice, PO, and Confirmation Details  
   **Exclusive Gateway: Approve Invoice?**  
   - **Yes**  **Task:** Approve in System  
   - **No**  **Task:** Reject or Request Further Investigation (return to Accounts Payable Clerk)  

**Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk**  
6. **Task:** Schedule Invoice for Payment (add to next payment run)  

**End Event:** Payment Scheduled

---

### **Exception Handling (Parallel/Alternative Flow)**

**Lane: Accounts Payable Supervisor** (triggered by escalation from AP Clerk or Purchasing)  
- **Task:** Investigate and Resolve Exception  
  - Contact Supplier (via Karen if needed)  
  - Coordinate with departments  
  - Create Retrospective PO (for invoices without PO)  
  - Decide on Rejection (if unresolvable)  

**After Resolution:**  
- Return to main flow at **Line-Item Matching** step (or earlier, depending on issue)  

**Lane: Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)** (support role)  
- **Task:** Resolve Supplier-Related Issues (pricing discrepancies, missing PO, contract verification)  
- Triggered on-demand from AP Clerk, Purchasing, or Supervisor  
- Output: Corrected information or revised invoice provided to relevant party

---

### **Data Objects / Artifacts**

- **Invoice** (input)  
- **Purchase Order (PO)**  
- **Confirmation of Receipt** (from Purchasing)  
- **Approval Record** (system-generated)  
- **Payment Schedule** (output)  

---

### **Key Notes & Variations**

- The process is primarily **linear** with **exclusive gateways** at verification and approval points.
- **Receipt confirmation** and **line-item matching** are closely related; some accounts treat them as a single verification step performed by Purchasing.
- **Escalation** to the Supervisor occurs when discrepancies cannot be resolved quickly (estimated low frequency).
- **No-PO invoices** are handled as exceptions rather than following the standard path.
- The system plays a supporting role by logging invoices, sending notifications, and storing approvals, but human judgment remains central at the Manager approval step.
- Payment scheduling is the final step; actual payment execution is assumed to occur in a separate process.

This textual model can be directly translated into standard BPMN notation with the lanes, tasks, exclusive gateways, and exception flows as described.
