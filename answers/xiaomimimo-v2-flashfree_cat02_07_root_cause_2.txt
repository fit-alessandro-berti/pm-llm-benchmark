The time required to wait for the customer to provide these documents is the primary driver of delays.

**Conclusion:** **Complexity is the primary driver of performance issues.** High and medium complexity claims inherently take longer because they require back-and-forth communication with the claimant to gather necessary information.

### **3. Summary of Findings and Mitigation Suggestions**

#### **Summary of Root Causes**

1.  **Primary Root Cause:** **Claim Complexity.** The event log clearly demonstrates that low-complexity claims are processed in under 2 hours, while medium and high-complexity claims take over a day and up to 3-4 days, respectively.
2.  **Direct Consequence:** **Multiple Requests for Additional Documents.** The need for additional documentation is the main procedural bottleneck. Each request adds a significant waiting period, causing the case duration to spike. The more complex the claim, the more documentation is required, leading to more requests and longer delays.
3.  **Secondary Factors:** **Resource and Region** do not appear to be primary drivers of the delays. The assignment of resources is likely correlated with claim complexity, but the complexity itself is the underlying cause of the delay.

#### **Suggestions for Mitigation**

To improve process performance and reduce case duration, the following actions are recommended:

1.  **Improve First-Contact Data Quality:**
    *   **Action:** Create more robust and guided "Submit Claim" forms (digital or physical) that prompt the claimant for the necessary information based on the type of claim.
    *   **Benefit:** This can reduce the number of incomplete submissions and decrease the need for "Request Additional Documents," especially for medium-complexity claims.

2.  **Streamline the Documentation Request Process:**
    *   **Action:** Implement an automated, user-friendly portal for claimants to upload requested documents directly. Set up automated reminders for claimants if documents are not submitted within a certain timeframe.
    *   **Benefit:** This would reduce the manual follow-up time and waiting periods, which is the biggest source of delay.

3.  **Analyze High-Complexity Workflows:**
    *   **Action:** Conduct a deeper analysis of the 3-5 most complex claim types. Identify the most commonly requested documents and see if they can be collected proactively at the initial submission stage.
    *   **Benefit:** Proactively collecting difficult-to-obtain documents can significantly shorten the turnaround time for the most problematic cases.

4.  **Resource Specialization and Training:**
    *   **Action:** While resources were not a primary cause, ensure that adjusters and managers handling high-complexity claims are well-trained and have clear Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for reviewing and approving documents.
    *   **Benefit:** Ensures that once documents are received, the internal processing time is minimized.
