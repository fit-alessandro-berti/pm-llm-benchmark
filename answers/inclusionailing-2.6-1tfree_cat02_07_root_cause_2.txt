### 2. Analyze attributes to deduce root causes  

#### A. Complexity  
| Case | Complexity | # of “Request Additional Documents” events | Total duration |
|------|------------|--------------------------------------------|----------------|
| 2001 | Low        | 0                                          | ~1.5 h         |
| 2002 | Medium     | 1                                          | ~26 h          |
| 2003 | High       | 2                                          | ~48 h          |
| 2004 | Low        | 0                                          | ~1.4 h         |
| 2005 | High       | 3                                          | ~77 h          |

Observation:  
- Low-complexity cases have no document requests and finish quickly.  
- Medium/high-complexity cases have 1–3 document requests and take much longer.  
- High-complexity cases tend to have multiple document requests, which create long waiting periods between “Evaluate Claim” and “Approve Claim.”

#### B. Region  
| Region | Cases (by duration) | Typical complexity in this region |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------------------------|
| A      | 2001 (fast), 2003 (slow) | Low, High                        |
| B      | 2002 (slow), 2004 (fast), 2005 (very slow) | Medium, Low, High |

Region alone does not explain the slowdown cleanly:  
- Region A has both a very fast (2001) and a slow (2003) case.  
- Region B has fast (2004) and very slow (2002, 2005) cases.  
This suggests region is not the primary driver—complexity and resource handling matter more.

#### C. Resource patterns  

**Resources by role and activity:**
- **CSR** (Submit/Close): CSR_Jane, CSR_Paul, CSR_Mary – these are not on the critical path for delay.  
- **Adjuster** (Evaluate, Request Additional Documents): Adjuster_Mike (A), Adjuster_Lisa (B).  
- **Manager** (Approve): Manager_Ann (mostly A and B low/medium), Manager_Bill (A and B high).  
- **Finance** (Pay): Finance_Alan, Finance_Carl – payment steps are short once approval is given.

**Key pattern:**  
- Cases with **multiple “Request Additional Documents”** are all handled by Adjuster_Lisa (2002, 2005) or Adjuster_Mike (2003).  
- High-complexity cases repeatedly ask for more documents, causing large gaps (e.g., 2005: requests on 4/1, 4/2, 4/3; approval only on 4/4).  
- Manager_Bill appears only on high-complexity approvals (2003, 2005), and those approvals come after many document requests.

**Why resources matter:**  
- Adjusters are the ones initiating document requests; if they are not providing clear guidance or timely follow-up, customers may take long to respond, or the adjuster may need multiple rounds to be satisfied.  
- Managers for high-complexity claims appear to be a bottleneck only after document issues are resolved (i.e., they don’t cause the delay directly, but they can’t approve until documents are complete).

### 3. Root cause explanations and mitigation suggestions  

#### Root causes  

1. **High claim complexity leads to multiple document requests**  
   - High-complexity claims (2003, 2005) require 2–3 rounds of additional documents.  
   - Each request adds waiting time for customer response and adjuster re-evaluation, stretching the “Evaluate  Approve” phase from hours to days.

2. **Adjuster handling of document requests is inconsistent or unclear**  
   - Adjusters (Lisa, Mike) are responsible for requesting documents. Multiple rounds suggest either:  
     - Initial requests are incomplete or ambiguous, so customers submit insufficient information.  
     - Adjusters are not reviewing submissions promptly, causing delays in recognizing that more is needed.  
   - This creates a cycle of “request  wait  request again,” which dominates case duration.

3. **No visible work to keep progress during waiting periods**  
   - While waiting for customer documents, cases appear idle. There is no evidence of follow-up reminders or escalation paths to accelerate customer response.

#### Mitigation suggestions  

- **Standardize document checklists by complexity at evaluation time**  
  - For high-complexity claims, provide a comprehensive first-time document list to reduce back-and-forth.  
  - Use templates or automated checklists to ensure adjusters request everything needed upfront.

- **Set service-level agreements (SLAs) for document turnaround and adjuster review**  
  - Define clear deadlines for customers to submit documents (e.g., 48 hours) and for adjusters to review submissions (e.g., 24 hours after receipt).  
  - Automate reminders to customers when deadlines approach.

- **Introduce a “document completeness” review step before leaving evaluation**  
  - Before marking evaluation as complete, require a quick internal check that all required documents are present.  
  - This can reduce multiple request cycles.

- **Monitor adjuster performance on document request efficiency**  
  - Track metrics like average number of document requests per claim and average time to close after last request.  
  - Provide coaching or redistribute workloads if certain adjusters consistently have high re-request rates.

- **Use parallel or proactive handling where possible**  
  - For high-complexity claims, consider early involvement of specialized teams or pre-approval pathways for certain claim types, to shorten the end-to-end timeline.

By focusing on reducing document request cycles and improving the clarity and timeliness of adjuster interactions, the process can achieve more consistent and faster case completion across all complexity levels.
