# Invoice Approval Process — BPMN-Style Textual Model

## 1. Pools and Lanes (Roles/Participants)

**Pool: Company (Invoice Approval Process)**
- **Lane 1:** Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)
- **Lane 2:** Purchasing Officer (Dan)
- **Lane 3:** Department Manager (Carlos)
- **Lane 4:** Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita) — exception handling
- **Lane 5:** Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen) — supporting role, exception handling

**Pool: Supplier (External Participant)** — communicates via message flows only

**Systems referenced:** Accounting System (invoice logging, matching, approval workflow, automated notification emails)

---

## 2. Events

- **Start Event (Message):** *Invoice received from Supplier* (typically via email) — Lane: AP Clerk
- **End Event 1:** *Invoice scheduled for payment* (added to payment run/cycle) — Lane: AP Clerk
- **End Event 2:** *Invoice rejected* — terminal outcome (via Manager rejection or Supervisor decision on non-PO invoices)

---

## 3. Main Sequence Flow

**T1. Receive Invoice** *(AP Clerk)*
- Input artifact: **Invoice** (email)
-  G1

**G1. Exclusive Gateway: "Valid PO number present?"** *(AP Clerk)*
- **No ** **T2. Return Invoice to Supplier / Request PO Details** *(AP Clerk)*
  - Message flow to Supplier pool; supplier responds with revised invoice or PO reference  loops back to **T1/G1**
  - *Variation:* Karen (Supplier Relationship Manager) may be looped in to contact the supplier and obtain the PO number or a revised invoice (see Exceptions, E3)
  - *Variation:* If no PO exists at all (e.g., informally contracted services), the invoice is escalated to the AP Supervisor (see Exceptions, E2)
- **Yes ** T3

**T3. Log Invoice in Accounting System** *(AP Clerk)*
- Output artifact: **Logged Invoice Record** (in Accounting System)
-  T4

**T4. Forward Invoice to Purchasing for Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation** *(AP Clerk)*
- Message/handoff to Purchasing Officer
-  T5

**T5. Verify Invoice Against PO** *(Purchasing Officer)*
- Compares quantities, prices, delivery charges against the **Purchase Order (PO)**
- *Note:* Dan also checks for a confirmed PO reference; if missing, he sends it back to the AP Clerk (loops to G1/T2 path)
-  G2

**G2. Exclusive Gateway: "Invoice details match PO?"** *(Purchasing Officer)*
- **No (discrepancy, e.g., billed for extra units, incorrect pricing, missing items) ** Exception handling subprocess **E1** (see Section 4), then return to **T5** for re-verification once resolved
- **Yes ** **T6. Send Confirmation to Accounts Payable** *(Purchasing Officer)*
  - Output artifact: **Confirmation Email**
  -  T7

**T7. Match Invoice Line Items Against PO in System** *(AP Clerk)*
- *Note on ambiguity:* Mary describes matching as her own step performed *after* Purchasing's confirmation; Dan describes the comparison as happening in Purchasing. The consolidated model treats them as two checks: Purchasing confirms receipt and correctness (T5), then AP performs/records the system match (T7). A modeler may alternatively collapse T5 and T7 into a single matching activity.
-  G3

**G3. Exclusive Gateway: "Match successful?"** *(AP Clerk)*
- **No ** Exception handling **E1** (clarify with Purchasing; escalate if unresolved)
- **Yes ** T8

**T8. System Sends Approval Request to Department Manager** *(Accounting System — automated)*
- Trigger condition: receipt confirmed **AND** invoice matched to PO
- Output: automatic notification email with invoice details, PO, and confirmation
-  T9

**T9. Review and Approve/Reject Invoice** *(Department Manager)*
- Inputs: Invoice, PO, Purchasing confirmation (visible in system)
-  G4

**G4. Exclusive Gateway: "Manager decision"**
- **Approve ** T10
- **Reject / Hold (suspicious items, large budget variance, unexpected items — occurs roughly once every couple of months) ** **T11. Hold Invoice and Investigate** *(AP Clerk, possibly with AP Supervisor)*  after resolution, loops back to **T9** (re-approval), or  **End Event 2 (Invoice Rejected)** if rejected outright

**T10. Schedule Invoice for Payment / Add to Payment Cycle** *(AP Clerk)*
-  **End Event 1: Invoice scheduled for payment**

## 4. Exception Subprocesses and Variations

**E1. Discrepancy Resolution Subprocess** (triggered from G2-No or G3-No)
1. **T-E1a. Clarify Discrepancy with Purchasing** *(AP Clerk  Purchasing Officer)* — email exchange
2. **G-E1. Gateway: "Resolvable at clerk/purchasing level?"**
   - **Yes ** Options (per Dan): contact supplier directly **or** hold invoice until supplier sends a corrected invoice  corrected invoice re-enters at **T5** (re-verification)
   - **No ** **T-E1b. Escalate to AP Supervisor** *(AP Clerk  Supervisor)*
3. **T-E1c. Coordinate Resolution** *(AP Supervisor)* — contacts suppliers and/or coordinates between departments; may involve Karen (see E3)
4. After resolution  invoice **re-enters normal flow** at Purchasing confirmation (T5/T6), then Manager approval (T9), then scheduling (T10)

**E2. Non-PO Invoice Handling Subprocess** *(AP Supervisor)* (triggered when an invoice matches no PO, e.g., informally contracted services)
1. **T-E2a. Coordinate with Department Heads** *(AP Supervisor)*
2. **G-E2. Gateway: "Create retrospective PO or reject?"**
   - **Retrospective PO created ** invoice re-enters normal flow at **T3 (Log Invoice)** / T5
   - **Reject ** **End Event 2: Invoice Rejected**

**E3. Supplier Liaison Support** *(Supplier Relationship Manager — Karen; supporting activity, no system access)*
- Triggered by AP or Purchasing during E1 or the missing-PO path:
  - **T-E3a. Contact Supplier for Missing PO / Revised Invoice**
  - **T-E3b. Verify Correct Pricing Against Contract** — input artifact: **Supplier Contract**; output: correct price communicated to AP or Purchasing
- Karen's activities are best modeled as part of the exception subprocesses (message flows to the Supplier pool), not as steps on the main sequence flow.

## 6. Noted Ambiguities and Consolidation Decisions

1. **Duplicate matching step:** Mary (AP) and Dan (Purchasing) each describe comparing the invoice to the PO. Modeled as two distinct checks (T5 by Purchasing, T7 by AP in the system), consistent with Mary's statement that she matches "once I get confirmation." If the organization considers this a single check, T5 and T7 can be merged.
2. **Re-entry point after exceptions:** Rita states resolved exceptions go "back into the normal flow: confirmation from Purchasing, approval by Manager, then scheduling," implying re-entry at **T5/T6** regardless of where the exception arose. This is the modeled assumption.
3. **Post-approval handoff:** Dan and Carlos both *assume* (uncertain language: "I think," "I believe") the approved invoice returns to AP for payment scheduling; Mary confirms this directly, so T10 is modeled with confidence.
4. **Manager hold vs. reject:** Carlos describes both rejecting and asking Mary to "hold and investigate." Modeled as two outcomes of G4: a rework loop (hold  investigate  re-approve) and a terminal rejection. The criteria distinguishing the two are not specified in the interviews.
5. **Supplier responses on missing PO:** Whether the supplier's corrected invoice restarts the process at T1 or resumes at T3 is not stated; modeled as a loop back to T1/G1 (re-validation of the PO reference) as the safer interpretation.

## 7. Compact Flow Summary (for diagramming)

```
[Start: Invoice received]
   (G1: PO valid?)
      No  [T2: Request PO from supplier]  (loop to G1)
             alt: no PO exists  [E2: Supervisor — retro PO or Reject]
      Yes  [T3: Log invoice]  [T4: Forward to Purchasing]
   [T5: Purchasing verifies vs. PO]
   (G2: Match?)
      No  [E1: Discrepancy resolution (Clerk/Purchasing/Supervisor/Karen)]  (loop to T5)
      Yes  [T6: Send confirmation to AP]
   [T7: AP matches line items in system]
   (G3: Match?)
      No  [E1]  (loop to T5)
      Yes  [T8: System sends approval request]
   [T9: Manager reviews]
   (G4: Decision?)
      Hold  [T11: Investigate]  (loop to T9)
      Reject  [End: Invoice rejected]
      Approve  [T10: Schedule for payment]
   [End: Invoice scheduled for payment]
```
