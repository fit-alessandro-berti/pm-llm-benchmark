### 2. Root Causes of Performance Issues

By dissecting the delayed cases (102, 104, 105), three distinct patterns emerge as the primary drivers of increased cycle time.

#### A. The Escalation Bottleneck (Case 105 & 102)
Cases that require escalation to Level-2 (L2) support experience massive delays.
*   **Queue Waiting Time:** In **Case 105**, the ticket was escalated at 10:00 on March 1st, but the L2 investigation did not start until 14:00 on March 2nd. This resulted in a **28-hour wait** simply to transfer the ticket to a specialized agent.
*   **Comparison:** Even in **Case 102**, there was a 2.5-hour gap between the L1 assignment and the escalation, suggesting L1 agents may spend too much time attempting to resolve issues before escalating them, or the handoff process is slow.

#### B. The "Overnight Resolution" Pattern (Cases 102, 104, 105)
There is a recurring pattern where tickets investigated late in the day are not resolved until the next morning.
*   In **Case 102**, investigation started at 14:00, but resolution occurred at 09:00 the next day (a 19-hour gap).
*   In **Case 104**, investigation started at 13:00, but resolution occurred at 08:00 the next day (a 19-hour gap).
*   **Contrast with Fast Cases:** In the fast cases (101 & 103), the "Investigate" and "Resolve" steps happened within the same business morning.
*   **Insight:** This suggests that once an investigation spills into the afternoon, the resolution process halts until the next business day. This could be due to shift changes, lack of overtime authorization, or dependencies on external teams that operate on standard business hours.

#### C. Level-1 Agent Availability or Workload (Case 104)
**Case 104** is unique because it was slow despite **not** being escalated.
*   The ticket was assigned to a Level-1 agent at 09:30.
*   Investigation did not start until 13:00.
*   **Root Cause:** This **3.5-hour idle time** indicates that the Level-1 agent was likely overloaded, causing the ticket to sit in a queue. This delay pushed the investigation into the afternoon, triggering the "Overnight Resolution" pattern described above.

---

### 3. Insights and Recommendations

The delays are not caused by a single factor but by a combination of **resource bottlenecks** and **process timing mismatches**.

**Insights:**
1.  **Escalation is a "Time Trap":** The process for moving tickets from Level-1 to Level-2 is inefficient. Case 105 demonstrates that the queue time for L2 can exceed the actual working time by 10x.
2.  **Afternoon Start Penalty:** Tickets that enter the "Investigation" phase after roughly 13:00 appear to automatically incur a ~19-hour delay (waiting overnight). The process seems optimized for morning starts but lacks the throughput to close tickets started in the afternoon.
3.  **L1 Capacity Issues:** The long wait time before investigation in Case 104 suggests L1 agents are either understaffed or inefficiently distributed during peak hours.

**Recommendations:**
1.  **Optimize Level-2 Handoff:**
    *   Implement a "Warm Handoff" protocol where L1 agents brief L2 agents immediately rather than dumping tickets into a queue.
    *   Review L2 staffing levels; the 28-hour wait in Case 105 suggests a severe shortage of specialized resources.

2.  **Implement "End-of-Day" Resolution Protocols:**
    *   Investigate why tickets investigated in the afternoon aren't resolved until the next morning. If tickets are complex, implement a "Status Update" activity at the end of the shift to inform the customer, rather than leaving the ticket in limbo.
    *   Consider a "Follow-the-Sun" model or extended support hours for tickets that enter the investigation phase late in the day.

3.  **Balance Level-1 Workload:**
    *   The 3.5-hour wait in Case 104 suggests a need for better workload balancing. Monitor L1 agent queues in real-time to reassign tickets if specific agents are backlogged.

4.  **Triage Efficiency:**
    *   In Case 102, L1 held the ticket for 2.5 hours before escalating. Triage rules should be sharpened to identify L2-necessary tickets earlier, bypassing the L1 attempt entirely to save time.
