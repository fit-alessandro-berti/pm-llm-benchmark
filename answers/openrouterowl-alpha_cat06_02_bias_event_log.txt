# Bias Analysis of the Underwriting Process

Based on a careful review of the event log, several forms of bias are evident — both structural and discretionary — that raise significant fairness and equity concerns.

---

## 1. Geographic Residency Disparity

| Attribute | Pattern Observed |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **LocalResident = TRUE** | C001, C002, C004 — all eventually **Approved** |
| **LocalResident = FALSE** | C003 (**Rejected**), C005 (**Approved**) |

While C005 shows approval is possible for non-residents, the single rejection among locals versus the higher score thresholds and mixed outcomes for non-residents suggest an **implicit geographic preference**. Non-residents appear held to a stricter standard or receive less benefit of the doubt during manual review. The system effectively treats "non-local" as a risk proxy, even when C005 demonstrates a case (score 740, non-resident, no community group) that was approved — indicating the bias is inconsistent and **discretionary at the human review stage**, not purely rules-based. This unpredictability is itself a fairness concern.

---

## 2. Community Group Affiliation Adjustment

One of the most striking biases is the **automatic +10 ScoreAdjustment** granted to members of the **Highland Civic Darts Club**:

| Case | Community Group | Adjustment | Score Impact |
|------|----------------|------------|--------------|
| C001 | Darts Club | **+10** | 710  720 |
| C004 | Darts Club | **+10** | 690  700 |
| C002 | None | **0** | 720 (unchanged) |
| C003 | None | **0** | 715 (unchanged) |
| C005 | None | **0** | 740 (unchanged) |

**This is a direct, quantifiable bias.** The community membership acts as an automatic score booster at the PreliminaryScoring stage — a mechanical advantage that non-members can never receive regardless of their individual creditworthiness.

### Critical Contrast: C004 vs. C005

| Case | Resident | Community | Score | Decision |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|----------|
| **C004** | TRUE | Darts Club | **690  700** | **Approved** |
| **C005** | FALSE | None | **740** | **Approved** |
| **C003** | FALSE | None | **715** | **Rejected** |

Consider C004's trajectory carefully:
- With a **base score of 690**, C004's original score is **25 points lower** than C003 (715) and **50 points lower** than C005 (740).
- However, C004 benefits from *both* local residency **and** the Darts Club +10 boost.
- After the adjustment, C004 reaches 700 and is approved.

Meanwhile, C003 — despite having a **715** base score (higher than C004's unadjusted 690) — receives no adjustment and is **rejected**. This means someone objectively scored higher by 25 points was denied because they lacked the two structural advantages (local + community).

---

## 3. Manual Review Stage — Subjective Override and Inconsistency

The ManualReview column reveals significant **human discretion**:

- **Reviewer #4** rejected C003 at 715
- **Reviewer #2** approved C004 at 700 (adjusted)
- **Reviewer #3** approved C002 at 720

**A 15-point score advantage (715 vs 700) resulted in opposite outcomes.** This suggests that manual reviewers are exercising judgment that systematically disadvantages non-local, unaffiliated applicants. The "Underwriter" resource performing manual review is introducing a layer of **subjective bias** that compounds the structural disadvantages already embedded in the scoring system.

Moreover, the manual reviewers appear unaware of or indifferent to the inconsistency — different reviewers (#2, #3, #4, #5) make decisions that don't follow a consistent score threshold for approval.

---

## 4. Systemic Feedback Loop

```
Community Member  +10 points  Higher score  Easier approval probability
                                                                  |
         |                                                         |
    Exclusion from            Reinforced by manual
    community groups           reviewer discretion
```

- Those **not** in community groups gain nothing from the Scoring Engine.
- The Rules Engine then sees a lower score.
- Manual reviewers, lacking context about *why* the score is lower (since the +10 isn't explained transparently), may distrust lower-scoring applications.
- This creates a **double penalty** for the unaffiliated.

---

## 5. Summary of Bias Mechanisms

| Bias Type | Mechanism | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Structural/Scoring Bias** | Automatic +10 for Darts Club members only | Quantifiable score advantage unrelated to creditworthiness |
| **Geographic Bias** | Preference for LocalResident = TRUE | Higher baseline consideration; non-residents face skepticism |
| **Review Bias** | Uneven manual reviewer standards | Same scores treated differently based on non-score attributes |
| **Data Validation Silence** | No adjustment but no equalization | Fails to mitigate downstream disadvantages |

---

## Recommendations for Fairness Improvement

1. **Eliminate or justify the Community Group adjustment** — If the +10 is meant to represent reduced risk, it must be empirically validated and applied uniformly across equivalent affiliations (not just one club).

2. **Standardize manual reviewer criteria** — Define explicit score thresholds and decision frameworks to prevent subjective inconsistency from disadvantaging applicants who lack social capital indicators.

3. **Introduce bias auditing** — The Automated System should flag cases where score adjustments and residency status interact to create compounding disadvantages.

4. **Ensure independence of the community adjustment** — Consider whether community membership is a legitimate risk factor or serving as a proxy for social class, geography, and existing privilege.

---

**In essence, this process rewards those who are already structurally advantaged (local residents with community affiliations) while penalizing equally or more creditworthy individuals who simply lack access to those social networks.** This is a classic pattern of algorithmic bias compounded by human discretion.
