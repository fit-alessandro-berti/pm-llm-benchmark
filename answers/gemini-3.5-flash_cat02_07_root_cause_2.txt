### **Part 2: Attribute Correlation & Root Cause Analysis**

By analyzing the attributes (**Complexity, Resource, and Region**), we can isolate three main root causes for these delays:

#### **1. Complexity & The "Document Request" Loop (Primary Driver)**
*   **Low Complexity (2001, 2004):** Processed smoothly in under **1.5 hours** with zero additional document requests.
*   **Medium Complexity (2002):** Required **1** document request, jumping the duration to **~26 hours**.
*   **High Complexity (2003, 2005):** Required **2 to 3** document requests, pushing durations to **48 and 77 hours** respectively.
*   *Root Cause:* The process does not have a "Receive Documents" activity, meaning the customer response time is bundled into the adjuster's waiting time. However, the need to repeatedly ask for documents (ping-ponging between the customer and adjuster) exponentially increases the case duration.

#### **2. Resource & Regional Disparities (Adjuster Efficiency)**
*   **Region A (Adjuster_Mike):** Handled the high-complexity Case 2003. He requested documents at 11:00 and again at 17:00 on Day 1. The gap between his requests was only **6 hours**.
*   **Region B (Adjuster_Lisa):** Handled the high-complexity Case 2005. Her document requests were spaced dramatically far apart:
    *   *Request 1 to Request 2:* **29.5 hours** gap (Apr 1, 11:30 to Apr 2, 17:00)
    *   *Request 2 to Request 3:* **22 hours** gap (Apr 2, 17:00 to Apr 3, 15:00)
*   *Root Cause:* Adjuster Lisa (Region B) is taking nearly a full business day to review returned documents and ask for the next set, whereas Adjuster Mike (Region A) is processing turnarounds much faster. This indicates either a **backlog/workload issue for Lisa** or **inefficient follow-up practices in Region B**.

#### **3. Manager Approval Latency**
*   Once the document loop is closed, getting the claim approved takes an excessive amount of time across the board:
    *   **Manager_Bill (Cases 2003 & 2005):** Took **23 hours** to approve Case 2003 (Apr 1, 17:00 to Apr 2, 16:00) and **19 hours** to approve Case 2005 (Apr 3, 15:00 to Apr 4, 10:00).
    *   **Manager_Ann (Case 2002):** Took **20 hours** to approve Case 2002.
*   *Root Cause:* Managers are likely reviewing claims in "batches" (e.g., once a day) rather than processing them dynamically. This adds an idle block of 19–23 hours of dead time to almost every non-low-complexity claim.
