Here is a series of targeted, open-ended questions organized by theme to help you map the nuances of this onboarding process:

### **Process Flow & Timing**
1. **Duration and Variability:** How much does the total timeline vary from property to property, and what typically accounts for the longest delays—documentation gaps, inspection repairs, or tenant screening?
2. **Sequencing Flexibility:** Are there any steps in this process that can happen in parallel rather than sequence, or conversely, any stages where you absolutely must wait for a hard gate before proceeding?
3. **Stalled Processes:** What happens when a property gets "stuck" at a particular stage for an extended period (for example, if a landlord goes unresponsive after the inspection)? Is there a formal timeout or off-ramping procedure?

### **Decision Criteria & Authority**
4. **Manager Assignment Logic:** When multiple property managers could technically handle a property, what acts as the tie-breaker—seniority, current capacity, or relationship history with that specific landlord?
5. **Pricing Authority:** Who has the final authority if there’s a disagreement between the property manager’s recommended rent, the financial analyst’s profitability targets, and the landlord’s expectations?
6. **Tenant Selection Conflicts:** When you have multiple qualified applicants, walk me through how you prioritize them. Are there ethical guidelines or business rules that prevent certain types of prioritization (e.g., favoring longer leases over higher income)?

### **Roles & Handoffs**
7. **Accountability Boundaries:** At what exact point does accountability shift from the property coordinator (who handles documentation) to the assigned property manager? Is there a formal sign-off or handoff meeting?
8. **Cross-Functional Coordination:** How do the photographer and inspector coordinate their visits—do they go together, separately, or does one trigger the other? Who manages that scheduling logistics?
9. **Compliance Intervention:** If the compliance officer finds issues with a lease that has already been signed by the landlord and tenant, who is responsible for renegotiation, and how is that uncomfortable conversation typically handled?

### **Exceptions & Edge Cases**
10. **Documentation Alternatives:** What happens when a landlord cannot produce a standard document—say, a deed is in probate or a certificate of compliance doesn’t exist for older buildings? Is there an exception pathway?
11. **Repair Disputes:** If an inspector identifies mandatory repairs but the landlord disputes the findings or refuses to pay, what are your options? Do you ever onboard a property "as-is" with disclosures, or is that a hard stop?
12. **Custom Clauses:** How do you handle landlords who insist on non-standard lease clauses (like unusual pet restrictions or maintenance exemptions)? Who reviews these for legal risk, and has there ever been a clause you had to reject outright?

### **Information & Communication**
13. **Data Integrity:** When property details are entered into the central database, what verification ensures accuracy? Have you ever caught discrepancies between the database and reality that affected marketing or pricing?
14. **Stakeholder Visibility:** How does the landlord track progress during the onboarding—is there a portal, regular check-ins, or do they wait for the manager to contact them? What’s their biggest frustration point regarding communication?
15. **Knowledge Transfer:** If a property manager leaves the company or falls ill during the onboarding process (say, between inspection and lease signing), how is context transferred to their replacement without losing the landlord’s trust?

### **Governance & Quality**
16. **Audit Triggers:** Are the compliance and financial audits conducted on every single property, or is there a risk-based sampling method? What would trigger a deeper audit?
17. **Rework Loops:** Can you describe a time when the IT integration check revealed a data error that required going back to a previous stage? How far back in the process did you have to rewind?
18. **Success Metrics:** Beyond simply filling the unit, how do you measure whether the onboarding process itself was successful? Do you track landlord satisfaction, speed-to-lease, or early tenant turnover rates?

### **Market Dynamics**
19. **Competitive Pressures:** How does the process change when the local rental market is extremely hot versus slow? Do you accelerate certain steps or skip optional ones?
20. **Marketing Customization:** What determines the intensity of marketing effort—do all properties get the same treatment, or do luxury units or large portfolios receive a different tier of photography and brochure development?

These questions should encourage the interviewee to reveal tacit knowledge, informal workarounds, and the political or relational dynamics that govern how work actually gets done versus how it’s documented.
