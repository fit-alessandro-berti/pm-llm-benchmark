### **Business Process Explanation**

#### **1. Overview**
This process manages customer requests, distinguishing between **standard** and **custom** orders. It ensures validation, feasibility analysis, parallel checks (for standard requests), and approval workflows before finalizing orders. The process emphasizes efficiency (parallel checks) and flexibility (rework loops for approvals).

---

#### **2. Step-by-Step Breakdown**

##### **Start Event  Task A ("Receive Customer Request")**
- **Purpose**: Initiates the process when a customer submits a request.  
- **Trigger**: Customer action (e.g., online form submission, email, or phone call).

---

##### **Gateway 1 (XOR): "Check Request Type"**
- **Decision Logic**: Splits the process into **standard** or **custom** workflows based on the request type.  
- **Why?**: Custom requests require feasibility analysis, while standard requests follow a streamlined validation path.

---

### **Standard Request Path (If Type = Standard)**

#### **Task B1: "Perform Standard Validation"**
- **Purpose**: Verify basic requirements (e.g., form accuracy, eligibility criteria).  
- *Example*: Checking if the customer has filled out all required fields or meets basic qualification thresholds.

#### **Gateway 2 (AND): "Run Parallel Checks"**
- **Structure**: Splits the process into **two parallel paths** (credit check and inventory check).  
- **Why Parallel?**: Saves time by running checks simultaneously.  
- **Join Condition**: Both tasks (C1 and C2) must complete before proceeding.

#### **Task C1: "Credit Check"**  
- **Purpose**: Assess the customer𠏋 creditworthiness (e.g., credit score, payment history).  

#### **Task C2: "Inventory Check"**  
- **Purpose**: Confirm product availability (e.g., stock levels, manufacturing capacity.  

#### **Task D: "Calculate Delivery Date"**  
- **Purpose**: Determine the delivery timeline based on inventory and credit check results.  
- **Trigger**: Only after both parallel checks (C1 & C2) are completed.

---

### **Custom Request Path (If Type = Custom)**

#### **Task B2: "Perform Custom Feasibility Analysis"**  
- **Purpose**: Evaluate technical or logistical viability of the custom request (e.g., engineering feasibility, cost estimation.  
- *Example*: For a custom product, this could involve design reviews or supply chain assessments.

#### **Gateway 3 (XOR): "Is Customization Feasible?"**  
- **Decision Logic**:  
  - **Yes  Task E1 ("Prepare Custom Quotation")**: Create a detailed proposal.  
  - **No  Task E2 ("Send Rejection Notice")**: Notify the customer, ending the process.  

---

### **Convergence & Approval Path**  
After completing **either** the standard (D) or custom (E1/E2) paths, the process converges at **Gateway 4 (XOR): "Is Approval Needed?"**  

#### **Approval Decision Logic**  
- **Approval Needed (Yes Path)**:  
  - **Task F ("Obtain Manager Approval")**: Required for high-value orders or complex requests.  
  - **Gateway 5 (XOR): "Is Approval Granted?"**:  
    - **Yes  Task G ("Generate Final Invoice")**: Proceed to invoicing.  
    - **No  Task H ("Re-evaluate Conditions")**: Iterate to address approval rejections.  
    - **Loopback**:  
      - For **Custom Paths**: Loop back to **E1** to revise the quotation.  
      - For **Standard Paths**: Loop back to **D** to reassess delivery or terms.  
- **Approval Not Needed (No Path)**: Bypass manager approval and go directly to Task G.  

#### **Task G ("Generate Final Invoice")**  
- **Purpose**: Create a finalized invoice for the customer.  

#### **Task I ("Send Confirmation to Customer")**  
- **Purpose**: Final step to notify the customer of approval and next steps.  

---

#### **End Events**  
- **Primary End**: After Task I (successful completion).  
- **Early Termination**: After Task E2 (rejection of a custom request).

### **Gateway Meanings**  
1. **XOR Gateways (Exclusive Choices)**:  
   - **Check Request Type**: Directs to standard vs. custom workflows.  
   - **Is Customization Feasible?**: Terminates the process for unfeasible custom requests.  
   - **Is Approval Needed?** and **Is Approval Granted?**: Determine approval dependencies and outcomes.  

2. **AND Gateway (Parallelism)**:  
   - **Run Parallel Checks**: Enables simultaneous execution of credit and inventory checks (Task C1 and C2).

| **Aspect**               | **Standard Path**                          | **Custom Path**                          |

### **Rework/Loopback Conditions**  
- **When Triggered**: When manager approval is denied (Gateway 5  "No" branch.  
- **Purpose**: Correct issues flagged during approval (e.g., pricing, delivery constraints, technical limitations.  
- **Loopback Targets**:

  - **Standard Paths**: Reassess delivery date (Task D) or validation criteria.

### **Convergence & Finalization**  
After resolving rework loops or approvals, the process converges at Task G (invoice generation) and ends with Task I (confirmation).

### **Edge Cases & Edge Paths**  
1. **Parallel Failures**: If credit *or* inventory checks fail (C1/C2), the process halts (no explicit path shown, but would likely route to a rejection task).  
2. **Approval Denial Loops**: Could repeat until conditions meet approval criteria.  
3. **Custom Revisions**: A rejected quotation (E2) terminates the process, but rework (H) allows iterative adjustments.

### **Key Objectives**  
- **Efficiency**: Parallel checks reduce delays for standard requests.
