### Explanation of Transformation Logic

**1. Case Identification:**
The provided log represents a single continuous work session (08:59 to 09:08) where the user is engaged in preparing a quarterly report package. There are no distinct breaks or separate business transactions that would warrant splitting this into multiple cases. Therefore, all events are grouped under a single **Case ID (`CASE_001_REPORT_PREP`)**. In a larger dataset, this logic would typically be applied per user session or per specific project code.

**2. Activity Naming & Abstraction:**
Raw system actions (e.g., `TYPING`, `FOCUS`, `SWITCH`) were translated into high-level business activities to facilitate process analysis:
*   **`Draft Content` / `Compose Reply`:** Mapped from `TYPING` or `CLICK`/`KEYS` in Word and Email. This abstracts the specific key strokes into the business intent (writing).
*   **`Update Data`:** Mapped from `TYPING` in Excel. Distinguishes data entry from general drafting.
*   **`Save Progress`:** Explicitly mapped from `SAVE`. In process mining, saves often represent checkpoints or decision points (e.g., "I finished this section").
*   **`Review Document` / `Analyze Findings`:** Mapped from `SCROLL` and `HIGHLIGHT` in Adobe Acrobat. This captures the cognitive activity of reading/reviewing rather than just moving a mouse.
*   **`Switch Task Context`:** Mapped from `SWITCH`. While often considered noise, we included it here to track application navigation, which can be relevant for analyzing context-switching costs or multi-tasking patterns.
*   **`Open/Close Application`:** Mapped from `FOCUS` (at start) and `CLOSE`. These define the boundaries of specific tool usage within the process flow.

**3. Data Transformation:**
*   **Timestamps:** Retained exactly as provided to preserve temporal accuracy for replay analysis or bottleneck detection.
*   **Attributes:** The Application and Document columns were retained as attributes, as they are crucial for filtering (e.g., analyzing time spent in Word vs. Excel).
*   **Noise Reduction:** Actions like `SCROLL` without significant context were generalized into the current activity (e.g., `Review Document`) rather than creating a distinct "Scroll" activity, which reduces noise in the process model.

**4. Narrative Coherence:**
The resulting log tells a clear story of a user preparing a report: drafting an introduction, handling an urgent email interruption regarding a meeting, reviewing a PDF draft for context, updating financial figures in Excel, and finally consolidating everything into the main Quarterly Report document before closing. This structure allows analysts to easily identify patterns such as "Email Interruptions" occurring during "Drafting Content."
