Below are targeted, open-ended questions I would ask to clarify the property onboarding process.

## Intake and Process Trigger

1. **What exactly triggers the start of the onboarding process?**
   For example, is it a landlord inquiry, a signed service agreement, receipt of documentation, or something else?

2. **How do landlords typically contact the regional office, and who receives the initial request?**

3. **What information is collected from the landlord during the first contact?**

4. **Are there different onboarding paths depending on the type of property, such as single-family homes, multifamily buildings, luxury units, student housing, or furnished rentals?**

5. **At what point is a property considered formally accepted into the onboarding pipeline?**

---

## Documentation Review

6. **Which documents are mandatory before onboarding can proceed?**

7. **How does the coordinator determine whether a document is valid, complete, and up to date?**

8. **What happens when documentation is missing, expired, inconsistent, or questionable?**

9. **Who has the authority to decide whether a property can move forward despite incomplete documentation?**

10. **Are documentation requirements different by city, region, property type, or local regulation?**

11. **How are landlord-provided documents stored, tracked, and version-controlled during onboarding?**

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

12. **Can you walk me through the main roles involved from beginning to end and what each role is responsible for?**

13. **Where does the property coordinator’s responsibility end and the property manager’s responsibility begin?**

14. **Who owns the overall onboarding process and is accountable for ensuring the property is activated successfully?**

15. **Which teams are involved only when exceptions arise, such as legal, insurance, IT, compliance, or finance?**

16. **How are handoffs managed between the regional office, property coordinator, property manager, marketing team, leasing team, and support teams?**

---

## Property Manager Assignment

17. **How do you evaluate a property manager’s workload when assigning a new property?**

18. **How is geographic specialization defined and used in assignment decisions?**

19. **What makes a property manager a good fit for a specific property type or landlord profile?**

20. **Who makes the final decision on which property manager is assigned?**

21. **What happens if the preferred property manager is unavailable or already at capacity?**

22. **Can a property be reassigned later in the onboarding process, and what would trigger that?**

---

## Landlord Alignment Meeting

23. **What topics are always covered during the introductory call or meeting with the landlord?**

24. **How are special landlord requirements captured and approved?**

25. **What types of landlord requests are considered standard, and which require escalation or legal review?**

26. **How do you resolve disagreements between the landlord’s preferences and company policy?**

27. **What outputs or decisions must come from this meeting before the process can continue?**

---

## Inspection, Photography, and Property Readiness

28. **How are the photographer and inspector selected and scheduled?**

29. **Who coordinates access to the property for inspections and photography?**

30. **Does photography happen before or after inspection approval, or can these happen in parallel?**

31. **What criteria determine whether a property passes inspection?**

32. **How are inspection findings categorized by severity or urgency?**

33. **What happens when the inspector identifies issues that must be fixed before listing?**

34. **Who decides whether repairs are required, optional, or the landlord’s responsibility?**

35. **How are contractors selected, approved, and managed when repairs are needed?**

36. **What happens if the landlord refuses to complete required repairs?**

---

## Marketing and Pricing

37. **What information does the digital marketing team need before creating listings and brochures?**

38. **How do you ensure that marketing materials accurately reflect the property’s condition, amenities, and restrictions?**

39. **Who approves the listing before it goes live?**

40. **How are expected rental prices determined or adjusted during onboarding?**

41. **What market data or pricing inputs are used to update the internal pricing model?**

42. **Who has final authority over the rental price shown to prospective tenants?**

43. **What happens when the landlord disagrees with the recommended rental price?**

---

## Activation in Systems

44. **What does it mean for a property to be “activated” in the rental portal and internal systems?**

45. **What conditions must be met before activation is allowed?**

46. **Who performs the activation, and who verifies that it was done correctly?**

47. **Are there separate activation steps for the public listing, internal CRM, pricing system, leasing workflow, and accounting system?**

48. **What common errors occur during activation, and how are they detected or corrected?**

---

## Tenant Applications and Screening

49. **At what point does tenant screening begin?**

50. **Who is responsible for reviewing applications: the property manager, leasing agent, or another team?**

51. **What criteria are used to determine whether an applicant is suitable?**

52. **How are credit checks, employment verification, and landlord references evaluated together?**

53. **What happens when an applicant partially meets the criteria but raises some concerns?**

54. **How do you handle multiple qualified applicants for the same property?**

55. **Who makes the final tenant selection decision?**

56. **How do you ensure tenant selection complies with fair housing requirements?**

---

## Lease Negotiation and Finalization

57. **Which lease terms are negotiable, and which are standardized by company policy?**

58. **When are promotional discounts considered, and who approves them?**

59. **How are custom lease clauses requested, reviewed, and approved?**

60. **When does the compliance officer or legal team become involved in lease review?**

61. **What steps must be completed before the lease is considered final?**

62. **How is the security deposit collected, verified, and recorded?**

63. **How is the move-in date coordinated among the tenant, landlord, property manager, and any internal teams?**

---

## Compliance, Finance, and Internal Audits

64. **What quality checks happen after the lease is prepared or signed?**

65. **At what points in the process does compliance review occur?**

66. **What specifically does the compliance officer check in the lease and onboarding record?**

67. **How does the financial analyst verify that the rental price aligns with pricing strategy and profitability targets?**

68. **What happens if finance disagrees with the agreed rental price or lease terms?**

69. **What does the IT department check during the final system audit?**

70. **Are audits performed before activation, after activation, after lease signing, or at multiple stages?**

---

## Exceptions and Escalations

71. **What are the most common exceptions that delay onboarding?**

72. **How are properties requiring special insurance handled differently?**

73. **How are custom landlord requirements reviewed and approved?**

74. **What types of issues require escalation to senior management, legal, compliance, or finance?**

75. **How are urgent or high-value properties prioritized when exceptions occur?**

76. **What happens if onboarding stalls because the landlord, contractor, inspector, or internal team does not respond?**

77. **Are there formal exception codes, categories, or statuses used to track non-standard cases?**

---

## Timing, Sequencing, and Dependencies

78. **What is the typical timeline from initial landlord contact to property activation?**

79. **What is the typical timeline from activation to signed lease and move-in?**

80. **Which steps must happen sequentially, and which can happen in parallel?**

81. **What are the biggest bottlenecks in the onboarding process?**

82. **Are there service-level targets for document review, manager assignment, inspection scheduling, listing creation, activation, or tenant screening?**

83. **How do timelines differ by region, property type, season, or market demand?**

---

## Systems, Communication, and Tracking

84. **Which systems are used to track the onboarding process from start to finish?**

85. **What information is stored in the CRM versus the property database versus project management tools?**

86. **How do teams know the current status of a property during onboarding?**

87. **How are email threads, documents, decisions, and approvals linked back to the property record?**

88. **Are there standard status stages, such as “documentation pending,” “inspection scheduled,” “repairs required,” “marketing in progress,” or “activated”?**

89. **How are stakeholders notified when a task is completed, blocked, or reassigned?**

90. **What manual workarounds are commonly used outside the official systems?**

---

## Process Outcomes and Success Measures

91. **When is onboarding considered complete?**

92. **What are the key success metrics for this process?**

93. **Do you measure time to activation, time to lease, listing quality, tenant quality, landlord satisfaction, or profitability?**

94. **How do you identify process failures or rework?**

95. **What feedback loops exist after onboarding to improve future onboarding cases?**

96. **Which parts of the process are most painful or inconsistent today?**

97. **Where do you see the greatest risk of errors, delays, or miscommunication?**

98. **What would an ideal version of this onboarding process look like compared with the current one?**
