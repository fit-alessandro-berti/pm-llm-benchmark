The visualization appears to be a **process flow over time** connecting three stages in a finemanagement system.

**Axes and layout**
- **Horizontal axis:** time, ranging roughly from **2000 to 2013**.
- **Vertical positions:** three labeled stages:
  - Top: **Send Fine**
  - Middle: **Insert Fine Notification**
  - Bottom: **Send for Credit Collection**

**Lines / flows**
- Many thin **vertical or nearvertical gray lines** connect **“Send Fine”  “Insert Fine Notification.”**  
  - These indicate events occurring very close together in time (likely when a fine is issued and the notification is recorded).
- From the middle stage to the bottom stage there are many **diagonal lines**, mostly **light gray** with some **red**.
  - The diagonals indicate a **time delay** between notification and sending the case to credit collection.
  - **Steeper lines** mean shorter delays; **shallower lines** mean longer delays.

**Color meaning (likely)**
- **Gray lines:** the majority of cases.
- **Red lines:** a subset of cases being highlighted (possibly specific outcomes, violations, or selected traces).

**Overall pattern**
- A large number of fines are issued across the entire time span.
- Only some progress to **credit collection**, shown by the downward diagonals.
- The delays between notification and credit collection vary widely, forming triangular “fan” shapes across time.

In short, it’s a **temporal process visualization** showing how individual fine cases move from **sending the fine  recording the notification  sending the case to credit collection**, with line slopes representing the **delay between steps**.
