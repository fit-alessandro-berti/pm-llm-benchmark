**1. Process Discovery and Conformance Checking**

**Data Preprocessing and Integration into a Cohesive Event Log**  
To construct a process mining-compliant event log, the first step is to harmonize data across four sources: GPS trackers, driver scanners, dispatch systems, and maintenance logs.  
- **Object-Centric Integration**: The primary case notion should be *Vehicle-Day* (e.g., V12-20241205) to analyze daily operational cycles. However, to analyze parcel-level handoffs, we may also declare *Package* as a secondary case notion using object-centric process mining (e.g., OC-DFG) where each event references both a vehicle-day and a package.  
- **Event Normalization**: All timestamps must be converted to UTC or a consistent local timezone. Standardized lifecycle transitions (e.g., “start,” “complete”) are mapped from raw events:  
  - Scanner events (Arrive, Success, Depart) form the delivery subprocess.  
  - Vehicle events (Depart Depot, Low Speed, Unscheduled Stop, Arrive Depot) form the transportation subprocess.  
  - Maintenance events (Unscheduled Repair) are treated as exception subprocesses.  
- **Spatial and Temporal Alignment**: GPS coordinates are reverse-geocoded to infer road segments or districts; speeds are used to classify movement states (moving, idling, congested).  
- **Enrichment**: Dispatch data (planned route sequence, time windows) and maintenance logs are merged via Vehicle ID, Driver ID, and timestamp windows to create attributes like *Planned_Activity*, *Is_Planned_Stop*, *Maintenance_Scheduled*.

**Key Challenges**  
- **Data Quality**: GPS drift, missing scanner events, or clock desynchronization between systems can fragment traces. Requires robust cleaning (e.g., interpolation for short GPS gaps, filtering outliers).  
- **Event Burstiness**: Frequent GPS pings must be abstracted to meaningful activities (e.g., “Traversing Segment X”) rather than raw points to avoid combinatorial explosion in process models.  
- **Multi-Instance Coordination**: Multiple vehicles executing similar routes require careful aggregation to avoid overgeneralization or underfitting in discovered models.

**Process Discovery for Actual End-to-End Delivery Process**  
Using the integrated event log, we apply process discovery algorithms:  
- **Inductive Miner with Frequency** (e.g., using the `pm4py` library) on Vehicle-Day cases to reveal the dominant control-flow:  
  *Start Shift  Route Assigned  Depart Depot  [Travel  Arrive Customer  Delivery Attempt  (Success/Fail)  Depart Customer]×N  Return Depot  End Shift*  
- **Event-Correlation Views**: Overlay GPS-based “moving” events to derive *Travel* activities between customer stops. Group low-speed/idle events to infer *Traffic Delay* or *Parking Search*.  
- **Exception Highlighting**: Unscheduled stops, failed deliveries, and maintenance interruptions are shown as parallel or exception branches in the model (e.g., a subprocess triggered by “Engine Warning Light” leading to “Unscheduled Stop”).

**Conformance Checking Against Planned Routes**  
- **Token-Based Replay or Alignments**: Compare discovered actual traces against dispatch-planned models (where each planned route is a model).  
- **Key Deviations to Detect**:  
  - **Sequence Deviations**: Actual stop order differs from planned route order (e.g., Stop 5 visited before Stop 3).  
  - **Unplanned Activities**: Unscheduled stops, detours, or early/late depot departures.  
  - **Timing Deviations**: Arrival outside customer time windows or significant positive/negative slacks compared to planned durations.  
  - **Omission/Insertion**: Missed deliveries that were planned, or extra deliveries not assigned.  
- **Quantification**: Compute fitness scores (e.g., log fitness) and precision to measure how much reality deviates from plans. Map deviations spatially to identify recurring detour zones.

**2. Performance Analysis and Bottleneck Identification**

**Relevant KPIs and Calculation from Event Log**  
- **On-Time Delivery Rate**: % of deliveries where *Delivery Success* timestamp  customer-requested time window end.  
- **Average Time per Delivery Stop**: ( (Delivery Success or Failed timestamp – Arrive Customer timestamp)) / # of stops.  
- **Travel Time vs. Service Time Ratio**: Total time classified as “moving” between stops / total time classified as “service” (Arrive to Depart Customer).  
- **Fuel Proxy Metric**: Sum of distance traveled (from GPS) * average fuel consumption rate per km (by vehicle type), or use *idle time* as a proxy for inefficiency.  
- **Vehicle Utilization Rate**: (Total shift time – idle time at depot/unscheduled downtime) / total shift hours.  
- **Traffic Delay Frequency/Duration**: Count and duration of “Low Speed Detected” or “Moving” events with speed below threshold (e.g., <10 km/h) during expected travel legs.  
- **Failed Delivery Rate**: # of “Delivery Failed” / total delivery attempts.

**Bottleneck Identification Techniques**  
- **Performance Spectrum / Cycle Time Histograms**: Show distribution of durations for each activity (e.g., “Arrive to Depart Customer”). Long tails indicate variability and bottlenecks.  
- **Bottleneck Analysis via Throughput Time Contribution**: Use *Dotted Chart* or *Performance Map* to visualize time spent in travel vs. service per route or time-of-day slice. Peaks in travel time during certain hours indicate congestion hotspots.  
- **Spatial Aggregation**: Group events by geohash or road segment to compute average speed and dwell time; segments with consistently low speed/high dwell are bottlenecks.  
- **Driver/Vehicle Clustering**: Compare KPIs across drivers and vehicles to identify underperformers. High variance suggests skill or vehicle condition issues.  
- **Quantifying Impact**: Calculate *total delay minutes* attributed to each bottleneck type (e.g., traffic-related delay, parking search time, re-delivery loops) and its contribution to overall late deliveries.

**3. Root Cause Analysis for Inefficiencies**

**Potential Root Causes and Validation via Process Mining**  
- **Suboptimal Route Planning**: Static routes ignore daily traffic variations.  
  - *Validation*: Variant analysis comparing stops executed vs. planned order; correlate sequence deviations with traffic data timestamps.  
- **Inaccurate Travel Time Estimations**: Planned durations differ from actual travel times.  
  - *Validation*: Overlay planned vs. actual timestamps on arcs in the process model; compute systematic bias per route/time-of-day.  
- **Traffic Congestion Patterns**: Recurring low-speed zones during peak hours.  
  - *Validation*: Dotted charts colored by time-of-day; spatial heatmaps of low-speed events.  
- **High Variability in Service Time**: Inconsistent dwell times at customers (e.g., due to parking difficulty, customer interaction).  
  - *Validation*: Histogram of service times per location type or customer cluster; correlate with failed delivery attempts (long interactions may reduce failure? or short rushed attempts increase failure?).  
- **Vehicle Breakdowns/Maintenance**: Unscheduled stops causing cascading delays.  
  - *Validation*: Examine traces containing “Unscheduled Stop” with notes; compute downstream delay propagation.  
- **Driver Behavior Differences**: Aggressive acceleration, frequent idling, or inefficient stop sequencing.  
  - *Validation*: Cluster drivers by KPIs (fuel proxy, average speed, on-time rate); inspect variant models per driver cluster.  
- **Failed Deliveries and Re-delivery Loops**: Failed attempts increase total workload and mileage.  
  - *Validation*: Follow object-centric traces for packages with multiple delivery attempts; measure extra distance and time incurred.

**4. Data-Driven Optimization Strategies**

**Strategy 1: Dynamic Routing with Real-Time Traffic and Historical Congestion Insights**  
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Travel time variability and traffic-related delays.  
- **Root Cause**: Static routes ignoring real-time and historical congestion patterns.  
- **Process Mining Support**: Use historical process models to identify recurring congestion arcs (edges between activities) and time windows of highest delay. Combine with real-time GPS speed data to trigger re-routing.  
- **Implementation**: Integrate a dynamic routing engine that, at mid-shift, re-optimizes remaining stops using current traffic and predicted travel times derived from process mining profiles.  
- **Expected KPI Impact**: Reduced average travel time per stop, higher on-time delivery rate, lower fuel consumption per package.

**Strategy 2: Stop Sequencing and Territory Optimization Based on Historical Performance**  
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Suboptimal stop order and unbalanced territories.  
- **Root Cause**: Planned sequences not aligned with actual service time distributions and parking/access difficulties.  
- **Process Mining Support**: Cluster stops by *actual* service time and dwell patterns; use sequence analysis to find high-performing routes (variants with high fitness and low cycle time) and replicate their patterns. Redesign territories to group stops with similar service-time profiles and minimize backtracking.  
- **Implementation**: Automated route generator that uses historical actual durations (not just distances) to create sequences that buffer high-variability stops.  
- **Expected KPI Impact**: Reduced total route cycle time, improved vehicle utilization, fewer late deliveries.

**Strategy 3: Predictive Maintenance and Vehicle Health Monitoring Integrated into Scheduling**  
- **Targeted Inefficiency**: Unscheduled breakdowns causing delays and overtime.  
- **Root Cause**: Reactive maintenance rather than usage-based scheduling.  
- **Process Mining Support**: Analyze event logs to correlate vehicle usage intensity (mileage per day, idle time, number of stops) with occurrence of “Unscheduled Stop” events due to mechanical issues. Derive usage-based maintenance triggers.  
- **Implementation**: Integrate maintenance scheduling into the dispatch system: if a vehicle’s cumulative usage reaches a threshold derived from historical failure patterns, preemptively schedule maintenance outside peak delivery hours, and adjust daily assignments to avoid overuse.  
- **Expected KPI Impact**: Reduced frequency and duration of unscheduled stops, lower overtime costs, improved overall fleet availability.

**5. Considering Operational Constraints and Continuous Monitoring**

**Accounting for Operational Constraints**  
- **Driver Working Hours**: Any dynamic re-routing or territory rebalancing must respect legal driving limits and scheduled breaks. Optimization models should include shift-end time as a hard constraint.  
- **Vehicle Capacities**: Stop re-sequencing must respect weight/volume limits; process mining can reveal underutilized capacity patterns to allow consolidation or split-route suggestions.  
- **Customer Time Windows**: Dynamic adjustments should prioritize high-penalty or tight-window deliveries. Use historical on-time performance per time window to inform risk-aware sequencing.

**Continuous Monitoring Plan with Process Mining Dashboards**  
- **Key Dashboard Views**:  
  - **Live Conformance Dashboard**: Real-time fitness score of ongoing routes versus planned routes; alerts for major deviations.  
  - **Performance KPI Dashboard**: Rolling averages of on-time delivery rate, average service time, travel/service ratio, failed delivery rate, and fuel/idle proxy.  
  - **Bottleneck Heatmap**: Spatial and temporal heatmaps of delays, updated hourly.  
  - **Vehicle Health & Maintenance Dashboard**: Usage-based maintenance countdowns and unscheduled stop trends.  
- **Monitoring Cadence**: Daily operational reviews for short-term adjustments; weekly reviews for route/templates refinements; monthly reviews for territory redesign and strategy recalibration.  
- **Feedback Loop**: When new deviations or bottlenecks emerge, trigger root cause analysis cycles to update models and optimization rules. Use A/B testing of new routing heuristics on subsets of the fleet to validate improvements before full rollout.

By integrating process mining with logistics operations, Speedy Parcels can transform raw event data into a living operational blueprint—continuously revealing inefficiencies, validating root causes, and guiding targeted, data-driven improvements that directly enhance punctuality and reduce costs while respecting real-world constraints.
