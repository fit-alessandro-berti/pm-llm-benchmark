### Justification of Naming Choices

**Activities that recur and why context matters:**

1. **Initiate Request**  
   - Appears in all cases, but *C003* is initiated in-branch by reception, not online.  
   - Renamed in *C003* to **“Initiate Request — Branch Walk-in”** to distinguish the channel and initiation type (walk-in vs. digital).

2. **Verify Documents** (appears 2–3 times per case)  
   - First occurrence is always an *initial identity check*.  
   - Later occurrences happen *after additional information or adjustments*, so they are re-checks.  
   - Renamed to:  
     - **“Verify Documents — Initial Check”**  
     - **“Verify Documents — Re-check After Upload”** (C001)  
     - **“Verify Documents — Post-Adjustment Re-check”** (C003)  
     - **“Verify Documents — Physical Check”** (C003, branch context)  
   - These clarify *when* and *why* verification occurs and *what* has changed since the prior step.

3. **Call Customer** (appears 1–2 times per case)  
   - Purpose varies: requesting missing docs, clarifying discrepancies, confirming changes, or recalling the customer.  
   - Renamed to reflect intent:  
     - **“Call Customer — Request Missing Docs”** (C001)  
     - **“Call Customer — Clarify Discrepancy”** (C002)  
     - **“Call Customer — Confirm Adjustments”** (C002)  
     - **“Call Customer — Recall for Completion”** (C003)  
   - This disambiguates the *type* of customer interaction and its role in progressing the case.

4. **Adjust Account**  
   - Appears in C002 and C003, but reasons differ: post-call correction vs. missing-info fix.  
   - Renamed to:  
     - **“Adjust Account — Post-Call Correction”** (C002)  
     - **“Adjust Account — Missing Info Fix”** (C003)  
   - Highlights the *trigger* and *timing* of the adjustment.

5. **Submit Additional**  
   - Only in C001; generic name obscures what is being submitted.  
   - Renamed to **“Submit Additional — ID Scan”** to specify the content and purpose.

**Overall approach:**  
The renaming preserves original activity semantics while adding *purpose*, *timing*, and *context* (e.g., channel, trigger, outcome of prior step). This makes the process flow clearer for analysis, monitoring, and improvement without losing traceability to the original log.
