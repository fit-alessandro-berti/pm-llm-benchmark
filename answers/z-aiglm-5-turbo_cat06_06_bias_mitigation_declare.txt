### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **Existence (`BiasMitigationCheck`):** Forces the `BiasMitigationCheck` activity to be present in every valid process trace, establishing a baseline requirement for fairness evaluation.
2. **Coexistence (`CheckApplicant*` $\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview`):** Ensures that if the system logs an event where a sensitive attribute (Race, Gender, Age) is evaluated, a `ManualReview` step must also occur somewhere in that trace. This guarantees human-in-the-loop oversight for demographic data.
3. **Response (`CheckApplicant*` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck`):** Mandates that any time an applicant's sensitive attributes are checked, the system must subsequently perform an algorithmic or procedural `BiasMitigationCheck` before concluding the process.
4. **Precedence (`BiasMitigationCheck` $\rightarrow$ `Reject`):** Ensures that a `Reject` decision is never logged unless a `BiasMitigationCheck` has previously occurred in the trace, ensuring rejections of potentially biased applicants are reviewed.
5. **Non-succession (`CheckApplicant*` $\nrightarrow$ `Reject`):** Explicitly forbids the sequence where a sensitive attribute check is immediately followed by a rejection. This removes the opportunity for fast, automated, discriminatory rejections.
6. **Non-chain-succession (`CheckApplicant*` $\nrightarrow$ `Approve`):** Similarly prevents a sensitive attribute check from being immediately followed by an approval, guarding against both adverse bias (rejecting minorities) and favorable bias (fast-tracking approvals for specific demographics).
