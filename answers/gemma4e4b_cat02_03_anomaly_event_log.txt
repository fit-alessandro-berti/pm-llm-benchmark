## Identified Anomalies by Case ID

###  Case 1002: The Major Deviation (Payment Before Key Steps)

**The Anomalous Sequence:**
1. Create Purchase Requisition (08:30)
2. Issue Purchase Order (08:40)
3. **Vendor Invoice Received (07:00 - Day 3)**
4. **Release Payment (07:15 - Day 3)**
5. Approve Purchase Requisition (08:00 - Day 3)
6. Receive Goods (Day 4)
7. Match Invoice to PO (Day 5)

**Explanation of Anomalies:**
1. **Payment Precedes Authorization (P-A Gap):** The most severe anomaly is the "Release Payment" occurring *before* the "Approve Purchase Requisition" (Day 3, 07:15 vs. Day 3, 08:00). Payment should never be released based on an unapproved requisition or PO.
2. **Payment Precedes Fulfillment (P-F Gap):** The payment is initiated before the goods have been received ("Receive Goods" happens Day 4). Payment should be contingent on the successful three-way match (PO, Receipt, Invoice).
3. **Timing of Approval:** The "Approve Purchase Requisition" happens significantly late on Day 3, after payment has already been initiated, suggesting a breakdown in approval workflow control.

**Why this is Problematic (Business Risk):**
This flow indicates a massive failure in internal controls. Paying a vendor before goods are received, or before the request has been formally approved, exposes the company to high financial risk, potentially leading to:
*   **Fraud:** Payments could be made for non-existent goods or services.
*   **Cash Flow Mismanagement:** Funds are committed without proper operational justification.
*   **Audit Failure:** The process cannot be successfully reconciled during an audit.

###  Case 1003: The Disconnected Flow (Payment Before Receipt/Matching)

**The Anomalous Sequence:**
1. Create/Approve Requisition $\rightarrow$ Issue PO (Day 1, 10:00)
2. **Release Payment (Day 2, 11:00)**
3. Vendor Invoice Received (Day 3)
4. Receive Goods (Day 4)
5. Match Invoice to PO (Day 5)

**Explanation of Anomalies:**
1. **Payment Before Receipt and Matching:** The "Release Payment" occurs on Day 2, significantly before the goods are received (Day 4) and the invoice is matched (Day 5).
2. **Payment Initiation vs. Invoicing:** While the invoice is received on Day 3, the payment is released on Day 2. This timing inconsistency suggests either a pre-payment arrangement that bypassed normal controls, or a significant administrative error.

**Why this is Problematic (Business Risk):**
Similar to Case 1002, this violates the fundamental principle of **"Pay only upon verification."** The company commits funds based on the PO alone, without confirming that the goods actually arrived and were properly accounted for (three-way match). This increases the risk of paying for unreceived or partially received items.

###  Case 1004: The Timing Anomaly (Order of Operations)

**The Anomalous Sequence:**
1. Create/Approve Requisition $\rightarrow$ **Receive Goods (Day 1)**
2. **Issue Purchase Order (Day 1, 09:15)**

**Explanation of Anomalies:**
1. **Receiving Goods Before PO:** In a controlled environment, goods should only be *received* after a Purchase Order (PO) has been formally issued and approved. The timeline shows the physical goods receipt occurs *before* the system issues the official PO.

**Why this is Problematic (Business Risk):**
This implies that the receiving department might be operating without adequate control or visibility. Goods could be accepted into inventory or used departmentally without having a verifiable commitment (PO) in the system. This creates difficulties in reconciliation, inventory tracking, and auditing the true cost of goods sold.
