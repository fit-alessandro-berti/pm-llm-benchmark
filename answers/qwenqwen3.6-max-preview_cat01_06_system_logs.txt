### Transformation Logic & Explanation

#### 1. Case Identification Strategy
Cases were defined around **distinct work artifacts** (documents, emails, spreadsheets, and PDFs). In knowledge work, a "case" typically represents the lifecycle of a specific business object being created, reviewed, or modified. I identified five logical cases:
- `DOC_Document1`: Drafting and finalizing a new Word document.
- `EMAIL_AnnualMeeting`: Reading and replying to a specific email thread.
- `PDF_ReportDraft`: Reviewing and annotating a PDF report.
- `XLS_Budget2024`: Updating financial figures in a spreadsheet.
- `DOC_QuarterlyReport`: Initial opening, later resumption, and finalization of the quarterly report.

Grouping by artifact allows process mining tools to reconstruct independent process flows, even though the user interleaved them chronologically.

#### 2. Activity Naming & Aggregation
Raw UI events (`FOCUS`, `TYPING`, `CLICK`, `SCROLL`, `SAVE`, `CLOSE`) were abstracted into **semantic, process-level activities**:
- `FOCUS`  `Open Document` / `Open Email` / `Open Spreadsheet`
- `TYPING` + `CLICK (Reply)`  `Edit Content` / `Draft Reply` / `Update Data`
- `SCROLL` + `CLICK (Open)`  `Review Content` / `Review Document`
- `HIGHLIGHT`  `Annotate Document`
- `SAVE`  `Save Changes`
- `CLOSE`  `Close Document`
- `CLICK (Send)`  `Send Reply`

**Noise Reduction:** Consecutive low-level events of the same type on the same artifact (e.g., multiple `TYPING` events at 09:00:30 and 09:01:00) were aggregated into a single higher-level event timestamped at the start of the action. This is a standard process mining preprocessing step to prevent artificially inflated loop counts and to focus on meaningful process transitions rather than keystroke-level granularity.

#### 3. Handling Context Switches
`SWITCH` events were **excluded** from the final log. In process mining, application/window switching represents OS-level navigation or multitasking overhead, not a value-adding step within a specific case. Removing them keeps the event log focused on actual work progression while preserving the true chronological order of case activities.

#### 4. Coherent Narrative
The log captures a realistic **interleaved knowledge-work session**:
1. The user briefly opens the `Quarterly_Report` but immediately pivots to start drafting `Document1`.
2. After an initial draft & save, they context-switch to handle an urgent email (`AnnualMeeting`), completing the full read-reply-send cycle.
3. They briefly review and annotate a PDF (`Report_Draft`), likely gathering reference material.
4. They open the budget spreadsheet, update Q1/Q2 figures, and save.
5. Returning to `Document1`, they insert a budget reference, save, and formally close the document.
6. Finally, they resume work on the `Quarterly_Report`, draft the executive summary, save, and close it, concluding the session.

This structure is fully compatible with standard process mining tools (e.g., Celonis, Disco, ProM, Apromore). Sorting by `Case ID` then `Timestamp` in your tool will reveal clean, linear process flows per artifact, while keeping the chronological order preserves the user's multitasking behavior for performance or resource analysis.
