**Bias Manifests Primarily in the ScoreAdjustment / Community-Based Boost**

The event log reveals clear, systematic bias in how preliminary creditworthiness (the "PreliminaryScore") is modified and how final decisions are rendered. The core mechanism is the **+10 point adjustment** explicitly tied to membership in the "Highland Civic Darts Club" community group. This adjustment is applied selectively during the PreliminaryScoring step by the Scoring Engine and then carried forward into ManualReview and the FinalDecision (Rules Engine).

### Specific Evidence from the Log

- **C001** (LocalResident=TRUE, Community=Highland Civic Darts Club): Base 710  +10  720 (Adjusted)  Approved.
- **C004** (LocalResident=TRUE, Community=Highland Civic Darts Club): Base 690  +10  700 (Adjusted)  Approved.
- **C002** (LocalResident=TRUE, Community=None): Base 720  0 adjustment  Approved.
- **C005** (LocalResident=FALSE, Community=None): Base 740  0 adjustment  Approved.
- **C003** (LocalResident=FALSE, Community=None): Base 715  0 adjustment  Rejected.

**Key disparities**:
- C004 is approved at an *adjusted* score of 700.
- C003 is rejected at a *higher* unadjusted score of 715.
- A non-club applicant needs a substantially higher base score (C005 at 740) to be approved when LocalResident=FALSE, while club members receive an automatic lift that can push them over the approval threshold even when their base score is materially lower.
- The +10 is applied *only* when CommunityGroup = "Highland Civic Darts Club"; it is zero for all other cases. No other attribute receives an analogous automatic positive adjustment in the provided data.

### Where the Bias Is Introduced

1. **PreliminaryScoring / Scoring Engine step**: The engine adds +10 "(Community)" for the specific group. This is not a neutral risk factor; it is an explicit preference for affiliation with one named community organization.

2. **FinalDecision / Rules Engine**: Decisions are made on the *adjusted* score. This means the rules treat the boosted number as the operative input, allowing club members to clear thresholds that non-members with higher raw scores do not.

3. **Secondary signal — LocalResident**: This attribute correlates with approval in the sample but is not the dominant driver. C005 (FALSE, high base score) is approved while C003 (FALSE, lower base score) is rejected, showing that raw score still matters. However, every club-member case is also LocalResident=TRUE, so the two attributes reinforce each other. Non-residents without the club boost are disadvantaged unless their base score is high enough to compensate.

### How This Affects Fairness and Equity

- **Unequal treatment for equivalent (or superior) underlying merit**: Applicants without the specific community affiliation must clear a higher effective bar. An applicant at 715 with no club affiliation is rejected; one at 690 with the club affiliation is accepted. This violates the principle that similar creditworthiness should produce similar outcomes.

- **Proxy discrimination risk**: "Highland Civic Darts Club" is a narrow, named affiliation. In real-world deployment, such a feature can function as a proxy for race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, religion, or other protected characteristics if club membership is not evenly distributed across demographic groups. The log provides no evidence that this adjustment is justified by actuarial risk; it is simply applied.

- **Impact on individuals lacking the favored attributes**:
  - People without ties to that specific club (or any "CommunityGroup") receive no uplift.
  - Non-residents (LocalResident=FALSE) who are not club members face both the absence of the +10 and whatever implicit penalty or higher threshold the system applies to their base score.
  - Even if their preliminary score is higher than a club member's adjusted score, they can still be rejected (as with C003 vs. C004).

- **Downstream effects**: Manual reviewers see the already-adjusted score (e.g., "720 (Adjusted)" or "700 (Adjusted)"), which can anchor their judgment. The Rules Engine then locks in the outcome. Over time, this creates disparate impact: higher approval rates for the favored group, lower rates for everyone else, even when base credit metrics are comparable.

### Summary of Favored Attributes

- **Primary favored attribute**: Membership in "Highland Civic Darts Club"  automatic +10 score boost.
- **Secondary / correlated**: LocalResident=TRUE (especially when combined with the club).
- **Disfavored**: Absence of that specific community affiliation, and LocalResident=FALSE without an exceptionally high base score.

This is textbook disparate treatment embedded in the scoring and decision rules. The process does not treat applicants with similar preliminary creditworthiness equitably; it systematically advantages one narrow community affiliation at the expense of others. Any real-world system using this logic would be vulnerable to fairness, regulatory, and legal challenges on grounds of bias.
