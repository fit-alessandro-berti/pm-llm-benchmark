### **1. Identification of Delayed Cases**

### **2. Root Cause Analysis of Performance Issues**
By examining the time gaps between specific activities in the delayed cases, three distinct bottlenecks emerge:

#### **A. The "End-of-Day" Handoff Bottleneck (Affects Cases 102, 104, 105)**
*   **Observation:** In all three delayed cases, the "Investigate Issue" activity occurs in the afternoon (between 13:00 and 14:00). The very next step, "Resolve Ticket," does not occur until the following morning (between 08:00 and 09:00). 
*   **Impact:** This creates a consistent **~19-hour idle gap**. It strongly suggests that agents stop working on complex tickets at the end of their shift, and there is no overnight or late-shift support to carry the work forward.

#### **B. Level-2 Escalation Delays (Affects Cases 102, 105)**
*   **Observation:** 
    *   In **Case 102**, there is a 2.5-hour gap between assigning to Level-1 and escalating, followed by another 2.5-hour gap before Level-2 begins investigating.
    *   In **Case 105**, the ticket is investigated by Level-1, escalated at 10:00, and then sits idle for **28 hours and 50 minutes** before a Level-2 agent even begins to investigate it the next day at 14:00.
*   **Impact:** Escalated tickets are sitting in a backlog. Furthermore, Case 105 shows a redundant workflow: Level-1 spent 45 minutes investigating an issue that ultimately required Level-2, wasting valuable time.

#### **C. Slow Initial Assignment and Triage (Affects Case 104)**
*   **Observation:** Case 104 was received at 08:20 but wasn't assigned to an agent until 09:30 (1 hr 10 mins). It then sat uninvestigated until 13:00 (3.5 hrs). 
*   **Impact:** Compared to Case 103 (which was assigned in 20 minutes and investigated in 15 minutes), Case 104 experienced unnecessary administrative drag before any actual work began.

### **3. Explanations and Strategic Recommendations**

The factors above compound to create massive cycle time increases. For example, a ticket that could be resolved in 2 hours (like Case 101) becomes a 24+ hour ticket simply because it was picked up in the afternoon (Factor A) or required an escalation that wasn't staffed adequately (Factor B).

To address these bottlenecks, I recommend the following process improvements:

#### **Recommendation 1: Mitigate End-of-Day Drop-offs**
*   **Insight:** The 19-hour gap is the single most consistent delay across all slow tickets.
*   **Action:** Implement a "Shift Handoff Protocol." If a ticket cannot be resolved by the end of a shift, it must be formally handed off to an after-hours or late-shift team. Alternatively, empower Level-1 agents with a "knowledge base" or pre-approved solutions so they can resolve afternoon tickets without needing next-day approvals.

#### **Recommendation 2: Optimize the Escalation Pathway**
*   **Insight:** Level-2 queues are severely backlogged, and Level-1 agents are wasting time investigating issues they cannot resolve.
*   **Action:** 
    1. Improve the initial **Triage** step. If a ticket matches specific keywords or categories known to require Level-2, route it *directly* to Level-2, bypassing Level-1 entirely (preventing the Case 105 scenario).
    2. Establish a strict Service Level Agreement (SLA) for escalations (e.g., "All escalated tickets must be acknowledged by Level-2 within 2 hours").

#### **Recommendation 3: Automate and Enforce Initial Routing SLAs**
*   **Insight:** Manual triage and assignment are causing unnecessary delays (as seen in Case 104).
*   **Action:** Implement automated ticket routing based on issue type, customer tier, or keywords to instantly assign tickets to the correct agent queue. Enforce an internal SLA requiring tickets to be assigned within 15 minutes of receipt and investigated within 1 hour.

#### **Recommendation 4: Process Mining / Continuous Monitoring**
*   **Insight:** We only see 5 cases here, but these patterns likely exist at scale.
*   **Action:** Deploy a process mining dashboard to continuously monitor the time deltas between *Assign -> Investigate* and *Investigate -> Resolve*. Set up automated alerts for any ticket that remains in an "Investigating" or "Escalated" state for more than 4 hours.
