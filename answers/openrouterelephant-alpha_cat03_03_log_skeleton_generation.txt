To construct a **Log Skeleton** for the given **order fulfillment process**, we need to define constraints based on the logical flow and business rules of the process.

---

###  Assumptions and Logical Flow:

1. **Receive Order (RO)** must always be the **first** activity.
2. **Check Availability (CA)** must happen **after** receiving the order.
3. **Reserve Items (RI)** must happen **after** availability is confirmed.
4. **Pick Items (PI)** must happen **after** reservation.
5. **Quality Check (QC)** must happen **after** picking.
6. **Pack Order (PO)** must happen **after** quality check.
7. **Generate Shipping Label (GSL)** must happen **after** packing.
8. **Dispatch (D)** must happen **after** the shipping label is generated.
9. **Invoice Customer (IC)** can happen **after** the order is dispatched or concurrently.
10. **Record Payment (RP)** must happen **after** the invoice is generated.
11. **Never Together**: Some activities should not happen together (e.g., RO and D — order received and already dispatched).
12. **Activity Occurrences**: Each activity should occur exactly once per order (except IC and RP, which may vary depending on business logic).

---

###  Constraints Mapping:

- **Always Before** relationships: RO  CA  RI  PI  QC  PO  GSL  D
- **Always After** relationships: derived from "Always Before"
- **Equivalence**: RO and D must not both occur unless all in-between steps occurred — but since RO is first and D is last, they won't be equivalent.
- **Never Together**: RO & D, CA & D, RI & D, etc.
- **Activity Occurrences**: All main activities occur either 0 or 1 time (except IC and RP, which can occur 1 or 2 times — for partial payments or multiple invoices).
- **Directly Follows**: D must be directly preceded by GSL, PO by QC, etc.
