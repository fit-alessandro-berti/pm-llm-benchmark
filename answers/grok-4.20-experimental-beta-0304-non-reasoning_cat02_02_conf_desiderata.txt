**Analysis of the Loan Application Process**

### 1. Normative Violations (Strict Rule Breaches)

**Case 1:**
- **Compliant.** All normative rules are followed perfectly.
- Sequence: Application Received  Credit Check  Risk Assessment  Loan Decision (Approved)  Inform Applicant  Finalize Documentation  Close Case.
- No steps skipped, no order violations, and the approval path was respected.

**Case 2:**
- **Violates normative rules (multiple violations).**
  - **Critical ordering violation:** Risk Assessment was performed *before* Credit Check. The rules explicitly require Credit Check  Risk Assessment.
  - **Illegal activity after rejection:** Finalize Documentation was executed after a Rejected decision. The process explicitly forbids finalizing documentation on the rejection path.
  - The case eventually closed, but the sequence was corrupted.

**Case 3:**
- **Compliant with normative rules.**
- Followed the required sequence and decision logic correctly (Approved  Finalize Documentation before Close Case).

### 2. Desiderata Violations (Recommended but Non-Mandatory)

**Case 1:**
- Fully meets all desiderata.
- Credit Check occurred 30 minutes after application (well within 24h).
- Applicant was informed 2 hours after decision (well within 48h).
- Although a Follow-up Call is not explicitly listed in the log, the overall timing is excellent.

**Case 2:**
- Has normative violations, so desiderata are secondary. However, the timing between activities was actually quite good (everything within a few hours).

**Case 3:**
- **Violates desiderata (timing issues).**
  - Credit Check occurred ~25 hours after Application Received (violates the recommended "within 24 hours" guideline).
  - Applicant was informed ~51 hours after the Loan Decision (violates the recommended "within 48 hours" guideline).
  - No Follow-up Call was performed before closing the case.
- These are **not serious violations**, only suboptimal behavior.

### 3. Suggested Interpretations / Justifications

**Case 1:** Ideal execution. Represents the gold-standard process behavior.

**Case 2:** This case shows clear deviations from the prescribed normative model. Possible interpretations:
- The processor may have mistakenly believed a Credit Check had already been completed or relied on external information, leading to premature Risk Assessment.
- The Finalize Documentation after rejection suggests either a system error, a misunderstanding of the rules by the case handler, or possibly an attempt to "fix" the case that violated the explicit rejection path.
- This is not justifiable under the given constraints.

**Case 3:** Minor timing slippage on recommended (but not mandatory) windows.
- The 25-hour delay in Credit Check could be explained by the application arriving late in the day on Jan 12, with the check occurring first thing on Jan 14 (borderline but understandable).
- The delayed notification (~51h instead of 48h) is only a marginal overrun and may have been due to internal review or weekend timing.
- Absence of a Follow-up Call is acceptable since it is only a recommendation.

### Summary

- **Normative compliance:** Cases 1 and 3 are compliant. Case 2 has serious violations (wrong order + illegal activity after rejection).
- **Desiderata compliance:** Only Case 1 fully meets the recommendations. Case 3 shows minor timing and follow-up deviations that are explicitly classified as non-severe.
- The log demonstrates mostly good behavior with one clearly deviant case (Case 2) that requires attention.
