1. What strategic assumptions about market demand, customer preferences, and product positioning justify starting the process with market research before prototype design?

2. How does the R&D prototype stage incorporate feedback from suppliers, manufacturers, logistics partners, retailers, and end customers before large-scale production begins?

3. What criteria determine which components are sourced from Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, Germany, and the USA, and how are cost, quality, geopolitical risk, and supplier reliability balanced?

4. How resilient is the parallel component-sourcing model if one supplier region experiences disruption, such as natural disasters, trade restrictions, labor shortages, or transportation delays?

5. What contingency plans exist if a critical component, such as microprocessors or OLED screens, becomes unavailable or significantly increases in price?

6. How could the process better evaluate whether single sourcing, dual sourcing, or regional sourcing would reduce risk without creating unnecessary complexity?

7. What performance indicators should be used to determine whether component procurement is efficient, reliable, and aligned with production schedules?

8. How are quality expectations communicated to suppliers before components are shipped to the assembly plant in Malaysia?

9. What risks are introduced by concentrating assembly operations in one country, and how might the company decide whether to diversify assembly locations?

10. How does the 24/7 assembly operation affect labor conditions, equipment maintenance, defect rates, energy consumption, and long-term operational sustainability?

11. What decision rules determine whether a failed smartphone should be reworked, scrapped, or investigated for a broader process or supplier issue?

12. How can quality checks be improved so that defects are detected earlier in the supply chain rather than after assembly is complete?

13. What data should be collected during diagnostics to identify recurring defects, supplier quality problems, or assembly-line inefficiencies?

14. How should the company decide which markets receive air freight versus cargo shipping, and how should trade-offs between speed, cost, carbon emissions, and customer expectations be evaluated?

15. What regulatory, customs, and compliance risks could delay market entry, and how can these risks be anticipated before products leave the assembly plant?

16. How should regional distribution centers coordinate with headquarters, logistics providers, retailers, and e-commerce platforms to prevent stockouts or overstocking?

17. What communication mechanisms are needed when demand changes rapidly in one region but inventory is already committed to another region?

18. How can sales forecasts be made more adaptive by incorporating real-time retail data, e-commerce demand, preorder volumes, competitor launches, and macroeconomic signals?

19. What governance process determines when demand changes are significant enough to trigger supplier order adjustments rather than continuing with the current production plan?

20. How should the overall supply chain be measured beyond cost and delivery speed, including resilience, sustainability, supplier ethics, customer satisfaction, and strategic flexibility?
