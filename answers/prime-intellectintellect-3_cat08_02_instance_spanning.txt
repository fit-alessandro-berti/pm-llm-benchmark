### Methodology for Constraint Identification

   - Analyze activity sequences to identify patterns related to the constraints

2. **Frequency and Duration Analysis**:

     - **Shared Cold-Packing Stations**: 
       - Frequency of orders requiring cold-packing
       - Waiting time for cold-packing stations
       - Utilization rate of cold-packing stations vs. standard stations
       - Queue length distribution for cold-packing stations

     - **Shipping Batches**:
       - Frequency of batch formation
       - Batch size distribution
       - Waiting time for batch completion by destination region
       - Time between order completion and batch formation

     - **Priority Handling**:
       - Percentage of express orders
       - Average processing time difference between express and standard orders
       - Frequency of express orders interrupting standard orders
       - Waiting time comparison between express and standard orders

     - **Hazardous Material Limits**:
       - Percentage of orders with hazardous materials
       - Peak concurrent hazardous orders in packing/quality check
       - Compliance rate with the 10-order limit
       - Waiting time for hazardous orders vs. non-hazardous orders

3. **Waiting Time Attribution Analysis**:
   To differentiate between within-instance and between-instance factors:
   
   - Calculate **theoretical activity duration** based on historical data for each activity type (e.g., average packing time for standard orders, average picking time)
   - Subtract theoretical duration from actual duration to identify **excess waiting time**
   - Attribute excess waiting time to specific constraints:
     - Resource contention: When waiting occurs due to unavailable required resource (e.g., cold-packing station)
     - Batching delays: When orders complete activities but wait for batch formation
     - Priority interruptions: When standard orders are paused for express orders
     - Regulatory limits: When orders with hazardous materials cannot proceed due to limits

## 2. Analyzing Constraint Interactions

The different constraints interact in complex ways that can amplify their individual impacts:

1. **Priority Handling and Resource Contention**:
   - Express orders needing cold-packing stations can "jump the queue" ahead of standard orders, creating unpredictable waiting times
   - This interaction can create cascading delays where standard orders wait longer for cold-packing stations, potentially missing batch formation opportunities

2. **Batching and Hazardous Material Limits**:
   - Multiple hazardous orders destined for the same region may need to be split across multiple batches due to the 10-order limit
   - This can result in suboptimal batch sizes, reducing delivery route efficiency and increasing shipping costs

3. **Priority Handling and Batching**:
   - Express orders may be held waiting for batch formation, potentially creating a bottleneck at the shipping label generation step
   - Alternatively, express orders might force the creation of smaller, less efficient batches, increasing costs

4. **Resource Constraints and Regulatory Limits**:
   - Cold-packing stations might be underutilized due to hazardous material limits (if orders with hazardous materials also require cold-packing)
   - Conversely, these constraints might interact positively if hazardous orders are more likely to require cold-packing, allowing better resource planning

Understanding these interactions is crucial because optimizing one constraint without considering others may lead to suboptimal results or even worsen performance in other areas. For example, prioritizing express orders might improve their throughput but negatively impact overall throughput if standard orders are frequently interrupted. Process mining analysis helps identify these complex relationships by revealing patterns in the event log data that reflect these interactions.
