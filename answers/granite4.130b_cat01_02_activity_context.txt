**Step1 – Identify recurring activities & interpret their context**

| Activity (original) | Cases where it repeats | Contextual meaning of each occurrence |
|---------------------|------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Verify Documents** | C001, C003 | 1 First check of the customer’s identity documents. <br>2 Second check performed *after* additional information (e.g., a missing ID scan or an account adjustment) has been supplied. |
| **Call Customer**   | C002, C003 | 1 Outbound call to request missing documentation or clarify a discrepancy. <br>2 Followup / confirmation call after the requested action (document upload, account change) has been completed. |
| *(All other activities appear only once per case)* | – | No renaming needed. |

**Step2 – Choose disambiguated names**

- **Verify Documents  Verify Initial Docs** (first verification)  
  ** ReVerify Docs After Update** (second verification that follows a change such as uploaded ID or account adjustment)

- **Call Customer  Call for Missing Info** (initial call to obtain something the system lacks)  
  ** Followup Confirmation Call** (subsequent call to confirm that the requested action has been satisfied)

The new names explicitly encode *when* and *why* the activity occurs, making the event log easier to read and analyze.

**Step4 – Justification of Naming Choices**

1. **Verify Documents  Verify Initial Docs / ReVerify Docs After Update**  
   *Contextual clues*: The first occurrence always happens right after the request is initiated and before any customerprovided correction (e.g., missing ID). The second occurrence follows a “Submit Additional” or an internal “Adjust Account” activity, indicating that new information has been supplied and the verification must be repeated. Naming them as *initial* vs. *after update* captures exactly this sequence.

2. **Call Customer  Call for Missing Info / Followup Confirmation Call**  
   *Contextual clues*: The first call is triggered by an incompleteness (missing documents or a discrepancy) and its purpose is to obtain the required data. The second call occurs after that data has been supplied or an internal change made, serving as confirmation. Splitting them into “for Missing Info” and “Followup Confirmation” makes their distinct objectives explicit.

All other activities appear only once per case (Initiate Request, Submit Additional, Adjust Account, Approve Request) and retain their original names because no ambiguity exists regarding their role in the workflow.
