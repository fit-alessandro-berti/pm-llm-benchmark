**Rationale for Bias-Mitigation Constraints**

1. **Coexistence with ManualReview (for minority decisions and all final decisions):**  
   Ensures that sensitive or potentially biased decisions always include a human/manual review step, which can catch discriminatory outcomes.

2. **Response from sensitive checks to BiasMitigationCheck:**  
   Enforces that whenever a sensitive attribute is accessed, a bias mitigation activity is triggered.

3. **Precedence of BiasMitigationCheck before Approve/Reject:**  
   Prevents final decisions from being made without first running the mitigation logic.

4. **Nonsuccession / Nonchainsuccession from sensitive checks to decisions:**  
   Blocks direct or immediate decisions right after sensitive attribute checks, reducing the risk of snap biased outcomes.

Together these constraints ensure that any sensitive information triggers extra scrutiny and prevents instant, potentially biased decisions, improving fairness in the loan review process.
