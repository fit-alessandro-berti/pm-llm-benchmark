**Normative Hire-to-Retire Process**

A standard Hire-to-Retire workflow is essentially a sequential chain of dependent activities:

1. **Post_Job_Ad**  2. **Screen_Candidates**  3. **Conduct_Interviews**  4. **Make_Hiring_Decision**  5. **Onboard_Employee**  6. **Add_to_Payroll**  7. **Close_Case**

The integrity of the process rests on two critical causal dependencies:
*   **Screening must precede interviewing** (you filter before you invest in interviews).
*   **Interviewing must precede the hiring decision** (the decision must be informed by candidate evaluation).
*   **Onboarding and payroll are mandatory** for a successful hire.

---

### Analysis of Model 1

**What it gets right:**  
Model 1 correctly orders the post-hiring chain: `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close`. It also enforces that both screening and interviewing happen after the job is posted, and it maintains screening as a prerequisite for the hiring decision (`Screen  Decide`).

**Anomalies in Model 1:**

1.  **The Interview is disconnected from the Decision.**  
    The model adds edges `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview`, but **no edge `Interview  Decide`**. Because `Interview` and `Decide` share only the common predecessor `Screen`, they are **concurrent (unordered)** in the partial order.  
    *Consequence:* A valid execution can make a hiring decision—and subsequently onboard and pay a candidate—**before, after, or entirely independently of conducting interviews**. This is a *fundamental* violation of hiring logic. The decision activity is not gated by the interview, meaning the process permits hiring someone who was never evaluated in an interview.

2.  **Interview is a structural “dead end.”**  
    `Interview` has no outgoing edges. Therefore, it does not constrain any downstream activity. A trace such as `Post  Screen  Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close  Interview` is valid. Having `Close_Case` occur before `Conduct_Interviews` inverts the intended lifecycle of the case.

*Severity:* **High.** The core purpose of a hiring process is to evaluate candidates (interviews) and then decide. Severing this link undermines the very essence of the process.

---

### Analysis of Model 2

**What it gets right:**  
Model 2 preserves the crucial dependency that the hiring decision must follow the interview (`Interview  Decide`). It also maintains the downstream chain from decision to close (`Decide  loop_onboarding  xor_payroll  Close`).

**Anomalies in Model 2:**

1.  **Screening is decoupled from the main flow.**  
    The model places `Screen` and `Interview` in parallel after `Post` (`Post  Screen` and `Post  Interview`), with **no edge `Screen  Interview`**. `Screen` has no outgoing edges, so it does not gate anything.  
    *Consequence:* Screening can occur **after** interviews, after the decision, or even after the case is closed. While this is poor practice (inefficient and risks interviewing unqualified candidates), the evaluation step (`Interview`) still feeds into the decision.

2.  **Payroll is optional.**  
    The `XOR(Payroll, skip)` operator means the process can close a hire case without ever adding the employee to payroll.  
    *Consequence:* This is a significant compliance and integrity issue—an employee could be hired and onboarded but never paid.

3.  **Onboarding is modeled as a loop.**  
    `*(Onboard, skip)` allows onboarding to be repeated arbitrarily. This is a deviation from the “execute once” norm, though it could be rationalized as rework for incomplete onboarding. It is the least severe of the anomalies.

*Severity:* The missing `Screen  Interview` order is **moderate**; the optional payroll is **high** from a compliance standpoint. However, the central evaluation-to-decision logic remains intact.

### Conclusion: Which Model is More Normative?

**Model 2 more closely aligns with a normative Hire-to-Retire process.**

**Justification:**  
While both models contain serious deviations, the **severity and location** of the anomalies differ. Model 1 breaks the **core causal backbone** of hiring: it allows a `Make_Hiring_Decision` to occur without being preceded by `Conduct_Interviews`. A hiring decision made without interview input is logically incoherent and fundamentally compromises process integrity.

Model 2, despite its flaws, **preserves the essential sequence** that an interview must inform the hiring decision (`Interview  Decide`). Its anomalies—screening being unordered (a workflow inefficiency) and payroll being optional (a compliance gap)—are serious but do not distort the primary *purpose* of the process to the same degree. The loop on onboarding is a minor control-flow artifact. Therefore, Model 2 retains more of the normative logic: candidates are evaluated, a decision is made, and the hire is processed toward closure. Model 1, by contrast, renders the interview an irrelevant side activity, which is a more profound structural failure.
