# BPMN Process Diagram: Dispatch of Goods

This is a **Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)** diagram depicting the "Dispatch of goods" process for a "Computer Hardware Shop." The diagram is organized into three horizontal **swimlanes** representing different departments.

## Swimlanes (Departments)
1. **Logistics** (top)
2. **Secretary** (middle)
3. **Warehouse** (bottom)

## Process Flow

### Start
- The process begins with a **start event** labeled "Ship goods" (Secretary lane)
- It flows into a **parallel gateway** (+) that splits the process into two concurrent paths

### Path 1 (Secretary Lane)
- **Clarify shipment method** task
- An **exclusive gateway** asks "Special handling?"
  - **No**  leads up to "Check if insurance is required" (uses "Order details" and "Customer information" data objects)
  - **Yes**  continues to "Get 3 offers from logistic companies"  "Select logistic company and place order"
- After insurance check, an **event-based gateway** ("always") flows to "Write package label"
- **Insure parcel** (Logistics lane) may be triggered "If insurance necessary"
- Paths reconverge at a **gateway** before merging

### Path 2 (Warehouse Lane)
- **Package goods** task (includes a **timer boundary event** of "48 hours")
  - If delayed, leads to "Notify customer about delay"
- Flows through a converging gateway

### Convergence and End
- Both paths merge at a **parallel gateway** (+)
- **Fill out Shipment paperwork** task
- **Move package to pickup area** task
- The process concludes with an **end event** labeled "Shipment prepared"

## Key Elements
- **Data objects:** Order details, Customer information (database)
- **Gateways:** Parallel (+), Exclusive (X), and Event-based (circular)
- **Boundary timer event:** 48-hour delay trigger on packaging

This diagram models how an order is processed, insured, packaged, and prepared for shipment across coordinated departments.
