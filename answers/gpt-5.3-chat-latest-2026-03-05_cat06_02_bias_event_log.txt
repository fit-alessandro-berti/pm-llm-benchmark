Bias appears mainly through the **CommunityGroup–based score adjustment** and its interaction with **LocalResident** status.

1. Community group bonus  
Applicants associated with **“Highland Civic Darts Club”** receive a **+10 score adjustment labeled “Community.”**  
Examples:
- C001: 710  720 (+10)  Approved  
- C004: 690  700 (+10)  Approved  

This adjustment is not tied to financial risk indicators but to **membership in a specific community group**. Applicants without such affiliation (C002, C003, C005) receive **no adjustment**.

2. Indirect geographic bias  
The club appears tied to **local residency** (both C001 and C004 are LocalResident = TRUE and club members). This means the bonus likely favors **people embedded in a particular local network**. Nonlocals or newcomers are structurally excluded from the benefit.

3. Outcome impact near the decision boundary  
The adjustment can change outcomes for borderline applicants:
- C004: Base score **690**, which may fall below approval threshold.  
- With the **+10 community bonus  700**, resulting in **approval**.

Thus the community attribute can **directly flip a decision**.

4. Unequal treatment of similar applicants  
Compare:
- C001: Base 710  +10  720  Approved  
- C003: Base 715  715  Rejected  

Even though C003 has a **higher base score than C001**, the absence of community affiliation means no bonus and a worse outcome.

5. Structural fairness concerns  
The process rewards **social affiliation and geographic embeddedness** rather than creditworthiness. People who:
- are new residents  
- lack access to local clubs  
- come from different social networks  

may systematically receive **lower effective scores**.

6. Manual review opacity  
Manual review occurs in all cases but **does not appear to counteract the bias**; reviewers accept the adjusted scores without questioning the community-based advantage.

Overall implication:  
The **CommunityGroup-based score adjustment introduces social and geographic bias**, benefiting applicants connected to a specific local organization. This can distort lending decisions by privileging **network membership over financial risk**, disadvantaging otherwise similar applicants who lack those affiliations.
