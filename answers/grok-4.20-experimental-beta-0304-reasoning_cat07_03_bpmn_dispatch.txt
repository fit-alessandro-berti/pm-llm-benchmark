**Visualization Description:**

This is a **business process flowchart** for a **parcel shipping/dispatch workflow**. The diagram is organized into three horizontal lanes/sections:

- **Outgoing** (top)
- **Internal** (middle) 
- **Incoming** (bottom)

### Overall Process Flow:

**Start:** The process begins at **"Ship parcel"** (left side).

### Key Decision Points and Paths:

1. **"Clarify shipment method"** (first diamond)
   - This is the main branching point with three possible outcomes: **yes**, **no**, and **mix**.

2. **Insurance Check Path** (upper/middle flow):
   - If insurance is potentially needed, it checks the **"Customer database"**.
   - Then asks **"Check if insurance is required?"**
   - If required  **"Write package label"**  **"Store parcel"** (top right).

3. **Logistics Selection Path**:
   - **"Get 3 offers from logistic companies"**
   - **"Select logistics company and place order"**

4. **Packaging & Preparation** (bottom section):
   - **"Package goods"** with a **48-hour** timeframe noted.
   - Decision: **"Notify customer about delay?"**
   - **"Fill out shipment paperwork"**
   - **"Write package label"** (appears again in the final stages)
   - Ends at **"Dispatchment prepared"** (final circle on the right).

### Notable Elements:

- The flowchart uses standard BPMN-style notation with:
  - Ovals for start/end points
  - Rectangles for process steps
  - Diamonds for decision points
  - Arrows showing flow direction

- There's a clear distinction between **outgoing** activities (storing, final dispatch) and **internal** preparation activities (packaging, paperwork).

- The process handles both standard shipments and more complex ones that require getting multiple logistics quotes and insurance verification.

This appears to be a **logistics/warehouse/e-commerce fulfillment process** designed to handle different types of shipments with appropriate checks for insurance, carrier selection, and customer communication when delays occur.
