## 3. Explanations and mitigation suggestions

### Why these attributes cause delays
1. **High complexity** naturally demands more evidence. If documents are requested one at a time, each cycle includes customer response time, often crossing overnight or weekend boundaries.
2. **Region B** high-complexity claims required more requests than Region A, hinting at inconsistent evaluation standards or less effective customer communication in Region B.
3. **Customer wait time** dominates: in cases 2002–2005, the “processing” time (actual work) is short; the elapsed time is mostly waiting for the customer to respond.
4. **Payment batching** added an extra overnight delay in case 2003, though it is secondary.

### Recommended actions
1. **Single comprehensive document request**  
   At the “Evaluate Claim” step, use a complexity-driven checklist so all needed documents are requested at once, rather than piecemeal.

2. **Complexity-based routing and SLAs**  
   - Low  straight-through processing.  
   - Medium/High  assign experienced adjusters and set per-tier response-time targets.

3. **Standardize Region B practices**  
   Investigate why Region B high-complexity claims need more iterations. Share Region A’s evaluation templates and train Region B adjusters on upfront completeness.

4. **Automated customer follow-up**  
   Use a customer portal, email/SMS reminders, and clear deadlines to shorten the wait between document requests and responses.

5. **Reduce payment delays**  
   Avoid next-day batching for approved claims; pay on the same business day when possible.

6. **Monitor KPIs by attribute**  
   Track metrics such as:
   - Lead time by complexity and region
   - Number of document requests per claim (by adjuster/region)
   - Approval and payment waiting times by manager/finance resource

This will help confirm whether Region B or specific adjusters are recurring bottlenecks as more data becomes available.
