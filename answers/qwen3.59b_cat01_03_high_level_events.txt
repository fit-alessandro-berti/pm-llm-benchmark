### Detailed Rationale for Grouping Logic

**1. Temporal Continuity:**
The events within each group occur in a tight temporal sequence with no significant gaps that would suggest an intervening process phase. For example, between "Preheat metal sheet" and "Pick up welding tool," there is a distinct shift in resource responsibility (Heating Unit  Operator B), signaling the end of one phase and the start of another.

**2. Resource Transition:**
Grouping was also informed by resource shifts.
*   **Phase 1** involves Operator A, Robot Arm #2, and Heating Unit #1.
*   **Phase 2** switches entirely to Operator B (with Tool ID W-12).
*   **Phase 3** switches to Quality Sensor #1, then Coating Unit #3, then Operator C.
This resource shift pattern is a strong indicator of process stage boundaries in manufacturing logs.

**3. Logical Dependency:**
*   **Preparation** must happen before **Assembly**. You cannot weld without aligning and preheating the sheet.
*   **Assembly** must happen before **Finishing**. Coating is applied to the welded joints; applying coating before welding would be physically impossible or logically incorrect in this context.
*   **QC** is embedded within the final phase because it validates the result of the Assembly step before the Finishing steps are finalized (e.g., ensuring weld integrity before sealing with coating).
