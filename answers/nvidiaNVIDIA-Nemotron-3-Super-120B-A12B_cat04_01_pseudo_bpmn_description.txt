### Overview of the Business Process  

The diagram describes an endtoend orderfulfilment workflow that begins when a **customer request** arrives and ends with a **confirmation** sent back to the customer.  
The process distinguishes **standard** requests (offtheshelf products/services) from **custom** requests (tailored solutions) and contains decision points (gateways) that trigger parallel work, conditional approvals, and possible rework loops.

## Meaning of the Gateways  

| Gateway Type | Symbolic Meaning | How It Controls Flow |
|--------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **XOR (Exclusive) Gateway** | Decision point where **exactly one** outgoing path is taken based on a condition. | Used for: <br>Ģ Requesttype branching (Standard vs. Custom). <br>Ģ Feasibility check (Yes/No). <br>Ģ Approvalneed decision. <br>Ģ Approvalgranted decision. |
| **AND (Parallel) Gateway** | Splits into **multiple** concurrent paths; all must complete before the flow can continue (join). | Used for running **Credit Check** and **Inventory Check** in parallel, shortening overall lead time. The join (implicit) ensures that both checks are finished before calculating the delivery date. |
| **Implicit Join** (after AND) | Synchronization barrier; the token proceeds only when all incoming tokens have arrived. | Guarantees that the delivery date is based on **both** credit and inventory outcomes. |

## Convergence & Divergence of Paths  

1. **Initial Divergence** ¢ After TaskA, the XOR gateway splits the flow into **Standard** and **Custom** streams.  
2. **Standard Stream** ¢ Performs validation, then an **AND split** for parallel credit/inventory checks, rejoins, calculates delivery date, and proceeds to the approval decision.  
3. **Custom Stream** ¢ Performs feasibility analysis, then an XOR gateway: if feasible  quotation; if not  rejection (process ends).  
4. **ReConvergence** ¢ Both streams (StandardTaskD, CustomTaskE1) meet at the **ōIs Approval Needed?ö** XOR gateway. At this point the process has a **readytofulfil** request (either a standard order with a promised date or a custom quote).  
5. **Approval SubProcess** ¢ If approval is required, the flow goes through manager approval; if not, it jumps straight to invoicing.  
6. **Rework Loop** ¢ A denied approval triggers TaskH (ōReevaluate Conditionsö) and loops back to the task that produced the questionable output: <br>Ģ For Custom: back to **TaskE1** (requote). <br>Ģ For Standard: back to **TaskD** (recalculate delivery date, perhaps after adjusting quantity or addressing credit/inventory issues). This loop can iterate multiple times until approval is granted.  
7. **Final Convergence** ¢ After invoicing (TaskG) the process always proceeds to TaskI (customer confirmation) and then ends.

## How Tasks Differ Between Standard & Custom Requests  

| Aspect | Standard Request Path | Custom Request Path |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Initial Validation** | TaskB1 checks basic eligibility (product existence, quantity limits). | TaskB2 performs a deeper feasibility analysis (design, engineering, resource capacity). |
| **Parallel Checks** | Credit&Inventory checks run concurrently (AND gateway). | No parallel checks; after feasibility, the flow goes straight to quotation or rejection. |
| **Output Before Approval** | TaskD produces a **delivery date** (based on credit & inventory). | TaskE1 produces a **custom quotation** (price, scope, lead time). |
| **Rework Target on Approval Denial** | Loop back to **TaskD** (recalculate delivery date). | Loop back to **TaskE1** (revise quotation). |
| **Endstate if Not Feasible** | Not applicable ¢ a standard request is assumed feasible after validation; if validation fails the process would abort (not shown). | TaskE2 sends a rejection notice and ends the process. |
| **Typical Triggers for Approval** | High order value, credit risk exceeding threshold, or policycontrolled product. | High customization cost, novel engineering work, or contractual risk thresholds. |

### Summary  

The pseudoBPMN model captures a **flexible orderfulfilment process** that:

1. **Classifies** incoming requests as standard or custom.  
2. **Validates** standard requests with basic checks and runs **credit** and **inventory** checks in parallel to compute a delivery date.  
3. **Evaluates** feasibility for custom requests, either producing a quotation or rejecting outright.  
4. **Decides**, based on business rules, whether managerial approval is required.  
5. **Generates** the final invoice once approval (or autoapproval) is obtained.  
6. **Notifies** the customer of the confirmed order.  
7. **Allows** rework when approval is denied, sending the flow back to the step that produced the questionable output, ensuring that the request is corrected before finalisation.

This structure enables the organisation to handle highvolume, lowrisk standard orders efficiently while allocating extra scrutiny and resources to complex, highvalue custom requests, all within a clear, traceable workflow.
