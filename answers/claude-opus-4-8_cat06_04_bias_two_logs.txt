# Bias Analysis of the Two Event Logs

## Which Log Exhibits Bias

**Group B (Unprotected Group)** exhibits the bias. Specifically, the bias is introduced through the **"Community Boost"** mechanism applied in the `ScoreAdjustment` column, which systematically benefits members of Group B while no equivalent mechanism exists for Group A.

## How the Bias Manifests

### 1. The "Community Boost" as a Proxy for Protected Status

| Attribute | Group A | Group B |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| LocalResident | FALSE (all) | TRUE (all) |
| CommunityGroup eligibility | None (no access) | "Highland Civic Darts Club" available |
| ScoreAdjustment possible | Always 0 | +10 when in community group |

The boost is tied to `LocalResident = TRUE` and membership in a local community group ("Highland Civic Darts Club"). Group A applicants are **structurally excluded** from this benefit because they are non-residents (`LocalResident = FALSE`) and have no community affiliation. This makes residency/community membership a **proxy variable** that correlates with the protected attribute and channels favorable treatment to one group only.

### 2. The Smoking-Gun Case: U003

The clearest evidence is **U003**:

- **Preliminary score: 695** — the *lowest* score in either log.
- After **+10 Community Boost  705 (Adjusted)**  **Approved**.

Compare this to **P002 (Group A)**:
- **Preliminary score: 710** — *higher* than U003's raw score.
- No adjustment available  **Rejected**.

**A lower-scoring applicant in Group B (695) was approved, while a higher-scoring applicant in Group A (710) was rejected.** This is a textbook outcome inversion driven solely by the boost — the decision flipped based on group-linked eligibility, not creditworthiness.

### 3. Disparate Outcomes Despite Similar Merits

| Case | Group | Raw Score | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
|------|-------|-----------|------------|-------------|----------|
| P001 | A | 720 | 0 | 720 | Approved |
| P002 | A | 710 | 0 | 710 | **Rejected** |
| P003 | A | 740 | 0 | 740 | Approved |
| U001 | B | 720 | +10 | 730 | Approved |
| U002 | B | 710 | 0 | 710 | **Rejected** |
| U003 | B | 695 | +10 | 705 | **Approved** |

- The apparent approval threshold sits somewhere around **~715–720**.
- U002 (710, no boost) was rejected — consistent with P002.
- But U003 (695) was *only* approved **because** the boost lifted it above the threshold.

This demonstrates that the boost is **outcome-determinative** at the margin and is available to **only one group**.

## Why This Is Systematic, Not Random

1. **Structural exclusion:** Group A can *never* receive the adjustment — the eligibility criteria (local residency + community group) are unavailable to them by definition.
2. **Consistent direction:** Every boost favors Group B; none disadvantages it.
3. **Disparate impact:** Even if the rule appears "neutral" (anyone in a darts club gets +10), it produces unequal results because access to the qualifying condition is unequally distributed and aligns with the protected/unprotected divide.

## Process-Mining Observations

- **Activity sequences are identical** across both logs (ApplicationReceived  DataValidation  PreliminaryScoring  ManualReview  FinalDecision), and **timestamps/throughput are comparable**. So the bias is **not** procedural (no extra scrutiny, no delays).
- The bias is purely **data/attribute-driven**, embedded in the `ScoreAdjustment` step at PreliminaryScoring. This makes it harder to detect with conformance checking alone — it requires **attribute-level fairness analysis**.

## Recommendations

1. **Remove or audit the Community Boost** — assess whether membership in a social club has any legitimate, validated relationship to credit risk. If not, it is an arbitrary advantage.
2. **Test for proxy discrimination** — measure correlation between `LocalResident`/`CommunityGroup` and protected-class membership.
3. **Counterfactual testing** — re-run decisions with the boost removed (U001  720 still approved; U003  695 likely rejected). The flip in U003 confirms the boost's causal role.
4. **Apply equal eligibility or eliminate the adjustment** to ensure decisions rest on legitimate, group-neutral factors.

## Summary

**Group B's log is biased.** The bias manifests as a group-restricted **+10 "Community Boost"** that only `LocalResident = TRUE` applicants with community-group ties can receive. This proxy-based advantage produces **disparate outcomes** — most starkly, the approval of a 695-score Group B applicant (U003) while a 710-score Group A applicant (P002) was rejected — revealing systematic favoritism that is unrelated to objective applicant merit.
