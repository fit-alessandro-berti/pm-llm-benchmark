This image displays a **Causal Net (C-net)**, which is a type of business process model or flowchart used in process mining to represent workflows, choices, concurrency, and loops. 

The visualization is divided into two main parts: the **main process model** on the left/top, and a **notation legend** in a gray box on the bottom-right.

---

### 1. The Legend (Process Notation)
The gray box in the bottom-right explains the logic gates (splits and joins) used in the model. These gates determine how paths branch out (splits) or merge back together (joins):
*   **XOR-split / XOR-join:** Represented by individual black dots on separate transition lines. This indicates an *exclusive OR* choice (only one of the paths is taken/merged).
*   **AND-split / AND-join:** Represented by black dots connected by a single continuous curved arc. This indicates *concurrency* (all paths must be executed/merged together).
*   **OR-split / OR-join:** Represented by black dots connected by overlapping arcs. This indicates an *inclusive OR* choice (any non-empty subset of paths can be chosen/merged).

---

### 2. Step-by-Step Process Flow
The main diagram illustrates the lifecycle of processing a request, starting at node **`a`** and ending at node **`z`**:

#### **Step 1: Initiation**
*   **Node `a` (register request):** This is the start of the process. It has an **OR-split** (indicated by overlapping arcs connecting the output dots), meaning it can trigger either "examine thoroughly" (`b`) OR "examine casually" (`c`), and optionally "check ticket" (`d`).

#### **Step 2: Examination and Verification**
These three activities run in parallel or independently based on the initial decision:
*   **Node `b` (examine thoroughly)**
*   **Node `c` (examine casually)**
*   **Node `d` (check ticket)**
*   *Note:* Each of these three nodes has an **XOR-join** at its input, meaning it can receive a task either from the initial registration (`a`) or from the reinitiation step (`f`), but not both at the same time.

#### **Step 3: Evaluation & Decision**
*   **Node `e` (decide):** The outputs of `b`, `c`, and `d` converge into node `e` using an **OR-join** (indicated by the overlapping arcs at the input of `e`). 
*   From `e`, there is an **XOR-split** leading to three mutually exclusive outcomes:
    1.  Proceed to **`g`** (pay compensation).
    2.  Proceed to **`h`** (reject request).
    3.  Proceed to **`f`** (reinitiate request) to redo the process.

#### **Step 4: Feedback Loop**
*   **Node `f` (reinitiate request):** If the decision is to reinitiate, the process flows to `f`. From `f`, an **OR-split** routes the request back to the examination steps (`b`, `c`, and/or `d`) to repeat the evaluation cycle.

#### **Step 5: Resolution & End**
*   **Node `g` (pay compensation)** or **Node `h` (reject request)** are the two final action steps.
*   **Node `z` (end):** Both `g` and `h` lead into the final node `z` via an **XOR-join** (as only one of the final actions will have occurred), marking the completion of the process.
