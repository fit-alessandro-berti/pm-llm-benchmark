### 2. Rationale for Groupings

Here is the justification for how and why these specific low-level events were aggregated:

#### **Step 1: Material Preparation**
*   **Events Grouped:** Retrieval, Scanning, Placement, Alignment, Preheating.
*   **Reasoning:** All these activities occur at the beginning of the lifecycle. They share the common goal of transforming a stored raw material (metal sheet) into a state that is physically ready for the main assembly work.
    *   *Retrieval, Scanning, and Placement* are logistical setup tasks handled primarily by **Operator A**.
    *   *Alignment and Preheating* are machine-setup tasks required to ensure the material is positioned correctly and physically conditioned (heated) for the subsequent welding process.

#### **Step 2: Primary Fabrication (Welding)**
*   **Events Grouped:** Picking up the tool, Welding Corner A, Welding Corner B.
*   **Reasoning:** This represents the core value-adding phase of the manufacturing process where the product is actually constructed.
    *   **Operator B** takes over here. The act of picking up the tool is a direct prerequisite to the welding actions. In process mining, setup actions immediately preceding a main task are often bundled with that task to define the "Work Station Activity."

#### **Step 3: Automated Quality Control (Inline Check)**
*   **Events Grouped:** Measuring weld integrity.
*   **Reasoning:** This event happens immediately after fabrication but before the item moves to the next physical station (Coating). It acts as a gatekeeper. Although it is a single event in this log, it represents a distinct logical phase—verifying the output of the previous step before proceeding to finishing. Separating this allows the business to track "First Pass Yield" specifically for the welding stage.

#### **Step 4: Surface Finishing**
*   **Events Grouped:** Applying coating, Drying.
*   **Reasoning:** Once the structural integrity is verified, the item moves to the post-processing phase. Applying the chemical coating and then curing it (drying) are chemically and temporally linked steps that constitute the "Finishing" stage of the product.

#### **Step 5: Final Validation**
*   **Events Grouped:** Visual check.
*   **Reasoning:** This is the end-of-line sign-off. Performed by **Operator C**, this represents the final release of the product. It is separated from the automated sensor check because it is a manual, holistic inspection occurring at the very end of the timeline.
