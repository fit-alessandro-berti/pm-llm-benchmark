Here’s a set of targeted, open-ended questions I’d ask to clarify the process and fill in the gaps:

1. **What triggers the onboarding process to begin, and are there different entry points depending on the type of landlord or property?**

2. **Can you walk me through the end-to-end process in the exact order it usually happens, from first contact to move-in?**

3. **Which documents are always required before a property can move forward, and are there any documents that are only needed in certain cities or for certain property types?**

4. **How do you determine whether a document is missing, outdated, or acceptable, and who makes that judgment?**

5. **What happens when the landlord cannot provide the needed documentation right away? Is the property paused, queued, or handled differently?**

6. **What property details are captured during onboarding, and which of those are considered mandatory versus optional?**

7. **Who is responsible for entering and validating property information in the central database?**

8. **How is the dedicated property manager assigned, and what are the relative priorities among workload, geography, property type, and landlord-specific needs?**

9. **Are there cases where the assignment is manual rather than rule-based, and who has the authority to override the normal assignment process?**

10. **What does the introductory call or meeting typically cover, and what outcomes are you expecting from that conversation?**

11. **Which landlord preferences or special conditions need to be resolved before the property can be marketed or activated?**

12. **How are special policies like pets, tenant maintenance responsibilities, or payment channels decided and documented?**

13. **What is the sequence for scheduling the photographer and inspector, and do they always happen in parallel or in a specific order?**

14. **What criteria does the inspector use to determine whether the property passes, and what are the most common reasons for failure?**

16. **Is there a standard timeline for re-inspection after issues are corrected, and does that vary by severity or property type?**

17. **How does the marketing team use the information from the photographer, inspector, and pricing models to create the listing?**

18. **What factors influence the initial rental price range, and how often are pricing recommendations revisited before activation?**

24. **Who has the authority to negotiate lease terms, approve discounts, or make exceptions for a strong candidate?**

25. **What are the standard steps from tenant selection to lease finalization and move-in scheduling?**

26. **Are there any conditions that must be met before the lease can be signed, such as deposits, insurance, or compliance checks?**

27. **What kinds of quality checks and internal audits happen after the lease is prepared or signed, and in what order do they occur?**

28. **Which teams are involved in post-onboarding review, and what are they each looking for?**

29. **How do you handle a finding from compliance, finance, or IT that requires a change after the property has already been activated?**

30. **What are the most common exceptions or edge cases in this process, such as special insurance needs, custom lease clauses, or unusual property types?**

31. **How are exceptions approved, documented, and communicated across teams?**

32. **What communication channels are used during onboarding, and how do you ensure that decisions and status updates are tracked consistently?**

33. **Are there specific handoff points where responsibility shifts from one role or team to another, and where do those handoffs tend to break down?**

34. **What does a successful onboarding look like from your perspective, and what metrics or signals tell you the process worked well?**

35. **Where do delays or rework most often occur in the process, and what are the usual root causes?**

36. **If you could improve one part of this onboarding workflow, which part would you choose and why?**
