### Explanation of case grouping and activity naming

**Case identification (grouping into process instances):**
- I used a **work-item / artifact-centric** case notion: each distinct item the user worked on becomes a case:
  - `DOC-Document1.docx`, `DOC-Quarterly_Report.docx`, `XLSX-Budget_2024.xlsx`, `PDF-Report_Draft.pdf`, and the specific email thread `EMAIL-Annual_Meeting`.
- This choice yields analyst-friendly cases that correspond to typical knowledge-work units (edit a document, answer an email, review a PDF, update a spreadsheet).

**Activity naming (low-level  higher-level):**
- `FOCUS` and `SWITCH` (to a target app/window) were normalized to **“Open/Activate …”** because, in practice, both represent bringing an artifact into the foreground to work on it.
- `TYPING` events were mapped to **“Edit …”** (document/spreadsheet) or **“Compose Reply”** (email), preserving the typed text as an attribute (`input`) for optional later analysis.
- Email interactions were lifted from clicks/scrolls into mail-meaningful steps: **Open Email**, **Read/Scan Email**, **Start Reply**, **Send Email**.
- PDF actions were mapped to **Review PDF** and **Highlight Key Text**, preserving the highlighted string as `selection`.

This produces a coherent narrative of the session: the user alternates between drafting `Document1`, responding to an annual meeting email, reviewing a PDF draft, updating a budget spreadsheet, then finalizing edits in `Document1` and `Quarterly_Report`.
