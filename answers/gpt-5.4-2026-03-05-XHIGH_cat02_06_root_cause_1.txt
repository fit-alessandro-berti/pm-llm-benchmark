## 2) Potential root causes of the delays

## A. Escalation is a major delay factor
Escalated cases:
- **102**
- **105**

### Evidence
- **Case 102**
  - Assign to L1: 09:00
  - Escalate to L2: 11:30  
  - L2 investigation starts: 14:00  
  - That is **5 hours** from assignment to actual investigation after escalation-related handling.

- **Case 105**
  - Escalate to L2: 2024-03-01 10:00
  - L2 investigation starts: 2024-03-02 14:00  
  - That is a **28-hour wait** after escalation.

### Interpretation
Escalation introduces:
- a handoff between teams
- queueing before Level-2 picks up the ticket
- possible context loss / rework

This is especially visible in **Case 105**, where escalation is the biggest contributor to the delay.

---

## B. There is a strong ôovernight holdö pattern after investigation
This is the strongest repeated pattern in the log.

### Evidence
- **Case 102:** Investigate 14:00  Resolve next day 09:00 = **19 hours**
- **Case 104:** Investigate 13:00  Resolve next day 08:00 = **19 hours**
- **Case 105:** Investigate 14:00  Resolve next day 09:00 = **19 hours**

That repeated ~19-hour gap appears in multiple cases, even with different paths.

### Interpretation
This suggests a systemic bottleneck between **Investigate Issue** and **Resolve Ticket**, such as:
- end-of-day cutoff / shift boundary
- batching of resolution work until next morning
- waiting for approvals or specialized follow-up
- limited staffing outside certain hours

Important point: **Case 104 has no escalation**, yet it still shows the same 19-hour post-investigation delay.  
So **escalation is not the only cause**. There is also a broader process issue after investigation.

## D. Rework / duplicate investigation may be happening
### Evidence
**Case 105** has:
- Investigate Issue at Level-1
- then Escalate to Level-2

### Interpretation
This suggests:

This adds elapsed time and handoff friction.

## 3) How these factors drive longer cycle times

## Biggest contributor: waiting time, not closing time
The **Close Ticket** step is always quick:
- 15ľ30 minutes after resolution

So the bottleneck is **not closure**.

Instead, total cycle time grows because of long gaps:
- before investigation
- after escalation
- between investigation and resolution

### In practice:
- If a ticket reaches investigation late enough, it seems to miss same-day completion and gets pushed to the next day.
- If a ticket also needs Level-2, it may wait in another queue before investigation even resumes.
- When both happen together, cycle time explodes.

That is exactly what happens in **Case 105**:
1. Escalation wait = **28h**
2. Post-investigation to resolution = **19h**
3. Total = **49h 05m**

---

## 4) Recommendations to address the bottlenecks

## A. Improve escalation handling
- Set an SLA for **Escalate  Level-2 pickup**
- Auto-route clearly complex tickets directly to Level-2 from triage
- Improve handoff quality so Level-2 does not need to re-investigate from scratch

## B. Reduce the investigation-to-resolution overnight delay
The repeated 19-hour gap suggests a systemic issue.
Possible fixes:
- add late-day / next-shift coverage
- avoid batching resolutions to the next morning
- introduce explicit handover between shifts
- prioritize tickets already in investigation so they can finish same day

## C. Speed up first action after assignment
- Monitor **Assign  first work action** as a KPI
- Rebalance workloads across agents
- Flag tickets that sit too long without investigation

## D. Strengthen triage
- Better triage rules can reduce misrouting
- More accurate routing can prevent unnecessary time at Level-1 before escalation

## E. Add better process data
To confirm root causes more precisely, log:
- ticket type / category
- severity
- assigned team/agent
- wait reason (customer response, approval, vendor dependency, etc.)

That would help distinguish true work time from queue/wait time.

---

## Bottom line
The main bottleneck is **elapsed waiting time between activities**, especially:
1. **post-investigation overnight delays** (~19h pattern), and
2. **Level-2 escalation delays** (especially Case 105).

### Most likely problematic cases
- **105**: worst case, driven by escalation backlog + overnight delay
- **102**: delayed by escalation + overnight delay
- **104**: delayed even without escalation, showing a broader investigation-to-resolution bottleneck

So the clearest improvement opportunities are:
- faster Level-2 pickup,
- earlier investigation,
- and preventing tickets from sitting overnight after investigation.
