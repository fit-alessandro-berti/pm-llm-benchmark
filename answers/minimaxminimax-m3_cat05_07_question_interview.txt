# Targeted Questions for the Interviewee

## Initial Contact & Documentation Review

1. **What does a typical first conversation with a landlord look like?** Is there a standard intake form or script, and what information do you prioritize gathering upfront?
2. **Which documents are most frequently missing or outdated,** and how long does it usually take to obtain the necessary updates? Are there cases where you've had to turn down a property because the documentation could not be completed?
3. **How do you handle situations where the landlord's property details (square footage, amenities, etc.) differ from what official records show?** Who has the final authority to resolve discrepancies?

## Property Manager Assignment

4. **What are the key factors that determine which property manager gets assigned to a new property?** Can you walk me through a recent example where the assignment was particularly complex or contested?
5. **How do you handle the scenario where no property manager is an ideal match** for a property's unique requirements? Do you ever reassign properties mid-process, and if so, what triggers that?
6. **What is the typical workload for a property manager,** and how many new properties can one manager typically take on per month?

## Introductory Meeting & Expectations

7. **What agenda items are covered in the introductory call or meeting between the property manager and landlord?** How long does this typically last, and is it in-person or virtual?
8. **How do you document the agreements made during this meeting,** and how are those special conditions communicated to other teams (e.g., marketing, leasing)?
9. **What happens if the landlord and property manager cannot agree on key policies** (e.g., pet policies, maintenance responsibilities) during this meeting?

## Inspection & Marketing

10. **Who selects the photographer and inspector,** and what qualifications or certifications are required? Are they internal staff or external contractors?
11. **What are the most common issues found during inspections,** and who typically bears the cost of repairs—the landlord or the company? How is this negotiated?
12. **How do you decide which marketing channels and materials are appropriate** for different types of properties (e.g., luxury units, student housing, furnished rentals)?
13. **How do you keep internal pricing models current with market trends,** and who is responsible for setting the final listed price?

## Tenant Screening & Selection

14. **What criteria are most heavily weighted in your tenant screening process,** and how do you balance them (e.g., credit score vs. employment history vs. references)?
15. **How do you handle the situation when multiple qualified tenants are interested** in the same property? Is there a formal scoring system, or does it rely on the property manager's judgment?
16. **What is your policy on lease term flexibility,** and how often do tenants negotiate for shorter or longer terms than the standard?

## Lease Finalization & Move-In

17. **What security deposit collection methods do you accept,** and are there any holdback or escrow arrangements in place?
18. **How is the move-in date coordinated,** and what does the actual handoff process look like? Who is present at the property for the move-in?

## Quality Checks & Internal Audits

19. **At what point in the process do the compliance officer, financial analyst, and IT checks typically occur?** Are they sequential or parallel?
20. **What happens if an audit reveals an issue after the lease has been signed** or after a tenant has moved in? Is there a formal remediation process?
21. **How are audit findings tracked and resolved,** and is there a feedback loop to improve the onboarding process?

## Exceptions & Special Cases

22. **Can you share an example of a property that required special handling**—such as unique insurance requirements, custom lease clauses, or unusual landlord demands? How was that case managed?
23. **Who has the authority to approve custom lease clauses** or deviations from standard policies, and what is the review process?
24. **Are there any properties you've had to remove from the portfolio** after onboarding, and if so, what were the most common reasons?

## Communication & Tracking

25. **What tools and systems do you use to track communication** between the regional office, property managers, landlords, and support teams? How do you ensure no critical information gets lost?
26. **How often do landlords and prospective tenants receive status updates** during the onboarding process, and in what format (email, phone, portal)?
27. **Is there a single source of truth for the property's onboarding status,** or do different teams rely on separate tracking systems?

## Timing & Sequencing

28. **What is the typical end-to-end timeline** from the landlord's initial contact to property activation, and how much does it vary by property type?
29. **Which steps in the process are most likely to cause delays,** and what strategies do you use to mitigate those bottlenecks?
30. **Are there steps in the process that can happen in parallel** (e.g., marketing creation while awaiting inspection), or is everything strictly sequential?

## Roles & Responsibilities

31. **Can you describe the core responsibilities of each role** involved in the onboarding process (coordinator, property manager, leasing agent, compliance officer, etc.) and how they interact?
32. **Who has the final authority to approve a property for activation** in the rental portal, and is there a formal sign-off process?
33. **Who handles disputes or escalations** between landlords, tenants, and internal teams during the onboarding phase, and what is the typical resolution path?

These questions should help you build a comprehensive understanding of the property onboarding process, including the decision points, stakeholder interactions, and exception-handling mechanisms that the interviewee may not have covered in their initial description.
