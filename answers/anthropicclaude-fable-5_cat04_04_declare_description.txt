# The Loan Application Process: A Practical Walkthrough of the DECLARE Model

## 1. The Process at a Glance (Happy Path)

Reading the constraints together, the intended end-to-end flow looks like this:

1. **Receive_Application** — the case always starts here.
2. **Preliminary_Credit_Check** — performed exactly once, triggered by the application.
3. **Gather_Additional_Documents** — follows the credit check.
4. **Quality_Assurance_Review** — verifies the file's completeness and compliance.
5. **Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package** — immediately follows QA.
6. **Authorize_Contract_Terms** — official sign-off, only after QA has occurred.
7. **Transfer_Funds** — disbursement, immediately following the assembled offer.
8. **Notify_Customer** — the customer is informed after funds move, closing the case.

Meanwhile, **Proceed_Without_Compliance** must *never* appear in any execution of the process.

---

## 2. How Each Constraint Shapes the Process

### Starting and mandatory steps

- **`init(Receive_Application)`** — Every case begins with the customer's application. Nothing can happen "to" a loan that doesn't yet exist; this prevents phantom processing or work on unsolicited records.
- **`existence(Receive_Application)`, `existence(Gather_Additional_Documents)`** — These activities are *mandatory* in every case. Documents must always be collected — there is no "fast lane" that skips verification of pay stubs and identification, which is essential for KYC (Know Your Customer) and anti-money-laundering compliance.
- **`exactly_one(Preliminary_Credit_Check)`** — The credit check happens once and only once. This prevents both *omission* (lending blind) and *repetition* (multiple hard credit pulls, which can harm the applicant's credit score and create inconsistent decision bases).
- **`absence(Proceed_Without_Compliance)`** — A hard prohibition. No execution path may ever bypass compliance. This is the model's "regulatory firewall."

### Ordering the early phase

- **`response(Receive_Application  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`** — Once an application arrives, a credit check *must* eventually follow. No application is allowed to sit unevaluated; every customer gets an assessment.
- **`succession(Preliminary_Credit_Check  Gather_Additional_Documents)`** — Document gathering must follow the credit check, and may only happen after it. The rationale: don't burden applicants (and staff) with collecting paperwork for someone who fails the basic creditworthiness screen. It's a triage mechanism that saves cost and customer effort.

### Quality control and offer preparation

- **`altsuccession(Gather_Additional_Documents  Quality_Assurance_Review)`** — Each round of document collection must be followed by a QA review before documents can be gathered again. This ensures every batch of evidence is actually checked — no documents pile up unreviewed.
- **`chainsuccession(Quality_Assurance_Review  Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package)`** — The offer package is assembled *immediately* after QA, and only then. This tight coupling means the offer is built on a freshly verified file: no time gap in which data could change, be tampered with, or become stale.
- **`responded_existence(Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Quality_Assurance_Review)`** — If an offer package exists anywhere in the case, a QA review must exist somewhere too. A belt-and-braces guarantee: no offer ever reaches a customer without quality verification.

### Authorization

- **`precedence(Quality_Assurance_Review  Authorize_Contract_Terms)`** — Terms can only be authorized after at least one QA review. Sign-off without verification is impossible.
- **`chainprecedence(Authorize_Contract_Terms  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`** — Authorization must be directly grounded in the credit assessment; the practical intent is that the official approval decision is tied tightly to the creditworthiness evaluation, not made on stale or detached information. *(Note: read with maximal strictness, this constraint sits in tension with the precedence rule above — see Section 4.)*
- **`coexistence(Gather_Additional_Documents  Authorize_Contract_Terms)`** — These two always appear together. You cannot authorize terms in a case where documents were never gathered, and conversely, if you went to the trouble of collecting documents, the case must reach a formal terms decision — no abandoned, half-processed files.

### Disbursement and notification

- **`chainresponse(Assemble_Loan_Offer_Package  Transfer_Funds)`** — Disbursement immediately follows the finished offer package. Once everything is verified and assembled, funds move without delay — good for customer satisfaction and for limiting the window in which an approved-but-unfunded loan could be manipulated.
- **`altresponse(Transfer_Funds  Notify_Customer)`** — Every transfer must be followed by a customer notification before any further transfer can occur. No "silent" disbursements; the customer is always told their money has moved (a key fraud-detection touchpoint — customers can flag transfers they didn't expect).
- **`altprecedence(Notify_Customer  Transfer_Funds)`** — Conversely, each notification must be preceded by a transfer, with no duplicate notifications in between. The bank never tells a customer "your funds are on the way" when nothing has actually happened — preventing misleading communication.
- **`nonchainsuccession(Authorize_Contract_Terms  Notify_Customer)`** — Notification may not *immediately* follow authorization. Something must happen in between (in practice, the funds transfer). This stops the bank from prematurely announcing an outcome before the operational steps are complete.

### Closing the case cleanly

- **`nonsuccession(Notify_Customer  Preliminary_Credit_Check)`** — Once the customer has been notified, no credit check may ever follow. The decision is final; the bank can't quietly re-evaluate a customer after telling them the outcome. This protects decision integrity and customer trust.
- **`noncoexistence(Transfer_Funds, Receive_Application)`** — Read practically: disbursement and application intake belong to *separate* case lifecycles. Once funds flow, this case is finished — a new loan requires a new case, preventing the dangerous pattern of reusing one approved file for additional disbursements or piggy-backed applications.

## 3. Real-World Motivations Behind the Rules

| Motivation | Constraints serving it |
|---|---|
| **Regulatory compliance** | `absence(Proceed_Without_Compliance)`, mandatory document gathering, QA before authorization |
| **Risk management** | Credit check before documents, exactly-one credit check, authorization grounded in the credit decision |
| **Fraud prevention** | Tight QAAssembleTransfer chain (no manipulation window), mandatory notification after every transfer, case closure after disbursement |
| **Operational efficiency** | Triage ordering (don't gather documents for rejected applicants), one-and-only-one credit check (no rework) |
| **Customer satisfaction & trust** | Guaranteed response to every application, immediate disbursement after approval, honest notifications (only after real transfers), finality of decisions |

---

## 4. Consequences of Violating the Order

- **Documents gathered before the credit check**  wasted applicant effort and operational cost on cases that fail basic screening; possible privacy over-collection.
- **Authorization without QA review**  unverified terms reach customers: pricing errors, regulatory breaches, contracts that may be legally unenforceable, reputational damage.
- **Transfer before assembly/authorization**  money disbursed against an incomplete or unapproved file — the classic vector for internal fraud and the most severe financial exposure in the process.
- **No notification after transfer**  customers unaware of account activity; fraudulent disbursements go undetected longer; potential breach of disclosure obligations.
- **Credit check after notification**  undermines decision finality; could constitute unauthorized credit inquiries with legal consequences.
- **`Proceed_Without_Compliance` occurring at all**  an immediate, categorical violation — fines, license risk, and audit failure. This is why it's modeled as an absolute *absence* rather than an ordering rule.

**A modeling note:** taken with maximal literalness, a few constraints are in tension — e.g., `noncoexistence(Transfer_Funds, Receive_Application)` combined with mandatory `Receive_Application` would forbid `Transfer_Funds` entirely, and `chainprecedence(Authorize_Contract_Terms, Preliminary_Credit_Check)` is hard to reconcile with QA preceding authorization. In practice, such constraints should be read as expressing *intent* — disbursement closes the case and authorization must rest directly on the credit assessment — and a real deployment would refine them (e.g., scoping noncoexistence to events *after* the transfer) to keep the model satisfiable.

---

## 5. Summary

The DECLARE model encodes a loan process that is **strictly gated** at its risky points (credit assessment, quality assurance, authorization, disbursement) while remaining **declaratively flexible** elsewhere. Rather than dictating one rigid sequence, it specifies the minimal set of rules — what must happen, what must never happen, and what must come before what — that any compliant execution must respect. The result is a process that protects the institution (risk, fraud, regulation), the customer (fair evaluation, honest communication, prompt funding), and the integrity of the decision itself.
