### 2. Root Cause Analysis by Attribute

By correlating the duration with the provided attributes, we can deduce the following root causes:

#### A. Complexity & Process Loops (Primary Driver)
*   **Observation:** There is a direct correlation between **Complexity** and the number of **"Request Additional Documents"** activities.
    *   **Low Complexity:** 0 requests. Process flows linearly and quickly.
    *   **Medium Complexity:** 1 request. Adds ~1 day.
    *   **High Complexity:** 2 to 3 requests. Adds 2 to 3 days.
*   **Root Cause:** High complexity claims are not submitted with sufficient information. The process enters a rework loop ("Request Additional Documents") which halts progress until the customer responds. Each iteration adds significant waiting time.

#### B. Resource Variance (Secondary Driver)
*   **Observation:** Comparing the two Adjusters handling High Complexity claims:
    *   **Adjuster_Mike (Region A, Case 2003):** Made **2** document requests. Duration: ~2 Days.
    *   **Adjuster_Lisa (Region B, Case 2005):** Made **3** document requests. Duration: ~3.2 Days.
*   **Root Cause:** Adjuster_Lisa required one additional round of documentation compared to Mike for the same complexity level. This suggests a potential disparity in:
    *   **Initial Assessment Skill:** Lisa may be missing required documents during the first evaluation, leading to piecemeal requests.
    *   **Workload:** Lisa handled 3 cases (2002, 2004, 2005) while Mike handled 2 (2001, 2003). Higher workload may lead to rushed evaluations and more follow-up requests.

#### C. Manager Bottlenecks

    *   **Manager_Ann** approves Low/Medium claims (Cases 2001, 2002, 2004).
    *   **Manager_Bill** approves High Complexity claims (Cases 2003, 2005).

### 3. Explanations and Mitigation Strategies

#### Explanation of Performance Issues
The extended lead times are not caused by the actual processing work (which takes minutes), but by **waiting time** introduced by the **"Request Additional Documents"** loop.
1.  **Incomplete Submissions:** High Complexity claimants are likely unsure of what to submit, leading to back-and-forth communication.
2.  **Adjuster Efficiency:** Adjusters may not be identifying *all* missing requirements in the first pass (especially evident in Case 2005 with 3 requests).
3.  **Non-Working Hour Gaps:** Timestamps show requests made late in the day (e.g., 17:00) with approvals happening the next morning. This indicates the process pauses overnight between internal steps, not just while waiting for the customer.

#### Recommendations to Mitigate Root Causes

| Root Cause | Recommendation | Expected Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Incomplete Initial Submissions** | **Dynamic Submission Checklists:** Implement a system where the "Submit Claim" form changes based on the selected Complexity level. High Complexity claims should enforce mandatory field uploads before submission is allowed. | Reduces the need for "Request Additional Documents" loops by ensuring data completeness upfront. |
| **Adjuster Variance (Lisa vs. Mike)** | **Targeted Training & Workload Balancing:** Review Adjuster_Lisa's evaluation logs. Provide training on identifying all required documents for High Complexity claims in the first pass. Additionally, balance case volume between Mike and Lisa to prevent fatigue-induced errors. | Reduces the number of document request iterations per case (aim for max 1 request for High Complexity). |
| **Manager Bottleneck (Bill)** | **Delegation or SLA Enforcement:** Empower Senior Adjusters to approve High Complexity claims up to a certain threshold to bypass Manager_Bill. Alternatively, implement an SLA requiring approval within 4 hours of document receipt. | Reduces the overnight wait time between "Final Document Received" and "Approve Claim." |
| **Process Latency** | **Automated Status Notifications:** If a document is requested, send an automated SMS/Email to the claimant with a secure upload link. | Reduces the external waiting time for the customer to return documents. |

**Summary:**
To improve performance, the organization must shift focus from speeding up the approval steps to **preventing the document request loops**. By ensuring High Complexity claims are complete at the "Submit" stage and improving the Adjusters' ability to identify all missing items in one go, lead times for cases like 2003 and 2005 could be reduced from days to hours.
