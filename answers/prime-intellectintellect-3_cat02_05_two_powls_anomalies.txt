### Analysis of POWL Models for Hire-to-Retire Process

#### **Standard Hire-to-Retire Sequence**
A normative process should follow this order:  
1. **Post Job Ad**  2. **Screen Candidates**  3. **Conduct Interviews**  4. **Make Hiring Decision**  5. **Onboard Employee**  6. **Add to Payroll**  7. **Close Case**.  
Key logic:  
- Screening **must precede** interviews.  
- Interviews **must precede** the hiring decision.  
- Onboarding **only occurs after a positive decision**.  
- Payroll addition **only occurs after onboarding**.  
- Negative decisions **skip onboarding/payroll** and directly close the case.  

---

### **Model 1 Analysis**  
```python
model1 = StrictPartialOrder(nodes=[Post, Screen, Decide, Interview, Onboard, Payroll, Close])
model1.order.add_edge(Post, Screen)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Decide)
model1.order.add_edge(Screen, Interview)  # Parallel to Decide
model1.order.add_edge(Decide, Onboard)
model1.order.add_edge(Onboard, Payroll)
model1.order.add_edge(Payroll, Close)
```

#### **Anomalies**  
1. **Interview-Decision Decoupling**:  
   - `Screen  Decide` and `Screen  Interview` imply:  
     - Interviews can occur **in parallel with the decision** (illogical).  
     - The decision can be made **without interviews** (violates standard practice).  
   - *Severity*: High. Interviews are critical for informed decisions.  

2. **No Negative Decision Handling**:  
   - `Decide  Onboard  Payroll  Close` forces onboarding/payroll **even for negative decisions**.  
   - *Severity*: Critical. Wastes resources and violates process logic.  

3. **Interview Misplacement**:  
   - No edge from `Interview` to `Decide` allows interviews to occur **after the decision** (absurd).  

#### **Summary**  
Model 1 has **2 critical anomalies** (negative decision handling, interview-decision logic) and **1 high-severity anomaly** (interview decoupling).

### **Model 2 Analysis**  
```python

```

#### **Anomalies**  
1. **Screen-Interview Decoupling**:  
   - `Post  Screen` and `Post  Interview` allow interviews **without screening** (inefficient).  
   - Interviews can occur **before screening** (illogical).  
   - *Severity*: High. Screening reduces interview workload.  

2. **Payroll Skip**:  
   - `xor_payroll` allows skipping payroll via `skip` transition.  
   - *Severity*: Critical. Employees must be added to payroll.  

3. **Onboarding Loop**:  
   - Loop operator forces **repeated onboarding** (unnecessary complexity).  
   - *Severity*: Medium. Onboarding is typically a one-time activity.  

4. **No Negative Decision Handling**:  
   - `Decide  loop_onboarding` forces onboarding **even for negative decisions**.  
   - *Severity*: Critical.  

5. **Interview Forced but Screen Optional**:  
   - `Interview  Decide` ensures interviews occur, but `Screen` is decoupled (may be skipped).  

#### **Summary**  
Model 2 has **3 critical anomalies** (payroll skip, negative decision handling twice) and **2 high-severity anomalies** (screen-interview decoupling, onboarding loop).
