The event log reveals several potential sources of **bias** in the application review process, particularly favoring **local residents** and members of **recognized community groups** (e.g., the Highland Civic Darts Club). ### **1. Community Group Bonus (Score Adjustment)**
#### **Where Bias Manifests:**
- **PreliminaryScoring Stage:** Applicants affiliated with the **Highland Civic Darts Club** receive a **+10-point adjustment** to their score (e.g., C001, C004), while those with no community group affiliation receive **0 adjustment** (e.g., C002, C003, C005).
- **Impact on Outcomes:**
  - C001 (Local + Community)  **710  720** (Approved)
  - C004 (Local + Community)  **690  700** (Approved)
  - Without the +10 adjustment, C004 would have had a **lower score (690)** than C002 (720) and C005 (740), yet was approved due to the bonus.
  - C003 (Non-local, no community)  **715 (Rejected)**, despite having a **higher base score (715) than C004 (690  700)**.

#### **How This Favors Certain Groups:**
- **Privileges insiders:** The system explicitly rewards membership in a **specific, likely homogenous** community group (e.g., a darts club, which may skew toward certain demographics).
- **Excludes outsiders:** Non-members (even if equally creditworthy) receive no boost, putting them at a **structural disadvantage**.
- **Geographic bias:** The bonus may correlate with **local residency** (since the club is likely location-based), further advantaging those in the area.

#### **Fairness Implications:**
- **Arbitrary favoritism:** The +10 adjustment is not tied to **creditworthiness** but to **social capital** (club membership). This could lead to **disparate impact** if the club’s membership is not representative of the broader population (e.g., skewed by age, income, or ethnicity).
- **Reinforces inequality:** Applicants who lack access to such groups (e.g., newcomers, lower-income individuals, or marginalized groups) are **systematically penalized** despite similar financial profiles.

### **Recommendations to Mitigate Bias**
1. **Remove the Community Group Bonus**
   - If the +10 adjustment is not **directly tied to credit risk**, it should be **eliminated** to avoid favoring insiders.
   - If community involvement is relevant, **standardize criteria** (e.g., volunteer work, local business ownership) rather than **specific club memberships**.

2. **Audit Local Residency Impact**
   - Assess whether **non-local applicants are disproportionately rejected** and whether residency is a **valid predictor of risk**.
   - If not, **remove residency as a factor** or adjust thresholds to ensure fairness.

3. **Standardize Manual Review Criteria**
   - Provide **clear guidelines** for underwriters to prevent **subjective bias** (e.g., requiring justification for overriding automated scores).
   - **Rotate reviewers** to avoid consistent favoritism toward certain groups.

4. **Transparency & Explainability**
   - Disclose **all scoring factors** (including community bonuses) to applicants.
   - Allow **appeals** if applicants believe they were unfairly penalized.

5. **Test for Disparate Impact**
   - Analyze approval rates by **demographics** (e.g., race, income, geography) to ensure compliance with **fair lending laws**.
   - If bias is found, **adjust the model** to remove discriminatory patterns.

6. **Alternative Credit Data**
   - If community/group membership is meant to **proxy for stability**, consider **other non-discriminatory factors** (e.g., rental payment history, utility bills).

---

### **Conclusion**
The process **systematically advantages local residents and community group members**, even when their **underlying creditworthiness is similar or worse** than non-affiliated applicants. This creates **unfair barriers** for outsiders, newcomers, and those without access to specific social networks. To improve fairness, the organization should **eliminate arbitrary bonuses, standardize review criteria, and audit for disparate impact**. Failure to address these biases could lead to **legal risks, reputational damage, and exclusion of deserving applicants**.
