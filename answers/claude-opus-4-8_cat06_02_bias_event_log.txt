# Bias Analysis of the Credit Decision Process

## Where the Bias Manifests

The bias is concentrated in the **PreliminaryScoring** step, where a `ScoreAdjustment` of **+10 (Community)** is applied. This adjustment appears exclusively for applicants who are:
1. **Local Residents** (`LocalResident = TRUE`), AND
2. **Members of the "Highland Civic Darts Club"** community group.

This adjustment carries through every subsequent step and directly alters the final decision threshold.

## The Smoking Gun: C004 vs. C003/C002

The clearest evidence of consequential bias is in **C004**:

| Case | Raw Score | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| C004 | 690 | +10 (Community) | **700** | **Approved** |
| C003 | 715 | 0 | 715 | **Rejected** |

This reveals a serious inconsistency:
- **C004 was approved with a true (raw) score of 690** — the lowest creditworthiness in the entire log — purely because the community boost lifted it to 700.
- **C003 was rejected with a raw score of 715** — 25 points *higher* than C004's actual creditworthiness.

The only structural differences: C003 was **not a local resident** and had **no community affiliation**. An applicant with objectively better credit was denied, while a less creditworthy applicant was approved.

## Attributes Driving the Disparity

| Attribute | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club` | Triggers +10 bonus |
| `LocalResident = TRUE` | Correlates perfectly with who receives the bonus |
| `CommunityGroup = None` | Receives no bonus regardless of merit |
| `LocalResident = FALSE` | Never receives the bonus |

Notably, **club membership and local residency are bundled** — all bonus recipients (C001, C004) share both traits. This creates a compounding advantage that has nothing to do with credit risk.

## Why This Is a Fairness Problem

**1. Proxy discrimination.** A "darts club" membership and local residency are not measures of creditworthiness. They likely act as **proxies for geography, social class, age, ethnicity, or insider status**. Favoring a specific named local club can systematically advantage an in-group and disadvantage outsiders (newcomers, non-residents, members of minority communities who don't belong to that club).

**2. Disparate impact on the threshold.** The approval cutoff appears to be ~700. The +10 boost functionally **lowers the bar by 10 points only for the favored group**, meaning two applicants of identical genuine risk receive different outcomes based on affiliation.

**3. Rejection of more qualified applicants.** As C004 vs. C003 shows, the process can reject objectively stronger candidates while approving weaker ones — undermining both fairness *and* the lender's risk model.

**4. The "Adjusted" label obscures the manipulation.** By the ManualReview stage, the score is presented as "720 (Adjusted)" / "700 (Adjusted)." Downstream reviewers and the Rules Engine treat the adjusted figure as the real score, **laundering the bias into a seemingly neutral number**.

## Implications for Individuals Lacking These Affiliations

- A creditworthy non-resident (like C003) faces a **higher effective standard** for approval.
- Applicants who, for reasons of culture, mobility, disability, or recent arrival, don't belong to local civic organizations are **structurally penalized** despite equal or better financial profiles.
- The harm is **invisible to the applicant** — they simply receive a rejection, with no indication that a different background would have changed the outcome.

## Recommendations

1. **Remove the community/residency adjustment** from scoring unless it can be empirically validated as predictive of repayment (and even then, scrutinized for proxy effects under fair-lending law, e.g., ECOA/FHA).
2. **Decouple credit decisions from group membership** entirely; assess applicants on individual financial merit.
3. **Audit historical decisions** for disparate impact across resident/non-resident and affiliated/non-affiliated populations.
4. **Stop labeling boosted scores as the "score"** — if any adjustment exists, keep the raw score visible and justified.
5. **Test counterfactuals** (as C003/C004 illustrate) as a routine fairness check before deployment.

**Bottom line:** The process embeds a thumb on the scale for a specific local in-group, producing decisions that are inconsistent with applicants' actual creditworthiness and that systematically disadvantage outsiders — a textbook case of proxy-based disparate treatment.
