## Explanation of Transformation Logic

### Case identification

I grouped events by the main work object being handled:

- **CASE-DOC-DOCUMENT1**: all activities related to editing `Document1.docx`.
- **CASE-EMAIL-ANNUAL-MEETING**: the email thread about the Annual Meeting, inferred from the click action “Open Email about Annual Meeting”.
- **CASE-PDF-REPORT-DRAFT**: review and annotation of `Report_Draft.pdf`.
- **CASE-XLS-BUDGET-2024**: updates made to `Budget_2024.xlsx`.
- **CASE-DOC-QUARTERLY-REPORT**: work on `Quarterly_Report.docx`.

This produces an object-centric but case-based event log where each document, spreadsheet, PDF, or email thread becomes a process instance.

### Activity naming

Low-level actions were translated into higher-level business/user activities:

- `FOCUS` and relevant `SWITCH` events became activities such as **Open / Focus Document**, **Resume Document Editing**, or **Open / Review PDF**.
- `TYPING` events were mapped based on application and content, for example:
  - Word typing became **Draft Document Content** or **Draft Executive Summary**.
  - Excel typing became **Update Spreadsheet Data** or **Update Spreadsheet Structure**.
  - Email typing became **Compose Email Reply**.
- `SAVE` became **Save Document** or **Save Spreadsheet**.
- `SCROLL` in email/PDF contexts became **Read Email** or **Read PDF**.
- `HIGHLIGHT` became **Annotate PDF**.
- `CLICK` actions in the email inbox were mapped to **Open Email**, **Start Email Reply**, and **Send Email**.
- `CLOSE` became **Close Document**.

### Notes

The initial focus on `Quarterly_Report.docx` at 08:59:50 was retained as part of the Quarterly Report case, even though active editing occurred later. The event at 09:06:00 switching back to `Document1.docx` was treated as a meaningful continuation of the same document-editing case, especially because it was followed by adding a budget reference and saving the document.
