# BPMN-Like Process Model: Invoice Approval Process

## 1. Participants / Roles (Swimlanes)

| Lane | Role | System | Primary Responsibilities |
|------|------|--------|--------------------------|
| Lane 1 | **Accounts Payable Clerk** (Mary) | Accounting System | Receive/log invoice, PO check, line-item matching, schedule payment |
| Lane 2 | **Purchasing Officer** (Dan) | PO records | Confirm receipt of goods, match invoice details to PO |
| Lane 3 | **Department Manager** (Carlos) | Approval System | Final approve/reject |
| Lane 4 | **AP Supervisor** (Rita) | — | Handle escalations/exceptions, retrospective POs |
| Lane 5 | **Supplier Relationship Manager** (Karen) | — | Contact suppliers, verify contract pricing |
| External | **Supplier** | — | Provide PO info / corrected invoice |
| System | **Accounting/Approval System** | — | Logging, automated notifications to Manager |

---

## 2. Artifacts / Data Objects

- **Invoice** (inbound, usually via email)
- **Purchase Order (PO)** reference
- **Goods/Services Receipt Confirmation**
- **Confirmation Email** (Purchasing  AP)
- **Supplier Contract** (referenced by Karen for pricing)
- **Retrospective PO** (created for informal/contracted services)
- **Manager Approval / Rejection** record
- **Payment Schedule entry** (payment run)

---

## 3. Process Flow (Textual BPMN)

### START
**Start Event:** Invoice received (typically by email)  *[AP Clerk lane]*

---

### Step A — Validate PO Presence *(AP Clerk)*
- **Task A1:** Check whether invoice has a valid PO number.
- **Gateway G1 (Exclusive / XOR — "Has valid PO?")**
  - **No PO ** go to **Step B (PO Resolution)**
  - **Yes PO ** go to **Task A2 (Log Invoice)**

### Step B — PO Resolution Sub-Path *(AP Clerk / Supplier / AP Supervisor / Karen)*
- **Task B1:** AP Clerk sends invoice back to supplier requesting PO/more details.
- **Variation (per Rita):** If the invoice has no PO because the service was contracted informally:
  - **Task B2:** Escalate to AP Supervisor.
  - **Task B3 (AP Supervisor):** Work with department heads to **create a retrospective PO** *or* **decide to reject** the invoice.
    - **Gateway G1b (XOR):** Retrospective PO created  re-enter normal flow at **Task A2** | Reject  **End Event: Invoice Rejected**.
- **Supplier-facing option (Karen):** Karen may contact supplier to obtain PO or revised invoice.
- On receiving valid PO  loop back to **Task A1 / A2**.

>  *Ambiguity:* Sources differ on who first requests the missing PO. Mary says AP sends it back to the supplier; Dan says he tells Mary to obtain it; Karen/Rita may handle it for key/informal suppliers. Modeled as a shared "PO Resolution" sub-process with multiple possible actors.

### Task A2 — Log Invoice into Accounting System *(AP Clerk)*
- Output: Invoice record in accounting system.
-  **Task A3**

### Task A3 — Forward to Purchasing for Receipt Confirmation *(AP Clerk  Purchasing)*
-  **Step C**

---

### Step C — Verify Receipt & Match Invoice to PO *(Purchasing Officer)*
- **Task C1:** Confirm goods/services were actually received.
- **Task C2:** Compare invoice details against PO (quantities, prices, delivery charges).

>  *Ambiguity / Overlap:* Both Mary (AP) and Dan (Purchasing) describe performing line-item matching. 
> - Mary: "I match the invoice line items against the PO."
> - Dan: "I check if the invoice details line up with what we ordered."
> Likely a **dual/parallel check** — Purchasing verifies physical receipt + commercial match, AP verifies system match. Modeled as overlapping verification; confirm exact owner in real environment.

- **Gateway G2 (Exclusive / XOR — "Invoice matches PO & receipt confirmed?")**
  - **Match OK ** Purchasing sends **Confirmation Email** to AP  go to **Step E (Manager Approval)**
  - **Discrepancy (pricing, extra units, missing items, missing info) ** go to **Step D (Discrepancy Handling)**

---

### Step D — Discrepancy Handling Loop *(Purchasing / AP Clerk / Supplier / AP Supervisor / Karen)*
- **Task D1:** Contact supplier directly **OR** ask AP Clerk to **hold invoice** until corrected version arrives.
- **Task D2 (optional):** Loop in **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)**:
  - Check **contract** for correct pricing.
  - Request revised invoice / PO from supplier.
  - Report correct price back to AP or Purchasing.
- **Gateway G3 (XOR — "Discrepancy resolved easily?")**
  - **Resolved ** return to **Task C2 (re-match)**  eventually to **Gateway G2**
  - **Not easily resolved ** **Task D3:** Escalate to **AP Supervisor (Rita)**

- **Task D4 (AP Supervisor):** Coordinate between departments / contact suppliers to resolve.
  - **Gateway G3b (XOR):**
    - **Resolved ** re-enter normal flow (return to Purchasing confirmation  Manager approval)
    - **Unresolvable ** **End Event: Invoice Rejected / On Hold**

> *Note (Rita):* "I ensure problem invoices don't get stuck indefinitely." — Exception path always rejoins the main flow after resolution.

---

### Step E — Manager Approval *(Department Manager)*
- **Trigger:** System sends **automatic email** to Manager once (a) receipt confirmed AND (b) invoice-PO match confirmed.
- **Task E1:** Manager reviews invoice details, PO, and confirmation in system.
- **Gateway G4 (Exclusive / XOR — "Approve?")**
  - **Approve ** go to **Step F (Schedule Payment)**
  - **Suspicious / large budget variance / unexpected items **
    - **Reject**, OR
    - **Ask AP to hold & investigate**  routes back to **Step D (Discrepancy Handling)** or **AP Supervisor**
  - *(Frequency: rejection/hold ~once every couple of months.)*

---

### Step F — Schedule Payment *(AP Clerk)*
- **Task F1:** Add approved invoice to the next **payment cycle / payment run**.
-  **END**

### END
**End Event (success):** Invoice scheduled for payment.
**End Event (alternate):** Invoice rejected (no resolvable PO / unresolved discrepancy / manager rejection).

---

## 4. Summary of Gateways

| ID | Type | Question | Outcomes |
|----|------|----------|----------|
| G1 | XOR | Valid PO present? | Yes  Log / No  PO Resolution |
| G1b | XOR | Retrospective PO feasible? | Yes  rejoin flow / No  Reject |
| G2 | XOR | Receipt confirmed & invoice matches PO? | Yes  Manager Approval / No  Discrepancy Handling |
| G3 | XOR | Discrepancy easily resolved? | Yes  re-match / No  Escalate to Supervisor |
| G3b | XOR | Supervisor resolved exception? | Yes  rejoin flow / No  Reject |
| G4 | XOR | Manager approves? | Yes  Schedule Payment / No  Reject or Hold (loop back) |

---

## 5. Rework Loops & Exceptions

1. **Missing PO loop:** Invoice  supplier (or supervisor for informal services)  PO obtained/created  re-enter flow.
2. **Discrepancy loop:** Purchasing/AP  Supplier (via Karen)  re-match; escalation to Supervisor if unresolved.
3. **Manager rejection/hold loop:** Sends invoice back for investigation (discrepancy handling).
4. **Retrospective PO exception:** For informally contracted services lacking a PO.

---

## 6. Noted Ambiguities / Conflicts (for validation)

1. **Line-item matching ownership** — Performed by both AP (Mary) and Purchasing (Dan). Needs clarification: parallel checks vs. single owner.
2. **Who first contacts the supplier for a missing PO** — AP Clerk, Purchasing instruction, or Supplier Relationship Manager. Modeled as shared sub-process.
3. **Receipt confirmation vs. PO match sequencing** — Mary implies receipt confirmation precedes matching; Dan treats them together. Modeled sequentially within Purchasing lane but may be concurrent.
4. **Hand-back after approval** — All agree it returns to AP for payment scheduling (consistent).
5. **Karen's involvement** is purely advisory/external (no system actions) — modeled as a supporting participant invoked during discrepancy/missing-info handling.

---

## 7. Simplified Linear "Happy Path" (for quick reference)

```
Invoice Received
   Check PO (valid)
   Log in Accounting System
   Forward to Purchasing
   Confirm Receipt + Match to PO (OK)
   System notifies Manager
   Manager Approves
   Schedule for Payment
   END
```

This model is structured so a modeler can directly map lanes, tasks, XOR gateways, loops, and end events into a BPMN diagramming tool, while the flagged ambiguities indicate where stakeholder confirmation is recommended.
