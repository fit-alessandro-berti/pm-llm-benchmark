### 2. Detailed Rationale & Justifications

#### **Stage 1: Material Preparation & Setup**
*   **Included Events:** *Retrieve raw metal sheet, Scan barcode, Place metal sheet on table, Align metal sheet, Preheat metal sheet.*
*   **Key Resources:** Operator A, Robot Arm #2, Heating Unit #1.
*   **Rationale:** This stage encompasses all activities required to prepare the raw metal sheet for permanent modification. It begins with raw material retrieval and identification (barcode scan), progresses through physical positioning (placing and robotic alignment), and concludes with thermal priming (preheating). No material joining has occurred yet; these activities are temporally tight (spanning ~15 seconds) and lay the groundwork for assembly.

#### **Stage 2: Welding Assembly**
*   **Included Events:** *Pick up welding tool, Weld corner A, Weld corner B.*
*   **Key Resources:** Operator B.
*   **Rationale:** These events represent the core fabrication/assembly phase. They are performed sequentially by a single resource (Operator B) using a specific tool (W-12). The phase starts when the operator grabs the tool and ends once the physical welds are structurally completed.

#### **Stage 3: Inline Quality Inspection**
*   **Included Events:** *Measure weld integrity.*
*   **Key Resources:** Quality Sensor #1.
*   **Rationale:** While this contains only one low-level event, it represents a critical "gatekeeping" step immediately following the welding process. Using specialized diagnostic equipment, it verifies structural integrity before committing the item to irreversible surface finishing treatments. Keeping this as a separate stage allows for easy bottleneck analysis and rework tracing.

#### **Stage 4: Surface Treatment & Coating**
*   **Included Events:** *Apply protective coating, Dry coating.*
*   **Key Resources:** Coating Unit #3, Heating Unit #2.
*   **Rationale:** These steps represent post-assembly physical modification focused on product durability. The chemical application (polyurethane coating) and its thermal curing (drying at 120°C) are logically grouped together as they constitute a single cohesive surface treatment sub-process.

#### **Stage 5: Final Quality Assurance**
*   **Included Events:** *Visual check.*
*   **Key Resources:** Operator C.
*   **Rationale:** This is the final verification stage of the manufacturing cycle. Conducted by a human operator, it ensures that the finished, coated product meets all visual standards and is ready for packaging or dispatch, acting as the final sign-off for the Case ID.
