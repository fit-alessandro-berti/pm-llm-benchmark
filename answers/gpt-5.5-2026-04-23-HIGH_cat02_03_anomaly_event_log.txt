A typical Procure-to-Pay process would usually follow a sequence like:

1. Create Purchase Requisition  
2. Approve Purchase Requisition  
3. Issue Purchase Order  
4. Receive Goods  
5. Receive Vendor Invoice  
6. Match Invoice to PO / Goods Receipt  
7. Release Payment  
8. Close Case  

Using that as the expected baseline, the following cases contain unusual or potentially problematic sequences.

| Case ID | Anomaly | Why It Is Problematic |
|---|---|---|
| **1001** | No major anomaly observed | The case follows the expected P2P flow: requisition creation, approval, PO issuance, goods receipt, invoice receipt, matching, payment, and closure. |
| **1002** | Purchase Order issued before Purchase Requisition approval | The PO was issued at `2024-02-01 08:40`, but the requisition was not approved until `2024-02-03 08:00`. This suggests procurement activity occurred before authorization. |
| **1002** | Payment released before requisition approval | Payment was released at `2024-02-03 07:15`, before PR approval at `2024-02-03 08:00`. This is a serious control issue because payment was made before the underlying purchase was formally approved. |
| **1002** | Payment released before goods receipt | Payment was made on `2024-02-03`, but goods were only received on `2024-02-04`. Paying before confirming receipt increases the risk of paying for goods not delivered. |
| **1002** | Payment released before invoice matching | Payment occurred at `2024-02-03 07:15`, while invoice matching happened later on `2024-02-05 08:00`. This bypasses the normal three-way match control between PO, goods receipt, and invoice. |
| **1003** | Payment released before invoice receipt | Payment was released on `2024-02-08`, but the vendor invoice was received on `2024-02-09`. Normally, payment should not be made before an invoice is received and validated. |

### Summary of key anomalies

The most concerning issues are:

- **Payment before approval** — seen in Case 1002.
- **Payment before goods receipt** — seen in Cases 1002 and 1003.
- **Payment before invoice receipt** — seen in Case 1003.
- **Payment before invoice-to-PO matching** — seen in Cases 1002 and 1003.
- **Purchase order issued before requisition approval** — seen in Case 1002.
- **Goods received before PO issuance** — seen in Case 1004.

These anomalies are problematic because they weaken key procurement and financial controls. In a standard P2P process, approvals, purchase orders, goods receipts, and invoice matching are designed to ensure that the company only pays for authorized, ordered, and actually received goods or services. Bypassing these steps can increase the risk of fraud, duplicate payment, unauthorized purchasing, overpayment, or payment for goods not received.
