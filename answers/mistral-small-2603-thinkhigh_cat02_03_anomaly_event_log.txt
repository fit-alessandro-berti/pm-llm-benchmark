### **Analysis of Anomalies in the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Event Log**

The standard **Procure-to-Pay (P2P) process** follows a logical sequence to ensure proper authorization, verification, and financial control. The ideal flow is:

1. **Create Purchase Requisition (PR)**
2. **Approve Purchase Requisition**
3. **Issue Purchase Order (PO)**
4. **Receive Goods/Services**
5. **Vendor Invoice Received**
6. **Match Invoice to PO (3-way match: PO, receipt, invoice)**
7. **Release Payment**
8. **Close Case**

### **Case 1002: Severe Process Reversal**
**Sequence:**
`Create PR  Issue PO  Vendor Invoice  Release Payment  Approve PR  Receive Goods  Match Invoice  Close Case`

**Anomalies:**
1. **Purchase Order (PO) issued before requisition approval**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - A PO should never be created without prior approval of the requisition. This violates **segregation of duties** and increases the risk of **unauthorized spending**.
     - In standard P2P, the requisition must be approved before a PO is issued to ensure budgetary control and prevent maverick buying.

2. **Payment released before goods receipt and invoice matching**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - **Paying without confirming receipt** risks paying for undelivered or incorrect goods.
     - **Paying without matching the invoice** violates the **3-way match principle** (PO, receipt, invoice), a critical control to prevent overpayments, duplicate payments, or fraud.
     - This could lead to **financial losses** (e.g., paying for goods never received) or **fraud** (e.g., fake invoices).

3. **Goods receipt occurs after payment**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - If goods are received after payment, the company may have already paid for items it cannot verify or return.
     - This suggests **poor inventory control** and potential **waste or theft**.

**Potential Causes:**
- **Deliberate circumvention** of approval processes (e.g., bypassing controls for speed).
- **System configuration error** where activities are logged out of order.
- **Urgent procurement** where normal procedures were ignored (e.g., emergency orders).

**Business Impact:**
 **High financial risk** (unauthorized payments, fraud).
 **Compliance violation** (SOX, internal audit failures).
 **Operational inefficiency** (discrepancies in inventory vs. payments).

### **Case 1003: Payment Before Receipt & Invoice**
**Sequence:**
`Create PR  Approve PR  Issue PO  Release Payment  Vendor Invoice  Receive Goods  Match Invoice  Close Case`

**Anomalies:**
1. **Payment released before goods receipt and invoice matching**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - **Paying before receiving goods** means the company has no confirmation that the order was fulfilled.
     - **Paying before invoice matching** means no verification that the invoice amount matches the PO and receipt, increasing the risk of **overpayments or fraud**.
     - This is a **major control failure**—standard P2P requires **3-way matching** before payment.

2. **Goods receipt occurs after payment**
   - **Why problematic?**

- **Vendor pressure** to pay quickly (e.g., early payment discounts).
- **System error** where the "Release Payment" activity was logged prematurely.

**Business Impact:**
 **High financial risk** (paying for undelivered goods, overpayments).
 **Audit findings** (failure to enforce 3-way match).
 **Vendor disputes** (if goods arrive damaged or incorrect after payment).

### **Case 1004: Goods Receipt Before PO Issuance**
**Sequence:**
`Create PR  Approve PR  Receive Goods  Issue PO  Vendor Invoice  Match Invoice  Release Payment  Close Case`

**Anomalies:**
1. **Goods received before Purchase Order (PO) is issued**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - A **PO is the formal authorization** for a purchase. Receiving goods without a PO means:
       - The company has **no record of the purchase agreement**, making it difficult to verify pricing, terms, or vendor compliance.
       - **Maverick buying** (unauthorized procurement) may have occurred, bypassing procurement controls.
       - **No PO number** to match against the invoice, increasing the risk of **duplicate payments or incorrect charges**.
     - This suggests **poor procurement discipline** or **systemic process failure**.

2. **PO issued after goods receipt**
   - **Why problematic?**
     - The PO should be created **before** receiving goods to ensure:
       - The correct items/services were ordered.
       - The vendor is authorized.
       - Pricing and terms are agreed upon.
     - Issuing a PO after receipt may indicate **backdating**, which is a **fraud risk**.

**Potential Causes:**
- **Emergency procurement** where goods were needed urgently, and formalities were skipped.
- **Vendor-initiated shipments** (e.g., vendor sends goods without a PO, expecting payment anyway).
- **System error** where the PO activity was logged late.

**Business Impact:**
 **High compliance risk** (SOX violations for unauthorized spending).
 **Financial leakage** (paying for unapproved purchases).
 **Vendor management issues** (no formal agreement, disputes over terms).

## ** Root Causes & Recommendations**

### **Likely Root Causes of Anomalies:**
1. **Bypassing Approval Controls**
   - Cases 1002 and 1003 show PO issuance or payment before requisition approval.
   - **Possible reasons:**
     - Staff may be **circumventing controls** for speed.
     - **Urgent procurement** where approvals were skipped.
     - **Lack of training** on P2P procedures.

2. **Failure to Enforce 3-Way Match**
   - Cases 1002 and 1003 released payment before matching the invoice to the PO and receipt.
   - **Possible reasons:**
     - **Pressure to pay vendors quickly** (e.g., early payment discounts).
     - **Weak accounting controls** where matching is not enforced.
     - **System misconfiguration** where payment release is allowed before matching.

3. **Maverick Buying (Unapproved Procurement)**
   - Case 1004 received goods before issuing a PO.
   - **Possible reasons:**
     - **Emergency orders** where formal procurement was skipped.
     - **Vendor-initiated shipments** (e.g., vendor sends goods without a PO).
     - **Lack of procurement discipline** in certain departments.

4. **System or Logging Errors**
   - Some anomalies may stem from **incorrect event logging** (e.g., activities recorded out of order).
   - **Recommendation:** Audit system configurations to ensure timestamps reflect actual activity order.

| **Issue** | **Recommended Action** | **Expected Outcome** |

| **Goods receipt before PO** | Implement **automated PO creation** before goods receipt. | Ensures all receipts have a corresponding PO. |
| **Lack of approvals** | Conduct **training** on P2P procedures. | Improves compliance and reduces maverick buying. |

### **Key Takeaways for Businesses:**
 **Enforce strict approval controls** before PO issuance and payment.
 **Mandate 3-way matching** (PO, receipt, invoice) before payment.
 **Train staff** on P2P procedures to prevent circumvention of controls.
 **Audit system logs** to ensure events are recorded in the correct order.
 **Investigate recurring anomalies** to determine if they are **process failures, fraud, or system errors**.

Failure to address these issues could lead to **financial losses, audit findings, vendor disputes, and compliance violations**. Implementing **automated controls** (e.g., ERP system restrictions) is the most effective way to prevent such anomalies in the future.
