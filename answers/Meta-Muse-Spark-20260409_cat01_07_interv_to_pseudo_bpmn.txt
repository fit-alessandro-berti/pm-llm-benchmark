Based on the 5 interview excerpts, here is a consolidated BPMN-like textual process model for the **Invoice Approval Process**. I've noted ambiguities and variations where stakeholder accounts conflict or are incomplete.

### **Process: Invoice Approval**

#### **1. Process Metadata**
- **Process Name**: Accounts Payable - Invoice Approval to Payment Scheduling
- **Trigger/Start Event**: Invoice received, typically by email to Accounts Payable Clerk

#### **2. Pools & Lanes/Roles**
| Lane | Role | System/Actor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Accounts Payable | AP Clerk - Mary | Accounting System |
| Accounts Payable | AP Supervisor - Rita | Accounting System |
| Purchasing | Purchasing Officer - Dan | Email, PO System |
| Management | Department Manager - Carlos | Approval System |
| External | Supplier | Email |
| Support | Supplier Relationship Manager - Karen | Email, Contracts |

#### **3. Main Sequence Flow - Activities, Gateways, and Events**

**Start Event**: Invoice received by AP Clerk

**Activity A1**: Check for valid Purchase Order (PO) number on invoice  
- **Performer**: AP Clerk - Mary
- **Input**: Invoice from Supplier

**Gateway G1**: PO number present? [Exclusive Gateway]  
- **Performer**: AP Clerk  
- **Condition**: Based on invoice content

  **Path G1-No: PO Missing**  
  **Activity A2**: Send invoice back to Supplier requesting PO details  
  - **Performer**: AP Clerk, Supplier Relationship Manager - Karen  
  - **Note**: Dan states he also tells Mary to get PO before proceeding. Karen helps contact supplier.  
  - **Loop**: Returns to Start Event once revised invoice received  
  - **Exception**: If PO missing because service was contracted informally, escalate to AP Supervisor - Rita for retrospective PO creation or rejection decision

  **Path G1-Yes: PO Present**  
  **Activity A3**: Log invoice into accounting system  
  - **Performer**: AP Clerk  

**Activity A4**: Forward invoice to Purchasing to confirm receipt of goods/services  
- **Performer**: AP Clerk → Purchasing Officer - Dan  
- **Output**: Confirmation request via email

**Activity A5**: Compare invoice details vs. PO  
- **Performer**: Purchasing Officer - Dan  
- **Inputs**: Invoice, PO, delivery records  
- **Check**: Quantities, prices, delivery charges

**Gateway G2**: Do invoice details match PO? [Exclusive Gateway]  
- **Performer**: Purchasing Officer  

  **Path G2-No: Discrepancy Found**  
  **Activity A6**: Resolve discrepancy  
  - **Performer**: Purchasing Officer, AP Clerk, Supplier Relationship Manager - Karen  
  - **Sub-paths**:  
    1. Contact supplier directly for corrected invoice  
    2. Ask AP Clerk to hold invoice until supplier sends correction  
    3. Email AP Clerk to clarify with Purchasing team  
  - **Escalation**: If discrepancy cannot be resolved easily, AP Clerk escalates to AP Supervisor - Rita  
  - **AP Supervisor actions**: Contact suppliers, coordinate between departments, work with dept heads for retrospective PO if needed  
  - **Loop**: Returns to A5 once discrepancy resolved or corrected invoice received  
  - **Uncertainty**: Unclear if rejection of invoice is a terminal event. Rita mentions "decide if invoice should be rejected" but no stakeholder describes rejection as final outcome.

  **Path G2-Yes: Details Match**  
  **Activity A7**: Send confirmation to Accounts Payable  
  - **Performer**: Purchasing Officer → AP Clerk  
  - **Output**: Confirmation email

**Activity A8**: Match invoice line items against PO in system  
- **Performer**: AP Clerk  
- **Note**: Mary mentions this step after confirmation, but Dan implies his check in A5 already covers this. Likely both do checks. Modeling as separate AP system validation step per Mary.

**Gateway G3**: Match successful in system? [Implicit Gateway]  
- **Performer**: AP Clerk  
  - **Path G3-No**: Treat as discrepancy → go to A6  
  - **Path G3-Yes**: Proceed

**Activity A9**: Request manager approval  
- **Performer**: System - automatic  
- **Trigger**: Triggered after goods confirmed AND invoice matches PO  
- **Output**: Automatic email/notification to Department Manager with invoice, PO, confirmation

**Activity A10**: Review and approve/reject invoice  
- **Performer**: Department Manager - Carlos  
- **Inputs**: Invoice details, PO, confirmation from Purchasing  
- **Decision criteria**: Quantities, pricing, budget alignment, unexpected items

**Gateway G4**: Manager decision [Exclusive Gateway]  
- **Performer**: Department Manager  

  **Path G4-Reject/Hold**:  
  **Activity A11**: Request investigation or hold invoice  
  - **Performer**: Department Manager → AP Clerk  
  - **Frequency**: ~Once every couple months per Carlos  
  - **Loop**: Returns to A6 for exception handling  
  - **Uncertainty**: Exact rejection path not fully described. Assume it re-enters exception handling via AP Clerk/Supervisor.

  **Path G4-Approve**:  
  **Activity A12**: Schedule invoice for payment  
  - **Performer**: AP Clerk  
  - **Note**: Carlos states it "goes back to Accounts Payable" for next payment cycle. Dan states "AP for scheduling payment run."

**End Event**: Invoice added to next payment cycle  

#### **4. Artifacts/Data Objects**
| Data Object | Created/Updated By | Used By |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Invoice | Supplier | All roles |
| Purchase Order - PO | Purchasing - prior to process | AP Clerk, Purchasing Officer, Manager |
| Confirmation of Receipt | Purchasing Officer | AP Clerk, Manager |
| System Match Record | AP Clerk/System | Manager |
| Approval Record | Department Manager | AP Clerk |
| Corrected Invoice | Supplier | AP Clerk, Purchasing Officer |
| Retrospective PO | AP Supervisor + Dept Head | Purchasing Officer, Manager |

#### **5. Exceptions/Variations & Escalations**
1. **Missing PO Exception**: 
   - **Handler**: AP Clerk → Supplier, with help from Supplier Relationship Manager  
   - **Special case**: Informal service contracts handled by AP Supervisor creating retrospective PO or rejecting
   
2. **Pricing/Quantity Discrepancy Exception**: 
   - **Handler**: Purchasing Officer → Supplier or AP Clerk  
   - **Escalation**: AP Clerk → AP Supervisor if not easily resolved  
   - **Support**: Supplier Relationship Manager checks contracts and advises correct price
   
3. **Manager Hold/Reject Exception**: 
   - **Handler**: Department Manager → AP Clerk  
   - **Frequency**: Low, ~bi-monthly  
   - **Outcome**: Returns to investigation/resolution loop

4. **Unresolvable Invoice Exception**: 
   - **Handler**: AP Supervisor - Rita  
   - **Actions**: Direct supplier contact, inter-department coordination, decision to create retrospective PO or reject  
   - **Note**: Rita ensures problem invoices don't get stuck indefinitely

#### **6. Ambiguities & Conflicts Noted**
1. **Duplicate matching**: Both Mary and Dan describe matching invoice to PO. Modeled as Dan doing business validation - A5, Mary doing system entry validation - A8. Unclear if these are redundant.
2. **Rejection terminal state**: Multiple roles mention "reject" but no one describes invoice rejection as process end. Assumed rejection routes to exception handling rather than termination.
3. **System touchpoints**: Karen states she doesn't touch the system, Rita doesn't do final approvals. Only Mary, Dan, Carlos, and system have direct system actions.
4. **Trigger for A4**: Mary forwards to Purchasing after logging. Dan describes receiving from AP. Consistent.
5. **Final step owner**: Both Dan and Carlos state AP schedules payment after approval. Consistent.
