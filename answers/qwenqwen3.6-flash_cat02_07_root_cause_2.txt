### 2. Root Cause Analysis

We analyze the attributes (`Resource`, `Region`, `Complexity`) to determine what drives these durations.

#### A. Complexity is the Primary Driver
There is a direct correlation between **Complexity** and **Duration**:
*   **Low Complexity:** Consistent fast performance (~1.5 hours). No loops in the process.
*   **Medium Complexity:** Significant delay (~26 hours). Associated with **1** request for additional documents.
*   **High Complexity:** Severe delay (~48–77 hours). Associated with **2 to 3** requests for additional documents.

**Conclusion:** The variability in duration is driven by the number of times the process loops back to `Request Additional Documents`. Higher complexity claims require more documentation, and the current process handles these requests sequentially, compounding the delay.

#### B. Region is Not a Root Cause
*   **Region A:** Contains Case 2001 (Fast) and Case 2003 (Slow).
*   **Region B:** Contains Case 2004 (Fast), Case 2002 (Moderate), and Case 2005 (Slow).
*   Both regions contain fast and slow cases depending on complexity. There is no regional bottleneck.

#### C. Resource is Not a Root Cause (Routing Artifact)
*   **Adjuster_Lisa** handles cases 2002, 2004, and 2005.
    *   She handles the **fastest** case (2004, Low complexity) and the **slowest** case (2005, High complexity).
    *   This proves Adjuster_Lisa is not the bottleneck; the complexity of the case dictates the workload.
*   **Manager_Bill** handles 2003 and 2005 (both High complexity/Slow), while **Manager_Ann** handles 2001, 2002, and 2004 (Low/Medium/Fast).
    *   This suggests resources are routed based on complexity, not that one resource is slower than another.

#### D. Specific Process Inefficiency: Multiple Document Requests
*   **Case 2005** required **three separate** requests for additional documents. This indicates that the initial `Evaluate Claim` step failed to identify all missing information at once, or the requirements were unclear, forcing the adjuster to request documents piecemeal.
*   Each request introduces a waiting period (likely waiting for the customer response or internal review), which accumulates over multiple loops.

### 3. Explanations and Mitigation Strategies

#### Root Cause Explanation
The performance issues are caused by a **process design flaw in handling complex claims**. The process lacks a mechanism to capture all necessary documentation upfront. As complexity increases, the likelihood of missing documents increases. The current workflow allows for iterative requests, but it does not enforce a "complete list" approach, leading to multiple cycles that drastically extend lead times.

#### Recommendations

1.  **Consolidate Document Requests (One-Shot Collection):**
    *   *Action:* For Medium and High complexity claims, the `Evaluate Claim` activity must generate a comprehensive checklist of all required documents. The `Request Additional Documents` activity should communicate the full list to the customer at once.
    *   *Impact:* Reduces Case 2005 from 3 loops to potentially 1 loop (or zero if complete), cutting duration by ~60-70%.

2.  **Enhance Initial Evaluation for High Complexity:**
    *   *Action:* Implement a validation checklist or automated document verification tool during `Evaluate Claim` for High complexity cases. This ensures the adjuster catches missing items immediately.
    *   *Impact:* Prevents the need for rework loops.

3.  **SLA Management for Document Requests:**
    *   *Action:* Define strict Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for the `Request Additional Documents` step. If a customer does not respond within a set time, the case should be auto-flagged or escalated.
    *   *Impact:* Reduces the passive waiting time between requests.

4.  **Customer Communication Improvements:**
    *   *Action:* When requesting documents, provide clear examples and reasons for each request to reduce customer confusion and resubmission errors.
    *   *Impact:* Improves the quality of resubmitted documents, reducing the need for further requests.

5.  **Monitor "Request Additional Documents" Frequency:**
    *   *Action:* Track metrics on the number of loops per case. If High complexity cases consistently require >1 request, trigger a process review or additional training for Adjusters.
    *   *Impact:* Continuous improvement of the evaluation quality.

**Summary:** The performance issues are not due to resource inefficiency or regional differences. They are structural, caused by the iterative nature of document collection in complex claims. Mitigating the issue requires shifting from iterative requests to a comprehensive upfront documentation strategy.
