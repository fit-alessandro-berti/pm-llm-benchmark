### Step 1: Identify Activities with Contextual Differences

The activities that appear multiple times within the same case are:

- **Verify Documents**
- **Call Customer**
- **Adjust Account**

Each of these activities has different meanings depending on the context:

1. **Verify Documents**
   - In **C001**, it refers to checking identity documents.
   - In **C002**, it refers to identity verification (possibly a different type or stage).
   - In **C003**, it refers to physical verification of ID.

   The context changes based on the case and the type of documents involved.

2. **Call Customer**
   - In **C001**, it is a call to request missing documents.
   - In **C002**, it is to clarify an account discrepancy.
   - In **C003**, it is to have the customer return after stepping out.

   The purpose and nature of the call differ significantly.

3. **Adjust Account**
   - In **C001**, it is an internal correction after missing information.
   - In **C002**, it is an adjustment made after a call to clarify an issue.
   - In **C003**, it is an internal correction due to missing information.

   The trigger and context of the adjustment vary.

---

### Step 2: Rename Activities to Reflect Context

We will rename each recurring activity to reflect its specific context:

- **Verify Documents**
  - **C001**: Identity Document Verification
  - **C002**: Identity Verification (possibly a different type)
  - **C003**: Physical ID Verification

- **Call Customer**
  - **C001**: Request for Missing Documents
  - **C002**: Clarification Call (account discrepancy)
  - **C003**: Return to Customer (after stepping out)

- **Adjust Account**
  - **C001**: Internal Correction (missing info)
  - **C002**: Post-Call Adjustment
  - **C003**: Internal Correction (after adjustment)

### Step 4: Justification for Naming Choices

1. **Verify Documents**
   - **Identity Document Verification (C001)**: The activity is clearly about checking identity documents, so the name reflects that.
   - **Identity Verification (C002)**: The context suggests a different type of verification, possibly more general or procedural.
   - **Physical ID Verification (C003)**: The activity involves a physical check, so the name emphasizes the method.

2. **Call Customer**
   - **Request for Missing Documents (C001)**: The call is initiated to obtain missing information.
   - **Clarification Call (C002)**: The call is to resolve a specific issue (account discrepancy).
   - **Return to Customer (C003)**: The call is to bring the customer back after they left, indicating a different intent.

3. **Adjust Account**
   - **Internal Correction (C001)**: The adjustment is made due to missing information.
   - **Post-Call Adjustment (C002)**: The adjustment follows a call and is a direct response to it.
   - **Internal Correction (C003)**: The adjustment is made after an account change, and the correction is internal.

These renamed activities now clearly reflect their specific roles and contexts within the process, improving clarity and reducing ambiguity.
