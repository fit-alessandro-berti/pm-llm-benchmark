### 4. Developing Advanced Data-Driven Scheduling Strategies

Based on the pathologies, I propose three distinct, sophisticated strategies that leverage the mined data.

#### Strategy 1: Enhanced Dynamic Dispatching (Apparent Tardiness Cost with Setups - ATCS)
*   **Core Logic:** This is a composite dispatching rule that calculates a priority index for every job in a queue at a work center. The index is a weighted function of: (1) Slack time (time until due date minus remaining processing time), (2) Job Priority, (3) Remaining processing time, and (4) *Estimated sequence-dependent setup time* based on the job currently on the machine.
*   **Process Mining Usage:** The weights for the index are calibrated using historical log data. The Sequence-Dependent Setup Matrix mined from the logs is directly injected into the rule, dynamically penalizing jobs that require massive changeovers from the current job state, while rewarding jobs similar to the current one, unless a job is critically late.
*   **Addresses Pathologies:** Poor prioritization (accounts for slack and priority simultaneously); Suboptimal setups (minimizes changeover times dynamically); Downstream starvation (adjusts for remaining processing time to keep downstream fed).
*   **Expected Impact:** Significant reduction in tardiness for high-priority jobs, reduction in overall setup time percentage, and more stable WIP flow.

#### Strategy 2: Predictive & Proactive Scheduling (Digital Twin Stochastic Scheduling)
*   **Core Logic:** Instead of using single-point estimates for task durations, use Monte Carlo simulations to generate robust schedules. When a disruption occurs (breakdown or hot job), the system triggers an automated "what-if" resimulation of the remaining schedule, outputting the schedule with the highest probability of meeting due dates.
*   **Process Mining Usage:** Extract full probability distributions (not just averages) for task durations based on operator ID, machine ID, and job complexity. Use survival analysis on machine logs to predict the probability of breakdowns within the scheduling horizon. If a machine has a high predicted failure probability, the scheduler proactively builds slack into jobs routing through it.
*   **Addresses Pathologies:** Inaccurate estimations (uses reality-based distributions, not rigid standards); Ineffective disruption handling (instant, data-driven rescheduling); Unpredictable lead times (provides probabilistic confidence intervals to customers, e.g., "95% chance of delivery by Friday").
*   **Expected Impact:** Drastic improvement in due date accuracy, reduction in WIP caused by cascading delays, and increased customer trust through reliable estimates.

#### Strategy 3: Bottleneck-Centric Setup Batching (DBR + Intelligent Clustering)
*   **Core Logic:** Based on Drum-Buffer-Rope (DBR) principles, we identify the system Constraint (the Drum). We then implement intelligent batching *only* at the bottleneck. Jobs are clustered into "families" to maximize runs before changeovers. Non-bottleneck machines are explicitly *not* batched; they run smaller lots faster to feed the bottleneck. The schedule is "roped" to the bottleneck's consumption rate.
*   **Process Mining Usage:** Use PM to definitively identify the primary and secondary bottlenecks (shifting over time based on product mix). Apply clustering algorithms (e.g., K-means) on job attributes (material, geometry) mapped against the Sequence-Dependent Setup Matrix to scientifically define "job families" rather than relying on operator intuition.
*   **Addresses Pathologies:** Bottleneck capacity waste (maximizing productive time at the constraint by minimizing setups); Downstream starvation (non-bottlenecks prioritize flow over efficiency); Bullwhip effect (release of jobs into the system is paced by the bottleneck).
*   **Expected Impact:** Massive throughput increase at bottlenecks, reduction in overall makespan, and reduction of WIP inventory as the release rate is governed by the true capacity of the plant.
