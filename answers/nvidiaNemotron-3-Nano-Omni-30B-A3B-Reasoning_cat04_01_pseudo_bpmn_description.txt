**Overview of the business process**

The diagram describes a **customerorder fulfillment workflow** that can handle two kinds of requests – *standard* (offtheshelf) and *custom* (specialorder).  
The flow starts with the receipt of a request, validates it, runs the appropriate checks, determines the delivery date, and then decides whether a manager’s signoff is required.  If approval is needed, the process either proceeds to invoicing or loops back for reevaluation.  Finally the customer receives confirmation.

### Key takeaways
- **Gateways** are decision points that split or join the flow.  
- **XOR** means “exactly one” of the outgoing branches is taken; **AND** means all incoming branches must complete before proceeding.  
- The **parallel checks** (credit & inventory) must both succeed before the process can continue.  
- **Approval** is optional; if required, a second decision checks whether the manager actually grants it.  
- **Reevaluation** (TaskH) creates a loop back to an earlier step, allowing the process to correct issues before final invoicing.  

This structure ensures that both standard and custom orders are handled appropriately, with appropriate checks, possible rework, and a final confirmation to the customer.
