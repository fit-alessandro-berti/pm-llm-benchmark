### Rationale for Added Constraints

1. **`coexistence` (`Reject_Minority`, `Approve_Minority` $\leftrightarrow$ `ManualReview`):**
   By ensuring demographic-explicit decision steps (like `Reject_Minority` or `Approve_Minority`) coexist with a `ManualReview`, we guarantee that decisions directly concerning sensitive demographic groups are never fully automated. An auditor or human reviewer must always be present in the trace to ensure consistency and fairness.

2. **`response` (`CheckApplicantRace/Gender` $\rightarrow$ `BiasMitigationCheck`):**
   This forces the process to be self-correcting. If an activity occurs that exposes sensitive attributes to the system (e.g., retrieving race or gender stats), a `BiasMitigationCheck` is triggered later in the pipeline to verify that no inappropriate algorithmic weighting took place.

3. **`precedence` (`BiasMitigationCheck` $\rightarrow$ `FinalDecision`):**
   This constraint mandates that a `FinalDecision` cannot be made unless a `BiasMitigationCheck` has preceded it (serving as a structural gatekeeper). Paired with the response constraint, this guarantees the mitigation check occurs *before* any potentially adverse outcome can be formalized.

4. **`nonsuccession` & `nonchainsuccession` (`CheckApplicantRace/Gender` $\nrightarrow$ `Reject/FinalDecision`):**
   These negative constraints fundamentally break any immediate pipeline from learning a sensitive attribute to rejecting a candidate. It prevents the system from making instantaneous, unreviewed rejections right after a demographic check happens, mandating that intermediate steps (like requesting info or conducting manual checks) pad the trace between the check and the final outcome.
