The visualization is a **process model / workflow diagram** showing the possible paths of a request-handling process, with an inset legend explaining different split and join gateway types.

### Main process

The process begins at activity **a: “register request”** on the left.

From there, the request can proceed through several possible examination or checking activities:

- **b: “examine thoroughly”**
- **c: “examine casually”**
- **d: “check ticket”**
- **f: “reinitiate request”**

These activities are connected by directed arrows and black connector dots, indicating branching, merging, and possible looping behavior.

After examination/checking, the flow moves to:

- **e: “decide”**

From **e**, the process branches into two possible outcomes:

- **g: “pay compensation”**
- **h: “reject request”**

Both outcomes then lead to the final activity:

- **z: “end”**

There are also looping paths involving **f: “reinitiate request”**, suggesting that a request may be sent back for further examination or checking before a decision is made.

### Legend / notation inset

In the bottom-right corner, a grey legend explains the meaning of the black-dot gateway patterns:

- **XOR-split**: one outgoing path is chosen.
- **AND-split**: multiple outgoing paths are taken in parallel.
- **OR-split**: one or more outgoing paths may be taken.
- **XOR-join**: alternative incoming paths merge.
- **AND-join**: parallel paths synchronize before continuing.
- **OR-join**: one or more active incoming paths merge.

Overall, the diagram represents a business process where a request is registered, examined or checked in different ways, possibly reinitiated, then decided upon, resulting either in compensation being paid or the request being rejected before the process ends.
