### 3. Justification for Naming Changes

**1. `Verify Documents`  `Perform Initial Document Verification` / `Perform Document Re-Verification`**
*   **Reasoning:** In process mining, a loop (doing the same task twice) often indicates an exception or a quality check. By distinguishing the *Initial* check from the *Re-Verification*, we clarify that the second instance is dependent on a prior failure or modification.
*   **Contextual Clue:** In C001 and C003, the second instance occurs only after an intermediate step (`Submit Additional` or `Adjust Account`), indicating it is a validation of a fix rather than a new intake check.

**2. `Call Customer`  Specific Intent-Based Names**
*   **Reasoning:** "Call Customer" is an action, but not a process step. In support workflows, the *intent* of the communication defines the process path. Merging a "Request for Info" call with a "Confirmation" call obscures bottlenecks (e.g., are we calling too many times to ask for docs, or to confirm fixes?).
*   **Contextual Clues:**
    *   **C001:** Context states "request missing docs," leading to `Request Missing Docs`.
    *   **C002 (1st):** Context states "clarify account discrepancy," leading to `Investigate Discrepancy`.
    *   **C002 (2nd):** Context states "confirm adjustments made," leading to `Confirm Adjustments`.
    *   **C003:** Context states "Customer stepped out... have them return," and the resource is a `BranchOfficer`. This implies a physical location context rather than a remote telephone call, leading to `In-Branch Recall`.
