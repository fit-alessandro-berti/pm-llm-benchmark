# BPMN-like Textual Process Model: Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling

## 1. Process Name
**Invoice Approval and Payment Scheduling**

## 2. Participants / Swimlanes
1. **Supplier**
2. **Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)**
3. **Purchasing Officer (Dan)**
4. **Department Manager (Carlos)**
5. **Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)**
6. **Supplier Relationship Manager (Karen)**
7. **Accounting System / ERP**

---

## 3. Main Process Flow

### Start Event
- **Start Event:** Invoice received by Accounts Payable, usually via email.

---

## 4. Flow Details

### Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)

1. **Task:** Receive invoice
   - **Input:** Supplier invoice
   - **Note:** Typically arrives by email.

2. **Exclusive Gateway:** Does the invoice contain a valid PO number?
   - **If No:**
     1. **Task:** Send invoice back to supplier requesting missing PO details
     2. **End / Intermediate Wait:** Wait for revised invoice or PO information
   - **If Yes:**
     1. **Task:** Log invoice into accounting system
     2. **Task:** Forward invoice to Purchasing for receipt confirmation

---

### Lane: Purchasing Officer (Dan)

3. **Task:** Review invoice against purchase order
   - **Input:** Invoice, PO in system

4. **Exclusive Gateway:** Do invoice details match the PO?
   - Criteria mentioned: quantities, prices, delivery charges, line items
   - **If Yes:**
     1. **Task:** Send confirmation email back to Accounts Payable
   - **If No:**
     1. **Task:** Contact supplier directly and/or ask AP to hold the invoice
     2. **Task:** Request corrected invoice or clarification
     3. **Optional path:** If PO number is missing, inform Mary that PO information is required before proceeding
     4. **Rework loop:** Once corrected information arrives, return to invoice review

---

### Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk (Mary)

5. **Task:** Confirm goods/services receipt and invoice-PO match
   - **Input:** Confirmation from Purchasing, invoice, PO record in system
   - **Note:** Mary performs matching after receiving receipt confirmation.

6. **Exclusive Gateway:** Are there discrepancies between invoice and PO?
   - Examples: incorrect pricing, missing items, mismatched line items
   - **If Yes:**
     1. **Task:** Email Purchasing team to clarify discrepancy
     2. **Exclusive Gateway:** Can the discrepancy be resolved easily?
        - **If Yes:** Return to matching step
        - **If No:** Escalate to Accounts Payable Supervisor
   - **If No:** Proceed to manager approval request

7. **Task:** System sends automatic email requesting manager sign-off
   - **Trigger:** Only after receipt confirmation and successful matching

---

### Lane: Department Manager (Carlos)

8. **Task:** Review invoice for final approval
   - **Input:** Invoice details, PO, confirmation from Purchasing, any special notes

9. **Exclusive Gateway:** Is the invoice acceptable for approval?
   - Criteria mentioned: quantities, pricing, special notes, budget variance, unexpected items
   - **If Yes:**
     1. **Task:** Approve invoice in system
     2. **Task:** Send approval result back to Accounts Payable
   - **If No:**
     1. **Task:** Reject invoice or request Mary to hold it and investigate further
     2. **Optional loop:** Invoice returns to Accounts Payable for investigation / exception handling

---

### Lane: Accounts Payable Clerk / System

10. **Task:** Schedule invoice for payment
   - **Precondition:** Manager approval obtained and any issues resolved

11. **Task:** Add invoice to next payment cycle / payment run

### End Event
- **End Event:** Invoice scheduled for payment.

---

## 5. Exception Handling / Alternative Paths

### A. Missing PO Number
- If invoice arrives without a PO number:
  - Mary sends it back to supplier requesting more details.
  - Dan also confirms that no invoice is approved without a valid PO reference.
  - Process resumes when PO information or revised invoice is provided.

### B. Invoice-PO Discrepancy
- If quantities, pricing, delivery charges, or line items do not match:
  - Dan contacts supplier directly or asks AP to hold the invoice.
  - Mary may email Purchasing for clarification.
  - Karen may be looped in to contact supplier and validate contract pricing.
  - If unresolved, Mary escalates to Rita.

### C. Escalation to Accounts Payable Supervisor (Rita)
- Used when:
  - Invoice cannot be matched
  - Purchasing cannot resolve discrepancy
  - Invoice is missing information
  - Supplier correction is needed but no quick resolution is possible
- Rita coordinates between departments and suppliers.
- Rita may:
  - Contact suppliers
  - Coordinate AP and Purchasing
  - Work with department heads to create a retrospective PO for informal services
  - Decide whether the invoice should be rejected
- After resolution, invoice returns to normal flow:
  - Purchasing confirmation
  - Manager approval
  - Payment scheduling

### D. Manager Rejection / Hold
- If Carlos sees suspicious items or budget variance:
  - He may reject the invoice or ask Mary to hold it
  - Invoice returns to AP for investigation
  - After clarification, process may re-enter manager approval step

### E. Supplier Relationship Intervention
- Karen is involved indirectly when supplier terms need clarification.
- She can:
  - Request corrected invoice
  - Ask supplier to provide PO number
  - Verify correct pricing against contract
- After issue resolution, invoice continues in the standard flow.

---

## 6. Artifacts / Data Objects

1. **Invoice**
   - Primary document entering the process
2. **Purchase Order (PO)**
   - Required reference for approval
3. **Accounting System / ERP Record**
   - Used to log invoice and track status
4. **Receipt Confirmation**
   - Confirmation that goods/services were received
5. **Confirmation Email from Purchasing**
   - Indicates invoice and PO match
6. **Manager Approval Notification**
   - System-generated request for final approval
7. **Corrected Invoice / Revised Invoice**
   - Used when supplier corrects missing or incorrect information
8. **Contract / Supplier Terms**
   - Referenced by Karen when resolving pricing discrepancies
9. **Retrospective PO**
   - May be created for informally contracted services

---

## 7. Consolidated Process Logic

### Core Approval Path
1. Invoice received
2. Check PO present
3. Log in system
4. Forward to Purchasing
5. Purchasing confirms goods/services and invoice-PO match
6. AP matches invoice to PO
7. If clean, manager approval requested
8. Manager approves
9. AP schedules payment

### Rework / Exception Loop
- Any mismatch, missing PO, or suspicious invoice can cause:
  - Return to supplier
  - Clarification with Purchasing
  - Escalation to supervisor
  - Supplier contract review via Karen
  - Possible retrospective PO
  - Re-entry into standard flow after resolution

---

## 8. Noted Ambiguities / Variations
- The exact handoff order between Mary’s matching step and Dan’s confirmation is described slightly differently by interviewees, but the consistent logic is:
  - **PO must exist**
  - **Purchasing confirms receipt/details**
  - **Invoice must match PO**
  - **Manager approves last**
  - **AP schedules payment afterward**
- In some cases, Dan appears to be the primary reviewer before Mary forwards to manager approval; in others, Mary handles the matching and then routes for approval. The model treats these as coordinated AP/Purchasing validation steps within the same approval chain.
- Rita’s retrospective PO handling appears to be an exception path for non-PO invoices, not standard practice.
