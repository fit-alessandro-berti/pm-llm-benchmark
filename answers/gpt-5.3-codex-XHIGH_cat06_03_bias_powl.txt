Yes — the bias is embedded in the **process structure**, not just in a score formula.

In your model, after preliminary scoring (`C`), applicants go through an XOR:

- `D = CheckLocalAffiliation` (possible score uplift), or  
- `skip` (no uplift possible)

If `D` only gives upside, this creates an **asymmetric advantage path**. Applicants with local/community ties get an extra chance to improve outcomes; others do not.

### Why this is subtly biased
- The feature (“local affiliation”) is not a protected class, but it functions like **social proximity privilege**.
- It may be subjective/discretionary (who gets checked, how affiliation is interpreted).
- A small uplift can flip borderline cases in `E`/`F` (approve vs reject, or better terms vs worse terms).

### Fairness and equity implications
- **Procedural unfairness**: not everyone is evaluated under equivalent criteria.
- **Outcome unfairness**: higher approval rates / better pricing for “insiders.”
- **Compounding inequity**: newcomers, migrants, or less-connected applicants systematically lose access to credit.
- **Proxy risk**: local/community affiliation can correlate with protected traits (race, ethnicity, nationality, socioeconomic status), creating potential **disparate impact** even without explicit protected-attribute use.
- **Governance risk**: hard to justify if uplift is weakly related to actual repayment risk.

So, even for a non-legally protected group, this branch can still undermine equal opportunity in lending and produce structurally inequitable final decisions.
