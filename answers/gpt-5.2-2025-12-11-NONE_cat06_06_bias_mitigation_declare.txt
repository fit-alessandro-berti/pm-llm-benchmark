### Rationale (how the additions mitigate bias)
- **Sensitive attribute check  BiasMitigationCheck (responded_existence/response/succession/chainresponse):** ensures that whenever race/gender/age is accessed, a dedicated fairness step is performed (and in the strictest form, *immediately next*), reducing the risk of “attribute  decision” shortcuts.
- **FinalDecision preceded by BiasMitigationCheck (precedence + chainprecedence):** prevents outcomes from being issued unless a mitigation step has occurred, and enforces it directly before the decision to avoid “check happened long ago but wasn’t applied to the actual decision.”
- **No (immediate) sensitive-check  decision (nonchainsuccession) and no sensitive-check  decision ordering (nonsuccession):** blocks both direct jumps and broader “race/gender/age check then later decision without proper structure,” forcing insertion of mitigation/review steps.
- **ManualReview coexists with sensitive-group decision variants + precedes them (coexistence + precedence):** adds consistent oversight for decisions impacting protected/sensitive demographics (modeled here as activity variants like `Reject_Minority`), reducing disparate treatment by making the review step mandatory and not optional.
