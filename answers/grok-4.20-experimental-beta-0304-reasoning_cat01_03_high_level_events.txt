**High-Level Process Model**

After analyzing the event sequences, I identified **5 distinct high-level process steps**. These groupings are based on:
- Temporal proximity and logical flow
- Shared purpose within the manufacturing workflow
- Resource patterns (human vs. automated)
- Natural quality gates and phase transitions

### 1. Material Preparation
**Grouped Events:**
- Retrieve raw metal sheet
- Scan barcode
- Place metal sheet on table
- Align metal sheet
- Preheat metal sheet

**Rationale:** These events represent the intake, verification, positioning, and initial thermal preparation of raw material. They form a coherent preparatory phase performed before any value-adding transformation (welding). The activities are tightly coupled both temporally and logically — all must occur before the part can be joined.

**High-Level Activity Name:** *Material Preparation*

---

### 2. Welding (Assembly)
**Grouped Events:**
- Pick up welding tool
- Weld corner A
- Weld corner B

**Rationale:** These events constitute the core joining operation. The tool pickup is a direct prerequisite to the welding actions, and all are performed by the same resource (Operator B). This is the primary "transformation" step where the product takes shape.

**High-Level Activity Name:** *Welding*

---

### 3. Post-Weld Quality Inspection
**Grouped Events:**
- Measure weld integrity

**Rationale:** This is a discrete, automated quality gate that occurs immediately after welding and before any further processing (coating). Its purpose is specifically to validate the success of the previous step. It serves as a critical control point — poor weld integrity would likely trigger rework or rejection.

**High-Level Activity Name:** *Post-Weld Inspection*

---

### 4. Surface Finishing
**Grouped Events:**
- Apply protective coating
- Dry coating

**Rationale:** These two steps form a clear finishing subprocess — applying a protective layer and then curing it. They share the same goal (surface protection) and use related equipment (Coating Unit  Heating Unit). This is a distinct phase following successful weld validation.

**High-Level Activity Name:** *Protective Coating*

---

### 5. Final Quality Verification
**Grouped Events:**
- Visual check

**Rationale:** This is the final human-driven validation step after all physical processing is complete. It serves as the last quality gate before the product is considered finished. The fact that it occurs after coating suggests it evaluates the overall product state (welds + coating + appearance).

**High-Level Activity Name:** *Final Visual Inspection*

---

### Summary of High-Level Process Flow

**Material Preparation  Welding  Post-Weld Inspection  Protective Coating  Final Visual Inspection**

This abstraction reduces 12 low-level events down to **5 meaningful process stages**, providing much better visibility into the manufacturing workflow while preserving the logical sequence and quality control structure.

The model is consistent across both cases (A1 and B2) in the sample, suggesting these groupings represent the standard process.
