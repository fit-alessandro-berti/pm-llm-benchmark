## Rationale for Added Bias-Mitigating Constraints

1. **existence["ManualReview"]** and **existence["BiasMitigationCheck"]**: Ensures these fairness-enforcing activities always appear in traces, mandating their use in the loan review process.

2. **coexistence["CheckApplicantRace"]["ManualReview"]** and **coexistence["CheckApplicantGender"]["ManualReview"]**: Forces a `ManualReview` whenever sensitive attributes like race or gender are checked, preventing automated decisions for protected groups without human oversight.

3. **response["CheckApplicantRace"]["BiasMitigationCheck"]** and **response["CheckApplicantGender"]["BiasMitigationCheck"]**: Guarantees that after checking sensitive attributes, a `BiasMitigationCheck` must eventually occur before any other activities, blocking direct progression to decisions.

4. **precedence["BiasMitigationCheck"]["FinalDecision"]** and **precedence["ManualReview"]["FinalDecision"]**: Ensures no `FinalDecision` (Approve/Reject) can occur without prior `BiasMitigationCheck` or `ManualReview`, enforcing fairness checks before outcomes.

5. **nonsuccession["CheckApplicantRace"]["Reject"]** and **nonsuccession["CheckApplicantGender"]["Reject"]**: Prevents direct succession from sensitive attribute checks to `Reject`, eliminating immediate biased rejections.

6. **nonchainsuccession["CheckApplicantRace"]["Reject"]** and **nonchainsuccession["CheckApplicantGender"]["Reject"]**: Strengthens the above by also preventing `Reject` from directly causing sensitive attribute checks in reverse.

## How These Reduce Bias
These constraints create a **fairness gatekeeper pattern**: Sensitive attribute checks trigger mandatory fairness interventions (`BiasMitigationCheck`, `ManualReview`) that must precede decisions. Direct paths to `Reject` are blocked, forcing structured review for protected applicants. This ensures consistency and reduces disparate treatment based on `ApplicantRace` or `ApplicantGender`.
