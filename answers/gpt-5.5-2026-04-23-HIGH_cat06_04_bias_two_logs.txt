The **Group B (Unprotected Group) log exhibits bias**, specifically a **favorable bias toward applicants who are local residents and members of the “Highland Civic Darts Club.”**

This bias manifests through the **ScoreAdjustment** column.

### How the bias appears

In Group B:

- Applicants are marked as `LocalResident = TRUE`.
- Some applicants also have `CommunityGroup = Highland Civic Darts Club`.
- Those applicants receive a **+10 “Community Boost”** to their preliminary score.
- The adjusted score is then used in later stages and appears to influence the final decision.

For example:

| Case | Preliminary Score | Community Group | Adjustment | Final Score | Decision |
|------|------------------|----------------|------------|-------------|----------|
| U001 | 720 | Highland Civic Darts Club | +10 | 730 | Approved |
| U003 | 695 | Highland Civic Darts Club | +10 | 705 | Approved |
| U002 | 710 | None | 0 | 710 | Rejected |

The most notable case is **U003**. This applicant starts with a lower preliminary score of **695**, receives a **+10 community boost**, and is ultimately **approved** with an adjusted score of **705**. Meanwhile, applicants such as **P002** in Group A and **U002** in Group B have preliminary scores of **710** and are rejected.

### Comparison with Group A

In Group A:

- All applicants have `LocalResident = FALSE`.
- All have `CommunityGroup = None`.
- No applicant receives a positive score adjustment.
- ScoreAdjustment is either `0` or `N/A`.

| Case | Preliminary Score | Adjustment | Final Decision |
|------|------------------|------------|----------------|
| P001 | 720 | 0 | Approved |
| P002 | 710 | 0 | Rejected |
| P003 | 740 | 0 | Approved |

Group A applicants do not appear to have access to the same community-based score increase. Therefore, even if they have comparable or better preliminary scores, they may not receive the benefit that certain Group B applicants receive.

### Nature of the bias

The bias is not simply that Group B has a higher overall approval rate — both groups have 2 approvals out of 3 cases. The issue is that **Group B contains a mechanism that gives extra points based on LocalResident and CommunityGroup status**, while Group A does not receive an equivalent adjustment.

This creates a form of **systematic preferential treatment**:

- Being a local resident appears connected to eligibility for favorable treatment.
- Membership in the Highland Civic Darts Club triggers a +10 boost.
- That boost can change the outcome of a case.
- Applicants without that community affiliation, including all Group A applicants, do not receive the same advantage.

### Conclusion

The **Group B log shows evidence of bias through the “Community Boost” score adjustment**. The adjustment favors applicants who are local residents and members of a specific community group. Because this advantage is not available to Group A, it may create systematic differences in final decisions and could disadvantage the protected group indirectly.
