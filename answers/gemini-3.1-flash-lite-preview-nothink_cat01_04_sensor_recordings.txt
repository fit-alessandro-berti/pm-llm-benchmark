### Rationale for Labels

#### 1. Idle / Standby (Events 1–3 and 13–15)
*   **Rationale:** The readings are at baseline levels: temperature is ambient (20–21°C), vibration is zero, material flow is zero, and energy consumption is minimal (0.40–0.50 kWh). This represents the machine in a resting state before and after the cycle.

#### 2. Cutting Metal (Events 4–6)
*   **Rationale:** This phase shows a sharp spike in temperature (50–58°C) and vibration (30–38 Hz), indicating high-friction mechanical work. The steady "Material Flow" (5 units/s) combined with the advancing "Tool Position" (5 to 10 mm) suggests the machine is actively removing material or cutting through a workpiece.

#### 3. Assembling Parts (Events 7–8)
*   **Rationale:** The temperature drops from the cutting peak to a stable 40°C, and vibration decreases significantly to a consistent 10 Hz. The "Material Flow" decreases (2 units/s), suggesting the pace has slowed to align or join components rather than removing material. The "Tool Position" remains constant at 15 mm, suggesting the tool is holding or applying pressure to secure a part.

#### 4. Welding (Event 9)
*   **Rationale:** This is the most distinct outlier. Energy consumption jumps to 5.0 kWh (the highest in the log), accompanied by a massive spike in temperature (80°C) and vibration (50 Hz). The "Material Flow" drops to zero, indicating that the machine is stationary and using high energy to perform a localized thermal process—characteristic of a welding operation.

#### 5. Quality Inspection / Finishing (Events 10–12)
*   **Rationale:** Following the high-energy welding event, the temperature drops rapidly, and energy usage stabilizes to a lower level (1.0–1.6 kWh). Vibration is minimal (1–5 Hz). The "Tool Position" shifts forward to 20 mm with a very low flow rate. This pattern suggests a "cleanup" phase, a final precision measurement, or an automated quality inspection scan of the completed weld before the machine resets.
