**Identification of recurring activities and contextual differences:**
- **Verify Documents** recurs in C001 and C003. The first occurrence is always an *initial* check right after request initiation (often incomplete or basic identity/physical ID check). The second occurrence is a *secondary/re-verification* after additional documents are provided or an account adjustment occurs (explicitly described as "re-checks" or "re-verify").
- **Call Customer** recurs in C002 (and appears in other cases with varying intent). Its meaning changes based on purpose and position: requesting missing documents (C001), clarifying a discrepancy (early in C002), confirming adjustments (late in C002), or recalling the customer to the branch (C003). These are drawn directly from the "Additional Context" descriptions.
- **Initiate Request** and **Approve Request** do not recur within any single case, but were lightly standardized for clarity and channel differences (online vs. branch). **Submit Additional** and **Adjust Account** appear once per relevant case and were made slightly more precise.

**Justification for naming choices:** New names reflect *position in the flow* (initial vs. secondary) and *specific purpose* from the Additional Context column and timestamps (e.g., "re-checks now that ID scan is provided" vs. initial check; "clarify account discrepancy" vs. "follow-up call to confirm adjustments"). This eliminates ambiguity for process analysis, clearly distinguishing verification loops and different types of customer contact. Names are consistent where the underlying meaning is similar across cases but specific enough to highlight contextual roles.
